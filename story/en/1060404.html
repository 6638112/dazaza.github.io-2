DDT dump site off L.A. coast much bigger than scientists expected
DDT倾倒场地OFF L.A.海岸比科学家预期的要大得多

2021-04-28 11:17:46

When the research vessel Sally Ride set sail for Santa Catalina Island to map an underwater graveyard of DDT waste barrels, its crew had high hopes of documenting for the first time just how many corroded containers littered the seafloor off the coast of Los Angeles.

当研究船只骑行帆船为圣卡塔利娜岛举办苏达特垃圾桶的水下墓地时，它的船员首次寄予良好的记录似乎有多少腐蚀容器在洛杉矶海岸乱扔了海底。

But as the scientists on deck began interpreting sonar images gathered by two deep-sea robots, they were quickly overwhelmed. It was like trying to count stars in the Milky Way.

但随着甲板上的科学家开始解释由两个深海机器人收集的声纳图像，它们很快被淹没了。这就像尝试用银河系中的星星算。

The dumpsite, it turned out, was much, much bigger than expected. After spending two weeks surveying a swath of seafloor larger than the city of San Francisco, the scientists could find no end to the dumping ground. They could’ve kept going in any direction, they said, and uncovered even more.

事实证明，垃圾场很多，比预期大得多。在花费两周后测量比旧金山市大的海底，科学家们可能会发现倾销地没有结束。他们说，他们可能会陷入任何方向，并更加露出。

“I was pretty shocked that it just kept extending as far as it did,” said Eric Terrill of UC San Diego’s Scripps Institution of Oceanography, who led the mission of 31 scientists and crew members. “We couldn’t keep up with the flow of data coming in.”

“我非常震惊，它刚刚尽可能地延伸，”UC圣地亚哥的Scrips Ineachocion Isociption的Eric Terrill表示，他领导了31名科学家和船员的使命。 “我们无法跟上进入的数据流。”

Terrill shared these findings Monday in a U.S. congressional briefing led by Sen. Dianne Feinstein (D-Calif.), who has been pushing for action since The Times reported last fall that the nation’s largest DDT manufacturer once dumped its waste into the deep ocean. As many as half a million barrels could still be underwater today, according to old records and a recent UC Santa Barbara study that provided the first photos of this pollution bubbling 3,000 feet under the sea.

“This mission confirms my worst fear: that possibly hundreds of thousands of barrels and DDT-laced sediment were dumped just 12 miles off our coast,” said Feinstein, who said she plans to ask the U.S. Justice Department to look into companies that may have illegally dumped waste into the ocean and whether they can be held accountable.

“这项任务证实了我最害怕的恐惧：这可能是数十万桶和滴滴涕的沉积物偏离我们的海岸只倾倒了12英里，”费恩斯坦说，她计划要求美国司法部调查可能拥有的公司非法倾倒垃圾进入海洋，是否可以持有责任。

“I’m pleased the Biden administration shares my concern about this issue and took action quickly. It’s critical that this momentum continues,” she said. “We need everyone to come to the table with all the resources necessary to solve a problem of this size.”

“我很高兴拜登政府分享了我对这个问题的关注，并迅速采取行动。这势头仍在继续，这是至关重要的，“她说。 “我们需要大家与解决这个规模问题所必需的所有资源来到桌面上。”

For Terrill, trying to count the barrels was like the episode of “I Love Lucy” in which Lucy gets a job in a chocolate factory and struggles to keep up with a speeding conveyor belt. But as his team pored over gigabytes and gigabytes of sonar data, they were finally able to identify at least 27,000 barrel-sized anomalies — and more than 100,000 total debris objects on the seafloor — with the help of some computer analysis.

对于蒂尔，试图计算桶就像“我喜欢露西”的集中，露西在巧克力工厂获得了一份工作，并努力跟上超速传送带。但随着他的团队在千兆藤和声纳数据的千兆字节上欣赏千兆字节和千兆字节，他们终于能够在一些计算机分析的帮助下鉴定了至少27,000桶大小的桶大小的异常 - 以及超过100,000个总碎屑物体 - 在一些计算机分析。

The actual number of barrels could be even higher, he noted, because barrels that were half-buried by sediment, for example, may have been overlooked by the computer.

他指出，实际的桶数可能更高，因为例如，由沉积物半掩埋的桶可能已经被计算机忽略了。

The two-week expedition, made possible by a unique partnership between Scripps and the National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration’s Office of Marine and Aviation Operations, had deployed two high-tech robots that alternated scoping more than 36,000 acres of seafloor with high-resolution sonar: As one robot worked underwater, covering about 140 football fields per hour, the other recharged, offloaded its data and got recalibrated by scientists on deck.

这是两周的探险，通过扰乱和国家海洋和大气管理局的海洋和航空行动办公室之间的独特伙伴关系，已经部署了两家高新技术机器人，交替占地36,000英亩的海底，高分辨率的声纳：随着一个机器人在水下工作，覆盖每小时约140个足球场，另一个充电，卸载其数据并通过甲板上的科学家重新校验。

A number of unexpected challenges, including stubborn winds and heavy rain, complicated the process. When Terrill realized his crew would not be able to manually count each individual data point that the robots were collecting from the seafloor, he called in “the big data guns”: Sophia Merrifield, a physical oceanographer and researcher in the Marine Physical Laboratory at Scripps.

一些意想不到的挑战，包括顽固的风和大雨，复杂的过程。当蒂尔意识到他的机组人员将无法手动计算机器人从海底收集的每个数据点，他呼吁“大数据枪”：索菲亚Merrifield，Scrips海洋物理实验室的物理实验室和研究员。

Merrifield was astonished when Terrill sent her the first batch of sonar images. She jumped into action and started writing a computer algorithm that could sort through the immense amount of data while also accounting for the topography, the physics and the way sonar works in that part of the ocean.

当蒂尔送她第一批声纳图像时，Merrifield很惊讶。她跳进了行动，并开始写一项可以通过巨大数量的数据来编写一种计算机算法，同时也会考虑地形，物理和声纳在海洋的那一部分工作的方式。

“Think about the geology on the bottom, because that affects the acoustics,” she said. “We talked pretty constantly while [Terrill] was out there: What are ways that we could train an algorithm to detect these targets in an automated way?”

“想想底部的地质，因为这影响了声学，”她说。 “我们在那里出现了[Terrill]的时候谈得漂亮：我们可以训练一种以自动化方式检测这些目标的方法是什么？”

Another shock was the fact that the dumping was clearly not contained to one designated spot. Everywhere the crew surveyed, there were barrels, said Mark Gold, who has followed the DDT problem as a marine scientist since the 1990s and currently serves as Gov. Gavin Newsom’s deputy secretary for coastal and ocean policy.

另一个震惊是倾倒的事实明显没有包含在一个指定的地方。随着1990年代以来，Mark Gold的各种船员被调查的桶装是桶，他是海洋科学家，目前正在担任驻军兼海洋政策副局长。

“That to me was horrifying,” he said. “This is such a dark part of our environmental history.”

“对我来说是可怕的，”他说。 “这是我们环境历史的暗淡部分。”

Once hailed as a wonder pesticide, DDT saved crops and combated malaria across the world. The U.S. banned its use in 1972, but the chemical, dichlorodiphenyltrichloroethane, is so stable it continues to poison the environment and move up the food chain.

曾经被誉为奇迹农药，滴滴涕拯救了作物，并在全球结合疟疾。美国在1972年禁止使用，但化学品二氯甲酸二氯苯基氯乙烷是如此稳定，它继续毒害环境并向上移动食物链。

Significant amounts of DDT-related compounds are still accumulating today in Southern California dolphins, and a recent study linked the presence of these persistent chemicals to an aggressive cancer in sea lions.

大量的DDT相关化合物在南加州海豚中仍然积累，最近的研究将这些持久化学品的存在联系在海狮的侵略性癌症中。

For decades, the nation’s largest DDT maker operated its plant on the border of Los Angeles and Torrance. A $140-million Superfund battle in the 1990s exposed the company’s disposal of toxic waste through sewage pipes that poured into the sea — but all the DDT manufacturing waste that was dumped into the deep ocean had drawn comparatively little attention.

几十年来，全国最大的德尔特制造商在洛杉矶和托尔兰斯的边界运行了植物。 1990年代的140万美元的超级斗争通过倾注于海洋的污水管道，公开了公司的有毒废物 - 但倾倒到深海的所有DDT制造废物相对较少的关注。

Renewed outcry in recent months has focused on both the dumpsite and the Superfund site, which is still awaiting cleanup after more than 20 years of meetings and extensive studies.

最近几个月的重新屈服于垃圾场和超级优惠网站，在20多年的会议和广泛研究后仍在等待清理。

Gold, who has been in numerous high-level meetings on this issue, said that both state and federal agencies, including the U.S. Environmental Protection Agency, have been following the Scripps-NOAA mapping efforts with great interest. Revisiting this history of deep-sea dumping has also raised questions of what other toxic chemicals and contaminants, in addition to DDT, could still be in the ocean today. Records show that numerous companies had dumped industrial waste in this area for several decades, beginning in the 1930s.

在这个问题上一直处于众多高级别会议上的黄金，表示，包括美国环境保护局的国家和联邦机构，一直遵循奇皮夫 - Noaa以极大的兴趣训练努力。重新审视这种深海倾销的历史也提出了其他有毒化学品和污染物的问题，除了DDT之外，今天还可以在海洋中。记录表明，在20世纪30年代开始，众多公司在这个地区倾倒了这个地区的工业废物。

“I can’t tell you how thankful I am that NOAA and Scripps took this initiative to really get the ball rolling,” Gold said.

“我不能告诉你如何感谢Noaa和Scripps认为这项倡议真正得到了球滚动，”Gold说。

David Valentine, whose UC Santa Barbara research team first came across about 60 barrels on the seafloor while conducting a different research project, said the new mapping data revealed interesting hot spots — and “hot lines,” suggesting the path of a ship or barge as the dumping occurred — that will help scientists figure out where to focus further sediment and chemical studies.

David Valentine，其UC Santa Barbara研究团队在海底上遇到了大约60个桶，同时进行了一个不同的研究项目，表示新的映射数据显示有趣的热点 - 和“热线”，暗示船舶或驳船的路径倾倒发生 - 这将有助于科学家们省略在哪里聚焦进一步的沉积物和化学研究。

“We’re starting to see the patterns of waste dumping that are going to guide us as we attempt to define the whole scope of this problem,” said Valentine, who has been inundated with calls and emails from fellow researchers, government officials and members of the public since The Times published its report in October.

“我们开始看到正在指导我们的废物倾倒的模式，因为我们试图定义这个问题的整个范围，”Waltorine表示，他们被研究人员，政府官员和成员的呼叫和电子邮件淹没了自10月份发表其报告以来的公众。

It’s still unknown what’s inside any of these barrels — or how much this deep-sea pollution has been moving through the marine ecosystem, he said. “Right now, we’re seeing the tip of the iceberg.”

他说，它仍然是未知的内容，或者这种深海污染已经通过海洋生态系统进行了多少。 “现在，我们看到了冰山一角。”

He felt a jumble of emotions as he examined the new data — maps of the seafloor covered with so many mysterious dots that hinted at just how widespread the dumping could be.

当他审查了许多神秘点覆盖的海底的新数据 - 暗示了倾销时，他感到了混乱的情感。

“The Southern California offshore dumping industry clearly got away with dumping wherever they wanted,” he said, “and that really makes me wonder: What else did they get away with?”

“南加州近海倾销产业显然逃离了他们想要的倾销，”他说，“这真的让我想知道：他们还逃脱了什么？”

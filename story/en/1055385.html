Download PDF Abstract: A fundamental relation in celestial mechanics is Kepler's equation, linkingan orbit's mean anomaly to its eccentric anomaly and eccentricity. Beingtranscendental, the equation cannot be directly solved for eccentric anomaly byconventional treatments; much work has been devoted to approximate methods.Here, we give an explicit integral solution, utilizing methods recently appliedto the 'geometric goat problem' and to the dynamics of spherical collapse. Thesolution is given as a ratio of contour integrals; these can be efficientlycomputed via numerical integration for arbitrary eccentricities. The method isfound to be highly accurate in practice, with our C++ implementationoutperforming conventional root-finding and series approaches by a factorgreater than two.

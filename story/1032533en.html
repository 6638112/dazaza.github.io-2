托布勒地理第一定律

2020-11-01 03:57:32

Jump to navigation Jump to search The First Law of Geography, according to Waldo Tobler, is "everything is related to everything else, but near things are more related than distant things." [1] This first law is the foundation of the fundamental concepts of spatial dependence and spatial autocorrelation and is utilized specifically for the inverse distance weighting method for spatial interpolation and to support the regionalized variable theory for kriging. [2] It is a modern formulation of David Hume's principle of contiguity.

根据沃尔多·托布勒(Waldo Tobler)的说法，跳转到导航跳跃来搜索地理第一定律，一切都与其他一切相关，但近物比远物更相关。[1]这第一定律是空间相关性和空间自相关基本概念的基础，专门用于空间插值的反距离加权法，并支持克里格法的区域化变量理论。[2]它是大卫·休谟邻接性原则的现代表述。

Tobler first presented his seminal idea during a meeting of the International Geographical Union's Commission on Qualitative Methods held in 1969 and later published by him in 1970. Though simple in its presentation, this idea is profound. Without it, "the full range of conditions anywhere on the Earth's surface could in principle be found packed within any small area. There would be no regions of approximately homogeneous conditions to be described by giving attributes to area objects. Topographic surfaces would vary chaotically, with slopes that were everywhere infinite, and the contours of such surfaces would be infinitely dense and contorted. Spatial analysis, and indeed life itself, would be impossible." [3]

托布勒第一次提出他的开创性想法是在1969年举行的国际地理联盟定性方法委员会会议上，后来他在1970年发表了这一想法。这个想法虽然表达简单，但却是深刻的。如果没有它，地球表面任何地方的各种条件原则上都可以在任何一个小区域内找到。将不存在通过给区域对象赋予属性来描述近似同类条件的区域。地形表面会变得杂乱无章，到处都是无限的坡度，这样的表面的轮廓将是无限密集和扭曲的。空间分析，甚至是生命本身，都是不可能的。

Less well known is his second law, which complements the first: "The phenomenon external to an area of interest affects what goes on inside". [4]

不太为人所知的是他的第二定律，它补充了第一定律：兴趣区域外部的现象会影响内部发生的事情。[4]。

The theory is based upon the concept of the friction of distance "where distance itself hinders interaction between places. The farther two places are apart, the greater the hindrance", [5] or cost. For example, one is less likely to travel across town to purchase a sandwich than walk to the corner store for the same sandwich. In this example hindrance, or cost, can readily be counted in time (amount of time as well as value of time), transportation costs and personal muscle energy loss which are added to the price of the purchase and thus result in high levels of friction. The friction of distance and the increase in cost combine causing the distance decay effect.

该理论基于距离摩擦的概念，即距离本身会阻碍地方之间的相互作用。两个地方相距越远，障碍就越大，成本也就越高。例如，一个人不太可能穿过城市去买三明治，而不是步行到街角的商店去买同样的三明治。在这个例子中，障碍或成本可以很容易地计算在时间(时间量以及时间价值)、运输成本和个人肌肉能量损失上，它们被添加到购买价格中，从而导致高水平的摩擦。距离的摩擦和成本的增加共同导致了距离衰减效应。

The Future of Group Messaging
集团消息的未来

2021-03-11 00:44:52

But I love it. Group messaging is the glue that holds modern communication together. From the corporate Slack channels, the casual iMessage groups, and the entertaining Discord servers, we as people are increasingly spending time in group messages. But there's an Achilles heel to group messaging, a bad thread that runs deep into the fabric of digital communication. From the very beginning our systems have followed one crucial rule: New content must appear in line, no matter how many people are chatting. This one rule for our messaging feeds, this guideline for our conversations, remains largely unbroken, and has constrained group messages for far too long. Here, I’ll propose a solution that will set group messaging free.

但我爱它。集团消息是掌握现代通信的胶水。从公司松弛频道，休闲的IMESSage组和娱乐Discord服务器，我们越来越多地在集团信息中花费时间。但是，在一群人的消息传递中，一个伴随着数字通信的织物的糟糕线程。从一开始，我们的系统遵循一个关键规则：新内容必须在线出现，无论有多少人在聊天。这是我们的消息传递源的规则，这方面的对话指南仍然很大程度上是不间断的，并且已经过时了解了约束的群组信息。在这里，我将提出一个解决方案，将自由设置组消息传递。

Overlapping conversations are the bane of our modern existence. In physical spaces, people are easily able to create separate spaces for conversation by angling themselves, or moving to a separate area. But we can’t do that online. At least not very easily.

重叠的谈话是我们现代存在的祸根。在物理空间中，人们可以轻松地通过钓鱼自己创建单独的空间，或者通过钓鱼自己或移动到一个单独的区域。但我们不能在网上做到这一点。至少不是很容易。

Every group chat is currently pained by the same frustration: two conversations happening at once, each person talking over the other. The format for group messaging is still structured the same as a 2 person conversation, yet many groups have upwards of 100 users. To be fair, like any problem, there are attempted solutions. Most messaging platforms have attempted to solve this problem in one way or another:

每个小组聊天目前都是通过同样的挫败感：每次发生两次对话，每个人都在谈论。组消息传递的格式仍然是2个人对话的结构相同，但许多组都有100个用户。要公平，就像任何问题一样，有解决方案。大多数消息传递平台都试图以一种方式解决这个问题：

1. A simple quote, tagging a user who previously sent the message. 2. A reply feature, which tags the previous message. 3. A threaded chat, which opens in another window. And at this point, Slack’s method of creating a separate thread in response to any message is by far the most effective, but feels clunky within the interface, as if it were an afterthought. A few group messaging platforms, like Slack and Discord, take it a step further and allow topical rooms within separate channels. But as the number of people in the group increases, those overlapping conversations happen in these topical channels as well.

1.一个简单的报价，标记先前发送了消息的用户。 2.回复功能，标记上一个消息。 3.一个线程聊天，在另一个窗口中打开。此时，Slack的方法是响应于任何消息创建单独线程的方法是迄今为止最有效的，但界面内的笨重，似乎它是一个事后。一些组消息传递平台，如松弛和不和谐，请进一步迈出一步，允许单独的渠道内的局部房间。但随着集团人数的增加，这些局部渠道的重叠谈话也会发生。

Each of these attempts have merit, but neglect to maturely deal with the root issue: The in-line framework that defines messaging. So then, what is our solution? How do we make this experience better for everyone involved What is the future of group messaging?

这些尝试中的每一个都有优点，但疏忽成熟地处理根问题：定义消息传递的在线框架。那么，我们的解决方案是什么？我们如何为每个人参与其中涉及的人的未来是如何更好的？

It’s time for group messaging platforms to move away from a single-feed format. It’s time to make content accessible from outside the main message view. To evolve group messaging, we need to add another view to group messages. Another page. One for messaging. One for posts. Content. Everything else. ... "But Jarren!" You may say, "The whole point of messaging is to share live! We talk about things as they happen inside a chat thread!" Agreed. The message feed is the live expression of the group, with messages and posts directly in-line, while the content feed holds only the “high-priority” bits.

现在是Group Messaging平台远离单馈格格式的时候了。现在是时候从主留言视图外访问内容了。要进化组消息传递，我们需要将另一个视图添加到组消息。另一页。一个用于消息传递。一个用于帖子。内容。其他一切。 ...＆＃34;但jarren！＆＃34;你可以说，＆＃34;整个消息点是分享生活！我们谈论他们在聊天线程内发生的事情！＆＃34;同意。消息Feed是该组的实时表达式，通过邮件和帖子直接在线，而内容馈送仅保存“高优先级”位。

The message view remains largely untouched. This is the traditional group chat you know and love. The only difference: users comment on shared posts instead of simply replying in-line. If desired, a notifications tab could signal when comments are made offscreen.

消息视图在很大程度上保持不变。这是你认识和爱的传统群组聊天。唯一的区别：用户对共享帖子的评论而不是简单地回复在线。如果需要，当评论偏移屏幕时，通知选项卡可以发信号。

Conversations continue to happen naturally, and when a tangent is made, the comments can continue without crowding the normal message view. This is a group  within a chat. Images, videos, and links become posts, but so can a message or a collection of messages.

对话继续发生自然，并且当切相时，评论可以在不挤满正常消息视图的情况下继续。这是一个聊天中的一组。图像，视频和链接成为帖子，但可以留言或消息集。

The second view in our new group structure is a place to view posts. Notable content and the relevant comments are shown here. Just like scrolling through a facebook group, users are able to see recent happenings within their group chat. This is a place to engage, catch up, and reply. As a group continues to grow, and as a platform such as this matures, different sort styles could be added to show more relevant information. Again, not taking priority over the in-line message view, but adding context to a group experience.

我们新组结构中的第二个视图是要查看帖子的地方。这里显示了值得注意的内容和相关评论。就像通过Facebook小组滚动一样，用户能够在群组聊天中看到最近的事件。这是一个参与，赶上和回复的地方。由于一个小组继续增长，并且作为这样的平台，可以添加不同的排序样式以显示更多相关信息。同样，不优先于在线消息视图，但将上下文添加到组体验中。

The future of group messaging is a further maturation of the platform. Removing the wall between chats and groups will empower users to have higher quality conversations instead of simply being restrained by the structure of their social platforms. These two changes: Adding in-line posts to messaging, and adding a new view for posts and comments, would open a whole new world of social network possibilities. This is the social network killer. I’m sharing this concept to give back to the industry that has given me so much. Group chats are how I’ve maintained some of my deepest personal relationships, and I want to enable better conversations for everybody. I ask instead, share this idea, steal this idea. I want this functionality inside of Discord, Slack, Messenger, Telegram, iMessage and Signal. I’ve barely scraped the surface to the potential of this format. A mature social platform with messaging at the core can be so much more than a sum of its parts.

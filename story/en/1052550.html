Tim Berners-Lee Says Too Many Young People Are Excluded From Web
Tim Berners-Lee说太多年轻人被排除在网上

2021-03-16 04:15:55

In a letter to mark 32 years of the web, its founder says getting 2.2 billion fully online must be a priority

在致马克32年的信中，它的创始人表示，在线全网上获得22亿美元必须是优先事项

Too many young people around the world are excluded from accessing the web, and getting them online should be a priority for the post-Covid era, Tim Berners-Lee has said.

世界各地的年轻人被排除在访问网络之外，并在线获取它们应该是Covid Era后的优先事项，Tim Berners-Lee表示。

In a letter published to mark the 32nd birthday of the web, its founder says the opportunity “to reimagine our world and create something better” in the aftermath of Covid-19 must be channelled to getting internet access to the third of people aged between 15 and 24 who are offline.

在发布的一封信中标记为网络的32岁生日时，它的创始人表示，在Covid-19的后果中，它的机会“重新想象我们的世界并创造了更好的东西”，必须被窜为让互联网访问15岁之间的三分之二和24岁是离线的。

“The influence of young people is felt across their communities and online networks,” Berners-Lee writes. “But today we’re seeing just a fraction of what’s possible. Because while we talk about a generation of ‘digital natives’, far too many young people remain excluded and unable to use the web to share their talents and ideas.

“在社区和在线网络中感受到年轻人的影响，”伯尔斯 - 李写道。 “但今天我们看到了一小部分可能。因为在我们谈论一代“数字本地人”的时候，太多的年轻人仍然被排除在外，无法使用网络分享他们的才能和想法。

“A third of young people have no internet access at all. Many more lack the data, devices and reliable connection they need to make the most of the web. In fact, only the top third of under-25s have a home internet connection, according to Unicef, leaving 2.2 billion young people without the stable access they need to learn online, which has helped so many others continue their education during the pandemic.”

“三分之一的年轻人根本没有互联网。更多缺乏数据，设备和可靠连接，他们需要充分利用Web。事实上，只有25岁以下的前三分之一都有一个家庭互联网连接，据儿童基金会辞据，没有稳定的访问，他们需要在线学习，这有助于这么多人在大流行期间继续教育。“

Even though young people are more likely than the typical global citizen to have internet access – roughly half the world is online, but the figure rises to 70% of people aged between 15 and 25 – Berners-Lee argues that aiming to connect every young person in the world to the web would reap dividends.

虽然年轻人比典型的全球公民更有可能互联网接入 - 大约有一半的世界上在线，但该数字升至15至25岁的人的70％ - 伯纳尔李认为，旨在连接每个年轻人在世界上，网上将获得股息。

He also says doing so would be relatively cheap compared with the cost of many government programmes launched over the last 12 months. He estimates that an investment of $428bn (£307bn) over the next decade would provide everyone with a quality broadband connection.

他还说，与过去12个月的许多政府方案的成本相比，相比，相比之下。他估计，未来十年的投资428亿美元（3070亿英镑）将为每个人提供质量宽带连接。

Rosemary Leith, who co-founded the Web Foundation with Berners-Lee, said access to the web should be a basic right for young people, similar to education. “If half a generation of young people are unable to use the tools to thrive in a digital world – to learn new skills, run businesses, build communities, participate in democratic debate – society as a whole will miss out on their talents, ideas and efforts,” she said.

与Berners-Lee共同创立的Web基金会的罗斯玛丽莱蒂斯表示，对网络的访问应该是年轻人的基本权利，类似于教育。 “如果一代年轻人无法使用这些工具在数字世界中茁壮成长 - 要学习新技能，运行企业，建造社区，作为一个整体参与民主辩论 - 社会将错过他们的才华，想法和努力，“她说。

The need to bring young people online was demonstrated during the Covid pandemic, as countries around the world moved to remote learning by default. The UK government was accused of falling short on its promises to provide laptops to poorer pupils months into the national lockdown.

“More than three-quarters of our year 10 pupils do not have access, regularly and consistently, to a device or the internet at home,” Steve Howell, the headteacher at the City of Birmingham school, said in June. “The most disadvantaged pupils are hardest hit with IT poverty, and the fact this has taken so long is really making things worse.”

“四分之三，我们的10个以上的学生在伯明翰学校的校长·豪尔·斯蒂夫·豪威尔（Steve Howell）于6月说：”超过四分之一的学生就没有往来，在家“最弱势的学生们陷入困境中最难打击，这事实这么长时间真的让事情变得更糟。”

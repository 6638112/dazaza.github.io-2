Interview with String Theorist Michio Kaku
采访弦理论Michio Kaku

2021-04-04 05:23:35

The physicist on Newton finding inspiration amid the great plague, how the multiverse can unite religions, and why a ‘theory of everything’ is within our grasp

牛顿的物理学家在伟大的瘟疫中发现灵感，多个人可以联合宗教，以及为什么“一切理论”是在我们的掌握中

M ichio Kaku is a professor of theoretical physics at City College, New York, a proponent of string theory but also a well-known populariser of science, with multiple TV appearances and several bestselling books behind him. His latest book, The God Equation, is a clear and accessible examination of the quest to combine Einstein’s general relativity with quantum theory to create an all-encompassing “theory of everything” about the nature of the universe.

M ICHIO KAKU是纽约城市学院理论物理学教授，是一个串理论的支持者，也是一个知名的科学普照，拥有多个电视外观和他后面的几本畅销书。他的最新书籍是神方程，是一个明确而且可访问的审查，以便将爱因斯坦与量子理论相结合的追求，以创造一个关于宇宙性质的全包的“一切理论”。

How close do you believe science is to accomplishing a theory of everything? Well, I think we actually have the theory but not in its final form. It hasn’t been tested yet and Nobel prize winners have taken opposite points of view concerning something called string theory. I’m the co-founder of string field theory, which is one of the main branches of string theory, so I have some “skin in the game”. I try to be fair and balanced. I think we’re on the verge of a new era. New experiments are being done to detect deviations from the Standard Model. Plus, we have the mystery of dark matter. Any of these unexplored areas could give a clue as to the theory of everything.

您认为科学的关系是如何实现一切的理论？好吧，我认为我们实际上有理论，但不是最终形式。它尚未进行测试，诺贝尔奖获奖者已经采取了关于称为串理论的东西的相反观点。我是字符串字段理论的联合创始人，这是弦理论的主要分支之一，所以我在游戏中有一些“皮肤”。我试着公平和平衡。我认为我们正在濒临一个新的时代。正在进行新实验以检测标准模型的偏差。另外，我们有暗物质的神秘。任何这些未开发的地区都可以向所有人提供一切的线索。

String theory involves a great deal of theoretical physics, fiendish mathematics and mind-boggling abstraction. Do you think the general public is able to grasp the details of this debate? I think the public is curious as to what the consequences of this theory could be. The universe in some sense is like a chess game and for 2,000 years we’ve been trying to figure out how the pawns move. And now we’re beginning to understand how the queen moves and how you get a checkmate. The destiny of science is to become like grandmasters, to solve this puzzle that we call the universe. There are outstanding questions that the public wants to have answers for. For example, time travel, other dimensions, wormholes. What happened before the big bang? What’s on the other side of a black hole? None of these questions can be answered within the framework of Einstein’s theory. You have to go beyond Einstein into the quantum theory.

字符串理论涉及大量理论物理，恶魔数学和令人难以置信的抽象。您认为公众能够掌握这场辩论的细节吗？我认为公众很好奇这一理论的后果可能是什么。宇宙在某种意义上就像国际象棋比赛，我们一直试图弄清楚典当行动的举动。现在我们开始了解女王如何移动以及你是如何得到的。科学的命运就是变得像奶奶一样，解决我们称之为宇宙的难题。公众希望有卓越的问题。例如，时间旅行，其他尺寸，虫洞。大爆炸前发生了什么？什么是黑洞的另一面？在爱因斯坦理论的框架内，这些问题都没有任何回答。你必须超越爱因斯坦进入量子理论。

How much, do you think, would Isaac Newton understand of your book? I think he would appreciate it. In 1666 we had the great plague. Cambridge University was shut down and a 23-year-old boy was sent home, and he saw an apple fall on his estate. And then he realised that the laws that control an apple are the same laws that control the moon. So the epidemic gave Isaac Newton an opportunity to sit down and follow the mathematics of falling apples and falling moons. But of course there was no mathematics at that time. He couldn’t solve the problem so he created his own mathematics. That’s what we are doing now. We, too, are being hit by the plague. We, too, are confined to our desks. And we, too, are creating new mathematics.

你觉得多少，艾萨克·牛顿了解你的书？我想他会很欣赏它。在1666年，我们有很棒的瘟疫。剑桥大学被关闭，一个23岁的男孩被送回家，他看到了一个苹果落在了他的遗产。然后他意识到控制苹果的法律是控制月球的同一法律。因此，疫情给了Isaac Newton有机会坐下来遵循落下苹果和下降卫星的数学。但当然当时没有数学。他无法解决问题，所以他创造了自己的数学。这就是我们现在正在做的事情。我们也被瘟疫击中了。我们也局限于我们的书桌。我们也在创造新的数学。

In the 60s, all we had to do was go to Congress and say one word: Russia. Then Congress would say: How much?

在60年代，我们所要做的就是转到国会并说出一个字：俄罗斯。大会会说：多少钱？

Some physicists see the search for an all-encompassing theory as misleadingly reductionist. How is your work received in these circles? I’ll be very blunt, there’s a split, a split that we haven’t seen for many a decade. I’m reminded of the Solvay Conference, when Niels Bohr and Albert Einstein took opposing view on quantum theory in one of the greatest debates in the history of science. Well, string theory has also created a tremendous amount of interest, as well as a backlash. People say, well, where is the proof? Quite frankly we don’t have the proof, in the same way that Newton did not have the proof of his inverse square law back in 1666. Sometimes, the mathematics and the ideas are ahead of the concrete experimental data. That’s where the Large Hadron Collider comes into play.

一些物理学家看到一个全包的理论作为误导性的减少者。你的工作是如何收到的这些圈子？我会非常钝，有一个分裂，一个我们几十多年没有见过的分裂。当Niels Bohr和Albert Einstein在科学史上最大的辩论之一中对量子理论对相反的辩论相反，我提醒了Solvay会议。嗯，弦理论也创造了巨大的兴趣，以及反弹。人们说，好吧，证据在哪里？坦率地坦率地说，我们没有证据，以与牛顿没有证明他的逆行法律的证明在1666年。有时，数学和思想领先于具体的实验数据。这就是大型强子撞机投入游戏的地方。

The LHC has made headlines recently with its findings on how the beauty quark behaves. Will this have an impact on the theory of everything? The Standard Model is the theory of almost everything. It works spectacularly well but it’s one of the ugliest theories proposed so far. There’s this avalanche of experimental numbers you have to put in by hand. But in string theory the Standard Model just pops right out. With just a few assumptions you get the entire Standard Model. So the point here is that we need experimental proof and the LHC may give us hints of a deviation in the Standard Model and that’s where this post-LHC physics comes into play.

最近，LHC已经制定了头条新闻，了解美容夸克如何表现。这会对一切的理论产生影响吗？标准模型是几乎所有内容的理论。它的起步良好，但它是最重要的丑陋理论之一。你必须用这种实验号码的雪崩。但是，在字符串理论中，标准型号恰好就会出现。只需几个假设，您可以获得整个标准模型。因此，这里的目的是我们需要实验证据，LHC可以给我们在标准模型中偏离的暗示，这就是这一后LHC物理学发挥作用的地方。

You’ve been compared to Carl Sagan in your ability to convey complex science in an accessible form. How important is it to reach a large audience? We had a big shock in the 1990s when we physicists proposed the super collider. It was much bigger than the Large Hadron Collider. It was to be outside Dallas, Texas, but it was cancelled. What went wrong? On one of the last day of hearings, a congressman asked: “Will we find God with your machine? If so I will vote for it.” The poor physicist who had to answer that question didn’t know what to say. We should have said, this is a Genesis machine that will create the conditions of the greatest invention of all time – the universe. Unfortunately, we said Higgs boson. And people said, $10bn for another subatomic particle? And they cancelled the machine.

您已经与Carl Sagan进行了比较，以便以可访问的形式传达复杂的科学。达到大量观众有多重要？当我们的物理学家提出超级撞机时，我们在20世纪90年代震惊了。它比大型强子撞机更大。它在德克萨斯州达拉斯以外，但被取消了。什么地方出了错？在听证会的最后一天之一，一个国会议员问：“我们会和你的机器一起找到上帝吗？如果是这样，我会投票给它。“不得不回答这个问题的贫困物理学家不知道该说些什么。我们应该说，这是一个创世纪机器，它将创造有史以来最伟大的发明的条件 - 宇宙。不幸的是，我们说希格斯博斯森。和人们说，另一个子颗粒的10亿美元？他们取消了机器。

Do colleagues resent your popular touch? Let’s be honest, Carl Sagan experienced a backlash when he started to enter the public arena. There was a vote to have him initiated into the National Academy of Sciences and he was denied. The super collider was cancelled because we were in the ivory tower and had no connection with the taxpayer who was footing the bill. And then along comes Stephen Hawking. He generated so much interest and was a real physicist at the cutting edge of science, not a mere “populariser” – the criticism lodged against Sagan. So I think it was humbling. We have to sing for our supper. During the 60s, all we had to do was go to Congress and say one word: Russia. Then Congress would say two words: How much? Those days are gone.

同事们怨恨你流行的触感吗？让我们诚实，卡尔萨吉队在开始进入公共场地时经历了彻底的速度。有一张投票让他发起了国家科学院，他被否认。超级撞机被取消，因为我们在象牙塔中，没有与纳税人进行账单的纳税人。然后刚刚来了斯蒂芬霍金。他产生了如此多的兴趣，并且是科学的最前沿的真实物理学，而不是仅仅是“受欢迎的人” - 对抗萨昂的批评。所以我认为这是羞辱。我们必须为晚餐唱歌。在60年代期间，我们所要做的就是转到国会并说出一个字：俄罗斯。大会会说两个字：多少钱？那些日子已经一去不复返了。

You believe that within a century we will make contact with an alien civilisation. Are you worried about what they may entail? Soon we’ll have the web telescope up in orbit and we’ll have thousands of planets to look at, and that’s why I think the chances are quite high that we may make contact with an alien civilisation. There are some colleagues of mine that believe we should reach out to them. I think that’s a terrible idea. We all know what happened to Montezuma when he met Cortés in Mexico so many hundreds of years ago. Now, personally, I think that aliens out there would be friendly but we can’t gamble on it. So I think we will make contact but we should do it very carefully.

你相信，在一个世纪之内，我们将与外星人文明接触。你担心他们可能需要什么吗？很快我们就会在轨道上有网眼望远镜，我们将有数千个行星来看看，这就是为什么我认为可能与外国文明接触的机会很高。我有一些同事认为我们应该伸向他们。我认为这是一个可怕的想法。我们都知道在墨西哥在墨西哥的Cortés遇到了这么多数百年前的时候发生了什么。现在，个人，我认为外星人会有友好，但我们不能赌博。所以我想我们会接触，但我们应该非常谨慎地做到这一点。

There are many brilliant scientists whose contributions you discuss in the book. Which one, for you, stands out above the rest? Newton is at number one, because, almost out of nothing, out of an era of witchcraft and sorcery, he comes up with the mathematics of the universe, he comes up with a theory of almost everything. That’s incredible. Einstein piggybacked on Newton, using the calculus of Newton to work out the dynamics of curved spacetime and general relativity. They are like supernovas, blindingly brilliant and illuminating the entire landscape and changing human destiny. Newton’s laws of motion set into motion the foundation for the Industrial Revolution. A person like that comes along once every several centuries.

有许多辉煌的科学家，您在书中讨论的贡献。哪一个，适合你，脱颖而出？牛顿处于第一名，因为，几乎没有什么，从巫术和巫术的时代出来，他出现了宇宙的数学，他突出了几乎所有东西的理论。这是令人难以置信的。爱因斯坦在牛顿扛在牛顿，使用牛顿的微积分来解决弯曲时空和一般相对性的动态。他们就像超级游戏，蒙上辉煌，照亮整个景观和改变人类命运。牛顿的议案法案旨在为工业革命的基础设立。像这样的人每次几个世纪。

You describe yourself as an agnostic. Has your research led you closer or further away from the idea of a designer God? Stephen Hawking said that he didn’t believe in God because the big bang happened instantly and there was no time for God to create a universe, therefore God couldn’t exist. I have a different point of view. My parents were Buddhists and in Buddhism there is Nirvana, timelessness, no beginning and no end. But my parents put me in a Presbyterian church, so I went to Sunday school every week and learned about Genesis and how the universe was created in seven days. Now with the multiverse idea we can meld these two diametrically opposed paradigms together. According to string theory, big bangs are happening all the time. Even as we speak, Genesis is taking place somewhere in the cosmos. And what is the universe expanding into? Nirvana. Eleven-dimensional hyperspace is Nirvana. So you can have Buddhism and Judeo-Christian philosophy in one theory.

你将自己描述为不可知论者。您的研究是否导致您更近或远离设计师上帝的想法？斯蒂芬霍金说，他不相信上帝，因为大爆炸立即发生，而且没有时间创造一个宇宙，因此上帝无法存在。我有一个不同的观点。我的父母是佛教徒和佛教，有涅ana，永恒，没有开始，没有结局。但我的父母把我放在长老会教堂里，所以我每周都去了星期天学校，并了解了创世纪以及宇宙如何在七天内创造。现在，随着多个人的想法，我们可以将这两个直射相对的范例混合在一起。根据字符串理论，大刘海一直在发生。即使我们说，即使在宇宙中的某个地方就会发生成因。什么是宇宙扩张的？ Nirvana。十一个高度高空间是Nirvana。所以你可以在一个理论上拥有佛教和犹太教 - 基督教哲学。

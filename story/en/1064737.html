Why you should quit ranked session-based multiplayer video games
为什么要退出排名基于会话的多人视频游戏

2021-06-10 08:54:50

This post is a revelation of an addicted gamer with 4k hours in Dota 2and 1k hours/600$+ spent in Hearthstone. Half of the time it is needed to becomea master chef, or a music producer, or a good fiction writer, if you believe in a 10k hour rule.But apparently not enough time to become even a half-decent player, since I never brokethrough the 3800 MMR ceiling in Dota 2. Addiction had a chance to break my family and I regretthe lost time, so I wrote this as a warning to other gamers.

这篇文章是一个上瘾的游戏玩家的启示，在Dota中有4k小时2和1k小时/ 600 $ +在炉斯通。一半的时间需要掌握厨师，或音乐制作人，或者是一个好小说作家，如果你相信10k小时的规则。明显没有足够的时间成为一个半体面的球员，因为我从不闯入在Dota的3800 mmr天花板中。成瘾有机会打破我的家人，我后悔失去的时间，所以我把这作为对其他游戏玩家的警告。

There are plot-based video games, which inevitably end after a playthrough(with some exceptions).Then there are open-world video games, into which you can log in and out at anytime without losing progress. Then there are session-based video games,where you play a game/round/session, it ends and then you’re done, or you can repeat.Think Civilization, Tetris, roguelikes like Binding of Isaac, etc.And then there are multiplayer session-based video games (MSBVGs), where you compete againstother players, like Apex Legends, Dota 2 and Hearthstone. And these last ones arekind of a malignant force.

有基于情节的视频游戏，这不可避免地在播放之后结束（有一些例外）。然后有开放世界的视频游戏，您可以随时登录和退出，而不会失去进度。然后有基于会话的视频游戏，在哪里玩游戏/圆形/会话，它结束，然后你已经完成了，或者你可以重复.Think文明，俄亥俄州的罗格里克等，那么有些基于多人的基于会话的视频游戏（MSBVGS），您可以在那里竞争与Apex图例，DotA 2和炉匠相对。而这些最后的是恶性力量。

The covert malignacy this type of games has is their addictiveness (duh!).But not the usual kind of addictiveness that people imagine in the context of videogames. Not the WoW kind. The evil corruption that multiplayer session-based video gamesbestow upon you is their competitiveness. It is a subtle, but persistent power,that over time made me, a grown man with a 40-hour work week, believe thatI can and should compete with professional players and streamers and become astreamer myself. All this because of a number.

这种类型的恶性气氛这种类型的游戏已经是他们的令人上瘾（Duh！）。但是人们在电子游戏的背景下想象的常见令人上瘾。不是魔兽世界。基于多人会议的视频游戏在您身上的邪恶腐败是他们的竞争力。这是一个微妙的，但持续的力量，随着时间的推移，一个带有40小时工作周的成长的男人，相信那可以和应该与专业的球员和娱乐器竞争，并自己成为七分之一。这一切都是因为一个数字。

Most MSBVGs nowadays contain some kind of rating system which is tied to a number.Almost every rating system stems from Elo rating system,invented for chess, which makes sense, as chess itself was the most played multiplayersession-based game before games had any video in them. Chess players had to beat countless opponentsto prove their worth, unlike professional football for example, which is mostly tied toleagues or tournaments and thus predefined set of opponents and no incentive in rating system more complexthan who-beated-who-more table.

现在大多数MSBVGS都包含某种评级系统，该系统与数字相关联。最符合ELO评级系统的每个评级系统，这是象棋，这是有道理的，因为国际象棋本身是最播放的基于多人游戏，在游戏之前获得了任何视频。在他们之中。例如，国际象棋球员不得不击败无数的对手证明他们的价值，与专业的足球不同，这是大多数绑定的甲状腺或锦标赛，因此预定义的对手，并且在评级系统中没有激励，更加复杂的是谁殴打的人 - 谁更多的桌子。

Game developers try to hide the rating behind fancy facades like medals in Dota 2(herald, legend, divine) or ranks in Apex Legends (silver, gold, diamond), but inthe end it is still a number. A number that has the power to make the player obsessiveabout it. Which is what happened to me. Twice, in two different games.

游戏开发人员试图隐藏花式外墙背后的评级，如Dota 2（Herald，Legend，Divine）或Apex传说中的奖牌（银，金，钻石），但结束它仍然是一个数字。一个有能力让玩家痴迷的数字。这就是我发生的事情。两次，在两个不同的游戏中。

First, somewhere around 2015, I got obsessed with my Hearthstone rank. I really liked the game,and then, never before playing collectible card games, wanted to become a Legend (the best possiblerank in Hearthstone). It took me three months, 600$ pumped into 3 or 4 meta decks, andcountless hours of playtime and watching streams and youtube videos. In the end I got to Legend.Was it worth it? Well, I got a fancy cardback, that’s it. I haven’t opened Hearthstone since2016, so the cardback is forced to rust in some datacenter in a byte form forever, never againseeing the battlefield.

首先，在2015年左右的某个地方，我痴迷于我的磨石等级。我真的很喜欢游戏，然后，从来没有在玩收藏的纸牌游戏之前，想要成为一个传奇（赫斯特斯通最好的Possiblerank）。我花了三个月，600美元泵成3或4个元甲板，无可计可的播放时间和观看流和YouTube视频。最后我到了传说。你值得吗？好吧，我有一个花哨的载体，就是这样。自2016年以来，我没有打开炉山，因此载体被迫以一个字节形式的一些数据中心生锈，永远不会再次探索战场。

Then, around 2019 I started to seriously grind Dota 2. I wanted to get to Titan (thebest medal in Dota 2, top 0.1% players), which would,in my own eyes, make me eligible for streaming. Streaming was a common theme in my mindwhen it came to MSBVGs. I tried to stream Hearthstone when I had Legend, and Ifiercely tried to gain Dota 2 rating number as quickly as possible. But it was not supposedto happen. I just could not break through the 3500 MMR (match making rating) consistently.

然后，2019年左右，我开始严重研磨DotA 2.我想到泰坦（Dota 2中最值的奖牌，0.1％的球员），在我自己的眼中，这将让我有资格进入流媒体。流动是在MSBVGS的思想中是一个普遍的主题。当我有传奇时，我试图在赫斯特斯斯通时散步，而ifiercely试图尽快获得Dota 2评级号。但这并不是应该发生的。我只是不能始终突破3500 mmr（匹配评级）。

One Dota 2 game takes anywhere from 20 to 70 minutes. I came home from work each day andtried to play at least 4 games to become better. I tried to push for 8 games each day of the weekend.I watched Dota streams as my only entertainment instead of TV shows and movies, sometimes at work.Had I lost too much games in a row, I became angry and spilled this anger on my innocent wife.I stopped spending quality time with her, due to playing almost all my free time. At some pointshe started to avoid coming home, because “you’re playing anyway, there is nothing for me at home”.

一个DotA 2游戏从20到70分钟到达。我每天都在回家中下班回家，玩至少4场比赛变得更好。我试图每天都在周末每天推出8场比赛。我看着Dota流作为我唯一的娱乐，而不是电视节目和电影，有时在工作中。哈德我丢失了太多的游戏，我变得愤怒，洒了这种愤怒我无辜的妻子。我因播放几乎所有的空闲时间而停止和她一起度过了优质的时光。在某些方面，开始避免回家，因为“无论如何你在玩，家里都没有给我”。

Only this raised the alert in my head. I started playing less. One day me and my wife weresitting in a bedroom, and she just suggested - “why won’t you just delete Dota, it is givingyou nothing anyway”. Then something clicked in my head. “Yeah, why wouldn’t I”.I got to my PC, opened Steam and deleted the game.

只有这在我的头脑中提出了警戒。我开始少玩耍。有一天，我和我的妻子在一间卧室里徘徊，她刚才建议 - “你为什么不删除dota，无论如何都没有给你”。然后点击了我的脑袋。 “是的，为什么我不会”。我拿到我的电脑，打开蒸汽并删除了游戏。

One day has passed, two days, a week. I haven’t felt that I lost anything valuable at all.I slowly regained interest in other entertainment, be it movies, other video games, or meetingwith friends. I started to work more, made a blog (thanks for reading it right now btw!),finished a personal SaaS project. Retrospectively, quitting ranked Dota was one of the bestdecisions in my life. I think this decision is the best for anyone who is not already a big streameror a professional esports player. Even if you think you’re close to breakthrough to twitch or stadiumglory, you should consider jumping off that train.

有一天过去了，两天，一周。我没有觉得我根本失去了任何有价值的东西。我慢慢恢复了对其他娱乐活动的兴趣，是电影，其他视频游戏，或与朋友会面。我开始工作了，制作了一个博客（谢谢你现在读它，现在完成了一个私人SaaS项目。回顾性地，戒烟排名Dota是我生命中最畅销的。我认为这一决定是最好的任何人都是一个专业esports播放器的人。即使您认为您接近抽搐或浮现突破，您也应该考虑跳下来训练。

Let’s compare being in top 100 players in Dota 2 to opening restaurant on a busiest streetin a metropolis. When you’ll open a restaurant, you’ll face competition of maybe 50 other restaurantsin an area close to you. But if you want to become a top 100 player in Dota, you need to face the odds of1 to 400000 other players. Which in restaurant terms is like being competitive with the whole world.And let’s face it, you still can be profitable as a mediocre restaurant on a busy street, than asa mediocre player. Mediocre players are not payed at all.

让我们在Dota 2的前100名球员比较到开设最繁忙的街头的餐厅。当您打开一家餐馆时，您将面临50家其他地区的竞争，靠近您的其他地区。但如果你想成为Dota中的前100名球员，你需要面对其他球员的赔率为1到40万名球员。在餐厅术语中，这就像与整个世界的竞争力一样。让我们面对它，你仍然可以在繁忙的街道上的平庸餐厅获得盈利，而不是Asa Mediocre Player。平庸的球员根本没有支付。

This is because there is another logical reason not to play MSBVGs - therating systems are essentially zero-sum games.For you to gain the rating another person has to lose it. For myself I choose not to play zero-sum games anymore,and focus on wealth creation games instead. Because real life MMRin $ net worth can be fun to grind too.

这是因为还有另一种逻辑原因不播放MSBVGS的系统基本上是零和游戏。为了获得另一个人来丢失它的评分。对自己来说，我选择不再播放零和游戏，并专注于财富创建游戏。因为现实生活MMRIN $ NET值得可以很有趣。

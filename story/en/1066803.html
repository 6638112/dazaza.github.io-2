I paid $300K to quit a startup
我支付了300万美元来退出启动

2021-06-19 08:58:51

I was an early employee at a hyper-growth startup. Life was rich until I tried to quit. This is the story of why I had to pay $300K to unshackle myself.  There’s surprisingly little coverage on this topic, maybe because nobody is incentivized to reveal the unsavory truth, or because VCs and founders get a lot more airtime. Here’s everything I wish I knew before I joined a startup. I hope it can save you from burning piles of money like I did.  ‍

我是一个超级增长启动的早期员工。在我试图戒烟之前，生活是富有的。这是为什么我不得不支付300万美元的故事来包裹自己。对这个话题令人惊讶的是，也许是因为没有人被激励揭示令人讨厌的真理，或者因为VCS和创始人获得了更多的通话时间。这是我希望在我加入启动之前所知道的一切。我希望它可以拯救你从燃烧的钱，就像我一样。

For all the talk around “disruption”, startups exert a surprising inertia over employees. “Golden handcuffs” is most often used to describe the inertia experienced by big tech employees who make a ton of money and have a hard time leaving. It also applies to startup employees who make a ton of paper gains and have a hard time coming up with real money to keep them.  ‍

对于周围的所有谈话，“中断”，初创公司对员工发挥了令人惊讶的惯性。 “金色手铐”最常用于描述制造大量资金的大型技术员工经历的惯性，并难以离开。它还适用于制作大量纸质收益的员工，并努力地提出真正的金钱来保留它们。

Working at a startup generally requires taking a salary cut. The deal is sweetened with equity — an ownership stake that can mint millions. The most common equity vehicle is the stock option. It’s the option to buy a certain # of shares at a set price, aka the strike price. That’s right. You don’t own shares of the company, you own the right to buy them. There are two more catches:

在创业公司工作通常需要削减薪水。交易与股权相糖 - 可以造成数百万的所有权股权。最常见的股票是股票期权。它是购买一定的股票的选项，又是罢工价格。这是正确的。你不拥有公司的股票，你拥有购买它们的权利。有两个捕获量：

Options usually expire within 90 days of your leaving — so you have to either pay up or give them up

期权通常在您离开的90天内到期 - 所以您必须支付或给予它们

If you pay up, you also owe taxes on your paper gains — the difference between your strike price and the current valuation

如果您付出代价，您还会缴纳纸质收益 - 您的罢工价格与当前估值之间的差异

#2 is dangerous because your taxes grow with the valuation. Even if you have a cheap strike price, your tax burden can be multiples higher. In fact, your total bill can end up costing more than the startup has ever paid you in cash! Yes, it is actually possible to be net negative after devoting years of your life as an employee. In my case, the price tag for my vested shares was $100K, but the tax bill will be another $200K. That’s a ton of cash just to keep the shares I worked for, with no guarantees that they will be worth real money. ‍

＃2是危险的，因为您的税收随着估值而增长。即使您有廉价的罢工价格，您的税收负担也可以倍增。事实上，您的总账单最终可能超过初创公司以现金支付的成本！是的，实际上可以在作为员工的生命年度致力于历史的阶段净负面。在我的案例中，我归属股票的价格标签是100万美元，但税收法案将是另一次100万美元。这是一大吨现金，只是为了保持我工作的股票，没有保证他们将值得真钱。

The financial defaults are terrible for startup employees, but they don’t need to be this way. You can and should negotiate the fine print, where all the devils lie. Exceptions are never granted unless you ask. Especially true if you are a strong hire and/or the startup really needs to fill the position (which is more common than you think). First order of business is the exercise window. Instead of taking the punishing 90 day window, ask for 10 years. You can frame it like this: “I believe in the potential of X and I’m excited to take part in the upside. It’s important that I can do that regardless of my cash position at a specific moment in time.” Best time to ask is before you sign the offer. This is when you have peak leverage, and if you make it the final sticking point, you’re more likely to get it. To gain an upper hand, try to secure competing offers that have more favorable terms.  Does negotiating make you look petty? No, if anything, it shows you’ve done your homework. Not negotiating terrible terms betrays your naivete — like me when I signed my offer. Whatever you settle on, make sure to get it in writing. Many a verbal promise is broken when there’s large sums of money at stake. If you still wind up with a crazy bill, see if the next company you join is willing to pay your switching cost. I forgot to do this when negotiating with Instagram, but it can work, especially with bigger companies. The downside is that if you need the new company to bail you out of your old one, you are at their mercy.  More points you can negotiate for:

财务违约对于启动员工来说是可怕的，但他们不需要这种方式。你可以，应该协商精美的印刷品，所有魔鬼都在撒谎。除非您问，否则绝不会授予例外。尤其如此，如果您是强大的租购和/或启动真的需要填补位置（这比你想象的更常见）。第一顺序是锻炼窗口。而不是采取惩罚90天窗口，要求10年。你可以像这样框架：“我相信X的潜力，我很高兴参加上面。重要的是，无论我的现金职位在特定时刻，我都可以这么做。“在您签署优惠之前，请求的最佳时间。这是当你有峰值杠杆率的时候，如果你做到了最终的粘性点，你更有可能得到它。为了获得上手，尝试确保具有更有利的术语的竞争优惠。谈判让你看起来很小吗？不，如果有的话，它表明你已经完成了你的作业。没有谈判可怕的术语背叛你的天真 - 当我签署我的报价时就像我一样。无论你结算什么，都要写作。当有大量资金的股份时，许多口头承诺被打破了。如果您仍然用疯狂的账单结束，请看看您加入的下一件公司是否愿意支付您的交换成本。在与Instagram协商时，我忘了这样做，但它可以工作，特别是与更大的公司。缺点是，如果你需要新公司拿出你的旧公司，你就会怜悯你。更多要点，您可以洽谈：

RSUs instead of stock options: restricted stock units have a strike price of $0, but early-stage startups may not offer this

RSU而不是股票期权：受限制的股单位有0美元的罢工价格，但早期的初创公司可能不会提供这个

Even if you don’t get everything you want, you’ll still develop a better understanding of how accessible your equity really is. Sometimes a higher salary is better than heaps of “life-changing” stock options you can never afford. One more escape valve that just came to my attention: 83b election. If you believe in the growth potential of your shares AND you expect to stay and vest, you can choose to pay taxes on your full grant upfront, rather than on your paper gains down the line. This may save you a lot on taxes, but also comes with a dose of downsides:

即使你没有得到你想要的一切，你仍然会更好地了解你的公平真正的方式。有时薪水更高，比你永远不起的“生命变化”的股票期权更高。一个刚刚引起我的逃生阀：83B选举。如果您相信您股票的增长潜力，并且您希望留下来并将，您可以选择在完全授予税款前期纳税，而不是您的纸张上线。这可能会为您节省大量税收，而且还提供了一剂缺点：

You can end up worse off if the shares decline in value OR you don’t fully vest ‍

如果股票在价值下降或你没有完全背心，你最终可能会更糟

As much as startups talk about growing the pie, the finite pool of shares is treated as zero sum. There are only ever 100 points on a cap table. When you give up your options, they go back into the company pool. Your unused options can then be recycled to fund new employee packages.   Here's how a VC sums it up: extending the exercise window prioritizes the well-being of former employees over existing ones. What startup in its right mind would do that?! Probably one that recognizes it was built, brick by brick, from the hands of early employees. Sadly, 91.4% do not recognize this.  It’s also hard to decipher the terms in your offer package. If every startup had to walk employees through how the numbers play out, a lot of offers would go unsigned because they’re absurd. Instead, we're sold on the opportunity to “make a dent in the universe”. It’s rarely about the money until the money becomes real.  ‍

尽可能多的初创公司谈论成长馅饼，有限的股票被视为零和。帽桌上只有100分。当您放弃您的选择时，他们会回到公司池中。然后可以回收您未使用的选项以为新的员工包提供资金。在这里＆＃39; vc如何总结它：扩展练习窗口优先考虑前雇员的福祉。它的正确思想会做什么？！可能是一个认识到它的建造，砖块砖，从早期员工的手中建造。可悲的是，91.4％不承认这一点。在您的优惠包中也很难破译条款。如果每个初创企业都必须通过数字的数量如何播放，很多优惠将无法签名，因为它们是荒谬的。相反，我们＆＃39;重新销售“在宇宙中做出凹陷”的机会。这很少有钱，直到钱变得真实。

Most people advised me to give up some shares, or find a third-party to front the cash and split the upside — both more rational choices than the one I took.  In the end, watching years of hard work evaporate in 90 days far exceeded the pain of just paying for the damn lottery ticket. I’m lucky I had access to that cash. Many people don’t. They are forced to give up their shares or stay shackled to a company in hopes of liquidity some day. I never joined a startup to get rich. But I did naively assume that if all went well, I would ride off into the sunset, having paid for the equity in sweat. I didn’t comb through the fine print because I didn’t know any better.  Now you do! Maybe I should rebrand my $300K bill as a premium MBA — it’s sure taught me a lot, and I hope it brings you a wealth of lessons too. ‍

大多数人建议我放弃一些股票，或者找到第三方来前往现金并分裂上行 - 这两个都比我所采取的选择更多的合理选择。最终，在90天的时间里看着多年的努力工作超过了只需支付该死的彩票票的痛苦。我很幸运，我可以获得现金。很多人都没有。他们被迫放弃他们的股票或停留在一天的公司希望有一天的流动资金。我从未加入启动以致富。但我真的假设如果一切顺利，我会乘坐夕阳，为汗水的股权支付。我没有梳理精美的印刷，因为我不知道更好。你现在做！也许我应该重新打配我的300万美元的账单作为高级MBA - 这肯定会教我​​很多，我希望它也为您带来丰富的课程。

Early Van Gogh sketches discovered in a book
早期梵高素描在书中发现

2021-06-29 22:27:06

Adventures with Van Gogh is a weekly blog by Martin Bailey, our long-standing correspondent and expert on the artist. Published every Friday, his stories will range from newsy items about this most intriguing artist to scholarly pieces based on his own meticulous investigations and discoveries.

Van Gogh冒险是Martin Bailey，我们的长期记者和艺术家专家的每周博客。每周五发布，他的故事将根据他自己的细致调查和发现，他的故事范围从新闻中的关于这个最有趣的艺术家到学术作品。

Reproduced here for the first time: a trio of sketches from Vincent’s village—designed as a bookmark

首次再现在这里：Vincent的村庄设计的三人草图设计为书签

One of Van Gogh’s most unusual artworks has just emerged—three unknown sketches of peasants. They are drawn on a thin vertical strip of paper, a curious format for an artist to choose. The drawings were found in a novel on the French peasantry and the paper is exactly the same height as the book.

梵高的最不寻常的作品之一刚刚出现了三个未知的农民草图。它们是在薄的垂直纸条上绘制，一种艺术家选择的好奇格式。在法国农民的一部小说中发现了图纸，纸张与本书完全相同。

The three sketches are reproduced by The Art Newspaper here for the first time. Discoveries of totally unknown Van Gogh drawings are now rare—perhaps once or twice in a decade. The sheet has just gone on display at the Van Gogh Museum in Amsterdam, in a new exhibition on acquisitions, Here to Stay (until 12 September). Although it is presented in the show as simply a trio of sketches, the artwork almost certainly served an unexpected purpose.

这三个草图是第一次由艺术报纸复制。在十年中发现完全未知的梵高图纸现在罕见或两次。该片刚刚在阿姆斯特丹的Van Gogh博物馆展出，在此次收购的新展览中，这里留下来（直到9月12日）。虽然它在展会中呈现，但只有三个草图，艺术品几乎肯定提供了意想不到的目的。

Teio Meedendorp, a senior researcher at the Amsterdam museum, told us: “I believe that it was made as a bookmark. It would be curious to draw three sketches in this configuration unless there was a reason, and it fits snugly inside the book in which it was found.”

阿姆斯特丹博物馆的高级研究员Teio Meedendorp告诉我们：“我相信它被作为书签。除非有一个原因，否则在这种配置中绘制三个草图会很奇怪，并且它在被发现的书中贴心地舒服。“

Vincent, an avid reader, gave his copy of a novel on the French peasantry to his Dutch artist friend Anthon van Rappard. Writing to Van Rappard on 15 June 1883, he said he would be posting a package to him in Utrecht, with prints and a book by the French writers Emile Erckmann and Alexandre Chatrian, whom he greatly admired. Histoire d’un Paysan recounts the story of the French Revolution through the eyes of an Alsace peasant. Illustrated by Théophile Schuler, Vincent assured his friend that “I do think you’ll find the Erckmann-Chatrian beautiful”.

Vincent是一个狂热的读者，给了他对荷兰艺术家朋友Anthon Van Rappard的法国农民的一部小说。他在1883年6月15日写给van Rappard，他说他将在UTRECHT发布一揽子，用印刷品和一本书，由法国作家埃米尔埃克尔曼和亚历山大·钟师的书，他大钦佩。 Histoired d'联合国Paysan通过Alsace农民的眼睛讲述了法国革命的故事。文森特通过ThéophileSchuler说明，向他的朋友向他的朋友提供了“我认为你会发现Erckmann-Chatrian美丽”。

Van Rappard died in 1892, aged just 33, and the book then passed to his wife Henriëtte, who lived until 1910. Histoire d’un Paysan, together with the bookmark, was quietly preserved by her family until 2019, when both were privately sold to the Van Gogh Museum.

van rappard于1892年去世，达到了33岁，然后将其妻子亨里雅特特传递给了1910年的妻子亨利蒂斯，曾与书签一起致力于她的家庭悄然保存，直到2019年私人销售到梵高博物馆。

The pencil drawings are crude, since they were done soon after Vincent set out to become an artist, but they are important since so few of his early informal sketches survive. Stylistically, they can be dated to autumn 1881, when he was living in his parent’s village of Etten and was deeply interested in capturing figures of the local peasants at work.

铅笔图形是原油，因为他们在文森特出发后很快就完成了艺术家，但他们很重要，因为他早期的非正式草图生存了很少。风格主义性地，他们可以在1881年秋季日期，当他住在他的父母的Etten村，对工作中当地农民的数字深感很感兴趣。

It was not until two years later that Vincent sent Histoire d’un Paysan to Van Rappard, suggesting that the bookmark had lain forgotten in the novel and was not noticed when he wrapped up the package. If Vincent had set out to give Van Rappard a few sketches, he would have been much more likely to have sent very recent ones, to demonstrate how his art was developing.

直到两年后，Vincent向Van Rappard发送了Histoire d'联合国Paysan，这表明书签在小说中忘记了，当他包裹包裹时没有被注意到。如果文森特已经出示给Van Rappard一些草图，他将更有可能发送最近的速度，以证明他的艺术是如何发展的。

On the 28cm-high bookmark, the upper sketch shows a woman walking by a pollarded tree. The middle one portrays a seated man beside a fire, a work reminiscent of a finished drawing which Van Gogh made in November 1881, Old Man at the Fireside (now Kröller-Müller Museum, Otterlo). The lower sketch depicts a woman in profile. Van Gogh may well have seen the figures as loosely illustrating the Erckmann-Chatrian novel.

在28厘米高的书签上，上素描显示了一个由恐怖树行走的女人。中间人描绘了一个坐着的人旁边，一个坐着的人在火灾旁边描绘了一个清醒的工作，让人想起了1881年11月的van Gogh，逃火的老人（现在Kröller-Müller博物馆，Otterlo）。较低的草图描绘了一个女人的概况。 Van Gogh可能已经看到这些数字松散地说明了Erckmann-Chatrian小说。

The front of the book is signed “Vincent”. He had sent the book to Van Rappard “to read” and it is unclear whether the signature indicates that it was a gift or was a polite reminder that it was a loan and should be returned.

本书的前部签署了“文森特”。他把这本书寄给了Van Rappard“读书”，目前尚不清楚签名是否表明这是礼物，或者是一个礼貌提醒，这是贷款，应该退还。

In October, just over year after sending the book, Van Rappard visited Vincent in Nuenen—but without returning the book. During the visit Van Gogh sketched a portrait of his friend, the largest drawn portrait in his entire oeuvre. Only the top half of the work survives.

10月，在送书之后的一年中，Van Rappard访问了Nuenen的文森特 - 但没有回到这本书。在访问van gogh期间，勾勒出他的朋友的肖像，是他整个Oeuvre中最大的绘制肖像。只有工作的上半部分才能存活。

Sadly, their friendship came to an abrupt end in May 1885, after Van Rappard wrote critically about Vincent’s only lithograph, which depicted his painting of The Potato Eaters. Vincent, who was furious, dismembered the portrait, neatly slicing it in half. The bottom half has been lost, but he soon reused the reverse of the top half to make rough sketches of a pair of women and a man carrying a bundle of wood.

遗憾的是，在100月1885年5月，他们的友谊在Vincent的唯一石英仪录音机批判后，他们在1885年5月突然结束了，这描绘了他对马铃薯食鸟的绘画。愤怒，愤怒地贬低了肖像，整齐地切成两半。下半场已经丢失了，但他很快就重复了上半部分的反面，使一对妇女和一个携带一束木头的男人的粗略草图。

Thanks to this recycling, the most important half of the Van Rappard portrait, with the eyes, has been saved—poignant evidence of Van Gogh’s closest friendship during his Dutch artistic years.

由于这种回收，van Rappard肖像的最重要的一半，凭借眼睛，在他的荷兰艺术年内van Gogh最亲近的友谊已经挽救了豪华的证据。

• Christie’s is to sell a drawing of The Potato Harvester (Aardappelrooier) in London on 30 June, estimated at £300,000-£500,000. Dating from June-July 1885, it was done in Nuenen, the village in the south of the Netherlands. Fork in hand, the peasant is digging up potatoes, while smoking his pipe. The Potato Harvester was originally owned by the distinguished Dutch art historian H.P. Bremmer.

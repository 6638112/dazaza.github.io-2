“阅读的权利”(1997)

2020-10-24 07:29:45

From The Road To Tycho, a collection of articles about the antecedents of the Lunarian Revolution, published in Luna City in 2096.

《从路到第谷》是一部关于卢尼亚革命前人的文集，2096年在露娜城出版。

For Dan Halbert, the road to Tycho began in college—when LissaLenz asked to borrow his computer. Hers had broken down, and unlessshe could borrow another, she would fail her midterm project. Therewas no one she dared ask, except Dan.

对丹·哈尔伯特来说，第谷之路始于大学--当时利萨·伦茨向他借了一台电脑。她的车坏了，除非她能再借一辆，否则她的期中项目就会失败。除了丹，她不敢问任何人。

This put Dan in a dilemma. He had to help her—but if he lenther his computer, she might read his books. Aside from the fact thatyou could go to prison for many years for letting someone else readyour books, the very idea shocked him at first. Like everyone, he hadbeen taught since elementary school that sharing books was nasty andwrong—something that only pirates would do.

这让丹进退两难。他必须帮助她--但如果他把电脑借给她，她可能会读他的书。除了你可能会因为让别人读你的书而被判入狱多年这一事实外，这个想法一开始就让他感到震惊。像每个人一样，他从小学开始就被教导分享书籍是肮脏和错误的-这是只有海盗才会做的事情。

And there wasn't much chance that the SPA—the SoftwareProtection Authority—would fail to catch him. In his softwareclass, Dan had learned that each book had a copyright monitor thatreported when and where it was read, and by whom, to CentralLicensing. (They used this information to catch reading pirates, butalso to sell personal interest profiles to retailers.) The next timehis computer was networked, Central Licensing would find out. He, ascomputer owner, would receive the harshest punishment—for nottaking pains to prevent the crime.

而且，SPA--软件保护局--不太可能抓不到他。在他的软件课上，丹了解到每本书都有一个版权监视器，可以向中央许可中心报告它是在何时何地、由谁阅读的。(他们利用这些信息来抓到阅读盗版的人，但也用来向零售商出售个人兴趣档案。)。下次他的电脑联网时，中央许可中心就会发现。作为一名电脑所有者，他将受到最严厉的惩罚--因为他没有竭尽全力防止犯罪。

Of course, Lissa did not necessarily intend to read his books. Shemight want the computer only to write her midterm. But Dan knew shecame from a middle-class family and could hardly afford the tuition,let alone her reading fees. Reading his books might be the only wayshe could graduate. He understood this situation; he himself had hadto borrow to pay for all the research papers he read. (Ten percent of thosefees went to the researchers who wrote the papers; since Dan aimed foran academic career, he could hope that his own research papers, iffrequently referenced, would bring in enough to repay this loan.)

当然，丽莎并不一定要读他的书。她可能只想让电脑写她的期中考试。但是丹从一个中产阶级家庭认识了她，几乎付不起学费，更不用说她的阅读费了。读他的书可能是她毕业的唯一途径。他理解这种情况；他自己不得不借钱来支付他读的所有研究论文的费用。(其中10%的费用流向了撰写论文的研究人员；由于丹的目标是从事学术事业，他可以希望自己的研究论文(经常被引用)能带来足够的收入来偿还这笔贷款。)。

Later on, Dan would learn there was a time when anyone could go to thelibrary and read journal articles, and even books, without having topay. There were independent scholars who read thousands of pageswithout government library grants. But in the 1990s, both commercialand nonprofit journal publishers had begun charging fees for access.By 2047, libraries offering free public access to scholarly literaturewere a dim memory.

后来，丹了解到，曾经有一段时间，任何人都可以去图书馆阅读期刊文章，甚至是书籍，而不需要支付费用。有一些独立学者在没有政府图书馆拨款的情况下阅读了数千页。但在20世纪90年代，商业性和非营利性期刊出版商都开始收取访问费，到2047年，向公众免费提供学术文献的图书馆已经成为模糊的记忆。

There were ways, of course, to get around the SPA and CentralLicensing. They were themselves illegal. Dan had had a classmate insoftware, Frank Martucci, who had obtained an illicit debugging tool,and used it to skip over the copyright monitor code when readingbooks. But he had told too many friends about it, and one of themturned him in to the SPA for a reward (students deep in debt wereeasily tempted into betrayal). In 2047, Frank was in prison, not forpirate reading, but for possessing a debugger.

当然，有办法绕过SPA和中央许可中心。他们本身就是非法的。丹有一位软件专业的同学，弗兰克·马图奇，他得到了一个非法的调试工具，并在阅读书籍时用它跳过了版权监视器代码。但他告诉了太多朋友这件事，其中一个朋友为了奖励而向SPA告发了他(深陷债务的学生很容易受到背叛的诱惑)。2047年，弗兰克入狱，不是因为盗版阅读，而是因为拥有调试器。

Dan would later learn that there was a time when anyone could havedebugging tools. There were even free debugging tools available on CDor downloadable over the net. But ordinary users started using themto bypass copyright monitors, and eventually a judge ruled that thishad become their principal use in actual practice. This meant theywere illegal; the debuggers' developers were sent to prison.

丹后来了解到，曾经有一段时间，任何人都可以拥有调试工具。甚至可以从网上下载CDOR上的免费调试工具。但普通用户开始使用它们来绕过版权监控，最终法官裁定，这已成为他们在实际操作中的主要用途。这意味着他们是非法的；调试器开发人员被送进了监狱。

Programmers still needed debugging tools, of course, but debuggervendors in 2047 distributed numbered copies only, and only toofficially licensed and bonded programmers. The debugger Dan used insoftware class was kept behind a special firewall so that it could beused only for class exercises.

当然，程序员仍然需要调试工具，但2047年的调试器供应商只分发编号的副本，而且只分发给官方许可的和有约束力的程序员。丹在软件课上使用的调试器被放在一个特殊的防火墙后面，这样它只能用于课堂练习。

It was also possible to bypass the copyright monitors by installing amodified system kernel. Dan would eventually find out about the freekernels, even entire free operating systems, that had existed aroundthe turn of the century. But not only were they illegal, likedebuggers—you could not install one if you had one, withoutknowing your computer's root password. And neitherthe FBI norMicrosoft Support would tell you that.

也可以通过安装修改后的系统内核来绕过版权监视器。丹最终会发现在世纪之交就已经存在的免费内核，甚至整个免费操作系统。但它们不仅是非法的，就像调试器一样-如果你有调试器，你就不能安装一个，因为你不知道你的计算机的根密码。无论是FBI还是微软的支持人员都不会告诉你这一点。

Dan concluded that he couldn't simply lend Lissa his computer. But hecouldn't refuse to help her, because he loved her. Every chance tospeak with her filled him with delight. And that she chose him to askfor help, that could mean she loved him too.

丹得出结论，他不能简单地把电脑借给丽莎。但是他不能拒绝帮助她，因为他爱她。每一次与她交谈的机会都使他欣喜若狂。她选择他来寻求帮助，这可能意味着她也爱他。

Dan resolved the dilemma by doing something even moreunthinkable—he lent her the computer, and told her his password.This way, if Lissa read his books, Central Licensing would think hewas reading them. It was still a crime, but the SPA would notautomatically find out about it. They would only find out if Lissareported him.

丹做了一件更不可思议的事情来解决这个难题--他把电脑借给她，并告诉她他的密码。这样，如果丽莎读了他的书，中央授权中心就会认为他在看书。这仍然是一种犯罪，但SPA不会自动发现这一点。他们只会知道利萨是否举报了他。

Of course, if the school ever found out that he had given Lissa hisown password, it would be curtains for both of them as students,regardless of what she had used it for. School policy was that anyinterference with their means of monitoring students' computer use wasgrounds for disciplinary action. It didn't matter whether you didanything harmful—the offense was making it hard for theadministrators to check on you. They assumed this meant you weredoing something else forbidden, and they did not need to know what itwas.

当然，如果学校发现他给了丽莎自己的密码，这对他们两个学生来说都是无关紧要的，不管她把密码用来做什么。学校的政策是，任何干扰他们监控学生使用计算机的手段都是纪律处分的理由。你是否做了任何有害的事都无关紧要--这种冒犯让管理员很难检查你。他们认为这意味着你在做其他被禁止的事情，他们不需要知道是什么。

Students were not usually expelled for this—not directly.Instead they were banned from the school computer systems, and wouldinevitably fail all their classes.

学生通常不会因此而被开除--不是直接开除。相反，他们被禁止进入学校的计算机系统，而且难免会导致所有课程不及格。

Later, Dan would learn that this kind of university policy startedonly in the 1980s, when university students in large numbers beganusing computers. Previously, universities maintained a differentapproach to student discipline; they punished activities that wereharmful, not those that merely raised suspicion.

后来，丹了解到，这种大学政策是在20世纪80年代才开始的，当时大学生大量使用计算机。以前，大学对学生纪律采取不同的方法；他们惩罚有害的活动，而不是那些只会引起怀疑的活动。

Lissa did not report Dan to the SPA. His decision to help her led totheir marriage, and also led them to question what they had beentaught about piracy as children. The couple began reading about thehistory of copyright, about the Soviet Union and its restrictions oncopying, and even the original United States Constitution. They movedto Luna, where they found others who had likewise gravitated away fromthe long arm of the SPA. When the Tycho Uprising began in 2062, theuniversal right to read soon became one of its central aims.

丽莎没有向SPA举报丹。他帮助她的决定导致了他们的婚姻，也让他们质疑他们小时候是怎么对待海盗的。这对夫妇开始阅读版权的历史，关于苏联及其对复制的限制，甚至是美国宪法的原版。他们搬到了露娜，在那里他们发现了其他同样从SPA的长臂上被吸引过来的人。当第谷起义在2062年开始时，全民阅读权很快成为其核心目标之一。

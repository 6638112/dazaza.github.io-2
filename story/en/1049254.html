McKinsey Scandal Claims Firm's Leader
麦肯锡丑闻声称公司领导人

2021-02-25 08:12:39

McKinsey is continuing to reckon with its role in the opioid crisis, with the consulting giant announcing on Wednesday that its global managing partner, Kevin Sneader, would leave the firm. Sneader's departure, which stemmed from a vote of 600 senior partners, follows McKinsey's $574 million legal battle to settle claims that its advice had "exacerbated the deadly US opioid crisis." The firm counseled Purdue Pharma, the maker of the painkiller OxyContin, on the payment of “rebates” to drugstores predicated on the number of people who died or became addicted after using the drug.

麦肯锡将继续考虑其在阿片类药物危机中的作用，这家咨询巨头周三宣布其全球管理合伙人凯文·斯内德（Kevin Sneader）将离开公司。 Sneader的离职是由600位高级合伙人投票决定的，随后麦肯锡（McKinsey）进行了5.74亿美元的法律诉讼，以解决其建议加剧了美国致命的阿片类药物危机。该公司为止痛药OxyContin的制造商普度制药（Purdue Pharma）提供了法律咨询，建议其根据药房死亡或上瘾的人数向药店支付“回扣”。

During Sneader's "tumultuous tenure", the firm has dealt with controversies related to its work in Saudi Arabia and South Africa, where it eventually returned "tens of millions of dollars in fees," The New York Times reports.

在Sneader的动荡任期中，该公司处理了与在沙特阿拉伯和南非的工作有关的争议，最终在该州退还了数千万美元的费用， ＃34;纽约时报报道。

McKinsey has also been involved in a recent controversy for its work with the French government in connection with the rollout of the coronavirus vaccine, which was "derided for being far too slow."

麦肯锡还因其与法国政府在冠状病毒疫苗的推出方面的合作而参与了最近的争议，这被认为是太慢了。

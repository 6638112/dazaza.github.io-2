Legal Design, a designer’s perspective on this new practice
法律设计，设计师对这种新做法的看法

2021-02-27 05:03:11

Legal Design is an approach theorised since 2014 by Margaret Hagan at Stanford University in California. This particularly innovative approach brings the digital transformation in law to a new angle of reflection with a collaborative methodology involving several stakeholders (legal experts, designers, developers and especially users).

Legal Design是加州斯坦福大学的玛格丽特·哈根（Margaret Hagan）自2014年以来提出的一种理论方法。这种特别创新的方法通过涉及多个利益相关者（法律专家，设计师，开发人员，尤其是用户）的协作方法，将法律数字化转型带入了新的视角。

In France, the year 2017 was a turning point with the emergence of a new trend that seems to be continuing and consolidating: lawyers themselves are becoming players in the field of innovation. The digital transformation of law is appearing from a new and much more collaborative angle. Law has become an object of innovation on which lawyers now work together with technical profiles in order to create new products and services with high added value.

在法国，2017年是一个转折点，新趋势的出现似乎正在持续和巩固：律师本身正在成为创新领域的参与者。法律的数字化转型正在从一个新的，更具协作性的角度出现。法律已经成为创新的对象，律师现在可以与技术档案一起工作，以创造具有高附加值的新产品和服务。

It is in this new context that legal design is being developed. It is an outstanding illustration which demonstrates that innovation in law is not exclusively technological but also involves rethinking the way in which law is designed. Legal design is a collaborative approach full of promises, provided it is thoroughly grasped through its subtleties and complexity.

正是在这种新背景下，法律设计得以发展。这是一个杰出的例证，表明法律创新不仅是技术创新，而且还涉及对法律设计方式的重新思考。法律设计是一种充满希望的协作方法，前提是它的精妙之处和复杂性将其彻底掌握。

From my point of view, law has always been something inaccessible. Almost all of us have experienced a situation where we had to sign a document without paying too much attention to the important lines. This anxious feeling of reading incomprehensible legal paragraphs. A feeling of failure when a problem arises because we did not read the right line that contained the statement of our rights.

从我的角度来看，法律一直是难以接近的。几乎我们所有人都经历过这样一种情况，即我们不得不在不十分注意重要事项的前提下签署文件。阅读令人难以理解的法律段落的这种焦虑感。出现问题时会感到失败，因为我们没有阅读包含我们权利声明的正确内容。

From my point of view as a French citizen and designer, Legal Design is an innovative discipline which reflects a new approach to law in order to make it more accessible and understandable for citizens. Today, I notice that the deliverables that emerge in Legal Design projects are essentially related to legal documentation in order to make them more honest for the citizen. Tomorrow, the approach will take on a new dimension by rethinking the essence of law by working on services as to optimize access to justice, particularly in its digitalization.

从我作为法国公民和设计师的角度来看，法律设计是一门创新的学科，它反映了一种新的法律方法，以使公民可以更容易地理解和理解法律。今天，我注意到法律设计项目中出现的可交付成果实质上与法律文档有关，以使它们对公民更加诚实。明天，该方法将通过重新研究法律的本质而进入一个新的层面，即致力于服务，以优化诉诸司法的途径，特别是在数字化方面。

Design, tech and law are three areas which collaborate with each others to achieve the best possible result. But in my opinion, there is one last entity missing in this pattern, which is the user being at the center of the process! In the UX Design (User eXperience Design) approach, the user is at the center of the creation process throughout all the project.

设计，技术和法律是相互协作以实现最佳结果的三个领域。但是我认为，该模式中缺少最后一个实体，那就是用户处于流程的中心！在UX设计（用户体验设计）方法中，在整个项目中，用户都是创建过程的中心。

我们可以看到，这个定义非常完整，因为它涵盖了设计的所有领域，无论是工业设计，服务设计，产品设计，游戏设计，UX设计等。但是，我认为这个定义仍然很复杂。如果非设计师试图通过阅读这句话来理解我们的工作，我怀疑他是否掌握了我们职业的精髓，即：

Design is everywhere. In our daily lives, we use products, whether physical, digital or services that have been conceived by designers. For many, design is only about the visual aspect of a product. We’ve all heard the expression “It’s design!” as a crude expression of “I think it’s beautiful!”. However, it is not about what the product looks like, but more about its functionality, practicality and aesthetics. In any case, it is important to remember that design is multidisciplinary and uses, among other things, methodologies and tools to solve sometimes complex problems, offering better products, services and functionalities, and above all to ask the right questions. In UX Design, empathy and understanding of the user’s needs are also central notions of our profession.

设计无处不在。在我们的日常生活中，我们使用设计师设计的产品，无论是物理产品，数字产品还是服务。对于许多人来说，设计仅与产品的视觉方面有关。我们都听到过“这是设计！”一词。作为“我认为它很漂亮！”的粗略表达。但是，这与产品的外观无关，而与产品的功能性，实用性和美观性有关。无论如何，重要的是要记住设计是多学科的，除其他外，它使用方法和工具来解决有时很复杂的问题，提供更好的产品，服务和功能，最重要的是提出正确的问题。在UX设计中，同理心和对用户需求的理解也是我们专业的核心概念。

Created in 1980 by Rolfe Faste, Design Thinking is a non-linear methodology which consists of applying, through a collaborative process, analytical and intuitive thinking in five steps: empathize, define, ideate, prototype and test.

由罗尔夫·法斯特（Rolfe Faste）于1980年创建的“设计思维”是一种非线性方法，包括通过协作过程在五个步骤中应用分析性思维和直观思维：同情，定义，构想，原型和测试。

In 1990, Donald Norman coined the term “UX Design” as a way of describing the quality of the user experience, which includes all aspects of the end-user’s interaction with the business, its services and its products.

1990年，唐纳德·诺曼（Donald Norman）创造了“ UX设计”一词，用以描述用户体验的质量，其中包括最终用户与企业，服务和产品的互动的各个方面。

From the beginning of a new project, several disciplines are important to consider, such as the exploration of user data (UX research), the analysis and formalization of the message we want to design (UX design), the artistic direction we will apply to the document (graphic design) or a user interface (UI) if the future product tends towards an interface.

从一个新项目的开始，就需要考虑几个学科，例如用户数据的探索（UX研究），我们要设计的消息的分析和形式化（UX设计）以及我们将要应用的艺术指导。文档（图形设计）或用户界面（UI）（如果将来的产品倾向于界面）。

In our Legal Design projects, we apply this iterative methodology, taking into account all the actors in the process, i.e. designer, lawyer, developer, client and users, from exploring data to evaluation by testing the solution with the target users. Throughout the project, we will start by collecting data in a qualitative and/or quantitative way through user research in order to analyze it and study the problem (the “why”) of the product / service. Then we will develop the solution which will allow us to answer it in a desired direction (the “how”). In this solution, we will work on the content (the message), the location of its content for a good readability (ergonomics) or the graphic layer that will complete its visual aspect (aesthetics). This is one of the cases which demonstrates the multidisciplinarity in our profession of designer.

在我们的法律设计项目中，我们采用了这种迭代方法，并考虑了过程中的所有参与者，即设计师，律师，开发人员，客户和用户，从探索数据到通过与目标用户测试解决方案进行评估。在整个项目中，我们将首先通过用户研究以定性和/或定量的方式收集数据，以便对其进行分析并研究产品/服务的问题（“为什么”）。然后，我们将开发解决方案，使我们能够在所需的方向（“如何”）上回答它。在此解决方案中，我们将处理内容（消息），内容位置以提高可读性（人机工程学）或将完成视觉效果的图形层（美学）。这是表明我们的设计师行业具有多学科性的案例之一。

To bring a user-oriented project to life, you need to gather several skills. If you are not a designer, you can practice UX Design workshops because you don’t need to have a design background to animate or practice them. However, there are notable differences in a workshop animated by a UX designer who is experienced in practicing. The first difference is the empathy characteristic of a designer. It is important to always bring the subject being worked on back to the study of the project and user-centered issue. Then, a designer has extensive experience and knowledge of many tools which can be flexibly adapted in practice and implementation of a methodology adapted to the product / service. Thanks to this, he is constantly working on improving his methodology through practice and feedback. Finally, designers constantly work on their creativity, which allows them to bring the different typologies of a product / service to fruition.

为了使面向用户的项目栩栩如生，您需要掌握一些技能。如果您不是设计师，则可以参加UX设计讲习班，因为您不需要具有设计背景来进行动画或练习。但是，由经验丰富的UX设计师制作的工作室存在显着差异。第一个区别是设计师的同理心特征。始终将正在研究的主题重新带回研究项目和以用户为中心的问题，这一点很重要。然后，设计人员对许多工具具有丰富的经验和知识，可以在实践和实施适合产品/服务的方法时灵活地进行调整。因此，他不断地通过实践和反馈来改进自己的方法。最后，设计师会不断地发挥自己的创造力，这使他们能够将产品/服务的不同类型付诸实践。

UX Design is an accessible approach in which a designer will bring added value in the animation of workshops by guiding the reflections while not losing the objective of the project.

UX设计是一种可访问的方法，通过该方法设计师可以通过引导思考而又不失项目目标的情况下，在车间动画中带来附加值。

When we are involved in a user-centric project, we have to work with real users, i.e. those who will use the future product/service.

当我们参与以用户为中心的项目时，我们必须与实际用户（即将使用未来产品/服务的用户）合作。

I had the chance to participate with my colleague legal counsel Sidy JUSTE at The Legal Design Summit in Helsinki in 2019! This one-day conference was a great source of information on the actors of innovations in law and technology. Nevertheless, there were still quite a few actors from the world of design to take part in this gigantic brainstorming, as there could be today in the design landscape.

我有机会与我的同事法律顾问Sidy JUSTE参加了2019年在赫尔辛基举行的法律设计峰会！这次为期一天的会议是有关法律和技术创新参与者的重要信息来源。然而，就像今天的设计界一样，仍然有很多来自设计领域的演员参加这种巨大的头脑风暴。

If I had to place Legal Design following this conference in the Gartner Hype Cycle, which illustrates the five major stages that each innovative concept goes through before reaching its stage of maturity, I would put it on the sidelines of phase 2, which represents “the peak of inflated expectations” and which results in a hype around this new approach.

如果我必须在本次会议之后将法律设计放在Gartner Hype Cycle中，它说明了每个创新概念在达到其成熟阶段之前经历的五个主要阶段，我将其放在阶段2的边缘，这表示“期望过高的高峰”，这导致对这种新方法的大肆宣传。

Working on Legal Design projects without designers will have a potential impact in the legal message but will potentially lose its primary usefulness towards its users. It is important to remember that the persons who conceive the new product/service are not the future users and that there will be a gap between the usefulness of your product and the future users.

在没有设计师的情况下进行法律设计项目可能会对法律信息产生潜在影响，但可能会失去其对用户的主要用处。重要的是要记住，构思新产品/服务的人不是未来的用户，并且您的产品的实用性和未来的用户之间将存在差距。

The expertise of workshops related to a user-oriented method has a usefulness in the learning and evolution of Legal Design BUT does not make everything in the work of reflection and conception of a product which is physical (printed) or digital. Lawyers must not reduce design to a set of tools that you take out of the hat to become creative.

与面向用户的方法有关的研讨会的专业知识在法律设计的学习和发展中很有用，但BUT并不能完全反映和反映物理（印刷）或数字产品的概念。律师不得将设计简化为您想出办法才能发挥创造力的一组工具。

If we work on Legal Design projects without a legal expert, we will have a document with a graphic facilitation and (maybe?)a strong impact, but the legal message will not have been carefully thought out and there could be legal breaches that would lose the legitimacy of the document. As a result, it could be more complex to read and understand or it could be legally invalid.

如果我们在没有法律专家的情况下从事法律设计项目，我们将拥有一个图形化的文档，并且（可能吗？）具有很强的影响力，但是法律信息不会被仔细考虑，并且可能会违反法律，从而导致损失文件的合法性。结果，阅读和理解可能更加复杂，或者在法律上无效。

Legal Design is more than just infographics describing new laws or a new experience for the GDPR compliance by accepting cookies from a website. It is a global approach in which the entities involved will work on the user’s experience before, during and after their interactions with the product/service. It is necessary to think in a more global way and not just a product as such which will respond to a primary use of reading. Legal Design will be built in a team composed of a designer and a legal expert. Together, this duo will complement each other in the many issues related to this discipline.

法律设计不仅仅是通过接受网站的cookie来描述新法律或GDPR合规性新体验的信息图表。这是一种全球性方法，所涉及的实体将在与产品/服务进行交互之前，之中和之后根据用户的体验进行工作。有必要以更全球化的方式进行思考，而不仅仅是思考将主要用于阅读的产品本身。法律设计将建立在一个由设计师和法律专家组成的团队中。在一起，这对二人组将在与该学科有关的许多问题中相互补充。

To open a dialogue between our two professions, which are so different but so complementary.

在我们两个截然不同但互补的专业之间展开对话。

To define a common vocabulary for a better understanding and adaptation of the discourse between legal and design.

定义通用词汇表，以更好地理解和适应法律与设计之间的论述。

To work together in order to create a synergy with an innovative impact on future products / services

携手合作，以创造对未来产品/服务产生创新影响的协同作用

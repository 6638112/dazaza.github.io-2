How I Practice Piano
我如何练习钢琴

2021-05-10 07:49:03

This is my usual method of practicing, gleaned from my teacher, old posts on the Piano Street forums, Graham Fitch’s Practising the Piano, and various other books and resources. It’s rare that I follow these steps exactly, but they are an ideal to aim for.

这是我通常的练习方法，从我的老师收集，钢琴街头论坛的老帖子，格雷厄姆·惠田练习钢琴，以及各种其他书籍和资源。我很罕见地遵循这些步骤，但它们是瞄准的理想选择。

Choose one section of the piece to practice, which is short enough that you can memorize it in a few tries. If you can’t, break it down into even smaller pieces. If it’s too easy to memorize, choose a bigger section. Include the first notes of the following section to make joining sections easier later on. For example, if your section is the first two bars of the piece, include the first note from bar three.

选择这件作品的一部分练习，这很短，你可以在几次尝试中记住它。如果你不能，将它陷入较小的作品。如果它太容易记忆，请选择更大的部分。包括以下部分的第一个音符，以便在后面更轻松地进行加入部分。例如，如果您的部分是该件的前两个条，请包括来自栏三个的第一个音符。

Practice the section slowly enough, at first, to avoid making mistakes. If you keep making mistakes when practicing then you are just going to get good at making them, so it’s best to avoid them in the first place. Use the correct fingering, dynamics, articulation, etc. from the very beginning; you are never going to get around to “fixing” it later. Do not work on this section for more than twenty minutes. Start hands separately at a slow tempo using the metronome. If you can play the section five times in a row without hesitating or making a mistake, increase the tempo and practice again, until you can play at that tempo five times in a row. When one hand is in danger of getting tired, switch to practicing the other one. Aim to increase the speed to above the target performance tempo, so that playing at performance tempo is easier by comparison.

起初慢慢地练习这些部分，以避免犯错误。如果你在练习时不断犯错，那么你就会善于制作它们，所以最好避开它们。从一开始就使用正确的指法，动态，关节等;你以后永远不会绕过“修复”它。在这个部分上不起超过二十分钟。使用节拍器在慢速速度下分开开始。如果您可以在不犹豫或犯错的情况下连续五次播放部分，并且再次增加节奏和练习，直到您可以连续五次播放该节奏。当一只手有疲倦的危险时，切换到练习另一个。旨在将速度提高到目标性能节奏的速度，以便在性能速度上进行比较更容易。

Once you can play each hand individually above the target tempo, practice hands together. Start with a tempo even slower than you think you need—if you are having trouble coordinating, reduce the speed. Once you can play it flawlessly and without hesitation at least five times in a row, start increasing the speed in increments. Aim to get to a speed above the target performance tempo.

一旦你可以单独地在目标节奏上方播放，练习双手。从节奏开始速度比你认为需要慢 - 如果您在协调时遇到问题，请降低速度。一旦您可以完美无瑕，毫不犹豫地连续犹豫，开始以增量增加速度。旨在达到目标性能节奏的速度。

Once you can play the section hands together above the target performance tempo, continue practicing it until you can’t get it wrong—but no longer than the twenty-minute limit. Some sections will take much less than twenty minutes to master. Others will take more, which is a sign that the section you’ve chosen is too big and should be broken down further.

一旦你可以在目标性能节奏上方播放一部分手，继续练习它，直到你不能得到错误 - 但不超过二十分钟的限制。有些部分将花费远远超过20分钟到掌握。其他人将需要更多，这是一个标志，你所选择的部分太大，应该进一步分解。

Start working on a different section, either of the same piece or a different piece, and don’t touch this section again until the next day. The next day, if you can play the section perfectly in only a few tries, move on to learning a different section. If not, repeat the steps above again. It will take less time the second time around.

开始在不同的部分上工作，同一部件或不同的部分，并且在第二天之前不要再次触摸这一部分。第二天，如果您只能在几次尝试中完美地播放该部分，请继续学习不同的部分。如果没有，请重复上面的步骤。第二次，它将花费更少的时间。

Once two consecutive sections have been mastered, work on joining them together as a new section. By adding the first note of the next section during practice, it is much easier to join sections together at this step.

一旦掌握了两个连续的部分，就可以作为新部分加入它们。通过在实践期间添加下一节的第一个音符，在此步骤将在一起加入段更容易。

Continue in this way, working on small sections and joining them together, until the entire piece is learned.

继续以这种方式，在小部分上工作并在一起加入它们，直到学习整个部分。

While increasing the tempo, you may reach a point where you can’t play any faster, no matter how hard you try. What is likely happening is that the hand motions you are using—which come naturally at the slower tempo you started with—simply will not work at such high speeds. You are basically trying to run by speed-walking faster and faster. One way to get around this is to try playing the section at full speed first, ignoring mistakes, just to figure out what the necessary motions feel like. Then, you can try to practice those motions at a slow speed, not the motions that are intuitive at a slow speed.

在增加节奏的同时，无论您如何努力尝试，您都可能达到无法播放的点。可能发生的是，您使用的手动运动 - 在您开始的较慢节奏中自然地实现 - 只需以如此高速工作。您基本上尝试通过速度行走速度更快，更快地运行。解决这一目标的一种方法是尝试以全速播放部分，忽略错误，只是弄清楚必要的动作所觉得。然后，您可以尝试以缓慢的速度练习这些动作，而不是速度慢速直观的动作。

It is a good idea to occasionally play a piece very slowly, even after you’ve mastered it. If you only play the piece fast, the piece will deteriorate as mistakes inevitably get introduced and never get fixed. Finally, it’s a good idea to occasionally play hands separately, especially for counterpoint, even after you’ve mastered the piece. In fact, for counterpoint it’s a good idea to learn each voice individually with the correct fingering (even if that means passing the voice back and forth between the hands) and occasionally play each voice separately.

即使在你掌握它之后，偶尔也很慢地播放一块是个好主意。如果你只能快速播放这件作品，那么这件作品将变得恶化，因为不可避免地引入并从未得到固定的错误。最后，即使在你掌握了这件作品之后，偶尔播放手是个好主意，尤其是对抵消。事实上，为了对比，通过正确的指法来单独学习每个声音是一个好主意（即使这意味着在手之间来回通过声音），偶尔分别播放每个声音。

Union that lost its bid to represent Amazon workers in AL files objections with NLRB, claiming the company improperly pressured workers to oppose unionization
失去其竞标的联盟代表亚马逊工作人员与NLRB的反对意见，声称公司不当地迫使工人反对联盟

2021-04-19 22:24:48

SEATTLE — The union that lost its bid to represent Amazon warehouse staff in Bessemer, Ala., earlier this month is now seeking to overturn the election results, claiming in a legal filing that the e-commerce giant improperly pressured workers to oppose unionization.

西雅图 - 失去竞标的联盟代表亚马尔，阿拉伯贝塞姆尔的亚马逊仓库工作人员，现在，本月早些时候正在寻求推翻选举结果，以法律提出，电子商务巨头不当地迫使工人反对工会。

The Retail, Wholesale and Department Store Union filed its objections with the National Labor Relations Board Friday, a move that triggers a hearing process to resolve its claims. Labor laws require the agency to set that hearing within 15 business days of the vote tally, or by April 30. If the union succeeds, the labor board could call for a new election.

零售，批发和百货商店联盟将与国家劳工关系委员会周五提出反对，这是一个触发听证程序来解决其索赔的举措。劳动法要求原子能机构在投票的15个工作日内或在4月30日之前设定该听证会。如果联盟成功，劳工委员会可以致电新的选举。

The union said it would file objections when it lost the high-profile vote April 9, a lopsided defeat in which 1,798 workers opposed unionization and 738 supported it. That margin of victory left another 505 ballots uncounted because they would not have changed the outcome.

联盟表示，当它失去高调投票4月9日时，它会提出异议，这是一个不平衡的失败，其中1,798名员工反对工会和738个支持它。那个胜利的边际留下了另外505个选票，因为他们不会改变结果。

The RWDSU alleged Amazon’s tactics in the election “constitute conduct which prevented a free and uncoerced exercise of choice by the employees,” adding the actions “constitute grounds to set the election aside.”

Rwdsu所谓的选举中的亚马逊的策略“构成了雇员阻止自由和不开心行使的行为，”增加行动“构成一个地方，以便在一边设定选举。”

Amazon spokeswoman Heather Knox questioned the RWDSU’s challenge in the aftermath of the Bessemer staff overwhelmingly opposing unionization.

亚马逊发言人希瑟·克诺克斯质疑rwdsu在贝塞尔员工的后果中的挑战绝大多数反对工会。

“Rather than accepting these employees’ choice, the union seems determined to continue misrepresenting the facts in order to drive its own agenda,” Knox said in an emailed statement. “We look forward to the next steps in the legal process.”

“而不是接受这些员工的选择，该联盟似乎决定继续歪曲事实，以便推动自己的议程，”诺克斯在一封电子邮件发表中说。 “我们期待法律程序的下一步。”

While many of the union’s allegations echo claims it made during the course of the organizing drive, it made a series of new allegations as well. In its filing, the union accused Amazon of firing a union supporter “for passing out union authorization cards in non-working areas.” The RWDSU also accused the company of disciplining “an outspoken supporter” of the union for challenging Amazon’s arguments during mandatory meetings. The union didn’t name either worker or disclose the specific discipline.

虽然许多联盟的指控呼应声称在组织驾驶过程中索赔，但它也制作了一系列新的指控。在其申请中，Union指定了亚马逊向工会支持者解雇了“用于在非工作领域传递联盟授权卡”。 RWDSU还指责公司在强制会议期间挑战亚马逊的争论挑战亚马逊的论据。联盟没有命名为工人或披露具体的学科。

The RWDSU included a charge it previously leveled at Amazon over a U.S. Postal Service mailbox that popped up in front of the warehouse just after voting started. The union obtained emails through Freedom of Information Act requests that show that Amazon employees pressed the Postal Service to install a mailbox just as the seven-week mail-in balloting began.

RWDSU包括之前在亚马逊上升级的费用，在仓库前面突然出现在投票开始后的仓库。联盟通过信息法案的自由获得了电子邮件，表明亚马逊员工按下邮政服务安装邮箱，就像七周的邮件投票开始。

In its filing, the union argued the mailbox, which has no U.S. Postal Service markings, created the impression that Amazon had a role in collecting and counting ballots, potentially influencing their votes. And it noted that the NLRB, which set the rules for the election, rejected Amazon’s request to put ballot boxes at the warehouse for in-person voting. The labor board cited concerns about the safety of Amazon workers and agency staff members during the coronavirus pandemic.

在其申请中，工会争论邮箱，邮箱没有美国邮政服务标记，创造了亚马逊在收集和计数选票中发挥作用的印象，可能会影响他们的投票。并指出，NLRB，该列出选举规则，拒绝了亚马逊的要求将投票箱放在仓库中的人入住的投票。劳工委员会对冠状病毒大流行期间亚马逊工人和机构工作人员的安全引起了担忧。

Amazon has previously defended the mailbox placement, saying it made voting easier for workers, and that only the Postal Service had access to it.

亚马逊以前曾为邮箱放置辩护，说它为工人做出了投票，并且只有邮政服务就可以访问它。

The union also claimed in its filing that Amazon’s successful effort to convince local county officials to change the timing of a traffic light just outside the warehouse improperly inhibited its ability to engage with workers at the end of their shifts. Amazon has said it requested the change to improve traffic flow.

联盟还声称，亚马逊成功地让当地县官员更改了仓库外的交通灯的时间不正确地抑制其在其班次结束时互动的能力。亚马逊表示要求改变改善交通流量。

And the union also alleged that a financial offer Amazon made to lure unhappy workers to quit amounted to an improper incentive to weed out pro-union workers. Amazon has countered those charges, noting that it began offering financial incentives to quit in 2014 and extends the initiative to all its North American warehouse staff.

联盟还据称，亚马逊的财务报价使诱惑不快乐的工人戒掉的措施，对杂草的激励不当。亚马逊已经反驳了这些指控，并指出它开始在2014年辞职的财务激励措施，并扩展了所有北美仓库工作人员的倡议。

The RWDSU filing also claims Amazon threatened layoffs, and even closing the warehouse, if workers voted for union representation. It alleges Amazon “disparately enforced” its pandemic-related social distancing policy in the warehouse, permitting anti-union staff to campaign but preventing pro-union workers from having the same sort of access.

RWDSU申请还声称亚马逊受到威胁的裁员，甚至关闭仓库，如果工人投票赞成联盟代表。它涉及亚马逊“贬低”它在仓库中的大流行相关的社会疏远政策，允许反工会员工竞选，但防止接合工人有同样的访问。

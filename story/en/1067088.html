'Google's No-Click Searches -- Good Or Evil?'
'谷歌的没有点击搜索 - 好或邪恶？

2021-06-21 05:29:04

That Google is the dominant force in web and mobile search won’t surprise you. What might, though, is that roughly two thirds of the searches on Google never leave the search results page. That is, the searchers get what they are looking for without leaving Google. These are called “no-click” or “zero-click” searches.

谷歌是网络和移动搜索中的主要力量不会让您感到惊讶。但是，可能的是，谷歌上的搜索大约三分之二的搜索永远不会离开搜索结果页面。也就是说，搜索者在不离开谷歌的情况下获取他们正在寻找的东西。这些称为“no-click”或“零点击”搜索。

The percentages vary a bit depending on device, geography, and the precise definition of “no-click,” but it’s clear that Google is retaining a large share of searchers within its domain. Search expert Rand Fishkin, who compiled much of this data, thinks that the percentage of no-click searches will continue to rise.

百分比取决于设备，地理位置和“no-click”的精确定义，因此百分比变化了一部分，但很明显谷歌在其域内保留了大量搜索者。搜索专家Rand Fishkin，编译大部分数据，认为禁止点击搜索的百分比将继续上升。

In their early years, search engines were expected to produce a set of links to the most relevant web pages. “Ten blue links” were the norm. Over time, that evolved—ads appeared, often looking much like the natural, or “organic,” results. Links to images, videos, and other content types also began to appear.

在早期，搜索引擎预计将生成一组与最相关的网页的链接。 “十个蓝色链接”是常态。随着时间的推移，即进化广告出现，通常看起来很像自然，或“有机”的结果。还开始出现到图像，视频和其他内容类型的链接。

Even then, to get the information you wanted, you still clicked through to another site. That was good news for both entrepreneurs and large brands: if you created content that people wanted, Google would send them to you—for free!

即便如此，要获取您想要的信息，您仍然单击到另一个站点。这对企业家和大品牌来说是个好消息：如果你创造了人们想要的内容，谷歌会把它们发送给你 - 免费！

When Google launched, there were already popular search engines—AltaVista, Yahoo, Excite, Lycos and others. These competitors had a lot in common. First, their search functionality was a small text entry box on a page cluttered with ads, news, content, directory links, and other distracting elements. They all saw being a “portal,” a monetized doorway to everything on the Web, as their path to success.

谷歌推出时，已经有流行的搜索引擎 - 阿尔塔维斯塔，雅虎，兴奋，Lycos等。这些竞争对手有很多共同之处。首先，他们的搜索功能是一个小型文本输入框，在页面上混乱了广告，新闻，内容，目录链接和其他分散注意力元素。他们都看到了一个“门户网站”，是一个货币化的门口到了网络上的一切，因为他们的成功之路。

Second, their search results weren’t very good. Because their rankings were highly dependent on page content, those rankings could be manipulated by optimizers. Even when there was no aggressive optimization, often the results were not good answers to the search because so many pages were designed in a way that confused the search engine algorithms.

其次，他们的搜索结果并不是很好。因为他们的排名高度依赖于页面内容，所以可以通过优化器操纵这些排名。即使在没有积极的优化时，结果也不会对搜索的答案并不好，因为许多页面是以困惑搜索引擎算法的方式设计的。

Google is an early case study in my book Friction, where I show how they focused on minimizing user effort. Their home page was essentially white space with a search box in the middle—no confusion or distraction. This focus on the user’s intent stood in stark contrast to their competition.

谷歌是我的书摩擦早期的案例研究，我展示了他们如何专注于最大限度地减少用户努力。他们的主页基本上是白色空间，中间没有混乱或分心的搜索框。这种关注用户的意图与他们的竞争对手的鲜明对比。

The big difference, though, was in the quality of Google’s results. Their PageRank algorithm created a map of the Web as a giant recommendation engine. On competing search engines, a user might go through pages of results, clicking on many links, before finding what they wanted. With Google, the desired result was likely the top one, or at least in the top few.

但是，谷歌的结果质量很大。他们的PageRank算法创建了一个Web的地图作为巨型推荐引擎。在竞争搜索引擎上，用户可能会通过结果，然后单击许多链接，然后在找到他们想要的内容之前。通过Google，所需的结果可能是顶部，或者至少在顶部。

Over time, Google continued to deliver ever more relevant links while also making manipulating results more difficult. But no detail missed their attention. For example, the user who goes to Google.com finds the cursor already positioned in the search box. A small thing, perhaps but it saves every user a click or tap. Even today, I constantly encounter web pages where entering something is to sole purpose of the page but the cursor isn’t pre-positioned for me.

随着时间的推移，谷歌继续提供更相关的联系，同时也使操纵结果更加困难。但没有细节错过了他们的注意。例如，去Google.com的用户发现已经位于搜索框中的光标。也许是一件小事，但它可以节省每个用户点击或点击。即使在今天，我也经常遇到网页，进入某些东西是为了唯一的目的，但光标不是预先定位的。

Another major keystroke saver was predictive search. Start typing in the search box, and Google immediately begins to offer guesses for your search term. Type “w” and the first two suggestions will be “weather” and “Walmart,” no doubt the two most common searches starting with that letter. Additional characters spawn new high-probability guesses.

另一个主要的击键保护程序是预测的搜索。在搜索框中开始键入，谷歌立即开始为您的搜索词提供猜测。类型“W”和前两个建议将是“天气”和“沃尔玛”，毫无疑问，这两个最常见的搜索是从该信中开始的。其他字符产生了新的高概率猜测。

In my own experience, it’s rare that I have to type in a complete search phrase. In fact, Google’s suggestions are often a better way of phrasing what I wanted and likely yield more relevant results.

在我自己的体验中，我必须输入完整的搜索短语。事实上，谷歌的建议往往是一种更好的方式对我想要的东西和可能产生更多相关结果的方法。

Here’s the dilemma for Google: once all the user has to do is type a character or two to search, how does further reduce user effort? The answer is simple: if Google understands what the user wants, why not just deliver the information instead of a link to the information?

这是Google的困境：一旦所有用户都必须键入一个字符或两个搜索，如何进一步减少用户努力？答案很简单：如果谷歌了解用户想要什么，为什么不只是提供信息而不是信息链接？

Increasingly, Google tries to provide the information the searcher wants on the search results page. For example, if one clicks “weather” after typing “w,” Google provides a large amount of weather data for the user’s location at the top of the results: current conditions, plus hourly and daily forecasts. Most users probably find what they need without having to click through to weather.com, where the data is sourced.

尤为地，Google尝试在搜索结果页面上提供搜索者想要的信息。例如，如果在键入“W”之后单击“天气”，Google会在结果的顶部提供用户位置的大量天气数据：当前条件，加小时和每日预报。大多数用户可能会发现他们需要的东西，而无需单击到Weather.com，其中数据被源。

Delivering the desired information in a simple, standard format saves more than the initial click. If the user navigates to a new site, they may have to expend additional effort to acclimate to the new site’s design and to search the page for the desired information.

以简单的标准格式提供所需的信息，从初始单击中保存多。如果用户导航到新站点，则可能需要花费额外的精力来适应新的网站的设计，并搜索页面以获取所需信息。

In some cases, the effort differential could be quite high. Imagine, for example, scrolling through a lengthy FAQ page vs. Google delivering only the necessary answer. Even weather.com offers only a five-day forecast on its home page - if you want to know what’s predicted for a week from the current day, you’ll have to click to a different page.

在某些情况下，努力差异可能相当高。例如，想象一下，滚动到Google的冗长常见问题解答只提供必要的答案。甚至Weather.com即使是在其主页上只提供了五天的预测 - 如果您想知道当前一天的一周内预测的内容，您只需点击另一个页面。

User experience can suffer, too. More than once I’ve clicked through to the site from where Google obtained an answer and been assaulted by cookie warnings, pop-ups, and slow-loading pages packed with advertising.

用户体验也可能受到影响。一旦我点击了谷歌获得了答案的地方，并且一直被Cookie警告，弹出窗口攻击和被广告包装的缓慢加载页面攻击的网站。

A no-click result seems like a win for users, and it almost always is. The loser, if there is one, is the website where Google found the information. Users who might have lingered, consumed other content, subscribed, bought something, or created ad impressions now never get to the website.

一个单击鼠标效果似乎是用户的胜利，但它几乎总是是。失败者，如果有的话，是谷歌找到了这些信息的网站。可能已经徘徊，消耗其他内容，订阅，购买的东西或创建广告印象的用户现在永远不会到网站。

Google is being assailed on all sides from regulators trying to rein in their power. Ohio wants them to be regulated like a public utility. U.S. politicians are drafting antitrust legislation.

谷歌正在宣布侦查者的各方都在试图控制权力。俄亥俄州希望他们像公用事业一样受到监管。美国政客正在制定反垄断立法。

What’s different about the current anti-trust sentiment affecting not just Google but other big tech companies is that consumers may not agree they are being harmed. Historically, with no competition, monopolies charged higher prices and frequently offered poor service. If you live an an area with only one high speed internet provider, you may still experience this.

目前的反信托情绪影响不仅仅是谷歌，而且其他大型技术公司的不同之处在于消费者可能不同意他们受到伤害。从历史上看，没有竞争，垄断价格收取更高的价格，经常提供差的服务。如果您只有一家高速互联网提供商的一个区域，您仍可能遇到此问题。

The big tech companies aren’t monopolies in the classic sense, but dominate because of their scale and integration. No company, large or small, has the logistics infrastructure that Amazon does. Amazon’s customers, though, aren’t complaining. They love free, next-day delivery for almost anything. Facebook has billions of daily users, and none of them pay Facebook to use their services.

大科技公司在经典意义上并不垄断，而是因为他们的规模和整合而占主导地位。没有公司，大或小，具有亚马逊的物流基础设施。但是，亚马逊的客户不抱怨。他们喜欢自由，下一天的交付几乎任何东西。 Facebook拥有数十亿日用户，而且他们都没有支付Facebook以使用他们的服务。

Similarly, few users feel they are harmed by Google’s dominance. Users prefer Google despite very credible alternatives like Microsoft’s Bing. A key factor in keeping Google users loyal is that they can find what they need with lower effort. As long as using Google is faster and easier, searchers will gravitate to it.

同样，很少有用户觉得他们受到谷歌的主导地位伤害。用户喜欢谷歌尽管像微软的Bing这样非常可靠的替代方案。保持Google用户忠诚的关键因素是他们可以以较低的努力找到他们需要的东西。只要使用谷歌更快，更容易，搜索都会倾向于它。

In fact, Google itself disputes that third party websites are being harmed as described by Fishkin. They note that many searches don’t result in a click because the searcher refines their query or uses a link like “related searches.” They also point out that users can interact with a business directly without having to click. For example, a customer who viewed the address and operating hours of a local business could visit that business despite the lack of a click.

事实上，谷歌本身争议第三方网站被伤害，如鱼金所描述的。他们注意到许多搜索不会导致点击，因为搜索者改进了他们的查询或使用“相关搜索等链接”。他们还指出，用户可以直接与企业互动而无需单击。例如，顾客在当地业务的地址和营业时间查看的客户可能会访问这项业务，尽管缺少点击次数。

Beyond effort-saving, an additional factor that ensures no-click searches are here to stay is the explosion in smart speaker use. If you ask your Google Assistant or Alexa a question, you don’t want multiple options to get the information. You want the answer.

除了努力节约之外，还有一个确保禁止搜索的额外因素是智能扬声器使用中的爆炸。如果您要求您的Google助手或Alexa一个问题，则不希望多个选项获取信息。你想要答案。

I predict that Google will continue to use and expand its no-click results. Absent legislative or regulatory intervention, they have no reason to impair their user experience. Google’s popularity in search doesn’t stem primarily from monopolistic trickery, but from their relentless focus on making finding information faster and easier for their users.

我预测谷歌将继续使用并扩展其禁止的结果。没有立法或监管干预，他们没有理由削弱他们的用户体验。谷歌在搜索中的流行情况不会主要来自垄断诡计，而是从他们的无情的重点介绍，对他们的用户更快，更轻松地进行信息。

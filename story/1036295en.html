You’re not anonymous. I know your name, email, and company (2012)
您不是匿名的。我知道您的名字，电子邮件和公司（2012年）

2020-11-24 21:14:36

Sumit Suman recently visited a site, did not sign up for anything, did not connect via social media, but got a personal email from the site the next day.

Sumit Suman最近访问了一个网站，没有进行任何注册，没有通过社交媒体建立联系，但是第二天从该网站收到了一封个人电子邮件。

I’ve learned that there is a “website intelligence” network that tracks form submissions across their customer network.  So, if a visitors fills out a form on Site A with their name and email, Site B knows their name and email too as soon as they land on the site.

我了解到，有一个“网站情报”网络可以跟踪其客户网络中的表单提交情况。因此，如果访问者在站点A上填写其姓名和电子邮件的表单，则站点B登陆该网站后也立即知道其姓名和电子邮件。

It all started 2 weeks ago when I got a promotional email (anonymized to avoid promotion) offering to

这一切始于2周前，当时我收到了一封促销电子邮件（匿名以避免促销），

I get B2B marketing emails all the time but what caught my eye was the inclusion of a report snapshot for 42Floors.com showing names, companies, and emails of site visitors and the information seemed plausible.

我一直都在收到B2B营销电子邮件，但引起我注意的是，其中包含42Floors.com的报告快照，其中显示了网站访问者的姓名，公司和电子邮件，并且这些信息似乎合理。

I was both skeptical and concerned so I replied to the sales rep and asked how they could identify 42Floors’ visitors without something like an email link click-through.  His reply was forthright:

我既怀疑又担心，所以我回答了销售代表，并询问他们如何在没有电子邮件链接点击之类的东西的情况下识别42Floors的访客。他的回答是直截了当的：

For example, if [a visitor] went to XYZ.com and filled out a web form and then [the visitor] later visited 42floors.com, [42Floors] would be able to identify [the visitor] by name/email as well as company details even though [the visitor] never filled out a web form on [42Floors.com].

例如，如果[访问者]进入XYZ.com并填写了Web表单，然后[访问者]后来访问了42floors.com，则[42Floors]可以通过名称/电子邮件以及即使[访客]从未在[42Floors.com]上填写网络表格，公司的详细信息也是如此。

I was still skeptical.  So I signed up for a demo account and installed (and hastily removed) the tracker .  As promised, I began to see personally identifying information about our anonymous visitors.  Here are the live reports from this week.

我仍然持怀疑态度。因此，我注册了一个模拟帐户并安装（并匆匆删除了）tracker。按照承诺，我开始看到有关我们匿名访问者的个人识别信息。这是本周的现场报道。

Although only a small subset of users were identified with an email, that is likely due to the fact that this particular network is one of the smaller ones and hence only has information on a small percentage of all internet users.  I will be following up with an analysis of the reach of their larger peers.

尽管只有一小部分用户使用电子邮件标识，但这很可能是由于该特定网络是较小的网络之一，因此仅包含有关所有互联网用户的一小部分的信息。我将对他们的较大同龄人的影响力进行分析。

Expectations of privacy When a user visits a site without ever having voluntarily supplied information to that site, should the user have an expectation that their identity is private until they chose to reveal it?

对隐私的期望当用户在未自愿向该站点提供信息的情况下访问该站点时，该用户是否应该期望自己的身份是私有的，直到他们选择将其公开为止？

A real-world analogue would be this scenario: You drive to Home Depot and walk in.  Closed-circuit cameras match your face against a database of every shopper that has used a credit card at Walmart or Target and identifies you by name, address, and phone.  If you happen to walk out the front door without buying anything your phone buzzes with a text message from Home Depot offering you a 10% discount good for the next hour.

在这种情况下，可能是现实世界的模拟情况：您开车去Home Depot走进去。闭路摄像机将您的脸庞与在Walmart或Target上使用过信用卡并通过姓名，地址，和电话。如果您碰巧走出前门却不买任何东西，手机就会发出嗡嗡声，并显示来自Home Depot的短信，可在接下来的一个小时为您提供10％的折扣。

Farfetched?  I don’t think so.  I expect to see the first iterations of this Home Depot scenario become reality within a few years time.  All the necessary pieces already exist, they just haven’t been combined yet.

牵强？我不这么认为。我希望看到这种Home Depot方案的第一次迭代将在几年内成为现实。所有必要的部分已经存在，但尚未合并。

It’s inevitable, it’s going to happen, but it shouldn’t The realization that I’m being personally identified by name as I surf the net is deeply discomforting.  At 42Floors, we’ve made the decision not to use any visitor identification tools.  As for my own Macbook, I’ll probably write a browser plugin ala AdBlock to kill the trackers that make this identification possible.

这是不可避免的，它将要发生，但不应该这样。意识到我在网上冲浪时被人名识别的事实令人非常不自在。在42楼，我们已决定不使用任何访客识别工具。至于我自己的Macbook，我可能会写一个浏览器插件ala AdBlock杀死使这种识别成为可能的跟踪器。

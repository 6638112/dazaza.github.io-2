Beeple Mania
泥沼

2021-02-24 22:00:57

He makes digital art—pixels on screens depicting bizarre, hilarious, disturbing, and sometimes grotesque images. He smashes together pop culture, technology, and postapocalyptic terror into blistering commentaries on the way we live. A recent frame depicted Donald Trump wearing a leather mask and stripper’s pasties, taking a whip to the coronavirus bug (title: “Trump Dominating Covid”). On the day Jeff Bezos announced he was kicking himself upstairs, Beeple imagined the Amazon founder as a massive, threatening octopus emerging from the ocean as military helicopters circled above (“Release the Bezos”).

他制作数字艺术-屏幕上的像素描绘了奇异的，热闹的，令人不安的，有时甚至是怪诞的图像。他将流行文化，技术和世界末日的恐怖融合在一起，成为对我们生活方式的热烈评论。最近的一幅画框描绘了唐纳德·特朗普戴着皮面具和脱衣舞娘的馅饼，鞭打冠状病毒的虫子（标题：“特朗普统治柯维德”）。杰夫·贝佐斯（Jeff Bezos）宣布将自己踢上楼的那一天，比普（Beeple）想象亚马逊的创始人是一只巨大的，威胁性的章鱼，随着军用直升机在高空盘旋而从海洋中冒出来（“释放贝佐斯”）。

Beeple has 1.8 million Instagram followers. His work has been shown at two Super Bowl halftime shows and at least one Justin Bieber concert, but he has no gallery representation or foothold in the traditional art world.

Beeple有180万个Instagram粉丝。他的作品曾在两场超级碗半场演出和至少一场贾斯汀·比伯音乐会上展出，但他在传统艺术界没有画廊代表或立足之地。

And yet in December the first extensive auction of his art grossed $3.5 million in a single weekend.

然而在12月，他的艺术作品首次大规模拍卖在一个周末内就获得了350万美元的收入。

That money? It went to one Mike Winkelmann, thirty-nine, father of two, husband of a schoolteacher, resident of a Charleston, South Carolina, suburb, driver of a “fucking Toyota Corolla piece of shit.”

那钱吗一名三十九岁的迈克·温克尔曼（Mike Winkelmann），二岁的父亲，一名学校老师的丈夫，南卡罗来纳州查尔斯顿的居民，郊区的一名“他妈的丰田花冠狗屎”司机。

See, Beeple is Mike Winkelmann. Mike Winkelmann is Beeple. And in the weird worlds of high fashion, fine art, and cryptocurrency, it doesn’t get any weirder than this story.

看得出，比普是迈克·温克尔曼。 Mike Winkelmann是Beeple。在高级时装，精美艺术品和加密货币的怪异世界中，没有比这个故事更奇怪的了。

Expectations for December’s auction were on the high side of earthbound. That Saturday afternoon, Winkelmann—who looks like a young Bill Gates dressed in Steve Kornacki cosplay and speaks with a Wisconsin accent straight out of an old SNL sketch—and his wife, Jen, went to his brother’s backyard to monitor the auction. Pretty quickly, his brother, a former electrical engineer for Boeing, was scrawling sales figures on a whiteboard while the kids ran around the firepit, oblivious to the fact that every half hour another piece of Beeple’s digital art was selling for more than $100,000.

十二月拍卖的期望值很高。那个星期六的下午，温克尔曼（看起来像年轻的比尔·盖茨（Bill Gates）穿着史蒂夫·科纳奇（Steve Kornacki）角色扮演，和威斯康星州的口音直接来自旧的SNL素描），他的妻子詹（Jen）到他哥哥的后院监视拍卖。很快，他的兄弟曾是波音公司的一名电气工程师，在孩子们围着火炉奔跑时，他在白板上草草了销售数字，而事实上，每半个小时另一本Beeple的数字艺术作品的售价就超过了100,000美元。

The artist himself couldn’t bear to watch, and mostly spent his time tweeting out photos of the next pieces and answering questions from would-be buyers. At one point, he received a direct message from the musician Sean Lennon—son of John—who tweeted: “I can’t manage to do anything today but watch these mfing @beeple auctions tick down,” adding: “god damn Beeple Mania have mercy.”

这位画家本人不愿观看，并主要花费时间在推特上发下几幅照片并回答潜在买家的问题。有一次，他收到了音乐家约翰约翰之子肖恩·列侬的直接信息，他在推特上写道：“我今天除了设法在@beeple拍卖会上努力之外，什么也做不了，”他补充说：“上帝该死的Beeple Mania怜悯。”

Ten pieces sold that day, and another eleven on Sunday, including the grand finale: one long, continuous digital loop of every single piece auctioned off that weekend. A stunning bid came in with one second remaining on the clock. “The Complete MF Collection,” as Beeple dubbed the work, sold for $777,777.

当天售出了十件作品，周日则售出了十一件作品，其中包括大结局：该周末拍卖的每件作品都有一个漫长而连续的数字循环。令人惊叹的竞标价还剩一秒。 Beeple称其为“完整的MF系列”，售价为777,777美元。

It was one of those things you hear about and think, Do I have any idea what’s going on in the world? Is this normal and I’m just waaaayy out of touch, baking my bread while people are out there buying digital artwork using cryptocurrency?

这是您听到并思考的事情之一，我是否知道世界上正在发生什么？这是正常现象吗？我只是不介意，在人们在那里使用加密货币购买数字作品的时候烤面包？

What, pray tell, are these collectors even buying? Why would someone pay $777,777 for an MP4? Couldn’t you watch that on Instagram for free?

祈祷告诉我们，这些收藏家还在买什么？为什么有人要为MP4支付777,777美元？您不能在Instagram上免费观看吗？

Theoretically, yes, but the crypto art scene uses blockchain technology to authenticate and identify a single, unique piece of digital art. To understand how that piece of art sells for the price of a one-bedroom apartment in Brooklyn, one needs a brief primer on something called nonfungible tokens, or NFTs—digital goods that are bought and sold on emerging websites like Nifty Gateway, which hosted the Beeple auction. Nifty Gateway was founded in 2018 by the absurdly named Duncan Cock Foster, twenty-six, and his twin brother, Griffin. When asked to explain NFTs, Duncan used this analogy: Imagine you owned a pair of expensive Air Jordans. If Nike went out of business, those sneakers wouldn’t suddenly disappear from your closet. Why should digital goods—like a Fortnite skin or an original Beeple—be any different?

从理论上讲，是的，但是加密艺术界使用区块链技术来认证和识别单个独特的数字艺术。为了了解这件艺术品是如何以布鲁克林一间卧室的公寓的价格出售的，需要简要介绍一下称为“不可替代的代币”（NFTs）的东西，即在Nifty Gateway等新兴网站上买卖的数字商品。 Beeple拍卖会。 Nifty Gateway由荒谬的26岁的邓肯·库克·福斯特（Duncan Cock Foster）和他的双胞胎兄弟格里芬（Griffin）于2018年创立。当被要求解释NFT时，邓肯使用了一个比喻：假设您拥有一双昂贵的Air Jordans。如果耐克倒闭，那些运动鞋不会突然从你的壁橱里消失。为什么数字商品（例如Fortnite的皮肤或原始的Beeple）会有所不同？

And so: Nifty Gateway—one of several upstart online marketplaces in a field that also includes sites called MakersPlace and SuperRare—mints a unique NFT, assigns it to a piece of digital art, and stores it forever in that company’s custodian wallet. Anyone with an Internet connection can log on and see who owns that piece.

等等：Nifty Gateway是该领域的几个新贵在线市场之一，其中还包括MakersPlace和SuperRare网站-铸造了独特的NFT，将其分配给数字艺术作品，并将其永久存储在该公司的托管钱包中。拥有Internet连接的任何人都可以登录并查看谁拥有该作品。

Nifty Gateway was acquired in late 2019 by a more famous set of twins, Cameron and Tyler Winklevoss, best known as the guys who said Mark Zuckerberg stole their idea for Facebook. The Winklevii rode the cryptocurrency wave to the Bloomberg Billionaires Index and are bullish on NFTs. They ask why collecting digital art should be any different from collecting rare baseball cards. An item is worth what the market will pay.

Nifty Gateway于2019年末被更著名的一对双胞胎Cameron和Tyler Winklevoss收购，他们以马克·扎克伯格（Mark Zuckerberg）偷走了他们对Facebook的想法而闻名。温克勒维（Winklevii）将加密货币浪潮推向了彭博亿万富翁指数（Bloomberg Billionaires Index），并看好NFT。他们问为什么收集数字艺术与收集稀有的棒球卡有什么不同。一件物品值得市场支付。

The fact that you can’t hang the art on the wall is beside the point, they contend. “Physicality is a bug, not a feature,” says Tyler. “There was no scarcity in ones and zeroes until you enter with the blockchain.”

他们争辩说，你不能将艺术品挂在墙上这一事实不重要。泰勒说：“物理性是一个错误，而不是功能。” “在您进入区块链之前，一和零不存在稀缺性。”

Put another way: Anyone can view a Beeple on Instagram. But blockchain makes it collectible.

换另一种方式：任何人都可以在Instagram上查看蜜蜂。但区块链可以收集。

Duncan Cock Foster—that’s seriously the family name—and his brother had launched Nifty Gateway with the hope of mainstreaming the market for NFTs, which had been complicated to buy and sell. (Most marketplaces required users to have Ethereum wallets.) For the December auction of Beeple’s work, they’d hoped to generate $500,000 in sales for the weekend. But when they crushed that number on Friday night, things started to get interesting. Cock Foster had been monitoring the proceedings from his apartment in New York’s Chinatown and was stunned—by the total figures but also by what this drop meant for the crypto scene. “We all felt like we were witnessing history,” he says. “This is one of the things, in retrospect, where people will say: How did we miss this?”

邓肯公鸡福斯特 - 这位家庭名称 - 他的兄弟已经推出了漂亮的网关，希望将市场纳入NFTS的希望，这一直被购买和销售复杂。 （大多数市场需要用户拥有Etereum钱包。）对于12月拍卖Beeple的工作，他们希望在周末产生500,000美元。但是当他们在星期五晚上粉碎那个号码时，事情开始变得有趣。公鸡福斯特一直在纽约唐人街的公寓的公寓监测诉讼，并被总数据震惊，而且通过这一跌幅为Crypto的场景意味着什么。 “我们都觉得我们正在见证历史，”他说。 “这是其中一个事情，回想起来，人们会说：我们是如何想念这个的？”

Or maybe NFTs are just the next Beanie Babies. It’s too soon to tell. Dan Kelly of the industry publication NonFungible.com, which tracks sales of NFTs, estimates the crypto art market to be at more than $20 million, which isn’t massive until you realize that most of that trading happened in the last twelve months. Hackatao, an artist duo who formed in Milan in 2007, sold $250,000 worth of NFTs last year on SuperRare. In October 2020, Christie’s—the venerable auction house—sold a physical canvas by the artist Robert Alice that quietly came with an NFT, Christie’s first shot at the crypto market; the lot was estimated to sell for $12,000 but traded for more than $130,000. Meanwhile, the week after Beeple’s record-breaking December drop, an artist called Pak did a collaboration with Trevor Jones that brought in $1.3 million, which led the blue-chip auction house Sotheby’s to tweet at Pak: “We are intrigued…”

或者也许NFT只是下一个豆豆婴儿。这太快了。丹凯利的行业出版物非良知网，它追踪NFT的销售，估计加密艺术市场超过2000万美元，直到你意识到最近十二个月发生的大多数交易。 2007年在米兰形成的艺术家Duo哈克涛在超级放牧去年销售了25万美元的NFTS。 2020年10月，克里斯蒂 - 贵族拍卖所卖掉了艺术家罗伯特阿利克斯的物理帆布，悄悄地带着NFT，克里斯蒂在加密市场的首次拍摄;该批次估计为12,000美元，但交易超过130,000美元。与此同时，一周在贝斯普尔的突破12月下降后，一位名为Pak的艺术家与三千万的特雷弗·琼斯合作，带来了130万美元，这引领了苏富比的蓝筹拍卖屋在Pak发布：“我们感兴趣......”

Of that exchange, Cameron Winklevoss says, “That’s like Barnes & Noble reaching out to say, ‘We saw your best seller. Do you want to talk?’”

在那个交换中，卡梅隆·沃克韦斯说：“就像巴恩斯＆amp一样;高尚的伸出手说，“我们看到了你最好的卖家。你想聊一下吗？'”

A few weeks after his record-breaking auction, Winkelmann FaceTimes me from his home office outside Charleston, where he moved in 2017 to escape the Midwest winters. He’s dressed in a beige half-zip mock-neck sweater in a room of beige carpeting.

在拍卖录制拍卖后几周，Winkelmann教我从查尔斯顿以外的家庭办公室，他于2017年搬迁，以逃离中西部的冬季。他穿着米色半拉链脖子毛衣在米色地毯的房间里。

There is no art hanging on the walls. Behind him you can see side-by-side sixty-five-inch flat-screen televisions—one tuned to CNN, the other to Fox News. “I never change the channel and they’re always on mute,” he says. The TVs, he says, are a “window to the outside world.”

墙上没有艺术。在他身后，你可以看到并排六十五英寸平面电视 - 一个调整到CNN，另一个到福克斯新闻。 “我永远不会改变频道，他们总是在静音上，”他说。他说，电视，是一个“到外界的窗口”。

The home is big and airy with a view of a palmetto tree, which makes his setup even more unlikely. Cables run from his computer monitor to the bathroom next door through a crude hole in the wall. Doing the work it takes to render 3D animation, his computers give off so much heat that he’s had to put them on a wood platform over the bathtub, and he jury-rigged an industrial AC unit over the sink that vents into the attic.

家里很宽敞，看法是一个棕榈树，这使他的设置更不可能。电缆从他的电脑显示器运行到隔壁的浴室，通过墙壁粗糙的洞。在进行渲染3D动画的工作中，他的电脑放弃了如此多的热量，即他必须将它们放在浴缸上的木头平台上，他陪伴在盆地上的工业交流单元，通风口进入阁楼。

His neighbors have no idea what he does, he says, which is probably good. Late last year, the revolutionary company Boston Dynamics posted a video of robots that could dance. It went viral, and Winkelmann put his spin on the moment, dropping the robots into a virtual strip club where patrons threw dollar bills at them.

他说，他的邻居不知道他在做什么，这可能很好。去年底，革命性的公司波士顿动力公司发布了一段可以跳舞的机器人的视频。它开始风靡一时，温克尔曼（Winkelmann）转瞬即逝，将机器人扔进了一个虚拟的脱衣舞俱乐部，顾客在那儿向他们投掷钞票。

Scott Glassgold, an L.A.-based producer, who was behind the 2018 Pedro Pascal ( Game of Thrones, Narcos) sci-fi movie Prospect, is working with Janelle Monáe’s production company on a TV pitch based on Beeple’s work (to be written by Philip Gelatt of Netflix’s Love, Death & Robots). He marvels at the dichotomy between Beeple’s art and Winkelmann the man. When Winkelmann travels to L.A., the two often drop in to a Koreatown diner for hamburgers, and Glassgold compared the experience to the movie Fight Club:

总部位于洛杉矶的制片人斯科特·格拉斯戈尔德（Scott Glassgold）执导了2018年的佩德罗·帕斯卡（Pedro Pascal）（《权力的游戏》，纳尔科斯（Narcos））科幻电影《展望》（Prospect）。 Netflix的《爱，死与机器人》的盖拉特（Gelatt）。他惊叹于Beeple的艺术与男人的Winkelmann之间的二分法。温克尔曼前往洛杉矶时，两个人经常去韩国城的一个汉堡包小餐馆，格拉斯哥德将这段经历与电影《搏击俱乐部》进行了比较：

“You sit down, you’re like, ‘I’m having lunch with Ed Norton.’ We’re talking about family and regular things,” he says. “Then you leave and get in your car and see the image he posted, and it’s this crazy bizarre thing. It’s like, Is that the same person? Was that Tyler Durden or Ed Norton?”—referring to Norton’s Jekyll-and-Hyde performance in the 1999 film.

他说：“您坐下，就像是'我正在和埃德·诺顿共进午餐。'我们正在谈论家庭和日常事务。” “然后，您离开并坐上车，看看他张贴的图像，这是一件疯狂的奇怪事情。就像，是同一个人吗？是泰勒·德登还是埃德·诺顿？”-指的是诺顿在1999年电影中的《吉基和海德》表演。

Winkelmann grew up in North Fond du Lac, Wisconsin, a town of 5,000 people about an hour outside of Milwaukee. His dad was an electrical engineer; his mother worked at a local senior center. Winkelmann graduated from Purdue in 2003 with a computer-science degree but no artistic training. (He chose the name Beeple after a 1980s toy whose nose lit up in response to light and sound, which was loosely connected to the kind of early art he was making.)

温克尔曼（Winkelmann）在威斯康星州的北丰杜克（North Fond du Lac）长大，该镇有5000人，距密尔沃基（Milwaukee）约一个小时。他的父亲是一名电气工程师。他的母亲在当地的高级中心工作。温克尔曼（Winkelmann）于2003年毕业于普渡大学，拥有计算机科学学位，但没有接受过艺术培训。 （他在1980年代的玩具中选择了Beeple这个名字，该玩具的鼻子因光线和声音而发光，这与他所制作的早期艺术品有着松散的联系。）

After a brief career designing corporate websites, Winkelmann heard about an artist in the UK who’d gotten attention by doing a sketch every day. Winkelmann ran with the idea, but in animation—using a program called Cinema 4D to do a daily study. He called his project “Everydays.” The series launched on the first of May, 2007, and in the years since, it has evolved from rudimentary cubes to images of a dystopian future with a wink at the culture. Alongside things like a Michael Jackson AI gestating a human baby, his art has evolved to include sharp critiques of government and social media (including a zombie Mark Zuckerberg). He professes not to have spent much time examining his own psyche. “Yeah, the sexual milking, lactating stuff?” he said. “I don’t know where the hell that shit’s coming from.”

在设计公司网站的短暂职业生涯之后，温克尔曼听说了一位英国艺术家，他每天都通过素描来引起人们的注意。温克尔曼坚持这一想法，但在动画方面使用了名为Cinema 4D的程序进行日常学习。他称自己的项目为“每天”。该系列于2007年5月1日推出，从那以后的几年里，它已从基本的多维数据集演变为反乌托邦式的图像，并带有一种眨眼的文化。除了像迈克尔·杰克逊（Michael Jackson）的人工智能正在抚养婴儿一样，他的艺术已经演变成对政府和社交媒体（包括僵尸马克·扎克伯格）的尖锐批评。他自称没有花太多时间检查自己的心理。 “是的，性挤奶，哺乳期的东西？”他说。 “我不知道狗屎从哪里来。”

Winkelmann eventually focused on freelance projects for companies like Possible Productions in Los Angeles, which does graphics and animation for live events, including the MTV VMAs and the Super Bowl. (When Shakira walked through digital fire at the Super Bowl, that was Beeple.) He has also, in his words, done “lame” work for Apple but also something for Elon Musk’s SpaceX that was so “friggin’ sweet,” he’d tell you about it if he weren’t subject to a nondisclosure agreement. But he kept up the “Everydays”—for thirteen years now and counting—and he hasn’t missed a single day. Not for his wedding or the birth of his two children or a cross-country move from Appleton, Wisconsin, to Charleston.

温克尔曼最终专注于为洛杉矶的可能的制作公司等公司从事自由项目，该公司为现场活动制作图形和动画，包括MTV VMA和超级碗。 （当夏奇拉（Shakira）在超级碗上经历数字火烧时，那是Beeple。）用他的话说，他还为苹果公司做过“ lam缝”工作，还为埃隆·马斯克（Elon Musk）的SpaceX做过一些“令人吃惊的甜蜜”的工作， d告诉您有关他是否未遵守保密协议的情况。但是他坚持了“每天”的做法-至今已有13年了，而且还在不断增加-他从未错过任何一天。不是因为他的婚礼，他的两个孩子的出生，或是因为他的国家从威斯康星州阿普尔顿转移到查尔斯顿。

The stunning visuals have attracted a diverse audience online. In 2018, Winkelmann received a direct message from Florent Buonomano, an artistic director at Louis Vuitton, who’d shared the work with his boss, artistic director Nicolas Ghesquière (“the design leader of his generation,” according to Vogue). Winkelmann knew so little about fashion that his first thought was: Is Louis Vuitton still alive? He was surprised to find out that while Vuitton had passed away in 1892, he was corresponding with the artistic director of an iconic luxury house with 160 years of rich history.

令人惊叹的视觉效果在网上吸引了众多观众。 2018年，温克尔曼收到了路易威登艺术总监弗洛伦特·布奥诺诺（Florent Buonomano）的直接信息，弗洛伦特·布奥诺玛诺与他的老板，艺术指导NicolasGhesquière（《 Vogue》称，“他那代人的设计领导者”）分享了作品。温克尔曼对时尚知之甚少，他的第一个念头是：路易威登还活着吗？他惊讶地发现，威登（Vuitton）于1892年去世，他当时是一家具有160年悠久历史的标志性豪宅的艺术总监。

Vuitton was hoping to digitally print some of Winkelmann’s art onto a collection that would debut in a runway show at the Louvre. (“I was immediately captured by Beeple’s futuristic universe,” said Ghesquière, who later collaborated with Winkelmann on digital window displays for some of the global Vuitton boutiques. “His work resonates strongly with today’s world.”) Vuitton paid Winkelmann a flat fee, but the artist didn’t believe any of this was real until he was sitting in the Louvre and the first model rounded the corner wearing one of his images across her body.

Vuitton希望将Winkelmann的某些艺术作品数字化印刷到一个收藏中，该收藏将在卢浮宫的时装秀上首次亮相。 （“盖斯奎尔说：“我立刻被比普尔的未来主义世界所吸引。”盖斯奎尔后来与温克尔曼合作，为一些威登全球专卖店进行了数字橱窗陈列。“他的作品在当今世界引起了强烈共鸣。”）威登向温克尔曼支付了固定费用，但这位画家一直不相信这一切是真实的，直到他坐在卢浮宫并且第一个模特将他的一幅图像横过她的身体摆在拐角处。

Cate Blanchett and Alicia Vikander were sitting front row. So was Winkelmann—in a suit he bought off the rack at Zara after his wife told him he couldn’t wear a brown fleece pullover he found in his closet. At the after-party, he ogled passed hors d’oeuvres beside Spike Lee and Michael Fassbender but didn’t dare introduce himself. “What the fuck am I going to say to Spike Lee? ‘Hi, I’m Beeple.’”

凯特·布兰切特（Cate Blanchett）和艾丽西亚·维坎德（Alicia Vikander）坐在前排。温克尔曼（Winkelmann）也是如此。在妻子告诉他不能穿他在壁橱里发现的棕色羊毛套头衫之后，他在扎拉（Zara）的架子上买了一套西装。在晚会上，他与史派克·李（Spike Lee）和迈克尔·法斯宾德（Michael Fassbender）并肩作战，但不敢自我介绍。 “我要对斯派克·李说什么？ ‘嗨，我是Beeple。’”

Selects from Beeple’s “Everydays” would appear on thirteen of forty-five pieces in the Spring/Summer 2019 ready-to-wear collection. Unrelated, a few months later, controversial comedian Joe Rogan reposted a Beeple animation that depicted Kim Jong Un as a supervillain armed with tentacles tipped with Hillary Clinton’s face, calling the artist’s work “amazing shit” and directing his followers to follow him.

Beeple的“ Everydays”中的精选商品将出现在2019春夏成衣系列中的13件中。几个月后，没有争议的喜剧演员乔·罗根（Joe Rogan）重新发布了一个Beeple动画，其中将金正恩（Kim Jong Un）描绘成一个超级小人，手持触角，上面贴着希拉里·克林顿的脸，称这位画家的作品“令人讨厌”，并指示他的追随者跟随他。

Despite the attention and his growing reach, Winkelmann never considered selling any of his work. Or rather, he didn’t really know how to sell it. This is a common problem. As John Crain, the cofounder of the NFT marketplace SuperRare, explained: “A lot of supertalented digital artists don’t fit the model for the contemporary art world. They don’t go to Art Basel. They’re active on GIF communities. They weren’t monetizing the work as fine art. They might have sold T-shirts on a Linktree.”

尽管受到了关注和不断扩大的影响力，但温克尔曼从未考虑出售他的任何作品。或更确切地说，他并不真正知道如何出售。这是一个普遍的问题。正如NFT市场SuperRare的联合创始人John Crain所说：“许多超级才华的数字艺术家并不适合当代艺术界。他们不会去巴塞尔艺术展。他们在GIF社区中很活跃。他们没有将艺术品当做货币来赚钱。他们可能在Linktree上出售了T恤。”

Then, in the latter half of 2020, Winkelmann started to hear about blockchain technology and the emerging crypto art market. A rep for Nifty Gateway had sent him a message in September 2020, noting Beeple’s popularity and asking if he’d consider doing a drop. Winkelmann ignored the message, thinking it was a random solicitation. But after seeing other artists he knew of making, in his words, a “buttload” of money, he finally did his homework. “If that guy is making a buttload of money,” he said, “I can probably make a shitload of money.”

然后，在2020年下半年，温克尔曼开始了解区块链技术和新兴的加密艺术市场。 Nifty Gateway的代表在2020年9月向他发送了一封邮件，指出Beeple的受欢迎程度，并询问他是否考虑掉线。温克尔曼不理会这条消息，认为这是一个随机的请求。但是，用他的话说，看到其他艺术家，他知道自己赚了一笔“大笔钱”之后，他终于做了功课。他说：“如果那个人赚了一大笔钱，我可能可以赚一大笔钱。”

In October, he tested his theory, auctioning off three pieces—which crashed the Nifty site. One of those digital pieces, an animation titled “Crossroad,” was unique in that the image would change depending on who won the November presidential election. It sold for $66,666.66.

十月份，他检验了他的理论，拍卖了三件作品，这使Nifty网站崩溃了。这些数字作品之一是名为“ Crossroad”的动画，它的独特之处在于图像会根据谁赢得11月总统大选而改变。成交价为66,666.66美元。

(Of the seemingly random price tags with repeating digits, one collector told me there is an “underlying art to bidding style” that involves both numerology and a little bit of peacocking. Winkelmann added his own theory: “This relates to the sort of ‘cryptography’ side of the space. The blockchain is really sort of like a giant number puzzle. I think that’s why you often see a much bigger emphasis placed on special numbers.”)

（在看似随机的带有重复数字的价格标签中，一位藏家告诉我，有一种“涉及竞价风格的底层艺术”，既涉及命理学，也涉及一些孔雀。温克尔曼补充说自己的理论：“这与“加密技术的一面。区块链确实有点像一个巨大的数字难题。我认为这就是为什么您经常看到更大的重点放在特殊数字上的原因。”）

Despite the impressive receipts, Winkelmann couldn’t quite shake the feeling—however fleeting—that without some physical object to go along with the NFT, he was kinda maybe selling “magic beans.”

尽管收据令人印象深刻，Winkelmann无法撼动这种感觉 - 无论如何，没有一些物理对象与NFT一起去，他有点可能出售“魔法豆”。

“Why would you spend $5,000 on an MP4? The difference between owning it and not owning it was just an email that said, ‘You won.’ I can see how you would call bullshit on that.” Despite what Tyler Winklevoss said about hardware versus software, other artists had had similar reservations, solving this problem by sending prints to winning bidders. But a print was such a different experience from the digital animation.

“你为什么要在MP4上花费5,000美元？拥有它而不是拥有它的区别只是一封电子邮件，“你赢了。”我可以看出你如何称之为废话。“尽管Tyler Winklevoss对硬件与软件说了什么，但其他艺术家曾有类似的预订，通过向赢得投标人发送印刷品来解决这个问题。但是，印刷品是从数字动画的不同经验。

Within days, Beeple had an idea to quiet skeptics and juice bidding. Inspired by collectibles like the vinyl toy market, Winkelmann and his wife set about designing a high-end physical object to go along with each sale. What they came up with was a marvel; picture a small LCD screen with a titanium back that displays the piece you just bought on a slow-moving loop. It’s like someone had asked Steve Jobs to redesign the Lucite box typically used to display baseball cards, but make it electric. Each one costs $500 to produce and is numbered and authenticated, with a front-facing QR code that takes you to a website that lists the provenance of that piece of art—who owned it before, and who owns it now.

在几天之内，Beeple对安静的怀疑论者和果汁竞标有一个想法。由像乙烯基玩具市场一样的收藏品，Winkelmann和他的妻子设置了设计高端物理对象以及每个销售。他们想出的是一个奇迹;图片一个带有钛的小液晶屏，展示你刚刚在慢动循环上购买的件。就像有人要求史蒂夫乔布斯重新设计透气盒，通常用于显示棒球卡，但是使它成为电动。每一个都需要500美元生产并编号和经过身份验证，前面面向前面的QR码，将您带到一个网站，该网站列出了本艺术品的出处 - 谁以前拥有它，谁现在拥有它。

“When I think prize,” Winkelmann said, “I think something you get in a fucking Cracker Jack box. I wanted to make this thing feel like it is the artwork. And it’s super tightly integrated with the NFT so that it feels like the same thing.”

“当我觉得奖品时，”Winkelmann说：“我认为你在他妈的饼干杰克盒中得到的东西。我想让这件事觉得这是艺术品。它与NFT超级密切地整合，使其感觉就像一样。“

Winkelmann set his next auction—the eventual record-breaking sale—for the weekend of December 11. While the catalog copy was predictably brash, reading: “hahahah, ok so we’re going balls deep on this motherfucker,” elements of the auction were fairly old-fashioned. Much in the way a traditional auction house would, Winkelmann arranged to preview (by Zoom or FaceTime) the twenty or so pieces for about a dozen high-end collectors in the crypto art scene, including a mysterious buyer calling himself MetaKovan (who is rumored to be in Singapore) and another, a twenty-seven-year-old crypto baller named Tim Kang, the son of Korean immigrants who left his job at Deutsche Bank to go all-in on blockchain, and saw the titanium object as a work of art in and of itself, and a sea change for the crypto marketplace.

Winkelmann设置了他的下一次拍卖 - 最终的纪录销售 - 在12月11日的周末 - 虽然目录副本是可预测的，但是读：“哈哈赫，好的，我们将在这个母亲的拍卖中深入打球相当老了。在传统拍卖行的方式，Winkelmann安排预览（通过Zoom或FaceTime）在加密艺术场景中大约十几个高端收藏家的二十个左右，包括一个呼唤自己的神秘买家梅卡莫克（谁是谣传在新加坡，另一个，一个二十七岁的加密球员被命名为蒂姆康，韩国移民的儿子在德意志银行留下了一连串区间，并作为工作看钛对象本身的艺术以及加密市场的海洋变化。

“It was like opening up your first iPhone from the box,” Kang told me.

“这就像从盒子里打开你的第一个iPhone，”康告诉我。

On the first day, Beeple auctioned off three numbered “open edition” pieces, each priced at $969, and set the time limit for five minutes. Nifty Gateway had added nine additional servers to handle the expected traffic, and the site held. Within five minutes, $582,000 worth of art had been sold.

在第一天，拍卖三个编号的“开放版”件，每件售价为969美元，并将时间限制设定五分钟。 Nifty Gateway添加了九个其他服务器来处理预期的流量，并且该网站持有。五分钟内，售出价值5.82千美元的艺术品。

Ten individual pieces would be auctioned off on Saturday and eleven on Sunday. Over the next two days, according to Nifty Gateway, twelve bidders would each place bids of more than $100,000. In the heat of the auction, a mystery soon unfolded. Users were repeatedly outbid by a series of buyers whose screen names all shared a single, curious connection: Each was named for a hill in Rome. An Internet sleuth later revealed that these pseudonyms all belonged to a single collector who’d purchased twenty of the twenty-one pieces.

周六和十一日星期天将拍卖十个单独的作品。在接下来的两天内，根据Nifty Gateway的说法，12名投标人每人都会出价超过100,000美元。在拍卖的热量中，一个很快展开的谜团。用户被一系列买家反复出标，这些买家屏幕名称都分享了单一，奇怪的连接：每个都被命名为罗马的山丘。互联网侦探之后透露，这些假名都属于一个购买二十一件二十一件的单个收藏家。

Kang succeeded in purchasing just one piece at auction, the finale—the complete collection, which he bought for $777,777, making it the most expensive NFT in history. Reached by phone in January, Kang (who now lives in Los Angeles) revealed for the first time that he never expected to win. His previous bid was $377,777. With one second left in play, he added $400,000 to his offer, throwing down a very expensive gauntlet. Per the auction’s rules, his bid reset the clock for an additional five minutes. “I assumed [MetaKovan] would outbid me—that was my threshold and I’d be out,” Kang said. He watched the clock nervously, thinking, He’s gonna outbid me, right?

康成功地在拍卖会上购买了一件，最终的完整系列，他买了777,777美元，使其成为历史上最昂贵的NFT。由电话于1月到达，康（现在住在洛杉矶）首次揭示他从未预料到赢得的第一次。他以前的出价是377,777美元。在比赛中留下了一秒钟，他向他的报价增加了40万美元的报价，击败了一个非常昂贵的手套。根据拍卖的规则，他的出价将时钟重置了五分钟。 “我认为[metakovan]会败我 - 这是我的门槛，我会出去，”康说。他紧张地看着时钟，思考，他会让我失去我，对吗？

“A lot of people, from an outsider perspective, they don’t understand why I did it. They might see it in a reckless way. That’s why I’m shitting my pants. I never really came out publicly. But I’m proud of it. I believe that it really validated the space. What I really wanted to stand for—when I put in that bid—was to show the world that this is the paradigm, this is the future.”

“很多人，从外人的角度来看，他们不明白为什么我做到了。他们可能以鲁莽的方式看到它。这就是为什么我散发着裤子。我从来没有公开出来。但我为此感到骄傲。我相信它真的验证了这个空间。我真正想要的是 - 当我投入那个投标时 - 就是向世界展示这是范式，这是未来。“

The sale was captured in perpetuity on the Beeple Twitter account. In a short video, the artist himself can be seen celebrating in his brother’s backyard as two people spray him with Champagne. Winkelmann cowers and giggles, repeatedly saying: “That’s cold as shit!”

在BEEPLE TWITTER账户上捕获销售。在短视频中，艺术家在他哥哥的后院可以看到艺术家，因为两个人用香槟喷洒他。 Winkelmann Cowers和Giggles，反复说：“那是狗屎的冷！”

The Beeple drop made $3.5 million—plus another $500,000 on the secondary market—in that weekend alone. Winkelmann retains 90 percent of the initial sale, and unlike the typical arrangement with a traditional art gallery, he’ll also earn 10 percent of the secondary sales. The December lots amounted to just twenty pieces from his “Everydays” series. That’s twenty frames out of thirteen years’worth of work.

只有350万美元，加上次级市场上的250万美元 - 仅在那个周末的次要市场上。 Winkelmann保留了90％的初始销售，并与与传统美术馆的典型安排不同，他还可以获得次要销售额的10％。 12月批次达到他的“每天”系列中的二十件。这是三十年代的工作中的二十帧。

As the pandemic accelerated trends toward digital everything, the same was true in the crypto art space. While a live auction could be a superspreader event, crypto buyers were able to view pieces of art as they were meant to be enjoyed: from the safety of their own phones.

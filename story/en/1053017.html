Could we fuel our jets using our sewage?

For many applications, liquid fuels remain the most practical energy supply—applications like aircraft and large ships being obvious examples. It's possible to avoid using fossil fuels for these applications, since there are many ways of producing biofuels. But we can't produce biofuels at a competitive price, leaving fossil fuels as the dominant option.

对于许多应用，液体燃料仍然是飞机和大型船舶的最实用的能源供应应用是明显的例子。它可以避免使用化石燃料进行这些应用，因为有很多生产生物燃料。但我们可以以竞争价格生产生物燃料，使化石燃料作为主导选择。

A group of US-based researchers has therefore looked into the prospect of converting food waste into jet fuel. Chemically, the results are excellent, producing material that can be blended with a bit of standard jet fuel to meet all regulatory standards. Economically, the situation is not nearly so great, only working at prices that were prevalent over five years ago. But the fact that the waste would otherwise put methane in the atmosphere as it decays more than offsets the carbon dioxide produced by the jet fuel in the blend. So a price on carbon could change the equation.

因此，一群美国的研究人员研究了将食物垃圾转化为喷射燃料的前景。化学上，结果是优异的，生产材料可以与一点标准喷射燃料混合，以满足所有调节标准。在经济上，这种情况几乎是如此伟大，只在五年前的价格上工作。但是，废物将在大气中放置甲烷的事实，因为它衰减的掉多于抵消喷射燃料在混合物中产生的二氧化碳。所以碳上的价格可以改变等式。

The work here is focused on what are called "wet wastes," which include things like food waste, animal manure, and sewage. As you might expect, we produce a lot of this stuff, with the authors estimating that its total energy content is roughly equivalent to 10 billion gallons of jet fuel every year. Due to the amount of water present, it's extremely energy-intensive to directly convert this waste to any sort of fuel, since the water has to be discarded. It is, however, possible to put the waste in an oxygen-free environment and have bacteria convert it to methane.

What the authors focus on is interrupting that process. If you grow the bacteria under the right conditions, they won't fully break down all the longer, complex fats. Instead, they'll stop at a point where much of the carbon in those cells is in the form of relatively short molecules that are four to eight carbons long. These typically have a couple of oxygens attached to one end of the carbon chain, making them weak acids.

作者侧重于什么是中断这种过程。如果在正确的条件下培养细菌，他们会赢得＆＃39; t完全分解所有更长的复杂脂肪。相反，它们＆＃39; LL停在那些细胞中大部分碳的一点是相对较短的分子的形式，这些碳是4至8个碳。这些通常具有连接到碳链的一端的氧气，使其弱酸。

Chemically, it's possible to cause these molecules to react in a way in which two of the weak acids merge into a single molecule, releasing water and a single molecule of carbon dioxide in the process. The resulting molecule is now nearly twice the length (two four-carbon molecules would fuse to form a seven-carbon molecule and release the other carbon as CO 2). And that's bringing the length up to the neighborhood of the typical hydrocarbon in jet fuel.

化学上，它可以使这些分子使这些分子以两种分子合并成单个分子，释放水和在该过程中的二氧化碳中的方式反应。所得分子现在几乎是长度的两倍（两种四碳分子将熔合以形成七碳分子并释放其他碳作为CO 2）。并且它将长度达到射流燃料中典型的烃类的长度。

The longer molecule still has oxygen attached, and there are two ways to get rid of it. One is a simple reaction with hydrogen and a cheap catalyst, which pops off the oxygen in the form of water. An alternative is a further fusion of another weak acid molecule, creating a more complicated branched structure. (This process also requires a reaction with hydrogen to convert the substance into a pure hydrocarbon when it's done.) The researchers demonstrated that with the right catalysts, both of these reactions work extremely well and produce a mix of hydrocarbons with properties similar to that of jet fuel.

较长的分子仍然有氧气附着，有两种方法可以摆脱它。一个是与氢气和廉价催化剂的简单反应，其以水的形式松开氧气。另一种方法是另一个弱酸分子的进一步融合，产生更复杂的支链结构。 （该方法还需要与氢气的反应，当它完成时，将物质转化为纯烃。）研究人员证明，用右催化剂，这两种反应非常好，并产生具有性质的烃的混合物与喷气燃料类似。

So the researchers now had a process. Feed the waste to a bacterial digester, stop the bacteria from producing methane, and isolate the short fatty acids from the digester. Then, put those through a couple of reactions, and out pops a mix of hydrocarbons that can be used as fuel.

所以研究人员现在有一个过程。将废物饲料到细菌蒸煮器中，从生产甲烷中停止细菌，并从消化器中分离短脂肪酸。然后，将那些通过几个反应，并突出了可用作燃料的烃的混合物。

Of course, there are some fairly specific requirements for what constitutes jet fuel, designed by aviation authorities to ensure the safety of flights and on-ground operations. And the fuels made by these two processes differed from standard jet fuel in some critical ways, like the flash point and freezing point, which determine the fuel's behavior in response to high and low temperatures, respectively.

当然，由航空机构设计的喷射燃料有一些相当具体的要求，以确保航班和地面运营的安全性。并且这两个过程制造的燃料以某种批判方式与标准喷射燃料不同，如闪点和冰点，其分别确定燃料＆＃39;响应高温和低温的行为。

This wasn't a problem if these biofuels were kept to under 10 percent of the total jet fuel mix. But it would cause problems if you wanted to make a mix that was predominantly biofuel. However, the two different reactions produced products that differed in opposite ways (one produced a higher flashpoint liquid, the other a lower flashpoint liquid, for example). So by mixing the two of these together, it was possible to make a jet fuel mix that was over 70 percent biofuel.

Because the products of these reactions had been purified at several steps along the way, the result was also a far cleaner-burning fuel, with soot production dropping by about 65 percent. It also had a slightly higher energy density than traditional jet fuel, which might be useful for some applications.

由于这些反应的产品在沿途的几个步骤中纯化，因此结果也是燃烧燃烧的燃料，烟灰产量下降约65％。它的能量密度略高于传统的喷射燃料，这可能对某些应用有用。

With all the catalysts and conditions worked out, the researchers turned to a rather critical question: how much would it cost? Assuming they could get a bit over 200 tons of food waste a day, they estimate the biofuel would be competitive with standard jet fuel at a price of about $2.50 a gallon. That number is not unreasonable, and jet fuel prices have spent several years above that price in the past, but it hasn't been in that range since 2014.

随着所有催化剂和条件的制定，研究人员转向了一个相当关键的问题：它会花多少钱？假设他们每天可以获得超过200吨的食物浪费，他们估计生物燃料将竞争标准喷气式燃料，价格约为2.50美元。这个数字并不是不合理的，喷气燃料价格已经花了几年以上的价格过去，但自2014年以来，它已经在这个范围内。

If you take carbon emissions into account, though, things shift considerably. That's because, if not diverted for some other use, the food waste would end up in a landfill and produce methane, which is a more potent greenhouse gas than carbon dioxide. As a result, diverting this carbon to fuel and burning it avoids those methane emissions. In the grand scheme of things, that more than offsets the 30 percent of the final fuel mix that comes from petroleum, causing the biofuel/jet fuel mix to produce negative emissions.

If there's a price on carbon, or some sort of credit for low carbon fuels, this can offset some of the price difference between petroleum-based jet fuel and the price needed to make the biofuel profitable. Right now, the US doesn't have a price on carbon, although some states do. The authors plug their numbers into a formula that estimates credits under California's Low Carbon Fuel Standard system, and they find that their biofuel is worth $3.71/gallon in avoided emissions.

The one issue here is that there are many circumstances where the food waste wouldn't be allowed to release methane into the atmosphere. A lot of it can also serve as feedstock for biodiesel production, so it would be diverted before it reached a landfill. And there are now landfills that capture the methane they would otherwise release and use that for power generation. So the jet fuel production might end up in competition for the food waste with other carbon neutral fuel processes.

Even as cyberspace is being touted as the hippest place to congregate since the original Woodstock, some experts now contend that estimates of the number of people actively using the Internet web of computer networks may be grossly exaggerated.

尽管自最初的伍德斯托克以来，网络空间一直被认为是最时髦的聚会场所，但一些专家现在仍在争辩说，积极使用计算机网络Internet的人数估计可能被夸大了。

There is still widespread agreement that the growth of the Internet, and the number of people using it, is exponential; it is seen doubling in size every year. But some network experts say the most commonly cited numbers -- 20 million to 30 million users worldwide -- may be many times too high.

人们仍然普遍认为，互联网的增长以及使用它的人数呈指数级增长。它的大小每年都在增加一倍。但是一些网络专家说，最常被引用的数字-全球2000万至3000万用户-可能高出很多倍。

"Suppose there were really only two million or three million," said John S. Quarterman, a highly regarded Internet demographer in Austin, Tex.

假设实际上只有200万或300万，得克萨斯州奥斯汀市一位备受推崇的互联网人口统计学家约翰·季特曼（John S. Quarterman）说。

In an assessment potentially chilling to all the businesses betting millions of dollars on the premise that they can sell advertising, information and products to the Internet masses, Mr. Quarterman believes that his lower numbers may be a more accurate count of people who are active and reachable on the computer network.

在一项评估中，所有可能以赌注数百万美元为前提的企业可能会感到寒心，因为他们可以向互联网大众出售广告，信息和产品，季特曼认为，较低的数字可能是更准确地反映活跃和在计算机网络上可以访问。

The latest comprehensive survey that tries to estimate the Internet's reach discovered more than 3.2 million "host" computers capable of communicating directly with other computers on the Internet. The data, released late last week by Mark K. Lottor of the consultants Network Wizards of Menlo Park, Calif., marked a stunning increasing of one million additional machines since the survey was last taken in January.

试图估计互联网覆盖范围的最新综合调查发现了超过320万个主机。能够直接与Internet上的其他计算机通信的计算机。上周下旬，由加州门洛帕克（Menlo Park）顾问公司Network Wizards的Mark K. Lottor发布的数据显示，自上次调查于1月份以来，新增的机器数量惊人地增加了100万台。

But while Mr. Quarterman and some other experts do not dispute this raw count of Internet computers, they take issue with the common assumption that each of those machines represents an Internet port of entry for perhaps 10 people working from desktop computers. Moreover, the skeptics suggest that as many as one million or more of those Internet ports, and the millions of people who use them, may be walled off to much of the potential incoming traffic on the Internet, as corporations erect electronic "fire walls" in response to mounting concern over computer security and intrusions by hackers.

但是，尽管Quarterman先生和其他一些专家对互联网计算机的原始数量没有异议，但他们普遍认为每个计算机都代表一个Internet入口端口（可能有10个人使用台式计算机工作）。此外，怀疑者认为，随着公司建立电子设备，多达一百个或更多的这些Internet端口以及数百万使用它们的人可能会被隔离在Internet上的许多潜在传入流量之外。防火墙为了应对对计算机安全和黑客入侵的日益关注。

If Mr. Quarterman's analysis is correct, then his calculations further blur the already vague notion that electronic culture and commerce is just around the corner. Certainly his assessment does much to debunk the myth of an interactive, information web that will eventually link everyone on the planet.

如果Quarterman先生的分析是正确的，那么他的计算将进一步模糊已经模糊的观念，即电子文化和商业指日可待。当然，他的评估极大地揭露了一个交互式信息网络的神话，该网络最终将连接地球上的每个人。

Estimates that there are more than 20 million users of the Internet have excited the imagination of businesses, politicians, social scientists, investors and journalists.

估计有超过2000万的Internet用户激发了企业，政客，社会科学家，投资者和新闻工作者的想象力。

The Internet has been described as a prototype of the Clinton Administration's proposed information superhighways. Companies seeking to tap a vast new customer base are spending millions to stake out storefronts in cyberspace, and Internet-related businesses are popping up in every city of significant size. An Internet electronic mail address is considered de rigueur for business cards today, and even the White House is "on the Net."

互联网已被描述为克林顿政府提议的信息高速公路的原型。寻求发掘大量新客户群的公司正花费数百万美元在网络空间中进行店面放样，并且与互联网相关的业务正在每个规模巨大的城市中涌现。如今，Internet电子邮件地址被认为是名片的必不可少的东西，甚至白宫都在网上。

The Internet is popular, all right. But is it a less dynamic force than we've all been led to believe?

互联网很流行，好吧。但是，这是否比我们所有人都相信的动力要小？

To be sure, there are those who contend that even the estimates of 20 million to 30 million Internet users are too low, if one counts the users of smaller public and private computer networks, like America Online and Prodigy, that are linked to the Internet mainly to exchange electronic mail. But such links do not allow users to employ some of the most significant features of the Internet, including some of the most impressive ones for conducting electronic commerce on the network, transferring and publishing files, and initiating complex information searches.

"The important variable is what the usage is, rather than what the numbers are," said Gary Chapman, coordinator of the 21st Century Project, a science and technology policy study group at the L.B.J. School of Public Affairs in Austin. "If it's just E-mail, that would have some certain kinds of implications. And for businesses and policy makers who are planning for a digital future where the Internet delivers tens of millions of interactive users freely sending and receiving all sorts of information over the Internet, the rise of "fire walls," or security barriers, is a significant impediment. Mike Schwartz, associate professor of computer science at the University of Colorado at Boulder, said a 1992 survey of the Internet found that an estimated 35 percent of all Internet computers were barricaded behind fire walls.

对于正在计划数字未来的企业和政策制定者，互联网将为数以千万计的交互式用户提供通过互联网自由发送和接收各种信息的“防火墙”的兴起。或安全屏障是一个重大障碍。科罗拉多大学博尔德分校计算机科学副教授迈克·施瓦兹（Mike Schwartz）说，1992年对互联网的调查发现，估计所有互联网计算机中的35％被挡在了防火墙后面。

"If you start looking at Internet services that require interactivity, things like billing or accounting across the Net, there might be nicer ways to do it if you could directly connect to people," Mr. Schwartz said. But if more and more Internet users erect one-way barriers, he added, "the services you can use are more limited."

Also, Internet surveyors agree, there is no way to know how many computers are truly active on the Internet -- as opposed to ones that are out of service, are turned on only infrequently, or exist only as an address with no corresponding machine. When attempts are made to contact a sampling of computers on the network, typically fewer than 30 percent respond.

此外，互联网测量师同意，没有办法知道互联网上真正有多少台计算机处于活动状态-与那些不可用，仅很少打开或仅作为没有相应机器的地址的计算机相对。尝试联系网络上的计算机样本时，通常不到30％的响应。

Despite his deflationary data, Mr. Quarterman still predicts that the Internet will eventually achieve and surpass the size now widely credited to it. But the future isn't here yet, he says. Opportunity Knocking

尽管有通货紧缩的数据，但Quarterman先生仍然预测，互联网最终将达到并超过目前被广泛认为的规模。他说，但是未来还没有到。机会敲门

Some of the biggest investors in the Internet are unswayed by suggestions that the numbers are overblown.

互联网上一些最大的投资者对这些数字被夸大的建议毫不动摇。

"Whether it's 6 million or 60 million, it's still an opportunity, and the biggest opportunity is within the business community," said Gail Grant, who manages development of Internet services for the Digital Equipment Corporation in Palo Alto, Calif.

无论是600万还是6000万，这仍然是一个机会，而最大的机会是在商业社区内部，盖尔·格兰特（Gail Grant）说，他为加利福尼亚帕洛阿尔托的数字设备公司管理互联网服务的开发。

Digital, one of the world's largest computer makers, has an estimated 40,000 computers connected to the Internet for interacting with employees and customers around the world. And the company is spending millions of dollars to develop Internet-related services, like distributing the company's product information and allowing potential customers to test powerful Digital computers remotely over the network.

Digital是世界上最大的计算机制造商之一，估计有40,000台连接到Internet的计算机，可以与世界各地的员工和客户进行交互。而且，该公司将花费数百万美元来开发与Internet相关的服务，例如分发公司的产品信息，并允许潜在的客户通过网络远程测试功能强大的数字计算机。

The latest census of the Internet, released last week by Mr. Lottor of Network Wizards, is part of the tally of Internet "host" computers undertaken several times a year. Host computers are the portals through which others can gain access to the Internet, and counting them is roughly analogous to counting the number of telephone numbers on the world's connected phone systems. Mr. Quarterman acknowledges the numbers as an historically reliable set of data.

Once the number of host computers on the Internet is determined, the next challenge is to estimate the number of people who use each host. The task might be equated to calculating the average number of people who can be reached at a given telephone number. How Use Is Calculated

一旦确定了Internet上的主机数量，下一个挑战就是估计使用每个主机的人数。该任务可能等同于计算在给定电话号码下可以联系的平均人数。如何计算使用量

The commonly accepted figure for computers has been 10 users per network node, a number that derived partly from a survey of corporations several years ago, and partly from the ease of multiplying big numbers by 10.

普遍接受的计算机数字是每个网络节点有10个用户，这一数字部分是根据几年前对公司的调查得出的，部分是由于将大数乘以10的简便性而得出的。

Based on 10 users per machine, Mr. Lottor's latest survey would yield 32 million Internet users worldwide, a number that was embraced quickly by businesses and organizations that champion the growth of the global Internet.

基于每台机器上的10个用户，Lottor先生的最新调查将在全球范围内吸引3200万互联网用户，这一数字很快被支持全球互联网发展的企业和组织所接受。

But the number of users per machine may be as small as one, which is often the case for someone who links his or her home computer to the Internet. Or it could be hundreds of thousands, as would be the case for a giant public network like America Online, which is forging Internet connections.

"I've got more I.P. addresses here than I have staff," said Brad Templeton, publisher of Clarinet Communications, an electronic publisher in San Jose, Calif., referring to Internet Protocol addresses, the defining characteristic in Mr. Lottor's survey. "I've got three machines at home with I.P. addresses. There are coke machines and toasters with I.P. addresses."

Mr. Lottor, in defense of his data, said he was more comfortable counting machines, not people. "I try not to get into the user count, because there isn't really any way to figure it out," he said. Pressed to make a guess, he said: "Five to ten users per machine sounds pretty reasonable."

But Mr. Quarterman questioned even that number, based on his own surveys of Internet system operators. Mr. Quarterman sent detailed questionnaires to 5,000 Internet system operators in his most recent survey, in January 1994, and concluded from the responses that the number of active computers was about 741,000 machines. Using his estimate of 3.5 users per machine, the active Internet population would be about 3 million users, he said. Because only about half the users are in the United States, the Internet population domestically would be 1.5 million.

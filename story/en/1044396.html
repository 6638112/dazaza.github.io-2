WhatsApp delays privacy changes amid a backlash and user exodus
WhatsApp在强烈反对和用户外流的情况下延迟隐私更改

2021-01-16 03:59:24

SAN FRANCISCO — WhatsApp said on Friday that it would delay a planned privacy update, as the Facebook-owned messaging service tries to stem an exodus of users worried about the changes.

旧金山— WhatsApp周五表示将推迟计划的隐私更新，因为这家Facebook拥有的消息服务试图阻止大量用户担心这些变化。

WhatsApp said it would push back the changes, to May 15 from Feb. 8, to give users more time to review what it planned to do.

WhatsApp表示将把更改从2月8日推迟到5月15日，以使用户有更多时间查看其计划做的事情。

This month, WhatsApp notified its users that it would give them new options to message businesses using the service and was updating its privacy terms. WhatsApp’s notification said users would have to accept the new terms by February or no longer have access to their accounts. Though little was actually changing, the company still needed user approval.

本月，WhatsApp通知其用户，它将为用户提供使用该服务向企业发送消息的新选项，并正在更新其隐私条款。 WhatsApp的通知称，用户必须在2月之前接受新条款，否则将无法再访问其帐户。尽管实际变化不大，但该公司仍需要用户批准。

Many users and some media outlets interpreted the notification as a marked shift in WhatsApp’s data-sharing practices, mistakenly believing that the company could now read peoples’ conversations and other personal data. Misinformation spread through the service, touching users around the world.

许多用户和一些媒体都将通知解释为WhatsApp数据共享做法的明显转变，错误地认为该公司现在可以阅读人们的对话和其他个人数据。错误信息通过该服务传播，触及了全世界的用户。

Millions of people flocked to other messaging services, including apps like Signal — which offers so-called end-to-end encryption like WhatsApp — and Telegram, which offers some encryption options. Earlier this week, Signal became the No. 1 app in India, one of WhatsApp’s biggest markets, on Apple and Android phones.

数以百万计的人涌向其他消息传递服务，包括诸如Signal（提供所谓的端到端加密，如WhatsApp）之类的应用程序以及提供一些加密选项的Telegram等应用程序。本周早些时候，Signal在Apple和Android手机上成为WhatsApp最大市场之一的印度第一大应用程序。

Now, WhatsApp executives are scrambling to assure users that its changes are minor, that it cannot read users’ messages, and that its services are more secure than some competitors.

现在，WhatsApp的管理人员正在争先恐后地向用户保证其更改很小，无法读取用户的消息，并且其服务比某些竞争对手更安全。

“WhatsApp helped bring end-to-end encryption to people across the world, and we are committed to defending this security technology now and in the future,” WhatsApp said in a company blog post. “With these updates, none of that is changing.”

WhatsApp在公司博客中说：“ WhatsApp帮助向世界各地的人们带来了端到端加密，我们致力于现在和将来捍卫这种安全技术。” “有了这些更新，所有这些都没有改变。”

Some limited information from WhatsApp is shared with Facebook, WhatsApp’s parent company. But the changes to WhatsApp’s terms of service to enable that occurred in 2016, and the terms have not been substantially updated since.

WhatsApp的部分有限信息已与WhatsApp的母公司Facebook共享。但是对WhatsApp启用的服务条款的更改发生在2016年，此后条款未进行实质性更新。

The fallout represents a rare misstep for the messaging giant, which Facebook bought in 2014 for $16 billion. For years, Mark Zuckerberg, Facebook’s chief executive, let WhatsApp operate largely as an independent entity, supported by Facebook’s infrastructure and resources. Over that period, WhatsApp grew to serve more than a billion users — most of them outside the United States.

对于Facebook来说，这一后果代表了罕见的失误.Facebook于2014年以160亿美元的价格收购了这一消息巨头。多年来，Facebook首席执行官马克·扎克伯格（Mark Zuckerberg）一直让WhatsApp在Facebook的基础设施和资源的支持下在很大程度上作为一个独立实体运营。在此期间，WhatsApp的服务范围扩大到超过10亿用户-其中大多数在美国境外。

That approach has changed in recent years. Jan Koum and Brian Acton, the founders of WhatsApp, left the company in 2018 after a falling out with Mr. Zuckerberg. Since then, Mr. Zuckerberg’s touch has grown heavier. He wants to stitch together the messaging services between Facebook, Instagram and WhatsApp, which will require years of engineering work.

近年来，这种方法已经改变。 WhatsApp的创始人扬·库姆（Jan Koum）和布莱恩·阿克顿（Brian Acton）在与扎克伯格先生闹翻后于2018年离开公司。从那以后，扎克伯格的感情越来越沉重。他希望将Facebook，Instagram和WhatsApp之间的消息传递服务整合在一起，这需要多年的工程工作。

While Mr. Zuckerberg has positioned Facebook as doubling down on user privacy, some former employees fear the integration could make apps like WhatsApp even less secure over time. WhatsApp is not yet connected to Messenger or Instagram.

扎克伯格曾将Facebook定位为用户隐私加倍的公司，但一些前雇员担心，随着时间的推移，这种整合可能会使WhatsApp之类的应用程序变得更加不安全。 WhatsApp尚未连接到Messenger或Instagram。

The furor over WhatsApp’s privacy changes is bitterly ironic, given the company’s struggles with misinformation on its service. WhatsApp has been used to distribute misinformation around elections in Brazil and other countries, which has been difficult to combat because of the closed, private nature of the service.

考虑到该公司在服务错误信息方面的努力，对WhatsApp隐私更改的热烈讽刺是极具讽刺意味的。 WhatsApp已用于在巴西和其他国家/地区的选举中散布错误信息，由于该服务具有封闭性，私有性，因此很难解决。

WhatsApp has begun sharing graphics in multiple languages detailing exactly what the privacy policy update will mean.

WhatsApp已开始以多种语言共享图形，详细说明了隐私策略更新的含义。

“There’s been a lot of misinformation causing concern, and we want to help everyone understand our principles and the facts,” the company said.

该公司表示：“有很多错误的信息引起关注，我们希望帮助所有人理解我们的原则和事实。”

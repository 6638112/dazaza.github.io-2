Exotic Animal Cafés Featuring Otters, Lizards and Owls Raise Alarms
带有水獭，蜥蜴和猫头鹰的异国动物咖啡馆发出警报

2021-01-01 08:31:55

Coffee shops where patrons can cuddle with a cat or pet a dog have sprung up in cities around the world in recent years. But some venues have gone beyond typical domesticated pets: during a visit to Japan, Sharne McMillan heard about otter cafés. She happened to be researching a rare Eurasian otter population for her Ph.D. at the University of Hong Kong, and her interest was piqued, so she investigated further. She found that babies of a different species than she studied, Asian small-clawed otters, could be bottle-fed or walked on leashes at some cafés, while adult otters might be available to play with or feed.

近年来，在世界各地的城市如雨后春笋般涌现，顾客可以与猫或宠物相依为命。但是有些地方已经超出了典型的家养宠物的范围：在访问日本期间，Sharne McMillan听说了水獭咖啡馆。她碰巧正在为博士学位研究稀有的欧亚水獭种群。在香港大学任教期间，她的兴趣激起了，因此她进行了进一步调查。她发现，与她研究的物种不同的婴儿是亚洲小爪水獭，可以在一些咖啡馆用瓶装喂养或用皮带牵引着走路，而成年水獭则可以玩耍或喂食。

Many humans see otters as furry, squeaky, cartoon-cute creatures that love to play—something like aquatic kittens. But they are also wild animals. McMillan was concerned because the small-clawed otter is designated as vulnerable on the International Union for Conservation of Nature’s (IUCN’s) Red List, which tracks the extinction risk of thousands of species. “It has a declining population in the wild, and the latest emerging threat to that species is the pet trade,” she says, adding that otter cafés might bolster the animals’ appeal as exotic pets. And the issue is not limited to otters. In follow-up research covering a broader swath of Asia and published this fall in Conservation Letters, she and her co-authors found these café s keep a broad range of exotic animals, from iguanas to owls to raccoons. The businesses “influence the accessibility and exposure of these exotic species to the visitors” and thus could fuel the overall exotic pet trade, McMillan says.

许多人将水獭看作是喜欢玩耍的毛茸茸，吱吱作响的卡通可爱生物，就像水生小猫一样。但是它们也是野生动物。麦克米兰担心，因为小爪水獭在国际自然保护联盟（IUCN）的红色名录中被指定为脆弱动物，该名录追踪了数千种物种的灭绝风险。她说：“野生种群数量在下降，对这一物种的最新威胁是宠物贸易。”她补充说，水獭咖啡馆可能会增强动物作为外来宠物的吸引力。问题不仅限于水獭。她和她的合著者在随后的研究中涵盖了更广阔的亚洲地区，并于今年秋天发表在《保护字母》上，发现这些咖啡馆保留着从鬣蜥到猫头鹰到浣熊的各种外来动物。麦克米伦说，这些企业“影响了这些外来物种对游客的可及性和接触性”，因此有可能推动整个外来宠物贸易。

To find these cafés, the researchers did online keyword searches, using English and local languages, in 10 regions in Asia. Of the 406 shops they turned up, 27 percent advertised having exotic animals encompassing 252 species of mammals, reptiles and birds. The IUCN classifies 234 of these as threatened—including potential threat from the wildlife trade—or as declining in wild populations. McMillan says the study likely underestimates the number of such cafes because they may not all have an online presence or appear in search results.

为了找到这些咖啡馆，研究人员在亚洲10个地区使用英语和当地语言进行了在线关键字搜索。在他们开设的406家商店中，有27％的公司做广告说有外来动物，其中包括252种哺乳动物，爬行动物和鸟类。 IUCN将其中234种归类为受威胁的物种，包括来自野生动植物贸易的潜在威胁，或归为野生种群数量减少的物种。麦克米兰（McMillan）说，这项研究可能低估了这类咖啡馆的数量，因为它们可能并不全都在线上出现或没有出现在搜索结果中。

Exotic animal cafés are a relatively recent phenomenon. The first appeared in 2011 in Japan, according to McMillan’s research, so she thinks they likely have not yet played a substantial role in driving the pet trade. But by providing in-person animal interactions, as well as social media accounts and Web sites that reach a wider audience, she says these cafés could be contributing to the perception of exotic species as acceptable and safe pets—without providing the context of their imperiled status. Some of the shops even offer animals for sale. McMillan’s research shows the number of these cafés has grown rapidly, notably in Japan, where more than 60 opened between 2011 and 2018. (Japan’s Ministry of the Environment did not respond to requests for comment.) Elsewhere, those in search of an unusual coffee break can also hang with water lizards in Vietnam, prairie dogs in Thailand and African grey parrots in South Korea. The IUCN already considers all of these animals to be under threat from the pet trade.

异国动物咖啡馆是一种相对较新的现象。麦克米兰（McMillan）的研究显示，第一次出现在2011年的日本，因此她认为它们可能尚未在推动宠物贸易中发挥重要作用。但她说，通过提供人与动物的互动以及社交媒体帐户和可覆盖更广泛受众的网站，这些咖啡馆可能会有助于将外来物种视为可接受和安全的宠物，而不会提供其受害的环境。状态。一些商店甚至出售动物。 McMillan的研究表明，这些咖啡馆的数量迅速增长，特别是在日本，2011年至2018年间有60多家咖啡馆开业。（日本环境省未回应置评请求。）越南的水蜥蜴，泰国的草原土拨鼠和韩国的非洲灰鹦鹉也可能会休息。 IUCN已经认为所有这些动物都受到宠物贸易的威胁。

Brett Scheffers, a conservation biologist at the University of Florida, who was not involved with the study, agrees these cafés could normalize exotic animals as pets and help raise demand. “There’s lots of misconceptions that these animals are domesticated,” he says. “Fundamentally, they are not.”

佛罗里达大学的保护生物学家布雷特·谢弗斯（Brett Scheffers）没有参与这项研究，他同意这些咖啡馆可以将外来动物当作宠物正常化，并有助于增加需求。他说：“人们对这些动物被驯化有很多误解。” “从根本上讲，它们不是。”

Exotic animals often need special diets, housing and medical care that owners may not be willing or able to provide, according to a 2017 study in Animals. And even in more conventional environments such as accredited zoos, much remains unknown about the effects of visitor interactions on animals, according to a 2019 review in Animals that examined the types of animal-visitor interactions offered globally.

根据2017年《动物》杂志的一项研究，外来动物通常需要主人可能不愿或无法提供的特殊饮食，住房和医疗服务。根据2019年《动物》（Animals）杂志中的一项评论，该报告对全球范围内提供的动物与游客互动的类型进行了评估，即使在更常规的环境中，例如经过认可的动物园，也仍然不清楚访客互动对动物的影响。

It is also unclear where cafés are obtaining their animals. Some claim to get them from legitimate captive breeders, McMillan says. But research by the nonprofit conservation group TRAFFIC found that, at least in the case of otters, they are part of the illegal wildlife trade. TRAFFIC’s investigation traced their origins and found incidents of wild otters being smuggled into Japan—but no evidence of legitimate, regulated breeding operations (as required by CITES, the body that regulates the international wildlife trade). Animal sourcing is a very “blurry area,” McMillan says. “We need to be thinking about that. Where are these animals coming from? How are they being impacted? And how does it link into the broader exotic pet trade issue?”

还不清楚咖啡馆在哪里获得动物。麦克米兰说，有些人声称是从合法的圈养繁殖者那里获得它们的。但是非营利性保护组织TRAFFIC的研究发现，至少在水獭的情况下，它们是非法野生动植物贸易的一部分。 TRAFFIC的调查追溯了它们的起源，并发现了野獭被偷运到日本的事件-但没有证据表明合法，规范的育种活动（根据国际野生动植物贸易监管机构CITES的要求）。麦克米伦说，动物资源采购是一个非常“模糊的领域”。 “我们需要考虑一下。这些动物从哪里来？它们如何受到影响？它如何与更广泛的异国宠物贸易问题联系起来？”

Scheffers’s own research has found that almost a fifth of terrestrial vertebrate species are traded, both legally and illegally. It is difficult to determine the sources of many of the animals involved, he notes, because there are “enormous information gaps,” even in the legal exotic and wild animal trade. Every species in trade should automatically be regulated “because of how quickly populations become depleted when demand increases,” he says.

谢弗斯自己的研究发现，近五分之一的陆生脊椎动物都在合法和非法地买卖。他指出，很难确定涉及的许多动物的来源，因为即使在合法的外来和野生动物贸易中，也存在“巨大的信息空白”。他说，贸易中的每个物种都应自动受到监管，“因为需求增加时人口会很快耗尽。”

There is also the possibility that café animals could escape—or be dumped or abandoned—and establish invasive populations, both McMillan and Scheffers say. A 2019 study in Frontiers in Ecology and the Environment found the exotic pet trade is a significant contributor to nonnative species invasions. At least three of the species McMillan found in cafés are highly invasive—and 45 of the 406 cafés her search found had closed—raising questions about the fate of those animals.

麦克米兰（McMillan）和谢弗斯（Scheffers）都说，咖啡馆的动物也有可能逃脱，抛弃或丢弃，并建立入侵种群。 2019年在《生态与环境前沿》中的一项研究发现，外来宠物贸易是造成外来物种入侵的重要因素。在咖啡馆中发现的麦克米兰物种中，至少有3种具有高度侵略性-她在搜索到的406家咖啡馆中有45家已经关闭-对这些动物的命运提出了疑问。

There is also the risk of diseases being transmitted to native animal populations—and, in some cases, to humans. The wildlife trade contributed to the spread of the H5N1 virus, monkeypox and other diseases, according to a 2007 study in the Journal of Wildlife Diseases.

还存在疾病传播给本地动物种群的风险，在某些情况下还会传播给人类。根据2007年《野生动物疾病杂志》的一项研究，野生动物贸易促进了H5N1病毒，猴痘和其他疾病的传播。

Some of the cafés’ Web sites say they offer education to patrons, as well as safe and comfortable homes for animals, McMillan says. But more research is needed on local wildlife trade regulations, the pet trade and captive animal welfare to see if those claims hold up, she says.

麦克米伦说，一些咖啡馆的网站说，它们为顾客提供教育，并为动物提供安全舒适的家园。她说，但是需要对当地野生动植物贸易法规，宠物贸易和圈养动物福利进行更多研究，以了解这些主张是否成立。

She adds that any regulations and management should be implemented on a regional basis because the trade in wildlife and popular exotic animals varies greatly by area, as evinced by the different species kept in cafés in different regions. Local economic and cultural contexts should also be considered in future investigations to make it more likely that rules will be followed.

她补充说，任何法规和管理都应在地区范围内实施，因为野生生物和流行外来动物的贸易因地区而异，正如不同地区咖啡馆中保存的不同物种所证明的那样。在未来的调查中也应考虑当地的经济和文化背景，以使更有可能遵守规则。

McMillan hopes her research fuels these discussions and helps potential café visitors, including international tourists, make informed decisions. “In a lot of people’s minds, it’s just an innocent visit,” she says. “But there is the potential that you are having an impact on wild populations.”

麦克米兰（McMillan）希望她的研究能够促进这些讨论，并帮助包括国际游客在内的潜在咖啡馆游客做出明智的决定。她说：“在很多人的心中，这只是一次无辜的访问。” “但是您有可能对野生种群产生影响。”

在监狱围墙后面，猫和囚犯通过

2020-10-24 09:36:18

Cats can’t judge men on their wrongdoings. They cannot gauge the gravity of crime, and cannot judge the ink on people’s skin.

猫不能根据男人的错误行为来评判他们。他们无法判断犯罪的严重性，也无法判断人们皮肤上的墨水。

Cats are unable to distinguish between street clothes and prison uniforms –– and that’s exactly what makes the relationship between the men at Pendleton Correctional Facility, a maximum-security prison outside of Indianapolis, and the cats that live there, so special.

猫无法区分便衣和监狱制服-这正是彭德尔顿惩教设施(印第安纳波利斯郊外一座戒备森严的监狱)的男人和住在那里的猫之间的关系如此特别的原因。

For six hours a day, seven days a week, a handful of men receive unqualified love from the more than 20 cats that live in the prison as part of the FORWARD program, or Felines and Offenders Rehabilitation with Affection, Reformation and Dedication. In exchange for care and a place to stay before being adopted, the cats at Pendleton offer inmates untampered, non-judgemental affection.

在一周七天、每天六个小时的时间里，少数人从监狱里生活的20多只猫那里得到无条件的爱，这是前进计划(Forward Program)的一部分，也就是带着感情、改造和奉献的FLINS和罪犯康复。为了换取被收养前的照顾和住宿，彭德尔顿的猫向囚犯提供未经篡改的、不加评判的感情。

“The offenders really get a lot out of it,” said Michelle Rains, an administrative assistant at Pendleton who is deeply invested in the program. “Once you bond with animals, they don’t judge you. Those cats rely on the offenders.”

彭德尔顿大学的行政助理米歇尔·雷恩斯(Michelle Rains)对该项目投入了大量资金，她说：“违法者真的从中获益良多。”“一旦你和动物建立了联系，它们就不会评判你了。那些猫依赖于违法者。“。

Through the 5-year-old program, a select few incarcerated men are paid 20 cents an hour to spend their days caring for abandoned and abused cats, preparing them for adoption. Or, as some inmates will say, for a reason to get up in the morning.

通过这个成立5年的项目，少数被监禁的男子每小时获得20美分的报酬，让他们每天照顾被遗弃和虐待的猫，为它们被收养做好准备。或者，就像一些囚犯会说的那样，是因为早上起床的原因。

In partnership with the Animal Protection League of Indiana, the program removes cats from a traditional shelter and places them in the prison’s “cat sanctuary,” a wide-open room with scratching posts, climbing structures and nooks to hide in.

该项目与印第安纳州动物保护联盟(Animal Protection League Of Indiana)合作，将猫从传统的收容所移走，并将它们安置在监狱的“猫庇护所”，这是一个敞开的房间，有抓抓柱、攀爬结构和躲藏猫的角落。

The program houses them with incarcerated caregivers, who, incidentally, gain skills such as empathy, responsibility and self-esteem.

该计划为他们安排了被监禁的照顾者，顺便说一句，他们获得了同理心、责任感和自尊等技能。

“I feel that we’ve already took enough from society,” said Anthony LaRussa, one of the inmates in charge of caring for the cats. “Whatever (we) did, we’re being judged for it. When we’re able to come to a program like this, we’re able to give back, and not for just us, but for the animals.”

负责照顾这些猫的囚犯之一安东尼·拉鲁萨(Anthony LaRussa)说：“我觉得我们已经从社会上拿走了足够的东西。”“无论(我们)做了什么，我们都会因此受到评判。当我们能够参加这样的项目时，我们就能够回馈社会，不仅是为了我们自己，也是为了动物们。“

Inmates in the United States have long been tasked with often-undesirable duties in the name of rehabilitation or meager incentives.

长期以来，美国的囚犯一直以改过自新或微薄的激励的名义承担着往往不受欢迎的职责。

Some have been recruited to fight the raging wildfires tearing through the west coast, while others have been tasked with digging graves on the east coast. Others, however, find their place in animal-based rehabilitation programs like that at Pendleton.

一些人被招募来扑灭席卷西海岸的肆虐的野火，而另一些人的任务是在东海岸挖掘坟墓。然而，其他人在以动物为基础的康复项目中找到了自己的位置，比如彭德尔顿的项目。

Prison pet programs started in the 1980s as a way to decrease recidivism, or the rate at which released inmates reoffend. The programs started with a primary focus on dogs, but have since grown to include cats and in some cases, even horses.

监狱宠物项目始于20世纪80年代，目的是减少累犯率，即释放囚犯的再犯罪率。这些项目一开始主要关注狗，但后来发展到包括猫，在某些情况下，甚至包括马。

Monica Solinas-Saunders, an associate professor at Indiana University whose research focuses on incarceration and offender rehabilitation, said these types of programs have shown significant improvements in recidivism rates –– and on inmates’ soft skills, or the personal attributes that help people navigate their environment.

印第安纳大学(Indiana University)副教授莫妮卡·索利纳斯-桑德斯(Monica Solinas-Saunders)的研究重点是监禁和罪犯改过自新，她说，这些类型的项目在累犯率-以及囚犯的软技能，即帮助人们驾驭环境的个人属性-方面都有了显著的改善。

“The evidence from the research shows these pet programs actually have a fantastic effect on increased personal responsibility for offenders,” Solinas-Saunders said. “It increases self-esteem and self-efficacy. It also increases empathy, which is something we are really appreciating these days, in terms of understanding each other’s pain.”

索利纳斯-桑德斯说：“来自研究的证据表明，这些宠物项目实际上对增加违法者的个人责任有奇妙的效果。”“它增加了自尊和自我效能感。它还增加了同理心，这是我们现在真正欣赏的东西，就理解彼此的痛苦而言。“。

Beyond that, pet programs have resulted in improved relationships within the facility, between the involved offenders and other incarcerated individuals, and between offenders and the staff and administrators –– which is exactly the intended outcome of programs aimed toward rehabilitation.

除此之外，宠物项目改善了设施内的关系，改善了涉案罪犯与其他被监禁个人之间的关系，以及罪犯与工作人员和管理人员之间的关系--这正是旨在改过自新的计划的预期结果。

Studies on prison pet programs have also shown increased emotional intelligence and coping skills, she said, as well as improved work ethic and employability, which improve the chances of job readiness and are critical factors in successfully re-entering society and reducing recidivism.

她说，对监狱宠物项目的研究还显示，情商和应对技能有所提高，职业道德和就业能力也有所改善，这提高了就业准备的机会，是成功重新进入社会和减少累犯率的关键因素。

Maleah Stringer, the Animal Protection League's executive director who has developed numerous prison programs, estimates there are 400 cats and kittens in the shelter right now. And that's not including fosters or those outsourced to programs such as FORWARD.

动物保护联盟(Animal Protection League)的执行董事马莉亚·斯特林格(Maleah Stringer)已经制定了许多监狱项目，她估计目前收容所里大约有400只猫和小猫。这还不包括寄养人员或外包给“前进”等项目的人员。

“We get overwhelmed with animals," Stringer said, "so this is just one way for us to have another outlet to put them so we, quite frankly, don't have to euthanize.

“我们被动物淹没了，”斯特林格说，“所以这只是我们有另一种方式来释放它们的一种方式，坦率地说，我们不必实施安乐死。”

“The tragedy of the American animal sheltering system is that people do not understand what happens in shelters and how hard it is," she added. "Most shelters are understaffed, underfunded, have limited resources, and are asked for miracles every day."

她补充说：“美国动物收容所制度的悲剧在于，人们不知道收容所里发生了什么，也不知道收容所有多难。”大多数庇护所人手不足，资金不足，资源有限，每天都被要求创造奇迹。

Stringer has been working with prisons since 2006, when she helped the National Greyhound Foundation place dogs in correctional facilities. While trying to collect donations for the program, she found immediate resistance because of the connection with prisons.

斯特林格自2006年以来一直与监狱合作，当时她帮助国家灰狗基金会将狗安置在惩教设施中。当她试图为该项目筹集捐款时，由于与监狱的联系，她发现了直接的阻力。

“The response I got at that time was not positive at all," Stringer said. "I was told people did not want to pay for criminals to have pets."

“我当时得到的反应完全不是正面的，”斯特林格说。有人告诉我，人们不想花钱让罪犯养宠物。

“When I went into this program with prisoners, I was not an advocate for the inmates," she continued. "I didn’t like them. This was a means to an end to save these animals."

“当我和囚犯一起参加这个项目时，我并不是囚犯的代言人，”她继续说。我不喜欢他们。这是拯救这些动物的一种手段。

The public's attitude changed, she said, as she brought volunteers to interact with the inmates and see how seriously they cared for the animals. As time wore on, her own perception of incarcerated people evolved as well, and she has since hired about 20 former offenders to work at the APL.

她说，公众的态度发生了变化，因为她带来了志愿者，与囚犯们密切互动，看看他们对动物的关爱有多认真。随着时间的推移，她自己对被监禁的人的看法也发生了变化，自那以后，她已经聘请了大约20名前罪犯在APL工作。

"Our mission is making second chances possible through the human-animal connection," Stringer said. "Because of that connection with the animals, we’re able to help offenders who are serious about trying to do it differently when they get out."

斯特林格说：我们的任务是通过人与动物之间的联系，让第二次机会成为可能。由于与动物的这种联系，我们能够帮助那些真正想要在出狱后采取不同做法的罪犯。

Solinas-Saunders said that nationwide, many prison administrations hesitate to implement rehabilitation programs into maximum-security facilities such as Pendleton, due to the fact that offenders there may never leave the prison.

索利纳斯-桑德斯说，在全国范围内，许多监狱管理部门对在彭德尔顿等戒备森严的设施实施康复计划犹豫不决，因为那里的罪犯可能永远不会离开监狱。

Their logic begs the question: Why invest in rehabilitation if inmates may not re-enter society?

他们的逻辑回避了一个问题：如果囚犯可能无法重新进入社会，为什么要投资于改过自新呢？

“Why not involve them to better their experience?” Solinas-Saunders countered. “We need to be more empathetic toward incarcerated people in general. They are part of our community, even if we don’t see them every day.”

“为什么不让他们参与进来，改善他们的体验呢？”索利纳斯-桑德斯反驳道。“总的来说，我们需要对被监禁的人有更多的同情心。他们是我们社区的一部分，即使我们不是每天都能见到他们。“。

And, although Pendleton is a maximum security prison, many of its inmates will eventually return to society.

而且，虽然彭德尔顿是一座戒备森严的监狱，但它的许多囚犯最终都会重返社会。

Rains said involving offenders with animal programs plays a critical role in improving their lives after incarceration.

RAINS说，让罪犯参与动物项目对于改善他们入狱后的生活起着至关重要的作用。

“The more programs you have for the offenders, the better the outcome,” she said. “Our goal, ultimately, is that when an offender is released, he comes out a better person than when he came in. My personal opinion is that dealing with animals is a great way to improve someone’s outlook.”

“你为罪犯提供的项目越多，结果就越好，”她说。“我们的最终目标是，当罪犯被释放时，他出来的时候会比他进来时更好。”我个人的看法是，与动物打交道是改善一个人看法的好方法。“。

LaRussa, who is behind bars for conspiracy to commit robbery — which was botched and ended with accomplices murdering four people — has been involved in the program since 2017, when he fell for a 7-year-old cat named Clover. He and his wife have since adopted Clover, and she lives at home with LaRussa’s wife.

拉鲁萨因合谋抢劫而入狱-这件事搞砸了，最终被几名同伙杀害了四人。自2017年以来，他一直参与这个项目，当时他爱上了一只7岁的猫，名叫三叶草。他和他的妻子后来收养了克洛弗，她和拉鲁萨的妻子住在家里。

Every day, LaRussa and his peers start their morning by 7 a.m. They report to the cat sanctuary, where, like clockwork, the cats await them, dozens of tiny faces longing for the door to open.

每天，LaRussa和他的同龄人从早上7点开始他们的早晨。他们向猫咪保护区报到，在那里，猫咪们像发条一样等待着他们，几十张小脸渴望着门能打开。

“They know these cats depend on them on a daily basis,” said Christina Conyers, an administrative assistant with the program. “It’s their human. They need them.”

“他们知道这些猫每天都依赖它们，”该项目的行政助理克里斯蒂娜·科尼尔斯(Christina Conyers)说。“这是他们的人类。他们需要他们。“

The caregivers spend their days cleaning the cat sanctuary, changing litter boxes, and feeding and giving water to the cats. Everything but medical care is under the inmates’ purview –– trimming nails, brushing hair and cleaning ears are all among their daily duties.

照顾者们每天都在打扫猫咪保护区，更换猫砂箱，给猫咪喂食和饮水。除了医疗护理外，其他一切都在囚犯的职权范围内-修剪指甲、梳头和清洁耳朵都是他们的日常职责。

“I believe it’s changed me a lot,” LaRussa said. “I've grown, from even just the little time that I’ve been here until now. We’re all incarcerated. Whether you are selfish or not, you learn to care about something other than yourself. Now, it’s all about (the cats) and trying to help them in the best way possible.”

“我相信这改变了我很多，”LaRussa说。“我已经成长了，甚至从我在这里的一小段时间到现在也是如此。我们都被监禁了。不管你是否自私，你都要学会关心自己以外的事情。现在，这一切都是为了(猫)，并试图以最好的方式帮助它们。“。

Tori Kypreos, the program supervisor at Pendleton, has watched inmates move through the program and evolve as a result.

彭德尔顿监狱的项目主管托里·凯普雷奥斯(Tori Kypreos)观察了囚犯们在整个项目中的进展，并因此得到了发展。

“It teaches them responsibility,” Kypreos said. “It teaches them there are other things that are important than what they believe is important. The cats rely on them immensely, so just seeing that they’re dedicated to coming in every day to help and take care of these cats, to watch the cats grow, is very important.”

“这教会了他们责任，”Kypreos说。“它告诉他们，除了他们认为重要的东西之外，还有其他事情是重要的。猫非常依赖它们，所以看到它们每天都来帮助和照顾这些猫，看着它们成长，这是非常重要的。“。

The cats and the inmates, both bound by prison walls and troubled pasts, mend each other day by day.

猫和囚犯都被监狱的围墙和麻烦的过去捆绑在一起，一天又一天地互相修复。

“It gives me a purpose to get up every morning,” said Joseph Coleman, a cat caregiver who is in prison for homicide. “These cats need somebody like I need them. I need them just as much as they need me. It’s really a Godsend. When I'm in here with the cats, I’m not thinking about out there.”

“这给了我每天早上起床的目的，”因杀人罪入狱的猫饲养员约瑟夫·科尔曼(Joseph Coleman)说。“这些猫需要一个人，就像我需要他们一样。我需要他们，就像他们需要我一样。这真是天赐之物。当我和猫在一起的时候，我不会想到外面。“。

Each of the individuals involved, from the administrators to the offenders, said they wished the general public knew the cats were up for adoption. They all would love to see a cat meet its “forever family,” they said.

从管理人员到违法者，参与其中的每一个人都表示，他们希望普通公众知道这些猫是可以收养的。他们说，他们都希望看到一只猫见到它的“永远的家人”。

Before coming to Pendleton, many of the cats were surrendered by abusive households or found on the street. While they would traditionally be housed in a shelter, they are now able to roam the cat sanctuary, interacting with people more regularly, socializing them and increasing their chances of adoption.

在来到彭德尔顿之前，许多猫被虐待的家庭投降或在街上被发现。虽然它们传统上会被安置在收容所里，但它们现在可以在猫咪保护区漫步，更频繁地与人们互动，与他们进行社交，并增加他们被收养的机会。

Some with particularly troubled pasts are hard to warm up to and care for, LaRussa said. But it’s his job to let them know they’re cared for, to not give up.

LaRussa说，一些有特别麻烦的过去的人很难温暖和照顾。但他的工作是让他们知道他们受到照顾，不放弃。

“A lot of us have gave up in life, me included,” LaRussa said. “With them, I just don’t want to give up.”

“我们很多人在生活中都放弃了，包括我，”拉鲁萨说。“和他们在一起，我就是不想放弃。”

Anyone interested in adopting a cat from Pendleton should submit an application on the Animal Protection League's website, or message the staff on their Facebook page (@Pendleton Correctional Facility_FORWARD). Due to the pandemic, they are currently unable to let anyone into the facility to meet the cats.

任何有兴趣从彭德尔顿领养猫的人都应该在动物保护联盟的网站上提交申请，或者在他们的Facebook页面上给工作人员发消息(@Pendleton和惩教机构_Forward)。由于大流行，他们目前无法让任何人进入设施与猫见面。

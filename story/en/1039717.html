California regulators fine Uber $59M for failing to give info on sexual assault cases, will cancel license if Uber fails to pay and provide info within 30 days
加州监管机构因未能提供性侵犯案件信息而向Uber罚款5900万美元，如果Uber在30天内未能付款并提供信息，它将取消许可

2020-12-15 07:25:22

Uber has 30 days to pay a $59 million fine to California’s Public Utilities Commission (CPUC) for failing to answer the regulator’s questions about a damning safety report released by the company in December 2019. If Uber doesn’t pay up and answer the outstanding questions, CPUC could suspend the company’s license to operate in the state, an administrative law judge ruled on Monday.

Uber有30天的时间向加利福尼亚州公用事业委员会（CPUC）支付5900万美元的罚款，原因是该公司未能回答监管机构有关该公司于2019年12月发布的有害安全报告的问题。周一，一位行政法法官裁定，CPUC可以暂停该公司在该州的经营许可证。

It’s the latest development in Uber’s long history of trouble with violence and assault between its drivers and passengers — trouble that competitor Lyft shares, too. Uber did not immediately respond to a request for comment. News of the fine was first reported by The San Francisco Chronicle.

这是优步（Uber）在司机和乘客之间遭受暴力和殴打的悠久历史中的最新发展-竞争对手Lyft也遇到了麻烦。优步没有立即回应置评请求。有关罚款的消息最早由《旧金山纪事报》报道。

Regulators want to know more about Uber’s own damning safety report, released in 2019

监管机构想了解有关Uber于2019年发布的该死的安全报告的更多信息

The report, which Uber itself called “jarring” at the time, detailed thousands of sexual assaults in the US that happened in 2017 and 2018 during trips taken with the company’s ride-hailing platform. While the 84-page report included a fair amount of data in aggregate, CPUC wanted to know more shortly after it was released — especially because Uber admitted in the fine print that the report did not “assess or take any position on whether any of the reported incidents actually occurred.”

该报告当时被Uber称为“震撼”，详细描述了2017年和2018年在该公司乘车平台旅行期间发生的数千起美国性侵犯事件。尽管这份84页的报告总共包含了相当数量的数据，但CPUC希望在发布后不久就知道更多-特别是因为Uber在细则中承认该报告没有“评估或采取任何立场，实际发生的事件。”

The CPUC has regulatory authority over transportation companies in the state and regularly investigates complaints against them. So it asked Uber a handful of questions about who authored the report, and also asked Uber for specific details on each incident of assault.

CPUC对州内的运输公司具有监管权，并定期调查对它们的投诉。因此，它向Uber询问了有关谁撰写报告的一些问题，还向Uber询问了每起袭击事件的具体细节。

Uber never answered the questions, claiming that further disclosure would present a privacy risk for both the assault survivors and its employees. In January 2020, a judge denied the company’s request to avoid answering, saying Uber could file the answers under seal in order to protect confidentiality. Uber continued to fight answering the CPUC’s questions throughout the year until up Monday’s ruling, though.

Uber从未回答过这些问题，声称进一步披露将对袭击幸存者及其员工均构成隐私风险。 2020年1月，法官驳回了公司避免回答的请求，称优步可以将答案密封保存，以保护机密。不过，Uber全年仍在努力回答CPUC的问题，直到星期一的裁决为止。

In the ruling, the judge described those efforts as little more than “specious legal roadblocks” meant to “frustrate the Commission’s ability to gather information” about whether Uber is operating safely. The judge arrived at the $59 million figure by levying a $7,500 fine for every specific time that Uber refused to answer each question during the process.

法官在裁决中将这些努力描述为“旨在阻止Uber是否安全运行的”故意的法律障碍”，旨在“挫败委员会收集信息的能力”。法官通过对Uber在此过程中拒绝回答每个问题的每个特定时间征收7,500美元的罚款，得出了5,900万美元的数字。

“Uber is a billion-dollar business that can easily afford to pay ... [e]ven during a pandemic where ridership has undoubtedly declined,” the judge wrote.

法官写道：“尤伯是一家市值十亿美元的企业，即使在乘客量无疑下降的大流行时期，也很容易负担得起。”

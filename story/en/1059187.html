Consumers are likely familiar with the front-end brands for these storefronts — with companies like Amazon, Shopify, Square, and Stripe owning attention — but it’s the tooling behind the curtain that is increasingly determining the competitiveness of individual stores.

消费者可能熟悉这些店面的前端品牌 - 与亚马逊，购物，广场和条纹相同的公司，但这是窗帘后面的工具，越来越多地确定个人商店的竞争力。

Klaviyo may not be a household name to consumers (at least, not yet), but in many ways, this startup has become the standard by which email marketers are judged today, triangulating against veterans Mailchimp and Constant Contact and riding the e-commerce wave to new heights.

Klaviyo可能不是消费者的家喻户晓的名字（至少，尚未），但在很多方面，这项启动已成为今天判断电子邮件营销人员的标准，这将针对退伍军人MailChimp和持续的接触和骑电子商务波来实现电子邮件营销人员新的高度。

Founded in 2012, this Boston-based company helps marketers personalize and automate their email messaging to customers. By now, most people are intimately familiar with these kinds of emails; if you’ve ever given your email address to an online store, the entreaties to come back to your abandoned cart or browse the latest sale are Klaviyo’s bread and butter.

基于波士顿的公司成立于2012年，帮助营销人员个性化和自动化他们的电子邮件给客户。到目前为止，大多数人都很熟悉这些电子邮件;如果您曾经给过您的电子邮件地址到在线商店，则会回到您被放弃的购物车或浏览最新销售的植物是Klaviyo的面包和黄油。

It may seem obvious in retrospect that email would grow to become a premier platform for marketing, but this wasn’t the case even a few years ago when social ads and search engine marketing were the dominant paradigm. Today, owned marketing and customer experience management are white-hot trends, and Klaviyo has surged from a lifestyle business to a multi-billion dollar behemoth in just a few short years. Its story is at the heart of the internet economy today, and the future.

回顾似乎是显而易见的，电子邮件将成长为销售的首选平台，但即使是几年前也不是当社会广告和搜索引擎营销是主导范式时，这并非如此。今天，拥有的营销和客户体验管理是白热趋势，Klaviyo在短短几年内从生活方式业务飙升到一亿美元的庞然大物。它的故事是今天互联网经济的核心，以及未来。

Klaviyo EC-1包含四个主要用品，编号为9,700个单词和43分钟的阅读时间。让我们来看看：

Part 1: Origin story “ How Klaviyo transformed from a lifestyle business into a $4.15B email titan” (2,600 words/10 minutes) — Explores the rise of Klaviyo from a database for e-commerce data into a modern email powerhouse as it successively learned from customers and bootstrapped in the absence of funding from accelerators and early VCs.

第1部分：原点故事“克拉维多从生活方式业务转变为4.15亿美元的电子邮件泰坦”（2,600字/ 10分钟） - 探索从数据库中的klaviyo的崛起，以便电子商务数据进入现代电子邮件推动力来自客户并在没有加速器和早期VCS的情况下脱离资金。

Part 2: Business and growth “ How Klaviyo used data and no-code to transform owned marketing” (3,000 words/12 minutes) — Analyzes Klaviyo’s recent growth and how marketers increasingly focus on owned marketing channels and customer experience management.

第2部分：业务和增长“Klaviyo使用数据和否则如何转换所有营销”（3,000字/ 12分钟） - 分析Klaviyo最近的增长以及营销人员如何越来越关注拥有的营销渠道和客户体验管理。

Part 3: Dynamics of e-commerce marketing “ Marketing in 2021 is emotional and not just transactional” (2,200 words/9 minutes) — To fully understand Klaviyo and this new world of martech, this article contextualizes how and why marketers are increasingly trying to personalize and build deeper emotional bonds with their customers outside of social media channels.

第3部分：电子商务营销的动态“2021年营销是情绪化的，而不仅仅是交易”（2,200字/ 9分钟） - 本文全面了解Klaviyo和这个新世界的Martech，营销人员越来越多地试图与社交媒体渠道以外的客户个性化和建立更深层次的情感债券。

Part 4: Lessons on startup growth “ Drama and quirk aren’t necessary for startup success” (1,900 words/8 minutes) — Founders shouldn’t have to keep learning the same lessons over and over again. Klaviyo offers a number of tried-and-true tutorials to understand how to build a competitive startup and not get bogged down in finding product-market fit and scaling.

第4部分：启动增长的课程“戏剧和怪癖不是启动成功所必需的”（1,900字/ 8分钟） - 创始人不得不一遍又一遍地继续学习相同的教训。 Klaviyo提供了许多尝试和真正的教程，了解如何构建竞争激烈的启动，而不是在寻找产品市场合适和缩放方面陷入困境。

British engineers develop process to turn moon dust into oxygen
英国工程师开发了将月尘转化为氧气的工艺

2020-11-28 06:53:47

Washington [US], November 27 (ANI): British engineers are fine-tuning a process that will be used to extract oxygen from lunar dust, leaving behind metal powders that could be 3D printed into construction materials for a Moon base.

华盛顿[美国]，11月27日（ANI）：英国工程师正在对将用于从月尘中提取氧气的过程进行微调，留下可以3D打印到月球基地建筑材料中的金属粉末。

It could be an early step to establishing an extra-terrestrial oxygen extraction plant. This would help to enable exploration and sustain life on the Moon while avoiding the enormous cost of sending materials from Earth.

这可能是建立地外氧气提取厂的第一步。这将有助于在月球上进行探索并维持生命，同时避免了从地球上运送材料的巨大费用。

The oxygen generated would mostly be used to make rocket fuel, but could also provide air for lunar settlers.

产生的氧气主要用于制造火箭燃料，但也可以为登月者提供空气。

The project is part of ESA's preparations to establish a permanent and sustainable lunar presence. Astronauts will live and work on the Moon, where they will help to develop and test technologies needed for missions farther into deep space.

该项目是欧空局为建立永久和可持续的月球存在而进行的准备工作的一部分。宇航员将在月球上生活和工作，在那里他们将帮助开发和测试更深入太空任务所需的技术。

Lunar regolith, the thin layer of dusty rock that blankets the Moon, is not so different from the minerals found on Earth. By weight, it contains about 45 percent oxygen which is bound to metals such as iron and titanium, making it unavailable.

月球长石是覆盖月球的薄薄的尘土岩石层，与地球上发现的矿物没有太大不同。按重量计，它含有约45％的氧，这些氧与诸如铁和钛的金属结合，因此无法使用。

British company Metalysis has already developed a mineral extraction process that is used by industries on Earth to produce metals for manufacturing.

英国公司Metalysis已经开发了一种矿物提取工艺，该工艺已被地球上的工业生产用于制造的金属。

The electrochemical process takes place in a specially designed chamber - the ones used for research are about the size of a washing machine. Oxygen-containing material is submerged in molten salt, heated to 950 degrees Celsius.

电化学过程在专门设计的腔室内进行-用于研究的腔大约与洗衣机的大小有关。含氧材料浸入熔融盐中，加热到950摄氏度。

A current is then passed through it, which triggers the oxygen to be extracted and migrate across the liquid salt to collect at an electrode, leaving behind a mixture of metal powders.

然后有电流流过，这触发了氧气的提取，并流过液态盐，在电极上聚集，留下了金属粉末的混合物。

As part of the current project, Metalysis engineers are fine-tuning the technique with its lunar application in mind.

作为当前项目的一部分，Metalysis工程师会在考虑月球应用的情况下对其进行微调。

The big difference is that, on Earth, the oxygen generated is not needed, but in space, it will be the most important product of the process. This means it needs to be engineered to produce as much of the gas as possible.

最大的区别是，在地球上不需要生成氧气，但在太空中它将是该过程最重要的产物。这意味着需要对其进行工程设计以产生尽可能多的气体。

Engineers will tinker with the process by adjusting the electrical current and reagents to boost the amount of oxygen while trying to reduce the temperature needed to produce it. This will help bring down the energy required, which is already at a premium on the Moon.

工程师将通过调节电流和试剂来增加氧气的量，同时尝试降低生产氧气的温度，从而改善这一过程。这将有助于降低所需的能量，而这种能量在月球上已经非常宝贵。

They will also work to reduce the size of the chamber the process takes place in so that it can be transported efficiently to the Moon.

他们还将努力减小过程发生的房间的大小，以便可以将其有效地运输到月球。

In parallel, ESA and Metalysis have challenged innovators to develop an in-process monitoring system that could be used to keep track of oxygen production in future lunar extraction plants.

同时，ESA和Metalysis挑战了创新者开发的过程中监控系统，该系统可用于跟踪未来月球提取厂中的氧气生产。

"Some years ago we realised that the seemingly unimportant by-product of our terrestrial mineral extraction process could have far-reaching applications in space exploration," said Ian Mellor, managing director at Metalysis"We look forward to continuing to explore with ESA, and our industrial partners, how to get our Earthly technology space-ready," added Mellor.

Metalysis总经理Ian Mellor说：“几年前，我们意识到陆地矿物提取过程中看似不重要的副产品可能在太空探索中具有深远的应用。我们期待继续与ESA进行探索，并且我们的工业合作伙伴，如何使我们的地球技术做好太空准备，” Mellor补充说。

"This exciting project forms part of ESA's wider Space Resources Strategy which will help us demonstrate how material already present on the Moon can be used sustainably to support long-term space endeavours," said Advenit Makaya, the ESA materials engineer who is overseeing the project.

负责该项目的ESA材料工程师Advenit Makaya表示：“这个令人兴奋的项目是ESA广泛的空间资源战略的一部分，它将帮助我们证明月球上已经存在的材料如何可持续地用于支持长期的太空事业。” 。

"The project will help us learn more about Metalysis' process, and may even be a stepping stone to establishing an automated pilot oxygen plant on the Moon - with the added bonus of metal alloys that could be used by 3D printers to create construction materials," added Makaya.

“该项目将帮助我们了解有关Metalysis的过程的更多信息，甚至可能成为在月球上建立自动化的先导制氧设备的垫脚石-额外的金属合金可以被3D打印机用来制造建筑材料， ” Makaya补充说。

"In the future, if we want to travel extensively in space and set up bases on the Moon and Mars, then we will need to make or find the things required to support life - food, water and breathable air," said Sue Horne, head of space exploration at the UK Space Agency.

苏·霍恩说：“将来，如果我们想在太空中广泛旅行并在月球和火星上建立基地，那么我们将需要制造或找到维持生命所需的东西-食物，水和透气性。”英国航天局太空探索主管。

"The involvement of Metalysis in a programme that aims to do just that, by producing oxygen on a lunar setting, will showcase the UK's space credentials on the world-stage and help unlock breakthroughs that bring future space exploration a step closer," added Horne. (ANI)

Horne补充说：“ Metalysis参与旨在通过在月球环境下产生氧气来实现这一目标的计划，这将展示英国在世界舞台上的太空资格，并帮助解锁使未来太空探索更近一步的突破，” 。 （ANI）

Lili, a neobank aimed at freelancers to use a single account for both work and personal use, raises $55M Series B led by Group 11
Lili是一个旨在使用单一账户的自由职业人员的新田纳金公司，为两人使用的单一账户，由第11组领导的B领导5500万系列

2021-05-11 22:10:48

Lili, which provides a way for freelancers to use a single account both to manage their work and personal financial lives, along with tools to help manage the particular financial and accounting demands that come with being a one-person business, has picked up $55 million in a Series B round that it will be using to continue expanding its platform.

Lili，它为自由职业者提供了一种方式来使用单个帐户来管理他们的工作和个人金融生活，以及帮助管理成为一人生业务的特定财务和会计要求的工具，已获得5500万美元在B型系列中，它将使用它来继续扩展其平台。

Today the company already provides users with a single account and payment card that can be used both for personal and business expenses, and an “assistant” that analyses expenses and budget for paying future taxes based on your incomings. Down the line it will also be expanding into invoicing, payment management and credit (eg, loans or cash advances) for its customers.

今天，该公司已经为用户提供了一个单一的帐户和支付卡，可用于个人和商业费用，以及分析根据您的收入支付未来税款的费用和预算的“助理”。下线它也将扩展到其客户的发票，付款管理和信贷（例如，贷款或现金进展）。

“This is about financial services for freelancers,” co-founder and CEO Lilac Bar David said in an interview. “We are redefining a new kind of user, who is between a consumer and a business and has specific needs. If you look at the economy and what the future of work will look like, this really needs to be addressed.”

“这是关于自由职业者的金融服务，”联合创始人和首席执行官丁香·巴拉德大卫在接受采访时说。 “我们正在重新定义一种新的用户，谁是消费者和企业之间并具有特定需求。如果你看看经济，工作的未来看起来像是什么样的，这真的需要解决。“

Lili is based out of New York and Israel, and its focus is primarily on the U.S. market, where Bar David said the opportunity is big enough to keep Lili occupied for some time. It is estimated that currently 40% of workers are freelance, and that proportion is projected to rise to just over 50% of all working people by 2027.

Lili是以纽约和以色列为止，其重点主要是美国市场，酒吧大卫说机会足以让Lili保持一段时间。据估计，目前有40％的工人是自由职业者，比例预计将增加2027年的所有劳动人民的50％以上。

The funding is being led by Group 11, a VC with a strong focus on fintech, with Target Global, AltaIR and previous investors also participating. (That list of past backers include Zeev Ventures, Foundation Capital and the Google for Startups accelerator.) The company has now raised $80 million and is not disclosing its valuation yet.

资金是由第11组领导的，一名VC，凭借强烈关注金融气，目标全球，Altair和以前的投资者也参与。 （过去的支持者名单包括Zeev Ventures，基金会资本和谷歌的初创公司加速器。）该公司现已提高了8000万美元，并未披露其估值。

Launched in January 2020, just before the pandemic really kicked off, Lili has picked up 200,000 customers to date, Bar David tells me.

在2020年1月推出，就在大流行真的被开始之前，莉莉已经拿到了20万家客户迄今为止，酒吧大卫告诉我。

Its growth was very much on the back of a significant shift in the working world put into motion by the Covid-19 crisis, both in terms of a new cadre of potential users; and in terms of how the product is delivered.

这一增长在工作世界中的重大转变后面非常重要，在Covid-19危机方面，既有新的潜在用户一样;并且就如何交付产品而言。

On new users, while freelancers have always been present in the economy, their numbers suddenly swelled. In some cases, people were being forced into pausing their normal employment (for example when say a public venue like a theater had to temporarily close its doors; and in others they were simply laid off as businesses no longer appeared viable. In both cases, it led to a sudden surge of people becoming enterprising with their skills to make a living, rather than trying to apply for new full-time work during the pandemic.

在新用户，虽然自由职业者一直存在于经济中，但他们的数字突然膨胀。在某些情况下，人们被迫暂停正常就业（例如，当像剧院这样的公共场所不得不暂时关闭其大门;在其他人中，他们只是被制定，因为企业不再出现可行。在这两种情况下，它导致人们突然涌现，以企业为进取的技能来实现生活，而不是试图在大流行期间申请新的全职工作。

It turns out this was a particularly acute predicament for women. More than 5 million women lost jobs in the wake of the pandemic, Bar David said, and many of them turned to freelance work. Lili says that more than half, almost 60%, of its new users are women.

事实证明，这是女性特别令人敏锐的困境。在大流行，酒吧大卫说，超过500万名女性失去了工作，其中许多人转向自由工作。莉莉说，一半以上，近60％，其新用户是女性。

In terms of the technology, Bar David noted that for those looking for a new kind of account to help manage finances needed something flexible and completely usable without the need to visit a physical bank branch.

就技术而言，Bar David指出，对于那些寻找新的账户来帮助管理财务需要灵活，完全可用的东西，而无需访问物理银行分行。

“We were definitely in the right place at the right time,” she said. “Not only did Covid have an impact on freelance economy, but it mean no access to bank branches. That meant a greater willingness to adopt a service like ours, and that had a huge effect.”

“我们肯定在正确的时间在正确的地方，”她说。 “Covid不仅对自由经济产生了影响，而且意味着没有进入银行分支机构。这意味着更愿意采用像我们这样的服务，并且这一效果巨大。“

Freelancing — whatever your actual skill or profession happens to be — has a lot to recommend it. You are, as they say, your own boss: you can choose which hours your work, what work you take on, how to execute it, and whether you want to promote yourself to work on other things without worrying if your manager will endorse or beat down or perhaps worst of all ignore or dismiss your ambition. You have only your own motivation, and luck, to praise or constructively criticize or sympathize with in your annual reviews with yourself about how you have been doing.

自由职业者 - 无论你的实际技能或职业如何发生 - 都要推荐它。正如他们所说，你自己的老板：你可以选择你的工作时间，你接受哪些工作时间，如何执行它，以及你是否想宣传自己在其他事情上努力，如果你的经理会赞同或者击败或者可能是最忽视或忽视你的野心。您只有自己的动机和运气，赞美或建设性地批评或同情您在与自己的年度审查中的审查，了解你是如何做的。

But — and I can say this from very direct experience — it can be a real pain in the ass. You have to figure out how to healthily manage your time well, and not take on too much, or panic when not enough work is coming in. You are constantly fighting your own corner when those contracting your services to exploit you or underpay you, with the understanding that pushing back too much might find out you out of a job altogether. You may not get the chances and work you were hoping to get, and then you don’t get paid for the shots you never take.

但是 - 我可以从非常直接的经验中说出来 - 它可能是屁股中的真正痛苦。你必须弄清楚如何健康地管理你的时间，而不是在太多的时候，当没有足够的工作即可进来时恐慌。当那些签订你的服务来利用你或下行你的人时，你正在不断地战斗你的角落。对推迟太多的理解可能会完全找出你的工作。你可能没有得到你希望得到的机会和工作，然后你不会为你从不接受的镜头得到报酬。

And that is before the considerable efforts you need to put in to effectively become your own business manager. That’s tracking your expenses, deciding how to itemize things that you spend money on to align them with your taxes, and of course budgeting yourself each month based on your incomings, and using that budget wisely in ways that make the most sense for you as a consumer in the world, and you as a business person. If this kind of stuff is not your strongest suit, well, that’s tough. You have to do it anyway.

这是在有效地成为您自己的业务经理所需的相当大的努力之前。这是在追踪您的费用，决定如何将您花钱的事项与您的税收对齐，以及每个月根据您的收入将自己预算，并明智地使用该预算以使您最有意义地为您提供最大意义的方式世界上的消费者，以及你作为商务人士。如果这种东西不是你最强壮的西装，那就太难了。无论如何，你必须这样做。

Lili is an interesting and catchy idea because of how it’s identified all of those transactional issues, and is building tools to help people manage all of it. This is not just about having a business card to manage payments, but a bigger system that tracks what you are spending and learns from it to give you advice on how to manage your taxes and expenses, what you should be keeping back each month to pay towards income tax filings, and more. Typically, freelancers either do this on the fly from their personal accounts or set up separate business accounts to try to manage it, but neither of those options really presents a clean and easy to use user experience.

Lili是一个有趣而有趣的想法，因为它是如何确定所有这些交易问题，并且是建造工具，以帮助人们管理所有这些。这不仅仅是有一个名片来管理付款，而是一个更大的系统，这些系统跟踪你正在支出的东西，并从中学习，为您提供有关如何管理税费和费用的建议，您应该每月保留税款的建议走向所得税申请等。通常，自由职业者要么从他们的个人账户那里执行此操作，要么设置单独的业务帐户以尝试管理它，但这些选项都没有真正呈现干净且易于使用的用户体验。

Over time, the plan will be to continue adding in more tools and integrate with a wider set of third-party applications that freelancers might use to invoice customers or pay out invoices themselves. It already has integrations with Venmo and QuickBooks, and more will be coming, Bar David said, “w hatever a self-employed person needs to be successful,” she said.

随着时间的推移，该计划将继续在更多的工具中添加更多的工具，并与自由职业者可能用来发票客户或自己支付发票的更广泛的第三方应用程序集成。它已经与Venmo和Quickbooks的一体化，而且更多将来会来，Bar David说，“W Hatingver A自雇人员需要成功，”她说。

There are now dozens of neobanks on the market, but Lili’s specific focus on these self-employed freelancers is what has helped it stand out.

现在有几十个新手，但Lili对这些自雇自由职业者的专注是有助于它的突出。

“Lili is currently the only fintech platform designed to address the needs of this rapidly growing sector of entrepreneurs,” said Group 11 Founding Partner Dovi Frances, in a statement. “We anticipate the freelancer economy will continue to robustly grow well beyond the pandemic tailwinds. Lili is poised to see exponential growth by continuing to offer this demographic essential, effective and intuitive tools to manage what was previously an extremely complex and inefficient way of doing business.”

“Lili是目前唯一唯一旨在满足这一迅速增长的企业家的需求的Fintech平台，”第11组创始伙伴Dovi Frances表示，在一份声明中。 “我们预计自由职业者经济将继续强劲地长出大流行的尾风。 Lili通过持续提供这种人口基本，有效和直观的工具来管理以来，以管理以前是一个非常复杂和低效的企业的途径来看，看到指数增长。“

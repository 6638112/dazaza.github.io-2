特朗普政府与科学开战，美国病例激增至新高峰

2020-10-20 22:21:57

The US is climbing toward a third peak in the coronavirus pandemic as the Trump Administration continues to belittle public health experts and their advice.

随着特朗普政府继续贬低公共卫生专家及其建议，美国正向冠状病毒大流行的第三个高峰攀升。

The country’s seven-day average of new daily cases has increased about 33 percent from just two weeks ago, according to tracking by the COVID Tracking Project. On Friday, there were 68,000 new cases recorded, a high not seen since July. The current seven-day average is around 57,000 new cases per day.

根据COVID跟踪项目的跟踪，该国每天平均7天的新病例比仅仅两周前增加了约33%。周五，记录到的新病例为6.8万例，为7月份以来的最高水平。目前的七天平均每天约有57,000例新病例。

The surge is diffuse. Last week, 17 states set their own new records for cases in a single day. Those states include nine of 12 states in the Midwest and six of 11 in the west, according to the tracking project. Altogether, the US once again has the highest seven-day average of new cases of any country in the world, according to tracking by Johns Hopkins University researchers.

这股浪潮是弥漫的。上周，17个州在一天内创造了自己的病例新纪录。根据跟踪项目，这些州包括中西部12个州中的9个和西部11个州中的6个。根据约翰·霍普金斯大学(Johns Hopkins University)研究人员的跟踪，总的来说，美国再次成为世界上新病例最多的国家。

Hospitalizations are also on the rise in the US, leading experts to worry about a subsequent rise in the death toll. The latest projection from the oft-cited Institute for Health Metrics and Evaluation (IHME) at the University of Washington estimates that the cumulative US death toll from the pandemic could reach nearly 390,000 by February 1, 2021—if some restrictions are reimposed in hot spots. The projection notes that the toll may be as high as nearly 483,000 if restrictions are only eased from here or as low as around 315,000 if restrictions are reimposed and there is universal mask wearing.

美国的住院人数也在上升，这让专家们担心随后的死亡人数会上升。华盛顿大学(University Of Washington)经常被引用的健康指标与评估研究所(IHME)的最新预测估计，如果在热点地区重新实施一些限制，到2021年2月1日，美国因流感大流行的累计死亡人数可能会达到近39万人。预测指出，如果只从这里放松限制，死亡人数可能高达近48.3万人，如果重新实施限制，并戴着通用口罩，死亡人数可能低至31.5万人左右。

The current US death toll from the pandemic is just over 220,000, by far the largest in the world, according to Johns Hopkins. Per capita, only three countries have higher death rates than the US: Brazil, Spain, and Mexico.

根据约翰·霍普金斯大学的数据，目前美国死于流感的人数刚刚超过22万人，是迄今为止世界上死亡人数最多的国家。按人均计算，只有三个国家的死亡率高于美国：巴西、西班牙和墨西哥。

While the picture of the US is grim and looks to be getting grimmer, experts at the World Health Organization on Monday continued to offer a glimmer of hope. “There is no inevitable second wave or next peak. It's within our control,” Maria Van Kerkhove, WHO technical lead on COVID-19, said in the press conference from Geneva. She and the other WHO experts highlighted—again—the tried-and-true mitigation efforts that can break chains of transmission during this pandemic: physical distancing, hand hygiene, avoiding crowds, mask wearing, case finding, isolating the infected, tracing contacts, testing, and quarantining exposed.

虽然美国的形势严峻，而且看起来越来越严峻，但世界卫生组织(World Health Organization)的专家周一继续提供了一线希望。“没有不可避免的第二波或下一波高峰。这在我们的控制之内，“世卫组织新冠肺炎技术负责人Maria Van Kerkhove在日内瓦的新闻发布会上说。她和世卫组织的其他专家再次强调了能够在这场大流行期间打破传播链的久经考验的缓解努力：物理距离、手卫生、避开人群、戴口罩、发现病例、隔离感染者、追踪接触者、检测和隔离暴露的人。

“It's within our hands as individuals but also with strong government leadership, clear national plans implemented with targeted, controlled measures at local levels we can prevent additional subsequent waves of this happening,” she emphasized.

她强调：“这是在我们个人手中，也是在强有力的政府领导下，明确的国家计划，在地方层面实施有针对性的、有控制的措施，我们可以防止随后发生更多这样的事情。”

Job descriptions should show a salary or salary range
职位描述应该显示薪水或薪水范围

2021-04-26 10:44:11

Startups are big fans of ‘competitive salaries’. Head to a tech jobs board and you’ll see what I mean.

初创公司是“竞争性薪水”的大粉丝。前往科技工作板，你会看到我的意思。

But all this competitiveness has to stop. Every single job description should show a salary or salary range — and there are no excuses.

但所有这些竞争力都必须停止。每一个职位描述都应显示薪水或薪水范围 - 并且没有借口。

When a company offers a ‘competitive salary’, the messy business of salary negotiation stays private — and is less likely to work in certain candidates’ favour.

当公司提供“有竞争力的工资”时，薪资谈判的凌乱业务私密 - 在某些候选人的青睐中不太可能工作。

After all, if you can nab a great hire for less than you were expecting to pay, all the better, right? Wrong. I bet that your company careers page says you care deeply about diversity, inclusion, transparency and openness — and a ‘competitive salary’ is not walking the walk.

毕竟，如果你能留下少量雇用，比你期望付钱，更好，对吧？错误的。我敢打赌，你的公司职业页面说你深深地关心多样性，包容，透明度和开放性 - 以及“竞争力的薪水”并不散步。

When you don’t publicise salary information you are perpetuating the substantial pay gap that exists for women, people of colour, LGBTQ+ and disabled people.

当您不宣传薪资信息时，您延续了女性，颜色人民，LGBTQ +和残疾人存在的实质性薪酬差距。

As a woman, society brought me up to be polite — not to ‘stir the pot’— and certainly not to talk about money. I’m unlikely to bring up the topic of salary with a potential boss. If I look too money-driven, I’ll likely be seen as cold and ruthless where a man might be seen as ambitious and straight-shooting (if you don’t believe me, read the ‘Howard vs Heidi’ study).

作为一个女人，社会给我带来了礼貌 - 不要“搅拌锅 - 肯定不会谈论金钱。我不太可能与潜在的老板提出薪水的主题。如果我看起来过于赚钱，我可能会被视为寒冷和无情的地方，在那里一个男人可能被视为雄心勃勃和直接拍摄（如果你不相信我，请阅读'霍华德vs heidi'的学习）。

When the conversation comes round to money, I might be asked, ‘What do you earn now?’ and it’s likely I’ll then be offered a salary in the same ballpark. The pay gap I was already experiencing will continue. When companies were banned from asking about salary history in the US, it led to an average increase of 8% in pay offered to women candidates and a 13% increase for Black candidates.

当谈话来到金钱时，我可能会被问到，“你现在赚到了什么？”，当时我可能会在同一个球场提供一个薪水。我已经遇到的薪酬差距将继续。当公司因询问美国的薪资历史时，它导致妇女候选人的薪酬平均增加了8％，黑客候选人增加了13％。

If not asked about my previous salary, I’ll be asked, ‘What are your salary expectations?’ I’m likely to underestimate my worth. A recent study shows that women expect to earn less than men, which directly leads to lower salary offers — these findings make sense given the confidence gap between men and women.

如果没有被问到我以前的薪水，我会被问到，“你的薪水期望是什么？”我可能会低估我的价值。最近的一项研究表明，女性期望赚低人民，这直接导致较低的工资优惠 - 鉴于男女之间的信心差距，这些调查结果是有道理的。

Determining my market value is made harder by the fact no job description tells me my expected salary. It’s a vicious cycle.

决定我的市场价值越来越难以求职，没有求职告诉我我的预期薪水。这是一个恶性循环。

There’s no excuse for not publishing a salary or salary range. If you don’t know exactly at what level you want to hire, publish two job descriptions with two salary bands. Not sure whether you want to hire a chief of staff or a more junior ops manager? Put up a job spec for both, with different salary bands on each.

没有出版薪水或工资范围没有借口。如果您不确定在您想要雇用的级别，请发布两个薪资乐队的两个职位描述。不确定您是否要聘请一名员工或更多的初级OPS经理？为两者施加任何薪水乐队。

“If you don’t know exactly at what level you want to hire, publish two job descriptions with two salary bands.”

“如果您不确定您想要雇用的级别，请发布两个薪水乐队的两个职位描述。”

If you’re worried about putting off potential candidates who are underpaid in their current jobs, acknowledge this in the job description and encourage them to apply anyway: ‘We’ve benchmarked this role and think a fair salary will be between £X and £Y, depending on experience. If you match our requirements but are paid far less than this, get in touch, we want to hear from you.’

如果您担心将潜在的候选人留在其当前就业机会中的潜在候选人，请在职位描述中承认这一点，并鼓励他们申请申请：“我们已经基准了这一角色并认为公平的薪水将在X和£之间介于££之间y，取决于经验。如果您符合我们的要求，但支付远低于此，请联系，我们希望收到您的来信。

If you’re worried about putting off people who will want more than the salary you’ve stated, then you’ve not stated the right salary. If you can afford more, why haven’t you published that? If you make exceptions for those confident enough to ask for more, you’re making exceptions for predominantly straight white men.

如果你担心让那些想要超过你所说的薪水的人，那么你没有说合适的薪水。如果你能买得起更多，你为什么不发布那个？如果您对这些充满自信的人进行了例外，可以询问更多，您可以为主要是直白男性制作异常。

If you’re worried that your current staff will find out they’re underpaid, stop underpaying them. Pay discrimination is illegal.

如果您担心您当前的员工会发现他们的薪水，请停止欠他们。薪酬歧视是非法的。

“If you’re worried that your current staff will find out they’re underpaid, stop underpaying them.”

“如果您担心您当前的员工会发现他们的薪水，请停止低估它们。”

If your company doesn’t publish salary information candidates will assume that you’re either trying to save money by underpaying candidates or that you don’t want your current staff to know they’re underpaid. Both are huge red flags.

如果您的公司不公布薪资信息，候选人将假设您要么尝试通过账面才能省钱，或者您不希望您当前的员工知道他们是未付的。两者都是巨大的红旗。

Not only is it the right thing to do, it also helps you attract more candidates. Research from charity sector campaign Show the Salary showed that on one job board recruiters got twice the number of applicants when they showed the salary. So what’s stopping you?

它不仅是正确的事情，它也有助于您吸引更多的候选人。慈善部门运动的研究表明，在一个职位委员会招聘人员展示薪酬时，招聘人员招聘人员招聘人员的薪酬。那是什么阻止你？

I don’t want to be celebrated. I don’t need to be empowered.

我不想被庆祝。我不需要赋权。

Instead, I want to live in a world where people don’t get paid less because of their gender, ethnicity, sexuality or disability.

相反，我想住在一个人的世界里，因为他们的性别，种族，性行为或残疾而言不会减少报酬。

承诺在所有职位描述中发布薪酬信息，让我们走向我们所有人都知道我们的市场价值的世界。在Salaryplegce.com上签署承诺或者在下一个多样性和包容小组之前至少教育自己，通过阅读薪酬并展示薪资网站。

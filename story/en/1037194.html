This system was invented by William Clifford and is more commonly known asClifford algebra. It's actually older than the vector algebra that we use today(due to Gibbs) and includes it as a subset. Over the years, various parts ofClifford algebra have been reinvented independently by many people who foundthey needed it, often not realizing that all those parts belonged in onesystem. This suggests that Clifford had the right idea, and that geometricalgebra, not the reduced version we use today, deserves to be the standard"vector algebra."

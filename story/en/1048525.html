WhatsApp to move ahead with privacy update despite backlash
尽管遭到强烈反对，WhatsApp仍将继续进行隐私更新

2021-02-19 21:50:05

Feb 18 (Reuters) - Facebook Inc’s WhatsApp said on Thursday it will go ahead with its controversial privacy policy update but will allow users to read it at “their own pace” and will also display a banner providing additional information.

2月18日（路透社）-Facebook Inc.的WhatsApp周四表示，它将继续进行有争议的隐私政策更新，但将允许用户以“他们自己的速度”阅读它，并且还将显示提供其他信息的标语。

The messaging platform laid out fresh terms in January, aimed at increasing business transactions on the platform.

该消息传递平台在1月份提出了新的条款，旨在增加该平台上的业务交易。

The policy update would allow owner Facebook and its subsidiaries collect user data, including their phone number and location, which sparked a global outcry and a rush of new users to competitors Telegram and Signal, among others.

该政策更新将允许所有者Facebook及其子公司收集用户数据，包括他们的电话号码和位置，这引发了全球的强烈抗议，并吸引了新用户涌向竞争对手Telegram和Signal等。

WhatsApp then moved to delay the new policy launch to May from February and sought to clarify the update was focused on allowing users to message with businesses and would not affect personal conversations, which will continue to have end-to-end encryption.

然后，WhatsApp决定将新政策的发布时间从2月推迟到5月，并试图澄清该更新的重点是允许用户向企业发送消息，并且不会影响个人对话，因为个人对话将继续采用端到端加密。

In its latest blog post, WhatsApp said it will start reminding users to review and accept updates to keep using the messaging platform.

WhatsApp在其最新博客中表示，它将开始提醒用户查看并接受更新，以继续使用消息传递平台。

“We’ve also included more information to try and address concerns we’re hearing,” it added. (Reporting by Shubham Kalia in Bengaluru; Editing by Krishna Chandra Eluri)

它补充说：“我们还提供了更多信息，以尝试解决我们所听到的问题。” （Shubham Kalia在班加罗尔报道； Krishna Chandra Eluri编辑）

A Star Is Born
一颗星星诞生了

2021-06-17 03:15:41

Sumerian pictographic writing includes a sign for “star” that looks like a modern asterisk. These early writings from five thousand years ago are the first known depiction of an asterisk; however, it seems unlikely that these pictograms are the forerunner of the symbol we use today. Palaeographers know that Aristarchus of Samothrace (220–143 ) used an asterisk symbol when editing Homer in the second century , because later scholars wrote about him doing so. Physical examples of Aristarchus’ asterisks have not survived, so we cannot know their physical shape, but as the word asterisk derives from the Greek asteriskos, meaning “little star,” an assumption has been made that they resembled a small star. Aristarchus used the symbols to mark places in Homer’s text that he was copying where he thought passages were from another source. By the third century Origen of Alexandria had adopted the asterisk when compiling the Hexapla—a Greek translation of the Jewish scriptures, the Septuagint. Origen used the asterisk to demarcate texts that he had added to the Septuagint from the original Hebrew. Both these early uses of the asterisk are as an editing tool, to notify the reader that the passage they are reading should be read with caution.

苏美尔的象形文字包括“明星”，看起来像一个现代化的星号标志。五千年前的这些早期的作品是第一个已知的星号描述;然而，这些象形图似乎不太可能是我们今天使用的符号的先驱。众科学家知道Samothrace（220-143）的Aristarchus在第二世纪编辑归人时使用星号符号，因为后来的学者写了关于他这样做的事。阿里斯塔克斯星号的物理例子还没有下来，所以我们无法知道其物理形状，但作为从希腊asteriskos字星号导出，意思是‘小星星’的假设已经提出，他们像一个小明星。 aristarchus使用符号来标记在本垒打的文本中的地点，他正在复制他认为段落来自另一个来源的地方。到了第三世纪亚历山大的奥利金编纂的犹太经文，的septuagint的hexapla - 希腊翻译时采取了星号。 Origen使用星号划定他从原始希伯来语添加到Septuagint的文本。星号的这些早期用途都是编辑工具，以通知读者应该谨慎地读取他们正在阅读的段落。

In the medieval period the asterisk continued to be employed in the copying of Bibles to flag up text from other sources. It also was increasingly used as a signe de renvoi (sign of return)—a graphic symbol which indicates where a correction or insertion should be made, with a corresponding mark in the margin with the correct text inserted. The asterisk is also found in medieval texts as a sign of omission. The use of the asterisk by scribes copying the Bible continued with the advent of the printing press; early printed Bibles, such as Robert Estienne’s 1532 Latin Bible, make use of an asterisk. Scribes did not always use the modern asterisk shape, some instead adopting a hooked cross with dots between each arm. However, when the asterisk was cut into type it was rendered as the five- or six-pointed star, and this is the form that has largely endured.

在中世纪时期，星号继续在复制圣经中的复制中，以从其他来源标记文本。它也越来越多地用作Signe de Renvoi（返回符号）-a图形符号，该符号指示应该在其中进行校正或插入的位置，在边缘中的相应标记插入正确的文本。星号也在中世纪文本中找到作为遗漏的标志。通过抄写抄写们用印刷机的出现来使用星号;早期印刷的圣经，如罗伯特·埃斯蒂安的1532拉丁圣经，利用星号。 Scribes并不总是使用现代星号形状，而是在每个臂之间采用钩状的十字架。然而，当将星号切入类型时，它被呈现为五个或六角星，这是主要待遇的形式。

The asterisk (often used interchangeably with the dagger or obelus) persisted as an editing mark but was also frequently used as a caveat, showing that the passage highlighted by the asterisk was served by a footnote or side note. By the eighteenth century the asterisk was being deployed as a sort of censorship, covering up letters to represent a d**n vulgar word without actually b****y spelling it out. But, as W. Somerset Maugham points out, this has become somewhat outmoded: “We have long passed the Victorian era when asterisks were followed after a certain interval by a baby.”

星号（通常与匕首或obelus互换使用）作为编辑标记，但也经常被用作警告，表明星号突出的通道由脚注或侧面笔记提供。在十八世纪，星号被部署为一种审查，覆盖了字母来代表一个d ** n粗俗的词而没有实际上b **** y拼写出来。但是，正如W.萨默塞特·穆帕姆所指出的那样，这已经变得有些过时了：“当一个婴儿一定的间隔后，我们长期以来一直通过了维多利亚时代的时代。”

A similar method, however, is still employed in comics, where it is known as grawlix, although the swear words are usually represented by a series of graphical glyphs, for example %@~#$!, rather than just asterisks. One of the problems with using asterisks to deaden the effect of a swear word is that it just draws attention to it (sometimes simply because one spends ages trying to work out which word the author is censoring).

然而，类似的方法仍然在漫画中使用，其中称为Grawlix，尽管发誓单词通常由一系列图形字形表示，例如％@〜＃$！，而不是星号。使用星号减少咒骂词的效果的问题之一是它只是引起了它的注意（有时只是因为一个人花在试图制定作者被审查的词）。

In modern printed books the asterisk is most likely to show up as a method to mark footnotes. In advertising and on packaging it is generally a caveat—an advert might state “Free Beer*” but when you follow the asterisk you discover in the terms and conditions that to claim the free beer you must sign over your firstborn. Online and on instant messaging, asterisks have become increasingly useful and now provide a series of services; for example, to show emphasis, in the way italics are used on the printed page. This use probably started on certain online forums where to make a word show up as bold it needed to be surrounded by asterisks, like *this*. This convention then crept online where, rather than using bold to show emphasis, the asterisks serve the purpose instead.

在现代印刷书中，星号最有可能显示为标记脚注的方法。在广告和包装上，它一般是一种警告 - 广告可能会陈述“免费啤酒*”，但当您遵循您发现免费啤酒的条款和条件中的星号时，您必须签署您的经常签名。在线和即时消息传递，星号已越来越有用，现在提供一系列服务;例如，要显示强调，以斜体在打印页面上使用的方式。这次使用可能在某些在线论坛上开始，在某些在线论坛上，在那里将一个单词显示为粗体，它需要被星号包围，如*这个*。此惯例然后悄悄地在线，而不是使用粗体来表现出强调，星号服务于此目的。

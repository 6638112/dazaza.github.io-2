育种者培育出最好的新苹果

2020-11-09 12:31:49

This year, Cornell University released three new apple varieties: the scab-resistant Cordera, the crisp and mild Pink Luster, and the complexly flavored Firecracker.

今年，康奈尔大学推出了三个新的苹果品种：抗赤霉病的Cordera，清脆温和的粉色光泽，以及口味复杂的鞭炮。

Collectively, they're a culmination of decades of work by apple breeders, who are out in orchards around the world, testing out apple trees by the thousands to create new varieties that are delicious, store for a long time, more disease-resistant, put less physical strain on growers, and less resource strain on the land where they grow.

总体而言，这些成果是苹果育种者数十年努力的结果，他们在世界各地的果园里对数以千计的苹果树进行试验，以培育出美味、长期储存、更具抗病能力、给种植者带来的身体压力较小、对种植土地的资源压力较小的新品种。

In Geneva, New York, Cornell's College of Agriculture and Life Sciences AgriTech tests around 50 acres of apple trees under the purview of Dr. Susan Brown, a Cornell University professor and the head of Cornell’s storied apple breeding program that began in 1880.

在纽约日内瓦，康奈尔大学农业与生命科学学院在苏珊·布朗(Susan Brown)博士的指导下，对大约50英亩的苹果树进行了测试。布朗博士是康奈尔大学教授，也是康奈尔著名的苹果育种项目的负责人，该项目始于1880年。

“You plant out apples and it's like they scream, they just want to be diverse,” she said.

她说：“你种出苹果，就好像他们在尖叫，他们只是想要多样化。”

Apples have a propensity to jumble up their own genes, Brown said. If you take a McIntosh apple and plant the seeds inside of it, the resulting trees won't produce McIntosh apples.

布朗说，苹果有把自己的基因搞混的倾向。如果你把麦金托什苹果种在里面，结果树不会结出麦金托什苹果。

Creating new apples in a controlled way, which involves cross-breeding two kinds of apples to produce hybrid seeds, is a long process with a low chance of success. For a little perspective, the SnapDragon apple that came out of Cornell in 2013 was the program’s fastest release.

以可控的方式创造新苹果，包括杂交两种苹果以产生杂交种子，是一个漫长的过程，成功率很低。简单来说，2013年康奈尔大学(Cornell)推出的骁龙苹果(Snapgon Apple)是该程序发布速度最快的一款。

“You're gonna laugh,” Brown said. “The SnapDragon was 11 years from making the cross to commercialization.”

布朗说：“你会笑的。”“从制造十字架到商业化，骁龙花了11年时间。”

From Geneva, if you drive west on Interstate 90 for 2,500 miles you'll reach Wenatchee, Washington. Dr. Kate Evans, a Washington State University professor and breeder in the WSU Tree Fruit Research and Extension Center, said she plants about 15,000 to 20,000 unique cross-bred seeds here a year.

从日内瓦出发，如果你在90号州际公路上向西行驶2500英里，你就会到达华盛顿州的韦纳奇。华盛顿州立大学(Washington State University)教授、华盛顿州立大学林果研究和推广中心(WSU Tree Fruit Research And Expansion Center)的育种员凯特·埃文斯(Kate Evans)博士说，她每年在这里种植大约1.5万到2万颗独特的杂交种子。

Washington’s program began in 1994 and had its first wide release, the Cosmic Crisp, in 2017. Evans said that part of the impetus for the program was to create an apple suited to the town known as the Apple Capital of the World and its surrounding arid region.

华盛顿的计划始于1994年，并于2017年首次广泛发行，名为宇宙脆(Cosic Crisp)。埃文斯说，该项目的部分推动力是为了创造一种适合这个被称为世界苹果之都的小镇及其周边干旱地区的苹果。

“Every apple that was grown here, even though we are the major U.S. producer, was bred and selected for other environments where there were other challenges,” she said. “Here we are in this pretty unique climate for growing apples and nothing had been selected for this particular unique climate.”

她说：“这里种植的每一个苹果，尽管我们是美国的主要生产商，都是在其他有其他挑战的环境中培育和选择的。”“在这里，我们处在一个非常适合种植苹果的独特气候中，没有任何东西被选在这个特殊的气候中。”

Breeding apples takes a lot of time and energy, but for breeders, growers, and apple eaters alike, developing new varieties is a crucial part of the continued cultivation of the United States’ most consumed fruit.

培育苹果需要花费大量的时间和精力，但对于育种者、种植者和吃苹果的人来说，开发新品种是继续种植美国消费最多的水果的关键部分。

On an early October day at The Orchards of Concklin in Pomona, New York, farmer Scott Hill rifled through a crate of RubyFrosts and a crate of SnapDragons, both released by Cornell in 2013. The RubyFrosts were plump and deeply red. The SnapDragons were a little taller with a lighter complexion and more green and yellow splotches. Under that skin lies some of the tastiest apple you can have the pleasure of biting into. They're Hill’s favorite.

10月初的一天，在纽约州波莫纳的康克林果园，农民斯科特·希尔(Scott Hill)在康奈尔大学(Cornell)2013年发布的一箱红宝石霜(RubyFrost)和一箱金鱼龙(Snapdragons)之间来回穿梭。红宝石霜是丰满的和深红色的。金鱼龙个子稍高，肤色较浅，绿色和黄色斑点较多。在那层皮下，隐藏着一些你可以享受到的美味苹果。它们是希尔的最爱。

About 30 yards away grow rows of Macoun trees. They’re notably taller than the dwarf trees growing the latest apples and many older varieties like Honeycrisp and Gala that make up about two-thirds of the entire orchard. Macouns were released out of Cornell’s breeding program in 1923. While tasty, they have a short season (just three weeks) compared to other, newer varieties.

大约30码外长着一排排的麦昆树。它们明显比生长最新苹果和许多较老品种(如蜜脆和嘎拉)的矮树高，这些品种约占整个果园的三分之二。1923年，Macouns从康奈尔大学的繁育计划中被放生。虽然味道鲜美，但与其他较新的品种相比，它们的季节较短(只有三周)。

“In 20 years, you're not going to be able to find Macouns,” Hill said, noting the short window of profitability. “It's simple math at the end of the day.”

希尔说：“20年后，你再也找不到Macouns了。”他指出，盈利的窗口期很短。“归根结底，这只是一个简单的数学问题。”

Mark Lagoner, a farmer who grows apples in Williamson, New York, has two of Cornell's three newest varieties: Pink Luster and Firecracker. At Lagoner Farms they have about 150 trees of each apple, he said. They pick and press Firecrackers themselves to make hard cider while the Pink Lusters are popular among U-Pick visitors.

在纽约州威廉姆森种植苹果的农民马克·拉格纳(Mark Lager)拥有康奈尔大学三个最新品种中的两个：粉色光泽苹果和爆竹苹果。他说，在拉格纳农场，每个苹果大约有150棵树。他们自己挑选和压制鞭炮来酿造烈性苹果酒，而粉色光泽在U-Pick游客中很受欢迎。

“The crunchiness, the flavor, the hue; it's a nice size apple,” he said. “It has the Honeycrisp characteristics. Everybody likes that. Pink Luster has a lot of that.”

他说：“苹果的脆度、味道和色调都很不错。”“它有蜂蜜般酥脆的特性。每个人都喜欢这样。粉色光泽(Pink Lster)就有很多这方面的特点。“

The Honeycrisp, the third most produced apple in the U.S. according to the U.S. Apple Association's 2019 report, is another product of breeding. It was released out of the University of Minnesota’s breeding program in 1991 and slowly grew in popularity. The Pink Luster is a cross between a Honeycrisp and a Gala.

根据美国苹果协会2019年的报告，蜜脆是美国产量第三大的苹果，也是另一种育种产品。1991年，它从明尼苏达大学的育种项目中被释放出来，并慢慢地变得流行起来。粉色光泽是蜜糖和晚会的结合体。

To begin breeding a new apple like the Pink Luster, breeders start by looking at different apples’ traits they want to combine to make a crossbreed that contains the best attributes of two parent apples, sometimes looking at DNA markers to target certain desirable genes. Kate Evans explained that with a hypothetical.

为了开始培育像粉色光泽这样的新苹果，育种者首先要观察不同苹果的性状，他们想要把它们结合在一起，形成一个包含两个亲本苹果最好属性的杂交品种，有时还会寻找DNA标记，以确定特定的理想基因。凯特·埃文斯用一个假设解释了这一点。

“We want ultra crispness, so let's use Honeycrisp and let's use something else,” she said. “Maybe we want to increase its firmness so we might use Cripps Pink.”

她说：“我们想要超级酥脆，所以让我们用蜂蜜脆，让我们用其他东西。”“也许我们想增加它的坚固性，这样我们就可以使用Cripps Pink了。”

Taste, texture, and color are important traits for breeders as are more hidden characteristics, like disease resistance or the ability to hold up in storage for months.

味道、质地和颜色是饲养者的重要特征，还有更隐蔽的特征，比如抗病能力或保存数月的能力。

To create a cross-breed between two parent apples, breeders take a cue from pollinators like bees and butterflies. Pollinators use flowers as a food source, and in a symbiotic exchange their bodies collect pollen from the blooms. When they bop over to another flower, they drop the pollen off from the previous flower. Flowers have little parts at the center of their blooms called stigmas that collect the deposited pollen and use it to fertilize their reproductive egg cells. Once fertilized, those egg cells become seeds. All flowering plants reproduce by sharing pollen, and apple trees are flowering plants.

为了在两个亲本苹果之间创造杂交品种，育种者从蜜蜂和蝴蝶等传粉者那里得到启示。传粉者以花为食物来源，在共生交换中，它们的身体从花朵中收集花粉。当它们跳到另一朵花上时，它们会从前一朵花上取下花粉。花在花的中心有一个叫做柱头的小部分，它收集沉积的花粉，并用它来使生殖卵细胞受精。一旦受精，这些卵细胞就会变成种子。所有开花植物都通过共享花粉繁殖，苹果树就是开花植物。

To more directly control that process, breeders like Brown and Evans cross-pollinate apple trees by hand. If they have a Honeycrisp parent and a Cripps Pink parent, they'll taken the pollen from the Honeycrisp tree's flower and place it on the stigma of the Cripps Pink tree's flower. On that Cripps Pink tree, a Cripps Pink apple will grow, but inside of that Cripps Pink apple will be cross-bred seeds.

为了更直接地控制这一过程，像布朗和埃文斯这样的育种者用手给苹果树异花授粉。如果他们的父母分别是蜜脆和粉红，他们就会从蜜脆树的花朵中提取花粉，并将花粉放在粉红蜜糖树的花柱上。(译者注：蜜糖树的花朵是粉色的，如果他们的父母是蜜脆的父母，那么他们就会从蜜脆树的花朵中提取花粉，并将花粉放在粉色蜜糖树的花柱上。)。在那棵克里普斯粉色苹果树上，克里普斯粉色苹果树上会长出一颗克里普斯粉色苹果树，但在那棵克里普斯粉色苹果树里面会长出杂交种子。

“We're doing what bees do but in a more controlled way,” Evans said. Breeders aren’t splicing together apples in labs. They’re out in the fields doing it the same way people have been doing it for hundreds of years.

埃文斯说：“我们正在做蜜蜂做的事情，但方式更有节制。”饲养员不会在实验室里拼接苹果。他们在田野里做这件事，就像几百年来人们一直做的那样。

“Every one of those seeds has our Honeycrisp and Cripps Pink parents but every single one of those seeds is different because it's got a randomized assortment of the genes within it, like you and your siblings,” Evans said.

埃文斯说：“每一颗种子都有我们的蜜脆和粉红的父母，但每一颗种子都是不同的，因为它的基因是随机组合的，就像你和你的兄弟姐妹一样。”

Those cross-bred seeds are planted and grown into seedlings. To quicken the growing process and reduce land usage, parts of those tiny cross-bred seedlings' shoots called scions are cut off and grafted onto rootstock.

这些杂交种子被种植并长成幼苗。为了加快生长过程，减少土地使用，这些被称为接穗的微小杂交幼苗的一部分被剪下并嫁接到砧木上。

Rootstocks make up the bottom root systems and trunks of apple trees and are bred independently from scions, Gennaro Fazio explained. Fazio is a plant research geneticist at the USDA Agricultural Research Service’s Plant Genetic Resources Unit in Geneva, New York. He’s been breeding rootstocks there since 2001.

Gennaro Fazio解释说，砧木构成苹果树的底部根系和树干，是独立于接穗培育的。法齐奥是美国农业部农业研究服务处位于纽约日内瓦的植物遗传资源部的植物研究遗传学家。自2001年以来，他一直在那里培育砧木。

Just like the cross-bred apple seedlings, rootstocks are themselves apple trees, but they aren't bred to produce tasty fruit. They're bred with focuses on disease resistance, shorter height, and longevity.

就像杂交的苹果苗一样，砧木本身也是苹果树，但它们并不是为了结出美味的果实而培育的。他们的繁殖注重抗病、矮小和长寿。

Like the process to produce scions, rootstocks are cross-pollinated to get these desired traits, but Fazio doesn't do it by hand.

就像生产接穗的过程一样，砧木是通过异花授粉来获得这些想要的特征的，但法齐奥不是手工完成的。

“We do it the cheap and simple way,” Fazio said. “We just take two of the rootstocks that we want to be parents, we cover those plants up with a big tent that is bee proof, and we put bee hives inside, and we let bees do the crossing.”

法齐奥说：“我们的做法既便宜又简单。”“我们只需要取两个我们想做父母的砧木，用一个大帐篷盖住这些植物，这样就不会有蜜蜂了。我们把蜂箱放在里面，然后让蜜蜂进行穿越。”

Using good rootstock can speed up the growth process for apples. In some cases, it cuts the waiting time for a tree to grow and start fruiting from 10 years or longer down to just two or three, although apple tree timelines can vary by years. Rootstocks are already much further along in development than the scions, and grafting a scion onto them allows scions to focus on growing (hopefully) tasty apples rather than worrying about growing big.

使用好的砧木可以加快苹果的生长进程。在某些情况下，它将等待一棵树生长并开始结果的时间从10年或更长时间缩短到只有两到三年，尽管苹果树的时间表可能会因年而异。砧木已经比接穗发育得更远了，嫁接到砧木上可以让接穗专注于种植(希望)美味的苹果，而不是担心长得更大。

Out of the thousands of rootstocks Fazio works with, many are killed early on either because they just don’t grow well or they don’t stand up to the diseases with which they’re inoculated. If a rootstock isn’t at the top of its game, it won’t make the cut.

在法齐奥工作的数千个砧木中，许多人很早就被杀死了，要么是因为它们生长不好，要么是因为它们不能抵抗接种的疾病。如果一个砧木不在游戏的顶端，它就不会入围。

“The way we look at it is like it's a conveyor belt of material,” Evans said. “Every year new stuff gets added in at the beginning (new seedling material) but also every year stuff falls off the belt because it has a fatal flaw, there's something wrong with it… What you're left with at the end is maybe one or two elite selections and you had thousands at the beginning.”

埃文斯说：“我们认为它就像是一条材料传送带。”“每年开始都会有新的材料加入(新的苗木材料)，但每年也会有新的材料从腰带上掉下来，因为它有一个致命的缺陷，那就是它有问题。”…。最后，你可能只剩下一两个精英选拔，而一开始你就有数千个。“

There are countless reasons why an apple won’t make the cut. Brown listed off a few potential problems, including a calyx (the bottom part) that’s too open and could allow disease in, or disorders in the lenticels (holes in the apple’s porous skin) that similarly open them up to diseases and can cause the skin to russet (turn brown and harden), something that's increasingly becoming a problem with climate-change-induced stress.

苹果无法入围的原因不胜枚举。布朗列举了几个潜在的问题，包括花萼(底部)太开放，可能会让疾病进入，或者皮孔(苹果多孔的皮肤上的孔洞)的失调，同样会使苹果容易生病，并可能导致皮肤黄褐变(变黄变硬)，这正日益成为气候变化引发的压力的问题。

Sometimes apples are culled because they taste too similarly to their parents. Sometimes they just taste bad.

有时，苹果会被剔除，因为它们的味道与它们的父母太相似了。有时候它们就是味道不好。

Brown along with her colleagues, students, and whoever else is down for some apple roulette will bite into and taste thousands of apples as they walk through the orchard, taking notes along the way.

布朗和她的同事、学生以及任何想吃苹果轮盘赌的人都会在穿过果园时咬着、品尝数以千计的苹果，并在沿途做笔记。

“You might taste something that has a hint of cherry, or anise/fennel, or you might get green pepper, or diesel, and you can't tell by looking at it,” Brown said. “Even with DNA markers you can't always pick those out.”

布朗说：“你可能尝到了樱桃、茴香或茴香的味道，或者你可能尝到了青椒或柴油，但你看上去是分不出来的。”“即使有DNA标记，你也不能总是把它们挑出来。”

The best ones are picked for their next test: Storage. Without a long, stable storage life that ideally can stretch from four to nine months, potential apples get the axe.

最好的会被挑选出来进行下一次测试：存储。如果没有长时间的、稳定的储存期(理想情况下可以延长到4到9个月)，潜在的苹果就会被砍掉。

There are other pieces of the apple puzzle to consider too, like how apples and rootstocks grow consistently in different climates or are affected by outside factors. Insects and diseases like apple scab or fire blight can devastate crops, which can financially hurt growers and lead to food security issues. To fight these problems, some growers protect their crops by spraying pesticides. Others, like Fazio, hope to create apple breeds that allow growers to use less pesticides and fertilizers.

苹果之谜的其他部分也需要考虑，比如苹果和砧木如何在不同的气候条件下持续生长，或者受到外界因素的影响。昆虫和疾病，如苹果赤霉病或火疫病，可能会摧毁农作物，这可能会给种植者造成经济上的伤害，并导致粮食安全问题。为了解决这些问题，一些种植者通过喷洒杀虫剂来保护他们的作物。其他人，如法齐奥，希望创造出允许种植者使用更少杀虫剂和化肥的苹果品种。

“Apples are some of the most sprayed fruit in the world, just because we like nice clean apples,” Fazio said. “We as a conglomeration of scientists working in apple breeding and many others, we're not just trying to create new, better tasting, more productive apple trees, we're also trying to make apple growing a lot more sustainable."

法齐奥说：“苹果是世界上喷洒最多的水果之一，就因为我们喜欢干净可口的苹果。”“作为一个致力于苹果育种和其他许多领域的科学家群体，我们不仅在努力创造新的、味道更好、产量更高的苹果树，我们还在努力让苹果的生长更加可持续。”

The Orchards of Concklin's Hill said some of the disease-resistant trees do well when separated from other varieties, but when close to other, more susceptible apples, nature finds a way.

康克林山庄的果园表示，一些抗病树种在与其他品种分开时表现良好，但当靠近其他更易受感染的苹果时，自然会找到办法。

“My experience in the field is that fungus, insects, they will overcome whatever it is you're out there doing,” he said.

他说：“我在这一领域的经验是，真菌和昆虫会克服你在外面做的任何事情。”

It’s almost a constant battle of breeders versus nature. And with the changing climate, breeders need to be conscious of a variety of incoming factors that could affect apple production.

这几乎是饲养者与大自然之间持续不断的战斗。随着气候的变化，育种者需要意识到各种可能影响苹果产量的即将到来的因素。

“Climate change is going to be a huge challenge,” Brown said. “That's not something we can breed for immediately but we need to be cognizant of it: the changes, the new invasive species that we're seeing, the droughts, precipitation patterns, the effect of these environments on quality attributes.”

布朗说：“气候变化将是一个巨大的挑战。”“这不是我们可以立即培育的东西，但我们需要意识到：变化，我们正在看到的新的入侵物种，干旱，降水模式，以及这些环境对质量属性的影响。”

There’s no one-size-fits-all solution here. As the world changes and tastes change, breeders will keep breeding to get the best apples they can.

这里没有万能的解决方案。随着世界的变化和口味的变化，育种者将继续育种，以获得他们所能获得的最好的苹果。

The idea of breeding some kind of perfect supertree of apples is a pipe dream, Hill said.

希尔说，培育某种完美的苹果树的想法是白日做梦。

“I don't think that works in nature," he said. "Even if something's perfect, it doesn't stay that way for long.”

他说：“我认为这在自然界是行不通的。”即使某件事是完美的，它也不会保持很长时间。

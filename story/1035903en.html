Experimental Antibody Treatment for Covid-19 Patients Wins 'Emergency Approval' in America
针对Covid-19患者的实验性抗体治疗在美国获得“紧急批准”

2020-11-22 21:14:21

The Food and Drug Administration on Saturday granted emergency authorization to the experimental antibody treatment given to President Trump last month when he developed covid-19, the disease caused by the novel coronavirus.

美国食品和药物管理局周六批准了对特朗普总统上个月给予他的实验性抗体治疗的紧急授权，当时他开发了由新型冠状病毒引起的疾病covid-19。

The drug, made by Regeneron Pharmaceuticals, is designed to prevent infected people from developing severe illness. Instead of waiting for the body to develop its own protective immune response, the drug imitates the body’s natural defenses. It is the second drug of this type — called a monoclonal antibody — to be cleared for treating covid-19. The FDA authorized Eli Lilly & Co.’s drug on Nov. 9.

该药由Regeneron Pharmaceuticals制造，旨在防止感染者患上严重疾病。该药物模仿人体的天然防御系统，而不是等待人体发展自己的保护性免疫反应。它是第二种被称为单克隆抗体的药物，已被批准用于治疗covid-19。 FDA在11月9日授权礼来公司（Eli Lilly＆Co.）

Regeneron’s drug is a cocktail of two monoclonal antibodies, called casirivimab and imdevimab. The FDA said in authorizing the cocktail that it may be effective in treating mild to moderate covid-19 in adults and children 12 or older, and is indicated for those at high risk of developing severe illness. Doctors hope the drugs will keep those patients from being hospitalized.

Regeneron的药物是两种单克隆抗体的混合物，分别称为casirivimab和imdevimab。 FDA在授权该鸡尾酒时说，它可能有效治疗成人和12岁或12岁以上儿童的轻度至中度covid-19，并适用于罹患严重疾病的高风险人群。医生希望这些药物能使这些患者免于住院。

But as with the Lilly treatment, the Regeneron drug is a biological product that is complicated and time-consuming to make; initially, it will be in short supply. The shortages, coupled with the complexities of administering the intravenous medication, have raised concerns about whether people with the greatest need will be able to get it.

但是，与礼来公司的疗法一样，Regeneron药物是一种生物产品，制造复杂且耗时。最初，它将供不应求。短缺，再加上静脉注射药物的复杂性，引起了人们对最需要的人是否能够获得这种药物的担忧。

Regeneron executives said on the company’s earnings call in early November that they project having enough doses for 80,000 patients by the end of November, and 300,000 total doses by the end of January.

Regeneron高管在11月初的公司电话会议上表示，他们预计到11月底将有足够的剂量用于80,000名患者，到1月底将有300,000剂总剂量。

Trump received an infusion of Regeneron’s investigational drug Oct. 2 after he tested positive for the novel coronavirus and began showing symptoms of covid-19. He received the drug through a compassionate-use program that allows people to get unapproved drugs.

特朗普于10月2日接受了Regeneron的研究药物输注，此前他对新型冠状病毒测试呈阳性，并开始表现出covid-19症状。他通过富有同情心的使用计划接收毒品，该计划允许人们获取未经批准的毒品。

In tweets and videos after he was released from the hospital, Trump inaccurately described the Regeneron drug as a cure and pressed the FDA to quickly clear the medication. It was several weeks, however, before the agency took action.

特朗普在出院后的推文和视频中，不准确地将再生元药物描述为一种治疗方法，并敦促FDA迅速清除该药物。然而，过了几周，该机构才采取行动。

Housing and Urban Development Secretary Ben Carson disclosed on Facebook on Friday that he had also been cleared to receive the Regeneron drug for covid-19, through Trump’s intervention, “which I am convinced saved my life.” Chris Christie, the former Republican governor of New Jersey, received access to the Lilly drug during his bout with the disease.

住房和城市发展部长本·卡森（Ben Carson）周五在Facebook上透露，通过特朗普的干预，他也被批准接受cogene-19的再生元药物，“我坚信这可以挽救我的生命。”新泽西州前共和党州长克里斯·克里斯蒂（Chris Christie）在患上这种病的回合期间接受了礼来（Lilly）毒品的治疗。

Monoclonal antibodies, which are concocted in laboratories, are proteins that mimic the immune system’s ability to attack the virus. Regeneron’s covid-19 drug is manufactured in cells from genetically engineered hamsters.

实验室配制的单克隆抗体是模仿免疫系统攻击病毒能力的蛋白质。 Regeneron的covid-19药物是使用转基因仓鼠在细胞中生产的。

In a clinical trial, the Regeneron drug reduced hospitalizations or emergency room visits when given to people at high risk of developing severe disease. It was also shown to reduce the amount of virus in people’s bodies. The safety and effectiveness of the drug will continue to be studied. It is not authorized for use in hospitalized patients.

在一项临床试验中，Regeneron药物用于高危人群，可以减少住院或急诊就诊的机会。还显示它可以减少人体内的病毒数量。该药物的安全性和有效性将继续进行研究。未经许可，不得用于住院患者。

In a study published Oct. 28 in the New England Journal of Medicine, researchers said the Lilly cocktail lowered the risk of follow-up medical visits and reduced levels of virus in people with mild to moderate symptoms of covid-19.

在10月28日发表于《新英格兰医学杂志》上的一项研究中，研究人员说，礼来鸡尾酒降低了进行covid-19症状的患者的后续医疗就诊的风险，并降低了病毒水平。

The progress on monoclonal antibodies comes as pharmaceutical and biotech companies are racing to produce coronavirus vaccines. Pfizer and Moderna might get emergency clearance for their vaccines by year’s end; Pfizer applied for FDA authorization Friday. But it will take months to vaccinate the population of the United States, much less the world, and the shots may not work for everyone. The antibody treatments can play an important role in making the disease less dangerous.

随着制药和生物技术公司竞相生产冠状病毒疫苗，单克隆抗体取得了进展。辉瑞和Moderna可能会在年底前获得疫苗的紧急检查；辉瑞制药周五申请了FDA授权。但是，要给美国人口（而不是全世界）接种疫苗需要几个月的时间，而且注射疫苗可能并不适合所有人。抗体治疗可在降低疾病危险性方面发挥重要作用。

Still, if the pandemic keeps raging, the treatments will be in short supply. Unlike conventional pills, these drugs are synthesized by living organisms in specialized reactors, at a biological pace that can’t be rushed. A worldwide scramble to find capacity to make the drugs is underway, with companies striking deals with competitors to increase their manufacturing capabilities.

但是，如果大流行继续蔓延，治疗将供不应求。与传统药丸不同，这些药物是由活有机体在专门的反应堆中合成的，其生物学步伐是无法匆忙的。为了寻找生产这种药物的能力，全球范围内的争夺正在进行中，各公司与竞争对手达成协议以提高其制造能力。

Through Operation Warp Speed, the Trump administration effort to speed up treatments and vaccines, the government has bought 300,000 doses each from Lilly and Regeneron at a cost of about $1,250 and $1,500 per dose, respectively, with an option to buy hundreds of thousands more. But that comes as the nation is approaching 200,000 new coronavirus cases each day.

通过特朗普政府为加快治疗速度和疫苗研发工作而采取的``扭曲速度行动''（Warp Warp Speed），政府已分别从礼来公司和再生元分别购买了300,000剂剂量，每剂价格分别约为1,250美元和1,500美元，并且可以选择购买数十万种。但这是随着美国每天接近20万例新的冠状病毒病例而来的。

In a sign of how quickly the supply could be used up as the pandemic surges across the country, 40 percent of the doses of Lilly’s bamlanivimab has already been allocated to states.

礼来的迹象表明，随着全国大流行的爆发，供应将很快用尽，礼来40％的bamlanivimab剂量已分配给各州。

Because of the limited supply, the number of doses each state receives will be determined by the number of confirmed cases and hospitalizations in a given week, federal officials said.

联邦官员说，由于供应有限，每个州接受的剂量数量将由一周内确诊病例和住院人数决定。

In a recent briefing, Health and Human Services Secretary Alex Azar said the antibody treatments have “the potential to save thousands of lives and significantly reduce the disease’s burden on the health-care system.”

卫生和公共服务部长亚历克斯·阿扎尔（Alex Azar）在最近的简报中说，抗体疗法具有“挽救数千人生命并显着减轻疾病对医疗保健系统负担的潜力。”

Where the antibody drugs will be administered is not clear, particularly because covid-19 patients are contagious and can’t be given the drug at established infusion clinics alongside people with compromised immune systems.

抗体药物的给药地点尚不清楚，尤其是因为covid-19患者具有传染性，无法在既定的输液诊所与免疫系统受损的人一起服用该药物。

“You can’t send them to places where cancer patients are being treated,” said Mark McClellan, director of the Duke-Margolis Center for Health Policy and a former FDA commissioner. He said that many hospital systems have extra space they could dedicate to the antibody treatments, or could set up pop-up centers. Home-infusion providers such as CVS could administer the drug in nursing homes and people’s residences, he said.

杜克-玛格丽斯健康政策中心主任，前FDA专员Mark McClellan说：“您不能将他们送到癌症患者接受治疗的地方。”他说，许多医院系统都有额外的空间可用于抗体治疗，或者可以设置弹出中心。他说，像CVS这样的家庭输液服务提供商可以在疗养院和人们的住所中使用这种药物。

Because of the expected shortages, doctors have expressed concerns that the medicines won’t find their way to patients who need them most, especially in disadvantaged communities.

由于预计会出现短缺，医生表示担心药物无法找到最需要的患者，特别是在处境不利的社区。

Trump administration officials at the recent coronavirus task force briefing promised to distribute the treatment fairly and swiftly, basing distribution on a strategy similar to what is used for remdesivir, an antiviral drug for hospitalized patients. The administration’s rollout of remdesivir was plagued by problems in its early phases, including sending doses to hospitals with no eligible patients, but officials said they had learned from those mistakes.

特朗普政府官员在最近的冠状病毒工作队简报中承诺，将根据与用于住院患者的抗病毒药物瑞地昔韦相似的策略，公平，迅速地分配治疗。当局在推出remdesivir的初期阶段曾遇到问题，其中包括向没有合格患者的医院给药，但官员们说，他们已经从这些错误中学到了东西。

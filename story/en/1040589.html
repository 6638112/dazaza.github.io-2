NOAA expects La Niña weather patterns through March
NOAA预计三月份的拉尼娜天气模式

2020-12-19 02:41:49

NOAA released its monthly weather update Thursday, looking back at the fall and ahead through the rest of winter. As we close in on the (good riddance) end of 2020, its global temperature status is coming into focus. It’s looking like a bit of a coin flip between the year being the warmest or second warmest on record, depending on how you estimate the odds.

NOAA周四发布了每月天气更新，回顾了秋天以及整个冬季的剩余时间。当我们接近2020年底（良好的淘汰）时，其全球温度状况便成为焦点。看起来是有记录以来最热的年份或第二个最热年份之间的硬币翻转，具体取决于您估算赔率的方式。

Globally, November was the second warmest on record, while the autumn period of September through November was the third warmest. The fact that this is true despite moderate La Niña conditions in the Pacific is notable, as those conditions bring cold, deep water up to the surface, which normally drags down the global average temperature.

在全球范围内，11月是有记录以来第二高的季节，而从9月到11月的秋季是第三高的季节。尽管太平洋的拉尼娜条件温和，但事实仍然如此，因为这些条件将冷的深水带到地表，这通常会拖累全球平均温度。

At this point, 2020’s only competition for the warmest year on record is 2016, which was boosted by a strong El Niño. (That means more of the equatorial Pacific was covered by warm surface water.) The two years are so close that some datasets may even rank them in different order than others. NASA’s Gavin Schmidt, for example, estimates over 90 percent odds of setting a new record, but NOAA’s latest estimate is about 55 percent.

在这一点上，2020年是有记录以来最热的一年的唯一比赛是2016年，这是强劲的厄尔尼诺现象推动的。 （这意味着赤道太平洋的更多区域被温暖的地表水覆盖。）两年来是如此接近，以至于某些数据集甚至可能以与其他数据集不同的顺序对其进行排名。例如，美国宇航局（NASA）的加文·施密特（Gavin Schmidt）估计创下新纪录的几率超过90％，但美国国家航空航天局（NOAA）的最新估计约为55％。

For the contiguous US, November was pretty consistently warmer than normal and dry. Specifically, it was the fourth warmest November on record and the 33rd driest. For the autumn season as a whole, it was the 11th warmest and only a little below average for precipitation—though that obscures a big east-west divide in rainfall.

对于临近的美国，11月一直比正常干燥的天气温暖。具体来说，这是有记录以来的第四最温暖的十一月和最干旱的第三十三。在整个秋季，它是全球第11个最暖的季节，降水量仅略低于平均水平，尽管掩盖了东西向的巨大降雨差异。

November’s weather was dominated by high pressure ridges across the US, which is why the weather pattern was so consistent. The only exception was the second week of the month, when a low pressure trough formed in the West. That brought cooler temperatures and some precipitation, particularly farther north. At the same time, the influence of Hurricane Eta produced rain in Florida and up the mid-Atlantic.

11月的天气以全美国的高压脊为主，这就是为什么天气模式如此一致的原因。唯一的例外是当月的第二个星期，西方形成了低压槽。这带来了凉爽的温度和一些降水，尤其是在更北的地方。同时，飓风埃塔（Eta）的影响在佛罗里达州和大西洋中部地区产生降雨。

NOAA put a spotlight on Florida this month, where 2020 has so far been the warmest year on record. The state has been on a roll for about five years now, with 65 out of the last 68 months coming in above the long-term average—including the last 31 months straight. The last few years really stand out, as you can see in the chart below.

NOAA本月将重点放在佛罗里达州，到目前为止，2020年是有记录以来最热的一年。该州已经运转了大约五年，过去68个月中有65个月高于长期平均水平，包括最近31个月。如下表所示，过去几年确实很出色。

The trend in nighttime warming has been notably stronger than daytime warming, though that basic pattern is fairly common. As an example, this autumn saw 246 warm records set for daily high temperatures in Florida, but 687 records for warm daily lows. (There were just 19 new record cold daily lows.) Along with long-term warming driven by greenhouse gases, there are a few other factors that help explain this, Florida state climatologist David Zierden said. Combined with sea surface warming around Florida, humidity has trended upward, holding more heat in the air through the night.

尽管这种基本模式相当普遍，但夜间变暖的趋势明显比白天变暖。例如，今年秋天佛罗里达创下246项高温记录，创下每日最高气温，但创下687项记录，创下每日最低气温。佛罗里达州气候学家戴维·齐登（David Zierden）说（随着温室气体导致的长期变暖，只有19个新的每日最低纪录。）还有其他一些因素可以解释这一点。加上佛罗里达州附近的海面变暖，湿度呈上升趋势，整夜在空气中保持更多热量。

Zooming back around, the widespread warm and dry weather pushed drought to cover an additional four percent of the US last month, putting fully 49 percent of the country in that category. A primary area of expansion was the Southeast, where La Niña conditions tend to keep things particularly dry.

回过头来看，广泛的温暖和干燥的天气促使干旱在上个月覆盖了美国另外4％的土地，使该国的全部土地占49％。扩张的主要地区是东南部，拉尼娜地区的条件往往会使东西特别干燥。

Speaking of La Niña, it is still expected to remain through the winter, but the spring forecast is edging back toward neutral (neither La Niña nor El Niño) conditions. By summer, neutral conditions are now favored.

说到拉尼娜，它仍然有望在整个冬季保持不变，但是春季的预报正朝着中性（拉尼娜和厄尔尼诺都没有）的方向发展。到夏季，现在有利于中性条件。

Still, the outlook for the next few months is dominated by La Niña patterns. The January outlook generally tilts toward above-average temperatures, with the exception of the northwest US up through southeast Alaska. Due to the way the winter jet stream tends to set up during a La Niña, the southern tier of the US is likely to be dry, while much of the northern tier is likely to be wet. The Northern Plains region is an exception there, with equal chances of wetter or drier weather in the outlook.

尽管如此，未来几个月的前景仍将以拉尼娜模式为主。除了美国西北部至阿拉斯加东南部以外，一月的展望总体上倾向于高于平均水平的气温。由于冬季气流倾向于在拉尼娜期间形成，因此美国的南部地区可能是干旱的，而北部地区的大部分可能是潮湿的。北部平原地区是个例外，前景晴朗或干燥的机会均等。

The outlook for January through March is a stronger version of the same. Cooler temperatures may stretch into the Northern Plains from the Pacific Northwest, and so does the wetter outlook. The area of wetter weather pushes down from the Great Lakes into the Ohio Valley area, which is partly an expectation based on common storm tracks in the past.

从一月到三月的前景是相同的更强版本。较冷的温度可能会从太平洋西北部延伸到北部平原，湿润的前景也是如此。天气较为潮湿的地区从大湖区（Great Lakes）降落至俄亥俄河谷地区（Ohio Valley Valley），这在一定程度上是基于过去常见的暴风雨的预期。

All this means that drought is expected to grow across the Central Plains and Gulf region but continue to improve in the Pacific Northwest and in the Northeast.

所有这些意味着，预计中原和海湾地区的干旱将会加剧，但西北太平洋地区和东北地区将继续改善。

North of the border, the current Environment and Climate Change Canada two- to four-month outlook covers January to March. Above-average temperatures are favored in the far southeast, with below-normal temperatures most everywhere else. The precipitation outlook is patchy, with above-normal amounts favored from British Columbia into the Prairies, in the Great Lakes region, and parts of Eastern Canada.

在边境以北，目前的加拿大环境与气候变化展望期为1至3月，为期2至4个月。东南偏偏偏偏高于平均水平的温度，其他地方偏偏偏低温度。降水前景不规则，不列颠哥伦比亚省进入大草原地区，大湖地区和加拿大东部部分地区，降水量偏高。

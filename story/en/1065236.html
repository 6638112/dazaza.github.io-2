Sony Music Is Wiping Out the Old Debts of Catalog Artists
索尼音乐正在擦掉目录艺术家的旧债务

2021-06-12 07:45:57

Last June, protests broke out across the music industry as record executives questioned the business’ long history of racial inequity. Ron Sweeney, a veteran music attorney, offered one proposal that he believed could help labels begin to atone for their past injustices: “With respect to Black artists signed to you prior to 2000,” he wrote, “…zero out their unrecouped royalty balances and let their royalties flow to them so they can support themselves.”

去年6月，抗议活动突破了音乐行业，因为纪录高管质疑业务的种族不平等历史悠久。 Ron Sweeney是一位退伍军人音乐律师提供了一个提案，他认为可以帮助标签开始因为他们过去的不公正而被赎罪：“在2000年之前签署了你的黑人艺术家，”他写道，“......零分开的版税余额让他们的版税让他们流向他们，以便他们支持自己。“

Music Entertainment appeared to take a step in this direction for all its legacy artists on Thursday. In a letter sent to the company’s partners obtained by Rolling Stone, Sony said it “will no longer apply existing unrecouped balances to artists and participant earnings generated on or after January 1, 2021 for eligible artists and participants.”

音乐娱乐似乎在星期四朝这个方向迈出了这一点。索尼表示，在发给公司合作伙伴的一封信中，索尼表示，它“将不再将现有的Unsuped余额与符合条件的艺术家和参与者或参与者在1月1日或之后生成的艺术家和参与者盈利。”

While the word “unrecouped” may cause some eyes to glaze over, the term can be of crucial importance for artists. Traditionally, when an artist signs a deal with a record label, that company gives the artist an advance payment. The label may also promise other forms of financial support, from covering recording costs to marketing budgets to funding a radio campaign.

虽然“未推迟”这个词可能导致一些眼睛釉上，但该术语对于艺术家来说可能对艺术家至关重要。传统上，当艺术家签署与记录标签的交易时，该公司为艺术家提供了预付款。标签还可以承诺其他形式的财政支持，从筹集营销预算来为资助无线电运动提供营销预算。

However, artists will only receive royalties from their album after the release earns the label back its initial investment, or recoups. (Though in some cases, not all costs incurred by the label are recoupable, depending on an artist’s leverage when negotiating a deal.)

但是，艺术家只在发布返回其初始投资或收到的标签后获得专辑的特许权使用费。 （尽管在某些情况下，并非标签产生的所有费用都会被报复，具体取决于艺术家在谈判交易时的杠杆。）

Acts may be focused on maximizing the size of their advance payment — both because it’s immediate cash in hand, and also potentially because it gives them bragging rights in an industry where hype is currency — rather than their long-term earning potential. That’s where recoupment starts to cause headaches.

行为可以专注于最大化其预付款的规模 - 既是因为它是掌握的即时现金，也可能是因为它在炒作货币的行业中提供了吹牛的权利 - 而不是他们的长期收入潜力。这就是收回开始引起头痛的地方。

To recoup a record contract at a standard music industry royalty rate — in recent years, managers say that’s probably between 15 and 20 percent — an artist has to have a significant amount of commercial success. According to a Record Deal Simulator put together by the company CreateSafe, if a label signs an artist for a 20 percent royalty and doles out $1,000,000 in the modern streaming economy, the artist has to generate 1.3 billion streams — no easy feat — before any royalties can be generated. If acts fail to exceed that threshold, streams will not earn them any new income. (The Record Deal Simulator focuses on streaming income since streams are the primary driver of music consumption today, though legacy artists would have relied primarily on old-fashioned sales to recoup.)

以标准的音乐行业特许权使用费率收回纪录合约 - 近年来，经理人表示，这可能在15到20％之间 - 艺术家必须拥有大量的商业成功。根据公司CreateSafe的唱片公司组合在一起，如果标签签署艺术家，签署了20％的艺术家，艺术家必须在13亿辆溪流上产生13亿美元 - 在任何特许权使用费之前可以生成。如果行为未能超过该门槛，则流不会获得任何新的收入。 （记录交易模拟器侧重于流动收入，因为它是今天的音乐消费的主要驱动因素，尽管传统艺术家将主要依赖于老式的销售来收回。）

Sony did not say it would “zero out” unrecouped balances, as Sweeney suggested, and the company made clear that it is not “modifying existing contracts.” However, in an effort to “creat[e] more payment opportunities for our long-standing artists… around the world,” Sony promised to clear the way for royalties to flow to some artists still in debt to the label, “pay[ing] through on existing unrecouped balances to increase the ability of those who qualify to receive more money from the uses of their music.”

索尼并没有说它会“零”未推迟的余额，因为斯斯丁建议，公司明确表示不“修改现有合同”。然而，为了“创造[E]为我们的长期艺术家提供更多的支付机会......在世界各地，”索尼答应明确为特许权使用费用流向仍然债务的艺术家的方式，“支付[ING ]通过现有的未推迟余额，以提高那些有资格从他们音乐的用途获得更多资金的人的能力。“

“I’m actually smiling. Something really good finally happened for the people who need it most,” Sweeney tells  Rolling Stone when asked about Sony’s latest announcement. “There are a tremendous amount of artists who will now benefit and see money that they would never otherwise receive.”

“我真的很微笑。真正善良的事情终于发生了最需要的人，“斯威斯在被问及索尼的最新公告时告诉滚石。 “现在有巨大的艺术家，现在将受益，看看他们永远不会接受的资金。”

For artists to qualify to start receiving royalties even if they’re unrecouped, the acts must have signed to a Sony company before 2000. In addition, artists must not have received any advance payments from the company since 2000. The letter did not say how many artists would be impacted or how much money would start being paid out to artists under the new plan.

对于艺术家有资格开始接收特许权使用费，即使它们是未推迟的，该行为也必须在2000年之前向索尼公司签署。此外，艺术家不得自2000年以来收到公司的任何预付款。这封信没有说如何许多艺术家会受到影响，或者在新计划下会向艺术家支付多少钱。

“With unrecouped balances, a lot of times artists don’t see additional funds for years to come,” says Stephanie K. Hay, an entertainment attorney. “I think this is, in general, a good faith effort [by Sony] and a good step forward.”

“随着不应调整的余额，很多时候艺术家们多年来没有看到额外的资金，”娱乐律师斯蒂芬妮K. Hay说。 “我认为这是一般来说，善意的努力[索尼]和前进的良好一步。”

There is some music industry precedent for passing royalties or other forms of income to artists even if they are still in debt to their labels. Beggars Group, which comprises a prominent group of indie labels, told Rolling Stone last year  that it zeroes out unrecouped balances 15 years after the company ends its active relationship with an artist, which allows royalties to start flowing to that act.

即使他们仍然债务到他们的标签，有一些音乐行业的先例是将特许权使用费或其他形式的收入传递给艺术家。乞丐集团包括一个突出的独立标签，去年告诉滚石，达到了公司与艺术家的积极关系后15年来解除了15年的余额，这允许特许权使用费，开始流向这种行为。

In addition, both Sony and Universal Music Group said that when they sold off their shares in Spotify, some of the proceeds would be distributed to artists regardless of their recoupment status. Sony sold off roughly half its shares in the streaming giant in 2018.

此外，索尼和普遍的音乐集团表示，当他们在Spotify中销售股票时，一些收益将被分发给艺术家，无论其收回状态如何。索尼在2018年在流媒体巨头售出了大约一半的股票。

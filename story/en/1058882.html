The Duties of John von Neumann’s Assistant in the 1930s (2020)
John Von Neumann在20世纪30年代助理的职责（2020年）

2021-04-17 10:41:53

“What, I asked, are the duties of an assistant to Professor von Neumann?”

“我问的是什么，是von Neumann教授助理的职责？”

The year is 1933. A 26 year-old former graduate student of Columbia University, Edgar R. Lorch (1907–1990) has just completed his Ph.D. in mathematics and is, in his own words “by some miracle” awarded a National Research Council Fellowship for a year of postdoctoral studies at Harvard University.

今年是1933年。一名26岁的哥伦比亚大学前任研究生，Edgar R. Lorch（1907-1990）刚刚完成了他的博士学位。在数学中，以他自己的话语“由一些奇迹”授予哈佛大学博士后研究的全国研究理事会奖学金。

Worried that the ongoing Great Depression will leave him unemployed, as the fellowship nears its end, Lorch applies for a one-year extension to visit Princeton and study under Professor John von Neumann (1903–1957) at the Institute for Advanced Study.

担心持续的大萧条将使他失业，因为奖学金附近的结束，Lorch申请了一年的延伸，参观普林斯顿，并在John Von Neumann（1903-1957教授）在高级研究所的研究所进行。

Lorch’s Ph.D. thesis entitled “Elementary Transformations” had regarded Joseph Liouville (1809–1882)’s result showing the “impossibility of evaluating certain indefinite integrals, and of solving certain differential equations, in terms of elementary functions”. Elementary functions, Lorch defined as “those which are obtained in a finite number of steps by performing algebraic operations and taking exponentials and logarithms”. One of the results of his thesis was later published in the Annals of Mathematics 33(2) under the same title.

洛奇的博士。题为“基本转型”的论文认为，Joseph Liouville（1809-1882）的结果表明，在基本功能方面，“不可能评估某些无限积分以及求解某些微分方程”的结果。基本函数，Lorch定义为“通过执行代数操作并采取幂幂和逻辑上以有限的步骤获得的那些。他论文的结果之一后来发表在同一标题下数学33（2）的历史。

For his fellowship, Lorch had been assigned supervisor Marshall H. Stone (1903–1989). An American mathematician working in real and functional analysis, Stone had been the author of the epic work Linear Transformations in Hilbert Space, a 622-page extensive treatment of the subject which Lorch would later comment was “more often quoted than read”.

对于他的奖学金，Lorch已被分配主管Marshall H. Stone（1903-1989）。一位美国数学家在实际和功能分析中工作，石头一直是希尔伯特空间的史诗工作线性变换的作者，一个622页的大量治疗，Lorch将在后来评论中的评论是“比读得比读书更常用”。

“Stone had a special sense of humour. At one of our infrequent meetings I mentioned some problems as possible candidates for research topics. About the best one of my problems, Stone said, “Oh, I don’t know. Somebody must have worked on that problem already”. The following week, while browsing in Widener Library, I came upon an article containing the complete solution to the problem. The author: M. H. Stone.”

“石头有一种特殊的幽默感。在我们一个不常见的会议之一，我提到了尽可能可能的研究主题候选人的问题。关于我最好的问题，石头说，“哦，我不知道。有人必须已经在这个问题上工作了“。接下来的一周，在浏览Wondener库时，我介绍了一个包含完整解决问题的文章。作者：M. H. Stone。“

As Lorch recounts, together with Stone the two agreed that his fellowship would be best spent learning more about Hilbert and Banach spaces. This would invariably involve a “frontal attack on Stone’s […] Linear Transformations in Hilbert Space”. Stone noted to Lorch that the two mathematicians in the world who could be most helpful in his development at the time (in addition to himself) would be Frederick Riesz (188–1956) and John von Neumann. Riesz was a Hungarian mathematician Lorch had never heard of who lived “on the other side of the world” in Hungary. von Neumann of course, was a few short hours’ train ride away from Cambridge, at the IAS in Princeton.

随着Lorch Regounts，两人同意他的奖学金将最好地学习更多关于希尔伯特和Banach空间的信息。这总是涉及“对希尔伯特空间的石头线性变换的”正面攻击“。石头指出，洛奇那个世界上两个数学家在他当时的发展中最有用（除了他自己）将是Frederick Riesz（188-1956）和John Von Neumann。 Riesz是一位匈牙利数学家洛奇从来没有听说过匈牙利的谁生活在“世界另一边”。 von neumann当然，在普林斯顿的IAS，距离剑桥有几个小时的火车骑行。

Lorch’s NRC fellowship was for $1,600 for twelve months (about $31,400 when adjusted for inflation). The stipend enabled him to live in Boston “ like a Bohemian” while being given the necessary Harvard “ coat of varnish” needed to impress future employers. As the year went on, due to the ongoing great depression, Lorch however worried that it would not be enough. And so, he applied for a one-year extension to the National Research Council to visit von Neumann the Institute for Advanced Study:

Lorch的NRC奖学金为1,600美元的十二个月（调整通货膨胀时约31,400美元）。津贴使他能够生活在波士顿“像一个波希米亚一样”，同时获得了必要的哈佛“清漆”需要给未来的雇主留下深刻印象。随着年份的前进，由于持续的大萧条，洛奇却担心这是不够的。因此，他申请了向国家研究委员会延长的一年延伸，参观Von Neumann学院的高级研究所：

“I gingerly went home, and sure enough, there was a letter from the Institute. The type-print covered the whole page — success! I would be able to live another year.”

“我小心翼翼地回家了，肯定有足够的问题，研究所有一封信。类型打印涵盖整页 - 成功！我又能举行一年。“

Lorch had been offered a position as the assistant to John von Neumann. As he writes, “in order to further distinguish [professors at the Institute for Advanced Study] from ordinary mortals teaching at Columbia, Yale, and other universities, each professor [at the IAS] had an assistant”.

Lorch已被提供作为John Von Neumann助理的职位。当他写道时，“为了进一步区分[高级研究所教授]在哥伦比亚，耶鲁和其他大学的普通凡人教学中，每个教授[IAS]的教授都有助理”。

Although Lorch had seen von Neumann lecture several times, he did not know him personally. He did however recall a glorious episode of meeting von Neumann at a party, where the latter had ended up in a recitation race (von Neumann had an eidetic memory) with Norbert Wiener of MIT:

虽然Lorch见过冯Neumann讲座几次，但他并不是个人不认识他。然而，他确实回忆起一个在一个派对上会议的光荣发作，后者已经在叙述的比赛中最终结束了诺伯特·诺伯特·诺伯特：

“Somehow, someone recited a line from Lewis Carroll’s “The Hunting of the Snark”. Norbert Wiener with his usual ebullience and sonorous voice, began reciting from line 1. Johnny started off in pursuit. Norbert accelerated, but Johnny came up even. We held our breaths as the lines poured out, on and on until they reached the end in a dead heat”

“不知何故，有人从刘易斯卡罗尔的”狩猎蛇“中回到了一条线。诺伯特维纳用他平时的沸腾和铿son的声音，开始从第1.约翰尼开始追求。诺伯特加速了，但Johnny甚至出现了。我们呼吸着呼吸，因为线条倒出来，直到他们达到了死海的终点“

Both child prodigies, Wiener had been “ trained” by his father from an early age, including in Greek and Latin classics and Germany poetry, starting at the age of five. von Neumann conversed in Ancient Greek at age six and was reading Emile Borel’s Théorie des Fonctions (“On some points in the theory of functions” ) at age twelve (Leonard, 2010).

父亲的潜品，维纳曾经从他的父亲那里被“训练”，包括希腊和拉丁古典和德国诗歌，从五岁开始。 Von Neumann在六岁的古希腊语中交谈，在十二岁（Leonard，2010）时，六岁的古希腊语在古希腊语中读书，并正在阅读Emile Borel的ThéorieDesFonctions（“关于职能理论”）。

Thrilled to learn of the offer from the Institute, Lorch made the trip to Princeton to meet with Oswald Veblen (1880–1960), its Director at that time.

令人兴奋地了解研究所的优惠，Lorch将普林斯顿旅行与Oswald Veblen（1880-1960），当时董事会面。

“What,” asked I, “are the duties of an assistant to Professor von Neumann?”

“什么，”问我，“是冯·诺曼教授助理的职责？”

“Answer with a mixture of surprise and distain, that a mere private second class should ask such a question about a four star general.”

“用惊喜和喧嚣的混合物回答，只有私人第二级课程应该问一个关于四星级一般的问题。”

Veblen nevertheless answered. The following were the four principal duties of von Neumann’s assistant in the 1930s:

Veblen仍然回答。以下是20世纪30年代von Neumann助理的四项主要职责：

1.To attend von Neumann’s lectures and take copious notes, complete unfinished proofs, see them through the secretarial jungle, and promptly circulate them to all American university libraries.

1.参加Von Neumann的讲座并采取大量的笔记，完整的未完成证据，通过秘书丛林来看，并迅速将它们传递给所有美国大学图书馆。

Von Neumann lectured on operator theory. His lectures were on Mondays, Tuesdays and Wednesdays. Lorch writes that keeping up with the brilliant professor on this task alone amounted to “consuming the entire energies of a younger person, who had to be not only well-meaning but sharp, fast, clever and tough.”

Von Neumann在操作员理论上讲课。他的讲座在周一，周二和星期三。 Lorch写道，他们独自追求这项任务的辉煌教授相当于“消耗年轻人的整个能量，他们不仅不仅是普遍意义，而且令人挑剔，快速，聪明，艰难的。”

One of von Neumann’s brothers later described audiences’ difficulties in keeping up with the great man’s lectures in the following way:

Von Neumann的兄弟之一后来描述了观众在跟随伟大的讲座方面的困难：

"His fluid line of thought was difficult for those less gifted to follow. He was notorious for dashing out equations on a small portion of the available blackboard and erasing expressions before students could copy them." - Excerpt, John von Neumann: As Seen by his Brother by N.A. Vonneuman (1987)

＆＃34;他的流体线思想对于那些不太有意的人来说很难。在学生可以复制之前，他在一小部分可用的黑板和擦除表达式上删除方程时令人讨厌。＆＃34; - Excerpt，John Von Neumann：由他的兄弟由N.A. Vonneuman（1987）所见

Because von Neumann was by 1933 working for an American institution, it was expected that the Professor’s papers would appear in English (not German, as most of his previous papers had). As such, rather than von Neumann going through the strain of writing in English, his assistant was given the task of translating his works on his behalf.

因为冯·诺伊曼为1933年为美国机构工作，预计教授的论文将出现英语（不是德语，因为他之前的大多数论文都有）。因此，而不是von neumann经历英语写作的伤害，他的助手被赋予了代表他的作品的任务。

3. To be von Neumann’s assistant as Editor of the Annals of Mathematics. This meant reading through every manuscript accepted for publication, underlying Greek letters in red and German letters in green, and circling italics. Also writing in the margins all necessary instructions to printers.

3.作为von Neumann的助手作为数学史的编辑。这意味着通过接受出版物的每个手稿，以红色和德语字母的底层希腊字母在绿色，以及盘旋斜体。还在边缘写入打印机的所有必要说明。

Lorch describes the “hazards of being an editorial assistant of the Annals of Mathematics in the 1930s” by way of the following anecdote about a manuscript submitted by Soviet mathematician Lev Pontryagin (1908–1988):

Lorch描述了“20世纪30年代纪念数学史册的危险”，通过以下轶事关于苏联Mathematician Lev Pontryagin（1908-1988）提交的稿件：

“[Because] paper was then exceptionally scarce in the Soviet Union, Pontryagin had taken wrapping paper, torn it into appropriate-sized pieces, and gone to work on his typewriter. Unfortunately, Pontryagin was blind. The wrapping paper was torn unevenly, and a good portion of the words and symbols in the margins were missing.“

“[因为]纸张在苏联出现异常稀缺，Pontryagin已经服用了包装纸，将其撕成了适当的碎片，并在打字机上工作。不幸的是，Pontryagin是盲目的。包装纸不均匀地撕裂，并且缺少边缘中的符号和符号的良好部分。“

“No matter, the Annals of Mathematics editorial assistant retyped the letter, supplying all the missing symbols.”

“无论如何，数学编辑助理的历史记错了，提供了所有缺少的符号。”

4. To go once a week to the printers of the Annals of Mathematics in Baltimore in order to instruct them in the art of typesetting mathematical symbols with subscripts, superscripts, subsubscripts, etc.

4.每周去巴尔的摩数学史册的打印机，以指导他们用下标，上标，子提让等排版数学符号的艺术。

The Annals had traditionally been printed in Hamburg, Germany but due to increasing anti-semitism had moved printing to Baltimore in the United States. As American printers were not yet accustomed with the practice of typesetting mathematical symbols, they needed to be taught.

该年纳尔传统上印在德国汉堡，但由于反犹太主义的增加，在美国将印刷到巴尔的摩。随着美国打印机尚未习惯于排版数学符号的实践，他们需要被教导。

A few days after returning to Cambridge, I received a letter from the Dean of the Graduate School at my alma mater, Columbia University. The contents made me radiant. In view of my "outstanding work in scholarship and research," the letter said, I had been awarded a Cutting Traveling Fellowship for the following academic year. There were no conditions on the award, except to use it for travel, presumably in Europe. The stipend was for $1,800 (more than I was currently receiving as a National Research Council fellow). What should I do? [...] Here I had the opportunity to work next to the most brilliant mathematician of his generation. But the job entailed such onerous duties that only someone with an iron constitution could survive. My constitution, it so happened, was not made by iron. It was made of reeds and bamboo sticks, very satisfactory under moderate pressures, but completely incapable of standing a huge overload. [...] I already had in mind the professor whom I wanted to visit: Frederick Riesz of Hungary - the man suggested by Professor Stone. Riesz worked in a town I had never heard of, Szeged. I went to my atlas and found it: the second city in Hungary. There was a university: Ferencz-Jozsef Tudomány Egyetem. I was mouthing my first words in a strange new language. [...] The die was cast. Off to Szeged! - Excerpt, "Szeged in 1934" by Edgar R. Lorch, edited by Reuben Hersh

回到剑桥后几天，我收到了哥伦比亚大学校友研究生院院长的一封信。内容让我辐射。鉴于我的＆＃34;奖学金和研究的杰出工作，＆＃34;这封信说，我已被授予以下学年的削减旅行奖学金。奖项没有条件，除了使用它的旅行，大概在欧洲。津贴为1,800美元（超过我目前被接受为国家研究理事会的人）。我应该怎么办？在这里，我有机会在他一代人的最辉煌的数学家旁边工作。但这项工作只需要如此繁重的职责，只有有铁宪法的人可以生存。我的宪法，它发生了，不是由铁制成的。它是由芦苇和竹棒制成的，在中压下非常令人满意，但完全无法站立巨大过载。我已经铭记了我想去的教授：匈牙利的弗雷德里克里耶斯 - 教授斯通教授建议的人。 Riesz在一个城镇工作，我从未听说过，Szeged。我去了我的地图集，发现了它：匈牙利的第二个城市。有一个大学：ferencz-jozseftudomány埃伊。我以一种奇怪的新语言嘲笑我的第一个单词。 [...]骰子被铸造了。关闭szeged！ - 摘录，＆＃34;在1934年的别人兄弟＃34;由Edgar R. Lorch，由Reuben Hersh编辑

Lorch went on to have a productive year in Szeged and develop an exceptionally good relationship with Frederick Riesz:

Lorch继续在Szeged中有一个富有成效的一年，并与Frederick Riesz制定了一个特别良好的关系：

The optimal relation between a mentor and his disciple is seldom achieved. The relation between Riesz and me was optimal. It was warm, intimate, continuous, without pressure, very calm, leaving each of us free to develop his own thoughts.

导师和他的门徒之间的最佳关系很少实现。 riesz和我之间的关系是最佳的。它是温暖，亲密，连续的，没有压力，非常平静，让我们每个人都可以自由发展自己的想法。

My stay with him was a perfect amalgam of a healthy, pleasurable life and an uninterrupted communication of mathematical ideas. Access to mutual discussion was free, but never overused. Frigyes Riesz was indeed a perfect teacher and a warm companion.

我和他在一起是一个健康，愉快的生活的完美汞合金，也是数学思想的不间断交流。获得相互讨论是免费的，但从未过度使用过。 Frigyes Riesz确实是一个完美的老师和温暖的伴侣。

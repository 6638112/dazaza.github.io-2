Video shows brazen theft at S.F. Walgreens as shoplifting drives out retailers

Rampant shoplifting has shuttered more than a dozen San Francisco pharmacies in recent years — and a new video shows why.

近年来，猖獗的入店行窃已经百现比超过十几个旧金山药房 - 而新的视频显示为什么。

The video, taken Monday afternoon at the Walgreens at the corner of Gough and Fell streets, shows a man with a bicycle filling a garbage bag with items as a patron and a security guard watch, filming the incident on their cell phones. The man places the shopping bag in the basket of his bicycle and rides down the aisle toward the bystanders.

该视频，在谷谷街的角落的Walgreens周一下午拍摄，展示了一个带有自行车填充物品作为顾客的垃圾袋的人，并将事件拍摄在其手机上。该男子将购物袋放在骑自行车的篮子里，然后骑下过道朝着旁观者。

The security guard attempts to grab the bag of pharmacy items in an off-camera exchange, but the man bicycles to the door and exits moments later with the bag of stolen goods.

保安人员试图在相机交换中抓住药房项目的袋子，但是男子自行车到门口，然后用赃物包出去时刻。

“This is exactly why I held a hearing on organized retail theft and am pushing for greater accountability on shoplifting,” Supervisor Ahsha Safaí tweeted in response to the video of the brazen theft.

“这正是为什么我曾经有关有组织的零售盗窃的聆讯，并且正在推动入店行窃的责任，”SheShaSafaí推文是以肆无忌惮的盗窃视频推文。

Safaí held a hearing on retail crimes last month with District Attorney Chesa Boudin, retailers, police and probation departments. The hearings came as Walgreens and CVS continued to close pharmacies around the city in response to the uptick in thefts.

Safaí上个月举行了关于零售犯罪的听证会，参加了District Atrorney Chesa Boudin，零售商，警察和试用部门。听证会随着Walgreens和CVS继续响应Furts的上升而在城市周围关闭药店。

“The video depicts a crime and we have reported it to the police,” said Phil Caruso, a Walgreens spokesperson. “Unfortunately, this is another example of blatant retail theft which is an ongoing problem for retailers in San Francisco.”

“视频描绘了犯罪，我们已经向警方报告了警察，”沃尔格森发言人菲尔卡鲁斯说。 “遗憾的是，这是公然零售盗窃的另一个例子，这是旧金山零售商的持续问题。”

Walgreens has closed 17 stores in San Francisco in the past five years. Theft in the pharmaceutical chain’s 53 remaining stores is four times the average for stores elsewhere in the country, and the chain spends 35 times more on security guards in the city than elsewhere, Jason Cunningham, regional vice president for pharmacy and retail operations in California and Hawaii, said at the hearing in May.

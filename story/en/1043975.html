The Confusing World of USB
USB的混乱世界

2021-01-11 16:32:52

For decades, my perception of USB was that of a technology both simple and reliable. You plug it and it works. The two first iterations [1] [2] freed PCs from a badly fragmented connector world made of RJ-45 (Ethernet), DA-15 (Joystick), DE-9 (Serial), DIN (PS/2), and DB-25 (Parallel). When USB-3.0 came out, USB-IF had the good idea to color code its ports. All you had to do was to "check for blue" in the chain to get your 5 Gbit/s. Even better, around the same time were introduced type-C connectors. Not only the world was a faster place, now we could plug things with one try instead of three. Up to that point in time, it was a good tech stack. Yet in 2013 things started to become confusing.

几十年来，我对USB的看法一直是既简单又可靠的技术。您将其插入即可使用。最初的两次迭代[1] [2]使PC摆脱了由RJ-45（以太网），DA-15（游戏杆），DE-9（串行），DIN（PS / 2）和DB组成的零散连接器世界-25（平行）。当USB-3.0出现时，USB-IF最好对端口进行颜色编码。您所要做的就是＆＃34;检查蓝色＆＃34;在链中获得5 Gbit / s。更好的是，大约在同一时间推出了C型连接器。不仅世界变得更快，而且现在我们可以一次尝试而不是三次尝试进行操作。到那时为止，这是一个很好的技术堆栈。然而在2013年，事情开始变得混乱。

With USB 3.1 the bandwidth doubled to 10 Gbit/s. But USB-IF called it "USB 3.1 Gen 2" with the "old" USB 3.0 to be refereed to as "USB 3.1 Gen1". They did it again in 2017 with what people would have liked to be "USB 3.2". We got 20 Gbit/s except it was to be called "USB 3.2 Gen 2x2". Accordingly "USB 3.1 Gen 2" became "USB 3.2 Gen 1x1" and "USB 3.1 Gen 1" was renamed "USB 3.2 Gen 2x1".

USB-3.X cables features 8 wires. Two of then are for power and the other six carry data. Two data wires are dedicated to USB-2 legacy traffic while the other four are for USB-3.X SuperSpeed transfer. In 2014, VESA had the idea to re-purpose some of the wires and published "DisplayPort Alternate Mode" specs [3]. Display Port signal could now be carried over 1, 2, or 4 of the 3.X wires (calling them lanes). It works fine as long as one or two lanes are used for DP 1.2. But to display a 4K60Hz image, DP 1.2 bandwidth requirements demands to use all four lanes which leaves only two USB-2 wires for data. This leaves a lot of "implementation dependent" behavior up to people who build USB hubs. Thanks to DP 1.4, the bandwidth requirement can be lowered via DSP lossless compression. This allows 40k60 on two lanes with lanes available for USB-3.X traffic but it can be tricky to discover which hub can do DP 1.4.

USB-3.X电缆具有8根电线。然后，其中两个用于供电，另外六个用于传输数据。两条数据线专用于USB-2旧式通信，而另外四条数据线用于USB-3.X SuperSpeed传输。在2014年，VESA提出了重新使用某些电线的想法，并发布了“ #DisplayPort备用模式”。规格[3]。显示端口信号现在可以通过1、2或4条3.X导线（称为通道）传送。只要DP 1.2使用一个或两个通道，它就可以正常工作。但是要显示4K60Hz图像，DP 1.2带宽要求要求使用所有四个通道，仅留下两条USB-2线进行数据传输。这就留下了许多与实现相关的信息。行为取决于构建USB集线器的人员。借助DP 1.4，可通过DSP无损压缩降低带宽要求。这允许在两个通道上使用40k60的通道，这些通道可用于USB-3.X流量，但是要发现哪个集线器可以执行DP 1.4可能很棘手。

USB-2 provides up to 2.5W. USB-3 brings it to 4.5W and USB-3.2 takes it to 7.5W. In what I assume was an effort to accommodate vendors who wanted to build chargers, USB-IF published standalone power protocols. There is USB-BC (Battery Charging) v1, v1.1, and v1.2. Then came USB-PD (Power Delivery) v1, v2, and v3. Which USB-PD version is included in each USB-X "core" specs is something I did not take the time to establish but I would expect it to be non-trivial. On top of these open specs exist a myriad of proprietary cell-phones "fast charging" protocols. Motorola has "Rapid Charging" and "Turbo Power" [4]. OnePlus has "Dash Charge" / "Warp Charge" while Realme have "Dart Charge" (both based on VOOC 2.0, VOOC 3.0, SuperVOOC, VOOC 4, or SuperVOOC 2.0 [5]). Finally Qualcomm phone have "Quick Charge" [6] v1, v2, v3, v4 and v4+.

USB-4 specs [7] were released in August 2019. In terms of transfer speed there are two flavors, 20 Gbit/s and 40 Gbit/s named respectively "USB4 Gen 2×2" and "USB4 Gen 3×2". There is an impressive list of features that come with this version. Among them, several of Thunderbolt-3 tunneling capabilities. Unfortunately a lot of them are optional depending on the type of device involved. When these devices become available, the difficulty from a customer stand point will be to identify which feature a constructor has decided to implement. Wikipedia has a nice summary matrix. Also notice that it seems there are three ways to perform what at first sight is the same thing with USB-C Alt-Mode, USB4 DP Tunneling and TB3 DP Tunneling possibly supported simultaneously.

The USB world has reached a complexity level which exceeds the amount of time I can dedicate to understanding it. While I was researching USB hubs, I kept on running into their Thunderbolt-3 counterparts which seemed not only superior in capabilities but also far simpler. The advertisement flier from Intel sums it up. Thanks to "minimum solution requirements", customers "know what they are getting". Yes it favors something proprietary over something open since Thunderbolt is 100% owned by Intel who also produce all controllers. Yes, it is more expensive. But it seems to "just work" like USB used to. And so, for the foreseeable future, the first thing I will look at in a new laptop/desktop/hub will be direct support for Thunderbolt-3.

USB世界已经达到了一个复杂的程度，超过了我专门用于理解它的时间。在研究USB集线器时，我一直在与他们的Thunderbolt-3同类产品竞争，这不仅功能强大，而且简单得多。英特尔的广告传单对此进行了总结。多亏了“最低解决方案要求”，客户才能知道他们得到了什么。是的，它比开放产品更偏爱专有产品，因为Thunderbolt由Intel拥有100％的所有权，该公司还生产所有控制器。是的，它更昂贵。但这似乎可以正常工作。就像以前的USB。因此，在可预见的将来，我将在新笔记本电脑/台式机/集线器中看到的第一件事将是对Thunderbolt-3的直接支持。

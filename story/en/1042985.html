Apple will let Amphetamine app stay in the App Store
苹果将让安非他明应用程序留在App Store中

2021-01-03 09:32:38

The developer of Amphetamine, an app that prevents Macs from going into sleep mode, says Apple told him it violated App Store guidelines, even though it’s been in the App Store since 2014, and has nothing to do with drug use. Not long after The Verge reached out to Apple for comment on Saturday however, the company reversed its decision, and the app will be able to stay up with its current name and logo.

Amphetamine的开发人员说，苹果公司告诉他，苯丙胺违反了App Store的指导方针，即使该应用程序自2014年以来一直在App Store中使用，并且与吸毒无关。不过，在周六The Verge与Apple取得联系后不久，该公司撤销了其决定，该应用程序将能够保持其当前名称和徽标。

William C. Gustafson said in January 1st posts on Reddit and Github that Apple had informed him he had two weeks to “remove all references to the word ‘amphetamine’ and remove the pill from the icon.” If he failed to do so, Gustafson wrote, Apple said it would remove the app from the App Store on January 12th. The logo features a cartoon image of a pill.

威廉·C·古斯塔夫森（William C. Gustafson）在1月1日在Reddit和Github上的帖子中说，苹果已经通知他，他有两个星期的时间“删除所有对“安非他明”一词的引用，并从图标中删除药丸。”古斯塔夫森写道，如果他没有这样做，苹果表示将在1月12日从App Store中删除该应用程序。徽标具有药丸的卡通形象。

Gustafson told The Verge he got a call Saturday from Apple granting his appeal— but he didn’t have insight into how the app was flagged in the first place. “I specifically asked Apple on the phone if this was a result of customer complaints and Apple’s response was ‘I don’t think so,’” he said. “I found it odd that this issue came up out of nowhere. I wasn’t in the middle of trying to update Amphetamine or anything. Just sitting at home with my kids enjoying our holiday and got the violation/rejection email from Apple.”

古斯塔夫森（Gustafson）告诉《边缘报》（The Verge），他在周六接到了苹果公司的电话，呼吁他上诉。他说：“我特别在电话中问苹果这是否是客户投诉的结果，而苹果的回应是'我不这么认为'。” “我发现这个问题无处不在很奇怪。我并没有尝试更新苯丙胺或其他任何东西。只是和我的孩子一起坐在家里享受我们的假期，并收到苹果的违规/拒绝电子邮件。”

Gustafson says Apple contacted him on December 29th and told him Amphetamine “appears to promote inappropriate use of controlled substances. Specifically, your app name and icon include references to controlled substances, pills.”

古斯塔夫森说，苹果公司于12月29日与他联系，并告诉他安非他明“似乎是在促进不当使用受控物质。具体来说，您的应用名称和图标包括对受控物质，药丸的引用。”

The free macOS app has been downloaded more than 432,000 times, with a 4.8 rating, Gustafson said, noting that Apple even featured Amphetamine in an Mac App Store story. He said he had numerous interactions with Apple employees for updates to the app since its launch, with no one objecting to the name or logo until now.

Gustafson说，免费的macOS应用程序已被下载超过43.2万次，评级为4.8，并指出苹果甚至在Mac App Store故事中使用了苯丙胺。他说，自从该应用程序发布以来，他与Apple员工进行了多次互动，以更新该应用程序，到目前为止，没有人反对该名称或徽标。

The specific App Store guideline Gustafson was accused of violating is this one, which states “Apps that encourage consumption of tobacco or vape products, illegal drugs, or excessive amounts of alcohol are not permitted on the App Store. Apps that encourage minors to consume any of these substances will be rejected. Facilitating the sale of marijuana, tobacco, or controlled substances (except for licensed pharmacies) isn’t allowed.”

App Store的特定指南Gustafson被指控违反了该指南，该指南指出：“ App Store上禁止使用鼓励吸烟或使用vape产品，非法药物或过量酒精的应用。鼓励未成年人食用这些物质的应用将被拒绝。禁止为销售大麻，烟草或受控物质（获得许可的药店除外）提供便利。”

Gustafson says Amphetamine does none of these things, and said changing the name of the app would have wrecked its brand recognition and potentially made it harder for users to find future updates.

古斯塔夫森说，安非他明不做这些事情，并说更改应用程序的名称可能会破坏其品牌知名度，并可能使用户更难找到未来的更新。

Gustafson initially said he didn’t expect his appeal to be successful, and indeed, Apple typically hews pretty closely to its App Store rules in most cases. The company has faced pushback from developers on several fronts in recent months, with big industry companies including Spotify, Tile, and Epic Games forming a group called the Coalition for App Fairness. It says Apple’s rules create an uneven playing field in its App Stores.

古斯塔夫森（Gustafson）最初表示，他并不希望自己的上诉会成功，实际上，在大多数情况下，苹果通常会严格遵守其App Store规则。近几个月来，该公司在多个方面都面临开发者的压制，包括Spotify，Tile和Epic Games在内的大型行业公司组成了一个名为App Fairness的联盟。苹果公司的规则在其App Store中造成了不平衡的竞争环境。

Basecamp co-founder David Heinemeier Hansson battled with Apple last summer over the mobile app design of his company’s email client, Hey. And Epic Games filed a lawsuit against Apple in August after the iOS version of its battle royale game Fortnite was removed from the App Store. Epic had implemented its own payment processing system into the iOS version of Fortnite, which goes against App Store rules.

Basecamp的联合创始人David Heinemeier Hansson去年夏天与Apple争夺了公司电子邮件客户端Hey的移动应用程序设计。 Epic Games于8月份对Apple提起诉讼，原因是该公司的大逃杀游戏Fortnite的iOS版本已从App Store中删除。 Epic已在Fortnite的iOS版本中实现了自己的付款处理系统，这违反了App Store的规定。

亚马逊销毁了150万美元的卖家库存-现在无家可归

2020-10-28 11:36:58

Barak Govani made a big bet on Amazon.com earlier this year that he now regrets. He shut his New York Speed clothing store on Los Angeles' storied Melrose Avenue, packed up $US1.5 million ($2.1 million) in inventory and shipped it to Amazon warehouses around the country, putting his fate in the hands of a company that has routinely presented itself to the world as a friend of small business.

巴拉克·戈瓦尼(Barak Govani)今年早些时候在亚马逊(Amazon.com)下了大赌注，现在他后悔了。他关闭了位于洛杉矶梅尔罗斯大道(Melrose Avenue)的New York Speed服装店，打包了150万美元(210万美元)的库存，运往全国各地的亚马逊仓库，将自己的命运交到了一家经常向世界展示自己是小企业朋友的公司手中。

Today, the 41-year-old retail veteran is broke and couch-surfs between his mother's home and his sister's place. Govani hopes to start anew by getting Amazon to pay him for inventory the company destroyed after suggesting his products could be fake - an accusation Govani strenuously denies.

今天，这位41岁的零售业老手身无分文，在他母亲的家和他妹妹的家之间沙发冲浪。戈瓦尼希望通过让亚马逊支付他的库存费用来重新开始，亚马逊在暗示他的产品可能是假货后销毁了库存-戈瓦尼极力否认这一指控。

His lawyer in September sent a demand for $US800,000, along with invoices to verify his merchandise came directly from fashion brands, and they're waiting for Amazon's response.

他的律师在9月份要求他赔偿80万美元，并附上发票，以证实他的商品直接来自时尚品牌，他们正在等待亚马逊的回应。

"All my life, I'd wake up at 5.30am and work 40, 50, 60 hours each week," Govani said. "That inventory was everything I had. Amazon ruined my life, and I did nothing wrong."

戈瓦尼说，我这一辈子都是早上5点半起床，每周工作40、50、60个小时。那份库存就是我的全部。亚马逊毁了我的生活，我没有做错任何事。

Amazon has become the world's largest e-commerce company in large part thanks to the millions of third-party merchants who have chosen to set up shop on its sprawling marketplace. Small- and medium-size businesses are responsible for more than half of the goods the company sells to customers around the world, moving 3.4 billion products alone in the year ending May 31. The average small business has annual sales of $US160,000 on Amazon, up about 60 per cent from the previous year.

亚马逊已经成为世界上最大的电子商务公司，这在很大程度上要归功于数百万选择在其庞大的市场上开店的第三方商家。在该公司向全球客户销售的商品中，有一半以上来自中小企业，在截至5月31日的一年里，仅中小企业就销售了34亿件产品。小企业在亚马逊上的平均年销售额为16万美元，比前一年增长了约60%。

In blogs and news releases, Amazon highlights the success of these merchants as a win-win. Lost in the public-relations glare are merchants like Govani.

在博客和新闻稿中，亚马逊强调这些商家的成功是双赢的。像戈瓦尼这样的商人在公关的耀眼光芒中迷失了方向。

Stories like his have swirled for years in online merchant forums and conferences. Amazon can suspend sellers at any time for any reason, cutting off their livelihoods and freezing their money for weeks or months. The merchants must navigate a largely automated, guilty-until-proven-innocent process in which Amazon serves as judge and jury. Their emails and calls can go unanswered, or Amazon's replies are incomprehensible, making sellers suspect they're at the mercy of algorithms with little human oversight.

像他这样的故事多年来一直在在线商家论坛和会议上流传。亚马逊可以随时以任何理由暂停卖家，切断他们的生计，并将他们的资金冻结数周或数月。商家必须驾驭一个基本上自动化的、有罪的、直到被证明无罪的过程，亚马逊在这个过程中担任法官和陪审团。他们的电子邮件和电话可能无人应答，或者亚马逊的回复令人费解，这让卖家怀疑他们受到了算法的摆布，几乎没有人的监督。

That inventory was everything I had. Amazon ruined my life, and I did nothing wrong

Recourse is limited because, when merchants set up shop on Amazon, they waive their right to a day in court by agreeing to binding arbitration to resolve any disputes. Amazon doesn't negotiate terms with merchants. The boiler plate agreement is take-it-or-leave-it, a telling reminder of who has the upper hand in the relationship.

追索权是有限的，因为当商家在亚马逊上开店时，他们放弃了在法庭上呆一天的权利，同意通过有约束力的仲裁来解决任何纠纷。亚马逊不与商家协商条款。样板协议是一个要么接受要么放弃的协议，这清楚地提醒人们，谁在两国关系中占据上风。

How Amazon treats third-party sellers is at the heart of a recent House Judiciary Committee report concluding that big technology companies often abuse their power over smaller partners. The committee's recommendations include providing adequate recourse to sellers and eliminating forced arbitration clauses from contracts that deprives them from filing a lawsuit.

亚马逊如何对待第三方卖家是众议院司法委员会(House Judiciary Committee)最近一份报告的核心内容，该报告得出结论称，大型科技公司经常对规模较小的合作伙伴滥用权力。该委员会的建议包括向卖家提供足够的追索权，取消合同中剥夺他们提起诉讼的强制仲裁条款。

"Because of the severe financial repercussions associated with suspension or delisting, many Amazon third-party sellers live in fear of the company," the report states. "This is because Amazon's internal dispute resolution system is characterised by uncertainty, unresponsiveness and opaque decision-making processes."

报告称，由于停牌或退市带来的严重财务影响，许多亚马逊第三方卖家生活在对该公司的恐惧中。这是因为亚马逊的内部纠纷解决系统的特点是不确定、反应迟钝和决策过程不透明。

In an emailed statement, an Amazon spokeswoman said the company worked hard to ensure products sold on its site were authentic and to provide a fair dispute resolution process.

在一份通过电子邮件发送的声明中，亚马逊的一位女发言人表示，公司努力确保其网站上出售的产品是正品，并提供公平的纠纷解决程序。

Govani has been selling clothes for more than 20 years, mostly brands such as Hugo Boss, Calvin Klein and Lucky. About a decade ago, he began supplementing his store sales by putting merchandise on web marketplaces including Amazon and eBay. Amazon emerged as the most effective partner, accounting for more than 90 per cent of his online sales.

Govani销售服装已有20多年的历史，主要是雨果·博斯(Hugo Boss)、卡尔文·克莱恩(Calvin Klein)和Lucky等品牌。大约十年前，他开始通过在亚马逊(Amazon)和易趣(EBay)等网络市场上投放商品来补充他的门店销售。亚马逊成为最有效的合作伙伴，占他在线销售额的90%以上。

Initially, Govani stored, packed and shipped all the orders himself, paying the company a commission on each sale. Amazon representatives suggested he try Fulfillment by Amazon, a service that handles that for additional fees. Govani decided to send all of his inventory to Amazon, becoming one of 450,000 businesses to try the service in the year ending May 31.

最初，戈瓦尼亲自储存、包装和运输所有订单，并向公司支付每笔销售的佣金。亚马逊代表建议他尝试亚马逊履行服务，这是一项收取额外费用的服务。Govani决定将他所有的库存发送到亚马逊，成为截至5月31日的一年中尝试这项服务的45万家企业之一。

Govani's problems began soon afterward. In April, Amazon emailed him to say his account was being reviewed based on four customer complaints over "inauthentic" products.

戈瓦尼的问题很快就开始了。今年4月，亚马逊给他发了一封电子邮件，说他的账户正在接受审查，因为有四个客户投诉他的产品是不正宗的。

"In order to ensure that customers can shop with confidence on Amazon, we take 'inauthentic' complaints seriously," the message said. "The sale of counterfeit products on Amazon is strictly prohibited."

为了确保顾客可以放心地在亚马逊上购物，我们认真对待不真实的投诉，信息中写道。严禁在亚马逊上销售假冒产品。

One complainant from San Rafael, California, was unhappy with the fit of a $US125 Lucky Moto jacket and said she was "wondering if this was a fake". Govani refunded her money and explained that jackets made with different materials fitted differently.

一位来自加利福尼亚州圣拉斐尔的原告对一件价值125美元的幸运摩托夹克的合身感到不满，她说她在怀疑这是不是假的。戈瓦尼退还了她的钱，并解释说，用不同材料制成的夹克穿起来不同。

Another shopper was upset that his Calvin Klein underpants arrived in a damaged carton. "Box is pretty much broken, the item is a knock off," the shopper said in a product review. It's not clear why Amazon characterised the other two complaints - about apparel wrapped in tissue paper and shipped in envelopes - as "inauthentic".

另一位购物者对他的Calvin Klein内裤被装在一个破损的纸箱里送到时感到不安。这位购物者在一次产品评论中说，盒子几乎坏了，这件商品是仿冒品。目前尚不清楚为什么亚马逊会将另外两起投诉--用纸巾包裹和信封装运的衣服--定性为不真实。

Govani appealed against the suspension and submitted invoices from the brands to Amazon. His invoices were more than a year old because that's when he had purchased the inventory, but Amazon wanted invoices from the previous 365 days. Govani was notified that his inventory would be destroyed if he didn't reclaim it by mid-July. Merchants must pay Amazon additional fees for inventory removal.

Govani对暂停提出上诉，并向亚马逊提交了这些品牌的发票。他的发票是一年多以前的，因为那是他购买库存的时候，但亚马逊想要的是之前365天的发票。戈瓦尼接到通知，如果他不在7月中旬之前取回库存，他的库存将被销毁。商家必须向亚马逊支付额外的库存移除费用。

Hoping to have his account reinstated and continue selling on the site, Govani put off the decision. He received a total of 11 emails from Amazon each giving him different dates at which time his inventory would be destroyed if he hadn't removed it. He sought clarity from Amazon about the conflicting dates. When he tried to submit an inventory removal order through Amazon's web portal, it wouldn't let him.

戈瓦尼希望恢复他的账户，并继续在网站上销售，他推迟了这一决定。他总共收到了11封来自亚马逊的电子邮件，每封邮件都告诉了他不同的日期，如果他没有删除他的库存，那么在这个日期他的库存就会被销毁。他要求亚马逊澄清相互冲突的日期。当他试图通过亚马逊的门户网站提交库存清除订单时，它不让他这么做。

The spokeswoman said Amazon repeatedly asked Govani to provide evidence that the products sold were authentic but that the invoices he sent were either illegible or didn't match the records of the brand owners.

这位女发言人说，亚马逊一再要求戈瓦尼提供证据，证明所售产品是正品，但他寄出的发票要么难以辨认，要么与品牌所有者的记录不符。

"After being unable to resolve the matter following several appeals as part of our dispute resolution process, we informed this seller six separate times that they needed to remove their inventory from our store by specific dates or it would be destroyed," she said. "The seller failed to request to remove their inventory by the dates provided."

她说：作为我们争议解决程序的一部分，在几次上诉后未能解决问题后，我们分别六次通知这位卖家，他们需要在特定日期前将库存从我们的商店移走，否则库存将被销毁。"；卖家未能要求在提供的日期之前清空库存。"；

At the end of July, Govani sent a panicked email to chief executive officer Jeff Bezos. He exchanged emails with an Amazon representative named "Brigitte M.", who promised to investigate. Brigitte M. gave her final response on August 3, reiterating that there was nothing else she could do.

7月底，戈瓦尼向首席执行官杰夫·贝佐斯发出了一封惊慌失措的电子邮件。他与亚马逊一位名叫布里吉特·M·M·布里吉特(Brigitte M.&34；)的代表交换了电子邮件，后者承诺将进行调查。布里吉特·M在8月3日给出了最后的回应，重申她无能为力。

Amazon has been more aggressive in using algorithms to knock suspected counterfeits off the site, and consultants say Govani's experience is common.

亚马逊在使用算法将可疑的仿冒品从网站上剔除方面更加积极，顾问们表示，戈瓦尼的经历是常见的。

Chris McCabe, a former Amazonian who now helps merchants navigate the appeal process, says suspensions based on complaints about inauthentic products are rising and often lodged by rival merchants looking to sabotage a competitor.

前亚马逊人克里斯·麦凯布(Chris McCabe)现在帮助商家驾驭上诉过程，他表示，因投诉假冒产品而被暂停的情况正在上升，而且往往是由竞争对手提出的，目的是破坏竞争对手。

"Numerous sellers find themselves appealing [against] this endlessly only to hear that their invoices aren't acceptable or their suppliers aren't verifiable, but without any indication as to why," said McCabe, who testified before the House panel. "Or they receive no reply."

在众议院小组作证的麦凯布说，许多卖家发现自己没完没了地上诉[反对]这一点，结果却听到他们的发票不可接受，或者他们的供应商无法核实，但没有任何迹象说明原因。或者他们没有收到回复。"；

If Amazon declines to reimburse Govani for his inventory, his next step is binding arbitration, which the House panel said gives Amazon the upper hand in disputes. From 2014 to 2019, only 163 Amazon merchants initiated arbitration proceedings against the company because "sellers are generally aware that the process is unfair and unlikely to result in a meaningful remedy", the report states.

如果亚马逊拒绝偿还Govani的库存，他的下一步是具有约束力的仲裁，众议院小组表示，这让亚马逊在争端中占据上风。报告称，从2014年到2019年，只有163家亚马逊商家对该公司提起仲裁诉讼，因为卖家普遍意识到这一过程是不公平的，不太可能产生有意义的补救措施。

Govani's attorney Mario Simonyan, who has represented other Amazon sellers in similar disputes, suspects the company will make a lowball offer, forcing Govani to decide if he wants to pay for arbitration, which can cost tens of thousands of dollars.

Govani&39；的律师马里奥·西蒙尼安(Mario Simonyan)曾在类似纠纷中代表亚马逊的其他卖家，他怀疑亚马逊会提出低价报价，迫使Govani决定是否愿意支付仲裁费用，仲裁费用可能高达数万美元。

Govani said he is eager to start a new business in an entirely new field - possibly construction - even though he spent his career in retail.

Govani说，他渴望在一个全新的领域开始一项新的业务-可能是建筑业-尽管他的职业生涯是在零售业度过的。

"I don't want to go back to online retail even though it's the only thing I'm good at," Govani said. "I'm so scared to sell on Amazon. This has been traumatising."

戈瓦尼说：我不想回到网上零售行业，尽管这是我唯一擅长的事情。我太害怕在亚马逊上卖东西了。这真是令人伤脑筋。"；

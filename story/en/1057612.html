Elon Musk's Boring Company Finally Unveils Las Vegas Tunnel
Elon Musk的无聊公司终于推出了拉斯维加斯隧道

2021-04-11 16:09:40

In a revelation that surprised absolutely nobody who’s been paying even the smallest bit of attention to Elon Musk’s under-Vegas tunnel project for the past few years, the underground loop beneath the Las Vegas Convention center was finally unveiled today, and it’s about as exciting as a sheet of unpainted drywall discarded in a closed office park. It’s just some Tesla Model 3s driving slowly in a tube, and if that gets you excited then I hope you enjoy the many orgasms you get while driving down a spiral ramp in a parking deck.

在一个启示中，对于过去几年来说，绝对没有人的注意力甚至没有人对埃龙麝香斯特拉斯维加斯州隧道项目的最小注意，拉斯维加斯会议中心下面的地下循环今天终于揭幕了，这是令人兴奋的一张未绘的干墙丢弃在封闭的办公室公园。这只是一些特斯拉模型3s在管中慢慢慢慢地驾驶，如果这让你兴奋，那么我希望你喜欢在停车甲板上驾驶螺旋坡道时获得的许多高潮。

As far as the Keno thing goes, growing up, my friend’s dad used to tell us that “a dead man could play Keno,” though I’m not really sure what I was supposed to do with that information. Just know that Keno is lame, and so is Elon Musk’s Las Vegas Convention Center Loop system.

就肯诺的事情而言，成长起来，我的朋友的父亲曾经告诉我们“一个死人可以玩Keno”，虽然我并不肯定我应该用这种信息做些什么。只要知道keno是蹩脚的，所以elon musk的拉斯维加斯会议中心循环系统也是如此。

Las Vegas media were invited to the opening of the underground loop, which is 1.7 miles in length, with three stops, and cost $53.5 million to build.

Las Vegas媒体被邀请到地下环的开放，长度为1.7英里，三站止损，耗费5350万美元。

So far, there are 11 Tesla vehicles that drive in the tunnel, with human drivers, as Tesla does not currently have an autonomous driving system to do the job without a human at the wheel. Speed for the cars is limited to 35 mph in the tunnel, which is about 10 mph less than the top speed of a 1908 Ford Model T.

到目前为止，有11个Tesla车辆在隧道中驱动，随着Tesla目前没有自动驾驶系统，在没有人的情况下做这项工作。隧道速度限制在隧道中的35英里/小时，比1908福特模型T的顶峰小约10英里/小时。

Musk’s The Boring Company own the machines that dug the tunnels, and those machines, some of which were heavily modified by the company, are capable of using the excess dirt from the tunnel to turn into bricks, which is pretty cool, I guess:

麝香的无聊公司拥有挖掘隧道的机器，以及这些机器，其中一些机器由公司大量修改，能够使用隧道的多余污垢变成砖块，这是非常酷的，我猜：

Raw, humid thrills of brick-making aside, all this really is are some Teslas driving in tunnels lined with LED lights.

原始，潮湿的刺穿砖块，所有这一切都是一些特斯拉斯在隧道中驾驶，带有LED灯。

Sure, it’s a 45-minute walk (correction, more like 20 minutes, sorry) on the surface and only a few minutes ride underneath, but the system is still remarkably bad at moving large numbers of people per hour, the metric normally used to evaluate mass transit systems.

当然，这是一个45分钟的步行（更正，更像20分钟，对不起）在表面上只有几分钟的骑行，但系统仍然非常糟糕，每小时移动大量人数，通常习惯于评估质谱系统。

While it was originally intended to move up to 4,400 people per hour, fire regulations will limit the system to moving between 800 and 1,200 people per hour. That said, it looks like the company still states the 4,400 number, when used with 62 cars in the tunnel, though based on the safety issues, this does not seem likely.

虽然最初打算每小时移动高达4,400人，但消防法规将限制系统每小时移动800至1200人。也就是说，它看起来像公司仍然指出4,400号码，当与隧道中的62辆汽车一起使用时，虽然基于安全问题，但这似乎没有。

That’s in the same ballpark as normal vehicular street traffic for private cars (600 to 1,600 people per hour) and a lot less than a dedicated bus lane (4,000 to 8,000 per hour) — hell, normal 60-passenger buses can do about 1,800 per hour, if we have them going back and forth every two minutes or so.

这与私家车的正常车辆街道交通相同（每小时600至1,600人），比专门的公交车道（每小时4,000到8,000人） - 地狱，正常60乘客公共汽车可以做大约1,800辆小时，如果我们让他们每两分钟左右来回来回。

A dumb old sidewalk can move 9,000 people an hour! But that’s walking, which is what animals do, and it takes a while and has the potential to make you sweat.

一个哑的旧人行道可以每小时移动9,000人！但这就是行走，这是动物所做的，它需要一段时间，有可能让你出汗。

Proposed moving high-speed sidewalks, similar to the ThyssenKrupp ACCEL system used in the Toronto Pearson International airport, are expected to move about 7,000 people per hour, and such a system would be far cheaper and easier to build.

建议移动的高速人行道，类似于多伦多Pearson国际机场中使用的Thyssenkrupp Accel系统，预计每小时约有7,000人，此类系统将更便宜，更容易构建。

It’s just Teslas driving slowly in tunnels. It’s not just me saying that — Elon freely admits this, too:

它只是特斯拉斯在隧道中慢慢驾驶。这不仅仅是我说 - 艾隆也自由地承认这一点：

Even if I wanted to be really charitable to the World’s Richest Man and say only nice things about the Vegas Loop, I’m not really sure what I’d say? I mean, it beats walking, sort of, right?

即使我想对世界上最富有的男人真的慈善，而是只对拉斯维加斯循环说好话，我并不肯定我会说什么？我的意思是，它击败行走，有点，对吗？

Some hardcore Friends Of Elon are trying hard to find the upside by imagining a future full of things, like Tesla robotaxis, that aren’t even close to existing yet:

一些铁核埃隆的朋友们努力通过想象一个充满诸如特斯拉机器人的未来来努力找到上行，甚至没有接近现有：

I mean, it’s not like autonomous short-range people carrying systems don’t exist: Morgantown, West Virginia has had an automated transportation system called the PRT in operation since 1975 and even that Ford-administration project feels light years more advanced than people driving Teslas slowly driving in tunnels does today.

我的意思是，它不像自动短期的人携带系统不存在：Morgantown，西弗吉尼亚州的自动交通系统自1975年以来，甚至那个福特行政项目甚至比人民更加先进的驾驶Teslas今天慢慢地驾驶隧道。

The PRT also moves about 16,000 riders per day on average (though it’s been proven to move almost double that number) over a 3.6 mile loop, for a cost of $62 million back in 1975. In today’s dollars that would be over $300 million, which is more than the Vegas loop cost, but also is about three and a half times bigger and moves many more passengers.

该PRT平均每天每天大约16,000名骑手移动（尽管已被证明将数量几乎翻倍移动）超过3.6英里的循环，其成本为1975年的6200,000美元。在今天的美元中，这将超过3亿美元，其中不仅仅是拉斯维加斯循环成本，而且也大约是三倍，更大的时间更多，移动了更多的乘客。

Also, Space Mountain would fit in a lot better with the whole Vegas aesthetic, really.

此外，空间山脉会在整个Vegas美学方面更好地适应很多。

Maybe at some point higher-capacity vehicles will be in the tunnel or it’ll expand to be the full proposed Vegas Loop or, I don’t know something will change to make any of this make actual sense.

也许在某些时候，更高容量的车辆将在隧道中，或者它将扩展到完整的拉斯维加斯循环，或者，我不知道有些东西会改变，以使任何这一切都做出实际意义。

The system is also supposed to work like a ride-hailing service, though with three points to get in and out of the tunnel and planned stations, I don’t see how that will really work?

该系统也应该像乘车一样的服务，但是有三个要点进出隧道和计划的车站，我看不到它真正有效的？

As it stands now, we have a few Teslas driving around in long, narrow loops under the convention center, saving you a bit of walking but doing every other part of the job of moving people worse than almost any other solution.

正如现在所掌握的那样，我们在会议中心的长期狭窄的循环中有几个特斯拉斯驾驶，节省了一些散步，但是在几乎任何其他解决方案中都会散步，但是每隔一部分移动的人的工作。

Profile of MrBeast, the most subscribed YouTuber in the US, who took a bumpy road to fame through stunt philanthropy, as he branches out into other businesses
MRBEST，美国最订阅的Youtuber的档案，曾经通过特技慈善作用的颠簸道路，因为他分支到其他企业

2021-05-06 00:21:32

Jimmy Donaldson, 22, a YouTube megastar better known as Mr. Beast, operates six YouTube channels with names like MrBeast, MrBeast Shorts and Beast Reacts that together have 91 million subscribers. People have viewed his videos more than 13 billion times. And last year as the pandemic raged, he became the most-subscribed YouTube creator in the country.

22岁吉米唐纳森，一位YouTube Megastar更像是野兽先生，使用像MRBeast，MRBeast短裤和野兽一样的名字，共同拥有9100万用户的名字。人们已浏览他的视频超过130亿次。去年随着大流行的肆虐，他成为该国最先订阅的YouTube创作者。

But unlike other YouTubers who have been satisfied with social media success, Mr. Donaldson wants more.

但与其他对社交媒体成功的其他人来说不同，Donaldson先生想要更多。

“I really want to be Elon one day,” he wrote last year on Twitter, referring to Elon Musk, the chief executive of Tesla and SpaceX and one of the world’s richest people. Mr. Donaldson later listed Mr. Musk as his “ dream collaborator.”

“我真的想成为伊隆人，”他去年写在Twitter上，参考了特斯拉和太斯克萨克的首席执行官和世界上最富有人之一的Elon Musk。 Donaldson先生后来将Musk先生作为他的“梦想合作者”。

To be more like Elon, Mr. Donaldson has been busy using his brand to make inroads in the business and tech worlds. He has launched a mobile gaming app and a “ ghost kitchen” chain, MrBeast Burger, which has sold more than one million burgers. In March, he became an adviser to a financial network that provides business tools to online content creators. Last month, he signed a deal with a company to distribute his content across a range of social media platforms.

更像是艾龙，唐纳森先生忙着使用他的品牌在商业和科技世界中进入。他推出了一个手机游戏应用程序和“鬼厨”链，MRBeast Burger，已售出超过一百万汉堡。 3月，他成为金融网络的顾问，为在线内容创建者提供业务工具。上个月，他与一家公司签署了一项协议，在各种社交媒体平台上分发他的内容。

What Mr. Donaldson is doing may serve as a blueprint for the next generation of creators and influencers, who now number more than 50 million around the world and have become a formidable business force. The creator economy is the “fastest-growing type of small business,” according to a report last year by SignalFire, a venture capital firm.

Donaldson先生正在做的是，这是下一代创作者和影响者的蓝图，他们现在遍布全球超过5000万，并成为一个强大的企业力量。创造商经济是“增长最快的小型企业”，据某个风险投资公司去年的报告称。

“Mr. Beast is one of those creators who’s an incredibly savvy entrepreneur,” said Wayne Hu, a partner at SignalFire. “He’s got a fantastic content strategy, but at the same time he’s equally innovative and prolific in the experiments he’s running to monetize his audience.”

“先生。野兽是那些是一个令人难以置信的精明企业家的创造者之一，“信号火伴侣的伙伴说。 “他有一个梦幻般的内容战略，但同时他同样创新和多产的实验，他正在努力将他的观众货币化。”

Yet Mr. Donaldson’s rise as a creator-entrepreneur, like that of many a YouTuber of his ilk, has been far from smooth. His early videos and posts were criticized for their use of slurs and offensive jokes. Several former employees said the working conditions at his companies — Mr. Donaldson has set up a variety of corporate entities including MrBeastYouTube, MrBeastCrypto and Rubber Duck Company — have been rife with favoritism and bullying.

然而，Donaldson先生作为一个创造者企业家的崛起，就像他伊利克的许多Youtuber一样，这一直远离光滑。他的早期视频和帖子因其使用逃亡和冒犯笑话而受到批评。若干前员工表示，他的公司的工作条件 - Donaldson先生已经成立了各种公司实体，包括MRBeastyoutube，MRBeastcrypto和橡皮鸭公司 - 一直享受偏袒和欺凌。

Last month, Mr. Donaldson also faced backlash from fans who lost significant amounts of money on a cryptocurrency scheme he had promoted and invested in. And he is trying to maintain rapid growth on a notoriously fickle platform. How he does and how his companies perform will be scrutinized to see whether creators can parlay their social media success into a sustainable array of businesses.

上个月，Donaldson先生还面临着粉丝的反对，他们在他晋升和投入的加密货币上损失了大量资金。他正试图在一个令人惊叹的森林平台上保持快速增长。他如何以及如何审查他的公司的表现，以了解创造者是否可以将社会媒体的成功纳入可持续的业务。

Mr. Donaldson declined to be interviewed. A representative for him declined to address the working conditions at his companies but said of the videos with offensive content: “When Jimmy was a teenager and was first starting out, he carelessly used, on more than one occasion, a gay slur. Jimmy knows there is no excuse for homophobic rhetoric.” The representative added that Mr. Donaldson “has grown up and matured into someone that doesn’t speak like that.”

唐纳森先生拒绝接受采访。他的代表拒绝解决他公司的工作条件，而是通过进攻内容的视频说：“当Jimmy是一个少年时，他首先开始，他不小心地使用了一个以上的同性恋者。吉米知道同性恋言论没有借口。“该代表补充说，唐纳森先生“已经成长并成熟到那些不那样的人身上。”

“I think Mr. Beast inspires all of Gen Z,” said Josh Richards, 19, a TikTok creator in Los Angeles with nearly 25 million followers. “He’s giving a lot of kids a new path to take, to teach these young kids on how to be entrepreneurial, not just to get a lot of views or become famous.”

“我认为野兽先生激发了所有的Gen Z，”Josh Richards，19，一个洛杉矶的Tiktok Creator，洛杉矶有近2500万追随者。 “他给了很多孩子的新道路，教这些年轻的孩子如何成为创业，而不仅仅是为了得到很多意见或成名。”

Like many members of Generation Z, Mr. Donaldson, who grew up in Greenville, N.C., founded a YouTube channel when he was in middle school, back in 2012.

就像生成Z的许多成员一样，在格林维尔，N.C中长大的唐纳森先生在中学时创立了一个YouTube频道，回到2012年。

To crack YouTube’s recommendation algorithm, he initially cycled through different genres of video making. He posted videos of himself playing games like Call of Duty, commented on YouTube drama, uploaded funny video compilations and livestreamed himself reacting to videos on the internet.

为了破解YouTube的推荐算法，他最初通过不同的视频制作循环。他发布了自己玩职责的游戏的视频，评论了YouTube戏剧，上传了有趣的视频编译，并将自己的反应对互联网上的视频作出反应。

Then in 2018, he mastered the format that would make him a star: stunt philanthropy. Mr. Donaldson filmed himself giving away thousands of dollars in cash to random people, including his Uber driver or people experiencing homelessness, capturing their shock and joy in the process. The money initially came mostly from brand sponsorships.

然后在2018年，他掌握了使他成为明星的格式：特许慈善事业。 Donaldson先生拍摄自己以随机的人赠送数千美元，包括他的优步司机或遇到无家可归的人，在这个过程中捕捉他们的震惊和喜悦。这笔钱最初来自品牌赞助。

It turned out to be a perfect viral recipe that mixed money, a larger-than-life persona and wholesome reactions. Millions began watching his YouTube videos. Mr. Donaldson soon rebranded himself as “YouTube’s biggest philanthropist.”

事实证明，成为一个完美的病毒食谱，混合金钱，较大的人的角色和有益健康的反应。数百万开始观看他的YouTube视频。 Donaldson先生很快将自己作为“Youtube最大的慈善家”重新建立。

The combination was also lucrative. Though Mr. Donaldson gave away increasingly large amounts — from $ 100,000 to $1 million — he made it all back and more with the advertising that ran alongside the videos. He also sold merchandise like socks ($18), water bottles ($27) and T-shirts ($28).

该组合也有利可图。虽然Donaldson先生越来越大的金额 - 从100,000美元到100万美元 - 他已经回来了，更多地与视频一起奔跑。他还销售了袜子（18美元），水瓶（27美元）和T恤（28美元）。

“Once you know how to make a video go viral, it’s just about how to get as many out as possible,” he told Bloomberg in December. “You can practically make unlimited money.”

“一旦你知道如何制作视频去病毒，那就就是如何尽可能多地得到，”他在12月讲布鲁伯格。 “你几乎可以做出无限的钱。”

By 2018, Mr. Donaldson had about a dozen people working for him in a small office in Greenville. In early 2019, he moved his companies into a larger warehouse. His work force had grown to around two dozen, people who worked for him said, though it was hard to keep track because of an expanding group of contract production assistants who helped on video shoots.

到2018年，Donaldson先生在格林维尔的一个小型办公室里有十几个人工作。 2019年初，他将他的公司搬进了一个更大的仓库。他的劳动力已经发展到了大约二十大约，为他工作的人说，虽然很难跟踪，因为一组有助于视频拍摄的合同生产助手。

Many of his employees were part of a network of friends, family and acquaintances. Close friends acted as co-stars in his videos and developed YouTube followings off him.

他的许多员工都是朋友，家庭和熟人网络的一部分。亲密的朋友在他的视频中担任过集团，并开发了他的outtube追随者。

“When you’re a YouTuber starting out, it’s like a family,” said Josh Hyman, 35, a writer in Greenville who said he enjoyed working for Mr. Donaldson. “Everyone employed there was a friend of a friend.”

“当你是一个youtuber开始时，它就像一个家庭，”格林维尔的一位作家乔希·凯曼说，他说他喜欢唐纳森先生致力于努力。 “每个人都雇用了朋友的朋友。”

Last year, Mr. Donaldson expanded his business. In his gaming app, introduced in June, users were told to hold a finger down on the phone screen as long as possible. The last person to take the finger off won a cash prize up to $25,000. The app was such a hit that Mr. Donaldson relaunched it in March, raising the prize to $100,000.

去年，唐纳森先生扩大了他的业务。在他6月推出的游戏应用程序中，用户被告知用户尽可能长时间握住手机屏幕。将手指脱落的最后一个人赢得了最高可达25,000美元的现金奖项。该应用程序如此受欢迎，即唐纳森先生在3月份重新启动它，将奖品提高到100,000美元。

Mr. Donaldson also invested in tech start-ups, including a new type of gaming controller. In December, he started MrBeast Burger, which does not have a physical presence but allows diners to order food through delivery apps like Grubhub. He joined the audio chat app Clubhouse this year and became a regular, hobnobbing with investors and tech executives.

Donaldson先生还投资于技术初创企业，包括一种新型的游戏控制器。 12月，他开始了Mrbeast汉堡，没有身体存在，但允许食客通过Grubhub等交付应用程序订购食物。他今年加入了音频聊天App Clubhouse，并成为投资者和技术高管的常规，幸福。

In March, Mr. Donaldson teamed up with the Creative Juice financial network to introduce Juice Funds, a $2 million investment fund that offers creators up to $250,000 in exchange for equity in their YouTube channels. Many fans praised him on social media for personally investing his money, though the funding came from Creative Juice investors.

3月份，唐纳森先生与创意果汁金融网络联合起来，介绍果汁基金，一个200万美元的投资基金，提供高达25万美元的创作者以换取youtube频道的公平。许多粉丝赞扬了他在社交媒体上，虽然资金来自创意果汁投资者。

Sima Gandhi, chief executive of Creative Juice, said that the company planned to work with many creators on Juice Funds and that Mr. Donaldson was the first. “Our aspiration is to roll out a series of funds with different partner creators,” she said.

Creative Juice的首席执行官Sima Gandhi表示，该公司计划与果汁基金的许多创造者合作，唐纳森先生是第一个。 “我们的愿望是推出一系列与不同的合作伙伴创造者的资金，”她说。

Last month, Mr. Donaldson signed a partnership with Jellysmack, which will distribute and monetize Mr. Beast content across Facebook, Snapchat and other platforms.

上个月，Donaldson先生与Jellysmack签署了伙伴关系，将在Facebook，Snapchat和其他平台上分发和货币化野兽内容先生。

In Greenville, many went to work for Mr. Donaldson because of the philanthropic nature of his videos, said 11 people who worked for or with him, four of whom asked to remain anonymous because they had signed confidentiality agreements. While he was sometimes generous, they said, his demeanor changed when the cameras weren’t around. They described a difficult work environment.

在格林维尔，许多人在他的视频的慈善性质上为唐纳森先生工作，11人曾经为他或与他工作过，其中四个人被要求保持匿名，因为他们签了保密协议。他们说，虽然他有时慷慨，但是当相机不在身边时，他的风度变化了。他们描述了一个艰难的工作环境。

Matt Turner, 20, an editor for Mr. Donaldson from February 2018 to September 2019, said Mr. Donaldson had berated him almost every day. Mr. Donaldson often called him by a phrase used to insult people with mental disabilities, Mr. Turner said, leaving him in tears.

Matt Turner，20，唐纳森先生于2018年2月至2019年9月，唐纳森先生几乎每天都击败了他。特纳先生说，唐纳森先生经常被用来侮辱心理残疾人的短语。

Mr. Turner, who did not grow up with Mr. Donaldson, said that while his boss regularly featured his hometown friends in videos, he had struggled to get acknowledgment.

特纳先生没有与唐纳德先生长大的人说，虽然他的老板经常出现在视频中的嘉宾，但他努力得到承认。

“I was not to be credited for anything I did,” Mr. Turner said. “I’d ask for credit, he’d credit someone else.”

“特纳先生说：”我不记得我所做的任何事情。“ “我会要求信誉，他会赞同别人。”

Nate Anderson, 22, who moved to Greenville to work for Mr. Donaldson in March 2018, quit after a week over what he said were unreasonable demands. He said Mr. Donaldson was a perfectionist.

22岁的Nate Anderson于2018年3月搬到Greenville致力于Donaldson先生，在一周后戒掉了他所说的是不合理的要求。他说唐纳森先生是一个完美主义者。

“Nothing ever worked for him,” Mr. Anderson said. “He always wanted it a certain way.”

“没有任何努力为他工作，”安德森先生说。 “他总是以某种方式想要它。”

Mr. Anderson later uploaded to his YouTube channel a tell-all video, “My Experience Editing for Mr. Beast (Worst Week of My Life),” which he said had brought death threats and hateful comments from Mr. Beast’s dedicated fans, many of whom are children.

安德森先生后来上传到了他的YouTube频道一个告诉所有视频，“我对野兽先生（我生命中最糟糕的一周）的经验编辑，”他说，他说从野兽的专门粉丝先生带来了死亡威胁和仇恨评论谁是孩子。

Mr. Turner had a similar experience. “These kids don’t even know what they’re saying,” he said. “They’re just in this cult of YouTube stardom where they don’t want to see their biggest creator fall.”

特纳先生有类似的经验。 “这些孩子甚至不知道他们在说什么，”他说。 “他们只是在这种崇拜的友情中，他们不想看到他们最大的创造者堕落。”

Akash Rathod, 20, a sophomore in college and a Mr. Beast fan, said he was troubled by Mr. Donaldson’s silence on online mob attacks.

艾克斯·罗斯特（Akash Rathod）在大学和野兽·粉丝先生的大学生表示，他对唐纳森先生对在线暴徒袭击的沉默感到困扰。

“There needs to be more from Mr. Beast on the issues his fans are causing,” he said. “It’s not enough just to make positive videos.”

他说：“他的粉丝造成的问题需要更多来自野兽先生的问题。” “只是为了制作积极的视频是不够的。”

The behind-the-scenes strife has been imperceptible to those who watch Mr. Donaldson’s videos casually.

幕后的冲突对那些随便观看Donaldson先生的视频的人来说是不可知的。

Ryan Morey, 25, a software developer in New Jersey who prefers nontraditional pronouns and Mx. for a courtesy title, often watches Mr. Donaldson’s videos with their partner, Emily Woods, 24, over dinner. They became fans of Mr. Donaldson’s videos after YouTube’s recommendations algorithm surfaced his content.

Ryan Morey，25，新泽西州的软件开发人员喜欢非传统代词和MX。对于礼貌的冠军，经常用他们的伴侣，艾米莉伍兹，24岁时观看Donaldson先生的视频。在YouTube的建议算法浮出水面后，他们成为Donaldson的视频的粉丝。

“He takes a lot of inspiration directly from Elon Musk for better or for worse,” Mx. Morey said. “It’s the cult of personality, all ambition all the time.”

“他直接从伊隆麝香尿了很多灵感，无论好坏，”MX。莫雷说。 “这是个性的崇拜，一直抱着所有野心。”

Ms. Woods said she had recently bought a Mr. Beast sweatshirt and baseball cap, partly because she felt that giving him her money would lead to some good in the world. In 2019, Mr. Donaldson planted 20 million trees to celebrate hitting 20 million subscribers. In March, he started a YouTube channel, Beast Philanthropy, which has grown to over 3.2 million subscribers.

伍兹女士说，她最近买了一位野兽先生和棒球帽，部分原因是她觉得给他她的钱会导致世界上有些好事。 2019年，Donaldson先生种植了2000万棵树，以庆祝击中2000万用户。三月开始，他开始了一个YouTube渠道，野兽慈善事业，已经发展到超过320万用户。

Mr. Donaldson now employs about 50 people, according to his representative. Former employees said a core of a dozen writers, editors and production assistants worked tirelessly to pull off stunts like buying everything in a store, giving people $1 million and only a minute to spend it, building the world’s largest Lego tower or buying an island.

根据他的代表，Donaldson先生现在雇用了大约50人。前雇员表示，十几个作家的核心，编辑和制作助理不知疲倦地努力推出特技，如在商店购买一切，给予100万美元，只需一分钟，建立世界上最大的乐高塔或购买岛屿。

His primary YouTube channel alone is generating up to $3.1 million a month, according to data from SocialBlade, a YouTube analytics service. At the current rate of growth, Mr. Donaldson is projected to overtake PewDiePie as the most subscribed creator in the world in the next year, according to SocialBlade.

根据社会博客的数据，他单独的主要YouTube频道每月为每月产生高达310万美元的渠道，这是一个YouTube Analytics服务。根据社会经尔的说法，在目前的增长率下，Donaldson先生预计将在明年将Pewdiepie作为世界上最认可的创造者。

“He believes that you can do whatever you want in today’s environment,” said Dee Murthy, chief executive of the Five Four Group, a brand incubator in Los Angeles.

“他认为，您可以在当今的环境中做任何您想要的事情，”五四集团首席执行官Dee Murthy表示，这是洛杉矶的品牌孵化器。

“You’re going to see this proliferation of creators creating these very disruptive businesses,” he said.

“你将看到创造者的这种扩散创造了这些非常繁体的企业，”他说。

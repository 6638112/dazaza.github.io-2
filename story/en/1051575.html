Macabre Mystery of a British Family’s Skull-Topped Spoons
英国家庭头骨顶级勺子的玛雅神秘

2021-03-11 04:34:45

In 1904, a collector named J.T. Micklethwaite brought a rare “death’s-head” spoon to a meeting of the Society of Antiquaries of London. Declaring it “too grim to be put to ordinary use,” he pointed to the engraved skull at the top of its stem, complemented by the message “Live to Die” on one side and “Die to Live” on the other. Micklethwaite theorized that the 17th-century spoon must have been a funerary gift. While such mementos—including funeral rings and funeral biscuits—were not uncommon at the time, Micklethwaite was looking at the wrong end of life: The macabre piece of silverware was likely a gift for a baby.

1904年，一个名为J.T的收藏家。 Micklethwaite将一个罕见的“死亡人头”汤匙带到了伦敦大陆社会的会议上。宣称它“太长了普通使用”，他指着茎上的雕刻的头骨，辅以一边的消息“活着死去”，另一方面“死于生活”。 Micklethwaite理论上，17世纪的勺子一定是葬礼礼物。虽然这种纪念品包括葬礼戒指和葬礼饼干 - 当时并不少见，Micklethwaite看着错误的生活结束：Macabre一块银器可能是一个婴儿的礼物。

There are just a handful of original death’s-head spoons in museums and private collections today. Designed between the 1660s and late 1670s, they all bear the skull, the morbid mottoes, and the coat of arms of the Stricklands of Boynton Hall. A wealthy Yorkshire clan, the family’s prosperity can be traced back to William Strickland, a navigator who accompanied Sebastian Cabot to the Americas and claimed to have introduced the turkey to England in the mid-1500s. (To catch a glimpse of this legacy, visitors can stop by the family’s memorial chapel, where bibles rest atop a turkey-shaped lectern.) With the earnings from his voyages, William purchased several estates, including Boynton. Some Strickland descendants parlayed their prestige into parliamentary positions; others used the family funds to buy skull-topped spoons. But who commissioned the silverware and, more importantly, why?

今天在博物馆和私人收藏中只有一部分原始死亡勺子。在1660年代和1670年代后期之间设计，他们都承受了骷髅般的博物馆的头骨，病态的塔架和博伊顿大厅的斯特里克兰的徽章。一个富裕的约克郡氏族，家庭的繁荣可以追溯到William Strickland，这是一个陪同Sebastian Cabot的导航员到美洲，并声称在15世纪中期将土耳其推向英国。 （为了瞥见这种遗产，游客可以在家庭的纪念教堂停下来，圣经在土耳其讲台上休息。）随着他的航行的收益，威廉购买了几个庄园，包括Boynton。一些斯里克兰后代将他们的声望陷入议会职位;其他人使用家庭资金购买头骨顶级的勺子。但谁委托银器，更重要的是，为什么？

“It was purely because of the skull that people thought it was a funeral spoon,” says David Constable, who investigates the provenance of the death’s-head spoons in his book Silver Spoons of Britain 1200–1710. To debunk the funeral theory, Constable points to the year each spoon was designed. Historic British silver includes helpful hallmarks noting its creation year and region. Constable, who owns a death’s-head spoon from the early 1660s, compared the year of each spoon’s creation with Strickland birth records and realized that the dates lined up closely with the birth years of Strickland children. He concluded that the silver artifacts were originally christening gifts commissioned by Lady Frances Strickland, the children’s grandmother.

“这纯粹是因为人们认为这是一个葬礼勺子，”大卫警员说，他调查了他在英国书中银匙的死亡勺子的出处.1200-1710。揭穿葬礼理论，每个勺子都设计了一年的警员。历史悠久的英国银包括注意到其创建年和地区的有用的标志。在16世纪60年代初拥有死亡的头勺，将每个勺子的创造年度与斯特里克兰出生记录相比，并意识到与斯特里克兰儿童的出生年度紧密排队的日期。他得出结论，银色文物最初是由弗朗西斯夫人斯特里克兰夫人委托的洗礼礼品。

“You’ve heard of the saying ‘born with a silver spoon in your mouth,’” Constable says. “I think she was trying to give that memento of the silver spoon and to really personalize it.”

“你听说过你嘴里的银色勺子的说法，”威尔布尔说。 “我觉得她正试图给那些银勺的纪念，并真正个性化它。”

The family’s silverware even inspired riffs on the original Strickland skull-spoon style. The Merchant Adventurers’ Hall, a museum in York, has an original Strickland death’s-head and another family’s version in its collection. Along with bearing the arms of the Cromptons of Driffield, the other spoon diverges from the original death’s-head style in several ways: While the Strickland spoons feature a stem that ends in a disc shape and block lettering that spells out “Live to Die” on the front, the Cromptons’ stem ends in a three-pronged design and features “Dye to Live” in cursive on the front.

这个家庭的银器甚至激发了原始斯特里克兰头骨 - 勺子风格的侵略。 Merchant Adventurers's Hall是约克博物馆，拥有一个原始的Strickland死亡人员和另一个家庭版本。伴随着Driffield的克隆龙的臂，另一个勺子以多种方式从原始死亡人员的头部风格分流：虽然斯特里克兰勺子具有以圆盘形状结束的茎，但块刻字阐述“活着”的刻痕刻字在前面，克朗佩顿的茎在前面的三管齐下的设计中以三管齐下的设计结束，并在前面的法学中的“染料”。

“I think what happened is people saw the Strickland family spoons and thought, ‘That sounds rather good. Let’s give it as a christening present to our daughter or son,’” Constable says.

“我认为，人们看到了斯特里克兰家庭勺子和思想，这听起来很好。让它成为礼物给我们的女儿或儿子的洗礼，“”威尔布尔说。

While it might seem odd to remind newborns of their mortality, it wasn’t unusual for 17th-century families to commemorate happy milestones such as christenings or weddings with death-focused art. Skulls were part of the memento mori motif, which reminded viewers to contemplate their inevitable end. In a 1560 marriage portrait, “The Judde Memorial,” newlyweds sternly pose beside a skull with a corpse in the foreground. (Lest anyone miss the point, a romantic inscription on the frame adds, “When we are deade and in owr graves, and all owre bones are rottun, by this shall we rememberd be, when we shulde be forgottyn.”) And just like a skull-topped christening gift, 17th-century vanitas paintings underscored the cycle of life by connecting birth and death with scenes of babies sleeping atop skulls.

虽然提醒新生儿可能似乎是奇怪的，但是17世纪的家庭对克里斯滕或婚礼等艺术等幸福的里程碑来说，这并不罕见。骷髅是纪念摩尔图案的一部分，这提醒观众思考他们的不可避免的结局。在一个1560婚姻肖像中，“犹太纪念馆”，新婚夫妇严厉地摆在一个头骨旁边，在前景中的尸体。 （以免任何人都错过了这一点，框架上的浪漫铭文增加了，“当我们陷入困境时，所有的猫头鹰都是罗伦，我们都会记住，当我们Shulde被忘记时，我们会记住。”）和就像17世纪的Vanitas绘画，17世纪的颅骨充值礼物通过连接出生和死亡的婴儿睡觉的婴儿的场景来强调生命周期。

“Back then, mortality was a more of an ever-present thing because you didn’t live as long,” says Lauren Marshall, the museum director at the Merchant Adventurers’ Hall. “And then you had a god-fearing society where everyone went to church and there was this idea of living a good, pious life because when you die, you’ll be judged. The skull being ever-present showed that death was always just around the corner, so it’s best to be good now.”

“那时，死亡率是一个永远存在的东西，因为你没有生活，”商人冒险家大厅博物馆总监Lauren Marshall说。 “然后你有一个令人敬畏的社会，每个人都去教堂，有这个虔诚的生活理念，因为当你死时，你会被判断。永远存在的头骨表明，死亡总是在拐角处，所以现在最好是擅长。“

At the Merchant Adventurers’ Hall, the skull-topped spoons continue to compel visitors of all ages to contemplate their mortality. “No matter how old you are or how young you are, they’re fascinating,” Marshall says. “They’re some of my favorite items in the collection because they elicit such a strong response from visitors. A lot of what we talk about can be quite dry, but this is interesting to everybody.”

在商人冒险家大厅，头骨顶部的勺子继续强迫所有年龄段的游客思考他们的死亡率。 “无论你多大年纪或你是多么年轻，他们都很迷人，”马歇尔说。 “他们是我最喜欢的一些物品，因为他们引起了来自访客的强烈反应。我们谈论的很多东西可以很干燥，但这对每个人都很有趣。“

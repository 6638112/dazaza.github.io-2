Alternative Music Notations

Many people struggle to learn to read and play music, and many give up before they become proficient. Could a better notation system make reading, writing, and playing music more enjoyable and easier to learn? We think so.

许多人努力学习阅读和播放音乐，并且在他们变得熟练之前放弃了。一个更好的符号系统可以让阅读，写作和播放音乐更愉快，更容易学习？我们这么认为。

Here is a chromatic scale on a traditional diatonic staff (above) and the same chromatic scale on a chromatic staff with five lines (below). This is just one of many versions of chromatic staff.

以下是传统的达阳员工（上图）的色标，以及五条线（下图）的色彩工作人员在相同的色标。这只是许多版本的彩色员工之一。

On a chromatic staff each note has its own line or space on the staff. On the traditional staff only seven notes have their own line or space, the notes from just one key (C major/A minor, the white keys on the piano). The remaining notes (the black keys) have to be represented by altering these seven notes with sharp signs (#) or flat signs (b), either in the key signature or as an accidental.

在彩色工作人员上，每个音符都有自己的线或空间。在传统员工只有七个笔记有自己的线条或空间，只有一个钥匙的笔记（C major / a minor，钢琴上的白色钥匙）。剩下的备注（黑色键）必须通过用尖锐的标志（＃）或平面标志（b）来改变这七个笔记，或者在关键签名或偶然的情况下来表示。

The chromatic staff approach can make music easier to read, play, and understand by improving upon aspects of traditional notation such as key signatures, accidentals, clefs, octaves, and intervals. The rest of this page shows how.

彩色员工方法可以使音乐更容易阅读，播放和理解，通过改善传统符号等关键签名，意外，克利夫斯，八度和间隔等方面。此页面的其余部分显示了方法。

There are many variations on this chromatic staff theme, with a number of different line patterns (see Notation Systems). We use a basic five-line version just to introduce the concept, not to suggest that it is the best one. We do not promote a particular alternative notation system, but document a variety of them and teach the general principles on which they are based.

这些色彩员工主题有许多变化，具有许多不同的线条模式（参见符号系统）。我们使用基本的五行版本只是为了介绍这个概念，而不是表明它是最好的。我们不宣传特定的替代符号系统，而是文档各种各样的文件，并教授他们所在的一般原则。

“The need for a new notation, or a radical improvement of the old, is greater than it seems, and the number of ingenious minds that have tackled the problem is greater than one might think.” — Arnold Schoenberg [1]

“对新的符号的需要或旧的激进改善大于它似乎，以及解决问题的巧妙思想的数量大于一个人可能会想到的。” - Arnold Schoenberg [1]

There are fifteen different key signatures to memorize in traditional notation, and one must always keep the current key signature in mind while playing. With a chromatic staff this is not necessary since a note’s position on the staff directly indicates what note to play. All keys are equally easy to read.

在传统的符号中记住，有十五个不同的关键签名，并且必须在玩时始终牢记当前的关键签名。由于笔记在工作人员对工作人员的立场直接表明播放的票据，因此没有必要。所有键都同样容易读取。

The traditional staff is based upon the notes from the key of C major and closely related tonalities. This makes all of the other keys and tonalities more difficult to read, even if they are just as easy to play on a given instrument. To become proficient in all keys one must master fifteen different key signatures which are progressively more complex and difficult to learn.

传统工作人员基于C重点和密切相关的色调的关键的票据。这使得所有其他键和色调更难以阅读，即使它们在给定的仪器上也很容易播放。为了精通所有钥匙，必须掌握十五个不同的关键签名，这些关键签名逐渐变得更加复杂，难以学习。

On a chromatic staff all of the keys are equally easy to read. There is no need to memorize and remember key signatures. Reading music is more direct as you simply play the notes that you see, without having to filter them through a memorized key signature.

在彩色的工作人员上，所有键都同样容易阅读。没有必要记住和记住关键签名。阅读音乐更直接，因为您只需播放您看到的音符，而无需通过记忆密钥签名来过滤它们。

In traditional notation, key signatures not only alter the pitches of the lines and spaces but also alert the reader to the music’s predominant key. With a chromatic staff, a much simpler alternative key signature system can be used for the latter purpose, helping the musician know which notes to expect.

在传统的符号中，关键签名不仅改变了线条和空间的音高，而且还提醒读者对音乐的主要密钥。通过彩色员工，可以使用更简单的替代关键签名系统来用于后者的目的，帮助音乐家知道预期的注释。

In traditional notation accidental signs must be used to represent accidentals — notes that are not in the current key signature. On a chromatic staff they are not needed since all notes have their own position on the staff, making it easier to see each note’s pitch and how it relates to other notes.

在传统的符号中，必须使用意外迹象来表示偶然的 - 不在当前关键签名中的笔记。在彩色员工上，他们不需要，因为所有笔记都有自己的工作人员自己的立场，使得更容易看到每个音符的音高以及它如何与其他笔记有关。

Music that requires many accidental signs in traditional notation can be visually challenging to read, especially if it is in an unfamiliar and complex key signature. The rule that an accidental sign applies to a given pitch until the end of the measure, unless it is cancelled by another one, opens up room for mistakes and gives the musician one more thing to remember. There is often confusion about whether an accidental applies to other octaves of the same note (it generally does not).

需要在传统符号中需要许多意外迹象的音乐可以在视觉上挑战阅读，特别是如果它处于陌生和复杂的重点签名。意外标志适用于给定音调的规则，直到措施结束，除非它被另一个人取消，否则向上出现错误，并让音乐家再记得更多。是否偶然适用于同一票据的其他八度（通常不会）。

On a chromatic staff accidental signs are not needed because each of the twelve notes of the chromatic scale has its own unique position on the staff, regardless of whether it is in the prevailing key. This makes it easier to identify the pitch of accidental notes and to see how they relate to surrounding notes.

在彩色员工的偶然标志上不需要，因为彩色刻度的每一十二张音符中的每一个都有自己独特的工作人员，无论它是否处于现行关键。这使得更容易识别意外票据的音高，并看出它们与周围备注的关系。

Optional “alternative accidental signs” may be used to simply indicate that a note falls outside of the current key (without affecting the note’s pitch), or they may be used to distinguish between enharmonically equivalent notes if desired (see Enharmonic Equivalents tutorial).

可选的“替代意外符号”可用于简单地指示音符落在当前键之外（不影响音符的音高），或者它们可以用于区分在需要的增强态等效音符（参见增强臂等同物教程）。

In traditional notation, staves that look the same may represent different sets of notes, depending on the clef symbol. On a typical chromatic staff the lines and spaces always represents the same notes, and an octave or register symbol simply indicates the staff’s pitch range.

在传统的符号中，看起来同样的柱子可以代表不同的笔记，具体取决于谱号符号。在典型的彩色员工上，线条和空间总是表示相同的音符，八度音阶或寄存器符号只是表示员工的音高范围。

Traditional notation is commonly written in four different clefs: treble, bass, alto, and tenor. Each clef changes which notes are represented by the lines and spaces of the staff. Keyboard players have the additional challenge of learning to read both treble and bass clefs at the same time. Key signatures appear at a different vertical position in each of the different clefs (see key signatures above).

传统符号通常用四种不同的谱号：高音，低音，阿尔托和男高音。每个谱号更改哪些笔记由员工的线条和空间表示。键盘玩家有额外的挑战，学习同时阅读高音和低音谱号。关键签名显示在每个不同谱系中的不同垂直位置（见上面的关键签名）。

On a traditional staff, two notes an octave apart do not look alike. If a note falls on a line, the note an octave higher or lower will fall on a space (and vice-versa).

在传统的员工，两个音符分开了一个八度，看起来不一样。如果一个纸条落在一条线上，请注意八度音高或更低的空间将落在空间（反之亦然）。

On chromatic staves like the one above, notes an octave apart look the same. Notes are easy to identify since a given note always has the same appearance regardless of its octave.

在上面的彩色塔顶上，音符除了看起来相同的八度。注意易于识别，因为给定的注释始终具有相同的外观，而不管其八度音程如何。

The traditional staff is based on an odd number of seven notes per octave (a diatonic scale), which is why notes an octave apart do not both appear on lines (or both on spaces). Chromatic staves are based on an even number of twelve notes per octave (a chromatic scale), so notes an octave apart can both fall on lines (or both on spaces).

传统的员工基于每个八度（Diatonic Sc​​ale）的奇数七个音符，这就是为什么注意到八度音程并不同时出现在线（或空格上）。色笛子基于每个八度（彩色刻度）的偶数十二个音符，因此笔记分开的速度可以落在线条上（或在空间上）。

Most chromatic staves “cycle on the octave” so that each octave looks the same. This allows them to be ‘stacked’ to form a continuous staff that spans two or more octaves as shown above. This accommodates the different pitch ranges of various instruments and reduces the need to use ledger lines.

大多数彩色柱子“octave上的周期”，以便每个八度度看起来都是一样的。这允许它们“堆叠”形成一个连续的工作人员，跨越两个或多个八度八页的八度高，如上所示。这适应各种仪器的不同间距范围，并减少了使用分类线的需要。

(Note that alternative notation systems that use a diatonic staff with irregularly spaced lines can also cycle on the octave so that notes an octave apart look the same. See for example, Nydana Notation.)

（请注意，使用具有不规则间隔线的替代符号系统，也可以循环八位曲面，以便延期八波视外观相同。例如，nydana表示法。）

Intervals that look the same in traditional notation may not be the same interval. In this illustration, whole steps and half steps are visually indistinguishable, as are major and minor thirds. What you see does not always correspond with what you hear and must play.

在传统符号中看起来相同的间隔可能不具有相同的间隔。在这个例证中，全部步骤和半步在视觉上无法区分，正如主要和次要的三分之一。你所看到的并不总是与你所听到的东西相对应。

Chromatic staves represent intervals more consistently and more accurately. As shown in this illustration of a C major scale, whole steps are always two notes on two neighboring lines or on two neighboring spaces. Half steps are always one note on a line and another on a neighboring space.

色笛子更加始终如一，更准确地表示间隔。如C重大规模的这个例证所示，整个步骤总是两个相邻线或两个相邻空间上的两个音符。半阶段总是在一条线上的一个音符，另一个在邻近空间上。

Traditional notation obscures the interval relationships between notes. For example, a major scale (a series of whole steps and half steps, as shown above) appears as a regular sequence of notes, while a whole tone scale (a series of identical whole steps) appears as an irregular sequence.

传统符号掩盖了笔记之间的间隔关系。例如，主要刻度（一系列整个步骤和半步，如上所示）显示为常规音符序列，而整个音调刻度（一系列相同的整个步骤）出现为不规则序列。

To fully identify an interval one must take into account the current clef sign, key signature, and any accidentals, going through the mental procedure of calculating the names of the individual notes before their interval relationship can be determined.

为了完全识别间隔，必须考虑到当前的谱号，关键签名和任何偶然，通过计算可以确定其间隔关系之前的单个笔记的名称的心理过程。

Chromatic staves give a consistent and accurate representation of the intervals between notes. This clearer representation of intervals makes it much easier to recognize the interval patterns that make up various scales, or any interval pattern prevalent within a given musical genre or tradition. It also simplifies the comprehension of harmony and music theory, for example, allowing easy recognition of major and minor thirds and the chords that are built from them. Finally, it would help with learning to improvise or play by ear since these are skills that entail playing by interval relationships.

色笛塔斯在纸币之间的间隔提供一致和准确的表示。该间隔的更清晰的表示使得识别构成各种尺度的间隔模式，或者在给定的音乐类型或传统中普遍的任何间隔模式。例如，它还简化了对和谐和音乐理论的理解，允许容易地识别主要和次要的三分之一和由它们构建的和弦。最后，它将有助于学习通过耳朵即兴创作或发挥，因为这些是需要按间隔关系进行播放的技能。

It may seem that chromatic staves are only suited for atonal music, but in fact they work well with all types of music: traditional or modern, tonal or atonal, etc. They are actually much better than traditional notation at representing the diatonic interval patterns that are the basis of tonal music. Diatonic interval patterns are built into the traditional staff, obscured from view and from awareness, but a chromatic staff makes them explicit, easy to perceive, and simple to understand.

它似乎似乎是色笛子只适用于分子音乐，但实际上它们与各种类型的音乐都很好：传统或现代，色调或分子等。它们实际上比传统的符号更好地代表了代表性的区间模式是音乐音乐的基础。在传统的员工中建立了频道间隔模式，从观点和意识掩盖，但是一个色彩的工作人员使他们明确，易于感知，而且易于理解。

All of these features of traditional music notation combine to make reading music much more difficult than it might be with a better notation system. For an analogy, imagine trying to do arithmetic with Roman numerals. It can be done, but the notation system makes a big difference. Of course it is important to view traditional notation in its broader historical context and to keep in mind the innovations and reforms that it has undergone over time. [2]

传统音乐符号的所有特征都结合了，使阅读音乐更加困难，而不是更好的符号系统。对于一个类比，想象一下，试图用罗马数字进行算术。它可以完成，但符号系统具有很大的差异。当然，在更广泛的历史背景下观看传统符号并牢记随着时间的推移所经历的创新和改革非常重要。 [2]

Alternative music notation systems with chromatic staves avoid each of these pitch-related difficulties, and offer significant advantages over traditional music notation. [3]

具有色塔的替代音乐符号系统避免了这些与传统音乐符号的诸多困难的每个困难。 [3]

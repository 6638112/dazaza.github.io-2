Google tells Australian Government it is considering withdrawing “completely”
Google告诉澳大利亚政府，它正在考虑“完全”撤资

2020-12-19 06:48:17

Google Australia boss Melanie Silva has claimed proposed laws to make the tech giant pay news outlets for use of content would “break” the search engine and change the experience for its users.

谷歌澳大利亚老板梅拉妮·席尔瓦（Melanie Silva）声称已提出法律，要求该科技巨头向新闻媒体支付使用内容的费用，这将“破坏”搜索引擎并改变其用户体验。

Ms Silva has rejected a final version of the federal government’s news media bargaining code, which was tabled in Parliament last week, arguing it remains unworkable and fails to understand how the search engine operates.

席尔瓦（Silva）女士拒绝了上周在议会上提交的联邦政府新闻媒体议价守则的最终版本，称该守则仍然行不通，并且不了解搜索引擎的运作方式。

“Unfortunately, while the government has made some changes, the legislation still falls far short of a workable code,” Ms Silva said in a blog posted on Friday morning. “As the legislation goes to a Senate committee for inquiry, it has serious problems that need to be worked through.”

席尔瓦女士在周五上午发布的博客中说：“不幸的是，尽管政府进行了一些修改，但该法规仍远未达到可行的法规。” “当立法进入参议院委员会进行调查时，它有严重的问题需要解决。”

Under the proposed law, if the tech giants cannot reach commercial agreements with news businesses within three months, the parties must enter a “final offer” arbitration process. A panel will be given the power to pick one of the parties’ offers for payment or find a more reasonable offer. The panel will take into account a range of factors including the value Google and Facebook give to news outlets through referral traffic. Inclusion of this value in arbitration was considered a major concession for the tech giants by the government.

根据拟议的法律，如果科技巨头无法在三个月内与新闻企业达成商业协议，则当事方必须进入“最终要约”仲裁程序。专家组将有权选择一方的付款要约或找到更合理的要约。该小组将考虑一系列因素，包括Google和Facebook通过引荐流量给新闻媒体的价值。政府认为，将这种价值纳入仲裁对科技巨头来说是一项重大让步。

Other requirements under the code include 14 days’ notice of algorithm changes and non-discrimination provisions to stop the tech giants taking retaliatory action against news organisations. The code did not change drastically from its draft form, but did take into account several concerns raised by Google and Facebook.

该规范的其他要求包括14天通知算法更改通知和不歧视条款，以阻止科技巨头对新闻机构采取报复行动。该代码并未从其草稿表中发生很大变化，但确实考虑了Google和Facebook提出的一些问题。

Some media companies cautiously welcomed the government’s announcement. Seven West Media chief executive James Warburton described the proposed laws as sensible and fair, while News Corp executive chairman Michael Miller declined to be interviewed but told The Australian Financial Review that he had no issue with the consideration of referral traffic.

一些媒体公司谨慎地欢迎政府的宣布。七家西方媒体首席执行官詹姆斯·沃伯顿（James Warburton）将拟议的法律描述为明智且公平的，而新闻集团执行主席迈克尔·米勒（Michael Miller）拒绝接受采访，但告诉《澳大利亚金融评论》，他对转介访问量没有任何问题。

Nine Entertainment Co boss Hugh Marks was more critical of the concessions and accused the government of bowing to threats from Facebook and Google. Nine owns this masthead.

九娱乐公司（Nine Entertainment Co.）的老板休·马克斯（Hugh Marks）对这一让步更为批评，并指责政府屈服于来自Facebook和谷歌的威胁。九拥有此刊头。

Facebook has said it plans to remove news articles from its platform while industry sources previously told The Sydney Morning Herald and The Age that Google had privately told the federal government it was considering withdrawing from the market completely.

Facebook已经表示计划从平台上删除新闻报道，而业内消息人士此前曾对《悉尼先驱晨报》和《时代报》表示，谷歌已经私下告诉联邦政府，它正在考虑完全退出市场。

Ms Silva remains frustrated with the government’s proposal despite the concessions. She says the requirement for Google to pay for the existence of links was an “unprecedented intervention” that would break how search engines operate.

尽管做出了让步，席尔瓦女士仍然对政府的提议感到沮丧。她说，要求谷歌为链接的存在支付费用是一项“前所未有的干预”，它将破坏搜索引擎的运作方式。

“No website and no search engine pays to connect people to other websites, yet the code would force Google to include and pay for links to news websites in the search results you see,” Ms Silva said. “This sets the groundwork to unravel the key principles of the open internet people use every day — something neither a search engine nor anyone who enjoys the benefits of the free and open web should accept.”

席尔瓦女士说：“没有网站，也没有搜索引擎为将人们连接到其他网站付费，但是该代码将迫使谷歌在您看到的搜索结果中包括并支付新闻网站的链接，” “这为揭示人们每天使用的开放式互联网的主要原则打下了基础-搜索引擎或任何享受免费开放式网络好处的人都不应接受。”

Ms Silva also raised concerns about notice of algorithm changes and other internal practices, arguing it would drive up operating costs and give news outlets special treatment. Her final issue remains with two-way arbitration.

Silva女士还对算法变更通知和其他内部实践表示关注，认为这样做会提高运营成本并给予新闻媒体特殊待遇。她的最后一件事仍然是双向仲裁。

“It imposes an unfair and unprecedented baseball arbitration model that considers only publishers’ costs, not Google’s....and requires the decision-maker to choose a single ‘final offer’,” she said. “We’ve identified these issues repeatedly during the consultation process because they would do serious damage to the fundamentals of our services — the reasons Australians choose to use Google in the first place.”

她说：“它强加了一种不公平，前所未有的棒球仲裁模式，该模式仅考虑发布者的费用，而不考虑谷歌的费用……，并要求决策者选择一个'最终要约'。” “我们在咨询过程中反复发现了这些问题，因为它们会对我们的服务基础造成严重损害，这也是澳大利亚人首先选择使用Google的原因。”

Ms Silva said she will continue to work with the government to amend the proposed legislation, but is is unclear whether there will be a resolution. Facebook has not made detailed public commentary on the proposed laws since they were finalised, but it is likely the tech giant shares some of the concerns of its rival Google.

席尔瓦女士说，她将继续与政府合作以修改拟议的立法，但目前尚不清楚是否会有解决方案。自最终定稿以来，Facebook尚未就拟议的法律进行详细的公开评论，但该科技巨头可能会与竞争对手Google共享一些担忧。

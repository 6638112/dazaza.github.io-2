为什么Airbnb是美国最有价值的私人公司

2020-10-17 07:04:08

While the entire professional class, and every elected official, all claim to be optimists, I see the world through gray-colored glasses. Vaccine by fall, herd immunity, a robust recovery, markets will continue to surge, AI …

虽然整个专业阶层和每一位民选官员都声称自己是乐观主义者，但我是通过灰色的眼镜来看待世界的。疫苗落地，羊群免疫，强劲复苏，市场将继续飙升，AI…。

Pessimists are underappreciated, as while optimists built the first plane, pessimists suggested seat belts. Both parties have a role. Neville Chamberlain, Charles Keating, and Bobbi Brown were all likely optimists. But I digress.

悲观主义者被低估了，因为虽然乐观主义者制造了第一架飞机，但悲观主义者建议系好安全带。两党都有自己的角色。内维尔·张伯伦、查尔斯·基廷和波比·布朗都可能是乐观主义者。但我离题了。

So, being angry and depressed doesn't mean I'm wrong. There are good companies that are overvalued (Tesla, Snowflake), good businesses whose emissions are bad for society (Facebook, Twitter), and firms that are just a menace (Uber). There are also firms that are all three (Palantir). However, occasionally there is a firm that is so gangster even I can't help but see the glass as half empty, versus empty. The most valuable private firm in America is Airbnb.

所以，生气和沮丧并不意味着我错了。有估值过高的好公司(特斯拉、雪花)，有排放对社会有害的好公司(Facebook、Twitter)，还有只是威胁的公司(Uber)。也有三家都是的公司(Palantir)。然而，偶尔会有一家公司如此强盗，甚至连我都忍不住认为杯子是半空的，而不是空的。美国最有价值的私人公司是Airbnb。

I believe this time next year, Airbnb will be the most valuable hospitality firm in the world and one of the world's 10 strongest brands. (Note: Rankings of "the world's best brands" are a desperate yelp for relevance from ad agencies begging clients to buy more media and cling to the nineties, the Brand Era.) The SF platform will likely be worth more than the three largest hotel firms, combined. Why?

我相信明年这个时候，Airbnb将成为世界上最有价值的酒店公司和世界十大最强大的品牌之一。(注：全球最佳品牌排行榜是广告公司不顾一切地要求客户购买更多媒体，并紧扣90年代品牌时代的呼声。)。SF平台的价值可能会超过三家最大的酒店公司的总和。为什么？

Ride hailing requires local supply (drivers) and demand (hailers). Hotels need local supply (hotel rooms) and regional demand (guests). But a global hotel brand requires both local supply and global demand, as guests are from all over the world. Airbnb has global supply, boasting more than  seven million listings worldwide — more than Marriott International, Hilton Worldwide, InterContinental Hotels Group, Wyndham Hotel Group, and Hyatt Hotels, combined. More impressive, and singular, Airbnb is the only hospitality brand that has the global awareness to generate unrivaled demand.

网约车需要当地的供应(司机)和需求(叫车人员)。酒店需要当地供应(酒店客房)和区域需求(客人)。但一个全球性的酒店品牌既需要当地的供应，也需要全球的需求，因为客人来自世界各地。Airbnb拥有全球供应，号称在全球拥有超过700万套房源-超过万豪国际(Marriott International)、希尔顿全球(Hilton Worldwide)、洲际酒店集团(InterContinental Hotels Group)、温德姆酒店集团(Wyndham Hotel Group)和凯悦酒店(Hyatt Hotels)的总和。更令人印象深刻和独特的是，Airbnb是唯一一个具有全球知名度的酒店品牌，能够产生无与伦比的需求。

In 2021, there will be more Airbnb  users in the US than people in California.

到2021年，美国的Airbnb用户将超过加州的用户。

Outside of luxury, which is not relevant/affordable to 90% of travelers, there isn't a truly global brand, until now. Google searches reflect that Airbnb has eclipsed the equity of century-old brands, in one decade, across markets big and small. While competitors may have equity in a specific market, no brand sits on the iron throne across all markets as Airbnb does.

奢侈品对90%的旅行者来说并不相关/负担得起，除了奢侈品之外，直到现在还没有一个真正的全球品牌。谷歌搜索显示，Airbnb在十年内，在大大小小的市场上，已经让百年老品牌的资产黯然失色。虽然竞争对手可能在特定市场拥有股权，但没有一个品牌像Airbnb那样坐在所有市场的铁王座上。

This equity also bests that of airlines and online booking engines that may have global awareness (like Qantas) but only regional relevance (i.e., not an option for intracontinental travel in markets outside Australia).

这一资产净值也超过了航空公司和在线预订引擎，它们可能具有全球知名度(如澳航)，但只具有地区性(即，澳大利亚以外的市场不能选择洲际旅行)。

So, how to value the gangster of all private gangsters? What is the benchmark and the corresponding multiple? It's clearly not a hotel business, but not a SaaS firm either. However, Airbnb is a tech firm and highly "disruptive." The firm has a greater share of employees with an engineering background than Amazon or Uber.

那么，如何评价所有私人黑帮中的黑帮呢？基准和相应的倍数是多少？它显然不是一家酒店业务，也不是一家SaaS公司。然而，Airbnb是一家科技公司，具有很强的颠覆性。与亚马逊或优步相比，该公司拥有更多具有工程背景的员工。

The only firms I can think of that have global demand/supply and brand equity, and an asset-light high-margin business are the credit card companies, which trade at 20+ multiples of revenue. Airbnb projects 2021 revenues of $5-6 billion, yielding a potential $100-120 billion valuation.

我能想到的唯一拥有全球需求/供应和品牌资产，以及资产轻量级高利润率业务的公司是信用卡公司，它们的交易价格是收入的20倍以上。Airbnb预计2021年营收将达到50-60亿美元，潜在估值为1,000亿-1,200亿美元。

In 2020, in the private markets, Airbnb shares have traded at a valuation ranging between $15-30 billion. The media pegs the IPO valuation at $30 billion. Put on your seatbelts, as the bankers will have no excuse to not price the shares at the high end of a recast (higher) range, and then reward their institutional clients with a Snowflake-like flurry when it begins trading. There's something about weather and cruise analogies that becomes more appealing as you get older.

2020年，在非公开市场，Airbnb的股票估值在150亿至300亿美元之间。媒体认为IPO估值为300亿美元。系好安全带，因为银行家们将没有借口不将股票定价在重铸(更高)区间的高端，然后在股票开始交易时像雪花一样奖励他们的机构客户。有一些关于天气和游轮的类比，随着年龄的增长变得更有吸引力。

In 1980, 38-year-old Muhammad Ali returned to the ring to fight a 30-year-old undefeated Larry Holmes. In the pre-fight examination Ali was unable to consistently touch his finger to his nose, had difficulty coordinating the muscles used in speaking, and could not hop on one foot. Boxing pundits describe the medical clearance to fight as criminal. I'm pretty sure the physician who cleared Ali for the fight now serves on the board of Facebook.

1980年，38岁的穆罕默德·阿里(Muhammad Ali)重返拳击场，与30岁的不败拉里·霍姆斯(Larry Holmes)展开较量。在赛前检查中，阿里的手指无法始终如一地触摸鼻子，说话时使用的肌肉难以协调，一只脚也不能跳。拳击专家将战斗的体检许可描述为犯罪。我非常肯定，这位为阿里洗脱罪名的医生现在是Facebook的董事会成员。

It's difficult to identify another sector, this large, that has one player more ascendant while the rest of the industry can't touch its nose. The hospitality sector has experienced an unprecedented shock that dwarfs 9/11 or any recession. Recessions, even wars, take occupancy levels down to 60%, or even 50%. No hotel owner or management firm modeled occupancy dropping to 0%. The result is an entire industry on its heels, except for one player that's on its toes, waiting to spring forward with an information-age Super Bowl ad, the Initial Public Offering, and begin firing a howitzer of capital as other firms return fire with squirt guns.

很难找到另一个行业，这么大的规模，有一个参与者更具优势，而业内其他公司却无法涉足它的鼻子。酒店业经历了前所未有的冲击，令9/11事件或任何经济衰退都相形见绌。经济衰退，甚至战争，都会使入住率降至60%，甚至50%。没有酒店业主或管理公司模拟入住率降至0%。其结果是，整个行业紧随其后，只有一家公司处于警觉状态，等待着推出信息时代的超级碗(Super Bowl)广告-首次公开募股(IPO)，并开始发射榴弹炮，而其他公司则用水枪还击。

Airbnb is also a better value than hotels, offering more space but with less COVID (no check-in, elevators, or common areas) at a lower cost. A crisis is a terrible thing to waste, and COVID afforded the CEO the cloud cover to cut costs and refocus on the core business. In May, Airbnb laid off a quarter of its staff (1,900 employees). CEO Chesky managed to pull a Bezos and was seen as a hero for his  empathetic approach to layoffs (generous severance, extended healthcare, and a  website of Airbnb employees who were laid off to help them find new leads). Firing people, sending out d--- pics — tomato/tomahto. Chesky and cofounders relinquished their salaries, cut pay in half for executives, and slashed nearly $1 billion in marketing expenses. The firm is in fighting shape.

Airbnb也比酒店更划算，它提供了更多的空间，但COVID更少(没有入住、电梯或公共区域)，成本更低。危机是一件可怕的事情，不能浪费，COVID为首席执行官提供了云覆盖，以削减成本并重新专注于核心业务。今年5月，Airbnb解雇了四分之一的员工(1900名员工)。首席执行官切斯基成功地效仿了贝佐斯，并因其富有同情心的裁员方式而被视为英雄(慷慨的遣散费，延长的医疗保健，以及Airbnb员工的官方网站，这些员工被解雇是为了帮助他们找到新的线索)。解雇员工，派人去-照片-西红柿/西红柿。切斯基和联合创始人放弃了他们的工资，将高管的薪酬削减了一半，并削减了近10亿美元的营销费用。这家公司正在奋力拼搏。

The reduced cost structure and market recovery mean the path to profitability has become bigger, better lit, and shorter. There are rumors the firm will accelerate into/through profitability in 2021. The story here won't be one of distant, but burgeoning, profits.

成本结构的降低和市场的复苏意味着盈利之路变得更大、更明亮、更短。有传言称，该公司将在2021年加速实现盈利。这里的故事不会是一个遥远但迅速增长的利润故事。

The story stock of 2020, where the narrative rode shotgun as the numbers sat quietly in the backseat, was Tesla. Airbnb will not electrify the world, but it will host it and reshape the resources required to let people tap into a basic instinct: to explore with others. What Airbnb lacks in story (unlikely Mr. Chesky can land two Brooklyn studio apartments on dual barges concurrently), it makes up in performance. There is no better vision than performance.

2020年的故事库是特斯拉(Tesla)，故事情节以猎枪为主，数字静静地坐在后座。Airbnb不会给世界带来电，但它会主办它，并重塑所需的资源，让人们利用一种基本的本能：与他人一起探索。Airbnb在故事上的不足(切斯基不太可能同时让布鲁克林的两套单间公寓在双驳船上着陆)，它在性能上弥补了这一点。没有比表演更好的愿景了。

This morning I was on Bloomberg TV discussing SPAC stocks. Despite the echoes of "It's different this time," I believe SPACs are canaries in the digital coal mine and will continue, over the medium- and long-term, to underperform the market (note: this year they have over-performed). My advice, if you want to play SPACs, is to buy a basket, as among the bastards (I'm rewatching all nine seasons of GOT with my son), a dragon will be birthed. Or you could just buy the dragon …  Airbnb.

今天早上，我在彭博电视台(Bloomberg TV)讨论SPAC股票。尽管有回声，但这一次有所不同，我认为SPAC是数字煤矿中的金丝雀，从中长期来看，它们的表现将继续逊于大盘(注意：今年它们的表现已经超出了市场)。我的建议是，如果你想玩SPAC，那就买个篮子吧，就像那些混蛋一样(我正和儿子一起重温“GOT”的所有九季)，一条龙就会诞生。或者你可以直接买龙…。爱彼迎。

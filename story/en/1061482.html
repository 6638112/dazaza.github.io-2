Tani Adewumi is now a chess master
Tani Adewumi现在是一位国际象棋大师

2021-05-09 04:33:40

Once upon a time a 7-year-old refugee living in a homeless shelter sat down at a chess board in school and learned how to play. His school then agreed to his mom’s plea to waive fees for him to join the chess club.

曾几何时，一个7岁的难民居住在无家可归者的庇护所，坐在学校的棋盘上，学会了如何玩。他的学校随后同意他妈妈的请求豁免他加入国际象棋俱乐部。

The boy wasn’t any good at first. His initial chess rating was 105, barely above the lowest possible rating, 100.

这名男孩起初并不擅长。他最初的棋评分为105，几乎没有高于可能的评级，100。

But the boy, Tanitoluwa Adewumi — better known as Tani — enjoyed chess as an escape from the chaos of the homeless shelter, and his skills progressed in stunning fashion. After little more than a year, at age 8, he won the New York State chess championship for his age group, beating well-coached children from rich private schools.

但是这个男孩，Tanitoluwa Adewumi - 更好地称为Tani - 享受国际象棋作为无家可归者庇护所的混乱，他的技能以惊人的方式进展。经过一年多的时间，在8岁时，他赢得了纽约州棋锦标赛的年龄组，击败了丰富的私立学校的教练儿童。

I wrote a couple of columns about Tani at that time, and readers responded by donating more than $250,000 to a GoFundMe campaign for Tani’s family, along with a year of free housing. It was heartwarming to see Tani running around the family’s new apartment, but I wondered: Is this kid really that good?

我当时写了几列关于Tani的一列，读者通过捐赠超过250,000美元的Tani家族的Gofundme活动，以及一年的免费住房。看到坦尼在家庭的新公寓周围跑来跑来令人心动，但我想知道：这个孩子真的很好吗？

It turns out he is. This month, as a fifth grader, Tani cruised through an in-person tournament in Connecticut open to advanced players of all ages and won every game. He emerged with a chess rating of 2223, making him a national master.

事实证明他是。本月，作为第五年级学生，Tani通过康涅狄格州康涅狄格州的独立锦标赛巡航，向所有年龄段的高级球员开放，并赢得了每场比赛。他出现了2223的象棋评级，使他成为一个国家硕士。

At 10 years 7 months and 28 days, Tani became the 28th-youngest person ever to become a chess master in the United States, according to John Hartmann of U.S. Chess. Tani had one of the fastest rises, for he began playing chess only at the relatively late age of 7. And he’s aiming higher.

John Hartmann的美国国际象棋的统计，在10年7个月和28天，Tani成为美国象棋大师的第28岁的人。塔尼有一个最快的崛起，因为他只开始在7年代较晚的时间里打入国际象棋。他的目标是更高。

“I want to be the youngest grandmaster,” he told me. “I want to have it when I’m 11 or 12.” The youngest person ever to become a grandmaster, Sergey Karjakin, achieved that honor at 12 years 7 months.

“我想成为最年轻的奶奶，”他告诉我。 “当我11或12 12时，我想拥有它。”有史以来最年轻的人成为奶奶谢尔盖·卡贾基纳，在12年7个月内实现了这一荣誉。

“I’m delighted to see Tani’s rapid progress,” said the former world chess champion Garry Kasparov. “The sky is the limit, and I’m the last person to say that chess is not a viable career path.”

“我很高兴看到塔尼的快速进步，”前世界象棋冠军加里卡拉夫·卡斯帕罗夫说。 “天空是极限，我是最后一个说国际象棋不是可行的职业道路。”

Tani has watched the Netflix series “The Queen’s Gambit,” about an orphan girl and outsider who proves a chess prodigy. “I definitely did see myself in it,” he said.

Tani观看了Netflix系列“女王的疯狂，”关于孤儿女孩和局外人证明国际象棋神童。 “我肯定看到了自己，”他说。

He may see himself more directly on the screen. A book Tani and his parents wrote about their journey has been optioned for a feature film by Paramount Pictures. The script is being written by Steven Conrad, who wrote “ The Pursuit of Happyness,” and Trevor Noah is to produce.

他可能会在屏幕上更直接地看到自己。一本书Tani和他的父母写了关于他们的旅程，这是一部由派拉蒙图片的一个特征电影的旅程。史蒂文·康拉德撰写的脚本是撰写“追求幸福”，Trevor Noah的制作。

“We look back and see where we came from and where we are today, and where we hope we’re going — and every time we look back we give thanks to God,” said his mother, Oluwatoyin Adewumi. She has just qualified as a patient care technician and is looking for work.

“我们回顾看看我们来自哪里以及我们今天的位置，我们希望我们要去哪里 - 每次我们都回头看，我们感谢上帝，”他的母亲，奥卢韦扬素Adewumi说。她刚刚获得患者护理技术人员，正在寻找工作。

The family fled Nigeria because of fears of Boko Haram, the terrorist group, according to his father, Kayode Adewumi, who now is a real estate agent with Douglas Elliman Real Estate.

根据他的父亲Kayode Adewumi的说法，家庭逃离尼日利亚，因为他的父亲，恐怖主义组织，现在是与道格拉斯Elliman房地产的房地产经纪人。

When Tani won the state championship, several private schools offered him places, but the family decided to keep him in the public school that had nurtured him. The Adewumis also used the $250,000 contributed by readers to start a foundation that helps other homeless people and refugees.

当Tani赢得国家冠军时，几所私立学校向他提供了他的地方，但该家庭决定让他在培育他的公立学校。 Adewumis还使用了读者贡献的250,000美元，以启动一个基础，帮助其他无家可归者和难民。

The Adewumis now live on Long Island, paying their own expenses, and the pandemic has been a struggle. Told that Tani needed a top chess coach to develop, the family scrimped and hired a grandmaster, Giorgi Kacheishvili, to coach Tani three times a week. “When the money is too much, I reduce it to two times a week,” his father said.

Adewumis现在住在长岛，支付自己的费用，大流行是一场斗争。告诉Tani需要一个顶级国际象棋教练来开发，家人拼凑和聘请了一位祖父，Giorgi Kacheishvili，每周三次教练Tani。 “当钱太多时，我每周将它减少到两次，”他的父亲说。

Another challenge is travel. Tani is sometimes invited to tournaments abroad, but can’t go while his immigration case is pending for fear he might not be allowed back into the United States.

另一个挑战是旅行。 Tani有时被邀请到国外的锦标赛，但不能达到他的移民案件等待恐惧，因为他可能不允许恢复美国。

The larger lesson of Tani’s story is simple: Talent is universal, while opportunity is not. In Tani’s case, everything came together. His homeless shelter was in a school district that had a chess club, the school waived fees, he had devoted parents who took him to every practice, he won the state tournament (by a hair) and readers responded with extraordinary generosity.

塔尼故事的较大课程很简单：人才是普遍的，而机会不是。在Tani的情况下，一切都走到了一起。他的无家可归者庇护所在学区内有一个国际象棋俱乐部，学校放弃费用，他曾献上了他追求他每一项练习的父母，他赢得了国家锦标赛（由头发）和读者以非凡的慷慨作出反应。

But opportunity shouldn’t require a perfect alignment of the stars. Winning state chess tournaments is not a scalable solution to child homelessness.

但机会不应该需要明星的完美对齐。赢得国家国际象棋锦标赛不是儿童无家可归的可扩展解决方案。

My challenge as a columnist is that readers often want to help extraordinary individuals like Tani whom I write about, but we need to support all children — including those who aren’t chess prodigies. That requires policy as well as philanthropy, so let me note: President Biden’s proposed investments in children, such as child tax credits and universal pre-K, would revolutionize opportunity for all struggling children.

我作为专栏作家的挑战是读者经常希望帮助我写的塔尼等特殊的人，但我们需要支持所有儿童 - 包括那些不是国际象棋的人。这需要政策和慈善事业，所以请告诉我：拜登总统拟议的儿童投资，如儿童税收抵免和普遍的前K，会彻底改变所有斗争的儿童的机会。

Maybe we can be inspired by the wisdom of America’s newest chess master. I asked Tani how he feels when he loses.

也许我们可以灵感来自美国最新的国际象棋大师的智慧。我问Tani在他失去时感觉如何。

“When you lose, you have made a mistake, and that can help you learn,” he told me. “I never lose. I learn.”

“当你失去时，你犯了一个错误，这可以帮助你学习，”他告诉我。 “我从来没有失去。我学习。”

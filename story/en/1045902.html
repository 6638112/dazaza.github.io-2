DIA uses purchased phone location data without warrants
DIA使用购买的电话位置数据而无须担保

2021-01-23 06:15:35

Written by The Defense Intelligence Agency has been using smartphone location data purchased from commercially available databases, according to an intelligence memo obtained by CyberScoop.

根据Cyber​​Scoop获得的情报备忘录，由美国国防情报局撰写的文章一直在使用从商业数据库购买的智能手机位置数据。

The DIA, which primarily provides intelligence to support U.S. military operations, has been gathering the location data on both Americans and non-U.S. citizens in five searches dating back two-and-a-half years, according to the memo, which was drafted by the DIA for the offices of Sen. Ron Wyden, D-Ore., states.

该备忘录起草的备忘录称，DIA主要提供情报以支持美国的军事行动，它已经在过去两年半的五次搜索中收集了美国人和非美国公民的位置数据。 DIA代表了俄勒冈州参议员Ron Wyden的办公室。

While agency did not describe what those searches encompassed, the memo makes clear that the agency is obtaining sensitive location data without a warrant. The Department of Homeland Security’s Immigration and Customs Enforcement previously suggested in a legal memo that government officials could track the locations of immigrants by buying cell phone data from private companies, rather than going trough typical court channels.

尽管代理商没有描述这些搜索的内容，但备忘录清楚地表明，代理商是在没有授权的情况下获取敏感位置数据。国土安全部的移民和海关执法部门先前在法律备忘录中建议，政府官员可以通过从私人公司购买手机数据来跟踪移民的位置，而不必经过典型的法院渠道。

Numerous news outlets — including Motherboard and the Wall Street Journal — have detailed ways that the U.S. government obtains user data from data brokers. Privacy advocates have taken notice, and Sen. Ron Wyden has indicated he will propose legislation meant to curb the practice, as the New York Times first reported.

许多新闻媒体，包括《主板》和《华尔街日报》，都有美国政府从数据经纪人那里获取用户数据的详细方式。隐私权倡导者已经注意到这一点，参议员罗恩·怀登（Ron Wyden）表示，他将提出旨在遏制这种做法的立法，正如《纽约时报》首次报道的那样。

“There ought to be transparency so that the American people know what kind of surveillance is being conducted on them,” Wyden said in a speech earlier this week on the Senate floor. The intelligence community, “instead of getting an order, just goes out and purchases the private records of Americans from these sleazy and unregulated commercial data brokers who are simply above the law” when they shouldn’t be, he said.

威登在本周早些时候在参议院的讲话中说：“应该保持透明，以便美国人民知道对他们进行了什么样的监视。”他说，情报界“没有得到命令，而是出去了，从这些懒散且不受监管的商业数据经纪人那里购买了美国人的私人记录，这些经纪人只是在法律之上”，他说。

The DIA memo in question does not name which commercial data broker or brokers the DIA is using, only adding that a DIA-funded agency has been purchasing the data. The agency clarified that it separates location data from Americans and foreign nationals into different databases.

所涉及的DIA备忘录未指定DIA使用的是哪个商业数据经纪人，仅补充说DIA资助的代理商已在购买数据。该机构澄清说，它将美国人和外国人的位置数据分离到不同的数据库中。

The Supreme Court ruled in 2018 in Carpenter v. United States that a warrant is required for phone companies to provide the U.S. government with phone location data. Chief Justice John Roberts, who wrote the majority opinion in that case, noted that although the individuals in that case had a legitimate expectation of privacy with respect to their cell phone location information, the decision was not meant to apply to “business records that might incidentally reveal location information.”

最高法院于2018年在Carpenter诉美国案中裁定，电话公司需要向美国政府提供电话位置数据的逮捕证。在该案中发表多数意见的首席法官约翰·罗伯茨（John Roberts）指出，尽管该案中的个人对手机位置信息有合理的隐私期望，但该决定并不意味着适用于“可能顺便透露位置信息。”

The DIA notes this distinction in its Jan. 15 memo to Wyden, adding that the agency “does not construe the Carpenter decision to require a judicial warrant endorsing purchase or use of commercially available data for intelligence purposes.”

DIA在1月15日给Wyden的备忘录中指出了这一区别，并补充说，该机构“不解释Carpenter关于要求司法授权为情报目的购买或使用商业数据的决定的决定。”

Avril Haines, President Joe Biden’s newly confirmed Director of National Intelligence, told lawmakers earlier this week during her confirmation hearing that she thinks it’s important that Americans have a clear picture of when the U.S. government is buying their private data and under what authorities.

乔·拜登总统新近确认的国家情报总监艾弗里尔·海恩斯（Avril Haines）本周早些时候在国会议员的确认听证会上对议员说，她认为，重要的是，美国人必须清楚了解美国政府何时购买其私人数据以及在什么当局的领导下。

In order to protect the defense industrial base against unwanted location tracking, the NSA itself issued a warning last year about the dangers of mobile devices leaking location data to unwanted parties. In a publication listing mitigations for users concerned about revealing their location to eavesdroppers, the NSA recommended applications, which can aggregate and collect location data they don’t need to function, be given as few location permissions as possible.

为了保护国防工业基础免受不必要的位置跟踪，美国国家安全局（NSA）本身去年就发出警告，警告移动设备将位置数据泄露给不需要的各方。在针对担心向窃听者透露其位置的用户的缓解措施的出版物中，NSA推荐的应用程序可以聚集并收集不需要运行的位置数据，并且应尽可能少地授予位置权限。

“Location data can be extremely valuable and must be protected. It can reveal details about the number of users in a location, user and supply movements, daily routines (user and organizational), and can expose otherwise unknown associations between users and locations,” the NSA warned.

“位置数据可能非常有价值，必须加以保护。它可以揭示有关位置用户数量，用户和供应活动，日常工作（用户和组织）的详细信息，并可以揭示用户和位置之间其他未知的关联。”

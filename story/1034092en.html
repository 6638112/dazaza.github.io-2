旧式科幻房屋楼梯顶端的怪异操纵杆

2020-11-10 01:59:18

Old San Francisco homes have all kinds of oddities. From split baths to push-button lights to picture rails, the city’s aged spaces add plenty of character to the place you call home.

旧金山的老房子有各种各样的怪异之处。从分体式浴缸到按钮灯再到画轨，这座城市的陈旧空间为这个你称之为家的地方增添了许多特色。

A popular feature of late 19th century Victorian to early 20th century Edwardian buildings with two to three units is levers at the top of a tall set of stairs that, when pulled, open the front door. Presumably, even those ancient San Franciscans didn’t want to walk all the way downstairs to let guests in and then have to climb back up.

19世纪末到20世纪初爱德华七世时代的建筑有一个很受欢迎的特点，那就是有两到三个单元的爱德华式建筑，在一组很高的楼梯的顶端安装了杠杆，当拉动楼梯时，杠杆就会打开前门。想必，即使是那些古老的旧金山人也不想走到楼下让客人进来，然后不得不爬回来。

“The middle class was coming into its own. They wanted the same luxuries as the upper class, and the industrial revolution helped the middle class live a better, simpler life,” said Bonnie Spindler, a real estate agent and "the Victorian Specialist" of San Francisco. “You didn’t have servants who would answer your door, but you could use this.”

他说：“中产阶级正在形成自己的特色。他们想要和上层阶级一样的奢侈品，工业革命帮助中产阶级过上了更好、更简单的生活，“旧金山的房地产经纪人、维多利亚时代的专家邦妮·斯平德勒(Bonnie Spindler)说。“你没有仆人来开门，但你可以用这个。”

Rob Thomson, president of the Victorian Alliance, said a lot of the building that were built between 1880 and 1930 were built for the middle-class family. These multi-unit, wood-framed homes included large staircases for the upper apartments, and if you couldn’t afford servants to open the door for you, these levers were the next best thing.

维多利亚联盟主席罗布·汤姆森(Rob Thomson)表示，这座建于1880年至1930年之间的建筑中，有很多都是为中产阶级家庭建造的。这些多单元的木结构房屋包括上层公寓的大楼梯，如果你请不起佣人为你开门，这些杠杆是第二好的选择。

There are a few patents for door opening systems filed in the 1890s and 1900s, the first of which is from 1890. As most of them were patented in San Francisco, it leads historians to believe these levers were especially common in the Bay Area. Since the area has few brick or other masonry homes, these were easier to install within the wood walls. The bellcrank levers, as they’re formally called, were connected with a long wire to a mechanism that opened the door.

在19世纪90年代和20世纪申请了几项开门系统的专利，其中第一项是从1890年开始申请的。由于它们中的大多数是在旧金山获得专利的，这使得历史学家认为这些杠杆在旧金山湾区特别常见。由于该地区几乎没有砖头或其他砖石房屋，这些房屋更容易安装在木墙内。它们被正式命名为钟形曲柄操纵杆，它们用一根长电线连接到一个打开车门的机械装置上。

One such patent was filed by D.B. Akard of San Francisco, who patented a door opening lever system in 1897 (US586375A and US590290A). He founded the Akard Door Opener Company, which operated from a shop in the Mission District until about the 1920s. Former San Francisco resident Randall Stevens later lived in the Akard home that also housed the shop and he said his unit, of course, had a lever, though it didn’t function.

旧金山的D.B.阿卡德就申请了这样一项专利，他在1897年申请了开门杠杆系统的专利(US586375A和US590290A)。他创立了阿卡德开门器公司，该公司在教务区的一家商店经营，直到20世纪20年代左右。前旧金山居民兰德尔·史蒂文斯(Randall Stevens)后来住在阿卡德(Akard)的家中，阿卡德也是这家商店的所在地。他说，他的房间当然有一个杠杆，尽管它不起作用。

While many no longer function, most people don’t remove them entirely because they’d have to tear out the entire wall to do so. Steve Smith, who runs Ohmega Salvage in Berkeley, said that most of the levers no longer work because the rods were so long and had to be routed through a lot of studs that needed to stay straight and align with each other. As houses settle over time and succumb to weather fluctuations, the rods could fall out of alignment and bend. Rust could also be a culprit, as the majority of the mechanisms were steel or iron. Lastly, if the door started to not hang or swing or close properly it would put more stress on the mechanism.

虽然许多墙不再起作用，但大多数人不会完全拆除它们，因为他们必须拆掉整堵墙才能做到这一点。史蒂夫·史密斯(Steve Smith)在伯克利经营欧米茄救助公司(Ohmega Salage)，他说，大多数操纵杆不再起作用，因为操纵杆太长了，必须穿过许多需要保持笔直和相互对准的螺柱。随着房屋随着时间的推移而下沉，并屈服于天气的波动，棒子可能会失去对齐并弯曲。生锈也可能是罪魁祸首，因为大多数机械装置都是钢或铁。最后，如果门开始不能正常悬挂、摆动或关闭，就会给机械带来更大的压力。

Smith said he currently has just one handle in stock and no other parts or pieces to go with it. The last working lever he encountered was in Oakland more than 10 years ago.

史密斯说，他目前只有一个手柄库存，没有其他部件或零件与之配套。他最后一次遇到工作杠杆是10多年前在奥克兰。

If you have a lever in your home, you may also have a “speaking tube,” which was a hole in the wall at the top of the stairs that you could talk to your incoming guests through. Pre-intercom, these were perhaps just as integral as the lever themselves.

如果你家里有杠杆，你可能还会有一个“会说话的管道”，这是楼梯顶端墙上的一个洞，你可以通过这个洞和你的来宾交谈。在对讲机出现之前，这些功能可能就像杠杆本身一样不可或缺。

The bellcrank levers have even popped up in popular films. In the 1968 film "Bullitt" Steve McQueen uses a lever to open the front door for a visitor to his apartment building in San Francisco. Pay attention in "Pursuit of Happyness," and you'll notice that while Will Smith may not let anyone in with the lever, his San Francisco home had one.

摇杆甚至出现在热门电影中。在1968年的这部电影中，史蒂夫·麦昆(Steve McQueen)用一根杠杆打开前门，让一位游客来到他位于旧金山的公寓楼。在《追求幸福》一书中，你会注意到，虽然威尔·史密斯可能不会让任何人带着操纵杆进来，但他在旧金山的家却有一个操纵杆。

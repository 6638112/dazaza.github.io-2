Robinhood says Gamestop volatility was a “1 in 3.5 million” black swan
罗宾汉表示Gamestop的波动率是“ 350万只黑天鹅”

2021-02-19 09:04:55

Robinhood's move to temporarily limit purchases of GameStop and other highly volatile stocks in late January was the overwhelming focus of today's House Committee on Financial Services hearing. At the hearing, Robinhood CEO Vlad Tenev said that extreme stock volatility that led to Robinhood's restriction was a " five sigma" event with a "1 in 3.5 million" chance of happening. That made the situation practically impossible for the company to plan for, Tenev said. "In the context of tens of thousands of days in the history of US stock market, a 1 in 3.5 million event is basically unmodelable."

罗宾汉（Robinhood）于1月下旬暂时限制购买GameStop和其他高波动性股票的举动，是今天众议院金融服务委员会听证会的压倒一切。在听证会上，罗宾汉首席执行官弗拉德·特内夫（Vlad Tenev）说，导致罗宾汉受到限制的极端股票波动是＆＃34;。五个西格玛活动中，350万中的＆＃34;＆＃34;发生的机会。特内夫说，这实际上使该公司无法计划这种情况。 ＆nbsp;＆nbsp;＆nbsp;＆nbsp;＆nbsp;＆nbsp;＆nbsp;＆nbsp;＆nbsp;＆nbsp;＆nbsp;＆nbsp;＆nbsp;＆nbsp;＆nbsp;＆nbsp;＆nbsp;＆nbsp;＆nbsp;＆nbsp;＆nbsp;＆nbsp;＆nbsp;＆nbsp;＆nbsp;＆nbsp;＆nbsp;＆nbsp;＆nbsp;＆nbsp;＆nbsp;＆nbsp;＆nbsp

As we've covered previously, the high volatility of GameStop and other so-called "meme stocks" last month meant Robinhood was suddenly forced to provide much more collateral to the stock clearing houses that actually process its trades. Tenev said Thursday that these collateral obligations increased tenfold between January 25 and January 28, as GameStop rose from $76 a share to over $347, then back down to $193. "Despite unprecedented conditions, what happened is unacceptable to us," Tenev said. "To our customers, I'm sorry, and I apologize. We're doing everything in our power to make sure this won't happen again."

如我们先前所述，GameStop和其他所谓的“ meme股票”的高波动性上个月意味着Robinhood突然被迫向实际处理其交易的股票清算所提供更多的抵押品。 Tenev周四表示，这些抵押品义务在1月25日至1月28日之间增加了十倍，GameStop从每股76美元升至逾347美元，然后回落至193美元。 ＆＃34;尽管条件空前，但是发生的事情对我们来说是不可接受的，＆＃34;特内夫说。对于我们的客户，对不起，我深表歉意。我们将尽力确保这种情况不会再次发生。

Tenev reiterated multiple times that Robinhood never had a liquidity issue in terms of covering its users' actual positions. But the company did lack immediate capital to cover those collateral requirements, which meant its only alternative was to temporarily limit sales of those stocks until it could raise more funds (or until the stocks became less volatile).

Tenev多次重申，Robinhood在覆盖用户方面从未存在过流动性问题。实际职位。但是该公司确实没有立即的资金来满足这些抵押品要求，这意味着它唯一的选择是暂时限制这些股票的销售，直到它可以筹集更多的资金（或者直到股票变得不那么波动为止）。

Some members of the House Financial Services Committee were not impressed with that quibbling over what "liquidity" means for Robinhood. "Your customers wanted to buy the stock," Rep. Van Taylor (R-TX) said at one point. "You wouldn’t let them do it because you didn’t have the capital to allow them to do it."

众议院金融服务委员会的一些成员对“流动性”问题的争论并没有留下深刻的印象。意味着罗宾汉。 ＆＃34;您的客户想购买股票，＆＃34;众议员范·泰勒（R-TX）说了一点。 ＆＃34;您不会让他们这样做，因为您没有足够的资金让他们这样做。

Tenev was also taken to task for not clearly and quickly communicating the reasons behind those restrictions to customers. Rep. Carolyn Maloney (D-NY) noted that Robinhood's initial January 28 blog post announcing the restrictions contained "no disclosures about capital requirements" and only "vague language about restricting trading." The initial impression, she implied, was that Robinhood was "[making] up the rules as you go along."

特内夫还因无法清晰，迅速地向客户传达这些限制背后的原因而承担了任务。众议员Carolyn Maloney（D-NY）指出，Robinhood在1月28日的第一篇博客文章中宣布了这些限制，其中没有披露任何资本要求。以及关于限制交易的模糊语言。＆＃34;她暗示，最初的印象是，罗宾汉（Robinhood）在制定规则时会制定规则。

Since it was forced to halt trading, Robinhood has attracted over $3.4 billion in additional investment that Tenev said "cushions the firm from market volatility and further black swan events." And while he was apologetic, Tenev also said that "other firms did similar things in restricting buying [of these volatile stocks], which suggests it is a systemic problem rather than a uniquely Robinhood problem."

自从被迫停止交易以来，Robinhood已经吸引了超过34亿美元的额外投资，Tenev表示这会从市场动荡和进一步的黑天鹅事件中缓冲该公司。而且，特内夫在道歉的同时还说，“其他公司在限制购买这些动荡股票方面也做了类似的事情，这表明这是一个系统性问题，而不是唯一的罗宾汉问题。”

But Rep. Alexandria Ocasio-Cortez (D-NY) pointed out that "when Robinhood prohibited it customers from purchasing additional shares of certain stocks, other brokerages merely adjusted margin requirements on these stocks." That, she suggests, might mean that "the issue isn't clearinghouses but that you simply didn't manage your own margin rules or failed to manage your own internal risks."

但是众议员亚历山大·奥卡西奥·科尔特斯（D-NY）指出，当罗宾汉禁止客户购买某些股票的额外股份时，其他经纪人只是调整了这些股票的保证金要求。她建议说，这可能意味着问题不是票据交换所，而是您根本没有管理自己的保证金规则或没有管理自己的内部风险。

Some on the committee also focused on Robinhood's business model, which generates a majority of its revenue by selling its "order flow" to market makers like Citadel (who then shave a little bit of profit off of the price improvement they can provide over the exchanges). "When you're not paying for it, it's not free," Rep. Brad Sherman (D-CA) said regarding Robinhood's commission-free trades. "You're the product, someone else [i.e., Citadel] is the customer."

委员会中的一些人还关注Robinhood的业务模式，该模式通过出售订单流来产生大部分收入。给像Citadel这样的做市商（然后他们从交易所可以提供的价格提高中削减一点利润）。 ＆＃34;如果您不付款，那它不是免费的，众议员Brad Sherman（D-CA）说到Robinhood的无佣金交易。 ＆＃34;您是产品，其他人（例如Citadel）就是客户。＆＃34;

"If removing revenues from payment for order-flow would cause the removal of commission-free trade, doesn't that mean trading on Robinhood isn't actually free to begin with, because you're just hiding the cost?" Ocasio-Cortez added in some pointed questioning.

＆＃34;如果从订单流付款中删除收入会导致取消无佣金交易，那并不意味着在Robinhood上进行交易实际上就不是免费的，因为您是只是隐藏成本？＆＃34; Ocasio-Cortez添加了一些针对性的质疑。

While Tenev was apologetic for the mistakes Robinhood made, he also placed some blame on what he called outdated and moribund trade settlement rules that place a lot of pressure on the system. Under the current T+2 timeline for stock settlement, market makers actually have two full days to officially settle a trade (i.e., transfer the shares/cash) after it's made. When a stock's price is especially volatile, the clearing house needs more collateral to cover the potential price changes that could happen on either side of the trade in that two-day interval.

特内夫（Tevev）为罗宾汉（Robinhood）犯下的错误表示歉意，但他也将责任归咎于他所说的过时和垂死的贸易结算规则，这给系统带来了很大压力。根据当前的T + 2股票结算时间表，做市商实际上有两整天的时间可以正式结算交易（即转让股票/现金）。当股票的价格特别波动时，票据交换所需要更多的抵押品，以覆盖在两天的时间间隔内交易双方可能发生的潜在价格变化。

"The existing two-day period to settle trade exposes investors and the industry to unnecessary risk," Tenev said. "There is no reason why the greatest financial system in the world can not settle trades in real time.... If we had real-time settlement capability and infrastructure was modernized, we wouldn’t have seen similar problems."

＆＃34;现有的两天贸易结算期使投资者和整个行业面临不必要的风险，＆＃34;特内夫说。 ＆＃34;世界上最大的金融系统没有理由不能实时结算交易...。如果我们具有实时结算功能并且基础设施得到了现代化，那么我们就不会遇到类似的问题。 ＃34;

Ken Griffin, CEO of Citadel, stopped short of asking for real-time trade settlement in his testimony to the committee. But he said that relaxing the rules to a one-day turnaround would "reduce counter-party risk holistically." While instant or same-day clearing of trades would be nice, Griffin said it would essentially require "all systems... to function at all times every day with no room for error."

城堡首席执行官肯·格里芬（Ken Griffin）在向委员会作证时没有要求实时贸易结算。但是他说，将规则放宽到一天的周转时间将“全面降低对手风险”。虽然即时或当天进行交易清算会很好，但格里芬表示，这实际上将要求所有系统每天都在所有时间运行，并且没有错误余地。

Rep. Andy Barr (R-KY) pointed out through his questioning that the current capital requirements were put in place in 2010's Dodd-Frank Wall Street Reform bill. "If anyone has a problem with [Robinhood]'s decision, their frustration should be with federal regulations," he said. Rep. William Timmons (R-SC) added that Dodd-Frank is "arguably to blame for this exorbitant capital requirement that you weren’t able to meet... Well-intentioned legislation is somewhat responsible."

众议员安迪·巴尔（R-KY）在质疑中指出，当前的资本要求已在2010年的《多德-弗兰克华尔街改革法案》中提出。如果任何人对[Robinhood]的决定有疑问，则他们的挫败感应该在于联邦法规，＆＃34;他说。众议员William Timmons（R-SC）补充说，多德-弗兰克（Dodd-Frank）可能是因为您无法满足这个高昂的资本要求而引起的……精心策划的立法在某种程度上是负责任的。

Other House members, like Rep. Rashida Tlaib (D-MI) argued forcefully for a 0.1 percent financial transactions tax, which would discourage speculative betting on meme stocks and limit algorithm-driven high-frequency trading. She noted that Hong Kong implemented a financial transactions tax that's twice as large, limiting high-frequency trading without hurting that market's significant growth.

众议院其他议员，例如众议员拉希达·特莱布（D-MI）强烈主张征收0.1％的金融交易税，这将阻止对模因股票的投机性押注，并限制算法驱动的高频交易。她指出，香港实施的金融交易税是其两倍，从而限制了高频交易，而不会损害该市场的显着增长。

But Jennifer Schulp, Director of Financial Regulation Studies at the free-market-focused Cato Institute, told the committee such taxes "often fail to raise money and distort trading in a way that's unforeseen. A financial-transaction tax might seem like a small imposition, but it often hurts individual investors' long-term goals by affecting institutions that do the trading."

但是，以自由市场为重点的卡托研究所（Cato Institute）的金融法规研究主任詹妮弗·舒尔普（Jennifer Schulp）告诉委员会，此类税收通常无法以无法预料的方式筹集资金并扭曲交易。一笔金融交易税看起来似乎很小，但通常会伤害个人投资者。通过影响进行交易的机构来实现长期目标。＆＃34;

While Schulp allowed that a financial-transactions tax might decrease total volatility in the market, she added that she doesn't think it would have had that effect in the particular case of GameStop's recent stock moves, which were driven primarily by heavy buying.

尽管舒尔普允许金融交易税可能会降低市场的整体波动性，但她补充说，她认为在GameStop最近的股票上涨的特定情况下，这不会产生这种影响。主要是通过大量购买。

To represent the retail traders who helped drive the GameStop bubble, the committee heard from Keith Gill, aka DeepFuckingValue on Reddit. He first invested in GameStop in mid-2019 (when the stock was valued at under $10) and heavily hyped the stock's value on the WallStreetBets subreddit. "The market was underestimating the prospects of GameStop's legacy business and overestimating the risks of bankruptcy," he said. "I grew up playing video games and shopping at GameStop, and I plan to keep shopping there. GameStop stores provide real value to gamers and real revenue to GameStop."

为了代表帮助推动GameStop泡沫的零售交易商，委员会从Keith Gill（又名Reddit上的DeepFuckingValue）那里获悉。他于2019年中期（当时股价低于10美元）首次投资GameStop，并在WallStreetBets subreddit上大力宣传了该股票的价值。 ＆＃34;市场低估了GameStop传统业务的前景，高估了破产风险，他说。 ＆＃34;我从小在GameStop玩视频游戏和购物，并且我打算继续在那里购物。 GameStop商店为游戏玩家提供了真正的价值，并为GameStop提供了实际的收入。＆＃34;

Gill, who has now made millions off an initial investment of about $50,000, said he still thinks GameStop is an attractive investment at its current price of about $40 a share. His optimism is thanks in large part to GameStop's potential to pivot to a more focused e-commerce strategy. "As for me, I like the stock," he said. "I'm as bullish as I've ever been on a potential turnaround, and I remain invested in the company."

吉尔现在已经从约5万美元的初始投资中获得了数百万美元的收益。他说，他仍然认为GameStop以目前的每股约40美元的价格是一项有吸引力的投资。他的乐观态度在很大程度上要归功于GameStop可以转向更专注的电子商务策略的潜力。 ＆＃34;至于我，我喜欢股票，＆＃34;他说。我一直以来都处于潜在的转机，因此我一直看涨，而我仍然投资于该公司。＆＃34;

Less represented at the hearing were the many other investors who bought into the stock at or near its peak in late January, only to see their investments crumble in days. Rep. Jim Himes (D-CT) brought up the case of Salvador Vergara, who took out a $20,000 personal loan to invest in GameStop only to see his stock's value go down 80 percent in days. "We need to be thoughtful about this," Himes said regarding how to deal with retail investors' access to the market.

在听证会上代表较少的是许多其他投资者，他们在1月下旬或接近最高点时买入了该股票，却看到他们的投资在几天内崩溃了。众议员Jim Himes（D-CT）提出了Salvador Vergara的案子，他借了20,000美元的个人贷款投资GameStop，结果发现他的股价在几天内下跌了80％。 ＆＃34;我们需要对此加以考虑，＆＃34;希姆斯谈到了如何与散户投资者打交道。进入市场。

The committee also heard from Reddit co-founder and CEO Steve Huffman, who said Reddit had found no evidence of bots or foreign agents playing a significant role in trying to unduly pump up any stock through the WallStreetBets subreddit. "We will comply with requests from regulators, but we believe the community was well within bounds of our own policies," he said.

该委员会还从Reddit联合创始人兼首席执行官史蒂夫·霍夫曼（Steve Huffman）那里获悉，他说Reddit找不到任何证据表明僵尸程序或外国代理商在试图通过WallStreetBets subreddit过度吸纳任何股票方面发挥了重要作用。 ＆＃34;我们将遵守监管机构的要求，但我们认为社区完全在我们自己的政策范围之内，＆＃34;他说。

"It's a real community," Huffman continued regarding WallStreetBets. "The self-deprecating jokes, memes, and crass at times language reflect that. There's a significant depth to the community based on the affinity users show for each other."

这是一个真实的社区，霍夫曼继续谈到WallStreetBets。 ＆＃34;自嘲的笑话，模因和无聊有时反映了这一点。根据用户之间的亲和力显示，社区具有很大的深度。

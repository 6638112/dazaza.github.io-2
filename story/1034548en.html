Today I'd like to show why the practice of paying for dates on sites like Match.com and eHarmony is fundamentally broken, and broken in ways that most people don't realize.

今天，我想展示一下为什么在Match.com和eHarmony这样的网站上为约会付费的做法从根本上被打破了，而且是以大多数人没有意识到的方式被打破的。

For another thing, as I'll explain, pay sites have a unique incentive to profit from their customers' disappointment.

另一方面，正如我将解释的那样，付费网站有一个独特的动机，那就是从客户的失望中获利。

As a founder of OkCupid I'm of course motivated to point out our competitors' flaws. So take what I have to say today with a grain of salt. But I intend to show, just by doing some simple calculations, that pay dating is a bad idea; actually, I won't be showing this so much as the pay sites themselves, because most of the data I'll use is from Match and eHarmony's own public statements. I'll list my sources at the bottom of the post, in case you want to check.

作为OkCupid的创始人，我当然有动机指出我们的竞争对手的缺陷。因此，对我今天要说的话持保留态度。但我想通过一些简单的计算表明，付费约会不是一个好主意；实际上，我不会像付费网站本身那样展示这一点，因为我将使用的大部分数据都来自Match和eHarmony自己的公开声明。我会在帖子的底部列出我的消息来源，以防你想查看。

eHarmony claims over 20 million members on their homepage, and their CEO, Greg Waldorf, reiterates that number regularly in interviews 1. If your goal is to find someone special, 20 million people is a lot of options—roughly a quarter of all singles in the U.S. This sounds awesome until you realize that most of these people can’t reply, because only paying customers are allowed to message.

EHarmony在他们的主页上声称有超过2000万会员，其首席执行官格雷格·沃尔多夫经常在第一次采访中重申这一数字。如果你的目标是找到一个特别的人，2000万人是一个很大的选择-大约占美国所有单身人士的四分之一。这听起来很棒，直到你意识到这些人中的大多数人无法回复，因为只有付费客户才能发短信。

So let's now ask the real question: of these 20 million people eHarmony claims you can flirt with, how many are actually able to flirt back? They closely guard their number of paid subscribers, with good reason. Nonetheless, we are able to deduce their base from known information. We'll give eHarmony the highest subscribership possible.

那么，现在让我们来问一个真正的问题：在eHarmony声称你可以调情的2000万人中，有多少人真的能够调情？他们严密保护自己的付费订户数量，这是有充分理由的。尽管如此，我们还是能够从已知信息中推断出他们的基础。我们会给eHarmony提供尽可能高的订阅量。

As you can see from the flow chart, the only way they don't make money is to show subscribers to other subscribers. It's the worst thing they can do for their business, because there's no potential for new profit growth there. Remember: the average account length is just six months, and people join for big blocks of time at once, so getting a new customer on board is better for them than squeezing another month or two out of a current subscriber. To get sign-ups, they need to pull in new people, and they do this by getting you to message their prospects.

正如你从流程图中看到的，他们不赚钱的唯一方式就是把订阅者展示给其他订阅者。这是他们能为自己的业务做的最糟糕的事情，因为那里没有新的利润增长潜力。记住：平均账号时长只有六个月，而且人们一次加入的时间很长，所以吸引一个新客户对他们来说比从现有订户那里挤出一两个月要好得多。为了获得注册，他们需要吸引新的人，他们通过让你向他们的潜在客户传达信息来做到这一点。

If you're a subscriber to a pay dating site, you are an important (though unwitting) part of that site's customer acquisition team. Of course, they don't want to show you too many ghosts, because you'll get frustrated and quit, but that doesn't change the fact that they're relying on you to reach out to non-payers and convince them, by way of your charming, heartfelt messages, to pull out their credit cards. If only a tiny fraction of your message gets a response, hey, that's okay, you're working for free. Wait a second… you're paying them.

如果你是付费约会网站的订户，你就是该网站客户获取团队中重要的一员(尽管不知情)。当然，他们不想给你看太多鬼魂，因为你会感到沮丧而辞职，但这并不能改变这样一个事实，即他们依赖你去联系那些不还款的人，并通过你迷人的、发自内心的信息说服他们掏出他们的信用卡。如果你的信息只有一小部分得到了回复，嘿，没关系，你的工作是免费的。等待第二个…。你在付钱给他们。

Now let's look how this skewed incentive affects the dating cycle, especially on sites like Match.com, where it's possible to for users set their own search terms.

Even more so than in real life, where fluid social situations can allow either gender to take the "lead", men drive interactions in online dating. Our data suggest that men send nearly 4 times as many first messages as women and conduct about twice the match searches. Thus, to examine how the problem of ghost profiles affects the men on pay dating sites is to examine their effect on the whole system.

A major selling point for the big for-pay dating sites Match and eHarmony is how many millions of members they have, and they drop massive numbers in their press releases and in talks with reporters. Of course, there's a solid rationale to wanting your dating site to seem gigantic. When people look for love, they want as many options as possible.

If you're interested: a somewhat wordy discussion of eHarmony's marriage rate vs. the national average eHarmony's 6.2% marriage rate is higher than the 4.5% U.S. average I calculated for comparison to Match.com, and several readers have asked whether that means joining eHarmony actually does help you get married. I believe it does not, and here's why.

如果你感兴趣：关于eHarmony的结婚率与全国平均结婚率(6.2%)的冗长讨论，比我计算的与Match.com相比的4.5%的美国平均结婚率还要高。有几位读者问道，这是否意味着加入eHarmony真的有助于你结婚。我认为它不会，这就是原因所在。

Remember, to get that 4.5%, I assumed that every person in the U.S. who's not married or living with a partner is “single and looking". That's obviously false, but I did it to be as generous as possible to Match. In that section, the more people I assumed to be “single and looking", the lower the nationwide marriage rate, thus the lower the ratio of U.S. marriages to Match.com marriages.

记住，为了得到4.5%的收入，我假设美国所有未婚或未婚同居的人都是“单身且相貌出众”。这显然是错误的，但我这样做是为了尽可能慷慨地与之匹配。在这一部分，我认为“单身和长相好看”的人越多，全国的结婚率就越低，因此美国结婚人数与Match.com结婚人数的比例也就越低。

If we want to make the same comparison for eHarmony, since their site is so much more focused on matrimony, we really should get a more realistic marriage rate for the nation as a whole. I couldn't find any hard data on this, but I will venture a guess that the nationwide marriage rate is around 9%.

如果我们想对eHarmony进行同样的比较，因为他们的网站更关注婚姻，我们真的应该得到一个更现实的全国结婚率。在这方面我找不到任何确凿的数据，但我敢猜测全国的结婚率在9%左右。

The Census's definition of "single" is anyone over 18 who's not married or living with a romantic partner. That includes prisoners, senior citizens, college students, shut-ins, swinging bachelors, and your friend who's been dating the same guy for a year but doesn't live with him. I don't think it is a stretch to say that half the 89.8 million people we used to be fair to Match aren't actually looking for a partner. That puts the nationwide marriage rate among people actually "on the market" at about 9%. Of course, nearly everyone on eHarmony is looking for a partner. So 9% to 6.2% is the apples-to-apples comparison, and it means that you're about 50% more likely to be married if you're not using eHarmony. Again, this is just an educated guess, but I believe it's in the neighborhood of the truth.

人口普查对单身的定义是指18岁以上未婚或未婚同居的人。这包括囚犯、老年人、大学生、闭门不出的单身汉，以及你的朋友，他和同一个男人约会了一年，但没有和他住在一起。我认为可以毫不夸张地说，我们过去公平配对的8980万人中，有一半没有真正寻找伴侣。这使得市场上实际的全国结婚率约为9%。当然，eHarmony上的几乎每个人都在寻找伴侣。因此，9%到6.2%是苹果和苹果之间的比较，这意味着如果你不使用eHarmony，你结婚的可能性要高出50%。再说一次，这只是一个有根据的猜测，但我相信它接近事实。

Note also that this implies that the 12.4:1 ratio I calculated for Match is probably closer to 25:1. Again, this is just an educated guess. That's why I left it out of the main article.

还要注意的是，这意味着我为Match计算的12.4：1的比例可能更接近25：1。同样，这只是一个有根据的猜测。这就是为什么我在正文中把它省略了。

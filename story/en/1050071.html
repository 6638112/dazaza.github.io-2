Judge approves $650 million Facebook privacy settlement over facial recognition feature
法官批准6.5亿美元的面部识别功能的Facebook隐私解决方案

2021-03-01 14:00:20

A federal judge on Friday gave final approval to a $650 million Facebook class action privacy settlement and ordered the 1.6 million members of the class in Illinois who submitted claims to be paid “as expeditiously as possible.”

周五，一名联邦法官最终批准了一项价值6.5亿美元的Facebook集体诉讼隐私和解协议，并命令伊利诺伊州的160万该集体成员“尽可能迅速地”获得报酬。

Chicago attorney Jay Edelson sued Facebook in Cook County Circuit Court back in 2015, alleging that the platform’s use of facial recognition tagging was not allowed under the Illinois Biometric Information Privacy Act. The lawsuit claimed that Facebook’s Tag Suggestions tool, which scanned faces in users’ photos and offered suggestions about who the person might be, stored biometric data without users’ consent in violation of the Illinois law.

芝加哥律师杰伊·埃德森（Jay Edelson）早在2015年就在库克县巡回法院（Cook County Circuit Court）起诉Facebook，声称根据《伊利诺伊州生物识别信息隐私法》，该平台禁止使用面部识别标签。该诉讼声称，Facebook的“标签建议”工具可扫描用户照片中的面部，并提供有关该人可能是谁的建议，这些存储的生物特征数据未经用户同意即违反了伊利诺伊州法律。

The three named plaintiffs in the suit will each receive $5,000 and others in the class will get at least $345 each, according to the order by Judge James Donato of the Northern District of California. Donato said the settlement was a “landmark result” and a “major win for consumers in the hotly contested area of digital privacy.”

根据加利福尼亚州北区法官詹姆斯·多纳托的命令，诉讼中的三名具名原告将各获得5,000美元，班级中的其他原告将至少获得345美元。多纳托说，和解是“具有里程碑意义的结果”，也是“在竞争激烈的数字隐私领域中消费者的重大胜利。”

“We are pleased to have reached a settlement so we can move past this matter, which is in the best interest of our community and our shareholders,” Facebook said in a statement.

Facebook在一份声明中说：“我们很高兴达成和解，以便我们能够解决此问题，这符合我们社区和股东的最大利益。”

“It’s a big deal,” Edelson told The Chicago Tribune. “It sends a pretty clear message that in Illinois, biometric privacy rights are here to stay.”

“这很重要，”埃德森告诉《芝加哥论坛报》。 “它发出了一个非常明确的信息，即在伊利诺伊州，生物识别隐私权将继续存在。”

让我们终于开始繁荣起来：密苏里州第一位黑人国会女议员

2020-11-06 14:27:56

In August, progressive activist and nurse Cori Bush shocked the Democratic establishment when she unseated 20-year congressional veteran Rep. Lacy Clay for the Democratic nomination to Missouri's 1st Congressional District. Her victory against Clay all but ensured winning the seat on Tuesday, making Bush the first Black woman to represent Missouri in Congress. It's been a long time coming.

8月，进步活动家、护士科里·布什(Cori Bush)在密苏里州第一国会选区的民主党提名中击败了拥有20年经验的国会资深众议员莱西·克莱(Lacy Clay)，震惊了民主党建制派。她对克莱的胜利几乎确保了她在周二赢得这个席位，使布什成为第一位在国会代表密苏里州的黑人女性。这已经是很久以前的事了。

Bush had attempted to primary Clay in 2018, receiving an endorsement from Alexandria Ocasio-Cortez. And while she lost that primary to Clay, it was clear the incumbent was not the formidable legacy candidate that he had been perceived to be. On Tuesday night, that dream came true for Bush and her supporters as she won decisively and gave a truly inspiring speech.

布什曾试图在2018年让克莱初选，并得到了亚历山德里亚·奥卡西奥-科尔特斯(Alexandria Ocasio-Cortez)的支持。虽然她在初选中输给了克莱，但很明显，现任总统并不是他被认为是令人敬畏的遗产候选人。周二晚上，布什和她的支持者梦想成真，她取得了决定性的胜利，并发表了一场真正鼓舞人心的演讲。

CONGRESSWOMAN-ELECT CORI BUSH: So, I was running. I was that person running for my life, across a parking lot. Running from an abuser. I remember one day hearing bullets whiz past my head, and at that moment I wondered, How do I make it out of this life? I was uninsured. I've been that uninsured person, hoping my healthcare provider wouldn't embarrass me by asking if I had insurance. I wondered, how will I bear this? I was a single parent. I've been that single parent, struggling paycheck to paycheck. Sitting outside the payday loan office wondering, how much more will I have to sacrifice? I was that COVID-19 patient. I've been that COVID-19 patient, gasping for breath, wondering how long will it be before I can breathe again? I'm still that person. I'm proud to stand before you today knowing it was this person, with these experiences, that moved the voters of St. Louis to do something historic. St. Louis, my city. My home, my community. We have been surviving and grinding, and just scraping by for so long and now this is our moment to finally start living. Let's finally start living. Let's finally start growing. Let's finally start thriving. So, as the first Black woman, and also the first nurse, and single mother to have the honor to represent Missouri in the United States Congress let me say this: To the Black women, the Black girls, the nurses, the essential workers, the single mothers—this is our moment.

当选国会女议员科里·布什：所以，我当时在竞选。我就是那个在停车场对面逃命的人。从施虐者身边逃跑。我记得有一天听到子弹从我头顶呼啸而过，那一刻我在想，我该如何度过这一生？我没有保险。我就是那个没有保险的人，希望我的医疗保健提供者不会问我有没有保险，这会让我难堪。我在想，我该怎么忍受这一切呢？我是个单亲妈妈。我一直是那个单亲父母，一份又一份地挣扎着挣钱。坐在发薪日贷款办公室外想，我还需要牺牲多少？我就是那个新冠肺炎病人。我就是那个新冠肺炎的病人，上气不接下气，在想过多久我才能再次呼吸？我还是那个人。今天，我很自豪地站在你们面前，知道正是这位拥有这些经历的人感动了圣路易斯的选民，让他们做了一些历史性的事情。圣路易斯，我的城市。我的家，我的社区。我们一直在生存和磨练，勉强维持了这么长时间，现在是我们终于开始生活的时候了。让我们终于开始生活吧。让我们终于开始成长吧。让我们终于开始欣欣向荣吧。因此，作为第一位黑人女性，也是第一位有幸代表密苏里州参加美国国会的护士和单身母亲，让我对黑人女性、黑人女孩、护士、基本工作者和单身母亲说：这是我们的时刻。

Bush went on to talk about representing the people in the America we all share, "not Trump's America" or the America led by "the small-mindedness of a powerful few, but the imagination of a mass movement that includes all of us." She talked about how she will be carrying her constituents with her everywhere she goes and to every room she works in. She gave this very moving reminder of what leadership and representative democracy can promise to be. BUSH: St. Louis, if you know nothing else, you remember this: Your congresswoman-elect, soon-to-be congresswoman loves you. Your congresswoman-elect, soon-to-be congresswoman loves you; and I need you to get that. Because if I love you, I care that you eat. If I love you, I care that you have shelter and adequate safe housing. If I love you, I care that you have clean water and clean air, and you have a livable wage. If I love you, I care that the police don't murder you. If I love you, I care that you make it home safely. If I love you, I care that you are able to have a dignity, and have a quality of life the same as the next person, the same as those that don't look like you. That didn't grow up the same way you did. Those that don't have the same socioeconomic status as you. I care.

布什接着谈到了如何代表我们共同拥有的美国人民，不是特朗普领导的美国，也不是少数有权势的人领导的美国，而是一场包括我们所有人在内的群众运动的想象力。她谈到了她将如何带着她的选民走到哪里，带到她工作的每个房间。她非常感人地提醒人们，领导力和代议制民主可以承诺成为什么样的人。布什：圣路易斯，如果你什么都不知道，那就记住这一点：你当选的国会女议员，即将成为国会女议员的人爱你。你的当选国会女议员、即将成为国会女议员的人爱你；我需要你明白这一点。因为如果我爱你，我在乎你吃什么。如果我爱你，我会关心你是否有住所和足够的安全住房。如果我爱你，我关心的是你有干净的水和干净的空气，你有可以生活的工资。如果我爱你，我在乎警察不会谋杀你。如果我爱你，我会关心你平安到家。如果我爱你，我在乎的是你能不能有尊严，有和别人一样的生活质量，和那些看起来不像你的人一样。那并不像你那样成长。那些没有和你一样的社会经济地位的人。我在乎。

Bush finished by raising her fist in the sky, along with the family and friends that stood on stage with her, and made the pledge to walk "arm in arm, with our fists in the air, ready to serve each other until every single one of us is free."

布什最后举起拳头，和与她一起站在舞台上的家人和朋友一起，誓言要手挽手、高举拳头，随时准备为彼此服务，直到我们每个人都获得自由。

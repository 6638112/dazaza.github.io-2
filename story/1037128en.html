Pandemic has deepened the digital education divide in India, where ~38% of households with students had no smartphone in the house, according to a recent survey
最近的一项调查显示，大流行病加剧了印度的数字教育鸿沟，在印度，有学生的家庭中约有38％的家庭没有智能手机。

2020-11-29 20:12:07

India Children across India are struggling to keep up because they don’t have the tech tools for online classes

印度印度各地的孩子都在努力跟上，因为他们没有在线课程的技术工具

GALLA, India—Remote learning during the pandemic has been painful, even for children with the best computers and broadband. Imagine trying to do it all on a cheap cellphone with a 2G connection.

印度加拉-大流行期间的远程学习一直很痛苦，即使对于拥有最好计算机和宽带的孩子来说也是如此。想象一下，尝试在具有2G连接的廉价手机上完成所有操作。

That is just part of the e-learning challenge that millions of families are going through now in India.

这只是印度数百万家庭正在经历的电子学习挑战的一部分。

Take the Bisht family who live in a village in the Himalayan foothills in the rural state of Uttarakhand. Three girls in grade school. One phone. No Wi-Fi.

以比什特一家为例，他家住在北阿坎德邦农村州喜马拉雅山麓的一个村庄里。小学三年级的三个女孩。一个电话没有Wi-Fi。

The girls ration their class time, picking only the ones they think are most important. When they log in, their father’s phone won’t allow streaming video so they just listen to audio.

女孩们分配他们的上课时间，只挑选她们认为最重要的时间。当他们登录时，他们父亲的电话将不允许流式传输视频，因此他们只能听音频。

The signal comes and goes so they run all over the house to get the best reception. That is generally outside on the roof, a problem because winter is coming.

信号来来去去，使它们遍及整个房子以获得最佳的接收效果。那通常是在屋顶的外面，这是一个问题，因为冬天来了。

“It’s going to be really cold,” said Komal, 10, the oldest. “But do I have a choice?”

年龄最大的10岁的科马尔说：“天气真的很冷。” “但是我有选择吗？”

India has more than 300 million people enrolled in schools, more than any other country according to Unesco, the United Nations’ culture and heritage organization. Around half of the student body are children in primary and preschool and a most of them have been learning from home since March.

联合国文化和遗产组织联合国教科文组织说，印度有3亿人入学，比任何其他国家都多。大约一半的学生是小学和学龄前儿童，他们中的大多数自三月以来一直在家学习。

India’s underfunded education system can’t cope with study from home, education experts say. Most families don’t have the multiple devices and internet connections needed to allow e-learning.

教育专家说，印度教育系统资金不足，无法应付在家学习的需要。大多数家庭没有支持电子学习所需的多种设备和互联网连接。

The average primary schoolteacher in India has 33 students, twice the number in the U.S., according to Unesco. Meanwhile, families rarely have computers or laptops, and even smartphones are scarce.

联合国教科文组织说，印度的小学老师平均有33名学生，是美国的两倍。同时，家庭很少拥有计算机或笔记本电脑，甚至智能手机也很少。

Only around 16% of households have two or more smartphones according to one recent status of education report from ASER Centre, a New Delhi think tank. Around 46% had one and a whopping 38% had no smartphone in the house.

根据新德里智囊机构ASER中心最近的一份教育报告，只有大约16％的家庭拥有两部或更多部智能手机。大约46％的人只有一部手机，而高达38％的人家里没有智能手机。

“This is a grave situation,” said R. Panth, an education specialist and former director at Nalanda University. “We don’t have the basic tools of e-learning including high-speed internet, [and a] continuous power supply, not just in rural areas, but even in the cities.”

“这是一个严重的情况，”教育专家，纳兰达大学前任主任R. Panth说。 “我们没有在线学习的基本工具，包括高速互联网，（和）持续供电，不仅在农村地区，甚至在城市。”

Just taking a break isn’t an option for most. Families need to have their children in school to free up the parents to work and to keep them on track to pass the rigorous series of exams that—for most Indians—are the only way to raise incomes.

大多数情况下，休息不是唯一的选择。家庭需要让他们的孩子在学校读书，以腾出父母的工作空间，并使他们按部就班地通过一系列严格的考试，对于大多数印度人来说，这是增加收入的唯一途径。

Many parents don’t want their children to return to physical schools. With close to 9 million Covid-19 cases in India now, only the U.S. has been worse hit. Families want their children home and learning but it is close to impossible for most to take part in live, online classes.

许多父母不希望自己的孩子重返体育学校。目前在印度有近900万例Covid-19病例，只有美国受到的打击更严重。家庭希望孩子们回家和学习，但是大多数人几乎不可能参加在线实时课程。

Teachers, students and parents have been inventing different workarounds. Some are recording classes and sending them via WhatsApp to be downloaded and watched later, for example. Other parents travel miles to free Wi-Fi spots to download videos and assignments for their children. Others are gathering small numbers of students, sitting them, socially distanced, in village courtyards for lessons. A lot of parents are just trying to force feed their children textbooks.

老师，学生和父母已经发明了不同的解决方法。例如，其中一些正在录制课程并通过WhatsApp发送，以便稍后下载和观看。其他父母则需要数英里才能到达免费的Wi-Fi地点，为孩子下载视频和作业。其他人则聚集了少量的学生，以社交方式坐在乡村的院子里上课。许多父母只是试图强迫他们的孩子教科书。

Komal’s teacher, Savita Goswami, said a lot of her students have phones but can’t sit and share because they will be too close together. Some that have phones don’t have the money to pay for the extra data charges. She has asked for government support as she has been using her own money to top up their data plans and keep them in class.

Komal的老师Savita Goswami说，她的很多学生都有电话，但不能坐下来分享，因为他们在一起太近了。一些装有电话的人没有钱支付额外的数据费用。她一直在用自己的钱来充值他们的数据计划并保持在课堂上，因此她寻求政府的支持。

“This is a tough situation for us,” she said. “We can support one or two students but we can’t help everyone.”

她说：“对我们来说这是一个艰难的处境。” “我们可以支持一两个学生，但我们不能帮助所有人。”

India’s government is painfully aware that there is no quick tech fix. It has been canceling holidays and putting off exams even as it has refused to reopen most schools, and is turning to older technology for help.

印度政府痛苦地意识到，没有快速的技术解决方案。它一直在取消假期并推迟考试，尽管它拒绝重新开放大多数学校，并且正在寻求较旧的技术来寻求帮助。

It is promoting classes through local cable television as well as radio. Only 11% of the students in the households surveyed by ASER had participated in a live online class in the week of the survey. Around 59% had been learning through textbooks, 22% had been taught through recorded video classes, 20% through television classes and 3% by radio.

它正在通过当地的有线电视和广播来促进课堂教学。在调查的一周中，ASER调查的家庭中只有11％的学生参加了在线实时课堂。大约59％的人通过课本学习，22％的人通过录制的视频课学习，20％的人通过电视课学习，3％的人通过广播学习。

Another go-to fix is having teachers call each of their students and their parents regularly to walk them through lessons.

另一个解决方法是让老师定期给每个学生和他们的父母打电话，以指导他们完成课程。

One government guide, outlining how teachers should weather this storm, suggested they call about four students and four parents a day on rotation. It also said teachers should make full use of off-the-shelf, free technology—WhatsApp, Facebook Twitter Instagram and even Blogger and LinkedIn—to interact with and educate their pupils.

一位政府指南概述了教师应该如何度过这场风暴，建议他们每天轮流叫约四名学生和四名父母。它还说，教师应该充分利用现成的免费技术-WhatsApp，Facebook Twitter Instagram甚至Blogger和LinkedIn，来与学生互动并教育他们。

Teachers could also gather YouTube videos on subjects they wish to teach, it suggests, and set them up with subtitles so their students will understand.

老师建议，他们也可以收集他们想教的YouTube视频，并为它们设置字幕，以便他们的学生理解。

Some teachers are finding even simpler ways to allow socially-distanced learning. One school in the western state of Maharashtra painted simplified lessons on 300 walls in the neighborhood. One village in the poor state of Jharkhand had students sit in front of their homes and listen to classes broadcast over loudspeakers.

一些老师正在寻找更简单的方法来进行社会远程学习。西部马哈拉施特拉邦的一所学校在附近的300堵墙上画了简化的课程。贾坎德邦（Jharkhand）贫穷州的一个村庄里，有学生坐在他们家门前，用扩音器收听广播课。

The damage from the forced learning from home is already apparent, at least with the youngest students. The percentage of students aged six to 10 who weren’t in school jumped to 5.3% this year from 1.8% two years ago, the ASER report said.

至少对于最小的学生来说，在家中强迫学习的危害已经显而易见。 ASER报告称，今年6至10岁未上学的学生比例从两年前的1.8％跃升至5.3％。

Ten months into the closure of most physical schools, there is no end in sight.

大多数体育学校关闭十个月后，眼前的景象就没有结束。

Parents in Mumbai, Bangalore and elsewhere have protested against limiting the number of online classes and returning children to school. Most Indians don’t trust the public or private school systems to protect their children while the coronavirus is still spreading.

孟买，班加罗尔和其他地方的父母抗议，反对限制在线课程的数量和让孩子重返学校。在冠状病毒仍在传播的同时，大多数印度人不信任公立或私立学校系统来保护自己的孩子。

Student Komal wishes she could go back to school. She has figured out the best spots in the house for mobile coverage and that if she dims the screen the battery will last longer.

学生Komal希望她能回到学校。她已经找到了房屋中移动覆盖范围内的最佳位置，并且如果将屏幕调暗，电池的使用寿命将更长。

Her father, a cook, has had less work since coronavirus struck but is trying to save money for another phone. With two, the family could at least keep one charged, avoid blackouts and keep his daughters online.

自从冠状病毒袭击以来，她的父亲是一名厨师，工作量减少了，但她试图为另一部手机省钱。如果有两个人，这个家庭至少可以让一个人受命，避免停电并使他的女儿们上网。

“I’m always late signing in and can’t keep up,” Komal said. “I‘m not sure I’ll make it to the next grade.”

“我总是迟到登录，无法跟上，” Komal说。 “我不确定会升入下一个年级。”

Waymo和戴姆勒卡车正在合作，将在“未来几年”在美国制造和销售自动驾驶货运卡车

2020-10-28 08:08:40

Waymo and Daimler Trucks are teaming up on autonomous haulage, aiming to build and sell a production self-driving truck in the US. The collaboration will see Waymo contribute the driverless tech that is powering its Waymo One autonomous ride-hailing program, which recently came out of closed beta in Phoenix, Arizona. There, members of the program can summon a fully-autonomous Waymo vehicle from their phone, much in the way that they might an Uber or Lyft. Back in 2019, Waymo began offering rides to the public – in closed beta – in autonomous vehicles without a human safety driver in the car; traditionally, that driver would be present at the wheel, to take over in case the system encountered a problem it couldn’t deal with itself. Now the technology is being applied to much larger vehicles. Waymo has always been upfront about seeing self-driving trucks as part of its future, and the deal with Daimler Trucks North America gives it a heavyweight partner for that vision. Daimler, indeed, is no stranger to the concept, having tested out its own self-driving truck tech all the way back in 2015. The focus of the collaboration will be the Freightliner Cascadia truck, which will be outfitted with the Waymo Driver platform. It’s a Class 8 vehicle, and already billed as one of the most advanced the Daimler-owned brand offers. It comes as standard with the so-called Detroit Assurance 5.0 suite of active safety tech, including active brake assistance and adaptive cruise control, while lane departure warnings and lane-keeping are optional. The Waymo Driver, though, will be far more capable than that. The Level 4 system envisaged for the trucks will mean they will be able to handle all driving functions, though not necessarily in every condition: heavy inclement weather, for example, could prove a challenge, though Waymo has been testing in different locations around the US to give its system a taste of alternative climates. That’s included autonomous truck testing, though as with Waymo’s passenger car partnerships the future always looked likely to involve manufacturing partners rather than an in-house truck. All the same, the path from bold self-driving idea to actual commercial haulage is a tough one, and has claimed several startups hoping to achieve the same. Earlier this year, Starsky Robotics shut down its own self-driving and remote control truck company, with its CEO sharing some cold home truths about the challenges the industry faced. Waymo and Daimler’s driverless trucks won’t be ready to purchase any time soon, it seems. “The autonomous Freightliner Cascadia truck, equipped with the Waymo Driver, will be available to customers in the U.S. in the coming years,” the two companies say, giving no more specific timeline than that. “Waymo and Daimler Trucks will investigate expansion to other markets and brands in the near future.”

Waymo和戴姆勒(Daimler)卡车正在合作进行自动运输，目标是在美国制造和销售一款量产的自动驾驶卡车。此次合作将使Waymo贡献无人驾驶技术，该技术正在为其Waymo One自动叫车项目提供动力，该项目最近在亚利桑那州凤凰城进行了封闭测试。在那里，该项目的成员可以用手机召唤一辆全自动驾驶的Waymo车辆，就像他们可能召唤优步(Uber)或Lyft一样。早在2019年，Waymo就开始向公众提供自动驾驶汽车的乘车服务--在封闭的测试版中，车内没有人类安全司机；传统上，司机会出现在方向盘上，在系统遇到无法自行处理的问题时接手。现在，这项技术正被应用到更大的车辆上。Waymo一直坦率地将自动驾驶卡车视为其未来的一部分，与戴姆勒卡车北美公司(Daimler Trucks North America)的交易使其成为这一愿景的重量级合作伙伴。事实上，戴姆勒对这一概念并不陌生，早在2015年就测试了自己的自动驾驶卡车技术。合作的重点将是Freightliner Cascadia卡车，它将配备Waymo司机平台。这是一辆8级车，已经被标榜为戴姆勒旗下品牌提供的最先进的车型之一。它是所谓的底特律保险5.0主动安全技术套件的标配，包括主动刹车辅助和自适应巡航控制，而车道偏离警告和车道保持是可选的。不过，Waymo司机的能力将远远超过这一点。为卡车设想的4级系统将意味着它们将能够处理所有驾驶功能，尽管不一定在所有条件下：例如，恶劣天气可能会被证明是一个挑战，尽管Waymo一直在美国各地的不同地点进行测试，让其系统体验一下不同的气候。这包括自动卡车测试，尽管就像Waymo的乘用车合作伙伴一样，未来看起来总是可能涉及制造合作伙伴，而不是内部卡车。尽管如此，从大胆的自动驾驶想法到实际的商业运输的道路是艰难的，已经有几家初创公司希望实现同样的目标。今年早些时候，Starsky Robotics关闭了自己的自动驾驶和遥控卡车公司，其首席执行官分享了该行业面临的挑战的一些冷酷的家庭真相。Waymo和戴姆勒(Daimler)的无人驾驶卡车似乎不会很快准备好购买。两家公司表示：“配备Waymo司机的自动货运客机卡斯卡迪亚(Cascadia)卡车将在未来几年向美国客户推出。”但没有给出更具体的时间表。“Waymo和戴姆勒卡车将在不久的将来研究向其他市场和品牌的扩张.”

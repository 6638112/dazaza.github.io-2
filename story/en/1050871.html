Procrastination is flight, deadline is fight, freeze is staring at the screen
拖延是飞行，截止日期是战斗，冻结正在盯着屏幕

2021-03-07 09:19:55

When a deer sees an incoming car, it freezes. For us, it looks stupid. But for the deer, this instinctive reaction seems to be the best. And it would be the best reaction if the car were a predator: too big to fight, too fast to outrun. By freezing it hopes that the beast fails to spot it, or gets distracted.

当一只鹿看到传入的汽车时，它冻结了。对我们来说，看起来很愚蠢。但对于鹿来说，这种本能反应似乎是最好的。如果汽车是捕食者，这将是最好的反应：太大而无法战斗，太快了。通过冻结，希望野兽未能发现它，或分心。

Yet, the car isn’t a predator. And an otherwise rational “freeze” response puts our dear deer’s life in danger, and at the driver’s reflex mercy.

然而，汽车不是捕食者。另一种理性的“冻结”反应使我们亲爱的鹿的生命危险，并在司机的反射怜悯中。

Fight, flight, and freeze are fear responses. All of these responses make sense and can save our lives. I bet they saved your physical health or even life. Maybe even it saved you from a car — as you froze before entering a road, or you jumped away in time.

战斗，飞行和冻结是恐惧的反应。所有这些反应都有道理，可以挽救我们的生活。我打赌他们拯救了你的身体健康甚至生活。也许甚至它甚至把你救了你 - 在进入道路之前冻结，或者你及时跳出来。

Yet, there are circumstances, in which these reactions are maladaptive. In this blog post, I leave physical danger aside and explore fear responses related to processing emotions and body sensations.

然而，存在这些反应的情况是不适的。在这篇博文章中，我留下了身体危险，探索与加工情绪和身体感觉相关的恐惧反应。

From a distance, procrastination looks like a time management problem. There is growing evidence that it is rather an emotional management problem — a flight response. Instead of facing the problem, we evade it. And how do other fear responses work? Here we go:

从远处，拖延看起来像一个时间管理问题。还有日益增长的证据表明它是情绪管理问题 - 飞行反应。我们逃避了它而不是面对问题。以及其他恐惧的反应如何工作？开始了：

FLIGHT: procrastination (“let’s keep it at a temporal distance!”)

航班：拖延（“让我们保持时间距离！”）

FREEZE: staring at the “chapter 1” of an unwritten report, piece of code, or email

冻结：盯着未写的报​​告，代码或电子邮件的“第1章”

If procrastination is a fear response, guilt-tripping is not helpful. Self-flagellation intensifies the problem. Self-compassion may help, as it reduces the root cause.

如果拖延是恐惧的反应，那么内疚绊倒并不有帮助。自坠落集加剧了这个问题。自同情可能有助于减少根本原因。

Identifying the source of fear is important. What are we afraid of — disappointing others, disappointing ourselves (e.g. due to perfectionism), or maybe it reminds us of similar tasks which we failed, etc. I was horrified to realize that sometimes it is a PTSD-like panic reaction.

识别恐惧源很重要。我们害怕 - 令人失望，令人失望，令人失望（例如，由于完美主义），或者它可能让我们想起我们失败的类似任务等。我被吓坏了，意识到有时它是一种类似的恐慌反应。

Why Is It So Hard to Do Something That Should Be Easy? — How to ADHD video by Jessica McCabe and Brendan Mahan on “the wall of awful” metaphor (it directly inspired this section, and maybe the whole blog post in general)

为什么这么难做到应该变得容易的事情？ - 如何通过Jessica McCabe和Brendan Mahan在“可怕的墙上”的录像机（它直接启发了这一部分，也许是整个博客文章）

What are you afraid more of — loneliness or being in chains? Do you under- or overreact? What annoys you more: a partner being unresponsive, or too pushy and controlling?

你害怕更多 - 孤独或被链条？你是否在或过度反应？什么让你烦恼更多：一个伴侣没有反应，或者太咄咄逼人和控制？

As we all know, there is the Fountain of (Relationship) Life, secure attachment style. Feeling secure enough that we are fine with the ups and downs of relationships — reacting, but not overreacting. It is not easy, as a combination of our wiring, upbringing, and the current situation is likely to head us in one (or more) unhealthy directions. Sure, we have our inclinations — but the secure attachment style is a direction for growth.

众所周知，有（关系）生活，安全附着风格的喷泉。感觉足够安全，我们对关系的起伏和沮丧 - 反应，但没有过度反应。作为我们的布线，成长和当前情况的组合，这并不容易，可能会以一个（或多个）不健康的方向领导我们。当然，我们有倾向 - 但安全的附着式风格是增长的方向。

This idea is close to non-neediness — to be self-sufficient enough that we don’t put our self-esteem in other hands. It means being OK with hearing “no”. We can be interested in someone but don’t need to chase. If we need space, we can calmly spell out our boundaries, but not run preemptively. And if the boundaries are broken, we can walk away.

这个想法与不必要的人接近 - 自给自足，我们不会把自尊放在其他手中。听到“否”意味着还可以。我们对某人感兴趣，但不需要追逐。如果我们需要空间，我们可以平静地拼出我们的界限，但不能先发产权。如果界限被打破，我们可以走开。

“Models — How to Attract Women Through Honesty” by Mark Manson, which I recommend regardless of your gender, orientation, or even if you are dating or not

“模型 - 如何通过Mark Manson通过诚实吸引妇女，我建议您推荐，无论您的性别，方向，甚至是您的约会还是不约会

You accidentally hit your finger with a hammer. What’s your first reaction? And then: what do you do next? Do you try to resist pain, talk with someone to shift focus, or try hard to not feel it at all?

你不小心用锤子击中手指。你的第一次反应是什么？然后：你接下来做什么？你试图抵制痛苦，与某人谈谈焦点，还是努力难以感受到它？

There is nothing bad in dropping a swear word (in Poland, we traditionally use one beginning with “k”). But once you know what’s going on, and know that your body is not in a state that requires immediate action, a fear-like response won’t help.

丢弃一个发誓的词（在波兰，我们传统上没有任何用“k”开始）。但是一旦你知道正在发生什么，并且知道你的身体不是一个需要立即采取行动的国家，恐惧的反应无济于事。

Next time try to… focus on the sensation. In a mindfulness way, observing and experiencing, but not judging. It won’t make the pain disappear, but it will make it less troublesome.

下次尝试...专注于感觉。以谨慎的方式，观察和经历，但不判断。它不会让疼痛消失，但它会使它不那么麻烦。

I don’t recommend hitting yourself with a hammer — neither healthy nor safe! I do recommend experimenting with other stimuli and stressors. Tried muscles after an exercise, burning sensation from chili peppers, the heat of sauna — to name a few.

我不建议用锤子击中自己 - 既不健康也不安全！我建议尝试尝试其他刺激和压力。在运动后尝试了肌肉，从辣椒烧感觉，桑拿的热量 - 名称。

Personally, I do ice swimming. I started it in Nov 2020. Had I started it earlier — I would have gone in the “resist!” mode, proving to myself (if not others) how much cold can I bear. It didn’t save me from another fear reaction, though. During my second time in the water, I got drawn into a conversation. Instead of spending 2–3 minutes (as planned), I spent 12 minutes. Sure, I spent longer, but by fleeing from the sensation. A few weeks later I met a skinny woman, who just started ice swimming, yet was able to stay 15 min. First, I thought that she has superhuman mind & body control skills. Later, I learned that she is dissociated from the body — a common pattern for nerds, who had fled from their flesh to their heads.

就个人而言，我做冰游泳。我在2020年11月开始它。我早些时候开始 - 我会进入“抗拒！”模式，证明自己（如果不是别人）我可以忍受多少寒冷。但是，它并没有拯救我另一个恐惧反应。在我的第二次在水中，我被吸引到谈话中。而不是花2-3分钟（按计划），我花了12分钟。当然，我花了更长的，但通过逃离感觉。几个星期后，我遇到了一个刚开始冰游泳的瘦妇，但能够留在15分钟。首先，我以为她有超人的心灵＆amp;身体控制技巧。后来，我了解到，她与身体分开 - 一种常见的书呆子的模式，他们从他们的肉体逃到他们的头上。

There is a different approach — embracing the sensation of cold. I like to call it “mindfulness, between the two states of water”. Such openness goes beyond our subjective discomfort. We need to open our blood vessels to warm our hands and feet. In some sense, we need to tell our body that we are not in danger, everything is under control, and we have well enough heat to keep all of our parts warm & happy. And staying long enough that I want to go, but short enough that I don’t want to flee.

有一种不同的方法 - 拥抱感冒的感觉。我喜欢称之为“谨慎，在两个水中”。这种开放性超出了我们主观的不适。我们需要打开我们的血管来温暖我们的手脚。在某种意义上，我们需要告诉我们的身体，我们没有处于危险之中，一切都在控制，我们有足够的热量来保持我们所有的零件温暖＆amp;快乐的。并保持足够长，但我想去，但足够短，我不想逃离。

“If you are at war with yourself, you will always lose” — as Stefan, my ice swimming mentor, keeps saying.

“如果你和自己在战争，你将永远失败” - 作为斯特凡，我的冰游泳导师，一直说。

Similarly as with our body — what do you do with difficult emotions? From small conflicts and frustrations to deep wounds from unsatisfied childhood needs, worst traumas, and feelings that bother you on a daily basis?

与我们的身体同样 - 你对困难的情绪做了什么？从小冲突和挫折到来自不满足的童年需求，最糟糕的创伤和每天困扰你的感情？

It took me a lot of time that my intellectualism and objectivity were a flight response. Instead of addressing the problem, I avoided it, by distancing it from myself. It was not (as I claimed, and believed) a more advanced, rational approach. Sure, rational analysis helps (here I agree with stoics) — especially in frameworks such as Nonviolent Communication by Marshall Rosenberg, which combines emotions with objectivity (the book is wonderful; for a teaser, I recommend How to Deliver Constructive Feedback in Difficult Situations). If we subtract emotions — it is a flight or freeze response, and it is not helpful in the long run.

我花了很多时间，即我的智力主义和客观性是一个飞行反应。我通过从自己倾斜它来避免解决问题而不是解决问题。它不是（正如我声称，并相信）更先进，合理的方法。肯定，理性分析有所帮助（这里我同意Stoics） - 特别是在Marshall Rosenberg的非暴力沟通等框架中，这将情绪与客观性相结合（书籍很精彩;对于一款预告片，我建议如何在困难的情况下提供建设性反馈） 。如果我们减去情感 - 它是一架飞行或冻结反应，从长远来看，它并不有用。

Processing hard emotions is often about accepting them, integrating them. It might happen gradually, and you fail to notice it. Other times it is a moment of insight, impossible to miss. This intense, somewhat strange, calming feeling of reconnection — of once-a-horror, now something seen in detail, with full awareness, but not causing you an emotional cascade. It feels as if you were again one, reassembled from splinters.

处理艰难的情绪通常是接受它们，整合它们。它可能会逐渐发生，你没有注意到它。其他时候这是一个洞察力，不可能错过。这种激烈，有点奇怪，镇静的重新连接 - 一旦恐怖，现在有一些细节看，具有充分意识，但不会导致你是一个情绪化的级联。感觉好像你再次是一个，从碎片中重新组装。

It does not mean that you suddenly start enjoying your pain (as with the hammer and the finger), or turning suffering into some mystical masochism. It’s about accepting that you are who you are. 这并不意味着你突然开始享受你的痛苦（与锤子和手指一样），或者转变为一些神秘的受虐狂。这是关于接受你是谁。这种集成的一个例子是蠕动模型的5个阶段。虽然它可能不是一个普遍的，但事实证明，这些阶段映射到恐惧响应中：

A lot of research on psychedelics and MDMA in psychotherapy is about integrating emotions. Sometimes the original cause is unknown to the patient, and they help discover that. Other times it is known, but the distress is so big that it is hard to explore. MDMA (inducing a state similar to an after-orgasm bliss) allows exploration with calmness yet full clarity. Substances such as LSD or psilocybin dissolve ego (which is a defense mechanism) and prompt a state of connecting ideas, experiences, emotions.

对心理治疗中的迷幻学和MDMA进行了大量研究，是关于整合情绪。有时，患者原因是未知的，他们有助于发现。其他时候它是众所周知的，但痛苦是如此大，难以探索。 MDMA（诱导类似于高潮之后的状态）允许勘探与平静但充分清晰度。 LSD或Psilocybin等物质溶解了自我（这是防御机制），并提示一种连接思想，经验，情绪的状态。

The deepest wounds are multilayered, a calcified shell on a calcified shell. After dissolving one part, we need to go deeper and deeper, as the root cause may go well into childhood.

最深的伤口是多层的，钙化壳上的钙化壳。在溶解一部分后，我们需要走得更远，更深刻，因为根本原因可能进入童年。

We’ve Got Depression All Wrong. It’s Trying to Save Us. —Psychology Today, on depression as the ultimate freeze response

我们有抑郁症都错了。它试图拯救我们。今天心理学，抑郁作为终极冻结反应

Realms of the Human Unconscious: Observations from LSD Research by Stanislav Grof, the grandfather of psychedelic research in psychotherapy; reading it gave me insight into darkest dreams, PTSD, and how to unentangle it

人类无意识的境界：斯坦尼斯拉夫·格罗夫（Stanislav Grof）的LSD研究的观察，心理治疗中的荧光研究祖父;阅读它让我洞察最黑暗的梦想，第四次，以及如何不受欢迎

Fear responses are natural. Yet, the same way as any other defensive responses can backfire (e.g. autoimmune diseases), fear can develop problems on its own, or turn into a self-perpetuating vicious cycle, as in this section from The Little Prince by Antoine de Saint-Exupéry:

恐惧反应是自然的。然而，与任何其他防御性反应一样可以反馈（例如自身免疫疾病）的方式，恐惧可以自己开发问题，或者转变为自我延续的恶性循环，就像Antoine de Saint-Exupéry的小王子那样：

Why are you drinking? — the little prince asked. - In order to forget — replied the drunkard. - To forget what? — inquired the little prince, who was already feeling sorry for him. - To forget that I am ashamed — the drunkard confessed, hanging his head. - Ashamed of what? — asked the little prince who wanted to help him. - Ashamed of drinking! — concluded the drunkard, withdrawing into total silence.

你为什么喝酒？ - 小王子问道。 - 为了忘记 - 回答醉汉。 - 忘记什么？ - 询问小王子，谁对他感到难过。 - 忘记我惭愧 - 醉酒融合了，挂着他的头。 - 惭愧的是什么？ - 问想帮助他的小王子。 - 惭愧喝酒！ - 结束了醉汉，撤回了总沉默。

My point is very far from “just don’t be afraid”. Being afraid of showing one’s fear is also fear. Instead, I suggest adopting a Zen concept that our minds should be like water — reacting, but not overreacting. Not frozen water, not a pond full of piranhas.

我的观点离“只是不要害怕”。害怕表达一个人的恐惧也是恐惧。相反，我建议采用禅宗概念，以至于我们的思想应该像水 - 反应一样，但没有过度反应。不是冷冻的水，不是一个充满食人鱼的池塘。

Being aware of one’s reactions is the first step. Whenever you feel distressed — try to check if it is a fight-like, flight-like, or freeze-like response. There may be patterns in your life — global, depending on the affected aspect of your life (e.g. body: mostly fight, emotions: mostly freeze), or a particular situation. When you notice the pattern — smile. You saw it. It is not a place to punish yourself for the fear response. An intellectual analysis is a powerful tool, but only when combined with emotions, not used instead.

意识到一个人的反应是第一步。每当你感到痛苦时 - 尝试检查它是否是争夺的，飞行等，或冻结的响应。您的生活中可能存在模式 - 全球性，取决于您生命中受影响的方面（例如身体：主要是战斗，情绪：大多冻结）或特定情况。当你注意到模式 - 微笑。你看到了它。这不是惩罚恐惧反应的地方。智力分析是一个强大的工具，但只有在与情绪相结合时，才能不使用。

Reintegrating parts of yourself takes time. Self-compassion is crucial, as well as kindness, warmth, and acceptance towards oneself. The environment in which we feel secure makes a day-and-night difference. Think of your broken parts as of a wounded kitten. They need time and care to recover. Pushing it to recover now, or to walk despite wounds, would be cruel. Don’t do to yourself what you wouldn’t do to a cute kitten.

重新融入自己需要时间。自我同情是至关重要的，以及善意，温暖和对自己的接受。我们觉得安全的环境使夜间差异。想到你受伤的小猫的破碎部分。他们需要时间并小心恢复。推动它现在恢复，或者尽管伤口走路，那将是残忍的。不要对自己做的，你不会对一只可爱的小猫做些什么。

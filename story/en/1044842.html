Can You Treat Loneliness by Creating an Imaginary Friend?
您可以通过创建一个想象中的朋友来对待孤独吗？

2021-01-18 13:11:33

Did you ever have an imaginary friend? If you didn’t, chances are you know someone who did. Imaginary companions, as scholars call them, are quite common, and aren’t strictly associated with childhood. They can last into the upper teen years. One of the most striking things about imaginary companions, which are generally considered harmless, is, of course, their apparent autonomy. To the child, although they know that it is an imaginary being, the friend apparently has a mind of its own—sometimes saying it’s too busy to play, for example. This can seem odd, because one of the most common reasons a child creates an imaginary friend is for company.

你有没有想象中的朋友？如果您没有这么做，那么您很可能认识某个曾经做过的人。像学者所说的想象中的同伴很普遍，并且与童年并不严格相关。他们可以持续到青少年时期。关于假想伴侣的最惊人的事情之一，通常被认为是无害的，当然是他们明显的自主权。对于孩子来说，尽管他们知道这是一种虚构的存在，但这位朋友显然有自己的想法-例如，有时说孩子太忙了，无法玩耍。这看起来很奇怪，因为孩子创建虚构朋友的最常见原因之一就是陪伴。

We can all relate to imaginary characters we can’t control—it’s the experience we have with the people we meet in our dreams. Dream characters appear autonomous, although they are not. It is more accurate to say that we do not have conscious control over our dream characters. Of course, some part of our mind is controlling those characters; we just experience the illusion that they have minds of their own. Over the last several years, a community of people, interacting mostly in online forums, like Reddit, have discovered a way to create something like imaginary companions as adults. This process is known as tulpamancy, and the people who engage in it call themselves “tulpamancers.”

我们都可以与我们无法控制的想象人物相关联，这是我们与我们在梦中相识的人在一起的经历。梦中角色看起来是自治的，尽管不是。准确地说，我们没有梦dream以求的角色。当然，我们头脑中的某些部分正在控制这些角色；我们只是体验到他们拥有自己的想法的错觉。在过去的几年中，一个主要在Reddit之类的在线论坛中进行互动的人社区已经发现了一种创建像想象中的同伴一样成人的方法。此过程称为“ tulpamance”，参与该过程的人称自己为“ tulpamancers”。

The term “tulpa” seems to originate from Tibetan Buddhism. Samuel Veissière, an anthropologist and cognitive scientist at McGill University,  describes tulpas as “imaginary companions who are said to have achieved full sentience after being conjured through ‘thoughtform’ meditative practice.” In other words, this is a benign hallucination. But unlike typical childhood imaginary companions, creating (or “forcing”) a tulpa often requires months of hard work. Tulpamancers imagine talking to the tulpa, sometimes for more than an hour a day, and eventually, perhaps after several months, the tulpa will start talking back.

“塔尔帕”一词似乎起源于藏传佛教。麦吉尔大学的人类学家和认知科学家塞缪尔·维西耶尔（SamuelVeissière）将塔尔帕斯描述为“想象中的同伴，据说他们在通过'思维形式'的冥想练习被召唤后已经获得了充分的感悟。”换句话说，这是一种良性的幻觉。但是与典型的儿时想象中的同伴不同，制造（或“强迫”）塔尔巴犬通常需要数月的艰苦工作。 Tulpamancers想象与tulpa交谈，有时一天会花费一个多小时，最终，也许几个月后，tulpa将开始回头。

What is interesting to me about this phenomenon, which is only now beginning to be studied scientifically, is the reason that people decide to create a tulpa in the first place: Most often they do it to relieve loneliness. This, too, seems to be connected with imaginary companions. When you have an imaginary friend, or a tulpa, you always have someone to talk to. It can be used as a way to escape unwanted solitude. (Others use them for advice, or to practice social situations that cause anxiety.) “There is likely no causal relation between tulpamancy and the development of psychopathology,” a 2017 paper concluded. “Tulpas are an experience of plurality [in consciousness] that seem to coexist with optimal functionality, happiness, and mental health.”

对我来说，这种现象的有趣之处在于，人们决定首先制造一种塔尔帕琴，而这只是为了减轻孤独感，这种现象才刚刚开始被科学地研究。这似乎也与虚构的伴侣有关。当您有一个虚构的朋友或塔尔巴舞时，总会有人与您交谈。它可用作逃避不必要的孤独的一种方式。 （其他人将其用作咨询或实践会引起焦虑的社交情况。）“ 2017年发表的一篇论文总结道，“在放疗和心理病理学发展之间可能没有因果关系”。 “塔尔帕斯人是一种（意识）多元的经历，似乎与最佳功能，幸福和心理健康并存。”

So, what at first may strike many as a strange curiosity can have practical value.  Some people, for various reasons, are forced into isolation. Take  solitary confinement. Prisoners hate it, and it is considered by many to be cruel, because isolating people for prolonged periods can cause them to emotionally break down.

因此，起初可能引起许多奇怪的好奇心可能具有实用价值。由于各种原因，有些人被迫隔离。单独隔离。囚犯讨厌它，而且许多人认为这是残酷的，因为长时间隔离人们会导致他们情绪崩溃。

Isolation can also come in the form of a rare nervous-system disorder that results in locked-in syndrome. This problem (which is the topic of the hit Metallica song “One”) is characterized by an inability to move the body, while consciousness remains completely intact. Typically, these patients can move their eyes up and down, or sometimes blink. One locked-in patient wrote an entire book about the experience, The Diving Bell and the Butterfly, by blinking his eye. (As you might imagine, it’s not a long book). Although locked-in patients might have people around them, they, too, experience isolation because their ability to communicate with other people is either non-existent or extraordinarily slow.

隔离也可以罕见的神经系统疾病的形式出现，导致锁定综合征。这个问题（这是热门的Metallica歌曲“ One”的主题）的特征是无法移动身体，而意识却保持完整。通常，这些患者可以上下移动眼睛，有时甚至眨眼。一名被锁定的患者眨了眨眼，就写了一本关于这种经历的完整书《潜水铃和蝴蝶》。 （您可能会想象，这不是一本很长的书）。尽管被锁定的患者周围可能会有人在身边，但他们也经历了孤立，因为他们与他人交流的能力不存在或异常缓慢。

The techniques of forcing a tulpa have not been scientifically validated, and now exist only as collected advice and recommendations from practitioners communicating on the internet. What many aspiring tulpamancers do is imagine their tulpa in a paracosm, an imaginary world, in as vivid detail as possible. They might ascribe things to the tulpa, by saying “You’re creative,” or “You like mambo music.” But you are not supposed to make the tulpa say anything in your imagination—when the tulpa is “ready,” it will speak to you.

强迫塔尔帕的技术尚未经过科学验证，现在仅作为从互联网上进行交流的从业人员收集的建议和建议而存在。许多有抱负的tulpamancers所做的就是尽可能地生动地想象他们的塔尔帕在一个幻想世界，一个虚构的世界中。他们可能会说“您很有创造力”或“您喜欢曼波音乐”，从而将事情归因于塔尔巴舞。但是，您不应该让图尔帕在您的想象中说出任何话-当图尔帕“准备就绪”时，它将对您说话。

If this proves effective, then there is a potential for isolated people to escape some of their loneliness through tulpamancy. Making this happen would only require these practices to be shown to people who have to spend a lot of time alone. For locked-in patients, it could be done by playing an audio recording of tulpamancy instruction. (Personally, I’m interested in engaging in a research project to help teach locked-in patients how to create tulpas, but I require a collaborator with access to locked-in patients—I’m still looking.)

如果这被证明是有效的，那么孤立的人就有可能通过喧嚣摆脱一些孤独。要做到这一点，只需要向那些不得不花费大量时间的人展示这些做法。对于锁定患者，可以通过播放tulpamancy指令的录音来完成。 （就我个人而言，我有兴趣参与一个研究项目，以帮助教会锁定的患者如何制作tulpas，但我需要协作者才能接触锁定的患者，但我仍在寻找。）

One of the main concerns of space travel is the  mental health and loneliness of astronauts. Like a locked-in patient, an astronaut’s communication with Earth is slowed down considerably by the limitations of the speed of light. Mars is between about four and 24 light-minutes away from Earth, depending on where these planets are in their orbits. Conversations where each communication takes four minutes to arrive are stilted, to say the least. So it is exciting to think that some solutions to the challenge of prolonged solitude might be achievable just through  the use of our own minds.

太空旅行的主要问题之一是宇航员的心理健康和孤独感。就像被锁定的病人一样，由于光速的限制，宇航员与地球的通讯速度大大降低。火星距离地球大约4至24光分钟，这取决于这些行星在其轨道上的位置。至少可以说，每次交流都需要四分钟才能到达的会话很令人沮丧。因此令人兴奋的是，仅通过使用我们自己的思想，就可能解决一些针对长期孤独的挑战的解决方案。

Baby infected with coronavirus in the womb – new study
婴儿在子宫内感染了冠状病毒–最新研究

2021-03-02 05:38:05

A pregnant woman with suspected COVID-19 was rushed by ambulance to Skåne University Hospital, in Malmo, Sweden, suffering from sudden severe abdominal pain. The doctors noticed that the unborn infant had an abnormally low heart rate, which can be a sign that the baby is not getting enough oxygen.

一名疑似COVID-19的孕妇被救护车送往瑞典马尔默的斯科讷大学医院，患上了突然的严重腹痛。医生注意到未出生的婴儿心率异常低，这可能表明婴儿没有得到足够的氧气。

The doctors performed an emergency caesarean section and delivered the baby within minutes. Blood tests from the baby confirmed it had severely low oxygen, and throat swabs showed that both mother and baby were suffering from COVID.

医生进行了紧急剖腹产，并在几分钟之内分娩了婴儿。婴儿的血液检查证实它的氧气非常低，喉咙拭子表明母亲和婴儿都患有COVID。

Using throat swabs from the mother and the newborn, the genome of the virus was sequenced to confirm the possibility that the infant had been infected with COVID while still in the womb. My colleagues and I – part of a study team at the hospital – found that the viral genome in the mother and the baby was identical. Since the baby had been isolated from the mother directly after the caesarean and had not come in contact with other family members when these tests were done, the findings confirmed that the baby was indeed infected before it was born.

使用来自母亲和新生儿的咽拭子，对该病毒的基因组进行了测序，以确认婴儿仍在子宫中而感染了COVID的可能性。我和我的同事（属于医院研究团队的一部分）发现，母亲和婴儿的病毒基因组是相同的。由于这些婴儿在剖腹产后立即与母亲隔离，并且在进行这些检查时没有与其他家庭成员接触，因此调查结果证实该婴儿确实在出生之前就已被感染。

However, a few days later, new genetic sequencing showed that the baby’s virus population had changed and contained a mutated version of the virus along with the original virus strain from the mother. To the best of our knowledge, this is the first case of a genetic change of the coronavirus in the unique setting of mother-to-foetus transmission before birth.

但是，几天后，新的基因测序表明婴儿的病毒种群发生了变化，其中包含该病毒的突变版本以及来自母亲的原始病毒株。据我们所知，这是在出生前母婴间传播的独特环境中冠状病毒遗传变化的第一例。

Although it is common for viruses to mutate, this mutation (called A107G) happened just five days after the baby was delivered. The genetic changes may have been stimulated by the baby coming in contact with the external environment outside the mother’s womb. However, it was surprising how quickly this single mutation occurred.

尽管病毒通常会发生突变，但这种突变（称为A107G）在婴儿出生后仅五天就发生了。婴儿与母亲子宫外的外部环境接触可能会刺激遗传改变。但是，令人惊讶的是，这种单一突变发生的速度如此之快。

The most important findings were the changes we saw in the placenta. The placenta takes blood and nutrients to the foetus and takes away waste and is critical for the growth and wellbeing of the foetus. We found that half the tissue was damaged. There was widespread inflammation, and we found coronavirus protein on both the mother’s and foetus’s side of the placenta. We also found coronavirus protein in all areas that were damaged by inflammation.

最重要的发现是我们在胎盘中看到的变化。胎盘将血液和营养物质带入胎儿，并带走了废物，这对胎儿的成长和健康至关重要。我们发现一半的组织被破坏了。发生了广泛的炎症，我们在胎盘的母亲侧和胎儿侧都发现了冠状病毒蛋白。我们还在所有被炎症破坏的区域中发现了冠状病毒蛋白。

The mother made a quick recovery from her COVID infection and was discharged four days after delivery, but the baby needed neonatal care since it was born prematurely (week 34 of pregnancy). The baby developed antibodies against the virus and had no severe symptoms after delivery. It was, therefore, the baby’s own immune system that neutralised the virus as we did not find any antibodies in the mother’s breast milk.

母亲从其COVID感染中迅速康复，并在分娩后四天出院，但由于婴儿早产（怀孕第34周），因此需要新生儿护理。婴儿产生了针对病毒的抗体，分娩后没有严重症状。因此，正是婴儿自身的免疫系统中和了病毒，因为我们在母亲的母乳中没有发现任何抗体。

Our study, which has just been published in The British Journal of Obstetrics and Gynaecology, is among only a handful of scientific papers that have investigated coronavirus transmission through the placenta. Previous studies have reported rapid placental failure and abnormal foetal heart rhythm, similar to what we found. But with thousands of pregnant women infected worldwide, mother-to-baby transmission in the womb seems to be a rare complication of COVID during pregnancy.

我们的研究刚刚发表在《英国妇产科学杂志》上，是少数研究冠状病毒通过胎盘传播的科学论文之一。先前的研究报道了快速的胎盘衰竭和胎儿心律异常，与我们发现的相似。但是，由于全世界有成千上万的孕妇受到感染，子宫内母婴传播似乎是怀孕期间罕见的COVID并发症。

Scientists think that this is because of the placental barrier that protects the baby in the womb from most infections. Also, the vital receptor needed for coronavirus entry into cells, called an ACE-2 receptor, only exists in low levels in the placenta.

科学家认为，这是因为胎盘屏障保护了子宫内的婴儿免受大多数感染。同样，冠状病毒进入细胞所需的重要受体，称为ACE-2受体，仅在胎盘中以低水平存在。

In rare cases, coronavirus can damage the placenta – leading to a lack of oxygen in the unborn child – even if the mother has a mild case of COVID in late pregnancy. Our findings suggest that perhaps we should rethink how we monitor pregnant women who have COVID, and they should be considered a more important risk group than we do today.

在极少数情况下，即使母亲在妊娠晚期患有轻度COVID病例，冠状病毒也可能损害胎盘-导致未出生的孩子缺氧。我们的发现表明，也许我们应该重新考虑如何监测患有COVID的孕妇，应该将其视为比我们今天更为重要的风险人群。

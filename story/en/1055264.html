Plans to Solve Earth’s Plastic Issue Using Controlled Black Holes
计划使用受控的黑洞解决地球的塑料问题

2021-04-01 19:33:33

Climate scientists and activists have praised a groundbreaking project by a UK based space company that could completely eradicate the planet’s plastic pollution problem by the year 2030. Space Forge, an ambitious startup that will use an in-orbit manufacturing platform to create the world’s newest supermaterials and bring them back for use on Earth, developed the idea whilst thinking of a solution to remove the ever increasing amounts of space junk that circles our planet. They are building the first Mini Hadron Collider to be launched and used in an extraterrestrial capacity. Combined with the unique environment of space, the team will use the MHC to create a series of controlled, nanoscopic black holes to attract, and indefinitely remove, the millions of pieces of human made debris floating in orbit. With dreams of being carbon negative, Space Forge are a company that have sustainability firmly rooted into their psyche. Not satisfied with merely fixing the burning issue of satellite space junk, they quickly went back to the drawing board to find solutions of how to bring these space made black holes back to Earth in order to efficiently dispose of plastic waste. Although transporting black holes is literally an impossible proposition, the technical team were able to develop a pioneering technique to achieve just this. By following the Process of Reverse Atmospheric Nucleus Charging, or PRANC, Space Forge are able to safely deliver small black holes back to a factory in Slough, UK, where the procedure of plastic spaghettification will begin. Whilst designing and implementing a task of this size isn’t easy, organising and working within disparate teams during a global pandemic was arguably the biggest challenge. Luckily, Space Forge drafted and tested their Mini Hadron Collider in Valispace, the smart browser based tool for complex hardware projects. Even though many members worked on this project simultaneously in different locations in all corners of the world, they could be sure that by using Valispace, critical data was accurate and visual project updates were able to be viewed by all departments at any time.

How the Pandemic Has Made the Creative Class Feel Free
大流行如何让创意课随意

2021-04-05 05:53:54

A few years ago, I served on my building’s co-op board with someone whose daily logistical demands made me realize I was just coasting.

几年前，我在我的建筑物的合作板上与当天后勤需求让我意识到我只是雪地的合作委员会。

An investment banker with a young family, he traveled every week, often to multiple cities within the course of several days. He might call into one of our monthly meetings from Dallas, but he had been in Tokyo the day before and was on his way to Brussels the next. Before the pandemic, his schedule put him in Asia once a quarter and in Europe nearly every week.

他每周旅行一家带有年轻家庭的投资银行家，经常在几天内到多个城市。他可能会召集来自达拉斯的每月一次会议之一，但他一天前一天都在东京，并在他的路上去布鲁塞尔下一个。在大流行之前，他的日程安排每周近一季度和欧洲将他放在亚洲。

When I caught up with him recently, I wondered what this unusual year of stillness had been like for him, whether he had grown antsy. How easily does the road warrior lay down his boarding pass, submit to the gods of house-tending and say, “Take me?” He had left New York on business only once during the entire year — flying to St. Louis and returning the same day.

当我最近赶上他时，我想知道这个不寻常的静止年份对他来说是对他的，无论他是否已经增长了疯狂。道路战士有多容易地躺在他的登机牌中，提交给房屋抚恤度的神，说：“带我？”他在全年里只离开了纽约的纽约 - 飞往圣路易斯，并在同一天返回。

“It was as though I had PTSD from that single trip,” he told me, recounting the bad food, the bumpy ride to the airport, the rickety plane, the terrible infrastructure, as if it had all come back in a whoosh. “I have not missed one second of domestic or international travel. What has replaced it? Exercise. Being with your kids. Greater productivity.

“就好像我从那个旅行那样接触了，”他告诉我，讲述了糟糕的食物，崎岖的骑行到机场，摇摇晃晃的飞机，可怕的基础设施，好像它都回到了一个whoosh。 “我没有错过国内或国际旅行的一秒钟。更换了什么？锻炼。和你的孩子在一起。更高的生产力。

“No one in my world is saying, ‘Wow, I can’t wait to get back to doing everything the way we did it before.’”

“我的世界中没有人说，”哇，我迫不及待地想回到我们之前所做的方式。“

What will we preserve of Covid life, when its tragedies and deprivations begin to recede? For people fortunate enough to have spent the past year working from their houses and apartments, even if at makeshift desks or in closets, the profound rearrangements to professional, domestic and social life have, in many cases, shifted priorities and brought a new kind of enlightenment.

当悲剧和剥夺开始后退后，我们将保留苍白的生活？对于人们幸运的是，在过去的一年里度过了他们的房屋和公寓工作，即使在临时书桌或壁橱里，在许多情况下，国内和社会生活的深刻排略，在许多情况下，会出现优先事项并带来了一种新的启示。

Even for those not in the habit of flying to Geneva every Monday, the time otherwise spent commuting has been put to better use — working, dog walking, running, grocery shopping, hugging the children you are no longer angrily pushing out the door in the morning, because there is nowhere to go.

即使是那些没有每周一飞往日内瓦的人，否则花费的时间已经被换取了更好的用途 - 工作，狗散步，跑步，杂货店，拥抱孩子你不再愤怒地推出门早上，因为没有去的地方。

In the fall, a working paper out of the Becker Friedman Institute at the University of Chicago revealed that Americans were commuting 60 million fewer hours per day, and they were devoting two-thirds of that time to themselves — to home improvement, chores, family, leisure and so on.

在秋季，芝加哥大学的Becker Friedman Institute的一份工作文件透露，美国人每天上下班6000万小时，他们将三分之二的时间奉献给自己 - 为家庭改善，家庭改善，休闲等等。

Residents of New York State had the longest commuting times of workers anywhere in the country — an average of 66 minutes a day. For all the new anxieties that gripped us, a certain ease and strengthened sense of connection evolved in tandem with them — at the very least, you now had an extra hour a day for Headspace or tele-therapy or Googling exes.

纽约州的居民在全国各地的工人上涨最长的时间 - 平均每天66分钟。对于所有抓住我们的新焦虑，一定的缓和和加强的联系感，其中与他们一起演变 - 至少你现在有一个额外的小时，每天有一个额外的时间，用于前空或远程治疗或谷葡萄酒exes。

Last month, the urban theorist Richard Florida struck a chord on Twitter when he expressed something many people were feeling but have been ashamed to admit — that there were aspects of lockdown they really liked. “I feel melancholy about returning to the old normal,” he wrote. “I have gotten off the rat race. I found something deeper in this strange pandemic life that I am not sure I want to give up.” Also accustomed to constant travel, he had been able to spend time with his young children.

上个月，当他表达许多人感受到的东西时，城市理查德佛罗里达州的一个和弦在Twitter上击中了一个和弦，但是被羞于承认 - 他们真的很喜欢锁定的方面。 “我觉得回到了旧正常的忧郁，”他写道。 “我已经脱离了老鼠。我发现这种奇怪的大流行生活中的东西更深，我不确定我想放弃。“也习惯于不断旅行，他能够与幼儿共度时光。

“They say the years before 4 are the most important,” he told me, “and I was there for a full year.’' Much to his surprise, and despite the limitations of lecturing via Zoom, he received the highest teacher ratings of his career. His class at the University of Toronto, The City and Business, benefited from all the guest speakers he could bring into an online classroom — experts from around the world who were unlikely to travel to Canada to spend 45 minutes talking about municipal financing.

Mr. Florida, who lives in the Rosedale section of Toronto, not far from the university, got to know his neighborhood in ways he hadn’t previously. “I used to bike on the weekdays before the pandemic, but I was the only man over 50 — there were women and children and nannies, and I was like this strange guy lurking around,” he said. But now, so many more people were outside and wandering, and Mr. Florida met them — men with whom he has formed close friendships. (During the past year, he biked more and drank less and lost 25 pounds, he told me.)

佛罗里达州先生，他住在多伦多的Rosedale段，距大学不远，以先前没有以前的方式了解他的邻居。 “我曾经在大流行前的工作日骑自行车，但我是唯一超过50的男人 - 有女性和儿童和保姆，我就像这个潜伏在这个陌生的家伙，”他说。但现在，更多的人在外面和徘徊，佛罗里达州先生遇到了他们 - 与他联系的男人，他已经形成了密切的友谊。 （在过去的一年里，他告诉我，他骑了更多，喝了25磅。）

The serendipity of social life now is something that I have really come to appreciate — texting a friend at 8 in the morning and meeting her for a walk, plans dictated only by the weather. When there is very little to do, there is very little to schedule, which means that it is hard to remember the last time you were forced to exchange 14 emails over three days to land on a dinner date with two other people — one of them flying in from Cleveland — six weeks down the line.

现在的社会生活的大肠是我真的来欣赏 - 早上8点发短信给朋友，并在散步上遇到她，计划只有天气决定。当较少的时候，很少有时间安排，这意味着很难记住你最后一次被迫在三天内交换14封电子邮件，以与另外两个人在晚餐日期到达晚餐日期 - 其中一个从克利夫兰飞往 - 六周左下。

The status competition that has always animated New York — social, professional, intellectual, parental — seems to have faded, if only because it is so much easier to avoid. One distinct advantage to reduced human contact is the marked decline in unpleasant encounters with the perpetually self-marketing — the people who can’t wait to tell you that their 4-year-old is reading “The Decameron,” or how great the skiing was in Alta over winter break. You know what you don’t have to do during a pandemic? You don’t have to go to a class potluck thrown by someone whose sole purpose is showing off a $2 million townhouse renovation. With oblivion comes psychic peace.

纽约始终动画的地位竞争 - 社会，专业，智力，父母 - 似乎已经褪色，如果只是因为它更容易避免。减少人类联系的一个明显的优势是令人不快的营销令人难以置疑的遭遇的显着下降 - 迫切不要等待告诉你他们4岁的人正在阅读“黛米，”或者滑雪有多伟大在阿尔塔在冬季休息。你知道在大流行期间你不必做些什么吗？您不必前往唯一目的展示200万美元的联排别装修的人抛出的课堂上。遗忘有精神和平。

The people who cannot isolate themselves from the obnoxiously wealthy are, of course, the people who serve them. If the current crisis has deepened economic inequality, it has also amplified the distinction between the routines of an emergent working-leisure class, in full control of how its time is managed, and an on-call class, consisting of workers who must remain forever in motion both to earn a living and to meet the needs of the affluent.

当然，不能从令人讨厌的富裕融入富裕的人的人是为他们服务的人。如果目前的危机加深了经济不平等，它还放大了紧急工作休闲课程的常规之间的区别，全面控制其时间如何管理，以及由必须保持永远持有的工人的呼叫课程议案既可以谋生，以满足富裕的需求。

Many people will be able to carry the best of the pandemic lifestyle into the future — getting on the Peloton, in between morning meetings. Companies, in many instances, will be delighted to shrink their real estate footprints and shift the burdens and expenses of office management onto employees operating at home.

许多人将能够携带最大的大流行生活方式进入未来 - 在早上会议之间迈出佩洛顿。在许多情况下，公司将很高兴地缩小他们的房地产足迹，并将办公室管理的负担和费用转移到家里运营的员工。

But millions of other people will be trapped in jobs that make getting a five-minute break to go to the bathroom an Olympian challenge. And in some sense, this will be if they are lucky enough to have the sort of jobs that won’t be affected by the emptying out of central business districts and the inevitable long-term decline of work travel.

但数百万其他人将被困在工作中，让休息五分钟休息一下奥林匹克挑战。在某种意义上，如果他们很幸运，那就足以让这些工作不会受到中央商业区排空的影响，以及工作旅行的不可避免的长期下降。

“A lot of people depend on that prior lifestyle,” my banker neighbor remarked. He expressed concern for all the attendant commerce at La Guardia and J.F.K. International. “The Uber driver, the pilot, the guy who gives me the coffee in the airport. I don’t need to be here, but lots of people in Queens need me to be.”

“很多人都依靠那个先前的生活方式，”我的银行家邻居评论道。他对La Guardia和J.F.K的所有服务员的商业表示关切。国际的。 “超级司机，飞行员，在机场给我咖啡的那个人。我不需要在这里，但是皇后士的很多人都需要我。“

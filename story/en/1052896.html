You may have noticed an influx of ads for furniture on your Instagram feed after looking for a new chair for your work-from-home setup, or promoted posts for a coffee shop that you’ve only ever walked past. Your phone’s apps collect and share a lot of information—from your location, to your browsing habits, to your search history.

您可能已经注意到您的Instagram Feed上的家具涌入了您的工作 - 从家庭设置的新椅子，或促进您只走过去的咖啡店的帖子。您的手机的应用程序会收集并分享您的浏览习惯，从您的位置收集并分享许多信息 - 您的搜索历史记录。

Apple announced in June 2020 that this spring it would begin requiring iPhone, iPad, and tvOS apps to get consent to share people’s data with third parties like data brokers and other apps.

Apple于2020年6月宣布，这个春天它将开始要求iPhone，iPad和TVOS应用程序同意与数据经纪人和其他应用程序中的第三方共享人的数据。

The move is a complete rethinking of privacy rights. Data collection has long operated under the premise that millions of people are fine with being tracked, their movements and behaviors shared and sold, unless they explicitly say no. Privacy settings are usually opt-out and often buried deep in an app’s settings. But soon people using iPhones will be asked to explicitly opt in to having their data shared among advertisers, apps, and data brokers.

此举是完全重新思考隐私权。数据收集长期以来在前提下运作，数百万人被履行，他们的运动和行为共享和出售，除非他们明确说不。隐私设置通常是选择退出，并且经常在应用程序的设置中深入埋入。但很快就会要求使用iPhone的人明确选择在广告商，应用和数据经纪人之间共享的数据。

Apple CEO Tim Cook explained the change in a Jan. 28 speech at the Computers, Privacy and Data Protection conference.

Apple Ceo Tim Cook解释了1月28日在计算机，隐私和数据保护会议上的演讲的变化。

“Technology does not need vast troves of personal data, stitched together across dozens of websites and apps, in order to succeed. Advertising existed and thrived for decades without it,” Cook said. “If a business is built on misleading users, on data exploitation, on choices that are no choices at all, then it does not deserve our praise. It deserves reform.”

“技术不需要庞大的个人数据拖网，缝合数十个网站和应用程序，以取得成功。厨师说，广告存在并繁荣了几十年。“ “如果在误导性用户上建立了一个商业，在数据剥削上，在没有选择的选择上，那么它不值得我们的赞誉。它值得改革。“

Some tech companies—namely the ones that rely on amassing personal data to sell advertisements to companies looking to reach specific demographics—are less than happy.

一些科技公司 - 即依靠散发个人数据来向寻求达到特定人口统计数据的公司销售广告的人 - 不到快乐。

In Facebook’s 2020 fourth quarter and full year earnings report, the company predicted a major hit to its ad targeting capabilities because of Apple’s privacy changes. And Google has warned app publishers that they “ may see a significant impact” to their ad revenue after the policies take effect.

在Facebook的2020年第四季度和全年收益报告中，由于苹果公司的隐私变化，该公司预测其广告目标能力的重大打击。在政策生效后，谷歌已经警告了应用程序发布者，他们“可能会看到重大影响”到他们的广告收入。

Facebook declined to comment for this story. Google declined to comment on Cook’s remarks.

Facebook拒绝评论这个故事。谷歌拒绝评论厨师的言论。

“This is actually a very good thing for most people,” Pete Snyder, senior privacy researcher at Brave Software and the co-chair of the W3C Privacy Interest Group, said. “The state of people’s privacy on iOS devices will be dramatically better than it is today.”

“这对大多数人来说，这实际上是一个非常好的东西，”勇敢的软件和W3C隐私利益集团的高级隐私研究员，高级隐私研究员，“勇敢的软件兴趣集团的高级隐私研究员。 “人们对iOS设备的隐私的状态将比今天更好地变得更好。”

While the forthcoming changes are significant, they don’t completely shield you from being tracked, particularly by the biggest tech firms, like Apple itself. Here’s a rundown of what to expect.

虽然即将到来的变化很重要，但他们并没有完全屏蔽你被追踪，特别是最大的技术公司，如苹果本身。这是一个预期的崩溃。

Currently, apps gather all sorts of information about you as you use them—that’s not going to change. What will change is how that information is shared with third parties, like data brokers and other tech companies.

目前，应用程序在使用它们时收集有关您的各种信息 - 这不会更改。改变是如何与第三方共享的信息，如数据经纪人和其他科技公司。

Right now, the vast majority of apps you download, whether to an Apple or an Android device, track you in pretty much the same way, through a unique identifier.

目前，您下载的绝大多数应用程序，是否到Apple或Android设备，通过唯一的标识符跟踪您的方式。

The Identifier for Advertisers, or IDFA, is a standard device identifier Apple created in 2012. Google has its own version for Android devices called the Google Advertising ID, or GAID.

广告商或IDFA的标识符是2012年创建的标准设备标识符Apple .Google拥有自己的版本，适用于名为Google广告ID或Gaid的Android设备。

So if you’re looking at pictures of cats on one app, and then checking basketball scores on another, both apps would get your IDFA to share with advertisers and data brokers who link your online movements to build a more complete profile of you.

因此，如果您正在查看一个应用程序上的猫的照片，然后在另一个应用程序上检查篮球得分，这两个应用程序都将获得IDFA与广告商和数据经纪人分享，他们将您的在线动作链接以构建您的更完整的个人资料。

And there are other ways the data you generate by using an app gets shared. Apps can gather and share granular details of your actions through “in-app events” collections, like what you’ve clicked on and what you’ve looked at.

使用应用程序使用您生成的数据有其他方式进行共享。应用程序可以通过“应用程序活动”集合来收集和分享您操作的粒度细节，例如您点击的内容以及您所看到的内容。

Under the current opt-out model, you can clear your history by resetting your IDFA or limit tracking by setting your IDFA to all zeroes. You can do this under Advertising in your privacy settings on your iOS device. Research from AppsFlyer, a mobile advertising firm, found that only about 25 percent of people turned this setting on in 2020.

在当前的选择退出模型下，您可以通过将IDFA设置为所有零来重置您的IDFA或限制跟踪来清除您的历史记录。您可以在iOS设备上的隐私设置中的广告下执行此操作。来自移动广告公司Appsflyer的研究发现，只有大约25％的人在2020年转为这个环境。

But that will become an opt-in model when Apple’s privacy change kicks in.

但是，当苹果的隐私改变踢球时，这将成为一个选择的模型。

Apple says its update will take effect in early spring, with iOS 14.5. Once that happens, any app that collects data about you and shares it with other companies for cross-tracking and advertising purposes will be required to get permission first.

Apple表示，它的更新将在早春生效，iOS 14.5。一旦发生这种情况，将首先需要收集有关您的数据并与其他公司共享其他公司进行交叉跟踪和广告目的的应用程序。

Without that consent, apps won’t be allowed to share any data they collect about you with other companies or data brokers for advertising purposes. Companies can still share data for other purposes—like preventing fraud or for analytics.

在没有同意的情况下，将不允许应用程序分享他们与其他公司或数据经纪人收集的任何数据以进行广告目的。公司仍然可以分享数据以获取其他目的，类似于防止欺诈或分析。

The changes only apply to Apple devices—Android’s app store has announced no similar changes.

该更改仅适用于Apple Devices-Android的App Store已宣布没有类似的更改。

And even for iPhone users, apps can still gather information about you under the new rules; they just can’t share that information for advertising purposes.

即使对于iPhone用户而言，应用程序仍然可以根据新规则收集有关您的信息;他们无法分享广告目的的信息。

Apple’s new policies prohibit tricks for getting consent, too. An app won’t be able to prevent access to its features because you won’t let them track you or offer incentives to users who allow tracking. The prompt can only show once—so you can’t be spammed with requests, either.

Apple的新政策也禁止获得同意的技巧。应用程序将无法阻止访问其功能，因为您不会让他们跟踪您或向允许跟踪的用户提供激励。提示只能显示一次 - 因此您不能用请求垃圾邮件。

Apps that don’t show the prompt aren’t allowed to share your data with third parties and won’t get your IDFA.

不显示提示的应用程序不允许使用第三方与您的数据共享，并不会获得您的IDFA。

AppsFlyer found that after several developers implemented Apple’s tracking request prompt early, 99 percent of people decided against giving them permission. Some apps will likely decide to simply stop sharing tracking information instead of implementing the prompt.

Appsflyer发现，在几个开发商实施苹果的跟踪请求提示后，99％的人决定给予他们许可。一些应用可能会决定只需停止共享跟踪信息而不是实现提示。

Serge Egelman, research director of the Usable Security & Privacy Group at the International Computer Science Institute, says most people don’t want to be tracked.

Isge egelman，可用的安全和amp的研究主任;国际计算机科学研究所隐私集团表示，大多数人不想被追踪。

“The reason why more people don’t opt out is because it’s very complicated,” Egelman said. “Given that we know that most consumers don’t want to be tracked and aren’t making informed decisions, it makes sense that you would switch to an opt-in model.”

“越来越多的人不选择的原因是因为它非常复杂，”埃格尔曼说。 “鉴于我们知道大多数消费者不想被追踪，并且没有做出明智的决定，它会导致你会切换到一个选择的模型。”

Companies can still track you through their own services, but they can’t share that information with anyone else without your permission. So although Spotify, for instance, can’t share data about your searches on its app to Facebook without your consent, Facebook can still use data you generate on its own services, including Instagram and Oculus, to build an image of who you are and what you like and use that profile to sell ads.

公司仍然可以通过自己的服务跟踪您，但如果没有您的许可，他们无法与其他人分享该信息。因此，虽然Spotify，但是，在未经您同意的情况下，不能将关于您的应用程序上搜索的数据分享到Facebook，Facebook仍然可以使用您自己的服务，包括Instagram和Oculus，构建您是谁的图像您喜欢什么并使用该配置文件来销售广告。

The more powerful the innate data-tracking capabilities of the app, the better they’re likely to fare under these changes, says Johnny Ryan, a senior fellow at the Open Markets Institute focused on privacy and antitrust.

Johnny Ryan在私隐和反垄断的高级研究所表示，该应用程序的先天数据跟踪能力越强大，他们可能会在这些变化下越好。

A company like “Google can come along and say, ‘We’re going to put the entire market in ourselves. Instead of having thousands of companies who provide advertising space, everyone should come to us,’ ” Ryan said.

像“谷歌一样的公司一样，我们将把整个市场放在自己身上。 Ryan说，而不是拥有一千家提供广告空间的公司，每个人都应该来找我们。“

In fact, Google has already said it won’t bother with data-sharing on Apple devices anymore.

事实上，谷歌已经表示，它不会在Apple Deviewore上的数据共享不再担心。

“We will no longer use information that falls under [App Tracking Transparency] for the handful of our iOS apps that currently use them for advertising purposes,” Matt Bryant, a Google Ads spokesperson, said.

谷歌广告发言人表示，“我们将不再使用当前使用它们的iOS应用程序的少数iOS应用程序来使用下降的信息

Google will have a plethora of data it collects on a first-party basis to use for advertising and will still be able to collect third-party data from apps where people have opted in, Ryan said.

谷歌将收集一系列数据，它会在一方上为广告使用，仍然能够从人们选择的应用程序中收集第三方数据，Ryan说。

Apple controls the IDFA tool, so the company should have the means to ensure apps are not using it without consent. But experts say it will be hard for Apple to prevent apps from sharing data in other ways and worry the company is going to rely too heavily on the honor system.

Apple控制IDFA工具，因此公司应该有手段，以确保应用程序未经同意。但专家表示，苹果将很难防止应用以其他方式分享数据，并担心公司将依靠荣誉制度依赖。

“The app developer can say they don’t do any tracking and then at the same time collect a bunch of different data points to uniquely identify that user over time,” Egelman said. “There’s not really any way that Apple or anyone else can automatically identify that unless they’re individually analyzing this particular app and what it’s sending.”

“App开发人员可以说他们不做任何跟踪，然后同时收集一堆不同的数据点以唯一地识别该用户随着时间的推移，”Egelman表示。 “除非他们单独分析这个特定应用程序以及发送的东西，否则苹果或其他人可以自动识别它。”

While Apple has capabilities to identify third-party trackers embedded in code during its app review process, following up to make sure that the first-party data isn’t being shared without permission can be difficult.

虽然Apple具有在应用程序审核过程中识别在代码中嵌入的第三方跟踪器的功能，但后续可确保未经权限不进行共享第一方数据可能很困难。

“If we learn that a developer is tracking users who ask not to be tracked, we will require that they update their practices to respect your choice, or their app may be rejected from the App Store,” Apple said in a white paper on privacy released in January.

“如果我们了解开发人员正在跟踪要求不被跟踪的用户，我们将要求他们更新他们的实践来尊重您的选择，或者他们的应用程序可能会被拒绝应用程序商店，”Apple在隐私本文中表示1月份发布。

Sean O’Brien, principal researcher at ExpressVPN’s Digital Security Lab, said it’ll be important for Apple to establish a rigorous auditing process to enforce their new policies.

ExpressVPN的数字安全实验室的主要研究人员Sean O'Brien表示，对于苹果来说，建立一个严格的审计过程来实施其新政策很重要。

“You need a combination of both automated scans and manual review, and you have to try to have a slower review process before you accept apps into your store,” O’Brien said.

“您需要两种自动扫描和手动评论的组合，您必须尝试在您在您的商店接受应用程序之前进行较慢的审查过程，”奥布莱恩说。

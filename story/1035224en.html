根据一项新的研究，小行星本努可能是空心的

2020-11-15 11:26:40

One of the more exciting things that NASA has done in recent memory is landing a probe on the surface of asteroid Bennu. OSIRIS-REx successfully touched down briefly on the asteroid’s surface and gobbled up a bunch of samples that will be returned to earth for study. After orbiting the asteroid for a couple of years, some new data has now been published that sheds more light on the asteroid’s composition. Researchers from the University of Colorado announced findings based on data captured by the spacecraft in the two years it was in orbit that shows the asteroid is likely hollow. Department of aerospace engineering sciences Daniel Scheeres said it appears the void in the asteroid center could hold a couple of football fields. Since the core of the asteroid appears to be weaker than the exterior, the asteroid’s survival over the long-term could be at risk. Scheeres says that in a million years or less, the entire asteroid could fly apart. By combining data recorded by OSIRIS-REx, the scientists could create a map of sorts of the gravity of the asteroid, suggesting that assumptions the inside was solid and rocky were wrong. The team believes that the asteroid’s rotation could be responsible for the void inside. Over time, Bennu’s rotation is gaining speed, and they think it’s in the process of spinning itself into pieces. Since the core is low density, it’s easier for the entire asteroid to fall apart as it spins. Now that measurements of the gravity field of the asteroid over, the team of scientists have wrapped up their work on the OSIRIS-REx mission. The results of their work have contributed to the plan to analyze samples that will be returned to earth by the spacecraft. The current plan will see the samples analyzed to determine the cohesion between grains, a key physical property that impacts the mass distribution observed in the study.

在最近的记忆中，NASA做过的一件更令人兴奋的事情是将探测器降落在小行星本努的表面。奥西里斯-霸王龙成功地在小行星表面短暂着陆，并吞噬了一堆样本，这些样本将被带回地球进行研究。在围绕这颗小行星运行了几年后，一些新的数据现在被公布了，这让人们对这颗小行星的组成有了更多的了解。科罗拉多大学的研究人员宣布了基于航天器在轨道上两年内捕获的数据的发现，表明小行星很可能是空心的。航天工程科学部的丹尼尔·舍尔斯说，小行星中心的空隙似乎可以容纳几个足球场。由于小行星的核心看起来比外部更弱，从长远来看，小行星的生存可能会受到威胁。舍尔斯说，在一百万年或更短的时间内，整个小行星可能会飞散。通过结合OSIRIS-Rex记录的数据，科学家们可以绘制出这颗小行星的重力地图，这表明内部是坚固的和岩石的假设是错误的。研究小组认为，小行星的自转可能是造成内部空洞的原因。随着时间的推移，本努的旋转速度越来越快，他们认为它正在将自己旋转成碎片。由于小行星的核心密度较低，因此整个小行星在自转时更容易解体。现在，小行星重力场的测量已经结束，科学家团队已经结束了他们在OSIRIS-REX任务中的工作。他们的工作成果为分析航天器将返回地球的样本的计划做出了贡献。目前的计划将对样本进行分析，以确定颗粒之间的凝聚力，这是影响研究中观察到的质量分布的关键物理特性。

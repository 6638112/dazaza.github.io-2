Russian teenager jailed over ‘Minecraft plot to blow up virtual spy HQ’
俄罗斯少年因“地雷船阴谋炸毁虚拟间谍总部”被判入狱

2022-02-13 10:43:06

Boy, 16, sentenced to five years for alleged plan to target FSB building created in computer game

16岁的男孩被判五年有期徒刑，罪名是涉嫌计划以电脑游戏中创建的FSB大楼为目标

A Russian teenager has been sentenced to five years in prison for allegedly planning to blow up a virtual FSB security service building in the video game Minecraft.

一名俄罗斯少年因涉嫌计划炸毁电子游戏Minecraft中的FSB安全服务虚拟大楼而被判处五年监禁。

The ruling falls into a broader pattern under President Vladimir Putin in which young Russians are put behind bars on controversial and preemptive terrorism charges.

在弗拉基米尔·普京总统的领导下，这项裁决属于一种更广泛的模式，即俄罗斯年轻人因有争议的、先发制人的恐怖主义指控而被关进监狱。

A military court in Siberia sentenced 16-year-old Nikita Uvarov to five years in a penal colony on charges of “training for terrorist activities”, the rights lawyer Pavel Chikov said on the messaging service Telegram.

维权律师帕维尔·奇科夫（Pavel Chikov）在消息服务电报中说，西伯利亚的一家军事法庭以“为恐怖活动提供培训”的罪名判处16岁的尼基塔·乌瓦罗夫在一个刑事殖民地五年监禁。

Two other defendants were cleared of criminal charges and handed suspended sentences because they cooperated with investigators, Chikov added.

奇科夫补充说，另外两名被告因与调查人员合作而被免除刑事指控，并被判处缓刑。

Uvarov and two other teenagers in the Siberian city of Kansk were detained in the summer of 2020 for spreading leaflets in support of a Moscow mathematician and anarchist activist who was on trial for vandalism.

2020年夏天，乌瓦罗夫和西伯利亚城市坎斯克的另外两名青少年因散发传单支持一名莫斯科数学家和无政府主义活动人士而被拘留，此人因故意破坏而受审。

They put one of the leaflets on a local building of the FSB, Russia’s powerful domestic security agency and successor of the Soviet-era KGB.

他们把其中一份传单贴在了俄罗斯联邦安全局的一栋当地建筑上。联邦安全局是俄罗斯强大的国内安全机构，也是苏联时代克格勃的继任者。

Police took their phones and said they found an exchange about plans to blow up an FSB building that they had created in the popular block-building game Minecraft.

警方拿走了他们的手机，说他们发现了一个关于炸毁FSB大楼的计划的交流，他们在流行的积木游戏Minecraft中创建了这座大楼。

According to investigators, the teenagers were also learning to make improvised explosive devices and practised detonating them in abandoned buildings.

据调查人员称，这些青少年还在学习制作简易爆炸装置，并在废弃建筑中练习引爆。

The teenagers initially faced more severe charges of “participating in a terrorist organisation”, but these were dropped due to lack of evidence.

这些青少年最初面临“参与恐怖组织”的更严厉指控，但由于缺乏证据，这些指控被撤销。

In his last words in court, reported by the Novaya Gazeta newspaper, Uvarov spoke about pressure from authorities during the investigation and denied planning to blow anything up.

据《新报》报道，乌瓦罗夫在法庭上的临终遗言中谈到了调查期间来自当局的压力，并否认计划炸毁任何东西。

He pleaded not guilty and said that if he was handed prison time, he would serve it “with a clear conscience and dignity”.

他不认罪，并表示如果他被判入狱，他将“问心无愧、有尊严地”服刑。

“For the last time in this court I want to say: I am not a terrorist,” he said.

“这是我最后一次在法庭上说：我不是恐怖分子，”他说。

It is not the first time in recent years that young Russians have received prison sentences for controversial terrorism charges. In August 2020, three young activists – who were accused of belonging to an anarchist group called “New Greatness” – were sentenced to time in jail for allegedly plotting to overthrow Putin’s government. Four others were handed suspended sentences in a case initiated by the FSB that supporters described as fabricated.

这不是近年来俄罗斯年轻人第一次因为有争议的恐怖主义指控而被判入狱。2020年8月，三名年轻活动人士——他们被指控属于一个名为“新伟大”的无政府主义组织——因涉嫌阴谋推翻普京政府而被判入狱。在FSB发起的一个被支持者称为捏造的案件中，另有四人被判缓刑。

In February 2020, seven young anarchists and anti-fascists were sentenced to between six and 18 years in prison on terror and other charges. Arrested in 2017 and 2018, most of the men said they had been tortured in custody with electrodes and beaten to extract a confession.

2020年2月，七名年轻的无政府主义者和反法西斯分子因恐怖和其他罪名被判处6至18年监禁。这些男子于2017年和2018年被捕，大多数人说，他们在拘留期间曾被用电极拷打和殴打以逼供。

Former Olympian Says Trans Athletes Will ‘Knock Women Out of Sport’
前奥林匹克表示，反式运动员将“击败妇女”

2021-06-24 08:28:37

Retired New Zealand weightlifter and former Olympian Tracey Lambrechs warned Thursday that transgender athletes will eventually “knock women out of sport” unless action is taken.

退休的新西兰举重者和前奥林匹克特蕾尼斯兰布雷克周四警告称，除非采取行动，否则转型运动员最终将“敲击妇女”。

Lambrechs competed in the same weight class as transgender athlete Laurel Hubbard after Hubbard, born biologically male, transitioned in 2013 and began competing in women’s weightlifting competitions. Lambrechs said she lost all of her weightlifting records soon after Hubbard began competing.

Lambrechs在Hubbard之后的Transgender Athlete Laurel Hubbard与Transgender Athlete Laurel Hubbard相同的体重级，于2013年转向过渡，并开始竞争女性的举重竞争。 Lambrechs表示，在哈伯德开始竞争之后，她很快就丢失了所有举重的纪录。

“I was like ‘What do you mean? There’s no one that’s close to me,’” Lambrechs said during an interview with Fox News, recalling a phone call she received from her coach letting her know that Hubbard had eclipsed her lifting records and beat her out of the number one spot for their weight class. “Laurel started weightlifting, and she competed on the weekend. So as of now, you’re number two.”

“我就像'你的意思是什么？没有人靠近我，“兰布雷克在接受福克斯新闻采访时说，回忆一下她从她的教练那里收到的电话，让她知道哈伯德被黯然失色，以击败她的重量，击败了她的头号班级。 “劳雷尔开始举重，她在周末竞争。所以，现在，你是两个人。“

Hubbard is set to become the first openly transgender person to compete in the Olympics after winning a spot on the New Zealand national team. At 43 years old, Hubbard is currently the oldest weightlifter competing.

在赢得新西兰国家队的胜利后，哈伯德将成为第一个在奥运会中竞争的公开跨国人。 43岁，哈伯德目前是最古老的举重者竞争。

Lambrechs said that trans athletes could eventually destroy women’s sports by forcing out all biologically female competitors.

Lambrechs表示，跨运动员最终可能通过强迫所有生物学女性的竞争对手摧毁妇女运动。

“It’s honestly going to knock women out of sport. Women are not going to want to participate in something where there isn’t opportunity for them to win medals or go to international competition,” she said.

“诚实地将妇女脱离运动。她说，妇女不会想参加任何没有机会的东西赢得奖牌或去国际竞争，“她说。

After Hubbard beat Lambrechs out of her records, Lambrechs was given little recourse if she wanted to continue competing.

Hubbard击败Lambrechs出来的记录后，如果她想继续竞争，Lambrechs很少追索。

“I was told by the national weightlifting body that I either needed to drop a weight category or look at retiring,” Lambrechs said. Lambrechs ultimately decided to drop a weight class so that she could continue competing.

“我被国家举重机构告诉我，我要么需要放弃重量类别或看退休，”Lambrechs说。 Lambrechs最终决定放弃重量级，以便她可以继续竞争。

Each nation is capped at sending just one athlete per weight class for major weightlifting competitions. Lambrechs lost nearly 40 pounds in three months to drop weight classes and continue competing.

每个国家都在为每个重量级的运动员举办一个运动员，以获得主要的举重竞争。 Lambrechs在三个月内丢失了近40磅的掉落课程并继续竞争。

“I just did what I had to do to accomplish my goal of competing at my third Commonwealth Games. It meant a lot to me to show the Commonwealth that I wasn’t knocked out that easily,” Lambrechs said.

“我刚刚做了我必须做的事情来实现我的第三届英联邦比赛的目标。这对我来说意味着很多，以展示英联邦很容易被淘汰出局，“Lambrechs说。

Lambrechs has previously claimed that athletes have been warned to be silent about transgender competitors in women’s sports. In May, she said that sport was being taken away from women because of committees allowing biological men to compete against them.

Lambrechs先前据称，运动员被警告者对妇女运动中的变性竞争对手沉默。 5月，她说，由于允许生物学位与他们竞争，体育运动被带走了妇女。

“I’m quite disappointed, quite disappointed for the female athlete who will lose out on that spot,” Lambrechs said. “We’re all about equality for women in sport, but right now that equality is being taken away from us.”

“我很失望，对那个将在那个地方失败的女运动员感到失望，”Lambrechs说。 “我们都是关于运动中女性的平等，但现在平等被带走了。”

“I’ve had female weightlifters come up to me and say, ‘What do we do? This isn’t fair, what do we do?’ Unfortunately, there’s nothing we can do because every time we voice it we get told to be quiet,” she added.

“我有女性举重者来找我说，”我们该怎么办？这不公平，我们做了什么？“不幸的是，我们没有任何东西可以做到，因为每次我们都声音我们被告知要安静，”她补充道。

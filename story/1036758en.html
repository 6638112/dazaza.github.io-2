Over 2,400 US Covid deaths in 24 hours, a six-month high
24小时内有超过2,400例美国Covid死亡，为六个月以来最高

2020-11-27 03:09:36

The United States on Wednesday registered more than 2,400 deaths from COVID-19 in 24 hours, according to the Johns Hopkins University tally—the highest daily toll in six months as the Thanksgiving holiday began. At 8:30pm (0030 GMT Thursday), the country had recorded a total of 262,080 COVID deaths, up by 2,439 in 24 hours. It also registered nearly 200,000 new cases.

约翰·霍普金斯大学（Johns Hopkins University）统计数据显示，周三美国在24小时内录得2400例COVID-19死亡，这是感恩节假期开始以来六个月以来的最高日收费。在晚上8:30（格林尼治标准时间星期四0030），该国记录了262,080例COVID死亡，在24小时内增加了2,439例。它还登记了近20万例新案件。

Travel appeared to be down the night before Thanksgiving, when Americans usually criss-cross the country to be with family and friends for a feast on Thursday marking one of their most important national holidays.

旅行似乎是在感恩节前一天晚上进行的，当时美国人通常在周四穿越全国与家人和朋友聚餐，这是他们最重要的国定假日之一。

In recent days, images on social media of crowded airports fueled concerns Americans were not taking the warnings seriously.

最近几天，拥挤机场的社交媒体上的图像激起了人们对美国人没有认真对待警告的担忧。

But one day before Thursday's celebrations, officials and passengers in Los Angeles noted an eerie quiet compared to previous years.

但是在周四的庆祝活动的前一天，洛杉矶的官员和乘客注意到与往年相比，这令人毛骨悚然。

US President-elect Joe Biden cited accelerating plans to deliver a vaccine as he appealed for his "weary" countrymen to dig deep.

美国总统当选人拜登引用加速计划提供的疫苗，他呼吁他的“疲惫”的同胞深入挖掘。

"There is real hope, tangible hope. So hang on. Don't let yourself surrender to the fatigue," Biden said in a televised Thanksgiving address.

拜登在电视转播的感恩节致辞中说：“有真正的希望，有希望，有希望。所以要坚持下去。不要让自己屈服于疲劳。”

"You will get your lives back. Life is going to return to normal. That will happen. This will not last forever."

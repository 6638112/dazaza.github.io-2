Ever wondered why drones only work in small?I've seen a lot of similar videos lately, suggesting quadcopter designs for anything from personal transport to cargo shipment to - well - lifting cars. It seems natural - small drones work, so just make them bigger, right?Here's why this doesn't work: A drone flies because the lift generated by its rotors is greater than the gravity pulling down the mass of its body. The lift of a rotor is a function of its surface area times speed.So here's a highly simplified example: Say your tiny drone generates 2g of lift per square-cm of rotor surface. For four rotors with 5cm radius this gives us 624g of lift. The drone's body is a 7cm cube at 1g per cm3 = 343g. This means it has a lift to weight ratio of 624g/343g = 1.81. Any value greater than one means there's more lift than weight, so it will fly.Alright, let's take our small drone and make it ten times bigger. Our 50cm radius rotors now generate 62,824g of lift - but our 70cm drone body weights a whopping 343,000g. That makes our weight to lift ratio just 0.18.So what happened? Rotor surfaces scale by a power of two. Body volume by a power of three. So while quadcopters work in small, as they grow bigger the weight to lift ratio shifts and quickly becomes unsustainable for flight.

A Fossil Squid Eating a Crustacean Being Eaten by a Shark
一只食物鱿鱼吃甲壳类动物被鲨鱼吃掉了

2021-05-14 09:15:15

A team of researchers has discovered a fossil they are describing as a leftover fall event in which one creature was in the process of eating another creature that was not consumed. In their paper published in the Swiss Journal of Palaeontology, the group describes the fossilized find and what it taught them about behavior between ancient cephalopods and vertebrate predators. Over the course of many years, paleontologists have unearthed fossils of creatures that were interacting at the time of their death—one such type of interaction involves a capturing prey. Prior researchers have called fossils of creatures just prior to being consumed "pabulites" (Latin for "leftovers.") In this new effort, the researchers studied an ancient pabulite that was about to be consumed by an ancient squid-like creature called a belemnite.

一支研究人员团队发现了一个化石，他们正在描述作为一个剩余的秋季事件，其中一个生物正在吃另一个未被消耗的生物。在瑞士古生物学杂志上发表的论文中，该小组描述了化石的发现以及它在古代头孢普德和脊椎动物捕食者之间教导了他们的行为。在多年的过程中，古生物学家已经出土的生物化石在他们死亡时互动 - 一种这种类型的互动涉及捕获猎物。先前的研究人员在消费之前称为生物化石; Pabulites＆＃34; （拉丁语＆＃34;剩菜。在这项新的努力中，研究人员研究了一个即将被叫做Banemnite的古老鱿鱼生物消耗的古老Pabulite。

The combined fossils were discovered by an amateur collector who found them in a quarry in Germany. Recently, one of the members of the research team happened across the collection and arranged for the State Museum of Natural History Stuttgart to purchase the specimens. Soon thereafter, a research team was assembled and the group began studying the find.

组合的化石被一个业余收藏家发现，他们在德国的猎物中找到了它们。最近，研究团队的一名成员发生在集合中，并安排为国家自然历史博物馆斯图加特购买标本。此后，此后很快，一支研究团队被组装，小组开始研究该发现。

Both of the specimens were still embedded in the fossilized sediment—one was a belemnite, the other a crustacean of the genus Proeryon—it had a body reminiscent of a lobster with long, thin claws. The belemnite was in excellent condition, allowing the researchers to see that a large portion of its upper soft body had been torn away by a predator. The Proeryon, on the other hand, was in poor condition, and the researchers believe that it was in the process of molting. Both fossils were dated to approximately 180 million years ago.

这两个标本仍然嵌入僵化的沉积物中 - 一个是一个BENEMAITE，另一个PRERYON属的甲壳类动物 - 它有一个身体让人想起长而薄的爪子的龙虾。 Banemnite条件良好，允许研究人员看到它的大部分柔软的身体被捕食者撕掉了。另一方面，Proferon处于贫困状态，研究人员认为这是在蜕皮的过程中。两只化石都在约1.5亿年前日期。

After careful study of the positioning of the two fossils, the researchers concluded that the belemnite was in the process of biting the crustacean, or perhaps the skin it was shedding. And while it was doing so, the belemnite was itself bitten by a larger predator—possibly an ancient shark. The bite, it turned out, was lethal. The belemnite, with the crustacean skin still in its mouth, sank to the bottom of the sea and died.

在仔细研究两种化石的定位后，研究人员得出结论认为，Banemnite正在咬甲壳类动物的过程中，或者也许是它被脱落的皮肤。虽然在这样做时，Belemnite本身被更大的捕食者咬伤 - 可能是一个古老的鲨鱼。它结果咬了，是致命的。 Belemite，甲壳动物的皮肤仍然在嘴里，沉到了海底并死了。

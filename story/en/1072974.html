France to build up to 14 new nuclear reactors by 2050
法国将在2050年前新建14座核反应堆

2022-02-14 08:24:41

French president says ‘renaissance’ of atomic energy industry will help end country’s reliance on fossil fuels

法国总统表示，原子能产业的“复兴”将有助于结束该国对化石燃料的依赖

Emmanuel Macron has announced a “renaissance” for the French nuclear industry with a vast programme to build as many as 14 new reactors, arguing that it would help end the country’s reliance on fossil fuels and make France carbon neutral by 2050.

埃马纽埃尔·马克龙宣布，法国核工业将“复兴”，并将建造多达14座新反应堆，并认为这将有助于结束该国对化石燃料的依赖，使法国碳中和的比例达到2050。

“What our country needs ... is the rebirth of France’s nuclear industry,” Macron said in a speech in the eastern industrial town of Belfort, in which he lauded the country’s technological prowess.

“我们国家需要的……是法国核工业的复兴，”马克龙在东部工业城镇贝尔福发表演讲时说，他在演讲中赞扬了法国的技术实力。

The centrist French president, who is expected to announce his campaign for re-election this month, is conscious of a growing debate about energy ahead of this spring’s presidential vote as costs to consumers rise. Environmental issues are also a growing concern among French voters.

这位中间派的法国总统预计将在本月宣布竞选连任，他意识到，随着消费者成本的上升，在今年春天的总统选举之前，关于能源的辩论越来越多。环境问题也是法国选民日益关注的问题。

Atomic energy provides about 70% of French electricity, and low-cost nuclear power has been a mainstay of the French economy since the 1970s, but recent attempts to build new-generation reactors to replace older models have become mired in cost overruns and delays.

原子能提供了法国70%的电力，自20世纪70年代以来，低成本核能一直是法国经济的支柱，但最近建造新一代反应堆以取代旧型号的尝试陷入了成本超支和延误的困境。

Presidential candidates on the right have supported more nuclear power plants saying France should have “sovereignty” over its electricity, while detractors on the left have warned of the cost and complexity of building new reactors. Environmentalists have raised safety concerns over radioactive waste that remains deadly for tens of thousands of years.

右翼的总统候选人支持更多的核电站，称法国应该对其电力拥有“主权”，而左翼的批评者则对建造新反应堆的成本和复杂性发出警告。环境保护主义者对放射性废物提出了安全担忧，这种废物在数万年内都是致命的。

Macron said French nuclear regulators were “unequalled” in their rigour and professionalism and that the decision to build new nuclear power plants was a “choice of progress, a choice of confidence in science and technology”.

马克龙表示，法国核监管机构的严谨性和专业性“无与伦比”，建设新核电站的决定是“对进步的选择，对科学技术信心的选择”。

He also announced a major acceleration in the development of solar and offshore wind power. He said France had no choice but to rely on renewables and nuclearand that the country would also have to consume significantly less energy in the next decades.

他还宣布将大力加快太阳能和海上风力发电的发展。他说，法国别无选择，只能依赖可再生能源和核能，而且该国在未来几十年也将不得不大幅减少能源消耗。

He said he would seek to extend the lives of all existing French nuclear plants where it was safe to do so.

他说，如果安全的话，他将寻求延长法国所有现有核电站的寿命。

The announcement comes at a difficult time for debt-laden, state-controlled energy provider, EDF, which faces delays and budget overuns on new nuclear plants in France and Britain, and corrosion problems in some of its ageing reactors.

这一宣布正值债务缠身、由国家控制的能源供应商法国电力公司（EDF）的艰难时刻。EDF面临着法国和英国新建核电站的延期和预算超支，以及一些老化反应堆的腐蚀问题。

Macron announced the construction of at least six new reactors by EDF by 2050, with an option for another eight.

马克龙宣布，到2050年，EDF将建造至少六座新反应堆，并可选择另外八座。

His recent focus on nuclear power marks a policy shift from the start of his presidency, when he had promised to reduce its share in France’s energy mix.

他最近对核能的关注标志着从他上任伊始的政策转变，当时他曾承诺减少核能在法国能源结构中的份额。

The French government lobbied hard and successfully to get the European Commission to label nuclear power “green” this month in a landmark review which means it can attract funding as a climate-friendly power source.

本月，法国政府进行了艰难而成功的游说，希望欧盟委员会在一项里程碑式的审查中，将核能列为“绿色”，这意味着它可以作为一种气候友好型电源吸引资金。

The Green presidential candidate, Yannick Jadot, said it was a moral imperative to progressively end France’s dependence on nuclear to protect the climate and French people’s safety. He said Macron’s project was backward-looking and would condemn France to a kind of “energy and industrial obsolescence”.

绿色总统候选人扬尼克·贾多特（Yannick Jadot）表示，为了保护气候和法国人民的安全，逐步结束法国对核能的依赖是一项道义上的当务之急。他说，马克龙的项目是向后看的，将使法国陷入一种“能源和工业过时”的状态。

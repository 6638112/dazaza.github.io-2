Why the New Macs Are So Short of Memory
为什么新的MAC是如此短暂的记忆

2021-04-24 03:29:02

Apple may have launched a new MacBook Pro last night, but if there’s one figure in Apple’s announcements that has ‘pros’ scratching their heads, it’s the tight limit on memory in these new machines.

苹果可能昨晚推出了一个新的MacBook专业人士，但如果Apple的公告中有一个数字，其中有“专业人士的头部”，这是这些新机器中内存的紧张限制。

None of the three new Macs based on Apple silicon, including the new MacBook Pro 13in, can be configured with more than 16GB of RAM. By comparison, the older Intel models can be shipped with double that amount, while the MacBook Pro 16in can accommodate 64GB - four times as much memory as there is in the latest MacBook Pro.

基于Apple Silicon的三个新Mac中没有包括新型MacBook Pro 13in，可以配置超过16GB的RAM。相比之下，较旧的Intel模型可以用DOUBLE提供DOUID的金额，而MACBOAK PRO 16IN可以容纳64GB - 在最新MacBook Pro中有4倍的内存。

That’s led many on social media to question: why is Apple being so tight with memory on the new MacBook Pro?

这是众多社交媒体的问题：为什么苹果在新的MacBook Pro上的内存如此紧张？

The MacBook Pro, based on Apple’s M1 silicon, has a different type of RAM to what’s gone before. This is what Apple is branding ‘unified memory’, where the RAM is part of the same unit as the processor, the graphics chip and several other key components. This is called a system on a chip (SoC), which is more commonly found in smartphones and tablets.

基于Apple的M1 Silicon的MacBook Pro具有不同类型的RAM，以前已经消失了。这是Apple品牌的“统一内存”，RAM是与处理器，图形芯片和其他几个关键组件相同的单元的一部分。这被称为芯片（SoC）上的系统，更常见于智能手机和平板电脑中。

As Apple describes it, all of those components can “access the same data without copying it between multiple pools of memory”, making the overall system more efficient. There is no separate allocation of memory for graphics and the CPU - they all share that one piece of “high-performance unified memory”.

随着Apple描述它，所有这些组件都可以“访问相同的数据而不在多个内存池之间复制它”，使整体系统更有效。图形和CPU没有单独分配内存 - 它们都分享了一块“高性能统一内存”。

With everything sharing the one pool of memory, you might think that Apple would want to include more RAM, not less than before, but the reams of benchmarks Apple shared last night suggest the company is confident that even a limited allocation of RAM will outperform most equivalent PCs.

随着所有内容共享一个内存池，您可能认为Apple希望包含更多的RAM，而不是少于以前，但昨晚共享的基准苹果的苹果公司建议该公司相信即使是RAM的有限配置也将优于最优异的等价PC。

We will have to wait for independent benchmarks to be carried out on the new Macs before anyone can verify those claims. The benchmark graphs that Apple showed in last night’s keynote were extremely vague, not revealing which specific PCs or PC processors the comparisons were being made with.

在任何人可以核实这些索赔之前，我们必须等待在新Mac上进行的独立基准。 Apple在昨晚的主题演讲中显示的基准图非常含糊，而不是揭示比较的特定PC或PC处理器正在进行。

There’s another reason Apple isn’t taking its traditional approach of charging customers a hefty premium to include more RAM in their MacBooks: it can’t. Currently, the M1 cannot support more than 16GB of memory.

还有另一个原因，苹果没有采取传统的对客户的传统方法，享受更多的溢价，以便在他们的MacBook中包含更多的RAM：它不能。目前，M1无法支持超过16GB的内存。

This explains why Apple was reasonably cautious with its launch last night, largely restricting the new Apple silicon to devices such as the MacBook Air and Mac mini, which traditionally aren’t used for highly demanding tasks. Powerhouse machines such as the MacBook Pro 16in, iMac Pro and Mac Pro are left on Intel processors, which don’t have the 16GB memory limit.

这解释了为什么Apple昨晚的发射是合理谨慎的，主要原因将新的Apple硅与MacBook Air和Mac Mini的设备限制在传统上不用于高苛刻的任务。 MacBook Pro 16in，imac Pro和Mac Pro等的强电机器留在英特尔处理器上，没有16GB内存限制。

Although Apple made lofty claims about the new devices’ ability to render 8K video and perform other intensive tasks, professionals looking for workstation-grade performance are unlikely to be swayed with such little memory on offer.

虽然Apple令人崇高的索引关于新设备渲染8K视频并执行其他密集型任务的能力，但寻找工作站级性能的专业人士不太可能随着所提供的那些小记忆而摇摆不大。

The professional community might not have to wait too long, however. Bloomberg reports that Apple is currently developing a new Mac Pro based on Apple silicon that is half the size of the current design, because of the increased power efficiency the new chips offer.

然而，专业社区可能不必等待太久。 Bloomberg报告说，Apple目前正在开发一个基于Apple硅的新型Mac Pro，这是当前设计的一半，因为新芯片提供的功率效率增加。

Apple’s traditional January event may be one for the pros to keep an eye on.

Apple的传统1月份活动可能是持有人的专业人士。

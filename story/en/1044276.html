EA’s hold over Star Wars games ends with Ubisoft’s open-world announcement [Updated]
EA对“星球大战”游戏的保留以Ubisoft的开放世界公告告终[更新]

2021-01-15 21:11:07

Original story 11:28am EST: Ubisoft will be publishing an open-world Star Wars game developed by The Division studio Massive Entertainment, the companies announced in a Wired story this morning.

原始故事11:28 am EST：育碧将发布由The Division工作室Massive Entertainment开发的星球大战开放世界游戏，两家公司今天早上在Wired故事中宣布。

Details are sparse, as even the specific characters and setting have yet to be revealed. But Wired's reporting suggests the game will be an open-world title, aiming for a longer play-time than a more linear story-driven game like 2019's Jedi: Fallen Order. The Division 2 and The Crew Director Julian Gerighty will serve as creative director on the game, which will use Massive's Snowdrop engine.

细节稀疏，因为甚至还没有透露具体的字符和设置。但是《连线》的报告表明，这款游戏将是一个开放世界的游戏，其目标是比诸如2019年的《绝地：堕落秩序》这样更具线性故事驱动的游戏更长的播放时间。 Division 2和The Crew导演Julian Gerighty将担任游戏的创意总监，该游戏将使用Massive的Snowdrop引擎。

The Ubisoft-published game will mark the apparent and abrupt end of Disney's exclusivity deal with Electronic Arts, signed in 2013. That deal was reportedly planned to last ten years, which would have taken it through most of 2023.

育碧发行的这款游戏将标志着迪士尼与电子艺界于2013年签署的独家交易的明显而突然的终结。据报道，该交易计划持续十年，这将使它持续到2023年的大部分时间。

The move away from EA is a little less surprising given the announcement this week that Disney is  reviving the Lucasfilm Games brand, an announcement that noted it is beginning "a new era" of games "developed in collaboration with the finest studios across the industry." Yesterday, that new era extended to include an upcoming Indiana Jones game developed by Wolfenstein: The New Order studio Machine Games and published by Bethesda. EA's short tenure leading the long-lived Star Wars video game brand has been a bumpy one. After 2015's Star Wars: Battlefront, the company announced three major Star Wars projects in 2016. One of those titles, a story-focused project led by Uncharted scribe Amy Hennig, was cancelled in late 2017 when EA shut Visceral Studios. Another turned into Star Wars: Battlefront 2, which quickly became a poster child for the problems with "pay-to-win" microtransactions in games upon its release in 2017, to the extent that EA was forced to remove real-money purchases from the game entirely. The third, Respawn's Jedi: Fallen Order, sold over ten million copies after its 2019 launch and could be the start of a new franchise of games, according to EA.

Intonarumori

2020-10-27 10:28:01

Intonarumori are experimental musical instruments invented and built by the Italian futurist Luigi Russolo between roughly 1910 and 1930. There were 27 varieties of intonarumori in total with different names.

Intonarumori是意大利未来学家Luigi Russolo在大约1910到1930年间发明和制造的实验乐器。有27个品种，不同的名称。

Russolo built these instruments to perform the music outlined in his Art of Noises manifesto. The instruments were completely acoustic, not electronic. The boxes had various types of internal construction to create different types of noise. Often a wheel was touching a string attached to a drum. The wheel rattled or bowed the strings, while the drum functioned as an acoustic resonator. Many of the instruments featured a handle on top of the box, which was used to vary the string tension. Pulling the handle raised the tone, and the horn attached to the box amplified the sound. Intonarumori ('noise makers' in Italian) made noise, but not at a very high volume, since they were all acoustic devices.

鲁索洛建造这些乐器是为了演奏他的噪音艺术宣言中概述的音乐。这些乐器完全是声学的，而不是电子的。这些盒子有不同类型的内部结构，以产生不同类型的噪音。轮子经常碰到连接在鼓上的绳子。轮子嘎嘎作响或弓弦，而鼓的功能就像一个声学谐振器。许多乐器的特点是在盒子的顶部有一个手柄，用来改变弦的张力。拉动把手提高了音调，盒子上的喇叭放大了声音。Intonarumori(意大利语中的噪音制造者)发出噪音，但音量不是很大，因为它们都是声学装置。

Most of Russolo's instruments were destroyed in Paris when the city was bombed during World War II. Others have simply disappeared. [1] Original sketches still exist, however, along with a few sound recordings of the original instruments. Based on these sources, three collections of reconstructions exist.

第二次世界大战期间，当巴黎遭到轰炸时，鲁索洛的大部分乐器都被毁了，其他的则干脆消失了。[1]然而，原始的草图仍然存在，还有一些原始乐器的录音。基于这些来源，存在三个重建集合。

As part of its celebration of the 100th anniversary of Italian Futurism, the Performa 09 biennial, in collaboration with the Experimental Media and Performing Arts Center (EMPAC) and the San Francisco Museum of Modern Art, invited Luciano Chessa (author of the book Luigi Russolo, Futurist. Noise, Visual Arts, and the Occult) to direct a reconstruction project to produce accurate replicas of Russolo’s legendary Intonarumori instruments. This project offered the set of 16 original intonarumori (8 noise families of 1-3 instruments each, in various registers) that Russolo built in Milan in the summer of 1913. These intonarumori were physically built by luthier Keith Cary in Winters, California, under Chessa’s direction and scientific supervision. The concert premiered at the San Francisco Museum of Modern Art on October 16, 2009, before traveling to New York City for its Performa 09 presentation at The Town Hall on November 12, 2009. [2] In September 2010 Chessa presented the recreated intonarumori in its first Italian appearance, a concert event at the Museum of Modern and Contemporary Art of Trento and Rovereto in Rovereto, Italy, as part of the Festival Transart, which featured performances by Nicholas Isherwood. [3]

作为意大利未来主义100周年庆祝活动的一部分，Performa 09双年展与实验媒体和表演艺术中心(EMPAC)和旧金山现代艺术博物馆合作，邀请了卢西亚诺·切萨(Luigi Russolo，未来主义者)一书的作者。(噪声、视觉艺术和神秘学)指导一个重建项目，以生产Russolo传奇Intonarumori乐器的准确复制品。这个项目提供了鲁索洛1913年夏天在米兰建造的16套原版intonarumori(8个噪音家族，每个家族1-3件乐器，在不同的登记册上)。这些音调是由加利福尼亚州温特斯的琴匠基思·卡里在切萨的指导和科学监督下实际建造的。这场音乐会于2009年10月16日在旧金山现代艺术博物馆首演，然后于2009年11月12日前往纽约市参加在市政厅举行的Performa 09演出。[2]2010年9月，Chessa首次在意大利亮相，展示了重建的intonarumori，这是在意大利罗弗莱托的Trento和Rovereto现当代艺术博物馆举行的音乐会活动，作为Transart节日的一部分，该节日以尼古拉斯·伊舍伍德(Nicholas Isherwood)的表演为特色。[3]。

With 2013 being the 100th anniversary of both The Art of Noises and John Cage’s birth, the curators of Carnegie Mellon University’s Wats:ON? Festival, [4] Golan Levin and Spike Wolff, felt the time was ripe for a presentation of noise and decided to reconstruct the forgotten Intonarumori instruments for the festival. [5] Carl Bajandas, a sculptor, an instrument builder, took the lead and built 10 Intonarumori instruments. Meanwhile, experimental composer, music technologist John Ozbay, has been asked to compose for the Intonarumori instruments. [6] [7] The performance took place in Carnegie Mellon University's Kresge Theatre on April 4, 2013. Followed by performances of electronic/experimental music artists, Jeremy Boyle, Michael Johnsen, Eric Singer and Lesley Flanigan. [4] [6] [8]

2013年是噪音艺术和约翰·凯奇诞辰100周年纪念日，卡内基梅隆大学的策展人“Wats：On？在音乐节上，戈兰·莱文和斯派克·沃尔夫认为展示噪音的时机已经成熟，并决定为音乐节重建被遗忘的因托纳鲁莫里乐器。[5]雕刻家卡尔·巴扬达斯(Carl Bajandas)是一名乐器制造者，他带头建造了10件Intonarumori乐器。与此同时，实验作曲家、音乐技术学家约翰·奥兹贝(John Ozbay)已被邀请为Intonarumori乐器作曲。[6][7]演出于2013年4月4日在卡内基梅隆大学的克雷格剧院举行。随后是电子/实验音乐艺术家杰里米·博伊尔、迈克尔·约翰森、埃里克·辛格和莱斯利·弗拉尼根的表演。[4][6][8]。

Dutch sound artist Wessel Westerveld made a series of replicas. Westerveld's replicas are the most professional versions with welded steel horns instead of cardboard and nailed steel cones like the Italian and NYC ones. The woodwork of these versions are also real hard wood instead of plywood. Westerveld has also made a few open variants with the sound system visible from the outside. [9] Westerveld performs regularly with his Intonarumori in collaboration with Dutch sound artist Yuri Landman. The Dutch replicas were shown and played by Westerveld at the Tuned City festivals in several cities, and a few times at the Gogbot Festival in Enschede. In 2012 Lee Ranaldo wrote a score for the instruments at the Performa Festival in NYC. In 2015 the Italian band King Tongue staged a tribute to Russolo at Circolo Filologico in Milan performing along with an orchestra of four intonarumori. [10] Czech composer Miroslav Pudlak's Intonarumori Concerto, for three Intonarumori soloists and chamber orchestra, was premiered by the Berg Orchestra in Prague in 2018. [11]

荷兰声音艺术家韦塞尔·韦斯特维尔德(Wessel Westveld)制作了一系列复制品。韦斯特维尔德的复制品是最专业的版本，采用焊接的钢角，而不是像意大利和纽约那样的纸板和钉子钢锥。这些版本的木制品也是真正的硬木，而不是胶合板。韦斯特维尔德还制作了一些开放式变种，从外部可以看到音响系统。[9]韦斯特维尔德与荷兰声音艺术家尤里·兰德曼(Yuri Landman)合作，定期与他的Intonarumori一起表演。这些荷兰复制品由韦斯特维尔德在几个城市的Tuned City音乐节上展示和演奏，并在恩斯赫德的Gogbot音乐节上展出和播放了几次。2012年，李·拉纳尔多在纽约市的Performa音乐节上为这些乐器谱曲。2015年，意大利乐队King Tick在米兰的Circolo Filologico与四人交响乐团一起表演了一场向Russolo致敬的活动。[10]捷克作曲家Miroslv Pudlak为三位Intonarumori独奏家和室内管弦乐队创作的Intonarumori协奏曲于2018年在布拉格由Berg交响乐团首演。[11]

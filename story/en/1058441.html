Federal regulators say hospitals and health insurers can't use code that hides healthcare pricing data from web searches
联邦监管机构表示医院和健康保险公司无法使用隐藏Web搜索的医疗保健定价数据的代码

2021-04-15 22:53:16

Federal regulators said healthcare pricing data that health insurers must post under a new requirement shouldn’t be blocked from web searches, issuing new guidance after The Wall Street Journal reported that hospitals used special coding that shielded such information from Google and other search engines.

联邦监管机构表示医疗保健定价数据，卫生保险公司必须在新要求下发布的数据不应从Web搜查中封锁，在华尔街日报报告的情况下发布新的指导，该医院使用了从谷歌和其他搜索引擎屏蔽了这些信息的特殊编码。

Under new federal requirements, both hospitals and insurers must reveal long-confidential pricing data, including the rates that insurers pay for services. Hospitals were supposed to post data at the start of this year, while insurers must comply beginning in 2022. The hospital industry had fought the requirement in court but lost.

根据新的联邦要求，医院和保险公司都必须揭示长机密定价数据，包括保险公司支付服务的费率。医院应该在今年年初发布数据，而保险公司必须在2022年开始遵守。医院行业在法庭上进行了要求，但迷失了。

Separately, leaders of the House Energy and Commerce Committee, including both Chairman Frank Pallone Jr. (D., N.J.) and ranking Republican Cathy McMorris Rodgers of Washington, sent a letter Tuesday to the federal Department of Health and Human Services calling for tough enforcement of the pricing-transparency requirements. The letter cited evidence of hospitals’ lack of compliance, including the Journal’s report on the search-blocking code.

The new guideline, from the Centers for Medicare and Medicaid Services, was released March 23, the day after the Journal’s article revealed that hundreds of hospital pricing-data websites included special coding that kept them from showing up in searches on Google, which is owned by Alphabet

从Medicare和Medicaid Service的中心发布的新指南于3月23日发布，日记文章后一天透露，数百家医院定价数据网站包括特殊编码，使他们不会在谷歌搜索中显示，这是拥有的通过字母表

The new CMS guidance was issued in an online technical forum on GitHub, a website and cloud-based service, that focuses on the insurer-pricing rule. The rule that applies to hospitals isn’t covered in that forum.

新的CMS指导是在GitHub，网站和基于云的服务的在线技术论坛上发布，专注于保险公司定价规则。在该论坛中申请到医院的规则。

A CMS spokesman said in a statement that the rule change “helps clarify the intent of this regulation—make the files public and accessible.” The agency “intends to provide more guidance on this in the future,” he said. The statement didn’t directly address whether CMS will issue similar guidance for hospitals, but it said the hospital regulation requires the data to be “easily accessible and void of barriers” and that digital files “be digitally searchable.” Previously, HHS has said it expects hospitals to comply with the pricing-transparency requirements and will enforce them.

一名CMS发言人在一份声明中表示，规则变更“有助于澄清这条规则的意图 - 使文件公开和可访问”。他说，原子能机构“打算为将来提供更多指导”。该声明没有直接解决CMS是否会对医院发出类似的指导，但它表示，医院监管要求将数据“易于访问的障碍物”，并且数字文件“是数字搜索”。此前，HHS表示，它预计医院符合定价透明度要求，并将执行它们。

In the GitHub posting about insurers’ data, CMS said, “to allow for search engine discoverability,” the page where the data files are hosted can’t have “rules such that give instructions to web crawlers to not index the page.”

在GitHub关于保险公司数据的帖子中，CMS表示，“允许搜索引擎可发现性”，“数据文件所托管的页面”不能具有“规则，使”Web爬网关指令不索引页面。“

The guidance flagged the same type of code that the Journal found embedded on hundreds of hospital websites. Among websites where the Journal found the blocking code were those for some of the biggest U.S. healthcare systems and some of the largest hospitals in cities including New York and Philadelphia. They included hospitals owned by HCA Healthcare Universal Health Services the University of Pennsylvania Health System and NYU Langone Health.

该指南标记了所发现的日志在数百家医院网站上的相同类型的代码。在期刊发现封锁代码的网站中是那些最大的美国医疗保健系统的一些最大的美国医疗保健系统以及城市中最大的医院，包括纽约和费城。他们包括HCA医疗保健普遍保健服务所拥有的医院宾夕法尼亚州卫生系统和NYU Langone Health。

After the Journal contacted them, many hospital owners, including HCA and Penn Medicine, removed the blocking code from their pricing-data sites. An HCA spokesman said the search blocker was “a legacy code that we’ve removed.”

在联系期刊后，许多包括HCA和Penn医学在内的医院所有者从其定价数据网站中删除了阻塞代码。 HCA发言人表示，搜索拦截器是“我们已删除的遗留代码”。

Penn Medicine and NYU Langone said that they used blocking code to direct patients first to information they considered more useful than raw pricing data, for which they also included links. Universal Health said it used the blocking code to ensure consumers acknowledge a disclosure statement before viewing prices.

Penn医学和Nyu Langone表示，他们使用阻止代码来指导患者首先向他们认为比原始定价数据更有用的信息，他们还包括链接。通用健康表示，它使用阻塞代码确保消费者在查看价格之前确认披露声明。

To identify webpages hidden from search results, the Journal wrote a program that read the contents of 3,190 disclosure pages whose addresses were provided by Turquoise Health Co., a startup working with the price-transparency data. The program searched for a tag in the pages’ background coding that instructs search engines not to index the page.

要识别隐藏的搜索结果中隐藏的网页，则记录写了一个程序，该程序读取了由绿松石卫生公司提供的地址提供的3,190个披露页面的内容，这是一个与价格 - 透明度数据一起使用的启动。该程序在页面的后台编码中搜索标记，指示搜索引擎不索引页面。

The Journal found 164 webpages hosting disclosure files for 307 hospitals that contained versions of that blocking syntax. Some pages include information for more than one hospital within a system. The code was removed from pages with data for 182 hospitals soon after the Journal contacted their owners.

该期刊发现了164个网页托管披露文件，用于307家医院包含该阻塞语法的版本。有些页面包括系统内的多个医院的信息。在期刊联系其所有者后，该代码将不久从带有182家医院的数据删除。

Uber drivers are workers, UK Supreme Court rules
Uber司机是工人，英国最高法院裁定

2021-02-19 18:38:17

Decision means drivers will be entitled to basic rights such as paid holidays, say lawyers

律师表示，决定意味着驾驶员将有权享有带薪假期等基本权利

The UK supreme court has dismissed Uber’s appeal against a landmark employment tribunal ruling that its drivers should be classed as workers with access to the minimum wage and paid holidays.

英国最高法院驳回了优步针对具有里程碑意义的就业法庭的上诉，该裁决裁定其司机应被归类为有权获得最低工资和带薪假期的工人。

Six justices handed down a unanimous decision backing the October 2016 employment tribunal ruling that could affect millions of workers in the gig economy.

六位大法官做出了一项一致决定，支持2016年10月就业法庭的裁决，该裁决可能会影响演出经济中数百万名工人。

The supreme court highlighted that any attempt by organisations to draft artificial contracts aimed at side-stepping basic protections were void and unenforceable.

最高法院强调，组织为草拟旨在回避基本保护措施的人为合同而进行的任何尝试都是无效且不可执行的。

Judges criticised the controversial contracts Uber asked their drivers to sign, saying they “can be seen to have as their object precluding a driver from claiming rights conferred on workers by the applicable legislation”.

法官批评了优步要求其驾驶员签署的有争议的合同，称这些合同“被视为以阻止驾驶员主张适用法律赋予工人权利为目的”。

In the judgment, Lord Leggatt said he was “not convinced” that the contractual arrangements Uber conducted with drivers was compliant with the regulatory regime supervised by Transport for London.

勒加特勋爵在判决书中说，他“不确信”优步与司机的合同安排符合伦敦交通局监督的监管制度。

James Farrar, the co-lead claimant and general secretary of the App Drivers and Couriers union, said: “This ruling will fundamentally re-order the gig economy and bring an end to rife exploitation of workers by means of algorithmic and contract trickery. Uber drivers are cruelly sold a false dream of endless flexibility and entrepreneurial freedom.

App Drivers and Couriers联盟的联合首席索赔人兼秘书长James Farrar表示：“这项裁决将从根本上重新调整零工经济，并通过算法和合同欺骗手段结束对工人的剥削。优步司机被残酷地卖掉了无休止的灵活性和创业自由的梦想。

“The reality has been illegally low pay, dangerously long hours and intense digital surveillance. I am delighted that workers at last have some remedy because of this ruling, but the government must urgently strengthen the law so that gig workers may also have access to sick pay and protection from unfair dismissal.”

“现实是非法的低薪，危险的长时间工作和严格的数字监控。我很高兴由于这项裁定，工人终于可以得到某种补救，但是政府必须紧急加强法律，以便演出工人也可以获得病假工资和免受不公正解雇的保护。”

Frances O’Grady, the general secretary of the TUC, said: “No company is above the law. Uber must play by the rules and stop denying its drivers basic rights at work.

TUC秘书长Frances O’Grady说：“没有任何公司可以超越法律。优步必须遵守规则，并停止否认其司机在工作中的基本权利。

“This ruling is an important win for gig economy workers and for common decency. Sham self-employment exploits people and lets companies dodge paying their fair share of tax.”

“这项裁定是零工经济工作者和共同体面的重要胜利。虚假的自谋职业剥削了人们，让公司逃避缴纳应得的税款。”

The case began when two Uber drivers, Farrar and Yaseen Aslam, took Uber to court on behalf of a group 19 others who argued that they were employed by the San Francisco-based company, rather than working for themselves.

该案始于两名Uber司机Farrar和Yaseen Aslam代表另外19人将Uber告上法庭，他们辩称他们是由这家位于旧金山的公司雇用的，而不是自己工作。

It is one of a string of cases challenging the self-employed status of gig-economy workers, including action against the minicab firm Addison Lee and the delivery groups CitySprint, Excel and eCourier.

这是挑战零工经济工人的自雇身份的一系列案件之一，包括对微型出租车公司Addison Lee以及交付集团CitySprint，Excel和eCourier的诉讼。

冠状病毒每年造成的死亡人数比预期多出近30万人。

The coronavirus pandemic has left about 299,000 more people dead in the United States than would be expected in a typical year, two-thirds of them from covid-19 and the rest from other causes, the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention reported Tuesday.

美国疾病控制和预防中心周二报道，冠状病毒大流行已导致美国死亡人数比通常一年的预期多29.9万人，其中三分之二死于新冠肺炎，其余死于其他原因。

The CDC said the novel coronavirus, which causes covid-19, has taken a disproportionate toll on Latinos and Blacks, as previous analyses have noted. But the CDC also found, surprisingly, that it has struck 25- to 44-year-olds very hard: Their “excess death” rate is up 26.5 percent over previous years, the largest change for any age group.

疾控中心表示，正如之前的分析所指出的那样，导致新冠肺炎的新型冠状病毒对拉丁裔和黑人造成了不成比例的损失。但令人惊讶的是，疾病预防控制中心也发现，这对25到44岁的人打击非常严重：他们的“超额死亡率”比前几年上升了26.5% %，是所有年龄段中变化最大的。

It is not clear whether that spike is caused by the shift in covid-19 deaths toward younger people between May and August or deaths from other causes, the CDC said.

疾控中心表示，目前尚不清楚这一激增是由新冠肺炎死亡人数在5月至8月期间向年轻人转移还是其他原因造成的。

The report comes with just two weeks left in a presidential campaign whose central issue is President Trump’s handling of the pandemic. Trump has sought at every turn, including in remarks Monday, to minimize the virus’s impact, despite a covid-19 death toll that is likely to be the third-leading cause of mortality in the United States this year, behind heart disease and cancer. That stance has proved to be the president’s enduring weakness as the election looms Nov. 3.

这份报告发布之际，距离总统竞选只剩下两周的时间，总统竞选的核心问题是特朗普总统对疫情的处理。特朗普一直在千方百计地寻求将病毒的影响降至最低，包括周一的讲话，尽管新冠肺炎的死亡人数很可能成为今年美国第三大死亡原因，仅次于心脏病和癌症。事实证明，随着11月3日大选的临近，这一立场已被证明是总统的持久弱点。

His Democratic opponent, former vice president Joe Biden, has made his plans to tackle the pandemic the major focus of his bid to capture the White House.

他的民主党对手、前副总统乔·拜登(Joe Biden)已将应对埃博拉疫情的计划作为他夺取白宫的主要重点。

“The number of people dying from this pandemic is higher than we think,” said Steven Woolf, director emeritus of the Center on Society and Health at Virginia Commonwealth University, who has conducted independent analyses of excess mortality. “This study shows it. Others have, as well.”

弗吉尼亚联邦大学社会与健康中心荣休主任史蒂文·伍尔夫(Steven Woolf)说，“死于这场大流行的人数比我们想象的要多。”伍尔夫对超额死亡率进行了独立分析。“这项研究显示了这一点。其他人也有。“。

The United States is in the midst of another sharp increase in coronavirus infections, this one centered in the upper Midwest and Plains states. The seven-day rolling average of cases, considered the most accurate barometer, is near 60,000 per day. At least 220,000 people have died of covid-19 so far, according to data kept by The Washington Post.

美国正处于冠状病毒感染的又一次急剧增加之中，这一次集中在中西部北部和平原州。被认为是最准确的晴雨表的7天滚动平均病例数接近每天6万例。根据华盛顿邮报保存的数据，到目前为止，至少有220,000人死于新冠肺炎。

The new CDC data covers Feb. 1 to Oct. 3. Woolf said the total is likely to reach 400,000 by the end of the year. The numbers were assembled by the National Center for Health Statistics, a unit of the CDC.

新的疾控中心数据涵盖了2月1日至10月3日的  。伍尔夫表示，到今年年底，总数可能会达到400,000人。这些数据是由疾控中心下属的国家健康统计中心(National Center For Health Statistics)收集的。

Outside analyses, including some by The Washington Post and researchers at Yale University, have found two main causes for excess deaths. Many probably were the result of covid-19, although they were not recorded that way on death certificates. Others are probably the result of deaths at home or in nursing homes from heart attacks, diabetes, strokes and Alzheimer’s disease, among people afraid to seek care in hospitals or unable to get it.

外部分析，包括“华盛顿邮报”和耶鲁大学的研究人员，发现了导致过度死亡的两个主要原因。许多可能是新冠肺炎的结果，尽管死亡证明上没有这样记录。另一些可能是在家中或疗养院死于心脏病发作、糖尿病、中风和阿尔茨海默氏症，这些人害怕到医院寻求治疗或无法得到治疗，其他原因可能是这些人在家里或疗养院死于心脏病发作、糖尿病、中风和阿尔茨海默氏症。

Overall, the CDC found that “excess deaths have occurred every week since March, 2020,” with a peak during the week of April 11 and another during the week ending Aug. 8. Those dates roughly coincide with the virus’s surge into the New York metro area near the start of the outbreak and a second major rise across the Sun Belt when many states reopened too soon in an effort to revive flagging economies.

总体而言，美国疾病控制与预防中心发现，“自2020年3月以来，每周都有超额死亡事件发生”，高峰出现在4月11日的那一周，还有一次是在8月8日结束的那一周。这两个日期大致与病毒在疫情爆发前夕涌入纽约大都会地区的时间不谋而合。当时，为了重振萎靡不振的经济，许多州过早地重新开放了大门，太阳地带也出现了第二次大规模的死亡人数上升。

All told, an estimated 299,028 more people died than would be expected in a typical year, which was defined as the average annual deaths from 2015 to 2019, the CDC reported. It said 198,081 of those fatalities were caused by covid-19, with the remainder attributable to other causes.

疾控中心报告称，据估计，通常一年的死亡人数估计比预期的多299028人，这一年的定义是从2015年到2019年的年均死亡人数。报告称，其中198,081人是由新冠肺炎造成的，其余的可归因于其他原因。

While the virus continues to prey mainly on older people and, disproportionately, African Americans and Latinos, the rate of excess mortality among 25- to 44-year-olds was less expected. Among 45- to 64-year-olds the increase was 14.4 percent, and among 65- to 74-year-olds it was 24.1 percent.

虽然该病毒继续主要侵害老年人，而且不成比例地侵害非裔美国人和拉丁裔，但25至44岁人群的超额死亡率较少预期。在45岁至64岁的人中，这一比例上升了14.4%，在65岁至74岁的人中，这一比例上升了24.1% 。

The unexpectedly high mortality rates for adults in the prime of their lives, from 25 to 65, has been a source of ongoing concern for public health experts and others in recent years, especially since a spike in deaths from drugs, alcohol and suicide was recognized.

近年来，25岁至65岁的壮年成年人死亡率出人意料地高，一直是公共卫生专家和其他人持续关注的问题，特别是在药物、酒精和自杀死亡人数激增之后。

Lauren M. Rossen, a senior health statistician at the National Center for Health Statistics and lead author of Tuesday’s report, said the death rate in the young-adult age group “was trending up before the pandemic.” But it’s still not clear why excess deaths for 25- to 44-year-olds rose so quickly.

美国国家卫生统计中心(National Center For Health Statistics)高级卫生统计师、周二报告的主要作者劳伦·M·罗森(Lauren M.Rossen)表示，青壮年人群的死亡率“在大流行前呈上升趋势”。但目前仍不清楚为什么25岁至44岁的超额死亡人数上升得如此之快。

“I don’t think we know, specifically, the answer to that question yet,” Rossen said.

罗森说：“我认为我们还不知道这个问题的具体答案。”

In part, the large change reflects the general good health and low death rate among young adults, said Jeremy S. Faust, an emergency physician at Brigham and Women’s Hospital in Boston who studies excess deaths. A sudden surge in deaths from covid-19 would change the percentages markedly. According to the CDC, 5,707 people in the 25-to-44-year-old age group have succumbed to covid-19 since the beginning of the pandemic in the United States.

波士顿布里格姆妇女医院(Brigham and Women‘s Hospital)的急诊医生杰里米·S·福斯特(Jeremy S.Faust)说，这种巨大的变化在一定程度上反映了年轻人的总体健康状况和较低的死亡率。新冠肺炎死亡人数的突然增加将显著改变这一比例。根据疾控中心的数据，自美国疫情开始以来，已有5,707名25岁至44岁的人死于新冠肺炎。

“It’s scary because on one hand young people still don’t die [from covid-19] very often,” he said. “But on the other hand, it’s a public health crisis.”

“这很可怕，因为一方面年轻人(死于新冠肺炎)仍然不多见，”他说。“但另一方面，这是一场公共卫生危机。”

Among racial groups, the majority of people killed by covid-19 are White. But for people of color, and especially Latinos, the new report emphasizes just how big a difference the pandemic has made in mortality.

在种族群体中，新冠肺炎杀害的大多数人是白人。但对于有色人种，特别是拉丁裔，这份新报告强调了大流行对死亡率的影响有多大。

For Whites, that means an excess death rate of 11.9 percent over a normal year. For Latinos, it is 53.6 percent, for Blacks 32.9 percent and for Asians 36.6 percent.

对于白人来说，这意味着比正常年份高出11.9个 %的超额死亡率。拉丁裔是53.6%的 ，黑人是32.9%的 ，亚裔是36.6%的 。

Possible explanations for the higher rates include underlying health conditions that may make people of color more vulnerable to covid-19 and jobs as essential workers that put more Blacks and Latinos in the path of the virus. But experts do not have a solid explanation for the data.

较高发病率的可能解释包括潜在的健康状况，这可能会让有色人种更容易受到新冠肺炎的影响，以及作为基本工人的工作会让更多的黑人和拉丁裔走上病毒传播的道路。但专家们对这些数据没有一个可靠的解释。

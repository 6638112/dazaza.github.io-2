The tech giants’ law-free bonanza is coming to an end on both sides of the Atlantic, but let’s speed up the process

科技巨头的免税大富翁在大西洋两岸都将终结，但让我们加快这一进程

I t’s always risky making predictions about the tech industry, but this year looks like being different, at least in the sense that there are two safe bets. One is that the attempts to regulate the tech giants that began last year will intensify; the second that we will be increasingly deluged by sanctimonious cant from Facebook & co as they seek to avoid democratic curbing of their unaccountable power.

对技术行业做出预测总是很冒险的，但是今年看起来有所不同，至少就两个安全的赌注而言。其一是，去年开始对高科技巨头进行监管的尝试将会加强；其次，我们将越来越多地被来自Facebook＆amp; amp;他们试图避免民主遏制其不负责任的权力。

On the regulation front, last year in the US, Alphabet, Google’s corporate owner, found itself facing major antitrust suits from 38 states as well as from the Department of Justice. On this side of the pond, there are preparations for a Digital Markets Unit with statutory powers that will be able to neatly sidestep the tricky definitional questions of what constitutes a monopoly in a digital age. Instead, the unit will decide on a case-by-case basis whether a particular tech company has “strategic market status” if it possesses “substantial, entrenched market power in at least one digital activity” or if it acts as an online “gateway” for other businesses. And if a company is judged to have this status, then penalties and regulations will be imposed on it.

在法规方面，去年在美国，谷歌的公司所有者Alphabet面临38个州和司法部的重大反托拉斯诉讼。在池塘的这一侧，正在为具有法定权力的数字市场部门做准备，这些部门将能够巧妙地避开关于什么构成数字时代的垄断这一棘手的定义性问题。取而代之的是，该部门将根据具体情况决定特定的科技公司是否具有“战略市场地位”，是否在至少一项数字活动中拥有“牢固的，根深蒂固的市场力量”，或者是否充当在线“门户” ”用于其他业务。而且，如果公司被判定为具有此身份，则将对其施加处罚和法规。

Over in Brussels, the European Union has come up with a new two-pronged legal framework for curbing digital power – the Digital Markets Act and the Digital Services Act. The Digital Markets Act is aimed at curbing anti-competitive practices in the tech industry (like buying up potential competitors before they can scale up) and will include fines of 10% of global revenues for infringers. The Digital Services Act, for its part, will oblige social media platforms to take more responsibility for illegal content on their platforms – scams, terrorist content, images of abuse, etc – for which they could face fines of up to 6% of global revenue if they fail to police content adequately. So the US and UK approach focuses on corporate behaviour; the EU approach focuses on defining what is allowed legally.

在布鲁塞尔，欧盟提出了一个新的两方面的法律框架来遏制数字权力-《数字市场法》和《数字服务法》。 《数字市场法案》旨在遏制技术行业的反竞争做法（例如在潜在竞争对手扩大规模之前先对其进行收购），并将对侵权者处以全球收入10％的罚款。就《数字服务法》而言，它将责成社交媒体平台对其平台上的非法内容（诈骗，恐怖分子内容，滥用图片等）承担更多责任，为此，他们可能面临最高全球收入的6％的罚款。如果他们未能充分监管内容。因此，美国和英国的方法侧重于公司行为。欧盟的方法侧重于定义法律允许的内容。

All of this action has been a long time coming and while it’s difficult to say exactly how it will play out, the bottom line is that the tech industry is – finally – going to become a regulated one. Its law-free bonanza is going to come to an end.

所有这些行动已经进行了很长一段时间，尽管很难确切说明如何发挥作用，但最重要的是，技术行业最终将成为受监管的行业。它的无法律禁令即将结束。

Joe Biden’s choices for top staff in his administration include a depressing proportion of former tech company stalwarts

乔·拜登（Joe Biden）担任高级管理人员的选择包括令人沮丧的前高科技公司支持者

The big question, though, is: when? Antitrust actions proceed at a glacial pace because of the complexity of the issues and the bottomless legal budgets of the companies involved. The judge in one of the big American antitrust cases against Google has said that he expects the case to get to court only in late 2023 and then it could run for several years (as the Microsoft case did in the 1990s).

不过，最大的问题是：何时？由于问题的复杂性以及所涉公司的无底法律预算，反托拉斯行动的步伐令人鼓舞。法官在美国针对Google的一桩重大反托拉斯案中表示，他预计该案仅在2023年下半年才能出庭，然后可能会持续数年（就像Microsoft在1990年代的案件一样）。

The problem with that, as the veteran anti-monopoly campaigner Matt Stoller has pointed out, is that the longer monopolistic behaviour goes on, the more damage (eg, to advertisers whose revenue is being stolen and other businesses whose property is being appropriated) is being done. Google had $170bn in revenue last year and is growing on average at 10-20% a year. On a conservative estimate of 10% growth, the company will add another $100bn to its revenue by 2025, when the case will still be in the court. Facebook, says Stoller, “is at $80bn of revenue this year, but it is growing faster, so the net increase of revenue is a roughly similar amount. In other words, if the claims of the government are credible, then the lengthy case, while perhaps necessary, is also enabling these monopolists to steal an additional $100bn apiece.”

正如资深反垄断运动家马特·斯托勒（Matt Stoller）所指出的那样，与此有关的问题是，垄断行为持续的时间越长，损害就越大（例如，对收入被盗的广告商和财产被盗用的其他企业的损害）。正在完成。 Google去年的收入为1700亿美元，并且平均每年以10-20％的速度增长。按照保守的估计增长10％，该公司到2025年将再增加1000亿美元的收入，届时该案仍将上法庭。斯托勒说，“ Facebook今年的收入为800亿美元，但增长速度更快，因此收入的净增幅大致相当。换句话说，如果政府的主张是可信的，那么冗长的案子虽然可能是必要的，但也使这些垄断者每人再窃取1000亿美元。”

What could speed up bringing these monopolists to account? A key factor is the vigour with which the US Department of Justice prosecutes its case(s). In the run-up to the 2020 election, the Democrats in Congress displayed an encouraging enthusiasm for tackling tech monopolies, but Joe Biden’s choices for top staff in his administration include a depressing proportion of former tech company stalwarts. And his vice-president-elect, Kamala Harris, consistently turned a blind eye to the anti-competitive acquisitions of the Silicon Valley giants throughout her time as California’s attorney general. So if people are hoping for antitrust zeal from the new US government, they may be in for disappointment.

如何加快将这些垄断者纳入考虑范围？一个关键因素是美国司法部提起诉讼的积极性。在2020年大选前夕，国会民主党人表现出对解决技术垄断的热情，但乔·拜登（Joe Biden）担任高级管理人员的选择包括令人沮丧的前科技公司支持者。和他的副总统当选人，卡马拉·哈里斯，始终视而不见硅谷巨头的反竞争收购在她的时间，加州总检察长。因此，如果人们希望美国新政府提出反托拉斯的热情，他们可能会感到失望。

Interestingly, Stoller suggests that another approach (inspired by the way trust-busters in the US acted in the 1930s) could have useful leverage on corporate behaviour from now on. Monopolisation isn’t just illegal, he points out, “it is in fact a crime, an appropriation of the rights and property of others by a dominant actor. The lengthy trial is essentially akin to saying that bank robbers getting to keep robbing banks until they are convicted and can probably keep the additional loot.”

有趣的是，斯托勒（Stoller）提出，从1930年代开始，另一种方法（受美国信托破坏者的行动方式启发）可以对公司行为产生有益的影响。他指出，垄断不仅是非法的，“事实上，这是一种犯罪，是占统治地位的行为者侵占他人权利和财产的行为。漫长的审判从本质上讲类似于说银行抢劫犯要继续抢劫银行，直到他们被定罪并可能保留额外的赃物为止。”

Since a basic principle of the rule of law is that crime shouldn’t pay, an addition of the possibility of criminal charges to the antitrust actions might, like the prospect of being hanged in the morning ( pace Dr Johnson), concentrate minds in Facebook, Google, Amazon and Apple. As an eternal optimist, I cannot think of a nicer prospect for 2021 than the sight of Mark Zuckerberg and Sundar Pichai in the dock – with Nick Clegg in attendance, taking notes. Happy new year!

修复一台已有37年历史的IBM F机械键盘

2020-11-09 06:53:25

I wanted to share my journey from start to finish to restore a 1983 IBM Model F XT mechanical keyboard to it's former glory. It includes the steps, mistakes, and additional hardware required to make it functional with a modern computer. This blog post is dedicated to my dad for teaching me about computers.

我想自始至终分享我的旅程，让1983年的IBM Model F XT机械键盘恢复昔日的辉煌。它包括使其与现代计算机一起工作所需的步骤、错误和附加硬件。这篇博文是献给我的父亲，他教会了我有关计算机的知识。

A few months ago, my dad asked if I was interested in taking ownership of his IBM model 5150 PC. Without hesitation, I said "yes!". The first project I wanted to tackle was to restore the 1983 Model F XT mechanical keyboard keyboard. In a sentimental andjourneyman/apprentice-sense, it felt like my dad was passing on the tools of his craft to continue the family line ofcomputer work and I couldn't pass that up.

几个月前，我父亲问我是否有兴趣购买他的IBM 5150型个人电脑。我毫不犹豫地说：好的！我想解决的第一个项目是修复1983年的F-XT机械键盘。在一种多愁善感的熟练工/学徒感中，我感觉我父亲是在传递他的手艺工具，以延续计算机工作的家族血统，我不能错过这一点。

The IBM Model F keyboards not only used the best switches, the materials used in their production (well over 5lbs ofsteel and other metals) means they will be working as good as new when it’s time to pass it on to your grandchildren.The problem...they just aren't made that way any more. The IBM Model F was discontinued in the 1980's. If you dofind a Model F, it will be some combination of dirty, broken and/or expensive, requiring hours of work to get itworking again!

IBM Model F键盘不仅使用了最好的开关，生产过程中使用的材料(远远超过5磅的钢和其他金属)意味着它们在传给孙辈的时候会像新的一样工作。问题是……它们不再是这样制造的。IBM Model F在20世纪80年代停产。如果你真的找到了Model F，它将是肮脏、破损和/或昂贵的组合，需要数小时的工作才能重新运行！

This tank of a keyboard weighs in at over 6 pounds, sounds like this, and at thetime of production, retailed for $300-400 in 1982 ($800-1000 dollars adjusted in today's dollars!) according to this review.

这个键盘槽的重量超过6磅，听起来是这样的，在生产的时候，1982年的零售价为300-400美元(按今天的美元调整后为800-1000美元！)。根据这篇评论。

The restoration did take a few hours and fortunately none of it involved a soldering iron or replacing any of theelectrical or physical components because it was already in great functioning shape...a true testament to the design.Even cooler, this blog post was typed up using the restored Model F keyboard!

修复过程确实花了几个小时，幸运的是，修复过程中没有使用烙铁，也没有更换任何电气或物理部件，因为它已经处于良好的运行状态……这是对设计的真实证明。甚至更酷的是，这篇博客文章是用修复后的F型键盘打印出来的！

Before beginning the project, I discovered ClickyKeyboards, a site "Specializing inthe restoration and collection of model M keyboards", the successor to the Model F. On ClickyKeyboards, there is asection dedicated to the adapters and converters that may be required to make older 5-pin DIN plug keyboards compatiblewith modern USB ports. One of the companies mentioned on ClickyKeyboards is Hagstrom Electronics, which sells keyboard encoders and protocol converters.Without looking too closely at the other products, I quickly purchased the KE18-XTAT-PS/2 which "converts an XTkeyboard into a PS/2 protocol keyboard" for $55. I was ecstatic that there was at least something to get me into PS/2land, because at that point I knew I could easily find a PS/2 to USB converter.

在开始这个项目之前，我发现了ClickyKeyboard，这是一个专门修复和收集M型键盘的网站，M型键盘是F型键盘的继任者。在ClickyKeyboard上，有一个专门的适配器和转换器，可以用来使较旧的5针DIN插头键盘与现代USB端口兼容。ClickyKeyboard上提到的一家公司是Hagstrom Electronics，它销售键盘编码器和协议转换器。在没有太仔细研究其他产品的情况下，我很快就买了KE18-XTAT-PS/2，它可以将XT键盘转换为PS/2协议键盘，价格为55美元。我欣喜若狂，至少有一些东西可以让我进入PS/2的领域，因为在这一点上，我知道我可以很容易地找到PS/2到USB的转换器。

After the KE18-XTAT-PS/2 arrived, I quickly fired up an ancient box (yes, that is Windows 2000!) with a PS/2 input onthe motherboard. I tested all the keys to ensure they still worked and they did! With confirmation that the keyboardstill worked, it was time to start the restoration.

KE18-XTAT-PS/2到达后，我很快打开了一个古老的盒子(没错，那就是Windows 2000！)。主板上有PS/2输入。我测试了所有的钥匙，以确保它们仍然有效，而且它们确实有效！确认键盘仍然有效后，是时候开始修复了。

The first step was to remove the metal casing and see what the state of the board was underneath the keys. There wasyears worth of debris, coffee stains, and gunk that had to be removed. In addition, there were a few spots withcorrosion on the board that needed to be addressed.

第一步是取下金属外壳，看看钥匙下面的板子是什么状态。有相当于多年的碎片、咖啡污渍和黏糊糊的东西必须清除。此外，电路板上有一些腐蚀斑点需要处理。

Once the casing was off, I utilized compressed air to clean out the key bunkers and get rid of any loose nastiness.Next, I tried using rubbing alcohol and Q-tips to try and remove some of the stickier stuff, but that didn'treally work.

脱壳后，我用压缩空气清理了关键的掩体，清除了任何松散的脏物。接下来，我试着用酒精和棉签试着去掉一些粘性的东西，但这确实奏效了(#39；t==##*_)。

I borrowed a rotary tool to buff out the corrosion. I used one of the provided bits that had soft plastic tentacles totry and buff out the corroded spots as gently as possible. There are likely better and more appropriate bits, but itdid the job. The rotary tool's power and RPMs were a bit overkill even on the lowest setting. Ideally, I would haveused one with fewer minimum RPMs and a more precise bit to get all the spots.

我借了一个旋转工具来消除腐蚀。我用的是一块带有柔软塑料触手的钻头，尽可能轻柔地把腐蚀的斑点擦得亮晶晶的。可能会有更好、更合适的部分，但它做到了这一点。即使在最低的设置下，旋转工具的功率和转速也有点过头了。理想情况下，我会使用最低RPM更少、位数更精确的一款来获得所有的光斑。

I was using the rotary tool under a bright overhead desk lamp, and with the way the light was reflecting, I didn'tnotice it was taking off the black finish and revealing the silver metal base. At that point, I just decided to buffoff the finish where I could to make it look more uniform. The silver metal is visible when the keys and cover are backon it, but it looks fine. Unfortunately, with the rotary head and bit size, I wasn't able to buff every last squareinch of the board, but I knew it'd be covered so I wasn't too concerned with it looking perfect.

我在头顶上明亮的台灯下使用旋转工具，光线反射的方式，我没有注意到它掉下了黑色的表面，露出了银色的金属底座。在这一点上，我只是决定在我能做到的地方抛光，让它看起来更均匀。当钥匙和盖子在背面时，银色金属是可见的，但它看起来很好。不幸的是，由于旋转头和钻头的大小，我不能将棋盘上的每一平方英寸都磨光，但我知道它会被覆盖，所以我并不太在意它看起来是否完美。

There was still some residue stuck to the board that I wanted to remove. I started out using a small eye glassscrewdriver to scrape it off, but it took some of the black finish off as well and didn't look that nice. At that point,a chemical pivot was required and I reached for the Goo Gone, which I should have done from thebeginning. The Goo Gone and a bit of elbow grease with Q-Tips did the trick in removing the stubborn gunk on the board.

还有一些残留物粘在黑板上，我想把它们去掉。我一开始是用一把小螺丝刀把它刮掉的，但它也去掉了一些黑色的部分，看起来不太好看。在这一点上，需要一个化学支点，我伸手去拿粘性物质，这是我从一开始就应该做的。粘性物质消失了，用Q-Tips擦了一点肘部的油脂，确实有效地去除了黑板上的顽固粘性物质。

With the board in good shape, it was time to tackle the actual keys. I first gave them a good wipe-down using deskcleaning wipes, which removed most of the discoloration and stains. However, they still didn't look as good as theycould, and I read that just soaking them in a bowl of dish soap and water for a few hours can do wonders...and it did.They keys look brand new.

由于棋盘状态良好，是时候开始处理实际的按键了。我先用擦桌子的湿巾把它们擦得干干净净，去除了大部分的变色和污渍。然而，它们看起来还是不尽如人意，我读到，仅仅是把它们浸泡在一碗洗碗皂和水中几个小时就能产生奇迹……果真如此。它们看起来像崭新的钥匙。

One mistake I made after washing them was to not let them completely dry on the inside. I placed the keys back on theboard too soon, and some water leaked onto a few of the springs causing a small amount of rusting (slightly visible inthe post-Goo Gone image). I gave them another soap soak and used the compressed air to really get out the water. Ialso let them air dry for 1-2 days before putting them back on the board.

洗完后我犯了一个错误，就是没有让它们内部完全干透。我太快把钥匙放回滑板上，一些水漏到了几个弹簧上，造成了少量的生锈(在Goo Gone之后的图像中略微可见)。我又给了他们一次肥皂浸泡，用压缩空气把水真正抽出来。我也会让它们风干1-2天，然后再把它们放回冲浪板上。

After successfully testing the actual keyboard and the restoration almost complete, it was time to search for a PS/2 toUSB cable. On a whim, I was back on the Hagstrom Electronic site and noticed they already had a small box, the KE-XTUSB, that converted the XT signal to USB,and it was the same price. They graciously allowed me to return the KE18-XTAT-PS/2 in exchange for the KE-XTUSB whichwas the same price. A few days later the KE-XTUSB arrived in the mail and I eagerly connected it to my current computer.

在成功测试了实际键盘并且恢复工作接近完成后，是时候搜索PS/2转USB电缆了。一时心血来潮，我回到了Hagstrom电子网站，注意到他们已经有了一个小盒子，Ke-XTUSB，可以将XT信号转换成USB，价格是一样的。他们很客气地允许我退还KE18-XTAT-PS/2，以换取价格相同的KE-XTUSB。几天后，KE-XTUSB收到了邮件，我急切地把它连接到我现在的电脑上。

Everything worked beautifully except for two keys. The "s" key's spring mechanism would either not detect a key pressor would be stuck in the depressed state blasting "sssssssssssssssssssssssssssssssssssss" across the screen. With abit of finagling, I got the key cap placed correctly so now it works like a champ. The other key that has issues is theaccountant's "+" near the 10 key pad. The spring had come off somehow and I super glued it back, but something stillisn't right and it fails to register key strokes. Not a huge loss since "+" can be achieved with another keycombination.

除了两把钥匙外，一切都运转得很好。按键的弹簧机构要么不会检测到按键按键被卡住，要么会使屏幕上的"；sssssssssssssssssssssssssssssssssssss"；处于按下状态。用一点小把戏，我把钥匙帽放对了，所以现在它就像冠军一样工作了。另一把有问题的钥匙是10键附近的会计师事务所。弹簧不知何故掉了下来，我用强力胶水把它粘了回去，但还是有什么不对劲，它无法记录击键。不会有太大的损失，因为用另一个组合键就可以实现。

Overall, I'm really impressed with how it looks and, when comparing it to thisunboxing video of a never before opened Model F, it looks about the same!

总体而言，我真的对它的外观印象深刻，当把它与这款从未打开过的Model F的拆箱视频进行比较时，它看起来几乎是一样的！

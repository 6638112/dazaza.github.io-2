Microsoft Coffee
微软咖啡

2021-04-02 23:46:53

On April Fools' Day 1996, exactly 25 years ago today, some Microsoft coworkers and I launched a complex and costly prank. We printed up hundreds of realistic shrink-wrapped boxes of a fake product, “Microsoft Coffee”, and snuck it into stores all around the Seattle area. Customers were confused, retail was upset, the local news and tech media covered it. So Microsoft’s PR flacks freaked out and covered it up. They scooped up all remaining boxes, denied Microsoft did the prank, and downplayed the scale of it to the media, and imposed a ban on real-world pranks, ensuring all pranks from then on would need to go through them, as virtual press releases. I’ve been carrying the secret for 25 years. Today, I'm coming clean. This is the untold true story of a corporate April Fools' prank gone wrong.

四月愚人节＆＃39; 1996年，正好25年前，今天，一些Microsoft同事和我推出了一个复杂且昂贵的恶作剧。我们印刷了数百个现实的缩小包装盒的假产品，“微软咖啡”，并将其困在西雅图地区周围的商店。客户很困惑，零售很沮丧，当地新闻和技术媒体覆盖。所以微软的PR弗拉基肆虐并覆盖它。他们舀起所有剩下的盒子，否认微软做了恶作剧，并将其落下了媒体的规模，并禁止在现实世界的恶作剧上，确保所有恶作剧从那时需要通过它们，作为虚拟新闻稿。我一直担任秘密25年。今天，我清洁。这是一项公司四月愚人节和＃39的无数真实故事;恶作剧出错了。

Things were different back in 1996. Most software was sold on floppy discs or CDs, in boxes you’d find at tech stores like Best Buy or Egghead Software. Microsoft dominated tech back then, with as much clout as today’s Amazon and Google combined. But Microsoft had a reputation, deserved or not, as more of an imitator than an innovator.

事情在1996年的情况下不同。大多数软件在软盘或CD上销售，在您在最佳购买或EGGHEAD软件等技术商店找到的盒子中。微软主导了技术，然后用与今天的亚马逊和谷歌相结合一样多的Clout。但微软的声誉，应得的，与创新者一样。

So when my coworkers and I decided to pull a traditional April Fools' tech prank, we thought it would be fun to poke fun at that reputation. Sun Microsystems had just come out with Java, we thought “Microsoft Coffee” sounded like a pale, tepid name for a me-too product.

所以当我的同事和我决定拉一个传统的四月傻瓜＆＃39;技术恶作剧，我们认为在那种声誉拍打乐趣会很有趣。 Sun Microsystems刚刚用Java出来，我们认为“微软咖啡”听起来像是一个苍白的，不温柔的名字，为我而不是产品。

But two things made our prank different from other pranks from tech companies. First, instead of just sending out a press release about a goofy fake product, we wanted to make a physical product that someone could hold in their hand, and ideally that someone on TV news would hold in front of the camera. (With Starbucks becoming a national brand, we might confuse local media into thinking Microsoft was actually making a coffee-related product; that coincidence helped us, because local news didn’t really know what Java was.) And second, unlike other pranks our prank didn’t just say ‘Microsoft is successful but nerdy’. Instead, it fed the idea that Microsoft kind of sucked as a company in some way; a lazy copycat.

但两件事使我们的恶作剧与科技公司的其他恶作剧不同。首先，不仅仅是关于一个关于愚蠢的假产品的新闻发布，我们想制作一个有人可以掌握的物理产品，理想情况下，电视新闻上的某人将在镜头前举行。 （与星巴克成为一个国家品牌，我们可能会使当地媒体混为思考微软实际上正在制作咖啡的产品;巧合帮助我们，因为当地新闻并不真正知道Java是什么。）和第二，与其他恶作剧不知道我们的恶作剧不只是说'微软成功但讨厌'。相反，它提出了以某种方式吮吸微软的想法;一个懒惰的模仿。

These two differences would lead to trouble, but we didn’t foresee that. We were busy arranging a graphic design of the box, putting easter egg jokes in the tech specs on the side, leveraging corporate partnerships, and prepping to make a run of several hundred boxes through one of Microsoft’s production printing systems. This was not only kind of expensive, but a commitment, a step that would in effect put an official stamp from Microsoft on the plan.

这两个差异会导致麻烦，但我们没有预见到那样。我们正忙于安排盒子的平面设计，将复活节彩蛋在Tech Specs上放在侧面，利用公司伙伴关系，并通过微软的生产印刷系统之一，准备乘坐几百盒。这不仅是一种昂贵的，而且是一种承诺，一个有效的一步，就是在计划上从微软展开官方邮票。

Another two people were preparing for delivery, organizing a route to drive efficiently to a number of stores, and to surreptitiously place the final, shrink-wrapped boxes, including fake SKU barcode, on the store shelves. By 2pm, after weeks of planning and design, Microsoft Coffee was “real”. In stores from Everett to Redmond to Tukwila. We treated the Egghead Software store in downtown Seattle as a flagship.

另外两个人正在准备交付，组织一条有效地驾驶到多家商店的路线，并偷偷地将最终收缩包裹的盒子放在商店货架上的伪滑雪条码。下午2点，经过几周的规划和设计，微软咖啡是“真实”。在埃弗雷特到雷德蒙德的商店到杜鲁瓦。我们将Egghead软件商店视为西雅图市中心为旗舰。

Soon, enough customers around town did find (and even attempt to buy!) the perplexing boxes that it created a buzz. We sent a Microsoft press release to several local stations on the benefits of Microsoft Coffee, a fax which was unauthorized by corporate communications of course. A second fax from company letterhead managed to get a small news crew to record a remote shot from the Seattle Egghead store.

很快，镇周围的客户确实发现了（甚至试图买！）它创造了一个嗡嗡声的令人困惑的盒子。我们将Microsoft新闻发布发布到几个本地电台，以了解Microsoft Coffee的好处，该传真当然是企业沟通未经授权的传真。来自公司信笺的第二次传真设法获取一个小消息，以录制距离西雅图Egghead商店的遥控器。

KOMO TV was the second to bite, and covered the prank twice. In the late afternoon broadcast, the anchor described the prank, and then said that Microsoft denied any involvement with it. As we watched the broadcast live, we laughed at each other, speculating who, exactly, KOMO had talked to. Then the anchor said “So I guess this [box] is just… fake.” And he showily dropped it in his wastebasket.

Komo TV是第二次咬，并覆盖了两次恶作剧。在下午晚些时候的广播中，锚地描述了恶作剧，然后表示微软否认任何参与它。当我们看着广播生活时，我们嘲笑对方，猜测谁，究竟，科莫谈到了。然后锚点说：所以我猜这[盒子]只是......假。“他夏上地把它放在了他的废纸篓里。

In the evening broadcast, the same anchor went through the story again, although this time he didn’t throw the box away. And this time there were more details from Microsoft PR, about how Microsoft was indeed working on “Java” (we weren’t, exactly), and that the Microsoft Coffee stunt was from outsiders. This was curious, and a bit unsettling. Usually Microsoft was happy to take credit for clever pranks from employees, because it showed we played hard besides working hard.

在晚上的广播中，同样的锚点再次穿过这个故事，虽然这次他没有扔掉盒子。此时来自Microsoft Pr的更多细节，了解微软确实在“Java”上的工作原理（我们并不完全），而且Microsoft Coffee Stunt来自外人。这很好奇，有点令人不安。通常微软很乐意为员工的聪明的恶作剧承担信誉，因为它显示除了努力工作之外努力。

Other news outlets and radio also covered the story, and by the time we went home that night, we were quite happy at the impact. After all, we had spent countless hours over the past month planning the prank.

其他新闻网点和收音机也涵盖了这个故事，那天晚上我们回家的时候，我们很高兴的影响。毕竟，我们在过去一个月规划恶作剧的数小时。

In the morning, we were giddy. But that feeling quickly turned to fear, as we learned that Microsoft PR had actually flipped out the day before. They had also called up stores and ordered them to hold all remaining Microsoft Coffee boxes, as staff would come to collect them.

早上，我们很眩晕。但是，随着我们了解到微软PR在前一天实际翻了出来的恐惧，这种感觉很快就会变得恐惧。他们还召集了商店，并命令他们举行所有剩余的微软咖啡盒，因为工作人员将来收集它们。

By the afternoon, the legal department got involved. We started piecing together exactly what had happened. When reports first came in on the late afternoon of April Fools', BillG said, in effect, that the prank was not in good taste, and that it made Microsoft look stupid rather than clever - especially as a catch-up to Sun Microsystems. We learned he was repeatedly calling the prank “in poor judgement” in meeting and internal memos.

下午，法律部门参与其中。我们开始拼凑在一起发生了发生的事情。当报告首次进入4月下午的傻瓜＆＃39;比布尔格说，恶作剧并不好好味道，而且它使微软看起来愚蠢而不是聪明 - 特别是作为阳光的追赶微系统。我们了解到他在会议和内部备忘录中一再呼吁他的恶作剧“在判断不佳”。

The PR flacks, on their own, tried to clean up and bury the whole thing, out of fear that BillG might get really angry about it. (He never did. Nor did Legal. In the end, it was all a huge overreaction by PR.) PR sent out a targeted email warning about “very serious consequences” to those involved, and that was enough to shut me up. Especially since I had involved others, who had families.

本着自己的PR闪闪发作，试图清理并埋葬整件事，害怕比尔格可能会对此生气。 （他从来没有。特别是因为我涉及别人，谁有家庭。

But PR’s plan to suppress the story only partially worked, so it seemed fitting for me to share this story 25 years later, while there are still some vestiges of it around. There’s the KOMO News footage, which some of us still have on a dusty VHS tape. There are a handful of print articles from tech magazines at the time. And one of us has the audio recording of the radio coverage. But most companies, even media companies, didn’t have any web presence in 1996, and many magazines have no surviving print archives from before the late 90s.

但PR的计划只会部分工作，所以它似乎为25年后分享这个故事，而仍然存在一些遗迹。有了Komo新闻镜头，我们中的一些人还在尘土飞扬的VHS磁带上。当时有少数关于科技杂志的印刷品。我们之一具有无线电覆盖的音频记录。但大多数公司，甚至媒体公司，1996年没有任何网络存在，许多杂志没有从90年代后期之前幸存的印刷档案。

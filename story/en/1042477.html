When His Mother Died of Covid-19, This Artist Started Tracking Her Online Data
当他的母亲去世于Covid-19时，这位艺术家开始追踪她的在线数据

2020-12-30 05:58:01

Janine Soleil, a child of the ’60s and self-described hippie, died of COVID-19 on May 14 in Queens, New York. She was 75. Never embracing modern technology, she sent her best friend letters typed on a typewriter up until a few years ago. When she died in May, her son, artist Dylan Gauthier, embarked on a multimedia art project about her digital data legacy, searching out what even someone who rejected the internet had left behind in electronic databases.

Janine Soleil是60年代的孩子，自称为嬉皮，于5月14日在纽约皇后区死于COVID-19。她75岁。她从不拥抱现代技术，直到几年前才寄给她最好的朋友在打字机上打字的信。当她于五月去世时，她的儿子画家Dylan Gauthier着手进行一项有关她的数字数据遗产的多媒体艺术项目，以寻找甚至拒绝互联网的人在电子数据库中留下的东西。

Born in Brooklyn in 1945, Soleil studied ballet at UCLA and spent time in the Haight-Ashbury district in San Francisco, before becoming a social worker and family therapist in Chino, California, and later the nearby small town of Quincy, California. After divorcing Gauthier’s father, she changed her last name to Soleil — a French word she picked because she liked the sound.

Soleil于1945年出生于布鲁克林，曾在UCLA学习芭蕾舞，并在旧金山的Haight-Ashbury区度过了一段时间，之后成为加利福尼亚州奇诺市以及后来的加利福尼亚州昆西小镇的社会工作者和家庭治疗师。与高迪耶（Gauthier）的父亲离婚后，她将姓氏改成了Soleil（她选择了法语，是因为她喜欢这种声音）。

As a young woman in California in the late ’60s and ’70s, Soleil was involved in progressive activism for environmental and feminist causes. Her best friend, Lena Ostroff, met Soleil in 1969 when they both worked at a feminist organization called the Los Angeles Women’s Center. The two women began a friendship that spanned decades, with Soleil visiting Ostroff in various places where she lived around the world. “We immediately felt like kindred spirits.”

作为60年代和70年代后期在加利福尼亚州的一名年轻女子，Soleil参与了针对环境和女权主义事业的积极行动。她的最好的朋友莉娜·奥斯特罗夫（Lena Ostroff）于1969年与Soleil会面，当时他们俩都在一个名为洛杉矶妇女中心的女权组织工作。两位女士开始了长达数十年的友谊，Soleil在世界各地的不同地方拜访了Ostroff。 “我们立即感到自己是同志。”

Soleil was a few years older and introduced Ostroff to some of the new and trendy things in California — some as simple as brown rice, and others like the breastfeeding support group La Leche League after Ostroff’s son was born. “She had a great influence on me,” Ostroff told BuzzFeed News. “She was my shopping guru. We used to shop together and she’d tell me I was picking out ‘old lady clothes.’”

索雷尔（Soleil）年龄大了几岁，并将奥斯特洛夫（Ostroff）引入了加利福尼亚的一些新潮和新潮事物中-有些像糙米一样简单，还有其他诸如奥斯特洛夫（Ostroff）儿子出生后的母乳喂养支持组织La Leche League。 “她对我影响很大，”奥斯特罗夫（Ostroff）告诉BuzzFeed新闻。 “她是我的购物大师。我们曾经一起逛街，她告诉我，我在挑选“老太太的衣服”。

As a therapist and social worker, Soleil’s life’s work was spreading kindness and helping others. “She talked about the starfish theory: You see a starfish on the beach, and you pick up a starfish and look at it and say, ‘Are you OK, starfish? OK, I'm going to put you back in the ocean,’” Gauthier told BuzzFeed News. “This is what gave her meaning in life, helping people, hearing their stories, laughing with them.”

作为治疗师和社会工作者，Soleil的一生都在传播善良并帮助他人。 “她谈到了海星理论：您在海滩上看到一个海星，然后捡起一个海星，看着它，然后说，‘您好吗，海星？好的，我要把你放回大海，’”高迪耶对BuzzFeed新闻说。 “这就是她赋予生活意义，帮助人们，聆听他们的故事，与他们一起大笑的意义。”

Geoffrey Quinsey, her longtime friend and partner, met her through an online dating site in the late ’90s. Her profile jumped out to him. “She wasn’t your average person. At that time I wasn’t able to tell exactly in what way she wasn’t average,” he told BuzzFeed News. Quinsey, who worked in IT, was more practical-minded. “I was immediately concerned that this flower child was perhaps not suited for me.” He was wrong — the two found a love and friendship that lasted over 20 years. “We were always an odd mix. She was always a ‘can't screw in a lightbulb’ person. She wasn't interested in things, she was interested in people.” After Soleil was diagnosed with dementia in her early seventies, Quinsey and Ostroff helped her move from California to a memory care facility in Queens to be closer to her son and his family. Quinsey now has her dog, Gracie.

她的长期朋友和伴侣Geoffrey Quinsey在90年代后期通过在线约会网站认识了她。她的个人资料跳到他身上。 “她不是你的普通人。当时我无法确切地告诉她她不是平均水平。”他对BuzzFeed新闻说。从事IT工作的Quinsey更加注重实际。 “我立即担心这个花童可能不适合我。”他错了，两人之间的爱情和友谊持续了20多年。 “我们总是很奇怪。她一直是个“搞砸灯泡”的人。她对事物不感兴趣，对人不感兴趣。”在Soleil于70年代初被诊断出患有痴呆症之后，Quinsey和Ostroff帮助她从加利福尼亚搬到皇后区的记忆保健机构，以使其与儿子及其家人更近。昆西现在有她的狗格蕾丝。

After his mother’s death, Gauthier conceived of Delete Me When I’m Gone: An Ethical Framework for Drawing Down Our Online Data Bodies, an artistic research project and multimedia work about the data that is left behind when a person — even someone who rejected digital technology — dies. The project is funded by the digital arts organization Eyebeam, as part of their “Rapid Response” program in response to COVID-19.

母亲去世后，高蒂埃（Gauthier）构思了《当我消失时删除我：降低在线数据实体的道德框架》，一项艺术研究项目和多媒体工作，涉及一个人（甚至是拒绝数字的人）留下的数据技术-死亡。该项目由数字艺术组织Eyebeam资助，作为其针对COVID-19的“快速响应”计划的一部分。

Gauthier’s multimedia project that will track her data and eventually incorporate it into a sculptural work, possibly including some of her ashes. It will also have an app where people can find out information about what happens to their data after they die. There will also be a section for contacting local governments to request or advocate for laws and policies around user data privacy.

Gauthier的多媒体项目将跟踪她的数据，并最终将其整合到雕塑作品中，其中可能还包括一些骨灰。它还将有一个应用程序，人们可以在该应用程序中找到有关数据死亡后发生的情况的信息。也将有一个部分与当地政府联系，以请求或倡导有关用户数据隐私的法律和政策。

Social media platforms like Facebook — and soon, Twitter — have steps for memorializing an account, and people are even starting to make plans for naming digital executors to handle things like their email and social accounts after they die. But Gauthier pointed out that this is about the content you make — your emails or tweets, not the kind of passive data that is floating out there like purchase history or your ad targeting data. “Even while people are making those more conscious plans, there’s not a lot that can be done about the data that we don’t necessarily have access to and companies aggregate, multiply, sell, and create database entries on,” said Gauthier. “That, multiplied with the amount of death happening around COVID — and the acceleration of data around contact tracking and medical data — these are things that people dealing with loss and tragedy are not thinking about.”

像Facebook这样的社交媒体平台-以及不久之后的Twitter-都有纪念帐户的步骤，人们甚至开始计划命名数字执行者去世后处理其电子邮件和社交帐户之类的事情。但是Gauthier指出，这与您制作的内容有关-您的电子邮件或tweet，而不是诸如购买历史记录或广告定位数据之类的无源数据。 Gauthier表示：“即使人们制定了更加自觉的计划，但对于我们不一定需要访问的数据以及公司在其上汇总，增加，出售和创建数据库条目的数据，也无法做很多事情。” “这与在COVID周围发生的死亡数量相乘-以及围绕联系跟踪和医疗数据的数据加速-这些是处理损失和悲剧的人们所没有想到的事情。”

“Becoming an executor as someone’s afterlife affairs exposed me to how much someone like her would have a residual presence online and how much data about her existed out there,” he said. “It made me think about the things we are intentionally and planning to leave in the world versus the things we are not, and thinking about visualizing this as the tip of the iceberg.”

他说：“在某人的来世事务中成为一名执行人，使我了解到像她这样的人在网上会有多少残留的人，以及在那里存在多少关于她的数据。” “这让我思考了我们有意并打算在世界上离开的事物与我们不存在的事物，并考虑将其可视化为冰山一角。”

“My mom was this total hippie Luddite,” said Gauthier. “I kind of think of that Luddism as this specific mental state that goes against the grain — someone who’s mad when Bob Dylan picks up an electric guitar. My mom framed herself as someone who rejected technology.”

“我妈妈就是这个嬉皮士Luddite，” Gauthier说。 “我有点把Luddism看作是一种与精神背道而驰的特定心理状态-当Bob Dylan拿起电吉他时发疯的人。我妈妈把自己装扮成拒绝技术的人。”

But while she may have rejected it, she could not escape it. Soleil had a phone and she owned a house, she bought things with credit cards and shopped online, all things that leave a breadcrumb trail for data brokers. (These are companies that buy and sell your data, including from credit reporting bureaus like TransUnion, sites like Spokeo that sell information like your address or phone number, or anonymized ad targeting data through big firms like Oracle or Acxiom. It’s possible to opt out of these big data brokers, but it’s not easy.)

但是尽管她可能拒绝了它，但是她无法逃脱它。 Soleil拥有电话，拥有一所房屋，她用信用卡购买了商品并在网上购物，所有这些都为数据经纪人留下了痕迹。 （这些公司买卖您的数据，包括TransUnion之类的信用报告机构，Spokeo之类的网站出售您的地址或电话号码之类的信息，或者通过Oracle或Acxiom等大公司出售匿名广告定位数据。可以选择退出这些大数据经纪人，但这并不容易。）

“As a Jewish Buddist person, she didn’t want to leave a trace that would leave a footprint on the planet," said Gauthier. “But all this data that was collected about her that is being stored in data farms, being bought and sold.”

“作为犹太佛教徒，她不想留下会在地球上留下足迹的痕迹，＆＃34;高迪耶说。 “但是收集到的关于她的所有这些数据都存储在数据场中，可以买卖。”

Soleil is survived by her son, her daughter-in-law, Kendra Sullivan, and grandson Demetri, as well as her brother Barry Beiderman, extended family, and dear friends. According to her son, she requested a party with lots of dancing sometime in the future. A memorial was held over Zoom.

Soleil的儿子，her妇Kendra Sullivan和孙子Demetri以及哥哥Barry Beiderman，大家庭和亲爱的朋友得以幸存。据她的儿子说，她要求在将来的某个时候举行一场舞会。在Zoom举行了纪念活动。

Testing for Divisibility by 19
19分度测试

2020-11-23 18:51:56

A couple of nights ago I was keeping Katara company while she revisedan essay on The Scarlet Letter (ugh) and to pass the time one of thethings I did was tinker with the tests for divisibility rules by 9and 11. In the course of this I discovered the following method fordivisibility by 19:

几天前的两个晚上，我在卡塔拉（Katara）陪同她修订《红字》（ugh）的一篇论文时陪在他身边，为了打发时间，我所做的一件事情是修改了9和11的除数规则测试。在此过程中，我发现了下列方法可除以19：

76 is a multiple of 19, so 2337 was also. But if you're not sureabout 76 you can compute 7+2·6 = 19 and if you're not sure about thatyou need more help than I can provide.

76是19的倍数，所以2337也是如此。但是，如果不确定76，则可以计算7 + 2·6 = 19，如果不确定，您需要的帮助比我能提供的更多。

I don't claim this is especially practical, but it is fun, notcompletely unworkable, and I hadn't seen anything like it before.

我并不是说这特别实用，但是它很有趣，不是完全不可行，而且我之前从未见过类似的东西。

Last time I wrote about thisEric Roode sent me a whole compendium of divisibility tests, includingone for divisibility by 19. It's a little like mine, but in reverse:group the digits in pairs, left to right; multiply each pair by 5 andthen add the next pair. Here's 2337 again:

上次我写这本书的时候，埃里克·罗德（Eric Roode）向我发送了整本可除性测试纲要，其中包括一份19分频的测试。这有点像我的，但是相反：将每对乘以5，然后加上下一对。再次是2337：

You can save a lot trouble by reducing mod 19 before themultiplication. So instead of the first step being 23·5 + 37 you canreduce the 23·5 to 4·5 = 20 and then add the 37 to get 57 right away.

您可以通过在乘法之前减少mod 19来节省很多麻烦。因此，代替将第一步为23·5 + 37，您可以将23·5减少到4·5 = 20，然后将37加为57。

You can do something similar with my method also. For the exampleabove, after the first step you get to 17, which you can reduce mod 19to -2, and then the next step is -2·2+3 = -1, and the final step is-1·2+2 = 0.

您也可以使用我的方法执行类似的操作。对于上面的示例，第一步之后到达17，可以将mod 19减少为-2，然后下一步是-2·2 + 3 = -1，最后一步是-1·2 + 2 = 0。

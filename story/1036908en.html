I passed 2 AWS Solution Architect exams in a month
我一个月内通过了2项AWS Solution Architect考试

2020-11-28 03:14:28

First, a confession: I’ve been an ardent follower of all things Cloud for a long time now. I am especially impressed by the range of AWS services although according to some, GCP is a better designed cloud platform. Even if you think the number of AWS services is a hot mess of confusing interfaces and conflicting nomenclature, you gotta admit that the sheer scale and experience of AWS is incredulous.

首先，要坦白：很长一段时间以来，我一直是Cloud所有事物的热心追随者。尽管有些人认为GCP是一个设计更好的云平台，但我对AWS服务的范围尤其印象深刻。即使您认为AWS服务的数量是混乱的界面和冲突的命名法的一团糟，您也必须承认AWS的庞大规模和经验令人难以置信。

I got interested in certificat ion when one of my colleagues who passed the exam shared his Udemy course experience with me. I started watching the Udemy courses on a Friday and kept increasing the speed to 2X until I finished all the 200 videos by the time the weekend was over. Once I finished with the videos, I thought to myself, that wasn’t so bad and jumped onto the AWS certification website and registered myself for the “AWS Solution Architect Associate” exam in the next 2 days. Once I registered, I went back to 2 other courses my colleague shared with me that contained practice exams. I started taking the practice exams (total of 9 across both courses) repeatedly until I was scoring close to 100%. Every time I got a question wrong, I made notes on the “why” that helped me gather additional info on a particular topic and in my notes, I gathered the relevant AWS website link or whitepaper link and read it. This helped much more than the course videos themselves which act as an intro to the topic but the exam tests your comparative knowledge on a topic across similar AWS services. For example, here’s a question:

当我一位通过考试的同事与我分享他的Udemy课程经验时，我对证书产生了兴趣。我从星期五开始观看Udemy课程，并一直将速度提高到2倍，直到周末结束前我完成了所有200部视频。观看完视频后，我心想，那还不错，然后跳到AWS认证网站上，并在接下来的两天内为“ AWS解决方案架构师助理”考试注册了自己。注册后，我又回到了我的同事与我共享的其他2门课程，其中包括实践考试。我开始反复参加实践考试（两个课程共9个），直到我的分数接近100％。每次遇到错误的问题时，我都会针对“为什么”做笔记，以帮助我收集有关特定主题的更多信息，并在笔记中收集相关的AWS网站链接或白皮书链接并进行阅读。这比课程视频本身的帮助要大得多，课程视频本身是该主题的介绍，但是该考试测试了您在类似的AWS服务中对该主题的比较知识。例如，这是一个问题：

A company needs to store data for 5 years. The company will need to have immediate and highly available access to the data at any point in time, but will not require frequent access.

公司需要将数据存储5年。该公司将需要在任何时间点都具有对数据的即时且高度可用的访问权限，但不需要频繁访问。

c. Transition objects from Amazon S3 Standard to Amazon S3 One Zone-Infrequent Access (S3 One Zone-IA)

C。将对象从Amazon S3标准迁移到Amazon S3一区不频繁访问（S3 One Zone-IA）

To answer this question, you not only need to know about the various storage classes in S3 but subtle differences between them when compared to each other. (The correct answer in this case “A” if you are wondering.)

要回答这个问题，您不仅需要了解S3中的各种存储类别，而且还需要了解它们之间的细微差异。 （如果您想知道，在这种情况下的正确答案是“ A”。）

So, once I was able to master the practice tests and went over my ad-hoc notes, I was able to successfully pass the exam.

因此，一旦我能够掌握练习测试并仔细阅读了便笺，便可以顺利通过考试。

You can get them a steep discount on Udemy if you shop around (up to 80% off)

如果您货比三家，可以在Udemy上获得他们的大幅折扣（最高可享受80％的折扣）

Lastly, if you haven’t worked with AWS before, don’t forget to follow along and get your hands dirty with the lab exercises during the course.

最后，如果您以前从未使用过AWS，请别忘了继续学习，并在课程学习期间动手练习。

My early success with the associate exam prompted me to register for the Professional exam right away while I am in the “zone” as it were :) and I scheduled my exam to a date in the next 2 weeks. Once I started with the Professional course on Udemy, I got my first jolt. The instructor opens the course with a warning that this is not a cake walk and it’s one of the toughest exams out there. I was fazed a bit but then I picked myself up and breezed through the video series in 3 days (and nights). This time, I did more hands-on work to follow along while the videos were playing. Once I finished the courses, I took some of the practice exams which held my next jolt in reserve. I didn’t even score passing grades in any of the practice exams, keep in mind that I already registered for the exam. My confidence was shaken… I was starting to realize that I became the victim of Dunning-Kruger effect. As I went through the questions I got wrong, it challenged the very notion of what I learnt so far. In other words, I fell into the ‘valley of despair’.

我在联考中获得的早期成功促使我立即进入“区域”时就注册了专业考试：)，并且将考试安排在接下来的两周内进行。一旦开始学习有关Udemy的专业课程，我就感到很头疼。讲师在开课时会警告您，这不是走蛋糕的路，这是目前最艰巨的考试之一。我有点发呆，但随后我在3天（和晚上）内振作起来，欣赏了视频系列。这次，我在视频播放时做了很多动手工作。完成课程后，我参加了一些实践考试，这使我的下一次备受关注。我什至没有在任何实践考试中获得及格分数，请记住我已经报名参加考试。我的信心动摇了……我开始意识到自己成为了邓宁-克鲁格效应的受害者。当我经历错误的问题时，它挑战了我到目前为止所学的概念。换句话说，我陷入了“绝望之谷”。

But I haven’t given up on the exam because I already scheduled it, so I pressed on and kept taking the practice exams. I had access to only 10 practice exams from Udemy so I kept repeating them. But the stress was catching up to me as a physical manifestation. I had all the classic symptoms of stress: aches, ulcers, mood swings, restless foot, crazy appetites, and one day I had drinks with friends to relax and couldn’t even hold the liquor down.

但是我没有放弃考试，因为我已经安排好考试了，所以我继续参加并继续练习考试。我只能参加Udemy的10项实践考试，所以我不断重复这些考试。但是压力正逐渐成为我的身体表现。我有所有的典型压力症状：疼痛，溃疡，情绪波动，脚不安，食欲不振，有一天，我和朋友一起喝酒放松身心，甚至连酒都喝不下去。

Finally, the day of exam arrived and I started the day with palpable dread in my stomach that rising up my bile. I couldn’t force myself to eat any breakfast but managed to drink some coffee. As I went through first few of the exam questions, the remnants of my resolve shattered. I couldn’t remember anything so while I was scrambling to finish the exam, my mind was just shutting down to say let’s just get this over with. I didn’t have time to review all the answers but finished the exam and I … FAILED … I was devastated. My wife and daughter consoled me and for the next 2 days I was down and wanted to give up on the exam altogether. Then, something changed: I listened to a Podcast episode from 2019 called “WOOP… There It is! 4 Steps to Achieve Your Goals”. https://www.npr.org/2016/05/10/477379965/woop-there-it-is-four-steps-to-achieve-your-goals

终于，考试的日子到了，我的肚子开始有了明显的恐惧，使胆汁上升。我不能强迫自己吃早餐，但设法喝了些咖啡。当我经历了头几个考试题时，我决心的余下部分就破灭了。我什么也记不清了，所以当我争先恐后地完成考试时，我的脑子只是停下来说让我们结束这件事。我没有时间复习所有答案，但完成了考试，我…失败了……我被毁了。我的妻子和女儿安慰我，接下来的两天我很沮丧，想完全放弃考试。然后，情况发生了变化：我听了2019年播客播出的一集，名为“ WOOP…！实现目标的4个步骤”。 https://www.npr.org/2016/05/10/477379965/woop-there-it-is-four-steps-to-achieve-your-goals

I went for a long walk and did some self-examination. My obstacle was speed, I needed to slow down and consider my answers are wrong before confirming. I knew what I had to do. I went back to the Internet and searched up on people who failed/passed the Professional exam, listened to YouTube videos of people who passed the exam. I understood that the approach that worked for Associate won’t work for Professional and I set about to correct it. I started by signing up for A Cloud Guru course by Scott Pletcher that was highly recommended by everyone. Scott’s course is shorter than other courses but it had a practical appeal to it that’s geared towards real architecture and of course the exam itself. Along with the videos, Scott provides a plethora of whitepapers and video links on relevant topics. I gathered all the material and started with the practice tests given at the end of the course. This time, I started taking notes on each topic on the differences, limitations and other minutiae. Unlike the Associate, the Professional exam is very verbose sometime the questions and answers fill the entire screen. I realized I needed a new approach to take the exam:

我走了很长一段路，做了一些自我检查。我的障碍是速度，我需要放慢速度，并在确认之前认为答案是错误的。我知道我该怎么办。我回到了互联网上，搜寻未通过/通过专业考试的人，听过通过考试的人的YouTube视频。我了解到适用于Associate的方法不适用于Professional，因此我将对其进行纠正。我首先注册了Scott Pletcher的“ A Cloud Guru”课程，该课程得到了所有人的强烈推荐。斯科特的课程比其他课程短，但它对实际建筑和考试本身具有实际吸引力。除了视频外，Scott还提供了大量有关相关主题的白皮书和视频链接。我收集了所有材料，并从课程结束时进行的实践测试开始。这次，我开始就每个主题的差异，局限性和其他细节做笔记。与助理考试不同，专业考试有时非常冗长，问题和答案遍布整个屏幕。我意识到我需要一种新的方式来参加考试：

Try to see if this question is similar to something you encountered in a practice test

尝试查看此问题是否类似于您在实践测试中遇到的问题

Before submitting, play the devil’s advocate on why your answer might be wrong using the highlight keywords in the question

提交之前，请使用问题中的高亮关键字扮演魔鬼的拥护者，以解释您的答案为何可能是错误的

The difference between the Associate and Professional exam is that in the Associate exam, the test is checking your ‘what is…’ knowledge, but the professional exam goes beyond that, sometimes the answers are counter-intuitive. This image below succinctly captures the different intentions of Associate Vs Professional exams:

联考和专业考试之间的区别在于，联考中的考试是在检查您的“什么是……”知识，但是专业考试的内容不止于此，有时答案是违反直觉的。下面的这张图简要地捕捉了大学生与专业考试的不同意图：

A company currently uses Amazon EBS and Amazon RDS for storage purposes. The company intends to use a pilot light approach for disaster recovery in a different AWS Region. The company has an RTO of 6 hours and an RPO of 24 hours.

一家公司当前将Amazon EBS和Amazon RDS用于存储目的。该公司打算在不同的AWS区域中使用指示灯来进行灾难恢复。该公司的RTO为6小时，RPO为24小时。

a. Use AWS Lambda to create daily EBS and RDS snapshots, and copy them to the disaster recovery region. Use Amazon Route 53 with active-passive failover configuration. Use Amazon EC2 in an Auto Scaling group with the capacity set to 0 in the disaster recovery region.

一种。使用AWS Lambda创建每日EBS和RDS快照，并将其复制到灾难恢复区域。将Amazon Route 53与主动-被动故障转移配置一起使用。在灾难恢复区域的容量设置为0的Auto Scaling组中使用Amazon EC2。

b. Use AWS Lambda to create daily EBS and RDS snapshots, and copy them to the disaster recovery region. Use Amazon Route 53 with active-active failover configuration. Use Amazon EC2 in an Auto Scaling group configured in the same way as in the primary region.

b。使用AWS Lambda创建每日EBS和RDS快照，并将其复制到灾难恢复区域。将Amazon Route 53与主动-主动故障转移配置一起使用。在以与主要区域相同的方式配置的Auto Scaling组中使用Amazon EC2。

c.Use Amazon ECS to handle long-running tasks to create daily EBS and RDS snapshots, and copy to the disaster recovery region. Use Amazon Route 53 with active-passive failover configuration. Use Amazon EC2 in an Auto Scaling group with the capacity set to 0 in the disaster recovery region.

c。使用Amazon ECS处理长期运行的任务，以创建每日EBS和RDS快照，并将其复制到灾难恢复区域。将Amazon Route 53与主动-被动故障转移配置一起使用。在灾难恢复区域的容量设置为0的Auto Scaling组中使用Amazon EC2。

d. Use EBS and RDS cross-region snapshot copy capability to create snapshots in the disaster recovery region. Use Amazon Route 53 with active-active failover configuration. Use Amazon EC2 in an Auto Scaling group with the capacity set to 0 in the disaster recovery region.

d。使用EBS和RDS跨区域快照复制功能在灾难恢复区域中创建快照。将Amazon Route 53与主动-主动故障转移配置一起使用。在灾难恢复区域的容量设置为0的Auto Scaling组中使用Amazon EC2。

I gathered all the practice exams that I took so far (around 12 of them across Udemy and aCloudGuru and Internet) that totals to around 1200 questions (with some repetitions obviously). I encountered a great website called pegacert that offers around 560 practice questions and the best part is, unlike a practice exam which doesn’t give you feedback on your answer right away, Pegacert tells you Yes/No instantly. I liked that approach so much I set about writing a small program to load my practice questions into a database to be by instant advocate. My daughter walked in to my office and asked me what I was doing I explained to her what I was working on, she said “you don’t have to do that, I can help you.” She played my proctor on chat where she would paste a question into the chat and I would answer and she would give me yes/no, if I got a question wrong, she would pin the question and give me explanation.

我收集了到目前为止参加的所有实践考试（其中Udemy，aCloudGuru和Internet上大约进行了12项考试），总计约1200个问题（显然有些重复）。我遇到了一个很棒的网站，名为pegacert，提供大约560个练习题，而最好的部分是，不同于练习考试不会立即为您提供答案的反馈，Pegacert会立即告诉您是/否。我非常喜欢这种方法，因此着手编写一个小程序以将我的练习问题加载到数据库中，以备不时之需。我的女儿走进我的办公室，问我在做什么，我向她解释了我在做什么，她说：“您不必这样做，我可以帮助您。”她在聊天中扮演我的督导员，将问题粘贴到聊天中，然后我会回答，她会回答我/是。

AWS doesn’t let you reschedule an exam after you fail for 14 days. So, when the rescheduling date rolled in, I booked myself for another exam set in about 10 days. This time, I opted for the extra 30 minutes allowance (this is for people with ESL) Now, I redoubled all my efforts, I went through the videos, read the whitepapers, watched some AWS re:Invent videos. More than all this, practice exam questions with explanations were my salvation, however, I hit another snag. Across the practice exams, some questions were repeated but the answers/explanations were not matching. Now, I had to look at each of the verbose questions and tried to find the right answer: I found examtopics.com that lets you discuss real questions from AWS exams and discuss answers. This opened up another Pandora’s box because not one person there agrees with another on any answer and they all have valid whitepapers/arguments as to why. This sowed seeds of doubt in my mind and I started questioning my readiness again. In the end, I wanted to trust Pegacert’s answers and hang on to my sanity. With the help of my daughter and Pegacert, I rinsed and repeated the question/answer sessions for the next 2 weeks.

失败14天后，AWS不允许您重新安排考试时间。因此，当重新安排日期到来时，我为自己预定了大约10天的另一门考试。这次，我选择了30分钟的额外津贴（这适用于ESL人士）现在，我加倍努力，浏览了视频，阅读了白皮书，观看了一些AWS re：Invent视频。更重要的是，练习考试的问题和解释是我的救恩，但是，我遇到了另一个障碍。在实践考试中，重复了一些问题，但答案/解释不匹配。现在，我不得不查看每个冗长的问题，并试图找到正确的答案：我找到了examtopics.com，可让您讨论来自AWS考试的真实问题并讨论答案。这打开了另一个Pandora的盒子，因为那里没有人在任何答案上都同意另一个人，而且他们都有关于原因的有效白皮书/论据。这在我心中播下了怀疑的种子，我开始再次质疑自己的准备情况。最后，我想相信Pegacert的回答并坚持自己的理智。在我的女儿和Pegacert的帮助下，我漂洗了一下，并在接下来的2周中重复了问题/答案会议。

On the day of the exam, I woke up, forced myself to eat breakfast, do some meditation and did some practice exams. Before the exam, spent my time watching some comedy sit-coms (Brooklyn Nine Nine is a family favorite) to relax. I didn’t tell anyone that I am taking the exam for real, I told my wife that I am doing a simulated exam (;)) because I told myself whats the worst? (Recall WOOP) I will be exactly where I am right now albeit $300 poorer. So, the exam rolled on, and as I addressed the first few questions, I was much more relaxed and was able to keep the physical dread that’s rising in me in check. I took my time, marked the questions I wasn’t sure of and had time to revise all the questions before the clock ran out and as I clicked on ‘End Exam’ I told myself, nothing matters, and I …. PASSED!!! … The funny thing is, though there are similarities to practice questions, I couldn’t exactly match 90% of the actual questions to practice questions, nonetheless, without those practice exams, I wouldn’t be here.

考试那天，我醒来，强迫自己吃早餐，冥想并做一些练习考试。考试前，我花时间看了一些喜剧情景喜剧（布鲁克林九岁家庭喜剧），以放松身心。我没有告诉任何人我正在参加真正的考试，我告诉妻子我正在进行模拟考试（;），因为我告诉自己最糟糕的是什么？ （回忆一下WOOP）尽管我的收入降低了$ 300，但我现在仍将是我的所在地。因此，考试继续进行，当我回答前几个问题时，我变得更加放松，并且能够控制不断增加的身体恐惧感。我花了时间，标记了我不确定的问题，并有时间在时钟用完之前修改所有问题，当我单击“期末考试”时，我告诉自己，没有任何问题，然后……。通过！！！ …有趣的是，尽管练习题有相似之处，但我无法完全匹配90％的实际题来练习题，但是，如果没有这些练习考试，我就不会在这里。

Examtopics.com [The search function on this site is useless, you can actually reach the exact question/discussion page by searching with question text on Google and clicking on results from Examtopics)

Examtopics.com [此网站上的搜索功能无用，您实际上可以通过在Google上用问题文本进行搜索并单击Examtopics的结果来到达确切的问题/讨论页面）

So, it took me 1 month from no AWS certification to 2 AWS certifications. Not sure what you can learn from this, but I would be more than willing to discuss any exam topic with anyone if they need pointers on why an answer is right/wrong. Instead of asking me to share my notes or questions, I encourage you to copy/paste from the practice exams you self that will let you focus on areas you need to focus on rather than my topics.

因此，我花了1个月的时间从没有AWS认证到获得2个AWS认证。不知道您可以从中学到什么，但是如果他们需要答案正确与错误的指示，我将非常乐意与任何人讨论任何考试主题。我鼓励您从自己的练习考试中复制/粘贴，而不是让我分享我的笔记或问题，这将使您专注于需要重点关注的领域而不是我的主题。

PS: After 2 weeks of writing this, I passed another AWS certification: “AWS Certified Machine Learning Specialty” using the same methodology.

PS：撰写本文2周后，我通过了另一项AWS认证：使用相同方法的“ AWS认证机器学习专业”。

Data Science for Psychologists
心理学家的数据科学

2021-03-11 09:58:42

This book provides an introduction to data science that is tailored to the needs of psychologists, but is also suitable for students of the humanities and other biological or social sciences.This audience typically has a basic familiarity with statistics, but rarely an idea how data is prepared and shaped for being analyzed and tested.By working with a variety of data types and many examples, this text teaches tools for transforming, summarizing, and visualizing data. By keeping our eyes open for the perils of misleading representations, the book fosters fundamental skills of data literacy and cultivates reproducible research practices that enable and precede any practical use of statistics.

本书介绍了对心理学家的需求量身定制的数据科学介绍，但也适合人文学科和其他生物或社会科学的学生。本次观众通常具有与统计数据的基本熟悉，但很少是如何数据的准备和成型用于分析和测试。使用各种数据类型和许多示例，本文教导了用于转换，总结和可视化数据的工具。通过让我们的眼睛为误导性象征的危险，书籍促进了数据素养的基本技能，培养可重复的研究实践，使得能够并先于任何实际使用统计数据。

The materials in this book are based on a course at the University of Konstanz in 2020/2021.The course is targeted at advanced undergraduate students and provides an introduction to data science in R (R Core Team, 2020) from a tidyverse (Wickham, Averick, et al., 2019) perspective. Book and course are supported by the R package ds4psy (Neth, 2020), which provides datasets and functions used in the examples and exercises.

本书中的材料基于2020/2021年Konstanz大学的课程。该课程是针对先进本科生的目标，并从一个Tidyverse（Wickham， Averick等，et al。，2019）透视。 R包DS4PSY（Neth，2020）支持书籍和课程，它提供了在示例和练习中使用的数据集和功能。

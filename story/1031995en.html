2020年不错的刺杀

2020-10-29 21:20:32

Jump to navigation Jump to search On 29 October 2020, three people were killed in a stabbing attack at Notre-Dame de Nice, a Roman Catholic basilica, in Nice, France. One of the victims, a woman, was beheaded by the attacker. [1] Several additional victims were injured. The attacker, who was shot by the police, was taken into custody. [2]

跳转到导航跳跃搜索2020年10月29日，在法国尼斯的一座罗马天主教大教堂尼斯圣母院发生刺杀事件，造成3人死亡。其中一名受害者是一名妇女，被袭击者斩首。[1]又有几名受害者受伤。被警方击毙的袭击者已被拘留。[2]。

The Mayor of Nice said this was a "terrorist attack", attributing it to Islamic radicalism. [3] [4] According to the mayor, the attacker continued shouting " Allahu Akbar" after he was arrested. [2]

尼斯市长称这是一起恐怖袭击，并将其归因于伊斯兰激进主义。[3][4]根据市长的说法，袭击者在被捕后继续高喊真主至大。[2]。

In recent years, France has seen several jihadist terrorist attacks, carried out by both Islamic State and al-Qaeda terrorist cells, and by lone wolf terrorists inspired by the two groups. Nice was the scene of a truck attack in 2016, which resulted in the deaths of 86 people; the attack occurred about 0.8 kilometres (0.50 mi) away from the scene of the 2020 attack. Days after that, a priest, Jacques Hamel, was murdered in an attack on a church at Normandy. [3] [5]

近年来，法国发生了几起圣战恐怖袭击事件，既有伊斯兰国和基地组织恐怖组织实施的袭击，也有受这两个组织启发的独狼恐怖分子发动的袭击。尼斯是2016年卡车袭击的现场，那次袭击导致86人死亡；袭击发生在距离2020年袭击现场约0.8公里(0.50英里)的地方。几天后，牧师雅克·哈默尔(Jacques Hamel)在诺曼底教堂遇袭身亡。[3][5]。

Two weeks prior this incident, Samuel Paty, a history teacher, was murdered and beheaded by a Chechen Muslim after showing to his students Charlie Hebdo cartoons depicting the Islamic prophet Muhammad. [6] Following the events, Turkish President Recep Tayyip Erdoğan called for a boycott of French products. Several protests across the Muslim world followed, in which photos of French President Emmanuel Macron were burned, chanting against France. [7]

事件发生前两周，历史教师塞缪尔·帕蒂在向学生展示描绘伊斯兰先知穆罕默德的“查理周刊”漫画后，被一名车臣穆斯林谋杀并斩首。[6]事件发生后，土耳其总统雷杰普·塔伊普·埃尔多安(Recep Tayyip ErdoğAn)呼吁抵制法国产品。随之而来的是穆斯林世界各地的几场抗议活动，法国总统伊曼纽尔·马克龙的照片被焚烧，高呼反对法国的口号。[7]。

According to Nice Mayor Christian Estrosi, the attack occurred in or around the premises of Notre-Dame de Nice, a Roman Catholic basilica, situated on the Avenue Jean Médecin in the centre of Nice. The attacker reportedly shouted "Allahu Akbar!" repeatedly, even after he was shot and detained by responding police. According to police sources, two women and one man were killed, with one of the women being decapitated. Estrosi said that two of the victims died inside the church, while the third died at a nearby bar after escaping from the church with serious injuries; one of the victims was the church warden. [1] [2] [8]

据尼斯市长克里斯蒂安·埃斯特罗西称，袭击发生在尼斯市中心Jean Médecin大道上的罗马天主教大教堂尼斯圣母院内或周围。据报道，袭击者反复高喊“真主至大”，甚至在他被回应的警察开枪并拘留之后也是如此。据警方消息人士称，两名女子和一名男子被杀，其中一名女子被斩首。埃斯特罗西说，其中两名受害者在教堂内死亡，第三名受重伤逃离教堂后在附近的酒吧死亡；其中一名受害者是教堂典狱长。[1][2][8]。

Interior Minister Gerald Darmanin said a police operation was underway in the city. [2] A bomb disposal unit responded to the crime scene, while heavily armed anti-terror police officers began patrolling the streets around the cathedral. [9] Within hours of the Nice attack, a man was shot and killed by police near Avignon after he threatened passersby with a handgun and shouted, "Allahu Akbar!" In addition, a security guard was injured in a knife attack at the French consulate in Saudi Arabia that same day. [1]

内政部长杰拉尔德·达尔马宁(Gerald Darmanin)表示，警方正在该市采取行动。[2]一支拆弹小分队赶到犯罪现场，全副武装的反恐警察开始在大教堂周围的街道上巡逻。[9]尼斯袭击事件发生后的几个小时内，一名男子在阿维尼翁附近被警方开枪打死，他用手枪威胁路人，并高喊：真主至大！此外，同一天，一名保安在法国驻沙特阿拉伯领事馆的持刀袭击中受伤。[1]。

Three people were killed in the attack: they were identified as a woman in her 70s, who was beheaded; a 45-year-old man who served as the church warden; and a woman in her 30s who died at a nearby bar after escaping from the church with serious injuries. [10]

三人在袭击中丧生：他们被确认为一名70多岁的女子，她被斩首；一名45岁的男子担任教堂典狱长；一名30多岁的女子因重伤逃离教堂后在附近的酒吧死亡。[10]。

A moment of silence was held at the National Assembly after news of the stabbing arrived. [3] The government confirmed that President Emmanuel Macron would visit the site of the attack later in the morning. [11]

刺杀消息传来后，国民议会举行了默哀仪式。[3]政府证实，总统伊曼纽尔·马克龙将在上午晚些时候视察袭击现场。[11]

European Parliament President David Sassoli, Spanish Prime Minister Pedro Sánchez, and Italian Prime Minister Giuseppe Conte all released statements expressing their condolences to the French people. [12] Just hours after the incident occurred, former Prime Minister of Malaysia Mahathir Mohamad said that Muslims “have the right to punish” the French. [13] Turkish foreign ministry released a statement expressing their condolences to the French people and stating that “no reason could legitimise or excuse killing someone or violence. Those who conducted this savage attack at a sacred place of worship do not clearly share any religious, humane or moral values." [14]

欧洲议会议长大卫·萨索利、西班牙首相佩德罗·桑切斯和意大利总理朱塞佩·孔戴都发表声明，向法国人民表示哀悼。[12]事件发生几个小时后，马来西亚前总理马哈蒂尔·穆罕默德(Mahathir Mohamad)表示，穆斯林“有权惩罚”法国人。[13]土耳其外交部发表声明，向法国人民表示哀悼，并表示“任何理由都不能使杀人或暴力合法化或开脱。那些在神圣的礼拜场所实施这场野蛮袭击的人显然没有任何宗教、人道或道德价值观。

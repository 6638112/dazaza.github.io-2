What color was “Apple Beige”?
“ Apple Beige”是什么颜色？

2021-01-28 22:16:34

I went looking for a defining color of its era. I found it in an old jar of paint.

我去寻找那个时代的标志性色彩。我在一个旧油漆罐中发现它。

Apple’s second computer — its first to have a case — launched in 1977, and that b oxy beige Apple II was soon everywhere: in classrooms, living rooms and offices. At the vanguard of a generation of personal computers to come, it featured a particular and carefully-chosen beige. But what did that look like? Those first machines — the ones that have escaped landfills anyway — have shifted in color over 40 years. The documented public record is sketchy and confused. But I stumbled upon a way to investigate what Apple Beige was like.

苹果电脑的第二台计算机（第一台装有保护套的计算机）于1977年问世，那令人震惊的米色Apple II随处可见：教室，客厅和办公室。在即将到来的一代个人计算机的先锋中，它采用了经过精心挑选的特殊米色。但是那是什么样的呢？最初的机器-还是逃脱了垃圾填埋场的机器-过去40年来颜色发生了变化。记录在案的公共记录是粗略而混乱的。但是我偶然发现了一种研究Apple Beige的方式。

I came by this relic, a remarkable artifact of the fledgling home computer era. It’s a small glass jar filled with an oil-based paint — there’s a little brush built into the cap, and the monochromatic label glued to it sports the old Apple Computer Inc logotype and the title “Apple Beige Touch-Up Paint”.

我来自这个遗物，这是刚刚起步的家用计算机时代的杰出产物。这是一个装有油性涂料的小玻璃罐-盖子上装有小刷子，粘在上面的单色标签带有旧的Apple Computer Inc徽标和标题“ Apple Beige Touch-Up Paint”。

This is the green-inflected cool beige of the original Apple II computer cases. Unlike later models, these first Apple cases were manufactured with a sprayed-on paint finish. Scrapes and other abuse could damage the surface. Hence the need for a touch-up paint, a handy item apparently available only to dealers for use on their repair benches.

这是原始Apple II电脑机壳的绿色变形米色。与以后的型号不同，这些最初的Apple外壳采用喷涂的油漆表面制造。刮伤和其他滥用可能会损坏表面。因此，需要一种补漆漆，一种方便使用的物品，显然只有经销商才能在维修台上使用。

It stands to reason this still-fresh paint may be the closest you can get today to that original color. I made a couple “swatches”, a layer on coated paper (which worked well) and two coats on a primered wood shim (worked less well). And I compared the swatches to a surviving Apple II and to other known color references.

可以断定，这种仍然新鲜的油漆可能是您今天可以最接近原始颜色的油漆。我做了几个“色板”，在铜版纸上铺了一层（效果很好），在底漆的木垫片上做了两层涂层（效果不好）。我将色板与尚存的Apple II和其他已知的颜色参考进行了比较。

Sort of! But it’s a bit unreliable. All paints shift color with exposure to light over time, and this is true of the painted case machines (Apple II, II+, early IIe). Interestingly, these early finishes have aged better than later ones. Apple was small and running so lean in those years that retooling for higher quality ABS injection molding (using integrally-dyed plastic) didn’t become a priority until about 1984. The dyed plastic computers are today mostly discolored to nauseating yellow-browns. (That includes all of the early Macs, too.)

有点！但这有点不可靠。随着时间的流逝，所有涂料都会随着时间的推移而改变颜色，这对于涂漆机壳机器（Apple II，II +，IIe早期）是正确的。有趣的是，这些早期涂料的老化效果要好于后来的涂料。苹果公司当时很小，而且运行状况非常苗条，以至于直到1984年左右，才开始着手为更高质量的ABS注塑成型（使用整体染色塑料）进行重新设计。如今，染色塑料计算机的颜色大多会变黄，令人讨厌。 （这也包括所有早期的Mac。）

I checked my homemade swatch of Apple Beige against the case of a reasonably well-preserved Apple II+ (circa 1980). It depends on the light you’re seeing it under, and although it’s very close, the old paint appears to be just a bit more yellow than the fresh swatch.

我检查了自制的Apple Beige色板是否与保存完好的Apple II +（约1980年）的情况相对。这取决于您在下面看到的光线，尽管它很近，但旧油漆似乎比新色板略带黄色。

This seems to confirm that the touch-up paint is the right original color, and also confirms that existing early Apple II computers don’t today look terribly different to their original finish. So what color is it?

这似乎证实了补漆是正确的原始颜色，也证实了现有的早期Apple II计算机在外观上与原始外观没有太大不同。那是什么颜色？

Jerry Manock was the industrial designer of the Apple II as well as later machines including the original Macs. Manock recalled in 2012 that the color he specified for the Apple II case was “Pantone 453”. Pantone is the industry-standard color matching system originally catering to print designers and more recently textiles and various other domains. This 2012 Manock factoid has been repeated elsewhere on the web. Case closed, right?

杰里·曼诺克（Jerry Manock）是Apple II以及后来的机器（包括原始Mac）的工业设计师。马诺克在2012年回忆说，他为Apple II外壳指定的颜色是“ Pantone 453”。 Pantone是行业标准的色彩匹配系统，最初是为印刷设计师以及最近的纺织品和其他各个领域提供的。这个2012年的Manock事实已在网络上的其他地方重复出现。结案了吧？

But Manock’s projects turn out to be tricky to pin down. In Paul Kunkel’s encyclopedic 1997 book “AppleDesign: The Work of the Apple Industrial Design Group,” Manock is said — in referring to the Macintosh — to have chosen “a tawny brown color known as [Pantone] 453, which he thought would age more gracefully than any other.”

但事实证明，马诺克的项目很难确定。在保罗·昆克尔（Paul Kunkel）1997年的百科全书“ AppleDesign：Apple工业设计小组的工作”中，Manock表示（在提到Macintosh时）选择了“黄褐色，称为[Pantone] 453，他认为这种颜色会老化很多”非常优雅。”

There is no detail in the book on the specific color of the much earlier Apple II. But presumably if the chosen finish had been identical, that deliberate continuity would have been indicated. Manock’s Macintosh work was taking place years after the Apple II was already in the marketplace.

本书没有详细介绍更早的Apple II的特定颜色。但是，如果所选的饰面是相同的，则可能会表明有意的连续性。在Apple II投放市场多年之后，Manock的Macintosh工作就开始了。

Apple’s first CEO, Michael Scott (true!), actually used the Apple II beige as an illustration of Steve Jobs’ notorious fussiness. Here he is in a 2011 interview:

苹果公司的第一任首席执行官迈克尔·斯科特（Michael Scott，是对的）实际上是使用Apple II米色来说明乔布斯臭名昭著的挑剔。他在2011年接受采访时：

…for all the standard colors of beige available in the world, of which there are thousands, none was exactly proper for [Steve Jobs]. So we actually had to create “Apple beige” and get that registered.

…对于世界上所有可用的米色标准颜色，有成千上万种，没有一种颜色适合[史蒂夫·乔布斯（Steve Jobs）]。因此，我们实际上必须创建“ Apple米色”并进行注册。

Yet Scott is also quoted in Walter Isaacson’s 2011 authorized biography of Jobs as saying

不过，沃尔特·艾萨克森（Walter Isaacson）在2011年的乔布斯传记中也引用了斯科特的话：

The Pantone company, which Apple used to specify colors for its plastic, had more than two thousand shades of beige. “None of them were good enough for Steve. […] He wanted to create a different shade, and I had to stop him.”

Pantone公司是苹果公司用来为其塑料指定颜色的公司，拥有2000多种米色。 “没有一个人对史蒂夫足够好。 […]他想创造一种不同的阴影，我不得不阻止他。”

There weren’t actually thousands of beiges; the 1970s Pantone color set contained fewer than 1,000 colors total, and as a friend explained to me, print designers don’t have a frequent need for beige, so those shades aren’t especially well represented.

实际上没有成千上万的米色； 1970年代的Pantone色彩组合总共包含不到1000种色彩，而且正如一位朋友向我解释的那样，印刷设计师对米色的需求并不高，因此这些阴影不能很好地表现出来。

That aside, Scott’s evidence seems mixed here. The biography seems to say that he forced Jobs to pick a Pantone (?) color, but the interview clearly says the case paint was a bespoke creation, signed off on by Jobs personally.

除此之外，斯科特的证据在这里似乎参差不齐。传记似乎说他强迫乔布斯选择Pantone（？）颜色，但采访中清楚地表明，外壳涂料是定制产品，由乔布斯亲自签署。

Here is a binder full of Pantone color chips from 1972, representing the extent of that color universe in the era. Each shade has a sequential number, and the highest values were in the 800s. There are two full sets of tear-away chips in this book, one on uncoated paper stock and one on coated paper stock.

这是装满1972年Pantone彩色芯片的活页夹，代表了当时那个色彩世界的程度。每个阴影都有一个序列号，最大值在800年代。本书中有两套完整的可撕开芯片，一套在未涂布纸浆上，一套在涂布纸浆上。

The coated vs. uncoated distinction remains today, and these original three-digit numbered colors are now designated with a “C” or a “U” suffix respectively. Since the 1970s, Pantone’s color selection offerings have proliferated, going beyond the print domain into textiles and paints, and adding many many more additional shades in between the older colors.

涂层与未涂层之间的区别至今仍然存在，这些原始的三位数编号的颜色现在分别以“ C”或“ U”后缀指定。自1970年代以来，Pantone的颜色选择产品激增，从印刷领域扩展到纺织品和油漆领域，并在旧颜色之间增加了许多其他色调。

No luck. This photo’s color levels make the comparison swatch look darker than in real life, but even in real life, the Apple Beige is darker and more green than 453 or anything else in this book. (If anything, shade 414 is closer.)

没有运气。这张照片的色彩水平使比较色板看起来比实际生活中的要暗，但是即使在现实生活中，Apple Beige也比453或本书中的其他任何东西都更暗，更绿色。 （如果有的话，阴影414更近。）

If you’re a design professional, I can now hear you screaming at me: you’re supposed to replace your Pantone chips every year or two — these things are a half-century old and can’t be trusted! Of course, that is correct. Here is a current example, photographed in natural light:

如果您是设计专业人士，我现在可以听到您大喊大叫：您应该每年或每两年更换一次Pantone芯片-这些东西已经有半个世纪的历史了，值得信赖！当然，这是正确的。这是在自然光下拍摄的当前示例：

Same problem. The 1972 book’s colors have gone a tiny bit off now, but the comparison is basically the same. It’s just not a close match. Note that in all these comparisons, the ambient lighting can make an enormous difference to absolute perception, and also in corresponding photos. You’ll have to take my word for the differences!

同样的问题。 1972年这本书的颜色现在略有下降，但是对比基本上是相同的。这不是一场紧密的比赛。请注意，在所有这些比较中，环境照明都会对绝对感知以及相应的照片产生巨大的影响。您必须对差异做出我的承诺！

So the Apple II was something other than Pantone 453. It seems clear that color was actually Manock’s reference for the later Macintosh case. Now even if a surviving Apple II case is very close to the fresh paint color, we still don’t know what color that is.

因此，Apple II不同于Pantone453。显然，颜色实际上是Manock在后来的Macintosh机壳中的参考。现在，即使幸存的Apple II外壳与新漆的颜色非常接近，我们仍然不知道它是什么颜色。

Fortunately the Pantone universe has expanded significantly since 1977. The print beiges still don’t have a very close match to Apple Beige, but it turns out, the textile/interiors color set does, and it is called “Overcast”:

幸运的是，自1977年以来，Pantone的领域有了很大的发展。米色的印刷品与Apple Beige的匹配程度仍然不高，但事实证明，纺织品/室内装潢的颜色却是如此，被称为“阴暗”：

A.k.a Pantone 14–0105 TPG. This is the best match across all of the contemporary fan book sets that my designer friends had at hand. It is not quiiiiiite indistinguishable, but it is verrrry close, with the greenishness that 453 lacks. If you are looking for a current Pantone lacquer color that matched the Apple II was when it was new in 1977, I’d tell you to ask for Overcast 14–0105 TPG.

也就是Pantone 14-0105 TPG。这是我的设计师朋友们手握的所有当代风扇书集中的最佳搭配。它不是不可区别的，但与453缺少的绿色相接近。如果您要寻找与Apple II于1977年新上市时相匹配的当前Pantone亮漆颜色，我会告诉您索要Overcast 14-0105 TPG。

I brought my little swatches down to the local hardware store, since they sell paint and have a selection of color samples there. The closest house paint they carry is PPG’s “ Heavy Hammock.” They sell Rust-Oleum spray enamel, and that has a near match with “ Gloss Almond.”

我将小样本带到当地的五金商店，因为它们出售油漆，并且在那里有各种颜色样本。他们携带的最接近的房屋涂料是PPG的“重型吊床”。他们出售Rust-Oleum喷涂瓷釉，与“ Gloss Almond”几乎匹配。

So if you want your house to look like an Apple II, ask for Heavy Hammock but maybe just a wee hair lighter toward Skipping Stone. For your deck furniture, go with the spray Gloss Almond. (You’re on your own for automotive.)

因此，如果您想让房屋看起来像Apple II，则可以购买沉重的吊床，但也可以将轻打火机吹向“跳过石”。对于您的甲板家具，请搭配喷涂“光泽杏仁”。 （您将独自驾驶汽车。）

好问题！我的Apple II +外壳上有一些很深的刮痕，可通过油漆表面上的凿子露出下面的塑料。我想我最好还是用补漆来补漆。结果如下：

It’s a pretty good match to the current color of the case, but you can see the patching if you look closely: the new dabs appear a hair lighter and more neutral in tone than the surrounding paint.

可以很好地匹配表壳的当前颜色，但是如果仔细观察，您会看到斑驳的痕迹：新的轻拍比周围的油漆看上去更轻盈，色调更中性。

I hope that deep in the bowels of Apple’s industrial design group archives, there exists an old sheet of paper with the color mix recipe for Apple Beige written down on it. Colors imprint in our collective memory and have the power to reflect cultural meaning. This beige ushered in personal computing, which eventually helped change the world.

我希望苹果公司工业设计小组档案的深处有一张旧纸，上面写着Apple Beige的配色配方。颜色印在我们的集体记忆中，并具有反映文化含义的能力。这种米色引入了个人计算机，最终帮助改变了世界。

UPDATE 1/27/21: I was able to contact Jerry Manock (!) and run this story by him. He is quite sure that Pantone 453 was the nominal reference color for the Apple II, but seems open to the idea that it may not have ended up exactly like that:

更新1/27/21：我能够联系杰里·马诺克（！），由他来讲述这个故事。他非常确定Pantone 453是Apple II的标称参考颜色，但似乎对这样的想法可能持怀疑态度：

The first Apple II’s were painted with a textured paint from a vendor (don’t remember the company… maybe Sherwin Williams) that were given a Pantone 453 reference. God knows how this paint varied from batch to batch… we didn’t have much QC at the time.

最初的Apple II涂有卖方（不记得该公司，也许是Sherwin Williams）的质感涂料，该涂料被赋予Pantone 453参考。上帝知道这种油漆的批次之间是如何变化的……我们当时没有太多的质量控制。

I have some follow up questions in to him to understand how this was (or wasn’t) related to the first Macintosh color, to try to create a through-line of accuracy in the record here. I’ll update this post more thoroughly once I understand the larger picture. In the meantime, it does seem plausible that the very original Apple Beige ended up being something more bespoke than even its designed intent.

我对他有一些后续问题，以了解这与（或不）与第一种Macintosh色彩有何关联，以便尝试在此处创建一条准确的记录。了解大图后，我将更彻底地更新此帖子。同时，看起来很原始的Apple Beige似乎比其设计意图更具定制性。

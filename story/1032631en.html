石刻自拍：900年前在西班牙发现的梅森隐藏的肖像画

2020-11-01 22:39:56

A British art historian’s painstaking study of the cathedral in Santiago de Compostela uncovered a medieval prank

一位英国艺术历史学家对圣地亚哥·德·孔波斯特拉的大教堂进行了艰苦的研究，发现了一个中世纪的恶作剧。

H e is a medieval in-joke, a male figure carved in the early 12th century for one of the world’s greatest cathedrals, but no one has known of his existence until now. The figure has gone unnoticed by millions of worshippers who have made the long pilgrimage to Santiago de Compostela in Galicia, north-western Spain over the centuries. He has looked down on them from the top of one of the many pillars that soar upwards, each decorated with carved foliage, among which he is concealed.

他是中世纪的笑柄，12世纪初为世界上最伟大的大教堂之一雕刻的男性形象，但直到现在还没有人知道他的存在。几个世纪以来，数百万朝圣者长途跋涉前往西班牙西北部加利西亚的圣地亚哥·德·孔波斯特拉(Santiago De Compostela)朝圣，但这个数字并没有引起数百万朝拜者的注意。他从高高耸立的众多柱子中的一根柱子的顶部俯视着它们，每根柱子上都装饰着雕刻的树叶，他就藏在其中。

Now he has been discovered by a British art scholar who believes that he was actually never meant to be seen because he is a self-portrait of a stonemason who worked on the cathedral in the 12th century.

现在，一位英国艺术学者发现了他，这位学者认为，他实际上从来就不是要被人看到的，因为他是一位12世纪在大教堂工作的石匠的自画像。

“You find this in medieval buildings,” Dr Jennifer Alexander told the Observer. “They’re usually in dark corners where only another stonemason would find them. This one is in a bit of the building where you’d have to be a stonemason to be up there to see it. It’s tucked away in among a whole set of capitals [the top of a column] that are otherwise plain.

詹妮弗·亚历山大博士告诉“观察家报”：“你可以在中世纪的建筑中发现这一点。”“它们通常在黑暗的角落里，只有另一个石匠才能找到它们。这个是在大楼的一小块地方，你必须是一个石匠才能在那里看到它。这是一座建筑的一小部分，你必须是一个石匠才能在那里看到它。它被藏在一整组大写字母(柱子的顶部)之间，否则它们就很普通了。

“It’s just such a charming connection between us and the person that carved it. It’s almost as if it was designed just for us to see it by those people working on the building. Of course, this stonemason probably had no idea that he’d have to wait so long to be spotted.”

“这是我们和雕刻者之间如此迷人的联系。它几乎就像是专门设计给我们看的，是那些在大楼里工作的人设计的。当然，这位石匠可能不知道他要等这么久才能被发现。“。

Despite the supreme talent of such craftsmen, they were completely anonymous, their names lost to history. This is the closest the mason got to signing his work.

尽管这些工匠才华横溢，但他们完全默默无闻，他们的名字消失在历史中。这是泥瓦匠离签名最近的一次了。

Alexander, a reader in art history at the University of Warwick, is a specialist in the architectural history of the great churches and cathedrals of the medieval period.

亚历山大是华威大学艺术史的读书人，是研究中世纪大教堂和大教堂建筑史的专家。

She discovered the figure in conducting an intricate survey of Santiago de Compostela cathedral, a Unesco world heritage site.

她在对联合国教科文组织世界遗产圣地亚哥·德·孔波斯特拉大教堂进行复杂的调查时发现了这个数字。

Its significance lies in its association with the Camino, the pilgrimage across hundreds of miles to reach the shrine of the apostle St James, which is within the cathedral. Such is its draw that. in 2019 alone, the official number of pilgrims reaching the city was some 350,000.

它的意义在于它与卡米诺的联系，卡米诺是穿越数百英里到达大教堂内的使徒圣詹姆斯神殿的朝圣之旅。这就是它的吸引力所在。仅在2019年，到达该市的官方朝圣者人数就约为35万人。

The current building was begun in the late 11th-century and is a masterpiece of Romanesque architecture, with later additions.

目前的建筑始建于11世纪末，是罗马式建筑的杰作，后来又增加了一些。

Alexander was conducting a stone-by-stone analysis to work out its construction sequence, in a project funded by the Galician regional government. It was when she was studying the capitals, about 13 metres above the pavement, that “this little figure popped out”, she recalled.

亚历山大正在进行一块石头的分析，以确定其建设顺序，这是由加利西亚地区政府资助的一个项目。她回忆说，正是当她研究人行道上方约13米处的首都时，“这个小身影突然冒了出来”。

“A lovely image of a chap hanging on to the middle of the capital as if his life depended on it. It’s in a row of identical off-the-peg capitals where they’ve been knocking them out in granite – ‘we need another 15 of that design’ – and suddenly there’s one that’s different. So we think it’s the man himself.

“一个可爱的小伙子紧抓着首都的中心，仿佛他的生命依赖于此。它位于一排完全相同的现成首都中，他们一直在用花岗岩将它们击倒-“我们还需要15个那种设计”-突然之间就有了一个不同的。所以我们认为是这个人自己干的。

“He emerges out of the capital and is clinging to it. It’s almost as if it’s swallowing him up.”

“他走出首都，紧紧抓住它不放。这几乎就像是在吞噬他。“。

The carved figure, which is about 30cm high, is depicted down to his waist. Alexander said: “He’s got a nice little smile. He’s pleased with himself. He’s splendidly carved, with a strongly characterised face.”

这个高约30厘米的雕像被描绘到他的腰部。亚历山大说：“他有一个可爱的小微笑。他对自己很满意。他雕刻精美，有一张很有个性的脸。“。

Medieval stonemasons learned their craft through apprenticeships that trained them in the back-breaking trade of cutting stone or using templates to create complex mouldings around doorways and other openings. The most promising masons would learn geometry so that they could design plans and manage construction sites.

中世纪的石匠通过学徒的方式学会了他们的技艺，这些学徒训练他们从事切割石头或使用模板在门口和其他开口周围制作复杂的模子的艰苦行业。最有前途的泥瓦匠会学习几何，这样他们就可以设计平面图和管理建筑工地。

Alexander said: “These masters had to have many skills since they were also responsible for engineering, supply of materials, hiring the workforce and dealing with the patron, who might be a senior member of the clergy or the nobility.”

亚历山大说：“这些大师必须有很多技能，因为他们还负责工程、材料供应、雇佣劳动力和与赞助人打交道，赞助人可能是高级神职人员或贵族。”

She added that such craftsmen have remained anonymous throughout the centuries even into more recent times: “When they were building Liverpool cathedral in the 20th century, they published lists of the craftsmen that worked on the building and they never mentioned stonemasons. So these are the unsung geniuses.”

她补充说，这样的工匠在几个世纪里一直保持匿名，甚至到最近也是如此：“当他们在20世纪建造利物浦大教堂时，他们公布了在这座建筑上工作的工匠的名单，他们从来没有提到过石匠。所以这些就是无名之辈了。“

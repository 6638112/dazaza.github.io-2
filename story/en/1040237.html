Study: VR seems to help prime people to have lucid dreams
研究：虚拟现实似乎可以帮助人们实现清晰的梦想

2020-12-17 09:18:32

Fascinating study from researchers with the Donders Sleep & Memory Lab  in the the Netherlands suggests that using VR helps prime the user to have more lucid dreams -- you know, the sensation of being conscious while having a dream, and having some control over what happens in the dream itself. Using VR inherently encourages the user to keep questioning whether the things they are seeing in front of them are real or not. And that constant Cartesian awareness, the researchers theorized, can bleed over into dreams.

Donders Sleep＆amp; amp; amp;的研究人员的有趣研究。荷兰的Memory Lab建议，使用VR可以使用户有更多清醒的梦-您知道做梦时有意识，并可以控制梦中发生的事情。从本质上讲，使用VR会鼓励用户继续质疑他们眼前的事物是否真实。研究人员从理论上讲，对笛卡尔的不断认识可以流向梦想。

To test this, they put volunteers in a VR rig and had them play a number of dream-like VR games. Then, they put the users in a realistic "normal" cafeteria setting in VR (above)... but then made that simulation become increasingly strange and dreamlike:

为了测试这一点，他们将志愿者放置在VR设备中，并让他们玩了许多梦dream以求的VR游戏。然后，他们将用户置于现实的＆＃34; normal＆＃34; VR中的自助餐厅设置（如上所述）...但是随后，模拟变得越来越奇怪和如梦似幻：

Every early session (first two weeks) included the custom-made ‘Spinoza Café' scenario which involved performing the role of a waiter or waitress—delivering food to customers in the university canteen—which became progressively bizarre (clocks running backwards; customers suddenly staring at the participant or changing into mannequins; gravity failing) under control of the researcher. To increase the dream-like character, changes occurred outside of the field of the participants, i.e. they did not directly witness any changes, but only the result of the change when moving their gaze/attention to a different field of view. This produced an aesthetic which closely resembles dreaming, since alterations to working memory (among other cognitive changes) render otherwise intrusive, non-sequitur inconsistencies frequent and unremarkable from within the dreaming state.

每个早期课程（前两周）都包括定制的“ SpinozaCafé”。这种场景涉及扮演服务员或女服务员的角色-在大学食堂为顾客提供食物-在研究人员的控制下变得越来越怪异（时钟向后倒转；顾客突然凝视参与者或变成人体模型；重力失灵）。为了增加像梦一样的性格，变化发生在参与者的视野之外，即他们没有直接见证任何变化，而只是将他们的目光/注意力转移到不同的视野时变化的结果。这产生了一种与梦境极为相似的美学，因为对工作记忆的改变（以及其他认知变化）使得在梦境状态下，其他情况下的侵入性，非半人半常的矛盾变得频繁且不明显。

VR training led to statistically significant increases in dream lucidity compared to the passive control group... It therefore stands to reason that the tentative gains seen in the VR group could be explained through a combination of several overlapping factors: dream-like VR scenarios provided a training ground for metacognitive reflections; bizarre and novel VR content was subsequently incorporated into participants' dream imagery; and this reminded them of the study goal when noticed. It is further worth considering whether the VR experience itself could have exerted some dissociative effects, which postspectively provided a fertile and convincing (dream-like) psychological state from which to question one's reality, as part of the required lucid dreaming training.

与被动对照组相比，VR训练在梦境上的清醒率具有统计学上的显着提高...因此，有理由认为，可以通过多个重叠因素的组合来解释在VR组中看到的试探性收获：提供了类似梦境的VR场景元认知思考的训练场；奇特新颖的VR内容随后被并入参与者。梦中的意象这让他们想起了学习目标。还值得考虑的是，VR体验本身是否可能会产生一些分离作用，作为要求进行的清醒梦境训练的一部分，这可能会提供一种肥沃而令人信服的（梦境般的）心理状态，从中可以质疑一个现实。 。

In other words, the very fact of the experiment might have been the thing that helped prime the lucid dreaming. There are many more qualifications to the study's results which you can read here, and they should be replicated by others before jumping to any conclusions.

换句话说，实验的事实可能是有助于实现清醒梦的事物。您可以在此处阅读研究结果的更多限定条件，在得出任何结论之前，应将其复制给他人。

That said, this could be another positive effect for VR: Not only giving us more imaginative options during our waking life, but during the 8 hours or so in which we often feel passive in the random experiences created by our subconscious.

也就是说，这可能是VR的另一个积极效果：不仅在我们的清醒生活中为我们提供了更多富有想象力的选择，而且在8小时左右的时间里，在潜意识创造的随机体验中，我们常常感到被动。

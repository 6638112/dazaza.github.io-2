Since updating to Android 11, I cannot take screenshots anymore. The screenshot is taken but then the notification appears stating that "Taking screenshots isn't allowed by the app or your organization." This happens all the time no matter where I try to screenshot, even when screenshoting the desktop. There is no G Suite account set up in the phone, so there is no organization that could prevent screenshotting. Also the new feature of screen recoding works without issues.

自从更新到安卓11，我就不能再截屏了。屏幕截图已被截取，但随后会出现通知，说明该应用程序或您的组织不允许截图。无论我在哪里截图，即使是在桌面截图时，这种情况也经常发生。手机中没有设置G Suite账号，因此没有任何组织可以阻止截图。此外，屏幕录制的新功能工作起来也没有任何问题。

UPDATE: Removing the ".nomedia" file from my snapshot folder solved the issue. Not sure whether I created the file or not, but apparently excluding it from the library breaks snapshots. Hope this helps!

@Wes, I had the same issue as well and your solution fixed it for me (on Pixel 4), thanks man! Also for those who read this and are unsure of what to do on Pixel 4, the "Files" app or shortcut got changed to one of the tiles on the pull-down menu (where you see you WiFi, Mobile Data, Bluetooth, etc.) and is titled "Storage". Once there, you'll want to go to " Browse" on the bottom right, and then select "Internal Storage". Once there, you'll want to scroll down to the "Pictures" folder. At the bottom is where you can find the ".nomedia" file. I deleted it and that worked for me, but you could moving it if you'd like. You may or may not need to turn on the setting to see hidden files in order to find this, so if you can't find it make sure to check that out.

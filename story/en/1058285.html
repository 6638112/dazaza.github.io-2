Serbian freelancers protest new taxation law that threatens push into poverty
塞尔维亚自由职业者抗议新的税收法，威胁要陷入贫困

2021-04-14 16:39:25

Around two thousand citizens of Serbia took to the streets of Belgrade on April 8 to protest a new tax scheme that would require digital workers – or those who work as freelancers over the internet- to pay exuberant retroactive taxes for the previous five years. They warn that if the government goes through with its plan, over 100,000 individuals now taxable will slip over the edge of poverty.

四千名塞尔维亚公民于4月8日前往贝尔格莱德的街道，以抗议新的税务计划，这些税务计划需要数字工人 - 或者在互联网上作为自由职业者工作的人来支付过去五年的令人满意的追溯税。他们警告说，如果政府通过其计划，现在有超过100,000人的纳税将在贫困的边缘上滑动。

The protesters, many coming from other cities across Serbia, set up a camp of tents in front of the parliament during the evening of April 7. The next morning, campers were joined by protesters in a 2-hour walk on the main Belgrade streets, stopping by government buildings to “greet” ministers of finance and labour, and the premier. They finished their walk with a promise to return in two weeks if the Serbian Parliament passes the law they strongly object to.

抗议者，许多来自塞尔维亚的其他城市，在4月7日晚上，在议会前面设立了一个帐篷阵营。第二天早上，露营者加入了抗议者，在贝尔格莱德街道的2小时步行下，政府建筑停止“迎接”财政和劳动力部长，以及总理。如果塞尔维亚议会通过他们强烈反对的法律，他们会在两周内完成他们的散步。

Freelancers’ protest. Background music: “Sistem te laže” (The System is lying to you) by Beogradski sindikat.

自由职业者的抗议。背景音乐：Beogradski Sindikat的“SISTEM TELAžE”（系统骗了你）。

The protests are organized by the Association of Internet Workers, or  Udruženje radnika na internetu,  an organization formed through a Facebook group with over 13 thousand members. Besides expressing their rejection to the new taxes, the protests were to debunk the government's claim that these taxes have the support of freelancers.

抗议活动由互联网工人协会或udruðenjeRadnikano互联网组织，该组织通过Facebook集团组成，拥有超过13000名成员。除了表达他们对新税收的拒绝之外，抗议活动是揭斤政府的声称这些税收是否有自由职业者的支持。

On March 29, the Association held its third round of negotiations with the government, and like previous attempts in February, talks failed  as the government refused to change most of their terms.

3月29日，该协会与政府举行的第三轮谈判，比如2月以前的尝试，因为政府拒绝改变其大部分条款时，会谈失败。

To add insult to injury, the government announced on March 31 that they have come to terms with  another organization that claims to represent the freelancers. This came as a bit of a shock to the digital community, since the organization in question was never part of the negotiation process nor organized any activities towards finding a solution for digital workers, but rather kept silent over the past six months, or voiced out their support to Association of Internet Workers demands.

为了增加伤害，政府于3月31日宣布，他们已经与另一个声称代表自由职业者的组织来实现。这是对数字社区的一点震惊，因为所讨论的组织从来都不是谈判过程的一部分，也不是为寻找数字工人寻找解决方案的任何活动，而是在过去的六个月中保持沉默，或者弥补他们对互联网工人的关联需求的支持。

The Serbian Prime Minister Ana Brnabić visited the camp before the April 8 protest started, offering “a cup of tea” to the campers who spent the night in freezing weather. They responded that they want new negotiations and a halt to the parliament's procedures of adopting a new tax law.

塞尔维亚总理AnaBrnabić在4月8日抗议开始之前访问了这个阵营，为露营者提供“一杯茶”，他们在寒冷的天气下度过了夜晚。他们回答说，他们希望新的谈判和停止议会＆＃39;采用新税法的程序。

For years, digital workers have been excluded from laws and regulations in Serbia. They have had no right to pension plans nor healthcare, and no way of defining their employment status legally.

多年来，数字工人被排除在塞尔维亚的法律法规之外。他们没有权利养老金计划和医疗保健，也没有办法合法地定义其就业状况。

They were only mentioned in an amendment made to the Pension and Disability Law in December 2019, that allowed them to add part of the income to their personal pension funds starting 2020, and then only if they were officially employed elsewhere. In the years before October 2020, upon inquiry, the Serbian tax authorities claimed that the line of work of people who earn their living by outsourcing services to foreign employers was not regulated by law, and that they had no framework for taxation.

它们仅在2019年12月向养老金和残疾法的修正案中提到，使他们能够将部分收入从他们的个人养老基金开始为2020年，然后只有在其他地方正式雇用。在2020年10月之前的几年内，塞尔维亚税务机关声称，通过法律监管，通过外包服务赚取生活的人的工作线，而且他们没有税收框架。

As freelancer and activist Miran Pogačar explained the situation in an interview with People’s Dispatch:

作为自由职业者和活动家MiranPogačar解释了与人民派遣采访中的情况：

Even though Serbia ranks fourth in Europe based on its market share in the global digital workforce, for years, digital workers in Serbia were invisible when it came to laws and regulations. They had no right to a pension or healthcare, and no way of defining their employment status legally.

尽管塞尔维亚在全球数字劳动力的市场份额基于其市场份额的欧洲排名第四，但多年来，塞尔维亚的数字工人在涉及法律法规时是隐形的。他们没有权利养老金或医疗保健，并没有办法在合法地定义其就业状况。

As the unemployment rates in Serbia are sky-high, the National Unemployment Office even recommended finding work with foreign companies. It even subsidized courses for digital workers without mentioning any obligations that come with freelance work.

随着塞尔维亚的失业率是Sky-High，国家失业办公室甚至建议与外国公司找到合作。它甚至为数字工人提供补贴课程，而不提及自由工作的任何义务。

At the same time, Serbian tax authorities had been misinforming citizens who inquired about their tax obligations. They claimed that our line of work wasn’t regulated by law, that they had no framework for taxation, and they sent out the same message: we weren’t supposed to pay anything, and we couldn’t if we wanted to.

与此同时，塞尔维亚税务机关一直误解了征用其税收义务的公民。他们声称我们的工作线没有法律规范，他们没有征税框架，他们发出了相同的消息：我们不应该支付任何费用，如果我们想，我们就无法支付任何费用

However, in the year of the pandemic and the devastating economic crisis, instead of amending the labour law and regulating Internet and freelance work, the authorities exploited an obscure legal loophole in the 2001 Personal Income Taxation Law, enabling them to initiate a sudden tax revision.

但是，在大流行和毁灭性的经济危机的年份，而不是修改劳动法和规范互联网和自由工作，当局在2001年的个人所得税法中阐述了一个模糊的法律漏洞，使他们能够启动突然税收修订。

Subsequently, on October 13, 2020, the Tax Administration demanded that all private individuals who had received money from abroad pay their taxes and social contributions for the preceding 6 years in a single instalment, including accrued interest.

随后，在2020年10月13日，税务局要求所有从国外收到资金的私人个人在单一分期付款中为先前的6年缴纳税收和社会捐款，包括应计利息。

This was the first time the Tax Administration had ever informed or warned digital workers of their alleged obligations, and did so through a Facebook post calling them to “declare income from abroad at their  own initiative.” According to the Tax Administration’s data, 99.7% of all those affected by the 2001 law had not paid taxes or contributions. Abruptly, the authorities demanded a payment of taxes and contributions in the future and retroactively for the last five years.

这是税务局首次获悉或警告其所谓的义务的数字工人，并通过一个呼吁他们“在他们自己的倡议下宣布来自国外收入”的Facebook发布。根据税务管理局的数据，受2001年法律影响的所有人的99.7％没有缴纳税收或捐款。当局突然，在过去五年中，未来并追溯到未来的税收和捐款。

Threatened digital workers responded by organizing two large peaceful protests on December 30 and January 16.

受威胁的数字工人在12月30日和1月16日组织两个大型和平抗议活动。

After the first protest, the President of Serbia Aleksandar Vučić issued a conciliatory statement,  saying that while everyone should pay taxes, the state authorities were indeed partially to blame and that he asked the government not to request payment for that period.

在第一次抗议之后，塞尔维亚·阿莱克萨·沃思·斯坦加总统发布了一份调解陈述，虽然每个人都应该纳税，但国家当局确实是部分责任的，并且他要求政府不要求该期限申请付款。

Ja mislim da je tim ljudima potrebno omogućiti da rade. Sad je jasno kakav porez treba da plate, ali nemojte da im naplaćujete retroaktivno.

Ja Mislim da Je Tim Ljudima PotbnoOmogućitida rade。悲伤的Je Jasno Kakav Porez Treba Da Plate，Ali Nemojte da imnaplažujeteretroaktivno。

I think that those people should be allowed to work. Since now it's clear what kind of tax they should pay, please don't charge them retroactively.

我认为这些人应该被允许工作。从现在开始＆＃39;清楚他们应该支付什么样的税款，请不要追溯给他们追溯给他们。

After the second protest, the government then accepted the freelancers’ offer to negotiate. The authorities proposed an interest write-off, and a reduction of the taxable sum to 57% of the total, although the latter was not guaranteed. The government also proposed a ten-year payment plan.

第二次抗议后，政府随后接受了自由职业者的谈判。当局提出了一个令人利息，虽然后者未得到保证，但仍将征收金额减少到总数的57％。政府还提出了一个十年的付款计划。

The freelancers estimated that combined with current taxes and contributions, the monthly sum would amount to over 50% of an individual's total income, provided that their income remains constant for the next ten years.

自由职业者估计与现有的税收和捐款相结合，每月金额将超过50％以上的总收入的50％以上，如果其收入在未来十年中仍然不变。

Due to the volatile and precarious nature of their work, this would push many of the  digital workers into extreme poverty. In case a single income is late, it could result in whole families becoming homeless. Moreover, they claim the government's offer is unconstitutional, violating Article 91, Section 3 of Serbian Constitution which states that the obligation of paying taxes and other dues shall be general and based on the economic ability of taxpayers.

由于他们的工作的挥发性和不稳定性，这将推动许多数字工人陷入极端贫困。如果单一收入迟到，它可能导致整个家庭变得无家可归。此外，他们声称政府和第39条的报价是违宪的，违反第91条，塞尔维亚宪法第3条，这些第91条规定缴纳税收和其他会费的义务将是一般性的，并以纳税人的经济能力为基础。

The freelancers unanimously agree that taxes must be paid. However, they consider the current rates are not only unjust but downright oppressive and unsustainable. The state is demanding that they pay for social benefits while denying them the means to exercise them.

自由职业者一致同意必须支付税款。然而，他们认为目前的速率不仅是不公正而是彻头彻尾的压迫和不可持续的。国家要求他们为社会福利付出代价，同时否认他们锻炼它们的手段。

The digital workers also find it unjust to impose taxation retroactively, because the market price of their work could not be adjusted in that period to include even possible taxation.  Using digital tools and surveys, freelancers came  to a joint decision that they can’t accept the government’s proposal.

数字工人还发现它不公正地追溯追税，因为他们的工作市场价格无法在该期间调整，以包括甚至可能的税收。使用数字工具和调查，自由职业者来到一个联合决定，他们不能接受政府的提议。

In January 2021, the Association contacted the Serbian Ombudsman, which  asked the Tax Administration to supply documentation regarding its activities related to digital workers and private individuals for the period of five years. The documentation could prove that the Tax Administration had failed to meet its obligations and misinformed digital workers for years.

2021年1月，该协会联系了塞尔维亚监察员，要求税务管理局向五年来提供关于其与数字工人和私人有关的活动的文件。该文件可以证明税务局未能履行其义务和误导性数字工人多年。

The association is a growing group of mostly young people that includes designers, teachers, translators, media specialists, programmers, and content creators (among others), who use protests and social-media campaigns to fight their way through the government-controlled media where they were essentially portrayed as tax-evading criminals.

该协会是一个越来越多的年轻人，包括设计师，教师，翻译人员，媒体专家，程序员和内容创作者（其中），他使用抗议和社交媒体活动来通过政府控制的媒体进行战斗他们基本上被描绘成逃税罪犯。

By using the internet, and by talking to relatives and friends, the freelancers managed to reshape public opinion about their situation in less than two months, and largely gained the sympathy and understanding of the general public. Nevertheless, Serbian authorities still refused to take into consideration the evident humanitarian issue at hand.

通过使用互联网，通过与亲戚和朋友交谈，自由职业者设法在不到两个月的情况下重塑公众舆论，并在很大程度上得到了对公众的同情和理解。尽管如此，塞尔维亚当局仍然拒绝考虑到掌握的明显人道主义问题。

Freelancers vow to continue protesting until a just solution is reached. They reiterate that they would be willing to pay taxes, but not in a way that would plunge them and their families into poverty, or rob  them of over half of their projected income for the next decade.

Freelance人誓要继续抗议，直到刚刚采取措施。他们重申，主题将愿意纳税，但不是一种将他们及其家庭陷入无限的方式，或者抢劫未来十年的预计收入超过一半。

Since the prime minister refused the protesters’ demands, they decided to continue camping until April 10 and join in solidarity a protest by environmental activists scheduled for Saturday.

由于总理拒绝了抗议者的要求，他们决定继续露营，直到4月10日，并通过安排周六的环境活动家加入团结一致。

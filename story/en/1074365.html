The professor who beat roulette
打败轮盘赌的教授

2022-02-21 04:33:47

On a warm night in May of 1969, a throng of awestruck gamblers crowded around a well-worn roulette table in the Italian Riviera.

1969年5月的一个温暖的夜晚，一群令人敬畏的赌徒围着意大利里维埃拉一张破旧的轮盘赌桌。

At the center stood a gangly 38-year-old medical professor in a rumpled suit. He’d just placed a $100,000 bet ($715,000 in 2019 dollars) on a single spin of the wheel. As the croupier unleashed the little white ball, the room went silent. He couldn’t possibly be this lucky… could he?

中心站着一位38岁的瘦长的医学教授，穿着皱巴巴的西装。他刚刚下了10万美元的赌注（2019年为71.5万美元），赌一圈。当蹲下的人打开白色小球时，房间里一片寂静。他不可能这么幸运吧？

But Dr. Richard Jarecki wasn’t leaving it up to chance. He’d spent thousands of hours devising an ingenious method of winning — and it would soon net him the modern equivalent of more than $8,000,000.

但是Richard Jarecki博士并没有把它留给机会。他花了数千个小时设计了一种巧妙的制胜方法，很快他就获得了相当于80多万美元的现代奖金。

Born in 1931 to a Jewish family in Stettin, Germany, Richard Jarecki was thrust into a world in chaos.

理查德·贾雷基1931年出生于德国斯特廷的一个犹太家庭，被推入一个混乱的世界。

Germany was in the throes of economic hardship, and the Nazi Party was gaining support with an anti-Semitic platform that blamed the country’s ills on Jewish citizens. Jarecki’s parents, a dermatologist and a shipping industry heiress, were gradually stripped of everything they had. Facing internment at the onset of WWII, they fled to America for a better life.

德国正处于经济困难的阵痛之中，纳粹党正通过一个反犹太主义纲领获得支持，该纲领将该国的弊病归咎于犹太公民。贾雷基的父母是皮肤科医生，也是航运业的女继承人，他们的所有财产都逐渐被剥夺。二战开始时，他们面临拘留，为了更好的生活逃到了美国。

In New Jersey, the young Jarecki found solace in games like rummy, skat, and bridge, and took great pleasure in “habitually winning money” from friends. Gifted with a brilliant mind that could retain numbers and statistics, he went to study medicine — a noble pursuit that pleased his father.

在新泽西州，年轻的贾雷基在拉米、斯卡特和桥牌等游戏中找到了慰藉，并从朋友那里“习惯性地赢钱”中获得了极大的乐趣。他有一个聪明的头脑，能够记住数字和统计数据，因此他去学习医学——这是一项令他父亲高兴的崇高追求。

As a young man in the ‘50s, Jarecki gained a reputation as one of the world’s foremost medical researchers.

作为50年代的一名年轻人，贾雷基获得了世界顶尖医学研究人员的声誉。

But he harbored a secret: His true passion lay in the dark, musty halls of casinos.

但他隐藏着一个秘密：他真正的激情在于黑暗、发霉的赌场大厅。

Sometime around 1960, Jarecki developed an obsession with roulette, a game where a little ball is spun around a randomly numbered, multi-colored wheel and the player places bets on where it will land.

大约在1960年的某个时候，贾雷基对轮盘赌产生了痴迷。轮盘赌是一种游戏，一个小球绕着一个随机编号的多色轮子旋转，玩家下注于它将落在哪里。

Though roulette was considered by many to be purely a game of chance, Jarecki was convinced that it could be “beat.”

虽然轮盘赌被许多人认为纯粹是一种机会游戏，但贾雷基确信它可以被“击败”

He noticed that at the end of each night, casinos would replace cards and dice with fresh sets — but the expensive roulette wheels went untouched and often stayed in service for decades before being replaced.

他注意到，每天晚上结束时，赌场都会用新的轮盘来替换纸牌和骰子——但昂贵的轮盘赌轮盘没有被碰过，而且在被替换之前通常会使用几十年。

Like any other machine, these wheels acquired wear and tear. Jarecki began to suspect that tiny defects — chips, dents, scratches, unlevel surfaces — might cause certain wheels to land on certain numbers more frequently than randomocity prescribed.

和其他机器一样，这些轮子也会磨损。Jarecki开始怀疑微小的缺陷——碎屑、凹痕、划痕、不平整的表面——可能会导致某些车轮比随机性规定的更频繁地落在特定的数字上。

The doctor spent weekends commuting between the operating table and the roulette table, manually recording thousands upon thousands of spins, and analyzing the data for statistical abnormalities.

医生周末在手术台和轮盘赌台之间来回奔波，手动记录成千上万次旋转，并分析数据中的统计异常。

“I [experimented] until I had a rough outline of a system based on the previous winning numbers,” he told the Sydney Morning Herald in 1969. “If numbers 1, 2, and 3 won the last 3 rounds, [I could determine] what was most likely to win the next 3.”

1969年，他告诉《悉尼先驱晨报》：“我（一直在试验），直到我根据之前的获奖数字大致勾勒出一个系统的轮廓。”。“如果1号、2号和3号在最后3轮中获胜，我可以确定下一轮最有可能获胜的是什么。”

Jarecki’s approach wasn’t new: Joseph Jagger, thought to be the “pioneer” of the so-called “biased wheel” strategy, had won hefty sums this way in the 1880s. In 1947, researchers Albert Hibbs and Dr. Roy Walford used the technique to buy a yacht and sail off into the Caribbean sunset. Then, there was Helmut Berlin, an ex-lathe operator who, in 1950, hired a team of cronies to track wheels and made off with $420,000.

贾雷基的方法并不新鲜：约瑟夫·贾格尔被认为是所谓“偏向车轮”战略的“先驱”，在19世纪80年代以这种方式赢得了巨额资金。1947年，研究人员阿尔伯特·希布斯（Albert Hibbs）和罗伊·沃尔福德（Roy Walford）博士（Dr.Roy Walford）利用这一技术购买了一艘游艇，驶向加勒比海的日落。还有赫尔穆特·伯林（Helmut Berlin），一位前车床操作员，他在1950年雇佣了一队密友来跟踪车轮，并以42万美元逃走。

But for Jarecki, it wasn’t about the money: He wanted to perfect the system, repeat it, and “beat” the wheel. It was a matter of man triumphing over machine.

但对贾雷基来说，这与钱无关：他想完善这个系统，重复一遍，然后“敲打”方向盘。这是一个人战胜机器的问题。

After months of collecting data, he scraped together $100 (his rainy day savings) and hit the casino. He’d never gambled — and though he trusted his research, he knew he was still up against “the element of chance.”

在收集了数月的数据后，他凑足了100美元（他在雨天的积蓄），去了赌场。他从未赌博过——尽管他相信自己的研究，但他知道自己仍然面临着“机会的因素”

In a matter of hours, he flipped his $100 into $5,000 (~$41,000 today). And with this validation, he turned to much higher stakes.

在几个小时内，他把100美元变成了5000美元（今天大约41000美元）。通过这种验证，他转向了更高的赌注。

In the mid-60s, Jarecki moved to Germany and took up a post at the University of Heidelberg to study electrophoresis and forensic medicine.

在60年代中期，JaRiki移居德国，在海德堡大学攻读电泳和法医学。

He’d recently won a highly prestigious peace prize (one of only 12 awarded worldwide) for his work on international cooperation in medicine, and, as a result, had gained entry into an elite group of doctors and scientists.

他最近因在国际医学合作方面的工作而获得了一项享有盛誉的和平奖（全世界仅有的12项和平奖之一），并因此进入了一个由医生和科学家组成的精英群体。

European roulette wheels offered better odds than American wheels: They had 37 slots instead of 38, reducing the casino’s edge over the player from 5.26% to 2.7%. And, as Jarecki would discover, they were just his type of machine — old, janky, and full of physical defects.

欧洲轮盘赌轮盘赌的赔率比美国轮盘赌轮盘赌的赔率高：他们有37个轮盘赌槽，而不是38个轮盘赌槽，这使赌场对玩家的优势从5.26%降至2.7%。而且，正如贾雷基会发现的那样，它们只是他那种类型的机器——陈旧、简陋，而且充满了物理缺陷。

With his wife, Carol, he scouted dozens of wheels at casinos around Europe, from Monte Carlo (Monaco), to Divonne-les-Bains (France), to Baden-Baden (Germany). The pair recruited a team of 8 “clockers” who posted up at these venues, sometimes recording as many as 20,000 spins over a month-long period.

他和妻子卡罗尔在欧洲各地的赌场里搜寻了几十个轮子，从蒙特卡洛（摩纳哥）、迪冯·莱班斯（法国）到巴登·巴登（德国）。两人招募了一个由8名“计时员”组成的团队，他们在这些场馆张贴帖子，有时在一个月的时间里记录多达20000次旋转。

After establishing which wheels were biased, he secured a £25,000 loan from a Swiss financier and spent 6 months candidly exacting his strategy. By the end of the run, he’d netted £625,000 (roughly $6,700,000 today).

在确定哪些车轮存在偏差后，他从一位瑞士金融家那里获得了2.5万英镑的贷款，并花了6个月时间坦率地严格执行他的策略。到比赛结束时，他净赚了62.5万英镑（今天大约670万美元）。

Jarecki’s victories made headlines in newspapers all over the world, from Kansas to Australia. Everyone wanted his “secret” — but he knew that if he wanted to replicate the feat, he’d have to conceal his true methodology.

从堪萨斯到澳大利亚，贾雷基的胜利成为世界各地报纸的头条新闻。每个人都想知道他的“秘密”——但他知道，如果他想复制这一壮举，就必须隐藏自己真正的方法论。

So, he concocted a “fanciful tale” for the press: He tallied roulette outcomes daily, then fed the information into an Atlas supercomputer, which told him which numbers to pick.

因此，他为媒体编造了一个“奇幻故事”：他每天统计轮盘赌的结果，然后将信息输入Atlas超级计算机，该计算机告诉他选择哪个数字。

At the time, wrote gambling historian, Russell Barnhart, in Beating the Wheel, “Computers were looked upon as creatures from outer space… Few persons, including casino managers, were vocationally qualified to distinguish myth from reality.”

当时，赌博历史学家拉塞尔·巴恩哈特（Russell Barnhart）在《打方向盘》一书中写道，“计算机被视为来自外太空的生物……几乎没有人，包括赌场经理，具备职业资格来区分神话和现实。”

Hiding behind this technological ruse, Jarecki continued to keep tabs on biased tables — and prepare for his next big move.

在这一技术诡计的背后，贾雷基继续关注有偏见的表格——并为他的下一个重大举措做好准备。

Flush with cash, Jarecki purchased a luxury apartment near San Remo, a palatial Italian casino on the shores of the Mediterranean.

贾雷基现金充裕，在地中海沿岸富丽堂皇的意大利赌场圣雷莫附近购买了一套豪华公寓。

Through studious observation, he identified a table that had a habit of landing on #33 far more than usual — a result of the “constant friction of the ball against the wheel.”

通过仔细观察，他发现一张桌子的落地习惯比平常多得多——这是“球与轮子不断摩擦”的结果

On a spring night in 1968, he drove his white Rolls Royce to the gambling den and, over 3 days, proceeded to win $48,000 ($360,000 today).

1968年的一个春夜，他开着他的白色劳斯莱斯（Rolls-Royce）来到赌场，在三天多的时间里，他赢得了4.8万美元（今天是36万美元）。

Eight months later he returned, winning $192,000 ($1,400,000) in a single weekend, and breaking the bank (depleting the casino’s on-hand cash) at two different wheels twice in one night. Teetering on bankruptcy, the casino owner had no option but to issue Jarecki a 15-day ban… for “being too good.”

八个月后，他回来了，在一个周末赢得了19.2万美元（14万美元），并在一个晚上两次用两个不同的轮子打破了银行（耗尽了赌场的手头现金）。由于濒临破产，赌场老板别无选择，只能以“太好”为由，向贾雷基发出15天的禁令

The night the ban was lifted, Jarecki returned and won another $100,000 ($717,000) — so much money that the casino had to give him a promissory note.

禁令解除的那天晚上，贾雷基回来了，又赢了10万美元（合71.7万美元）——这么多钱，赌场不得不给他一张期票。

When Jarecki showed up to a casino, large crowds would gather to witness the master at work. Many would mirror his every move, placing small bets on the same numbers.

当贾雷基出现在一家赌场时，大批人群聚集在一起，见证这位大师的工作。许多人会模仿他的一举一动，在相同的数字上下小赌注。

In a bid to outfox Jarecki, casino owners rearranged his favorite roulette wheels in different spots every night. But the professor knew every vein in the wood —  every nic, crack, scratch, and discoloration — and he always ferreted them out.

为了打败贾雷基，赌场老板每天晚上都在不同的地方重新安排他最喜欢的轮盘赌。但是这位教授知道木头上的每一条纹理——每一个裂纹、划痕和变色——他总是把它们挖出来。

“He is a menace to every casino in Europe,” casino owner, Signor Lardera, told the Sydney Morning Herald. “I don’t know how he does it exactly, but if he never returned to my casino I would be a very happy man.”

“他对欧洲的每家赌场都是一个威胁，”赌场老板拉德拉先生告诉《悉尼先驱晨报》。“我不知道他到底是怎么做到的，但如果他再也不回到我的赌场，我将是一个非常快乐的人。”

“If the casino directors don’t like to lose,” retorted Jarecki, “they should sell vegetables.”

“如果赌场经理不想输，”贾雷基反驳道，“他们应该卖蔬菜。”

Eventually, San Remo gave up and replaced all 24 of its roulette wheels at a steep cost to the house. It was, they ceded, the only way to stop the best player they’d ever seen. In the decades following Jarecki’s dominance, casinos invested heavily in monitoring their roulette tables for defects and building wheels less prone to bias. Today, most wheels have gone digital, run by algorithms programmed to favor the house.

最后，雷莫以24美元的价格把房子换成了轮盘赌。他们放弃了，这是阻止他们见过的最好球员的唯一方法。在贾雷基统治后的几十年里，赌场投入巨资监控其轮盘赌桌的缺陷，并建造不太容易产生偏差的轮子。如今，大多数车轮都已经数字化，由有利于房子的编程算法运行。

All told, Jarecki made a reported $1,250,000 ($8,000,000 today) placing hefty bets on biased roulette tables between 1964 and 1969.

据报道，贾雷茨基在1964年至1969年间在有偏轮盘赌桌上下了12.5万美元（今天为80万美元）的巨额赌注。

The Italian newspaper Il Giorno called him “The world’s most successful roulette player” — a reedy academic who “[didn’t] look like a gambler.” Once deemed to be an “egghead” on campus, he became “the hero of every student at his university.”

意大利报纸《意大利日报》称他为“世界上最成功的轮盘赌选手”——一位“看起来不像赌徒”的芦苇学者他曾被认为是校园里的“书呆子”，后来成了“大学里每个学生的英雄”

In 1973, Jarecki moved his family back to New Jersey, where he started a new career as a commodities broker. With the help of his billionaire brother, he multiplied his fortune 10 times over. He also passed down his penchant for games to his son, who, at the age of 9, became the youngest chess master in history.

1973年，贾雷基将家人搬回新泽西州，在那里他开始了商品经纪人的新职业。在亿万富翁哥哥的帮助下，他的财富翻了10倍。他还把自己对游戏的爱好传给了儿子，儿子9岁时成为历史上最年轻的国际象棋大师。

On occasion, casino owners would call him with partnership offers, but he never took the bait: “He [liked] to take money from the casinos,” his wife, Carol, told TheNew York Times, “not give it to them.”

有时，赌场老板会打电话向他提出合作意向，但他从未上钩：“他（喜欢）从赌场拿钱，”他的妻子卡罗尔告诉《纽约时报》，“而不是给他们。”

In the early ‘90s, Jarecki grew tired of Atlantic City and relocated to Manila, home to a flourishing (and loosely regulated) gambling scene. He remained there until his death in 2018, at the age of 87.

90年代初，贾雷基厌倦了大西洋城，搬到了马尼拉，那里有着繁荣（监管松散）的赌博场所。他一直留在那里，直到2018年去世，享年87岁。

Tucked in the corner of a bustling gambling hall, surrounded by neon lights and slot machines, he wagered his final bet. The wheel spun round and round. Like so many times before, the little white ball landed on his number.

他躲在一个熙熙攘攘的赌场角落里，周围是霓虹灯和老虎机，他下了最后一次赌注。轮子转了又转。就像以前很多次一样，这个白色的小球落在了他的号码上。

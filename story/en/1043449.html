UK Watchdog Begins Investigating Nvidia's $40 Billion Takeover of Arm
英国看门狗开始调查Nvidia以400亿美元收购Arm

2021-01-07 09:56:08

Britain’s competition watchdog has launched an investigation into the $40bn (£29.5bn) takeover of the UK-based chip designer Arm by the US company Nvidia.

英国竞争监管机构已对美国公司Nvidia以400亿美元（合295亿英镑）收购英国芯片设计师Arm展开调查。

The Competition and Markets Authority (CMA) has called for interested parties to submit views on the contentious deal before the launch of a formal investigation later this year.

竞争与市场管理局（CMA）呼吁有关各方在今年晚些时候启动正式调查之前，就该交易提出意见。

Arm Holdings, which employs 6,500 staff including 3,000 in the UK, is a global leader in designing chips for smartphones, computers and tablets. California-based Nvidia, a graphics chip specialist, announced its plan to buy the British tech group from Japan’s SoftBank in September.

Arm Holdings在设计用于智能手机，计算机和平板电脑的芯片方面处于全球领先地位，该公司在英国拥有6,000名员工，其中包括3,000名员工。图形芯片专家，总部位于加利福尼亚州的英伟达（Nvidia）于9月宣布了从日本软银（SoftBank）收购英国科技集团的计划。

SoftBank had acquired Arm for $32bn in 2016, when the Japanese company took advantage of the fall in value of the pound after the Brexit vote. Arm is based in Cambridge but has operations in a number of UK towns and cities, including Manchester, Belfast and Warwick.

软银在2016年以320亿美元的价格收购了Arm，当时这家日本公司利用脱欧公投后英镑贬值的优势。 Arm的总部位于剑桥，但在曼彻斯特，贝尔法斯特和沃里克等英国多个城镇都有业务。

Its chief executive, Simon Segars, acknowledged at the time of the Nvidia deal that it could take up to 18 months to win approval from regulators around the world.

它的首席执行官西蒙·塞加斯（Simon Segars）在与Nvidia达成交易时承认，要获得全球监管机构的批准可能需要长达18个月的时间。

On Wednesday, the chief executive of the CMA, Andrea Coscelli, said: “The chip technology industry is worth billions and critical to many of the products that we use most in our everyday lives. We will work closely with other competition authorities around the world to carefully consider the impact of the deal and ensure that it doesn’t ultimately result in consumers facing more expensive or lower-quality products.”

CMA首席执行官Andrea Coscelli周三表示：“芯片技术行业价值数十亿美元，对于我们日常生活中使用最多的许多产品至关重要。我们将与全球其他竞争管理机构紧密合作，仔细考虑交易的影响，并确保最终不会导致消费者面临价格更高或质量较低的产品。”

The CMA said it would examine the possible effect of the deal on competition in the UK and whether Arm had an “incentive to withdraw, raise prices or reduce the quality of its intellectual property licensing services to Nvidia’s rivals”.

CMA表示，将检查该交易对英国竞争的可能影响，以及Arm是否“有向Nvidia的竞争对手撤回，提高价格或降低其知识产权许可服务质量的诱因”。

If the investigation finds that the deal breaches UK competition rules, the CMA’s powers include the authority to block the takeover.

如果调查发现该交易违反英国竞争规则，则CMA的权力包括阻止收购的权力。

In October, Hermann Hauser, the co-founder of Arm, wrote to the House of Commons foreign affairs committee arguing that if the deal was allowed to proceed, the combined business would become the next US tech monopoly alongside companies such as Google and Facebook.

去年10月，Arm的联合创始人赫尔曼·豪瑟（Hermann Hauser）写信给下议院外交事务委员会，争辩说，如果允许交易继续进行，合并后的业务将成为谷歌和Facebook等公司的下一个美国技术垄断企业。

He has argued that because Nvidia is one of more than 500 users of Arm’s designs worldwide – which include Apple, Samsung and Qualcomm – becoming the Cambridge-based business’s parent company will destroy Arm’s “even-handed” model and ultimately kill the world-leading British tech company.

他认为，由于英伟达是Arm在全球500多个设计中的用户之一（包括苹果，三星和高通），成为总部位于剑桥的公司的母公司将破坏Arm的“平手”模式，并最终杀死世界领先的设计英国科技公司。

Hauser also told the BBC last year the deal would be “an absolute disaster for Cambridge, the UK and Europe”, adding that Nvidia would inevitably decide to relocate Arm to the US, leading to lost jobs.

豪瑟（Hauser）去年还告诉英国广播公司（BBC），这笔交易将“对剑桥，英国和欧洲构成绝对灾难”，并补充说，英伟达将不可避免地决定将Arm迁至美国，从而导致失业。

Hauser has said the government should use its powers to prevent the takeover, but has also backed indefinite legally binding conditions as an alternative. These include a guarantee to keep Arm’s staff in Cambridge, Belfast, Manchester and Warwick, and a promise that Nvidia does not get preferential treatment on new versions of the designer’s chips.

豪瑟（Hauser）曾表示，政府应利用其权力阻止并购，但也支持无限期具有法律约束力的条件作为替代方案。其中包括保证将Arm的员工留在剑桥，贝尔法斯特，曼彻斯特和沃里克，并保证英伟达不会在新版设计的芯片上获得优惠待遇。

The shadow business secretary, Ed Miliband, has also expressed concerns about the deal, and has called on the government to intervene to prevent parts of Arm from being moved out of the UK.

影子商业部长埃德·米利班德（Ed Miliband）也对该交易表示担忧，并呼吁政府进行干预，以防止Arm的某些部分被转移出英国。

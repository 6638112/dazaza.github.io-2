Some ISPs Exploited Covid Broadband Relief Program to Make an Extra Buck - While the $3.2 billion program provides relief to struggling Americans, some ISPs are abusing it to make an additional buck during a crisis.

Last May, the government began offering Americans struggling during the pandemic a $50 discount off of their broadband bill. But some U.S. broadband providers are already exploiting the program to drive consumers to even more expensive broadband plans.

最后，政府开始向大流行期间提供美国人挣扎的美国宽广账单的50美元折扣。但是，一些美国宽带提供商已经利用该计划推动消费者更昂贵的宽带计划。

Dubbed the Emergency Broadband Benefit (EBB), the $3.2 billion plan doles out money to your broadband provider, who then is supposed to give customers a $50 discount off their monthly bill. The discount jumps to $75 for those living on tribal lands. The program is scheduled to continue for as long as funding lasts, or six months after the end of the pandemic. 825 broadband providers are participating in the voluntary program, and most appear to be complying with its rules. But some ISPs saw the program as a revenue-boosting opportunity.

将紧急宽带福利（ebb）被称为32亿美元的计划Doles Out Money给您的宽带提供商，然后应该为客户提供每月账单的50美元折扣。对于生活在部落土地上的人员，折扣跃升至75美元。该计划计划在资金持续持续的情况下继续，或者在大流行结束后六个月。 825宽带提供商正在参与自愿计划，大部分似乎遵守其规则。但有些ISPS将该计划视为收入增强机会。

Verizon, for example, was caught by the Washington Post forcing customers to sign up for more expensive plans if they wanted the benefit. For some users, that meant they wound up paying even more money for broadband than they were before the discount was applied. The company also refused to offer the discount on its older (but still expensive) DSL lines. Once caught, Verizon issued a press release claiming it intended all along to give “customers a wide range of choices to best fit their needs.” Verizon also faced widespread criticism in 2018 for capping the wireless connections of firefighters, then attempting to upsell them to more expensive plans as they battled an historic California wildfire. Verizon hasn’t been alone in getting creative in terms of program compliance. AT&T, Charter, and T-Mobile have all limited which plans are eligible for the discount, something allowed under FCC rules. Protocol also recently reported that Charter, which sells broadband under the Spectrum brand, has been forcing eligible customers to opt in to full-price plans at sign up. If they refuse, they’re deemed ineligible for the emergency broadband discount.

例如，verizon被华盛顿邮政迫使客户报名参加更昂贵的计划，如果他们想要这种好处。对于一些用户来说，这意味着他们在折扣之前，他们伤到了宽带的更多钱。该公司还拒绝提供折扣（但仍然昂贵）的DSL线路。曾经抓住，Verizon发布了一款新闻稿，声称它的意图是为了给“客户有各种各样的选择，以最适合他们的需求”。 verizon还面临着2018年普遍的批评，用于盖上消防员的无线连接，然后试图将它们追随到更昂贵的计划，因为他们绑架了一个历史悠久的加利福尼亚野火。在方案合规方面，Verizon并不孤单。 AT＆amp; T，Charter和T-Mobile所有有限的计划有资格享受折扣，在FCC规则下允许的东西。议定书最近还报告说，在Spectrum品牌下销售宽带的宪章一直强迫符合条件的客户在注册时选择全价格计划。如果他们拒绝，他们认为不需要紧急宽带折扣。

“Charter has been slower to respond to the problems with its opt-in games, which can pose serious problems for low-income families by saddling them with an unexpectedly high bill after the EBB expires—a bill that can easily lead to debt and a myriad of other devastating consequences,” Dana Floberg, telecom expert at consumer group Free Press told Motherboard.

“宪章一直慢，以应对其选择退出游戏的问题，这可能通过鞍在颠覆性后，通过鞍一突出的账单来对低收入家庭带来严重的问题 - 这是一个很容易导致债务的法案无数其他破坏性后果，“消费者群体免费新闻的电信专家达娜·弗洛格erg告诉主板。

Floberg pointed to a recent study by the Consumer Financial Protection Bureau that found that one in five Americans owe money to a telecom provider, and collecting that debt is the second most profitable revenue stream for debt collectors behind medical debt. “If participating Emergency Broadband Benefit providers are making it harder for consumers to receive the support they need to get online, they need to knock it off,” acting FCC Chairwoman Jessica Rosenworcel told Motherboard in a statement. “I urge consumers who have faced this issue to come to us and share their experience at consumercomplaints.fcc.gov. It's one of the swiftest ways we can get to the bottom of what is going on and address it.” U.S. broadband is some of the most expensive in the developed world thanks to limited competition. While up to 42 million Americans have no access to broadband, 83 million more Americans live under a broadband monopoly, resulting in high prices, slow service, and terrible customer service — all issues that hit low income and marginalized Americans the hardest. Unfortunately, a temporary discount doesn’t fix the underlying lack of competition that plagues the broadband sector, or the widespread state and federal corruption that perpetuates it. Other approaches to the problem, like New York State’s attempt to force ISPs to offer low-income users $15 broadband tiers, have been met with telecom industry lawsuits.

Floberg指出了消费者金融保护局最近的一项研究，发现五分之一的美国人欠电信提供者，并收集债务是医疗债务背后的债务收藏家最有利可图的收入流。 “如果参与的紧急宽带福利提供商越来越难以消费者接受他们需要在线的支持，他们需要将其击倒，”Acting FCC主席Jessica Rosenworcel在一份声明中告诉主板。 “我敦促面临这个问题的消费者来我们并在CondureComplaints.fcc.gov分享他们的经验。它＆＃39;我们可以进入的最狡猾的方式之一并解决它。“由于竞争有限，美国宽带是发达国家中最昂贵的。虽然高达4200万美国人无法获得宽带，但更多的美国人居住在宽带垄断之下，导致价格高，服务缓慢，可怕的客户服务 - 所有问题都达到了低收入和边缘化的美国人最难的问题。不幸的是，临时折扣并没有解决困扰宽带部门的潜在缺乏竞争，或者延伸它的广泛状态和联邦腐败。解决问题的其他方法，如纽约州试图强迫ISP提供低收入用户15美元的宽带层，已达到电信行业诉讼。

Still, Floberg made it clear that the EBB program was providing genuine, broad relief to struggling Americans, but would likely need stricter guidelines if it were to be made permanent. “ISPs should be required to make all their internet plans eligible for a permanent broadband benefit,” Floberg said. “This would prevent ISPs from taking advantage of low-income customers by forcing them onto particular plans, and has the benefit of giving more power to the families themselves to decide what kinds of internet services best fit their needs.”

此外，Floberg仍然明确表明，潮流课程正在为努力提供真正的，广泛的救济，但如果要永久性，可能需要更加严格的指导方针。 “不应要求伊斯索普制定所有互联网计划，符合永久宽带利益，”Floberg说。 “这将防止ISP通过强迫他们在特定的计划中利用低收入客户，并有利于为家庭自己提供更多权力来决定哪种互联网服务最适合其需求。”

Those struggling to afford broadband during the Covid crisis can grab more detail on the temporary Emergency Broadband Benefit program at the FCC website.

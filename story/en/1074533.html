Tax Advantaged Accounts
税收优惠账户

2022-02-22 02:22:06

I have decided to write a high level overview I was forced to become familiar with over the past 3 years: US government incentivized ways to minimize taxes (as of 2022).

我决定写一篇我在过去三年里被迫熟悉的高层综述：美国政府激励的税收最小化方法（截至2022年）。

This blog will probably be most helpful to someone who is unfamiliar with these tax deductions, has the ability to save a couple tens of thousands a year, and wants to avoid taxes. My imagined demographic reading this is a tech worker or someone in the financial industries. The savings per year compound and can have an effect in the low hundreds of thousands by retirement assuming you start in early 20s. DISCLAIMER: This is not financial advice and you should do your own research. Think for yourself. I personally think these incentives are extremely convoluted and confusing, but the tax savings are great enough to bare with them. For example, if one can avoid the marginal tax rate while making $200k a year, one avoids 32% in federal taxes.

这个博客可能对那些不熟悉这些税收减免，有能力每年节省数万美元，并且想要避税的人最有帮助。我想象中的人口统计数据显示，这是一位科技工作者或金融行业的人士。假设你在20岁出头就开始退休，那么每年的储蓄是复合的，并且可能会影响到数十万人。免责声明：这不是财务建议，你应该自己做研究。你自己想想。我个人认为，这些激励措施极其复杂，令人困惑，但税收节约足以让它们脱颖而出。例如，如果一个人在年收入20万美元的情况下可以避免边际税率，那么就可以避免32%的联邦税。

There are 4 accounts that are common and relatively easy to open that I will discuss: HSA, 401K, IRA, 529

我将讨论4个常见且相对容易开立的账户：HSA、401K、IRA、529

Trad. is short for Traditional. Pre-Tax: The money going into an account has not been taxed, i.e. no income tax

贸易。是“传统”的缩写。税前：进入账户的钱没有纳税，即没有所得税

Post-Tax: The gains in the account are not taxed when withdrawn from the account, i.e. no capital gains

税后：账户中的收益在从账户中提取时不征税，即没有资本收益

Investments that are not post-tax free are taxed at and add to an individual's income tax rate at the time of withdrawal. If you are truly retired, you presumably have no other source of income, so these withdrawals would be your only income.

非税后免税的投资按个人#39征税；提取时的所得税税率。如果你真的退休了，你大概没有其他收入来源，所以这些提款将是你唯一的收入。

Some things to note: As long as the money is in the tax-advantaged account, any gains will be tax free. It is only when you start withdrawing that there may be tax/gift implications before attempting.

需要注意的是：只要钱在税收优惠账户中，任何收益都是免税的。只有当你开始取款时，在尝试之前可能会涉及税收/礼物。

For the retirements accounts (401K, IRA) early withdrawal fees before you reach "retirement age" (generally over 60, although this will likely increase)

对于退休账户（401K，IRA），在您达到"之前提前支取费用；退休年龄"岁；（一般超过60岁，但这可能会增加）

One can only have one type of 401K and one type of IRA (either traditional or Roth, and )

一个人只能有一种401K和一种IRA（传统或罗斯，以及）

HSA is short for Health Savings Account. The intended purposes of this account is actually as a pre-tax account that is only allowed to spend money on healthcare related items. However, one might be better served by simply not saving it and instead, investing, because this is technically the most tax advantaged account that I know of as it is both pre-tax and post-tax free. Once the owner of the account reaches age 65, it becomes like a regular retirement account that you own, and you can withdraw money out of it.

HSA是健康储蓄账户的缩写。该账户的预期用途实际上是一个税前账户，只允许在医疗保健相关项目上花钱。然而，不储蓄而投资可能会更好，因为这是我所知道的技术上最有利的税收优惠账户，因为它在税前和税后都是免税的。一旦账户所有者年满65岁，它就变成了你拥有的普通退休账户，你可以从中提取资金。

One can only open this if you have an High Deductible Health Plan (HDHP, as hinted at by the name, a plan with a high deductible as opposed to a PPO plan). If you are in your 20s with no dependents and are healthy, then it might be a good idea. One can calculate the breakeven point between a PPO account and a HDHP pretty easily, and once you hit the deductible for the HDHP, these accounts seem to be equivalent. As of 2022, one can only contribute $3650, so it is not a huge account though.

只有当你有一个高免赔额的健康计划（HDHP，顾名思义，是一个高免赔额的计划，而不是PPO计划）时，你才能打开这个页面。如果你20多岁，没有家属，身体健康，那么这可能是个好主意。你可以很容易地计算PPO账户和HDHP之间的盈亏平衡点，一旦你达到HDHP的免赔额，这些账户似乎是等价的。截至2022年，一个人只能贡献3650美元，因此这不是一个巨大的账户。

401Ks are attached to employment for some god-forsaken reason. As of 2022, one can contribute $20,500 per year. In addition, for a traditional 401K, one can contribute an additional after-tax (i.e. not pre-tax) $40,500. The primary reason to do this is the mega-backdoor 401K , which not all plans allow. My employer uses Empower Retirement, which does not allow the mega-backdoor unfortunately. No access until minimum age 55, although there are major easily Google-able exceptions such as the Substantially Equal Periodic Payment (SEPP) exception. If/when you leave your employment, you can (and maybe should) transfer this money into an IRA.

401K因为某种被上帝抛弃的原因而与工作联系在一起。到2022年，一个人每年可以捐款20500美元。此外，对于传统的401K，可以缴纳额外的税后（即非税前）40500美元。这样做的主要原因是巨型后门401K，并非所有计划都允许。我的雇主使用Empower Retirement，不幸的是，它不允许巨型后门。在最低年龄55岁之前不允许访问，尽管存在一些主要的易于通过谷歌搜索的例外情况，如实质上平等的定期付款（SEPP）例外。如果/当你离职时，你可以（或许应该）将这笔钱转入个人退休账户。

If you make six-figures, you likely cannot directly contribute to any IRA. However, the backdoor Roth IRA allows contributions. This backdoor is analogous to the mega-backdoor for the 401K, but is accessible by everyone. The 2022 IRA max contribution is $6000, so it is not a huge account. IRAs do not allow access until minimum age 59.5, although there are major easily Google-able exceptions such as the Substantially Equal Periodic Payment (SEPP) exception.

如果你赚了六位数，你很可能无法直接向任何个人退休账户捐款。然而，后门罗斯共和军允许捐款。这个后门类似于401K的巨型后门，但每个人都可以访问。2022年爱尔兰共和军最高捐款为6000美元，因此这不是一个巨大的账户。IRA在最低年龄59.5岁之前不允许访问，尽管存在一些主要的易于谷歌搜索的例外情况，例如实质上平等的定期付款（SEPP）例外。

Another important note is that you can convert pieces of a traditional IRA into a Roth IRA at any time with the converted amount counting as income. When you convert a traditional IRA to a Roth IRA, you will owe taxes on any money in the traditional IRA that would have been taxed when you withdrew it. That includes the tax-deductible contributions you made to the account as well as the tax-deferred earnings that have built up in it over the years. That money will be taxed as income in the year you make the conversion. Ideally, you would make this conversion in a year when you have little to no income (for example, during a long non-compete or when starting your own company).

另一个重要的注意事项是，你可以随时将传统的个人退休账户转换成罗斯个人退休账户，转换后的金额作为收入计算。当你将传统个人退休账户转换为罗斯个人退休账户时，你将对传统个人退休账户中的任何款项欠税，而这些款项在你提取时本应纳税。这包括您对该账户的可扣税供款，以及多年来积累的递延所得税。这笔钱将在你进行转换的那一年作为收入征税。理想情况下，你应该在收入很少甚至没有收入的一年内（例如，在长期竞业禁止期间或创办自己的公司时）进行这种转换。

This is account is only applicable if you have a family member going to school in the future. This can include unborn children (although one should pore over the tax implications). Contributions can be state income tax-deductible (e.g. in Illinois, contributions lead to a tax reimbursement up to $500)

此账户仅适用于您的家庭成员将来上学的情况。这可能包括未出生的孩子（尽管人们应该仔细研究税收的影响）。供款可以从州所得税中扣除（例如，在伊利诺伊州，供款可导致高达500美元的退税）

Money can come out tax free at any time as long as you spend it on "educational costs"

只要你把钱花在"；教育成本#34；

There is a common misconception that ETFs are strictly better/cheaper than mutual funds. Some mutual fund benefits: One does not need to worry about execution (granted, you can only execute at EOD)

人们普遍存在一种误解，认为ETF严格来说比共同基金更好/更便宜。一些共同基金的好处：不必担心执行（当然，你只能在EOD执行）

There is a misconception that mutual funds also always have more taxes, but firms like Vanguard uses "heartbeat" trades to reduce taxes on mutual funds (granted, they can't avoid all taxes). Vanguard has many funds with both mutual fund classes and an ETF class, and for the purposes of a retirement account that does not have to deal with these taxes, ETFs and mutual funds are very close.

有一种误解认为，共同基金也总是有更多的税收，但像先锋这样的公司使用"；心跳#34；减少共同基金税收的交易（当然，他们不能避免所有税收）。Vanguard旗下有许多基金，既有共同基金类别，也有ETF类别，就退休账户而言，ETF和共同基金的关系非常密切，而退休账户不必处理这些税费。

VTSAX is a fund that is "designed to provide investors with exposure to the entire U.S. equity market". The below image depicts the different classes of the same fund. One can see that if you have "institutional" level money (ie $5M), the mutual fund is actually cheaper than the ETF, so if you have $5M invested, you should just do the institutional level. The mutual fund with the lowest minimum is VTSAX, which has 4 basis points of fees a year, while the equivalent ETF VTI has 3 basis points of fees a year. If you have 1 million dollars, this will be an additional $100 of fees a year, so it will be a trade-off between DRIP and the extra bp in fees. One should keep in mind the tax efficiency of the products traded as well (e.g. bonds are not very tax efficient).

VTSAX是一个"；旨在为投资者提供整个美国股市的敞口；。下图描述了同一基金的不同类别。如果你有"；机构和#34；在资金层面（即500万美元），共同基金实际上比ETF便宜，所以如果你有500万美元的投资，你应该只做机构层面的投资。最低收费的共同基金是VTSAX，每年收费4个基点，而同等的ETF VTI每年收费3个基点。如果你有100万美元，这将是一年额外的100美元费用，因此这将是滴灌和额外的bp费用之间的权衡。人们还应该记住交易产品的税收效率（例如，债券的税收效率不高）。

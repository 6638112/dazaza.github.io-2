There are many misconceptions about rapid chess improvement. In this post I’m going to lay out a simple but effective way to get good at chess, fast.

人们对国际象棋的快速进步有很多误解。在这篇文章中，我将列出一种简单而有效的方法，让你快速地变得擅长国际象棋。

This system is based on lessons learned from my own chess improvement and from coaching others. The good news is that you can become better than the vast majority of other players with minimal but targeted effort.

这个系统是基于从我自己的国际象棋进步和指导别人身上学到的经验教训。好消息是，只要付出最少但有针对性的努力，你就可以变得比绝大多数其他玩家更好。

Magnus Carlsen’s meteoric rise to the top ranked player in the world (at age 19), the highest chess rating in history (age 22), and as of a few days ago, the title of World Chess Champion (age 22) has brought with it a renewed interest in chess. This is exciting, because Carlsen represents the first real hope of renewing chess’s mass appeal since the days of Bobby Fischer 1.

马格努斯·卡尔森(Magnus Carlsen)迅速崛起为世界排名第一的棋手(19岁)，获得了历史上最高的国际象棋评级(22岁)，而截至几天前，世界国际象棋冠军(22岁)的头衔又带来了人们对国际象棋的新兴趣。这是令人兴奋的，因为卡尔森代表了自鲍比·费舍尔一世(Bobby Fischer 1)以来第一次真正希望恢复国际象棋的大众吸引力。

In the context of discussions about Magnus Carlsen, many people mentioned that they enjoyed playing chess but quit because of the sheer time commitment it took to get “good.”

在关于Magnus Carlsen的讨论中，许多人提到他们喜欢下国际象棋，但放弃了，因为纯粹是因为需要花费大量的时间才能变得“好”。

I define “good” as the 90 th percentile among the player pool you’re competing against. In competitive chess in the United States, that means a United States Chess Federation (USCF) Elo rating of about 1800 2. If you’re a casual player playing against your friends, my guess is that 90 th percentile is around 900. Even though I was only rated 1100 when I first began playing competitively, I was already able to beat the vast majority of non-competitive players.

我对“好”的定义是在你的竞争对手中排在第90个百分位数。在美国的竞技国际象棋中，这意味着美国国际象棋联合会(USCF)的Elo评分约为1800 2。如果你是一个与朋友对决的休闲棋手，我猜第90个百分位数大约是900。尽管当我第一次参加竞技比赛时，我只得到了1100分，但我已经能够击败绝大多数非竞争性选手。

I actively trained for a period of about 3.5 years using a (much, much less disciplined) version of this system, during which my rating increased from 1100 to 1950, a 135 fold increase I strength 3. In one 12 month period I improved from 1198 to 1639. I improved even faster with my quick rating (games with less than 30 minutes per side), where I went from 1001 to 1740 in 15 months (75 fold increase in playing strength).

我积极训练了大约3.5年，使用的是这个体系的一个版本(纪律要差得多)，在此期间，我的评分从1100分提高到了1950分，强度3提高了135倍。在12个月的时间里，我从1198分提高到了1639分。我的快速评分(每边不到30分钟的比赛)提高得更快，我在15个月内从1001上升到1740(比赛强度增加了75倍)。

My first experience using these ideas with other players was in high school, when I began coaching the lowest ranked player in our chess club. Within a few months he had improved so rapidly that he represented the school in the state championships and won every single game in the tournament.

我第一次与其他棋手使用这些想法是在高中，当时我开始执教我们国际象棋俱乐部排名最低的棋手。在几个月内，他进步如此之快，以至于他代表学校参加了州锦标赛，并赢得了锦标赛的每一场比赛。

Given that I managed to do this despite my own inexperience and mistakes with studying chess and my own laziness, I’m convinced others can improve much more quickly if they follow this system strictly 4.

考虑到尽管我自己在学习国际象棋方面缺乏经验，犯了错误，而且我自己也很懒，但我还是做到了，我相信如果其他人严格遵循这个系统，他们可以进步得更快。

Since this article is meant for both casual and competitive players, I specify minimum rating requirements when appropriate. If you’re a casual player and this is overkill for your goals, skip to the footnotes for a much simpler system 5.

因为这篇文章是为休闲和竞技选手准备的，所以我在适当的时候指定了最低评级要求。如果你是一个随意的玩家，而这对你的目标来说是矫枉过正的，那么跳过脚注，使用一个简单得多的系统5。

To improve quickly you need to play often. If you are (or aspire to be) a competitive player, play as many over-the-board (OTB) tournaments as possible. In my heyday I played 3-4 tournaments per month. Online is not enough! Use online games (15 minutes per side or slower) to practice openings or for practice if there is no tournament for a while. If you’re a casual player, play OTB chess with your friends as much as you can, and play online if nobody wants to play with you.

要想快速提高，你需要经常上场。如果你是(或渴望成为)一名有竞争力的球员，尽可能多地参加全面(OTB)锦标赛。在我的全盛时期，我每月参加3-4场锦标赛。光在网上是不够的！使用在线游戏(每边15分钟或更慢)来练习开局，如果有一段时间没有锦标赛，也可以进行练习。如果你是一个休闲玩家，尽可能多地和你的朋友玩OTB国际象棋，如果没有人想和你玩，就在网上玩。

Since I first wrote this post, I’ve received a fair number of questions asking why OTB chess is so important and why online-only is insufficient. IM Andras Toth has an excellent discussion of this topic that explains it better than I could  (his channel is also chronically underrated–I highly recommend checking his other videos out!).

自从我第一次写这篇文章以来，我收到了相当多的问题，问为什么OTB国际象棋如此重要，为什么只在网上下棋是不够的。Im Andras Toth对这个话题进行了很好的讨论，比我更好地解释了这个话题(他的频道也长期被低估-我强烈推荐他的其他视频！)。

I did two types of tactics training. The first was “Chess Vision” and “Knight Sight” exercises, as described in this article. They may sound stupid, but they work. I did these exercises every day for two weeks initially, and then would do them the day of a tournament and once in a while as a refresher.

我做了两种战术训练。第一个是本文所描述的“国际象棋视觉”和“骑士视线”练习。它们听起来可能很愚蠢，但它们很管用。起初，我每天都做这些练习，持续了两周，然后在锦标赛当天做，偶尔也会作为补充。

My primary method of tactics training was using Chess Tactics for Beginners, which is absolutely fantastic. Since it may have compatibility issues with modern OSes, a good alternative is CT Art 4.0 available for both Android and iOS.

我的战术训练的主要方法是对初学者使用国际象棋战术，这绝对是奇妙的。由于CT Art4.0可能与现代操作系统存在兼容性问题，因此一个很好的选择是同时支持Android和iOS的CT Art 4.0。

If you only buy one thing to help your chess game, this should be it. I did 50 puzzles per day, every day, and once I finished the entire CD I repeated the process six more times. Online tactics sites usually don’t cut it, because they aren’t structured so that you learn based off previous ideas and many don’t incorporate the pedagogical features of Chess Tactics for Beginners/CT Art 4.0. Trust me, paying for the software is worth it.

如果你只买了一件东西来帮助你的棋局，那么这件应该就是了。我每天做50个拼图游戏，一旦我完成了整张CD，我又重复了这个过程六次。在线战术网站通常不会这么做，因为它们的结构不能让你根据以前的想法来学习，而且许多网站没有把初学者国际象棋战术的教学特点融入到CTArt4.0中。相信我，花钱买软件是值得的。

If I had to recommend a book to accompany such study (which is helpful, since the above software doesn’t actually have any explanatory text), I’d recommend Chess Tactics for the Tournament Player for intermediate players, and Winning Chess Tactics for less experienced players.

如果我必须推荐一本书来配合这样的研究(这很有帮助，因为上面的软件实际上没有任何解释文本)，我会推荐给中级选手的锦标赛选手的国际象棋战术，以及经验较少的棋手的获胜象棋战术。

I’ll admit, there is a bit of a leap between solving tactics puzzles and applying it to real games–obviously nobody’s going to tell you when a tactic is available, and you won’t be “primed” to find tactics the way you would be when solving a bunch of puzzles. To counteract this I created a binder of puzzles taken from tactics I missed in my games, and reviewed them from time to time.

我承认，解决战术难题和将其应用于真正的游戏之间有一点飞跃--很明显，没有人会告诉你什么时候有战术可用，你也不会像解决一堆难题时那样“准备”去寻找战术。为了抵消这一点，我创建了一个拼图活页夹，这些拼图取自我在游戏中错过的战术，并不时地对它们进行审查。

Analysis is by far the most undervalued part of chess training. As a kid I barely analyzed my games after tournaments, because I was lazy. This was a huge mistake—your games are worth their weight in gold! Learn algebraic chess notation so you can write down your moves, and analyze your games using the method outlined in this article. Use the analysis phase to brush up on your openings and endgames and practice your strategic play. If possible, have a stronger player go over your games with you after you’ve done your own analysis.

到目前为止，分析是国际象棋训练中最被低估的部分。当我还是个孩子的时候，我很少在比赛结束后分析我的比赛，因为我很懒。这是一个巨大的错误--你的游戏价值不菲！学习代数象棋记法，这样你就可以写下你的棋，并使用本文概述的方法分析你的棋局。利用分析阶段来温习你的开局和终局，并练习你的战略战术。如果可能的话，在你做完自己的分析之后，找一个实力更强的玩家和你一起检查你的游戏。

One big mistake is to rely heavily on computers for chess analysis. Too often, players use computers as a crutch to replace their own study of the game. Working through games on your own and trying to find the best moves and ideas is highly instructive. Computer analysis should be done only after you analyze the game on your own, so you can compare your analysis to the computer’s and unearth any mistakes you made in assessing critical positions in the game.

一大错误是严重依赖计算机进行国际象棋分析。太多时候，玩家用电脑作为拐杖来取代他们自己对游戏的学习。你自己在游戏中工作，试图找到最好的招数和想法，这是非常有启发性的。电脑分析应该在你自己分析游戏之后进行，这样你就可以将你的分析与电脑的分析进行比较，找出你在评估游戏中关键位置时所犯的任何错误。

One of the biggest mistakes players make is to devote massive amounts of time to openings. This is because openings tend to be very concrete, and beginners think that simply memorizing an opening will give them an unassailable advantage over their opponents 6.

球员们犯的最大错误之一就是把大量的时间花在开局上。这是因为开场白往往非常具体，初学者认为只要记住开场白就能比对手拥有无懈可击的优势。

Don’t bother spending any time studying openings outside of analyzing your games. Just make sure you know the basic opening principles. I teach my beginning students simple openings like the London System as white, and a kingside fianchetto system as black 7. These openings are simple, solid, can be played against virtually anything.

除了分析你的游戏之外，别费心花时间研究开场白。只要你知道基本的开场原则就行了。我教我的初学者简单的开场白，比如伦敦体系是白色的，国王端的五彩灯体系是黑色7。这些开场简单、坚实，几乎可以与任何东西对决。

Once you hit 1600, get a good opening book that gives you both specific moves and the ideas behind the opening. Don’t mindlessly memorize!

一旦你到了1600点，买一本好的开场白，告诉你具体的动作和开场背后的想法。不要盲目背诵！

If you’re a d4 player, I highly recommend Cox’s Starting Out: 1. d4! 8. An offbeat alternative is Summerscale’s A Killer Chess Opening Repertoire.

如果你是D4的玩家，我强烈推荐考克斯的开局：1.D4！8。另一个与众不同的选择是SummerScale的A Killer国际象棋开局曲目。

If you’re an e4 player it gets a bit trickier. The truth is, e4 is simply harder to play than d4 because it’s a lot easier to get in trouble if you don’t play precisely. This makes finding a single volume repertoire book a bit more challening, but here are some options:

如果你是e4玩家，事情就会变得有点棘手。事实是，e4比d4更难玩，因为如果你玩得不准确，它更容易惹上麻烦。这使得寻找一本单卷剧目书变得更加困难，但这里有一些选择：

Alburt’s Chess Openings for White, Explained. I haven’t personally used it, and I’ve heard some reasonable criticism around some of the lines chosen, but I think for most players it’ll probably be a good starting point.

阿尔伯特为怀特揭开了国际象棋的序幕，解释道。我还没有亲自使用过，我听到了一些合理的批评，但我认为对于大多数球员来说，这可能是一个很好的起点。

Emms’s Attacking with 1. e4 is a book I have used and enjoyed during my forays as an e4 player.

埃姆斯的进攻是1。E4是一本我作为E4球员出战时用过的书，我很喜欢这本书。

If you’re a c4 player, you’ll have to do some research on your own to find a good repertoire book. Being a hipster has its downsides.

如果你是一名C4演奏家，你必须自己做一些研究，才能找到一本好的曲目书。做潮人也有不利之处。

Obviously this depends on your opening preferences. Even here openings should not be your main focus. I only consult these books when analyzing my games to see where I deviated from established opening theory, occasionally supplemented by a chess database if there’s a line not covered in the book or I’d like to go more in-depth.

显然，这取决于你的开场喜好。即使在这里，空缺也不应该是你的主要关注点。我只是在分析我的游戏时才会参考这些书，看看我在哪里偏离了既定的开放理论，有时还会补充一个国际象棋数据库，看看书中是否有哪一行没有涉及到，或者我想要更深入地了解。

And if your first thought is, “Gautam, one of those books was published in 2006! I’ll be using outdated opening theory!” then I’m afraid you’re missing the point. If you’re below master or even International Master level, playing what world champions played in 2006 rather than what they played in 2020 will never be the reason you lose a game. Ever. That simply is not your bottleneck, and the time invested to try to constantly keep up with the latest won’t result in any rating gains.

如果你首先想到的是，“高谭市，其中一本书是在2006年出版的！我会用过时的开场白！“。那恐怕你没抓住重点。如果你的水平低于大师赛，甚至低于国际大师赛水平，那么打出世界冠军在2006年的比赛，而不是他们在2020年的比赛，永远不会是你输掉一场比赛的原因。永远不会。这根本不是你的瓶颈，花在不断跟上最新消息上的时间也不会带来任何评级提升。

Until you hit 1400-1500, you should be picking up strategic play from analyzing your games and going over annotated games. Once you hit that level, I recommend Silman’s The Amateur’s Mind and Seirawan’s Winning Chess Strategies.

在你达到1400-1500之前，你应该会从分析你的游戏和阅读注释游戏中学到战略。一旦达到这个级别，我推荐Silman的《业余头脑》和Seirawan的《决胜象棋战略》。

After learning the basic checkmates (King and Queen vs. King, King and Rook vs. King, etc.), Silman’s Complete Endgame Course is the only book you need. Study the appropriate section based on your rating, and only come back to it if it’s clear that you keep messing up endgames.

在学习了基本的死对头(国王和王后对阵国王，国王和路克对阵国王等等)之后，西尔曼完整的终极游戏课程是你唯一需要的书。根据你的评级研究适当的部分，只有当你清楚地知道你一直在搞砸游戏结束的时候才回来看。

Go over at least one annotated game a week (and more frequently if you’re a serious competitive player). A good annotated game book is Winning Chess Brilliancies by Seirawan. I hear the Mammoth Book of the World’s Greatest Chess Games is pretty good too, but I can’t personally vouch for it.

每周至少看一场带注释的比赛(如果你是一名认真的竞争对手，就更频繁地看一次)。一本很好的带注释的游戏书是Seirawan写的《赢得国际象棋比赛》。我听说世界上最伟大的国际象棋游戏的猛犸之书也很不错，但我个人不敢担保。

Magnus Carlsen is my favorite chess player. In equal positions where many grandmasters would agree to a draw, Carlsen patiently pushes and probes, waiting until his opponent cracks and then grinding out a win. Magnus Carlsen is the world’s best player because he doesn’t give up.

马格努斯·卡尔森是我最喜欢的棋手。在许多大师都会同意平局的平等位置上，卡尔森耐心地推进和探索，等待他的对手崩溃，然后努力取得胜利。马格努斯·卡尔森是世界上最好的球员，因为他不放弃。

When I was younger, I had an unfortunate habit of withdrawing from tournaments where I was doing badly. I made various excuses, but usually I withdrew because I had mentally given up after a few demoralizing losses. I did the same thing in chess games—after making a major mistake, I mentally gave up.

在我年轻的时候，我有一个不幸的习惯，就是在我表现不佳的比赛中退出。我找了各种各样的借口，但通常我会退出，因为在经历了几次士气低落的失败后，我在精神上放弃了。我在国际象棋比赛中也做了同样的事情--在犯了一个重大错误后，我在精神上放弃了。

If chess is anything, it is a game of second chances. Chess, like life, rewards perseverance. I’ve turned countless losses into draws and wins because my opponents got overconfident while I dug in. I’ve also turned wins into losses because I was too intimidated by my opponent’s rating or reputation.

如果说国际象棋有什么意义的话，那就是它是一场有第二次机会的游戏。象棋就像生活一样，奖励毅力。我已经把无数的失利变成了平局和胜利，因为当我坚守阵地时，我的对手变得过于自信。我也把胜利变成了失败，因为我被对手的评级或声誉吓坏了。

Study broader topics, like strategy or endgame, only when you feel like that topic is causing you to lose. For instance, only open a strategy book if you keep getting outplayed positionally. Otherwise, your default state should be studying tactics and analyzing your games.

学习更广泛的话题，比如战略或终局，只有当你觉得这个话题让你迷失方向的时候。例如，只有当你在位置上一直处于劣势时，才能打开一本战略书。否则，你的默认状态应该是研究战术和分析比赛。

The tl;dr of this training plan is: play a lot, analyze your games, and primarily study tactics. Your knowledge of openings, endgame, middlegame, etc. will come from analyzing your games and going over grandmaster games. Only study one of those specific topics if it is clear you are specifically losing because of that topic.

这个训练计划的目标是：多打比赛，分析比赛，主要学习战术。你对开局、终局、中间游戏等的了解将来自于分析你的游戏和复习特级大师游戏。只学习其中一个特定的主题，如果很明显你因为那个主题而输了。

This is just a compiled list of all the stuff I recommended in this article, and rating recommendations for each item.

这只是我在这篇文章中推荐的所有东西的汇编清单，以及对每一个条目的评级推荐。

The books will vary depending on your individual opening preferences. It is significantly more difficult to find a single repertoire book for 1 e4 openings that provides adequate depth and breadth, given that e4 is–objectively–a more difficult opening to play.

书籍将根据您个人的开篇喜好而有所不同。考虑到e4客观上是一个更难演奏的开场曲目，要为1个e4开场找到一本提供足够深度和广度的曲目明显要困难得多。

Chess Vision and Knight Sight exercises from 400 Points in 400 Days Part I (all levels)

400天400分象棋视力和骑士视力练习(上)(所有级别)

CT Art 4.0 for Android or iOS (All levels up to ~2000-2200, an alternative if Chess Tactics for Beginners won’t work on your computer)

适用于Android或iOS的CT Art 4.0(2000-2200以下的所有级别，如果初学者的国际象棋战术在您的电脑上不起作用，也可以选择)。

If you’re playing in tournaments, you’ll need three more items: a chess clock (pretty much mandatory, since tournaments don’t provide them), a tournament chess set (sometimes tournaments provide them, sometimes they don’t, and it’s useful to have a set to analyze between rounds), and a scorebook (optional, but highly recommended).

如果你参加锦标赛，你还需要三件物品：一个国际象棋时钟(几乎是强制性的，因为锦标赛不提供它们)，一套锦标赛国际象棋(有时锦标赛提供它们，有时不提供，在两轮之间有一套可以分析是很有用的)，还有一本记分簿(可选，但强烈推荐)。

Chronos Chess Clock: This is the clock most serious players use, because it’s built to last. Mine is ten years old and still running strong, despite lots of drops and falls. Two cheaper clocks I bought before my Chronos eventually broke; in the long run, the Chronos is the cheapest clock to buy. Nonetheless, if $100 is too much, I recommend the DGT North American Clock.

Chronos国际象棋时钟：这是大多数认真的棋手使用的时钟，因为它的设计是经久耐用的。我的车已经十年了，尽管跌倒了很多次，但仍然很健壮。在我的Chronos最终坏掉之前，我买了两个更便宜的钟；从长远来看，Chronos是最便宜的钟。尽管如此，如果100美元太多，我推荐DGT北美时钟。

9 – This doesn’t mean never, ever, resign. If you’re down a queen in an absolutely hopeless position against a strong opponent, it’s good etiquette to resign rather than needlessly drag on the game. A good rule of thumb is to ask yourself, “If my opponent were playing Magnus Carlsen in this position, would Carlsen be able to win?” If the answer is yes, keep playing. If it is no, then resign.

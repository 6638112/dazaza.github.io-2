What Happened After Silicon Valley Tried to Make Telecommuting Permanent
硅谷试图使远程办公永久化之后发生了什么

2020-11-29 16:43:06

After a barrage of criticism from Silicon Valley businesses and Bay Area mayors, Metropolitan Transportation Commission planners have backed off a requirement to have employees from big companies work from home three days a week.

在受到硅谷企业和湾区市长的批评之后，大都会运输委员会的计划者放弃了一项要求，要求大公司的员工每周三天在家工作。

A compromise plan approved unanimously by commissioners Friday calls for big companies to have 60 percent of their employees take sustainable commutes — by transit, bike or carpooling — by 2035. Regional planners are struggling to meet state-mandated cuts to greenhouse gas emissions by the middle of the next decade.

周五，专员们一致通过了一项折衷计划，要求大公司在2035年之前让60％的员工通过运输，骑自行车或拼车的方式进行可持续的通勤。区域规划师正在努力实现国家规定的中间削减温室气体排放的目标未来十年

Supporters say the compromise gives large employers more credit for their efforts to encourage sustainable commuting, and alleviates concerns a work-from-home proposal would decimate transit ridership.

支持者说，这项折衷方案使大型雇主对鼓励可持续通勤的努力给予更多的赞誉，并减轻了对在家工作的提议会减少过境乘车人的担忧。

“The work from home mandate is gone,” said MTC commissioner Nick Josefowitz. The new proposal, he added, “is a lot more flexible. It doesn’t penalize people who walk and bike and take transit.”

MTC专员尼克·约瑟夫维茨（Nick Josefowitz）说：“家庭委托的工作已经消失了。他补充说，新提案“更加灵活。它不会惩罚走路，骑自行车和乘搭公交的人。”

The original proposal called for companies with more than 25 employees to have workers work remotely 60 percent of the time. The goal was to reduce vehicle commmutes, help drive down greenhouse gas emissions and allow the Bay Area to meet state air quality goals.

最初的提案要求拥有25名以上员工的公司让工人60％的时间远程工作。目标是减少车辆通勤，帮助减少温室气体排放，并使海湾地区达到州空气质量目标。

California air quality mandates require the region to cut per capita emissions 19 percent by 2035. In a worst-case scenario, the state could disqualify the region for $100 million in transportation funds as soon as 2022 if the Bay Area fails to present a feasible environmental plan.

加利福尼亚州的空气质量法规要求该地区到2035年将人均排放量削减19％。在最坏的情况下，如果湾区未能提供可行的环境，该州可能会在2022年取消该地区1亿美元的运输资金资格。计划。

The proposal is a small section of the comprehensive report, Plan Bay Area 2050. The report, still in the draft stages, plots a course for the Bay Area to meet transportation, housing, environmental and economic challenges in the decades ahead.

该提案只是全面报告《 2050年海湾地区计划》的一小部分。该报告仍处于草案阶段，为海湾地区应对未来几十年的交通，住房，环境和经济挑战指明了道路。

Lawmakers, mayors and the business community railed against the remote work mandate, saying it would undercut the Bay Area’s economy and encourage large companies to re-locate to cheaper regions. Transit supporters said work-from-home requirements would cut train and bus use without clear proof it would reduce the mileage of vehicle trips and emissions.

议员，市长和工商界反对远程办公授权，称这将削弱湾区的经济，并鼓励大公司搬迁到较便宜的地区。运输部门的支持者说，在家工作的要求会减少火车和公共汽车的使用，而没有明确的证据会减少车辆出行和排放的里程。

The new proposal calls for no more than 40 percent of a company’s workforce to commute by auto on an average workday by 2035. Farms and employers with fewer than 50 workers would be exempt. The plan encourages companies to subsidize transit passes, bikes, on-site employee housing, and commuter shuttles, as well as helping workers afford housing in walkable, transit-rich communities.

新提议要求到2035年，公司平均不超过40％的员工通过汽车上下班。少于50名工人的农场和雇主将被豁免。该计划鼓励公司为过境通行证，自行车，现场员工住房和通勤班车提供补贴，并帮助工人负担得起可步行，交通便利的社区的住房费用。

Many large tech companies like Google and Facebook already provide shuttles and subsidize transit for their workers.

像Google和Facebook这样的许多大型科技公司已经提供班车，并为其员工提供交通补贴。

It also suggests companies discourage workers from single-vehicle commutes by reducing parking spaces and raising parking fees, compressing work schedules and eliminating personal desks in favor of shared work spaces.

它还建议公司通过减少停车位和提高停车费，压缩工作时间表并取消个人办公桌而使用共享工作空间来劝阻单车上下班的工人。

The new proposal was designed with input from state lawmakers, the mayors of San Francisco and San Jose, county supervisors, and officials from the tech industry and transit groups, Josefowitz said.

约瑟夫维茨说，新提案是在州议员，旧金山和圣何塞市市长，县监督以及科技行业和运输组织官员的意见下设计的。

“This is a much more effective policy,” said Josefowitz, chief of policy at the regional think tank SPUR. “This is figuring out how to do it better with everybody at the table.”

“这是一项更为有效的政策，”地区智库SPUR的政策负责人约瑟夫维茨（Josefowitz）说。 “这是在解决如何与餐桌上的每个人都做得更好的问题。”

Gwen Litvak of the business coalition Bay Area Council said the work-from-home mandate would have hurt urban centers and businesses. “The compromise will help revitalize downtowns, and gives business critical flexibility to have workers carpool, use public transit, ride bikes or walk, or even work remotely, but by their own choice,” she said.

商业联盟海湾地区委员会的格温·利特瓦克（Gwen Litvak）表示，在家工作的规定将伤害城市中心和企业。她说：“妥协将有助于振兴市中心，并为企业提供至关重要的灵活性，让员工拼车，使用公共交通，骑自行车或步行，甚至是远程工作，但要自行选择。”

San Jose Mayor Sam Liccardo said the revisions better reflect how his city is evolving — from a suburban, car-centric culture to a city focused on developing a dense commercial and residential core supported by a robust transit network.

圣何塞市市长山姆·利卡多（Sam Liccardo）说，修订内容更好地反映了他所在城市的发展方式-从郊区，以汽车为中心的文化，到专注于发展由强大的公交网络支持的密集商业和住宅核心的城市。

“It’s much better,” he said. “It gives the employers the flexibility they need.”

他说：“好多了。” “它为雇主提供了所需的灵活性。”

Google has already invested billions in San Jose to develop an environmentally-friendly urban campus, with high-rise offices and residences near Diridon Station. Salesforce is even further along in its urban projects in San Francisco.

Google已经在圣何塞投资数十亿美元，以开发一个环保城市校园，并在Diridon Station附近设有高层办公室和住宅。 Salesforce在旧金山的城市项目进一步发展。

Liccardo said part of Silicon Valley’s success springs from having talented employees working side-by-side, exchanging ideas and innovations. Remote work reduces some creative energy. “We cannot impose mandates that contradict the laws of human nature and the laws of creative industry,” he said.

利卡多说，硅谷成功的部分源于让才华横溢的员工并肩工作，交流思想和创新。远程工作减少了一些创造力。他说：“我们不能施加与人性定律和创意产业定律相抵触的命令。”

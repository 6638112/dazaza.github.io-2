US backs waiving patents on Covid vaccines
美国支持Covid疫苗的免除专利

2021-05-07 03:24:20

In a historic move, the US government has announced that it supports waiving patent protections for COVID-19 vaccines, a measure aimed at boosting supplies so that people around the world can get the shots. “The extraordinary circumstances of the COVID-19 pandemic call for extraordinary measures,” said US trade representative Katherine Tai in a statement.

在历史性的举动中，美国政府宣布它支持放弃Covid-19疫苗的专利保护，这一措施旨在提高供应，以便世界各地的人们可以获得镜头。 “Covid-19大流行呼吁出于非凡措施的非凡情况，”美国贸易代表凯瑟琳泰在一份声明中表示。

The move came on 5 May, the first of a two-day meeting of the general council of the World Trade Organization, based in Geneva, Switzerland. Until now, the United States, the European Union, the United Kingdom and Japan have blocked efforts brought by India and South Africa to make it legal to manufacture generic versions of COVID-19 vaccines.

该举措于5月5日，该举措是世界贸易组织总体议会的第一个，总部设在瑞士日内瓦。到目前为止，美国，欧洲联盟，英国和日本已阻止印度和南非带来的努力，使其合法地制造Covid-19疫苗的通用版本。

Former US presidents from both the Republican and the Democratic parties have staunchly defended intellectual-property rights, so the move by the administration of President Joe Biden has shocked people on both sides of the debate. “This marks a major shift in US policy in a pro-public-health way,” says Matthew Kavanagh, a global-health researcher at Georgetown University in Washington DC.

来自共和党和民主党派的前美国总统持久地捍卫了知识产权，所以乔·拜登总统的行政举动在辩论的双方震惊。 “这标志着美国乔治城大学全球卫生研究员Matthew Kavanagh说道，这标志着美国政策的重大转变，”华盛顿特区乔治城大学全球卫生研究员Matthew Kavanagh说。

Kavanagh is part of the growing chorus of health-policy and global-health researchers advocating patent waivers, as the gap between vaccination rates in rich and poor nations grows larger every day. Fewer than 1% of people in low-income countries have received COVID-19 vaccines. The researchers are quick to note, however, that a waiver on patents covering all aspects of COVID-19 vaccines would be just the first step in ramping up vaccine supply.

Kavanagh是倡导豁免的卫生政策和全球卫生研究人员越来越多的合唱的一部分，因为富人和贫穷国家的疫苗接种率之间的差距每天增长更大。低收入国家的少于1％的人已经收到了Covid-19疫苗。然而，研究人员很快就注明，涉及Covid-19疫苗的各个方面的专利豁免只是升高疫苗供应的第一步。

“It’s a one-two-three,” explains Rachel Cohen, US director for the non-profit Drugs and Neglected Diseases initiative in New York City. “First we need to remove patent obstacles, second we need to transfer the knowledge on how to make them, and step three is a massive investment in manufacturing capacity.”

“这是一二三三，”纽约市的非营利药和被忽视疾病倡议的雷切尔科恩·科赫尔科恩·科伦解释道。 “首先，我们需要去除专利障碍，其次需要转移关于如何制造它们的知识，第三步是制造能力的大规模投资。”

And at the moment, step one is far from complete. The World Trade Organization will not negotiate the details of which patents to adjust until all its member countries agree on some sort of waiver. Health-policy analysts speculate that other countries will follow in the footsteps of the United States, although the European Union might hold out beyond the end of the meeting. South Africa and India have proposed waivers on patents, not only on vaccines, but also on COVID-19-related medical devices, drugs and diagnostic technologies; so far, Tai’s statement mentions only vaccines.

而目前，第一步是远非完整的。世界贸易组织不会谈判详细说明，直到所有成员国就某种豁免达成一致。健康政策分析师推测，其他国家将在美国的脚步，尽管欧洲联盟可能超出会议结束时。南非和印度拟议在专利豁免，不仅在疫苗上，而且还在Covid-19相关的医疗装置，药物和诊断技术上;到目前为止，Tai的声明仅提到疫苗。

Drugmakers and others who oppose the measure say that waivers sabotage companies’ enormous investments in drug and vaccine development, which are compensated by their ability to set the price on products that they exclusively own. Normally, patents reward pharmaceutical companies by protecting their inventions from competition by generics for a limited time — US patents on drugs typically last for 20 years.

反对措施的药物制造商和其他人表示，豁免破坏遗产的巨大投资药物和疫苗开发，这通过他们为他们独家拥有的产品的能力来补偿。通常情况下，专利奖励制药公司通过保护他们的发明在普通的竞争中掌握有限的时间 - 美国药物专利通常持续20年。

Drug companies aren’t the only opponents of the measure. In a 25 April interview with Sky News, global health philanthropist and Microsoft co-founder Bill Gates argued against intellectual-property waivers, saying that manufacturers of generics couldn’t ramp up production quickly, and that vaccine quality could be compromised. After the US government's waiver announcement, the industry group Pharmaceutical Research and Manufacturers of America released a statement echoing these points, saying: “The Biden Administration has taken an unprecedented step that will undermine our global response to the pandemic and compromise safety.”

毒品公司不是唯一的措施对手。在4月25日采访中，全球卫生慈善家和微软联合创始人比尔盖茨争辩说，普通的豁免，称，仿古制造商无法快速增加产量，并且疫苗质量可能会受到损害。在美国政府之后，豁免公告，美国产业集团制药商研究和美国制造商发布了一份声明这些要点，称：“拜登政府采取了前所未有的一步，将破坏我们对大流行和妥协安全的全球反应。“

Proponents of the waiver disagree, pointing out that generics manufacturers have been supplying the world with high-quality vaccines and medicines for years. They point out that taxpayers helped to foot the bill for the development of several COVID-19 vaccines, and say that the claim that pharmaceutical companies must recoup all the costs is therefore unfair — especially during a crisis. Several other obstacles must be addressed, however, such as making sure distribution is equitable.

豁免的支持者不同意，指出仿制机制造商一直在为世界提供高质量的疫苗和药物。他们指出，纳税人有助于衡量几个Covid-19疫苗的票据，并说制药公司必须收回所有费用的索赔是不公平的 - 特别是在危机期间。然而，必须解决其他几个障碍，例如确保分发是公平的。

Cohen says: “These vaccines are an unparalleled triumph for science, but if only 20% or 30% of the world winds up benefiting, what is the point of the innovation?”

科恩说：“这些疫苗是科学的一个无与伦比的胜利，但如果只有20％或30％的世界迎接受益，那么创新的观点是什么？”

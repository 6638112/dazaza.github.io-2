A Mycological Foray: A new look at John Cage and his mushroom obsession (2020)
一个Mycological Foray：John Cage和他的蘑菇痴迷（2020年）

2021-06-12 13:52:01

The twentieth-century music composer John Cage was famous for his experimental approach to sounds, silences, and everything in between.  As it happens, Cage was also an avowed mushroom aficionado and the inspiration he drew from fungi played no small part in his work. Almost three decades after his death, the influence of his mycological interests on his biography is indisputable.  Here, in the twenty-first century, it’s impossible to talk about Cage’s music and theory without talking about the way that fungi permeates it.

二十世纪的音乐作曲家John Cage以他的声音，沉默和两者之间的一切而闻名。正如它所发生的那样，笼子也是一个公开的蘑菇Aficionado，他从真菌中吸引了他的作用，在他的工作中没有任何一小部分。他去世后的近三十年，他对他的传记的影响力是无可争辩的。在二十一世纪，在二十一世纪，不可能谈论笼子的音乐和理论而不谈论真菌渗透它的方式。

Cage’s life is full of oft-repeated mushroom anecdotes.  How he got his start in mushroom foraging during the Great Depression because he was a broke and hungry student.  (In his writings, Cage describes passing out from fatigue after eating only mushrooms for an entire week.)  How social mushroom forays lead to intellectual collaborations and theoretical developments.  (Cage met one of his students, Allan Kaprow, while on a mushroom hunt organized by American artist George Segal.)  How he created a guide for edible mushrooms near Stony Point, New York.  (His class on mushroom identification at the New School for Social Research was immensely popular.)  How Cage accidentally poisoned himself and some friends – Cage actually had to have his stomach pumped — after he mistakenly identified a poisonous hellebore (in the buttercup family) as skunk cabbage.

笼子的生命充满了重复的蘑菇轶事。他如何在大萧条期间在蘑菇觅食中开始，因为他是一个破产和饥饿的学生。 （在他的着作中，笼子描述了一整个星期只吃蘑菇后从疲劳中消失。）社交蘑菇的出现方式如何导致智力合作和理论发展。 （笼子遇到了他的一名学生，艾伦卡普罗，而在由美国艺术家乔治Segal组织的蘑菇狩猎。）他如何创建了纽约石港附近的食用蘑菇指南。 （他在新学校的蘑菇鉴定社会研究中的课程非常受欢迎。）笼子意外地毒害了自己，一些朋友 - 笼子实际上必须让他的胃泵送 - 在他错误地确定有毒的嚏根博（在毛茛家族中）之后臭鼬白菜。

In one particularly famous episode, in February 1959, Cage appeared on the Italian television program Lascia o Raddoppio ( Double or Nothing) and won five million lire (something like eight thousand dollars) by being able to name 24 white-spored agarics — edible mushrooms — that were mentioned in the Studies of American Fungi field guide.  Cage listed them in alphabetical order and then bought a Volkswagen bus for his partner, choreographer Merce Cunningham, and a piano for his home in Stony Point.

在一个特别着名的剧集中，1959年2月，笼子出现在意大利电视节目Lascia o Radoppio（双人物或全部），并通过能够命名24个白刺激 - 食用蘑菇，赢得了五百万个莱尔（像八千美元这样的东西） - 这在美国真菌场地指南的研究中提到。笼子按字母顺序列出了它们，然后为他的伴侣，编舞员Merce Cunningham和钢琴买了一辆大众车，为他的家处于石头点。

Perhaps more than anything else, however, mushrooms — especially in conjunction with Cage’s life, compositions, and approach to music theory — are full of metaphor.  From foraging and accidental encounter to death, decay, and chance itself, the lens of “mushrooms as metaphors” offers contemporary audiences insight into how Cage’s mycological expertise shaped his music and vice-versa.  In Atelier Éditions’ recent two-volume publication, John Cage: A Mycological Foray (edited by Ananda Pellerin, with texts by Kingston Trinder & Isabelle Bucklow) is a carefully curated exhibition of the experimental composer’s enchantment with fungi.

然而，也许更多的是蘑菇 - 特别是与笼子的生命，组成和音乐理论的方法相结合 - 充满了比喻。从觅食和偶然遭遇死亡，腐烂和机会本身，“蘑菇作为隐喻”的镜片提供了当代观众的洞察笼子的Mycological专业知识如何形成他的音乐，反之亦然。在AtelierÉditions的“最近的二批出版物”，John Cage：一个Mycological Foray（由Ananda Pellerin编辑，Kungston Trinder＆amp的文本）是伊莎贝尔巴克洛的文本）是与真菌的实验组合物魅力的精心策划展。

The first volume in Mycological Foray offers readers essays, photographs, archival ephemera, drawings, and excerpts from Cage’s work Diary: How to Improve the World (You Will Only Make Matters Worse).  The meandering, structure-free approach of both Cage’s writing and the editing of  Foray allow readers to explore through the bits and pieces of Cage’s creative progression, a process much like mushroom foraging itself.  Every page offers audiences a new way to see fungi as Cage saw them – something to be discovered, and the organism through which the natural world mediates between life and death.

Mycological Foray中的第一卷提供读者散文，照片，档案ePhemera，图纸和凯奇的工作日记中的摘录：如何改善世界（你只会更加糟糕）。蜿蜒的，笼子写作的蜿蜒，无结构的方法以及Foray的编辑允许读者通过笼子和笼子的创造性进展探索，一个像蘑菇觅食本身的过程。每个页面都提供观众作为笼子看到的真菌的新方式 - 要发现的东西，以及自然世界在生死之间调解的生物体。

“It’s that sense of discovering the hidden that the amateur mycologist found so thrilling about mushroom hunting,” writes  Allison Meir.  Cage described his intentionally non-structured approach to his Mushroom Book (1972) in a 1991 interview with John Retallack.  This style of writing was meant to show, “that ideas are to be found in the same way that you find wild mushrooms in the forest, by just looking.” In other words, ideas, like mushrooms, “come to you as things hidden.”  It’s through sharpening the senses (specifically through sight and touch) via mushroom foraging that Cage would have us also explore and contemplate sound.

“发现业余菌兵发现如此激动人的蘑菇狩猎，这就是发现隐藏的感觉，”艾莉森梅尔写道。笼子在1991年与John Retallack采访中描述了他对他的蘑菇书（1972）的故意非结构化的方法。这种写作风格旨在表现出来，“只要看着森林里发现野生蘑菇，就会发现想法。”换句话说，想法，如蘑菇，“像隐藏的东西一样来找你。”通过蘑菇觅食，笼子会让我们探索和思考声音，这是通过锐化的感官（特别是通过视线和触摸）。

Mushrooms are symbols of change; they are the organism and the process that breaks down once-living things, breaks them down into their most elemental bits (carbon, nitrogen, etc.), and returns them to the environment’s nutrient ecosystem.   Like the chance-controlled music that Cage became known for his philosophy of fungi isn’t far behind.  (The I Ching – a classic Chinese text about decision-making that depended on divination by casting lots – featured highly in Cage’s creative work.)  The chance of encounter — will we find mushrooms on a foraging trip? or not? — to say nothing of the type of encounter  between humans and mushrooms — are these poisonous? are they edible? — opens up the complexity of human-non-human interaction.  Cage’s mesostic poem in Mycological Foray’s first volume, “MUSHROOMS et Variationes,” is composed to capture the randomness and chance of mushroom encounters.

蘑菇是变化的象征;它们是生物体和流亡一次生物的过程，将它们分成最源头（碳，氮等），并将其返回环境的营养生态系统。就像机会控制的音乐一样，笼子因为他的真菌哲学而闻名并未落后。 （我是一个关于由铸造批量占用的决策的经典文本 - 在笼子的创造性工作中具有高度专注。）遇到的机会 - 我们会在觅食之旅上找到蘑菇吗？或不？ - 没有人类和蘑菇之间的遭遇类型 - 这些有毒吗？他们是食用吗？ - 开辟了人类非人类互动的复杂性。笼子在Mycological foray的第一个体积中的角化诗“蘑菇Et变异”，组成用于捕获蘑菇遇到的随机性和机会。

There’s a duality to fungi on many levels — life and decay, rottenness but resilience, a transgressive disgust or danger — that is juxtaposed with a chic bohemian-ness to Cage’s midcentury mushroom foraging events. Every photograph, excerpt, poem, and essay in John Cage: A Mycological Foray can be unpacked to find the duality that Cage emphasized was inherent in the organisms themselves.  On an even more literal level, the negotiation between composition and decomposition continues as the second section of John Cage: A Mycological Foray is written on paper made from recycled apple processing waste.  The idea seems to take a page, as it were, from Cage’s own Edible Drawings series (1990).  The choice in materiality reflects the curatorial consistency with John Cage’s artistic vision.

在许多级别的真菌中有二元性 - 生活和衰退，腐烂，诽谤，恢复力，违法的厌恶或危险 - 与别致的波西米亚 - 笼子的笼子中的Midcantury蘑菇觅食事件并列。每张照片，摘录，诗歌和约翰笼中的论文：一个Mycological foray可以解开，以找到笼子强调的二元性在生物体中固有。在更具文字层面，作文与分解之间的谈判继续作为John Cage的第二部分：在回收的苹果处理废物制成的纸上写了一个Mycological Foray。这个想法似乎拿了一页，因为它来自笼子自己的可食用图案系列（1990）。唯物性的选择反映了与John Cage的艺术愿景的策略一致性。

A significant part of what made John Cage’s compositions and music theory so compelling was the deconstruction of expected structure — sounds and silences weren’t where one expected to find them.  And just as fungi breaks down structure in nature, John Cage: A Mycological Foray offers readers a free-ranging look into Cage’s life, carefully framed through his lifelong fascination with nature’s best deconstructionists.

使John Cage的组成和音乐理论如此引人注目的重要组成部分是对预期结构的解构 - 声音和沉默并不是希望找到它们的地方。与真菌在自然中打破结构时，约翰笼子：一个Mycological foray为读者提供了一个自由程度的笼子的生活，通过他的终身迷恋与自然的最佳解构者造成终身迷恋。

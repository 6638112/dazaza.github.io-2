Mall department stores were struggling; the pandemic has pushed them to the edge

Adrienne Whyte used to go to the mall twice a week, where she might meet up with her personal shoppers at Neiman Marcus and Saks Fifth Avenue or scour Macy’s for bedding and kitchenware.

Adrienne Whyte曾经每周两次去商场，在那里她可能会在内马马坎马克斯的个人购物者见面，并坐在床上用品和厨具中萨克斯第五大道或刷新梅西。

But it’s been well over a year since she set foot in a department store — and she isn’t sure when, or whether, she will again.

但是自从她在百货商店踏上了一年以来，这一年时间已经好了 - 她不确定，或者是否再次。

“Now if I need something, I buy it online,” said the 72-year-old retiree from Falls Church, Va. “The department store is a one-stop shop, but so is the Internet.”

“现在，如果我需要一些东西，我在线购买它，”来自VA的瀑布教堂的72岁的退休人士说，“百货商店是一站式商店，但互联网也是如此。”

Department stores, once a middle-class mainstay of convenience and indulgence, had been spiraling downward long before the pandemic turbocharged online shopping and helped tip a number of big-name retailers into bankruptcy. Nearly 200 department stores have disappeared in the past year alone, and another 800 — or about half the country’s remaining mall-based locations — are expected to be shuttered by the end of 2025, according to commercial real estate firm Green Street.

Those closures, analysts say, will have a cascading effect on American shopping malls, which already are battling record-high vacancy rates and precipitous drops in foot traffic, as well as on the commercial real estate market and the broader economy.

分析师表示，这些封闭物将对美国商场有一种级联影响，这些商场已经正在战斗的记录高空闲率和脚踏交通的陡峭下降，以及商业房地产市场和更广泛的经济。

“There’s nothing department stores have done to make themselves particularly relevant in the 21st century, and the pandemic has only made that more clear,” said Mark Cohen, director of retail studies at Columbia Business School and former chief executive of Sears Canada. “They have too many stores, too many things, too many brands. The customer who used to be handcuffed to their local department store is no longer tethered because they have an online alternative that’s become even more attractive in the last year.”

“没有任何百货公司在21世纪做出特别相关的事情，大流行只会更加清楚，”哥伦比亚商学院零售研究主任Mark Cohen表示，哥伦比亚加拿大前任首席执行官Mark Cohen说。 “他们有太多的商店，太多的东西，太多的品牌。曾经被送到当地百货商店的客户不再被束缚，因为他们在线替代方案在去年变得更加有吸引力。“

The pandemic set off an economic chain reaction that rippled through the country’s department store chains, forcing several into Chapter 11 proceedings. Neiman Marcus, Stage Stores and J.C. Penney filed for bankruptcy last May, followed by Lord & Taylor and, most recently, Belk in February. Even companies on relatively stable footing, like Macy’s, are shuttering dozens of stores as they try to move away from traditional shopping malls. Overall sales at department stores plunged more than 40 percent at the beginning of the pandemic and have yet to make up for lost ground, according to Commerce Department data, as Americans do more of their shopping online and gravitate to specialty brands and discount chains.

Major brands like Macy’s, Nordstrom and Kohl’s have reported steep sales declines during the coronavirus crisis, which slashed demand for the clothing, shoes and formalwear that disproportionately fill their stores. However, analysts say, the pandemic’s most disconcerting legacy may be its imprint on consumer behavior, raising questions about the sector’s ability to win back customers and properly rebound even after life returns to normal.

梅西等主要品牌，Nordstrom和Kohl在冠状病毒危机中据报道陡峭的销售下降，这削减了对衣服，鞋子和正式填补他们商店的衣服的需求。然而，分析人士说，大流行最令人不安的遗产可能是对消费者行为的印记，提高了关于该部门赢回客户的能力，即使在生活恢复正常后也会纠正反弹的问题。

Whyte, for example, has built up a roster of specialty retailers she now buys from directly: Rothy’s for shoes, Lands’ End for clothing, Sephora for makeup. The former management consultant also has found that she can simply do with less.

“It used to be ‘see it, want it,'” she said. “But during the pandemic, I’ve realized I don’t need as much as I thought I did.”

The earliest American department store, Arnold Constable, was founded nearly 200 years ago in Manhattan. It sold an array of clothing, jewelry, handbags and stationery, earning it the nickname “the palace of trade.” The chain eventually expanded throughout New York and into Pennsylvania and New Jersey. It ended its run in 1975, after filing for bankruptcy.

最早的美国百货商店阿诺德警员在曼哈顿近200年前成立。它销售了一系列服装，珠宝，手提包和文具，赚取它的绰号“贸易宫”。该连锁最终在整个纽约拓展，进入宾夕法尼亚州和新泽西州。它在申请破产后，它于1975年结束了。

By then, Americans had fallen in love with department stores. Shopping malls proliferated across the country, anchored by national chains including Macy’s, J.C. Penney, Marshall Field’s and Bealls that offered the ease of one-stop shopping and an air of attainable luxury.

到那时，美国人曾经爱上了百货商店。在全国各地的商场增殖，由国内连锁店锚定，包括梅西，J.C.Penney，Marshall领域和贝尔尔，提供了一站式购物和可实现的奢华的空气。

But such retailers have lost much of their allure in the past decade, analysts say, as Americans were presented with more alternatives, including discounters like TJ Maxx and Ross Dress for Less and online specialty retailers like Stitch Fix and Everlane. The erosion of the middle class has also chipped away at the fortunes of chains such as Macy’s and J.C. Penney.

但是，由于美国人介绍了更多的替代方案，这些零售商在过去十年中失去了大部分诱惑，包括更多替代品，包括TJ Maxx和Ross连衣裙等折扣员，如针线固定和everlane。中产阶级的侵蚀也在梅西和J.C.Penney等连锁店的财富中剔除。

“In the old days, department stores offered convenience,” said Greg Portell, a partner in the consumer and retail practice of consulting firm Kearney. “But now that we can get that without leaving the couch, it’s no longer enough. Department stores have to offer more: They need smart curation, high-touch service and personalization.”

“在过去的咨询公司Kearney咨询公司消费者和零售实践的合伙人的合作伙伴，Greg Portell表示，”百货商店提供了便利。“ “但是现在我们可以在不离开沙发的情况下得到它，它不再足够了。百货商店必须提供更多：他们需要智能策策，高触控服务和个性化。“

To that end, Saks Fifth Avenue is spinning off its website into a stand-alone company, in hopes of doubling down on e-commerce. Nordstrom is offering virtual styling appointments and recently launched an online channel, where shoppers can buy merchandise during live-streamed events.

为此，SAKS第五大道正在将其网站上的网站旋转到一个独立的公司中，希望在电子商务上倍增。 Nordstrom正在提供虚拟造型约会，最近推出了一个在线频道，购物者可以在现场流动的活动期间购买商品。

At Neiman Marcus, the first department store chain to file for bankruptcy during the pandemic, executives say they have invested heavily in e-commerce, hosting “shoppable” virtual events and making store employees available by text, email and video chat.

在Neiman Marcus，第一个部门商店链条申请破产，在大流行期间，高管表示，他们已经在电子商务中投入了大量的投资，托管“可购物”虚拟事件，并通过文本，电子邮件和视频聊天提供商店员工。

“We’ve never had to make so many changes at once like we did in the past year,” said Lana Todorovich, president and chief merchandising officer of the Dallas-based retailer. “Everything, including our product assortments, changed in an incredibly short period of time.”

“我们从来没有像在过去一年那样曾经做过这么多的改变，”基于达拉斯的零售商总裁兼首席商品官Lana Todorovich说。 “一切都在包括我们的产品分类，在令人难以置信的短时间内变化。”

The company, she said, is stocking its shelves more frequently with “buy now, wear now” products and is seeing a resurgence in bridal wear, as well as swimwear and jewelry.

她说，该公司更频繁地使用“立即购买”的产品，并在新娘佩戴和泳装和珠宝中看到复兴。

Overall retail spending rebounded sharply in March, rising 9.8 percent after an unexpected dip in February, the Commerce Department reported this week. Department store sales rose 13 percent from a month earlier, boosted by recent stimulus checks and burgeoning demand for clothing and shoes, though they have yet to reach pre-pandemic levels.

But analysts say retailers’ challenges extend beyond short-term sales figures. They also must grapple with questions about the viability of shopping malls, for which vacancy rates hit 11.4 percent in the first quarter, compared with 10.5 percent the preceding three months, marking the biggest spike on record, according to Moody’s Analytics’ commercial real estate division.

“The department store as an all-encompassing emporium is a product of the 20th century and a victim of the 21st,” said Cohen of Columbia Business School. The best will prevail, he added, because people are not going to settle as they once did, “simply because they don’t have to.”

胡须税

2020-10-26 14:02:55

Jump to navigation Jump to search A beard tax is one of several taxes introduced throughout history on men who wear beards.

In 1535, King Henry VIII of England, who wore a beard himself, introduced a tax on beards. The tax was a graduated tax, varying with the wearer's social position. His daughter, Elizabeth I of England, also reintroduced the beard tax, taxing every beard of more than two weeks' growth, although it was poorly enforced. [1] Contemporary documentation of the Tudor beard tax, however, is lacking, and The National Archives has no record of such a tax having been instituted. [2]

1535年，英国国王亨利八世(King Henry VIII)自己也留着胡子，他引入了胡须税。这种税是一种累进税，根据佩戴者的社会地位不同而不同。他的女儿，英格兰的伊丽莎白一世，也重新引入了胡须税，对每一根超过两周的胡须都征税，尽管这项税收执行不力。[1]然而，缺乏都铎胡德税的现代文件，国家档案馆也没有开征这种税的记录。[2]。

In 1698, [3] Emperor Peter I of Russia instituted a beard tax to bring Russian society in line with Western European models. To enforce the ban on beards, the tsar empowered police to forcibly and publicly shave those who refused to pay the tax. [4] Resistance to going clean shaven was widespread with many believing that it was a religious requirement for a man to wear a beard. [5] The tax levied depended upon the status of the bearded man: those associated with the Imperial Court, military, or government were charged 60 rubles annually; wealthy merchants were charged 100 rubles per year while other merchants and townsfolk were charged 60 rubles per year; Muscovites were charged 30 rubles per year; and peasants were charged two half-kopeks every time they entered a city. [6]

1698年，[3]俄国皇帝彼得一世开征了胡须税，以使俄国社会与西欧模式保持一致。为了执行对胡须的禁令，沙皇授权警察强行公开为那些拒绝纳税的人剃毛。[4]人们普遍抵制把胡子刮得干干净净，许多人认为留胡子是宗教上的要求。[5]征税取决于留胡子的人的身份：与朝廷、军队或政府有关的人每年被征收60卢布；富商每年被征收100卢布，而其他商人和市民每年被征收60卢布；莫斯科人每年被征收30卢布；农民每次进入城市都被征收两个半戈比。[6]。

Those who paid the tax were required to carry a "beard token" (Бородовой знак). [7] This was a copper or silver token with a Russian Eagle on the reverse and on the obverse the lower part of a face with nose, mouth, whiskers, and beard. Several different versions were minted between the issuance of the decree and its lifting in 1772. [8] The common round version was inscribed with the words "money taken" (ДЕНГИ ВЗѦТЫ) on the obverse and the date in Cyrillic numerals (҂АѰЕ ГОДѸ, "Year 1705") on the reverse. A rhomboid version issued in 1725 was smooth on the reverse with the phrase "beard tax taken" (СБОРОДЫ ПОШЛИНА ВЗЯТА) on the obverse and "the beard is a superfluous burden" (БОРОДА ЛИШЬНАѦ ТѦГОТА) on the edge. [9] [10] Walter Hawkins published a paper in 1845 illustrating an example of the token from his own collection, and describing the history of the tax in Russia. [11]

那些缴税的人被要求携带胡须代币(Бородовойзнак)。[7]这是一枚铜币或银币，背面是一只俄国鹰，正面是一张长着鼻子、嘴巴、胡须和胡须的脸的下半部分。从颁布法令到1772年解除法令，铸造了几个不同的版本。[8]普通圆形版正面刻有Money Take(ДЕНГИВЗѦТЫ)字样，背面刻有西里尔数字日期(҂АѰЕГОДѸ，#34；Year 1705&34；)。1725年发行的菱形版背面流畅，正面写着胡须征税(СБОРОДЫПОШЛИНАВЗЯТА)，边缘写着胡须是多余的负担(БОРОДАЛИШЬНАѦТѦГОТА)。[9]沃尔特·霍金斯(Walter Hawkins)在1845年发表了一篇论文，举例说明了他自己收藏的代币的一个例子，并描述了俄罗斯的税收历史。[11]。

What science fiction can do
什么科幻小说可以做些什么

2021-04-11 14:41:38

It has become axiomatic to say that the world is becoming like science fiction (sf). From mobile phones that speak to us (reminding Star Trek fans of tricorders), to genetically modified foods, to the Internet of Things and the promise of self-driving cars, people in industrialized nations live immersed in technology. Daily life can thus at times seem like visions from the pulp sf of the 1920s and 1930s—either a world perfected by technology, manifested in events such as the 1939 World’s Fair, with its theme “The World of Tomorrow”; or a dystopian nightmare, such as Aldous Huxley’s Brave New World (1932).

它已成为公理的话，说世界变得像科幻小说（SF）。从与我们交谈的手机（提醒Star Trek Fans的Tricorders），转基因食品，到互联网和自驾车的承诺，工业化国家的人们沉浸在技术中。因此，日常生活似乎似乎是来自20世纪20年代和20世纪20年代的纸浆SF的愿景 - 无论是由技术完善的世界，都在1939年世界公平之类的事件中表现为主题“明天的世界”;或者是一种杜塞府的噩梦，如Aldous Huxley的勇敢新世界（1932年）。

Although there may be no precise demarcation of what the term science fiction denotes, certain images or narratives immediately come to mind: enhanced mobility via rocket ships or flying cars; immersion in digitized environments, from virtual entertainments to perhaps digitally transferring one’s consciousness to a new body; and the idea of the future, which may include radically different citizens such as self-conscious robots or genetically modified people, perhaps even aliens. A set of images thus conveys the core of science fiction, even if the boundaries at its edges are indistinct, a situation further complicated by the fact that different communities have diverse ideas in mind when they claim the label and try to define the genre it describes. Many fans and scholars of sf, for example, argue that its print form is inevitably more nuanced than media versions, a claim confusing to twenty-first-century audiences for whom “science fiction” is often synonymous with widely known media texts such as Star Trek, which has been on television or film screens in myriad iterations since 1966.

虽然术语科幻小说表示可能没有精确的划分，但立即想到某些图像或叙述：通过火箭船或飞行汽车增强流动性;从虚拟娱乐中沉浸在数字化环境中，从虚拟娱乐活动中可能以数字方式转移到一个新的身体;和未来的想法，这可能包括自然的机器人或遗传修饰的人类等自然不同的公民，也许是外星人。因此，一组图像传达了科幻小说的核心，即使其边缘的边界是模糊的，这种情况进一​​步复杂的是不同的社区在他们声称标签上索取的不同想法并尝试定义它描述的类型。例如，SF的许多粉丝和学者认为，它的印刷形式比媒体版本更细致，令人难以置疑，令人困惑的是“科幻小说”为“科幻小说”的观众往往是与明星之类的广泛知名媒体文本的同义词艰苦跋涉，自1966年以来一直在无数迭代的电视或电影屏幕上。

Idiosyncratic definitions and squabbles about the minutiae of genre borders will thus not get us very far. Science fiction is better conceptualized as a tendency, a phenomenon Gary Wolfe describes as a style that evaporates into and permeates much cultural production today. Istvan Csiscery-Ronay Jr. argues that we should understand sf as a mode rather than a genre, a way of experiencing the world that has become normalized in recent decades. He uses the term “science fictionality” to describe this way of perceiving and evaluating things as “if they were aspects of a work of science fiction.”

关于流派边界的细节的特殊定义和争吵将不会让我们远远得到我们。科幻小说更概念化作为一种​​趋势，戈里沃尔夫的现象描述为今天蒸发和渗透到今天的许多文化生产的风格。 Istvan Csiscery-Ronay Jr.认为我们应该理解SF作为一种模式而不是一种类型，一种经历近几十年来归一成的世界的方式。他利用“科学小说”一词来描述这种方式，以使这种方式感知和评估“如果他们是科幻小说的方面”。

These struggles over terminology illuminate one of the central tensions that has shaped the sf community as long as we have used this label: Is science fiction predominantly a genre that has to do with plausible scientific extrapolation, perhaps even with educating the public in scientific literacy? Or is it predominantly a literature of social change, often using futuristic technologies to establish that its stories take place in different worlds, but remaining more interested in social than scientific change? And how might sf help us conceptualize and respond to a world that has begun to resemble sf, in ways both marvelous and malign?

这些斗争术语术语照亮了一种形状的中央紧张局势，只要我们使用这个标签，这些标签是主要的，主要是一种与合理的科学推断有关的类型，也许甚至与教育公众在科学扫盲中呢？或者主要是社会变革的文献，通常利用未来派技术来确定其在不同的世界中发生的故事，但对社会的更感兴趣而不是科学的变化？ SF如何帮助我们概念化并回应已经开始类似于SF的世界，以奇妙和恶意的方式？

My preference is to understand science fiction as a cultural form that offers an “everyday” language for thinking about and responding to daily life in twenty-first century. Its engagement with science and with the motifs of sociotechnical culture—interplanetary travel, digitized experience and communications, genomic modification, artificial intelligence (AI), and more—is metaphorical, whether the worlds it posits might actually happen or not. The genre asks questions about the impact of science and technology on human experience, values, and ways of living, and even when it explores these issues through scenarios that science tells us are impossible, the genre uses such symbols to comment upon otherwise unnoticed aspects of our ordinary world.

我的偏好是理解科幻小说作为一种文化形式，提供“日常”语言，用于在二十一世纪的日常生活中思考和应对日常生活。它与科学与社会科学文化行星的主题接触，数字化经验和通信，基因组修改，人工智能（AI）以及更多的隐喻，是隐喻，无论是世界它是否可能实际发生或不发生。这些类型提出了关于科学和技术对人类经验，价值观和生活方式的影响的问题，即使通过科学告诉我们不可能的情况探讨这些问题，这种类型使用这些符号对其他不受伤的方面进行评论我们的普通世界。

Grasping what science fiction is then, requires understanding that this tension between scientific extrapolation and social change lies at its heart. It adopts a range of aesthetic styles and thematic preoccupations as it explores how the world might be otherwise. But Science Fiction focuses on what they share at their core: a vision of the world made otherwise and the possibilities that might flow from such change. My goal is to provide neither a detailed history of the genre nor a comprehensive listing of its most important works. Rather, following John Rieder, I am interested in focusing on what science fiction can do, how it has been described by a variety of constituencies in distinct ways for multiple ends.

然后抓住科幻小说，需要了解科学推断与社会变革之间的这种紧张在于它的心脏。它采用一系列美学风格和主题关注，因为它探讨了世界的方式。但科学小说着眼于他们分享到他们的核心：世界上的愿景以及可能从这种变化中流动的可能性。我的目标是提供流派的详细历史，也没有全面列出其最重要的作品。相反，在约翰·雷耶尔之后，我有兴趣关注科幻小说可以做什么，如何通过各种选区以不同的方式描述多端。

If we are living in a science fiction world, this book aims to help us articulate more precisely what that means and to prompt us to think about actively managing—rather than passively awaiting—this future.

如果我们居住在科幻世界，本书旨在帮助我们更加恰当地阐明这意味着并提示我们考虑积极管理 - 而不是被动地等待 - 这个未来。

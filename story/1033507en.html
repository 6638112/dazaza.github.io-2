Facebook表示，将驳斥有关在Facebook和Instagram上投票的说法，并限制与选举相关的Facebook Live流的分发

2020-11-06 10:47:18

Facebook is deploying “break glass” measures to restrict the flow of election misinformation as civil unrest grows during the wait for election results, the company said late Thursday.

Facebook周四晚些时候表示，在等待选举结果的过程中，随着国内动乱加剧，该公司正在部署“打破玻璃”措施，以限制选举错误信息的流动。

“As vote counting continues, we are seeing more reports of inaccurate claims about the election. While many of these claims have low engagement on our platform, we are taking additional temporary steps, which we’ve previously discussed, to keep this content from reaching more people,” the company said in a statement.

“随着计票工作的继续，我们看到更多关于选举不准确说法的报道。虽然其中许多声明在我们的平台上参与度较低，但我们正在采取额外的临时措施，正如我们之前讨论过的那样，以防止这些内容接触到更多的人，“该公司在一份声明中表示。

Facebook said it’s demoting content on Facebook and Instagram, including debunked claims about voting. It is also limiting the distribution of live videos that may relate to the election.

Facebook表示，它正在对Facebook和Instagram上的内容进行降级，包括否认有关投票的说法。它还限制了可能与选举有关的直播视频的分发。

These are some of the most significant steps by Facebook to curb potentially dangerous misinformation that has spread since Election Day, at times amplified by President Donald Trump or his allies. Trump’s social media posts on Facebook and Twitter have been flagged numerous times for being misleading. The prevailing theme from the Trump camp: Democrats are stealing the election.

这些是Facebook为遏制自选举日以来不断传播的潜在危险的错误信息而采取的一些最重要的举措，这些错误信息有时会被唐纳德·特朗普(Donald Trump)总统或他的盟友放大。特朗普在Facebook和Twitter上的社交媒体帖子被多次标记为具有误导性。特朗普阵营的主流主题是：民主党人在窃取选举。

In a news conference Thursday evening, Trump accused "big tech" of trying to "steal" the election, echoing the rallying cry of supporters on social media.

在周四晚上的新闻发布会上，特朗普指责科技巨头谷歌试图窃取选举，呼应了支持者在社交媒体上的战斗口号。

BuzzFeed News  reported Thursday that Facebook has seen a substantial increase in “violence and incitement trends,” meaning hashtags and search terms over the last five days suggest the possibility of danger rose nearly 45%.

BuzzFeed News周四报道称，脸书上出现的“暴力和煽动趋势”大幅增加，这意味着过去五天的标签和搜索词表明，发生危险的可能性上升了近45%。

"We have developed break-glass tools which do allow us to – if for a temporary period of time – effectively throw a blanket over a lot of content that would freely circulate on our platforms," Nick Clegg, Facebook's head of global affairs, told USA TODAY in September.

Facebook全球事务主管尼克·克莱格(Nick Clegg)9月对《今日美国》(USA Today)表示：我们已经开发出破镜工具，可以让我们--如果只是暂时的话--有效地遮盖在我们平台上自由流传的大量内容。

Clegg said the platform aimed to be “as responsible as we can to prevent that content, wittingly or otherwise, from aiding and abetting those who want to continue with the violence and civil strife that we're seeing on the ground."

克莱格说，该平台的目标是“尽我们所能防止这些内容，无论是有意还是无意，帮助和教唆那些想要继续我们在当地看到的暴力和内乱的人。”

Earlier Thursday, Facebook shut down a fast-growing pro-Trump “Stop the Steal” group calling for "boots on the ground to protect the integrity of the vote” over “worrying calls for violence.”

周四早些时候，Facebook关闭了一个快速增长的支持特朗普的“阻止盗窃”组织，该组织呼吁在地面部署军队，以保护投票的完整性，而不是“令人担忧的暴力呼声”。

The Facebook group, which attracted more than 350,000 members and nearly 7,000 posts in two days, was being used to organize protests with the rallying cry that Democratic challenger Joe Biden was trying to steal the election.

这个Facebook小组在两天内吸引了超过35万名成员和近7000个帖子，被用来组织抗议活动，战斗口号是民主党挑战者乔·拜登(Joe Biden)试图窃取选举。

It was named after a hashtag weaponized by President Donald Trump and his allies to boost unsubstantiated claims of voter fraud that could delegitimize a Biden win.

它是以唐纳德·特朗普(Donald Trump)总统及其盟友武器化的一个标签命名的，目的是支持未经证实的选民欺诈指控，这些指控可能会使拜登获胜合法化。

“In line with the exceptional measures that we are taking during this period of heightened tension, we have removed the Group ‘Stop the Steal,’ which was creating real-world events,” Facebook said in a statement. “The group was organized around the delegitimization of the election process, and we saw worrying calls for violence from some members of the group.”

脸书在一份声明中说：“为了配合我们在这段高度紧张的时期采取的特殊措施，我们已经下架了‘阻止偷窃’组织，该组织制造了真实世界的事件，”脸书在一份声明中说。“该组织是围绕选举程序合法化而组织的，我们看到该组织的一些成员发出了要求暴力的令人担忧的呼声。”

The #StopTheSteal hashtag was used in 2016 and 2019 and reemerged again in September, but didn't gain the resonance it's experiencing now. It took off the morning of Election Day, when attached to a Twitter post showing a poll watcher wrongly barred from a polling station in Pennsylvania. It soon spread more widely with the help of conservative actor James Woods and personalities Diamond and Silk.

#StopTheSteal标签曾在2016年和2019年被使用，并在9月份再次出现，但并没有像现在这样引起共鸣。它是在选举日早上起飞的，当时它附在Twitter上的一篇帖子上，帖子显示一名美国民调观察员被错误地禁止进入宾夕法尼亚州的一个投票站。在保守派演员詹姆斯·伍兹以及名人戴蒙德和西尔克的帮助下，这部电影很快就得到了更广泛的传播。

Disinformation researchers say Trump supporters are rallying around the hashtag, extending its reach. Real-world action such as street protests are being organized using the hashtag, they said.

虚假信息研究人员表示，特朗普的支持者正在围绕这个标签集会，扩大了它的影响范围。他们说，正在使用这个标签组织街头抗议等现实世界的行动。

While the volume of posts about the election is decreasing, Twitter is seeing a significant increase in election-related disinformation, according to Advance Democracy, a nonpartisan, nonprofit public interest research group which studies disinformation.

据研究虚假信息的无党派非营利性公共利益研究机构先进民主(Advance Democratic)称，虽然有关选举的帖子数量在减少，但Twitter上与选举相关的虚假信息却在大幅增加。

Research released late Thursday shows that two of the top five hashtags – #stopthesteal and #mailfraud – promoted unsubstantiated charges of voter fraud whereas on Wednesday, voter fraud-related hashtags didn't crack the top five.

周四晚些时候发布的研究显示，在排名前五的标签中，有两个标签--#阻止盗窃和#邮件欺诈--宣扬了对选民欺诈的未经证实的指控，而在周三，与选民欺诈相关的标签没有跻身前五名。

Use of the hashtag #stopthesteal tripled on Twitter, with QAnon accounts making up 9.3% of all posts, Advance Democracy found.

先进民主组织发现，推特上#阻止偷窃标签的使用量增加了两倍，QAnon账号占所有帖子的9.3%。

The #StopTheSteal hashtag is likely to endure long after the election, fueling conspiracy theories, according to the Election Integrity Partnership.

根据选举廉政伙伴关系的说法，#StopTheSteal#标签可能会在选举后很长一段时间内持续存在，从而助长阴谋论。

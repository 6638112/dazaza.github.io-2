Beverly Cleary was a new librarian in Yakima, Wash., when, as she later recalled, “A little boy faced me rather ferociously across the circulation desk and said, ‘Where are the books about kids like us?’ ”

Beverly Cleary是亚基马的一个新图书管理员，洗了。，当她后来回忆时，“一个小男孩面对我，相当凶猛地穿过循环办公桌，并说：”像我们这样的孩子在哪里？“

She was stumped. There were many volumes about precocious British tots with “nannies and pony carts,” she said, but none that would appeal to “grubby neighborhood kids” like the boy before her — or to the adventure-seeking girl she had once been.

她陷入僵局。她说，有许多关于预先英国人的卷，“保姆和小马购物车”，但没有那么吸引她在她面前的男孩那样呼吁“格鲁比邻里的孩子” - 或者曾经去过冒险的女孩。

That encounter in the library set Mrs. Cleary, who died March 25 at 104, on her way to becoming one of the most beloved children’s authors of all time, a chronicler of childhood who found the whole of human experience within the ordinary high jinks of growing up. Her publisher HarperCollins said she died in Carmel, Calif., but did not give a cause.

在图书馆遇到的是，在104年3月25日在104年3月25日去世的夫人，在成为有史以来最受欢迎的儿童作者之一，一位童年的纪念碑，他们在普通的高丛中找到了整个人类体验的童年成长。她的出版商Harpercollins表示，她在加利福尼亚州卡梅尔去世了，但没有给出原因。

Mrs. Cleary wrote more than 40 books, many about high-spirited youngsters such as the spunky Ramona Quimby and adventurous Henry Huggins, a third-grader with hair “like a scrubbing brush” and a knack for getting into gentle scrapes with his mutt, Ribsy.

In her stories, everyday problems — the challenges of managing an unwieldy paper route, dealing with a fractious sibling or coping with an absent parent — became tales of triumph.

在她的故事中，日常问题 - 管理笨重的纸质路线的挑战，处理一个不适的兄弟姐妹或应对缺席的父母 - 成为胜利的故事。

The books sold more than 85 million copies and became, like the works of Maurice Sendak and Theodor “Dr. Seuss” Geisel, essential reading for generations of schoolchildren. They earned Mrs. Cleary some of the highest distinctions in her field, including the Newbery Medal and Newbery Honor, as well as the National Medal of Arts, bestowed by President George W. Bush in 2003.

这本书销售了超过8500万份的副本，而且像Maurice Sendak和Theodor的作品一样。 Seuss“Geisel，几代学童的基本阅读。他们赢得了夫人在2003年由乔治·W·布什总统赐予纽伯利亚奖章和新手荣誉，包括纽伯奖章和新艺术勋章。

She aimed her stories squarely at an elementary school audience and hoped that, by creating relatable characters, she would inspire in her young readers a lifelong love of books. Her writing was distinguished by what essayist Benjamin Schwarz of the Atlantic magazine once called her gift for “photographic and psychological exactitude.”

她将她的故事正视在一所小学观众身上，并希望通过创造可关联的人物，她会激励她的年轻读者一生的终身爱。她的写作是尊重大西洋杂志的散文主义者本杰明·施瓦茨曾曾被称为“摄影和心理精确”的礼物。

Mrs. Cleary pulled heavily from memories of what she once described as her “free and wild” youth in Oregon, first on a farm and then in Depression-era Portland, employing what she called “all the bits of knowledge about children, reading and writing that had clung to me like burrs or dandelion fluff.”

清洁夫人曾在俄勒冈州的“自由和狂野”青年的回忆中，在俄勒冈州的“自由和狂野”青年中，然后在农场，然后在抑郁症 - 时代波特兰，雇用她所谓的“所有关于儿童知识，阅读和阅读和写给我的写作像毛刺或蒲公英绒毛一样。“

She frowned on the moralizing, didactic themes that dominated children’s literature in the first half of the 20th century, and set out not to impart wisdom but instead to portray children at play, and to capture their dialogue and the ways they sometimes venture into an adult world beyond their comprehension.

她在20世纪上半叶主导了儿童文学的道德化的道德化，并出发了不宣传智慧，而是为了描绘游戏，并捕捉他们的对话以及他们有时冒险进入成年人的方式世界超越了他们的理解。

Mrs. Cleary’s stories paved the way for the more mature subject matter of young-adult writers such as Judy Blume, who credited her as a significant influence.

Cleary夫人的故事为Judy Blume等年轻成年作家的主题铺平了道路，他们将她作为一个重大影响力。

The setting of Mrs. Cleary’s first work, “Henry Huggins” (1950), was modeled on Hancock Street in Portland, where she lived as a child. She gave the street a more evocative name in her book: Klickitat, after a nearby street with a name that reminded her of the sound of knitting needles.

Cleary夫人的第一份工作“Henry Huggins”（1950年）的设置在波兰德兰汉考克街上被建模，她作为一个孩子。她在她的书中提供了一个更令人兴奋的名字：克里克蒂特，在附近的街道之后，一个名字提醒她的针织针声音。

The boys she knew inspired “Henry Huggins’s” title character, who hunts for night crawlers in the park and struggles with whether to spend his silver dollar, a gift from a grandparent, on a pair of guppies at the pet store. (“He didn’t see how his mother could object to two quiet little fish that didn’t bark or track in mud or anything,” Mrs. Cleary wrote.)

她知道的男孩们激发了“亨利哈格汀的”标题角色，他在公园里捕猎夜爬虫，并努力与祖父母的一副祖父母，在宠物商店的一对吉普斯上挣扎。 （“他没有看到他的母亲如何反对两条安静的小鱼，这些小鱼没有吠叫或追踪泥土或任何东西，”Cleary夫人写道。）

“Henry Huggins” spawned five sequels and a spinoff series featuring Mrs. Cleary’s most beloved character, Ramona Quimby. She was the little sister of Henry’s friend Beatrice “Beezus” Quimby and was “tossed in,” Mrs. Cleary said, to move the story along.

“亨利哈格汀”催生了五个续集和一个分拆系列，以清晰的最亲爱的角色，ramona quimby。她是亨利的朋友Beatrice“Beezus”Quimby的小妹妹，并“扔进了”，Cleary夫人说，搬家。

Feisty, redheaded Ramona first received top billing in 1955 with the publication of “Beezus and Ramona.” Seven sequels followed, including “Ramona the Pest,” her 1968 classic about adjusting to kindergarten, and “Ramona and Her Mother” (1979), which earned a National Book Award in 1981 for children’s paperback fiction.

Feisty，Redheaded Ramona于1955年首次收到了“Beezus和Ramona”的出版物。七个续集，包括“瓦朗纳的害虫”，她的1968年经典调整幼儿园和“拉莫纳和她的母亲”（1979年），该奖项于1981年获得了一个国家的平装小说。

The “Ramona” books, the last of which appeared in 1999, gained a following that even Mrs. Cleary never expected.

“ramona”书籍，它在1999年出现的最后一个出现，甚至甚至是清洁夫人从未预期过。

“Little did I dream, to use a trite expression from books of my childhood, that she would take over books of her own, that she would grow and become a well-known and loved character,” she wrote in a memoir, “My Own Two Feet” (1995).

“我梦寐以求的小事，使用童年书籍的陈述，她会接受她自己的书籍，她会成长并成为一个着名和喜爱的性格，”她写在一个回忆录中，“我的拥有两英尺“（1995）。

A film adaptation of the first “Ramona” book, with the reversed title “Ramona and Beezus,” starred Joey King as Ramona and Selena Gomez as her older sister and was released in 2010 to mixed reviews. It was one of the few spinoffs tolerated by the author, who generally loathed the merchandising of her work.

一部电影适应第一个“ramona”书籍，与逆转的标题“斋月和闺房”主演Joey King作为Ramona和Selena Gomez作为她的姐姐，并于2010年发布到混合评论。这是作者宽容的少数几个散步之一，他通常会讨厌她的工作的销售。

Mrs. Cleary occasionally strayed from the realistic children’s fiction that was her hallmark, writing several young-adult novels about thwarted romances and first loves. She also wrote three children’s books about the adventurous rodent Ralph S. Mouse, beginning with “The Mouse and the Motorcycle” (1965).

Cleary夫人偶尔偏离了逼真的儿童小说，这是她的标志，写了几个关于挫败浪漫和第一次喜欢的年轻成人小说。她还写了三本关于冒险啮齿动物Ralph S.鼠标的儿童书籍，以“鼠标和摩托车”（1965年）开始。

She once told the Los Angeles Times that the “Ralph” novels emerged from a family trip to Britain, where she bought her son “some little cars and a little motorcycle” to play with after he became ill. When the family came home, she said, “a neighbor called me over to see a mouse that had fallen in a bucket in her garden. And the thought crossed my mind that that mouse was just the right size to ride that little motorcycle.”

她曾经告诉洛杉矶的时间是从家庭旅行到英国的“拉尔夫”小说，在那里她给她的儿子“有些小汽车和一点点摩托车”一起玩，以便在他生病之后玩耍。当家庭回家时，她说，“一名邻居叫我看看她花园里落下的桶。而这一思绪越过了我的想法，鼠标只是骑摩托车的正确尺寸。“

Her work took a darker tone in later years, as even idyllic Klickitat Street, where kids were allowed to play and walk to school without parental supervision, became the scene of anxiety, loneliness and a feeling of helplessness. By “Ramona Forever” (1984), the title character’s troubles shifted from kindergarten messes to worries about a new baby in the family, the death of a cat, her father’s unemployment and the departure of a beloved aunt.

她的工作在后来的几年里，甚至是田园诗般的Klickitat街，在没有父母监督的情况下允许孩子们玩耍并在没有父母的监督的情况下散步，成为焦虑，寂寞和无助的感觉。由“斋月永远”（1984年），标题角色的烦恼从幼儿园混乱转移到抱怨家庭中的一个新婴儿，一只猫的死亡，她父亲的失业和一个心爱的阿姨的离开。

Another poignant work, “Dear Mr. Henshaw” (1983), earned Mrs. Cleary the top honor in children’s literature, the Newbery Medal. The novel consists of letters between Leigh Botts, a schoolboy whose lunch is always getting stolen, and Boyd Henshaw, an author with whom Leigh began corresponding for a class assignment.

另一个尖锐的工作，“亲爱的亨氏先生”（1983年），赢得了夫人Cleary夫人在儿童文学中，纽伯利亚勋章。这部小说由Leigh Botts之间的信件组成，这是一个小学生，他的午餐总是被盗的，而Leigh开始对应一个班级分配的作者。

In a review for the New York Times, children’s writer Natalie Babbitt praised “Dear Mr. Henshaw” as Mrs. Cleary’s finest book. “Dialogue has always been one of the strongest parts of her work,” she wrote. “And here, where all is dialogue, that strength can shine alone and be double impressive.”

在对纽约时报的审查中，儿童作家娜塔莉巴巴特称赞“亲爱的亨斯先生”作为清晰的最优秀的书。 “对话一直是她工作中最强的部分之一，”她写道。 “在这里，所有人都在对话，那种力量可以单独闪耀，双重令人印象深刻。”

The book differed from her other works, Mrs. Cleary once observed, because it did not arise from a joke or funny idea. Leigh’s often-absent father is a truck driver, and his parents eventually divorce. There is no tidy ending.

这本书与她的其他作品有所不同，曾经观察过，因为它没有来自笑话或有趣的想法。 leigh的经常缺席的父亲是一辆卡车司机，他的父母最终离婚。没有整洁的结局。

“At first I was surprised because it wasn’t funny like your other books,” Leigh writes in one revealing letter to Henshaw, explaining that he had just finished the fictional author’s new work. He continued, “But then I got to thinking (you said authors should think) and decided a book doesn’t have to be funny to be good, although it often helps. This book did not need to be funny.”

“起初我很惊讶，因为它不像你的其他书一样有趣，”leigh写在一个透露的信中给汉语发来，解释说他刚刚完成了虚构作者的新工作。他继续，“但是我想思考（你说作者应该思考）并决定一本书不必很有趣，虽然经常有帮助。这本书不需要很有趣。“

Beverly Atlee Bunn was born April 12, 1916, in McMinnville, Ore., the nearest town with a hospital to the family farm in Yamhill. Her father, Lloyd, was the son of a farmer whose ancestors had arrived in Oregon by covered wagon in the mid-1800s.

Beverly Atlee Bunn于1916年4月12日出生，位于麦克蒙尼维尔，矿石，最近的城镇，位于山丘的家庭农场。她的父亲劳埃德是一个农民的儿子，他的祖先在1800年代中期被盖车抵达俄勒冈州俄勒冈州。

Her mother, the former Mable Atlee, was an aspiring writer who headed west from Michigan in the early 1900s to teach. She founded a library in Yamhill, but the family relocated to Portland after losing the farm in an economic downturn.

她的母亲是前的Mable Atlee，是一位抱负的作家，是20世纪初从密歇根州向西前往教学。她在山坡上创办了一个图书馆，但在经济衰退中失去农场后，家庭搬到了波特兰。

Her father worked as a bank security guard but was laid off during the Depression, a traumatic experience for the young Mrs. Cleary that inspired a similar episode in “Ramona and Her Father” (1977).

她的父亲作为银行保安人员工作，但在抑郁症期间被解雇，为年轻夫人的创伤体验，这激发了“斋月和她的父亲”（1977年）的类似集。

“I sat filled with anguish, unable to read, unable to do anything,” she wrote in her memoir “A Girl From Yamhill” (1988), recounting the moment when she learned her father had been fired. “How could anyone do such a thing to my father, who was so good, kind, reliable, and honest?”

“我坐满了痛苦，无法读，无法做任何事情，”她写在她的回忆录“一个来自山坡的女孩”（1988年），在她吸取父亲被解雇时叙述了这一刻。 “有人怎么能为我父亲这样做，谁是如此美好，善良，可靠，诚实？”

Chickenpox and then smallpox kept Mrs. Cleary out of first grade for a time, and when she returned, she was placed with the least-proficient group of readers. She was in third grade when she finally started to grasp the fundamentals of reading. She recalled the moment it all came together: the rainy afternoon at home when she stumbled across Lucy Fitch Perkins’s children’s novel “The Dutch Twins.”

水痘然后天花一直保持清晰的一年级，当她回来时，她被掌握了最小的读者。当她终于开始掌握阅读的基本原则时，她是三年级的。她回忆起这一切都走到了一起：当她偶然发现露出露西惠克斯的孩子的小说“荷兰双胞胎”时，在家里的下雨下午。

“I picked up a book,” she told the Philadelphia Inquirer. “My mother always kept them around, with high hopes. I looked at the pictures, and then the words, and discovered I was reading.”

“我拿起一本书，”她告诉费城询问者。 “我的母亲总是让他们保持在困难。我看着这些照片，然后是文字，发现我正在读书。“

In recent years, Mrs. Cleary’s birthday became a reading holiday of sorts, with libraries and schools across the country celebrating it as Drop Everything and Read Day.

近年来，Cleary夫人的生日成了一个读的假期，在全国各地的图书馆和学校庆祝它删除一切和读的一天。

She graduated with a bachelor’s degree in English in 1938 from the University of California at Berkeley. After receiving a second bachelor’s degree, in library science, from the University of Washington in 1939, she became a children’s librarian in Yakima.

她于1938年毕业于1938年在伯克利加州大学的英语学士学位。在1939年从华盛顿大学获得图书馆学学士学位后，她在亚基马成为一个孩子们的图书馆员。

Mrs. Cleary later settled in California’s Berkeley Hills, where she devoted herself to writing full time with the encouragement of her husband, Clarence Cleary, an accountant she married in 1940. He died in 2004. Survivors include their two children, Malcolm and Marianne; three grandchildren; and a great-grandchild.

清洁夫人后来在加州的伯克利山定居，在那里她致力于在1940年结婚的会计师的鼓励，恭喜她的丈夫，克拉伦斯清洁的鼓励，他致力于鼓励。他于2004年去世。幸存者包括他们的两个孩子，马尔科姆和玛丽安娜;三个孙子;和一个伟大的孙子。

In “My Own Two Feet,” Mrs. Cleary recalled a brief feeling of anxiety while working on her first book.

在“我自己的两英尺”中，闷闷不乐地回忆起在她的第一本书上工作时感到简短的焦虑感。

“It occurred to me that even though I was uncertain about writing, I knew how to tell a story,” she wrote, remembering her years as a librarian in Yakima. “What was writing for children but written storytelling? So in my imagination I stood once more before Yakima’s story-hour crowd as I typed the first sentence: ‘Henry Huggins was in the third grade.’ ”

“我想到了，即使我不确定写作，我知道如何讲述一个故事，”她写道，记得她作为雅基亚的图书馆员。 “孩子们为什么写作，但书写了讲故事？所以在我的想象中，我在yakima的故事时代人群中再次站起来，因为我打字第一句话：“亨利哈格汀在三年级。”

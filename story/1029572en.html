如何阅读地面天气图

2020-10-18 15:44:38

We’re surrounded by more weather data than we could ever use. Our phones chirp data points at us on command. Social media is filled with analyses from meteorologists around the world. Even the weather segment on broadcast news turns into a college-level meteorology course when active weather is on the horizon. But out of all the data we process, an old-fashioned surface map is still the best way to keep tabs on weather conditions anywhere.

我们周围的天气数据比我们所能使用的要多得多。我们的手机在指挥下向我们发出嗡嗡的数据点。社交媒体上充斥着来自世界各地气象学家的分析。当活跃的天气即将出现时，即使是广播新闻中的天气片段也会变成一门大学级的气象学课程。但是，在我们处理的所有数据中，老式的地形图仍然是跟踪任何地方天气状况的最佳方式。

Meteorologists have maps for every occasion, from monitoring a winter storm that’s dumping fresh powder on the slopes to tracking thunderstorms that can crash a camping trip. Most weather maps are dedicated to showing us only one variable, though, like current temperatures or upper-level winds. But it’s the basic surface map, with its station plots and wind barbs, that reveals the most information in the quickest way before we head outside. Still, detailed surface weather maps are only as useful as your ability to understand what they’re telling you. Here’s how to read them.

气象学家有各种场合的地图，从监测冬季暴风雨向山坡上倾倒新鲜粉末到跟踪可能导致露营旅行崩溃的雷雨，不一而足。然而，大多数天气图只致力于向我们显示一个变量，比如当前的温度或高层的风。但是，在我们出门之前，最快的方式揭示了最多的信息的是基本的地形图，以及它的空间站地块和风刺。尽管如此，详细的地面天气图的有用程度取决于你理解它们告诉你什么的能力。下面是如何阅读它们的方法。

Scientists needed a way to convey as much information as possible without making a map completely unreadable. Enter the station plot, an ingenious way to print lots of information in a tiny space. It takes a bit of deciphering to understand, but once you’ve got the hang of it, you can get the gist of a specific location’s weather in just a few minutes.

科学家需要一种方法来传达尽可能多的信息，而不会使地图完全不可读。进入车站图，这是一种巧妙的方式，可以在很小的空间内打印大量信息。这需要一些破译才能理解，但一旦你掌握了诀窍，你就可以在短短几分钟内掌握一个特定地点的天气要领。

The basic station plot shows temperature, dew point, wind direction, wind speed, cloud cover, precipitation, and air pressure, which are all measured at weather stations around the globe. That’s a tremendous amount of useful information packed into one little graphic—and some maps contain even more data, such as visibility and cloud heights and types.

基本站图显示温度、露点、风向、风速、云量、降水量和气压，所有这些都是在全球气象站测量的。这是一个小小的图表中包含的大量有用信息--有些地图甚至包含更多的数据，比如能见度、云层高度和类型。

A station plot always shows the current temperature on the top left, dew point on the bottom left, and air pressure on the top right. The units for temperature and dew point depend on the source that made the map; most surface maps issued in the United States use Fahrenheit, while organizations in most other countries use Celsius.

桩号图始终在左上角显示当前温度，在左下角显示露点，在右上角显示气压。温度和露点的单位取决于制作地图的来源；美国发布的大多数地形图使用华氏度，而大多数其他国家的组织使用摄氏度。

Air-pressure readings are important because they tell you how the atmosphere is moving around you. High pressure fosters calm skies perfect for hiking, while low pressure signals unsettled conditions and precipitation. Strong winds result from rapid pressure changes over short distances.

气压读数很重要，因为它们能告诉你周围的大气层是如何运动的。高压促进平静的天空，非常适合徒步旅行，而低压信号表明不稳定的条件和降水。强风是由短距离内气压的快速变化造成的。

Decoding air pressure on weather maps takes memorization and context clues to translate. Most station plots will depict air pressure as a set of three numbers, such as 994 or 112. This number shows the last three digits of a station’s air-pressure reading to the nearest tenth of a millibar (mb); the last number actually comes after the decimal point. For example, 994 would indicate a pressure reading of 999.4 millibars, while 112 would convert to 1011.2 millibars. (Meteorologists put a nine in front of bigger numbers and a ten in front of smaller numbers.) It’s helpful to keep in mind that it’s rare to see an air pressure higher than 1,040 millibars outside of a deep cold snap. It’s just as uncommon to see an air pressure lower than 980 millibars outside the eye of a hurricane or a nor’easter.

解码天气图上的气压需要记忆和上下文线索来翻译。大多数桩号图都会将气压描述为一组三个数字，例如994或112。这个数字显示了一个站的气压读数的最后三位数，精确到十分之一毫巴(Mb)；最后一个数字实际上在小数点之后。例如，994年的压力读数为999.4毫巴，而第112毫巴的压力读数将转换为1011.2毫巴。(气象学家在较大的数字前面放一个9，在较小的数字前面放一个10)。记住，在深度寒流之外，气压高于1040毫巴的情况很少见，这是很有帮助的。在飓风或东北风中心外，气压低于980毫巴的情况同样少见。

Wind barbs are the most visible portion of a station plot. They tell you the speed and direction of winds at the station. The barb always points in the direction from which the wind is blowing: if the barb points toward the southeast, it means the wind is blowing from the southeast.

风刺是桩号地块中最明显的部分。他们会告诉你车站的风速和风向。倒钩总是指着风吹的方向：如果倒钩指向东南风，那就意味着风是从东南风吹来的。

A wind barb contains half lines, full  lines, and flags to denote wind speeds. Speeds are always displayed in knots (kts), regardless of the units used for temperatures and dew points. (One knot approximately equals 1.151 miles per hour.) A half line represents five knots, a full line denotes ten knots, and flags are reserved for increments of 50 knots.

风刺包含半线、全线和旗帜以表示风速。速度始终以节(Kts)显示，与温度和露点使用的单位无关。(1节，约等于每小时1.151英里)半线代表5节，满线代表10节，旗帜保留为50节的增量。

The wind barbs above show winds blowing from the northwest at 35 knots (plot A) and the southeast at 75 knots (plot B). If the winds are calm, a thin circle will appear around the station plot (plot C).

上面的风刺显示，西北风以每小时35海里的速度吹来(A地块)，东南风以每小时75海里的速度吹来(B地块)。如果风平浪静，车站地块(C地块)周围会出现一个薄薄的圆圈。

A station-plot icon conveys cloud cover by the amount of shading inside the plot’s center dot. A hollow circle indicates clear skies, while a solid circle denotes overcast conditions that could ruin a backpacking trip. Each successive quarter shading of the dot indicates scattered clouds (25 percent filled), partly cloudy (50 percent), and mostly cloudy (75 percent).

桩号地块图标通过地块中心点内的着色量来表示云层。中空的圆圈表示晴朗的天空，而实心的圆圈表示阴天可能会毁了背包旅行。每一个连续的季度，点的阴影表示分散的云(25%充满)、部分多云(50%)和大部分多云(75%)。

Symbols representing precipitation type and intensity will appear directly to the left of a station plot. Solid dots represent rain, while asterisks represent snow, with intensity shown through the use of two symbols (light precipitation), three symbols (moderate precipitation), or four symbols (heavy precipitation). A thunderstorm is denoted by a long arrow in the shape of the letter R. While these are some of the more common symbols, there are plenty of others for precipitation types , including freezing drizzle and thundersnow.

表示降水类型和强度的符号将直接显示在桩号图的左侧。实心点代表雨，而星号代表雪，强度通过使用两个符号(小雨)、三个符号(中雨)或四个符号(大雨)来表示。雷雨由字母R形状的长箭头表示，虽然这些是一些更常见的符号，但还有很多其他的降水类型，包括冰雨和雷雪。

Some surface maps are analyzed by computer programs or human forecasters to add extra information that helps you understand current conditions and predict what’s coming. The most common value-added analyses on surface maps are isobars, pressure systems, and boundaries.

一些地形图由计算机程序或人类预报员分析，以添加额外的信息，帮助你了解当前情况并预测即将发生的事情。曲面地图上最常见的增值分析是等压线、压力系统和边界。

We’re used to seeing a blue H over a high-pressure center and a red L over a low-pressure system. Wind generally blows from areas of high pressure toward areas of low pressure. But even without those letters printed on the map, it’s usually easy to spot these features using isobars—lines drawn on a map that connect areas experiencing equally high or low air-pressure readings.

我们习惯于在高压中心上方看到蓝色H，在低压系统上方看到红色L。风一般从高压区吹向低压区。但是，即使没有在地图上打印这些字母，使用等压线通常也很容易发现这些要素-地图上绘制的线连接了经历同样高或低气压读数的不同地区。

These solid concentric lines  are helpful for identifying pressure centers and getting a general idea of where it’s windy, making them especially useful for outdoor enthusiasts about to set out on the next adventure. Isobars that are closely packed together show greater pressure changes over short distances, indicating rapidly changing weather conditions and gusty winds.

这些坚固的同心直线有助于识别压力中心，并大致了解哪里有风，这使得它们对即将出发进行下一次冒险的户外爱好者特别有用，紧密堆积在一起的等压线在短距离内显示出更大的压力变化，表明天气条件迅速变化和阵风。

A front is the boundary between two different air masses; they are ubiquitous on weather forecasts. The difference between the two regions can be subtle or dramatic—a strong cold front, for example, can drop temperatures dozens of degrees in a few minutes.

锋面是两个不同气团之间的边界；它们在天气预报中无处不在。这两个地区之间的差异可能是微妙的，也可能是戏剧性的-例如，一股强大的冷锋可以在几分钟内将气温降低几十度。

Cold fronts show cooler, drier air pushing into warmer, more humid air, which can produce powder-day snow. They’re typically shaded blue, with triangular flags pointing in the direction of the front’s forward movement. Warm fronts exist along the leading edge of warmer, humid air pushing into cooler, drier air. They can sometimes bring stormy weather and are usually marked by a red line with semicircles pointing in the direction of the front’s movement.

冷锋显示出更凉爽、更干燥的空气推向更温暖、更潮湿的空气，这可能会产生粉末状的积雪。它们通常是蓝色阴影，三角形旗帜指向前方前进的方向。暖锋沿着更温暖、更潮湿的空气前缘存在，推动着更凉爽、更干燥的空气。它们有时会带来暴风雨天气，通常用一条带半圆形的红线指示锋面的移动方向。

A stationary front lies along the edge of cooler and warmer air masses that aren’t advancing toward one another. These are denoted by alternating flags and semicircles (alternating in the same colors outlined above), each pointing toward their respective air mass.

一个静止的锋面位于较冷和较暖的气团的边缘，这些气团不会相互推进。这些由交替的旗帜和半圆形(以上面概述的相同颜色交替)表示，每个指向各自的气团。

Occluded fronts are shown in purple on weather maps. An occlusion occurs when cold air overtakes warm air near the center of a low-pressure system, pinching a region of warm air above the earth’s surface. A “triple point”—where a cold front, warm front, and occluded front meet—can sometimes serve as the focus for severe thunderstorms, which may ruin an afternoon run.

被遮挡的锋面在天气地图上以紫色显示。当冷空气超过低压系统中心附近的暖空气，挤压地球表面上方的一片暖空气时，就会发生阻塞。通常的“三点”-冷锋、暖锋和被遮挡的锋面相遇-有时可能会成为严重雷暴的焦点，这可能会破坏下午的跑步。

Dry lines, depicted on surface maps in beige, with hollow semicircles pointing in the direction of movement, are a type of front typically only seen on the southern plains. They’re a dividing line between humid air flowing in from the Gulf of Mexico and extremely dry air from the desert region out west. A dry line moves eastward in big leaps during the afternoon as a result of daytime heating. These fronts can serve as the catalyst for intense springtime tornado outbreaks in Texas and Oklahoma.

在地形图上用米色描绘的干线，中空的半圆形指向移动的方向，是一种通常只在南部平原上才能看到的锋面。它们是从墨西哥湾流入的潮湿空气和来自西部沙漠地区的极端干燥空气之间的分界线。由于白天的供暖，一条干线在下午以很大的跳跃向东移动。这些锋面可以成为德克萨斯州和俄克拉何马州春季龙卷风爆发的催化剂。

With these basics in mind, the best place to download reliable, up-to-date weather maps is the U.S. National Weather Service—just make sure you save them to your phone or bookmark the direct link in your phone’s browser before you head out. The agency’s products are free for public use, and the entire organization only costs each taxpayer a little more than a dollar a year. Various branches of the NWS issue a multitude of surface maps covering the United States. The Weather Prediction Center mostly handles everyday weather, like fronts and temperatures, while the  Storm Prediction Center handles severe thunderstorms and fire-weather outlooks. The Ocean Prediction Center offers detailed analyses of weather across the Atlantic, Pacific, and Arctic Oceans.

记住这些基本知识，下载可靠、最新天气地图的最佳地方是美国国家气象局-只需确保在出发前将它们保存到手机上或在手机浏览器中将直接链接添加为书签即可。该机构的产品是免费向公众使用的，整个机构每年只需花费每位纳税人一美元多一点。国家气象局的各个分支机构发布了大量覆盖美国的地形图。天气预测中心主要处理日常天气，如锋面和温度，而美国风暴预测中心主要处理严重的雷暴和火灾天气展望。海洋预测中心提供大西洋、太平洋和北冰洋的详细天气分析。

International weather organizations also provide fantastic services for their coverage areas, including Environment Canada, the UK’s Met Office, and Australia’s Bureau of Meteorology. Private companies and academic institutions also issue weather maps, such as the College of DuPage’s robust surface analyses.

国际气象组织也为其覆盖区域提供出色的服务，包括加拿大环境部、英国气象局和澳大利亚气象局。私营公司和学术机构也发布天气图，例如杜佩奇学院的强大的地表分析。

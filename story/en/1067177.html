Wealthiest U.S. executives paid little to nothing in federal income taxes
最富有的美国高管在联邦所得税中没有任何关注

2021-06-21 18:49:49

WASHINGTON—The 25 richest Americans including Jeff Bezos, Michael Bloomberg and Elon Musk paid relatively little — and sometimes nothing — in federal income taxes between 2014 and 2018, according to an analysis from the news organization ProPublica that was based on a trove of IRS tax data.

华盛顿 - 包括Jeff Bezos，Michael Bloomberg和Elon Musk在内的25名富有的美国人在2014年和2018年之间支付了相对较少的 - 而有时没有任何内容税，根据新闻组织Propublica的分析，该税是基于IRS税的一套税收的分析数据。

The analysis showed that the nation’s richest executives paid just a fraction of their wealth in taxes — $13.6 billion in federal income taxes on $401 billion of their wealth, which was tabulated by Forbes.

该分析表明，该国最富有的高管在税收的一小部分中仅支付了一小部分 - 136亿美元的联邦所得税，以401亿美元的财富，由福布斯列表。

The documents reveal the stark inequity in the U.S. tax system, as plutocrats such as Bezos, Bloomberg, Warren Buffett, Carl Icahn and Musk were able to benefit from a complex web of loopholes in the tax code and the fact that the United States puts its emphasis on taxing labor income versus wealth. Much of the wealth that the rich accrue — like shares in companies they run, vacation homes, yachts and other investments — is not considered “taxable income” unless those assets are sold and a gain is realized. Even then, there are loopholes in the tax code that can limit or erase all tax liability.

这些文件揭示了美国税制中的耻辱不公平，因为福克斯，彭博，沃伦·巴菲特，卡尔·伊曼和麝香等富思，可以从税法中的复杂网站中受益，以及美国将其施加的事实强调征税劳动收入与财富。富裕的累积的财富之外的大部分财富在他们跑的公司中，假期住宅，游艇和其他投资 - 除非销售这些资产并实现增长，否则不被视为“应税收入”。即便如此，税务代码中有漏洞可以限制或擦除所有税费。

The rare window into the tactics of the nation’s top billionaires comes as President Joe Biden is trying to overhaul the tax code so that corporations and the rich pay more. Biden has proposed raising the top marginal income tax rate to 39.6% from 37%, which would reverse the reduction ushered in by President Donald Trump’s 2017 tax cuts.

乔恩·拜登总统正试图改革税法，珍稀窗口进入全国最大亿万富翁的策略，使公司和富裕支付更多。拜登提出将顶级边际所得税税率从37％提高到39.6％，这将扭转唐纳德特朗普2017年税收税总统延迟的减少。

But the documents and the conclusions of the analysis could renew calls for Biden to consider a wealth tax, given that a higher marginal tax rate would do little to raise the tax bills of the 25 richest Americans. From 2014 to 2018, the 25 wealthiest Americans paid an average of 15.8%, or $13.6 billion, in personal federal income taxes.

但该文件和分析的结论可以更新拜登呼吁考虑财富税，因为更高的边际税率会对提高25个最富有的美国人的税收票据很少。从2014年到2018年，最富裕的美国人平均支付了35.8％，或136亿美元，以个人联邦所得税。

Lawmakers such as Sen. Elizabeth Warren, D-Mass., have championed the idea of placing a 2% tax on an individual’s net worth above $50 million — including the value of stocks, houses, boats and anything else a person owns, after subtracting any debts.

伊丽莎白沃伦，D质量等立法者。在减少后，将股票，房屋，船舶和其他人拥有的股票，房屋，船和其他任何其他人的价值在内的个人净税收上占据了2％的税收任何债务。

Warren said on Twitter that the ProPublica report showed “our tax system is rigged for billionaires who don’t make their fortunes through income, like working families do.”

Warren在Twitter上表示，Propublica报告显示“我们的税收制度因亿万富翁而被操纵，他们不会通过收入来幸运，如同工作家庭所做的。”

Biden and his advisers have deemed the idea of a wealth tax unworkable. Instead, he has sought $80 billion to beef up the IRS so it is better able to go after tax cheats. And he has proposed doubling the tax on capital gains — the proceeds of selling an asset like a stock or a boat — for people earning more than $1 million.

拜登和他的顾问认为是一个不可行的财富税的想法。相反，他已经寻求800亿美元的美国国税局，因此可以更好地追踪税务秘籍。他提出了对资本收益的税收加倍 - 销售资产的收益，如股票或船只 - 用于赚取超过100万美元的人。

At a New York Times DealBook event in February, Treasury Secretary Janet Yellen said that a wealth tax is “something that has very difficult implementation problems.” She suggested that other changes that would increase taxes on wealth that is transferred at death could have a similar effect. In March, however, Yellen suggested that she remained open-minded about a wealth tax.

在2月份的纽约时报销售情况下，财政部秘书珍妮特说，财富税是“具有非常艰巨的实施问题的东西”。她建议将在死亡中转移的财富增加的其他变化可能具有类似的效果。然而，3月份，耶伦建议她对财富税保持开放。

“Well, that’s something that we haven’t decided yet,” Yellen said on ABC News, before pointing to other tax ideas that would also impact the rich.

“好吧，这是我们尚未决定的事情，”Yellen在ABC新闻中说，在指向其他税务概念之前也会影响富人。

ProPublica did not reveal how it obtained the information, and it could not be independently verified by The New York Times. But the publication said the documents were provided to the outlet “in raw form, with no conditions or conclusions” and that it had run the information past every executive whose information was included in the article.

Propublica没有透露它如何获得的信息，并且无法被纽约时间独立验证。但出版物表示，该文件被提供给“以原始形式，没有条件或结论”的出口，并且它运行了这些信息，这些行政程序在文章中包含的信息。

“Every person whose tax information is described in this story was asked to comment,” ProPublica said, adding that those who responded “all said they had paid the taxes they owed.”

“在这个故事中描述了税收信息的每个人都被要求发表评论，”Propublica说，并补充说那些回答的人“所有人都说他们已经支付了他们所欠的税收。”

In a separate editors’ note, the outlet said it was publishing the information “quite selectively and carefully — because we believe it serves the public interest in fundamental ways, allowing readers to see patterns that were until now hidden.”

在一个单独的编辑笔记中，出口表示它正在发布“非常有选择地和仔细”发布信息 - 因为我们认为它以基本方式为公众利益提供服务，允许读者看到现在隐藏的模式。“

The report highlights the techniques that the wealthy often use to reduce their tax bills, including taking advantage of a complex web of loopholes and deductions that are perfectly legal and can significantly minimize tax liability. That includes borrowing huge sums of money backed by enormous stock holdings. Loans are not taxed and the interest that the executives pay on the borrowed money can be deducted from their tax bills.

该报告突出了富人经常用于减少税收票据的技术，包括利用完全合法的漏洞和扣除的复杂网站，可以大大减少税收责任。这包括借用由巨大股票持有支持的巨额金额。贷款不征税，并且高管在借来的金钱支付的利息可以从其税收票据中扣除。

In 2007, Bezos, the CEO of Amazon, paid nothing in federal income taxes even as his company’s stock price doubled. Four years later, as his wealth swelled to $18 billion, Bezos reported losses and received a tax credit of $4,000 for his children, according to ProPublica.

2007年，亚马逊首席执行官的Bezos，即使他的公司股价翻了一番，也没有在联邦所得税中支付任何费用。据Propublica称，四年后，随着他的财富膨胀至180亿美元，贝佐斯报告亏损并获得儿童为4,000美元的税收抵免。

Buffett, the CEO of Berkshire Hathaway, who has long said publicly that the tax code should hit the rich harder, paid just $23.7 million in taxes from 2014 to 2018, when his wealth rose by $24.3 billion.

Buffett，伯克郡Hathaway的首席执行官，他长期以来一直在公开表示，税法应达到富人，从2014年到2018年的税款仅支付2370万美元，因为他的财富上涨了243亿美元。

In 2018, Bloomberg, who controls the media giant Bloomberg LP, reported income of $1.9 billion and paid $70.7 million in income tax. According to the report, Bloomberg was able to reduce his tax bill through deductions, charitable donations and “credits for having paid foreign taxes.”

2018年，控制媒体巨头彭博会LP的彭博会报告收入为16亿美元，并支付了7070万美元所得税。根据该报告，Bloomberg能够通过扣除，慈善捐赠和“获得缴足外籍税的”学分“来减少税收法案。

In a statement to ProPublica, a spokesman for Bloomberg said they would “use all legal means at our disposal to determine which individual or government entity leaked these and ensure that they are held responsible.”

在彭博的发言人陈列国表示，他们将“使用我们所处理的所有法律手段来确定哪个个人或政府实体泄露这些并确保他们负责。”

The Treasury Department said that the federal government is working to determine how the tax records got released.

财政部表示，联邦政府正在努力确定税务记录如何发布。

“The unauthorized disclosure of confidential government information is illegal,” said Lily Adams, a Treasury spokeswoman. “The matter is being referred to the Office of the Inspector General, Treasury Inspector General for Tax Administration, Federal Bureau of Investigation, and the U.S. Attorney’s Office for the District of Columbia, all of whom have independent authority to investigate.”

“未经授权的保密政府信息披露是非法的，”财政部发言人莉莉亚当斯说。 “此事正在被提交纳税机构，联邦调查局和美国委托人办公室的税务管理局，美国司法委员会办公室的督察总督，所有这些都有独立权力调查。”

At a Senate Finance Committee hearing where he was testifying Tuesday, Charles Rettig, the IRS commissioner, said that he could not comment on the apparent breach at his agency but said that it was being scrutinized.

在一名参议院财务委员会的审理国税局委员的审理委员会，IRS委员的Charles Rettig表示，他无法评论他代理机构的明显违约，但表示正在审查。

“I can confirm that there is an investigation with respect to the allegations that the source of the information in that article came from the Internal Revenue Service,” Rettig said. “The investigators will investigate.”

“我可以确认对指控有调查，即该文章中的信息来源来自内部收入服务，”Rettig说。 “调查人员将调查。”

Sen. Ron Wyden, D-Ore., the chairman of the finance committee, told Rettig that he was concerned about the security of taxpayer data. He also emphasized that the disclosures made clear that the tax code needs to be rewritten.

Sen.Ron Wyden，D-Ore。，财务委员会主席，告诉Rettig，他担心纳税人数据的安全。他还强调，披露清楚明确表示需要重写税收。

“What this data reveals is that the country’s wealthiest, who profited immensely during the pandemic, have not been paying their fair share,” Wyden said, adding that he has proposals to fix that disparity.

“这数据显示的是，该国在大流行期间非常富裕的国家最富有的最富有的国家，并没有支付其公平份额，”威登表示，他补充说，他有提案来解决这种差异。

After the hearing, Wyden declined to offer specifics about his plan to address the issue, but emphasized his concern about unfairness in the tax code.

听证会后，威登拒绝提供关于他计划解决问题的规定，但强调了他对税法不公平的关注。

“The people I represent are doing work, like treating COVID patients, they’re paying taxes with every paycheck,” Wyden said. “The country’s wealthiest, including during the pandemic, profited handsomely and then do not pay their fair share because they have figured out with good lawyers and accountants how to defer and delay and postpone and almost do it in perpetuity.”

“我所代表的人正在做工作，就像对待Covid患者一样，他们正在与每个薪水支付税款，”Wyden说。 “该国最富有，包括在大流行期间，非常友好的利润，然后不支付他们的公平份额，因为他们已经讨论了良好的律师和会计师，如何推迟和推迟和推迟，但几乎在永恒中实现。”

Sen. Mike Crapo of Idaho, the top Republican on the committee, said that the disclosures added to his concern about a Biden administration proposal to give the IRS more access to the financial information of taxpayers. He suggested that the agency could not be trusted to keep the data secure.

委员会顶级共和党迈克克拉多的迈克克拉皮说，委员会的首选共和党人表示，披露他对拜登行政提案的关注，使IRS更多地获得纳税人的财务信息。他建议该机构不能值得信任，以保持数据安全。

The president said on Twitter on Tuesday that he was continuing to work with Republicans on infrastructure and jobs legislation and said that he would not seek a tax increase on anyone who earns less than $400,000.

总统周二在Twitter上说，他继续与共和党人合作，并表示他不会在任何不到400,000美元的人那里寻求税收增加。

“It’s long past time the wealthy and corporations pay their fair share,” Biden said.

“富裕和公司久的时间久违，”拜登说。

Orum, which speeds up ACH bank payments by using AI to assess risk, raises $21M Series A led by Bain Capital
ORUM通过使用AI评估风险来加速ACH银行支付，由Bain Capital提高2100万美元的系列

Today you can load a movie on Netflix in a second, catch an Uber in a minute and get groceries delivered in an hour. You’d think money would move just as quickly—and some of it does. But most of it, or about $62 trillion a year in the U.S., travels more slowly than a car, taking up to five days to arrive safely. That’s why consumers usually need to wait several days before they can make their first transaction after signing up for Robinhood or a digital bank like Chime.

今天，您可以在Netflix上装入一部电影，然后在一分钟内捕捉优步，并在一小时内送杂货。你认为金钱会尽快移动 - 它的一些情况。但大多数情况下，或者在美国，每年约62万亿美元，比汽车慢得多，需要5天才能安全到达。这就是为什么消费者通常需要等待几天，因为在注册抢劫队或数字银行时可以在抢劫之后才能在罗马时代举办第一步。

Orum, a New York startup with about 40 employees, aims to shrink money movement times down to 24 hours. It has raised $21 million in “Series A” funding led by Bain Capital. Venture firms Inspired Capital, Homebrew, Acrew, Primary, Clocktower, Box Group, Amex Ventures and SVB Capital also invested.

奥卢姆，一个大约40名员工的纽约初创公司，旨在将货币缩小到24小时。它筹集了2100万美元的贝恩首都领导的“系列”资金。风险公司启发了资本，自制，ACREW，小学，钟表，箱子集团，AMEX企业和SVB资本也投资。

Stephany Kirkpatrick founded Orum in 2019 after working for seven years at LearnVest, the financial planning fintech company started by Alexa von Tobel and acquired by Northwestern Mutual in 2015 for $375 million. Kirkpatrick, a certified financial planner, did everything from opening LearnVest’s first call center in Arizona to helping design its financial planning software—her name appears on its patents.

Stephany Kirkpatrick于2019年创办Oreum，在学习工作七年后，金融规划Fintech公司由Alexa Von Tobel开始，由2015年以3.75亿美元的3.75亿美元获得西北互联网。经过认证的金融计划者Kirkpatrick，从亚利桑那州开业的首次呼叫中心进行了帮助，以帮助设计其财务规划软件 - 她的名字出现在其专利上。

“At LearnVest I saw six to seven million people get a financial plan and maybe 10% execute it. Why is that?” the 39-year-old Orum CEO says. “It's because there’s so much friction in our financial system. People don't want to put money in high-yield savings, even though it's a better rate, because they're afraid that if they have an emergency, they can't get it out fast enough. I became obsessed with that problem.” Von Tobel was Kirkpatrick’s first backer, investing through her VC firm Inspired Capital. “This is a massive, complicated, meaty problem that plagues thousands of institutions,” von Tobel says.

“在学习中，我看到六到七百万人获得财务计划，也许10％执行它。这是为什么？”这位39岁的奥卢姆首席执行官说。 “它＆＃39;我们的金融系统中有如此多的摩擦。人们不想把钱放在高产储蓄中，即使它＆＃39;更好的速度，因为他们＆＃39;如果他们有紧急情况，他们就可以快速解决它足够的。我迷恋那个问题。“ Von Tobel是Kirkpatrick的第一个支持者，通过她的VC公司启发资本投资。 “这是一种巨大的，复杂，肉质的问题，困扰了数千个机构，”弗别特说。

Kirkpatrick started Orum because so much money moves slowly, but some transactions settle quickly. Punch in your credit card online or swipe it at a store, and a retailer knows almost instantly whether it went through. Card networks are fast, but also expensive—retailers cough up 2% of their sales when they accept cards. By contrast, anything transferred over the Automated Clearing House (ACH) network, such as your paycheck, automated bill payments and bank transfers, often costs just $0.30 but takes up to five days. ACH launched in 1974 and is run by Nacha, a nonprofit association funded by financial institutions.

Kirkpatrick开始orum，因为这么多的钱慢慢移动，但有些交易很快就会解决。在线打印在网上或在商店滑动它，零售商几乎立即知道是否经历过。卡网络速度快，而且昂贵的零售商也在接受卡时咳嗽2％的销售额。相比之下，通过自动化结算房屋（ACH）网络转移的任何东西，例如您的薪水，自动账单付款和银行转移，通常只需0.30美元，但最多需要五天。 ACH于1974年推出，由金融机构资助的非营利组织纳哈开展。

In addition to being slow, ACH carries very little data. When someone signs up for Chime and requests to transfer $2,000 from her Wells Fargo account, Chime initially has little information to judge whether this person actually has $2,000 in her Wells Fargo account. So it waits for the transaction to settle to fund the new account.

除了缓慢，ACH还具有很少的数据。当有人签署Chime和请求从她的Wells Fargo账户转移2,000美元时，最初没有信息判断该人是否实际上在她的Wells Fargo账户中有2,000美元。因此，它等待交易来满足于为新帐户提供资金。

To bridge this information gap, Orum’s “Foresight” product pulls in extra data through partnerships with financial institutions and uses machine learning to assess the risk of a transaction. For example, Orum can tell its customers that a given bank transfer is 99% likely to be safe. Receiving this information quickly lets the receiving bank advance funds to a consumer much faster—often the same day—and deliver a better user experience. “The advent of machine learning over the past five years made this [fast risk assessment] possible,” Kirkpatrick says.

为了通过金融机构的伙伴关系弥合这一信息差距，ORUM的“远见”产品通过伙伴关系提取额外数据，并使用机器学习来评估交易的风险。例如，ORUM可以告诉客户给定的银行转账是99％可能是安全的。收到此信息很快让您的收到银行向消费者提供更快的日常资金 - 通常是同一天 - 并提供更好的用户体验。 “过去五年的机器学习的出现使得这项[快速风险评估]可能，”Kirkpatrick说。

Orum’s 15 clients, which include digital bank SoFi and Memphis-based, 157-year-old bank First Horizon, have sped up 85% of their deposit transfers so they’re done in a day, compared with the three to four days it typically takes. They have also seen a 40% reduction in instances of “NSF” or insufficient funds. An NSF is when a customer tries to move money, but by the time the transaction is about to finalize, there isn’t enough money left in the account—like a bounced check. NSFs are bad for everyone, typically triggering a $35 fee for the consumer, a $5 fee for the financial institution and, if they happen often enough, causing Nacha to cut off a bank or fintech’s access to the ACH network.

Oreum的15个客户包括数字银行Sofi和基于孟菲斯的157岁的银行第一地平线，已经加快了85％的存款转移，因此它们在一天中完成，而通常它通常在三到四天内完成需要。他们还观察到“NSF”或基金不足的情况下减少了40％。 NSF是客户试图搬运金钱的时候，但随着交易即将完成的时间，在账户中没有足够的款项留下了反弹检查。 NSFS对每个人都不糟糕，通常为消费者触发35美元的费用，金融机构的5美元费用，如果它们经常发生，导致NACHA削减银行或Fintech对ACH网络的访问。

Orum’s second product, “Momentum,” is like a control center that directs which payment rails a transaction should travel on given the financial institution’s preferences for speed and cost. When a consumer initiates a transfer, Momentum routes it intelligently, whether that’s over ACH, a credit card network, banks’ new Real Time Payments Network or even, eventually, a blockchain-based settlement system.

ORUM的第二个产品，“动量”就像一个控制中心，指导经销轨道交易应该在鉴于金融机构的速度和成本的偏好方面旅行。当消费者启动转移时，势头智能地路由，无论是在ACP，信用卡网络，银行的新实时支付网络甚至最终，最终都是基于区块链的结算系统。

Orum went live with its first customer in September 2020 and is processing tens of millions of dollars in transactions a month. Its clients pay prices ranging from $5,000 to $25,000 a month for the service.

ORUM于2020年9月与其第一个客户一起生活，并在一个月内加工数千万美元的交易。其客户为服务每月支付5,000美元至25,000美元的价格。

The New York company is still at an early stage and faces many challenges. Technical integrations with financial institutions can be complicated and must take place “over a series of sprints” with Orum’s tech team, Kirkpatrick says. Orum has a waitlist of 50 clients.

纽约公司仍处于早期阶段，面临着许多挑战。 Kirkpatrick说，与金融机构的技术融合可能是复杂的，并且必须与Oreum的技术团队“超过一系列Sprints”。 orum有50个客户的候补名单。

Another challenge could arise if the U.S. government launched a central bank digital currency or “digital dollar” that dramatically sped up payments in America, reducing the need for Orum.

如果美国政府推出了央行数字货币或“数字美元”，可能会出现另一个挑战，或者“数字美元”在美国大大加快了美国的付款，减少了对羊眼的需求。

Matt Harris, an Orum investor and partner at Bain Capital, says the “most aggressive” implementation of a digital dollar would be where every American has a bank account at the Federal Reserve and could move his money instantly. Thousands of financial services companies would be at risk in that scenario, Harris says. We’d no longer need banks for cash management, Visa or MasterCard for payments, Venmo for money transfers. “I don't think that's what the Fed has in mind,” Harris says. “They’ve said they are not here to destroy the banking industry.” He thinks digital dollar accounts would be limited to a few purposes, like disbursement of government funds for stimulus payments.

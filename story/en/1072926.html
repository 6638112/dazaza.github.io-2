Heterosexual HIV diagnoses overtake those in gay men for first time in a decade
十年来，异性恋HIV诊断首次超过男同性恋

2022-02-13 18:23:18

For the first time in a decade, the number of new HIV diagnoses among heterosexuals is higher than for gay and bisexual men, according to new figures for England from the UK Health Security Agency.

根据英国卫生安全局（UK Health Security Agency）为英格兰提供的最新数据，十年来，异性恋者中新诊断的艾滋病病毒数量首次高于男同性恋和双性恋者。

Half of all new HIV diagnoses were in heterosexuals (49%) in England in 2020, compared to 45% in gay and bisexual men.

2020年，英国新诊断的艾滋病毒感染者中有一半是异性恋者（49%），而同性恋和双性恋男性的比例为45%。

This comes as free HIV test kits are made available for National HIV Testing Week, which launches today. Anyone who’s sexually active is encouraged to test and know their HIV status.

这是因为今天开始的全国艾滋病毒检测周提供了免费的艾滋病毒检测试剂盒。鼓励任何性活跃的人检测并了解自己的HIV状况。

The news highlights the changing shape of the domestic HIV epidemic despite a drop in testing caused by the COVID-19 pandemic. HIV testing by sexual health services among heterosexuals during 2020 fell by a third (33%), compared to a 7% decrease among gay and bisexual men. This makes the number of diagnoses among heterosexuals passing gay and bisexual men even more significant.

尽管2019冠状病毒疾病流行，但检测结果有所下降，但这一消息突显了国内艾滋病毒流行的变化。2020年，异性恋者通过性健康服务进行的艾滋病毒检测下降了三分之一（33%），而同性恋和双性恋男性的检测下降了7%。这使得异性恋者中超过同性恋和双性恋男性的诊断人数更加显著。

Heterosexuals were also far more likely to be diagnosed late, meaning damage to the immune system has already begun. More than half (51%) of women, 55% of heterosexual men and 66% of those aged 65 and over diagnosed with HIV in 2020 were diagnosed at a late stage. This compares to just 29% of gay and bisexual men. This is likely driven by a belief that they are not at risk of HIV, which is often reinforced by healthcare professionals.

异性恋者被诊断晚的可能性也大得多，这意味着免疫系统已经开始受到损害。2020年，超过一半（51%）的女性、55%的异性恋男性和66%的65岁及以上被诊断为HIV感染者是在晚期诊断的。相比之下，同性恋和双性恋男性的比例只有29%。这可能是因为他们相信自己没有感染艾滋病毒的风险，这一点往往得到医疗专业人员的支持。

Jackie, a heterosexual woman living with HIV, said: 'Not only did I never consider I could be living with HIV, neither did my doctor. I had no idea that women could get HIV until I tested positive. If you have ever been sexually active you should test regularly, it only takes once unprotected. Knowing your status is best for you and everyone else in your life.

杰基是一名感染艾滋病毒的异性恋女性，她说：'；我不仅没想过我能感染艾滋病，我的医生也没有。在我检测呈阳性之前，我不知道女性会感染HIV。如果你曾经有过性行为，你应该定期进行测试，只需要一次不受保护的测试。了解自己的身份对你和你生活中的其他人都是最好的。

'Recent developments mean HIV testing is free, quick and easy and HIV treatment is effective at controlling the virus and means I can't pass on the virus to anyone else. HIV can affect anyone, so everyone should test.'

'最近的发展意味着HIV检测是免费、快速和简单的，HIV治疗在控制病毒方面是有效的，这意味着我可以'；不要把病毒传染给别人。HIV可以影响任何人，所以每个人都应该进行检测'

Gay and bisexual men are still more impacted by HIV relative to population size, but targeted interventions for this group have led to one of the big success stories of the epidemic. New HIV diagnoses among gay and bisexual men began to fall in 2014 and that has continued every year since, with a fall of 70% between 2014 and 2020. The drop is most pronounced among white men and among those living in London.

相对于人口规模而言，同性恋和双性恋男性仍然更容易受到艾滋病毒的影响，但针对这一群体的有针对性的干预措施已经导致了这一流行病的重大成功案例之一。2014年，同性恋和双性恋男性中新的艾滋病毒诊断开始下降，此后每年都在下降，2014年至2020年间下降了70%。这种下降在白人和伦敦人中最为明显。

The fall in diagnoses among gay and bisexual men is a result of the growing availability of HIV prevention pill PrEP and targeted promotion of routine HIV testing followed by the rapid initiation of HIV treatment for those diagnosed, meaning they are more quickly virally suppressed and cannot pass on HIV.

男同性恋和双性恋男性的确诊率下降是因为越来越多的HIV预防药片制剂和有针对性地推广常规HIV检测，然后迅速开始对确诊者进行HIV治疗，这意味着他们会更快地受到病毒抑制，无法传播HIV。

We hope that, with greater awareness, increased testing and better access to PrEP for heterosexuals, this can be replicated across the wider population. Our message is that HIV can affect anyone and everyone needs to know how to protect themselves against HIV – regardless of sexuality, gender, ethnicity or age.

我们希望，通过提高认识、增加测试和更好地为异性恋者提供准备，这可以在更广泛的人群中推广。我们要传达的信息是，艾滋病毒可以影响任何人，每个人都需要知道如何保护自己免受艾滋病毒感染——无论性别、种族或年龄。

Increased HIV testing is crucial, because an estimated 5% of people living with HIV in the UK (4,660) are unaware, which can adversely affect their health and means they may pass HIV on. The UK is aiming to end new HIV cases by 2030, which is why maximising every opportunity for HIV testing is necessary for achieving that aim.

增加HIV检测至关重要，因为据估计，英国有5%的HIV感染者（4660人）没有意识到这一点，这可能会对他们的健康产生不利影响，并意味着他们可能会传播HIV。英国的目标是到2030年结束新的艾滋病毒病例，这就是为什么要实现这一目标，就必须最大限度地利用每一次艾滋病毒检测机会。

You can order a free HIV test kit to do at home during National HIV Testing Week, or you can visit your local sexual health clinic.

在全国HIV检测周期间，你可以在家里订购免费的HIV检测试剂盒，也可以去当地的性健康诊所。

Health and Social Care Secretary Sajid Javid said: 'It is fantastic to see the diagnosis rate of HIV fall further among gay and bisexual men as a result of testing and the routine commissioning of PrEP, but we must make sure this trend is reflected in all groups.

卫生和社会保障部长萨吉德·贾维德说'；由于检测和PrEP的常规调试，同性恋和双性恋男性的艾滋病毒诊断率进一步下降，这是非常美妙的，但我们必须确保这一趋势在所有群体中都得到反映。

'I am committed to our goal of no new HIV transmissions by 2030, but we cannot achieve it alone. We all have a part to play – HIV testing is free and confidential for everyone, and knowing your status will allow you to access appropriate prevention or treatment.'

'我致力于我们的目标，即到2030年不再有新的艾滋病毒传播，但我们无法单独实现这一目标。我们都有自己的责任——艾滋病毒检测对每个人都是免费和保密的，了解自己的状况将使你能够获得适当的预防或治疗'

Taku Mukiwa, Head of Health Programmes, said: 'For the first time in a decade there are more heterosexuals than gay and bisexual men being diagnosed with HIV. Heterosexuals also saw a far steeper drop in testing for HIV during COVID-19 lockdown and are far more likely to be diagnosed late.

卫生项目负责人Taku Mukiwa说：'；十年来，被诊断感染艾滋病毒的异性恋者首次超过男同性恋和双性恋者。异性恋者在2019冠状病毒疾病筛查中发现HIV检测的下降幅度更大，诊断的可能性更大。

'That’s why we need to see more heterosexuals getting tested to avoid anyone living with undiagnosed HIV for a long time. This is important for their own health as well as for efforts to stop HIV being passed on as the vast majority of people get HIV from someone who is unaware they have it.'

'这就是为什么我们需要看到更多的异性恋者接受检测，以避免长期感染未确诊的艾滋病毒。这对他们自己的健康以及阻止艾滋病毒传播的努力都很重要，因为绝大多数人都是从不知道自己感染了艾滋病毒的人那里感染的'

Kate Folkard, Interim Deputy Director of the HIV Division at the UK Health Security Agency, said: “We must address inequalities and find creative ways to achieve a reduction in transmission across all populations. To end HIV transmission, we need to diagnose people early, start treatment promptly and support people to continue with their treatment. Detecting HIV early allows effective treatment to start sooner and people diagnosed can expect to have a normal life expectancy.

Kate Folkard是英国卫生安全局HIV部门的临时副主任，说：“我们必须解决不平等问题，找到创造性的方法来减少所有人群中的传播。要结束艾滋病毒传播，我们需要及早诊断，及时开始治疗，并支持人们继续治疗。及早检测艾滋病毒可以让有效的治疗更早开始，而被诊断的人可以期待o有正常的预期寿命。

'In addition, people living with HIV who are treated and maintain an undetectable level of virus cannot pass HIV, known as Undetectable = Untransmittable (U=U). We encourage everyone to take up the offer of a free test, only by knowing your HIV status can you be empowered to take action. Through the HIV Action Plan, we will continue to work together with our partners across the health system to reach our goal of ending HIV transmission by 2030.'

'此外，艾滋病毒携带者如果接受治疗并保持不可检测的病毒水平，就不能通过艾滋病毒，即不可检测=不可传播（U=U）。我们鼓励每个人接受免费检测，只有了解自己的HIV状况，你才能有权采取行动。通过《艾滋病毒行动计划》，我们将继续与整个卫生系统的合作伙伴合作，以实现到2030年结束艾滋病毒传播的目标'

It was opened by Edward Lloyd (c. 1648–15 February 1713) on Tower Street in 1686. [1] [2] The establishment was a popular place for sailors, merchants and shipowners, and Lloyd catered to them by providing reliable shipping news. The shipping industry community frequented the place to discuss maritime insurance, shipbroking and foreign trade. [2] The dealings that took place led to the establishment of the insurance market Lloyd's of London, [ when?] Lloyd's Register and several related shipping and insurance businesses. [3]

它是由爱德华·劳埃德(c. 1648年至1713年2月15日)于1686年在塔街揭幕的。[1][2]这个机构是水手、商人和船东的好去处，劳合社通过提供可靠的航运新闻来迎合他们。航运界经常到这里来讨论海上保险、船舶经纪和对外贸易。[2]发生的这些交易导致了伦敦劳合社保险市场的建立，[什么时候？]。劳合社登记处和几个相关的航运和保险业务。[3]。

The coffee shop relocated to Lombard Street in December 1691. Lloyd had a pulpit installed in the new premises, from which maritime auction prices and shipping news were announced. [2] Candle auctions were held in the establishment, with lots frequently involving ships and shipping. [4] From 1696–1697 Lloyd also experimented with publishing a newspaper, Lloyd's News, reporting on shipping schedules and insurance agreements reached in the coffee house. [5] In 1713, the year of Edward Lloyd's death, he modified his will to assign the lease of the coffee house to his head waiter, William Newton, who then married one of Lloyd's daughters, Handy. Newton died the following year and Handy subsequently married Samuel Sheppard. She died in 1720 and Sheppard died in 1727, leaving the coffee house to his sister Elizabeth and her husband, Thomas Jemson. Jemson founded the Lloyd's List newspaper in 1734, similar to the previous Lloyd's News. [5] Merchants continued to discuss insurance matters here until 1774, when the participating members of the insurance arrangement formed a committee and moved to the Royal Exchange on Cornhill as the Society of Lloyd's.

这家咖啡店于1691年12月迁至隆巴德街。劳合社在新办公场所安装了一个讲坛，从中宣布了海上拍卖价格和航运消息。[2]在该设施内举行了烛光拍卖，拍卖品经常涉及船舶和航运。[4]从1696年到1697年，劳合社还尝试出版一份名为《劳合社新闻》(Lloyd‘s News)的报纸，报道在咖啡馆达成的船期和保险协议。[5]1713年，也就是爱德华·劳埃德去世的那一年，他修改了遗嘱，将咖啡馆的租赁权转让给了他的领班威廉·牛顿(William Newton)，后者随后娶了劳埃德的一个女儿汉迪(Handy)。牛顿于次年去世，汉迪随后与塞缪尔·谢泼德结婚。她于1720年去世，谢泼德于1727年去世，把咖啡馆留给了他的妹妹伊丽莎白和她的丈夫托马斯·杰姆森(Thomas Jemson)。詹姆森在1734年创办了劳合社名单报纸，与之前的劳合社新闻相似。[5]商人们继续在这里讨论保险问题，直到1774年，参与保险安排的成员组成了一个委员会，并作为劳合社搬到了康希尔的皇家交易所。

The 17th century original shop frontage of Lloyd's Coffee House is owned by Lloyd's of London and in 2011 was temporarily re-erected on display at the National Maritime Museum. [6] A blue plaque in Lombard Street commemorates the coffee house's second location (now occupied at ground level by Sainsbury's supermarket). [4] It was fictionalized in the 1936 film Lloyd's of London.

劳合社咖啡馆17世纪的原始店面归伦敦劳合社所有，2011年在国家海事博物馆临时重建。[6]朗伯德街的一块蓝色牌匾是为了纪念咖啡馆的第二个位置(现在位于一楼的是塞恩斯伯里超市)。[4]它在1936年的电影《伦敦劳合社》中被虚构。

Austrian Lloyd: Österreichischer Lloyd: an Austrian, major mediterranean shipping company founded in 1833, which after World War I became Lloyd Triestino

奥地利劳埃德：奥斯特雷希希舍尔劳埃德：奥地利一家主要的地中海航运公司，成立于1833年，第一次世界大战后成为劳埃德·特里斯蒂诺(Lloyd Triestino)。

Norddeutscher Lloyd, shipping, Germany, and the Lloyd (car) created by a subsidiary, and the Lloyd Werft dockyard they also own

Norddeutscher Lloyd，航运，德国，以及由一家子公司创建的Lloyd(汽车)，以及他们还拥有的Lloyd Werft造船厂。

Lloyds Bank and its related organisations are not named after the London coffee house; the bank was founded in Birmingham by Sampson Lloyd.

劳埃德银行及其相关组织并不是以伦敦咖啡馆的名字命名的；该银行是由桑普森·劳埃德在伯明翰创立的。

Who’s got two thumbs and shipped her first of 12 startups in 12 months on schedule? That’s right, this girl.

谁有两个大拇指可以按计划在12个月内交付她12家初创公司中的第一家？没错，这个女孩。

在开发的第一周，Blogging for Devs社区通过终身交易，订阅和我有史以来第一笔比特币交易（感谢Nico！）相结合，实现了5,000美元的收入。

赚钱的第一周并不能使我的书取得成功。

I’m thrilled at all the cool members who decided to join.Exactly as I’d hoped, each person adds something unique and interesting to the community,and I’m really excited to see where it goes.

我对加入这个决定的所有优秀成员感到非常兴奋。正如我所希望的那样，每个人都为社区添加了一些独特而有趣的东西，我真的很高兴看到它的去向。

在这篇文章中，我将在付费社区发布的第一周分享幕后花絮，并展示我11月份的发货情况。

在下面，您会发现图表，图形，屏幕截图，策略，思考和我的总体规划，该规划旨在通过病毒性生成的内容为Devs开发Blog

即使我本月开始加入社区，但社区本身还是在7月份作为“封闭Beta”推出的。

The newsletter that helped me connect with first 100 free members (and most of thepaid members — more on that later) was launched in May.

该新闻简报于5月发布，该新闻简报帮助我与最初的100个免费会员（以及大多数付费会员–随后更多）建立了联系。

Blogging for Devs Community is a paidcommunity for developers, makers, engineering managers, and technical founders to grow their blogsthrough feedback, collaboration, and continuous learning.

开发人员社区博客是一个付费社区，供开发人员，制造商，工程经理和技术创始人通过反馈，协作和持续学习来发展其博客。

We have accountability groups, virtual events and workshops, members-only resources and original content,and most importantly — super kind and knowledgeable members.

我们有问责制小组，虚拟活动和研讨会，会员专用资源和原始内容，最重要的是-超级友善和知识渊博的会员。

在10月，我构建了Events，会员RSS feed，注册，付款，登录页面，入门，个人资料，欢迎电子邮件序列以及少量的入门内容。我自己在Figma中设计了所有东西。有关构建产品的更多详细信息，您可以在我的Twitter线程中查看日常情况。

Success metrics: Why you won’t find an overall conversion rate in this report

成功指标：为什么在此报告中找不到整体转化率

My real goal for the community was to getgreat people in, and not to overly optimize for converting anyone with a pulse.

对于社区，我的真正目标是吸引更多的人加入，而不是过度优化以使任何有心人转变。

That’s why my biggest launch fear wasn’t that no one would sign up.

这就是为什么我最大的发射恐惧不是没有人会报名。

It was that people I don’t like would sign up. Spammers, self-promoters, attention-seekers,jerks.Thankfully it hasn’t been the case and everyone is awesome so far 🤞

是我不喜欢的人签约。垃圾邮件发送者，自我推广者，寻求关注者，混蛋。令人遗憾的是，情况并非如此，到目前为止，每个人都很棒

I try to scare those types away with a prominent Code of Conduct and an “Is this right for you?” checklist.

我试图通过突出的行为准则和“这对您合适吗？”来吓those那些类型的人。清单。

That’s why I have no idea what my “conversion rate” for the community is and I don’t actually care.

这就是为什么我不知道我对社区的“转化率”是什么，而我实际上并不在乎。

I do pay attention to the opt-in rate for my free newsletter, though, so don’t take this as, “Girl doesn’t care about metrics.”

不过，我确实关注免费电子报的选择率，因此请不要将其视为“女孩不在乎指标”。

The reason is, joining a community is a long-term thing.Something like this often takes time and multiple exposures for peopleto make the decision — it was the same for me and Rosieland and Trends.

原因是，加入社区是一项长期的工作。类似的事情通常需要时间，并且人们需要多次接触才能做出决定，这对我和Rosieland and Trends都是一样的。

What I can I learn about the members who did sign up, so I can find more people like them?

我可以了解到有关注册会员的信息，因此可以找到更多喜欢他们的人吗？

Were paying members wooed by my slick landing page, or convinced bya carefully crafted tweet?

是付费会员被我光滑的登录页面所吸引，还是被精心制作的推文说服了？

Most new members were already subscribed to my free weekly newsletter about blogging for developers, which is about 4,000 subscribers strong.

大多数新成员已经订阅了我关于开发人员博客的免费每周新闻，这大约有4,000个订阅者。

Every Friday for nearly six months, I’ve been sending out original content aboutblogging, SEO, going viral, attracting an audience, and a splash of motivational moments. It takes me an entire day to write.

在将近六个月的每个星期五中，我一直在发送有关博客，搜索引擎优化（SEO），病毒式传播，吸引受众以及激励性时刻的原创内容。我花了整整一天的时间来写作。

新闻简报的背景：我从5月开始撰写新闻简报，至今已发展到4,000多个订阅者。如果您想知道如何做，请查看我在这里和这里所做的文章。

Many people had multiple touchpoints: subscriber and on the waitlist, or followed me on twitter and subscriber to the newsletter.

许多人有多个接触点：订户和候补名单，或者在Twitter和订户上关注我。

但是，只有31％的新成员在Twitter上关注我。相反，我并不感到冒犯-我更喜欢它。

Now I know that if I want to grow the community, the best thing I can dois have more of the right people join the newsletter first.

现在我知道，如果我想发展社区，我能做的最好的事情就是让更多合适的人先加入新闻通讯。

That’s why my goal is to grow the newsletter, and trust that the communitywill eventually benefit from the best subscribers joining up.

这就是为什么我的目标是发展新闻通讯，并相信社区最终将从最佳订户中受益。

As I said in the tweet where I collected ideas for this article, the $5K revenue is not MRR (monthly recurring revenue).

正如我在收集本文想法的推文中所说，5K美元的收入不是MRR（每月经常性收入）。

Someone on Twitter recently asked me if I have any “thought leadership”to spare on the topic of lifetime pricing.

Twitter上最近有人问我，我是否有任何“思想领导力”可以节省终身定价的话题。

Offering a lifetime price at a lower cost than 2 years as a member isreally just a way for me to say “Thank you” to people who are in it for the long haul.

作为会员，以比2年更低的价格提供终生价格，这实际上是我向长期参与其中的人们说“谢谢”的一种方式。

You might see $5K in a week and think it sounds like a lot of money. If only onecould extrapolate that to $20k/mo, then I’d be set.

您可能会在一周内看到$ 5K，并认为这听起来很多钱。如果只有一个人可以将其推断为每月2万美元，那么我将被定为。

That’s why my November startup is a side project to scale reach of the newsletter,which should in turn drive the growth of the community.

这就是为什么我11月份的创业公司是一个扩展新闻通讯范围的附带项目，这反过来应该会推动社区的发展。

My next task is to expand the outermost ring with the kind of people who arelikely to join and enjoy the newsletter.

我的下一个任务是与最有可能加入并喜欢时事通讯的人一起扩大最外圈。

Although people still write me emails saying they’ve been following foryears, and just found out I have a newsletter — most of that is probably in the past.

尽管人们仍然写信给我，说他们一直在关注多年，才发现我有新闻通讯-其中大部分可能是过去的事情。

I really don’t want to spend all day on Twitter “growing my social media presence”.I’d rather be building cool shit and helping other people build cool shit, too.Let people find me through that.

我真的不想一整天都在Twitter上“增加我的社交媒体影响力”。我宁愿建立酷狗屎，也帮助其他人建立酷狗屎，让人们找到我。

Random mentions and links. I find out through welcome emails or throughstalking my backlink reports from time to time.

随机提及和链接。我会通过欢迎电子邮件或不时跟踪我的反向链接报告来查找信息。

我确实回覆了所有给我发电子邮件的人，但在美国大选后的前一周度过后，我却落后了一些

A prominent mention on Twitter, Indie Hackers, or the success of a subscriber’sblog post can push than number over 100.

在Twitter，独立黑客或订阅者的博客帖子上的成功提及可以使人数超过100。

问题？如今，口口相传还不足以使新闻快讯增长得足够快，并在短期内实现可持续发展。

Ironically, I’m also a blogger with no spare time to grow it writing a ton of individual blog posts 😅

具有讽刺意味的是，我也是一个没有多余时间来发展它的博客作者，撰写了大量个人博客文章blog

So how can I grow my newsletter about blogging, without blogging myself,and without relying on Twitter for all my traffic?

那么，如何在不写博客，不依赖Twitter的情况下发展关于博客的新闻通讯呢？

How I’ll build traffic to Blogging for Devs (while writing virtually no blog posts)

如何为Blogging for Devs建立流量（而实际上没有撰写博客文章）

The first one is this: Developers want to discover blogs written by other developers.Reading company engineering blogs is fine, but quality content from their peers or individualsthey admire is far more interesting.

第一个是：开发人员希望发现其他开发人员编写的博客。阅读公司工程博客很好，但是他们的同行或个人所欣赏的高质量内容要有趣得多。

Most places to discover developer content feature individual articles. HackerNews, Reddit, and Twitter all fundamentally work this way.

发现开发人员内容的大多数地方都有个别文章。 HackerNews，Reddit和Twitter基本上都以这种方式工作。

But what’s more interesting is the person behind the blog. Their personality, what makes them unique,inspiring, and worth following in their own right.

但是更有趣的是博客背后的人。他们的个性，使他们与众不同，鼓舞人心，值得自己追随。

Dear Tech Twitter: What is your NUMBER ONE favorite blog by someone in tech, who has a distinct writing style? Where you read their articles for their unique personality or voice? (Pls no list of twitter handles, direct link to the blog is 👍)

亲爱的技术Twitter：科技界人士，您的第一个网志最喜欢他的写作风格是什么？您在哪里阅读他们的文章，以了解他们的独特个性或声音？ （请没有Twitter句柄列表，直接链接到博客是👍）

理论的扩展（这次证据较少）：许多阅读技术博客的人也渴望创建自己的博客。

因此，如果我创建一个供人们发现最佳开发者博客的地方，那么出现的人们也很有可能也会喜欢我的新闻通讯。

My plan is to crowdsource recommendations for the best tech blogs across anumber of tech stacks, written by individual developers.

我的计划是在由个体开发人员撰写的众多技术堆栈中众包推荐最佳技术博客。

With this seed data, I’ll generate “Top 50” lists of the best technical blogsacross a variety of topics, designed to help people discover amazing authors and attract people to the newsletter.

借助这些种子数据，我将生成涵盖各个主题的最佳技术博客的“前50名”列表，旨在帮助人们发现出色的作者并吸引人们阅读新闻。

Which blogs are best will be curated by me for quality, then ranked by engagement on Twitter,favoring unique mentions and word-of-mouth recommendations by other developers.

我会挑选质量最好的博客，然后按在Twitter上的参与程度进行排名，以吸引其他开发者的独特提及和口碑推荐。

In turn, these each of my lists will rank for relevant keywords in Google, drivingsearch traffic to the website every month.

反过来，我的每个列表都将为Google中的相关关键字排名，从而每月增加网站的搜索量。

For example, my page about the best “JavaScript blogs” will hopefully show up firstfor that search term, and provide genuinely great recommendations of individualdevelopers who write about JavaScript.

例如，我关于最佳“ JavaScript博客”的页面有望首先显示该搜索词，并为撰写JavaScript的个人开发人员提供真正的出色建议。

I’m going to launch it on Hacker News at the end of the month, and will continue to tailorthe features, design, and copy around that audience.

我将在本月底在Hacker News上发布它，并将继续针对该受众群体定制功能，设计和复制。

I plan to add things I hope developers there will see and think, “That’s cool.”

我计划添加一些东西，希望在那里的开发人员能够看到并认为：“太酷了。”

For example: realtime data updates, data visualizations, tasteful SVG animations, and the ability to filter and sort the data.

例如：实时数据更新，数据可视化，精美的SVG动画以及过滤和排序数据的功能。

If I succeed, the avalanche of backlinks it receives by being on the front page of Hacker News will helpit rank higher and faster in Google.

如果我成功了，在Hacker News的首页上收到的大量反向链接将帮助它在Google中排名更高和更快。

If I can’t make Hacker News love it (or at least, upvote it), I’ll probably still rank. It might take a few months, though.

如果我不能让Hacker News喜欢它（或者至少要投票），我可能还会排名。不过，可能需要几个月的时间。

And since the content is generated, I can easily scale out into the longtailfor more niche technologies.

而且由于生成了内容，因此我可以轻松地将其扩展到更多的细分技术中。

If it sounds smart, it is. I also didn’t come up with it. I was originally inspired by Detailed.com’s blog rankings but there are a lot of places out theredoing generated content for SEO.

如果听起来很聪明，那就是。我也没有想到。最初，我是从Detailed.com的博客排名中获得启发的，但是有很多地方可以为SEO生成内容。

只是，其中大多数人的建议都很糟糕，而且看起来很丑陋（看着您的Feedspot）。这就是为什么我希望我能超过所有这些人，只要我能获得足够的链接。

NASA Plans Blockbuster Return To Venus
美国国家航空航天局计划重磅炸弹返回金星

2021-06-04 00:21:43

WASHINGTON, D.C.—Venus can no longer wait. NASA will send two new robotic missions to Earth’s hothouse twin, the agency’s new administrator, Bill Nelson, announced this afternoon at his “state of NASA” speech here at the agency’s headquarters. The announcement effectively creates a new Venus program in one fell swoop.

华盛顿，D.C.-Venus不能再等待。美国宇航局将向地球上的新机器人双胞胎派遣两个新的机器人任务，该机构的新管理员Bill Nelson，今天下午宣布他在该机构总部的“美国宇航局状态”演讲中。该公告有效地在一次俯冲下创造了一个新的金星计划。

The missions, together costing up to $1 billion, mark NASA’s first visit to the planet since the early 1990s, whereas nearby Mars has seen a host of robotic visitors. They’re expected to launch by the decade’s end. “We hope these missions will further our understanding of how Earth evolved and why it’s currently habitable when others in our Solar System are not,” Nelson, a former senator, said while announcing the selection. “This is really exciting stuff.”

任务，共耗费10亿美元，Mark Nasa自20世纪90年代初以来首次访问该地球，而附近的火星已经看到过了一系列机器人游客。他们预计将在十年的终端发射。 “我们希望这些任务进一步了解地球如何发展以及为什么当我们太阳系中的其他人并非如此居住时，为什么它是可居住的，”前参议员纳尔逊在宣布选择时表示。 “这真的是令人兴奋的东西。”

Planetary scientists who study Venus were elated at the news, after decades of failed proposals—including previous incarnations of the selected missions. “I am stunned. Very emotional,” says Patrick McGovern, a planetary scientist at the Lunar and Planetary Institute. McGovern and colleagues had long argued that sustained study of Venus, like the landings on Mars, could revolutionize the understanding of Earth’s early history. One mission alone would not do it. “And guess what, in one fell swoop NASA created that #VenusProgram, with two outstanding missions.”

学习金星的行星科学家在新闻中兴起，经过数十年的失败提案 - 包括所选任务的先前化身。 “我震惊了。非常情绪化，“农历和行星学院的行星科学家帕特里克麦戈尔顿说。麦戈尔恩及其同事们长期以来，持续研究金星，就像火星上的着陆，可以彻底改变对地球的早期历史的理解。单独一个使命不会这样做。 “并猜猜，在一个秋季举动中，美国国家航空航天局创造了#venusprack，有两个优秀的任务。”

The first mission selected from four finalists named in February 2020 is DAVINCI+ (Deep Atmosphere Venus Investigation of Noble gases, Chemistry, and Imaging Plus) from NASA’s Goddard Space Flight Center and led by James Garvin, chief scientist of Goddard’s scientific division. It will send an armored sphere plunging through the venusian atmosphere, its instruments measuring noble gases to sort out the planet’s origins and sniffing for sulfur and carbon near the surface for evidence of recent volcanic activity. DAVINCI+ will also include an orbiter to map the planet’s geology, including its mysterious highlands. (A previous DAVINCI proposal was led by Lori Glaze, now chief of planetary science at NASA headquarters, who excused herself from the mission selection process.)

从2月2020年2月份的四名决赛选手中选择的第一个任务是NASA戈达德太空飞行中心的Davinci +（深氛围，化学，化学和成像加上），由戈达德科学部门的首席科学家詹姆斯卡维林领导。它将通过Venusian氛围发送装甲球体，其仪器测量惰性气体，为近期火山活动附近的硫磺和碳而释放出行星的起源和嗅探。 Davinci +还将包括一个轨道登机机来映射地球地质的地质，包括其神秘的高地。 （以前的Davinci提案由Lori Glaze领导，现在是NASA总部的行星科学主任，他从特派团选择过程中解除了自己。）

Analyzing isotopes of these noble gases in the atmosphere could give scientists a window into whether Venus started with as much water as Earth—and whether the planet might still be hiding water, the lubricant of plate tectonics, deep in its interior. Probing the rock composition could reveal whether, as some researchers suspect, the slightly elevated regions called tesserae are remnants of continents.

在大气中分析了这些惰性气体的同位素可以将科学家们提供窗户，进入Venus是否从地球中的水开始，以及行星是否可能仍然隐藏水，板构造的润滑剂，在其内部深处。探测岩石组成可以揭示作为一些研究人员怀疑的，称为Tesserae的略微升高的区域是大洲的残余物。

The second Venus mission is VERITAS (Venus Emissivity, Radio Science, InSAR, Topography, and Spectroscopy) from NASA’s Jet Propulsion Laboratory and led by Suzanne Smrekar, a planetary scientist at JPL. It will use synthetic aperture radar to peer through the planet’s thick clouds and re-create its topography, revealing whether volcanoes or variants of tectonic plates are active on its surface. Previous missions have revealed evocative evidence of hot spots but lacked the resolution to answer questions about why Venus’s fate differed so starkly from Earth’s.

第二个金星任务是来自美国宇航局的喷气机推进实验室的Veritas（金星发射率，无线电科学，Insar，地形，地形和光谱学），由JPL的行星科学家Suzanne Smrekar领导。它将通过行星厚的云层使用合成孔径雷达并重新创造其地形，揭示了构造板的火山或变体在其表面上有效。以前的任务揭示了热点的令人兴奋的证据，但缺乏决议来回答有关为什么Venus命运从地球上如此持久地不同的问题。

The high-resolution radar could reveal surface features lost in the noise of old measurements, Smrekar says: perhaps chasms that resemble Earth’s midocean ridges, or the details of mysterious oval-shaped features called coronae, which could mark where plumes of hot material from Venus’s mantle are causing parts of the crust to sink under others. Smrekar suspects Venus is a good analog for the time when plate tectonics began on Earth. Its greenhouse-heated surface is cooling much more slowly than Earth’s and may only now be starting to crack into plates.

Smrekar说：高分辨率雷达可以揭示在旧测量噪声中损失的表面特征，也许是类似地上的山坡的追逐，或者神秘的椭圆形功能的细节称为coronae，这可以标记Venus的热材料的羽毛地幔导致地壳的部分地壳下沉。 Smrekar嫌疑人是一个良好的模拟，因为板块构造始于地球。其温室加热的表面冷却比地球更慢，现在只能开始裂成板块。

“We’re ushering in a new decade of Venus to understand how an Earth-like planet can become a hothouse,” Thomas Zurbuchen, NASA’s science chief, said in a statement announcing the selection. “Our goals are profound. It is not just understanding the evolution of planets and habitability in our own solar system, but extending beyond these boundaries to exoplanets.”

“我们迎来了一个新的金星的十年，了解地球样的星球如何成为一个温室，”NASA的科学院长托马斯·Zurbuchen，在宣布选择的声明中表示。 “我们的目标是深刻的。这不仅仅是了解我们自己的太阳系中行星和居民的演变，而是超越这些边界到外产上。“

The scientific case for exploring Venus has long been strong. No planet has more to say about how Earth came to be. Mars is tiny and frozen, its heat and atmosphere largely lost to space long ago. Venus could host active volcanoes, and it may have once featured oceans and continents, which are critical to the evolution of life. Plate tectonics roughly like Earth’s might have held sway there, or might be starting today, hidden under the clouds. Venus also proves by example that orbiting within a star’s “habitable zone” doesn’t guarantee a planet is suitable for life. Understanding how Venus’s atmosphere went bad and turned into a runaway greenhouse, boiling away any oceans and baking the surface, could help astronomers studying other solar systems distinguish truly Earth-like exoplanets from our evil twins.

探索金星的科学案件长期以来一直很强劲。没有星球更多地说出地球如何成为如何。火星是微小而冷冻的，它的热量和大气层很远在很久以前丢失到太空。金星可以举办活跃的火山，它可能曾经有过的海洋和大陆，这对生活的演变至关重要。板块构造大致像地球的可能已经在那里摇摆，或者可能正在今天开始，隐藏在云下。金星也证明，明星的“可居区”内的轨道不保证行星适合生活。了解金星的气氛如何变坏，变成了一个失控的温室，煮掉任何海洋并烘焙表面，可以帮助天文学家学习其他太阳能系统，区分真正的地球像邪恶的双胞胎的真正的地球的外产。

The case for Venus research gained additional attention in September 2020, when astronomers announced the detection of phosphine in the planet’s atmosphere, a potential indicator of life. Since then evidence of the detection has been hotly disputed, with no resolution yet in sight. The DAVINCI+ probe, although not designed for the job, could feasibly detect phosphine during its descent.

金星研究的案例于2020年9月在2020年9月获得了额外的关注，当时天文学家宣布在地球大气层中的磷化膦检测到潜在的生命指标。从那时起，检测的证据已经热烈争议，目前没有分辨率。 Davinci +探头，虽然没有为该工作设计，但在其下降期间可以可行地检测膦。

The previous two Discovery selections, made in 2017, are nearing completion. Lucy, launching in October 2020, will fly past six of Jupiter’s Trojan asteroids, which precede and follow the gas giant’s orbit. Psyche, launching in August 2022, will orbit a rare iron and nickel asteroid of the same name, believed to be the stripped-bare core of an ancient planetesimal. That Discovery round also featured two proposed missions to Venus among its five finalists. After they weren’t selected, planetary scientists despaired of the long gap between NASA missions to the planet.

在2017年制作的前两个发现选择即将结束。 Lucy在2020年10月推出，将飞过六个木星的木星特洛伊木马小行星，在瓦斯巨头的轨道之前。心灵，在2022年8月发射，将轨道是一个罕见的铁和镍小行星的同名，被认为是一个古代星期六的剥离核心。该发现轮还将两名拟议的任务推荐为五名决赛选手之间的金星。在他们没有被选中之后，行星科学家在美国宇航局与地球之间的长期间差距下绝望。

For more than 2 decades, NASA’s competitive Discovery program has supported low-cost planetary missions, with a goal of launching a new probe every 2 to 3 years. Although the federal budget crunch and cost overruns last decade derailed that cadence, the agency is now close to its preferred schedule. President Joe Biden’s administration has proposed an 18% boost to NASA’s planetary science budget for next year, though it remains to be seen whether Congress will go along with the increase.

超过2年的数十年来，美国宇航局的竞争激烈的发现计划支持了低成本的行星任务，其目标是每2至3年推出一次新探针。虽然上十年的联邦预算紧缩和成本超支销售，但该机构现在接近其首选日程。 Joe Biden总裁的政府提出了明年促进了NASA的行星科学预算18％的推动，尽管国会是否会随着增长而持续下去。

After NASA announced the four finalists last year, it gave them an additional year and $3 million to flesh out their final proposals, including some extra time because of the pandemic. It’s an invariably stressful process, even without the events of 2020, recalled Lindy Elkins-Tanton, principal investigator of Psyche and a planetary scientist at Arizona State University, Tempe, on Twitter earlier today. “The pressure of hundreds of people who had put their all into it, and organizations that had spent significant amounts of money supporting the pursuit,” she wrote. “Surreal, and unavoidable, like being on a hurtling train.”

在去年美国国家航空航天队宣布这四名决赛中，它给了他们额外的一年和300万美元，以摧毁他们的最终提案，包括额外的时间，因为大流行。即使没有2020年的活动，这是一个总是压力的过程，召回了Lindy Elkins-Tanton，Spence的主要调查员和亚利桑那州立大学的行星科学家，Tewpe今天早些时候。 “曾经把他们全部放入其中的数百人的压力，以及花费大量资金支持追求的组织，”她写道。 “超现实主义，不可避免的，就像在受伤的火车上一样。”

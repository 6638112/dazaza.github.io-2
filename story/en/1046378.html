Not just MagSafe: Apple reminds users not to hold iPhones near pacemakers
不仅仅是MagSafe：Apple提醒用户不要在起搏器旁拿着iPhone

2021-01-28 23:15:47

This week, Apple published clarifications to its support documents to address consumer concern that, because of the presence of the MagSafe magnet system in new iPhones, the iPhone 12 and its 2020 peers are particularly unsafe to hold in close proximity to an Implantable Cardioverter Defibrillator (ICD) or pacemaker.

本周，苹果发布了其支持文件的澄清说明，以解决消费者的担忧，由于新iPhone中存在MagSafe磁铁系统，iPhone 12及其2020年同类产品特别不安全地紧贴植入式心脏复律除颤器（ ICD）或起搏器。

The updated warning from Apple to customers repeats previous statements that keeping any iPhone within six inches of an ICD or pacemaker (or within 15 inches, while charging wirelessly) is unsafe. The warning also claims the iPhone 12 is not specifically more dangerous than other models. Several weeks back, Heart Rhythm Journal published results of a test wherein it repeatedly found that moving an iPhone 12 with MagSafe close to a patient's ICD interfered with the functioning of that lifesaving device. After that report, tech enthusiasts visited forums, Twitter, and Reddit to spread speculation that the iPhone 12 was particularly dangerous to people with pacemakers because of the introduction of MagSafe.

苹果公司向客户发出的更新警告重复了先前的声明，即将iPhone放在ICD或起搏器的六英寸范围内（或在无线充电时在15英寸的范围内）是不安全的。警告还声称，iPhone 12并不比其他型号特别危险。几周前，《心律杂志》（Heart Rhythm Journal）发表了一项测试结果，其中反复发现将装有MagSafe的iPhone 12靠近患者的ICD会干扰该救生设备的功能。报告发布后，技术爱好者访问了论坛，Twitter和Reddit，以猜测由于引入了MagSafe，iPhone 12对带有起搏器的人特别危险。

However, the journal didn't mention that modern smartphones already contained magnets and emit signals that necessitate that users with ICDs or pacemakers keep them several inches away from those medical devices, even before the introduction of MagSafe to the iPhone line in 2020.

但是，该杂志没有提到现代智能手机已经包含磁铁并发出信号，这要求使用ICD或起搏器的用户将它们与这些医疗设备保持几英寸的距离，甚至在2020年将MagSafe引入iPhone系列之前也是如此。

In other words, it is indeed unsafe to hold the iPhone 12 close to these medical devices—but it was also unsafe to do so with the iPhone 11, iPhone X, or any number of competing smartphones. In its support document update, Apple claims that the iPhone 12's MagSafe feature does not change its warning, because the warning was already in place.

换句话说，将iPhone 12靠近这些医疗设备确实是不安全的-但是使用iPhone 11，iPhone X或任何数量的竞争智能手机这样做也是不安全的。苹果在其支持文档更新中声称，iPhone 12的MagSafe功能不会更改其警告，因为该警告已经到位。

iPhone contains magnets as well as components and radios that emit electromagnetic fields. All MagSafe accessories (each sold separately) also contain magnets—and MagSafe Charger and MagSafe Duo Charger contain radios. These magnets and electromagnetic fields might interfere with medical devices.

iPhone包含磁铁以及发出电磁场的组件和收音机。所有的MagSafe附件（均单独出售）也包含磁铁-MagSafe充电器和MagSafe Duo充电器均包含收音机。这些磁铁和电磁场可能会干扰医疗设备。

Though all iPhone 12 models contain more magnets than prior iPhone models, they're not expected to pose a greater risk of magnetic interference to medical devices than prior iPhone models.

尽管所有iPhone 12型号都比以前的iPhone型号包含更多的磁铁，但预计它们不会比以前的iPhone型号对医疗设备造成更大的电磁干扰风险。

Medical devices such as implanted pacemakers and defibrillators might contain sensors that respond to magnets and radios when in close contact. To avoid any potential interactions with these devices, keep your iPhone and MagSafe accessories a safe distance away from your device (more than 6 inches / 15 cm apart or more than 12 inches / 30 cm apart if wirelessly charging). But consult with your physician and your device manufacturer for specific guidelines.

诸如植入式起搏器和除颤器之类的医疗设备可能包含传感器，这些传感器在紧密接触时会对磁铁和无线电产生响应。为避免与这些设备发生任何潜在的相互作用，请使iPhone和MagSafe配件与设备保持安全距离（如果无线充电，则相距6英寸/ 15厘米以上或相距12英寸/ 30厘米以上）。但是，请咨询您的医生和设备制造商以获取特定指南。

For those unfamiliar: MagSafe is a feature in new iPhones that among other things allows the devices to attach magnetically to a small Qi wireless charging pad. This magnetic alignment allows for more optimal charging speeds than in previous iPhone models.

对于那些不熟悉的人：MagSafe是新iPhone的一项功能，除其他功能外，它还使这些设备能够磁性连接至小型Qi无线充电板。与以前的iPhone型号相比，这种磁性对准可提供最佳的充电速度。

MagSafe was previously offered in Macs, too, albeit in a slightly different form. And recent reports suggest that it may soon return to the Mac.

MagSafe以前也曾在Mac中提供，尽管形式略有不同。最近的报道表明，它可能很快就会回到Mac。

Just so everyone knows they don't *break* a pacemaker or ICD they **trigger** functions that are designed to be controlled via magnet. Pacemakers have magnetically controlled functions so that the user can activate different pacing modes, or ICD functions by placing a strong magnet over the implant. The risk is that these functions are accidentally triggered by magnets in the environment. This functionality can often be turned off if it is not needed for standard therapy. Source: I'm a systems engineer at a medical device company that makes pacemakers and ICDs.

如此一来，每个人都知道他们不会“打破”起搏器或ICD，而是“触发”功能，这些功能旨在通过磁铁控制。起搏器具有磁性控制功能，因此用户可以通过在植入物上放置强力磁铁来激活不同的起搏模式或ICD功能。风险是这些功能会被环境中的磁铁意外触发。如果标准疗法不需要此功能，通常可以将其关闭。资料来源：我是一家制造起搏器和ICD的医疗设备公司的系统工程师。

Echoing chance909 above, but the magnet sensitivity on defibrillators is a feature NOT a bug. There are times when you need to stop an ICD from defibrillating a patient ( eg electro-cautery, lead malfunction, EMI etc) all ICDs respond to a magnet placed over them on the same way, halting defibrillation therapy. The only other way to do this is to have the manufacturer's specific programmer to program the therapies off which takes longer, assuming that you have someone trained to use the programmer on hand. Magnets are easier and quicker. Also the ICD sets off an audible alarm when you hold a magnet over it it so patients don't do it by accident.

The connected battlespace, part two: The fault in our (joint) stars
相连的战斗空间，第二部分：我们（联合）恒星的断层

2021-02-09 21:11:46

Former Secretary of Defense Donald Rumsfeld famously (or infamously) said in 2004, “You go to war with the army you have, not the army you might want or wish to have at a later time.” Over the course of the last twenty years of armed conflict, the US military and NATO allied forces have tried to evolve into the force they wished they had been at the beginning, rapidly evolving in some ways while staying very much the same in others.

前国防部长唐纳德·拉姆斯菲尔德（Donald Rumsfeld）在2004年著名（或臭名昭著）说：“您与拥有的军队开战，而不是以后可能想要或希望拥有的军队开战。”在武装冲突的最后二十年中，美国军方和北约盟军努力发展成为他们希望起步的部队，它们在某些方面迅速发展，而在其他方面却保持不变。

The wars in Afghanistan, Iraq, and Syria were in many ways a crucible for connected battlefield technologies—some of which were in their infancy during the 1991 Gulf War and others that were born out of urgent needs that arose as the wars became never-ending counterinsurgency operations with forces spread far and wide. But now the military faces the problem it deferred at the beginning of the so-called “Global War on Terror”—how to operate a connected battlefield in a world where the enemy is very capable in the air, in space, and in the electromagnetic spectrum.

阿富汗，伊拉克和叙利亚的战争在许多方面都是连接战场技术的坩埚，其中一些处于1991年海湾战争期间的初期，而其他战争则源于战争的无休止而产生的紧急需求叛乱行动与部队相距甚远。但是现在，军方面临着它在所谓的“全球反恐战争”开始时所推迟的问题—如何在一个敌人在空中，太空和电磁中非常有能力的世界中经营一个相连的战场光谱。

The new holy grail is “Joint All-Domain Command and Control,” or "JADC2" (pronounced "jad-see-two"). JADC2 is the aggregation of command and control for sea, air, space, land, electromagnetic spectrum, and other cyber-y things. It’s not going to happen overnight. But with the hard-learned lessons of the past few decades and the rise of technologies that can begin to help manage the information overload of the battlefield, JADC2 appears to need a lot less unobtainium that previous integrated battlespace plans were made of.

新的圣杯是“联合全域命令与控制”，或＆＃34; JADC2＆＃34;。 （发音为“ jad-see-two＆＃34;”）。 JADC2是对海洋，空中，太空，陆地，电磁频谱和其他网络事物的命令和控制的集合。这不会在一夜之间发生。但是，由于过去几十年的经验教训，以及可以开始帮助管理战场信息过载的技术的兴起，JADC2似乎比以前的综合战场计划所需的不确定性要少得多。

The basic building blocks for JADC2 have existed for a while. The problem has always been how to integrate them in a way that doesn’t warp US Air Force strategist Colonel John Boyd’s celebrated OODA loop into a circular decision-making firing squad. To be effective, the data firehose of the modern battlefield has to be turned into an orderly flow of information that supports rapid decisions, and those decisions need to reach the pointy end of things quickly enough to matter—at every level of operations. Things that need to be blown up need to be found and assigned to units that do the blowing up faster; changing conditions in any domain need to be accounted for and responded to before they affect the success of the mission. That kind of unified view requires networking together the sensors, the deciders, and the shooters—and transforming the raw data about the domains of operation into information that can easily be acted upon by the appropriate human in the chain. Building that network has been intrinsic to much of what the military has tried to do with force modernization since the 1991 Gulf War. The problem is that there are too many networks, and they don't have common data formats.

JADC2的基本构建块已经存在了一段时间。问题一直是如何以一种不会扭曲美国空军战略家约翰·博伊德上校著名的OODA循环的方式将它们整合到一个循环决策射击队中。为了有效发挥作用，现代战场的数据流必须转变为有序的信息流，以支持快速决策，而这些决策需要在每个操作级别上足够快地达到目标。需要找到需要炸毁的东西，并将其分配给进行炸毁的单位。在影响任务成功之前，必须考虑和应对任何领域中不断变化的条件。这种统一的观点要求将传感器，决策者和射击者联网在一起，并将有关作战领域的原始数据转换成可以由链中适当人员轻易采取行动的信息。自1991年海湾战争以来，军方试图进行部队现代化的大部分工作都是建立这种网络所固有的。问题在于网络太多，它们没有通用的数据格式。

Coming into the 21st century, the DOD had several different, largely disjointed visions of "joint C2" ("C2" stands for "command and control"). Some domains had been (sort of) managed, but cross-domain coordination remained (indeed, remains) a work in progress—especially between the Army and everyone else.

进入21世纪，DOD对“联合C2”具有多种截然不同的愿景。 （＆＃34; C2＆＃34;代表＆＃34;命令与控制＆＃34;）。一些领域已经过（某种）管理，但是跨领域的协调仍然（实际上，仍然是）仍在进行中，尤其是在陆军与其他所有人之间。

The first take on truly “joint” multi-domain command and control was the fusion of Air Force and Army projects that became the Joint Surveillance Target Attack Radar System (JSTARS). As conceived in the late 1980s, JSTARS would put a battlefield sensor and combat operations center aboard an aircraft, and transmit information about enemy armor and vehicles to tactical air and ground force commanders via mobile “Ground Station Modules.” Mind you, “mobile” did not mean “moving.”

真正实现“联合”多域指挥和控制的第一个任务是将空军和陆军项目融合在一起，成为联合监视目标攻击雷达系统（JSTARS）。按照1980年代后期的设想，JSTARS会将战场传感器和作战中心放置在飞机上，并通过移动的“地面站模块”将有关敌方装甲和车辆的信息传输给战术空中和地面部队的指挥官。请注意，“移动”并不意味着“移动”。

The concept was pushed out in development mode to assist during the Gulf War. Old Boeing 707 airframes were recycled and re-engined and turned into the E-8 Joint STARS aircraft. Equipped with a giant ground target motion indicator ( GMTI) radar system and onboard combat operations center and crewed by Air Force and specially trained Army personnel, the E-8 gave coalition forces a clear picture of Iraqi forces on the ground—and also detected SCUD missile launches.

该概念已在开发模式中推出，以在海湾战争期间提供协助。旧的波音707飞机机体被回收和重新设计，并变成了E-8联合STARS飞机。 E-8配备了巨型地面目标运动指示器（GMTI）雷达系统和机载作战中心，由空军和受过特别训练的陆军人员组成，E-8为联军提供了清晰的地面伊拉克部队图片，并且还检测到飞毛腿导弹发射。

The last of seventeen E-8C aircraft were delivered in 2005. But the role of the aircraft would change considerably as the wars in Afghanistan and Iraq progressed, becoming communication hubs and optical eyes in the sky for troops in the field fighting an enemy that didn’t exactly show up easily on ground radar.

2005年交付了17架E-8C飞机中的最后一架。但是，随着阿富汗和伊拉克战争的进行，飞机的作用将发生巨大变化，成为战场上与敌方敌人作战的部队的通讯枢纽和空中目光在地面雷达上无法精确显示。

The JSTARS program gave birth to the mother of all command, control, communications, computer, intelligence, surveillance, and reconnaissance programs (this is abbreviated as "C4ISR," and thankfully it's pronounced as an initialism). The Ground Station Module evolved into the Common Ground Station, a system based on commercial off-the-shelf computing technology; that, in turn, snowballed as it rolled among the intelligence systems of the various services and birthed the Distributed Common Ground System ( DCGS, pronounced “dee-sigs”)—a platform that was to bolt the "ISR" onto the "C4" across all the services.

JSTARS程序诞生了所有命令，控制，通信，计算机，情报，监视和侦察程序之母（缩写为＆＃34; C4ISR＆＃34 ;，谢天谢地，它发音为首字母缩写）。地面站模块演变成通用地面站，这是一个基于商业现成计算技术的系统；反过来，随着它在各种服务的智能系统之间滚动而滚雪球，并诞生了分布式公用地面系统（DCGS，发音为“ dee-sigs”），该平台可用来栓塞＆＃34; ISR＆＃34;到＆＃34; C4＆＃34;上所有服务。

The problems with DCGS’ rollout were legion. The hardware and software varied with service and application: there was  DCGS-A for the Army and Marines, which retained the CGS capability and provided a tactical view for ground commanders. Then there were the Navy’s DCGS-N and Air Force DCGS, which honestly are beasts of an entirely different sort. The DCGS systems did share some things, like a Common Object Request Broker (CORBA) architecture—much like the JTRS radios the Department of Defense was hoping would solve all their networking problems. As with JTRS—which, spoiler alert, was a giant mess—this was not necessarily a good thing. And since even DCGS-A carried a Secret-level classification, most soldiers in the field were not allowed to view DCGS data, making it less than ideal as a platform for information sharing downstream. Even at the joint command level, it was a bit of a mess because of the uncommonness of these “common” systems. At the beginning of the war in Iraq, Air Force Chief of Staff General John Jumper confessed that even coordination between the forces in one domain—Air—needed work. “You go into an [air operations center] and what will you see?" he asked. "Tribal representatives sitting down in front of tribal workstations, interpreting tribal hieroglyphics to the rest of us who are on watch. And then what happens? They stand up and walk over to another tribal representative and reveal their hieroglyphics, which are translated by the other tribe into its own hieroglyphics and entered into its own workstation.”

DCGS推出的问题很多。硬件和软件随服务和应用的不同而变化：陆军和海军陆战队有DCGS-A，它保留了CGS的能力并为地面指挥官提供了战术上的视野。然后是海军的DCGS-N和空军的DCGS，说实话，它们是完全不同种类的野兽。 DCGS系统确实共享了一些东西，例如通用对象请求代理（CORBA）架构-就像美国国防部希望解决所有网络问题的JTRS无线电一样。与JTRS一样（扰流器警报是一个巨大的混乱），这不一定是一件好事。而且，由于即使DCGS-A都进行了秘密级别的分类，因此该领域的大多数士兵也不被允许查看DCGS数据，这使其不理想用作下游信息共享的平台。即使在联合指挥级别，由于这些“通用”系统的不常见，也有些混乱。在伊拉克战争开始时，空军参谋长约翰·霍普（John Jumper）坦言，在一个领域（空军）中，即使部队之间也需要协调。 “您进入[航空运营中心]，您会看到什么？＆＃34;他问。 ＆＃34;部落代表坐在部落工作站前，向我们在场的其他人解释部落象形文字。然后会发生什么？他们站起来，走向另一个部落代表，展示他们的象形文字，这些文字被另一个部落翻译成自己的象形文字，并进入了自己的工作站。”

Today, the picture is better but still challenging. The Air Force wants to retire JSTARS, but Congress won’t let them until they have something better to replace it with. That “something,” the Air Force hopes, is the Advanced Battle Management System ( ABMS), which turns the battlefield into an Internet of Things.

如今，情况已经好转，但仍然充满挑战。空军想退役JSTARS，但直到他们有更好的替代品之前，国会才允许他们退役。空军希望，这种“东西”是先进的战斗管理系统（ABMS），它将战场变成物联网。

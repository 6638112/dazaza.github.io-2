What Delaware license plates tell us about social status signalling
特拉华州车牌告诉我们有关社会地位信号的信息

2020-11-22 16:26:53

The many forms of status signalling know no bounds: cars and handbags for some, exclusive club memberships and flamboyant philanthropy for others. But most of those things have actual, practical value (or at least aesthetic value) beyond their mere status-signalling qualities.

状态信号的多种形式是无止境的：某些人的汽车和手提包，俱乐部会员的专有资格和另一些人的华丽慈善事业。但是，这些东西中的大多数具有实际的，实用的价值（或至少是美学价值），而不仅仅是其具有标志性的品质。

The same is not obviously true of license plates – they have no inherent value aside from their essential licensing function.

车牌显然并非如此-除了基本的许可功能外，它们没有内在的价值。

Yet in the US state of Delaware, there is a thriving market for license plates. Unlike most other states, license plates in Delaware become the property of the registrant, forever, and can be transferred from one car to the next. They can also legally be transferred (i.e. sold) to a new owner.

然而，在美国特拉华州，车牌市场蓬勃发展。与其他大多数州不同，特拉华州的车牌永远是注册人的财产，可以从一辆车转移到另一辆车。它们也可以合法转让（即出售）给新所有者。

A new study published by researchers at Rice University examined this unique market to see how much people will pay for this purest of status symbols — a low-numbered Delaware plate.

赖斯大学研究人员发表的一项新研究调查了这个独特的市场，以了解人们将为这种最纯粹的身份符号（一个低编号的特拉华州牌照）支付多少钱。

These are not your garden-variety vanity plates. In 1941, the state of Delaware revamped its system for issuing the plates. The new system featured consecutively numbered plates beginning with the number 1.

这些不是您的花园式洗手盆。 1941年，特拉华州对发行印版的系统进行了改进。新系统以编号为1的连续编号车牌为特色。

The only difference between the plates is their place in the sequence. Yet people are apparently willing to pay six-figure sums for the lowest-numbered exemplars. And, implicitly, for the privilege of signalling to others that they can afford to do so. In 2008, for example, Delaware license plate number 6 sold at auction for $675,000. A local undertaker, the study explains, even advertises a low-numbered hearse “so that loved ones can go out in style.” In fact, these low-numbered plates are often more valuable than the cars they are affixed to.

板之间的唯一区别是它们在序列中的位置。然而，人们显然愿意为数量最少的示例支付六位数的费用。并且，隐含地，为了向他人发信号表示他们有能力这样做的特权。例如，2008年，特拉华州第6号车牌在拍卖会上以$ 675,000的价格售出。研究解释说，当地的承办人甚至在广告中宣传低调的灵车，“以便亲人能过时。实际上，这些低编号的车牌通常比固定在其上的汽车更有价值。

The researchers examined 831 Delaware license plate sales that took place between 1998 and 2019. Their data set included 36 two-digit plates, 140 three-digit plates, 532 four-digit plates, and 123 of the relatively undesirable five-digit plates.

研究人员检查了1998年至2019年之间的831个特拉华州车牌销售情况。他们的数据集包括36个两位数的车牌，140个三位数字的车牌，532个四位数字的车牌和123个相对不理想的五位数字的车牌。

Single-digit Delaware plates are so rare that the researchers did not have enough data to reliably estimate their prices. But they do mention that anecdotal evidence suggests they sell for “significantly more” than the two-digit plates.

特拉华州的个位数极少见，以至于研究人员没有足够的数据来可靠地估算其价格。但他们确实提到，轶事证据表明，它们的售价比两位数的车牌“高得多”。

The paper’s first finding, perhaps unsurprisingly, is that the lower-numbered plates fetched the highest prices. The average price for a 2-digit plate was about $212,000, and for a lowly 5-digit plate $870.

该论文的第一个发现，也许不足为奇，是编号较低的印版获得了最高的价格。两位数牌照的平均价格约为212,000美元，而五位数牌照的平均价格为870美元。

The researchers then compared the prices by digit category, i.e. two-digit plates, three-digit plates, etc. Though lower numbers did indeed get higher prices, these prices were “largely driven by the number of digits on the plate, not the number itself.” In other words, the relative price difference between plate numbers 98 and 101 was much greater than the difference between plate numbers 101 and 990.

然后，研究人员按数字类别（即两位数板块，三位数字板块等）比较了价格。尽管价格较低的确确实获得了较高的价格，但这些价格“主要是由标牌上的数字而不是数字驱动的”本身。”换句话说，板号98和101之间的相对价格差异远大于板号101和990之间的差异。

The average price for a two-digit plate was about ten times higher than a three-digit plate. Likewise, it was about 100 times higher than the price of a four-digit plate.

两位数牌照的平均价格约为三位数牌照的十倍。同样，它比四位数板的价格高约100倍。

The researchers write that this pattern “is consistent with the presence of exclusive clubs where membership is restricted,” in this case the two-digit club, three-digit club, etc. It is also consistent, they write, “with a strong status effect,” where social rank is determined by scarcity of the available spots (or available license plates).

研究人员写道，这种模式“与会员资格受到限制的独家俱乐部的存在相一致，”在这种情况下为两位数俱乐部，三位数俱乐部等。效果”，其中社会地位由可用位置（或可用车牌）的稀缺性决定。

Indeed, they write, “there is very little relation between price and the numerical value” of the plate within each digit category. Within the two-digit plate category, for example, the price difference between two sequential numbers (say 47 and 48) was only about $100. Based on the average price of $212,000, this “is economically insignificant and is also statistically indistinguishable from zero.”

实际上，他们写道，每个数字类别中的车牌“价格和数值之间几乎没有关系”。例如，在两位数的车牌类别中，两个连续数字（例如47和48）之间的价格差仅为100美元左右。基于平均价格212,000美元，这“在经济上微不足道，从统计上也无法与零区分开”。

The researchers also found that the demand for Delaware license plates is pretty inelastic. There was very little correlation between stock market prices and the prices of the plates. Likewise, the prices of the plates hardly changed during the 2008 financial crisis.

研究人员还发现，对特拉华州车牌的需求缺乏弹性。股市价格和板块价格之间几乎没有关联。同样，在2008年金融危机期间，这些板块的价格几乎没有变化。

In total, they estimate the overall value of the 2-digit to 5-digit Delaware plates (that is, numbers 10 to 99,999) at about $227 million. That represents about 0.4% of the state’s GDP.

他们估计2位数到5位数的特拉华州车牌的总价值（即10到99,999）约为2.27亿美元。这大约占该州GDP的0.4％。

In closing, they write, “the Delaware market simply allows us to document and calibrate cleanly how much people value their relative position in society.”

最后，他们写道：“特拉华州市场只是让我们能够清楚地记录和校准多少人珍视他们在社会中的相对地位。”

You can see pictures of these oddly valuable plates at The Delaware 3000, a site run by historian Jordan Irazbal.

您可以在历史学家约旦·伊拉兹巴尔（Jordan Irazbal）经营的The Delaware 3000上看到这些奇特的珍贵盘子的图片。

I think folks are correct to point out that someone who is determined to get themselves killed can do something like this with any car. Somebody who gets into a conventional car and puts a brick on the accelerator knows that he's likely committing suicide. I think there's a good chance that the guy honestly thought Autopilot would be able to safely navigate his neighborhood and he wanted to show off for his friend. I think it's possible that this belief was bolstered by Tesla's marketing and/or Elon's tweets about how Tesla was just months away from full self-driving. And so the guy didn't think he was risking his life by bypassing safety restrictions. If that turns out to be true, it will still be the case that the guy was acting recklessly and bears most of the blame for what happened. But I also think behavior like this is foreseeable. The more sophisticated your ADAS system is, and the more over-the-top your marketing is, the more likely customers are to decide the technology is infallible.

FCC's ongoing 5G wireless licenses auction has fetched over $69.8B after three weeks, beating the $44.9B sale of 4G licenses in 2015, and will resume January 4
FCC正在进行的5G无线许可证拍卖在三周后成交价超过$ 69.8B，在2015年超过$ 44.9B的4G许可证销售，并将于1月4日恢复

2020-12-24 21:54:28

The Federal Communications Commission’s ongoing sale of wireless licenses has fetched more than $69.8 billion after three weeks of bidding, a record sum that could alter cellphone carriers’ prospects for the next decade.

经过三周的竞标，联邦通信委员会正在进行的无线许可证销售已售出698亿美元以上，这一创纪录的价格可能会改变手机运营商未来十年的前景。

The auction proceeds have already topped the $44.9 billion raised in 2015 by an earlier sale of midrange cellular licenses, which U.S. cellphone carriers used at the time to enhance their 4G service. Those companies are now investing billions of dollars in the next wave of fifth-generation coverage. The 5G standard promises to speed the flow of data to phones and other wireless devices like personal computers, cars and industrial machinery.

通过早期出售中端蜂窝牌照，拍卖收益已经超过2015年筹集的449亿美元，当时美国手机运营商当时使用这些牌照来增强其4G服务。这些公司现在正为下一波第五代保险投资数十亿美元。 5G标准有望加快数据流向电话和其他无线设备（如个人计算机，汽车和工业机械）的速度。

The recent bids have blown past Wall Street’s highest forecasts, suggesting that several companies are fighting over the most valuable wireless rights. The 5G auction kicked off Dec. 8 and will pause for the holidays until Jan. 4, when total bids could move even higher.

最近的出价超过了华尔街的最高预测，这表明有几家公司正在为最有价值的无线权利而战。 5G拍卖于12月8日开始，并将在假期暂停直到1月4日，届时总出价可能会更高。

Each bid is swathed in secrecy until the auction process ends. Analysts expect big names like AT&T and Verizon Communications to walk away with a large share of the licenses to match assets that rival T-Mobile US Inc. captured with its February takeover of Sprint.

每个出价都是保密的，直到拍卖过程结束。分析师预计，像AT＆amp; T和Verizon Communications这样的大人物将放弃大量许可证，以匹配与T-Mobile US Inc.在2月份收购Sprint时获得的竞争对手的资产。

“Mid-band spectrum will be where 5G lives,” said Walt Piecyk, a telecom analyst for research firm LightShed Partners. He added that T-Mobile’s merger with Sprint “clearly put pressure on Verizon and AT&T” to match their rival’s war chest.

研究公司LightShed Partners的电信分析师沃尔特·皮塞克（Walt Piecyk）说：“中频频谱将成为5G的生命。”他补充说，T-Mobile与Sprint的合并“显然对Verizon和AT＆amp; T施加了压力”，以使其与竞争对手的战斗力匹敌。

“When the numbers get this big, you have to assume that everybody’s getting more aggressive,” Mr. Piecyk said.

皮西克说：“当数字变得如此之大时，你必须假设每个人都变得更加积极进取。”

Mobile service providers are also bidding against investment firms and new market entrants. Satellite provider Dish Network won many of the licenses sold in the 2015 auction. The company this month raised more cash through a $2 billion convertible-note offering to help fund more network investments. Dish is building its own cellphone network after buying assets and about 8 million customers from Sprint.

移动服务提供商也在竞标投资公司和新进入市场的公司。卫星提供商Dish Network赢得了2015年拍卖中出售的许多许可证。该公司本月通过发行20亿美元的可转换债券筹集了更多现金，以帮助资助更多的网络投资。在从Sprint购买资产和大约800万客户之后，Dish正在建立自己的手机网络。

Cable internet providers could also influence the auction’s outcome after years of experimentation with wireless services. Comcast and Charter Communications teamed up to bid in the current auction after they spent nearly $1 billion on a smaller license sale earlier this year.

经过多年的无线服务试验，有线互联网提供商也可能影响拍卖的结果。康卡斯特（Comcast）和宪章通讯（Charter Communications）在今年早些时候以近10亿美元的价格出售了较小的许可证之后，就联合竞标了这次拍卖。

The radio frequencies being offered range between 3.7 and 4 gigahertz, a middle-of-the-road range considered well-suited for 5G service. New 5G smartphones can already connect to those frequencies in other countries that have cleared the spectrum. The U.S. is also selling big chunks of spectrum at once, enhancing their value.

所提供的射频范围在3.7至4 GHz之间，这是非常适合5G服务的中等范围。新的5G智能手机已经可以连接到已清除频谱的其他国家/地区的频率。美国也同时出售大量频谱，以提高其价值。

The high offers benefit the U.S. Treasury, which will collect a windfall after the winners pay for their licenses. The victors will also need to spend at least $13 billion more to help modify equipment for a group of satellite companies that already use the frequencies. The satellite operators agreed to an FCC plan that shifts their TV transmissions to a narrower portion of the radio spectrum known as C-band.

高报价使美国财政部受益，在获奖者支付执照后，美国财政部将获得意外之财。获胜者还将需要至少花费130亿美元来帮助为已经使用该频率的一组卫星公司修改设备。卫星运营商同意一项FCC计划，该计划将其电视传输转移到称为C波段的无线电频谱的较窄部分。

Cellphone carriers can afford to commit to big payments given their low borrowing costs and relatively stable service revenue, said Raymond James analyst Frank Louthan.

雷蒙德·詹姆斯（Raymond James）分析师弗兰克·洛森（Frank Louthan）表示，鉴于手机运营商的借贷成本低且服务收入相对稳定，他们有能力承担大笔付款。

“Investors see these companies as some of the most secure around,” Mr. Louthan said. “I don’t think a slight change in a debt ratio would make much of a difference.”

Louthan先生说：“投资者将这些公司视为最安全的公司。” “我认为债务比率的微小变化不会带来很大的变化。”

The FCC won’t reveal the auction’s winners for several days after the auction ends, meaning the broader telecom industry could remain in suspense until February. Federal rules against coordinated bidding also limit what the auction participants can say about the process, restricting their ability to raise capital or discuss major deals involving spectrum.

FCC在拍卖结束后的几天内不会透露拍卖的获胜者，这意味着更广泛的电信行业可能会一直悬而未决，直到2月。联邦反对协调招标的规则也限制了拍卖参与者对拍卖过程的看法，限制了他们筹集资金或讨论涉及频谱的重大交易的能力。

The auction is a first step in a multiyear process. Wireless customers might not see the full band cleared for cellphone service until late 2023, though there is an early tranche slated to move by late 2021. Auction winners with no time to spare could also pay the incumbent satellite users larger fees to quicken their relocation. That would allow some companies to flip the switch on cell-tower gear that is already transmitting data on other frequencies--just not the bands in question. The change would result in faster and more reliable 5G service.

拍卖是多年过程中的第一步。无线客户可能要到2023年下半年才能看到整个频段的手机服务许可，尽管有一部分计划在2021年下半年前进行。届时，没有时间的拍卖优胜者还可以向现有的卫星用户支付更大的费用，以加快搬迁的速度。这样一来，一些公司就可以将已经在其他频率上传输数据的蜂窝塔式设备拨动开关，而不仅仅是相关频段。这一变化将带来更快，更可靠的5G服务。

“Time to market matters,” Mr. Louthan said. “We generally see prices get high when you can deploy spectrum quickly.”

Louthan先生说：“上市时间很重要。” “我们通常认为，当您可以快速部署频谱时，价格会很高。”

The priest and the dying soldier, 1962
1962年牧师和垂死的士兵

2021-04-03 07:46:39

Navy chaplain Luis Padilla gives last rites to a soldier wounded by sniper fire during a revolt in Venezuela. Braving the streets amid sniper fire, to offer last rites to the dying, the priest encountered a wounded soldier, who pulled himself up by clinging to the priest’s cassock, as bullets chewed up the concrete around them.

海军Chaplain Luis Padilla将最后的仪式给委内瑞拉反抗期间的狙击手击中的士兵。在狙击手火灾中冒着街道，为了向垂死的追求提供最后的仪式，牧师遇到了受伤的士兵，他通过紧紧抓住牧师的Cassock，因为子弹咀嚼它们周围的混凝土。

The photographer Hector Rondón Lovera, who had to lie flat to avoid getting shot, later said that he was unsure how he managed to take this picture: “I found myself in solid lead for forty-five minutes … I was flattened against the wall while bullets were flying when the priest appeared. The truth is, I don’t know how I took those pictures, lying on the ground.

摄影师HectorRondónLovera避免避免被射击，后来说他不确定他如何拍摄这张照片：“我发现自己在45分钟内找到了坚实的领先......我在墙上露出平坦当牧师出现时，子弹正在飞行。事实是，我不知道我是如何拍那样的照片，躺在地上。

Rondon shot the government soldier crawling his way up Navy chaplain Luis Padilla’s robe as Padilla looks in the direction of the rebel sniper fire. Government forces quickly took control of the town and over two days pounded the remaining rebels, that had taken cover in the Solano Castle, into submission. A handful that weren’t captured or killed were able to escape into the jungle”.

Rondon射击了政府士兵爬行了他的路上海军Chaplain Luis Padilla的长袍，因为Padilla沿着反叛狙击火火的方向看。政府力量迅速控制着镇，两天超过了剩下的叛乱分子，这些叛乱分子在Solano Castle封闭，进入了提交。没有捕获或杀死的少数人能够逃离丛林“。

Besides the priest’s bravery, he also knows the enemy will think a lot before shooting him (just imagine the propaganda) and the enemy soldiers are catholic and would refuse that order.

除了牧师的勇气之外，他还知道敌人会在射击他之前想到很多东西（立即想象宣传），敌人士兵是天主教徒，会拒绝这个订单。

Even more intense about this picture is the setting, in the background is a carnicería (a butcher’s shop). In Spanish, a carnicería means both a “butcher’s shop” and “slaughter, carnage”. The phrase “f ue una carnicería” (English equivalent: “it was carnage”) is so common in the Spanish language. The parallel really catches one’s eye and draws the horror of the scene even further.

更加激烈的这张照片是设置，在背景中是一个Carnicería（屠夫的商店）。在西班牙语中，一个Carnicería意味着一个“屠夫的商店”和“屠宰，狂欢”。这句话“F UE UnaCarnicería”（英语等同物：“这是狂欢节”）在西班牙语中是如此常见。平行真的抓住了一个人的眼睛，并进一步吸引了场景的恐怖。

The photo was taken on June 4 (1962) by Hector Rondón Lovera, photographer of Caracas, for the Venezuelan newspaper “La Republica”. It won the World Press Photo of the Year and the 1963 Pulitzer Prize for Photography. The original title of work is “ Aid From The Padre”.

这张照片于6月4日（1962年）由HectorRondónLovera，Caracas的摄影师为委内瑞拉报纸“La Republica”。它赢得了世界新闻照片和1963年普利策摄影奖。原始工作标题是“援助伙伴”。

El Porteñazo (2 June 1962 – 6 June 1962) was a short-lived military rebellion against the government of Rómulo Betancourt in Venezuela, in which rebels attempted to take over the city of Puerto Cabello and its Solano Castle. Venezuela’s constitution was only a year old in 1962, but already there have been two attempts to overthrow the government.

ElPorteñazo（1962年6月2日 - 1962年6月2日）是针对委内瑞拉的Rómulobetancourt政府的短暂的军事叛乱，其中叛乱分子试图接管波多卡洛省和索尔诺城堡的城市。委内瑞拉的宪法是1962年唯一的一岁，但已经有两次试图推翻政府。

The bloody struggle between government forces and guerrilla rebels in the naval base who had the support of the residents of Puerto Cabello was aggressive. The rebellion was crushed by 3 June, leaving more than 400 dead and 700 injured, and by 6 June the rebels’ stronghold of Solano Castle had fallen.

政府部队与游击队在海军基地之间的血腥斗争争夺居民的海军基地侵略性。 叛乱被击败了6月3日，留下了400多人死亡，700人受伤，6月6日，叛乱分子队的索尔诺城堡的责任已经下降。

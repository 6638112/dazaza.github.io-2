早期真核生物进化时间表揭开面纱

2020-11-01 20:00:30

By analyzing duplicates of thousands of genes, researchers have reconstructed the evolutionary events leading to the creation of eukaryotic cells, the precursors to virtually all life you can see with the naked eye. The evolutionary timeline from simple bacterial cells to complex eukaryotic cells progressed differently than previously thought. The study, a collaboration between the Comparative Genomics lab at IRB Barcelona and the University of Utrecht, has been published in Nature Ecology & Evolution. One of the most important and puzzling events in the evolution of life has been the origin of the first . Almost all lifeforms that we can perceive with the naked eye, such as algae, plants, animals and fungi, are made up of complex cells known as 'eukaryotes." A collaborative study between the groups of Toni Gabaldón, ICREA researcher at the Institute for Research in Biomedicine (IRB Barcelona) and the Barcelona Supercomputing Center (BSC-CNS), and Berend Snel at the University of Utrecht, has concluded that the first cell to incorporate a mitochondrion (considered the key step to the increased complexity of eukaryotic cells) already presented eukaryote-like complexity in structure and functions. This scenario serves as a bridge between the signs of complexity observed in some archaeal genomes and the proposed role of mitochondria in triggering eukaryogenesis.

通过分析数千个基因的重复，研究人员已经重建了导致真核细胞产生的进化事件，真核细胞是几乎所有你用肉眼可以看到的生命的前身。从简单细菌细胞到复杂真核细胞的进化时间表与之前认为的不同。这项研究由巴塞罗那IRB比较基因组实验室和乌得勒支大学合作完成，已发表在“自然生态与进化”杂志上。生命进化中最重要和最令人费解的事件之一就是第一个事件的起源。几乎所有我们用肉眼可以观察到的生命形式，如藻类、植物、动物和真菌，都是由被称为真核生物的复杂细胞组成的。一项由ICREA研究员、巴塞罗那生物医学研究所(IRB巴塞罗那)和巴塞罗那超级计算中心(BSC-CNS)的研究员托尼·加巴多恩(Toni Gabaldón)和乌得勒支大学(University Of Utrecht)的贝伦德·斯奈尔(Berend Snel)共同开展的一项研究得出结论，第一个包含线粒体的细胞(被认为是增加真核细胞复杂性的关键一步)在结构和功能上已经呈现出类似于核生物的复杂性。这一情景在一些古生菌基因组中观察到的复杂性迹象和线粒体在触发真核发生中的拟议作用之间起到了桥梁作用。

"The acquisition of mitochondria was considered either to be the crucial first step or the last step in the development of eukaryotic cell complexity," explains Gabaldón. "Our findings show that it was indeed a crucial event, but that it happened in a scenario where cell complexity had already increased."

获取线粒体被认为是发展真核细胞复杂性的关键的第一步或最后一步，加巴东解释说。我们的研究结果表明，这确实是一件至关重要的事情，但它发生在细胞复杂性已经增加的情况下。"；加巴多恩解释说，线粒体的获得被认为是真核细胞复杂性发展的关键第一步或最后一步。我们的研究结果表明，这确实是一件至关重要的事情，但它发生在细胞复杂性已经增加的情况下。

For roughly the first half of the history of life on Earth, the only forms of life were the relatively simple cells of bacteria. "Eukaryotic cells are larger, contain more DNA and are made up of compartments, each with their own task," explains first author Julian Vosseberg. "In that sense, you could compare bacterial cells with a tent, while eukaryotic cells are more like houses with several rooms."

在地球生命历史的前半部分，唯一的生命形式是相对简单的细菌细胞。第一作者朱利安·沃塞伯格解释说，真核细胞更大，含有更多的DNA，由不同的隔间组成，每个隔间都有自己的任务。从这个意义上说，你可以把细菌细胞比作帐篷，而真核细胞更像是有几个房间的房子。

How and when organisms traded the tent for a house is still a mystery, as there are no intermediate forms. One important moment in evolution was the origin of mitochondria, a component of eukaryotic cells that function as their 'power plants." Mitochondria were once free-living bacteria, but during evolution, they were absorbed by the ancestors of today's eukaryotic . As probably drove the increase in cell complexity, the researchers attempted to reconstruct the evolutionary events based on these genetic changes.

生物体如何以及何时用帐篷交换房子仍然是个谜，因为没有中间形式。进化中的一个重要时刻是线粒体的起源，线粒体是真核细胞的一个组成部分，起着动力装置的作用。线粒体曾经是自由生活的细菌，但在进化过程中，它们被今天的真核生物的祖先吸收了。由于可能推动了细胞复杂性的增加，研究人员试图根据这些基因变化重建进化事件。

"We can use the DNA of contemporary species to reconstruct evolutionary events. Our genes were formed over eons of evolution. They have changed dramatically over that time, but they still hold echoes of a distant past." Vosseberg adds, "We have a vast quantity of genetic material available, from a variety of organisms, and we can use computers to reconstruct the evolution of thousands of genes, including ancient gene duplications. These reconstructions have enabled us to uncover the timing of important intermediate steps."

我们可以利用当代物种的DNA来重建进化事件。我们的基因是在亿万年的进化过程中形成的。在那段时间里，它们发生了戏剧性的变化，但它们仍然保留着遥远过去的回声。沃塞伯格补充道，我们有来自各种有机体的大量遗传物质，我们可以使用计算机重建数千个基因的进化，包括古老的基因复制。这些重建使我们能够发现重要中间步骤的时间安排。

The co-corresponding author, Berend Snel, from the University of Utrecht, says, "Scientists did not have a timeline of these events. But now we've managed to reconstruct a rough timeline." To achieve this, the researchers adapted an existing method developed at Gabaldon's lab to create a new protocol, which has resulted in novel insights. These indicate that a lot of complex cellular machinery had evolved even before the symbiosis with mitochondria, including the development of transport within the cell and the cytoskeleton. "The symbiosis wasn't an event that served as the catalyst for everything else. We observed a peak in gene duplications much earlier in time, indicating that cell complexity had already increased before that moment," says Snel.

来自乌得勒支大学的共同通讯作者Berend Snel说，科学家们没有这些事件的时间表。但是现在我们已经设法重建了一条大致的时间线。为了实现这一点，研究人员采用了加巴尔登实验室开发的一种现有方法来创建一种新的方案，这产生了新的见解。这些表明，甚至在与线粒体共生之前，就已经进化出许多复杂的细胞机制，包括细胞内运输和细胞骨架的发展。共生并不是一个对其他一切都起到催化剂作用的事件。斯奈尔说，我们在更早的时间里观察到了基因复制的高峰，这表明细胞的复杂性在那一刻之前就已经增加了。

"Our study suggests that the ancestral host that acquired the mitochondrial endosymbiont had already developed some complexity in terms of a dynamic cytoskeleton and membrane trafficking" says Gabaldón. "This might have favored the establishment of symbiotic associations with other microorganisms, including the mitochondrial ancestor, which eventually became integrated."

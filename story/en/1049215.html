NASA's Mars Parachute Had a Hidden Code. Meet the People Who Cracked It
NASA的火星降落伞有隐藏密码。 认识破解它的人

2021-02-25 05:07:42

As NASA’s Perseverance rover fell through the Martian atmosphere last week, a video camera on the spacecraft captured the breakneck deployment of its parachute, which was decorated with splotches of reddish orange and white.

上周NASA的“恒心号”漫游车降落在火星大气层时，飞船上的摄像头捕捉到了其降落伞的爆炸性部署，上面装饰着红橙色和白色斑点。

During a news conference Monday, Allen Chen, the engineer in charge of the landing system, narrated what could be seen and learned in the slowed-down video.

在周一的新闻发布会上，负责着陆系统的工程师艾伦·陈（Allen Chen）讲述了在放慢速度的视频中可以看到和看到的东西。

He added, cryptically and nonchalantly, that his team hoped to inspire others. “Sometimes we leave messages in our work for others to find for that purpose,” he said. “So we invite you all to give it a shot and show your work.”

他含糊而又无拘无束地补充说，他的团队希望启发其他人。他说：“有时我们会在工作中留下信息，以供其他人找到。” “因此，我们邀请大家尝试一下并展示您的作品。”

Across the Atlantic Ocean, Maxence Abela, a 23-year-old computer science student in Paris, realized what Mr. Chen was saying: The seemingly random pattern on Perseverance’s parachute contained a code.

在大西洋彼岸，巴黎23岁的计算机科学系学生Maxence Abela意识到了Chen的话：Perseverance降落伞上看似随机的模式包含了一个代码。

He called his father, Jerome, a software engineer at Google working in London, and the two set to solving it.

他给父亲叫杰罗姆（Jerome）打电话，他是在伦敦工作的Google的软件工程师，两人一起解决了这个问题。

“We like those kinds of little challenges,” Mr. Abela said. “We didn’t think we would be able to solve it, but we would at least try.”

“我们喜欢这些小挑战，”阿贝拉表示。 “我们认为我们无法解决，但我们至少会尝试。”

Collaborating via teleconference, they downloaded the video, isolated images showing the fully inflated parachute and started piecing together the bits.

他们通过电话会议进行协作，下载了视频，孤立的图像，显​​示了完全膨胀的降落伞，并开始将这些位拼凑在一起。

So did others around the world, trading insights on Twitter and forums on Reddit. “It’s just exciting that NASA is putting these little puzzles in their missions,” said Adithya Balaji, a graduate student in computer science at Carnegie Mellon University in Pittsburgh who independently tackled the problem.

世界各地的其他人也是如此，他们在Twitter和Reddit上的论坛上交换见解。匹兹堡卡内基梅隆大学计算机科学专业的研究生Adithya Balaji说：“ NASA将这些小难题摆在他们的任务中，真是令人兴奋。”

Mr. Balaji compared the parachute puzzle to a couple of science fiction movies: “Contact,” where a scientist played by Jodie Foster unravels an alien message, and “The Martian,” where Matt Damon’s character Mark Watney communicates with people back on Earth using a similar code.

巴拉吉先生将降落伞的难题与几部科幻电影进行了比较：“联系”（Jodie Foster扮演的科学家揭示了一个外星人的信息）和“火星人”（马特·戴蒙的角色马克·沃特尼（Mark Watney）与地球上的人进行沟通）类似的代码。

“I think that it’s exciting that real life can be sometimes even more exciting than the movies,” Mr. Balaji said.

巴拉吉说：“我觉得现实生活有时甚至比电影更令人兴奋，”

The person who came up with the idea for embedding a message was Ian Clark, who led development of the parachute. NASA’s previous rover, Curiosity, used the same system when it successfully landed on Mars in 2012. But a failure of a prototype parachute intended for future missions spurred engineers to improve the design.

提出嵌入消息的想法的人是伊恩·克拉克（Ian Clark），他负责降落伞的开发。美国宇航局先前的漫游车“好奇号”（Curiosity）在2012年成功登陆火星时使用了相同的系统。但是，用于未来任务的原型降落伞的失败促使工程师改进了设计。

While watching video of a high-altitude test of the new parachute for Perseverance, Dr. Clark noticed that the checkerboard pattern on the canopy made it difficult to track how individual portions of the parachute unfurled and inflated.

克拉克博士在观看新降落伞的高空测试视频时，注意到树冠上的棋盘格图案使追踪降落伞的各个部分如何展开和充气变得困难。

Because Perseverance would be outfitted with video cameras, Dr. Clark wanted a pattern that would be visually distinct. That, in turn, provided an opportunity “to have a little fun with it,” he said.

由于“毅力”将配备摄像机，因此克拉克博士想要一种视觉上独特的图案。他说，这反过来又提供了一个“对其进行一些乐趣”的机会。

“I told them OK,” Mr. Wallace recalled. “Just make sure it was appropriate and couldn’t be misinterpreted.”

“我告诉他们行，”华莱士先生回忆道。 “只要确保它是适当的并且不会被误解。”

The 70-foot-wide parachute consisted of 80 strips of fabric radiating outward from the center to form a hemisphere-shape canopy, and each strip consisted of four pieces. Dr. Clark thus had 320 pieces to work with.

70英尺宽的降落伞由80条从中心向外辐射形成半球形檐篷的织物组成，每条由4件组成。因此，克拉克博士需要处理320件作品。

Some of his ideas would have required additional colors, but that could have threatened the parachute’s integrity if untested dyes weakened the fabric fibers.

他的一些想法可能还需要其他颜色，但是如果未经测试的染料削弱了织物纤维，那可能会威胁降落伞的完整性。

“We were unwilling to go to a cloth that was dyed in a color that we had never used before,” Mr. Wallace said.

华莱士说：“我们不愿去染一块以前从未使用过的颜色的衣服。”

Even a pattern of just orange and white, the two colors of previous parachutes, raised potential issues. “There’s all kinds of second-guessing questions,” Dr. Clark said. “Like could having more white than orange, or vice versa, mean that the parachute was going to warm up differently and maybe that would change its behavior?”

甚至以前的降落伞的两种颜色只有橙色和白色，也会引起潜在的问题。克拉克博士说：“有各种各样的猜测问题。” “像橘子一样多的白色，反之亦然，这意味着降落伞将以不同的方式预热，也许这会改变其行为？”

After all, mission managers would have been embarrassed if they had to explain how they lost a $2.7 billion mission because a parachute engineer had sneaked in a secret message.

毕竟，如果任务管理者必须解释自己是如何因一名降落伞工程师潜入秘密消息而损失了27亿美元的任务的，他们会感到很尴尬。

But Dr. Clark’s analysis showed no ill effects, and the plan went forward. Until this week, only about half a dozen people knew about it.

但是克拉克博士的分析显示没有不良影响，该计划得以实施。到本周为止，只有大约六个人对此有所了解。

When computer scientists see something in black and white — or, in this case, orange and white — they think of binary code, the 1s and 0s that are the language of computers. That was the first clue that the puzzle solvers pursued on Monday.

当计算机科学家看到黑色或白色（或本例中为橙色和白色）的东西时，他们会想到二进制代码，即1和0是计算机的语言。这是谜题解决者周一寻求的第一个线索。

For each orange section on the Perseverance parachute, Maxence Abela and his father wrote down a 1, and for each white section, they assigned a 0. That translated into a long string of 1s and 0s.

Maxence Abela和他的父亲为坚毅降落伞上的每个橙色部分写下了1，为每个白色部分分配了0。该结果转换为1和0的长字符串。

They thought that perhaps the digits could be rearranged into a picture, like the message that scientists broadcast in 1974 from the Arecibo radio telescope in Puerto Rico to tell distant alien civilizations of humans on Earth.

他们认为数字也许可以重新排列成图片，例如1974年科学家从波多黎各的Arecibo射电望远镜播报的信息，以告诉地球上遥远的人类文明。

“We couldn’t find anything that looked like anything,” Mr. Abela said.

阿贝拉说：“我们找不到任何看起来像什么的东西。”

They tried breaking up the digits into groups of 8 — a common practice used in computer programming — but that too yielded gibberish.

他们试图将数字分成8组-这是计算机编程中的一种常见做法-但这也产生了乱码。

Then the elder Mr. Abela noticed that the digits seemed to fit in groups of 10. “Every 10 bits, there would be three zeros in a row,” Maxence Abela said. That, they decided, was not a coincidence.

然后，长老阿贝拉先生注意到这些数字似乎适合以10为一组。“每10位，一行中将有三个零，”马克西斯·阿贝拉说。他们认为那不是巧合。

Still, the resulting numbers did not make sense until they realized they had read the 1s and 0s in the wrong direction, anticlockwise instead of clockwise. When they wrote down the digits in the opposite order, the 10-digit chunks of binary code translated into small numbers, which could then be assigned to letters. The number 1 corresponded to the letter A, the number 2 was B, 3 was C, 4 was D and so on.

不过，直到他们意识到以错误的方向（逆时针而非顺时针）读取1和0时，得出的数字才有意义。当他们以相反的顺序写下数字时，二进制代码的10位数字块将转换为小数字，然后可以将其分配给字母。数字1对应于字母A，数字2为B，3为C，4为D，依此类推。

Mr. Abela posted on Twitter his answer at 4:36 p.m. Eastern time, about two hours after Mr. Chen had dropped his cryptic hint during the news conference.

阿贝拉先生于下午4时36分在Twitter上发布了他的回答。东部时间，即陈先生在新闻发布会上放下神秘的暗示大约两个小时之后。

This is a credo often cited at NASA’s Jet Propulsion Laboratory in California, which built and operates Perseverance. It comes from “The Strenuous Life,” a speech by Teddy Roosevelt in 1899: “Far better it is to dare mighty things, to win glorious triumphs, even though checkered by failure, than to take rank with those poor spirits who neither enjoy much nor suffer much, because they live in a gray twilight that knows not victory nor defeat.”

这是在美国国家航空航天局位于加利福尼亚的喷气推进实验室建立和运作恒心的信条。摘自1899年泰迪·罗斯福（Teddy Roosevelt）的演讲《艰苦的生活》：“更好的是，敢于勇敢的事情，即使在失败的考验下，也能赢得光荣的胜利，而不是与那些既不享乐又无济于事的可怜的人一起排名。也不会遭受太大的损失，因为他们生活在不知道胜利与失败的灰色暮光中。”

There were still some numbers and letters in the fourth ring that neither Abela could make sense of. Those turned out to be the longitude and latitude of the NASA center: 34°11’58” N 118°10’31” W.

第四圈中还有一些数字和字母，亚贝拉都无法理解。原来是美国国家航空航天局中心的经度和纬度：北纬34°11’58”西经118°10’31”。

“If you look in Google Earth, if you type in those coordinates, I think you’re about 10 feet from the door of the JPL visitor center,” Dr. Clark said.

克拉克博士说：“如果您在Google Earth中查看，如果输入这些坐标，我认为您距JPL访客中心门口约10英尺。”

In the evening, Adam Steltzner, the chief engineer for Perseverance, posted an annotated diagram explaining the solution.

晚上，Perseverance的首席工程师Adam Steltzner发布了带注释的图表，说明了解决方案。

The parachute was not the only fun that the builders of the Perseverance rover had.

降落伞并不是恒心漫游者建造者唯一的乐趣。

Eagle-eyed observers spotted a series of drawings that represented the five rovers NASA has sent to Mars, from the small Sojourner in 1997 to Perseverance now.

目光敏锐的观察者发现了一系列代表美国宇航局发送给火星的五辆火星车的图纸，从1997年的小型旅居者到现在的恒心。

A plaque that will be used to calibrate one of the rover’s main cameras includes patches of colors, but there are also whimsical drawings that include DNA, a rocket and a dinosaur.

用来校准流动站主摄像机之一的一块牌子上有许多色块，但也有一些异想天开的图纸，包括DNA，火箭和恐龙。

On the edge of the calibration plaque is an inscription: “Are we alone? We came here to look for signs of life, and to collect samples of Mars for study on Earth. To those who follow, we wish a safe journey and the joy of discovery.”

校准板的边缘上有一个铭文：“我们一个人吗？我们来这里是为了寻找生命的迹象，并收集火星样本以便在地球上研究。对于那些追随者，我们希望旅途安全，发现愉快。”

Elsewhere on the rover is a piece of a Martian meteorite that landed on Earth and is now back on its original planet. That is to be used for calibration of SuperCam, an instrument that uses lasers and a camera to identify carbon-based molecules and other compounds in rocks and soil. (Before going back to Mars, the same well-traveled rock made a round-trip visit to the International Space Station.)

流浪者的其他地方是一块降落在地球上的火星陨石，现在又回到了它原来的行星上。这将用于SuperCam的校准，SuperCam是一种使用激光和照相机识别岩石和土壤中碳基分子和其他化合物的仪器。 （在返回火星之前，同一条走过的岩石曾往返国际空间站。）

Also on Perseverance are three small chips with the names of 10.9 million people stenciled on them, part of NASA’s efforts for the public to participate in its robotic missions. A more solemn addition was an aluminum plate that honors hardships of those affected by the coronavirus pandemic.

坚持不懈还印制了三个小芯片，上面印有1090万人的名字，这是美国宇航局为公众参与其机器人任务所做的努力的一部分。更为严肃的补充是一块铝板，用于纪念受冠状病毒大流行影响的人们的艰辛。

The practice of adding fun or solemn pieces to spacecraft is not new. In NASA jargon, it is called “festooning.” The two Voyager spacecraft that are now in interstellar space have discs full of images and sounds of Earth. Two earlier Mars rovers, Spirit and Opportunity, had parts made from the wreckage of the World Trade Center. The New Horizons spacecraft, which flew past Pluto in 2015, carries some of the ashes of Clyde Tombaugh, the astronomer who discovered that world.

向航天器添加趣味或庄重的作品的做法并不新鲜。在NASA的行话中，这被称为“花彩”。现在位于星际空间中的两架旅行者号飞船的光盘上充满了地球的图像和声音。早期的两个火星探测器“精神与机遇”（Spirit and Opportunity）的零件是由世界贸易中心的残骸制成的。 2015年飞越冥王星的“新视野”号飞船上载着发现了这个世界的天文学家克莱德·汤博的骨灰。

“There’s some things on the front of the vehicle that we’ll have a chance to see once we deploy the robot arm,” Mr. Wallace said. He declined to say what they were or provide hints.

华莱士先生说：“一旦部署机器人手臂，我们就会有机会看到车辆前部的一些东西。”他拒绝透露它们是什么或提供提示。

“We’re going to let people enjoy the imagery when it comes,” he said.

他说：“当图像出现时，我们将让人们欣赏它们。”

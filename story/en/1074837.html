Sensor Breakthrough Paves Way For Groundbreaking Map of World Under Earth Surface
传感器的突破为绘制突破性的地球表面下世界地图铺平了道路

2022-02-25 11:56:59

An object hidden below ground has been located using quantum technology—a long-awaited milestone with profound implications for industry, human knowledge and national security. University of Birmingham researchers from the UK National Quantum Technology Hub in Sensors and Timing have reported their achievement in Nature. It is the first in the world for a quantum gravity gradiometer outside of laboratory conditions.

利用量子技术找到了一个隐藏在地下的物体——这是一个期待已久的里程碑，对工业、人类知识和国家安全具有深远影响。英国伯明翰大学量子技术中心在传感器和计时方面的研究人员报告了他们在自然界的成就。这是世界上第一台非实验室条件下的量子重力梯度仪。

The quantum gravity gradiometer, which was developed under a contract for the Ministry of Defense and in the UKRI-funded Gravity Pioneer project, was used to find a tunnel buried outdoors in real-world conditions one meter below the ground surface. It wins an international race to take the technology outside.

量子重力梯度仪是根据国防部的合同开发的，也是在UKRI资助的重力先锋项目中开发的，用于在现实世界条件下发现埋在地下一米以下的室外隧道。它赢得了一场将技术带到国外的国际竞赛。

The sensor works by detecting variations in microgravity using the principles of quantum physics, which is based on manipulating nature at the sub-molecular level.

该传感器的工作原理是利用量子物理原理检测微重力的变化，量子物理原理基于在亚分子水平上操纵自然。

The success opens a commercial path to significantly improved mapping of what exists below ground level.

这一成功开辟了一条商业道路，大大改善了对地下存在物的测绘。

Professor Kai Bongs, head of cold atom physics at the University of Birmingham and principal investigator of the UK Quantum Technology Hub Sensors and Timing, said: "This is an 'Edison moment' in sensing that will transform society, human understanding and economies.

伯明翰大学冷原子物理系负责人Kai Bongs和英国量子技术中心传感器和计时的首席研究员说：这是一个'；爱迪生时刻'；这将改变社会、人类理解和经济。

"With this breakthrough we have the potential to end reliance on poor records and luck as we explore, build and repair. In addition, an underground map of what is currently invisible is now a significant step closer, ending a situation where we know more about Antarctica than what lies a few feet below our streets."

"有了这一突破，我们有可能在探索、建造和修复过程中结束对糟糕记录和运气的依赖。此外，一张目前看不见的地下地图现在离我们又近了一大步，结束了我们对南极洲的了解比街道下面几英尺深的地方更多的局面"

Current gravity sensors are limited by a range of environmental factors. A particular challenge is vibration, which limits the measurement time of all gravity sensors for survey applications. If these limitations can be addressed, surveys can become faster, more comprehensive and lower cost.

目前的重力传感器受到一系列环境因素的限制。一个特别的挑战是振动，这限制了测量应用中所有重力传感器的测量时间。如果能够解决这些限制，调查可以变得更快、更全面、成本更低。

The sensor developed by Dr. Michael Holynski, Head of Atom Interferometry at Birmingham and lead author of the study, and his team at Birmingham is a gravity gradiometer. Their system overcomes vibration and a variety of other environmental challenges in order to successfully apply in the field.

该传感器由伯明翰原子干涉仪负责人、该研究的主要作者迈克尔·霍林斯基博士及其伯明翰团队开发，是一种重力梯度仪。他们的系统克服了振动和其他各种环境挑战，以便成功地应用于该领域。

The successful detection, realized in collaboration with led by Professor Nicole Metje of the School of Engineering, is the culmination of a long-term development program that has been closely linked to end-users from its outset.

这项由工程学院的Nicole Metje教授领导的成功检测是一项长期发展计划的最终成果，该计划从一开始就与最终用户密切相关。

This breakthrough will allow future gravity surveys to be cheaper, more reliable and delivered 10 times faster, reducing the time needed for surveys from a month to a few days. It has the potential to open a range of new applications for survey, providing a new lens into the underground.

这一突破将使未来的重力测量变得更便宜、更可靠，交付速度提高10倍，将测量所需的时间从一个月减少到几天。它有可能为勘测打开一系列新的应用程序，为地下提供一个新的镜头。

Professor George Tuckwell, director for geoscience and engineering at RSK, said: "Detection of ground conditions such as mine workings, tunnels and unstable ground is fundamental to our ability to design, construct and maintain housing, industry and infrastructure. The improved capability that this new technology represents could transform how we map the ground and deliver these projects."

RSK地球科学与工程总监乔治·塔克韦尔教授说："；探测地面条件，如矿井、隧道和不稳定地面，对我们设计、建造和维护住房、工业和基础设施的能力至关重要。这项新技术所代表的改进能力可能会改变我们规划和交付这些项目的方式"

The breakthrough is a collaboration between the University of Birmingham, environmental, engineering and sustainability solutions provider RSK, Dstl (the Defense Science and Technology Laboratory, part of the UK Ministry of Defense), and technology company Teledyne e2v.

这项突破是伯明翰大学、环境、工程和可持续解决方案提供商RSK、Dstl（国防科学技术实验室、英国国防部的一部分）和技术公司TeleDeNe E2V的合作。

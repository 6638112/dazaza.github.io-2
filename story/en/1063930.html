Is our sun going into hibernation?

All About Space magazine takes you on an awe-inspiring journey through our solar system and beyond, from the amazing technology and spacecraft that enables humanity to venture into orbit, to the complexities of space science.

Solar activity refers to the state of the sun’s magnetic field and associated phenomena: sunspots, flares, solar wind and coronal ejections. During periods of minimal solar activity, such events are often uncommon and weak. During solar maximum, they’re at their strongest and most frequent. Magnetic field fluctuations on the sun can happen on drastically different timescales, ranging from seconds all the way to billions of years. When astronomers speak of a "slowdown" or a period of quiescence in the sun’s activity, it doesn’t mean the sun will stop shining, but that there’s a slowdown in activity.

太阳能是指太阳磁场的状态和相关现象：太阳黑子，耀斑，太阳风和冠状喷射。在最小的太阳能活动期间，这种事件往往罕见，弱。在太阳能最大值期间，他们处于最强大，最常见的。太阳的磁场波动可能发生在巨大不同的时间尺寸，从秒到数十亿年来。天文学家谈到一个＆＃34;放缓＆＃34;或者在阳光活动中的一段时间，这并不意味着太阳会停止闪耀，但在活动中存在放缓。

The sun has one particular rhythm, lasting approximately 11 years, in which its polar magnetic field flips polarity. Sunspots serve as an indicator of this change. Indeed, it’s often known as "the sunspot cycle."

太阳有一个特定的节奏，持续大约11年，其中极性磁场翻转极性。太阳黑子作为这种变化的指标。实际上，它通常被称为＆＃34;太阳黑子循环。＆＃34;

Although sunspots themselves were first observed in detail by Galileo, Christoph Scheiner and others from 1609 onwards, according to the British Library, the cyclical nature of their appearance and disappearance was first noted in 1775 by Danish astronomer Christian Horrebow.

据英国图书馆表示，虽然Sunspots本身是由Galileo，Christoph Schiner和其他1609名开始于1609年开始观察到的，但是在1775年由丹麦天文学家基督徒Horrewow排名第1775的外表和失踪的周期性。

It was then rediscovered in 1843 by Heinrich Schwabe. In 1848, Swiss astronomer Rudolf Wolf used Schwabe and others’ results, as well as performing his own observations, to calculate the 11-year cycle and a mathematical method to count the number of sunspots. This so-called “Wolf number” remains in use today, according to the National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration (NOAA).

然后在Heinrich Schwabe于1843年重新发现。 1848年，瑞士天文学家Rudolf Wolf使用Schwabe和其他结果，以及进行自己的观察，计算11年周期和数学方法计算太阳黑子数量。根据国家海洋和大气管理局（NOAA），今天所谓的“狼号码”仍在使用中。

Many other astronomers at the time either independently observed this cycle or were inspired by the results of others. Wolf’s own calculation of the 1755 to 1766 sunspot cycle was labeled as the first, and each sunspot cycle since then has been progressively numbered as such. We are now in Cycle 25.

当时许多其他天文学家无论是独立地观察到这个周期还是受到他人结果的启发。狼自己的1755到1766太阳黑子周期的计算被标记为第一，并且每个太阳黑子循环都是逐步编号的。我们现在处于循环25。

But sometimes the spots don’t appear at all. This was the case for 80 days of the first six months of the current solar cycle, which started in December 2019. It was greater still for the same period in Cycle 24, where there were 281 spot-free days. The period from 1645 to 1715 saw a near-total crash in sunspot numbers, where they could literally be counted on two hands.

但有时斑点根本没有出现。这是目前太阳循环的前六个月的80天的情况，该案件于2019年12月开始。在循环24中的同期，它仍然更大，在那里存在281天。 1645年至1715的期限在太阳黑子号码中看到了近乎总崩溃，在那里他们可以在两只手上计算。

Wolf struggled to piece together solar cycles before the mid-1700s because of this dearth of information, but it didn’t mean sunspots weren’t being observed. Many distinguished astronomers of the age, such as Giovanni Cassini, continued to make observations. This 70-year solar lull was later noted by German astronomer Gustav Spörer, which then later inspired the British-Irish husband and wife team Edward and Annie Maunder. The period has since been named the Maunder minimum, according to Encyclopedia Britannica.

由于这种缺乏信息，狼在1700年代中期之前努力将太阳循环队在一起，但这并不意味着太阳黑子没有被观察到。许多年龄的杰出天文学家，如Giovanni Cassini，继续观察。这座70年来的太阳能平静后来被德国天文学家GustavSpörer注意到，后来启发了英国爱尔兰夫妻队爱德华和安妮·马德队。根据百科全书的Britannica的说法，此期间已被评为最低限度。

There have been other lulls before and since, such as the Spörer and Dalton minima. However, Madhulika Guhathakurta, a heliophysicist at NASA, speaking in a personal capacity, questioned how the technology available then affected observations. "During the Maunder minimum, when we couldn't detect any sunspots, the question remains how well could we detect very faint sunspots then or even now?" she told All About Space via email. We don't know and have no measure of that. The uncertainty associated with detecting no sunspots is much harder than counting them during solar maximum."

In 2020 Cycle 25 had 80% more sunspots overall than the equivalent period for Cycle 24, suggesting that the current cycle may in fact be stronger, rather than weaker. The international Solar Cycle 25 Prediction Panel said in September 2020 that they expect Cycle 25 to be about as strong as Cycle 24. Has the consensus changed since then, or is it still the same?

在2020年的循环25中，Sunspots总共80％比循环24的等效时间更多，表明当前周期实际上可能更强大，而不是弱。国际太阳循环25预测小组在9月20日上表示，他们预计周期25就像循环24那样强大。从那时起达成共识，或者它仍然是相同的吗？

"The consensus has not changed," panel co-chair Doug Biesecker told All About Space. The consensus is still that the current cycle will be much like Cycle 24. "We have not seen anything that differs significantly in the early stages of this cycle that varies from the panel prediction of a peak of 115 [sunspots] in July 2025." Guhathakurta believes this cycle is not out of the ordinary. "Looking at sunspots as a non-physical index of solar activity, I think this solar cycle isn't unusual, especially in the context of the prior cycle. This cycle's sort of mimicking that. Even in the early 1800s and 1900s, you see cycles of this magnitude."

Guhathakurta认为这个循环并不是普通的。 ＆＃34;看着太阳黑子作为太阳能活动的非物理指数，我认为这种太阳循环是不寻常的，特别是在先前循环的上下文中。这个循环＆＃39; s一种模仿。即使在19世纪初和1900年代初，你也会看到这个幅度的周期。＆＃34;

Successfully predicting solar weather is certainly essential when testing scientific theories about how the sun works. But there’s a more pressing practical reason for doing so. Strong solar flare events and coronal mass ejections — most likely to occur around the time of solar maxima — can disrupt modern technology.

在测试科学理论时，成功预测太阳天气肯定是必不可少的。但是这样做的更加迫切的实际原因。强大的太阳耀斑事件和冠状大规模喷射 - 最有可能发生在太阳能最大的时间周围 - 可以扰乱现代技术。

The flux of high-energy particles can damage spacecraft, satellites and even ground-based power systems. The latter are particularly vulnerable, as solar radiation easily disturbs Earth’s magnetic field, inducing currents in long power lines. Such a geomagnetic storm destroyed large grid transformers and shut down the whole of Quebec, Canada, in March 1989, NASA reported. And this was just history repeating itself. In September 1859 a geomagnetic storm, dubbed the Carrington event, caused interesting problems with the telegraph system.

高能颗粒的通量会损坏航天器，卫星甚至基于地面的电力系统。后者特别容易受到攻击，作为太阳辐射容易扰动地球的磁场，诱导长电线的电流。 NASA报道，这种地磁风暴摧毁了大型电网变压器并关闭了加拿大魁北克的整个魁北克省。这只是历史重复自己。 1859年9月，一场地磁风暴被称为Carrington事件，导致电报系统有趣的问题。

Telegraph operators noticed that they could disconnect their batteries and work with just the induced currents from the storm — sometimes with improved results! To this day it is the most powerful coronal mass ejection on record. If a Carrington-level event were to happen now, it would cause widespread damage and disruption to power systems and satellites. As well as decimating electronics, any astronauts venturing beyond the protective blanket of Earth’s geomagnetic field — such as to the moon or Mars — would be in danger, something that NASA’s upcoming Artemis lunar program needs to keep in mind.

电报算子注意到，他们可以断开他们的电池并与风暴的诱导电流一起打开，有时会改善结果！到这一天，它是最强大的冠状大众群体。如果现在发生了Carrington级事件，它将导致电力系统和卫星的广泛损坏和中断。除了吸取电子器件，任何宇航员都冒昧地冒了地球地磁场的保护毯 - 例如月球或火星 - 将处于危险之中，美国宇航局即将到来的Artemis Lunar计划需要牢记。

Considering the stakes, coupled with the fact that the sun is such a complex system, there must be another way to glean information about its future behavior besides sunspots. Other tried-and-trusted methods exist, but there may well be another arrow in the quiver. A U.S.-U.K. team led by Scott McIntosh of the High Altitude Observatory at the National Center for Atmospheric Research in Boulder, Colorado, has looked at a phenomenon related to sunspot activity.

考虑到赌注，加上太阳是如此复杂的系统的事实，必须有另一种方法来收集除了太阳黑子之外的未来行为的信息。存在其他尝试和可信的方法，但QUIVIVE中可能会有另一个箭头。通过科罗拉多州博尔德（Colorado）国家大气研究中心的高海拔天文台的斯科特麦克林斯·斯科特麦克风的团队研究了与太阳黑子活动有关的现象。

Using data from sources such as NASA’s Solar Dynamics Observatory, the team looked at extreme ultraviolet and X-ray flashes in the solar corona. These so-called “bright points” in the sun’s atmosphere correlate with large areas of material flowing in the sun’s interior, which rotates faster than surface plasma, similar to rates seen for sunspots. Robert Leamon of the University of Maryland likened it to helium balloons being dragged along by weights attached to the bottom.

使用来自NASA的太阳能动态天文台等资源的数据，该团队在太阳能电晕闪光灯中闪烁了极端紫外线和X射线。这些所谓的“明亮点”在太阳的大气中与太阳内部流动的大型材料相关，这比表面等离子体旋转得更快，类似于为太阳黑子看到的速率。马里兰大学的罗伯特·莱蒙将它与氦气拖到底部的重量拖动。

Such a study is potentially more useful than the sunspot cycle as it shows magnetic polarity. In the 19th century, Richard Carrington and Spörer both discovered sunspots appearing at different latitudes during different points in the solar cycle, starting at mid-latitudes and migrating towards the equator at the end. Plotted against time, this leads to a distinctive “butterfly” diagram.

这样的研究可能比太阳黑子循环更有用，因为它显示磁极性。在19世纪，Richard Carrington和Spörer都发现了在太阳循环中不同点的不同纬度地出现的太阳黑子，从中纬度开始并在最后迁移到赤道。绘制的时间，这导致了一个独特的“蝴蝶”图。

But in the early 20th century, American astronomer George Ellery Hale proved the importance of solar magnetism by showing how a complete polar flip actually spans two sunspot cycles: a flip, then a flip back, according to NASA. This 22-year Hale cycle is what McIntosh’s team looks at. The bright points are markers of Hale cycle magnetic bands.

但在20世纪初，美国天文学家乔治·埃利海尔通过显示完整的极地翻转实际跨越两个太阳黑子循环：据美国航空航空航天局的翻转，然后翻转，据证明太阳能磁力的重要性。这个22年的Hale循环是McIntosh的团队的看法。明亮的点是空心循环磁带的标记。

This begs the question of why the wider solar scientific community doesn’t make more use of such observations in this way. "Folks have in the past — calling them ephemeral active regions — but predominantly they are locked into the ‘big white whales’ of solar activity: sunspots," McIntosh said. Just like sunspots, the magnetic bands travel the latitudes of the sun to meet at the equator, annihilating in what McIntosh calls "termination events." His team uses these termination events to identify complete 22-year magnetic cycles, as well as the 11-year sunspot cycles of the past.

Using these alongside predictions made for a 2020 terminator event, the team predicts that Solar Cycle 25 will, in fact, be strong. In marked contrast to scientific consensus, they say it will be among the strongest ever recorded.

If McIntosh’s study is right, however, what could it say about the solar interior? "That the magnetic systems inside the sun are much stronger than we anticipate and that they interact strongly to shape the production of spots," McIntosh said. The pressure of the magnetic field band relative to the surroundings is important: "My sense is that the sun really wants to be balanced, and what we see in terms of sunspots is the result of imbalance in that pressure — globally, locally and longitudinally." This means they think they’re only seeing the tip of the iceberg where the magnetic field is concerned.

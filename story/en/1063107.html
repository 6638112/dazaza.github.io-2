Our Favourite Uses of Typography in Watches

Good typography should be almost unnoticeable. Blending seamlessly into the rest of the design, it should tell you everything you need to know, without you being aware of it. Despite the many restrictions that are applied to dial layout, the creativity that can be seen in typography across horology is quite staggering. To put it simply, typography is the art and technique of arranging type to make written language legible and appealing when displayed. As the dial is the main point of interaction with a watch, it is arguably one of its most important parts, and certainly one that can produce the most emotion. This is why typeface can play such a vital, yet subtle, role in how we experience and feel about a certain piece.

良好的排版应该几乎不明显。无缝混合进入其余的设计中，它应该告诉你你需要知道的一切，没有你意识到它。尽管应用到表盘布局，可以在整个钟表排版可以看出是相当惊人的创造力的诸多限制。简单地说，排版是排列类型的艺术和技术，以便在显示时进行书面语言清晰易读。随着表盘是与手表互动的主要观点，可以说是其最重要的部分之一，肯定可以产生最大的情感。这就是为什么字体可以发挥如此至关重要的，但在我们经历和感受到某个部分的情况下的角色。

Typography is a space where different dynamics collide and come at play. The iconic Breguet numerals can take many different forms and connotations, depending on when they’re used and by whom.  Some other designs – such as the omnipresent “flat top 4” – were born out of practical necessity and production constraints. It’s also clear that when some manufacturers dedicate themselves to creating their own font, the impact can be significant. With such a rich history to draw on, we thought we would look back at what we consider some of the core principles and themes behind effective typography, as well as list some of our favourite examples in the process.

排版是一个不同动态碰撞并在播放的空间。根据它们使用的时间和谁，标志性的Broguet数字可以采取许多不同的形式和内涵。其他一些设计 - 如全能的“平坦4” - 诞生于实际必需品和生产限制。还可以清楚，当一些制造商奉献自己创造自己的字体时，影响可能很重要。通过如此丰富的历史绘制，我们认为我们会回顾我们认为有效排版的一些核心原则和主题，以及列出了在该过程中的一些最喜欢的例子。

A certain typographic-element can evolve over time. It can change its style, be used by different watchmakers and brands, and ultimately adopt different connotations. A prime example of this can be found with Breguet numerals, which have evolved in a rather interesting way over their history. Originally styled prior to the French Revolution by Abraham-Louis Breguet, it is said that they were formalised by the watchmaker in 1790. The watchmaker only ever deployed the design on his enamel dials, distinguishing them from the engine-turned, metal dials that he also produced. This tradition has been carried on by the brand which carries his name today, as you will only find these stylised Arabic numerals on enamel dials.

某个排版元素可以随时间演变。它可以改变其风格，由不同的制表商和品牌使用，最终采用不同的内涵。可以找到一个辉煌的例子，并用勒吉特数字找到，这已经以相当有趣的方式演变。据说原来在法国人革命之前由亚伯拉罕 - 路易斯布罗克雷特进行了称，他们在1790年被钟表制造商正式化。钟表匠只在他的珐琅表盘上部署了设计，将它们与发动机转动，金属拨号区分开也生产。这一传统在今天承担着名字的品牌，因为您只在搪瓷拨号中找到这些风格化的阿拉伯语数字。

Of course, Breguet numerals have become ubiquitous in watchmaking since their first appearance centuries ago. One brand that has deployed them to great effect over the years is Patek Philippe. Like Breguet, they only ever used these stylised Arabic numerals for special models, setting them apart from their standard production. Whilst Breguet chose to paint his numerals, Patek Philippe often adopted a more visually striking approach, in the form of applied Breguet numerals. These can be found across a few of their vintage pieces, from the time-only Calatravas to their more complicated chronographs.

当然，宝玑数字已经因为他们的首次亮相世纪前制表变得无处不在。多年来一直将它们部署到巨大效果的一个品牌是百息翡丽。像Broguet一样，他们只使用这些风格化的阿拉伯数字用于特殊模型，将它们与标准生产相结合。虽然Breguet选择涂上他的数字，但佩特克翡丽经常采用更具视觉上引人注目的方法，以应用的Broguet数字的形式。这些葡萄酒件中可以找到这些复古块，从时刻的Calatravas到他们更复杂的计时码表。

Lee Yuen-Rapati, a typeface designer who has created bespoke typefaces for several watch brands, argues that Breguet numerals are far more artistic than other equivalents. “Because these were originally painted,” Yuen-Rapati explains, “they contain far too many inconsistencies to be considered a true typeface, in my opinion.” However, it could be this lack of uniformity that has attracted us to this embellished script. It’s because these numerals evolved from hand-painted ones that they remain so characterful today. They were painted, then printed, then carved out of metal, giving them a unique design, which can only result from multiple iterations and evolution over time.

Lee Yuen-Rapati是一个为几个手表品牌创建定制字体而创建了定制字体的字体设计师，认为，Broguet数字远远超过其他等同物。 “由于这些最初画，”婉Rapati解释说，“它们含有太多的不一致被认为是一个真正的字体，在我的意见。”但是，这可能是这种缺乏统一，它已经吸引了我们这个缀饰的剧本。这是因为这些数字从手工绘制的数字演变，他们今天仍然如此特色。它们被涂漆，然后打印，然后雕刻出金属，给他们一个独特的设计，它只能通过多次迭代和演变来实现。

“This lettering has also become more attractive over the years thanks to the watches that it’s been put on,” points out Yuen-Rapati. Indeed, because Breguet numerals have become associated with rare pieces from the golden age of Patek Philippe in the ‘40s and ‘50s, there’s no doubt that they have become all the more appreciated as a result. Even Patek Philippe themselves gave them a further twist, when they imbed them with Art Deco aesthetics as part of their early watches created for Gondolo & Labouriau. Later in the 20 th century, it is telling that certain collectors who prized the history of the manufacture chose to have custom pieces created with Breguet numerals, as a nod to the past. One of the most noteworthy examples of this is Eric Clapton, the celebrated musician, who commissioned several unique pieces through Patek Philippe, including several perpetual calendar chronographs.

“这刻字在多年上也变得更具吸引力，因为它的手表已经被搁置，”指出了Yuen-Rapati。实际上，因为在40年代和'50年代普利克翡丽黄金时代的罕见碎片已经与罕见的碎片变得有关，毫无疑问，他们已经变得更加感谢。即使是百匹菲利普本身也让他们进一步扭曲，当他们覆盖着艺术Deco美学时，作为他们为Gondolo＆amp创造的早期手表的一部分; Labouriau。在20世纪的晚些时期，它讲述了珍惜制造历史的某些收藏家选择了用勒吉特数字创造的定制件，作为过去的点头。其中一个最值得注意的例子是Eric Clapton，庆祝的音乐家，通过百息菲利普委托几个独特的作品，包括几个永久日历计时码表。

Drawing inspiration from Patek Philippe, Dubuis made use of applied Breguet numerals across many of his early models.

借阅普伊斯·菲利普的灵感，Dubuis在他早期模型中的许多型号中使用了应用的Broguet数字。

Elsewhere, Breguet numerals have taken many different forms, with some brands taking direct inspiration from Patek Philippe, whilst other reinterpreted the original Breguet design in their own way. Roger Dubuis, an independent watchmaker from the late 20 th century, integrated the cursive numerals in many of his designs, as a direct homage to the manufacture where he spent the best part of two decades. For him, it was an aesthetic cue which symbolised the best that Patek Philippe had to offer, which is why they can be found across his Hommage and Sympathie pieces.

在其他地方，Broguet数字采取了许多不同的形式，其中一些品牌从佩特克翡丽开始直接启发，而其他品牌以自己的方式重新解释原始的酒保性设计。一个从20世纪末期的独立钟表制造厂罗杰·迪拜，在他的许多设计中综合了卷曲的数字，作为对制造商的直接致敬，他花了两十年的最佳部分。对他来说，这是一个象征着博士翡丽不得不提供的最佳审美线索，这就是为什么他们可以在他的恒星和同情的作品中找到。

Another independent, who took a rather different approach, was François-Paul Journe. With Breguet being a long-standing source of horological inspiration for the watchmaker, it makes sense that he would also adopt some of his aesthetic cues. Notably, his distinctive numerals are a play on the classic Breguet design. Despite that, Yuen-Rapati lists Journe’s numerals, and his typeface use overall, as one of his least favourite in the watch world. “A lot of what Journe does would be considered objectively wrong within typeface design. The thickness of his 1 and 2 are inconsistent and he uses a typeface that could be downloaded for free online,” Yuen-Rapati explains. “I’m not saying that this is a terrible thing. In fact, in context, the way he has adapted Breguet numerals means that they are instantly recognisable. Most watch fans would be able to distinguish Journe’s numerals without them sitting next to his name or on one of his watches. That’s very strong branding in my eyes.” This goes to show that the classical rules of typography don’t always have to be adhered to.

另一个独立的，谁采取了相当不同的方法，是François-Paul Journe。通过Broguet成为钟表匠的长期钟表，它有意义，他也将采用他的一些审美线索。值得注意的是，他的独特数字是经典的Broguet设计的戏剧。尽管如此，Yuen-Rapati列出了Journe的数字，以及他的字体整体使用，作为他在观看世界中最不喜欢的之一。 “在字体设计中，很多journe都会被视为错误的错误。 Yuen-Rapati解释说，他的1和2的厚度不一致，他使用可以免费下载的字体，“yuen-rapati解释道。 “我不是说这是一个可怕的事情。事实上，在语境中，他适应了Broguet数字的方式意味着它们即将识别。大多数手表粉丝都能够区分Journe的数字，而没有他们坐在他的名字旁边或他的一个手表。这是我眼中非常强大的品牌。“这是为了表明，经典的排版规则并不总是必须遵守。

Someone else who took things further still was Vianney Halter, who also integrated his own interpretation of Breguet numerals within some of his pieces. Fascinated with mechanics, science fiction and space exploration, Halter creates watches that are unlike any others. To give you an idea, his Classic model is a futuristic combination of the work of Antide Janvier - an astronomical clockmaker and role-model to Halter - an early submarine prototype and retro steam punk design influences. The fact that Breguet numerals could evolve from being hand painted by Breguet on enamel dials, to becoming synonymous with Patek Philippe to ultimately being given a futuristic twist by Vianney Halter shows how great typography can mutate over time. As it adopts various contexts and slightly different styles, it becomes all the more interesting for it.

其他人仍然是Vianney Halter，他还在一些作品中融入了他自己的掠夺数字的解释。对力学，科学小说和太空探索着迷，露背创造了与其他人不同的手表。为了给你一个想法，他的经典模型是抗讽刺的工作的未来组合 - 一个天文钟表匠和角色模型，挂钩 - 早期潜艇原型和复古蒸汽朋克设计影响。 Breguet数字可以从搪瓷拨号上涂抹的手工绘制的事实，使Patek Philippe成为Vianney Halter的未来派扭曲的同义，显示了最大的排版随着时间的推移。因为它采用各种情况和略微不同的样式，它变得更加有趣。

Though Breguet numerals were the result of pure artistic flourish, some of the other great executions of typography can emerge purely out of functional necessity. In the same way that the Rolex Submariner arguably became such an icon because it was designed with usability in mind, the same could be argued for certain elements of print. One such example is the “flat top 4”.

虽然Breguet数字是纯粹的艺术蓬勃发展的结果，但其他一些巨大的排版执行可以纯粹出现出功能必要性。以同样的方式使劳力士潜艇者可以成为这样的图标，因为它旨在考虑到可用性，可以针对某些印刷元素来争论同样的印刷元素。一个这样的例子是“平坦4”。

As wristwatches began to grow in popularity in the first half of the 20 th century, thanks to more reliable movements and more secure cases, the technology to mass produce dials was also developing. This is where pad printing comes in – a method used across a vast number of industries to uniformly transfer an image or text onto an uneven surface.

由于手表在20世纪上半世纪开始普及，因此由于更可靠的运动和更安全的案例，批量生产拨号的技术也在开发。这是焊盘打印进入的地方 - 在大量行业中使用的方法将图像或文本均匀地传送到不平坦的表面上。

顶点上的扁平前4位。 59 NAV手表是肮脏的十几个，由单色的一部分。

Pad printing uses a silicone pad – which is often cone shaped for dial printing – and something called a cliché, which is essentially a negative of whatever you want to appear on the dial, carved into a metal plate. In these recesses, that often measure about 18 to 25 microns deep, ink is poured. The pad is pressed onto this cliché and roughly half of the ink is transferred and carried over onto the dial. After the solvents in the ink have evaporated, which happens quite quickly, you are left with print that is roughly 8 microns high. Multiple passes can be made to create a thicker type. This is an incredibly accurate and repeatable process, which allows for the uniform printing of text on an uneven surface. This technology has not always been as precise as it is today. As inks have improved, the process has become far more accurate. Originally, certain considerations had to be incorporated into the typeface used, to stop the ink from pooling and apertures in numbers and letters from closing up.

垫印刷使用硅胶垫 - 通常是圆锥形用于拨号印刷的锥形 - 以及称为陈词滥调的东西，这基本上是你想要出现在表盘上的任何东西的负面，雕刻成金属板。在这些凹槽中，通常测量约18至25微米的深，涂上墨水。将垫压在该陈词上，大约一半的墨水被转移并延伸到表盘上。在油墨中的溶剂蒸发后，它很快发生，您留下了大约8微米的印刷品。可以使多次通过以创建更厚的类型。这是一个令人难以置信的准确和可重复的过程，它允许在不平整的表面上均匀地打印文本。这项技术并不总是如此精确。由于墨水已经改善，该过程变得更加准确。最初，某些考虑必须纳入所用的字体中，以阻止墨水的汇集和孔径和孔隙的孔隙。

One of the most famous typographic choices to have resulted from this production constraint is the so called “flat top 4”. Now found across a whole range of vintage pieces, the flat top 4 presents a few advantages. By widening the top of the digit, the aperture broadens out, increasing the size of the internal angles. This not only helped with preventing ink pooling, but it also enhanced legibility and clarity, which is especially important on the limited space offered by a watch dial. Once you notice it, it’s difficult to unsee this subtle design feature, which has appeared on a whole range of pieces, from complicated Patek Philippes to time-only Longines.

这种生产限制导致的最着名的印刷选择之一是所谓的“平坦4”。现在发现整个复古件，平坦4呈一些优点。通过加宽数字的顶部，光圈扩大，增加内角的尺寸。这不仅有助于防止墨水池，而且还提高了易读性和清晰度，这对手表拨号提供的有限空间尤为重要。一旦您注意到它，难以解开这种微妙的设计功能，这些功能出现在一系列的碎片上，从复杂的百匹菲律宾到仅仅时间的浪琴。

The bezel of a vintage Rolex Submariner with its flat top 4 reaching the very edge, courtesy of Lunar Oyster .

葡萄酒劳力士潜艇的边框，其平面前4个达到极端的边缘，由月球牡蛎提供。

The flat top 4 also extended beyond the dial, onto bezels and date windows. Because it occupies space in such an efficient manner, it lends itself particularly well to these areas of a watch, where numerals have to be constrained. As a result, it’s no surprise that many vintage date wheels feature the flat top 4, alongside many of the bezels from Rolex’s vintage models. It also has the added benefit of allowing the 4 to be slightly larger in a date window, where all numerals have the same space to occupy and need to make the most of it to be legible. The flat 4 has become so widespread within horology that it can still be found today across multiple brands and models, despite the need for it being arguably diminished, thanks to modern printing technologies.

扁平的前4还延伸到拨号之后，进入挡板和日期窗口。因为它以这样的有效方式占用空间，所以它特别适合于观察的这些区域，其中必须限制数字。因此，许多复古日期轮子都没有令人惊讶的是，许多复古的前四名，以及来自劳力士复古模型的许多挡板。它还具有允许4在日期窗口中稍微稍大的增加的好处，其中所有数字都具有相同的空间来占据，并且需要使最多的空间能够清晰清晰。平坦的4在钟表中普遍存在，它仍然可以在多个品牌和模型中找到它，这仍然需要在现代印刷技术中可以说是可以说的。

A lot can be said for the consistent use of the same typeface across a dial. Uniformity is often praised in the watch world, yet the times when this rule is broken show just how powerful type can be. An excellent example of this can be found on vintage chronographs from Heuer and Breitling, where the model name has been given its own typeface, completely different to that of the brand name from which it sits adjacent to.

可以说很多可以在拨号上一致使用相同的字体。均匀性往往在观察世界中称赞，然而这条规则被破坏的时代显示了如何强大的类型。这方面的一个很好的例子可以在Heuer和Breitling的老式计时码表中找到，其中型号名称已经获得了自己的字体，与其坐在一起的品牌名称完全不同。

Take the Seafarer watches made by Heuer for Abercrombie & Fitch. These pieces feature an austere, sans serif font for the “Abercrombie & Fitch Co”, paired with a more cursive, playful font for the “Seafarer” model name. The model and the brand sit in balanced opposition to one another, in a way that is extremely visually satisfying. It also does exactly what a good font should do, namely provoke certain associations – the brand is reliable and timeless, whilst the model evokes a sense of adventure and playfulness. Combined, they reach a perfect balance.

Some interesting, though slightly more subtle, examples can also be found from Rolex. Though the brand remains firmly restrained in its designs approach, a few interesting uses of contrasting typography can be found. Because Rolex is so controlled in everything they do, even the smallest of contrasts can be extremely powerful, such as the red line of text found on some early Milgausses or the so called “Red” Submariners. The interplay of serif and non-serif font – on some indications such as the “200 m = 600 ft” – also achieve this same effect.

有些有趣的是，虽然稍微微妙，但也可以从劳力士找到的例子。虽然品牌仍然坚定地抑制其设计方法，但可以找到一些有趣的对比排版的用途。因为劳力士在他们所做的一切中都是如此控制，即使是最小的对比度也可能是极其强大的，例如在一些早期米尔斯岛或所谓的“红色”潜艇上发现的红线。 Serif和非Serif字体的相互作用 - 在一些指示，如“200m = 600英尺” - 也实现了相同的效果。

If we look more closely at perhaps one of the most iconic wristwatch designs of the 20 th century, the Paul Newman Daytona, it also becomes obvious that much of the strength lies in the subtle contrasts which give balance to the design. In particular, the subdials gather conflicting shapes, with the rounded numerals and square painted indices, which are in natural tension with one another. Nowadays, looking back, many of these things appear harmonious, however much of the balance within these designs comes from the idea of gathering things that should otherwise not be brought together.

如果我们看起来更仔细地看出20世纪20世纪的最具标志性的手表设计之一，那么Paul Newman Daytona，它也很明显，大部分实力位于微妙的对比度，使其平衡为设计。特别是，子地区聚集冲突的形状，圆形数字和方形涂漆指数，它们彼此自然张力。如今，回顾，许多这些事情看起来和谐，但这些设计内的大部分平衡都来自收集应该换成的东西的想法。

Most brands, even some of the most established ones, do not have a typographic designer on their payroll. Rather, their typography will be under the control of their design team, which often means that the typeface used is rarely a bespoke one. However, when brands go through the effort of creating a design from scratch, the impact can be noticeable, due to the lack of originality which exists across the industry.

大多数品牌，甚至一些最成熟的品牌，在他们的工资单上没有印刷设计师。相反，他们的排版将在他们的设计团队的控制下，这通常意味着所使用的字体很少是定制的字体。然而，当品牌通过划痕创造设计的努力时，由于行业中存在的原创性缺乏缺乏原创性，影响可能是显而易见的。

The first major name that gets brought up in this conversation is often A. Lange & Söhne. When the Glashütte-based brand relaunched in 1994, it opted for a typeface known as Engravers, invented by the German-born engraver Robert Wiebking in 1899. However, in 2012, the brand decided to rework this typeface into something of their own creation and introduced this modernised version of Engravers MT on the Grande Lange 1 that came out the same year. It was then added to each new model as they came out in the subsequent years.

在这次谈话中提出的第一个主要名称往往是A. Lange＆amp; Söhne。当基于Glashütte的品牌于1994年重新启动时，它选择了一个称为雕报器的字体，由德国出生的雕塑罗伯特Wiebking于1899年发明。然而，在2012年，该品牌决定将这个字体返回为自己创造的东西在同年出现的Grande Lange 1上介绍了这一现代化的雕刻版本。然后在随后的几年中加入了每个新模式。

Beyond some slight adjustments to the thickness, the brand also added small serifs onto the lettering that, while almost imperceptible at first, can be rather obvious if you place an older and newer piece next to one another. These adjustments wouldn’t have been possible back in 1899 when Wiebking first designed the typeface, due to the technology that was around at the time. Now that we can be far more precise with our printing, it is possible to create smaller details consistently, over multiple prints.

除了对厚度的一些轻微调整之外，该品牌还将小丝绸添加到刻字上，虽然几乎是难以察觉的，如果你在彼此相邻地放置较旧的和更新的件，那么可能相当明显。由于当时周围的技术，在1899年，在1899年，这些调整不会在1899年开始。现在我们可以更精确地使用我们的打印，可以在多个印刷品上一致地创建较小的细节。

One of the most interesting additions to this typeface was the flat ampersand in the logo. Not something that was original to Engravers, this is now an instantly recognisable element of Lange branding. During his incredibly in-depth breakdown of A. Lange & Söhne’s typeface in collaboration with SJX, designer Larry Peh shows how this ampersand came from an amalgamation of different typefaces and fits perfectly with the brand and its curved logo.

这个字体上最有趣的补充之一是标志中的扁平＆符。不是雕刻的原创的东西，这现在是Lange品牌的瞬间可识别的元素。在他令人难以置信的深入崩溃期间，A. lange＆amp; Söhne的字体与SJX合作，Designer Larry Peh展示了这款安培如何来自不同字体的融合，并与品牌及其弯曲的徽标完美地配合。

The design of a bespoke typeface for the Slim d’Hermès line, courtesy of Hermès .

While the work of A. Lange & Söhne might have been a more subtle refresh, we can find a bolder direction from a rather unexpected source, Hermès. In 2015, the Parisian brand employed the skills of Philippe Apeloig – who has work displayed in the MoMA collection – to incorporate the spirit of their Slim d’Hermès into a new typeface for the collection. Notable for its disrupted lines and empty space, the bare minimum is used to convey each numeral. Each one is made up of just three different shapes: a straight line, a circle, and a right angle, all with sections missing. It could be argued that the most significant feature of this typeface is what’s been removed, rather than what’s been included.

虽然A. lange＆amp的工作; Söhne可能一直是一个更微妙的刷新，我们可以从一个相当意外的来源，Hermès找到更大胆的方向。 2015年，巴黎品牌采用Philippe Apeloig的技能 - 莫纳集合中展示的工作 - 将他们的苗条D'Hermès的精神纳入了集合的新字体。值得注意的是其中断的线条和空的空间，裸露的最小值用于传送每个数字。每个人都是只有三种不同的形状：一条直线，圆形和直角，都缺少部分。可以说这个字体最重要的特征是被删除的，而不是所包括的特征。

A rather unexpected instance of a brand using a completely different typeface for just one model is the Patek Philippe 5212A weekly calendar. Perhaps designed to reflect the singularity of the rarely seen complication, this reference was printed with a handwritten typeface that, when studied, almost looks shaky. While this could appear like a mistake at first, it was revealed that the typeface was in fact chosen by their design team to “recall an epoch in the not too distant past when notes were still written by hand in paper diaries.”

使用完全不同的字体仅为一个型号的品牌的一个相当意外的品牌实例是Patek Philippe 5212a每周日历。也许旨在反映很少见到的并发症的奇点，这个参考用手写的字体印刷，当学习时，几乎看起来很摇摇欲坠。虽然这可能是一个错误起初，但据透露，字体由他们的设计团队选择了“当纸上仍然用手写的时，在不太遥远的过去召回一个时代。”

From the above, you may have gathered that typography is quite a subtle area of design, where small details can have a rather significant impact, whether we’re aware of this or not. In order to finish off this article, we thought we would demonstrate this one last time by looking at the evolution in typography on a single model. Because many of the variables – such as the case, layout, and complication – remain the same, we’re able to see just how powerful type can be in affecting the overall personality a certain piece.

As such, we thought we’d look more closely at a reference that’s close to our hearts, the Patek Philippe 3940. The perpetual calendar marks an interesting period in the manufacture’s history, when their watches transitioned from vintage aesthetics to more contemporary ones. Four different series have been identified by collectors, from 1985 to 2007. Between the first and second series, the “Patek Philippe Genève” signature evolves from a smaller serif-typeface to a far wider sans serif one. The accent above the second “E” of “Genève” also disappears. This certainly helps with the legibility of the brand name, though this arguably happens at the expense of the classic aesthetic embodied in the first few pieces.

因此，我们认为我们将仔细阅读靠近我们心灵的参考，Patek Philippe 3940。这一百年日历标志着制造历史中有趣的时期，当他们的手表从复古美学转变为更同意的人时。来自1985年至2007年的收藏家已经确定了四个不同的系列。第一和第二系列之间，“普拉克翡丽生成”签名从较小的Serif-typeface演变为一个远更广泛的Sans Serif。 “Genève”的第二个“E”之上的口音也消失了。这肯定有助于品牌名称的易读性，尽管这可能会以前几块所体现的经典美学为代价。

As the reference evolves, this brand signature becomes gradually larger and more muted, whilst the subdials also begin to evolve to a more contemporary font. As a result, if you hold a 3940 from 1985 and one produced twenty years later, they are on the surface, almost identical. However, the subtle differences in typeface size and font mean that one feels distinctly like a vintage piece, whilst the other feels much more modern. Beyond the obvious element of rarity, this is why collectors have tended to gravitate towards the earlier executions of this perpetual calendar, as they speak to a certain Patek Philippe aesthetic which is long lost today.

A similarly powerful detail can be found in the evolution of the Audemars Piguet signature, which occurred during the last decade of the 20 th century. Prior to this, Audemars Piguet employed a classic, restrained font to apply their name to dials. All the letters were the same size, with a few subtle details imbuing these pieces with a certain vintage appeal – such as the flat top “A” or the serif details on the “G” or the “T”. However, early in the ‘90s, Audemars Piguet transitioned to a much more contemporary brand signature, which they still employ to this day. If you compare two identical pieces from the period – such as their Quantième Perpetuel or a Royal Oak – the effect can be rather powerful, such that one feels firmly grounded in a vintage aesthetic, whilst the other feels more like a modern piece. These details are subtle, but their impact is certainly significant.

在众所周知的仔猪签名的演变中可以找到类似强有力的细节，这在20世纪的最后十年内发生。在此之前，Audemars Pigueet聘请了一个经典的受限制的字体，以将其名称拨打拨打。所有字母的大小都是相同的大小，有一些微妙的细节，将这些碎片充满了某种复古吸引力 - 例如“g”或“t”上的平顶“a”或Serif细节。然而，在90年代的早期，Audemars Piguetod转变为更加现代的品牌签名，他们仍然雇用到这一天。如果你从这个时期比较两个相同的作品 - 例如他们的QuantièmePerpletuel或皇家橡树 - 这种效果可能相当强大，使得一个人感觉牢牢地接地在复古美学中，而另一个感觉更像是现代化的作品。这些细节是微妙的，但它们的影响肯定很重要。

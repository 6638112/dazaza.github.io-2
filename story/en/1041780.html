Learning to program at 57 years young
在57岁时学习编程

2020-12-25 23:14:03

Always loved tech but never learned to program. If I learned at this old age, is there any chance of getting hired if I learned?

一直热爱技术，但从未学会编程。如果我这么大的年纪，如果我学会了，是否有被录用的机会？

level 1 Honestly there's a depressing amount of age discrimination in the industry so it's not going to be easy. This does seem to be getting better though (slowly).

诚实地说，行业中年龄歧视的数量令人沮丧，因此这绝非易事。不过，这似乎确实有所改善（缓慢）。

My advice would be aim for the bigger, more traditional companies that don't think every developer has to be 12. Also capitalize on your life experience. Most younger people are good at writing code but don't always see the bigger picture. This is your advantage and you should use it.

我的建议是针对那些不认为每个开发人员都必须是12岁的更大，更传统的公司。还要利用您的生活经验。大多数年轻人擅长编写代码，但总不会看到更大的前景。这是您的优势，应该使用它。

level 2 I'd like to add that OP can leverage their age as experience in other fields. 20 years of experience in sales might come in handing building CRM software...

第2级我想补充一点，OP可以利用他们的年龄作为其他领域的经验。拥有20年销售经验的人员可能会构建CRM软件...

level 2 At 45 now - I started into programming 3 years ago. Age discrimination happens - but not at the level most claim. Does it exist? Yes. However there is WAY too much work out there to get done and bad developers as well for companies to turn away a solid candidate just cause he/she has gray hair.

2级现在已经45岁了-3年前我开始编程。发生年龄歧视-但不是大多数人声称的那样。是否存在？是。但是，有太多的工作要做，而且糟糕的开发人员也让公司拒绝可靠的候选人，只是因为他/她头发白发。

Sad to say as someone who is older - the issue is more old people interview badly cause they usually have sat somewhere for 10+ years - and the tech they know is dated.

说来可悲的人谁是旧的 - 问题是更老的人面试严重的原因，他们通常都坐在某处10年以上 - 他们知道技术是过时了。

If some 24 year old walks in fresh knowing React + .Net solidly with a good portfolio but no experience and some 50 year old has the same.... a hiring manager might start thinking for the same price point I can get the guy with just as good chops but also carrying 20+ years business knowledge and practical (non-coding) business sense.

如果某个24岁的年轻人对React + .Net有了一个全新的认识，并且拥有良好的投资组合，但没有经验，而50岁的年轻人具有相同的经验....招聘经理可能会开始考虑相同的价格点，那么我可以不仅具有出色的排骨，而且还具有20多年的业务知识和实用（非编码）业务意识。

I know which as a hiring manager I would go with if all were equal. I also know which my last 3 employers went with too.

我知道，如果所有人都平等的话，我会选择哪一位招聘经理。我也知道我的最后三位雇主也去了哪位。

level 2 Can someone explain why age matters in an industry were knowledge and skills should matter more?

级别2有人可以解释为什么年龄在一个行业中很重要，而知识和技能应该更重要吗？

level 2 To add to this as well, soft skills come into play as well. I'd imagine with your life experience you've honed your soft skills. Being able to communicate with other devs and non-technical people (in addition to being teachable) is almost more important than being super technical. In that vein, being able to articulate the problem you're trying to solve is very helpful as well, especially when it comes to googling something. No one is an encyclopedia

2级除此之外，软技能也要发挥作用。我想像着您的生活经历，您已经磨练了自己的软技能。能够与其他开发人员和非技术人员进行交流（除了可以教书）比超级技术更重要。从这个意义上讲，能够阐明您要解决的问题也非常有帮助，尤其是在谷歌搜索时。没有人是百科全书

level 2 Yes, companies would rather go through 20-somethings once every 6 months instead of hiring somebody older who would not quit that fast.

级别2是的，公司宁愿每6个月经历20件事，而不是雇用不会很快辞职的老员工。

level 2 Has something dramatically changed in recent years? The last time I saw a relevant statistic, only a small portion of 1% of the global population knew how to write a webpage or an app. Somewhere between 20-30m software developers out of a population of 7800m.

级别2近年来是否发生了重大变化？我上次看到相关统计信息时，全球人口中只有1％的一小部分知道如何编写网页或应用。在7800m的人口中，大约有20-30m的软件开发人员。

level 2 Would not discourage OP looking at small shops for the first job or so. Small, local businesses with small (and admittedly more rudimentary) teams can be more likely to take a chance on the person rather than the resume. You can still join the lifers at the Fortune 500 companies afterwards if that be your marmalade.

级别2不会阻止OP为第一份工作寻找小商店。小型，本地企业组成的团队规模较小（当然是更基础的）可能会比这个简历更有机会抓住这个人。如果那是您的果酱，您之后仍然可以加入《财富》 500强公司。

level 2 Larger companies might have stricter qualification/experience requirements meanwhile a startup hiring anyone off the street for $15 bucks might take anyone

级别2较大的公司可能具有更严格的资格/经验要求，而一家初创公司以15美元的价格在街上雇用任何人，可能会带走任何人

All you need to do is find a place that would be happy to have a much older person (social justice, diversity and so on) this might be hard to find in a small startup but you could do it. Maybe a female or minority owner. And hope they walk the walk when they talk the talk

您需要做的就是找到一个乐于拥有一个老年人的地方（社会公正，多元化等等），这在一家小型创业公司中可能很难找到，但是您可以做到。可能是女性或少数族裔所有者。希望他们说话时能走路

level 2 I agree there is a ton of age discrimination in this industry, in addition to sex/gender discrimination, but its really like many industries in that way. Like, companies aren't jumping at the chance of hiring 55 year old nurses with no experience when there is a pool of freshly educated 25 year olds willing to be paid less just to get their first gig.

2级我同意，除了性别歧视之外，这个行业还存在很多年龄歧视，但实际上与许多行业一样。例如，当有一批刚受过教育的25岁的年轻人愿意为获得第一份工作而得到的报酬较低时，公司就不会急于雇用没有经验的55岁的护士。

I still think op could get hired but the bar is certainly much higher. Still, if op is passionate about it, their skill and desire to obtain the position will speak for itself! I also agree with you that they should capitalize on their life experiences as that will probably be their biggest advantage

我仍然认为可以聘请op，但门槛肯定要高得多。尽管如此，如果op对此充满热情，那么他们获得职位的技能和渴望将说明一切！我也同意您的看法，他们应该利用自己的生活经验，因为这可能是他们最大的优势

level 2 I work in an old company that's fortune 100. I do programming and yeah there's certainly a lot of older guys here.

级别2：我在一家财富100强的老公司工作。我从事编程工作，是的，这里肯定有很多年纪较大的人。

I'd prefer OP who is learning some modem programming principles with life and work experience over the guys that learned programming in the 80s/90s and have been stagnant for a couple decades, but are supposed to advise us on hardware and software to buy.

我更喜欢OP，他们比那些在80年代/ 90年代学习编程并且停滞了几十年的家伙学习了一些具有生活和工作经验的现代编程原理，但是应该为我们提供有关硬件和软件方面的建议购买。

My team has done more with less because we spend proper time researching and using our resources. A lot of the more senior corporate guys succumb to sales pitches from sales reps, because they can't be bothered to research.

我的团队用更少的钱做更多的事情，因为我们花了适当的时间研究和使用我们的资源。许多高级公司的负责人都屈服于销售代表的销售建议，因为他们不愿意去研究。

Thankfully about once a quarter we field questions about how we're "so much farther than the rest of the division/corporation" it's all philosophy buddy.

值得庆幸的是，大约每季度一次，我们提出的问题是，我们与该部门/公司的其余部分相比有何优势？这是所有哲学伙伴。

Keep pushing on OP. I, as a 24 year old, would be happy to work with you. As long as you're open to new experiences and being challenged in your thought processes you'll do just fine.

继续推动OP。我今年24岁，很乐意与您合作。只要您愿意接受新的经历并在思维过程中受到挑战，您就可以做的很好。

level 1 I'm nearly 50. I went back to college got a degree graduated 2 years ago and now Im employed as a dev. I didnt encounter any ageism and I got calls for FAANG interviews. So yes it is doable but not easy.

1级，我快50岁了。我回到大学两年前获得了学位，现在我被聘为开发人员。我没有遇到任何年龄歧视，并且接到了FAANG面试的电话。是的，这是可行的，但并不容易。

level 1 Age might be an issue . . . except that last I heard there were millions of unfilled CS jobs in the US alone, so although it might be harder to get a job, you'll probably be hired if you keep applying.

1级年龄可能是一个问题。 。 。除了最后一次听说，仅在美国就有数百万个CS职位空缺，因此尽管找工作可能会比较困难，但如果您继续申请，很可能会被录用。

level 1 I'm in my 50's and released my first Android app a few years ago. I've no plans on working for someone but I have spoke to people at meetup.com who do the interviewing. They also announce jobs at these meetup's so the same people who will interview you are the ones you already know. I was also told that consulting work is achievable (I don't know how accurate this is.)

我50岁时达到1级水平，并于几年前发布了我的第一个Android应用程序。我没有为某人工作的计划，但我已经在metup.com与进行面试的人进行了交谈。他们还会在这些聚会上宣布职位，因此与您面试的人就是您已经认识的人。还告诉我，咨询工作是可以实现的（我不知道这样做有多准确。）

What harm is there in trying? Even if you don't get a job you have a skill that is both useful for yourself and to others plus you might earn a few extra dollars that may help when you retire.

尝试有什么害处？即使您没有找到一份工作，您所拥有的技能也对自己和他人都有用，此外，您可能还会赚到一些额外的钱，这些钱可能对您退休时有所帮助。

level 2 The harm in trying is that it takes a lot of time and effort, and not everyone will be able to find programming useful. There is a significant opportunity cost.

级别2尝试的弊端在于这需要花费大量时间和精力，并且并不是每个人都能发现有用的编程。机会成本很高。

level 2 I think this comment is spot on. Learning to program could still be a fun and rewarding experience for OP regardless of whether it actually leads to a job or not.

2级，我认为这条评论很准确。不管是否真正找到工作，学习编程对于OP仍然可能是一种有趣且有益的体验。

Also OP might be able to find some use for it at their current job to make things easier. For example if they have some kind of generic office job or something there's probably a lot of mundane stuff that could be automated using some basic python.

OP可能还可以在当前工作中找到一些用途，以使事情变得容易。 例如，如果他们有某种通用的办公室工作，或者可能有很多平凡的东西，可以使用一些基本的python将其自动化。

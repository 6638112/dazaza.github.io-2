BuzzFeed Strikes Deal To Buy HuffPost From Verizon Media
BuzzFeed罢工协议从Verizon Media购买HuffPost

2020-11-22 00:03:41

BuzzFeed is set to acquire HuffPost in a stock deal with Verizon Media, The Wall Street Journal's Benjamin Mullin and Keach Hagey first reported Thursday.

《华尔街日报》的本杰明·穆林和凯奇·哈吉（Keach Hagey）周四首次报道，BuzzFeed将通过与Verizon Media的股票交易收购HuffPost。

In addition to the all-stock deal, Verizon is investing cash in BuzzFeed, according to The Journal.

据《华尔街日报》报道，除了全股票交易，Verizon还向BuzzFeed投资现金。

As a result of the deal, the two newsrooms will be able to syndicate each other's content and team up for advertising deals.

达成交易的结果是，两个新闻编辑室将能够联合发布彼此的内容并合作进行广告交易。

Jonah Peretti, the BuzzFeed founder and CEO and a cofounder of The Huffington Post, will be in charge of the expanded media giant.

BuzzFeed的创始人兼首席执行官，《赫芬顿邮报》的联合创始人乔纳·佩雷蒂（Jonah Peretti）将负责扩大的媒体巨头。

The two newsrooms will continue to operate as separate entities, the companies said in a press release.

两家公司在新闻稿中说，这两个新闻编辑室将继续作为独立实体运营。

"Verizon Media's strategy has evolved over the past two years to focus on our core strengths — ads, commerce, content and subscriptions," Verizon Media CEO Guru Gowrappan said in the release. "We've created a powerhouse ecosystem, built on a trusted network, that delivers an end-to-end experience for consumers and advertisers. The partnership with BuzzFeed complements our roadmap while also accelerating our transformation and growth."

Verizon Media首席执行官Guru Gowrappan在新闻稿中说：“过去两年，Verizon Media的战略已经发展，以专注于我们的核心优势-广告，商业，内容和订阅。” “我们已经建立了一个建立在可信赖网络上的强大生态系统，该生态系统可以为消费者和广告商提供端到端的体验。与BuzzFeed的合作伙伴关系可以完善我们的路线图，同时也可以加快我们的转型和增长。”

Peretti said in the statement: "We're excited about our partnership with Verizon Media, and mutual benefits that will come from syndicating content across each other's properties, collaborating on innovative ad products and the future of commerce, and tapping into the strength and creativity of Verizon Media Immersive.

佩雷蒂在声明中说：“我们为与Verizon Media的合作伙伴关系而感到兴奋，并从彼此财产的联合组织，创新广告产品和商业的未来合作以及挖掘力量和创造力中获得了共同利益。 Verizon Media沉浸式。

"I have vivid memories of growing HuffPost into a major news outlet in its early years, but BuzzFeed is making this acquisition because we believe in the future of HuffPost and the potential it has to continue to define the media landscape for years to come," Peretti said. "With the addition of HuffPost, our media network will have more users, spending significantly more time with our content than any of our peers."

“我对将HuffPost成长为早期的主要新闻媒体充满了美好的回忆，但BuzzFeed之所以进行此项收购，是因为我们相信HuffPost的未来以及其在未来几年内必须继续定义媒体格局的潜力，”佩雷蒂说。 “随着HuffPost的加入，我们的媒体网络将拥有更多的用户，与我们的同行相比，我们在内容上花费的时间明显更多。”

Arianna Huffington, the HuffPost founder, said on Twitter: "So happy to see HuffPost and Buzzfeed coming together 15 years after Jonah Peretti started HuffPost with Kenny Lerer and me. Such exciting news and looking forward to all that's to come!"

HuffPost创始人Arianna Huffington在Twitter上说：“很高兴看到Jonah Peretti与Kenny Lerer和我一起创办HuffPost 15年后，HuffPost和Buzzfeed合并在一起。如此令人振奋的消息，并期待着一切！”

BuzzFeed will also help look for an editor in chief for HuffPost, which has been without one since Lydia Polgreen left for Gimlet Media in the spring.

BuzzFeed还将帮助寻找《 HuffPost》的主编，自从Lydia Polgreen在春季离开Gimlet Media以来，就一直没有这个主编。

The deal comes in a tumultuous period for media and especially venture-capital-backed digital media companies.

对于媒体，尤其是风险资本支持的数字媒体公司来说，这笔交易正处于动荡时期。

What was supposed to be a year of stabilization and profitability for these companies changed when the coronavirus pandemic struck. Once high-flying companies like BuzzFeed, Vice Media, and Group Nine variously cut pay, laid off staffers, and rewrote expectations as advertisers slashed marketing budgets as much as 50%.

当冠状病毒大流行袭来时，这些公司本来应该保持稳定和盈利的一年发生了变化。像BuzzFeed，Vice Media和Group Nine这样有发展前景的公司曾经不同程度地削减薪水，裁员，重新编写期望，因为广告商削减了多达50％的营销预算。

The period was a particularly tough test for BuzzFeed, a heavily funded digital media company that made an ambitious but costly bet on news, with a newsroom that reached 300 people.

这个时期对于BuzzFeed来说是一个特别艰难的考验，BuzzFeed是一家资金雄厚的数字媒体公司，对新闻进行了野心勃勃的赌注，但新闻编辑室的人数却达到300人。

Earlier, venture-backed media companies ran into trouble when the Facebook traffic they relied on was cut back and advertisers spent more of their budgets with Google and Facebook.

早些时候，由风险投资支持的媒体公司遇到了麻烦，因为他们所依赖的Facebook流量减少了，广告商将更多预算花在了Google和Facebook上。

The media outlets branched out to revenue streams like subscriptions, events, and e-commerce when advertising failed to bring in enough revenue to justify their hefty valuations, but those efforts were only partially successful.

当广告未能带来足够的收入来证明其庞大的估值是合理的时候，媒体分支机构就进入了订阅，事件和电子商务等收入来源，但是这些努力只是部分成功。

Media companies have already begun consolidating. In the latter half of 2019, Vice Media agreed to acquire Refinery29 for mostly stock. Group Nine acquired PopSugar, and Vox Media acquired New York Media.

媒体公司已经开始合并。在2019年下半年，Vice Media同意以大部分股票收购Refinery29。第九集团收购了PopSugar，Vox Media收购了New York Media。

"We're going through a period that's unprecedented; we don't know how it's going to end," Eric Hippeau, a partner at Lerer Hippeau Ventures, whose portfolio includes Axios, BuzzFeed, and Group Nine, said earlier this year.

Lerer Hippeau Ventures的合伙人埃里克·希佩奥（Eric Hippeau）今年早些时候表示：“我们正在经历前所未有的时期；我们不知道它的结局如何。”

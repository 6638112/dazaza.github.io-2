Vox Media, Complex Networks, and BDG are considering ditching Google's AMP for their own mobile-optimized articles; The Washington Post dropped AMP last summer
Vox Media、Complex Networks和BDG正在考虑放弃谷歌的AMP，推出自己的手机优化文章；《华盛顿邮报》去年夏天放弃了AMP

2022-02-25 09:28:41

Several large online publishers are taking steps to abandon a program from Alphabet GOOG 3.99% Google that was billed as a way to optimize readers’ mobile-browsing experience, saying it generates less advertising revenue.

几家大型在线出版商正在采取措施，放弃Alphabet GOOG 3.99%的谷歌（Google）推出的一个项目，该项目被宣传为优化读者移动浏览体验的一种方式，称其产生的广告收入较少。

Companies including Vox Media LLC, BuzzFeed BZFD -6.67% Complex Networks and Bustle parent BDG said they have started testing or are considering using their own versions of mobile-optimized article pages, instead of building them using the Accelerated Mobile Pages framework, which Google introduced in 2015 and is supported by an open-source working group. The Washington Post has gone a step further, abandoning AMP last summer.

包括Vox Media LLC、BuzzFeed BZFD（6.67%复杂网络）和Bustle母公司BDG在内的公司表示，他们已经开始测试或正在考虑使用自己版本的移动优化文章页面，而不是使用加速移动页面框架构建，这是谷歌在2015年推出的，并得到了一个开源工作组的支持。《华盛顿邮报》更进一步，在去年夏天放弃了AMP。

A potential exit from AMP would make media companies slightly less reliant on Google, whose dominance in digital advertising has strained its relationship with publishers and been referenced in a December 2020 lawsuit by state attorneys general alleging anticompetitive behavior.

退出AMP的可能性将使媒体公司对谷歌的依赖程度略有降低，谷歌在数字广告领域的主导地位已使其与出版商的关系紧张，并在2020年12月的一场指控反竞争行为的州检察长诉讼中被提及。

A recently unredacted version of the lawsuit alleged that AMP pages—which are hosted on Google’s servers—have been specifically designed to make it more difficult for ad space to be auctioned on platforms other than Google’s ad exchange. It also alleged that Google made ads that didn’t use AMP load with a one-second delay.

最近一个未经修改的诉讼版本声称，托管在谷歌服务器上的AMP页面是专门设计的，目的是让广告空间更难在谷歌广告交易所以外的平台上拍卖。它还声称，谷歌制作的广告没有使用AMP加载，只是延迟了一秒钟。

A Google spokesman at the time said the lawsuit’s claims about AMP were false and said its engineers designed the system to load webpages faster.

谷歌当时的一位发言人表示，诉讼中有关AMP的说法是错误的，并表示其工程师设计该系统是为了更快地加载网页。

Media executives have said dropping AMP would give them more control over their page designs and ad formats, and make it easier for them to sell ad space in auctions that include a greater number of ad marketplaces through a system known as header bidding, ultimately boosting competition and prices for their ad space.

媒体高管曾表示，放弃AMP将让他们对自己的页面设计和广告格式有更多的控制权，并使他们更容易通过标题竞价系统在包括更多广告市场的拍卖中出售广告空间，最终提高广告空间的竞争和价格。

Google said it is possible for publishers to sell ad space on AMP pages through header bidding, but the practice has its downsides, including using more data and causing webpages to load more slowly.

谷歌表示，出版商可以通过标题竞价出售AMP页面上的广告空间，但这种做法也有其缺点，包括使用更多数据，导致网页加载速度较慢。

“AMP continues to be a helpful way for sites—even those without large digital teams—to easily create great web experiences,” a Google spokeswoman said.

“AMP仍然是一种有用的方式，即使是那些没有大型数字团队的网站，也能轻松地创造伟大的网络体验，”谷歌发言人说。

Media executives and consultants said they expect non-AMP pages to garner at least 20% more advertising revenue than AMP pages in most cases, without affecting loading speeds.

媒体高管和顾问表示，他们预计在大多数情况下，非AMP页面的广告收入至少比AMP页面多20%，而不会影响加载速度。

“You can make your own webpages perform really well without making compromises Google requires to make AMP work,” said Tyler Love, chief technology officer at BDG, which owns brands such as Bustle, Gawker, Nylon and W.

BDG的首席技术官泰勒·洛夫（Tyler Love）说：“你可以让自己的网页表现得非常好，而不必做出谷歌要求的妥协，让AMP发挥作用。”BDG拥有Bustle、Gawker、尼龙和W等品牌。

Google said it offers its own alternative to header bidding, which it calls Open Bidding.

谷歌表示，它提供了自己的标题竞价替代方案，称之为公开竞价。

Some publishers said they started winding down their AMP efforts after Google last year tweaked its algorithm so that pages’ loading speeds, interactivity and visual stability became factored into its search rankings. The new standards were designed to improve users’ content experience and publishers’ search-result performance, Google had said, without having to create separate AMP pages. At the time, Google also said it would stop featuring only AMP content in its top-stories carousel.

一些出版商表示，在谷歌去年调整了算法，将页面的加载速度、交互性和视觉稳定性纳入搜索排名后，他们开始减少AMP的努力。谷歌曾表示，新标准旨在改善用户的内容体验和出版商的搜索结果性能，而无需创建单独的AMP页面。当时，谷歌还表示，它将停止在其顶级故事轮播中只播放AMP内容。

“If there’s equal opportunity to do AMP and non-AMP pages, we’ll focus on non-AMP pages,” said Christian Baesler, chief executive of Complex Networks and chief operating officer of BuzzFeed. Its publications include Complex and Sole Collector.

“如果有平等的机会做AMP和非AMP页面，我们将专注于非AMP页面，”复杂网络首席执行官兼BuzzFeed首席运营官克里斯蒂安·贝斯勒（Christian Baesler）说。其出版物包括复杂的和唯一的收藏家。

Washington Post director of product Dave Merrell said the news organization stopped publishing AMP versions of its articles last summer after Google rolled out its new technology standards. Two other major news publishers, New York Times and Dow Jones & Co.—the publisher of The Wall Street Journal and a unit of News Corp —declined to comment.

《华盛顿邮报》产品总监戴夫·梅雷尔（Dave Merrell）表示，在谷歌推出新的技术标准后，新闻机构于去年夏天停止发布AMP版本的文章。另外两家主要新闻出版商，纽约时报和道琼斯；《华尔街日报》出版商、新闻集团旗下的公司拒绝置评。

Ryan Pauley, the chief revenue officer of Vox Media—whose titles include The Verge, Vox and New York Magazine—said that beyond the increased ad revenue, he expects non-AMP pages to make it easier for paywalled websites to achieve subscription growth. Google said publishers can take steps to cap the number of free articles readers can see on AMP and non-AMP pages.

Vox Media的首席营收官瑞安·保利（Ryan Pauley）说，除了增加广告收入之外，他预计非AMP页面将使付费网站更容易实现订阅增长。该公司的标题包括《Verge》、《Vox》和《纽约杂志》。谷歌表示，出版商可以采取措施限制读者在AMP和非AMP页面上可以看到的免费文章数量。

The relationship between news publishers and Google has been fraught through the years—and goes beyond digital advertising. Executives at major news organizations, including News Corp, have long criticized Google for using news content in its products without paying the publishers that provide it. Google in 2020 said it would pay publishers a total of more than $1 billion for three years to license news content. News Corp is among the publishers that have reached content-licensing deals with Google.

多年来，新闻出版商和谷歌之间的关系一直令人担忧，而且超出了数字广告的范畴。包括新闻集团（news Corp）在内的主要新闻机构的高管长期以来一直批评谷歌在其产品中使用新闻内容，而没有向提供新闻内容的出版商付费。谷歌在2020年表示，它将在三年内向出版商支付总额超过10亿美元的费用，以授权新闻内容。新闻集团是与谷歌达成内容许可协议的出版商之一。

When Google introduced AMP in 2015, the company said it would create a better experience for digital publishers and readers. Media companies signed on, creating special article pages that were meant to load more quickly on mobile phones, eliminate some pesky ads and make it easier to find content. Google said at the time the program would make the “World Wide Web great again.”

谷歌在2015年推出AMP时表示，它将为数字出版商和读者创造更好的体验。媒体公司签约，创建了专门的文章页面，旨在更快地在手机上加载，消除一些讨厌的广告，并使内容更容易查找。谷歌当时表示，该项目将使“万维网再次伟大”

Some publishers say they are wary that moving away from AMP could hurt their traffic.

一些出版商表示，他们担心离开AMP可能会影响他们的流量。

“The only thing I worry about is missed opportunity costs we’re not aware of,” BDG’s Mr. Love said. For instance, it is unclear whether having AMP would harm organic search in the long run, or whether a non-AMP page would have the same chance of appearing in a special Google section like the Discover feature for Android phones, he said.

“我唯一担心的是我们没有意识到的错失机会成本，”BDG的洛夫说。例如，他说，目前尚不清楚从长远来看，使用AMP是否会损害有机搜索，或者非AMP页面是否有同样的机会出现在谷歌的一个特殊部分，比如Android手机的发现功能。

Google declined to comment on the specific concerns and said AMP has never been a ranking signal in search results.

谷歌拒绝就具体问题置评，并表示AMP从来都不是搜索结果中的排名信号。

Teardown of “Dishy McFlatface,” the SpaceX Starlink user terminal

Engineer Ken Keiter recently came into possession of one SpaceX Starlink user terminal, the satellite dish that SpaceX nicknamed "Dishy McFlatface." But instead of plugging it in and getting Internet access from SpaceX's low Earth orbit (LEO) satellites, Keiter decided to take Dishy apart to see what's inside.

工程师肯·基特（Ken Keiter）最近拥有一个SpaceX Starlink用户终端，这是SpaceX绰号为“ Dishy McFlatface”的卫星天线。但是，基特没有将其插入并从SpaceX的低地球轨道（LEO）卫星获得Internet访问，而是决定将Dishy拆开以查看里面的东西。

The teardown process destroyed portions of the device. "I would love to actually test out the [Starlink] service and clearly I didn't get a chance to, as this went a little bit further than I was intending," Keiter said toward the end of the 55-minute teardown video he posted on YouTube last week.

Keiter, who lives in Portland, Oregon, was impressed by the Starlink team's work. "It's rare to see something of this complexity in a consumer product," he said in reference to the device's printed circuit board (PCB), which he measured at 19.75" by 21.5".

住在俄勒冈州波特兰市的Keiter对Starlink团队的工作印象深刻。很难在消费产品中看到这种复杂性的东西，他提到了该设备的印刷电路板（PCB），他的测量值为19.75; 21.5＆＃34;。

Keiter was intrigued by the Ethernet cable. "A lot of people have been asking why you can't replace the cable on your own, 'why can't I just have a jack that I plug my own cable into in the back of Dishy?' Well, there's a really interesting reason for this and it has to do with power delivery," Keiter said.

凯特（Keiter）对以太网电缆很感兴趣。很多人一直在问为什么您不能自己更换电缆，为什么我不能只有一个插孔将我自己的电缆插入背面迪西的？嗯，这有一个非常有趣的原因，它与功率传输有关，基特说。

Power over Ethernet is usually limited to about 30 watts, but new standards allow for greater power delivery that can meet Dishy's need for about 100 watts, Keiter said. Dishy uses a thick, "well-shielded" Ethernet cable that can deliver data and the required power without over-heating, he said.

Keiter说，以太网供电通常限制在30瓦左右，但是新标准允许更大的功率输出，可以满足Dishy大约100瓦的需求。 Dishy使用了厚实的＆屏蔽良好的＆＃34;他说，以太网电缆可以传输数据和所需的功率而不会过热。

Inside the satellite dish, Keiter found Dishy's PCB and phased-array antenna assembly, all in one large disc protected by a metal shield. "This is the entire brains of Dishy and look how thin it is. it's insane," he said. He also noted that "there are really good RF reasons for having a massive shield over the whole back of it." As Hackaday wrote in an article on Keiter's teardown, it "appears that the antenna is a self-contained computer of sorts, complete with ARM processor and RAM to run the software that aims the phased array. Speaking of which, it should come as no surprise to find that not only are the ICs that drive the dizzying array of antenna elements the most numerous components on the PCB, but that they appear to be some kind of custom silicon designed specifically for SpaceX."

正如Hackaday在有关Keiter拆解的文章中所写的那样，它看起来是天线是一种自包含的计算机，配有ARM处理器和RAM来运行针对相控阵的软件。说到这，毫不奇怪地发现，驱动天线元件令人眼花array乱的IC不仅是PCB上数量最多的组件，而且似乎是专门为SpaceX设计的某种定制硅。 ＆＃34;

Generally speaking, Dishy obviously is not user-repairable. But Keiter said he can imagine some people putting the PCB and antenna array into a different enclosure. "You could design, realistically, your own base for Dishy pretty easily and I'm guessing that is in fact what some people may choose to do," he said.

一般来说，Dishy显然不是用户可修复的。但是Keiter说，他可以想象有人将PCB和天线阵列放在不同的外壳中。实际上，您可以轻松地为Dishy设计自己的基地，而我猜想这实际上是某些人可能会选择这样做的地方，＆＃34;他说。

With a Starlink beta having recently begun, SpaceX is seeking US permission to deploy up to 5 million user terminals and is already authorized to deploy 1 million. SpaceX is charging $499 for the user terminal and $99 a month for broadband service.

随着Starlink Beta的启动，SpaceX正在寻求美国的许可，以部署多达500万个用户终端，并且已经被授权部署100万个用户终端。 SpaceX向用户终端收取499美元的费用，为宽带服务每月收取99美元的费用。

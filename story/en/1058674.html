Striking Telecom Workers Are Building a Community-Owned ISP

For more than four years, New York City area unionized employees of Charter Communications have been on strike, waiting for the company to budge in contract negotiations. In their free time, they’ve been building a worker-owned ISP of their own, with a focus on expanding affordable access during the Covid-19 pandemic.

四年以上，纽约市区联合雇员宪章沟通已罢工，等待公司达成协议谈判。在他们的空闲时间，他们一直在建立自己的工人拥有的ISP，重点是在Covid-19大流行期间扩大经济访问。

Dubbed People’s Choice Communications, the ISP is part of a growing trend of local communities building their own broadband networks. Frustrated by slow speeds, high prices, and spotty coverage, more than 750 towns and cities have built some type of locally-owned and operated broadband network, a trend that’s accelerating during the pandemic.

被称为人们的选择通信，ISP是建立自己的宽带网络的当地社区日益增长的趋势的一部分。令人沮丧的速度缓慢，高价格和差异覆盖，超过750个城镇建立了某种类型的本地拥有和经营的宽带网络，这是在大流行期间加速的趋势。

“After we build out a network in your building, it transfers to cooperative ownership, so profits are returned to users,” the organization’s website states. “We are able to provide high-speed service in most cases for $10 to $20 a month. No more cable company ripping you off, and as an owner, you have a vote in policies like data privacy.” People’s Choice is also a bit unique given it’s staffed entirely by striking IBEW Local #3 members.

“在我们建立建筑物中建立网络后，它会转移到合作所有权，因此利润返回给用户，”组织的网站国家。 “我们能够在大多数情况下提供高速服务，每月10％到20美元。没有更多的有线电视公司扯掉你，作为所有者，您可以在数据隐私等政策中投票。“鉴于它的人员完全是通过引人注目的是，人们的选择也有点独一无二。

“For over 40 years we built New York City's cable infrastructure,” project lead Troy Walcott told Motherboard. “Then Spectrum came in and decided we should no longer have jobs because we are in a union and customers should pay more for less service because they have no other choice.”

“超过40年，我们建造了纽约市＆＃39; S电缆基础设施，”项目领先Troy Walcott告诉主板。 “那么频谱进来并决定我们不再拥有就业机会，因为我们在联盟，客户应该为较少的服务支付更多，因为他们没有其他选择。”

The operation, which utilizes both fixed line and wireless solutions, has already delivered broadband to several schools and housing complexes in both The Bronx and Manhattan. The venture follows on the heels of similar local efforts like NYC Mesh geared toward expanding the city’s access to affordable broadband during the pandemic. The Charter standoff is now the longest ongoing strike in U.S. history. The problem began shortly after Charter (operating under the brand name Spectrum) acquired Time Warner Cable in 2016. After the acquisition, Walcott says the company made it abundantly clear it wasn’t flexible on issues like pensions and healthcare. “That was evident when one of their chief negotiators promised on his mother’s grave at the negotiating table that we would never get back our retirement and medical benefits,” Walcott said. As a result, the company’s 1,800 unionized workers began their strike on March 23, 2017, and have been on strike ever since. Not only do union officials say the company has been unwilling to meaningfully negotiate, it replaced the majority of the workers with subcontracted labor, then actively attempted to decertify the union in 2019. Walcott told me that the company broke off all communications with the workers “a long time ago.”

利用固定线路和无线解决方案的操作已经向布朗克斯和曼哈顿提供了几所学校和住宅区的宽带。该冒险在纽约网格中遵循类似当地努力的鞋跟，因为纽约网眼在大流行期间扩大城市的可负担得起的宽带。宪章立场现在是美国历史上最长的罢工。该问题开始于宪章后不久（在品牌名称频谱下运营）于2016年获取时代华纳电缆。收购后，沃尔科特表示，该公司在养老金和医疗等问题上发出了丰富的问题​​。沃尔科特说：“当他们在母亲的坟墓上承诺的谈判表中承诺的一名谈判表中，这是明显的，我们永远不会恢复我们的退休和医疗福利。”因此，该公司的1,800名工人于2017年3月23日开始罢工，从那时起就开始罢工。工会官员不仅有意义地谈判，它取代了大多数工人与分包劳动力，然后在2019年积极地试图定制联盟。沃尔科特告诉我，该公司突破了与工人的所有沟通“很久以前。”

“As they see it they have no reason to communicate or reach out,” he said. “They own the city.  No elected official, state or city entity is going to make them do anything. They control the largest part of the city's communication, they even own New York's news. So as they see it, why would they ever reach out to speak to the people they are looking to destroy?”

“因为他们看到它，他们没有理由沟通或伸出援手，”他说。 “他们拥有这个城市。没有民选官员，州或城市实体将让他们做任何事。他们控制着城市的最大部分＆＃39;他们甚至拥有纽约和＃39的新闻。所以当他们看到它时，为什么他们曾经伸出过与他们想要毁灭的人交谈？“

Union workers weren’t the only ones frustrated by Charter’s behavior in the wake of the merger.

Union Worker在合并之后，宪章行为的行为并不是唯一沮丧的人。

In 2018, The New York Public Service Commission threatened to kick Charter out of New York State for what regulators said were years of broken promises. The PSC accused Charter of not only failing to meet a merger condition requiring it to deploy broadband to an additional 145,000 homes in the state, but for actively misleading regulators. The two sides settled in 2019. Charter was also sued by the New York Attorney General for advertising broadband speeds the company couldn’t provide, has been repeatedly sued for socking subscribers with predatory fees, and generally sees some of the lowest customer satisfaction ratings in America. Last week, the company was fined $19 million for making misleading claims about its competitors.

2018年，纽约公共服务委员会威胁到纽约州的宪章，为哪些监管机构表示是多年的破产。 PSC指责的章程不仅无法满足要求它将宽带部署到国家的额外145,000个房屋的合并条件，而且用于积极误导监管机构。双方于2019年结算。宪章也由纽约授权队的纽约律师队加起来，该公司无法提供，这一直反复起诉具有掠夺性费用的袜子用户，并且一般看到一些最低的客户满意度评级美国。上周，该公司被罚款1900万美元，以赋予其竞争对手的误导性索赔。

According to Walcott, the creation of the new ISP was not only driven by Charter’s apathy to both its union employees and subscribers, but by the estimated 1.5 million New Yorkers who lack access to a fixed or wireless broadband connection during Covid. “Watching every elected official constantly talk about finding a way to fix a problem while ignoring the solution we were already giving them motivated us,” he said. “Having elected officials thank us quietly for our sacrifice but not say anything about our strike publicly motivated us. Seeing customers denied service during COVID because they had outstanding bills motivated us.”

根据沃尔科特的说法，新ISP的创建不仅由宪章对其联盟员工和订阅者的冷漠驱动，而且由估计有150万纽约人缺乏在Covid期间获得固定或无线宽带连接的纽约人。 “观看每个选举的官员不断谈论找到一种解决问题的方法，同时忽略了我们已经给予我们的解决方案，”他说。 “说完民选官员感谢我们悄悄地为我们的牺牲，但不能说我们的罢工任何公开激励我们。在Covid期间，看到客户否认服务，因为他们的优秀账单有动力。“

Last year, New York City unveiled a $157 million proposal to try and shore up broadband access in the city. Walcott said the organization reached out to New York City officials to see if they’d be willing to coordinate on the project, but never heard back.

去年，纽约市公布了1.57亿美元的建议，试图在城市宽带宽带接入。沃尔科特表示，该组织向纽约市官员伸出援手，看看他们是否愿意在项目上协调，但从未听过。

“The city talked about support for us but never actually put action to it,” Walcott said. “We put plans forward for municipal ownership of the system which officials in the city claimed to support, and would get back to us—but never did. So we did it on our own. Our biggest struggle was doing all of this with less than nothing.“

“这座城市谈到了对我们的支持，但实际上从未对此进行行动，”沃尔科特说。 “我们为该制度的市政所有权制定了该市的官员声称支持的官员，并将回到美国 - 但从未如此。所以我们自己这样做了。我们最大的斗争正在尽一切不到任何东西。“

More than 100M Americans have diabetes or prediabetes (2017)
超过1亿美国人患有糖尿病或前驱糖尿病（2017）

2021-01-28 22:11:16

More than 100 million U.S. adults are now living with diabetes or prediabetes, according to a new report pdf icon released today by the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC). The report finds that as of 2015, 30.3 million Americans – 9.4 percent of the U.S. population –have diabetes. Another 84.1 million have prediabetes, a condition that if not treated often leads to type 2 diabetes within five years.

根据疾病控制与预防中心（CDC）今天发布的新报告pdf图标，目前有1亿多美国成年人患有糖尿病或前驱糖尿病。该报告发现，截至2015年，有3030万美国人（占美国人口的9.4％）患有糖尿病。另有8410万人患有糖尿病前期，如果不及时治疗，通常会在五年内导致2型糖尿病。

The report confirms that the rate of new diabetes diagnoses remains steady. However, the disease continues to represent a growing health problem: Diabetes was the seventh leading cause of death in the U.S. in 2015. The report also includes county-level data for the first time, and shows that some areas of the country bear a heavier diabetes burden than others.

该报告证实，新的糖尿病诊断率保持稳定。但是，该疾病仍然代表着日益严重的健康问题：2015年，糖尿病是美国第七大主要死因。该报告还首次包括县级数据，表明该国某些地区的病情较重糖尿病负担比别人大。

“Although these findings reveal some progress in diabetes management and prevention, there are still too many Americans with diabetes and prediabetes,” said CDC Director Brenda Fitzgerald, M.D. “More than a third of U.S. adults have prediabetes, and the majority don’t know it. Now, more than ever, we must step up our efforts to reduce the burden of this serious disease.”

CDC主任布伦达·菲茨杰拉德（Brenda Fitzgerald）医师表示：“尽管这些发现揭示了糖尿病管理和预防方面的一些进步，但仍有太多的美国人患有糖尿病和前驱糖尿病”，“美国超过三分之一的成年人患有前驱糖尿病，而大多数人不知道它。现在，我们必须比以往任何时候都更加努力地减轻这种严重疾病的负担。”

Diabetes is a serious disease that can often be managed through physical activity, diet, and the appropriate use of insulin and other medications to control blood sugar levels. People with diabetes are at increased risk of serious health complications including premature death, vision loss, heart disease, stroke, kidney failure, and amputation of toes, feet, or legs.

糖尿病是一种严重的疾病，通常可以通过体育锻炼，饮食以及适当使用胰岛素和其他药物来控制血糖水平来控制。糖尿病患者面临严重健康并发症的风险增加，包括早逝，视力丧失，心脏病，中风，肾衰竭以及脚趾，脚或腿的截肢。

The National Diabetes Statistics Report, released approximately every two years, provides information on diabetes prevalence and incidence, prediabetes, risk factors for complications, acute and long-term complications, mortality, and costs in the U.S.

大约每两年发布一次的《国家糖尿病统计报告》提供了有关美国糖尿病患病率和发病率，糖尿病前期，并发症的危险因素，急性和长期并发症，死亡率以及费用的信息。

In 2015, an estimated 1.5 million new cases of diabetes were diagnosed among people ages 18 and older.

2015年，在18岁及以上的人群中，估计诊断出150万新的糖尿病病例。

Nearly 1 in 4 four adults living with diabetes – 7.2 million Americans – didn’t know they had the condition. Only 11.6 percent of adults with prediabetes knew they had it.

大约有四分之四的成年人患有糖尿病-720万美国人-不知道自己患有这种疾病。患有前驱糖尿病的成年人中只有11.6％知道自己患有糖尿病。

Rates of diagnosed diabetes increased with age. Among adults ages 18-44, 4 percent had diabetes. Among those ages 45-64 years, 17 percent had diabetes. And among those ages 65 years and older, 25 percent had diabetes.

糖尿病的诊断率随年龄增长而增加。在18-44岁的成年人中，有4％患有糖尿病。在45-64岁之间，有17％患有糖尿病。在65岁以上的人群中，有25％患有糖尿病。

Rates of diagnosed diabetes were higher among American Indians/Alaska Natives (15.1 percent), non-Hispanic blacks (12.7 percent), and Hispanics (12.1 percent), compared to Asians (8.0 percent) and non-Hispanic whites (7.4 percent).

在美洲印第安人/阿拉斯加原住民中（15.1％），非西班牙裔黑人（12.7％）和西班牙裔（12.1％），糖尿病的诊断率高于亚洲人（8.0％）和非西班牙裔白人（7.4％）。

Diabetes prevalence varied significantly by education. Among U.S. adults with less than a high school education, 12.6 percent had diabetes.   Among those with a high school education, 9.5 percent had diabetes; and among those with more than a high school education, 7.2 percent had diabetes.

糖尿病的患病率受教育程度差异很大。在美国高中以下文化程度的成年人中，有12.6％患有糖尿病。在接受过高中教育的人群中，有9.5％患有糖尿病。在高中以上文化程度的人群中，有7.2％患有糖尿病。

More men (36.6 percent) had prediabetes than women (29.3 percent). Rates were similar among women and men across racial/ethnic groups or educational levels.

患有前驱糖尿病的男性（36.6％）多于女性（29.3％）。在种族/族裔群体或教育水平上，男女之间的比率相似。

The southern and Appalachian areas of the United States had the highest rates of diagnosed diabetes and of new diabetes cases.

美国的南部和阿巴拉契亚地区诊断出的糖尿病和新发糖尿病的比率最高。

“Consistent with previous trends, our research shows that diabetes cases are still increasing, although not as quickly as in previous years,” said Ann Albright, Ph.D., R.D., director of CDC’s Division of Diabetes Translation. “Diabetes is a contributing factor to so many other serious health conditions. By addressing diabetes, we limit other health problems such as heart disease, stroke, nerve and kidney diseases, and vision loss.”

CDC糖尿病翻译部主任Ann Albright博士说：“与以前的趋势一致，我们的研究表明，糖尿病病例仍在增加，尽管没有以前几年那么快。”糖尿病是许多其他严重健康状况的成因。通过解决糖尿病，我们可以限制其他健康问题，例如心脏病，中风，神经和肾脏疾病以及视力丧失。”

To reduce the impact of prediabetes and type 2 diabetes, CDC established the National Diabetes Prevention Program (National DPP) external icon , which provides the framework for type 2 diabetes prevention efforts in the U.S.

为了减少前驱糖尿病和2型糖尿病的影响，疾病预防控制中心建立了国家糖尿病预防计划（National DPP）外部图标，该图标为美国2型糖尿病预防工作提供了框架。

Based on the landmark Diabetes Prevention Program research findings funded by the National Institutes of Health (NIH), the National DPP includes an evidence-based, year-long, behavior change program to improve eating habits and increase physical activity to lose a modest amount of weight and significantly reduce the risk of type 2 diabetes.

根据美国国立卫生研究院（NIH）资助的具有里程碑意义的糖尿病预防计划研究结果，国家DPP包括一项循证，为期一年的行为改变计划，以改善饮食习惯和增加体育锻炼，以减少适量的体重并显着降低2型糖尿病的风险。

To raise awareness and help people with prediabetes know where they stand and how to prevent type 2 diabetes, CDC, the American Diabetes Association (ADA), and the American Medical Association (AMA), partnered with the Ad Council to launch the first national public service advertising (PSA) campaign about prediabetes. These humorous PSAs in English and Spanish encourage people to take a short online test at DoIHavePrediabetes.org external icon to learn their risk.

CDC，美国糖尿病协会（ADA）和美国医学协会（AMA）与广告委员会合作，发起了首次全国性的公众宣传活动，以提高认识并帮助患有糖尿病的人了解他们的病情以及如何预防2型糖尿病。有关糖尿病前期的服务广告（PSA）运动。这些英语和西班牙语幽默的PSA鼓励人们在DoIHavePrediabetes.org外部图标上进行简短的在线测试，以了解他们的风险。

To prevent serious and costly diabetes complications, states are working with CDC to improve access to diabetes self-management education (DSME), with an emphasis on DSME programs that meet national quality standards. Additionally, CDC and NIH’s Natural Experiments for Translation in Diabetes (NEXT-D) study evaluates existing data to determine whether diabetes prevention and control interventions and policies are effective under real-life conditions.

为了预防严重且代价高昂的糖尿病并发症，各州正在与疾病预防控制中心合作，以改善获得糖尿病自我管理教育（DSME）的机会，重点是符合国家质量标准的DSME计划。此外，CDC和NIH的《糖尿病自然翻译实验》（NEXT-D）研究评估了现有数据，以确定糖尿病的预防和控制干预措施和政策在现实生活中是否有效。

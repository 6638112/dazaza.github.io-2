Spotify Is Launching Podcast Subscriptions, and Unlike Apple Won't Take a Cut From Creators

希望成为行业的播客第一播客的分销商 - 并且愿意放弃一些收入，以抵消进入播客订阅。

Next week, Spotify will launch its podcast subscription option for partners. But the company will be letting content creators keep 100% of the subscription fees: Spotify will not take a cut of podcast subscription revenue, sources confirmed, as first reported by the Wall Street Journal. By contrast, Apple will keep up to 30% of podcast subscription fees under its program, which is launching next month.

下周，Spotify将为合作伙伴推出其播客订阅选项。但公司将允许内容创作者保留100％的订阅费：Spotify不会削减播客订阅收入，来源确认，如华尔街日报首次报道。相比之下，苹果将在下个月推出的计划下持续高达30％的播客订购费。

Currently, Spotify doesn’t allow customers to pay for subscriptions through Apple in-app purchases — and Spotify has been a very vocal critic of Apple’s App Store policies, which has included lodging a formal complaint with the European Union alleging anticompetitive behavior. Similarly, you won’t be able to purchase Spotify podcast subscriptions through Apple.

目前，Spotify不允许客户通过Apple In-App购买支付订阅 - 并且Spotify一直是Apple的App Store政策的一个非常乐队批评，其中包括为欧洲联盟提出正式投诉，导致欧洲联盟可能导致反竞争行为。同样，您将无法通过Apple购买Spotify Podcast订阅。

Spotify has said it was going to test paid subscriptions for podcasters to allow them to offer exclusive content to subscribers, officially announcing that during its February “Stream On” event. But it has not yet provided details on how that will work.

Spotify表示将要测试Podcyers的付费订阅，以便他们为订阅者提供专属内容，正式宣布在其2月份的“赛事中”。但它尚未提供有关如何工作的详细信息。

Meanwhile, in conjunction with Spotify’s podcast subscription rollout, Facebook plans to launch an integrated version of Spotify’s audio player — a project called “Project Boombox” — starting next week to provide access to songs and podcasts directly within the Facebook app. That’s part of a broader embrace of social audio and audio-messaging features that Facebook announced Monday.

同时，与Spotify的Podcast订阅卷展栏一起，Facebook计划推出Spotify的Audio Player的集成版本 - 一个名为“项目博博”的项目 - 下周开始，以便在Facebook应用程序中直接提供对歌曲和播客的访问。这是Facebook星期一宣布的更广泛拥有的社交音频和音频消息传递功能的一部分。

With Apple Podcasts Subscriptions, as is the case under existing App Store policies, the tech giant will keep 30% of podcast-subscription fees in the first year and 15% in subsequent years. On top of that, creators must pay $19.99 per year to gain access to the Apple Podcasters Program, which includes tools required to offer premium subscriptions on Apple Podcasts. Pricing is set by creators (and billed monthly by default). Podcasters also can select which perks included with each plan — for example, ad-free listening, exclusive bonus content, and early or exclusive access to new series.

与Apple Podcasts订阅有关，就在现有的应用程序商店政策下，Tech Giant将在第一年保留30％的播客 - 订阅费，随后几年将是15％。最重要的是，创作者每年必须支付19.99美元即可进入Apple Podcasters计划，其中包括在Apple Podcasts上提供优质订阅所需的工具。定价由创作者设置（默认情况下并每月收费）。 Podcaster也可以选择每个计划中包含的津贴 - 例如，无广告聆听，独家奖金内容以及对新系列的早期或独家访问。

Why is Spotify going to let podcasters keep all the coin from premium subscriptions on the platform? At a high level, Spotify wants to make it as attractive as possible for creators to make Spotify their preferred platform — and to dissuade them from participating in Apple Podcasts Subscriptions.

为什么Spotify将让Podcasters从平台上的高级订阅中保留所有硬币？在高级别，Spotify希望使其尽可能有吸引力，以便创造者讨论他们的首选平台 - 并劝阻它们参加Apple Podcasts订阅。

Spotify’s bet is that the move will keep its podcast flywheel spinning by attracting more total listeners (which it monetizes through ads and Spotify Premium paid plans). Also unlike Apple, Spotify is spending big bucks for exclusive podcast programming, ranging from Joe Rogan to Prince Harry and Meghan Markle. Spotify can sweeten the pot for those partners by letting them keep all the money from any premium subscriptions they offer on the platform.

Spotify的赌注是，通过吸引更多的倾听者（通过广告和Spotiumed Premium Provans提供货币化），这一举措将保持其播客飞轮旋转。同样与Apple不同，Spotify正在为独家播客编程支出大雄鹿，从Joe Rogan到哈里王子和Meghan Markle。 Spotify可以通过让他们将所有资金从平台上提供的任何高级订阅中保留所有资金来甜蜜的锅。

According to Spotify, as of the fourth quarter of 2020, it had 2.2 million podcasts on the platform (up from 1.9 million podcasts the prior quarter and up more than threefold year over year). Despite some skepticism on Wall Street that the podcast investment is paying off, Spotify said it’s . “We now feel reasonably confident that we can prove out that causality of having podcast and the benefit it is having on user growth and retention, and then having podcast is a positive contributor to [long-term value] per subscriber,” CFO Paul Vogel said.

根据Spotify，截至2020年第四季度，它在平台上有220万播客（高于上一季度的190万播客，超过年度超过三年）。尽管播客投资正在支付播客投资，但Spotify仍然存在一些怀疑。 “我们现在觉得合理地确信我们可以证明具有播客的因果关系以及对用户增长和保留的福利，然后播客是一个每个订户的[长期价值]的积极贡献者，”CFO Paul Vogel说。

In reporting Q4 results, Spotify said 25% of its total user base engaged with podcast content in the quarter (up from 22% in Q3 and 16% in the fourth quarter of 2019). In addition, podcast consumption hours in Q4 nearly doubled year-over-year, according to the audio streamer.

在报告Q4结果时，Spotify在本季度与播客含量的总用户群（2019年第四季度为22％和16％的季度汇播内容的25％。此外，根据音频流媒体，Q4的播客时间在Q4中几乎翻了一番。

Spotify kicked off its podcast-growth initiative in early 2019, buying podcast studios  Gimlet Media and  Parcast along with podcast self-publishing platform Anchor — paying nearly $400 million for them, with additional potential payouts over four years. Last year Spotify bought  Bill Simmons’ The Ringer podcast and media startup in a deal worth up to nearly $200 million and spent  $235 million in cash to buy podcast publishing and ad company Megaphone.

不使用VPN服务

2020-10-23 18:20:46

No, seriously, don't. You're probably reading this because you've asked what VPN service to use, and this is the answer.

不，说真的，不要这样。你读这篇文章可能是因为你问过要使用什么VPN服务，而这就是答案。

Note: The content in this post does not apply to using VPN for their intended purpose; that is, as a virtual private (internal) network. It only applies to using it as a glorified proxy, which is what every third-party "VPN provider" does.

注：此帖子中的内容不适用于将VPN用于其预期目的；即，作为虚拟专用(内部)网络。它只适用于将其用作美化的代理，这是每个第三方VPN提供商都会做的。

There's also this article about VPN services, which is honestly better written (and has more cat pictures!) than my article.

还有这篇关于VPN服务的文章，说实话写得更好(而且有更多的猫咪图片！)。而不是我的文章。

Because a VPN in this sense is just a glorified proxy. The VPN provider can see all your traffic, and do with it what they want - including logging.

因为在这个意义上的VPN只是一个美化的代理。VPN提供商可以查看您的所有流量，并随心所欲地对其进行处理-包括日志记录。

There is no way for you to verify that, and of course this is what a malicious VPN provider would claim as well. In short: the only safe assumption is that every VPN provider logs.

您无法验证这一点，当然这也是恶意VPN提供商会声称的。简而言之：唯一安全的假设是每个VPN提供商都记录日志。

And remember that it is in a VPN provider's best interest to log their users - it lets them deflect blame to the customer, if they ever were to get into legal trouble. The $10/month that you're paying for your VPN service doesn't even pay for the lawyer's coffee, so expect them to hand you over.

请记住，VPN提供商登录他们的用户符合他们的最大利益--如果他们陷入法律麻烦，这可以让他们将责任转移到客户身上。你每月为VPN服务支付的10美元甚至连律师的咖啡费用都没有，所以期待他们会把钱交给你。

I'll believe that when HideMyAss goes out of business. They gave up their users years ago, and this was widely publicized. The reality is that most of their customers will either not care or not even be aware of it.

当HideMyAss倒闭时，我会相信这一点。他们在几年前就放弃了用户，这一点得到了广泛的宣传。现实情况是，他们的大多数客户要么不在乎，要么甚至没有意识到这一点。

Doesn't matter. You're still connecting to their service from your own IP, and they can log that.

这无关紧要。您仍在从您自己的IP连接到他们的服务，他们可以记录这一点。

VPNs don't provide privacy, with a few exceptions (detailed below). They are just a proxy. If somebody wants to tap your connection, they can still do so - they just have to do so at a different point (ie. when your traffic leaves the VPN server).

VPN不提供隐私，但有几个例外(详细说明如下)。他们只是一个代理人。如果有人想要利用你的连接，他们仍然可以这样做-他们只需要在不同的点上这样做(即。当您的流量离开VPN服务器时)。

Use SSL/TLS and HTTPS (for centralized services), or end-to-end encryption (for social or P2P applications). VPNs can't magically encrypt your traffic - it's simply not technically possible. If the endpoint expects plaintext, there is nothing you can do about that.

使用SSL/TLS和HTTPS(用于集中式服务)或端到端加密(用于社交或P2P应用)。VPN不能神奇地加密你的流量--这在技术上是不可能的。如果端点需要纯文本，您对此无能为力。

When using a VPN, the only encrypted part of the connection is from you to the VPN provider. From the VPN provider onwards, it is the same as it would have been without a VPN. And remember, the VPN provider can see and mess with all your traffic.

使用VPN时，连接的唯一加密部分是从您到VPN提供商。从VPN提供商开始，它与没有VPN的情况相同。请记住，VPN提供商可能会看到并扰乱您的所有流量。

Your IP address is a largely irrelevant metric in modern tracking systems. Marketers have gotten wise to these kind of tactics, and combined with increased adoption of CGNAT and an ever-increasing amount of devices per household, it just isn't a reliable data point anymore.

在现代跟踪系统中，您的IP地址在很大程度上是一个无关紧要的指标。营销人员已经意识到了这类策略，再加上CGNAT越来越多地被采用，每个家庭的设备数量也在不断增加，它就不再是一个可靠的数据点了。

Marketers will almost always use some kind of other metric to identify and distinguish you. That can be anything from a useragent to a fingerprinting profile. A VPN cannot prevent this.

营销者几乎总是会使用某种其他的衡量标准来识别和区分你。它可以是从用户代理到指纹配置文件的任何东西。VPN无法阻止这种情况。

You are on a known-hostile network (eg. a public airport WiFi access point, or an ISP that is known to use MITM), and you want to work around that.

您在一个已知的敌意网络上(例如，公共机场WiFi接入点，或已知使用MITM的ISP)，您想要解决这个问题。

You want to hide your IP from a very specific set of non-government-sanctioned adversaries - for example, circumventing a ban in a chatroom or preventing anti-piracy scareletters.

你想要隐藏你的IP，不让一组非常具体的非政府批准的对手看到-例如，绕过聊天室的禁令，或者阻止反盗版恐吓。

In the second case, you'd probably just want a regular proxy specifically for that traffic - sending all of your traffic over a VPN provider (like is the default with almost every VPN client) will still result in the provider being able to snoop on and mess with your traffic.

在第二种情况下，您可能只需要一个专门针对该流量的常规代理-通过VPN提供商发送您的所有流量(就像几乎每个VPN客户端的默认设置一样)仍然会导致提供商能够窥探和干扰您的流量。(=

However, in practice, just don't use a VPN provider at all, even for these cases.

然而，在实践中，根本不要使用VPN提供商，即使在这些情况下也是如此。

If you absolutely need a VPN, and you understand what its limitations are, purchase a VPS and set up your own (either using something like Streisand or manually - I recommend using Wireguard). I will not recommend any specific providers (diversity is good!), but there are plenty of cheap ones to be found on LowEndTalk.

如果你绝对需要VPN，并且你知道它的限制是什么，那就买个VPS，然后自己设置(要么使用史翠珊(Streisand)之类的东西，要么手动--我推荐使用Wireguard)。我不会推荐任何具体的提供商(多样性很好！)，但在LowEndTalk上可以找到很多便宜的提供商。

A VPN provider specifically seeks out those who are looking for privacy, and who may thus have interesting traffic. Statistically speaking, it is more likely that a VPN provider will be malicious or a honeypot, than that an arbitrary generic VPS provider will be.

VPN提供商专门寻找那些正在寻找隐私的人，因此他们可能会有感兴趣的流量。从统计上讲，与任意的通用VPS提供商相比，VPN提供商更有可能是恶意的或蜜罐。

Because it's easy money. You just set up OpenVPN on a few servers, and essentially start reselling bandwidth with a markup. You can make every promise in the world, because nobody can verify them. You don't even have to know what you're doing, because again, nobody can verify what you say. It is 100% snake-oil.

因为这是很容易赚到的钱。您只需在几台服务器上设置OpenVPN，实质上就是开始转售带标记的带宽。你可以做出世界上所有的承诺，因为没有人能证实它们。你甚至不需要知道你在做什么，因为再说一次，没有人能证实你说的话。这是百分之百的蛇油。

So yes, VPN services do serve a purpose - it's just one that benefits the provider, not you.

因此，是的，VPN服务确实是有目的的--它只是一个让提供商受益的服务，而不是您。

B2B commerce and financing startup OfBusiness raises $160M led by SoftBank Vision Fund 2 at a valuation of $1.5B, becoming the 18th Indian unicorn for the year
B2B 商务和融资初创公司 OfBusiness 以 1.5B 美元的估值筹集了 1.6 亿美元，由 SoftBank Vision Fund 2 领投，成为今年第 18 家印度独角兽

2021-07-31 13:17:57

OfBusiness has raised fresh funding of $160 million led by SoftBank Vision Fund-II, valuing the company at $1.5 billion, its cofounder and chief executive Asish Mohapatra told ET. The business-to-business( B2B) commerce platform is now the 18th Indian unicorn—startups valued over $ 1 billion—for the year, as record inflow of capital is pumped into the country’s startup economy. While SoftBank put in $150 million into OfBusiness in this round, Falcon Edge Capital and Matrix Partners contributed the balance. Falcon Edge had earlier led a $110 million financing round in the company, in April, when OfBusiness was valued at $800 million. Mohapatra said he will use the new capital for acquisitions and strategic investments to gain greater control over the supply chain of its core segments like foodgrains, metals, plastics, petrochemicals, industrial chemicals, food grains and building materials. “We have been profitable but (acquisitions) will increase our profit margins into double digits. We have reached a scale where we need meaningful control (over the supply chain),” he said. Launched five years ago, OfBusiness is currently at a revenue run-rate of $1.1 billion, based on its July revenue. The company typically earns margins of 5-10% but (acquisitions) could help OfBusiness take margins up to the early teens and then near the 20% mark. “We are looking at companies that have revenue in the range of Rs 20-200 crore,” said CEO Mohapatra. ET first reported about SoftBank’s potential investment in OfBusiness on June 2. So far this year, SoftBank has backed social commerce startup Meesho and banking technology platform Zeta, taking both to the club. ET reported last month that the Japanese tech investor is likely to double down on portfolio firms like SaaS (software-as-a-service) platform MindTickle with a new cash infusion at a valuation of over $1 billion. Other Verticals While the core business for OfBusiness is facilitating commerce, it also has a financing platform--Oxyzo--which is operated through its own non-banking financial company (NBFC) licence. The lending vertical, the second largest business vertical, has a loan book of $220 million which is growing 80% annually. According to Mohapatra, it has seen non-performing assets (NPA) of less than 1%. The company also has a SaaS vertical which helps SMEs with online procurement of various tenders in the manufacturing and infrastructure sectors--both Indian and global. While commerce contributes 55% of its total net revenue, lending and SaaS have a pie of 43% and 2%, respectively. In terms of profits, lending is its biggest contributor followed by commerce. “We are still investing heavily into the commerce business as it continues to grow. We are also starting private labels in 17-18 products and investing in its distribution,” Mohapatra added. OfBusiness currently has a profit-before-tax run-rate of under $30 million which is expected to increase to $60 million by FY22 on the back of planned expansion and acquisitions. B2B Surge While consumer internet firms continue to raise record capital from investors, B2B commerce platforms like OfBusiness, Infra.Market, Moglix and Zetwerk have also gained prominence over the last 12 months-both in operations and fund raising from marquee investors. These companies are not necessarily direct competitors. “The 60 million SMEs in India are underserved by the existing offline supply and finance chain. OfBusiness has successfully built a full-stack solution enabling commerce and financing through a single marketplace which is digitising the SMEs. We have been impressed by the team’s market leadership, growth and focus on profitability and eager to be part of OfBusiness,” said Sumer Juneja, partner, SoftBank Investment Advisers.

其联合创始人兼首席执行官 Asish Mohapatra 告诉 ET，OfBusiness 已经筹集了 1.6 亿美元的新资金，由 SoftBank Vision Fund-II 领投，公司估值为 15 亿美元。随着创纪录的资金流入该国的初创经济，这个企业对企业 (B2B) 商务平台现在是今年第 18 家印度独角兽企业——估值超过 10 亿美元的初创企业。软银在这一轮向 OfBusiness 投入了 1.5 亿美元，而 Falcon Edge Capital 和 Matrix Partners 则贡献了余额。 Falcon Edge 早些时候曾在 4 月份领投了该公司的 1.1 亿美元融资，当时 OfBusiness 的估值为 8 亿美元。 Mohapatra 表示，他将利用新资本进行收购和战略投资，以更好地控制其核心部门的供应链，如粮食、金属、塑料、石化、工业化学品、粮食和建筑材料。 “我们一直在盈利，但（收购）将使我们的利润率提高到两位数。我们已经达到了需要有效控制（对供应链）的规模，”他说。根据其 7 月的收入，OfBusiness 于五年前推出，目前的收入运行率为 11 亿美元。该公司的利润率通常为 5-10%，但（收购）可以帮助 OfBusiness 将利润率提高到十几岁，然后接近 20%。 “我们正在寻找收入在 20-20 亿卢比之间的公司，”首席执行官 Mohapatra 说。 ET 于 6 月 2 日首次报道了软银对 OfBusiness 的潜在投资。 今年到目前为止，软银已支持社交商务初创公司 Meesho 和银行技术平台 Zeta，将两者都带入了俱乐部。 ET 上个月报道称，这家日本科技投资者可能会以超过 10 亿美元的估值向 SaaS（软件即服务）平台 MindTickle 等投资组合公司加倍注资。其他垂直领域 虽然 OfBusiness 的核心业务是促进商业发展，但它还有一个融资平台——Oxyzo——通过自己的非银行金融公司 (NBFC) 许可证运营。纵向贷款是第二大业务纵向业务，其贷款额为 2.2 亿美元，每年增长 80%。根据 Mohapatra 的说法，它的不良资产 (NPA) 不到 1%。该公司还有一个 SaaS 垂直行业，可帮助中小企业在线采购印度和全球制造业和基础设施领域的各种招标。虽然商业贡献了其总净收入的 55%，但贷款和 SaaS 分别占了 43% 和 2%。在利润方面，贷款是其最大的贡献者，其次是商业。 “随着商业业务的持续增长，我们仍在对其进行大量投资。我们还在 17-18 种产品中启动自有品牌，并对其分销进行投资，”Mohapatra 补充道。 OfBusiness 目前的税前利润低于 3000 万美元，在计划的扩张和收购的支持下，预计到 22 财年将增加到 6000 万美元。 B2B 激增 虽然消费互联网公司继续从投资者那里筹集创纪录的资金，但在过去的 12 个月里，OfBusiness、Infra.Market、Moglix 和 Zetwerk 等 B2B 商务平台也在运营和从大型投资者那里筹集资金方面取得了突出地位。这些公司不一定是直接竞争对手。 “现有的线下供应和金融链无法为印度的 6000 万中小企业提供服务。 OfBusiness 已成功构建了一个全栈解决方案，通过单一市场实现商业和融资，该市场将中小企业数字化。我们对团队的市场领导地位、增长和对盈利能力的关注以及渴望成为 OfBusiness 的一部分印象深刻，”软银投资顾问合伙人 Sumer Juneja 说。

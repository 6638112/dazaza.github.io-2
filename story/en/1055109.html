We begin to meet without knowing exactly why. We know we have common interests. We know that we feel understood in each other’s company, and that’s a good start. We know our enthusiasm won’t be misread, that the intensity of our curiosity is wordlessly accepted. We gather ourselves together, we work on each other’s projects and we keep an eye out for the others tuned to the same frequency. A few dozen at first, then a few hundred, eventually thousands. Our interests expand as the tribe expands. Like energy attracts like, and soon hundreds of localized gatherings bloom around us all over the world. And even though life changes - school ends, responsibilities accrue - the signal is always there. Calling us back to the company of the people who understand us, who light up at the same things we do. We want to share what we’ve learned. We want to learn what others have to share. We want to teach and be taught and move the culture forward. We want to do our part to construct a future as free and open, as secure and as resilient as the tribe that’s building it. That’s the signal. That’s why the years, the miles and any number of calamities don’t stop it.

我们开始在没有知道原因的情况下见面。我们知道我们有共同的兴趣。我们知道我们觉得在彼此的公司中觉得理解，这是一个很好的开端。我们知道我们的热情不会误读，我们的好奇心的强度是无知的。我们聚集在一起，我们互相努力的项目，我们对其他调整到相同频率的别人留意。起初几十个，然后几百，最终成千上万。我们的利益随着部落扩大而扩大。像能量吸引的人一样，很快就有数百家本地化的聚会在世界各地绽放。虽然生活变化 - 学校结束，责任accrue - 信号始终存在。致电我们回到了解我们的人的公司，谁在我们所做的同样的事情点亮。我们希望分享我们所学到的内容。我们想了解其他人必须分享的内容。我们想教导和教导并向文化前进。我们想做我们的部门来建立一个自由和开放的未来，尽可能安全，并且作为建立它的部落。这是信号。这就是为什么多年来，里程和任何数量的灾难不会阻止它。

This year’s visual inspiration started with video games. Specifically, Atari 2600 box art. We picked the box art because it represents a gateway into another world, a signal beacon that called a generation into the digital space where the internet would one day bloom. Two sets of images on the box. Vivid cartoons to tell you what the game meant to portray, and clumsy, fat-pixeled images that showed actual gameplay. ‘This is that’, the boxes said. ‘When you see this, imagine that’. For those with the imagination to see it clearly, the message becomes ‘translate this into that’. Once a certain type of kid learns a trick like that, there is no turning back.

今年的视觉灵感始于视频游戏。具体而言，Atari 2600盒艺术。我们选择了盒子艺术，因为它代表了进入另一个世界的网关，一个信号标信标被称为互联网将有一天绽放的数字空间。两套图像在盒子上。生动的漫画告诉您游戏意味着描绘，笨拙，胖胖的像素图像，显示出实际的游戏。 “这是”，盒子说。 “当你看到这个时，想象一下'。对于那些想象力看它清楚的人来说，消息变得“将其翻译成那”。一旦某种类型的孩子学习这样的伎俩，就没有回头。

A simple equation kicks open the door, and you realize even you can make pixels do things. Maybe better things, if you learn the cipher. Master the rules that govern this hidden world, and there is almost nothing you can’t do. Not everyone who chased 8-bit ghosts or shot Space Invaders became a hacker. But many saw the door and were called to walk through it, because for some of us, the signal can’t be denied.

一个简单的等式踢开了门，你即使你也可以让像素做事。如果你学习密码，可能会更好的事情。掌握管理这个隐藏世界的规则，几乎没有任何东西。不是每个追逐8位幽灵或射击空间入侵者的人成为黑客。但是许多人看到了门，被叫走过它，因为我们的一些人，信号不能被拒绝。

Similar things were happening in the world of music.Electronic instruments - samplers that could emulate real instruments, drum machines that could sound like a live drum kit - were suddenly available to musicians and hobbyists alike. The value proposition was that you could create music even if you didn’t have a drummer handy, or add strings to your composition without hiring a quartet. For those with a particular bent of mind, however, this missed the point. Why would you waste these amazing machines re-creating sounds you already knew? For these kids, the real power of these electronic tools is the ability to sound like nothing at all that has ever come before. Like video games, these awkward boxes covered in switches and knobs, trailing wires in all directions, opened a door. On the other side? Hip-hop. Freestyle. Electro. Techno. The New World. And once you see that music can be anything you imagine, there is no way back. The signal has you. TRON Disney’s deeply weird, hypnotic attempt to bring 1982 audiences inside the computer is the most on-theme movie that could possibly exist. The programs are personified. Some of them take their names from real Unix commands. There are literal (and awesome) bugs running around that destroy sloppy programs. The protagonist is a human so obsessed with programming that he gets himself inside the machine to do physical battle with a runaway process that has become sentient and evil.

Tron Disney的深入奇怪，催眠尝试在计算机内带来1982个受众，是最可能存在的主题电影。这些计划是填写的。其中一些人从真正的UNIX命令中获取他们的名字。遍布销毁邋的程序的文字（和令人敬畏的）错误。主角是一种如此迷恋的编程，他在机器里面得到了自己的身体战斗，与已经成为感知和邪恶的失控流程。

Max Headroom Max Headroom is the unstoppable signal personified. Created to be a lowly AI veejay, he hits a nerve and gets a UK TV movie to flesh out his backstory. That movie spawns a one-season UK series and a fourteen-episode, two season run on US TV. These TV shows, in turn, spawn books, songs, comics, video games and even a turn as spokes-head for Coca-Cola. That would probably have been proof enough that the 80s were looking for a snarky, slick-talking robot best friend. But Max Headroom wasn’t done with us. On the 22 of November, 1987 Max jumped the air gap. That night WGN TV in Chicago had its airwaves hijacked twice by a pirate in full Max Headroom regalia. He spoke about Coca-Cola, cartoons and on-air personalities from WGN, and ended the show with a weird spanking segment. The perpetrators remain at large. Max Headroom is now free to roam in reality prime, and is occasionally spotted in movies and rap videos.

MAX HEADROM MAX HEADROM是一个不可阻挡的信号。创建是一个低veejay，他击中了一个神经并获得英国电视电影来肉体肉体。这部电影产生了一个赛季英国系列和十四集，在美国电视上运行了四个集。这些电视表明，又会出现了书籍，歌曲，漫画，视频游戏，甚至作为助攻头的辐条。这可能已经证明了80年代正在寻找一个蛇子，光滑的机器人最好的朋友。但最大的净空没有我们完成。 1987年11月22日，最大跳跃了气隙。那天晚上，芝加哥的WGN电视的电视通过全球净水室Regalia的海盗劫持了两次。他谈到了WGN的可口可乐，漫画和空中人物，并以奇怪的打屁股段结束了节目。肇事者仍然很大。 Max Headroom现在可以在现实素数中自由漫游，偶尔在电影和说唱视频中发现。

A lot of great science fiction from this time period deals with a particular anxiety - the nagging fear that the world you know is hiding a larger, stranger world you cannot yet perceive. Philip K. Dick’s VALIS is a masterpiece about the call of the unseen world on those who sense its presence. Ready Player One is a fun romp through the creative explosion that accompanied all the new shiny toys that the early 80s brought is. And if you haven’t read Neuromancer, don’t finish this guide - just read Neuromancer.

从这段时间的焦虑造成了很多伟大的科幻小说 - 唠叨的恐惧是你所知道的世界隐藏一个更大的陌生世界，你无法感知。 Philip K. Dick's Valis是一个关于未知世界的召唤的杰作。随着80年代初期所带来的，通过创造性的爆炸，就绪球员们是一个有趣的爆炸。如果您还没有读取神经元库，请不要完成本指南 - 只是读了神经元语。

And more importantly, there are T-files. For years free textfiles were a primary signal vector. Anybody with an internet connection could grab the latest Phrack for all the latest hacker news, counterculture goodies and maybe catch the announcement of nmap. One could follow the Cult of the Dead Cow online for their patented blend of tech and gonzo. Soon enough, a subtle door opened. Those who had to know more figured out how to go through it. Many did, maybe even you. Or the person who inspired you. The signal wins out. The eternal call brings us together to work on a better world, and to spread its gospel - to bring in the others, until all of us are found.

更重要的是，有T文件。多年来，免费文本文件是主要信号矢量。有互联网连接的人可以抓住所有最新的黑客新闻，对逆产量的好吃的东西，也许可以抓住NMAP的宣布。一个人可以追随死牛的崇拜，以获得他们的专利混合物和冈卓。很快，一个微妙的门打开了。那些不得不了解更多人的人如何经历它。很多人，也许甚至你。或者激发你的人。信号赢了。永恒的呼叫将我们共同努力工作，以更好的世界，并传播其福音 - 将其他人带来，直到我们所有人都被发现。

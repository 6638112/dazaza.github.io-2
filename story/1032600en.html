今年的图标设计潮流是不是太过火了？

2020-11-01 19:58:48

A number of big brands have recently subjected their logos and icons to controversial makeovers (we're looking at you Google). With most recent redesigns adopting the flat design trend, you'd expect things to look a little, well, flat. But, according to one designer, brands are now beginning to face a very different problem.

一些大品牌最近对他们的标志和图标进行了有争议的改造(我们正在谷歌上看着你)。随着最近的重新设计采用了扁平化的设计趋势，你会认为事情看起来有点，嗯，扁平化。但是，根据一位设计师的说法，品牌现在开始面临一个非常不同的问题。

Apparently, the problem lies in the cognitive load facing users when looking at the apps laid out next to each other in situ. With oh-so-similar rainbow-fueled colour palettes and whacky uses of shape, are icons in danger of becoming too much to process? See the tweet below, and compare with our pick of the iOS app icon designs.

显然，问题在于用户在就地查看并排摆放的应用程序时面临的认知负荷。哦，有如此相似的彩虹燃料调色板和古怪的形状使用，图标是否有变得太多而无法处理的危险？请看下面的推文，并与我们精选的iOS应用程序图标设计进行比较。

Tobias van Schneider posted the above tweet. He comments that "the cognitive load is real", and there's no doubt that looking at the image is an intense experience. These icons smack you in the face with vibrancy, but the end result is that it's hard to process exactly what you're seeing. It takes concentration to distinguish the icons from each other, which isn't necessarily something designers want to achieve. In short, it's overwhelming.

托拜厄斯·范·施耐德发布了上述推文。他评论说，认知负担是真实的，毫无疑问，看着这张照片是一种强烈的体验。这些图标充满活力地敲打着你的脸，但最终的结果是很难准确地处理你所看到的东西。区分不同的图标需要集中注意力，这不一定是设计师想要实现的。简而言之，这是压倒性的。

Though an array of shapes has been used to create all of these icons, the abstract concepts are so similar in style that none jump off the page. Throughout the thread, Google is named as one of the main culprits, and the below tweet succinctly sums up the issue:

尽管使用了一组形状来创建所有这些图标，但这些抽象概念在风格上非常相似，以至于没有一个从页面上跳过。在整个帖子中，谷歌被列为主要罪魁祸首之一，下面的推文简明扼要地总结了这个问题：

The conversation is packed full of excellent design nouse, with critique covering a range of points from the exact issues with the design trend, to trends from the past users would love to see return.

这场对话充斥着优秀的设计灵感，评论涵盖了一系列观点，从设计趋势的确切问题，到过去用户希望看到回报的趋势。

With another nod at Google, there are users who would love to see a return to previous icon iterations (and we had to include that great pun, too).

对谷歌的又一次点头表明，有些用户希望看到回到以前的图标迭代(而且我们还必须包含那个很棒的双关语)。

Are brands surfing this rainbow-wave as a rebellion against some of the more tame flat redesigns of late? Or is it here to stay? We're certainly hoping for a return to skeuomorphism in 2021 (see the rest of our UI/UX design wish list right here), which would simplify things a little (and prevent us having to wear sunglasses whenever we look at our home screens).

各品牌在这股彩虹浪中冲浪，是不是在反抗最近一些较为温和的公寓重新设计呢？或者它会留在这里？我们当然希望在2021年回归超形态化(参见我们UI/UX设计愿望清单的其余部分)，这将使事情变得简单一些(而且我们每次看主屏幕时都不用戴墨镜)。

It's crucial for brands to remember that they don't have to shout in our faces to get our attention, and doing so can often be off-putting. Individuality is key, and we're ready to see some understated personality.

品牌要记住，他们不一定非得在我们面前大喊大叫才能引起我们的注意，这一点至关重要，这样做往往会让人反感。个性是关键，我们准备看到一些低调的个性。

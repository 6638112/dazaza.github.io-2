M1 Macs: Truth and Truthiness
M1 Macs：真理与真理

2020-12-04 20:56:16

We knew this to be true: Computers could run fast and hot, or slow and cool. For laptops in particular, the best you could hope for is a middle ground: fast enough and cool enough. But if you wanted a machine that ran really fast, it wasn’t going to run cool (and wasn’t going to last long on battery), and if you wanted a computer that ran cool (and lasted long on battery), it wasn’t going to be fast.

我们知道这是事实：计算机可以快速运行，也可以运行缓慢，也可以运行缓慢。特别是对于笔记本电脑，您所希望的最好是中间立场：足够快和足够凉爽。但是，如果您想要一台运行速度非常快的机器，那它就不会运行很酷（也不会持续使用很长时间），并且如果您想要一台运行速度很酷的计算机（并持续很长时间使用电池），不会很快。

We knew this to be true because that was the way things were. But now, with the M1 Macs, it’s not. M1 Macs run very fast and do so while remaining very cool and lasting mind-bogglingly long on battery. It was a fundamental trade-off inherent to PC computing, and now we don’t have to make it.

我们知道这是真的，因为那是事实。但是现在，有了M1 Mac，事实并非如此。 M1 Macs可以非常快地运行，并且可以保持非常凉爽，并且持久的续航时间令人难以置信。这是PC计算所固有的基本权衡，现在我们不必这样做。

We should have known better, because iPhones and iPads run fast and cool, but, they’re different. Right? They’re over there, running iOS, while Macs and other PCs are over here, running MacOS and Windows. But they’re not really different. They’re all just computers. And the best aspects of those computers — running fast and cool — obviously ought to be true for all computers.

我们应该更好地了解，因为iPhone和iPad运行起来既快又酷，但是它们是不同的。对？他们在那边运行iOS，而Mac和其他PC在那边运行MacOS和Windows。但是它们并没有真正的不同。它们全都是电脑。而且，这些计算机的最佳方面-“快速而又凉快地运行-”显然应该对所有计算机都适用。

M1 Macs embarrass all other PCs — all Intel-based Macs, including automobile-priced Mac Pros, and every single machine running Windows or Linux. Those machines are just standing around in their underwear now because the M1 stole all their pants. Well, that just doesn’t happen, your instincts tell you. One company, even a company like Apple, doesn’t just embarrass the entire rest of a highly-competitive longstanding industry. But just because something hasn’t happened — or hasn’t happened in a very long while — doesn’t mean it can’t happen. And in this case, it just happened.

M1 Mac使所有其他PC（包括基于汽车的Mac Pro，以及运行Windows或Linux的每台计算机）上的所有其他基于Intel的Mac感到尴尬。由于M1偷走了他们的所有裤子，所以这些机器现在只穿着内衣。好吧，这不会发生，您的直觉告诉您。一个公司，甚至像Apple这样的公司，都不会让整个高度竞争的长期行业感到尴尬。但是，只是因为没有发生什么事-或很长时间没有发生过事-并不意味着就不会发生。在这种情况下，它只是发生了。

Another long-held belief: emulating or translating apps compiled for a different architecture is necessarily going to be irritatingly slow and somewhat incompatible at best, and unusably slow and wildly incompatible as a general rule. But Apple’s Rosetta 2 translation layer for running x86 software on Apple Silicon is a technical marvel. Remarkably compatible, and so fast that when combined with the pure speed of the M1 chips themselves, it actually runs Intel Mac software as fast or faster than on most actual Intel Mac hardware. It’s not like Intel apps running in Rosetta run OK, and native Apple Silicon apps run well; it’s more like Intel apps in Rosetta run well and Apple Silicon apps run even better. Yes, of course you want apps compiled to run natively, but most users running most apps — including some professional apps — won’t notice.

另一个长期存在的信念是：仿真或翻译为不同体系结构编译的应用程序会令人烦恼，缓慢，并且充其量是不兼容的，而通常来说，速度却是缓慢且完全不兼容的。但是，用于在Apple Silicon上运行x86软件的Apple的Rosetta 2转换层是一个技术奇迹。如此出色的兼容性，以及如此之快的速度，以致于与M1芯片本身的纯速度结合使用时，它实际上以比大多数实际的Intel Mac硬件更快或更快的速度运行Intel Mac软件。这并不是说Rosetta中运行的Intel应用程序运行正常，而本机Apple Silicon应用程序运行良好；这更像是Rosetta中的Intel应用运行良好，Apple Silicon应用运行得更好。是的，您当然希望编译的应用程序在本机运行，但是大多数运行大多数应用程序的用户（包括某些专业应用程序）不会被注意到。

Those of us who’ve been paying attention aren’t surprised that the M1 Macs have overturned these assumptions about what tradeoffs are unavoidable in terms of performance per watt. And it shouldn’t be a surprise that Apple had a remarkably good translation solution ready to go (despite the fact that yours truly, once again, wrongly predicted otherwise). It’s just a delight to see it finally come to fruition.

我们当中一直关注的人并不惊讶于M1 Mac颠覆了关于每瓦性能不可避免的折衷的假设。而且，苹果公司已经准备好推出非常出色的翻译解决方案也就不足为奇了（尽管事实是，您的确又一次错误地预测了事实）。很高兴看到它终于实现。

For the industry as a whole, though, the M1 Macs have dropped as a bit of a shock. One reason for this, I think, is that Apple’s silicon prowess in iOS devices has been a slow boil. iPhones and iPads are better computers — faster and more efficient — than their Android competitors. But it’s been an annual incremental game. And it’s hard to tell what’s attributable to iOS’s software efficiency vs. Android and what’s attributable to Apple’s silicon prowess vs. Qualcomm and Samsung and whoever else is making chips for Android devices.

不过，对于整个行业而言，M1 Macs的跌幅令人有些震惊。我认为，这样做的原因之一是，苹果公司在iOS设备中的硅技术发展缓慢。 iPhone和iPad比其Android竞争对手是更好的计算机-“更快，更高效”。但这是一年一度的增量游戏。而且，很难说出iOS相对于Android的软件效率是什么，苹果的硅实力与Qualcomm和三星的对比是什么，以及其他为Android设备制造芯片的人。

M1 Macs completely upend what we can and should expect from PCs. It’s a breakthrough along the lines of the iPhone itself in 2007.

M1 Macs完全颠覆了PC所能及应有的功能。这是2007年iPhone本身的突破。

The adage is, “If it sounds too good to be true, it probably is.” Emphasis on probably — the M1 Macs are an exception. They really are that good. But, like the iPhone in 2007, there are people who refuse to believe it.

谚语是：“如果听起来好得令人难以置信，那就可能是。”重点可能放在M1 Mac上，这是一个例外。他们真的很好。但是，就像2007年的iPhone一样，有些人拒绝相信它。

Exhibit A: Patrick Moorhead, whose review for Forbes — “ Apple MacBook Pro 13” M1 Review — Why You Might Want to Pass” —  took off when published last week.

图表A：帕特里克·穆德黑德（Patrick Moorhead）在上周发表的《福布斯》（Forbes）“ Apple MacBook Pro 13” M1评论“为什么要通过”的评论升。

Moorhead’s review bears little relation to the reality of the M1 Macs, but plays right into expectations of the status quo. ARM chips are efficient and Apple’s ARM chips are the best, but none of them are a threat to Intel and AMD’s x86 chips for high-end performance. Emulation is slow and buggy and it will take years for a lot of important software to be updated to run natively. None of that’s true in the M1 Macs’ case, but that’s the premise of Moorhead’s article.

Moorhead的评论与M1 Macs的现实关系不大，但完全符合对现状的期望。 ARM芯片是高效的，Apple的ARM芯片是最好的，但是它们都不会对英特尔和AMD的x86芯片的高端性能构成威胁。仿真是缓慢且容易出错的，要更新许多重要软件以在本机上运行，​​将需要数年的时间。在M1 Mac电脑中，这都不是真的，但这是Moorhead文章的前提。

And some folks bought it — some out of reasonable skepticism, and some out of stupidity.

有些人买了它-有些是出于合理的怀疑，有些是出于愚蠢。

I’ve read the first batch of Apple MacBook Pro 13″ M1 reviewsand you’d be hard to find anything negative about the newlaptop. At the worst, there were some complaints about the iOSapp experience, but on the whole, early reviews, described thenew MacBook Pro 13″ essentially as God’s gift to the notebookwanting masses.

我已经阅读了第一批Apple MacBook Pro 13″ M1的评论，而您很难发现有关新笔记本电脑的任何负面信息。最糟糕的情况是，有人对iOSapp的使用感到不满，但总的来说，早期的评论将新的MacBook Pro 13”形容为上帝对笔记本人群的礼物。

Don’t get me wrong, there were some very positive things about thenew laptop. The new M1 processor is impressive, but far fromperfect — it has many warts, that nearly nobody is discussing.

不要误会我的意思，新笔记本电脑有一些非常积极的方面。新的M1处理器令人印象深刻，但远非完美-它有很多疣，几乎没有人在讨论。

Not one of these purported “warts” with the M1 is ever mentioned. Whatever real problems Moorhead ran into (and some were indeed real), they’re all related to software issues. Either MacOS 11 Big Sur itself, or Rosetta, or apps he’s trying to use that have compatibility issues with MacOS 11 or Rosetta or both. But: software. Those issues are real, and the fact that M1 Macs require MacOS 11 Big Sur is the single biggest reason why some Mac users can’t or at least shouldn’t buy one just yet. Even if there were no architecture transition, in normal years it’s completely reasonable for many users to delay upgrading to major new releases of MacOS. I didn’t upgrade my old MacBook Pro to MacOS 10.15 Catalina until August of this year.

从来没有提到过M1这些所谓的“疣”。无论Moorhead遇到什么实际问题（有些确实是现实问题），它们都与软件问题有关。 MacOS 11 Big Sur本身，Rosetta或他尝试使用的与MacOS 11或Rosetta或两者都有兼容性问题的应用程序。但是：软件。这些问题是真实的，而M1 Mac需要MacOS 11 Big Sur的事实是某些Mac用户无法或至少现在不应该购买的唯一最大原因。即使不进行架构转换，通常情况下，许多用户推迟升级到主要的新MacOS版本也是完全合理的。直到今年8月，我才将旧的MacBook Pro升级到MacOS 10.15 Catalina。

Here’s a terrific piece by Peter Steinberger — “ Apple Silicon M1: A Developer’s Perspective” — that goes into great detail about how amazing the M1 is but also how many essential developer tools aren’t compatible or fully compatible with MacOS 11 on Apple Silicon yet. Steinberger’s conclusion:

这是彼得·斯坦伯格（Peter Steinberger）的绝妙作品-“ Apple Silicon M1：开发人员的观点”-详细介绍了M1的惊人之处，以及有多少基本的开发人员工具与Apple Silicon上的MacOS 11不兼容或不完全兼容。斯坦伯格的结论：

The new M1 MacBooks are fast, beautiful and silent and the hype isabsolutely justified. There’s still a lot to do on thesoftware-front to catch up, and the bugs around older iOSSimulators are especially problematic.

新款M1 MacBook速度快，美观，安静，并且绝对值得炒作。要赶上软件方面的工作，还有很多工作要做，而旧版iOSSimulator的错误尤其成问题。

All of that can be fixed in software and the whole industry iscurrently working on making the experience better, so by nextyear, when Apple updates the 16-inch MacBook Pro and releases thenext generation of their M chip line, it should be absolutelypossible to use a M1 Mac as main dev machine.

所有这些都可以通过软件修复，目前整个行业都在努力改善体验，因此，到明年，当苹果更新16英寸MacBook Pro并发布其下一代M芯片系列时，绝对有可能使用M1 Mac作为主要开发机器。

Steinberger’s is a scrupulously fair conclusion. Perversely, developers, who by nature of their profession best understand exactly what an architecture transition like this entails, might be among the few professions who can’t yet move their primary computing to an Apple Silicon device by nature of the software tools they depend upon. (Some developers can move now, and — because Xcode running on the M1 compiles code so much faster than any Intel MacBook — are rejoicing.)

斯坦伯格的结论是非常谨慎的。反过来说，根据自己的专业性质，他们最能确切地理解这种架构转换所需要的开发人员，可能是少数仍无法依靠其所依赖的软件工具将主要计算转移到Apple Silicon设备的专业人士中。 。 （有些开发人员现在可以搬家了，因为在M1上运行的Xcode的编译速度比任何英特尔MacBook都快得多，所以很高兴。）

Moorhead, on the other hand, claims that it’s the M1 that has — again, his word — “warts”:

另一方面，Moorhead声称是M1具有－再次是他的－“疣”：

I think the new MacBook Pro 13” M1 will likely be fine forusers who use 100% Apple software, stay primarily in Safari anddon’t need to connect it to a bunch of peripherals, and have alot of money.

我认为，新款MacBook Pro 13” M1对于使用100％苹果软件，主要呆在Safari中并且不需要将其连接到大量外围设备并且有很多钱的用户来说，可能是个不错的选择。

Is Chrome “100 percent Apple software”? Because Chrome runs better on the M1 than it does on any other Mac. Yes, Safari is faster and more energy efficient than Chrome, but Safari is faster than Chrome on Intel-based Macs as well. “And have a lot of money” — I have no idea what that even means other than being able to buy a Mac in the first place, which seems to me a given. But while the entry price for a MacBook Air remains $999, the effective usefulness of the new M1 $999 MacBook Air is far higher than that of the $999 Intel-based MacBook Air it replaced. The M1 is good news even for Mac users on a smaller budget.

Chrome是“ 100％苹果软件”吗？因为Chrome在M1上比在其他Mac上运行更好。是的，Safari在浏览器上比Chrome更快，更节能，但在基于Intel的Mac上，Safari也比Chrome更快。 “而且有很多钱” —我不知道这除了意味着首先购买Mac之外还有什么意思。但是，虽然MacBook Air的入门价格仍为999美元，但新款M1售价999美元的MacBook Air的有效效用却远高于它所取代的基于999美元的基于Intel的MacBook Air。即使对于预算较小的Mac用户，M1也是个好消息。

I wanted to provide some balance to those early reviews anddiscuss who I believe shouldn’t consider the new MacBook Pro 13”M1. I know that may sound negative, but I call it balance.I’ve used my unit for nearly five days and here is my assessmentof whom I think should avoid it.

我想为那些我认为不应该考虑使用新款MacBook Pro 13” M1的早期评论和讨论提供一些平衡。我知道这听起来可能很消极，但我称其为平衡。我已经用了将近5天的设备，这是我对应该避免使用的设备的评估。

Stephen Colbert famously quipped at the 2006 White House Correspondants Dinner that “ reality has a well-known liberal bias.” Well, the reality of the M1 has an Apple bias. There is no “ Well, here’s the downside” with regard to the state of Apple Silicon versus the entire rest of the industry. Yes, the M1 is a consumer chip with consumer limits — two USB ports, a maximum of 16 GB of RAM — but that’s the nature of these Apple Silicon Mac models, not all Apple Silicon Mac models to come.

斯蒂芬·科尔伯特（Stephen Colbert）在2006年白宫通讯员晚宴上打趣地说：“现实存在着众所周知的自由主义偏见。”好吧，M1的现实有苹果的偏见。关于Apple Silicon和整个行业的状况，没有“好，这是不利”。是的，M1是一种消费类芯片，具有消费类限制-“两个USB端口，最大16 GB RAM”-但这是这些Apple Silicon Mac型号的本质，并非所有即将上市的Apple Silicon Mac型号。

There is no balance, if by balance, you’re looking for a story that says any PC hardware, ARM or x86, is competitive in any way with the M1 Macs for low-energy computing. I’m reminded of another quote, from then-CEO Ed Colligan of then-company Palm in November 2006, a few months ahead of the iPhone’s introduction:

两者之间没有平衡，要想找到一个平衡的故事，那就是说任何PC硬件（ARM或x86）都可以与M1 Macs进行低能耗计算的竞争。我想起了另一句话：2006年11月，时任公司Palm的时任首席执行官埃德·科利根（Ed Colligan），比iPhone推出早了几个月：

Responding to questions from New York Times correspondent JohnMarkoff at a Churchill Club breakfast gathering Thursday morning,Colligan laughed off the idea that any company — including thewildly popular Apple Computer — could easily win customers in thefinicky smart-phone sector.

在星期四早晨的丘吉尔俱乐部早餐聚会上，纽约时报记者约翰·马克夫（JohnMarkoff）回答了问题，科利根（Colligan）嘲笑了这样的想法，即任何一家公司（包括广受欢迎的苹果计算机公司）都可以轻易赢得小巧的智能手机领域的客户。

“We’ve learned and struggled for a few years here figuring out howto make a decent phone,” he said. “PC guys are not going to justfigure this out. They’re not going to just walk in.”

他说：“我们在这里已经学习并奋斗了几年，想方设法制作出像样的手机。” “ PC专家们不会仅仅以此为理由。他们不会只是走进去。”

Patrick Moorhead’s M1 Mac review can be paraphrased as “ Intel and AMD have learned and struggled for a few years here figuring out how to make decent PC chips. Apple hasn’t just magically figured this out. They’re not going to just walk in.”

帕特里克·穆德黑德（Patrick Moorhead）对M1 Mac的评论可以这样解释：“英特尔和AMD在这里已经学习并奋斗了几年，弄清楚如何制造出体面的PC芯片。苹果公司不仅仅神奇地发现了这一点。他们不会只是走进去。”

The difference is, Colligan at least hadn’t seen the iPhone yet. Moorhead has an M1 MacBook Pro in his hands. He either should know better, or does know better and wrote this grossly misleading tripe anyway, knowing there was an Apple-skeptical audience willing to lap it up.

所不同的是，Colligan至少还没有看过iPhone。 Moorhead手中拥有M1 MacBook Pro。他要么知道得更多，要么确实知道得更好，无论如何，他写了这封严重误导性的牛肚，知道有一个苹果怀疑论者愿意将它包起来。

We’ve seen this before. In 2013, Apple announced the iPhone 5S with the A7 chip, the first 64-bit mobile CPU — years ahead of industry expectations. Here’s what Moorhead said then:

我们以前见过。 2013年，苹果发布了带有A7芯片的iPhone 5S，这是第一个64位移动CPU，比业界预期提前了几年。 Moorhead当时的话是这样的：

“Adding 64-bit processor capabilities adds nothing to the userexperience today, as it would requires over four gigabytes ofmemory,” Patrick Moorhead of Moor Insights and Strategy, and aformer executive at AMD, told AllThingsD. “Most phones today onlyhave one to two gigabytes of memory, and it will be years beforethe norm is four.”

“增加64位处理器功能对当今的用户体验没有任何影响，因为这将需要超过4 GB的内存，” Moor Insights and Strategy的AMD前执行官Patrick Moorhead告诉AllThingsD。 “如今，大多数手机只有一到两个千兆字节的内存，而要达到这一标准还需要几年的时间。”

In 2015, Apple shipped the first iPad Pro, with the A9X chip. Aaron Souppouris wrote a piece for Engadget headlined “ The iPad Pro’s Chip Is Not a Big Deal”. You will never guess who Souppouris’s expert source was:

2015年，Apple推出了首款配备A9X芯片的iPad Pro。亚伦·索普普里斯（Aaron Souppouris）为Engadget写了一篇标题为“ iPad Pro的芯片不是什么大事”的文章。您将永远不会猜到Souppouris的专家来源是：

But what of the benchmark tests that show the iPad Prooutperforming Intel’s Core M processor, and even coming close toIntel’s MacBook Pro range? Don’t believe them. Patrick Moorhead, ahighly respected analyst with a strong background in chips, urgescaution, especially when it comes to comparing GeekBench numbers,as many have. “GeekBench is a synthetic, mobile benchmark,”Moorhead tells Engadget. “The benchmark code is more like mobileapplication code than it is desktop code.” Using GeekBench to testA9X versus Intel chips is “like comparing apples and oranges or anSUV with a sedan on the straight-away,” he explains. […]

但是，有哪些基准测试能证明iPad Pro性能优于Intel的Core M处理器，甚至接近Intel的MacBook Pro系列？不要相信他们。帕特里克·摩尔黑德（Patrick Moorhead）是一位备受推崇的分析师，他在筹码方面拥有深厚的背景，他极力提倡谨慎，特别是在比较GeekBench数字时，就像许多人一样。 “ GeekBench是一个综合的移动基准测试，” Moorhead告诉Engadget。 “基准代码更像是移动应用程序代码，而不是桌面代码。”他解释说，使用GeekBench来测试A9X和Intel芯片就像“直接比较苹果和橘子或带有轿车的SUV一样”。 […]

Apple doesn’t have such a large legacy to support — it only movedto Intel chips nine years ago — but there would be no perceivablebenefit to switching an existing x86 platform to ARM. “I do notbelieve ARM-based chips will be powering Macs in the next fewyears,” said Moorhead. “I do believe Apple will attempt to scaleup the iPad Pro even further, which could potentially eat intoMacbook [ sic] sales.” […]

苹果公司并没有足够的遗产来支持它-“它只是在9年前才转移到英特尔芯片上-”，但是将现有的x86平台转换为ARM并没有明显的好处。 Moorhead说：“我不相信基于ARM的芯片将在未来几年内为Mac提供动力。” “我相信苹果将尝试进一步扩大iPad Pro的规模，这可能会蚕食Macbook的销售。” […]

According to Moorhead, Intel came close to putting one of itschips inside the iPad Pro. “[The iPad Pro] business is open toboth Apple’s own ARM-based AX chips and Intel,” he explained,“Intel is fighting hard to get that business and I believe almosthad [the iPad Pro contract] with the new Skylake-based Core M hadit been available earlier.”

根据Moorhead的说法，英特尔几乎将其一块芯片放入iPad Pro。他说：“ [iPad Pro]业务对苹果自己的基于ARM的AX芯片和英特尔都开放，”英特尔正在努力争取这一业务，我相信[iPad Pro合同]几乎与基于Skylake的新Core我早些时候有空。”

So on the M1 Mac rave-review side, we have every major professional reviewer, along with dozens and dozens of ordinary M1 Mac purchasers out in the real world, doing real things, who just can’t believe what the M1 is capable of.

因此，在M1 Mac好评如潮的一面，我们有每个主要的专业评论家，以及现实世界中数十个普通的M1 Mac购买者，他们在做真实的事情，简直不敢相信M1的能力。

And on the other side, we have Patrick Moorhead, the guy who said Apple’s 64-bit A7 chip in the iPhone 5S was no big deal; that we shouldn’t have believed the benchmarks showing the original iPad Pro pantsing Intel’s chips five years ago; and that not only was Apple unlikely to switch the Mac away from Intel, but that Intel, in fact, was this close to getting its chips into the iPad Pro.

另一方面，我们有Patrick Moorhead，他说苹果在iPhone 5S中使用64位A7芯片没什么大不了的。我们不应该相信五年前显示基准iPad Pro削弱英特尔芯片的基准；苹果不仅不太可能将Mac放弃使用Intel，而且实际上Intel已经接近将其芯片应用于iPad Pro。

At the risk of making this a very boring review, all I have to sayis basically that all of the above is so far holding as true. Thiscomputer is nuts. Compiling tons of things in the backgrounddoesn’t slow down Safari web browsing, somehow. I haven’t had toplug it in once since I fully charged it up two days ago.Performance is so quiet and cool that I feel like my terminalcompiling a bunch of things is actually an SSH into a muchstronger workstation located somewhere else. I actually discoveredthat I’ve had an instinct of measuring my MacBook’s CPU usage byfeeling the heat on the strip of aluminum right above the TouchBar, and I can’t even do that anymore now. Because even if the M1MacBook Pro has been running at 100% on all cores for ten straightminutes, you’ll barely feel it getting warm.

冒使这个评论很无聊的风险，我只想说基本上所有上述观点都是正确的。这台计算机很疯狂。在某种程度上，在后台编译大量内容并不会减慢Safari Web浏览的速度。自从两天前将其充满电以来，我再也不需要插入它。性能是如此安静和酷炫，以至于我感觉终端编译一堆东西实际上是将SSH连接到位于其他地方的强大得多的工作站。我实际上发现我有一种本能，就是通过感觉到TouchBar上方铝条上的热量来测量MacBook的CPU使用率，而现在我什至不能做。因为即使M1MacBook Pro在所有内核上以100％的速度运行了十分钟，您也几乎不会感觉到它变热。

Kobeissi is both technically scrupulous (and clearly knowledgeable) but also doesn’t hide his genuine enthusiasm. The M1 is exciting! Even I am using exclamation marks. And what a keen point Kobeissi makes about subconsciously equating a laptop working hard with physical manifestations — as heat you can feel and fan noise you can hear. It is, for now, utterly uncanny that when an M1 MacBook exerts itself computationally it neither gets warm nor makes any noise. Soon though, that will seem utterly normal, and it’ll be every other laptop in the world, the ones that do get hot and do make noise (or never go fast in the first place) that will seem wrong and weird.

Kobeissi不仅在技术上严格（而且知识渊博），但也没有掩饰他的真诚热情。 M1令人兴奋！甚至我都在使用感叹号。而Kobeissi敏锐地意识到，潜意识中将笔记本电脑与物理表现相提并论，就是感觉到热量和风扇噪音。到目前为止，绝对不可思议的是，当M1 MacBook在计算上发挥作用时，它既不会变热，也不会发出任何噪音。不过很快，这似乎将是完全正常的，并且世界上所有其他笔记本电脑都会变得发热量并且发出噪音（或者一开始就不会快速运转），这似乎是错误的和怪异的。

This graph is completely worthless. Honestly. Nobody knows to thisday what Apple meant by “Latest PC laptop chip”, and both axesaren’t even numbered. It’s stupid that Apple still pulls this sortof thing off, and it immediately makes you question just how goodthe M1 is really supposed to be.

该图完全没有价值。老实说到目前为止，没人知道Apple所说的“最新PC笔记本电脑芯片”是什么意思，而且两个轴都没有编号。苹果仍然会拖延这种事情，这很愚蠢，这立刻让您质疑M1到底应该有多好。

I get it. It’s a pure Bezos chart. With no scale on either axis and a comparison against an unnamed competitor, there’s no way to verify that it’s true.

我知道了。这是一个纯贝索斯图表。由于两个轴上都没有刻度，并且无法与未命名的竞争对手进行比较，因此无法验证它是否正确。

But I also get why Apple presented it this way, because while it’s not meaningful in any scientific sense, per se, it is very much an accurate illustration of the M1 vs. its competition overall. Is this a graph of exporting video? Or compiling code? Or running complex web applications in a browser? Or playing a game? Yes. That’s what this graph is trying to say: yes to all of it. The M1 is up here, running fast and cool, and all the competing chips from Intel, AMD, Qualcomm, Samsung, and whoever else are down there, running hot or running slow, looking around for their recently stolen pants.

但我也明白了苹果为何以这种方式提出它，因为尽管从任何科学意义上讲，它本身都没有意义，但它实际上是M1与它的整体竞争情况的准确例证。这是导出视频的图表吗？还是编译代码？还是在浏览器中运行复杂的Web应用程序？还是玩游戏？是。这就是这张图表要说的：对所有图表均是。 M1在这里运行，既快又酷，并且来自英特尔，AMD，高通，三星以及其他任何竞争对手的所有竞争性芯片都在这里运行，无论是热还是慢，都在寻找他们最近被盗的裤子。

Today was the first commercial sale of cell-cultured meat in human history, at $23 per entrée, the biggest milestone since the first hamburger in 2013 for $300,000.
今天是人类历史上首例细胞培养肉的商业销售，每宗要价23美元，这是自2013年首个汉堡以30万美元创下的最大里程碑。

2020-12-20 00:54:04

On Saturday, lab-grown chicken made by U.S. start-up Eat Just will make its historic debut at restaurant 1880 in Singapore, after the country's food agency approved the sale of cultured meat.

星期六，由美国初创公司Eat Just生产的实验室饲养的鸡肉将在新加坡的食品代理商批准出售养殖肉后，在新加坡的1880餐厅首次亮相。

The lab-grown chicken will be the first cultured meat sold and served at a restaurant, according to a press release from Eat Just.

根据Eat Just的新闻稿，实验室种植的鸡肉将成为在餐厅出售和供应的第一批养殖肉。

At 1880, which is known for innovate menus and socially conscious members, according to its website, the GOOD Meat Cultured Chicken will be available in a trio of sample dishes: bao bun with crispy sesame cultured chicken and spring onion; phyllo puff pastry with cultured chicken and black bean puree; and a crispy maple waffle with cultured chicken with spices and hot sauce.

1880年，以创新的菜单和具有社会意识的成员而闻名，根据其网站，良好的肉类培养鸡肉将以三样样品提供：包子，脆皮芝麻培养的鸡肉和葱；菲洛酥配培养鸡肉和黑豆泥；酥脆的枫华夫饼配以培根鸡肉和香料和辣酱。

The trio will cost about $23, Josh Tetrick, co-founder and CEO of Eat Just tells CNBC Make It.

Eat Just的联合创始人兼首席执行官Josh Tetrick告诉CNBC Make It，三人组的价格约为23美元。

Tetrick, who founded Eat Just in 2011, says the company started developing cultured chicken using animal cells in 2016. Since 2011, the company has raised $300 million to date and was last valued at $1.2 billion.

泰特里克（Tetrick）于2011年创立Eat Just，他表示该公司于2016年开始使用动物细胞开发培养的鸡。自2011年以来，该公司迄今已筹集了3亿美元，最近的估值为12亿美元。

To create GOOD Chicken, a small amount of animal cells is taken from poultry. The cells are then fed nutrients like amino acids, carbohydrates, minerals, fats and vitamins — the same types of nutrients that animals needs to grow and multiply, according to Just Eat. From there, the cells are grown into meat at a rapid rate using a bioreactor. (Tetrick compares the process to brewing beer.)

要制作好鸡，需要从家禽中提取少量动物细胞。根据Just Eat的说法，然后向细胞喂食氨基酸，碳水化合物，矿物质，脂肪和维生素等营养物质-动物需要生长和繁殖的相同类型的营养物质。从那里开始，使用生物反应器将细胞快速生长为肉。 （Tetrick将过程与酿造啤酒进行了比较。）

From start to finish, the process takes about 14 days to create cultured chicken. That's faster than traditional chicken, which takes about 45 days to go from chick to slaughter, according to Tetrick. Just Eat has manufacturing facilities in both northern California and Singapore to produce the product.

从开始到结束，整个过程大约需要14天才能制作出培养的鸡肉。泰特里克说，这比传统的鸡快，从鸡到屠宰大约需要45天。 Just Eat在北加州和新加坡均设有制造厂来生产产品。

While Tetrick would not disclose how much its costs to make GOOD Chicken, he says the company isn't making any money with the current sale price.

尽管泰特里克（Tetrick）没有透露制造“好鸡”的成本，但他说公司目前的销售价格无法赚钱。

"We didn't set it at a cost where we're making money, but we do think we are in a place where we can continue to scale up," he says. Tetrick says Eat Just has been working toward gaining regulatory approval from the U.S. and other countries.

我们并没有以赚钱为代价来设定费用，但我们确实认为我们处于可以继续扩大规模的地方，他说。泰特里克说Eat Eat一直在努力争取美国和其他国家/地区的监管批准。

Tetrick adds that it took the Singaporian government more than two years to approve cultured chicken for sale. "They appointed a safety panel. They looked at the quality of the cell line, the process of the manufacturing and the determined that this is safe," he says.

Tetrick补充说，新加坡政府花了两年多的时间才批准出售养殖鸡。 ＆＃34;他们任命了一个安全小组。他们研究了细胞系的质量，制造过程并确定这是安全的，＆＃34;他说。

While The Food and Drug Administration and the U.S. Department of Agriculture have overseen the regulation of cell-based seafood since 2019, cultured meat, like plant-based meat products, will likely face opposition from traditional meat producers, such as the U.S. Cattlemen's Association, according to a CNBC report.

自2019年以来，美国食品药品监督管理局（FDA）和美国农业部（US Department of Agriculture）一直监管基于细胞的海产品的监管，但养殖肉类（如植物类肉类产品）可能会面临美国肉鸡生产商等传统肉类生产商的反对根据CNBC的报告。

In addition to regulatory approval, high production costs is one of the biggest barriers lab-grown meat start-ups face.

除了获得监管机构的批准外，高昂的生产成本也是实验室种植肉类初创企业面临的最大障碍之一。

According to Bloomberg, it costs $400 to $2,000 a kilogram (2.2 pounds) to make cultivated meat. In 2013, Dutch start-up Mosa Meat said it cost $280,000 to make its lab-grown burger, but more recently it has found a way to bring costs down. By 2021, Mosa Meat says it hopes to sell its lab-grown patties at around $10, according to Reuters.

据彭博社报道，制作肉的成本为每公斤（2.2磅）400至2,000美元。 2013年，荷兰初创公司Mosa Meat称，制作实验室生产的汉堡的成本为280,000美元，但最近它找到了降低成本的方法。据路透社报道，Mosa Meat表示，到2021年，它希望以10美元左右的价格出售其实验室生产的肉饼。

By 2030, the cell-based meat market is projected to reach $140 billion, according to forecast complied by Blue Horizon Corp.

根据Blue Horizo​​n Corp.的预测，到2030年，基于细胞的肉类市场预计将达到1400亿美元。

Lab-grown, or "clean meat," start-ups have also attracted the attention of big-time investors. For instance, San Leandro, California-headquartered Memphis Meats counts Bill Gates, Ricard Branson and Tyson Foods Inc. among its investors.

实验室种植或干净的肉， 初创企业也吸引了大投资者的注意。 例如，总部位于加利福尼亚州圣莱安德罗的孟菲斯肉类公司将比尔·盖茨，里卡德·布兰森和泰森食品公司列为投资者。

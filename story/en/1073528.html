San Francisco police linked a woman to a crime using DNA from her rape exam
旧金山警方利用强奸案中的DNA将一名妇女与犯罪联系在一起

2022-02-15 21:22:48

The San Francisco police crime lab has been entering sexual assault victims’ DNA profiles in a database used to identify suspects in crimes, District Attorney Chesa Boudin said Monday, an allegation that raises legal and ethical questions regarding the privacy rights of victims.

旧金山警察犯罪实验室已经在一个用来识别犯罪嫌疑人的数据库中输入性侵犯受害者的DNA档案，地区检察官Chesa Boudin星期一说，这一指控引发了关于受害者隐私权的法律和伦理问题。

Boudin said his office was made aware of the purported practice last week, after a woman’s DNA collected years ago as part of a rape exam was used to link her to a recent property crime.

Boudin说，他的办公室上周得知了这一据称的做法，几年前，一名女性在强奸检查中收集的DNA被用来将她与最近的财产犯罪联系起来。

If DNA from a rape kit were used without consent for purposes other than investigating the underlying rape case, it may be a violation of constitutional protections against unreasonable searches and seizures as well as California’s Victims’ Bill of Rights, Boudin said.

布丁说，如果在未经同意的情况下，将强奸工具包中的DNA用于调查潜在强奸案以外的目的，这可能违反了宪法对不合理搜查和扣押的保护，以及加利福尼亚州的《受害者权利法案》。

Such a practice could also create another deterrent to sexual assault victims coming forward with allegations that are already underreported to law enforcement.

这种做法还可以为性侵犯受害者提供另一种威慑，使他们不敢向执法部门举报已经少报的指控。

“The primary concern that I and my office have ... is with detecting and preventing future crime,” Boudin said. “We want San Francisco to be as safe as possible, (and) we want survivors of sexual assault to feel comfortable and safe reporting and cooperating with law enforcement.”

布丁说：“我和我的办公室最关心的……是侦查和预防未来的犯罪。”。“我们希望旧金山尽可能安全，并且我们希望性侵犯的幸存者能够感到舒适和安全，并与执法部门合作。”

Boudin said his office is not aware of how many times a sexual assault victim’s DNA may have been used to secure their arrest in a later case. Citing privacy concerns, Boudin offered few specifics about the case that he said alerted his office to the crime lab’s policy, but said it concerned a woman who received a rape exam in a domestic violence and sexual abuse case years ago.

Boudin说，他的办公室不知道性侵犯受害者的DNA在后来的案件中被用于确保他们被捕的次数。Boudin以隐私问题为由，没有提供有关该案的具体细节，他说，该案提醒了他的办公室注意犯罪实验室的政策，但他说，该案涉及一名多年前在家庭暴力和性虐待案件中接受强奸检查的妇女。

The woman, Boudin said, was recently arrested on suspicion of a felony property crime, with police identifying her based on the rape-kit evidence she gave as a victim, Boudin said.

布丁说，这名女子最近因涉嫌重罪财产犯罪而被捕，警方根据她作为受害者提供的强奸工具证据对她进行了辨认，布丁说。

Boudin said his office was investigating the scope of the practice, as well as studying what it would do with any cases that used this type of evidence. The database potentially includes thousands of victims’ DNA profiles, with entries over “many, many years,” Boudin said.

布丁说，他的办公室正在调查这种做法的范围，并研究如何处理任何使用这种证据的案件。Boudin说，该数据库可能包含数千名受害者的DNA档案，其中包含“很多很多年”的条目。

San Francisco Police Chief Bill Scott said “I think the questions raised by our district attorney today are sufficiently concerning that I have asked my assistant chief for operations to work with our Investigations Bureau to thoroughly review the matter, and report back to me and to our D.A.’s office partners.”

旧金山市公安局局长Bill Scott说：“我认为，今天我们地方检察官提出的问题，充分考虑到我要求我的助理局长与我们的调查局合作，彻底审查这件事，并向我和D.A.的办公室伙伴汇报。”

Scott said it was possible the suspect in the property crime case may have been identified through a DNA match in a non-victim DNA database.

斯科特说，这起财产犯罪案件的嫌疑人可能是通过非受害者DNA数据库中的DNA匹配来确定的。

Still, Scott said, “We must never create disincentives for crime victims to cooperate with police, and if it’s true that DNA collected from a rape or sexual assault victim has been used by SFPD to identify and apprehend that person as a suspect in another crime, I’m committed to ending the practice.”

尽管如此，斯科特说，“我们绝不能阻止犯罪受害者与警方合作，如果从强奸或性侵犯受害者身上收集的DNA真的被SFPD用于确认和逮捕另一犯罪嫌疑人，我将致力于结束这种做法。”

The department’s existing DNA collection policies, Scott said, “have been legally vetted and conform with state and national forensic standards.”

斯科特说，该部门现有的DNA收集政策“已经过法律审查，符合州和国家法医标准。”

The San Francisco Police Department’s crime lab maintains several DNA databases populated largely by DNA profiles gathered from suspects or people convicted of a crime. The DNA entered in the databases is routinely compared with evidence found at crime scenes.

旧金山警察局的犯罪实验室保存了大量DNA数据库，这些数据库主要是由犯罪嫌疑人或犯罪嫌疑人收集的DNA图谱。数据库中输入的DNA通常会与犯罪现场发现的证据进行比较。

There are local, state and national databases, which may carry different criteria for when DNA gets entered. The most well-known of these databases is the Combined DNA Index System, which can connect DNA profiles across the country.

有地方、州和国家数据库，这些数据库可能包含不同的DNA输入标准。这些数据库中最著名的是联合DNA索引系统，它可以连接全国各地的DNA图谱。

The database where DNA is entered from rape kits is used solely to solve San Francisco crimes, Boudin said.

Bodin说，从强奸案中输入DNA的数据库仅用于解决旧金山犯罪。

D.A. officials said they don’t believe the practice of linking suspects to crimes using DNA collected from rape kits is disclosed in a waiver victims sign before an exam. Boudin said, “Even if it were mentioned somewhere in the fine print, is that an appropriate waiver to seek from a victim who’s just come in and reported a sex assault? Absolutely not.”

D.A.官员说，他们不相信在检查前受害者的弃权签名中披露了使用从强奸工具包中收集的DNA将嫌疑人与犯罪联系起来的做法。Boudin说，“即使在细则的某个地方提到了这一点，这是否是一种适当的豁免，可以向刚进来报案的受害者寻求帮助？绝对不是。”

San Francisco Supervisor Hillary Ronen said she was alerted to the alleged practice this weekend, and that she has submitted an inquiry to the City Attorney’s Office to draft legislation to prevent DNA evidence — or any sort of evidence collected from a victim’s rape kit — to be used for anything other than investigating that rape itself.

旧金山监督员Hillary Ronen说，她对本周末被指控的做法感到警觉，她已经向市检察官办公室提交了一份调查案，起草法律以防止DNA证据——或者从受害者的强奸工具箱收集到的任何证据——用于调查强奸本身。

“Now that I know that it is an issue I want to stop it immediately in San Francisco,” she said. “But I think that this should not be done at any level of government, anywhere.”

“既然我知道这是一个问题，我想立即停止在旧金山，”她说。“但我认为这不应该在任何级别的政府、任何地方进行。”

Joanne Archambault, chief executive officer for the advocacy group End Violence Against Women International, said she doesn’t believe a victim’s DNA should be entered into a database at any level.

倡导组织“结束对妇女的暴力行为国际”的首席执行官乔安妮·阿坎博说，她认为不应将受害者的DNA输入任何级别的数据库。

“In this situation, I think it’s inappropriate unless a victim is advised of that possibility at the time of the forensic medical examination,” she said. “And I doubt very much that that is happening.”

“在这种情况下，我认为这是不合适的，除非在法医检查时告知受害者这种可能性，”她说。“我非常怀疑这种情况是否会发生。”

Camille Cooper, vice president of public policy at the Rape, Abuse & Incest National Network, said she had never before heard of law enforcement using a victim’s DNA to connect them to other crimes.

卡米尔·库珀（Camille Cooper），强奸、虐待和虐待协会公共政策副总裁；她说，她以前从未听说过执法部门利用受害者的DNA将他们与其他犯罪联系起来。

State Senator Scott Wiener called the alleged practice “extremely troubling” and said his office was looking into whether state law allows rape-kit DNA to be used in future, unrelated cases.

州参议员斯科特·维纳（Scott Wiener）称这一指控“极其令人不安”，并表示他的办公室正在调查州法律是否允许在未来的无关案件中使用强奸工具包DNA。

“If necessary, I’ll seriously consider introducing state legislation to ban the practice,” he said.

“如果有必要，我会认真考虑引入州立法来禁止这种做法。”他说。

Leonardo da Vinci’s drawings have unique microbiomes, study finds
研究发现莱昂纳多·达·芬奇的绘画具有独特的微生物组

2020-12-23 06:26:05

Microbiomes are all the scientific rage, even in art conservation, where studying the microbial species that congregate on works of art may lead to new ways to slow down the deterioration of priceless aging artwork, as well as potentially unmask counterfeits. For instance, scientists have analyzed the microbes found on seven of Leonardo da Vinci's drawings, according to  a recent paper published in the journal Frontiers in Microbiology. And back in March, scientists at the J. Craig Venter Institute (JCVI) collected and analyzed swabs taken from centuries-old art in a private collection housed in Florence, Italy, and published their findings in the journal Microbial Ecology.

甚至在艺术保护中，微生物群落都是科学风尚，在那里研究聚集在艺术品上的微生物物种可能会导致新的方法，以减缓无价陈旧艺术品的恶化，并可能掩盖假冒品。例如，根据发表在《微生物学前沿》（Frontiers in Microbiology）上的最新论文，科学家们分析了达芬奇（Leonardo da Vinci）七幅绘画中发现的微生物。早在三月，J。Craig Venter研究所（JCVI）的科学家在意大利佛罗伦萨的一个私人收藏中收集并分析了百年历史的拭子，并将其发现发表在《微生物生态学》杂志上。

The researchers behind the earlier March paper were JCVI geneticists who collaborated with the Leonardo da Vinci DNA Project in France. The work built on a prior study looking for microbial signatures and possible geographic patterns in hairs collected from people in the District of Columbia and San Diego, California. They concluded from that analysis that microbes could be a useful geographic signature.

3月初论文背后的研究人员是JCVI遗传学家，他们与法国的达芬奇DNA项目合作。该工作建立在先前的研究基础之上，该研究旨在寻找从哥伦比亚特区和加利福尼亚圣地亚哥的人们收集的头发中的微生物特征和可能的地理图案。他们从分析中得出结论，微生物可能是有用的地理标志。

For the March study, the JCVI geneticists took swabs of microbes from Renaissance-style pieces and confirmed the presence of so-called "oxidase positive" microbes on painted wood and canvas surfaces. These microbes munch on the compounds found in paint, glue, and cellulose (found in paper, canvas, and wood), in turn producing water or hydrogen peroxide as byproducts.

在三月份的研究中，JCVI遗传学家从文艺复兴时期的碎片中抽出了微生物，并确认存在所谓的“氧化酶阳性”。木材和帆布表面上的微生物。这些微生物会侵蚀油漆，胶水和纤维素（在纸张，帆布和木材中发现）中发现的化合物，进而产生水或过氧化氢作为副产物。

"Such byproducts are likely to influence the presence of mold and the overall rate of deterioration," the authors noted in their paper. "Though prior studies have attempted to characterize the microbial composition associated with artwork decay, our results summarize the first large-scale genomics-based study to understand the microbial communities associated with aging artwork."

As an added bonus, they found they could discern between microbial populations on different types of materials. Specifically, stone and marble art fostered more diverse populations than paintings, possibly due to the "porous nature of stone and marble harboring additional organisms and potentially moisture and nutrients, along with the likelihood of biofilm formation," they wrote. Oil paintings offered more meager nutrients for microbes to metabolize, in contrast.

另外，他们发现他们可以区分不同类型材料上的微生物种群。具体而言，石材和大理石艺术品比绘画所吸引的人口更多，这可能是由于石材和大理石的多孔性质蕴藏着额外的生物以及潜在的水分和养分，以及形成生物膜的可能性，＆＃34;他们写。相比之下，油画为微生物提供了更多的微薄营养。

The authors acknowledged the small sample size, but they nonetheless concluded that microbial signatures could be used to differentiate artwork according to the materials used. As always, more research is needed. "Of particular interest would be the presence and activity of oil-degrading enzymes," the authors wrote. "Such approaches will lead to fully understanding which organism(s) are responsible for the rapid decay of artwork while potentially using this information to target these organisms to prevent degradation."

Guadalupe Piñar and her team at the University of Natural Resources and Life Sciences in Vienna, Austria, collaborated with conservators from the Instituto Centrale per la Patologa degli Archivi e del Libro (ICPAL) for the microbiome analysis of the Leonardo da Vinci drawings. Last year, Piñar et al. relied upon microbiome analysis to study the storage conditions of three statues retrieved from smugglers, as well as pinpointing their possible geographical origins. Earlier this year, they analyzed the microbiome of 1,000-year-old parchments, from which they were able to deduce the animals whose skins were used to make the parchments.

瓜达卢佩·皮纳尔（GuadalupePiñar）和她在奥地利维也纳自然资源与生命科学大学的研究小组与来自Patologa degli Archivi e del Libro中央研究所（ICPAL）的保管人合作，对莱昂纳多·达·芬奇图纸进行了微生物组分析。去年，Piñar等人。依靠微生物组分析来研究从走私者身上取回的三个雕像的储存条件，并查明它们可能的地理起源。今年早些时候，他们分析了具有1000年历史的羊皮纸的微生物组，从中可以推断出使用皮肤制作羊皮纸的动物。

For this latest paper, Piñar's team turned to a third-generation sequencing method known as Nanopore, which uses protein nanopores embedded in a polymer membrane for sequencing. It comes with a portable, pocket-sized sensing device, the MinION, making it ideal for cultural heritage studies. For the Leonardo drawings, Piñar et al. combined the Nanopore sequencing with a whole-genome-amplification protocol.

对于最新的论文，Piñar的团队转向了第三代测序方法，即纳米孔，该方法使用嵌入聚合物膜中的蛋白质纳米孔进行测序。它配有便携式，袖珍型感应设备MinION，非常适合文化遗产研究。对于Leonardo的绘画，Piñar等人。结合了纳米孔测序和全基因组扩增方案。

The ICPAL conservators used a delicate, noninvasive microaspiration (i.e., filtering suction) sampling method to collect dust particles, microbial cells, and other debris from small surface areas on both the recto and verso of each drawing. Then the DNA was extracted, amplified, and sequenced. The Austrian team used optical microscopy to image features of interest in all seven drawings and scanning electron microscopy (SEM) to analyze all the micro-objects gathered from the drawings.

ICPAL保护人员使用精细，无创的微抽吸（即过滤抽吸）采样方法从每幅图的正反两面的小表面积收集灰尘颗粒，微生物细胞和其他碎屑。然后提取DNA，扩增并测序。奥地利团队使用光学显微镜对所有七幅图纸中的感兴趣特征进行成像，并使用扫描电子显微镜（SEM）分析从图纸中收集的所有微观对象。

Each drawing had its own unique microbiome—an "independent molecular profile or biological pedigree." But Piñar et al. were surprised to find that, overall, bacteria dominated fungi in the drawings' microbiomes, contradicting widespread belief that fungi would be more dominant, given their higher potential to colonize on paper-based works. The researchers detected no visible biodeterioration on the drawing, apart from foxing stains (small yellow-brown spots or blotches).

每张图纸都有自己独特的微生物组-独立的分子图谱或生物学谱系。但是Piñar等。惊奇地发现，总体上，细菌占主导地位的真菌。微生物群系，因为人们普遍认为真菌在纸质作品上具有更高的潜力，这与人们普遍认为真菌将占主导地位背道而驰。研究人员除发现foxing污渍（黄褐色小斑点或斑点）外，在图纸上未发现明显的生物恶化现象。

Much of those bacteria are typically found in human microbiomes, suggesting they found their way onto the drawings while being handled during restoration—although one could speculate about whether it came from the artist himself. (The authors note that bacteria in dust could "remain in suspension" for long periods.) Other bacteria were typical of insect microbiomes and may have been introduced long ago by flies depositing excrement on the drawings. Those droppings showed up under imaging analysis as waxy brown incrustations in the fibers.

这些细菌中的大多数通常在人类微生物区系中发现，这表明它们在修复过程中被发现时便进入了图纸，尽管人们可以推测出它是否来自艺术家本人。 （作者指出，灰尘中的细菌可能会长期保持悬浮状态。）其他细菌是昆虫微生物组的典型特征，可能是很早以前通过将粪便堆积在图纸上而引入的。这些残渣在影像分析中显示为纤维中的蜡状棕色结壳。

The Austrian/Italian team were unable to conclude definitively whether any of the microbial contaminants date back to Leonardo's time. It seems far more likely that the human microbial elements are due to more recent restoration work. This is most likely to be the case for the drawing designated L4 ( Studi delle gambe anteriori di un cavallo) in particular, which showed the least biodiversity and the heaviest contamination from human DNA. The authors hypothesize further that the recipe Leonardo used—"a preparatory layer made with powdered calcinated chicken bones, white lead, indigo... mixed with animal gelatin"—may have interfered with the preservation of the microbiome of L4, so only recent DNA remained.

奥地利/意大利小组无法最终确定是否有任何微生物污染物可以追溯到莱昂纳多的时间。人类微生物元素似乎更有可能是由于最近的修复工作所致。特别是指定为L4的图纸（最有可能的生物）和来自人类DNA的污染最严重的情况就是这种情况。作者进一步假设，莱昂纳多使用的配方-由煅烧的鸡骨粉，白铅，靛蓝...与动物明胶混合制成的预备层-可能会干扰L4微生物组的保存。 ，因此只剩下最近的DNA。

But Piñar insists that being able to track this kind of data is nonetheless highly valuable. "The sensitivity of the Nanopore sequencing method offers a great tool for the monitoring of objects of art. It allows the assessment of the microbiomes and the visualization of its variations due to detrimental situations," she said. "This can be used as a bio-archive of the objects' history, providing a kind of fingerprint for current and future comparisons."

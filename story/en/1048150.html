Russia's Secretive Space Cannon, the Only Gun Ever Fired in Space
俄罗斯的秘密太空大炮，这是太空中唯一发射的枪

2021-02-18 07:20:05

Based on a design that was intended to protect bombers, the R-23M cannon had an unusual and closely guard design, to begin with.

R-23M加农炮基于旨在保护轰炸机的设计，首先具有非同寻常且严密的防护设计。

More than four decades after the Soviet Union's 23mm R-23M automatic cannon became the first and only gun to have actually been fired in space, at least that we know about, a new picture of this secretive weapon has emerged. The image offers the clearest look to date of the entire Shchit-1 self-defense system that armed the Almaz OPS-2 military space station, which orbited the Earth for around eight months between 1974 and 1975.

苏联的23毫米R-23M自动加农炮成为第一门也是唯一一门实际上已经在太空发射的枪，至少在我们所知道的四十年后，这种秘密武器的新图景出现了。该图像提供了武装Almaz OPS-2军用空间站的整个Shchit-1自卫系统的最清晰外观，该系统在1974年至1975年之间绕地球旋转了八个月。

Russian author Anatoly Zak, who also runs the website RussianSpaceWeb.com, spotted the Shchit-1 system in images the Russian Ministry of Defense released of Defense Minister Sergei Shoigu's visit to NPO Mashinostroyenia earlier in 2021. NPO Mashinostroyenia, originally known simply as OKB-52, is a design bureau best known for the development of missiles, rockets, and spacecraft, including work on the Almaz military space program during the Cold War.

俄罗斯作家阿纳托利·扎克（Anatoly Zak）也在网站RussianSpaceWeb.com上运营，在俄罗斯国防部国防部长谢尔盖·绍伊古（Sergei Shoigu）2021年初访问NPO Mashinostroyenia的照片中发现了Shchit-1系统。就像OKB-52一样，是一个设计局，以开发导弹，火箭和航天器而闻名，其中包括冷战时期的Almaz军事太空计划。

For years, the R-23M and the Almaz program were both wrapped in multiple layers of secrecy for various reasons. The fact that the OPS-2 space station was armed at all did not emerge until after the fall of the Soviet Union in 1991. There was only one available picture of the R-23M prior to 2015, and not a very good one, which may have only reflected a prototype design and did not show the complete Shchit-1 system. In 2015, a special aired on TV Zvezda, the official television channel of the Russian Ministry of Defense, which included the first real look, seen above, of the entire space-based configuration.

多年来，由于各种原因，R-23M和Almaz程序都被包裹在多层保密中。直到1991年苏联解体之后，才真正拥有OPS-2空间站的事实。在2015年之前只有一张R-23M的照片，而并不是一张很好的照片，可能只反映了原型设计，而没有显示完整的Shchit-1系统。 2015年，俄罗斯国防部官方电视频道Zvezda电视开播了特别节目，其中包括整个天基配置的第一张真实影像，如上图所示。

Development of the standard terrestrial R-23, designed primarily as self-defense weapons for bombers, began in the 1950s and was treated as a highly sensitive project. The brainchild of Aron Rikhter at KB Tochmash, a design bureau then headed up by Aleksandr Nudelman, the idea had been to craft a more compact gun that would be easier to fit inside an aircraft and particularly well-suited for turreted applications. Rikhter had worked directly with Nudelman on the earlier, unrelated 23mm NR-23, as well as the larger 30mm NR-30, both of which saw widespread use on Soviet aircraft.

标准陆地R-23的开发始于1950年代，最初被设计为轰炸机的自卫武器，被视为高度敏感的项目。由亚历山大·纳德曼（Aleksandr Nudelman）领导的设计局KB Tochmash的阿隆·里赫特（Aron Rikhter）的想法是，制造一种更紧凑的枪支，这种枪支更易于安装在飞机内部，特别适合于炮塔应用。 Rikhter直接与Nudelman合作开发了较早的，不相关的23mm NR-23，以及较大的30mm NR-30，两者都在苏联飞机上得到了广泛使用。

The R-23 is a so-called revolver cannon, which, as its name indicates, feeds ammunition into the firing position by way of a number of revolver-like cylinders. This feed mechanism, which is similar in some very broad respects to Gatling-type designs, allows for a higher rate of fire than can be achieved with more conventional single-barrel guns.

R-23是所谓的左轮大炮，顾名思义，它通过许多类似左轮手枪的弹药将弹药送入射击位置。这种进给机构在某些方面与盖特林式设计非常相似，与传统的单管火炮相比，其射速更高。

However, Rikhter's gun, unlike most other revolver cannons, feeds ammunition from the magazine backward into the cylinders, rather than from the rear or the side. This places the feed mechanism in the center of the gun, helping to shorten its overall length.

但是，与大多数其他左轮手炮不同，Rikhter的枪将弹药从弹匣向后送入圆柱体，而不是从后方或侧面。这会将进纸机构放置在喷枪的中央，有助于缩短其总长度。

On top of that, the R-23 uses unique 23x260mm cartridges that have a so-called telescoped configuration. This means that the projectile is nestled completely inside the cartridge case, rather than sticking out of the front. The general concept behind ammunition of this type is to shorten the overall length of the round, and, by extension, the weapon that fires it, without any significant loss of performance over a gun firing a similar traditional round.

最重要的是，R-23使用独特的23x260mm墨盒，具有所谓的伸缩式配置。这意味着弹丸完全套在弹壳内，而不是从前部伸出。这种弹药背后的一般概念是缩短弹药的总长度，并通过扩大其发射武器，而不会像传统的类似弹药那样显着降低性能。

The Tupolev Tu-22 Blinder bomber was the first aircraft to be designed to carry the R-23, with one of the guns to be mounted in a remote-controlled, radar-directed turret in the tail. Delays in the development of the cannon meant that the first Blinders entered service years before their tail guns were ready.

图波列夫（Tupolev）Tu-22 Blinder轰炸机是设计用于运载R-23的第一架飞机，其中一门火炮安装在机尾的遥控雷达定向炮塔中。加农炮的开发延误意味着第一批Blinder在他们的尾炮准备好之前已经服役了数年。

In the end, the Tu-22 was the only aircraft to be armed with the R-23. This helped keep the very existence of the gun largely secret until the first Blinders were exported to Iraq and Libya in the 1970s. An actual example did not reportedly fall into Western hands until one was recovered from the wreckage of a Libyan Tu-22 that French forces had shot down over Chad in 1987. That conflict between Chad and Libya, in general, was an intelligence bonanza for the West.

最后，Tu-22是唯一配备R-23的飞机。直到1970年代第一批Blinders出口到伊拉克和利比亚之前，这基本上都将枪的存在保密了。据报道，一个实际例子并没有落入西方人的手中，直到一个人从1987年法国部队在乍得炸毁的利比亚Tu-22残骸中找到了一个。乍得与利比亚之间的冲突，总体而言，是该国情报的大富翁。西。

It's not clear whether or not the development of the R-23M for space-based applications contributed to the secrecy surrounding the weapon or if it was chosen because it was so closely guarded to start with. Its compact nature no doubt helped make it an obvious choice for attaching to a spacecraft, where weight and physical space are always at a premium.

目前尚不清楚用于天基应用的R-23M的开发是否对武器的保密性有所贡献，还是因为一开始就被严密保护而选择了它。毫无疑问，它的紧凑性使其成为附着在航天器上的明显选择，因为航天器的重量和物理空间总是很重要的。

Based on the still limited information that has emerged to date, the Shchit-1 self-defense system consisted of a single R-23M cannon in a rigid mount with a magazine mounted on top of the center portion of the gun. What modifications may have been made to the weapon itself to ensure it would function properly in zero gravity are unclear.

根据迄今仍未得到的有限信息，Shchit-1自卫系统由一个装在刚性安装架中的R-23M大炮和一个装在弹枪中央部分顶部的弹匣组成。目前尚不清楚对武器本身进行哪些修改以确保其在零重力下正常运行。

"From the dawn of the Space Age, the secrecy-obsessed Soviet military was terrified by the prospect of American spacecraft approaching and inspecting Soviet military satellites—which, according to the Kremlin's propaganda, were not even supposed to exist," Anatoly Zak wrote in a piece on the R-23M for Popular Mechanics in 2015. "This wasn't crazy. The fear of attack on spacecraft was real, with both sides of the Iron Curtain developing anti-satellite weapons. It seemed perfectly logical in the 1960s that military and piloted spacecraft would need self-defense weapons."

从太空时代开始，对秘密保密的苏联军方对美国飞船接近并检查苏联军方卫星的前景感到恐惧-根据克里姆林宫的宣传，这甚至不应该存在，＆＃34;阿纳托利·扎克（Anatoly Zak）在2015年发表的《 R-23M流行力学》一文中写道。这并不疯狂。铁幕的两边都在研发反卫星武器，这种担心确实存在。在1960年代，军事和飞行员航天器将需要自卫武器似乎是完全合乎逻辑的。

The Almaz military space stations, work on which first began in the early 1960s, were intended to be armed from the beginning. Before shifting to the R-23M, Zak says that KB Tochmash had been working on a compact 14.5mm weapon for space-based use.

始于1960年代初的Almaz军事空间站原本打算从一开始就武装起来。扎克说，在转移到R-23M之前，KB Tochmash一直在研究一种紧凑的14.5毫米太空武器。

The Almaz stations were expected to be multi-purpose military platforms in space. The plan was for the first types to be configured primarily for intelligence, surveillance, and reconnaissance missions with cameras and other sensors. Delays with the development of the sensor package held up the entire program. In 1971, the Soviet Union launched a civilian space station, Salyut, that incorporated some of the design work developed under the Almaz program, together with components from the venerable Soyuz spacecraft.

预计Almaz站将成为太空中的多功能军事平台。该计划是针对第一种类型的，主要配置为使用摄像头和其他传感器进行情报，监视和侦察任务。传感器套件的开发拖延了整个程序。 1971年，苏联发射了一个民用空间站Salyut，其中包括了Almaz计划下开发的一些设计工作以及古老的联盟号飞船的组件。

The Salyut program ultimately provided a cover for the Almaz space stations. Of the seven Salyut space stations launched between 1971 and 1991, three were actually military types.

礼炮计划最终为Almaz空间站提供了掩护。在1971年至1991年之间发射的七个礼炮空间站中，实际上有三个是军事类型的。

The first Almaz station, referred to publicly as Salyut-2 and also known as OPS-1, was launched in 1973. However, an accident on board shortly after launch forced the Soviets to abandon it before an actual crew could be sent up to join it.

第一个Almaz站于1973年发射，公开称为Salyut-2，也称为OPS-1。但是，发射后不久在机上发生事故，迫使苏联人放弃了该站，然后才派遣实际机组人员加入它。

OPS-2, also called Salyut-3, followed in 1974. This station, armed with the R-23M, successfully reached orbit on June 25 of that year, but a two-man crew did not arrive until July. They remained there for 15 days, during which they tested the onboard telescopic Agat-1 Earth-observation camera, which is said to have been able to produce imagery of the ground with a resolution of better than three meters.

OPS-2，也称为Salyut-3，于1974年问世。配备R-23M的该站于当年6月25日成功到达轨道，但只有两个人的乘员组直到7月才到达。他们在那里呆了15天，在此期间，他们对车载望远镜Agat-1的地球观测相机进行了测试，据说该相机能够产生分辨率超过三米的地面图像。

A second crew failed to reach the station later in 1974 after their Soyuz spacecraft suffered a malfunction. The third mission to OPS-2 was subsequently canceled and it operated in an unmanned mode until it deorbited on January 24, 1975. In September 1974, personnel on the ground remotely ejected a pod with film from the Agat-1 camera, which subsequently returned to Earth and was recovered.

1974年晚些时候，联盟号飞船发生故障后，第二名乘员未能到达该站。随后取消了对OPS-2的第三次飞行任务，并一直以无人驾驶模式运行，直到1975年1月24日脱离轨道。1974年9月，地面人员从Agat-1照相机远程弹出了一个装有胶卷的吊舱，随后该吊舱返回到地球并被回收。

The test of the R-23M cannon occurred on the station's very last day in orbit. Without any way to fully simulate on the ground how firing the gun would impact OPS-2, the plan had always been to test it in orbit without anyone on board. The shock and recoil not only had the potential to damage the station but, in the vacuum of space, those forces raised the possibility that shooting the weapon could send it flying in a dangerously unpredictable fashion.

R-23M加农炮的测试是在太空站的最后一天进行的。由于没有任何方法可以在地面上完全模拟枪支发射对OPS-2的影响，因此该计划一直是在没有任何人员在机的情况下在轨道上进行测试。冲击和后坐力不仅有可能损坏驻地，而且在太空真空中，这些部队增加了射击武器可能使它以危险的无法预测的方式飞行的可能性。

"The outpost [OPS-2] ignited its jet thrusters simultaneously with firing the cannon to counteract the weapon's powerful recoil," Anatoly Zak explained in the story for Popular Mechanics back in 2015. "According to various sources, the cannon fired from one to three blasts [bursts], reportedly firing around 20 shells in all. They burned up in the atmosphere, too."

＆＃34;前哨[OPS-2]在发射大炮的同时点燃了其喷气推进器，以抵消武器强大的后坐力，阿纳托利·扎克（Anatoly Zak）在2015年为《大众机械》撰写的故事中对此作了解释。＆＃34;据各种消息来源，这门大炮发射了1到3次爆炸，据说总共发射了20发炮弹。他们也在大气中燃烧。＆＃34;

Exactly what happened during the test remains classified to this day. The next Almaz space station, known as OPS-3 and Salyut-5, had no weaponry on board, at least that we know of. Plans for an OPS-4 station included a new Shchit-2 self-defense system that was reportedly designed to fire missile-like interceptors, but no pictures of that weapon are known to exist publicly.

测试期间发生的确切情况至今仍归类。下一个Almaz空间站，称为OPS-3和Salyut-5，至少没有我们知道的机载武器。 OPS-4站的计划包括一个新的Shchit-2自卫系统，据报道该系统设计为可发射类似导弹的拦截器，但已知该武器的图片尚未公开。

The Soviets did also develop specialized self-defense guns for cosmonauts, such as the TP-82 seen in the video below, but these were intended to be used on Earth, not in space. You can read more about them in this past War Zone piece.

苏联也确实为宇航员开发了专用的自卫枪，例如下面的视频中所看到的TP-82，但这些枪是要在地球上而不是太空中使用的。您可以在上一个战区文章中阅读有关它们的更多信息。

How effective the Shchit-1 system would ever have been had it seen more widespread use is certainly debatable. The fixed mounting meant that the entire space station had to be rotated to aim it, which would reportedly have been done using a simple optical sight.

如果看到Shchit-1系统得到更广泛的使用，它到底有多有效，这值得商de。固定的安装方式意味着必须旋转整个空间站才能对准目标，据报道这是使用简单的光学瞄准镜完成的。

In the years since the test of the R-23M in space, the prospect of attacks on satellites and other spacecraft certainly hasn't gone away, nor has an interest in weapons that could be used in orbit for defensive or offensive purposes. Last year, the head of U.S. Space Force, the U.S. military's newest branch, publicly accused Russia of having tested an on-orbit projectile-based anti-satellite weapon. It's interesting to note that what little we know about the Shchit-2 system would seem to fit this general description.

自从在太空中对R-23M进行测试以来，对卫星和其他航天器的攻击前景肯定没有消失，对用于防御或进攻目的的在轨武器也没有兴趣。去年，美国军方最新分支机构美国太空部队负责人公开指责俄罗斯测试了一种基于在轨弹丸的反卫星武器。有趣的是，我们对Shchit-2系统了解甚少，似乎适合这种一般描述。

At the same time, Russia has also been testing relatively small inspector satellites, which have the ability to get very close to other objects in space. This could allow them to function as orbital spies, as well as " killer satellites," using various means of attack to damage, disable, or destroy a target, including by simply smashing into it.

同时，俄罗斯也一直在测试相对较小的检查卫星，这些卫星具有非常接近太空中其他物体的能力。这可以让它们充当轨道间谍以及＆＃34;杀手级卫星，＆＃34;使用各种攻击手段来破坏，破坏或摧毁目标，包括简单地砸入目标。

These kinds of threats have prompted discussion in other countries, including the United States and France, about the development of potential countermeasures, if they don't exist already in the classified realm. The French military, specifically, says it is now working on space-based "guardian satellites" that will be armed with lasers, though it's unclear if they will be powerful enough to physically damage or destroy an incoming threat or will just be designed as dazzlers to blind or confuse enemy sensor and seeker systems.

这些威胁促使其他国家（包括美国和法国）就潜在对策的发展进行讨论，如果它们尚未存在于机密领域中。特别是法国军方表示，目前正在研究天基“卫士”卫星。虽然尚不清楚它们是否强大到足以物理损坏或摧毁即将到来的威胁，还是只是被设计成使敌人目眩或迷惑敌方传感器和搜寻器系统的武器，但尚不清楚。

The story of the R-23M certainly highlights just how complex and sensitive the matter of developing even the most basic weapons for space-based applications remains, a full 46 years after the Soviets conducted the one known test of an automatic cannon in orbit.

R-23M的故事无疑凸显了即使为太空应用开发最基本的武器，仍然存在着多么复杂和敏感的问题，这是在苏联人进行了一次已知的自动轨道大炮试验整整46年之后。

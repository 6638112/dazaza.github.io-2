Privacy matters even if “you have nothing to hide”
即使“您无所可藏”，隐私也很重要

2020-12-14 22:25:37

The overwhelming majority of people do not care about privacy. They ask why they should care if they have “nothing to hide”. Yet this idea of hiding is fundamentally flawed.

绝大多数人不关心隐私。他们问为什么他们应该关心自己是否“没有隐藏”。但是，这种隐藏的想法从根本上是有缺陷的。

The United Nations state that privacy is a human right, just like the right to education is. So why on earth would you give that up? The famous (and somewhat obligatory) quote from Edward Snowden, the NSA whistleblower:

联合国指出，隐私是一项人权，就像受教育的权利一样。那你为什么要放弃呢？国家安全局举报人爱德华·斯诺登（Edward Snowden）的著名（有些强制性）名言：

Arguing that you don't care about the right to privacy because you have nothing to hide is no different than saying you don't care about free speech because you have nothing to say.

争辩说您不关心隐私权是因为您没有什么可隐瞒的，这与说您不在乎言论自由是因为您无话可说。

The question about privacy is not about hiding, it is about sharing. It is not that I have nothing to hide, I just do not have anything I want to share, especially unknowingly. This whole narrative that privacy is only for nefarious people is pushed by those who quite clearly care alot about privacy. Mark Zuckerberg spent tens of millions of dollars buying the houses that surrounded his home, for example. The irony is painful.

关于隐私的问题不是关于隐藏，而是关于共享。不是我没有什么可隐藏的，我只是没有任何我想分享的东西，尤其是在不知不觉中。隐私仅适用于邪恶人群的整个叙述是由那些非常清楚地关心隐私的人推动的。例如，马克·扎克伯格（Mark Zuckerberg）花了数千万美元购买了包围他房屋的房屋。具有讽刺意味的是痛苦的。

We all care about our privacy, though we might not know it. It is why we lock the door when we go to the toilet; or why we close the curtains to stop people looking into our homes.

尽管我们可能并不了解我们的隐私，但我们都在乎我们的隐私。这就是为什么我们上厕所时要锁门的原因。或为什么我们关闭窗帘以阻止人们进入我们的房屋。

In her essay for Aeon Magazine, Carissa Véliz argued that privacy is power. It is the power “to show you ads... and to predict your behaviour”. But it is also the power to influence. Increasingly, Big Tech, the likes of Facebook, Amazon and Microsoft, are sharing more and more information with governments. In turn, governments are starting to learn more and more about their citizens. Facebook allows governments to arrest people planning to participate in protests before they have even begun – and this is the tip of the iceberg.

CarissaVéliz在《 Aeon Magazine》的论文中指出，隐私就是力量。它是“向您展示广告...并预测您的行为”的力量。但这也是影响力。像Facebook，Amazon和Microsoft之类的Big Tech越来越多地与政府共享越来越多的信息。反过来，政府也开始越来越多地了解其公民。 Facebook允许政府逮捕计划参加抗议活动的人，甚至还没有开始-这是冰山一角。

Our privacy is eroding. As a society, we are beginning to accept this lack of privacy to be normal – and this is extremely dangerous. Privacy provides a place for us to be ourselves, to express ourselves in new ways without fear of being judged by preying eyes. If we lose privacy then we lose this ability to experiment, and, more importantly, we lose our power.

我们的隐私正在受到侵蚀。作为一个社会，我们开始接受这种缺乏隐私的现象，这是正常现象–这是极其危险的。隐私为我们提供了一个成为自己的地方，以新的方式表达自己，而不必担心被掠夺者的眼睛判断。如果我们失去了隐私，那么我们将失去进行实验的能力，更重要的是，我们将失去力量。

The war is not lost, however. We paved the path for Big Tech, though we no longer have to follow it. If we change our actions then a lot can be achieved.

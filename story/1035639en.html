Futurism Transformed the Art World by Worshipping Technology (2014)
未来主义通过崇拜技术改变了艺术世界（2014）

2020-11-21 21:24:57

Skip to main content The late 19th and early 20th centuries gave birth to modern marvels previous generations could scarcely dream of. Many of those marvels rested squarely in the realm of technology; Edison's practical incandescent lamp was newly patented, the Ford Model T had just reached the assembly line, and powered flight was finally more than just a sketch in da Vinci's codices. With all this going on, no group got quite as swept up in technology's promise for the future as the Italian Futurists. For them, planes, trains, and automobiles weren't just the avant garde. In many ways, the future was a way of life, consuming national identity and even approaching religious experience.

跳转到主要内容19世纪末和20世纪初，前几代人几乎梦dream以求的现代奇迹诞生了。这些奇迹很多都落在技术领域。爱迪生的实用白炽灯获得了新的专利，福特T型车刚刚进入装配线，动力飞行最终不仅仅是达芬奇抄袭中的草图。在所有这些过程中，没有哪个团队比意大利未来主义者更早地获得了技术对未来的承诺。对他们而言，飞机，火车和汽车不只是前卫。在许多方面，未来是一种生活方式，消耗着民族身份甚至接近宗教经验。

Futurism got its start in 1909 with Filippo Tommaso Marinetti. A poet and fierce nationalist, Marinetti penned The Futurist Manifesto after a drive in Milan resulted in a (evidently life-altering) minor car accident. In the manifesto — the first of many written in the years that followed — he exalts speed and violence, along with a wholesale rejection of the past in favor of a technological present. In one of his many exultations, he writes, "We declare that the splendor of the world has been enriched by a new beauty: the beauty of speed."

未来主义始于1909年的Filippo Tommaso Marinetti。 Marinetti是一位诗人和凶猛的民族主义者，在米兰驾车导致（显然改变生命的）轻微车祸后，写下了《未来主义者宣言》。在宣言中（此后多年中的第一个宣言），他高举了速度和暴力，并全面摒弃了过去，转而支持技术现在。在他的众多狂喜之一中，他写道：“我们宣布，世界的辉煌已经被一种新的美感所丰富：速度之美。”

"We declare that the splendor of the world has been enriched by a new beauty: the beauty of speed." Marinetti drew in followers quickly, including preeminent artists like Umberto Boccioni, who would all experiment with Marinetti's ideals in painting, sculpture, music, poetry, photography, and film. Futurism became a major force in the art world and a bona fide social movement, albeit a contentious and often paradoxical one. It was unabashedly anti-woman, but Benedetta Cappa was both a major figure in its history and Marinetti's wife. Futurists called for anarchy, but found themselves often aligned (perhaps unavoidably) with Mussolini's Fascists. Nevertheless, their art was incredibly influential up until Marinetti's death in 1944, informing later movements like Art Deco and Dadaism.

“我们宣布，世界的辉煌已被一种新的美感所丰富：速度之美。” Marinetti迅速吸引了追随者，其中包括像Umberto Boccioni这样的杰出艺术家，他们都将在绘画，雕塑，音乐，诗歌，摄影和电影方面尝试Marinetti的理想。未来主义已成为艺术界的主要力量和真诚的社会运动，尽管它是有争议的，而且常常是自相矛盾的运动。它毫不掩饰地是反女的，但贝内塔塔·卡帕（Benedetta Cappa）既是其历史上的重要人物，也是马里内蒂（Marinetti）的妻子。未来主义者呼吁无政府状态，但发现自己经常与墨索里尼的法西斯主义者结盟（也许不可避免）。然而，直到马里内蒂（Marinetti）在1944年去世之前，他们的艺术才具有不可思议的影响力，为后来的运动（例如装饰艺术和达达主义）提供了信息。

The Guggenheim Museum in New York City is currently showing an exhibit entitled Italian Futurism, 1909-1944: Reconstructing the Universe. There, some of the finest examples of Futurist art can be found, some of which you can see below. Take the trip if you get the chance.

纽约市的古根海姆博物馆目前正在展出名为1909-1944年的意大利未来主义：重建宇宙的展览。在这里，您可以找到一些未来主义艺术的最好例子，其中一些您可以在下面看到。如果有机会，那就去旅行。

Boccioni, as both a painter and sculptor, was a central figure in Futurism. 'Unique Forms of Continuity in Space,' wherein he depicts the fluid movement of the human body, is considered one of the movement's masterpieces and currently appears on the Italian 20 cent euro coin. (© The Metropolitan Museum of Art)

博西奥尼既是画家又是雕塑家，是未来主义的中心人物。 “独特的空间连续性形式”描述了人体的流畅运动，被认为是运动的杰作之一，目前出现在意大利20欧元硬币上。 （©大都会艺术博物馆）

Futurism's artists glorified speed and motion, and painter Giacomo Balla helped capture both in the movement's first wave. In 'The Hand of the Violinist,' the musician's hand is made to look like it's vibrating through space. (© 2014 Artists Rights Society (ARS), New York / SIAE, Rome)

未来主义的艺术家美化了速度和动作，画家贾科莫·巴拉（Giacomo Balla）帮助捕捉了运动的第一波风潮。在“小提琴手”中，音乐家的手看起来像是在太空中振动一样。 （©2014艺术家权利协会（ARS），纽约/ SIAE，罗马）

In the years following World War I, Futurism's "cult of the machine" adored the train as a symbol of speed, power, and modernity — ideals they made frequent mention of in their many manifestos. This ethos was captured in their 'arte meccanica' (machine aesthetics), of which 'Speeding Train' is a perfect example. (Courtesy of Fondazione Cassa di risparmio della Provincia di Macerata)

第一次世界大战后的几年中，未来主义的“机器崇拜”将火车视为速度，力量和现代性的象征，他们在许多宣言中都经常提及这些理想。这种精神体现在他们的“机械美学”（机械美学）中，其中“超速火车”就是一个很好的例子。 （由Fondazione Cassa di risparmio della Provincia di Macerata提供）

Futurists had the lofty goal of "reconstructing the universe." As such, their art extended beyond paintings and sculpture and into furniture and even toys. Dottori, for example, created this vision of a dining room set to achieve the 'opera d'arte totale' or total work of art. (© 2014 Artists Rights Society (ARS), New York / SIAE, Rome the Peggy Guggenheim Collection, Venice)

未来主义者的崇高目标是“重建宇宙”。因此，他们的艺术范围从绘画和雕塑扩展到家具甚至玩具。例如，多托里（Dottori）创造了餐厅愿景，以实现“整体艺术”或整体艺术品。 （©2014艺术家权利协会（ARS），纽约/ SIAE，罗马佩吉古根海姆美术馆收藏，威尼斯）

Benedetta was a highly-regarded artist and writer, and just happened to be married to Futurism's founder F.T. Marinetti. 'Synthesis of Aerial Communications' is one of three major murals she painted, depicting telecommunications over air, land, and sea. Like all Futurist works of art, it's forward-looking, but the mural is also (somewhat paradoxically) meant to evoke the ancient frescoes of Pompeii. (© Benedetta Cappa Marinetti, used by permission of Vittoria Marinetti and Luce Marinetti’s heirs)

贝内德塔（Benedetta）是一位备受推崇的艺术家和作家，刚好嫁给了未来主义的创始人F.T.马林蒂。 “空中通信综合”是她绘制的三幅主要壁画之一，描绘了空中，陆地和海上的电信。像所有未来主义艺术品一样，它具有前瞻性，但壁画（有点自相矛盾）意在唤起庞贝古壁画。 （©Benedetta Cappa Marinetti，经Vittoria Marinetti和Luce Marinetti的继承人许可使用）

'Aeropittura' or 'aero painting' was the last major expression of Futurism's second wave. By then, painters had moved on to the airplane as the height of technological advancement, especially as Mussolini's Fascists exalted Italy's military prowess in the air. Artists like Crali experimented with dizzying perspectives and views of iconic Italian landscapes. (© 2014 Artists Rights Society (ARS), New York/SIAE, Rome)

“ Aeropittura”或“航空绘画”是未来主义第二波的最后一个主要表达。那时，随着技术进步的高度，画家们已经搬上飞机，尤其是墨索里尼的法西斯主义者高举空中的意大利军事实力。像Crali这样的艺术家尝试着以令人眼花with乱的视角观察意大利标志性景观。 （©2014艺术家权利协会（ARS），纽约/ SIAE，罗马）

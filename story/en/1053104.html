Intel Rocket Lake-S desktop gaming CPUs are here

2021-03-18 04:23:25

This week, Intel announced their 11th generation S-series desktop CPUs, code named Rocket Lake-S. These are gaming-oriented processors optimized for high clock rates and performance, coming in 19 SKUs ranging from i5-11400T through 19-11900K.

本周，英特尔宣布了第11代S系列桌面CPU，代码名为Rocket Lake-s。这些是针对赌博的处理器，优化了高时的时钟速率和性能，从I5-11400T到19-11900K的19个SKU。

The new chips, based on Intel's Cypress Cove architecture, claim up to 19% increase in Instructions per Clock cycle—a very familiar figure, since it's the same number AMD claimed for gen-on-gen IPC uplift between its Zen 2 and Zen 3 architectures. We'll do some hands-on benchmarking in the near future to determine how important the "up to" hedge on that claim matters. In the meantime, we're cautiously optimistic about the up to 19% IPC and up to 50% iGPU performance Intel is claiming. As usual, the really big numbers Intel shows for the new generation of processors don't have much to do with general purpose CPU performance—they're tied pretty directly to finding AVX-512 optimized workloads. But the 19% isn't tied to AVX-512, and it has not come at the price of reduced clock speeds or higher rated TDP either.

Intel is also releasing a new Z590 motherboard chipset. Boards built on Z590 will offer 20 PCI Express 4.0 lanes, USB 3.2 Gen 2x2 20Gbps ports, Thunderbolt 4, and improved Direct Memory Interface. The new boards will also support 10th gen processors—but those older CPUs don't have PCIe 4.0 support, so don't expect a new Z590 board to unlock it.

英特尔也释放了新的Z590主板芯片组。基于Z590的板将提供20个PCI Express 4.0泳道，USB 3.2 Gen 2x2 20Gbps端口，Thunderbolt 4和改进的直接存储器接口。新董事会还将支持第10个Gen处理器 - 但那些旧的CPU Don＆＃39; T有PCIe 4.0支持，所以不要期望一个新的Z590董事会解锁它。

Rocket Lake-S CPUs are incompatible with H410 or B460 boards, and compatibility with Z490 is uncertain at best. If you want a Rocket Lake-S CPU, we strongly recommend a new Z590 board to go with it.

In another familiar story for Intel, what the new generation does give up is core count—although only at the Core i9 level. The i9-10900K was a 10 core, 125W TDP part with maximum single-core boost of 5.3GHz; the i9-11900K matches the TDP and frequency but drops to eight cores. Core i7 and Core i5 CPUs remain 8c/16t and 6c/12t, respectively.

制作一个热处理烤箱

2020-10-18 00:29:00

This project has been on my to do list for quite some time. About three years ago I bought the parts needed to get it done, but never did set aside the time needed to actually do it. I can give all kinds of reasons for not building it sooner, but primarily I didn’t have a real need for it and no definite projects that use it, until now. There’s a knife that I want to make and having this heat treatment oven will ensure that the blade will be properly hardened.

这个项目在我的待办事项清单上已经有很长一段时间了。大约三年前，我买了完成它所需的部件，但从来没有预留出实际完成它所需的时间。我可以给出各种不尽快建造它的理由，但主要是直到现在，我并没有真正需要它，也没有明确的项目使用它。我想做一把刀，用这个热处理烤箱可以确保刀刃得到适当的硬化。

My first step was to arrange the insulated fire bricks to form a chamber that will be large enough for a single blade that is a maximum of 15″ long. These bricks are 9″ long, 4-1/2″ wide and 2-1/2″ thick and are rated for 2300 degrees Fahrenheit:

我的第一步是布置隔热耐火砖，形成一个房间，这个房间足够大，可以容纳最多15英寸长的单个刀片。这些砖长9“，宽4-1/2”，厚2-1/2“，额定温度为2300华氏度：

This leaves a chamber that is 4″ wide, 4-1/2″ high and 15-1/2″ deep, which is plenty of space for any single blade that I would make. Of course, knives are not the only thing that needs heat treating – plane blades and chisels or other tools can also benefit greatly from precise hardening.

这就留下了一个4“宽，4-1/2”高，15-1/2“深的腔室，这对于我要做的任何一个刀片来说都是足够的空间。当然，刀具并不是唯一需要热处理的东西-平面刀片和凿子或其他工具也可以从精确淬火中受益匪浅。

I’m making the outer case of the oven from 18 gauge sheet metal salvaged from doors that I cut windows into, back while doing my old day job. I cut the parts and tacked them together so that the bricks would fit inside snuggly, then just tacked the parts together using the welder:

我在做日常工作的时候，正在用门上打捞出来的18英寸的金属板做烤箱的外壳，这些金属是我把窗户切进门里的。我把零件切开，然后把它们钉在一起，这样砖块就可以紧贴在里面了，然后就用焊接机把零件钉在一起就行了：

I made a handle to easily move the oven. Notice that it is offset toward the front to make up for extra weight of the door:

我做了一个手柄，可以很容易地移动烤箱。请注意，它朝前偏移，以弥补门的额外重量：

Speaking of the door, it is swung on inexpensive tee hinges that I welded on. I used a piece of sheet metal to shim the door up to space it away from the case slightly so that it won’t bind:

说到门，它是在我焊接的便宜的T形铰链上转动的。我用一块金属片把门垫起来，使它稍微远离箱子，这样它就不会粘合了：

To act as a handle and double as a latch, I welded on this strip:

为了充当把手和插销的双重作用，我焊接在这个条子上：

I added the rectangular nuts to the side for the latch to slip into. This mainly provides support for the door when closed to keep it from sagging:

我把矩形螺母加到一边，好让插销滑进去。这主要是在门关闭时提供支撑，以防止其下垂：

With the outside of the oven done for now, I turned my attention to grooving the bricks for the heating element to fit into. To do that I made this simple tool:

现在烤箱的外部已经做好了，我把注意力转到了为加热元件开槽的砖块上。为此，我制作了这个简单的工具：

Just a piece of sheet metal the right width screwed to a block, with another block that acts as an edge guide.

只需将一块宽度合适的钣金拧到一个块上，另一个块作为边缘导向即可。

The stretched out element was then put into the groove and I used staples made from the element wire to hold it in place:

然后将拉伸的元件放入槽中，我使用元件导线制成的订书机将其固定到位：

After I had it and the bricks installed in the oven, I hooked up a cord temporarily to test it:

在我把它和砖块装进烤箱后，我临时连接了一根绳子来测试它：

Next up, the controls for the oven centre around a PID to regulate the temperature, a thermocouple to sense the temperature inside the oven, and a relay to switch the element off and on. This schematic shows the basic circuit:

接下来，烤箱的控制器围绕着一个PID来调节温度，一个热电偶来感测烤箱内部的温度，以及一个继电器来开关元件。此原理图显示了基本电路：

My oven operates on 120VAC and has an element that is around 1500 watts, so I used a 15 amp circuit breaker in place of the fuse and switch combination.

我的烤箱工作在120VAC上，有一个大约1500瓦的元件，所以我用了一个15安培的断路器来代替保险丝和开关的组合。

Where the element leads come through the side of the case, I attached the connectors and used a piece of heat resistant perf board to act as an insulator to keep the connectors from touching the case:

在元件导线穿过外壳侧面的地方，我连接了连接器，并使用了一块耐热的性能板作为绝缘体，以防止连接器接触外壳：

The connection here must be mechanical, such as shown and the wires that connect to it should also be heat resistant.

这里的连接必须是机械的，如图所示，连接到它的电线也应该是耐热的。

The back panel of the control box is a piece of 1/8″ thick aluminum that also acts as the heatsink for the solid state relay:

控制箱的后面板是一块1/8英寸厚的铝板，也用作固态继电器的散热器：

I cut out the front panel with tabs on the edge to fasten it to the case with sheet metal screws. In the videos near the bottom of this page I show how I cut out the square hole for the PID and the rectangular hole for the circuit breaker / switch:

我剪下了前面板，边缘有卡舌，用钣金螺丝把它固定在箱子上。在本页底部附近的视频中，我将展示如何切割PID的方形孔和断路器/开关的矩形孔：

The wiring is done before the control box is assembled, since it’s easier to get everything hooked up and check for mistakes. Note the ground wire (green) that is fastened to the back panel of the control box. All exposed metal surfaces of an appliance like this need to be connected to this ground wire:

接线是在控制箱组装之前完成的，因为这样更容易将所有东西连接起来并检查是否有错误。注意固定在控制箱背板上的接地线(绿色)。像这样的电器的所有裸露金属表面都需要连接到此接地线：

With everything connected, I ran a test to make sure everything was working properly before putting on the cover:

所有东西都连接起来后，在盖上封面之前，我进行了一次测试，以确保所有东西都工作正常：

I set the PID for 1000 degrees Fahrenheit and it had no problem reaching that and holding it there.

我将PID设置为华氏1000度，它可以毫不费力地达到并保持在那里。

Next, I did a more practical test. I have this old file that I want to make a knife from an dit needs to be annealed (softened) before I can do that:

接下来，我做了一个更实际的测试。我有一个旧文件夹，我想从DIT制作一把刀，需要先退火(软化)，然后才能这样做：

To anneal a hardened metal, you heat it up to the right temperature and then leave it to cool slowly:

要使硬化的金属退火，您需要将其加热到合适的温度，然后让其慢慢冷却：

Since this file steel is close in properties to W1 steel, I used that and heated it up to 1400 degrees before shutting it off and leaving it to cool overnight:

由于此锉钢在性能上与W1钢相近，我使用了它并将其加热到1400度，然后将其关闭并让其隔夜冷却：

The next day the file was softened to the point where it could be filed with another file. It’s now ready to be worked and formed into the knife I want to make.

第二天，文件被软化到可以与另一个文件一起归档的程度。现在可以加工了，可以做成我想做的那把刀了。

The next test was to see if the oven would reach the maximum temperature that I want it to, and that’s 1900 degrees Fahrenheit for heat treating stainless steel:

下一个测试是看看烤箱是否会达到我想要的最高温度，那就是1900华氏度(对于热处理的不锈钢来说是1900华氏度)：

Running it at that high temp did heat up the case and the control box, so I thought that some vent holes would be smart to let some cool air circulate:

在这么高的温度下运行它确实会加热外壳和控制箱，所以我认为一些通风孔会很聪明，让一些冷空气流通：

Also at high heat, I noticed the door was coming slightly open, so I added these screw clamps to keep it tightly closed:

同样在高温下，我注意到门是微微打开的，所以我加了这些螺丝钳，让它紧紧地关着：

The oven will be more efficient if it keeps in as much of the heat as possible.

如果烤箱能尽可能多地保持热量，它的效率会更高。

I’ve no plans to paint it, since it is purely a utilitarian build and besides, I kind of like the industrial look it has.

我没有计划粉刷它，因为它纯粹是一个实用的建筑，而且我还有点喜欢它的工业外观。

This home was built in 1905 and remained a rental property throughout mostof its existence. In 2000, HP decided that they needed to acquire the houseso they could properly preserve its history. What was a modest middle classhome in the 1930s was bought for 1.7 million dollars in 2000. Over the next5 years, the house was restored back to what it was like in 1939 (exceptwhere compliance with modern code didn't allow.) In 2007, the property wasadded to the National Register of Historic Places. Dave Packard had gone to Schenectady to work at General Electric. He wastold that there was no future in electronics at General Electric and thathe should instead concentrate on generators, motors and other heavier equipment.Bill Hewlett was finishing up his graduate work at Stanford and the two decidedto pursue their earlier plan of starting their own business. The name HP(vs. PH) was chosen by a coin toss. For $45 per month, the Packards rentedthe first floor of the house, which was chosen specifically because it hada garage that they could work in. Bill Hewlett moved into the little shacknext to the garage. At first they didn't know what they were going to build so they experimentedwith ideas like using electrical currents to activate muscles for exercise.(HP never offered such a device but other companies did! Electrical deviceswere seen as the cure to many ills at this time.) Eventually they chose to build a new type of oscillator based on work BillHewlett did at Stanford and chose "200A" for their first model number togive the impression that they had been doing this for some time. They originallychose a price of $54.40 based on a historical slogan. When they later foundthat their closest competitor was a General Radio product that sold for $400,they decided to change the price to a still comparatively modest $71.50,which allowed them to better fund their young company. One of the first sales was to Disney. Disney wanted a number of changes whichresulted in the model 200B. Disney bought 8 of 200Bs to use in the productionof Fantasia. Dave Packard brought the used drill press back from GE in his car. Much of the work was done in the garage but there's only so far you can gowith a used drill press and hand tools so they also relied on friends. Forexample the dials were made by calibrating in the shop and marking the lineswith a pencil. Then Dave Packard took them to Charlie Litton's shop (LittonIndustries) and engraved them. Because Charlie Litton liked to work until2 or 3 AM, his equipment was free for use by Dave Packard in the mornings.Years later a flood destroyed much of Litton's equipment and HP's facilitieshad improved to the point that they could return the favor and keep Litton'sproducts shipping. At the end of the garage is a radio set. Dave Packard had an interest inradio since his childhood and some wiring that appeared to be an antennawas found while restoring the garage so it is believed that Dave engagedin his hobby here as well. Period wiring and switches are visible though the lights are really runningfrom hidden code-compliant wiring. The clear bulbs are there because that'swhat people expect of that era, though in reality, frosted bulbs were alreadycommon. If you look carefully you can spot some other updates appropriateto a landmark such as fire suppression and video surveillance. Finally, here is a snapshot of a picture on the wall of HP Building 3 onPage Mill Road. The door on the left leads to the first floor that Dave and Lu Packard rented.The door on the right leads to stairs to the second floor (where the landlady,Mrs. Spencer, lived.) An oscillator on the mantel? No home is complete without one! When Bill andDave sent out their first marketing material. the oscillator was placed onthe mantel for a photograph. They didn't expect much of a response from theirfirst attempt but they did receive orders - including orders with checks. From this angle, the dining room gives the impression of a large home, however,this dining room also contained a Murphy bed, and each night they moved thetable and chairs so they could lower the bed. Space was really quite tight. A few images from Lu Packard's scrapbook follow: The oscillator was painted in the garage and then the paint was baked onusing the oven in the kitchen. There's a cover in the "baking position" below.Bill and Dave later made an oven of their own out of an broken refrigeratorbut soon learned that the insulation used in the refrigerator was flammable!Everyone lived to a healthy old age despite all the paint baking in the kitchen. When you enter Bill Hewlett's shack, the first thing you see is his desk. Standing in the same place and turning to the right, you see... everythingelse! That's it! These two photos show his living space. Two items are notperiod-correct: there was a toilet which HP chose not to restore and thefloor was not wood but... dirt! It was the depths of the great depressionand Bill Hewlett loved the outdoors so perhaps living in what wasn't muchmore than a tent wasn't too hard for him. Other people rented that tiny spacein the decades that followed. Because the HP home and garage are in a neighborhood, they are normally impossibleto visit. The curator was very lucky to get invited by someone who has since left the company.

这座房子建于1905年，在它存在的大部分时间里都是出租的。2000年，惠普决定他们需要收购这些房子，这样他们才能妥善地保存其历史。20世纪30年代的一座中等档次的房子在2000年以170万美元的价格购得。在接下来的5年里，这座房子恢复到了1939年的样子(除了遵守现代法规是不允许的)。2007年，这处房产被列入国家历史名胜名录。戴夫·帕卡德去了斯克内克塔迪，在通用电气工作。他断言，通用电气的电子行业没有前途，应该专注于发电机、电机和其他较重的设备。比尔·休利特即将完成在斯坦福大学的研究生学业，两人决定继续他们早先的创业计划。HP(vs.PH)这个名字是用掷硬币的方式选择的。帕卡德夫妇以每月45美元的价格租下了这座房子的一楼，之所以特别选择这座房子，是因为那里有一个他们可以工作的车库。比尔·休利特搬进了车库旁边的小棚屋。起初，他们不知道他们要建造什么，所以他们试验了一些想法，比如用电流激活肌肉来锻炼。(惠普从来没有提供过这样的设备，但其他公司提供了！)。当时，电气设备被认为是治疗许多疾病的良药。)。最终，他们选择在比尔·休利特(BillHewlett)在斯坦福大学工作的基础上制造一种新型振荡器，并选择了200A&34；作为他们的第一个型号，以给人一种他们已经做了一段时间的印象。他们最初根据一句历史口号选择了54.4美元的价格。后来，当他们发现最接近的竞争对手是通用无线电(General Radio)的一款产品，售价为400美元时，他们决定将价格改为仍然相对适中的71.5美元，这样他们就可以更好地为他们年轻的公司提供资金。最早的销售之一是卖给迪士尼的。迪士尼想要一些改变，这些改变导致了200B型的出现。迪士尼购买了8个200B，用于制作“幻想曲”。戴夫·帕卡德(Dave Packard)用他的车把通用电气用过的钻床带回来了。很多工作都是在车库里完成的，但到目前为止，你只能用二手钻床和手动工具来做，所以他们也要依靠朋友。例如，刻度盘是通过在商店里校准并用铅笔在线条上画上记号来制作的。然后，戴夫·帕卡德把它们带到查理·利顿的商店(LittonIndustries)，并把它们刻了下来。因为查理·利顿喜欢工作到凌晨2点或3点，他的设备在早上免费供戴夫·帕卡德使用。几年后，一场洪水摧毁了利顿的大部分设备，惠普的设施也得到了改善，他们可以报答他的恩惠，让利顿的产品继续发货。车库的尽头有一台收音机。戴夫·帕卡德从小就对收音机感兴趣，在修复车库时发现了一些似乎是天线的电线，所以人们相信戴夫也在这里从事他的爱好。虽然灯实际上是通过隐藏的符合代码的布线运行的，但仍然可以看到周期布线和开关。透明灯泡的存在是因为这是人们对那个时代的期望，尽管在现实中，磨砂灯泡已经很常见了。如果你仔细观察，你可以发现一些其他适合地标的更新，比如灭火和视频监控。最后，这是位于佩奇磨坊路上的惠普3号楼墙上的一张快照。左边的门通向戴夫和卢帕卡德租的一楼，右边的门通向楼梯，通向二楼(女房东斯宾塞夫人住的地方)。壁炉架上的振荡器？没有一个家是完整的！当比尔和戴夫发出他们的第一份营销材料时。振荡器被放在壁炉架上拍照。他们并没有期望从第一次尝试中得到多少回应，但他们确实收到了订单--包括带支票的订单。从这个角度看，餐厅给人的感觉是一个很大的家，然而，这个餐厅也有一张墨菲床，每天晚上他们都会移动桌子和椅子，这样他们就可以把床放低了。空间真的很紧张。下面是卢帕卡德剪贴簿上的几张照片：振荡器是在车库里画的，然后用厨房的烤箱烘烤。下面的烤架上有个盖子。比尔和戴夫后来用一台坏冰箱自己做了一个烤箱，但他们很快就知道冰箱里的绝缘层是易燃的！尽管厨房里有很多油漆在烘烤，但每个人都活到了健康的老年。当你走进比尔·休利特的小屋时，首先看到的是他的办公桌。站在同样的位置，然后向右转，你会看到...。其他的一切！就是这样！这两张照片展示了他的生活空间。有两件东西不符合句号：有一个马桶惠普选择不修复，地板不是木头的，但...。泥土！那是大洋洲的最深处。

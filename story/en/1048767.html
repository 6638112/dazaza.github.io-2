Facebook's Australia ban is acute for people in places like Fiji, who rely on phone plans with cheap Facebook access as going to news websites costs more data
Facebook的澳大利亚禁令对于像斐济这样的地方的人来说非常严厉，他们依赖电话计划和廉价的Facebook访问权限，因为前往新闻网站会花费更多数据

2021-02-21 09:49:44

Media experts fear that communities reliant on the site for information will suffer significant impact

媒体专家担心，依赖该站点获取信息的社区将遭受重大影响

Facebook’s ban on Australian news will cut off a vital source of authoritative information for the Pacific region, government and industry analysts have warned.

政府和行业分析师警告称，Facebook对澳大利亚新闻的禁令将切断太平洋地区权威信息的重要来源。

Across the Pacific, thousands have found their access to news blocked, or severely limited, after the tech giant wiped all news on the platform in Australia in response to proposed legislation that would require Facebook to pay for content from media groups.

在这家科技巨头根据要求立法要求Facebook向媒体集团支付内容的拟议立法后，在澳大利亚擦除了该平台上的所有新闻之后，在整个太平洋地区，成千上万的人发现他们对新闻的访问受到了阻止或受到严重限制。

Across the Pacific, thousands of people are on pre-paid data phone plans which include cheap access to Facebook. Those on limited incomes can get news through the social network, but cannot go to original source websites without using more data, and spending more money.

在整个太平洋地区，数千人正在使用预付费数据电话计划，其中包括廉价访问Facebook。收入有限的人可以通过社交网络获取新闻，但是如果不使用更多数据和花费更多钱就无法访问原始来源网站。

The region’s largest telco provider, Digicel, with a presence in Fiji, Nauru, Papua New Guinea, Samoa, Tonga and Vanuatu, offers affordable mobile data plans with free or cheap access to Facebook.

该地区最大的电信服务提供商Digicel在斐济，瑙鲁，巴布亚新几内亚，萨摩亚，汤加和瓦努阿图设有分支机构，提供可负担的移动数据套餐，可免费或廉价访问Facebook。

In Australia, news from Pacific sites also appeared to be blocked, a significant impediment for diaspora communities and seasonal workers.

在澳大利亚，太平洋地区的新闻似乎也被封锁，这对侨民社区和季节性工人来说是一个重大障碍。

From Australia, the Guardian visited the Samoa Observer, Vanuatu’s Daily Post, the Fiji Times, and Papua New Guinea’s Post Courier. None had visible posts.

监护人从澳大利亚访问了萨摩亚观察员，瓦努阿图的每日邮报，斐济时报和巴布亚新几内亚的邮政快递。没有人有可见的​​帖子。

Dr Amanda Watson, a research fellow at the Australian National University’s Coral Bell School of Asia Pacific Affairs, and an expert in digital technology use in the Pacific, said there was widespread confusion across the Pacific about the practical ramifications of Facebook’s Australian news ban.

澳大利亚国立大学Coral Bell亚太事务学院研究员，太平洋地区数字技术使用专家Amanda Watson博士说，整个太平洋地区对Facebook澳大利亚新闻禁令的实际影响感到困惑。

“There has not been any clear, accessible and accurate information put out for Facebook users or anything particularly targeted at Facebook users in the Pacific that has explained parameters of this decision.”

“没有为Facebook用户提供任何清晰，可访问且准确的信息，也没有针对太平洋地区Facebook用户的任何特别信息解释了该决定的参数。”

Watson said for many in the Pacific, Facebook was the entry point to, and even the extent of, the internet.

沃森说，对于太平洋地区的许多人来说，Facebook是互联网乃至互联网的入口。

“Facebook is the primary platform, because a number of telco providers offer cheaper Facebook data, or bonus Facebook data. Many Pacific Islanders might know how to do some basic Facebooking, but it’s questionable if they would be able to open an internet search engine and search for news, or go to a particular web address. There are technical confidence issues, and that’s linked to education levels in the Pacific, and how long people have had access to the internet.”

“ Facebook是主要平台，因为许多电信提供商都提供更便宜的Facebook数据或额外的Facebook数据。许多太平洋岛民可能会知道如何做一些基本的Facebooking，但是如果他们能够打开互联网搜索引擎来搜索新闻或访问特定的网址，那将是一个问题。存在技术上的信任问题，这与太平洋地区的教育水平以及人们上网的时间长短有关。”

Bob Howarth, country correspondent for Timor-Leste and PNG for Reporters Sans Frontières, and the former managing director and publisher of PNG’s Post Courier, said “the Facebook ban on Australian news pages will have a significant impact on Pacific users, especially many regional news providers”.

东帝汶国家记者和PNG记者SansFrontières的国家通讯员Bob Howarth，以及PNG的Post Courier的前常务董事和发行人说：“ Facebook禁止澳大利亚新闻页面的使用将对太平洋用户，特别是许多地区新闻产生重大影响提供者”。

“As someone who regularly checks literally dozens of Facebook pages, especially in PNG and Timor-Leste, many use the Australian pages for sharing breaking news and a source of ideas and angles for their own news reporting.”

“作为经常检查字面上的数十个Facebook页面（尤其是在PNG和东帝汶的人），许多人使用澳大利亚的页面共享突发新闻以及他们自己的新闻报道的想法和角度。”

Articles reposted from Australian news sources are often used in the Pacific to rebut misinformation being spread on Facebook, Watson and Howarth said.

沃森和霍华斯说，从澳大利亚新闻来源转载的文章在太平洋地区经常被用来反驳在Facebook上传播的错误信息。

“One very popular page in PNG seems to attract more than its fair share of long-longs [an ill-informed person in pidgin] opposing vaccination as the Covid pandemic quietly spreads daily,” Howarth said.

霍瓦尔斯说：“随着科维奇大流行每天悄然蔓延，PNG上一个非常受欢迎的页面似乎吸引了更多反对接种疫苗的长期购买者（一个知情人士，在pidgin中）。”

The founder of The Pacific Newsroom, Sue Ahearn, told the Guardian the internet had revolutionised communications across the Pacific – historically a region where communication had been difficult – and enabled the instantaneous sharing of news and information that had previously taken weeks or months.

太平洋新闻编辑室的创始人苏·阿赫恩（Sue Ahearn）告诉《卫报》，互联网彻底改变了整个太平洋地区的通讯方式（历史上一直是通讯困难的地区），并可以即时共享过去数周或数月的新闻和信息。

“Facebook and social media are not the be all and end all but they are vital as sources of information. Radio and TV and newspapers remain important, but technology has really woken up the Pacific. People are able to share material right around the region and Facebook is the key platform for that.”

“ Facebook和社交媒体并不能一and而就，但它们作为信息来源至关重要。广播电视和报纸仍然很重要，但是技术确实唤醒了太平洋。人们可以在该地区附近共享材料，而Facebook是实现这一目标的关键平台。”

Ahearn said the dissemination of accurate and impartial news was vital to countering misinformation across the region.

阿赫恩说，传播准确，公正的新闻对于应对整个地区的错误信息至关重要。

“For instance, there is so much misinformation in PNG on Covid – people say ‘I don’t believe Melanesians can catch Covid’ or ‘I don’t believe what the government says about vaccines’. It’s really important that that misinformation can be countered, and articles from Australian sources are valuable for that.”

“例如，在Covid上PNG中存在很多误导信息-人们说'我不相信美拉尼西亚人能够赶上Covid'或'我不相信政府对疫苗所说的话'。可以反驳错误信息，这一点非常重要，而澳大利亚来源的文章对此很有价值。”

Ahearn said the Pacific Newsroom Facebook page had been “overwhelmed” with responses to the Facebook Australian news ban.

阿赫恩说，对太平洋澳大利亚新闻禁令的回应使太平洋新闻编辑室的Facebook页面“不知所措”。

“From people all around the world: Fijians in South Sudan, Tongans in Utah, Pacific Islanders are everywhere, and they are telling us they are not seeing anything out of Australia.”

“来自世界各地的人们：南苏丹的斐济人，犹他州的汤加人，太平洋岛民无处不在，他们告诉我们他们看不到澳大利亚以外的任何东西。”

Australia’s minister for international development and the Pacific, Zed Seselja, has labelled Facebook’s actions “disappointing”, and argued the tech giant was “impeding public access to high-quality journalism in Australia and across the Pacific”.

澳大利亚国际发展与太平洋部部长泽德·塞瑟利亚（Zed Seselja）称Facebook的举动“令人失望”，并称这家科技巨头“正在阻碍澳大利亚和整个太平洋地区公众获取高质量新闻的机会”。

“In many Pacific countries Facebook is the primary avenue to access legitimate Australian news content, and for many Pacific Islanders, Australian news is a key source of reliable, fact-checked, balanced information,” he said.

他说：“在许多太平洋国家，Facebook是访问合法澳大利亚新闻内容的主要途径，对于许多太平洋岛民而言，澳大利亚新闻是可靠，经过事实核查，平衡的信息的重要来源。”

William Easton, the managing director of Facebook Australia said New Zealand, said Australia’s proposed media bargaining law had misunderstood the nature of the relationship between the platform and news publishers, and had forced the tech company into restricting news in Australia.

Facebook澳大利亚常务董事威廉·伊斯顿（William Easton）表示，新西兰表示，澳大利亚拟议的媒体讨价还价法误解了该平台与新闻发布者之间关系的性质，并迫使这家科技公司限制了澳大利亚的新闻。

He said the company had chosen to block news “with a heavy heart”.

他说，该公司选择“以沉重的心情”阻止新闻。

“Unfortunately, this means people and news organisations in Australia are now restricted from posting news links and sharing or viewing Australian and international news content on Facebook. Globally, posting and sharing news links from Australian publishers is also restricted.”

“不幸的是，这意味着澳大利亚的人们和新闻组织现在被限制发布新闻链接以及在Facebook上共享或查看澳大利亚和国际新闻内容。在全球范围内，发布和共享澳大利亚出版商的新闻链接也受到限制。”

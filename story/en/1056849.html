Most kids with serious inflammatory illness had mild Covid-19
大多数具有严重炎症疾病的孩子患有轻度Covid-19

2021-04-08 03:13:52

Most children with a serious inflammatory illness linked to the coronavirus had initial infections with no symptoms or only mild ones, new U.S. research shows.

大多数具有与冠状病毒相关的严重炎症疾病的儿童初始感染没有症状或仅为轻微的，新的美国。

The unusual post-infection condition tends to be milder in kids who were sicker with COVID-19, although more than half of affected youngsters received intensive hospital care, according to an analysis by the federal Centers for Disease Control and Prevention. The findings were published Tuesday in JAMA Pediatrics.

据联邦疾病控制和预防中心的分析，虽然超过一半的受影响的青少年受到影响的儿童，但不寻常的感染后病情往往更温和。调查结果于周二在贾马特儿科出版。

The study represents the largest analysis to date on U.S. cases of multisystem inflammatory syndrome in children, or MIS-C, and bolsters evidence that it is a delayed immune response to COVID-19.

该研究代表了对儿童的多系统炎症综合征或MIS-C的多系统炎症综合征的案件的最大分析，并证明它是对Covid-19的延迟免疫应答。

The study included almost 1,800 cases reported to the CDC from March 2020 through mid-January. Most were in kids younger than 15 but the study included those up to age 20.

该研究包括从2020年3月至1月中旬向CDC报告的近1,800例。大多数人在15多个孩子中，但研究包括20岁的20岁。

Upticks in cases have occurred two to five weeks after COVID-19 peaks and have followed the spread of initial infections from urban to rural areas, the researchers said. More recent CDC data indicate there’s another emerging peak in the pediatric condition consistent with that trend.

研究人员表示，在Covid-19峰峰后两到五周的案件发生了两到五周，并遵循了城市到农村地区的初始感染的传播。最近的CDC数据表明儿科条件的另一个新兴峰值与这种趋势一致。

State-reported cases through March 29 totaled 3,185 and included 36 deaths, the CDC’s website shows. State reports aren’t always timely so it is uncertain how many U.S. children developed the illness since the study ended.

CDC的网站展示，国家报告的案件总计3,185税总额为3,185名，其中包括36人死亡人员。国家报告并不总是及时的，所以它不确定自研究结束以来有多少美国儿童开发了疾病。

Most kids who have had COVID-19 haven’t developed the post-infection illness. Almost 3.5 million U.S. children and teens have tested positive for the coronavirus, according to data compiled by the American Academy of Pediatrics and the Children’s Hospital Assn.

大多数患有Covid-19的孩子都没有开发过感染后疾病。根据美国儿科学院和儿童医院Assn汇编的数据，近350万美国儿童和青少年对冠状病毒进行了阳性。

The condition was first reported in Europe in late winter and spring of last year. Some cases, especially those that follow silent, undiagnosed coronavirus infections, may be mistaken for Kawasaki disease, a rare condition that can cause red skin, swelling and heart problems.

该病症是在欧洲举报于去年冬季和去年春季的。一些病例，特别是那些沉默的腐败，未确诊的冠状病毒感染，可能会对川崎病进行误，这是一种罕见的病症，可导致红色皮肤，肿胀和心脏问题。

Dr. Sean O’Leary, vice chairman of the pediatrics academy’s infectious diseases committee, says the inflammatory condition typically causes children to become very sick very quickly, but that most “respond very well to treatment and the vast majority get completely better.”

儿科学院的传染病委员会副主席Sean O'Leary博士说，炎症状况通常会导致儿童变得非常迅速，但大多数人“对治疗非常好，绝大多数完全变得完全更好。”

The best way to prevent it is to prevent COVID-19 infections, “which vaccines are very good at doing,” he said. COVID-19 vaccine studies in children are underway.

预防它的最佳方法是预防Covid-19感染，“哪种疫苗非常擅长做，”他说。 Covid-19儿童疫苗研究正在进行中。

In the CDC analysis, fever was among the most common symptoms. Abdominal pain, vomiting, diarrhea and red skin rash occurred in at least half of affected kids. Almost one-third had heart inflammation or other cardiac involvement. These symptoms were least common among children up to age 4, who were also less likely to require intensive care than older children.

在CDC分析中，发烧是最常见的症状之一。腹痛，呕吐，腹泻和红色皮疹发生在至少一半的受影响的孩子中。几乎三分之一的心脏炎症或其他心脏受累。这些症状在4岁以下的儿童中最不常见，他们也不太可能需要重症监护而不是年龄较大的儿童。

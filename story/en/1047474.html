DOJ drops out of a lawsuit seeking to block CA from enacting its own net neutrality law; the case will still proceed with backing from various industry groups
司法部退出诉讼，试图阻止CA制定自己的网络中立性法律； 此案仍将得到各行业集团的支持

2021-02-09 20:47:51

In a signal of things to come from the , the Justice Department on Monday dropped out of a lawsuit that seeks to block California from enacting its own law.

为了预示即将发生的事情，美国司法部周一退出了诉讼，该诉讼试图阻止加利福尼亚州制定自己的法律。

The acting chair of the , Democrat Jessica Rosenworcel, hailed the decision and said that the FCC is now “charting a course” to restore net neutrality nationwide.

民主党人杰西卡·罗森沃尔（Jessica Rosenworcel）的代理主席对这一决定表示赞赏，并表示FCC现在正在“制定路线”以恢复全国范围的网络中立性。

“By taking this step, Washington is listening to the American people, who overwhelmingly support an open internet, and is charting a course to once again make net neutrality the law of the land,” Rosenworcel said.

Rosenworcel说：“通过采取这一步骤，华盛顿正在听取美国人民的声音，他们压倒性地支持开放的互联网，并正在制定一条使网络中立成为国家法律的路线。”

The FCC is currently deadlocked with two Republicans and two Democrats. Biden will have to appoint a third Democrat, subject to Senate confirmation, in order to reestablish net neutrality nationwide.

FCC目前与两名共和党人和两名民主党人陷入僵局。为了在全国范围内恢复网络中立，拜登必须任命第三位民主党人，但须经参议院确认。

The Obama administration’s FCC first enacted net neutrality regulations in 2015. The rules forbade internet providers from establishing “fast lanes” for certain content, or blocking or “throttling” other content. The Trump administration reversed the decision, repealing the regulations in December 2017.

奥巴马政府的FCC于2015年首次颁布了网络中立性法规。该法规禁止互联网提供商为某些内容建立“快速通道”，或阻止或“限制”其他内容。特朗普政府撤销了该决定，于2017年12月废除了该规定。

Former California Gov. Jerry Brown signed a law — SB 822 — that enacted California’s own net neutrality standards in September 2018. The Trump Justice Department filed suit the same day, arguing that the law was preempted by the FCC’s regulation.

前加利福尼亚州州长杰里·布朗（Jerry Brown）签署了SB 822号法律，该法律于2018年9月制定了加利福尼亚州自己的净中立标准。特朗普司法部于同日提起诉讼，称该法律被FCC法规抢占。

The law was originally set to take effect on Jan. 1, 2019, but has been on hold for more than two years pending the outcome of the case.

该法律原定于2019年1月1日生效，但在案件结案之前已搁置了两年多。

The DOJ’s motion for an injunction was scheduled to go before U.S. District Judge John A. Mendez in Sacramento on Feb. 23. On Jan. 15, the judge asked the DOJ to advise by Tuesday whether the Biden administration would proceed. The DOJ formally dropped out of the case on Monday.

美国司法部原本要求禁令的动议原定于2月23日在美国萨克拉曼多州地方法院法官约翰·A·门德斯（John A. Mendez）进行，1月15日，法官要求美国司法部在周二前告知拜登政府是否会继续进行。司法部星期一正式退出了诉讼。

The case will still proceed — and the law remains on hold — as several trade groups representing the cable industry also joined as plaintiffs. They include the American Cable Association, CTIA – The Wireless Association, NCTA – The Internet & Television Association, and USTelecom – The Broadband Association.

由于代表电缆行业的几个贸易组织也作为原告加入，此案仍将继续进行，法律仍被搁置。它们包括美国电缆协会，CTIA –无线协会，NCTA –互联网和电视协会和USTelecom –宽带协会。

Xavier Becerra, the California attorney general, issued a statement praising the administration’s decision.

加州司法部长哈维尔·贝塞拉（Xavier Becerra）发表声明，对政府的决定表示赞赏。

“We applaud the Biden administration for withdrawing the federal government’s challenge to California’s net neutrality law,” Becerra said. “This action affirms the importance of a free and open internet to maintain a competitive marketplace and a fair democracy. We will keep fighting in court to defend net neutrality for all Californians.”

贝塞拉说：“我们为拜登政府撤回联邦政府对加利福尼亚的网络中立法的挑战表示赞赏。” “这项行动肯定了免费开放的互联网对于维持竞争市场和公平民主的重要性。我们将继续在法庭上为捍卫所有加利福尼亚人的网络中立而战。”

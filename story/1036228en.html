Why is life expectancy in the US lower than in other rich countries?
为什么美国的预期寿命比其他富裕国家低？

2020-11-24 09:19:30

Why do Americans have a lower life expectancy than people in other rich countries, despite paying so much more for health care?

为什么美国人要付出比其他富裕国家更高的预期寿命，却要付出更多的医疗费用呢？

The short summary of what I will discuss below is that Americans suffer higher death rates from smoking, obesity, homicides, opioid overdoses, suicides, road accidents, and infant deaths. In addition to this, deeper poverty and less access to healthcare mean Americans at lower incomes die at a younger age than poor people in other rich countries.

我将在下面讨论的简短摘要是，美国人因吸烟，肥胖，杀人，阿片类药物过量，自杀，交通事故和婴儿死亡而死亡率更高。除此之外，更深的贫困和更少的医疗保健意味着与其他富裕国家的穷人相比，收入较低的美国人比年轻人更年轻。

The US clearly stands out as the chart shows: Americans spend far more on health than any other country in the world, yet the life expectancy of the American population is shorter than in other rich countries that spend far less.

如图所示，美国显然脱颖而出：美国人在健康方面的支出比世界上任何其他国家都要多，但是美国人的预期寿命却比其他花费少得多的富裕国家要短。

The chart here doesn’t just show the latest data points, but how life expectancy and health spending have changed during the last five decades. The arrows start in 1970 and connect the annual data points for both metrics, showing the change over time.

此处的图表不仅显示了最新的数据点，还显示了过去五十年中的预期寿命和医疗支出的变化。箭头从1970年开始，连接两个指标的年度数据点，显示了随时间的变化。

In the 1970s the US didn’t stand out at all, it does so now because life expectancy increased much more slowly than in other countries. At the same time health spending in the U.S. increased much more rapidly, particularly since the mid-1980s. The consequence of these two exceptional developments is that the US followed the much flatter trajectory that the chart shows.

在1970年代，美国根本没有脱颖而出，它之所以如此，是因为预期寿命的增长比其他国家慢得多。同时，美国的医疗保健支出增长更快，尤其是自1980年代中期以来。这两个非凡发展的结果是，美国遵循了图表所显示的更为平坦的轨迹。

The unequal development over recent decades led to an inequality between the US and other rich countries. In the US health spending per capita is up to four times higher, yet life expectancy is lower than in all of these countries.

近几十年来的不平等发展导致美国与其他富裕国家之间的不平等。在美国，人均医疗支出高出四倍，但预期寿命却低于所有这些国家。

The US has achieved very substantial progress in health outcomes over the last 140 years: in 1880 the life expectancy of Americans was 39 years, since then it has doubled. But this extremely positive trend has come to an end. While life expectancy for people around the world continued to increase, life expectancy of Americans has declined since 2014. With the pandemic of 2020 – which already caused more than 225,000 deaths due to COVID-19 and 300,000 excess deaths – it is unfortunately already certain that the decline of life expectancy in the US will continue this year. 1

在过去的140年中，美国在健康结果方面取得了非常可观的进步：1880年，美国人的预期寿命为39岁，从那时起，它就翻了一番。但是，这种极其积极的趋势已经结束。尽管全球人民的预期寿命持续增加，但自2014年以来，美国人的预期寿命一直在下降。由于2020年的大流行（由于COVID-19造成的225,000多人死亡和300,000例超额死亡），不幸的是已经可以肯定美国的预期寿命将在今年继续下降。 1个

One could write an entire book on what this chart shows us. Here I want to look at one key question: why is life expectancy in the US shorter? Which causes of death are more common in the US than in other rich countries?

一个人可以写一本关于这个图表显示给我们的书。在这里，我想看一个关键问题：为什么美国的预期寿命较短？在美国，哪些死因比其他富裕国家更常见？

I won’t lay out what is behind the shown long-term improvements in life expectancy (on this you might find our entry on life expectancy helpful) or why we see such large differences in health spending (on this see Lorenzoni, Belloni, & Sassi (2014) and our entry on financing healthcare). 2

我不会说明预期寿命的长期改善背后的原因（对此，您可能会发现我们的预期寿命条目很有帮助）或为什么我们会看到医疗支出如此巨大的差异（有关此信息，请参阅洛伦佐尼，贝洛尼和Sassi（2014）和我们关于医疗保健融资的条目）。 2

To understand some of the key reasons that explain the comparatively low life expectancy of Americans, I will look at mortality rates cause-by-cause. Causes of deaths that kill younger people are particularly important – life expectancy captures the average age of death and the average declines strongly when people die at a young age.

为了理解解释美国人的预期寿命相对较低的一些关键原因，我将研究原因导致的死亡率。导致年轻人死亡的死亡原因尤为重要-预期寿命涵盖了平均死亡年龄，而人们在年轻时死亡则大大降低了平均寿命。

Tobacco smoking is one of the world’s largest health problems today. 8.1 million people die prematurely from smoking every year. Half of them are people younger than 70.

吸烟是当今世界上最大的健康问题之一。每年有810万人因吸烟过早死亡。其中一半是70岁以下的人。

Smoking was extremely common in today’s rich countries between 1950 and 2000 as the first chart shows. Smoking was more widespread in the US than in Europe or Japan. As a consequence Americans died at higher rates from the consequences of smoking as the second chart shows (most of these people died from lung cancer).

如第一张图表所示，在1950年至2000年之间，当今富裕国家的吸烟极为普遍。在美国，吸烟比在欧洲或日本更普遍。结果，第二个图表显示，美国人死于吸烟的死亡率更高（大多数人死于肺癌）。

But the chart also shows that smoking peaked earlier in the US. Since there is a lag of two to three decades between the peak of smoking and the peak of smoking mortality, this should be positive news for the US going forward. The country is over this mortality peak and we should expect the decline in lung cancer death rates in the US to continue in the coming years.

但图表还显示，吸烟在美国达到高峰。由于吸烟高峰与吸烟死亡率高峰之间存在两到三个十年的延迟，因此这对美国向前发展应该是一个积极的消息。该国已经超过了死亡率高峰，我们应该期望美国的肺癌死亡率在未来几年内继续下降。

More than two-thirds of Americans ( 70%) are overweight and more than one-third ( 36%) is obese.

超过三分之二的美国人（70％）超重，而超过三分之一（36％）的人肥胖。

Obesity is a key risk factor for many of the leading causes of death in rich countries, including heart disease, diabetes, some cancers, and stroke. 3 Estimates of the death rate from obesity-related factors in the US are higher than for other countries as the chart shows. To improve population health it will be key for all countries to make progress against obesity, for the US it is especially important.

肥胖是富裕国家许多主要死亡原因的关键危险因素，包括心脏病，糖尿病，某些癌症和中风。 3如图所示，美国肥胖相关因素导致的死亡率估计值高于其他国家。对于改善人口健康而言，对所有国家来说，对抗肥胖都是至关重要的，对于美国而言，这一点尤其重要。

This chart compares homicide rates in the US with a number of other rich countries. No matter which other rich country you add to the chart, you won’t find one with a higher homicide rate. The homicide rate in the US is much higher than in other rich countries.

该图表将美国与其他许多富裕国家的凶杀率进行了比较。无论您将哪个其他富国添加到图表中，都不会找到凶杀率更高的国家。美国的凶杀率远高于其他富裕国家。

As most homicide victims are young, this contributes to the lower level of life expectancy in the US in comparison with other rich countries.

由于大多数凶杀案受害人都是年轻的，因此与其他富裕国家相比，这导致美国的预期寿命降低。

This is of course important but it cannot explain the growing inequality in life expectancy between the US and other countries over time. Over the last few decades homicide rates have declined more in the US than in other rich countries. In relative terms the faster decline of homicide rates in the US has reduced the relative difference in life expectancy between countries.

这当然很重要，但不能解释美国和其他国家之间随着时间推移而不断增长的预期寿命不平等。在过去的几十年中，美国的凶杀率下降幅度超过了其他富裕国家。相对而言，美国凶杀率的快速下降减少了国家之间预期寿命的相对差异。

This chart shows the death rate from opioid overdoses. This is a cause of death where the US very clearly stands out.

该图显示了阿片类药物过量引起的死亡率。这是导致死亡的原因，美国非常明显地突出了这一点。

In the US the death rate has increased more than 10-fold since 1990, while opioid overdoses have remained an extremely rare cause of death in other countries. No other country in the world has seen a surge in opioid overdose deaths as large as the US. Today the US has by far the highest opioid overdose death rate.

自1990年以来，美国的死亡率增加了10倍以上，而在其他国家，阿片类药物的过量使用仍然是极为罕见的死亡原因。世界上没有其他国家能像美国这样的阿片类药物过量死亡人数激增的国家。今天，美国的阿片类药物过量死亡率最高。

Opioid overdoses are still thankfully a relatively rare cause of death overall (it is the cause of death of 1.7% of Americans), but these deaths affect life expectancy because many victims are relatively young.

值得庆幸的是，阿片类药物过量仍然是总体上相对罕见的死亡原因（占美国1.7％的死亡原因），但由于许多受害者相对年轻，这些死亡影响了预期寿命。

Ruhm (2017) carefully studied opioid mortality in the US on a local level and found that the availability and the prescription of opioid-based painkillers is the main driver of the opioid mortality surge in the US. 4 This contributes to an explanation of both aspects that the first chart above highlights: lower life expectancy and higher healthcare costs.

Ruhm（2017）在美国本地对阿片类药物的死亡率进行了仔细研究，发现基于阿片类药物的止痛药的可获得性和处方是美国阿片类药物死亡率飙升的主要驱动力。 4这有助于对以上第一张图表突出显示的两个方面进行解释：预期寿命缩短和医疗费用增加。

Suicides have increased slightly over the past generation in the US, as the chart here shows.

如上图所示，在美国的上一代中，自杀人数略有增加。

This is not the case in many other rich countries – you can switch to any other country in the world in the chart. Also globally suicide rates have fallen substantially over recent years.

在许多其他富裕国家中并非如此-您可以在图表中切换到世界上任何其他国家。近年来，全球自杀率也大幅下降。

The US stands out in particular in suicides from firearms, which are much rarer in countries around the world.

美国在枪支自杀中尤其引人注目，而枪支自杀在世界各国中更为罕见。

Suicides are also among the few causes of death that are a high risk for younger people. The age distribution of suicides and the fact that suicides are rising in the US and falling in many other rich countries explains why this is another cause of death that contributes to the divergence in life expectancy that we are trying to explain.

自杀也是年轻人死亡的极少数死亡原因之一。自杀的年龄分布以及自杀在美国上升而在许多其他富裕国家中下降的事实说明了为什么这是造成我们预期的寿命差异的另一个死亡原因。

Deaths in road accidents are also much more common in the US than in most other rich countries.

在美国，与大多数其他富裕国家相比，道路交通事故死亡也更为普遍。

The chart shows that in many countries road deaths are at least 50% less common.

图表显示，在许多国家，道路交通事故死亡的发生率至少降低了50％。

Most Americans who die in road accidents are young, which is why this cause of death contributes significantly to the gap in life expectancy between the US and other countries.

大多数死于道路交通事故的美国人都是年轻的，这就是为什么这种死亡原因是造成美国与其他国家之间预期寿命差距很大的原因。

So far we’ve only covered causes of deaths where the US stands out as having a higher death rate. But the US also stands out in having higher economic inequality and poverty than other rich countries, 5 and this matters for the health outcomes as well.

到目前为止，我们只讨论了导致死亡的原因，而美国以其较高的死亡率着称。但是，美国在经济不平等和贫困方面也比其他富裕国家要高出5，这对健康结果也很重要。

While Americans have a higher average income than people in most other rich countries, the incomes of the poorest Americans are lower than the incomes of the poorest people in other rich countries.

尽管美国人的平均收入高于其他大多数富裕国家的人民，但最贫穷的美国人的收入却低于其他富裕国家的最穷人民的收入。

Infant mortality – the share of infants that don’t survive the first year of their life – is higher in the US than in just about every other rich country. You can again add countries to the chart here. A high infant mortality matters very much for life expectancy as the very short life span pulls down the average strongly.

在美国，婴儿死亡率（未满一岁的婴儿所占的比例）比其他所有富裕国家都高。您可以在此处再次将国家/地区添加到图表中。高婴儿死亡率对预期寿命非常重要，因为很短的寿命会大大降低平均寿命。

The research by Alice Chen, Emily Oster, and Heidi Williams (2016) shows that while the mortality rates for babies of well-off people in the US and European countries are similar, there are larger differences between the mortality rates of the infants born to poorer people. 6 As the authors put it: “the observed higher US postneonatal mortality relative to Europe is due entirely, or almost entirely, to higher mortality among disadvantaged groups.”

爱丽丝·陈（Alice Chen），艾米丽·奥斯特（Emily Oster）和海蒂·威廉姆斯（Heidi Williams）（2016）的研究表明，尽管美国和欧洲国家富裕人群的婴儿死亡率相似，但婴儿出生时的死亡率差异更大穷人。 6正如作者所说：“观察到的美国新生儿出生后相对于欧洲的死亡率较高，完全或几乎完全是由于弱势群体的死亡率较高。”

Not just the mortality of infants is higher in the US, maternal mortality is also much higher. And while maternal mortality is falling in almost all countries in the world the death of mothers is becoming more common in the US.

在美国不仅婴儿死亡率更高，孕产妇死亡率也更高。虽然世界上几乎所有国家的孕产妇死亡率都在下降，但母亲的死亡在美国却越来越普遍。

Poorer Americans are already substantially worse off within the first year of life. Over the life course the disadvantages accumulate. The inequality of life expectancy is large in the US, the difference between the poorest 1% and the richest 1% in the US is 14.6 years. 7 And this income gradient in life expectancy has widened recently.

在生活的第一年，贫穷的美国人已经变得更加糟糕。在整个生命过程中，不利因素不断累积。在美国，预期寿命的不平等现象很大，美国最贫穷的1％和最富有的1％之间的差距为14.6岁。 7而且，这种预期寿命的收入梯度最近有所扩大。

The low life expectancy of poorer Americans is a big part of why the average life expectancy in the US is lower than in other rich countries. Kinge et al (2019) study the difference between the US and Norway. 8 Like the US, Norway is exceptionally rich, but it is much less unequal. As a consequence Norwegians in the lower half of the income distribution are richer than Americans in the lower half of the income distribution. The lower level of income inequality in Norway is mirrored by a lower inequality in life expectancy. The life expectancy of Norwegians in the lower-middle income segment is about 3 to 4 years higher than the life expectancy of Americans in that same income segment.

较贫穷的美国人的低预期寿命是为什么美国的平均预期寿命低于其他富裕国家的重要原因。 Kinge等人（2019）研究了美国和挪威之间的差异。 8像美国一样，挪威非常富有，但不平等程度却要差得多。结果，收入分配中下半部分的挪威人比收入分配中下半部分的美国人富裕。挪威较低的收入不平等现象反映在预期寿命中较低的不平等现象上。挪威中低收入阶层的预期寿命比同一收入阶层的美国人的预期寿命高约3至4年。

The world as a whole has achieved very rapid progress; global life expectancy has increased to over 70 years. The US isn’t keeping up with this progress – because of the high inequality and slow development of the US there are now 19 US counties where people have a lower life expectancy than the global average. 9

整个世界取得了非常迅速的进步。全球预期寿命已增加到70多年。美国无法跟上这一进步-由于美国的高度不平等和发展缓慢，现在美国有19个县的预期寿命低于全球平均水平。 9

Among rich countries the US stands out as a country where the population does not have universal access to health insurance.

在富裕国家中，美国是一个人口无法普遍获得医疗保险的国家。

The chart here shows the situation in 2011, unfortunately the latest global data that I am aware of. In most rich countries it is the case that everyone is covered by health insurance, but not in the US. The US has expanded healthcare coverage in recent years, but a significant share of its population is still uninsured. 10

此处的图表显示了2011年的情况，很遗憾，我了解到了最新的全球数据。在大多数富裕国家，所有人都有医疗保险，但在美国却没有。近年来，美国扩大了医疗保健的覆盖范围，但仍然有很大一部分人口没有医疗保险。 10

This explains part of the difference in life expectancy according to the extensive study of the US National Research Council 11 The authors note that for older Americans this is a small factor, due to the availability of Medicare, but overall conclude: “Certainly, the lack of universal access to health care in the United States has increased mortality and reduced life expectancy.”

根据美国国家研究委员会的广泛研究，这解释了预期寿命的部分差异。11作者指出，对于老年美国人来说，这是一个很小的因素，因为可以享受医疗保险，但总体而言，结论是：美国普遍获得医疗服务的比例增加了死亡率，缩短了预期寿命。”

In addition to my overview I recommend two research publications that study in detail why in terms of life expectancy the US is falling behind other rich countries. 12 And you can also use our extensive data presentation on causes of deaths to explore this yourself.

除了概述之外，我还推荐两个研究出版物，详细研究为什么美国在预期寿命方面落后于其他富裕国家。 12您还可以使用我们提供的有关死亡原因的详尽数据介绍自己进行探索。

Every country should and can do better in this respect, but a particular weakness of the US is a lack of success in preventing poor health. Many of the important factors – smoking, obesity, violence, poverty – are not about providing better healthcare for those that need it, but about preventing poor health outcomes in the first place.

每个国家在这方面都应该而且可以做得更好，但是美国的一个特别弱点是在预防不良健康方面缺乏成功。许多重要因素-吸烟，肥胖，暴力，贫穷-并不是要为有需要的人提供更好的医疗保健，而是首先要预防不良的健康状况。

The failure to prevent poor health is a factor that is contributing to both developments shown in the first chart. They worsen the health of the American population and they are expensive for the healthcare system.

无法预防健康状况不佳是导致第一张图表所示的两个发展的因素。它们使美国人的健康状况恶化，并且对于医疗系统而言是昂贵的。

Now, in the middle of a global pandemic there is little reason to hope that the US can reverse the recent trend of declining life expectancy. For the years ahead a focus on the high death rates of causes of deaths that kill younger people can be the starting point to get the US population back on track towards a longer and healthier life.

现在，在全球大流行中，没有理由希望美国能够扭转预期寿命下降的最新趋势。在未来的几年中，关注导致年轻人死亡的死亡原因的高死亡率，可以成为使美国人口重回更长寿，更健康的起点。

“Diseases of Despair” Have Soared in the United States
在美国，“绝望疾病”飙升

2020-11-24 05:17:27

According to data published in the journal BMJ Open, “diseases of despair” soared over the past decade in the United States. In the study, diseases of despair were defined as diagnoses related to alcohol dependency, substance misuse and suicidal thoughts or behaviors and analyzed among the following age groups: under the age of 12 months; ages 1 to 17; 18 to 34; 35 to 54; 55 to 75; and those aged 75-plus.

根据BMJ Open杂志上发布的数据，在过去十年中，“绝望疾病”在美国飙升。在这项研究中，绝望疾病定义为与酒精依赖，滥用药物和自杀念头或行为有关的诊断，并在以下年龄组中进行分析：12个月以下； 1至17岁； 18至34； 35至54； 55至75；以及75岁以上的人。

The researchers examined data on 12 million people who were enrolled in Highmark, a large U.S.-based health insurance company, between 2007 and 2018 and who had valid, detailed claims on file. Highmark members are concentrated in states that have been disproportionately affected by "deaths of despair": Pennsylvania, West Virginia, and Delaware.

研究人员检查了2007年至2018年间在美国大型健康保险公司Highmark登记的1200万人的数据，这些人有有效，详细的索赔记录。 Highmark成员集中在受“绝望之死”影响最大的州：宾夕法尼亚州，西弗吉尼亚州和特拉华州。

Overall, 1 in 20 (515,830; just over 4%) of those insured were reported to have at least one disease of despair at some point during the monitoring period. Some 58.5% were male, with an average age of 36. Of these, over half (54%) were diagnosed with an alcohol-related disorder; just over 44% were diagnosed with a substance-related disorder; and suicidal thoughts or behaviors were reported in just over 16%. Just under 13% had more than one of these conditions.

总体而言，据报告，在监测期间的某个时间点，有20人中有1人（515,830；略高于4％）患有至少一种绝望疾病。男性约58.5％，平均年龄36岁。其中一半以上（54％）被诊断出患有酒精相关疾病；略高于44％的人被诊断出患有与物质有关的疾病；报告自杀念头或行为的人超过16％。不到13％的人有以上条件之一。

Between 2009 and 2018, the rate of diseases of despair increased by 68%. The rate of alcohol-related, substance-related, and suicide-related diagnoses rose by 37%, 94% and 170%, respectively. The largest increase in alcohol- and substance-related diagnoses was seen among 55-to-74-year-olds: 59% and 172%, respectively. The largest increase in alcohol- and substance-related diagnoses was seen among 55-to-74-year-olds: 59% and 172%, respectively.

在2009年至2018年期间，绝望疾病的发病率增加了68％。与酒精有关，与毒品有关和与自杀有关的诊断率分别上升了37％，94％和170％。在55至74岁的人群中，与酒精和药物相关的诊断上升幅度最大：分别为59％和172％。在55至74岁的人群中，与酒精和药物相关的诊断上升幅度最大：分别为59％和172％。

While the absolute numbers of suicide-related diagnoses were lower than for other types of diseases of despair, the relative increases were large. Among 1-to-17-year-olds, the rate increased by 287%, and it increased by 210% among 18-to-34-year-olds. A relative increase of at least 70% occurred in all other age groups. Disease-of-despair diagnoses were associated with significantly higher rates of anxiety and mood disorders and schizophrenia for both men and women across all age groups.

尽管自杀相关诊断的绝对数量低于其他绝望疾病类型，但相对增长却很大。在1至17岁的人群中，该比例上升了287％，而在18至34岁的人群中，这一比例上升了210％。在所有其他年龄段中，至少有70％的相对增加。在所有年龄段的男性和女性，绝望疾病的诊断与焦虑和情绪障碍和精神分裂症的发生率显着相关。

Between 2015 and 2017, life expectancy fell year on year in the U.S., the longest sustained decline since 1915-18. And deaths among middle-aged white non-Hispanic men and women rose sharply between 1999 and 2015. These premature deaths are largely attributable to accidental overdose, alcohol-related disease, and suicide.

在2015年至2017年之间，美国的预期寿命逐年下降，这是1915-18年以来持续时间最长的一次。在1999年至2015年间，非西班牙裔中年白人男女的死亡率急剧上升。这些过早死亡在很大程度上归因于意外的服药过量，与酒精有关的疾病和自杀。

Such "deaths of despair" coincided with decades of economic decline, particularly among those with low levels of education, loss of social safety nets, and falling wages and family incomes in the U.S., all of which are thought to have contributed to growing feelings of despair.

这种“绝望的死亡”与数十年来的经济衰退相吻合，特别是在美国那些受教育程度低，社会安全网丧失，工资和家庭收入下降的人当中，所有这些都导致人们对美国的感情日益增强。绝望。

“Diseases of despair” may affect the well-being of family members and friends now more than ever. It’s important to note that this study was conducted before the onslaught of COVID-19, so it’s likely that the incidences of diseases of despair have risen due to pandemic-related stress. If you or someone you know is struggling with alcohol or substance abuse, suicidal thoughts, depression, anxiety, or other mental health challenges, seek help.

“绝望疾病”现在可能比以往任何时候都更加影响家人和朋友的福祉。值得注意的是，这项研究是在COVID-19袭击之前进行的，因此，由于大流行相关的压力，导致绝望疾病的发生率可能上升了。如果您或您认识的某人在酗酒或滥用药物，自杀念头，抑郁，焦虑或其他精神健康问题上挣扎，请寻求帮助。

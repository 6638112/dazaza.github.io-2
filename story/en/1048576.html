Valve gets dragged into Apple and Epic’s legal fight over Fortnite
Valve被拖入Apple和Epic关于Fortnite的法律诉讼中

2021-02-20 02:16:38

Apple's bitter legal dispute with Epic Games over the distribution of Fortnite on iOS has now managed to rope in Valve. Apple has subpoenaed the Steam maker for a wide range of PC game sales data it says is crucial to its case. But Valve is fighting back against that subpoena, saying its information is proprietary, not relevant to Apple's case, and would be costly and difficult to generate.

苹果公司与Epic Games就Fortnite在iOS上的发行而发生的激烈法律纠纷现在已经在Valve中获得成功。苹果已经向Steam制造商传唤了其广泛的PC游戏销售数据，该数据对这起案件至关重要。但瓦尔（Valve）正在反击该传票，称其信息是专有的，与苹果公司的案子无关，而且信息昂贵且难以生成。

As discussed in a joint letter from Apple and Valve filed with the Northern District of California court this week, Apple's November subpoena seeks two large categories of information that Valve is refusing to provide:

正如Apple和Valve本周在加利福尼亚北部地区法院提起的联合信函中所讨论的那样，Apple的11月传票寻求Valve拒绝提供的两大类信息：

Documents sufficient to show Valve’s: (a) total yearly sales of apps and in-app products; (b) annual advertising revenues from Steam; (c) annual sales of external products attributable to Steam; (d) annual revenues from Steam; and (e) annual earnings (whether gross or net) from Steam; as well as

足以显示Valve的文件：（a）应用和应用内产品的年度总销售额； （b）Steam的年度广告收入； （c）归属于Steam的外部产品的年度销售额； （d）Steam的年收入； （e）Steam的年度收入（无论是毛收入还是净收入）；也

(a) The name of each App on Steam; (b) the date range when the App was available on Steam; and (c) the price of the App and any in-app product available on Steam.

（a）Steam上每个应用程序的名称； （b）该应用在Steam上可用的日期范围； （c）该应用程序和Steam上可用的任何应用程序内产品的价格。

While Valve has responded to some other parts of Apple's subpoena, Apple says that information has been "so heavily redacted that Apple cannot discern what information they might contain."

瓦尔（Valve）对苹果传票的其他部分做出了回应，但苹果表示，该信息已被大量修改，以至于苹果无法辨别它们可能包含哪些信息。

The requested information, Apple says, is key to establishing other platforms that "may be economic substitutes" for the iOS App Store when it comes to distributing Fortnite, as discussed in previous rulings in the case. Valve's information is "crucial for calculating the total size of the market for Epic’s available digital distribution channels," Apple says.

苹果表示，所要求的信息对于建立其他可能成为经济替代品的平台至关重要。涉及Fortnite的iOS App Store发行，如该案例先前的裁决所述。 Valve的信息对于计算Epic可用的数字分销渠道的市场总规模至关重要。苹果说。

But Valve argues that Steam is an unrelated sideshow in the battle between Apple and Epic. "Valve does not make or sell phones, tablets, or video games for mobile devices, or otherwise compete in the mobile market," the company writes. " Fortnite is not available on Steam, and Epic has publicly and unequivocally stated it will not offer Fortnite on Steam unless Valve changes its business model."

While Apple says that Valve "admits that the information requested exists in some undisclosed, readily accessible format," Valve says complying would involve "an overwhelming amount of work" and "would impose an extraordinary burden on Valve to query, process, and combine a massive amount of [information] to create the documents Apple seeks."

Apple cites a previous court order for similar sales information from Samsung in arguing that the court should compel Valve to comply. But Valve argues that "Apple, Google, and Samsung compete with each other in the mobile app market. Valve does not compete in that market."

I'm sure Apple would love to have detailed information about Steam's sales. But I'm having trouble seeing how it's germane to this case - especially as Epic has chosen not to publish on Steam for exactly the reason it doesn't want to publish on the App Store - the percentage of revenue. However, the difference is Epic could make its own PC launcher. I can easily see Apple subpoena-ing Epic's PC sales information as a monopoly-recourse angle.

我确定Apple希望获得有关Steam销售的详细信息。但是我很难看到它与这种情况有何关联-特别是因为Epic出于不希望在App Store上发布的确切原因选择不在Steam上发布-百分比的收入。但是，不同之处在于Epic可以制造自己的PC启动器。我可以很容易地看到苹果公司将Epic的PC销售信息作为一种垄断追索权。

A key part of antitrust litigation (which this lawsuit basically is) is figuring out what the market is, and how big it is. Apple is trying to argue that "the market is big, so big that phones are just a small piece of it, and therefore we're not a monopoly." It would be easier to do that if they could show evidence that the PC gaming market is huge (which cannot be proven from Epic's data, because most games are bought on Steam). The fact that Fortnite isn't on Steam is irrelevant; it could be on Steam, but Epic chose not to put it there, so Steam is part of the market, or so Apple's argument goes. OTOH, their argument is kinda shit. Mobile phones are very different from PCs in both form and function, and the fact that you can play Fortnite on both is not IMHO dispositive.

反垄断诉讼的关键部分（此诉讼基本上是该诉讼）是弄清市场是什么，以及市场有多大。 苹果试图争辩说，市场很大，市场如此之大，以至于手机只是其中的一小部分，因此我们不是垄断者。 如果他们可以证明PC游戏市场巨大（这无法从Epic的数据中证明，因为大多数游戏都是在Steam上购买的），则这样做会更容易。 Fortnite不在Steam上这一事实无关紧要； 它可能在Steam上，但是Epic选择不将其放到那里，因此Steam成为了市场的一部分，或者苹果的说法也是如此。 太太，他们的论点有点糟。 手机在形式和功能上都与PC完全不同，并且您可以在两者上玩Fortnite的事实并非恕我直言。

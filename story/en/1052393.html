Tim Berners-Lee says internet access should be a basic right
Tim Berners-Lee表示，互联网访问应该是一个基本权利

2021-03-15 07:57:01

Tim Berners-Lee, the inventor of the world wide web, writes in a new post on the internet’s 32nd anniversary that governments must work to ensure global broadband access by the year 2030 in order to bridge the digital divide and better hold tech companies accountable.

蒂姆伯尔斯 - 李，万维网发明人，在互联网32周年的新帖子中写道，政府必须努力确保2030年的全球宽带接入，以便弥合数字鸿沟，更好地拥有科技公司负责任。

“Governments need to pass effective laws that govern technology and hold companies to account for creating responsible products and services,” Berners-Lee writes in the post co-authored by his Web Foundation co-founder Rosemary Leith.

“政府需要通过管理技术和持有公司的有效法律，以便为创建负责任的产品和服务，”伯尔斯 - 李写在他的Web基金会联合创始人迷迭香里斯的职位。

“Far too many young people remain excluded and unable to use the web to share their talents and ideas.”

“太多的年轻人仍然被排除在外，无法使用网络分享他们的才能和想法。”

“Far too many young people remain excluded and unable to use the web to share their talents and ideas,” the post states. And for every young person without internet access, there’s a “lost opportunity for new ideas and innovations that can serve humanity.”

“太多的年轻人仍然被排除在外，无法使用网络分享他们的才能和想法，”邮政。对于没有互联网访问的每个年轻人，有一个“可以为人类服务的新想法和创新的失去机会”。

The breadth of the digital divide has come into sharp focus during the coronavirus pandemic, as people tried to work and learn from home, often on less-than-ideal internet connections. According to the Pew Research Center, in the US alone, only two-thirds of people living in rural areas have broadband access, compared to 79 percent of people living in suburbs. And the Federal Communications Commission’s universally disliked broadband maps don’t give a totally clear picture of the state of broadband access in the US.

由于人们试图从家中工作和学习，而且经常在不太理想的互联网连接中，数字分裂的广度剧焦于冠状病毒大流行病。据PEW研究中心称，在美国独自一人中，只有三分之二的居住在农村地区的人有宽带接入，而占有郊区的79％。和联邦通信委员会的普遍不喜欢的宽带地图不会在美国完全清晰地了解宽带接入状态。

Berners-Lee and Leith write that worldwide, the problem is similarly bad. “Only the top third of under-25s have a home internet connection, according to UNICEF, leaving 2.2 billion young people without the stable access they need to learn online, which has helped so many others continue their education during the pandemic.”

Berners-Lee和Leith写了这个世界，这个问题同样糟糕。 “据儿童基金会称，只有25岁以下的前三名拥有家庭互联网连接，留下了22亿名年轻人，没有稳定的访问，他们需要在线学习，这有助于这么多人在大流行期间继续教育。”

Berners-Lee and Leith also cite the need to protect young people online from abuse and misinformation, “which threatens their participation and can force them from platforms altogether,” they write. “This is especially true for those disproportionately targeted on the basis of their race, religion, sexuality, abilities, and gender.”

伯尔斯 - 李和雷斯还引用了在线保护年轻人免受滥用和误导，“这威胁他们的参与，可以完全迫使他们从平台上迫使他们。” “这对于那些不成比例地针对他们的种族，宗教，性行为，能力和性别的人来说尤其如此。”

The pair call for access to the internet to be recognized as a basic right, and they say the costs of making sure every young person is connected “is well within our reach. Through funding for network infrastructure, subsidies, and support for community networks, we can get the web into the hands of every young person on earth.” “An all-out push to connect the world will make sure that young people do not fall through the cracks.”

该货币对呼叫访问互联网被识别为基本权利，他们说确保每一个年轻人所连接的成本很好。通过为网络基础设施，补贴和对社区网络的支持提供资金，我们可以将网络放入地球上每个年轻人的手中。“ “全力以赴连接世界将确保年轻人不会陷入裂缝。”

Berners-Lee and Leith say that the Alliance for Affordable Internet (A4AI), an initiative of the Web Foundation, “has calculated that $428 billion of additional investment over ten years would provide everyone a quality broadband connection. To put this in perspective, that amounts to the equivalent of just $116 per person for the 3.7 billion people who remain offline today.”

伯纳 - 李和雷斯说，经济适用互联网（A4AI）的联盟，网络基金会的一项倡议，“已经计算出4.28亿美元的额外投资超过十年将为每个人提供优质的宽带连接。为了透视，这适用于今天的370亿人只有116美元，只有116美元。“

Providing universal broadband access within the next ten years “would deliver around $8.7 trillion in direct economic benefits,” they write, citing a new analysis by the Tony Blair Institute for Global Change. “We can’t afford not to do it.”

他们写道，在接下来的十年内提供普遍的宽带接入“将在直接的经济效益中提供大约87万亿美元，”他们写的是Tony Blair研究所的全球变革研究所的新分析。 “我们不能不这样做。”

The case of the missing dark matter: new suspect found in galactic mystery
暗物质失踪案：银河系神秘中发现新嫌疑人

2020-11-28 10:22:00

A faraway galaxy with almost no dark matter has threatened to break our theory of galaxy formation. New evidence suggests the galaxy isn't an anomaly—but a victim of theft. Dark matter—an invisible substance as enigmatic as its name suggests—is a key ingredient in helping galaxies form and stay alive.

几乎没有暗物质的遥远星系威胁要打破我们的星系形成理论。新证据表明，银河系不是反常现象，而是盗窃的受害者。暗物质（顾名思义是一种看不见的神秘物质）是帮助星系形成和维持生命的关键成分。

It creates the strong gravity needed to spark galaxy formation and keep existing galaxies structurally intact.

它产生了激发星系形成并保持现有星系结构完整所需的强大引力。

But astronomers have been puzzled since last year's discovery of 'NGC1052-DF4', a stable and long-lived galaxy with almost no dark matter. How can the galaxy exist without this important ingredient? Are our theories about galaxy formation wrong?

但是，自去年发现“ NGC1052-DF4”以来，天文学家一直感到困惑。没有这一重要成分，星系将如何存在？我们关于星系形成的理论是错误的吗？

Today, an international study led by UNSW Sydney suggests the dark matter was there to begin with—it's just been stolen by a greedy neighbour.

今天，由悉尼新南威尔士大学领导的一项国际研究表明，暗物质从一开始就是被贪婪的邻居偷走的。

"The dark matter isn't there because it's already been removed," says Dr. Mireia Montes, lead author of the study and astronomical researcher at UNSW Science and the Space Telescope Science Institute.

该研究的主要作者，新南威尔士大学科学和太空望远镜科学研究所的天文学研究员米雷亚·蒙特斯博士说：“暗物质并不存在，因为它已经被去除了。”

"We found that the from the nearby massive galaxy NGC1035 is removing its stars—and dark matter."

“我们发现附近的大型星系NGC1035正在清除其恒星和暗物质。”

The research, published today in The Astrophysical Journal, provides an explanation for why so much dark matter is missing from the galaxy without contradicting our existing understanding of galaxy formation.

今天发表在《天体物理学杂志》上的这项研究提供了一个解释，说明了为什么星系中会丢失这么多暗物质，而又不与我们对星系形成的现有理解相矛盾。

"When two galaxies are passing close to each other, they suffer from each other's gravitational pull," says Dr. Montes. "Our very deep imaging found faint stars being pulled away by the larger galaxy—an interaction called ' '."

蒙特斯博士说：“当两个星系彼此靠近时，它们会受到彼此的引力的影响。” “我们非常深入的成像发现昏暗的恒星被更大的星系拉走了，这种相互作用称为''。

The same phenomenon can also be found on Earth: in our case, the Moon's gravitational pull influences Earth's ocean tides. But tidal disruption can cause galaxies—which aren't as solid as Earth or the Moon—to bend and lose their shape.

在地球上也可以发现相同的现象：在我们的情况下，月球的引力会影响地球的海洋潮汐。但是，潮汐破坏会导致星系弯曲（形状不如地球或月球），并使它们变形。

If the tidal disruption theory is correct, the smaller galaxy NGC1052-DF4 will soon start to show more signs of deterioration. It could eventually disintegrate entirely.

如果潮汐干扰理论是正确的，那么较小的星系NGC1052-DF4将很快开始显示出更多的退化迹象。它最终可能会完全瓦解。

"Tidal stripping would remove a significant percentage of dark matter before affecting the stars," says Dr. Montes.

蒙特斯博士说：“潮汐剥离将在影响恒星之前去除相当大比例的暗物质。”

"If the stars are starting to be disrupted now, most of the dark matter has already escaped."

“如果星星现在开始被破坏，那么大多数暗物质已经逃脱了。”

Dr. Ignacio Trujillo, co-author of the paper and researcher at the Instituto de Astrofísica de Canarias (IAC), says "With time, the galaxy will end being cannibalised by the large system around it (NGC1035), with at least some of their stars free floating in the ".

该论文的共同作者，加那利大学天文学研究所（IAC）研究员Ignacio Trujillo博士说：“随着时间的流逝，星系将被周围的大型系统（NGC1035）吞噬掉。他们的星星自由漂浮在“。

Dr. Montes and her colleagues used powerful telescopes and deep imaging techniques—including long-exposure photography of up to 60 hours—to find the faint clues in the outer edges of the galaxy.

蒙特斯博士和她的同事们使用了功能强大的望远镜和深度成像技术（包括长达60小时的长时间曝光摄影）来发现星系外边缘的暗淡线索。

These techniques are capable of illuminating very dim stars and galaxies, or what astronomers call the 'low surface brightness' of the universe.

这些技术能够照亮非常昏暗的恒星和星系，或者被天文学家称为宇宙的“低表面亮度”。

"Initial papers showed that the galaxy has a very 'relaxed' symmetrical shape, suggesting that no outside forces were perturbing it," says Dr. Montes.

蒙特斯博士说：“初步研究表明，星系具有非常'松弛'的对称形状，这表明没有外界力量在干扰它。”

"But our deep images show that this galaxy is in fact being affected by its neighbour galaxy—it's just caught in the beginning of the interaction.

“但是我们的深层图像显示，这个星系实际上正受到其邻近星系的影响-它只是在交互作用的开始就被捕获了。

"The inner part of the galaxy keeps its shape, but the outer, fainter parts are where you see these 'tidal tails': stars that have been already separated from the galaxy."

“星系的内部保持其形状，但是较暗的外部是您看到这些“潮汐尾巴”的地方：已经从银河系中分离出来的恒星。”

The observations were made using the IAC80 Telescope, the Gran Telescopio Canarias, and the Hubble Space Telescope—one of the largest telescopes in space.

这些观测是使用IAC80望远镜，Gran Telescopio Canarias和哈勃太空望远镜（太空中最大的望远镜之一）进行的。

As is an invisible force, it can only be observed by how stellar objects—like stars and galaxies—interact with the space around them.

作为一种看不见的力，只能通过恒星物体（如恒星和星系）与它们周围的空间相互作用的方式来观察。

"Ultra-deep imaging is hard not only because of the huge amount of time you need to reach such depths, but the extremely careful processing of the data needed to preserve the faintest structures," says Mr Raúl Infante-Sainz, Ph.D. candidate at IAC and second author of this study.

劳尔·因凡特·塞恩茨（RaúlInfante-Sainz）博士说：“超深成像很难，不仅因为要到达如此深的深度需要花费大量的时间，而且要极其谨慎地处理保存最微弱的结构所需的数据，” IAC的候选人，本研究的第二作者。

"We needed to look for features that are 1000 times fainter than the darkest sky visible on Earth," says Dr. Montes.

蒙特斯博士说：“我们需要寻找比地球上最黑暗的天空暗淡1000倍的特征。”

The Vera C. Rubin Observatory, a state-of-the-art optical facility currently under construction in Chile, will soon take deep imaging capabilities to new levels.

维拉·C·鲁宾天文台是智利目前正在建设的最先进的光学设施，它将把深层成像能力提升到新的水平。

The observatory's main project will be Legacy Survey for Space and Time (LSST): a ten-year imaging survey that will provide the deepest ever images of the Southern Hemisphere's night sky.

天文台的主要项目将是时空传统调查（LSST）：一项为期十年的影像调查，它将提供南半球夜空有史以来最深的图像。

Professor Sarah Brough, an astronomer at UNSW Science, is leading Australia's involvement in this project.

UNSW Science的天文学家Sarah Brough教授正在领导澳大利亚参与该项目。

"LSST will revolutionise low surface brightness astronomy, transforming our understanding of galaxy evolution," she says.

她说：“ ​​LSST将彻底改变低表面亮度天文学，改变我们对星系演化的理解。”

"It will provide extremely deep imaging data over the entire Southern Sky, which will be vital to the success of future Australian astronomical surveys and science."

“它将提供整个南部天空的极其深层的成像数据，这对于未来澳大利亚天文测量和科学的成功至关重要。”

The LSST camera, which will be roughly the size of a small car, will enable researchers to detect low surface brightness . It will also be capable of identifying faint features around and within galaxy clusters.

LSST摄像头大约只有一辆小型汽车，可以让研究人员检测低表面亮度。它还将能够识别星系团周围和内部的微弱特征。

While the ten-year survey won't kick off until 2023, scientists are looking forward to the opportunities for galactic exploration.

尽管这项为期十年的调查要到2023年才能开始，但科学家们期待着银河探索的机会。

"This work is an example of how important it is to have deep images to understand apparently weird things in the Universe," says Dr. Montes.

蒙特斯博士说：“这项工作说明了拥有深层图像来理解宇宙中看来很奇怪的事物有多么重要。”

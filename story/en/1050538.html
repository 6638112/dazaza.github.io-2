Intel hit with $2.2 billion patent judgment
英特尔以22亿美元的专利判决受到打击

2021-03-03 06:46:56

A Texas jury has ordered Intel to pay $2.18 billion in damages for infringing two patents. The lawsuit was filed by VLSI Technology LLC, a 4-year-old firm that Intel says has no products and no sources of revenue besides patent litigation.

德克萨斯州陪审团已裁定英特尔因侵犯两项专利而支付21.8亿美元的赔偿金。该诉讼是由拥有4年历史的VLSI Technology LLC提起的，英特尔表示，除了专利诉讼外，它没有产品，也没有任何收入来源。

The patents at issue in the case previously belonged to NXP Semiconductors, a Dutch company that spun off from Philips in 2006. NXP acquired the patents when it bought Freescale Semiconductor (itself a spinoff of Motorola) in 2015. Intel's lawyer told jurors that NXP would get a portion of the proceeds from the lawsuit.

该案中的有争议专利以前属于NXP Semiconductors，这是一家荷兰公司，于2006年从飞利浦分拆出来。NXP在2015年收购飞思卡尔半导体（本身就是摩托罗拉的子公司）时获得了这些专利。英特尔律师告诉陪审团认为，恩智浦将从诉讼中获得部分收益。

The two patents focus on methods for minimizing the power consumption of computing chips. One way to do this is by varying the system voltage: setting a higher voltage when high performance is needed, then lowering the voltage to conserve power afterwards. One  patent claims the concept of storing information about a memory chip's minimum voltage in nonvolatile memory so the system can ensure that the memory circuit has a high-enough voltage.

两项专利的重点在于最小化计算芯片功耗的方法。一种方法是通过改变系统电压：在需要高性能时设置较高的电压，然后降低电压以节省功率。一项专利要求保护在非易失性存储器中存储有关存储芯片的最低电压信息的概念，以便系统可以确保存储电路具有足够高的电压。

VLSI's other patent focuses on altering clock frequencies as a power-saving technique. Once again, raising the clock frequency of an electrical bus can increase system performance but consume more power. NXP's second patent is based on the idea that a device on a bus can a change to the clock frequency when it needs faster performance.

VLSI的另一项专利着重于改变时钟频率作为一种节能技术。同样，提高电气总线的时钟频率可以提高系统性能，但会消耗更多功率。恩智浦的第二项专利基于这样的想法：总线上的设备可以在需要更快的性能时更改时钟频率。

At trial, Intel argued that it hadn't copied these techniques from NXP or Freescale and that, in fact, Intel had invented its own, more sophisticated, techniques to accomplish these objectives. But a jury in Waco, Texas, wasn't convinced.

在审判中，英特尔辩称它没有从NXP或飞思卡尔那里复制这些技术，实际上，英特尔发明了自己的，更复杂的技术来实现这些目标。但是，德克萨斯州韦科的陪审团没有说服。

Intel had tried to get the trial postponed, arguing to a federal appeals court that COVID-19 rates in the Waco area were too high to hold the trial safely. The company also argued that it would be better to hold the trial in Austin, where Intel has a campus. But with the Austin courtroom closed, Judge Alan Albright decided to hold the case in Waco. In January, the Federal Circuit Appeals Court gave the green light.

英特尔曾试图推迟审理，并向联邦上诉法院辩称，韦科地区的COVID-19费率过高，无法安全地进行审理。该公司还辩称，最好在英特尔有校园的奥斯丁举行审判。但随着奥斯丁法庭关闭，艾伦·奥尔布赖特法官决定将案件保留在韦科。一月份，联邦巡回上诉法院开了绿灯。

The case isn't over. Intel may ask the judge to find the jury misapplied the law. If that fails, Intel will then have the opportunity to appeal the case. If the ruling is upheld, it would be one of the largest patent judgments in US history.

案件还没有结束。英特尔可能会要求法官裁定陪审团不当适用法律。如果失败，英特尔将有机会对此案提起上诉。如果判决维持不变，那将是美国历史上最大的专利判决之一。

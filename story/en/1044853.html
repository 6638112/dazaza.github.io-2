Gary Taubes: 'Obesity isn’t a calorie problem, it’s a hormone problem'

加里·陶伯斯（Gary Taubes）：肥胖不是卡路里问题，而是激素问题。

The author of The Case for Keto argues that conventional approaches to tackling obesity and diabetes aren’t working, and that low-carbohydrate diets could be the way forward

The Case for Keto的作者认为，解决肥胖和糖尿病的常规方法无效，低碳水化合物饮食可能是未来的发展之路

O ver the past two decades, the UK’s rates of obesity and type 2 diabetes have spiralled, something that has invariably been blamed on our intake of saturated fat. Conventional nutrition science argues this leads to elevated cholesterol levels and a greater risk of heart disease, but journalist Gary Taubes believes we need to rethink this idea. Over the past 20 years, Taubes has suggested that fat has been unfairly demonised, and instead our excessive carbohydrate and sugar consumption is to blame for many of these societal health problems, a concept that has begun to interest increasing numbers of scientists. In his new book, The Case for Keto, Taubes discusses the potential benefits of the ketogenic diet, a low-carbohydrate, high-fat diet that is being studied as a potential treatment for a range of diseases, from obesity and diabetes, to even cancer and Alzheimer’s.

在过去的二十年中，英国的肥胖率和2型糖尿病呈螺旋上升趋势，这总是归因于我们摄入的饱和脂肪。传统的营养科学认为这会导致胆固醇水平升高和患心脏病的风险增加，但记者Gary Taubes认为我们需要重新考虑这一想法。在过去的20年中，陶伯斯（Taubes）提出脂肪被不公平地妖魔化了，相反，我们对许多此类社会健康问题的碳水化合物和糖类消耗过多，使这一概念受到了越来越多的科学家的关注。 Taubes在他的新书《 Keto的情况》中讨论了生酮饮食的潜在好处，这种低碳水化合物，高脂肪的饮食正在被研究为治疗多种疾病的潜在方法，从肥胖，糖尿病到甚至癌症和老年痴呆症。

You’ve long been one of the biggest advocates for the benefits of low -carbohydrate, high -fat diets. How did this all begin? I did an investigative piece for the journal Science back in 2001 on dietary fat and heart disease. I interviewed around 140 researchers and administrators, and I concluded that there was never really compelling evidence for this low-fat diet we’d all been told to eat since the mid-1980s. When writing the story, I had a National Institute of Health administrator say to me: “When we told everyone to go on low-fat diets, we thought if nothing else they’d lose weight, because fat is the densest calorie in the diet. And instead they started eating more carbohydrates and everyone got fatter.” So I always had it my head that one of the main things that caused the obesity epidemic was this switch to a low-fat, high-carbohydrate diet.

您长期以来一直是低碳水化合物，高脂肪饮食的最大倡导者之一。这一切是如何开始的？ 2001年，我为《科学》杂志做了一篇有关饮食脂肪和心脏病的调查文章。我采访了约140名研究人员和管理人员，得出的结论是，从1980年代中期开始，我们都被告知要吃这种低脂饮食，从没有真正令人信服的证据。在写这个故事时，我有一个美国国立卫生研究院的管理员对我说：“当我们告诉每个人都要低脂饮食时，我们认为他们是否会减肥，因为脂肪是饮食中最密集的卡路里。相反，他们开始吃更多的碳水化合物，每个人都变得肥胖。”因此，我总是下定决心，导致肥胖流行的主要原因之一就是转向低脂，高碳水化合物饮食。

In the book you point out that in 20 years, obesity and type 2 diabetes rates have doubled in the UK. Why do you think this has happened? We have this fundamental belief system about obesity, that it’s caused by caloric imbalance or overeating. So the idea is that we’re taking in more calories than we’re expending, and so the cure is to eat less, and reduce our calorie intake. I think that’s biologically naive, and rather than being about calories, obesity is actually a hormonal regulatory defect. The conventional approaches don’t work because while we can sustain eating less for a while, eventually the hunger gets us because we’re semi-starving ourselves. So, wrong approach – and when it fails, we blame the patients for not sustaining it.

在书中，您指出，在20年中，英国的肥胖和2型糖尿病发病率翻了一番。您为什么认为这发生了？我们有关于肥胖的基本信念体系，它是由热量失衡或暴饮暴食引起的。因此，我们的想法是摄入的卡路里比消耗的卡路里更多，因此，治愈的方法是少吃东西，减少卡路里的摄入。我认为肥胖是天真的，与肥胖无关，肥胖实际上是一种激素调节缺陷。传统的方法行不通，因为虽然我们可以少吃一会儿，但由于我们半饿着肚子，最终饥饿使我们感到饥饿。因此，错误的方法–当方法失败时，我们责怪患者没有坚持下去。

The ketogenic diet is a widely accepted dietary treatment for epilepsy, but in your book you go further, describing it as a potential solution to the obesity epidemic and type 2 diabetes. What is the thinking for why a ketogenic diet can help in these cases? What I argue in the book is that obesity is not a caloric imbalance problem, it’s a hormonal regulation problem. Fat accumulation is primarily regulated by the hormone insulin, and the idea is that for those who are obese, diabetic, or predisposed, they have to minimise their insulin levels to solve the problem. By restricting carbohydrate, the ketogenic diet minimises insulin, and so instead of accumulating fat, your body starts mobilising it, and synthesising ketones out of it to use as fuel.

生酮饮食是癫痫的一种广泛接受的饮食疗法，但是在您的书中，您走得更远，将其描述为肥胖症流行和2型糖尿病的潜在解决方案。为什么在这些情况下生酮饮食可以提供帮助呢？我在书中论证的是，肥胖症不是热量平衡失调的问题，而是激素调节的问题。脂肪的积累主要由激素胰岛素调节，其思想是，对于肥胖，糖尿病或易患肥胖症的人，他们必须最小化其胰岛素水平才能解决该问题。通过限制碳水化合物，生酮饮食可将胰岛素降至最低，因此，您的身体开始动员脂肪，而不是积累脂肪，并从中合成酮用作燃料。

No human population has ever evolved to be vegan. If they had animal products, they consumed them

从未有人进化为素食主义者。如果他们有动物产品，他们就食用它们

While there is some evidence that restricting carbohydrate intake can help obese patients, and those with type 2 diabetes, many doctors and nutrition experts feel that it remains relatively limited. What do we know so far? The most impressive research has been done by a Californian startup called Virta Health. They did a clinical trial at Indiana University where subjects either received conventional nutritional advice and medications, or a well formulated ketogenic diet. Over two years, the patients on the diet had remarkable results, effectively putting their diabetes in remission. The argument against this is that it wasn’t a randomised clinical trial, which would compare the ketogenic diet to other dietary therapies. But studies like this are causing a shift in the medical community, and people are embracing the idea that carbohydrates are fattening. As an example, there’s a Facebook group of female physicians in Canada, eating low-carbohydrate, high-fat diets for their weight, and there’s 4,000 of them in this group, which equates to around one in 10 female physicians in Canada.

尽管有证据表明，限制碳水化合物的摄入可以帮助肥胖的患者以及患有2型糖尿病的患者，但许多医生和营养专家认为，碳水化合物的使用仍然相对有限。到目前为止我们知道什么？最令人印象深刻的研究是由加利福尼亚的一家名为Virta Health的初创公司完成的。他们在印第安纳大学进行了一项临床试验，受试者要么接受常规的营养建议和药物治疗，要么制定良好的生酮饮食。两年多来，饮食控制的患者取得了显著成效，有效地使糖尿病得以缓解。对此的争论是，这不是一项随机临床试验，它将生酮饮食与其他饮食疗法进行比较。但是像这样的研究正在引起医学界的转变，人们开始接受碳水化合物在增肥的想法。例如，加拿大有一个Facebook女医生小组，他们吃低碳水化合物，高脂肪的饮食来减轻体重，该小组中有4000人，大约相当于加拿大十分之一的女医生。

One of the main criticisms of the science around the ketogenic diet is that most of the studies are only short-term, for a couple of months, and the diets are often poorly defined. Do we need better quality data? In an ideal world, the diabetes and the obesity research communities would say: “Between us, it’s costing over $1bn a day in direct medical care in the US, and if we spent one day’s worth of these costs doing rigorous clinical trials of different diets, we might learn enough to save trillions of dollars down the line.” Ideally, we would have a trial involving several tens of thousands of people on the spectrum from overweight to obese, and pre-diabetic to diabetics. You could compare four diets, for example the ketogenic diet, vegetarian/vegan, Mediterranean, and the Dash diet, and then follow them for three years minimum. It would cost in the region of $20-30m, but it would hopefully tell you which diet helps people live longer.

围绕生酮饮食的科学的主要批评之一是，大多数研究只是短期的，持续了几个月，而且饮食的定义往往不明确。我们是否需要质量更好的数据？在理想的世界中，糖尿病患者和肥胖症研究团体会说：“在我们之间，在美国，每天的直接医疗费用超过10亿美元，如果我们将其中一天的费用花费在严格的各种临床试验上，饮食，我们可能会学到足够的知识来节省下万亿美元。”理想情况下，我们将进行一项试验，涉及从超重到肥胖以及从糖尿病前到糖尿病在内的数以万计的人群。您可以比较四种饮食，例如生酮饮食，素食/纯素食，地中海饮食和达什饮食，然后至少遵循三年。它的成本约为20-30m，但它可以告诉您哪种饮食可以帮助人们更长寿。

There are some scientists looking at whether ketogenic diets could be an adjuvant cancer therapy, sensitising tumours to treatment, and in 2020 a study came out suggesting that low -carbohydrate diets may help prevent age-related damage to the brain. What do you think about this research? The cancer research makes a lot of sense, that tumours rely on us gobbling down blood sugar. There’s always been evidence that if you increase insulin, you can stimulate tumour growth. This would explain the increased cancer rates in populations when they transition to western diets, and begin to manifest obesity and diabetes. But it’s still a hypothesis, and we need more clinical evidence proving that these diets can help. At the moment there are 262 clinical trials running, looking at ketogenic diets for everything from Alzheimer’s disease to traumatic brain injury. If more of these studies start showing benefit, I think it will shift people’s thinking.

有一些科学家正在研究生酮饮食是否可以作为辅助性癌症疗法，使肿瘤对治疗更加敏感。2020年的一项研究表明，低碳水化合物饮食可能有助于预防与年龄有关的大脑损害。您如何看待这项研究？癌症研究很有意义，肿瘤依赖我们吞噬血糖。一直有证据表明，如果您增加胰岛素，可以刺激肿瘤的生长。这可以解释为什么当人们过渡到西方饮食并开始表现出肥胖和糖尿病时，癌症的发病率会上升。但这仍然是一个假设，我们需要更多的临床证据证明这些饮食可以提供帮助。目前，有262项临床试验正在进行中，研究生酮饮食对从阿尔茨海默氏病到颅脑外伤等各种疾病的影响。如果更多的这些研究开始显示出益处，我认为它将改变人们的思维方式。

One of the common criticisms often levelled at the ketogenic diet is that it’s too restrictive (with less than 50g of carbohydrate a day). Is it a practical long -term solution? If you’ve got a medical problem that might be diet-related, then it makes sense to see if you can find what foods are triggering it and what not to eat so you don’t have to live with that problem. I have a friend I met while researching this book, who comes from a family with a history of obesity, and weighed 400lb (28st 8lb/181.4kg) at age 18. He had never walked away from a meal not still hungry until he started the ketogenic diet. He lost 130lb (59kg) in four months, and the last time I saw him, he weighed about 230lb (104.3kg). So for people who lose 150lb on the diet, get to be lean, mean, and eat to satiety – for them, it’s not too restrictive. For other people, maybe it is.

生酮饮食经常引起人们的普遍批评之一，就是它的限制性太强（每天摄入少于50克的碳水化合物）。这是实用的长期解决方案吗？如果您遇到了可能与饮食有关的医疗问题，那么就可以查看是否可以找到引发该疾病的食物以及不吃什么，从而不必再忍受这个问题。我有一个我在研究本书时遇到的朋友，他来自一个有肥胖病史的家庭，他18岁时重达400磅（28磅8磅/181.4千克），直到他开始吃不饿的饭菜生酮饮食。他在四个月内减掉了130磅（59千克），而我上次见到他时，他的体重约为230磅（104.3千克）。因此，对于那些在饮食上损失150磅的人，要保持苗条，卑鄙并进食饱腹感-对他们而言，这并不是太严格的限制。对于其他人，也许是。

The ketogenic diet typically involves eating a higher protein intake. As it’s been gaining in popularity in recent years, so too has veganism , which has the opposite paradigm. What is your perspective? The vegan diet does worry me because there’s another fundamental principle here, which is that we should eat what we have evolved to eat, and no human population has ever evolved to be vegan. If they had animal products available, they consumed them, which would suggest that there are reasons to consume animal products and not only plants, which go beyond gastronomic preferences. But if someone’s struggling with their weight, or is becoming diabetic, they could certainly try a vegan or a vegetarian diet. If they get healthier, then great, but if they don’t get healthier and they don’t have ethical issues with eating animals, then ideally they would say, perhaps I should try this carbohydrate-restricted diet with animal products.

生酮饮食通常包括摄入较高的蛋白质。近年来，随着素食主义的兴起，素食主义也正相反。您的看法是什么？素食确实使我担心，因为这里还有另一个基本原则，那就是我们应该吃进化后的食物，而且从来没有人进化过素食。如果他们有动物产品，他们就会食用它们，这表明不仅有植物方面的原因，还有其他原因不仅是植物，而且还有动物产品的消费。但是，如果某人正在为自己的体重而苦苦挣扎，或者变得肥胖，那么他们当然可以尝试素食或素食。如果他们变得更健康，那就太好了，但是如果他们变得不健康，并且在吃动物方面没有道德上的问题，那么理想情况下他们会说，也许我应该尝试这种限制碳水化合物的饮食搭配动物产品。

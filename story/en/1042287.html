Study sheds new light on polar explorer’s final hours, 100+ years later
研究揭示了100多年后极地探险者的最后工作时间

2020-12-29 06:51:37

Over 100 years ago, a Danish explorer named Jørgen Brønlund perished during an expedition to northeast Greenland, along with two members of his expedition. He left behind a diary detailing his last moments, with a black spot underneath his final signature. Scientists have now analyzed that spot using a variety of techniques to determine its composition, thereby shedding fresh light on Brønlund's final hours, according to a November paper published in the journal Archaeometry.

一百多年前，一位名叫约尔根·布朗伦德（JørgenBrønlund）的丹麦探险家在格陵兰东北部探险队中与两名探险队成员一起丧生。他留下了日记，详细描述了自己的最后时刻，在他的最后签名下有一个黑点。根据发表在《考古学》杂志上的11月论文，科学家们现在已经使用多种技术分析了该斑点，以确定其组成，从而在布伦德的最后几个小时里散发出了新鲜光。

Northeast Greenland is still one of the most hostile regions of the Arctic, with only the Sirius Patrol of the Danish Army occasionally crossing the frozen expanse on dog sledges during the coldest part of the year. Back in 1906, when the Denmark Expedition launched, many parts of the region had not yet been mapped; that was a primary objective of the expedition, along with various scientific studies. (Alfred Wegener was among the scientists in the expedition.)

格陵兰东北部仍然是北极最敌对的地区之一，只有丹麦军的小天狼星巡逻队在一年中最冷的时候偶尔越过冰冻的雪橇在狗的雪橇上。早在1906年，丹麦远征军发射升空时，该地区的许多地区尚未绘制地图。那是远征队的主要目标，以及各种科学研究。 （阿尔弗雷德·韦格纳是探险队的科学家之一。）

The expedition sailed to Greenland on board the SS Danmark, landing in August 1906 and establishing a base camp (depot) called Danmarkshavn. Members were assigned to sledge teams to head northward. Jørgen Brønlund was part of Sledge Team 1, along with expedition commander Ludvig Mylius-Erichsen and Niels Peter Høeg Hagen. A significant part of their mission was to discover whether the so-called Peary Land (discovered by Robert Peary in 1891) was a peninsula—in which case it would remain part of the Danish Kingdom—or an island, in which case the US would claim it as a US territory.

探险队乘SS丹马克号航行到格陵兰，于1906年8月登陆并建立了一个名为Danmarkshavn的基地营（仓库）。成员被分配到雪橇队向北前进。约尔根·布朗伦（JørgenBrønlund）与远征军司令卢德维格·迈利乌斯·埃里克森（Ludvig Mylius-Erichsen）和尼尔斯·彼得·霍格·哈根（Niels PeterHøegHagen）一起是雪橇1号队的成员。他们任务的重要部分是发现所谓的Peary Land（由罗伯特·皮瑞（Robert Peary）在1891年发现）是一个半岛（在这种情况下仍将是丹麦王国的一部分）或一个岛屿，在这种情况下，美国将声称它是美国领土。

The sledge teams headed north in March 1907, hampered by buckling and cracking ice as well as broken-down sledges. Mylius-Erichsen, Brønlund, and Høeg Hagen decided to follow the shoreline, but the commander became increasingly concerned as that route unexpectedly led them northeast. That meant a greater distance to their ultimate goal, as time and provisions grew short. When he and his two sledge mates entered the unmapped Danmark Fjord, it turned out to be a dead end, forcing them to backtrack northeast to return to the mouth of the fjord—wasting precious time. Melting ice would make passage back to Danmarkshavn impossible, and they didn't want to get stuck in the area without sufficient food and supplies.

1907年3月，雪橇车队向北行驶，受到冰块屈曲和破裂以及雪橇损坏的阻碍。 Mylius-Erichsen，Brønlund和HøegHagen决定沿海岸线行进，但由于那条路线意外地将他们引向东北，因此指挥官越来越担心。随着时间和条款的缩短，这意味着离他们的最终目标还有一段距离。当他和他的两个雪橇伴侣进入未映射的丹马克峡湾时，事实证明这是一条死胡同，迫使他们向东北退回，回到峡湾口，浪费了宝贵的时间。融化的冰将使人们无法返回丹马克沙芬，他们不想在没有足够食物和供应的情况下陷在该地区。

That's when Mylius-Erichsen made the fateful decision not to return to the ship, but to head west in hopes of reaching Navy Cliff at the head of Independence Fjord. By this time the summer thaw had set in, and hunting conditions were poor, plus their shoes had worn through from trekking over the stony ground. In short, as Brønlund noted in his diary, their situation was dire: "no food, no foot gear, and several hundred miles to the ship. Our prospects are very bad indeed."

那是Mylius-Erichsen做出的决定性的决定，不返回战舰，而是向西行驶，以期到达独立峡湾首领的海军悬崖。到了这个时候，夏天的融雪开始了，狩猎条件很差，加上他们的鞋子由于在石质地面上徒步旅行而磨损了。简而言之，正如布朗伦（Brønlund）在他的日记中所指出的那样，他们的处境非常糟糕：没有食物，没有脚齿轮，离船只有几百英里。我们的前景确实非常糟糕。＆＃34;

By September, they were forced to try to cross the frozen waters of the 79-Fjord. They were down to one sledge and four dogs. Høeg Hagen died first, from exhaustion, on November 15, 1907, with Mylius-Erichsen passing ten days later. Brønlund actually made it to the other side of the fjord, to one of the expedition's existing depots. But the darkness and severe frostbite in his feet prevented him from going any further. He took shelter in a cave and wrote his final diary entry:

到了9月，他们被迫尝试穿越79峡湾的冰冻水域。他们只剩下一只雪橇和四只狗。霍格·哈根（HøegHagen）于1907年11月15日因精疲力竭而首先去世，十天后Mylius-Erichsen逝世。实际上，布朗伦（Brønlund）到达了峡湾的另一侧，到达了探险队现有的一个仓库中。但是他脚下的黑暗和严厉的冻伤使他无法前进。他在一个山洞里避难并写下了他的最后日记：

I perished in 77° N lat., under the hardships of the return journey over the inland ice in November. I reached this place under a waning moon, and cannot go on, because of my frozen feet and the darkness. The bodies of the others are in the middle of the fjord. Hagen died on November 15, Mylius-Erichsen some ten days later.

在11月返回内陆冰川的艰辛旅程中，我在北纬77度遇难。由于脚冻和黑暗，我在月亮渐弱的情况下到达了这个地方，无法继续前进。其他人的尸体在峡湾中部。大约十天后，哈根于11月15日去世，Mylius-Erichsen死了。

The following spring, two other expedition members found Brønlund's body, with the diary safely stored in the sledge at his feet. They buried him where he died, and the headland is known today as Brønlund's Grave. As for the rest of the Danish Expedition, it was remarkably successful—some 51 reports were published—although that success was overshadowed by the tragic deaths of Brønlund, Mylius-Erichsen, and Høeg Hagen.

次年春天，另外两名探险队成员发现了布隆伦德的遗体，日记安全地存放在他脚下的雪橇上。他们将他埋葬在他死的地方，今天的岬角被称为布隆伦德坟墓。至于其余的丹麦探险队，则取得了显著成就，发表了约51篇报道，尽管这一成功被布伦德（Brønlund），玛格丽特·埃里克森（Mylius-Erichsen）和霍格·哈根（HøegHagen）的惨死所掩盖。

The 172-page diary is currently housed in the Royal Library in Copenhagen. Below Brønlund's signature was an odd-looking black spot. In 1993, the spot was removed with a pocket knife by a reader. "This was done without permission which is of course both unorthodox and illegal," the authors noted in their paper. Fortunately, the sample ended up with the National Museum of Denmark's Natural Science Unit, but despite being examined by many experts, no conclusions could be drawn about what the material might be.

这份长达172页的日记目前存放在哥本哈根的皇家图书馆中。布伦隆德的签名下面是一个看起来很奇怪的黑点。 1993年，读者用小刀将其移除。 ＆＃34;这是在未经许可的情况下完成的，这当然是非正统的和非法的，＆＃34;作者在论文中指出。幸运的是，该样品最终归丹麦国家博物馆的自然科学部门所有，但尽管经过了许多专家的审查，仍无法得出有关材料可能是什么的结论。

That's where the current study comes in. Back in 2018, Kaare Lund Rasmussen and several colleagues at the University of Southern Denmark collaborated with the National Museum and Royal Library to make a definitive determination of the sample's composition. The team took small samples from the black spot and analyzed them with X-ray fluorescence, synchrotron radiation powder x-ray diffraction, Raman spectroscopy, and gas chromatography mass spectrometry, among other methods.

这就是当前研究的目的。早在2018年，Kaare Lund Rasmussen和南丹麦大学的几位同事与国家博物馆和皇家图书馆合作，对样品的成分做出了确定的确定。该小组从黑点中抽取了少量样品，并通过X射线荧光，同步辐射粉末X射线衍射，拉曼光谱和气相色谱质谱法等对其进行了分析。

They discovered that the spot was made up of burnt rubber, vegetable oil, fish or animal fats, petroleum, and feces. There was also goethite, a mineral common to the environment. The burnt rubber likely came from a gasket in the Lux burner Brønlund had among his supplies, and may have been burnt as the doomed explorer struggled to light a fire to keep warm, using any potentially flammable materials available to him. (The burner was recovered in 1973 by the Sirius Patrol.) The authors surmise that Brønlund was in the cave roughly five or six days before he died, given that the sledge case was barely half-empty.

他们发现该地点由燃烧的橡胶，植物油，鱼类或动物脂肪，石油和粪便组成。还有针铁矿，这是环境中常见的一种矿物。烧焦的橡胶很可能来自Brønlund在他的补给品中使用的勒克斯燃烧器中的垫圈，并且可能是由于注定的探险家努力使用可用的任何易燃材料来点燃火以保暖而被烧掉的。 （燃烧器是在1973年由Sirius Patrol回收的。）作者推测，Brønlund死前大约五到六天就在山洞里，因为雪橇盒几乎是半空的。

"This new knowledge gives a unique insight into Brønlund's last hours," said Rasmussen. "I see for me, how he, weakened and with dirty, shaking hands, fumbled in an attempt to light the burner, but failed. He had to find something else to get the burner going. You can use paper or oiled fabric, but it is difficult. We think he tried with the oils available, because the black spot contains traces of vegetable oil and oils that may come from fish, animals or wax candles."

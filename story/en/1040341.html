Whatnot raises $4M as it gets into livestreamed auctions and Pokémon cards
Whatnot通过直播拍卖和神奇宝贝卡筹集了400万美元

2020-12-18 15:07:31

Originally focused on being a platform for safely reselling FunkoPop vinyl figurines, Whatnot has since expanded into other types of collectables, including Pokémon cards, sports cards and FiGPiNs.

Whatnot最初专注于安全销售FunkoPop乙烯基小雕像的平台，此后便扩展到其他类型的收藏品，包括神奇宝贝卡，运动卡和FiGPiN。

The company has also expanded beyond simple buying/selling mechanics, embracing a trend that was already proving popular before the pandemic struck and has only grown since: livestreamed video sales and auctions.

该公司还超越了简单的购买/销售机制，迎来了一种在流行病爆发之前就已经证明很流行的趋势，并且这种趋势直到现在才得以发展：实时视频销售和拍卖。

If you’ve never fallen down the rabbit hole that is collecting, perhaps you haven’t seen this kind of sale — but once you’ve tuned in, it’s hard to stop. Popular amongst groups like Disney pin traders, live video auctions are … well, they’re exactly what they sound like. The host fires up a livestream, a notification going out to their fanbase of fellow collectors that it’s time for a sale. Their phone camera pointed at the item up for grabs (often spinning in place on a turntable to give a full view of its condition), the host’s job is part auctioneer, part hype man. They’ve got to know the ins and outs of the item in question, interact with folks in chat, and generally just keep the energy high so that viewers stick around.

如果您从未跌倒正在收集的兔子洞，也许您从未见过这种销售方式，但是一旦调入，就很难停下来。实时视频拍卖在诸如迪士尼大头针交易商这样的团体中很受欢迎……嗯，它们的确像听起来那样。主持人启动了一个直播节目，并向收藏家的同伴们发出了通知，通知他们该进行拍卖了。他们的手机摄像头指着要抢的物品（通常在转盘上旋转以充分查看其状况），主持人的工作是拍卖师，炒作。他们必须了解相关商品的来龙去脉，在聊天中与其他人互动，并且通常只是保持精力充沛，以便观众留在身边。

A lot of these auctions happen on platforms like Instagram, which … aren’t really built for this. Selling an item there means manually connecting with buyers after the fact, figuring out payments and hoping no one backs out. Whatnot wants to specialize in it and has spent the last few months building out livestreaming tools accordingly. Streams are handled directly through their mobile app, as is all the payment and processing on the seller’s end. I popped into a few random streams last night, and they each had dozens to hundreds of people watching and bidding on items like Mumm-Ra Funko Pops, with users trying to guess what might show up in the stream next.

其中许多拍卖都发生在Instagram之类的平台上，而…并不是真的为此而建立的。在那卖东西意味着在事发后与买家手动建立联系，弄清楚付款并希望没有人退缩。 Whatnot想要专门研究它，并且在过去的几个月中一直在相应地构建实时流工具。流直接通过其移动应用进行处理，卖家端的所有付款和处理过程也一样。昨晚我突然出现在一些随机流中，他们每个人都有数十至数百人在观看和竞标Mumm-Ra Funko Pops之类的项目，用户试图猜测下一步可能会出现什么。

Another increasingly popular livestreaming concept for Whatnot is that of the “card break.” Users pool their money to buy an entire box of trading card packs — often boxes that are no longer being produced and can cost thousands of dollars to obtain. Each user gets a number, each number tied to a pack (or packs) within the box. Each pack is opened on the livestream, its contents sent to the (hopefully?) lucky owner tied to that pack’s number.

Whatnot的另一个越来越流行的直播概念是“破牌”。用户花钱购买一整盒交易卡包-通常不再生产这些盒，并且可能要花费数千美元才能获得。每个用户都会得到一个数字，每个数字都与框中的一个或多个包装捆绑在一起。每个包都会在直播中打开，其内容会发送给（希望是）与该包的号码绑定在一起的幸运的所有者。

Whatnot co-founder Logan Head calls the company’s growth in recent months “explosive,” telling me that additions like livestreaming have helped them grow “at least 100% month-over-month” as of late. The Whatnot team has grown as well. It’s currently sitting at 12 employees and is actively looking for more engineers — thus the need to raise. The company tells me that this $4 million seed round was backed by Scribble Ventures, Operator Partners, Wonder Ventures, Y Combinator and a number of angel investors.

Whatnot的共同创始人Logan Head称该公司最近几个月的增长是“爆炸性的”，他告诉我，直播等附加功能最近使他们“至少每月增长100％”。 Whatnot团队也不断壮大。该公司目前有12名员工，并且正在积极寻找更多的工程师，因此需要提高。该公司告诉我，这笔400万美元的种子轮投资由Scribble Ventures，Operator Partners，Wonder Ventures，Y Combinator和许多天使投资人支持。

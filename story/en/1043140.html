Squatters issue death threats to archaeologist who discovered Caral
Squ屋对发现Caral的考古学家发出死亡威胁

2021-01-04 20:40:25

Illegal squatters have invaded the ruins of the oldest city in the Americas, and made death threats against Ruth Shady, the celebrated Peruvian archaeologist who discovered the 5,000-year-old civilization.

非法擅自占地者入侵了美洲最古老的城市的废墟，并对著名的秘鲁考古学家露丝·沙迪（Ruth Shady）造成了死亡威胁，后者发现了具有5000年历史的文明。

The threats came via telephone calls and messages to various workers at the archeological site at the height of Peru’s Covid-19 pandemic. They followed reports to the police and prosecutors about the invasions of the ancient ruins of Caral.

威胁是通过电话和向秘鲁Covid-19大流行高峰时期的考古现场的各种工人发出的消息来实现的。他们跟踪了向警察和检察官举报有关Caral古代遗址的入侵情况。

“They called the site’s lawyer and said if he continued to protect me they would kill him, along with me, and bury us five metres below the ground,” said Shady, 73.

现年73岁的谢迪说：“他们打电话给现场的律师，说如果他继续保护我，他们会杀了他，并和我一起埋在地下五米处。”

“Then they killed our dog as a warning. They poisoned her, as if to say, look at what will happen to you,” she said.

“然后他们警告了我们杀死了我们的狗。他们毒死了她，好像是在说，看看你会发生什么。”她说。

It is not the first time Shady has been threatened or attacked. In 2003, she was shot in the chest during an assault on the 626-hectare (1,546-acre) archaeological complex which was declared a Unesco world heritage site in 2009.

Shady并不是第一次受到威胁或攻击。 2003年，她袭击了626公顷（1,546英亩）的考古遗址，并于2009年被宣布为联合国教科文组织世界遗产。

After nine invasions of the sacred city during the pandemic period, Shady and her team repeatedly asked the authorities to intervene.

在大流行期间，圣城遭到9次入侵，Shady和她的团队一再要求当局进行干预。

“There is a feeling that there is no authority dedicated to the protection and defence of our heritage. It’s a huge worry,” she said.

“有一种感觉，就是没有专门的机构来保护和捍卫我们的遗产。这是一个巨大的担忧，”她说。

In July, squatters using a heavy digger knocked down adobe walls and tore up the ground destroying ancient ceramics, tombs containing mummies, textiles and household remains, before police and the site’s staff were able to stop them.

今年7月，擅自占地者使用重型挖掘机将土坯墙推倒并撕毁地面，摧毁了古老的陶瓷，包含木乃伊的坟墓，纺织品和家庭遗骸，随后警察和现场工作人员阻止了他们。

As a result of Shady’s pleas, a police car now patrols the archeological site day and night but nothing has been done to punish or evict the land invaders.

由于谢迪（Shady）的恳求，现在有一辆警车日夜巡逻考古现场，但没有采取任何行动惩罚或驱逐土地入侵者。

The squatters are believed to belong to a single extended family, and claim the land was given to them in the 1970s during Peru’s controversial agrarian land reform which was pushed through by a leftist military dictatorship.

据信这些擅自占地者属于一个大家庭，并声称该土地是在1970年代秘鲁有争议的土地改革期间由左派军事独裁政权推动的，而土地改革是在1970年代完成的。

Shady denies the claim: “They do not have a single land title. The owner of the land is the Peruvian state.”

Shady否认了这一主张：“他们没有一个地名。土地的所有者是秘鲁国家。”

A planned eviction of one of the squatters was thwarted in December when a local prosecutor and official failed to give the order to proceed despite having the support of police officers, Shady said.

沙迪说，12月，原计划驱逐擅自占地者的计划遭到挫败，原因是当地检察官和官员尽管得到了警察的支持，却未下达命令继续进行。

Land prices in the area have risen from around $5,000 per hectare to as much as $50,000 per hectare, as outsiders rush to buy land around the prestigious archaeological site which is surrounded by a 56 sq mile buffer zone.

该地区的土地价格已从每公顷5,000美元左右上涨至每公顷50,000美元，这是因为局外人急于在这个著名的考古遗址周围购买土地，该遗址周围有56平方英里的缓冲区。

Shady, who was named on the BBC’s 100 Women list last year, first visited Caral in 1978. But it was not until 1994 that she discovered the ancient city and began to properly excavate the site, which is situated on a dry desert terrace overlooking the Supe river valley nearly 200km (124 miles) north of Lima.

Shady去年曾入选BBC的100名女性榜单，她于1978年首次访问了Caral。但是直到1994年，她才发现了这座古城并开始适当发掘该遗址，该遗址位于一个干燥的沙漠露台上，俯瞰着沙漠。苏佩河谷在利马以北近200公里（124英里）处。

What she uncovered was the “oldest centre of civilization in the Americas” which Unesco describes as “exceptionally well-preserved” with a complex architectural design with “monumental stone and earthen platform mounts and sunken circular courts”. Organic material found at the site has been carbon-dated back to 2627 BCE

她发现的是“美洲最古老的文明中心”，联合国教科文组织将其描述为“保存得特别好”，其建筑设计复杂，带有“纪念碑石和土制的平台架以及下沉的圆形庭院”。在该地点发现的有机材料已追溯至公元前2627年

Shady and her team continue to investigate and excavate a dozen former settlements, half of the 24 situated in the Supe valley which form part of the Caral-Supe civilization. Their findings have revealed musical instruments such as flutes made of animal and bird bones and evidence of the cultivation of multi-coloured cotton used in textiles.

Shady和她的团队继续调查和发掘十二个以前的定居点，这是位于Supe山谷的24个聚居区中的一半，这些聚居区属于Caral-Supe文明。他们的发现揭示了诸如动物和鸟骨头制成的长笛之类的乐器，并证明了用于纺织品的多色棉的种植。

“We can’t allow archeological sites to continue being invaded and destroyed because it is an unwritten history and we recover that history through our investigation,” said Shady. “If we can’t do that it is like burning a book which no one will ever read.”

沙迪说：“我们不允许考古遗址继续受到入侵和破坏，因为这是不成文的历史，我们通过调查来恢复历史。” “如果我们做不到，那就像在烧一本没人会读过的书。”

“I hope we can continue to investigate and continue to recover our history because it has such an interesting message,” she added. “It was a very, very peaceful society. We have not found even a single walled settlement.”

她补充说：“我希望我们能够继续调查并继续恢复我们的历史，因为它传达了如此有趣的信息。” “这是一个非常非常和平的社会。我们甚至都没有找到一个隔离墙。”

“There is message there that we, human beings, should live in harmony between ourselves and nature,” Shady concluded. “We are living through this pandemic, in part, due to our mistreatment of nature.”

沙迪总结说：“有信息表明，我们人类应该在自己与自然之间和谐相处。” “部分原因是我们对自然的虐待，使我们生活在这种大流行中。”

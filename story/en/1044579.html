High Frequency Trading over Shortwave Radio
通过短波无线电进行高频交易

2021-01-17 01:57:05

Since 2014 this blog has extensively covered the wireless networks built by high-frequency trading (HFT) firms or network providers to reduce latencies between the different exchanges around the world (market makers need fast connectivity to manage risk, news traders also need to be fast, etc.). This epic investigation on microwave, which started with HFT in my backyard, will be fully reported in a book I’m currently writing (in French for now). As I’m quite busy with this writing (and other/more interesting matters about market structure), I didn’t really have the time to check out what I have been hearing about “shortwave” or “high frequency” radio. This is the way high-frequency trading firms may use shortwave radio to directly connect widely-separated locations (in short, traders are willing to use shortwave to cross oceans with less latency than any fiber – like Hibernia). But recently I got more intel about the situation (and some fun anecdotes). With some help from the US, I found that a firm purchased a field for more than 1$M to build towers and antennas; with some help from the EU, I got hints about Germany; and I dug into UK public records. I even met, last March in Amsterdam, people involved in those projects. Not surprisingly, at least five HFT/market making firms showed up behind the shell companies/names they use to hide. The usual suspects. Above all, I have been contacted recently by someone from Chicago, Bob, who decided to investigate the “shortwave” networks in his backyard. Today I’m pleased to host Bob as a new guest writer on this blog. This first part of the “Shortwave Trading” series is released at the same time Bob is talking about what he found at the STAC Summit in Chicago. Next parts will follow soon.

自2014年以来，此博客广泛涵盖了由高频交易（HFT）公司或网络提供商构建的无线网络，以减少全球不同交易所之间的延迟（做市商需要快速连接以管理风险，新闻交易者也需要快速等）。这项史诗般的微波调查始于我家后院的HFT，将在我目前正在写的书中（目前以法语）全面报道。由于我忙于撰写本文（以及有关市场结构的其他/更有趣的问题），所以我真的没有时间查看我听到的有关“短波”或“高频”广播的信息。这就是高频交易公司可以使用短波无线电直接连接相距较远的位置的方式（简而言之，交易商愿意使用短波以比任何光纤（例如Hibernia）都少的延迟来穿越海洋）。但是最近我对情况有了更多的了解（以及一些有趣的轶事）。在美国的一些帮助下，我发现一家公司以超过1百万美元的价格购买了一块田地来建造塔和天线。在欧盟的一些帮助下，我对德国有了一些暗示。我研究了英国的公共记录。我什至于去年三月在阿姆斯特丹遇到了参与这些项目的人。毫不奇怪，至少有五家HFT /做市商出现在他们用来隐藏的空壳公司/名称后面。普通嫌疑犯。最重要的是，最近有来自芝加哥的Bob与我联系，他决定研究他家后院的“短波”网络。今天，我很高兴在此博客中主持Bob作为新的客座作家。 Bob在谈论他在芝加哥的STAC峰会上发现的内容的同时，发布了“短波交易”系列的第一部分。接下来的部分将很快推出。

I’m Bob Van Valzah and I’m on a “gardening leave” between jobs working as a Performance Engineer for high-frequency trading firms in Chicago. I recently stumbled onto the first evidence of shortwave trading at a site in West Chicago, Illinois and then used Federal Communications Commission (FCC) database research techniques pioneered by the owner of this blog to find two more sites. My non-disclosure agreements with employers would typically prevent me from talking about this, but my trading-related discoveries are my own while on gardening leave. The results of my research may be of interest here and there, so I offered to do some guest posting on this blog. I’ll have to go quiet again in July when I head back to work.

我是Bob Van Valzah，我是芝加哥高频交易公司的绩效工程师，在工作之间享有“休假”。我最近在伊利诺伊州西芝加哥的一个站点上偶然发现了短波交易的第一个证据，然后使用了由该博客的所有者率先使用的联邦通信委员会（FCC）数据库研究技术来找到另外两个站点。我与雇主的保密协议通常会阻止我谈论此事，但是与我的交易相关的发现是我在休假期间的发现。我的研究结果可能在这里和那里都很有趣，因此我愿意在此博客上做一些嘉宾发布。我要在7月再次上班时再安静一点。

It might be reasonable to assume that “West Chicago” was just a western part of Chicago, Illinois. In fact, it’s a city in its own right, twenty some miles west of the big city. A typical weekend will find me cycling down a bike path that runs nearby. In March, muddy path conditions caused me to detour through an industrial park in West Chicago where the West Chicago Tower Mystery began. I looked up from the handlebars and saw this tower.

可以合理地假设“西芝加哥”只是伊利诺伊州芝加哥的西部地区。实际上，它本身就是一座城市，位于大城市以西二十英里处。一个典型的周末会发现我沿着附近的自行车道骑自行车。在三月，泥泞的道路条件使我绕过西芝加哥的一个工业园区，在那里开始了西芝加哥塔之谜。我从车把上抬起头，看到了这座塔。

I’m an amateur radio operator, or “ham,” (KE9YQ) and those four big antennas look a lot like ones that a ham would use to talk across an ocean. I’ll just call them shortwave antennas here, but practitioners of aluminum feng shui (antenna design) will recognize them as log periodic. There’s also a microwave dish antenna between the top two shortwave antennas. This much I could tell just by looking.

我是业余无线电操作员，又称“火腿”（KE9YQ），这四个大天线看起来很像火腿用来在大洋彼岸说话的天线。我在这里只称其为短波天线，但是铝制风水（天线设计）的从业人员会把它们识别为对数周期的。前两个短波天线之间还有一个微波碟形天线。仅凭外观我就能说出这么多。

Most of the radio towers you see each day are cell towers. There are 215,000 of them in the US. They differ from other radio towers in that they will almost always have one or more triangular structures with three or more directional antennas on each side of the triangle. A grid of cell towers covers an area with roughly hexagonal cells so that you get a good signal everywhere.

您每天看到的大多数无线电塔都是蜂窝塔。在美国有215,000。它们与其他无线电塔的不同之处在于，它们几乎总是具有一个或多个三角形结构，在三角形的每一侧均具有三个或更多定向天线。细胞塔的网格覆盖了大约六边形细胞的区域，因此您到处都能获得良好的信号。

The West Chicago tower had no triangular structure, so I was pretty sure it wasn’t a cell tower. And these antennas were huge compared to regular cell tower antennas. I could literally see them 1/2 mile away. Some cell towers do have microwave dish antennas when it’s hard to bring fiber to a site, but shortwave antennas didn’t seem to go together with microwave in my mind.

西芝加哥塔没有三角形结构，所以我很确定它不是手机塔。与常规蜂窝塔天线相比，这些天线非常庞大。我真的可以看到他们1/2英里远。当很难将光纤带到现场时，有些手机发射塔确实装有微波碟形天线，但在我看来，短波天线似乎并没有与微波一起使用。

With my curiosity up, I pedaled to the base of the tower and took a look around. Like all cell towers, there’s a barbed wire perimeter fence and I could see a power meter where electricity enters the site. The panel allowed for up to four power meters because it’s common for two or more carriers to share a single site. There’s also a master power switch for each carrier so that they can shut off their juice for maintenance without knocking the other carriers off the air.

出于好奇，我踏上了塔的底部，环顾四周。像所有的通讯塔一样，那里有一个铁丝网围栏，我可以看到电表将电力输入现场。该面板最多可容纳四个功率计，因为两个或多个运营商共享一个站点是很常见的。每个载具都有一个主电源开关，这样他们就可以关闭榨汁进行维护，而不必敲开其他载具。

I walked right up to the fence and zoomed in on the power meter. It said “ U.S. Cellular.” They are indeed a regional cellular carrier, but they only serve 23 U.S. states and wouldn’t want their signals crossing any oceans.

我径直走向篱笆，放大了功率计。它说“美国蜂窝电话”。它们确实是地区性的蜂窝运营商，但是它们仅服务于美国23个州，并且不希望它们的信号跨越任何大洋。

Mystery #2: Why would a regional cellular carrier pay for power to cross oceans? Riding on a hot day, I may stop for a drink in the shade of a cell tower, so I’ve seen what a lot of cell sites look like. There’s often a small building inside the perimeter fence to hold the equipment that runs the cell site. The ground inside the perimeter fence is typically coarsely crushed rock to prevent weeds from growing.

谜团2：为什么区域性蜂窝运营商要为穿越大洋的力量付费？在炎热的日子里，我可能会停在蜂窝塔的树荫下喝一杯，所以我看到了很多蜂窝站点的样子。外围围墙内通常有一栋小建筑物，用来容纳运行牢房的设备。外围围栏内的地面通常是粗碎的岩石，以防止杂草生长。

An odd thing about this “cell site” was that it had a garbage pile inside the fence. Cell sites are unmanned and don’t need much maintenance. Crews who do work there typically leave the site clean and take their garbage with them. There is no trash pickup at sites like this, so it seemed odd that there’d be cans full of lunch wrappers and cardboard boxes. If this was a cell site, it was maintained by much sloppier crews than the ones I’d typically seen.

这个“牢房”的奇怪之处在于它在篱笆内有一个垃圾堆。牢房站点无人值守，不需要太多维护。在那工作的工作人员通常会使现场保持清洁，并随身携带垃圾。像这样的场所没有垃圾收集器，所以罐子里装满了午餐包装纸和纸箱似乎很奇怪。如果这是一个牢房站点，那么它由比我通常见过的工作人员少得多的工作人员维护。

One box in the garbage pile caught my eye so I zoomed in through the fence and got the best shot of the label I could with my iPhone. I had never heard of the company Ettus Research or the USRP X300 device. With all the cell sites in the world, you’d think they’d be made from off-the-shelf parts, not stuff cooked up in a research and development lab.

垃圾堆中的一个盒子引起了我的注意，因此我放大了篱笆，并获得了iPhone所能拍摄的最佳标签。我从未听说过Ettus Research公司或USRP X300设备。对于世界上所有的网点，您都认为它们是由现成的零件制成的，而不是由研发实验室烹制的东西。

Mystery #3: Why would a cell site need equipment from a research company? Most any cell site with a tower will have a sign with a seven-digit number known as the FCC Antenna Structure Registration (ASR) number. If you were a cellular carrier out shopping for a good place to put your antennas, you could use this number to find the height of the tower and get other useful nuggets from the FCC database. I took a picture of the number since I thought it might come in handy when I got home.

谜题3：为什么一个细胞基地需要研究公司的设备？大多数带有塔的蜂窝小区站点都会带有一个七位数的标志，称为FCC天线结构注册（ASR）号。如果您是蜂窝运营商，想买一个放置天线的好地方，则可以使用此数字找到塔的高度，并从FCC数据库中获取其他有用的块。我为这个号码拍照，因为我认为到家后可能会派上用场。

Back home, I pulled up the FCC ASR database and found that the tower was indeed built by U.S. Cellular in 2009. Digging deeper, I also found a record that they had sold it in late 2014. This sort of transaction is pretty common in the business. As subscribers come and go, cellular carriers have to move their cell sites and keep adjusting their antenna heights to maintain good coverage everywhere.

回到家，我打开了FCC ASR数据库，发现这座塔楼确实是US Cellular在2009年建造的。深入挖掘，我还发现了他们在2014年底出售它的记录。商业。随着订户的来回，蜂窝运营商必须移动其蜂窝站点并不断调整其天线高度，以在任何地方保持良好的覆盖范围。

Google Street View is a wonderful tool for looking at the way the world used to be. I found a view of the tower from August 2012 when it had already been picked clean of all cellular antennas (they have resale value!), with only the tell-tale triangular mounting structure remaining at the top of the tower.

Google街景视图是一个很好的工具，可以让您了解过去的世界。我从2012年8月起就看到了塔楼的景象，当时塔楼已经被清理掉了所有蜂窝天线（它们具有转售价值！），而塔顶仅保留了讲故事的三角形安装结构。

So part of the history was becoming clear. The tower was built by U.S. Cellular in 2009, but fell into disuse sometime before 2012. It was eventually sold in late 2014.

因此历史的一部分变得清晰起来。该塔楼由美国蜂窝网络公司（U.S. Cellular）于2009年建造，但在2012年之前的某个时候就被废弃了。它最终于2014年底出售。

As is common with cell sites these days, the tower site was sold to Vertical Bridge, an antenna site management company. Instead of owning antenna sites themselves, it now seems common for carriers to just lease space on towers at antenna sites owned by management companies. So that’s no real clue about current ownership or usage of the tower.

如今，与蜂窝基站一样，塔基站被卖给了天线基站管理公司Vertical Bridge。如今，运营商似乎不再拥有天线站点本身，而是在管理公司拥有的天线站点的塔架上租用空间。因此，这不是有关塔的当前所有权或使用情况的真正线索。

Mystery #4: Where is the other end of that microwave link? Microwave links require a license for operation, so I returned to the FCC databases and looked for licenses associated with that tower. Each license gives the GPS coordinates of both ends of the link and the FCC has a helpful mapping function. It showed a direct link between the West Chicago tower and another tower right by the Chicago Mercantile Exchange! It was still just circumstantial evidence, but this was the first good clue that the West Chicago tower had something to do with trading. It was looking less and less like spooks were using the tower.

谜题4：微波链路的另一端在哪里？微波链接需要许可证才能进行操作，因此我返回了FCC数据库，并查找了与该塔相关的许可证。每个许可证都提供了链路两端的GPS坐标，而FCC具有有用的映射功能。它显示了西芝加哥塔与芝加哥商品交易所就在另一座塔之间的直接联系！仍然只是间接的证据，但这是西芝加哥塔楼与交易有关的第一个很好的线索。看起来越来越像幽灵在使用塔。

Mystery #5: What was in that box in the garbage pile? Google helpfully explains that Ettus Research is the leading provider of Software Defined Radios (SDR). These are the devices you need to make any kind of custom radio you want. Just change the software and you can send voice, pictures, video, or trading information. The particular model in the box is their top-of-the line, with an FPGA, two 10 gigabit Ethernet ports, and a PCIe bus interface. The pair that was in that box cost nearly $10,000. You don’t need gear like this at a cell site.

谜题5：垃圾堆里那个盒子里有什么？ Google有用地解释说Ettus Research是软件无线电（SDR）的领先提供商。这些是制作任意种类的自定义收音机所需的设备。只需更改软件，您就可以发送语音，图片，视频或交易信息。包装盒中的特殊型号是它们的顶级产品，带有一个FPGA，两个10 Gb以太网端口和一个PCIe总线接口。那个盒子里的那对花了将近10,000美元。您在牢房站点不需要这样的设备。

Mystery #6: What could be interesting across an ocean? I’m much better with the technology of trading rather than understanding how to make money with trading. But it seemed like a reasonable guess that prices on the markets at CME would be correlated with activity in European markets – let’s say, in Slough (UK) and Frankfurt (Germany). So I fired up Google Earth and plotted the lines that a radio signal would have to travel between West Chicago and the markets in London and Frankfurt.

谜题6：在整个海洋中，什么可能是有趣的？我更擅长交易技术，而不是了解如何通过交易赚钱。但这似乎是合理的猜测，芝商所的市场价格会与欧洲市场的活动相关联-例如，在Slough（英国）和Frankfurt（德国）。因此，我启动了Google Earth，并绘制了无线电信号在西芝加哥与伦敦和法兰克福市场之间传播的线路。

We are so used to seeing flattened projections of earth that it’s easy for a Chicagoan to think of Europe as being east of Chicago. But in fact the shortest way to Europe is northeast, at about a 45º angle.

我们已经习惯于看到平坦的地球投影，以至于芝加哥人很容易将欧洲视为芝加哥的东部。但是实际上，通往欧洲的最短途径是东北，呈45度角。

This angle is important because those shortwave antennas on the tower are directional. If you know you want to talk to Europe, why send any of your signal toward Guam? Directional shortwave antennas focus your signal in the direction of the pointy end of the antenna. In the photo of the tower above, I’m standing right under the path where the top antenna is pointing.

这个角度很重要，因为塔上的那些短波天线是定向的。如果您知道要与欧洲交谈，为什么要向关岛发送信号？定向短波天线将信号聚焦在天线尖端的方向上。在上方塔楼的照片中，我正站在顶部天线指向的路径下。

At shortwave frequencies, it’s difficult to make antennas with a narrow beam width. London and Frankfurt are only about 5º apart when you’re aiming from Chicago, so one antenna should hit both cities. This raises the question of why the tower needed four shortwave antennas, all pointed in pretty much the same direction.

在短波频率下，很难制造出窄波束宽度的天线。当您从芝加哥瞄准时，伦敦和法兰克福仅相隔5º，因此，两个城市都应该使用一根天线。这就提出了一个问题，即塔为何需要四个指向几乎相同方向的短波天线。

Some of the antennas on the tower focused their signal well, producing a more powerful signal, but only operate over a narrow range of shortwave frequencies. Others operate over a wide range of frequencies, but can’t produce a strong signal.

塔上的某些天线可以很好地集中其信号，从而产生更强大的信号，但只能在狭窄的短波频率范围内工作。其他的则可以在很宽的频率范围内工作，但不能产生强信号。

It also appeared that the second shortwave antenna up from the bottom had been damaged in a wind storm or perhaps poorly installed because four of its five elements had been knocked significantly out of the horizontal plane. Nonetheless, I was surprised when I visited site a few weeks later and found that the lower two antennas had been removed. So this site is being actively developed in the spring of 2018. They’re moving antennas around and they haven’t gotten rid of the garbage pile, so there’s still work to do.

似乎还发现，从底部向上的第二个短波天线在暴风雨中被损坏或安装不当，因为它的五个元素中的四个已被明显地从水平面上撞下来。但是，几周后当我访问现场时，我感到很惊讶，发现下部的两个天线已被拆除。因此，该网站将于2018年春季积极开发。他们正在移动天线，并且没有摆脱垃圾堆，因此仍有工作要做。

Bringing it all together, I’ve discovered that this West Chicago tower has all the ingredients needed to make a low-latency transatlantic market connection. I’ve also found two other sites around Chicago with massive shortwave antennas pointed at Europe and microwave links to CME, so there’s more than one horse in this race. I’ll document these sites in a coming post. Licenses also exist on the east coast. I’ll be looking for them on trip in mid-June. FCC searches have also turned up licenses in Anchorage, Alaska which is on the path from CME to Tokyo [ Sniper’s note: that reminds me I didn’t check recently what is going on around Go West]. Spotters are already at work looking for antennas around London and Frankfurt.

综上所述，我发现这座西芝加哥塔楼具有建立低延迟的跨大西洋市场联系所需的所有要素。我还在芝加哥附近的其他两个地点发现了指向欧洲的大型短波天线和通向CME的微波链路，因此本场比赛有不止一匹马。我将在以后的文章中记录这些网站。东海岸也有许可证。我将在六月中旬寻找他们。 FCC搜索还增加了阿拉斯加安克雷奇（Anchorage）的许可证，这是从CME到东京的路线[狙击手的说明：这提醒我我最近没有检查过West West周围的情况]。侦察员已经在寻找伦敦和法兰克福周围的天线。

Shortwave is no panacea. It’s unreliable, expensive, and very low bandwidth. Think dialup speeds. But you can’t beat it for latency. I plan to go into more detail on shortwave tradeoffs in a future post. You might be wondering if anybody can receive their signal, perhaps with the possibility of getting an order to market faster. Yes, anybody can receive it, but the sender would be foolish not to encrypt it in a way that can’t be cracked. This is easily done without impacting performance.

短波不是万能药。它不可靠，价格昂贵且带宽非常低。考虑拨号速度。但是您不能因延迟而战胜它。我计划在以后的文章中详细讨论短波权衡问题。您可能想知道是否有人可以收到他们的信号，也许可以更快地将订单推向市场。是的，任何人都可以接收它，但是发件人如果不以无法破解的方式对其进行加密将是愚蠢的。这很容易完成，而不会影响性能。

A muddy bike path diverted me from my usual route and I happened upon the tower. Being a ham, I recognized the shortwave antennas. I could see the potential, having worked in trading.

Italy's antitrust watchdog fines Apple €10M for alleged misleading iPhone water resistance claims in its promotional messages
意大利反托拉斯监督机构对苹果公司处以1000万欧元的罚款，原因是苹果在其促销信息中涉嫌误导iPhone的防水功能

2020-11-30 22:01:55

Apple has been slapped with a 10 million euro ($12 million) fine by Italy's antitrust watchdog for unfair commercial practices related to its iPhone marketing in the country.

意大利的反托拉斯监管机构已对苹果公司处以1000万欧元（合1200万美元）的罚款，原因是该公司与该国iPhone营销有关的不正当商业行为。

Specifically, Apple is being charged for misleading claims in promotional messages about how deep and how long iPhones can be submerged in water without being damaged.

具体来说，苹果公司在促销信息中误导了有关iPhone可以浸入水中多长时间而不会损坏的说法，因此受到指控。

In marketing materials related to ‌iPhone‌ 8, ‌iPhone‌ 8 Plus, iPhone XR, iPhone XS, ‌iPhone XS‌ Max, iPhone 11, ‌iPhone 11‌ Pro and iPhone 11 Pro Max, Apple said its iPhones were water resistant at a depth of between one and four meters for up to 30 minutes, depending on the model.

在与iPhone 8，iPhone 8 Plus，iPhone XR，iPhone XS，iPhone XS Max，iPhone 11，iPhone 11 Pro和iPhone 11 Pro Max有关的营销材料中，苹果表示其iPhone可以在1-4米的深度防水最多持续30分钟，具体取决于型号。

However, according to the country's competition regulator, the messages did not clarify that the claims are only true under specific conditions, for example during controlled laboratory tests with the use of static and pure water, and not in the normal conditions of use by consumers.

但是，据该国的竞争监管机构称，这些消息并未澄清这些声明仅在特定条件下才是正确的，例如在使用静态和纯水的受控实验室测试中，而不是在消费者正常使用条件下。

The regulator also took issue with Apple's warranty terms, which do not cover damage caused by liquids. The authority considered it inappropriate to push an "aggressive" commercial practice highlighting water resistance as a feature, while at the same time refusing to provide post-sales warranty assistance if the ‌iPhone‌ models in question suffer water damage.

监管机构还对Apple的保修条款提出了质疑，该保修条款不涵盖由液体引起的损坏。当局认为不宜推行以“耐水”为特征的“激进”商业惯例，同时如果有问题的“ iPhone”型号遭受水损坏，则拒绝提供售后保修帮助。

This isn't the first time Apple has fallen under the radar of Italy's antitrust watchdog. In 2018 Apple was fined 10 million euros (about $11.5 million USD) for "planned obsolescence" of its smartphones, following the regulator's investigation into reports of iPhone battery slowdowns.

这并不是苹果公司第一次陷入意大利反托拉斯监督机构的监视之下。在监管机构对iPhone电池速度下降的报道进行调查之后，2018年，苹果因其智能手机“计划淘汰”而被罚款1000万欧元（约合1150万美元）。

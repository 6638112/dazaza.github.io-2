Undocumented immigrants far less likely to commit crimes in US than citizens
无证移民在美国犯罪的可能性远低于公民

2020-12-08 04:32:28

Crime rates among undocumented immigrants are just a fraction of those of their U.S.-born neighbors, according to a first-of-its-kind analysis of Texas arrest and conviction records. Compared to , U.S. citizens were twice as likely to be arrested for violent felonies in Texas from 2012 to 2018, two-and-a-half times more likely to be arrested for felony drug crimes, and over four times more likely to be arrested for felony property crimes, according to a study published by University of Wisconsin-Madison researchers today in the Proceedings of the National Academy of Sciences.

根据对得克萨斯州逮捕和定罪记录的首次实证分析，无证移民中的犯罪率只是其美国出生邻居的一小部分。与2012年相比，美国公民在2012年至2018年因德克萨斯州的暴力重罪而被逮捕的可能性是两倍，因重罪而被逮捕的可能性是后者的两倍半，而因犯罪而被逮捕的可能性则高出四倍。威斯康星大学麦迪逊分校的研究人员今天在《美国国家科学院院刊》上发表的一项研究表明，重罪属于重罪。

With new access to Texas' computerized criminal history data for more than 1.8 million arrests over six years, UW-Madison sociology professor Michael Light and co-authors Jingying He and Jason Robey—who were both UW-Madison graduate students—were able to directly calculate the rates at which U.S.-born citizens, and undocumented immigrants were arrested for a range of felony offenses.

拥有德克萨斯州的新访问权限威斯康星大学麦迪逊分校的社会学教授迈克尔·莱特（Michael Light）和合著者何静英和贾森罗比（Jason Robey）都是威斯康星大学麦迪逊分校的研究生，他们在过去的六年中有超过180万次逮捕的计算机犯罪历史数据，能够直接计算出美国出生的公民和无证件移民因一系列重罪而被捕。

Previous studies, including others published by Light, have drawn similar, though less direct, conclusions. These studies were limited to comparing rate trends to immigration trends, because records matching crimes to the immigration status of perpetrators weren't available.

先前的研究，包括由Light发表的其他研究，都得出了相似但不那么直接的结论。这些研究仅限于将比率趋势与移民趋势进行比较，因为没有与犯罪者的犯罪状况相匹配的记录。

"It's like asking if rise when unemployment goes up. That's not the same as asking if commit more crimes," says Light, who led the new study. "Those are related questions, but not the same question."

就像问失业率上升时是否上升。这与询问是否犯下更多罪行是不同的，负责这项新研究的Light说。 ＆＃34;这些是相关的问题，但不是相同的问题。＆＃34;

One of the reasons Texas keeps such fine-grained records on offenders is the 's Secure Communities Program, which mandates sharing information on immigration status and is pitched as a way to deport criminals before they can commit more crimes in the United States

得克萨斯州对犯罪分子保持如此详尽的记录的原因之一是“安全社区计划”，该计划要求共享移民身份信息，并被视为一种驱逐罪犯的方式，以便他们在美国实施更多犯罪之前

While immigration is a top priority of federal law enforcement agencies, undocumented immigrants are far more likely to encounter local law enforcement officers than federal agents. Through Secure Communities, when local police book arrested suspects, their biometric information is compared to federal databases, giving U.S. Immigration and Customs Enforcement notice when a non-citizen is arrested.

虽然移民是联邦执法机构的头等大事，但无证移民比联邦特工更容易遇到当地执法人员。通过安全社区（Secure Communities），当当地警察的书中逮捕了嫌疑人时，他们的生物特征信息就会与联邦数据库进行比较，从而在非公民被捕时向美国移民和海关执法部门发出通知。

But at least two independent studies suggest Secure Communities didn't have any effect on crime rates, according to Light, despite deporting more than 200,000 people in its first four years.

但据Light称，至少有两项独立研究表明，安全社区对犯罪率没有任何影响，尽管在最初的四年中驱逐了20万人。

"If the plan was to make communities safer, to reduce the likelihood of, say, a felony violent assault in these communities through deportation, it did not deliver on that promise," Light says. "Our results help us understand why that is. The population of people we deported simply were not a unique criminal risk. Removing them isn't going to make you all that safer."

＆＃34;如果该计划是为了使社区更安全，以减少通过驱逐出境而在这些社区中实施重罪暴力袭击的可能性，则该计划没有兑现这一承诺，＆＃34;光说。 ＆＃34;我们的结果有助于我们理解为什么会这样。我们所驱逐的人口根本不是唯一的犯罪风险。删除它们并不会使您更安全。

While the new study can't describe why undocumented immigrants commit fewer crimes, it's a common finding that first generation immigrants tend to be less crime prone—and undocumented immigrants are, almost by definition, first generation immigrants. Light believes there are many reasons to expect a lower crime rate among undocumented immigrants.

尽管这项新研究无法描述为何无证移民犯罪的发生率更低，但这是一个普遍的发现，即第一代移民往往不那么容易犯罪-从定义上来说，无证移民几乎就是第一代移民。莱特（Light）认为，有很多理由期望无证移民的犯罪率会降低。

"They have a tremendous incentive to avoid criminal wrongdoing. The greatest fear among undocumented immigrants is getting in legal trouble that leads to deportation," says Light, whose work is supported by the National Science Foundation and National Institute of Justice.

＆＃34;他们有极大的动机来避免犯罪。在无证移民中，最大的恐惧是法律上的麻烦导致驱逐出境，＆＃34; Light说，他的工作得到了美国国家科学基金会和美国国家司法研究所的支持。

Another factor at work may be that immigration, especially illegal entry into the U.S., is not easy. It attracts people with particular motives.

起作用的另一个因素可能是移民，特别是非法进入美国并不容易。它吸引了具有特殊动机的人。

"There's lots of opportunity to commit crimes in Mexico and Venezuela and other places people are emigrating from," Light says. "The argument is that many people who want to immigrate are selected on attributes like ambition to achieve, to find economic opportunities, and those types of things aren't very highly correlated with having a criminal propensity."

在墨西哥和委内瑞拉以及其他人们移民的地方，有很多犯罪的机会，光说。 ＆＃34;论点是，许多想移民的人都是根据实现志向，寻找经济机会等属性来选择的，而这些类型的事物与犯罪倾向并没有高度关联。 ;

The researchers repeated their crime-rate analysis with subtle shifts in data—using convictions instead of arrests, misdemeanors in addition to felonies, size estimates of undocumented immigrant populations from both the Pew Research Center and the Center for Migration Studies.

研究人员在数据进行细微变化的情况下重复了犯罪率分析-使用定罪而不是逮捕，轻罪，重罪以及皮尤研究中心和移民研究中心对无证件移民人口的规模估计。

The much lower crime rates for undocumented immigrants remained in each case, results Light thinks should be useful in immigration policymaking.

莱特（Light）认为，在每种情况下，针对无证移民的犯罪率均要低得多。

"The conversation about undocumented immigration should be informed by the best empirical evidence," he says.

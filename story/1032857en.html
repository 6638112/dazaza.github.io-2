SpaceX Starlink测试仪将用户终端带入森林，获得120 Mbps

2020-11-03 05:15:57

SpaceX Starlink beta users are starting to share their experiences, confirming that the satellite service can provide fast broadband speeds and low latencies in remote areas. A beta tester who goes by the Reddit username Wandering-coder brought his new Starlink equipment and a portable power supply to a national forest in Idaho, where he connected to the Internet with 120Mbps download speeds.

SpaceX Starlink测试版用户开始分享他们的经验，证实了这项卫星服务可以在偏远地区提供快速的宽带速度和低的延迟。一位名为流浪编码器的Reddit测试者将他的新Starlink设备和便携式电源带到了爱达荷州的一个国家森林，在那里他以120 Mbps的下载速度连接到互联网。

Starlink "works beautifully," he wrote yesterday. "I did a real-time video call and some tests. My power supply is max 300w, and the drain for the whole system while active was around 116w." Starlink pulled that off in a place where Wandering-coder couldn't get any cellular service from Google Fi, which relies on the T-Mobile and US Cellular networks. "There is no cell here with any carrier," he wrote.

他昨天写道，StarLink效果非常好。我做了一个实时视频通话和一些测试。我的电源是最大300W，激活时整个系统的耗电量约为116W。在这个地方，流浪编码器无法从依赖T-Mobile和美国蜂窝网络的Google Fi获得任何蜂窝服务。这里没有任何运营商的牢房，他写道。

Wandering-coder used Starlink connectivity in the forest to make that post on Reddit and to upload a series of pictures to Imgur. Wandering-coder told Ars that he uses Starlink at home in North Idaho and that he conducted his test at the Hayden Creek Shooting Range in the Idaho panhandle's Coeur d'Alene National Forest.

流浪编码器使用森林中的Starlink连接在Reddit上发布了这篇帖子，并将一系列照片上传到Imgur。流浪编码器告诉Ars，他在北爱达荷州的家里使用Starlink，他是在爱达荷州狭长地带的海登溪射击场进行测试的，地点是爱达荷州的核心国家森林。

Here are some pictures from Wandering-coder's test in the forest and shots of the user terminal outside his home:

以下是流浪编码器在森林中测试的一些照片和用户终端在他家外面的照片：

With the Starlink user terminal/satellite dish placed on the ground in a relatively open part of the forest, Wandering-coder did a speed test that measured downloads of 120Mbps, uploads of 12Mbps, and latency of 37ms. He got worse results in a different, more heavily forested location where he placed the dish closer to the trees, because Starlink needs a clear line of sight to SpaceX satellites. "It didn't work well with a heavy tree canopy/trees directly in the line of sight, as expected," Wandering-coder wrote. "I would be connected only for about 5 seconds at a time. Make sure you have as clear a view of the sky as possible!"

将Starlink用户终端/卫星天线放置在森林中相对开阔的地面上，流浪编码器进行了速度测试，测试了下载速度为120 Mbps，上传速度为12 Mbps，延迟为37ms。他在一个不同的，森林更茂密的地方得到了更差的结果，在那里他把碟子放在离树更近的地方，因为Starlink需要清晰的视线才能看到SpaceX卫星。漫游编码器写道，与预期的一样，它在视线正对的大树树冠/树木的情况下效果不佳。我一次只能连接大约5秒钟。确保你尽可能清晰地看到天空！"；

Wandering-coder is not urging other Starlink testers to complete the same experiment, in case SpaceX objects to beta testers using the service away from their registered location. "All things considered, [it's] probably best to keep [the user terminal] where registered until there is official illumination on the topic," he wrote. "Just knowing mobility is possible, though, is nice."

漫游编码器没有敦促其他Starlink测试员完成同样的实验，以防SpaceX反对Beta测试员在远离注册地点使用这项服务。他写道，从所有方面考虑，最好将[用户终端]保持在注册的位置，直到这个话题有了官方的说明。不过，仅仅知道移动是可能的，这是件好事。

Wandering-coder also shared initial impressions in another Reddit post and a series of pictures of the equipment at his home. Here are some shots from unboxing the equipment:

流浪编码器也在另一篇Reddit帖子中分享了初步印象，并在他家分享了一系列设备的照片。以下是拆卸设备的一些照片：

"Everything is of an extreme build quality, and this works significantly better than I had ever imagined," Wandering-coder wrote. "It feels like it's from the future. Given a top-tier cell phone costs in the $1,000 range, I am completely amazed I have my hands on a setup like this for ~$500, so I am biased positively towards this service."

所有的东西都有极高的构建质量，这比我想象的要好得多，流浪编码器写道。感觉像是来自未来。考虑到一部顶级手机的价格在1000美元左右，我完全惊讶于我能花500美元买到这样的设备，所以我对这项服务有积极的偏见。

As revealed last week, the Starlink beta costs $99 a month plus $499 upfront for the user terminal, mounting tripod, and router.

正如上周透露的那样，Starlink测试版的费用是每月99美元，外加499美元的用户终端、安装三脚架和路由器的预付费用。

At home, Wandering-coder says he got 135Mbps download speeds, 25Mbps uploads, and 21ms latency when the dish was placed in a ground-level spot with "limited obstruction" between the dish and sky. He also tested the user terminal in a different spot with "significant obstruction" in the form of "bad weather, treetops, fences, [and] houses," he wrote. Even in that scenario, he reported download speeds of 46Mbps, upload speeds of 15Mbps, and 41ms latency. He hadn't placed the antenna on his roof yet when he conducted the tests.

在家里，漫游编码器说，当盘子放在地面上，盘子与天空之间的障碍物有限时，他的下载速度为135 Mbps，上传速度为25 Mbps，延迟为21ms。他写道，他还在另一个地方用严重的障碍物测试了用户终端，比如恶劣的天气、树梢、栅栏和房屋。即使在这种情况下，他报告的下载速度为46 Mbps，上传速度为15 Mbps，延迟为41ms。当他进行测试时，他还没有把天线放在屋顶上。

"Given all the obstructions for this connection at the moment, I am amazed at how well it works," he wrote. "Streaming, low-latency video conferencing, and gaming are all completely accessible with this service. Even for the beta, it appears as though they've under-estimated Starlink's capabilities, so I am excited to see it mature."

他写道，考虑到目前这种联系面临的所有障碍，我对它的工作效果感到惊讶。这项服务完全可以访问流媒体、低延迟视频会议和游戏。即使是测试版，他们似乎也低估了Starlink的能力，所以我很兴奋地看到它成熟了。

Though mounting a user terminal on one's roof is bound to be complicated, Wandering-coder wrote that everything else is pretty simple:

虽然在屋顶上安装一个用户终端肯定会很复杂，但漫游编码器写道，其他的一切都很简单：

Bootup is roughly 1 minute when plugged into power, then it takes anywhere from 1 minute to 15 minutes to properly orient mechanically. Positioning is all done automatically, so it's basically plug & play... There is a single powered Ethernet wire from the antenna array to the power supply, and a single powered Ethernet wire from the power supply to the router. The provided antenna wire is fixed to the antenna, but well over 100' (by estimation, I didn't measure the length). There is no setup involved besides physical placement and setting a name of the Wi-Fi network and password—all the tricky stuff is automated.

接通电源后，启动大约需要1分钟，然后需要1分钟到15分钟的时间才能机械地正确定位。定位都是自动完成的，所以基本上就是即插即用。从天线阵列到电源有一条单电源以太网线，从电源到路由器有一条单电源以太网线。提供的天线线是固定在天线上的，但远远超过100'；(据估计，我没有测量长度)。除了物理放置和设置Wi-Fi网络的名称和密码之外，不涉及任何设置-所有棘手的事情都是自动完成的。

New speed-test data collected by Ookla and published by PCMag last week found average Starlink download speeds of 79.5Mbps and average upload speeds of 13.8Mbps in October, when the service was in a more limited beta. The same data found average download speeds of 24.75Mbps for Viasat's Exede service and 19.84Mbps for HughesNet, both of which offer service from geostationary satellites. Upload speeds for Viasat and HughesNet were 3.25Mbps and 2.64Mbps, respectively.

由Ookla收集并由PCMag上周发布的新速度测试数据发现，10月份Starlink的平均下载速度为79.5 Mbps，平均上传速度为13.8 Mbps，当时该服务处于更有限的测试期。同样的数据发现，Viasat的Exude服务和HughesNet的平均下载速度分别为24.75 Mbps和19.84 Mbps，这两种服务都提供地球同步卫星服务。Viasat和HughesNet的上传速度分别为3.25 Mbps和2.64 Mbps。

Starlink's low Earth orbit satellites greatly outperformed the higher-orbit satellites on latency, with Starlink posting a 42ms average. Viasat and HughesNet came in at 643ms and 728ms, respectively, according to PCMag.

StarLink的低地球轨道卫星在延迟时间上大大超过了高轨道卫星，平均为42ms。根据PCMag的数据，ViaSat和HughesNet分别为643ms和728ms。

SpaceX's invitations to new users last week told them to expect "data speeds vary[ing] from 50Mbps to 150Mbps and latency from 20ms to 40ms over the next several months as we enhance the Starlink system... For latency, we expect to achieve 16ms to 19ms by summer 2021."

SpaceX上周向新用户发出的邀请函告诉他们，在接下来的几个月里，随着我们增强Starlink系统，预计数据速度在50 Mbps到150 Mbps之间，延迟在20ms到40ms之间。至于延迟，我们预计到2021年夏天将达到16ms到19ms。

One Montana resident posted a speed test result with a 174Mbps download speed, 33Mbps upload speed, and 39ms latency. "Way out in rural Montana where our alternative is to pay by the gig. Starlink will forever change the game," the beta tester wrote on Reddit yesterday.

一位蒙大拿州居民发布了速度测试结果，下载速度为174 Mbps，上传速度为33 Mbps，延迟为39ms。在蒙大拿州的乡村，我们的另一种选择是按演出付费。这位测试者昨天在Reddit上写道，StarLink将永远改变这个游戏。

The Starlink Reddit community has several resources for tracking beta progress, such as a list of user speed tests and a list of states where at least some people received beta invitations. Those states are Idaho, Michigan, Minnesota, Montana, Oregon, Washington, and Wisconsin. You can also check out one user's 10-second video for a sped-up look at the unboxing process.

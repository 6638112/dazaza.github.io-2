IMF sees legal, economic issues with El Salvador Bitcoin move
国际货币基金组织与萨尔瓦多比特币搬家看到合法的经济问题

2021-06-11 13:05:25

NEW YORK, June 10 (Reuters) - The International Monetary Fund said on Thursday it has a number of economic and legal concerns regarding the move from El Salvador to make bitcoin a parallel legal tender.

纽约6月10日（路透社） - 国际货币基金组织周四表示，有关从萨尔瓦多的举动使比特币成为平行法律招标的经济和法律问题。

El Salvador has become the first country in the world to adopt bitcoin as legal tender, with President Nayib Bukele touting its use for its potential to help Salvadorans living abroad to send remittances back home.

萨尔瓦多已成为世界上第一个作为法律招标的第一个国家，总统·纳布布克尔宣传其利用潜力，以帮助萨尔瓦多人在国外派遣汇款回家。

“Adoption of bitcoin as legal tender raises a number of macroeconomic, financial and legal issues that require very careful analysis,” said Gerry Rice, an IMF spokesman, during a scheduled press briefing.

“采用比特币作为法律招标提出了许多需要非常仔细分析的宏观经济，财务和法律问题，”国际货币基金组织发言人Gerry米说，在预定的新闻发布会上。

“We are following developments closely, and we’ll continue our consultations with the authorities.”

“我们正在密切开发，我们将继续与当局进行磋商。”

Rice said the Fund will later on Thursday meet with Bukele to discuss the bitcoin law. El Salvador is in discussions with the IMF seeking a near $1 billion program.

米饭表示，该基金稍后将于周四与Bukele会面讨论比特币法律。萨尔瓦多与国际货币基金组织寻求近10亿美元的计划进行讨论。

El Salvador’s law means bitcoin will have equal footing with the dollar, which became its official currency 20 years ago. (Reporting by Rodrigo Campos Editing by Chizu Nomiyama)

萨尔瓦多的法律意味着比特币将与美元相当于20年前的美元，这是20年前的官方货币。 （由Chizu Nomiyama编辑Rodrigo Campos的报告）

VR健康预约可以帮助患者解决饮食障碍问题

2020-10-25 23:35:48

Research from Kent, the Research centre on Interactive Media, Smart systems and Emerging technologies (RISE) Ltd and the University of Cyprus has revealed that Virtual Reality (VR) technology can have significant impact on the validity of remote health appointments for those with eating disorders, through a process called Virtual Reality Exposure Therapy (VRET).

肯特、互动媒体、智能系统和新兴技术研究中心(RISE)有限公司和塞浦路斯大学的研究显示，虚拟现实(VR)技术可以通过一种名为虚拟现实暴露疗法(VRET)的过程，对饮食障碍患者远程健康预约的有效性产生重大影响。

Exposure Therapy (ET) is a common behaviour therapy treatment for psychological problems involving fear and anxiety. Through repeated exposures to the object of fear or anxiety, participants develop better emotional management. Notably, Virtual Reality (VR) has demonstrated success in the treatment of anxiety, social and general phobias, low self-esteem, and Post-Traumatic Stress Disorder.

暴露疗法(ET)是一种常见的行为疗法，用于治疗涉及恐惧和焦虑的心理问题。通过反复暴露在恐惧或焦虑的对象中，参与者会发展出更好的情绪管理能力。值得注意的是，虚拟现实(VR)在治疗焦虑、社交和一般恐惧症、低自尊和创伤后应激障碍方面取得了成功。

This paper demonstrates the potential value of Multi-User Virtual Reality (MUVR) remote psychotherapy for those with body shape and weight concerns.

本文论证了多用户虚拟现实(MUVR)远程心理治疗对那些体型和体重有顾虑的人的潜在价值。

In the study, published in Human-Computer Interaction Journal , participants and therapists were fitted with VR Head-Mounted Displays and introduced to each other within the VR system. Participant would then customize their virtual avatar according to their look (body shape and size, skin tone and hair colour and shape). Participant and therapist were then “teleported” to two Virtual Environment interventions for several discussions, building up to the Mirror Exposure.

这项研究发表在“人机交互杂志”上，参与者和治疗师被安装了VR头盔显示器，并在VR系统中相互介绍。然后，参与者将根据他们的外貌(体型和大小、肤色、头发颜色和形状)定制他们的虚拟化身。参与者和治疗师随后被“传送”到两个虚拟环境干预中进行几次讨论，为镜子曝光做准备。

Mirror Exposure involves confrontation in a mirror with ones’ shape and body. In the MUVR, the participant faces the virtual avatar they customized to match their own physical body. Here, they were again able to adjust body shapes using virtual sliders, change clothing, skin tone, as well as hair style and colour. Clothing was then gradually reduced until the participant’s avatar was in their virtual underwear.

镜子曝光是指在镜子中与自己的形状和身体对峙。在MUVR中，参与者面对的是他们定制的虚拟化身，以匹配他们自己的身体。在这里，他们再次能够使用虚拟滑块调整身体形状，改变衣服，肤色，以及发型和颜色。然后，衣服逐渐减少，直到参与者的化身穿上了他们的虚拟内衣。

The participant was then asked to examine each part of their body and perform adjustments while describing their feelings, thoughts and concerns with the therapist, leading to virtual exposure therapy for the patient to their body shape and size through the customised avatar.

然后，参与者被要求检查他们身体的每个部位，并进行调整，同时向治疗师描述他们的感受、想法和担忧，从而通过定制的化身为患者提供虚拟暴露疗法，让他们了解自己的体型和体型。

The study found that the avatar of the therapist was vital to the participant. The cartoonish avatar facilitated greater openness from participants, whilst therapist avatars in human-form represented the idea of negative judgement. In post-session interviews, participants noted the lack of fear of judgement as enabling them to commit to the session’s aims.

研究发现，治疗师的化身对参与者至关重要。卡通化身促进了参与者更大的开放性，而人形的治疗师化身则代表了消极判断的想法。在会后访谈中，与会者指出，不惧怕评判使他们能够致力于会议的目标。

Dr Jim Ang, Senior Lecturer in Multimedia/Digital Systems in the  School of Engineering and Digital Arts and supervisor of the study said: ‘The potential of Virtual Reality being used in addressing health issues with patients, remotely and without the issue of potential judgement, is for VR to be utilised throughout the health sector. Without the issue of judgement, which people can fear in advance of even seeking medical advice, VR can give people the confidence to engage with and embrace medical advice. In terms of the technical capabilities, the potential for VR to aid in remote non-contact medical appointments between patients and practitioners is huge, due particular consideration in times of pandemic.’

伦敦大学工程与数字艺术学院多媒体/数字系统高级讲师、该研究的监督人Jim Ang博士说：“虚拟现实被用来远程解决患者的健康问题，而不存在潜在的判断问题，这就是虚拟现实在整个医疗部门得到应用的潜力。”没有判断的问题，人们甚至在寻求医疗建议之前就会害怕，VR可以给人们提供参与和接受医疗建议的信心。就技术能力而言，虚拟现实在帮助患者和医生之间进行远程非接触式医疗预约方面的潜力是巨大的，在混乱时期应该特别考虑到这一点。

Dr Maria Matsangidou, Research Associate at RISE Ltd and Experimental Researcher of the study said: ‘Multi-User Virtual Reality is an innovative medium for psychotherapeutic interventions that allows for the physical separation of therapist and patient, providing thus more ‘comfortable’ openness by the patients. Exposure to patient worries about body shape and size may exhibit anxious reactions, but through the remote exposure therapy this can elicit new learning that helps the patient to shape new experiences.’

RISE有限公司副研究员、该研究的实验研究员玛丽亚·马桑吉杜博士说：“多用户虚拟现实是心理治疗干预的一种创新媒介，允许治疗师和患者在身体上分离，从而为患者提供更‘舒适’的开放。”接触到患者对体型和体型的担忧可能会表现出焦虑的反应，但通过远程暴露疗法，这可以引发新的学习，帮助患者形成新的体验。

“Now I Can See Me” Designing a Multi-User Virtual Reality Remote Psychotherapy for Body Weight and Shape Concerns is published in Human-Computer Interaction Journal (Dr Maria Matsangidou, Dr Boris Otkhmezuri, Dr Chee Siang (Jim) Ang, University of Kent; Prof Riva Giuseppe, Prof Andrea Gaggioli, Università Cattolica del Sacro Cuore; Prof Marios Avraamides, Despoina Iosif, Dr Maria Karekla, University of Cyprus).

“现在我可以看到我”为体重和体型问题设计一个多用户虚拟现实远程心理治疗发表在人机交互杂志上(Kent大学的Maria Matsangidou博士，Boris Otkhmezuri博士，Chee Siang(Jim)Ang博士；以及Riva Giuseppe教授，Andrea Gaggioli教授，Sacro Cuore大学的Andrea Gaggioli教授；以及Marios Avraamides教授，Despoina Iosif教授，Maria Karekla博士)。

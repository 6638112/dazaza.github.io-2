Court Voids a ‘Tortured’ Trump Climate Rollback
法院宣告特朗普的气候遭受“折磨”

2021-01-20 05:02:29

WASHINGTON — A federal appeals court on Tuesday struck down the Trump administration’s plan to relax restrictions on greenhouse gas emissions from power plants, paving the way for President-elect Joseph R. Biden Jr. to enact new and stronger restrictions on power plants.

华盛顿 - 本周二，联邦上诉法院否决了特朗普政府的计划放宽对发电厂的温室气体排放的限制，从而为总统当选人拜登的方式颁布有关电厂新的，更强的限制。

The United States Court of Appeals for the District of Columbia called the Trump administration’s Affordable Clean Energy rule a “fundamental misconstruction” of the nation’s environmental laws, devised through a “tortured series of misreadings” of legal statute.

美国哥伦比亚特区上诉法院称，特朗普政府的“可负担的清洁能源”规则是对美国环境法的“根本性误解”，这是通过对法律法规的“一系列严峻误读”造成的。

On the last full day of the Trump presidency, it effectively ended the Environmental Protection Agency’s efforts to weaken and undermine climate change policies and capped a dismal string of failures in which courts threw out one deregulation after another. Experts have widely described the E.P.A.’s losing streak as one of the worst legal records of the agency in modern history.

在特朗普总统任期的最后一整天，它有效地结束了环境保护署削弱和破坏气候变化政策的努力，并终结了一系列惨淡的失败，在这些失败中，法院一再推翻了放松管制的措施。专家们广泛地描述了E.P.A.的连败是现代历史上最糟糕的法律记录之一。

The appeals court did not reinstate a 2015 regulation that President Barack Obama’s E.P.A. had enacted, which would have forced utilities to move away from coal and toward renewable energy to reduce emissions. But it rejected the Trump administration’s attempt to repeal and replace that rule with a toothless one.

上诉法院没有恢复2015年关于巴拉克·奥巴马（Barack Obama）总统的E.P.A.的规定。该法案已经颁布，这将迫使公用事业从煤炭转向使用可再生能源以减少排放。但它拒绝了特朗普政府试图废除该规则并将其替换为无牙规则的尝试。

Judges eviscerated the Trump administration’s core argument: that the only possible way to interpret the Clean Air Act of 1970 is that the federal government does not have the authority to set national restrictions on emissions or force states to move away from fossil fuel power. That argument that would have prevented Mr. Biden or any future administration from tackling climate change from power plants without an explicit new law from Congress.

法官们剔除了特朗普政府的核心论点：解释1970年《清洁空气法案》的唯一可能方法是，联邦政府无权对排放设置国家限制，或迫使各州摆脱化石燃料发电。这种论点本来可以阻止拜登先生或任何未来的政府在没有国会明确的新法律的情况下解决发电厂的气候变化问题。

The Trump administration, the judges said, “may not shirk its responsibility by imagining new limitations that the plain language of the statute does not clearly require.”

法官们说，特朗普政府“可能不会通过想象法规明文没有明确要求的新限制来推卸责任。”

Molly Block, a spokeswoman for the E.P.A., said the Trump administration was disappointed in the ruling and said it “risks injecting more uncertainty at a time when the nation needs regulatory stability.”

E.P.A.的女发言人莫莉·布洛克（Molly Block）表示，特朗普政府对该裁定感到失望，并表示“有可能在国家需要监管稳定之际注入更多不确定性”。

Environmental groups and legal experts saw the decision as a vindication of the argument that the government does have the authority to tackle climate change.

环保组织和法律专家将这一决定证明了政府确实有权应对气候变化这一论点。

“It’s a massive win,” said Jody Freeman, a professor of environmental law at Harvard University who served as an adviser to the Obama administration.

哈佛大学环境法学教授乔迪·弗里曼（Jody Freeman）说：“这是一次巨大的胜利。”

A core promise of the Biden campaign was to eliminate fossil fuel emissions from the power sector by 2035. With Tuesday’s ruling, the Biden administration will not have to wait for the legal fight over the Trump rule to play out before deciding whether or how to use regulation to tackle climate change, Ms. Freeman said. Instead, she said, the Biden E.P.A. can “go on the offense” immediately.

拜登（Biden）竞选活动的核心承诺是，到2035年消除电力部门的化石燃料排放。根据周二的裁决，拜登（Biden）政府在决定是否使用方式之前，不必等到针对特朗普规则的法律斗争结束弗里曼女士说​​，应对气候变化的法规。她说，相反，拜登E.P.A.可以立即“进攻”。

“The real win here is that the Trump administration failed to tie the Biden team’s hands,” Ms. Freeman said. “They wanted to lock in a narrow legal interpretation and make it impossible for a new administration to set ambitious standards for power plants. That was their whole strategy. And it went down to spectacular defeat.”

弗里曼女士说​​：“真正的胜利是，特朗普政府未能束缚拜登团队的双手。” “他们想锁定狭义的法律解释，使新一届政府无法为电厂设定雄心勃勃的标准。那是他们的整体策略。结果失败了。”

In 2015 the Obama administration enacted the Clean Power Plan, which aimed to cut emissions from the power sector 32 percent by 2030 compared to 2005 levels. To do so, it instructed every state to draft plans to eliminate carbon emissions from power plants by phasing out coal and increasing the generation of renewable energy.

2015年，奥巴马政府颁布了《清洁电力计划》，该计划的目标是到2030年将电力部门的排放量与2005年的水平相比减少32％。为此，它指示每个州制定计划，逐步淘汰煤炭并增加可再生能源的产生，以消除发电厂的碳排放。

The measure never went into effect. The Supreme Court in 2016 said states did not have to comply until a barrage of lawsuits from conservative states and the coal industry had been resolved. Shortly after the election of President Trump, his E.P.A. repealed the Clean Power Plan.

该措施从未生效。最高法院在2016年表示，在保守派州的一系列诉讼和煤炭行业得到解决之前，各州不必遵守。特朗普总统选举，他E.P.A.后不久废除了《清洁能源计划》。

Andrew Wheeler, the departing administrator of the E.P.A. and a former coal lobbyist, replaced the plan with the weaker Affordable Clean Energy rule, which maintained the law only allows the agency to set guidelines to reduce emissions at individual power plants with actions like increasing efficiency or upgrading boilers that do not threaten an entire power sector, such as coal.

E.P.A.即将离任的管理员Andrew Wheeler一位前煤炭游说者用较弱的“负担得起的清洁能源”规则代替了该计划，该规则坚持认为，该法律仅允许该机构制定指导方针，以提高效率或升级不会威胁整个电力的锅炉等行动来减少单个发电厂的排放。部门，例如煤炭。

Alex Flint, executive director of the Alliance for Market Solutions, a conservative group that advocates for a carbon tax, said the ruling created “a mess” for the owners of power plants, steel mills, cement kilns and other polluting industries.

倡导碳税的保守组织市场解决方案联盟执行董事亚历克斯·弗林特（Alex Flint）说，该裁决给发电厂，钢铁厂，水泥窑和其他污染行业的所有者造成了“混乱”。

“One administration pushes rules in one direction, and the next pushes them in the other. Then, a court throws out the rules. It is impossible to make efficient long-term decisions,” he said.

“一个主管部门将规则推向一个方向，而下一主管部门将它们推向另一个方向。然后，法院放弃了这些规则。做出有效的长期决策是不可能的。”他说。

He called for Congress to put a price on emissions “so that polluters can decide whether to continue operations and pay the cost of doing so or change their operations.”

他呼吁国会为排放定价，“以便污染者可以决定是否继续运营并支付运营成本或改变运营方式。”

Ms. Freeman said the administration could attempt some last-minute filings in the next 24 hours but saw their chances of success as very low.

弗里曼女士说​​，政府可能会在接下来的24小时内尝试提交一些最新的文件，但是他们获得成功的机会非常低。

Whether Mr. Biden will seek to again use regulation to curb power plant emissions is still being debated. Tuesday’s ruling, legal experts said, did not give him approval to do so, but it did give him the leeway to try. Any new effort would certainly be challenged by conservatives and would very likely face an uncertain future before the Supreme Court.

拜登是否会寻求再次利用法规来限制发电厂的排放仍在争论中。法律专家说，周二的裁决没有批准他这样做，但确实给了他尝试的余地。任何新的努力都肯定会受到保守派的挑战，很可能在最高法院面前面临不确定的未来。

A spokesman for the Biden transition team did not immediately respond to a request for comment.

拜登过渡小组的发言人没有立即回应置评请求。

Indonesia coral reef partially restored in extensive project
印度尼西亚珊瑚礁部分恢复了广泛的项目

2021-05-06 06:51:40

Off the southwestern part of the Indonesian island of Sulawesi is a tiny island called Palau Bontosua. Growing under the sea nearby are 46-foot-high letters spelling the word “hope”. The letters, made from innovative reef stars and rapidly growing coral, signal the next phase of the world’s largest coral restoration program.

在印度尼西亚的西南部的苏拉威西岛是一个叫帕劳滨瓜的小岛。附近的海面上涨是46英尺高的字母，拼写“希望”这个词。由创新的珊瑚礁和珊瑚快速增长的珊瑚制成的信件，信号是世界上最大的珊瑚恢复计划的下一阶段。

在Google地球上查看珊瑚礁，并观看这部电影的Youtube视频，这些电影致以珊瑚，您可以帮助生成资金来恢复世界各地的珊瑚礁。

Climate change has accelerated the destruction of coral reefs around the world—scientists say that without interventions 90 percent are at risk of dying this century, perhaps even by 2043. This puts at risk the food supply, the protection of coasts from storms and flooding, potential new pharmaceuticals, as well as tourism and the economic health of communities that rely on it.

气候变化加速了世界各地珊瑚礁的破坏，说，没有干预的情况下，90％的人面临本世纪的危险，也许甚至到2043年。这会冒险粮食供应，从风暴和洪水中保护海岸的风险，潜在的新药品，以及依靠它的社区的旅游和经济健康。

Coral restoration projects are an important part of reversing the damage, which is also caused by non-sustainable fishing techniques, pollution, and even by tourists swimming with the wrong sunscreen.

珊瑚恢复项目是逆转损害的重要组成部分，这也是由于不可持续的捕捞技术，污染甚至游泳者与错误的防晒霜引起的。

The SHEBA cat food brand and parent company Mars Inc. are spurring action with their new coral restoration project and the SHEBA Hope Reef, announced May 5, 2021. Already having invested more than $10 million in reef restoration over the last decade and $1 billion through its Sustainable In A Generation Plan, SHEBA and Mars now aim to restore the equivalent of 148 Olympic-sized pools of coral reefs by the end of 2029. And they hope to spark more change from others.

Sheba Cat Food Brand和母公司Mars Inc.正在与他们的新珊瑚恢复项目和Sheba Hope Reef宣布的行动，2021年5月5日宣布。在过去十年中已经在过去十年中投入了超过1000万美元的珊瑚礁恢复和10亿美元它的可持续计划在一代制定计划中，谢芭和火星现在旨在恢复2029年底的相当于148个奥运珊瑚礁池。他们希望引发更多的变化。

The Spermonde Archipelago off of Indonesia’s Sulawesi island is known as the Coral Triangle. Once an area with an abundance of undersea life, dynamite and cyanide fishing that began decades ago resulted in not only the capture of edible fish but the killing of corals and other parts of the food chain.

在印度尼西亚的苏拉威西岛岛上的Spermonde群岛被称为珊瑚三角。一旦一个具有十年前开始的下的海底生活，炸药和氰化物捕鱼的区域导致食用鱼的捕获而成，而是杀死珊瑚和食物链的其他部分。

Work to restore the reef known as Salisi’ Besar near the tiny island of Bontosua and its neighbor Badi began in 2019.

努力恢复被称为Salisi'Anar的珊瑚礁附近的珊瑚礁附近的珊瑚礁和邻居巴迪于2019年开始。

Reef stars are an essential part of rebuilding a reef quickly. Marine scientists worked for more than ten years to research, develop, and perfect them.

Reef Stars是快速重建珊瑚礁的重要组成部分。海洋科学家们在十多年上才能研究，发展和完善它们。

A reef star is a two-foot structure made of steel. It’s covered with a coat of resin and then with several layers of sand to make a rough surface. The reef stars join together to create a strong and stable web on the sea floor with lots of space for coral to grow.

珊瑚礁是由钢制成的双脚结构。它覆盖着一层树脂，然后用几层沙子制作粗糙的表面。珊瑚礁的星星加入在一起，在海底上创造一个强大而稳定的网络，有很多空间珊瑚成长。

The stars are handmade in local communities using locally-available materials so as to both increase employment and cut down the carbon footprint of transporting them from elsewhere. Carefully-selected coral fragments are sustainably harvested from nearby reefs that have similar or harsher environmental conditions compared with the planned new reef. The fragments are then attached to the reef stars, leaving plenty of room for growth.

星星是使用本地可用材料的当地社区手工制作，以便增加就业并减少从其他地方运送它们的碳足迹。与计划的新礁相比，精心挑选的珊瑚碎片是从附近的珊瑚礁可持续收获，附近的珊瑚礁相似或具有骚扰环境条件。然后将碎片连接到珊瑚礁恒星上，留下足够的成长空间。

A team of four divers can install about 250 reef stars in one day on the sea floor, with the help of more people who attach the coral fragments to the frames. In 20 days, they can plant about two acres of new reef.

四个潜水员团队可以在海底一天内安装大约250个珊瑚星，借助更多的人将珊瑚碎片附加到框架。在20天内，他们可以植入大约两英亩的新珊瑚礁。

Off Sulawesi, the SHEBA coral restoration project has already planted more then 19,000 reef stars seeded with more than 285,000 coral fragments. Each project is tailored to its particular environment with the goal of restoring the original ecological state.

苏拉威西队，Sheba珊瑚恢复项目已经种植了更多的19,000六星，种植了超过285,000个珊瑚碎片。每个项目都针对其特定的环境量身定制，目标是恢复原始生态状态。

The SHEBA Hope Reef near Bontosua is made of 840 reef stars and about 13,000 fragments of 42 different types of coral. The reef stars were arranged to spell out the word “hope” in 46-foot high, 30-foot wide letters and create a new reef almost 150 feet wide. It represents just 2.4% of the coral restoration work SHEBA is leading in the area.

Bontosua附近的Sheba Hope Reef由840礁恒星和约13,000种不同类型珊瑚的碎片制成。珊瑚礁明星被安排拼出46英尺高，30英尺宽的字母的“希望”一词，并创造一个近150英尺宽的新珊瑚礁。它代表了珊瑚恢复工作的2.4％，Sheba在该地区处于领先地位。

SHEBA worked with community ambassadors—representing key groups including youth, women, and those who work in fishing—to plan the project, secure agreements with various levels of government, and to create the reef stars and the reef itself. Also essential to the project was direct community involvement in developing ways to change behaviors and ensure sustainable livelihoods. The creation of the SHEBA Hope Reef also serves as a hub for training others including reef builders, ambassadors, and staff at national parks, NGOs, and governments so that the project can grow elsewhere.

Sheba与社区大使代表的关键团体，包括青年，妇女和那些在捕捞的人 - 计划项目，以各级政府的安全协议，并创造珊瑚礁星和珊瑚礁本身。对项目至关重要的是直接社区参与制定改变行为的方法，并确保可持续生计。 Sheba Hope Reef的创建也是培训他人在国家公园，非政府组织和政府的珊瑚礁建设者，大使和工作人员包括训练的枢纽，以便该项目可以在其他地方种植。

In less than three years, the replanted corals have grown to make the word “hope” visible from sky-high. Already, new types of corals not seeded on the reef stars are growing there too and the reef is attracting a variety of fish, even sharks, rays, and turtles. Since the reef was planted, coral cover has grown from five percent to 55 percent. Before long, the coral will have grown so much that the word “hope” will no longer be visible.

在不到三年的时间里，被复制的珊瑚已经生长，使得“希望”这个词从天空中看到“希望”。已经，没有在礁石恒星上播种的新类型的珊瑚也在那里生长，珊瑚礁吸引了各种鱼类，甚至鲨鱼，光线和龟。由于珊瑚礁种植，珊瑚盖已从5％到55％的增长。在长期之前，珊瑚将增长如此之外，“希望”这个词将不再可见。

More coral today means more fish—and a healthier planet—in the future. If you were to go snorkeling or diving at the Hope Reef, you’d likely see Hawksbill and Green turtles, eagle and devil rays, black tip reef sharks, and barracuda, as well as colorful butterflyfish, rabbitfish, angelfish, surgeonfish, Napoleon wrasse, and bump head parrotfish.

今天的珊瑚意味着更多的鱼和一个更健康的星球。如果你要在希望礁石潜水或潜水，你可能会看到鹰派和绿色的乌龟，鹰和魔鬼射线，黑尖礁鲨，以及五颜六色的蝴蝶鱼，兔子，神仙鱼，外科鱼，拿破仑濑鱼和凹凸头鹦嘴鱼。

Other SHEBA Hope Grows restoration projects are already in Xcalak in the Mexican Caribbean and Australia’s Great Barrier Reef. Additional sites for new reefs are being evaluated in the Maldives, the Seychelles, U.S. Virgin Islands, and Ningaloo, Australia too.

其他Sheba希望恢复项目已经在墨西哥加勒比和澳大利亚的大堡礁左右。新珊瑚礁的其他网站正在马尔代夫，塞舌尔，美国维尔京群岛和澳大利亚宁加罗等地区进行了评估。

SHEBA’s goal is to act as a catalyst for others to help save and restore reefs. Starting as a ripple, their coral restoration projects are creating waves of opportunities for even more action from governments, businesses big and small, and communities to protect the oceans they rely on.

Sheba的目标是充当他人的催化剂，帮助拯救和恢复珊瑚礁。作为涟漪开始，他们的珊瑚恢复项目正在为各国政府，大小和小的企业以及保护他们依赖的海洋的社区创造更多的机会浪潮。

“We’re thrilled to unveil Hope Reef and show that there really is hope for our oceans. Our efforts around the world to restore and regenerate these precious ecosystems are showing exciting results and having a positive impact on local communities,” said Mars Inc.’s chief marine scientist Professor David Smith in a May 5, 2021 statement. “We hope our efforts inspire others to join us so we can all play our part in helping prevent the extinction of our coral reefs.”

“我们很激动到希望礁石的希望，表明我们的海洋真的很有希望。我们在世界各地恢复和重新制定这些珍贵生态系统的努力表现出令人兴奋的结果并对当地社区产生积极影响，“Mars Inc.的首席海洋科学家David Smith表示，在2021年5月5日的陈述。 “我们希望我们的努力激励他人加入我们，所以我们都可以在帮助防止我们的珊瑚礁灭绝方面发挥作用。”

The world needs more awareness and action to protect and restore our oceans. The SHEBA Hope Reef shows that it’s possible to restore a reef in just a year or two. When hope leads to action, the loss of ocean habitat and biodiversity can be reversed.

世界需要更加意识和行动来保护和恢复海洋。 Sheba Hope Reef表明它可以在一两年内恢复珊瑚礁。当希望导致行动时，海洋栖息地和生物多样性的丧失可以逆转。

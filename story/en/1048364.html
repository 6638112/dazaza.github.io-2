The frame rate of the universe (2009)
宇宙帧率（2009）

2021-02-19 04:37:22

16 Jan 2009 I stumbled upon thisarticle which presents the hypothesis that the universe is a 3D projection of a 2Dsurface, like a giant hologram. I like to read about modern physics. It is so weird and I can't sayI really understand very much of it. But the descriptions provoke strange and fascinating imagesand thoughts in my head. Like this one:

2009年1月16日，我偶然发现了这篇文章，提出了一个假设，即宇宙是2D曲面的3D投影，就像一个巨大的全息图。我喜欢阅读有关现代物理学的文章。这太奇怪了，我不能说我真的很了解。但是这些描述在我脑海中激起了奇怪而迷人的图像和想法。像这个：

The article mentions the Planck length, which as I understand it isthe smallest distance there is. It's extremely small: 1.6 × 10 -35 meters, which makesit billions and billions of times smaller than an atom (or even a proton). I'm used to thinkingabout computer graphics, so I imagine the Planck length as the size of one "pixel" of the universe.Nothing can be smaller than a pixel. (The pixels of the universe are small, I calculate theresolution to correspond to 2.19 × 10 33 DPI.) The radius of the observable universe is4.4 × 10 26 meters. If we want to fit the universe into a box, its sides would have tobe twice that size. That is 5.4 × 10 61 Planck lengths. So that's the width, height anddepth of the universe in pixels. Quite a good resolution. (Actually since they are 3D cubes insteadof 2D squares, I should call them voxels instead of pixels. All 1.61 × 10 185 ofthem.)

这篇文章提到了普朗克长度，据我所知，它是最小的距离。它非常小：1.6×10 -35米，比原子（甚至是质子）小数十亿倍。我曾经考虑过计算机图形，所以我把普朗克长度想象为一个像素大小。宇宙中的任何事物都不能小于一个像素。 （宇宙的像素很小，我计算出的分辨率对应于2.19×10 33 DPI。）可观察到的宇宙的半径为4.4×10 26米。如果要把宇宙装进盒子里，它的边必须是那个大小的两倍。即5.4×10 61普朗克长度。因此，这是宇宙的宽度，高度和深度（以像素为单位）。相当好的分辨率。 （实际上，因为它们是3D立方体而不是2D正方形，所以我应该称它们为体素而不是像素。所有像素均为1.61×10185。）

There is also the Planck timewhich is the time it takes for light to travel one Planck length. As light is fast and the Plancklength is tiny (tiniest there is!), you can image that it's a very short period of time. Certainly.The Planck time is 5.39 × 10 -44 seconds. No measurable time can be shorter than thataccording to quantum physics. Thinking about graphics again, this is like a limit on the frame rateof the universe. Inverting the Planck time, I get 1.855 × 10 43. So by my surelyincorrect logic, we get the value of the universe's frame rate:

还有普朗克时间，这是光传播一个普朗克长度所花费的时间。由于光线很快，而普朗克长度很小（那里是最细的！），因此可以在很短的时间内成像。当然，普朗克时间是5.39×10 -44秒。可测量的时间不能短于根据量子物理学的时间。再次考虑图形，这就像是对宇宙帧速率的限制。反转普朗克时间，我得到1.855×1043。所以根据我肯定不正确的逻辑，我们得到了宇宙帧速率的值：

I hope I got the calculations right, but I'm pretty sure my interpretation of thequantum physics behind this are way off. It's still fascinating to think of the universe as acomputer simulation. Modern physics make it seem more like a video game than ever.

我希望我的计算正确，但是我很确定我对这背后的量子物理学的解释是遥遥无期的。将宇宙视为计算机模拟仍然令人着迷。现代物理学使它比以往更像是一个视频游戏。

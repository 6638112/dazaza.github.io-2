Fujifilm and IBM Set World Record With 580TB Magnetic Tapes

According to IBM, by the time we reach 2025 the world will be creating 175 zettabytes of data. That's 175 trillion gigabytes, and we need a cost-effective way of storing it all. The solution? A new prototype magnetic tape that breaks the recording density world record.

据IBM称，到2025年，世界将创造175 ZB的数据。这就是175万亿千兆字节，我们需要一种经济高效的方式来存储所有内容。解决方案？打破记录密度世界纪录的新型原型磁带。

Mark Lantz, Manager of Advanced Tape Technologies at IBM Research, explains how researchers at IBM and Fujifilm brought together more than 15 years of work to set a new world record in tape storage. What they achieved is an areal density of 317Gbpsi (gigabits per square inch), which translates to a single tape capable of storing 580 terabytes of data.

IBM Research高级磁带技术经理Mark Lantz解释了IBM和Fujifilm的研究人员如何将超过15年的工作联系在一起，以创下磁带存储的新世界纪录。他们实现了317Gbpsi（千兆字节/平方英寸）的面密度，这转化为能够存储580 TB数据的单个磁带。

In order to achieve such a high areal density, the research team had to develop a brand new tape and created Strontium Ferrite (SrFe) in the process. Existing magnetic tapes rely on Barium Ferrite (BaFe), but SrFe offers the potential for higher density storage in the same amount of tape. Alongside that, the team also "developed a family of new servo-mechanical technologies including a new servo pattern that is pre-recorded in the servo tracks, a prototype head actuator and a set of servo controllers." The end result is a very high capacity tape that can be read while moving at a speed of 15km/h (9.3mph).

为了实现如此高的面密度，研究团队必须开发一种全新的胶带并在此过程中创建锶铁氧体（SrFe）。现有的磁带依靠钡铁氧体（BaFe），但是SrFe可以在相同数量的磁带中提供更高密度的存储潜力。除此之外，该团队还开发了一系列新的伺服机械技术，包括预先记录在伺服磁道中的新伺服模式，原型磁头致动器和一组伺服控制器。最终结果是可以以15 km / h（9.3 mph）的速度移动时读取的高容量磁带。

Although tape is very old technology, having first been introduced in 1952, it has continued to improve and remains an extremely cheap (pennies per gigabyte) way of backing up lots of data. It also helps that the tapes remain readable for many decades and offer the advantage of allowing a physical barrier by default. Stealing data stored on these tapes would mean actually stealing the tapes and having access to the right equipment to read them.

尽管磁带是一项非常古老的技术，于1952年首次引入，但它一直在不断改进，并且仍然是一种非常便宜的备份大量数据的方式（每千兆字节1便士）。它还有助于使磁带保持数十年的可读性，并具有默认情况下允许物理屏障的优点。窃取存储在这些磁带上的数据将意味着实际上是在偷磁带并有权使用正确的设备来读取它们。

There's no word yet on when this new magnetic tape will leave the research lab and enter the data center, but it looks likely to serve as the cloud storage archival format of choice when it eventually does.

这种新型磁带何时离开研究实验室并进入数据中心尚无定论，但它最终可能会成为首选的云存储档案格式。

The History of Code is Free Speech (2020)
代码的历史是免费的语音（2020）

2021-06-13 14:35:12

In 1997, when I was working as head of Business Development and interim head of Development for the first PGP, Inc., it was still a violation of U.S. export control laws to send a copy of PGP-encryption software, or any sufficiently “strong encryption” software, outside of the United States. But PGP’s in-house legal counsel Bob Kohn, PGP’s outside legal counsel, Roz Thomsen, and I came up with a “crazy like a fox” idea to put the complete source code to PGP 3.0/5.0 into book form in the OCR-B font. That way, should the book be sent outside the country, the exportation could not be deemed to be a violation of export control laws. As Bob Kohn advised, any regulation that would prohibit the sale of books would be a violation of the First Amendment. At least, that was our hope. That idea was not only a clever one, it really worked.

1997年，当我担任第一个PGP，Inc的业务发展负责人和临时开发负责人时，它仍然是违反美国出口管制法的侵犯，发送了PGP加密软件的副本，或者任何充分的“强大”加密“软件，在美国之外。但PGP的内部法律律师Bob Kohn，PGP在法律顾问，Roz Thomsen，我想出了一个“像狐狸一样的疯狂”的想法，把完整的源代码放到了OCR-B中的书形式中的PGP 3.0 / 5.0字体。这样，如果该书在国外送出，则出口不能被视为违反出口管制法。正如鲍勃·科恩所建议的那样，任何禁止销售书籍的法规将违反第一次修正案。至少，这是我们的希望。这一想法不仅仅是一个聪明的人，真的很奏效。

After sending the book to the Library of Congress, Bob Kohn and I brought several copies to Bay Area book stores, including Printers Inc. on Central Avenue in Palo Alto, California. Bob fully expected that should a bookstore send the book overseas, no way would the government indict a bookstore for selling a book!

在向大会图书馆发送书后，Bob Kohn和我将几份到湾区书店，包括Ploifornia Palo Alto Central Avenue的Printers Inc.。鲍勃全面预期，书店应该派书海外，政府难以伸展一本书的书店！

But that process was taking too long. As PGP was in a rush to market, it fell to me to make sure we met our deadlines. As the most senior guy involved in the project, with the least to lose (as my amazing wife had not yet fallen for my charms), exporting the book became my problem. I secretly prepared a fully ready to go Fedex box of the book and, at a Cypherpunks meeting, handed it to Lucky Green who dropped it off at a Fedex location.

但那过程正在花费太久。由于PGP急于上市，它落在我身边，以确保我们达到截止日期。作为参与该项目的最高级人，最少丢失（因为我的惊人的妻子尚未为我的魅力堕落），导出这本书成为我的问题。我偷偷准备好准备去联邦快递的书，并在一个Cypherpunks会议上，将它交给幸运的绿色，幸运的绿色在联邦快递地点将其丢弃。

A few days later, the FedEx slip for that package was prominently displayed on a website located in Sweden. Of course, just a few days after that, Bob got a call from the U.S. Export Control Division of the Department of Commerce. As a result of that call, I became the second person to ever be investigated for illegally exporting strong cryptography. The first exporter was almost certainly Hal Finney but Phil Zimmerman bravely chose to take the heat instead.

几天后，在位于瑞典的网站上突出显示该套餐的联邦快递单。当然，在此之后几天，Bob从商务部的美国出口管制部门接到了电话。由于该呼吁，我成为第二个人被调查，以非法出口强大的密码学。第一个出口商几乎肯定是Hal Finney，但Phil Zimmerman勇敢地选择了热量。

Bob was quickly called in to the Office of the Export Control Division in San Jose, CA. He brought with him a copy of the PGP source code book, a box of PGP software, and a large poster we created explaining public key cryptography. As he tells the story, Bob walked out of the meeting not entirely sure whether our plan was going to work. When he got back to the office, he instructed the development team to take their PCs home every night and back up their work off-site, as we could be raided by the FBI at any time.

鲍勃很快被召集到加利福尼亚州圣何塞的出口管制部门办公室。他与他带来了一份PGP源代码书，一盒PGP软件的副本，以及我们创建的大型海报，我们创建了解释公钥加密。当他讲述这个故事时，鲍勃走出了会议，并不完全确定我们的计划是否要上班。当他回到办公室时，他指示开发团队每晚都把他们的电脑带回家并备份他们的工作场外，因为我们可以随时被FBI突击。

Two weeks later, Bob got a call from one of the export control agents he had met with. She informed him that, after her discussions with the Department of Commerce’s office of General Counsel, it turns out we were right after all: It’s not illegal to sell a book! Thus, their investigation was complete and within a few short months strong cryptography was removed from the Munitions List at ITAR and transferred to the Department of Commerce Control List to now be subject to “Commodity Classification” which was inexpensive and easy to license and generally exempted open source.

两周后，鲍勃接到了他遇到过的出口控制代理商之一的呼叫。她告诉他，在与商务部的总法律顾问讨论后，事实证明我们是不是：销售书籍并不是违法的！因此，他们的调查完成，在几个短暂的几个月内，强大的加密被从伊塔尔的弹药名单中删除，并转移到商务部控制清单，现在须遵守“商品分类”，这是廉价且易于许可的“商品分类”。开源。

To this day, Bob insists that PGP should never have had to resort to publishing the source code in book form. Not only does the First Amendment protect computer software, but the Second Amendment protects encryption. “The right to bear arms,” Bob told me recently, “includes the right not only to bear a sword, but also to bear a shield. The defensive shield of encryption is as important to a free state as any kind of offensive weapon necessary in the exercise of our natural right of self-defense,” he added.

到这一天，鲍勃坚持认为PGP不得不诉诸于在书形式中发布源代码。第一个修正案不仅保护计算机软件，而且还保护了第二修正案保护加密。 “携带武器的权利，”鲍勃最近告诉我“，不仅要承受剑，还要承担盾牌。他补充说，加密的防守盾与自由州作为任何类型的自然武器，“他补充说。

The US Government has always been very aware of the tension between the US Munitions List and the First Amendment. However, in a turnabout and to respond to the explosive meme of 3D printing firearms, in 2013 the Obama State Department threatened Defense Distributed for publishing CAD files. DD ultimately prevailed in getting a settlement from the US Government that included paying for some of their expenses. Since then the Attorney General’s of various anti-freedom states have been attacking everyone’s First Amendment rights. That culminated last Friday with a narrow injunction against new US regulations that would have removed all but machine code 3D printed firearms files from all export controls.

美国政府一直非常了解美国弹药名单和第一次修正案之间的紧张关系。然而，在一个转折带上并响应3D打印枪械的爆炸物MEME，2013年奥巴马国家部门威胁到发布CAD文件的威胁。 DD最终占有于美国政府的解决方案，其中包括支付一些费用。从那时起，律师将军的各种反自由国家一直在攻击每个人的第一次修正权。上周五持续了令人兴奋的禁令对新的美国法规，除了从所有导出控件中删除所有的机器代码3D打印的枪械文件。

I was one of the handful of people who created CodeisFreeSpeech.com. We received a fraudulent takedown notice from the Attorney General of New Jersey’s office which culminated in CiFS removing the CAD files and suing AG Grewal.

我是少数人创建代码的人之一。我们收到了新泽西州办公室律师将军的欺诈性发出通知，终止于CIFS删除CAD文件和起诉AG GREWAL。

I had thought that the new rulemaking would end much of our concern with the United States. But now that is not the case. And in this case, we’re going to make history rhyme.

我认为新的统治者将最终与美国关注我们的担忧。但现在不是这种情况。在这种情况下，我们将要历史韵。

Thanks again to Bob, Phil, Hal, and the rest of the ‘96-‘97 PGP development team.

再次感谢Bob，Phil，Hal，以及其余的'96 -'97 PGP开发团队。

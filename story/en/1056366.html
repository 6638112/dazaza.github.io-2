30-year-old Soviet TV adaptation of The Lord of the Rings surfaces on YouTube
30岁的苏联电视适应YouTube上的戒指表面

2021-04-06 05:40:02

After 30 years, a TV adaptation of J.R.R. Tolkien's  The Lord of the Rings long thought lost has resurfaced. The 1991 Soviet television adaptation has been uploaded to YouTube, in two one-hour videos.

30年后，电视适应J.R.R. Tolkien＆＃39;戒指的耶和华遗失的主遗失已经重新浮出来。 1991年苏联电视适应已经上传到YouTube，在两个一小时的视频中。

The film focuses on the events of the first book in the trilogy,  The Fellowship of the Ring, and features many elements that were excluded from the popular global theatrical release by director Peter Jackson, including an extended sequence featuring the character Tom Bombadil—one of the biggest omissions by the bigger-budget 2001 film far more of us have seen.

这部电影专注于三部曲中第一本书的事件，戒指的奖学金，并具有许多元素，这些元素被彼得杰克逊的主任杰克逊（Direct Peter Jackson）排除在外，包括一个具有字符Tom Bombadil-One的扩展序列2001年更大的预算中最大的遗漏了我们所看到的远远超过我们更多。

Originally broadcast on TV in 1991 (and then never aired again), the film was thought lost to time by those who had seen it. But as reported in The Guardian, Leningrad Television successor Channel 5 uploaded the film to its YouTube page with little fanfare, surprising fans who had given up on seeing the production again. It is believed to be the only adaptation of these books produced in the Soviet Union. For better or for worse, the primitive special effects and low budget are very apparent—moreso than in many other B movies of the time you may have seen. Grainy characters' arms are cropped out in the middle of the frame as they are set against fuzzy fake backgrounds. And the film employs a visual language that is altogether alien to modern cinema, with sets and costumes that look more at home in a low-budget theatrical production and characters who gaze into the camera directly when they speak with eerie commitment.

在1991年最初在电视上播出（然后再也不会播出），那些看过那些已经看到的那些电影迷失了。但正如监护人所报道的那样，Leningrad电视继任者频道5用很少的粉丝将电影上传到YouTube页面，令人惊讶的粉丝再次看到生产。据信是苏联生产这些书籍的唯一适应。无论好坏，原始的特殊效果和低预算都非常明显 - oreso比在您可能已经看到的许多其他电影中。颗粒状人物＆＃39;武器在框架的中间播出，因为它们是针对模糊的假背景。这部电影聘请了一种含有现代电影的视觉语言，套装和服装在低预算的戏剧生产和在令人毛骨悚然的承诺时直接凝视着相机的特征。

In other words, an Andrei Tarkovsky masterwork it is not. But the nostalgia is strong, in particular thanks to the soundtrack by Andrei Romanov, who performed with the popular Russian rock group Akvarium.

Titled Khraniteli ("Keepers"), the film is believed to be based on a Russian-language translation of Tolkien's work by Vladimir Muravyov and Andrey Kistyakovsky, and it is of course in Russian. But if you don't speak Russian, fret not: YouTube's autogenerated English closed captioning is adequate enough to give you the gist of what's happening.

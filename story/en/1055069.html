Instagram Is Sharing 79% of Your Personal Data with 3rd Parties
Instagram与第三方分享您的个人数据的79％

2021-03-30 00:30:36

We’ve all experienced this: one second you’re watching a product review on YouTube and the next you see an advert for exactly that product in your Instagram feed. While it still feels like some kind of dark magic is at work there, we’ve pretty much gotten used to this type of thing by now – even though there are instances when it still feels a bit spooky, especially when you’re certain you’ve only talked about a product or mentioned it in a personal message.

我们都经历过这一点：一秒钟，您正在观看YouTube的产品评论，接下来您可以在Instagram饲料中确切地看到广告。虽然它仍然感觉有点黑魔法在那里，但我们现在几乎已经习惯了这种类型的东西 - 尽管存在这种情况时它仍然感觉有点怪异，特别是当你确定你只有在个人信息中谈论产品或提到它。

The truth of the matter is: the apps and websites we’re using collect vast amounts of data about us, and, in many cases, this data is even passed on to third parties. This of course, shouldn’t happen without permission, which is why we usually have to agree to a long list of terms and conditions before using an app. (And honestly, when is the last time you’ve read these before clicking yes?)

问题的真相是：我们正在使用关于我们的大量数据的应用和网站，并且在许多情况下，这种数据甚至传递给第三方。这当然，不应发生在未经许可的情况下，这就是为什么我们通常必须同意在使用应用程序之前同意长期的条款和条件列表。 （老实说，在点击是之前，您最后一次阅读这些是什么时候？）

Last year, Apple made it a bit easier for consumers to understand what kind of data apps collect and how that data is being used. The company introduced privacy labels to apps in its App Store, categorizing personal data into 14 categories ranging from user location to purchases, search and browsing history and contact information.

去年，Apple使消费者更容易理解哪种数据应用程序收集以及如何使用数据。该公司将隐私标签推出到App Store中的应用程序，将个人数据分类为14个类别，从用户位置进行购买，搜索和浏览历史记录和联系信息。

Secure cloud provider pCloud has used this data to analyze a number of popular apps with respect to their collecting and sharing of user data. As the following chart shows, Instagram is most likely to share your personal information with third parties for advertising purposes. The popular app shares data from 11 out of 14 categories with third parties, putting it ahead of Facebook, which interestingly shares fewer data with external advertisers.

Analysis: Amazon's properties, including Amazon.com, WholeFoods.com, and Zappos.com, are blocking Google's new FLoC tracking system from gathering data
分析：Amazon的属性，包括Amazon.com，Wholefoods.com和Zappos.com，阻止了Google的新絮凝物跟踪系统收集数据

2021-06-15 20:45:41

Most of Amazon’s properties including Amazon.com, WholeFoods.com and Zappos.com are preventing Google’s tracking system FLoC — or Federated Learning of Cohorts — from gathering valuable data reflecting the products people research in Amazon’s vast e-commerce universe, according to website code analyzed by Digiday and three technology experts who helped Digiday review the code.

亚非福顿的大多数属性包括Amazon.com和Zappos.com正在防止谷歌的跟踪系统絮状物 - 或联合学习的队列 - 根据网站代码收集反映亚马逊广阔电子商务宇宙产品研究的宝贵数据通过挖掘多达三个技术专家分析，帮助挖掘挖掘码头。

As Google’s system gathers data about people’s web travels to inform how it categorizes them, Amazon’s under-the-radar move could not only be a significant blow to Google’s mission to guide the future of digital ad tracking after cookies die — it could give Amazon a leg up in its own efforts to sell advertising across what’s left of the open web.

由于Google的系统收集有关人员网络的数据，以告知其如何对其进行分类，亚马逊的下雷达举措不仅可以对谷歌的使命来指导饼干死后的数字广告跟踪未来的重大打击 - 它可以给亚马逊a腿部以自己的努力销售在开放网上剩下的左侧的广告。

“This move is in direct correlation with Google’s attempt to provide an alternative to the third-party cookie,” said Amanda Martin, vp of enterprise partnerships at digital agency Goodway Group. She called Amazon’s choice to block FLoC on most of its sites another example of the chess moves Google, Apple, Facebook and Amazon are making as data privacy pressures force the destruction of the foundation of data tracking across the internet:  the third party cookie. With the help of three technologists, Digiday watched last week as Amazon added code to its digital properties to block FLoC from tracking visitors using Google’s Chrome browser. For example, while earlier in the week WholeFoods.com and Woot.com did not include code to block FLoC, by Thursday Digiday saw that those sites did feature code telling Google’s system not to include activities of their visitors to inform cohorts or assign IDs. But Amazon’s blocking appears scattered. While one of the technologists saw both of those sites blocking FLoC, another did not, and said Amazon’s deployment could be evident on different servers in different parts of the country.

“这一举措与谷歌试图为第三方饼干提供替代方案直接相关，”数字代理Gogway集团的企业伙伴关系副总裁Amanda Martin表示。她叫亚马逊的选择阻止了大部分地点的絮状赛的另一个例子是谷歌，Apple，Facebook和亚马逊正在制作，因为数据隐私压力强迫遭到互联网上的数据跟踪基础：第三方饼干。在三位技术人员的帮助下，挖掘最后一周在亚马逊向其数字属性添加了代码，以阻止Floc使用Google Chrome浏览器跟踪访问者。例如，虽然在本周的早期wholefoods.com和woot.com未包括块絮凝的代码，但到了星期四的挖掘，那些网站确实特征代码告诉谷歌的系统不包括他们访问者的活动，以告知队列或分配ID。但亚马逊的阻止出现散落。虽然其中一位技术人员看到了阻止絮凝的那些网站，另一个没有，另一个没有，并且说亚马逊的部署可能在国家不同地区的不同服务器中明显。

There is a caveat regarding FLoC blocking on Whole Foods pages, however. While other Amazon-owned domains mentioned here that block FLoC do so using Google’s recommended approach involving sending a response header from HTML pages, Whole Foods blocking employs a tactic that sends an opt-out header from Amazon analytics requests. One technologist who asked not to be named for this story said the distinction is noteworthy because the approach Amazon has implemented for most sites employs the approach recommended by Google, and therefore “known to be 100% effective,” according to the technologist, who said the approach used to block FLoC on Whole Foods pages could be an oversight or a deliberate choice on Amazon’s part, possibly as a test of some kind. Google says FLoC is intended to protect people’s privacy because it uses machine learning to group them based on the web pages they have viewed rather than tracking them at the individual level. Right now, the system is in a pilot phase, assembling data indicating what websites, content and products people are interested in. Why Amazon is blocking FLoC

然而，在整个食物页面上有一个关于絮凝物的警告。虽然此处提到的其他亚马逊拥有的域，但是块絮凝物使用Google的推荐方法，涉及从HTML页面发送响应标题，但整个食品阻止使用策略从Amazon Analytics请求发送退出标题。一个人要求不被命名为这个故事的技术专家表示，这是值得注意的，因为亚马逊为大多数网站实施了谷歌推荐的方法，因此“已知为100％效力”，据该技术人员说用于阻止整个食品页面的絮凝物的方法可能是对亚马逊部分的监督或故意选择，可能是对某种的考验。谷歌表示，Floc旨在保护人们的隐私，因为它使用机器学习基于他们已查看的网页对它们进行分组而不是在个人级别跟踪它们。现在，系统处于导频阶段，组装数据指示人们感兴趣的网站，内容和产品。为什么亚马逊阻止絮凝物

While it may seem obvious that Amazon would want to throw a wrench in any Google initiative, the company has plenty of reasons for hindering FLoC’s success.

虽然似乎很明显，亚马逊希望在任何谷歌计划中抛出扳手，但该公司有很多原因阻碍了絮絮的成功。

First, Amazon wants to protect its intellectual property — the prized data it has that shows what products people research, review and buy online. With an influx of shoppers expected to flood to the site on its Prime Days on June 21 and 22, now is the time to put up an electric fence preventing Google from feeding off that valuable data trough. Already, as reported by Digiday last week, ad tech firms and agencies are  gathering and analyzing FLoC ID data in the hopes of improving targeting and identification capabilities. Put simply, it is not in Amazon’s best interest to let outsiders like Google or other ad tech firms take advantage of its valuable shopper data.

首先，亚马逊希望保护其知识产权 - 珍贵的数据显示，它显示了人们研究，审查和在线购买的产品。随着购物者的涌入预计将于6月21日和22日的主要日子涌入该网站，现在是施加电动栅栏的时间，防止谷歌喂掉该宝贵的数据槽。正如挖掘通讯上周报告的，广告科技公司和机构正在收集和分析絮凝ID数据，以提高靶向和识别能力。简而言之，它不是亚马逊的最大兴趣，让像谷歌或其他广告科技公司这样的外人利用其宝贵的购物者数据。

Without Amazon visitors in the mix, Google’s FLoC could be at a disadvantage, said one agency executive who spoke anonymously with Digiday. Had Amazon chosen not to block FLoC, the company could have assisted Google by enabling “significantly improved results of certain in-market shopping FLoCs,” said the exec. Already, Google’s claims about the performance of the method have been  subject to scrutiny.

如果没有亚马逊游客在混合中，谷歌的絮状物可能处于劣势，这是一个匿名的挖掘那个机构高管表示。亚马逊选择没有阻止絮状块，公司可以通过启用“明显改善某些市场购物流的结果”来协助谷歌，说。已经，谷歌的索赔对方法的表现已经受到审查。

Amazon’s move is a “huge restriction” on Google’s shopper data gathering, said one of the technologists who assisted Digiday in research for this story and asked not to be named. “What you browse on Amazon is who you are,” said the source, who works with ad tech-related data.

亚马逊的举动是谷歌购物者数据收集的“巨大限制”，这是一位在研究这个故事的研究中提供挖掘和要求不被命名的技术人员。 “你浏览亚马逊的是你是谁，”与广告技术相关的数据一起使用的来源说。

Plus, blocking FLoC is a competitive decision. Amazon has its own aspirations for grabbing more of the ad dollars Google controls by selling digital ads outside Amazon properties. As Amazon’s demand-side platform business takes off, the firm  plans to launch an identifier for tracking and measuring ads sold through the DSP and by publishers through Amazon’s publisher services division. “It strengthens [Amazon’s] DSP offering to block it,” said the agency exec.

此外，阻塞絮状物是竞争决定。亚马逊拥有自己的愿望，可以通过在亚马逊属性之外销售数字广告来抓取更多广告谷歌控件。随着亚马逊的需求侧平台业务起飞，该公司计划通过亚马逊的出版商服务部门来推出跟踪和测量广告的标识符，并通过发布商通过发布商。 “它加强了[亚马逊] DSP提出来阻止它，”代理执行。

“Why give Google an inch?” asked another Amazon-focused agency executive who also spoke with Digiday on condition of anonymity.

“为什么要给谷歌一英寸？”另外亚马逊专注的机构高管们在匿名的条件下与挖掘那些讨论过。

However, while impeding FLoC from becoming a preferred alternative to third-party cookie tracking could help Amazon, the company loses out on some information that could be of value to it by blocking FLoC. Like other  publishers preventing the tracking method, Amazon will not be able to intercept the clues FLoC IDs provide that give a window into people’s behavior. Arguably, that information is of limited value to Amazon considering the trove of logged-in shopper information it has at its disposal. Why an Amazon site is letting in FLoC  Amazon-owned e-commerce sites including WholeFoods, Zappos, ShopBop and GoodReads display code that block FLoC; however, at least at the time of this story’s publication, Amazon-owned bookselling site AbeBooks was not blocking it.

但是，在阻碍絮状物成为第三方Cookie跟踪的首选替代方案时可以帮助亚马逊，而该公司通过阻止絮凝物可以对其的一些信息丢失。与防止跟踪方法的其他出版商一样，亚马逊将无法拦截线索絮凝物ID，提供窗口进入人们的行为。可以说，考虑到它处于处置的登录购物者信息的格式，信息对亚马逊的价值有限。为什么亚马逊网站在弗洛克亚马逊拥有的电子商务网站，包括全方位，Zappos，ShopBop和Goodreads显示块的絮凝剂;然而，至少在这个故事的出版时，亚马逊拥有的书籍网站abebook没有阻止它。

Amazon could have a motive for enabling Google’s trackers to take note of what content and books AbeBooks visitors look at, the second agency executive speculated. “They don’t just do stuff,” said the exec regarding Amazon’s deliberate approach. “This has got to be a test of some kind,” said the exec. For instance, while Amazon has lots of data internally about people’s interests related to books, the company could want to learn what FLoC IDs tell it about the interests of AbeBooks visitors outside the site, they added.

第二个机构主管推测，亚马逊可以让谷歌的追踪者借助谷歌的追踪者来注意到的内容和书籍观察员的观察员。 “他们不仅仅是做的东西，”关于亚马逊的故意方法，执行官说。 “这必须是某种测试，”exec说。例如，虽然亚马逊在内部有很多关于与书籍有关的人的兴趣，但公司可以了解哪些絮凝物ID告诉它在网站外的Abebooks游客的利益。

Amazon might also want to see how its own ad tracking and targeting stacks up against Google’s in a control test of some sort. “You could then put Google in their own horse race without telling them,” said the exec.

亚马逊可能还希望了解自己的广告跟踪和定位在某种程序的控制测试中如何对谷歌进行堆叠。 “然后你可以在没有告诉他们的情况下把谷歌放在自己的赛马中，”exec说。

But, Amazon might be planning to stop FLoC tracking on AbeBooks, too, according to ad tech researcher Krzysztof Franaszek, who has  conducted FLoC research and helped verify Amazon’s FLoC blocking for this story. The company “probably has different teams responsible for those websites,” he said. “Maybe they haven’t gotten around to it.”

但是，亚马逊可能计划在Ad Tech研究员Krzysztof Franaszek进行Floc Research并帮助验证亚马逊的絮絮上为这个故事验证亚马逊的絮絮上，这也计划停止在Abebook上停止絮絮跟踪。公司“可能有不同的团队负责这些网站，”他说。 “也许他们没有绕过它。”

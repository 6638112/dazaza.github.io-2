MoviePass is relaunching – and it wants to track your eyeballs
MoviePass正在重新启动，它想追踪你的眼球

2022-02-13 12:47:58

The subscription movie ticket service is relaunching this summer, nearly three years after the company first folded in 2019, CEO Stacy Spikes said at a press event in Manhattan on Thursday.

该公司首席执行官斯泰西·斯派克斯（Stacy Spikes）周四在曼哈顿的一次新闻发布会上表示，订阅电影票服务将于今年夏天重新推出，距离该公司2019年首次倒闭已近三年。

MoviePass’ new subscription service won’t offer unlimited movies anymore, but will instead introduce a tiered subscription system, Spikes said. Similarly to fitness startup Classpass, MoviePass customers will buy credits that they can then redeem for movie showings.

斯派克斯说，MoviePass的新订阅服务将不再提供无限量的电影，而是将引入分层订阅系统。与健身初创公司Classpass类似，MoviePass的客户将购买信用卡，然后兑换成电影放映费。

But Spikes also showed off another way that customers will be able to earn credits: watching ads and having their eyeballs tracked.

但Spikes还展示了另一种客户可以获得积分的方式：观看广告并跟踪他们的眼球。

Using a feature called “pre-show,” MoviePass customers will get credits in exchange for watching ads on their phones. To make sure they’re actually watching, the app will track users’ eyeballs, Spikes said.

MoviePass的用户通过一个名为“pre show”的功能，可以通过手机观看广告获得积分。斯派克斯说，为了确保他们真的在观看，该应用程序将跟踪用户的眼球。

“What it does is it basically creates a transaction between you and the brand,” the CEO said, wearing a black turtleneck and pacing the stage in the style of Steve Jobs.

这位首席执行官穿着黑色高领毛衣，以史蒂夫·乔布斯（Steve Jobs）的风格在舞台上踱来踱去，他说：“它所做的基本上是在你和品牌之间创造一种交易。”。

“Your phone, your device uses your own facial detection,” Spikes added. “It doesn’t go to the cloud, nobody goes through anything other than you and your information in yours. And you opt in to do it on your own.”

斯派克斯补充道：“你的手机、你的设备使用你自己的面部检测。”。“它不会进入云端，除了你和你的信息之外，没有人会浏览任何东西。你选择自己去做。”

He portrayed the “pre-show” feature as an extension of product placement in movies.

他将“展前”功能描述为电影中植入式广告的延伸。

“I love product placement in movies,” Spikes said. “I love the cars, I love the watches, I love the clothes. I’m that person that sometimes has a notepad and I’m writing down, is that Hugo Boss?”

“我喜欢电影中的植入式广告，”斯派克斯说。“我喜欢汽车，我喜欢手表，我喜欢衣服。我就是那种有时会有记事本的人，我在写，是雨果·博斯吗？”

Under MoviePass’ new credit system, in-demand movies at prime times will require more credits, whereas matinees of less popular films will take fewer, Spikes said. He said the system would help movies fill theaters on slower days.

斯派克斯说，根据MoviePass的新学分制，黄金时段的热门电影将需要更多学分，而不太受欢迎的电影的日场将需要更少的学分。他说，该系统将有助于电影在慢节奏的日子里挤满影院。

A MoviePass spokesperson did not say how much credits will cost or provide details on differences between the “tiered plans” when reached for comment by The Post.

一位MoviePass发言人在接受《华盛顿邮报》采访时没有透露信用卡的费用，也没有提供“分层计划”之间差异的细节。

In its original incarnation, MoviePass had offered customers unlimited movie tickets for just $10 a month — a money-losing strategy that drew more than 3 million customers but ultimately led the company to bankruptcy after it bled more than $100 million in a single quarter.

在最初的版本中，MoviePass为客户提供了每月10美元的无限电影票——这是一种亏损策略，吸引了300多万客户，但最终导致该公司在一个季度内亏损超过1亿美元后破产。

Moviepass first hinted at its plans for a relaunch in November, when Spikes bought the company in New York bankruptcy court for a meager $14,000. He said in court filings that company was testing a “new proposed business model with a sample group of 1,000 customers” in late 2021.

Moviepass在11月首次暗示了其重新启动的计划，当时Spikes在纽约破产法庭以1.4万美元的微薄价格收购了该公司。他在法庭文件中说，公司在2021年底测试了一个“新的商业模式，有1000个客户的样本组”。

The filings did not provide details of the new business model but did show that Spikes wanted to launch a full-fledged MoviePass comeback in the fall or winter of 2022.

这些文件没有提供新商业模式的细节，但确实表明，斯派克希望在2022年秋冬季推出全面的电影通行证回归。

Spikes originally co-founded in 2011 but the service only gained steam in 2017 after it was purchased by Helios and Matheson Analytics, a data analytics firm. That was when the company introduced its $10 unlimited movie tickets deal, supercharging its growth.

Spikes最初于2011年联合创立，但该服务在2017年被Helios和数据分析公司Matheson Analytics收购后才获得发展。当时，该公司推出了10美元的无限制电影票交易，推动了其增长。

Spikes says that at the time he questioned whether the $10-a-month model was sustainable and says he was fired by the app’s new owners in 2018 over those concerns.

斯派克斯说，当时他质疑每月10美元的模式是否可持续，并说他在2018年因为这些担忧被该应用的新所有者解雇。

“There was a really big difference in the belief of the way the company should go forward,” Spikes said during Thursday’s presentation. “And we all kind of know what happened after that.”

斯派克斯在周四的演讲中说：“人们对公司前进方向的看法存在很大差异。”。“我们都知道那之后发生了什么。”

MoviePass ultimately folded after customers abused the company’s model by binge-watching the same films over and over, buying tickets for friends and family who didn’t subscribe to the service, and in some cases, purchasing a movie ticket just “to go to the restroom in Times Square,” Ted Farnsworth, then the CEO of Helios and Matheson Analytics, told The Post at the time of its shutdown in 2019.

Ted Farnsworth说，由于顾客滥用公司的模式，一遍又一遍地疯狂观看同一部电影，为没有订阅该服务的朋友和家人买票，在某些情况下，购买电影票只是为了“去时代广场的洗手间”，MoviePass最终倒闭，时任Helios and Matheson Analytics首席执行官的他在2019年停刊时告诉《华盛顿邮报》。

Adding to its problems was the fact that movie theater chains like AMC and Cinemark began to  roll out their own ticket subscription services, causing MoviePass to add new fees to the biggest films and prime time slots. In September 2019, it  shut down indefinitely, with its parent company  filing for bankruptcy four months later.

此外，AMC和Cinemark等电影院连锁店开始推出自己的订票服务，这导致MoviePass为最大的电影和黄金时段增加了新的费用。2019年9月，该公司无限期关闭，四个月后母公司申请破产。

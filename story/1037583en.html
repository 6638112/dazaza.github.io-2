Three Democratic lawmakers introduce a bill that would require stablecoin issuers to obtain a banking charter, approval from the Federal Reserve and FDIC, more
三位民主党议员提出一项法案，要求稳定币发行人获得银行宪章，并获得美联储和联邦存款保险公司的批准，更多

2020-12-04 21:24:43

A new U.S. congressional bill would require stablecoin issuers to secure bank charters and secure regulatory approval prior to circulating any stablecoins.

新的美国国会法案将要求稳定币发行人在发行任何稳定币之前获得银行宪章并获得监管部门的批准。

U.S. Representatives Rashida Tlaib (D-Mich.), Jesús “Chuy” García (D-Ill.) and Stephen Lynch (D-Mass.) introduced the Stablecoin Tethering and Bank Licensing Enforcement (STABLE) Act on Wednesday, writing in a press release that it would focus on regulating stablecoins, naming the stablecoin of the Facebook-led Libra project ( since renamed Diem) as one example.

美国代表拉希达·特莱布（D-密歇根州），耶苏斯·“丘伊”·加西亚（D-伊利诺伊州）和史蒂芬·林奇（D·马萨诸塞州）于周三介绍了《稳定币网络共享和银行执照执行法》（STABLE），并在新闻界撰文发布，它将专注于监管稳定币，以Facebook领导的Libra项目（自更名为Diem）命名为稳定币为例。

“Digital currencies, whose value is permanently pegged to or stabilized against a conventional currency like the dollar, pose new regulatory challenges while also represent a growing source of the market, liquidity, and credit risk,” the press release said.

新闻稿称：“数字货币的价值与美元等传统货币永久钉住或稳定在一起，在监管方面面临着新的挑战，同时也代表着越来越多的市场，流动性和信贷风险。”

The 18-page bill would specifically require stablecoin issuers to obtain a banking charter; require approval from the Federal Reserve, Federal Deposit Insurance Corporation and the issuer’s specific state or federal bank regulator; require those same entities to conduct an ongoing analysis of any systemic risk; and require issuers to have FDIC insurance or maintain reserves for easy conversion back into U.S. dollars.

长达18页的法案将特别要求稳定币发行人获得银行宪章；需要获得美联储，联邦存款保险公司和发行人的特定州或联邦银行监管机构的批准；要求这些实体对任何系统性风险进行持续分析；并要求发行人购买FDIC保险或保留准备金，以便轻松转换回美元。

This would apply to stablecoins pegged to other national or state currencies, the bill said.

该法案说，这将适用于与其他本国或本州货币挂钩的稳定币。

Chastity Murphy, an economic adviser to Rep. Tlaib, told CoinDesk that both state and federal bank charters would satisfy the bill’s requirements.

众议员特莱布（Tlaib）的经济顾问Chastity Murphy告诉CoinDesk，州和联邦银行宪章都将满足该法案的要求。

Rohan Grey, an assistant professor at the Willamette University College of Law in Oregon, told CoinDesk the bill is really defining what a deposit is as far as digital assets are concerned. In his view, stablecoins are effectively an internet-native form of a deposit.

俄勒冈州威拉米特大学法学院助理教授罗汉·格雷（Rohan Grey）告诉CoinDesk，该法案实际上定义了就数字资产而言的存款。在他看来，稳定币实际上是一种互联网本地存款形式。

“Any entity that wants to issue something that walks and talks like money or like a deposit should be regulated like a depository institution,” he said. Grey is an adviser for the bill.

他说：“任何想要发行诸如货币或存款之类的走路和说话的实体，都应像托管机构一样受到监管。”格雷是该法案的顾问。

The press release also mentioned a letter that the sponsors and cosponsors had previously sent to Acting Comptroller of the Currency Brian Brooks, that questioned the regulator’s focus on the digital asset space. Specifically, the lawmakers took issue with OCC interpretive letters on banks providing custody services to stablecoin issuers and other crypto platforms.

新闻稿还提到了赞助商和共同赞助商此前寄给货币代理代理局长布莱恩·布鲁克斯（Brian Brooks）的一封信，该信质疑监管机构对数字资产领域的关注。特别是，立法者在为稳定币发行人和其他加密平台提供托管服务的银行中发出了OCC解释性信。

The bill is designed to protect individuals, Rep. Tlaib said on Twitter and in the press release.

众议员特莱布在推特和新闻稿中说，该法案旨在保护个人。

In a tweet she wrote, “Preventing cryptocurrency providers from repeating the crimes against low- and moderate-income residents of color traditional big banks have is critically important.”

她在一条推文中写道：“防止加密货币提供商重复针对有色传统大银行的中低收入居民的犯罪至关重要。”

A number of stablecoin issuers currently operate in the U.S. without banking charters, including the CENTRE consortium (which is composed of Circle and Coinbase), Gemini and Paxos. Algorithmic stablecoins like basis.cash or crypto collateral tokens like DAI would also appear to fall under this bill.

目前，美国有多家没有银行宪章的稳定币发行人，包括CENTER财团（由Circle和Coinbase组成），Gemini和Paxos。诸如base.cash之类的算法稳定币或诸如DAI之类的加密抵押代币似乎也属于该法案之列。

“Any stablecoin that meets the statutory definition is eligible, because the focus is on what the coin promises (i.e., the obligation), not how it claims to be able to enforce the obligation (i.e., the collateral backing). Full-reserve, partial-reserve, algorithmically deteremined basket – these are all asset-side variations. The ‘deposit’ component focuses on the liability side promise,” Murphy said.

“符合法定定义的任何稳定币都是合格的，因为重点在于代币的承诺（即义务），而不是它声称如何能够履行义务（即抵押物）。完全保留，部分保留，算法确定的篮子–这些都是资产方面的变化。 “存款”部分着重于责任方承诺，”墨菲说。

Circle CEO Jeremy Allaire said the bill “would represent a huge step backwards” by limiting industry innovation.

Circle首席执行官杰里米·阿莱尔（Jeremy Allaire）表示，该法案将限制行业创新，“将意味着倒退一大步”。

“An enormous amount of the innovation brought to the underbanked and small businesses has been driven by non-bank fintech companies, and forcing crypto, fintech and blockchain companies into the enormous regulatory burdens of Federal Reserve and FDIC regulation and supervision is inconsistent with the goals of supporting innovation in the fair and inclusive delivery of payments that comes from stablecoins,” he said in a statement emailed to CoinDesk.

“非银行金融科技公司带给了资金不足的小企业的巨大创新，并且迫使加密货币，金融科技和区块链公司承担美联储和FDIC监管的巨大监管负担，这与目标不符。他在发给CoinDesk的一份声明中说：“在稳定和稳定的支付方式中，支持创新以公平，包容的方式进行支付。”

More effective ways to regulate stablecoins could come from new charters or other types of supervision, he said.

他说，更有效的监管稳定币的方法可能来自于新章程或其他类型的监管。

In a statement, Blockchain Association Executive Director Kristin Smith opposed the bill, saying, “While we have had sustained and constructive discussions with Rep. Tlaib’s office on this issue, we disagree with the perspective of this legislation and oppose this bill. It would strengthen the position of the most powerful financial institutions, while overlooking two core promises of decentralized networks: the chance to put more power in the hands of individual consumers and to catalyze innovation across payments and other financial services.”

区块链协会执行董事克里斯汀·史密斯（Kristin Smith）在一份声明中反对该法案，并说：“虽然我们与众议员办公室就此问题进行了持续和建设性的讨论，但我们不同意该立法的观点，并反对该法案。这将加强最强大的金融机构的地位，同时又会忽视去中心化网络的两个核心承诺：将更多的权力掌握在个人消费者手中，并促进支付和其他金融服务领域的创新的机会。”

Smith said the OCC’s guidance around stablecoins was a “progressive example” of how these tokens could be regulated in the U.S.

史密斯（Smith）说，OCC关于稳定币的指南是如何在美国监管这些代币的“一个进步的例子”。

The current congressional session will end in just a few weeks but Murphy said the bill will be introduced again next year.

本届国会会议将在短短几周内结束，但墨菲表示该法案将于明年再次提出。

“Often there’s a tendency to react in the moment to technological changes or innovation by saying, ‘stop, don’t do that’ and what this bill is trying to do is preserve this in a forward-looking way,” he said.

他说：“通常在当下对技术变革或创新会做出反应，说出'停止，不要这样做'，而这项法案试图做的就是保持这种前瞻性。”

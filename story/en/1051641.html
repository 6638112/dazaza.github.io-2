What We Can Learn from Japanese Prefab Homes

Thanks to cutting-edge design, mass production, and customization, prefabricated housing is common practice in Japan.

由于尖端设计，批量生产和定制，预制住房是日本的常见做法。

The highly innovative Japanese prefab housing industry has developed a user-orientated approach by integrating specially developed marketing, design and quality-orientated techniques into its production processes. Customization is delivered through holistic management and balancing the use of standard components with flexibility of assembly. With manufacturers continually striving to bridge the communication gap between themselves and their clients to offer successful mass-customized designs, homebuyers benefit from a sense of ownership and individuality. In turn, the manufacturers benefit from customer satisfaction and a positive reputation in the market.

通过将专门开发的营销，设计和质量方向的技术集成到其生产过程中，高度创新的日本预制外壳业制定了一种以用户为导向的方法。定制通过整体管理提供，并平衡使用组装灵活性的标准组件。随着制造商不断努力弥合自己与客户之间的沟通差距，提供成功的大规模定制设计，购房者受益于所有权和个性感。反过来，制造商受益于客户满意度和市场上的积极声誉。

Japan’s prefabricated-housing industry is a world leader in innovation, with efficient production processes, targeted marketing strategies and consumer outreach, and high-quality designs that offer variability and flexibility at affordable prices. A fully computerized system enables clients to customize their homes with standardized components. Unlike the common Western perception about prefabrication, in Japan factory-manufactured houses are seen as superior to conventionally built ones.

日本的预制房业是创新的世界领导者，具有高效的生产流程，有针对性的营销策略和消费者外展，以及以实惠价格提供可变性和灵活性的高质量设计。完全计算机化的系统使客户能够使用标准化组件自定义其房屋。与对预制的共同看法不同，在日本工厂制造的房屋被视为优于传统建造的房屋。

In the post-war period, Japan began experimenting with prefab housing to meet demand, and factory manufacturing became more popular. One of the reasons behind this trend was that houses have a short lifespan in Japan—an average of twenty-six years—and homebuyers often prefer to build their own. The country’s population is roughly twice that of the UK, yet over six times as much new housing is built in Japan than in the UK each year. A family that outgrows its home will simply tear it down and build a new one on the same site, and some companies now offer deals for house replacement: a maximum fifty-day period (including weekends) from moving out to moving in. This style of construction makes a fully customizable production process particularly advantageous.

在战后时期，日本开始尝试预制住房以满足需求，而工厂制造业变得更加流行。这种趋势背后的原因之一是，房屋在日本有短暂的寿命 - 平均二十六年 - 和购房者经常愿意建立自己的。该国的人口大致两倍于英国，但新住房的六倍于日本建于每年比英国建于日本。一个家庭，这些家庭将其家中的家庭将简单地撕毁它并在同一网站上建立一个新的一个，而一些公司现在提供房屋更换的优惠：最高五十日期（包括周末）搬出去移动。这种风格建筑采用完全可定制的生产过程特别有利。

The urgent demand for housing in the 1960s and ’70s also provided an opportunity, as conventional home construction was unable to saturate the housing market owing to a shortage of materials and labor. As a result, manufactured homes provided an affordable alternative, drastically accelerating their wider acceptance.

20世纪60年代和70年代的房屋紧急需求也提供了机会，因为由于材料和劳动力不足，传统的房屋建设无法使房地产市场饱和。因此，制造的房屋提供了一种实惠的替代方案，急剧加速他们更广泛的验收。

A main feature of Japanese prefab housing is the extensive system for mass-customization, with a high level of consumer participation throughout the process. A modular production method is used, with manufacturers focusing on a custom design for each unit, while mass-producing a variety of housing components for clients to choose from. This system is critical to satisfying the array of demands of the diverse homebuyer demography and to controlling costs through mass-production and economy of scale.

日本预制外壳的主要特点是大规模定制的广泛系统，在整个过程中具有高水平的消费者参与。使用模块化生产方法，制造商专注于每个单元的定制设计，同时批量生产各种外壳组件，可供客户选择。该系统对于满足各种购房者人口统计学的需求阵列至关重要，并通过大规模生产和规模经济控制成本。

The industry achieved its reputation through an attention to quality control. Manufacturers take pride in knowing that once their homes leave the highly rationalized assembly-line setting, few consumers can distinguish a factory-made home from a traditional "stick-built" one, in either Japanese or Western styles. Public education through information centers play a key role in advertising these homes, offering technical information about materials, construction methods and amenities. They also function as exhibition and consultation bases, where staff advise clients on customization, from external appearance to floorplans, with the aid of advanced computer technology.

该行业通过注意质量控制来实现其声誉。制造商在知道一旦他们的房屋留下高度合理的装配线设置时，少数消费者可以从传统的＆＃34中区分工厂的家庭;一，在日本或西方风格。公共教育通过信息中心在广告这些家庭方面发挥着关键作用，提供有关材料，施工方法和设施的技术信息。他们还担任展览和咨询基地，员工向客户提供定制，从外观到地板平板，借助先进的计算机技术。

In Japan, prefab homes are mass-customized using primarily a standardized component system for structural, exterior and interior arrangements. To provide the necessary flexibility, a modular system is employed, in which housing components are divided into the categories of volume, exterior, and interior, and fitted in a variety of ways using a universal connector system. The volume components are used in building the structure that determines the number and size of each type of room, while the interior and exterior components serve to coordinate the decorative and the functional elements that customize a home.

在日本，预制家庭主要使用标准化的组件系统，用于结构，外部和内部布置。为了提供必要的灵活性，采用模块化系统，其中壳体部件分为体积，外部和内部的类别，并使用通用连接器系统以各种方式配备。体积组件用于建立确定每种房间的数量和尺寸的结构，而内部和外部部件用于协调定制房屋的装饰和功能元件。

The concept of volume components often applies to modular pre-fabricated homes, because a panelized housing system does not define spatial limitations for a volume of interior space. In the design process of panelized homes, however, a modular logic is similarly applied, based on the size of the traditional tatami mat (90 x 180 cm, or 3 x 6 ft), which forms the basic unit of a room and provides a convenient measure against which manufacturers can produce unit components. Each structural unit is then treated as a box-shaped frame component, with spatial variations defined by a combination of these standard units.

体积组件的概念通常适用于模块化预制的房屋，因为块板化的外壳系统没有为一定程度的内部空间定义空间限制。然而，在块的房屋的设计过程中，基于传统榻榻米垫的尺寸（90 x 180cm，或3 x 6英尺），类似地应用模块化逻辑，该尺寸形成房间的基本单元并提供a方便的措施，制造商可以生产单位组件。然后将每个结构单元作为盒形框架组件处理，其具有由这些标准单元的组合限定的空间变化。

Exterior components that define the visual identity of a house from the outside—roof, walls, windows, verandas, balconies and entrances—are vital in fostering a sense of ownership, with the client participating in the design. Catalogues, design-consultancy services and information centers that offer real samples of built constructions are available to aid clients with their decisions and to put them at ease.

外部组件，定义外屋顶，墙壁，窗户，阳台，阳台和入口的房屋的视觉识别 - 对于培养所有权感，客户都是至关重要的，客户参与设计。目录，设计 - 咨询服务和提供建筑结构实际样本的信息中心可用于援助客户及其决定，并放松。

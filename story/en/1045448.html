Twitch’s Trump ban sustained after leaving office
Twitch的特朗普禁令在卸任后得以维持

2021-01-21 06:11:42

On Wednesday, an automated alert about Twitch account bans included a somewhat surprising account name: "@DonaldTrump." The surprise came because Twitch had already "indefinitely suspended" the former president's official Twitch channel on January 7, in the wake of his January 6 speech inciting a seditious riot—and a surge in Trump deplatforming as a result.

在星期三，有关Twitch帐户禁令的自动警报包括一个有点令人惊讶的帐户名称：＆＃34; @DonaldTrump。惊喜来自Twitch已经无限期暂停了。 1月7日，这位前总统的官方Twitch频道发表了煽动骚乱的言论，致使特朗普动荡，特朗普于1月6日发表讲话。

Following this Wednesday alert, Twitch confirmed to Ars Technica that this was no accident: Trump's account is indeed outright banned. Twitch continues to call the ban an "indefinite suspension," but it has not offered any timeline for its return or steps that its account holders (either Trump himself or any representatives) may take to reverse the decision. Wednesday's news lines up with a Tuesday claim by DW News reporter Dana Regev, who'd hinted to Twitch waiting until after President Biden's inauguration to make a firmer ruling on the previous ban.

星期三发出警报后，Twitch向Ars Technica确认这绝非偶然：特朗普的帐户的确被完全禁止了。 Twitch继续将该禁令称为“无限期暂停”，但它并未提供任何时间表，以要求其返回帐户的人或帐户持有人（特朗普本人或任何代表）可能采取的撤销该决定的步骤。周三的新闻与DW新闻记者达娜·雷格夫（Dana Regev）的周二声明相吻合，后者暗示特维奇要等到拜登总统就职后才对前一禁令做出更坚定的裁决。

The service took the rare step of outlining the exact reason for the ban, a courtesy generally not reserved to those affected. This lack of clarity emerged in particular when Twitch offered no explanation about banning Guy "DrDisrespect" Beahm in the wake of spreading COVID-19 misinformation.

这项服务采取了罕见的步骤，概述了禁令的确切原因，这是对受影响者通常没有的礼貌。尤其是当Twitch没有提供关于禁止Guy＆＃34; DrDisrespect＆＃34; Beahm在传播COVID-19错误信息之后。

In Trump's case, Twitch cited "the ongoing risk of further incitement of violence" as a primary reason for the ban. The statement, as issued to Ars Technica by a Twitch representative, continued:

在特朗普案中，特维奇（Twitch）引用了进一步煽动暴力的持续风险作为禁令的主要原因。 Twitch代表向Ars Technica发表的声明继续：

The President's statements continue to be interpreted as calls to action, and we are taking this action to remove the potential for harm to our community and the general public.

总统的讲话继续被视为呼吁采取行动，我们正在采取这一行动，以消除对我们的社区和广大公众造成伤害的可能性。

Twitch has clear rules that prohibit hateful conduct, harassment, or incitement of violence on our service, and we consider off-service events when making enforcement decisions. However, the events of the past weeks have highlighted a gap with respect to rhetoric that encourages violence, regardless of whether or not it was directly streamed on Twitch. We will be updating our policies as a result of our consideration of this situation.

Twitch有明确的规则，禁止在我们的服务上进行仇恨行为，骚扰或煽动暴力，并且我们在制定执法决策时会考虑非服务事件。但是，过去几周的事件凸显了在鼓励暴力的言论方面的差距，无论它是否直接在Twitch上流传。考虑到这种情况，我们将更新我们的政策。

Twitch has recently enacted sweeping new rules to allow moderators to take context into account when deciding on whether content qualifies as "hateful" speech. In December, its official channel cited specific gamer-slang terms as examples of this context-sensitive approach, but the resulting video became widely cited as official bans for certain words and a hint of jargon whack-a-mole, as opposed to an example of sweeping moderation changes to come.

Twitch最近颁布了详尽的新规则，以允许主持人在确定内容是否符合“令人讨厌”的条件时考虑到上下文。言语。在12月，其官方频道引用了特定的游戏玩家用语作为这种上下文相关方法的示例，但是与示例相反，所得到的视频被广泛引用为某些单词和一语行话的官方禁令。适度的变化即将到来。

Today's statement hints strongly that Twitch's rule-changing stance is far from over, with a clear eye to enforcing rules based on statements made outside of Twitch video streams.

今天的声明强烈暗示Twitch的规则更改立场远未结束，并明确地基于基于Twitch视频流之外的声明来执行规则。

The @DonaldTrump account, launched in October 2019, was used to either live-stream or rebroadcast official Trump speeches and affiliated events, as opposed to hosting the service's usual gaming-related video streams—all while hosting a chat channel for the account's followers. Twitch has long faced issues with chat toxicity, particularly with many streamers continuing to embrace the "Pepe frog" meme without denouncing white supremacists' embrace of the icon (a fact that led its original creator to sue those who co-opted the image without his consent). Trump's Twitch account had been in hot water previously, due to its rebroadcasts of rule-breaking speeches. His channel's last temporary ban, enacted on June 29, happened because it had aired his notorious statements about Mexico "sending... rapists" to the United States.

Info is available about which college majors pay off, students aren’t using it
提供有关哪些大学专业获得回报，学生没有使用的信息

2020-12-26 09:35:56

When the University of Texas system teamed up with the Census Bureau to show how much money graduates earn, broken down by major and campus, the idea was to help future students make good choices.

当德克萨斯大学系统与人口普查局合作显示毕业生的收入时（按专业和校园划分），其目的是帮助未来的学生做出更好的选择。

College is, after all, a huge investment, with costs consumers often criticize and toward which many have to borrow. If they knew that one major results in higher salaries than others — or that graduates from one university earn more than those with the exact same degree from another — wouldn’t they make the higher-paying choice?

毕竟，大学是一笔巨大的投资，它的成本经常遭到消费者的批评，许多人不得不向其借钱。如果他们知道一个专业的薪资比其他专业更高，或者一所大学的毕业生的收入比另一所大学的完全相同，那么他们会选择更高薪的选择吗？

Two years after the groundbreaking collaboration began, however, students haven’t seemed to alter course, said David Troutman, the system’s associate vice chancellor, who oversees the project.

该系统的副副总理戴维·特劳特曼（David Troutman）说，在开创性的合作开始两年后，学生似乎并没有改变课程。

“What we find is that they’re not changing their majors,” Troutman said. “They’re following their passions.”

“我们发现他们没有改变专业，” Troutman说。 “他们追随自己的激情。”

He and other advocates stress that they want students to continue following their passions. But they also want them to be aware that earnings vary widely among graduates, even when they have identical majors, from different universities and colleges, affecting not only their quality of life but their ability to repay their student loans.

他和其他倡导者强调，他们希望学生继续追随自己的激情。但是，他们也希望他们意识到，即使毕业生来自不同的大学和学院，他们的专业相同，但他们的收入差异却很大，这不仅影响了他们的生活质量，还影响了他们偿还学生贷款的能力。

That’s a principal reason more and more information about job opportunities and salaries is being made available to students and their families, most recently by the federal government, which this month expanded a feature of its College Scorecard website showing the earnings payoffs of 37,459 majors at 4,434 colleges and universities.

这是主要原因，联邦政府最近向学生及其家庭提供了有关工作机会和薪水的越来越多的信息，联邦政府本月扩大了其大学计分卡网站的功能，显示了37,459个专业的收入回报为4,434高校。

The Texas pilot is being copied in Colorado, Michigan, New York, Pennsylvania and Wisconsin. The California community college system has launched its own website showing median salaries of graduates in different fields; so has the state of Florida.

德克萨斯州的飞行员正在科罗拉多州，密歇根州，纽约州，宾夕法尼亚州和威斯康星州进行复制。加州社区大学系统已经启动了自己的网站，显示不同领域毕业生的中位数工资；佛罗里达州也是如此。

But even as this information becomes more readily available, there’s consensus that students generally aren’t consulting it when deciding where to go and what to study.

但是，即使这些信息变得越来越容易获得，人们仍普遍认为，学生在决定去哪里和学习什么时通常不会咨询它。

There’s also growing belief that increasing attention to the return on an investment in a college education, propelled by this pandemic year, may finally get them to start.

人们也越来越相信，在这一大流行年的推动下，越来越重视对大学教育投资回报的关注，最终可能会促使他们开始工作。

Knowing postgraduate placement rates and incomes “has not changed behavior, and that is hard to understand,” said Tony Carnevale, director of the Georgetown University Center on Education and the Workforce. But, he added, “it is about to change behavior.”

乔治敦大学教育与劳动力中心主任托尼·卡内维尔（Tony Carnevale）说，知道研究生的就业率和收入“并没有改变行为，这很难理解。”但是，他补充说，“这将改变行为。”

An analysis by the Georgetown center using College Scorecard data found that nurses with associate degrees from a community college in California make more than graduates of a dozen master’s degree programs at Harvard. Electrical and power transmission installers with associate degrees from a community college in West Virginia earn $80,400 in their first year, or more than twice the median income of bachelor’s degree recipients generally. In all, 27 percent of workers with associate degrees make more than the median salary for their counterparts with bachelor’s degrees.

乔治敦中心使用“大学记分卡”数据进行的分析发现，加利福尼亚州一所社区学院的副学士学位的毕业生比哈佛大学十几个硕士学位课程的毕业生要多。拥有西弗吉尼亚州一所社区大学副学士学位的电气和电力传输安装人员在第一年的收入为80,400美元，是一般学士学位获得者的平均收入的两倍以上。总体而言，拥有副学士学位的工人中，有27％的收入高于拥有学士学位的工人的中位工资。

Earnings vary widely not only by what kind of a degree a student gets, but where. Workers with undergraduate degrees in business administration make as little as $20,000 a year to as much as $100,000 in the first year after college, depending on which institution their degrees are from. Students with master’s degrees in educational administration and supervision from one private college in New York earn more than three times as much as graduates with the same degree from a public university in Georgia.

收入的变化不仅取决于学生获得的学位程度，而且取决于获得学位的程度。拥有工商管理学士学位的工人每年的收入低至20,000美元，大学毕业后第一年的收入高达100,000美元，具体取决于其学位来自哪个机构。在纽约一所私立大学获得教育管理和监督硕士学位的学生，其收入是在乔治亚州一所公立大学获得相同学位的毕业生的三倍以上。

But surveys find that students generally don’t know these things, or even what they can expect to make in the careers toward which they’re studying.

但调查发现，学生通常不知道这些事情，甚至不知道他们在所从事的职业中会做什么。

A survey of nearly 3,000 students by scholars at Rutgers University found that they overestimated their salary prospects. In another study, freshmen and sophomores at New York University overestimated the average earnings of male business graduates by $34,750. And only 13 percent of 376 community college students surveyed could correctly rank four general categories of majors by salary, according to yet another study, by scholars at Stanford and the universities of Michigan, Chicago and California at Irvine; those students overestimated earnings by an average of 13 percent.

罗格斯大学（Rutgers University）的学者对近3,000名学生进行的一项调查发现，他们高估了自己的薪资前景。在另一项研究中，纽约大学的新生和大二学生高估了男性商科毕业生的平均收入34,750美元。斯坦福大学和密歇根大学，芝加哥大学和加州大学尔湾分校的学者们进行的另一项研究显示，在接受调查的376名社区大学生中，只有13％可以按薪水正确地划分四个专业类别。这些学生平均高估了收入13％。

Once they were given accurate income information, 12 percent of the NYU freshmen and sophomores said it would make a difference in the major they chose. Some of the community college students also said that they would change their majors; for every 1 percent increase in salary associated with a major, the likelihood they would choose that major rose between 1.4 percent and 1.8 percent.

一旦获得准确的收入信息，纽约大学新生和大二学生中有12％的人表示，这将对他们选择的专业有所帮助。一些社区大学生还说，他们将改变专业。与专业相关的薪水每增加1％，他们选择该专业的可能性就会上升1.4％至1.8％。

But that requires them to have the facts. For now, Troutman said he was “in awe and dumbfounded” about why students continue to choose high-priced majors in low-paying fields.

但这要求他们掌握事实。特劳特曼说，就目前而言，他对学生为何继续在低薪领域选择高价专业感到“敬畏和傻眼”。

He said those who have been using the Texas earnings data do so not as undergraduates when deciding what to study, but in salary negotiations with employers after earning their degrees.

他说，那些一直在使用德克萨斯州收入数据的人在决定学习什么时并不像大学生那样，而是在获得学位后与雇主进行薪资谈判。

That students don’t know their likely future incomes well before they graduate is particularly surprising given that getting a good job is now the No. 1 reason they say they go to college, according to a nationwide survey of freshmen by an institute at the University of California at Los Angeles — edging out “learn[ing] more about things that interest me” — and that 84 percent said it was very important or essential to them to be financially very well off.

根据大学对大学新生的一项全国调查，鉴于找到一份好工作现在是他们说自己要上大学的首要原因，令学生们在毕业前不知道自己将来可能的收入的情况尤其令人惊讶。洛杉矶的加利福尼亚大学-渐进地“了解有关我感兴趣的事情的更多信息”-84％的人说，在财​​务上非常富裕对他们来说非常重要或必不可少。

But when asked why they attended particular colleges, only about half said it was because graduates of those schools got good jobs.

但是当被问及为什么他们要上特定的大学时，只有大约一半的人说这是因为这些学校的毕业生找到了好工作。

“Higher education is a really stubborn and inelastic institution,” said Rachel Baker, a professor of education at the University of California at Irvine, and one of the researchers who surveyed those community college students. “Prestige continues to matter, and students pick schools based on what’s local or those that have name recognition or because of the sports teams. People are making decisions that don’t align with a — quote, unquote — rational choice.”

加州大学欧文分校的教育学教授雷切尔·贝克（Rachel Baker）说：“高等教育是一个真正的固执和缺乏弹性的机构。” “声望仍然很重要，学生们会根据当地情况，具有知名度的学校或由于运动队而选择学校。人们在做出与报价（不报价）-理性选择不符的决策。

Knowing the long-term financial returns of a college education is about more than picking the most lucrative major. It’s also about knowing when the money borrowed for an education could exceed the income from a job to which it leads.

了解大学教育的长期财务回报远不只是选择最赚钱的专业。这也与知道为某项教育借的钱何时可能超过其所从事的工作的收入有关。

The Texas Public Policy Foundation, using College Scorecard data, found nearly 60 college and university programs in Texas for which students borrowed more than they earned in their first year after graduation — $34,700 in debt for a degree in communication and media studies that paid $22,000, for example; $31,000 for a degree in sociology that paid $24,700; and $33,906 for a degree in journalism that paid $20,900.

得克萨斯州公共政策基金会使用大学记分卡数据，在德克萨斯州发现了近60所大学课程，这些学生所借的贷款额比毕业后第一年的收入还多-负债34700美元，获得传播和媒体研究学位，支付了22,000美元，例如;社会学学位的学费为31,000美元，支付的学费为24,700美元；以及$ 33,906的新闻学位费用$ 20,900。

That’s why lenders to students scrutinize expected career payoffs, said Jeannie Tarkenton, co-founder and CEO of the private student loan company Funding U — not to bankroll only high earners, but to make sure the debt-to-earnings ratio makes sense.

私人学生贷款公司Funding U的联合创始人兼首席执行官让妮·塔肯顿（Jeannie Tarkenton）表示，这就是为什么放贷方审查学生的预期职业收益的原因-不仅要为高收入者提供资金，还要确保债务与收入之比有意义。

“We believe the world needs as many social workers as chemical engineers,” Tarkenton said. “But people need to understand the value of what they’re getting. Will you be able to eat? Will you be able to pay rent?”

塔肯顿说：“我们相信世界需要与化学工程师一样多的社会工作者。” “但是人们需要了解他们所获得的价值。你能吃饭吗？您可以支付租金吗？”

There are suggestions that students and their parents may now become more hard-nosed when selecting a major and a college, given the renewed focus this year on the cost versus the benefits of a higher education. Students have become much more serious in the pandemic about getting training that leads directly to jobs, at least in the near term, a survey by the Strada Center for Education Consumer Insights found.

有建议认为，鉴于今年重新将重点放在成本与高等教育的收益上，学生及其父母现在在选择专业和大学时可能会变得更加刻板。 Strada教育消费者洞见中心的一项调查发现，在流行病中，学生变得越来越认真，至少直接在短期内获得直接导致工作的培训。

“Consumer preferences have changed considerably since covid,” said Carol D’Amico, an executive vice president at Strada and former assistant U.S. secretary of education. Now more than in the past, she said, education “has to be relevant to a career path.”

Strada执行副总裁兼前美国教育部长助理Carol D’Amico说：“自从合作以来，消费者的偏好已经发生了很大变化。”她说，现在比过去更多了，教育“必须与职业道路有关。”

Even before this year, D’Amico said, people were changing the way they viewed college. A few years ago, “no one was even talking about students as consumers. Consumer information wasn’t part of the lingo. It has moved a lot.”

达米科说，甚至在今年之前，人们还在改变他们看大学的方式。几年前，“甚至没有人把学生当成消费者。消费者信息不是术语的一部分。它已经移动了很多。”

Texas legislators have required, starting this year, that the online form used by applicants to Texas public universities include prominent links to employment rates and some wage information. West Virginia lawmakers have ordered that, starting next fall, job demand and wage data be collected and shown to every high school student.

从今年开始，得克萨斯州立法者就要求德克萨斯州公立大学的申请人使用的在线表格必须包含指向就业率和一些工资信息的重要链接。西维吉尼亚州议员已下令，从明年秋天开始，收集工作需求和工资数据，并显示给每位高中生。

In Virginia, which already reports postgraduate earnings data, “I see growth in the analytics of the website. The emails I get asking for assistance and guidance are more frequent on these topics than they were in the past,” said Tod Massa, director of policy analytics for the State Council of Higher Education for Virginia.

在已经报告了研究生收入数据的弗吉尼亚州，“我看到了网站分析的增长。与过去相比，我收到的寻求帮助和指导的电子邮件比过去更加频繁。”弗吉尼亚州高等教育理事会政策分析主管Tod Massa说。

“My interest is in helping people make informed decisions,” Massa said. “But people have to be willing to make an informed decision, and they have to be willing to do the work, to find the information and to use it.”

“我的兴趣是帮助人们做出明智的决定，”马萨说。 “但是人们必须愿意做出明智的决定，他们也必须愿意做工作，查找信息并使用信息。”

Massa isn’t sure that, after the pandemic, there will be a rush to major in the fields with the highest pay. But he thinks people will begin to notice that Americans who have kept their jobs were disproportionately those with college educations.

Massa不确定，在大流行之后，是否会急着进入薪酬最高的领域。但他认为人们会开始注意到，保持工作的美国人与受过大学教育的人不成比例。

“They’re going to look around and say, ‘Wait a minute, the people who lost their jobs and were out of work, they were in jobs that didn’t require a college degree,’ ” he said. “That will change the discussion: ‘Am I going to be able to acquire that degree that has that stability?’ Is it the amount of money that matters or the ability to weather bad times?”

他说：“他们会四处看看，‘等一下，失去工作而失业的人，他们所从事的工作不需要大学学位，’” “这将改变讨论：‘我将能够获得具有那种稳定性的学位吗？’是重要的钱数还是度过难关的能力？”

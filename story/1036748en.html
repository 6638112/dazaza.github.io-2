The Pasta King Trusted Us–On Our Honor
面食王信任我们-以我们的荣誉

2020-11-27 02:11:29

Inside the spacious kitchen shed at his Cotati home where he made his famous pasta, Art Ibleto taped a sign to the large industrial refrigerator:

Art Ibleto在他的Cotati家中宽敞的厨房棚子里制作了他著名的面食，Art Ibleto在大工业冰箱上贴了一个标牌：

WE TRUST YOU. PLEASE TAKE WHAT YOU NEED AND LEAVE THE MONEY ON THE TABLE. —PASTA KING.

我们信任您。请携带您需要的东西，把钱留在桌子上。 —意大利面

For drivers who’d notice the “Pasta King” sign from the rural Sonoma County road and pull into in Ibleto’s driveway, the door to the kitchen was always unlocked. The fridge was always full of penne, marinara, pesto and lasagna. And when Ibleto wasn’t around to take people’s money, the desk was nearly always covered with folds of cash next to the yellow touch-tone landline phone and old Rolodex, accompanied by notes of appreciation.

对于那些注意到来自索诺玛县乡村道路的“ Pasta King”标志并驶入Ibleto车道的驾驶员，通往厨房的门始终未锁。冰箱里总是装满通心粉，marinara，香蒜酱和烤宽面条。而且，当Ibleto没要收钱时，桌子旁几乎总是堆满现金，旁边是黄色的按键式座机电话和旧的Rolodex，并附有感谢信。

Ibleto, who died Tuesday morning at the age of 94, was the kind of person for whom this honor system was natural, instead of novel. For all of Ibleto’s philanthropy and civic service to the Sonoma County community he adopted as a young immigrant from Italy, it’s this fact that sticks with many of the Pasta King’s fans most: he trusted you.

伊布尔托（Ibleto）于周二早晨去世，享年94岁。对于Ibleto作为索诺玛县社区的所有慈善事业和公民服务，他从意大利作为年轻移民就采用了这一事实，这是最受Pasta King粉丝欢迎的事实：他信任您。

The word “iconic” is overused these days. But Ibleto was, without question, a Sonoma County icon. The man was everywhere—at the Sonoma County Fairgrounds where he built his “ Spaghetti Palace” in the early 1970s; at Santa Rosa’s Wednesday Night Market; at all manner of charity feeds and bakes and luncheons and parties, many of which he donated his time and food; at special events, celebrations, weddings and funerals; and back at his home kitchen in Cotati, where he’d greet visitors in a thick Italian accent: “Ciao bella! You like pasta? Red or green?”

如今，“图标”一词已被滥用。但是毫无疑问，伊布莱托是索诺玛县的标志。这个人无处不在-在索诺玛县集市（Sonoma County Fairgrounds），他在1970年代初在那里建造了他的“意大利面条宫”。在圣罗莎（Santa Rosa）的星期三夜市；各种形式的慈善活动，烘烤，午餐和聚会，其中许多是他的时间和食物。在特别活动，庆典，婚礼和葬礼上；然后回到他在科塔蒂（Cotati）的家庭厨房，他用浓重的意大利口音向游客打招呼：你喜欢意大利面吗？红色还是绿色？”

Ibleto’s life wasn’t always so public. As a teenager in Italy drafted into Mussolini’s army, he escaped and joined the resistance forces as an underground freedom fighter, planting explosives on roads and railroads to thwart fascism’s spread across Europe. At 22, he immigrated to the United States and settled in Petaluma, trusting not only the people in his new chosen home, but in hard work, common sense and a we’re-all-in-this-together belief.

Ibleto的生活并不总是那么公开。当意大利的一名少年被选入墨索里尼的军队时，他逃脱并加入抵抗力量，成为地下自由战士，在公路和铁路上植入炸药，以阻止法西斯主义在欧洲的蔓延。 22岁时，他移民到美国并定居在Petaluma，不仅相信他新选择的家中的人们，而且相信他们的辛勤工作，常识和我们共同的信念。

Over at the Press Democrat, Ibleto’s longtime friend and biographer Chris Smith tells the details of the Pasta King’s long life, including his business ventures and philanthropy. It’s worth a read.

在新闻民主党中，伊布尔托的长期朋友兼传记作家克里斯·史密斯（Chris Smith）讲述了意大利面王（Pasta King）长寿的细节，包括他的生意和慈善事业。值得一读。

But Ibleto even touched those who hadn’t the faintest awareness of his stature in the community. Often, he ignored his own honor system and simply gave pasta away for free—to those down on their luck, or to people he thought looked too thin—a gesture that was rarely forgotten.

但是Ibleto甚至感动了那些对他在社区中的地位最不了解的人。通常，他会忽略自己的荣誉制度，而只是免费将意大利面送给那些运气不好的人或他认为很瘦的人，这种手势很少被遗忘。

The whole reason I’m writing this, probably, is because I was one of those people once, a skinny kid without the $6 for a plate of pasta at Santa Rosa’s Wednesday Night Market. Later, when my wife and I got married, it only made sense to hire the Pasta King to cater our wedding; even later, when we had a daughter, we brought her by Ibleto’s place so he could see the results.

我写这篇文章的全部原因可能是因为我曾经是其中一个人，一个瘦小的孩子，在圣罗莎（Santa Rosa）的星期三夜市上没有一盘面食的6美元。后来，当我和妻子结婚时，只有聘请意大利面王来满足我们的婚礼才有意义；甚至以后，当我们有一个女儿时，我们把她带到了Ibleto的住所，这样他就可以看到结果。

And of course, there was his loud, deep laugh. When, a few years back, I found myself without the proper change to leave on his old wooden desk for some lasagna, I knew I couldn’t cheat his trust. So I wrote him a letter when I got home and enclosed the missing amount, two quarters, taped to the letter, with two stamps to cover the extra weight.

当然，还有他的大声，深刻的笑声。几年前，当我发现自己没有适当的改动就留在他的旧木桌上吃烤宽面条时，我知道我无法欺骗他的信任。因此，我到家后给他写了一封信，并把遗失的部分用胶带粘贴在信件上，粘贴了两个季度，并贴有两个邮票以盖住多余的重量。

Ibleto never put the quarters in his till. Instead, he taped the letter to his refrigerator, along with the quarters and the stamps, in what many presumed was a display of the general public’s allegiance to the honor system.

Ibleto从来没有把宿舍放到他的耕作里。取而代之的是，他将这封信以及宿舍和邮票贴在冰箱上，许多人以为这是对公众对荣誉制度的忠诚的展示。

I learned the real reason later, when a friend reported stopping in and saying he knew the sender of the quarters. Ibleto pointed to the stamps, amused.

后来，我得知了真正的原因，当时有朋友报告说要停下来，说他知道宿舍的寄件人。 Ibleto笑了笑，指着邮票。

“He your friend? You see what he did?!” Ibleto said. “He spent 88 cents to send me 50 cents! Maybe I have a bridge to sell him!”

“他是你的朋友吗？你看他做了什么？！”伊布尔托说。他花了88美分送给我50美分！也许我有一座桥可以卖给他！”

And then, after a long, heavy laugh, he looked up and said, “So. You like pasta? Red or green?”

然后，经过漫长而沉重的笑声，他抬起头说：你喜欢意大利面吗？红色还是绿色？”

And that was the Pasta King. It truly won't be the same without his huge, gracious presence here in Sonoma County.

那就是面食王。没有他在索诺玛县的巨大而亲切的光临，这真的不会是一样的。

Clubhouse Criticized Over User Privacy Policies
会所批评用户隐私政策

2021-02-17 18:55:28

Did you get an invitation to join Clubhouse, the invite-only app that seemingly everyone is talking about? Before you decide to join the cool kids club, you might want to consider who will see you there, even if you take every available measure to keep your contacts private.

您是否收到邀请加入Clubhouse的邀请，Clubhouse是似乎只有每个人都在谈论的仅受邀请的应用程序？在决定加入酷儿童俱乐部之前，您可能需要考虑谁会在那里看到您，即使您已采取一切可行的措施将您的联系人保密。

Clubhouse is an audio-based app (still in beta) that allows users to create and join rooms where all kinds of topics are discussed. You can join speaking events or you can make your own room to chat. There’s also a big social component: You follow people, people follow you, and Clubhouse very much encourages these networks to form and grow. And as OneZero’s Will Oremus reported on Thursday, that’s led to a few privacy issues some Clubhouse users didn’t expect and can’t avoid, given the app’s lack of privacy controls or information about them.

Clubhouse是一个基于音频的应用程序（仍处于beta版），允许用户创建和加入讨论各种主题的会议室。您可以参加演讲活动，也可以自己聊天。还有一个很大的社会组成部分：您追随人们，人们追随您，而Clubhouse极大地鼓励了这些网络的形成和成长。而且，正如OneZero的Will Oremus在周四报道的那样，由于该应用缺乏隐私控制或有关他们的信息，这导致了一些Clubhouse用户没有想到并且无法避免的一些隐私问题。

The primary piece of Clubhouse’s user recommendation engine relies on access to your contacts. You actually can’t invite anyone else to the platform if you don’t grant it. If you do give the app access to your contacts, Clubhouse will show you everyone on your contact list who is also on Clubhouse. It will also urge you to invite those who aren’t and let you know as soon as someone in your contacts has joined so you can say hello to them. This is all pretty standard for an app trying to gain new users.

Clubhouse的用户推荐引擎的主要部分取决于对您联系人的访问。如果您未授予该平台，则实际上您无法邀请其他任何人加入该平台。如果您确实允许该应用访问您的联系人，则Clubhouse会向您显示联系人列表中同时也在Clubhouse上的所有人。它还会敦促您邀请没有邀请的人加入，并在联系人中的某人加入后立即通知您，以便您可以向他们问好。对于试图吸引新用户的应用程序来说，这完全是标准。

But what if you didn’t give Clubhouse access to your contacts, specifically because you didn’t want all or any of them to know you were there? I regret to inform you that Clubhouse has made it possible for them to know anyway, encourages them to follow you, and there isn’t much you can do about it. When I joined, I didn’t give Clubhouse access to my contacts; as has been my policy since childhood, only I may decide who enters my clubhouse. Nevertheless, a few minutes later, I had a bunch of followers from my contacts. Even worse: I got followers who weren’t in my contacts at all — but I was in theirs.

但是，如果您不授予Clubhouse访问您的联系人的权限，特别是因为您不希望所有或任何一个人知道您在那里，该怎么办？很遗憾地通知您，Clubhouse使他们无论如何都能知道，鼓励他们关注您，而您对此无能为力。加入后，我没有授予Clubhouse访问我的联系人的权限；从孩提时代起，我就一直采取政策，只有我才能决定谁进入我的俱乐部。然而，几分钟后，我的联络人吸引了一大批追随者。更糟糕的是：我的追随者根本不在我的联系人中，但我在他们的联系人中。

It turns out that your privacy on Clubhouse depends not just on what you do but also on what those who have your information in their contacts do. For now, you can only get invited to Clubhouse through your phone number, which is attached to your account and can’t be removed. So if someone has your phone number in their contacts, and they’ve given Clubhouse access to those contacts, they’ll get a notification when you join the app and a recommendation to follow you.

事实证明，您在Clubhouse上的隐私不仅取决于您所做的事情，还取决于联系人中拥有您信息的人的所作所为。目前，您只能通过您电话中的电话号码被邀请加入Clubhouse，该电话号码无法删除。因此，如果某人的联系人中有您的电话号码，并且授予了Clubhouse访问这些联系人的权限，那么当您加入该应用时，他们会收到通知，并会推荐您。

Clubhouse also encourages you to connect your Twitter and Instagram accounts, which could be another way for you to find people (or people to find you). Clubhouse did not respond to a request for comment on if or how the app does this, but it’s something to consider before you connect your social media accounts.

Clubhouse还鼓励您连接自己的Twitter和Instagram帐户，这可能是您找到人（或人们找到您）的另一种方式。 Clubhouse并未回应有关该应用程序是否执行此操作或如何执行此操作的评论请求，但是在您连接社交媒体帐户之前，需要考虑一下。

To be clear, Clubhouse isn’t the only app that is overly aggressive with its connection recommendations. Plenty of social media platforms use algorithms that take various factors into account, including your personal data and your contacts, to suggest people you should friend or follow. Those algorithms are very powerful, and yet somehow not powerful enough to avoid making recommendations that are creepy.

需要明确的是，Clubhouse并不是唯一对连接建议过于激进的应用。许多社交媒体平台使用的算法会考虑各种因素，包括您的个人数据和联系人，以建议您应该成为朋友或关注的对象。这些算法非常强大，但以某种方式还不够强大，无法避免提出令人毛骨悚然的建议。

Remember all those stories about Facebook’s “People You May Know” feature that recommended psychiatrists to their patients or random people they passed by on the street to each other? Facebook admitted that it recommended people based on their contacts, even if they weren’t in yours. But Facebook, which is hardly a shining beacon of best practices when it comes to privacy, now has a bunch of settings and ways to keep your profile reasonably locked down if you want it to be. You also don’t have to link your phone number to your Facebook profile.

还记得有关Facebook的“您可能认识的人”功能的所有那些故事，这些故事向他们的病人或在街上经过的随机人推荐精神科医生吗？ Facebook承认，即使他们不在您的身边，它也会根据他们的联系人推荐他们。但是，在隐私保护方面，Facebook并不是最佳实践的闪亮灯塔，现在，Facebook提供了许多设置和方法，可以根据需要合理地锁定您的个人资料。您也不必将电话号码链接到您的Facebook个人资料。

Such privacy options don’t currently exist on Clubhouse. In fact, the only mention of privacy I could find on the app was a link to its privacy policy, which, for most of Thursday, looked like this:

这样的隐私选项目前在Clubhouse上尚不存在。实际上，我只能在该应用程序上找到有关隐私的内容，它是其隐私政策的链接，在整个星期四的大部分时间里，该政策如下所示：

Clubhouse’s settings page appears to have some glitches. Sometimes the link led to a redirect to a real privacy policy, but most of the time it didn’t. This was also true for the What’s New, FAQ, Community Guidelines, and Terms of Service. (Clubhouse also didn’t respond to a request for comment on what was going on here.)

俱乐部会所的设置页面似乎存在一些故障。有时，该链接导致重定向到真实的隐私权政策，但大多数情况下并没有。新增功能，常见问题解答，社区准则和服务条款也是如此。 （俱乐部会所也未对此处的评论发表评论。）

While the app also has some measures to prevent abuse — the ability to block users, to make rooms private, and to report incidents — Clubhouse has been criticized for having poor or inadequate moderation tools that allow misinformation and hate speech to spread. The company says it’s working on improving them. Meanwhile, you can’t currently report potential violations of those terms without handing over your email address.

虽然该应用程序还具有一些防止滥用的措施-阻止用户，设置房间私密性和报告事件的能力-但Clubhouse被批评为使用不良或不适当的审核工具，允许错误信息和仇恨言论传播。该公司表示正在努力改善它们。同时，如果不移交您的电子邮件地址，您目前无法举报可能违反这些条款的行为。

It’s not clear why Clubhouse doesn’t have better options for users to manage their privacy or more information for users about how their data might be used or linked to them. The company is reportedly operating with a small staff, but it also has millions of users and millions of dollars worth of funding from major Silicon Valley venture capital firms, including Andreessen Horowitz, and a valuation of $1 billion. It’s not the first well-funded social media app to push the boundaries of data privacy. But you’d at least think Clubhouse would have learned from the unicorns that came before it.

目前尚不清楚为什么Clubhouse没有更好的选择供用户管理其隐私，或者没有向用户提供更多有关如何使用或链接其数据的信息。据报道，该公司的员工人数很少，但它也拥有数百万用户，并从包括安德森·霍洛维茨（Andreessen Horowitz）在内的硅谷主要风险投资公司获得数百万美元的资金，估值为10亿美元。这不是第一个资金雄厚的社交媒体应用程序，它突破了数据隐私的界限。但是，您至少会认为Clubhouse应该从之前的独角兽那里学到东西。

Seattle Windshield Pitting Epidemic
西雅图挡风玻璃小流行病

2020-11-23 08:32:40

Jump to navigation Jump to search The Seattle windshield pitting epidemic is a phenomenon which affected Bellingham, Seattle, and other communities of Washington state in April, 1954; it is considered an example of a mass delusion. [1] It was characterized by widespread observation of previously unnoticed windshield holes, pits and dings, leading residents to believe that a common causative agent was at work. It was originally thought to be the work of vandals but the rate of pitting was so great that residents began to attribute it to everything from sand flea eggs to nuclear bomb testing.

跳转到导航跳转搜索1954年4月，西雅图的挡风玻璃点蚀流行病影响了贝灵汉，西雅图和华盛顿州的其他社区。它被认为是大规模妄想的一个例子。 [1]它的特点是广泛观察到以前未注意到的挡风玻璃孔，凹坑和叮当声，使居民相信一种常见的致病因素正在起作用。它最初被认为是破坏者的工作，但点蚀的速度是如此之快，以至于居民开始将其归因于从跳蚤卵到核弹测试的一切事物。

Originating in Bellingham in March, police initially believed the work to be vandals using BB guns. However the pitting was soon observed in the nearby towns of Sedro Woolley and Mount Vernon and by mid-April, appeared to have spread to the town of Anacortes on Fidalgo Island.

警方三月份发源于贝灵汉，最初认为该工作是使用BB枪的破坏者。但是，很快在附近的塞德罗·伍利（Sedro Woolley）和弗农山（Mount Vernon）镇上发现了麻点，到4月中旬，这种麻点似乎已经蔓延到了菲达尔戈岛（Fidalgo Island）上的阿纳科尔特斯（Anacortes）镇。

Within a week, the news and the so-called "pitting epidemic" had reached metropolitan Seattle. As the newspapers began to feature the story, more and more reports of pitting were called in. Motorists began stopping police cars to report damage. Car lots and parking garages reported particularly severe attacks.

一周之内，这一消息和所谓的“点点流行病”就传到了西雅图。随着报纸开始报道这个故事，越来越多的关于轮胎腐蚀的报道被召唤出来。驾驶者开始停下警车报告损坏情况。停车场和车库报告了特别严重的袭击。

Some thought that a new million-watt radio transmitter at nearby Jim Creek Naval Radio Station was producing waves that caused physical oscillations in glass;

有人认为附近的吉姆克里克海军广播电台（Jim Creek Naval Radio Station）有一台新的百万瓦特无线电发射机正在产生波，从而引起玻璃的物理振荡。

Some reported seeing glass bubbles form right before their eyes, believing it to be the work of sand fleas.

一些人报告说在他们眼前就形成了玻璃气泡，认为这是跳蚤的产物。

By April 15, close to 3,000 windshields had been reported as affected. Mayor Allan Pomeroy contacted Washington Governor Arthur B. Langlie, then President Dwight D. Eisenhower asking for assistance. [2]

到4月15日，据报道有近3,000个挡风玻璃受到影响。市长艾伦·波默罗伊（Allan Pomeroy）与华盛顿州长亚瑟·B·兰格里（Arthur B.Langlie），然后是德怀特·D·艾森豪威尔总统联系，寻求援助。 [2]

Finally, Sergeant Max Allison of the Seattle police crime laboratory stated that the pitting reports consisted of "5 per cent hoodlum-ism, and 95 per cent public hysteria." By April 17, the pitting suddenly stopped.

最后，西雅图警察犯罪实验室的麦克斯·艾里森中士说，进站报告包括“ 5％的流氓主义和95％的公众歇斯底里”。到4月17日，麻点突然停止了。

The "Seattle Windshield Pitting Epidemic" as it is called has become a textbook case of collective delusion (not "mass hysteria" as reported). [3] Although natural windshield pitting had been going on for some time, it was only when the media called public attention to it that people actually looked at their windshields and saw damage they had never noticed before.

所谓的“西雅图挡风玻璃流行病”已成为集体妄想的教科书案例（据报道并非“大众歇斯底里”）。 [3]尽管自然的挡风玻璃点蚀现象已经持续了一段时间，但直到媒体呼吁公众注意时，人们才真正地看着自己的挡风玻璃并看到了他们从未注意到的损坏。

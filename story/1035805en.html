Why Amazon's Echo Shines an Ominous Red Light When Its Microphone is Muted
为什么亚马逊的Echo麦克风静音时会发出不祥的红光

2020-11-22 04:43:24

With Black Friday nearly upon us, you will no doubt see a slew of deals on a product that’s grown to be synonymous with Amazon itself: the Echo voice assistant speaker. Designed internally by Amazon’s secretive Lab126 hardware team, the first Echo launched in 2014 as a hard, black cylinder. Over the years, it’s spawned several iterations, including the smaller Echo Dot hockey pucks and screen-wielding Echo Shows, and each time, Amazon has expanded its base. In 2019, the company announced it had sold 100 million Alexa-powered devices to date.

随着黑色星期五的临近，毫无疑问，您将看到与亚马逊本身同义的产品的大量交易：Echo语音助手扬声器。由亚马逊秘密的Lab126硬件团队内部设计，2014年推出的第一款Echo是坚固的黑色圆柱体。多年来，它产生了许多迭代，包括较小的Echo Dot曲棍球冰球和挥舞着屏幕的Echo Show，并且每次，Amazon都会扩大其基础。在2019年，该公司宣布迄今为止已经售出了1亿台支持Alexa的设备。

But the fourth-generation Echo, launched earlier this year, has evolved into something with little to no resemblance to the original: not a plastic cylinder, but a cloth-wrapped sphere.

但是，今年初推出的第四代Echo，已经演变成与原始版本几乎没有相似之处：不是塑料圆筒，而是布包裹的球体。

Why a sphere, and why now? We explored that question in an exclusive interview with members of Amazon’s own design and product team.

为什么是球体，为什么现在？我们在与亚马逊自己的设计和产品团队的成员进行的独家采访中探讨了这个问题。

The short answer is that the sphere solved a lot of practical issues that the team faced when creating the new Echo. The longer answer is a story of Amazon’s unrelenting consumer testing, and an ever-changing concept of where and how technology fits inside our homes.

简短的答案是，该领域解决了团队在创建新Echo时面临的许多实际问题。更长的答案是关于亚马逊不断进行的消费者测试的故事，以及关于技术如何以及如何在我们的家庭中使用的不断变化的概念。

A Pringles can. That’s how Miriam Daniel, vice president of Echo and Alexa devices at Amazon, remembers the shape of the original Echo. The device was radical for its time. Of course we had Siri on iPhones, but there was no known object in our living rooms that we talked to on a regular basis. So the team anchored it in something more familiar: A speaker.

品客可以。这就是亚马逊Echo和Alexa设备副总裁Miriam Daniel记住原始Echo形状的方式。该设备在当时非常激进。当然，我们在iPhone上安装了Siri，但我们的客厅中没有定期与之交谈的已知物体。因此，团队将其锚定在更熟悉的事物中：发言人。

“At the time we also looked at a lot of form factors. The leading use case was music,” says Daniel. “If you want someone to understand this device and what it can do, you have to make something familiar to them. So making a speaker . . . made a lot of sense.”

“当时，我们还研究了许多外形尺寸。最主要的用例是音乐。”丹尼尔说。 “如果您希望某人了解此设备及其功能，则必须使他们熟悉一些东西。所以做个演讲者。 。 。很有道理。”

That explains the Echo’s large grill holes, to allow sound in and out, but what about the cylindrical shape?

这就解释了Echo的大烧烤孔，可以让声音进出，但是圆柱形状呢？

“Pure form follows function,” says Chris Green, vice president of industrial design at Lab126. The earliest Echo had seven microphones inside to catch someone’s speech. These seven microphones had to be physically separated from the speaker itself, or the device couldn’t hear someone talking over music. So mics point up out of the top of the device, while the speaker itself points down.

Lab126工业设计副总裁Chris Green说：“纯粹的形式遵循功能。”最早的Echo里面装有七个麦克风，可以听某人讲话。这七个麦克风必须与扬声器本身分开，否则设备听不到有人在听音乐。因此，麦克风指向设备顶部上方，而扬声器本身指向下方。

Over the past several years, however, Amazon has gotten better at capturing speech using fewer microphones. Now, Echoes need just two to four as opposed to seven.

但是，在过去的几年中，亚马逊在使用更少的麦克风捕获语音方面变得更好。现在，回声只需要2到4，而不是7。

“Technology has moved on,” says Green. “You can’t ignore that.”

格林说：“技术进步了。” “你不能忽略这一点。”

As the company approached its fourth generation of Echoes, designers had all sorts of ideas as to how the devices could evolve. But instead of just comparing these notes in a lab, they brought sketches and prototypes to a long-standing pool of more than 100 customers from across the globe, who offer early and repeated feedback, scoring what they see. This process is typical for Amazon product design.

当公司迈向第四代Echoes时，设计师对设备如何发展产生了各种各样的想法。但是，他们不只是在实验室中比较这些注释，而是将草图和原型带到了来自全球100多个客户的长期库中，他们提供了早期和反复的反馈，对他们的见解进行评分。此过程是Amazon产品设计的典型过程。

“We ask them questions, show concepts, roll in feedback, [iterate], and as time goes forward we make sure scores go up rather than down,” says Green. “It sounds silly, but you start at a utopian dream. [The Echo] is going to do this [or this], but it costs a lot of money, so you want to make sure you make the right tradeoffs as you move forward.”

格林说：“我们问他们问题，展示概念，反馈意见，反复重申，随着时间的推移，我们确保分数提高而不是降低。” “这听起来很愚蠢，但是您从一个乌托邦式的梦想开始[Echo]将要执行此操作，但它会花费很多钱，因此，您需要确保在前进时做出正确的权衡。”

Working closely with customers, Amazon was aware that the whole idea of a media cabinet, which holds your A/V devices and speakers, was eroding. Echoes are finding their way into the many rooms of people’s homes.

与客户紧密合作，亚马逊意识到容纳您的A / V设备和扬声器的媒体柜的整个构想正在逐渐消失。回声正在进入人们房屋的许多房间。

“If you put speakers next to a TV, people want them to look like the TV. They want holistic experience,” says Green. And the first-generation Echo was designed with a glossy black finish that’s comfortable inside an A/V system. “But as soon as you move those speakers away from the TV . . .[consumers] want them to blend into the personal items beside the bed or the kitchen bowl and spoons.”

“如果您将扬声器放在电视旁，人们会希望它们看起来像电视。他们想要整体体验。”格林说。第一代Echo的设计采用了光滑的黑色表面，可在A / V系统中舒适地使用。 “但是只要您将这些扬声器从电视上移开，就可以了。 。 。[消费者]希望他们将它们混入床旁或厨房的碗和勺子旁边的个人物品中。”

“We now knew that people put them everywhere, but we also knew we needed a new shape, to support a new audio technology,” Green concludes.

“我们现在知道人们将它们无处不在，但是我们也知道我们需要一种新的外形来支持新的音频技术，”格林总结道。

In this new arrangement, two tweeters (known for crisp highs) point right at the listener, and a subwoofer (known for bass) points toward the ceiling.

在这种新的布置中，两个高音扬声器（以高音高音为特点）指向听众，而低音炮（以低音为声）指向天花板。

“Think of it as three circles floating in space,” says Green. “And the natural form for those is basically a sphere.” Perhaps that idea doesn’t make a lot of sense until you actually conceptualize how circles live in 3D. In the above image, you can see how easily flat circles wrap their way around a ball.

格林说：“把它想象成在太空中漂浮的三个圆圈。” “这些的自然形式基本上是一个球体。”在您真正概念化圆圈在3D中的生活之前，这个想法可能没有多大意义。在上图中，您可以看到平坦的圆圈如何轻松地环绕球。

The sphere had other advantages too. A sphere has the lowest surface-to-volume ratio of any shape, meaning a spherical speaker could be small while packing in a lot of tech. Everyone inside Amazon began believing in the sphere, but there was a catch: brand equity. “You have this icon of a cylinder, but you want push in this new tech . . . and get it to a price point adopted by as many people as possible,” says Green. “We realized we had to make a hard choice.”

这个领域也有其他优势。球形具有任何形状中最低的表面积与体积之比，这意味着球形扬声器在包装很多技术时可能很小。亚马逊内部的每个人都开始相信这一领域，但有一个陷阱：品牌资产。 “您具有气缸图标，但您想推动这项新技术。 。 。并使其价格达到尽可能多的人接受的水平。”格林说。 “我们意识到我们必须做出艰难的选择。”

Consumer testing made that choice easier, though. Testers liked the sphere, but designers had one last problem to overcome. About 96% of all Echos are placed against a wall. That’s perhaps obvious, as Echos need to be plugged in. But the other 4-5% are generally on kitchen islands, where they occupy a central part of a room.

消费者测试使选择更加容易。测试人员喜欢这个领域，但是设计师要解决的最后一个问题。所有回声中约有96％靠墙放置。这很明显，因为需要插入Echos。但是其他4-5％通常在厨房岛上，它们占据了房间的中央部分。

To sound optimal, the new tweeter technology needs to be aimed directly at someone—or, at a minimum, not facing the opposite direction. A sphere doesn’t have a natural front, side, or back, however. “Initially, our assumption was a cable out the back would give it a front, but in a few situations it needed a tiny tweak,” says Green. So instead of going with a perfect sphere, as they’d planned, Amazon added a diagonal bias to the base. That bias cut gives it a directionality so the consumer naturally places it in a way that’s most conducive to its sound.

为了达到最佳效果，新的高音扬声器技术必须直接针对某人，或者至少不面向相反的方向。但是，球体没有自然的正面，侧面或背面。格林说：“起初，我们的假设是从后面引出一条电缆就可以使其向前，但在某些情况下，需要进行一些细微的调整。”因此，亚马逊并没有像他们计划的那样发展完美，而是在基础上增加了对角线偏差。偏斜的切割为它提供了方向性，因此消费者可以自然地以最有利于声音的方式放置它。

Anyone following smart speakers will notice that Apple also debuted a spherical assistant this year with its HomePod Mini. So I asked Green, was this just a case of convergent evolution? Or was some other trend driving both companies to a sphere?

任何使用智能扬声器的人都会注意到，苹果今年还通过其HomePod Mini首次推出了球形助手。所以我问格林，这仅仅是融合进化的例子吗？还是其他趋势将两家公司推向了一个新的领域？

“Zeitgeist is real. It just is,” laughs Green. “We have [superhero] TV shows like The Boys and Umbrella Academy come out at the same time.” He says he cannot speak to Apple’s design process but notes that the company is using a different speaker arrangement than Amazon. Apple did not respond to a request for comment for this story.

“时代精神是真实的。就是这样，”格林笑道。 “我们有[超级英雄]电视节目同时出现，例如The Boys和Umbrella Academy。”他说他无法谈及苹果的设计过程，但指出该公司使用的扬声器布置与亚马逊不同。苹果没有回应对此故事的置评请求。

The other most significant change to the spherical Echo was to the light pipe—that glowing ring on top of the Echo that signals it’s talking or thinking. For the fourth generation, that light pipe has been moved to the bottom of the device, to reflect off tables or countertops, and provide a more ambient lighting experience that blends into one’s environment—with a catch.

球形回声的另一个最重要的变化是导光管，即回声顶部的发光环，表明它在说话或思考。对于第四代产品，该光导管已移至设备的底部，以反射到桌子或台面上，并提供更多的环境照明体验，并与人为环境融为一体。

Once you hit the privacy button on your Echo, deafening it from hearing your speech, the ring glows a DEFCON 2 red until you unmute it. (Note: Google uses an orange to convey mute for its Assistant, as does Sony’s new PS5 controller that has a mic built in.) It’s not just overt; it’s borderline warlike, adding a Red October glow to your space. Echos have always glowed red when muted. Now your environment does, too.

一旦您按下Echo上的“隐私”按钮，使它听不到语音的声音，它就会发出DEFCON 2红色的光芒，直到您将其取消静音为止。 （注意：Google使用橙色来传达其Assistant的静音，内置了麦克风的Sony新款PS5控制器也是如此。）交战逼真，为您的空间增添了红色十月的光芒。当静音时，回声总是发红光。现在您的环境也可以。

When I mention this design decision, which seems to punish consumers who prefer privacy, Daniel acknowledges, but brushes off,  the criticism. “[Red] makes for a strong [statement]. There’s always a tradeoff. Is it too bright? Annoying? Too in your face?” she muses. But she argues that the greater benefit is that “it gives people a sense of comfort knowing the mic isn’t working.”

当我提到这个设计决定似乎惩罚了那些偏爱隐私的消费者时，丹尼尔承认，但是却避免了批评。 “ [红色]表示强烈的[声明]。总会有一个权衡。太亮了吗？烦人吗太在你脸上吗？”她沉思。但是她辩称，更大的好处是“知道麦克风不起作用，它使人们感到舒适。”

Now that Amazon has ditched the cylinder for the sphere, the only lingering question is whether or not Amazon will stick with spheres for years to come, or whether we’ll continue to see the company alter the product’s form.

现在，亚马逊已经抛弃了球体，剩下的唯一问题是，亚马逊是否会在未来几年内坚持使用球体，或者我们是否会继续看到该公司改变产品的形式。

“I can’t talk about what’s coming out in the future, but the hard decision, from going to a cylinder to a sphere, had to be backed up not only by technology but customer feedback. We have plenty of quotes, things like . . . people said the cylinder looks safe but the sphere looks special . . . looks from the future,” says Green. “That . . . gave us confidence to go with the sphere. Are we going to copy and paste it [next time]? We’ll go through the same process again. We’ll be very methodical on what we do next.”

“我无法谈论将来会发生什么，但是从气缸到球体的艰难决定不仅需要技术支持，还需要客户反馈。我们有很多引号，例如。 。 。人们说圆筒看起来安全，但是球形看起来很特别。 。 。展望未来。”格林说。 “那个。 。 。让我们有信心与这个领域保持一致。我们要复制并粘贴[下一次]吗？我们将再次执行相同的过程。我们将非常有条理地处理下一步的工作。”

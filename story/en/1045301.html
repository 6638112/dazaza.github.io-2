India asks Facebook's WhatsApp to withdraw privacy policy update
印度要求Facebook的WhatsApp撤回隐私政策更新

2021-01-20 14:50:11

NEW DELHI (Reuters) - India’s technology ministry has asked WhatsApp to withdraw changes to its privacy policy the messaging platform announced earlier this month, saying the new terms take away choice from Indian users.

印度技术部已要求WhatsApp撤消其消息传递平台本月早些时候宣布的隐私政策的更改，称新条款取消了印度用户的选择。

The demand creates a new headache for WhatsApp and its U.S. parent Facebook, which have placed big bets on the South Asian nation to expand their payments and other businesses.

需求给WhatsApp及其美国母公司Facebook带来了新的麻烦，后者已将大笔赌注押在了这个南亚国家上，以扩大其支付和其他业务。

“The proposed changes raise grave concerns regarding the implications for the choice and autonomy of Indian citizens,” the Ministry of Electronics and Information Technology wrote in an email to WhatsApp boss Will Cathcart dated Jan. 18.

电子和信息技术部在1月18日给WhatsApp老板Will Cathcart的电子邮件中写道：“拟议的变更引起了对印度公民选择和自治的影响的严重关切。”

“Therefore, you are called upon to withdraw the proposed changes,” the ministry wrote in the letter seen by Reuters.

该部在路透社看到的信中写道：“因此，要求您撤回拟议的变更。”

WhatsApp said in a statement it was working to address misinformation and remains available to answer any questions. “We wish to reinforce that this update does not expand our ability to share data with Facebook,” it said.

WhatsApp在一份声明中说，它正在努力解决错误信息，并且仍然可以回答任何问题。它说：“我们希望加强此更新不会扩大我们与Facebook共享数据的能力。”

California-based Facebook invested $5.7 billion last year in the digital unit of Indian conglomerate Reliance with a huge part of that aimed at drawing in tens of millions of traditional shop owners to use digital payments via WhatsApp.

去年，总部位于加利福尼亚州的Facebook向印度企业集团Reliance的数字部门投资了57亿美元，其中很大一部分旨在吸引数千万传统商店所有者通过WhatsApp使用数字支付。

With 400 million users in India, WhatsApp has big plans for the country’s growing digital payments space, including selling health insurance via partners.

WhatsApp在印度拥有4亿用户，为该国不断发展的数字支付领域制定了宏伟计划，包括通过合作伙伴出售健康保险。

Those aspirations could take a hit if Indians switch to rival messengers such as Signal and Telegram, downloads of which have surged after WhatsApp said on Jan. 4 it could share limited user data with Facebook and its group firms.

如果印度人转向诸如Signal和Telegram之类的竞争对手，这些愿望可能会受到打击。WhatsApp在1月4日表示，它可以与Facebook及其集团公司共享有限的用户数据后，其下载量激增。

It is of “great concern” that Indian users have not been given the choice to opt out of this data sharing with Facebook companies and they are being given less choice compared to the app’s European users, the tech ministry letter said.

科技部的一封信说，令人担忧的是，印度用户没有选择退出与Facebook公司共享数据的选择，与该应用程序的欧洲用户相比，他们的选择更少。

“This differential and discriminatory treatment of Indian and European users is attracting serious criticism and betrays a lack of respect for the rights and interest of Indian citizens who form a substantial portion of WhatsApp’s user base,” it said.

它说：“对印度和欧洲用户的这种区别对待和歧视对待正在引起严重的批评，并且背叛了对构成WhatsApp用户基础的印度公民的权益缺乏尊重的行为。”

The ministry asked WhatsApp to respond to 14 questions including on the categories of user data it collected, whether it profiled customers based on usage and cross-border data flows.

该部要求WhatsApp回答14个问题，包括收集的用户数据类别，是否根据使用情况和跨境数据流对客户进行了描述。

The company said last week it would delay the new policy launch to May from February, after facing criticism from users in India and elsewhere to the new terms.

该公司上周表示，在印度和其他地区的用户对新条款提出批评之后，它将把新政策从2月推迟到5月。

WhatsApp has launched a media advertising campaign in India to calm worried users. The update to the privacy policy has also resulted in two legal petitions in Indian courts.

WhatsApp已在印度发起了一项媒体广告活动，以平息担心的用户。隐私政策的更新还导致印度法院提出了两项​​法律申诉。

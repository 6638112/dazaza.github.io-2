俄勒冈州将少量海洛因和可卡因合法化

2020-11-04 13:36:14

OAKLAND, Calif. — The march to decriminalize drugs moved further across the nation on Tuesday despite continued federal prohibition.

加利福尼亚州奥克兰。-周二，尽管联邦政府继续禁止毒品合法化，但毒品合法化的游行在全国范围内进一步推进。

Oregon became the first state to decriminalize small amounts of cocaine, heroin, methamphetamine and other drugs. And in New Jersey and Arizona voters decisively passed laws legalizing recreational marijuana. Cannabis is now legal across a large bloc of states in the West — from Washington down to the Mexican border — and well beyond.

俄勒冈州成为第一个将少量可卡因、海洛因、甲基苯丙胺和其他毒品合法化的州。在新泽西州和亚利桑那州，选民果断通过了娱乐用大麻合法化的法律。现在，大麻在西方的一大批州-从华盛顿到墨西哥边境-乃至更远的地方都是合法的。

Cannabis was also on the ballot in Montana, Mississippi and South Dakota. If all of the marijuana measures pass, marijuana will be legal for medical use in three dozen states and recreational use will be allowed in 15.

在蒙大拿州、密西西比州和南达科他州的选票上也有大麻。如果所有的大麻措施都获得通过，大麻将在30多个州合法用于医疗用途，15个州将允许娱乐用途。

The Oregon measure would make possession of small amounts of what have long been considered harder drugs a violation, similar to a traffic ticket, and no longer punishable by jail time. The law would also fund drug addiction treatment from marijuana sales taxes.

俄勒冈州的这项措施将使拥有少量长期以来被认为较硬的毒品是违法的，类似于交通罚单，不再受到监禁的惩罚。这项法律还将从大麻销售税中为戒毒治疗提供资金。

“This is incredible,” said Kassandra Frederique, executive director of the advocacy group Drug Policy Alliance. “This is like taking a sledgehammer to the cornerstone of the drug war.”

倡导组织毒品政策联盟(Drug Policy Alliance)的执行董事卡桑德拉·弗雷德里克(Kassandra Frederique)说：“这简直令人难以置信。”“这就像拿大锤砸向毒品战争的基石.”

Possession of larger amounts could result in misdemeanor charges, and some cases that rise to what is considered a commercial level could still be charged as felonies.

拥有更大的金额可能会导致轻罪指控，一些上升到被认为是商业水平的案件仍可能被指控为重罪。

Ms. Frederique said passage of the measure showed that voters were eager for a new approach on drug policy to handle it as a health issue and prioritize treatment. She said she expected other states to follow suit, mentioning efforts in states such as California, Vermont and Washington.

弗雷德里克说，这项措施的通过表明，选民渴望在毒品政策上采取新的方法，将其作为一个健康问题来处理，并将治疗放在首位。她说，她预计其他州也会效仿，并提到了加州、佛蒙特州和华盛顿州等州的努力。

Separately, Oregon voters also legalized psilocybin, known as magic mushrooms, for people age 21 and older. Proponents said the move would allow the drug to be used to treat depression, anxiety and other conditions.

另外，俄勒冈州选民还将裸盖菇素(又称魔力蘑菇)合法化，供21岁及以上的人使用。支持者表示，此举将允许该药物用于治疗抑郁症、焦虑症和其他疾病。

Even in a year when the number of citizen initiatives in states across the country was sharply down from the last presidential election, the diverse slate of measures offered a chance to gauge the mood of the nation.

即使在全国各州的公民倡议数量比上次总统选举大幅下降的一年里，多样化的措施也提供了一个衡量国民情绪的机会。

In Florida, where the two presidential candidates were within a few points of each other, voters approved a pro-labor amendment to the Constitution that will raise the minimum wage incrementally to $15 an hour in 2026.

在佛罗里达州，两位总统候选人的支持率相差不到几个百分点，选民们批准了一项支持劳工的宪法修正案，该修正案将在2026年将最低工资逐步提高到每小时15美元。

Florida becomes the eighth state to enact a minimum wage of $15, according to the National Conference of State Legislatures, but the first state that Donald J. Trump carried in the 2016 presidential election. The District of Columbia has also enacted a $15 minimum wage.

根据全国州议会会议(National Conference Of State Legillatures)的数据，佛罗里达州成为第八个制定最低工资为15美元的州，但也是唐纳德·J·特朗普(Donald J.Trump)在2016年总统选举中赢得的第一个州。哥伦比亚特区也颁布了15美元的最低工资。

Florida’s measure, known as Amendment 2, earned a place on Tuesday’s ballot last December and needed at least 60 percent of the vote to pass. With 99 percent of the vote counted, the measure had slightly more than 61 percent.

佛罗里达州的这项被称为修正案2的措施在去年12月的周二投票中赢得了一席之地，需要至少60%的选票才能通过。99%的选票已经清点完毕，该法案的得票率略高于61%。

Under the measure, the state minimum wage would rise from its current hourly rate of $8.56 to $10 next September, and then increase by $1 every September through 2026. After that, annual increases would be tied to inflation.

根据这项措施，州最低工资将从目前的每小时8.56美元提高到明年9月的10美元，然后在2026年之前每年9月增加1美元。在此之后，每年的增长将与通胀挂钩。

A study by the Florida Policy Institute, a think tank backing the increase, found that the higher wage would directly benefit 2.5 million workers in the state.

支持涨薪的智库佛罗里达政策研究所(佛罗里达政策研究所)的一项研究发现，更高的工资将直接使该州250万工人受益。

The amendment was supported by unions and the Miami-Dade Democratic Party and was opposed by business organizations representing construction companies, citrus farmers, hotels and restaurants.

这项修正案得到了工会和迈阿密-戴德民主党的支持，遭到了代表建筑公司、柑橘种植者、酒店和餐馆的商业组织的反对。

In Louisiana a measure supported by two anti-abortion Democrats, Gov. John Bel Edwards and State Senator Katrina Jackson, passed comfortably. Amendment 1 would add these words to the State Constitution: “Nothing in this constitution shall be construed to secure or protect a right to abortion, or require the funding of abortion.”

在路易斯安那州，两名反堕胎民主党人，州长约翰·贝尔·爱德华兹(John Bel Edwards)和州参议员卡特里娜·杰克逊(Katrina Jackson)支持的一项措施顺利通过。修正案1将在州宪法中增加这样一句话：“本宪法中的任何内容不得解释为确保或保护堕胎权，或要求为堕胎提供资金。”

In Mississippi voters approved a new state flag with red, yellow and blue stripes, a magnolia flower and the words “In God We Trust.” The state’s previous flag, which dated to 1894 and contained a Confederate battle cross, was decommissioned by lawmakers in June.

在密西西比州，选民们批准了一面新的州国旗，上面有红、黄、蓝三色条纹，一朵木兰花，上面写着“我们信任上帝”。该州之前的旗帜可以追溯到1894年，上面有一个南部邦联的战斗十字架，在6月份被立法者退役。

There were 38 statewide citizen initiatives being decided across the country on Tuesday, about half the level of the last presidential election, when there were 72. Experts attribute the decline to the effect of the coronavirus, which has made gathering signatures more difficult. When all measures are tallied, including those placed by legislatures on the ballots, there are a total of 124 statewide ballot initiatives this year, down from 154 four years ago.

周二，全国各地正在决定38项全州公民倡议，大约是上次总统选举的一半，当时有72项。专家将下降归因于冠状病毒的影响，这使得收集签名变得更加困难。当所有措施都清点完毕，包括立法机构在选票上提出的措施，今年全州共有124项投票倡议，低于四年前的154项。

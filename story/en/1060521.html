Flipped an element in an old lens and got 'magic' bokeh (2018)
在旧镜头翻转一个元素，得到了“魔法”散景（2018年）

2021-05-05 20:30:14

Iranian photographer Alireza Rostami has been studying and tinkering with lenses for years, and a few years ago he made a discovery: by reversing one of the elements in a vintage lens, he found himself with a lens that produced beautiful and unusual “magic” bokeh. “Only a handful of countries can design and build photographic lenses,” Rostami tells PetaPixel. “I am a researcher in the field of photography lenses from Iran, and my country doesn’t have the technology for manufacturing cameras and lenses. I’m motivated to research and discover this technology, so I began studying and collecting information about the history of photographic lenses and, consequently, the use of reverse engineering for camera and lens autopsy.”

伊朗摄影师Alireza Rostami一直在学习和修补镜头多年，而且几年前他做了一个发现：通过逆转复古镜头中的一个元素，他发现自己用镜头产生美丽而异的“魔术”散景。 “只有少数各国可以设计和建造摄影镜头，”罗斯塔姆告诉Petapixel。 “我是伊朗摄影镜头领域的研究员，我的国家没有制造摄像头和镜头的技术。我有动力研究和发现这项技术，所以我开始学习和收集有关摄影镜片历史的信息，从而使用逆向工程来进行相机和镜片尸检。“

In around 2015, he became interested in Zeiss lenses but couldn’t afford to buy them. In reading about the history of Zeiss, Rostami found the Russian Helios and Zenit brands.

在2015年左右，他对蔡司镜头感兴趣，但买不起他们买不起。在阅读蔡司的历史中，罗斯塔姆发现了俄罗斯的Helios和Zenit品牌。

One of the lenses he started looking into was the Zenit MC Zenitar 50mm f/2, which features good optical quality and a relatively simple optical design that you can currently buy for about $70.

他开始研究的一个镜片是Zenit MC Zenitar 50mm F / 2，它具有良好的光学质量和相对简单的光学设计，您目前可以购买约70美元。

In studying the optical design, Rostami says he realized he could create a different bokeh by reversing one of the optical elements.

在研究光学设计时，Rostami表示他意识到他可以通过逆转其中一个光学元件来创造不同的散景。

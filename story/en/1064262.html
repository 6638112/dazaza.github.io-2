The Madman and the Dwarf
疯子和矮人

2021-06-08 15:53:36

Vincent Van Gogh (1853-90) and Henri Toulouse-Lautrec (1864-1901), eleven years his junior, both contracted syphilis and both died at the age of thirty-seven. Despite their completely different backgrounds, character and way of life, these freakish outsiders formed a strange friendship during the last four years of Vincent’s life.  They were drawn together by their passion for art, which relieved the agony of their lives.  They respected each other’s work, exhibited together, exchanged paintings and corresponded, though none of their letters have survived.

Vincent van Gogh（1853-90）和Henri Toulouse-Lautrec（1864-1901），11年他的初级初级梅毒，两人都在三十七岁时死亡。尽管他们完全不同的背景，性格和生活方式，但这些怪异的局外人在过去四年的文森特生活中形成了奇怪的友谊。他们被他们对艺术的热情被绘制在一起，这减轻了他们生命的痛苦。他们尊重彼此的工作，展出，交换绘画并相信，但他们的信件都没有幸存下来。

Vincent, the son of a Protestant minister, grew up in a dreary Dutch village. He had abortive careers as an art dealer, schoolteacher, bookseller, magazine illustrator, clergyman, and evangelical missionary among Belgian coal miners, and for years lived alone and impoverished in rural villages. After painting peasants and briefly studying at the Antwerp Academy, he moved to Paris to live with his brother Theo, an art dealer, who gave him psychological and financial support.

文森特，新教部长的儿子，在一个沉闷的荷兰村庄长大。他曾在比利时煤矿矿业中作为艺术经销商，小学家，书商，杂志Illustrator，牧师和福音派传教士中流产的职业生涯，并且多年来一直在农村村庄生活。在绘画农民并简要介绍安特卫普学院后，他搬到巴黎与他的兄弟天主一起生活，这是一家艺术经销商，他给了他心理和财政支持。

Lautrec was born in Albi, in southwest France near the Pyrenees, into a wealthy and disastrously inbred aristocratic family. He grew up on their inherited estates—his father a great sportsman, his mother frail and indulgent—and was educated at the elite Lycée Condorcet in Paris.  He broke both legs in two adolescent accidents and grew to be only four feet, eleven inches tall in Cuban heels. The worldly and sophisticated Lautrec had a defensive razor-sharp wit and confessed, ‘my family has done nothing for centuries.’

Lautrec出生于阿尔比，位于比利牛斯特附近的法国西南部，进入一个富裕而灾难性的近亲贵族家庭。他在遗传的庄园里长大 - 他的父亲是一个伟大的运动员，他的母亲虚弱和放纵 - 并在巴黎的精英Lycée露天电池教育。他在两个青少年事故中突破了两条腿，长大于四英尺，古巴脚跟11英寸高。世俗和良好的Lautrec有一个防守剃刀锋利的机智和承认，'我的家人只为几个世纪而做过。

Vincent’s blunt peasant features were disguised by a striking red beard. Lautrec, severely disabled and grotesquely ugly, could not hide his deformity and lurched along on crutches, lisping and drooling.  The singer Yvette Guilbert, whom he painted, was shocked on encountering his ‘enormous dark head, red face and very black beard; oily, greasy skin; a nose that could cover two faces; and a mouth like a gash from ear to ear, looking almost like an open wound!’

Vincent的钝好农民特征被一个醒目的红留胡子伪装。 Lautrec，严重残疾和粗暴地丑陋，无法隐藏他的畸形，并在拐杖，李斯平和流口水上徘徊。他画的歌手Yvette Guilbert令人震惊，遇到了他的“巨大的黑头，红色的脸和非常黑胡子;油腻，油腻的皮肤;鼻子可以覆盖两个面孔;嘴巴像耳朵到耳朵一样，看起来几乎像一个开放的伤口！'

They met as students, learning the basic skills of drawing and painting, in the studio of Fernand Cormon. The French critic Marc Tralbaut writes,

他们遇到了学生，学习绘画和绘画的基本技能，在Fernand Cormon的工作室里。法国评论家Marc Tralbaut写道，

Cormon, who was described by Toulouse-Lautrec as the thinnest and ugliest man in Paris [quite a compliment, coming from Lautrec], had gained a certain reputation from his large historic compositions, which are now completely forgotten.  He was an indifferent painter, but he was a good teacher, whose kindness and patience made up for his lack of talent and imagination.

由图卢兹 - Lautrec描述的Cormon作为巴黎最薄和丑陋的人[来自Lautrec]的最薄和丑陋的人，从他的大型历史作品中获得了一定的声誉，现在完全忘记了。他是一个漠不关心的画家，但他是一位好老师，善良和耐心弥补了他缺乏人才和想象力。

Lautrec’s biographer David Sweetman explains that Vincent was in a precarious mental state: ‘When he arrived at the studio in the autumn of 1886 he was already teetering on the brink of emotional collapse, enervated by the excitement of Paris, obsessed with wild enthusiasms, swinging between delirious happiness and sudden, uncontrollable rage.’  Julia Frey, another Lautrec biographer, describes Vincent’s weird and off-putting behaviour:

Lautrec的生物师David Sweetman解释说，文森特在1886年秋天到达工作室时，他已经在情绪崩溃的边缘上摇晃，由巴黎的兴奋陷入困境，迷恋狂野的热情，摆动在令人惊讶的幸福和突然，无法控制的愤怒之间。'Julia Frey是另一个Lautrec遗传学家，描述了文森特的奇怪和脱离行为：

Often morose and withdrawn, he worked silently in the studio, ignoring the students’ banter, then suddenly breaking into unsettling loquaciousness, engaging anyone near him with abrupt intensity. On those occasions he would launch into stuttering outbursts of bad French, loudly declaiming his theories on line and colour, and hissing through badly fitting false teeth.

经常莫罗斯和撤回，他默默地在工作室里工作，忽略了学生的聊天，然后突然突然脱落，让他附近的任何人带着突然的强度。在那些场合，他会发射令人讨厌的坏法语的爆发，大声地解除他的理论，在线和颜色，并通过严重贴合的假牙发出嘶嘶声。

Their art was as different as their personalities. Vincent worshipped Delacroix; Lautrec adored Degas.  Vincent’s jagged brush strokes and vertiginous swirls of paint seemed charged with an electric current; his expressionist art was feverish and agonized. Lautrec’s superb draftsmanship and incisive satire seemed effortless. But both artists believed that the subject of a picture was less important than its execution and that the painter must be committed to an ideal vision of art. Lautrec, one of the first to recognize Vincent’s talent, introduced him to the intoxicating pleasures of absinthe and to the louche cabarets of Montmartre.

他们的艺术与他们的个性不同。文森特崇拜德拉奇里克; Lautrec崇拜Degas。 Vincent的锯齿状刷笔画和汗湿漩涡似乎被电流充电;他的表现主义艺术狂热和痛苦。 Lautrec的卓越的平淡无奇，尖锐的讽刺似乎毫不费力。但两个艺术家都认为，图片的主题不如执行，而画家必须致力于理想的艺术愿景。 Lautrec是第一个认识到Vincent的人才之一的人，将他介绍了令人作呕的苦艾酒和蒙马特的凉鞋歌舞表演。

Sweetman also explains that Lautrec was perversely attracted to the boorish qualities in Vincent that repelled everyone else—and this enabled him to break through Vincent’s protective carapace:

甜蜜曼还解释说，劳雷茨仍然吸引了Vincent的粗糙品质，拒绝了其他人 - 这使他能够突破Vincent的防护陶瓷：

He found Vincent’s gaucheries and wearisome habits of alienating everyone appealing, for he too had suffered from public aversion to his appearance. The stumpy, black-clad aristocrat with his fleshy face and bulbous eyes peering through his pince-nez, and the emaciated Dutchman in his workman’s blue, with his hunched shoulders and suspicious looks, were soon familiar partners in the bars and cabarets of the Butte.

他找到了文森特的讲座和佩戴了疏远大家吸引力的佩戴习惯，因为他也遭受了公众厌恶他的外表。 Stumpy，Black-Clad贵族与他的肉体脸和球茎眼睛窥视着他的Pcle-Nez，以及他的工人蓝色的沮丧的荷兰人，他的猎物肩膀和可疑的外表，很快就是忠诚的伙伴在Butte的酒吧和歌舞表演中。

In a revealing dramatic passage, the model and painter Suzanne Valadon described Vincent’s crude social behaviour:

在揭示戏剧性的戏剧性段落中，模型和画家Suzanne Valadon描述了Vincent的粗暴社会行为：

I remember Van Gogh coming to our weekly gatherings at Lautrec’s. He arrived carrying a heavy canvas under his arm, put it down in a corner but well in the light, and waited for us to pay some attention to it.  No one took notice. He sat across from it, surveying the glances, seldom joining in the conversation.  Then, tired, he would leave, carrying back his latest work. But next week he would come back, commencing and recommencing with the same stratagem.

我记得梵高来到我们在Lautrec的每周聚会。他到了手臂下拿着一个沉重的帆布，把它放在一个角落里，但在光线下，等待我们注意它。没有人注意到。他坐在身上，调查一眼，很少加入谈话。然后，累了，他会离开，携带他的最新工作。但下周他会回来，开始和重新推荐使用相同的策略。

Unable to take part in the sophisticated talk about art, craving recognition and obsessed with his own work, Vincent couldn’t think of any other way to attract attention to his one-man show. Though the artists teasingly refused to acknowledge his picture, he persisted, week after week, in his hopeless and humiliating ploy.

无法参加精致的谈论艺术，渴望认可和痴迷于自己的工作，文森特无法想到任何其他方式吸引对他的单人秀的关注。虽然艺术家迟到拒绝承认他的照片，但他在一周之后持续到他无望和羞辱的伎俩。

Believing that Lautrec was very wealthy, Vincent pursued him with idealistic plans for an artists’ commune. Lautrec disliked these schemes and, perhaps impatient with his tedious friend, gave him excellent advice and made a shrewd prediction. He praised the light and colour of Provence and thought the brilliant atmosphere in the south of France would inspire him. In February 1888 Vincent left Paris for Arles and began to create his greatest work.

相信Lautrec非常富有，Vincent追求他的艺术家公社的理想主义计划。 Lautrec不喜欢这些计划，也许与他繁琐的朋友不耐烦，给了他出色的建议并提出了精明的预测。他赞扬普罗旺斯的光明和颜色，并认为法国南部的辉煌氛围会激励他。 1888年2月，文森特左巴黎阿尔勒斯并开始创造最大的工作。

In a reflective letter to Theo of August 11, 1888, Vincent compared his brilliantly coloured portrait of Patience Escalier, a Provençal herdsman and gardener, to Lautrec’s soft and subdued painting of a sad Parisian, ‘Young Woman at a Table: Poudre de Riz’ (face powder), which Theo owned.  Patience, with a yellow straw hat, ruddy complexion, grey beard and blue shepherd’s smock, rests his huge rough hands on a tall stick. In Lautrec’s grey-toned painting, the pale-faced woman, with a white blouse and billowing dark skirt, is seated facing the viewer with her arms crossed on the tablecloth and a red jar in front of her.  Trying to match Lautrec’s art and his own, Vincent felt the odd association and unusual contrast would make both pictures look more interesting:

在1888年8月11日至1888年8月11日的反思信中，文森特比较了他的耐心般的痛苦般的痛苦肖像，普罗斯群母和园丁，为Lautrec的柔软而撒上悲伤的巴黎人，'桌子上的年轻女子：Poudre de Riz' （面部粉末），Theo拥有。耐心，用黄色草帽，红豆肤色，灰色胡子和蓝色牧羊犬的手机，在一个高大的棍子上休息。在Lautrec的灰色定色画中，脸色苍白的妇女，带着白色的衬衫和滚筒黑暗的裙子，坐在桌子上坐在桌布和她面前的红色罐子里穿过观众。试图匹配Lautrec的艺术和他自己的，文森特感受到奇怪的关联和异常对比会使这两张图片看起来更有趣：

I don’t think my peasant will do any harm, for instance, to your Lautrec, and I even make so bold as to imagine that the Lautrec would appear still more distinguished by the contrast, and that mine would gain by the odd association, because that sunlit, sunburned quality, weatherbeaten by the full sun and open-air, would come even more into its own alongside the face powder and the fashionable clothes.

我不认为我的农民会造成任何伤害，例如，对你的Lautrec，我甚至如此大胆地想象Lautrec将仍然更加明显，并且矿井将由奇怪的协会获得，因为阳光照射，晒伤的质量，充满阳光和露天的风味，甚至更加融入脸部粉末和时尚的衣服。

The following year, in another letter to Theo, Vincent again praised his friend’s art: ‘There are some Lautrec’s [sic], which are very powerful in effect, among other things a “Ball at the Moulin de la Galette”, which is very good.’ A mutual friend reported, in contrast to Vincent’s earlier gauche efforts, that Lautrec was now ‘equally attentive to everything Van Gogh showed him.  He would take a deep breath, then look and listen. Every time his horizon broadened, he would clap his hands.’

次年，在另一封信给了神，文森特再次赞扬了他朋友的艺术：“有一些Lautrec的[SiC]，这是非常强大的，除了在Moulin de la Galette的另一个东西中，这是非常的好。“一个共同的朋友报道，与Vincent早期的Gauche的努力相比，Lautrec现在'同样关注Van Gogh向他展示的一切。他会深吸一口气，然后看看和倾听。每次他的地平线扩大，他都会拍手。

In early l887 Lautrec did a pastel on pasteboard, ‘Portrait of Vincent Van Gogh’.  He exchanged it for one of Vincent’s works and it is now in the Van Gogh Museum in Amsterdam. Vincent is seated at a table with a tall glass of cloudy absinthe in front of him, which probably accounts for his tense feeling. This highly alcoholic, anise-flavoured, green-coloured spirit, though not (as claimed) hallucinogenic and addictive, was banned by 1915 in most European countries. In ‘Poison’, Charles Baudelaire glorified the sublime effects of the ‘green fairy’: ‘Nothing can equal the fearsome marvel of your acid saliva, which plunges my remorseless soul into oblivion and, bringing vertigo, swirls it in a  swoon to the shores of death.’

在L887早期Lautrec在Pasteboard上做了一个柔和的粉彩，'Vincent Van Gogh'肖像。他为文森特的作品交换了它，现在在阿姆斯特丹的梵高博物馆。文森特坐在一张桌子上，在他面前有一块高大的阴天苦艾酒，这可能占他紧张的感觉。这种高度含酒精，易怒的味道，绿色的精神，虽然没有（如声称的）致幻和上瘾，但在大多数欧洲国家被禁止于1915年。在“毒药”中，查尔斯波德莱尔荣耀“绿色仙女”的崇高效应：'没有什么可以平等的酸唾液的令人毛骨悚然的奇迹，这将我的无数的灵魂陷入了遗忘，带来了眩晕，朝着海岸的昏昏欲睡漩涡死亡。

Vincent, seen in profile, with a high-arched nose, jagged red beard, blue shirt and brown jacket, leans forward with clenched hands and a fervent expression. Behind him is a blue zinc table with empty glasses, a six-panelled window and a swirl of yellow lines that suggest the effects of the absinthe. Frey notes that Lautrec imitated Vincent’s ‘multicoloured hatching and comma-like strokes’ to portray his anxious mood. Gazing straight ahead and lost in thought, Vincent may not have posed for this portrait but been caught in a moment of reflection or despair.

文森特，看到型材，带着高拱形的鼻子，锯齿状的红留胡子，蓝色衬衫和棕色夹克，向前倾向于握紧手和热情的表达。在他身后是一个蓝色锌桌，带空眼镜，六个镶板窗口和一个黄色线条的漩涡，表明苦艾酒的影响。弗雷注意到Lautrec模仿Vincent的'多彩多姿的孵化和逗号抚摸'，以描绘他焦虑的心情。直接凝视前方并失去思想，文森特可能没有为这个肖像提出，但已经陷入了反思或绝望的时刻。

Lautrec confirmed his friendship with the absent Vincent in a drunken banquet in January 1890.  He got into a violent dispute with the mentally unstable Belgian painter Henry de Groux—like Lautrec, a Velázquez dwarf—who insulted Vincent’s work and called him an ignoramus and a show-off. The two miniature men almost came to blows as Lautrec’s friends tried to keep him from demanding a duel. The dispute ended when the painter Paul Signac threatened to kill de Groux if he hurt Lautrec.

Lautrec于1890年1月确认了他在醉酒宴会中的缺席文森特的友谊。他与智力不稳定的比利时画家Henry de Groux-Like Lautrec陷入了暴力的争执，这是一个Velázquezdwarf-侮辱文森特的工作，并称他为Ignoramus和a炫耀。这两个微型男人几乎被吹，因为Lautrec的朋友试图让他要求他要求决斗。如果当他伤害了Lautrec，争议当画家保罗标志威胁要杀死De Groux时，争议结束了。

The last meeting of Vincent and Lautrec, who was an unexpected guest, took place at Theo’s Paris flat on July 6, 1890.  The two friends, so often the targets of mockery, were delighted to find someone else to mock.  They had a lot of fun at the expense of an undertaker’s assistant they had just met on the staircase.  Vincent was in unusually good spirits, and four days later told Theo, ‘I have very pleasant memories of this trip to Paris.  The picture by Lautrec, the portrait of a woman playing the piano [“Portrait de musicienne”], is very remarkable.  I was deeply moved by it.’

在1890年7月6日，在Ano的巴黎公寓举行了一个意想不到的客人的最后一次会议。这两个朋友经常是嘲弄的目标，很高兴找到别人嘲笑。他们在坐在楼梯上遇到的承诺助手的费用有很多乐趣。文森特在异常的精神中，四天后来告诉哈乔，“我对巴黎这次旅行有很愉快的回忆。 Lautrec的照片，踢钢琴的女人的肖像[“肖像de musicienne”，非常出色。我深深地感动了。“

On July 27, only three weeks after their last meeting, Vincent shot himself in Auvers, outside Paris, where he was being treated by Dr Gachet.  On July 31 Lautrec, who heard the news of Vincent’s death too late to join the cortège at his funeral (he was too short to be a pallbearer), sent his condolences to Theo and reaffirmed his comradeship with Vincent: ‘You know what a friend he was to me and how anxious he was to prove it.  Unfortunately, I can acknowledge all this only by shaking your hand cordially in front of a coffin, which I do.’

7月27日，在上次会议上只有三周，Vincent在巴黎以外的地方射杀了一个uvers，他被骚顿博士对待。在7月31日Lautrec，谁听到Vincent死亡的消息太晚加入了Cortège，他的葬礼（他太短暂的是一个帕尔伯勒），向Theo致以哀悼，并重申他与文森特的同志：'你知道什么是朋友他对我来说，他有多焦虑是为了证明它。不幸的是，我可以通过在棺材前面摇晃你的手来承认所有这一切。

Jeffrey Meyers, FRSL, has published  The Enemy: A Biography of Wyndham Lewis and  Wyndham Lewis: A Revaluation (both 1980).

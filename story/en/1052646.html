It's not being disallowed to sell something you own. It's being disallowed to mass purchase a product or coordinate to do so in order to gouge prices to make money. There's a big difference between you selling some TP you have in your closet when there's a shortage because you have extra and going and buying TP by the truckload so nobody can get any except through you and therefore making millions off of it. One of these is taking advantage of a market opportunity, the other is exacerbating a shortage to generate massive revenue. If you do this with securities, you go to jail for a very long time. Retailers don't do this because they extract their added value by making it readily available to you. That comes with costs they eat that you don't see. Sure, you could buy a truckload of TP cheaper from a manufacturer, but you need a truck, a driver, and a warehouse, and if you want to sell it, you now have delivery fees, business licensing, taxes, and possibly commercial real estate to put it up on shelves. It's much more convenient for you to just walk into a store and pick up 1 package, even if it costs 30-50% more.

It's reasonable to have a government deal with this issue. For instance, ticket sales became so notoriously bad due to botting and scalping that federal law was passed making that behavior illegal, because it's this situation but happens repeatedly, and it made scalpers very wealthy. Sony/Microsoft don't care because they sell every unit they make instantly no matter what. If the burden is on them to make them to order, it just takes money out of their pockets, so they have no incentive to do anything about it. What are you going to do, boycott an entire generation of consoles because they didn't figure out how to prevent scalpers from buying consoles? The retailers have no incentive for much the same reason -- they buy at wholesale and sell out instantly, and why should they care? The scalpers don't care because they make money. The only group being hurt is consumers, and when that happens, when nobody has an incentive to intervene, you have two options: 1) a massive boycott, which is just not going to happen here, and this problem will keep happening just like it did with ticket sales, 2) government regulation.

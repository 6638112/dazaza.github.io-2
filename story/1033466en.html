优步和Lyft将重新制定美国劳动法

2020-11-06 07:46:31

California voters’ decision to let Uber and other gig-economy companies continue to treat their workers as independent contractors has dealt a crushing blow to campaigners and legislators and paved the way for the companies to remake labor laws across the US.

加州选民决定允许优步(Uber)和其他零工经济公司继续将员工视为独立承包商，这对活动人士和立法者造成了沉重打击，并为这些公司在全美重新制定劳动法铺平了道路。

Voters in the state overwhelmingly approved Proposition 22 on Tuesday, exempting the companies from a new employment law passed last year. As a result, drivers in the state will not be classed as employees but can draw upon limited healthcare provisions and will earn a minimum rate of pay.

周二，该州选民以压倒性多数通过了22号提案，使这些公司不受去年通过的一项新劳动法的约束。因此，该州的司机将不被归类为雇员，但可以利用有限的医疗保健条款，并将获得最低工资。

The victory paves the way for similar legislation to be put in place across the US where, according to research from the investment bank Cowen, as many as 17 states are considering how to regulate the gig economy.

这一胜利为美国各地出台类似立法铺平了道路。投行考恩(Cowen)的研究显示，美国多达17个州正在考虑如何监管零工经济。

“California will certainly embolden them to try this kind of gambit as a way of protecting their business model,” said Professor David Weil, dean of the Heller School for Social Policy and Management at Brandeis University. “I think it’s clear that they have chosen to try different instruments of public policy to allow themselves to continue to be exempted from employer responsibilities.”

布兰代斯大学(Brandeis University)海勒社会政策与管理学院(Heller School For Social Policy And Management)院长戴维·韦尔(David Weil)教授表示：“加州肯定会鼓励他们尝试这种策略，以此作为保护其商业模式的一种方式。”“我认为很明显，他们选择了尝试不同的公共政策工具，以便让自己继续免除雇主的责任。”

California’s verdict came after months of fiery campaigning, with gig companies able to outspend the opposition by a ratio of about $10 to $1.

加州的判决是在经过几个月的激烈竞选后做出的，零工公司的支出比对手高出约10美元和1美元。

“My heart is heavy,” said Cherri Murphy, a rideshare driver and activist from Gig Workers Rising, in a video response posted on Twitter on Wednesday. “These corporations spent over $200 million on a corporate misinformation, deceptive campaign to rig our democratic process and to continue their exploitation of working people. It is a blasphemy and a sin.”

“我的心情很沉重，”Cherri Murphy在周三发布在Twitter上的一段视频中说，他是一名拼车司机，也是零工崛起组织的活动家。他说：“这些公司花费超过2亿美元进行企业虚假信息和欺骗性的运动，以操纵我们的民主进程，并继续剥削劳动人民。这是一种亵渎，是一种罪过。“。

Other opponents of the gig companies who have mobilized in key markets across the US admitted a painful defeat but held out hope that the labor movement would be re-energized if there is a change of power in Washington.

其他在美国各地关键市场动员起来的零工公司的反对者承认，他们遭遇了惨痛的失败，但他们仍抱有希望，认为如果华盛顿发生权力更迭，劳工运动将重新焕发活力。

“It was always going to be a long fight,” said William Fitzgerald, a former Google worker turned campaign coordinator for gig worker groups. “Three years ago, people said you couldn’t organize gig workers. Now you’ve got a broad-based coalition. The whole top of the Democratic ticket all stood with gig workers.”

威廉·菲茨杰拉德(William Fitzgerald)曾在谷歌工作，后来成为零工团体的竞选协调员。他说：“这永远是一场漫长的斗争。”“三年前，人们说你不能组织零工。现在你有了一个基础广泛的联盟。民主党候选人中的所有人都是零工。“

In California, though, the immediate battle is mostly over: the office of California’s state attorney general said it was still “reviewing” the result to determine how to proceed in its ongoing worker classification lawsuit against Uber and Lyft, in which, had Prop 22 failed, the state was almost certainly going to win.

然而，在加利福尼亚州，迫在眉睫的战斗已经基本结束：加利福尼亚州总检察长办公室表示，他们仍在“审查”结果，以确定如何继续进行针对优步(Uber)和Lyft的工人分类诉讼。在这起诉讼中，如果22号提案失败，该州几乎肯定会胜诉。

In Massachusetts, the state’s attorney general has sued Uber and Lyft over worker classification—a case that will be unaffected by California’s decision. Other measures could come into play in New York, Oregon, Washington state, New Jersey, and Illinois.

在马萨诸塞州，州总检察长已就工人分类问题起诉优步(Uber)和Lyft--该案不会受到加州裁决的影响。其他措施可能会在纽约、俄勒冈州、华盛顿州、新泽西州和伊利诺伊州发挥作用。

In response, the companies have tried to strike a conciliatory tone with unions, following a highly divisive Prop 22 campaign that was marred by vicious online sparring and accusations of foul play.

作为回应，这些公司试图与工会采取和解的语气，此前22号提案(Prop 22)的竞选活动引发了高度分歧，但遭到了网上恶性争执和对违规行为的指控。

“We’re ready to work with labor leaders and others to continue to build a stronger safety net for workers,” said John Zimmer, Lyft’s president, speaking to the Associated Press.

莱夫特总裁约翰·齐默对美联社说：“我们准备与劳工领袖和其他人合作，继续为工人建立更强大的安全网。”

Tony Xu, DoorDash’s chief executive, said his company was “looking ahead and across the country.”

DoorDash首席执行官托尼·徐(Tony Xu)说，他的公司“放眼未来，放眼全国”。

Dara Khosrowshahi, Uber’s chief executive, told the Financial Times prior to the vote that Prop 22’s passing would begin a process of Uber proactively working with states, seeking to avoid the prolonged court battles it has seen in California. “I’d like to spend less time in court if I can help it,” he said. “I think we are going to use this as an outline for a dialog that we have on a local basis.”

优步首席执行官达拉·科斯罗沙希(Dara Khosrowshahi)在投票前告诉英国“金融时报”，22号提案的通过将开启优步与各州积极合作的进程，以避免它在加州看到的旷日持久的法庭诉讼。他说：“如果可以的话，我想少花点时间在法庭上。”“我认为，我们将利用这一点作为我们在当地开展的对话的提纲。”

Weil, a former labor department official, said the fight could now hinge on national legislation. The Fair Labor Standards Act, which offers its own definition of what entails an employee-employer relationship, could override state laws, for example. A week ago, Uber pushed to carve out exemptions related to its business. A Joe Biden administration, suggested Weil, might be less amiable to the suggested changes.

前劳工部官员韦尔(Weil)表示，这场斗争现在可能取决于国家立法。例如，公平劳动标准法案(Fair Labor Standards Act)对什么是雇员-雇主关系做出了自己的定义，可以凌驾于州法律之上。一周前，优步推动了与其业务相关的豁免。韦尔表示，乔·拜登(Joe Biden)政府可能对提议的改变不那么友好。

“The Act could still be something that would subject Uber and Lyft, and those kinds of platforms, to obligations as an employer,” Weil said. “That could certainly happen, and I’m sure that’s not lost on those companies.”

韦尔说：“该法案可能仍然会让优步和Lyft以及这类平台承担雇主的义务。”“这当然是有可能发生的，我相信这些公司不会忽视这一点。”

But Moira Muntz, from the New York City-based Independent Drivers Guild, said she believed workers would perhaps now be best served by focusing attention on matters of representation rather than employment status—at least until such time when a broader reform of the law in the US is enacted.

但总部位于纽约市的独立司机协会(Independent Drivers Guild)的莫伊拉·芒茨(Moira Muntz)表示，她认为，至少在美国颁布更广泛的法律改革之前，现在关注代表权问题而不是就业地位可能是对工人最有利的。

“We would encourage state legislators all over the country to take notice of what happened in California, and take steps now to give gig workers the right to bargain for a labor contract,” she said. “State legislatures have the power to create a right to unionize, the right to collectively bargain for gig workers. We are urging them to do so and to do so quickly.”

她说：“我们会鼓励全国各地的州立法者注意加州发生的事情，现在就采取措施，让零工有权讨价还价签订劳动合同。”“州立法机构有权建立工会，有权为零工工人集体讨价还价。我们敦促他们这样做，而且要尽快做到这一点。“。

The guild, part of the influential Machinists Union, was able to negotiate rideshare drivers a tipping option within the apps and later a $27.86 an hour minimum wage, $17.22 after expenses.

该工会是颇具影响力的机械师工会(Machinist Union)的一部分，它能够就拼车司机在应用程序中提供小费选项进行谈判，后来谈判出每小时27.86美元的最低工资，扣除费用后为17.22美元。

By contrast, Prop 22’s promise of a $15.61 minimum could, according to one Uber-disputed study, come out as low as $5.61 an hour, after factoring in expenses and waiting periods, which are unpaid.

相比之下，根据优步(Uber)有争议的一项研究，22号提案承诺的最低15.61美元的最低时薪可能会低至每小时5.61美元，考虑到费用和等待时间(这些费用和等待期是没有支付的)。

Such calculations will move from theory to reality for hundreds of thousands of rideshare drivers across California in the coming weeks. Those new conditions, critics say, may provide the most powerful campaigning tool yet. “We’re eager to see the receipts at the end of the month,” Mr. Fitzgerald said.

在接下来的几周里，加州数十万拼车司机的这种计算将从理论变成现实。批评人士说，这些新的条件可能提供了迄今为止最强大的竞选工具。菲茨杰拉德说，“我们渴望在月底看到收据。”

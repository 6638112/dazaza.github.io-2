Before the Mayflower
五月花之前

2020-11-23 00:59:31

This year, the US looks back four centuries to an intrepid band of refugees making a perilous home in New England. The Mayflower pilgrims had been outlaws in England, members of an underground church known as the Brownists or Separatists. They believed church should be a voluntary community rather than a compulsory state religion. For their refusal to submit to the Church of England they had faced raids, prison, exile and death for the previous 60 years.

今年，美国回顾了四个世纪以来，一群勇敢的难民在新英格兰建立了危险的住房。五月花朝圣者在英格兰是非法的，是一个称为布朗主义者或分离主义者的地下教堂的成员。他们认为教会应该是一个自愿社区，而不是强制性的国教。由于拒绝服从英格兰教会，在过去的60年中，他们面临着突袭，监狱，流亡和死亡。

The pilgrims were not the first British settlers in North America. The officially sanctioned colony of Jamestown, Virginia, was 13 years old in 1620 and Roanoake colony, founded in the 1580s, had disappeared. What is less well known is that the Brownists themselves had made a previous expedition to North America. They had attempted to become the pilgrim fathers as early as 1597, trying to settle in Newfoundland.

朝圣者并不是北美最早的英国移民。正式批准的弗吉尼亚州詹姆斯敦（Jamestown）殖民地在1620年已有13岁，而1580年代建立的Roanoake殖民地则消失了。鲜为人知的是，布朗主义者本人此前曾去过北美。他们早在1597年就试图成为朝圣者之父，试图定居在纽芬兰。

The idea for a Canadian colony came out of the crisis surrounding the execution of the Separatist leaders Henry Barrow and John Greenwood on 6 April 1593, the morning after Parliament passed the Seditious Sectaries Act against them. The surviving Brownists were effectively banished by the Act, on pain of death, and so a large part of the underground church moved to the Netherlands.

1593年4月6日，即议会通过了针对他们的煽动宗派法之后的第二天，分离主义国家领导人亨利·巴罗和约翰·格林伍德被处决，此后的危机引发了加拿大殖民地的构想。该法案有效地消除了幸存的布朗主义者的痛苦，因此地下教会的很大一部分搬到了荷兰。

The more dangerous members, however, including their pastor Francis Johnson, were kept in prison in London. They petitioned the government for release, to no avail, until in March 1597 they came up with a new plan. The imprisoned Brownist leaders proposed to the Privy Council that they should be released into colonial expedition to Canada. The colony would claim fishing and hunting territory for England, the Separatists said, and ‘greatly annoy that bloody and persecuting Spaniard’, ensuring that the Americas did not fall entirely into the hands of the Catholic empire.

然而，包括他们的牧师弗朗西斯·约翰逊在内的危险程度更高的成员则被关在伦敦的监狱中。他们请政府释放，但无济于事，直到1597年3月，他们提出了一个新计划。被囚禁的布朗主义者领导人向枢密院提议，应将他们释放到加拿大进行殖民探险。分离主义者说，该殖民地将夺取英国的捕鱼和狩猎领土，并“极大地激怒了那个血腥和迫害西班牙的人”，确保美洲不会完全落入天主教帝国的手中。

The Separatists’ own reasons for going were similar to the later Mayflower pilgrims – to found a settlement where they would be free to practise the faith that made them outlaws in their homeland, transforming them into state-sanctioned pioneers and gaining their church a measure of authorisation for the first time. This first attempt was a more desperate gamble than the 1620 journey, though. These Brownists were prisoners whose lives were on the line, escaping their fate by going to a continent where no English settler had survived. The Mayflower pilgrims in contrast had already escaped English law before 1620 by migrating to the Netherlands and were embracing the dangers of North America out of a sense that God had called them to the promised land.

分离主义者走的原因与后来的五月花朝圣者相似，他们找到了一个定居点，在那里他们可以自由地实行使他们在自己的家乡成为非法的信仰，将他们转变为国家认可的先驱，并使他们的教会获得首次授权。但是，第一次尝试是比1620年旅程更加绝望的赌博。这些布朗主义者是囚犯，他们的生命迫在眉睫，他们逃往没有英国定居者生存的大陆，从而逃脱了命运。相比之下，五月花朝圣者已经移民到荷兰，从而逃脱了英国法律，在1620年前，他们出于上帝已将他们召唤到应许之地的感觉而拥抱北美的危险。

Four Separatists made the journey to Newfoundland in April and May 1597 in two ships, the dubiously named Hopewell and Chancewell – a generous provision compared with the Mayflower, which on its own had to carry 102 passengers with all their possessions and provisions. The four included Francis Johnson, the surviving leader of the movement, who travelled on the Hopewell, and his brother George on the Chancewell.

1597年4月和5月，四名分离主义者乘着两艘船前往纽芬兰，分别命名为Hopewell和Chancewell，这比五月花号（Mayflower）要慷慨，因为五月花号必须携带102名乘客的所有财产和规定。四个人中包括幸存的运动领袖弗朗西斯·约翰逊（Francis Johnson），他乘坐希望号（Hopewell）旅行，他的兄弟乔治（George）则是乔恩斯威尔（Chancewell）。

Arriving in Canadian waters in May, the ships were separated in the fog and the Hopewell reached its destination, the Magdalen Islands in the Gulf of St Lawrence, alone. They found the fishing there fantastic, catching 250 cod in an hour with four lines, though their attempts to catch walruses ended in them fleeing for their lives. Their greater problem was that the place was already rather crowded, with numerous French and Spanish ships and hundreds of indigenous Canadians, who had come from the mainland to hunt and fish. After a skirmish with the Europeans, the captain decided to try the north coast of the gulf, but the crew refused and turned the ship for home.

五月抵达加拿大水域时，船只在雾中分开，合和号仅在圣劳伦斯湾的马格达林群岛就到达了目的地。他们发现那里的捕鱼很棒，一小时内能钓到四条线的鳕鱼达250条，尽管他们试图捕捞海象最终以逃离生命而告终。他们更大的问题是这个地方已经很拥挤，有许多法国和西班牙的船只以及数百名来自大陆的加拿大人，他们来自大陆打猎和捕鱼。在与欧洲人发生冲突之后，船长决定尝试在海湾的北海岸，但船员拒绝了，将船转回家。

The Chancewell, meanwhile, found itself at Cape Breton Island, Nova Scotia, where it hit the rocks. The crew managed to get it into a bay, but it was raided by more French sailors who took all the settlers’ possessions. The captain offered his two Separatists a choice: stay on the island and be ‘devoured by the wild’; surrender to a French ship where ‘they should be urged to hear Mass’; or join the crew in trying to hijack a Spanish ship. George was too frightened to choose and asked the captain to make the choice for them, but he refused.

与此同时，Chancewell在新斯科舍省的布雷顿角岛（Cape Breton Island）发现了自己，并在那里撞到了岩石。船员设法将其送入海湾，但遭到更多占领者所有财产的法国水手袭击。船长为他的两名分离主义者提供了一个选择：留在岛上并被“野蛮吞噬”；投降到法国的一艘船上，“应敦促他们听到弥撒”；或与船员一起试图劫持一艘西班牙船。乔治害怕选择，并要求船长为他们做出选择，但他拒绝了。

At that moment they had the extraordinary luck – or providence, as the Separatists saw it – to spot the Hopewell passing on its return journey, and they flagged it down. Once reunited, they negotiated the return of some of their lost possessions and seized a French man-of-war and so were at least able to return home with as many ships as they started with. The Canadian adventure was over and the Separatists decided to settle in Amsterdam.

在那一刻，他们有非凡的运气–或如分离主义者所看到的天意–发现希望之路在返回途中逝去，并予以标明。团聚后，他们通过谈判交还了一些失落的财产，并缴获了一名法国战兵，因此他们至少能够带着开始时的许多船只返回家园。加拿大冒险结束了，分离主义者决定在阿姆斯特丹定居。

The Newfoundland expedition developed a tradition that the Mayflower pilgrims followed. Francis Johnson’s congregation also seem to have made two further attempts to settle in the new world before 1620, about which very little is known except that they ended in disaster. The journey of 1597 has had little recognition as an early colonial attempt on North America, partly because nothing came of it, but also because even those involved seem to have had little interest in the story. Francis Johnson wrote eight or so books without ever making any mention of the expedition. The captain of the Hopewell published his log, containing details of their explorations and their encounters with the French, but made no mention of any would-be settlers on board, apparently not being particularly keen to associate his name with the transportation of felons. George Johnson wrote a brief account of the journey, but it is buried in a much longer account of his theological and personal quarrels with his brother.

纽芬兰探险队发展了五月花朝圣者遵循的传统。弗朗西斯·约翰逊（Francis Johnson）的会众似乎也作了两次进一步的尝试，试图在1620年之前定居在新世界中，对此鲜为人知，除非他们以灾难告终。 1597年的旅行没有被视为北美的早期殖民尝试，部分原因是它什么也没得到，而且因为连涉入者似乎对这个故事都没有兴趣。弗朗西斯·约翰逊（Francis Johnson）写了八本左右的书，却丝毫没有提到这次探险。 Hopewell的船长发表了他的日志，其中包含了他们的探险经历以及与法国人的遭遇的细节，但没有提及船上可能有的定居者，显然他并不特别希望将他的名字与重罪犯的运输联系起来。乔治·约翰逊（George Johnson）简要介绍了这一旅程，但将其埋葬在与他的兄弟之间神学和个人争吵的更长篇幅中。

Because of this, little is known of the Separatists’ feelings about the expedition or the destination, their hopes, fears or disappointments. We do know that what they attempted and failed was achieved by their spiritual brothers and sisters 23 years later.

因此，对于分离主义者对远征或目的地，他们的希望，恐惧或失望的感受知之甚少。我们确实知道，他们的尝试和失败是23年后由他们的属灵兄弟姐妹实现的。

Stephen Tomkins is the author of The Journey to the Mayflower: God’s Outlaws & the Invention of Freedom (Hodder & Stoughton, 2020).

史蒂芬·汤姆金斯（Stephen Tomkins）是《五月花之旅：上帝的Out徒与自由的发明》（霍德与斯托顿，2020年）的作者。

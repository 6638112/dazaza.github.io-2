The U.S. Government Is Begging You to Destroy Moss Balls
美国政府正乞求你摧毁苔藓球

2021-03-17 03:21:24

There are several ways to wreck a moss ball. You might not want to, at first, because they’re quite charming, as far as aquarium accessories go—vivid green and damply hairy, like a Muppet that’s been through the wash. But if you choose to be merciless, you can pick from several modes of attack. You could seal it in a plastic bag and banish it to the freezer. You could submerge it in screaming-hot water for 60 seconds. You could dunk it in diluted bleach for 10 minutes, or straight-up vinegar for 20. Any of these approaches will also kill the real target: unwanted creatures bedding down on the small, fuzzy spheres.

有几种方法可以破坏苔藓球。起初你可能不想，因为它们非常迷人，就像水族馆配件一样生动的绿色和毛茸茸的毛茸茸，就像通过洗涤的muppet。但如果您选择无情，您可以从几种攻击方式中挑选。你可以把它密封在塑料袋里并将其移开到冰箱里。您可以将其淹没在尖叫热水60秒。你可以在稀释的漂白剂中扣篮10分钟，或直接醋20.这些方法中的任何一种也将杀死真实的目标：不需要的生物床上用品上小模糊的球体。

It’s sadism in the name of conservation. Pet stores in 32 American states were recently found to be selling moss balls studded with zebra mussels, Dreissena polymorpha, and the U.S. government wants the mollusks gone, one mangled moss ball at a time.

这是保守名称的虐待狂。最近发现32个美国国家的宠物商店销售散布斑马贻贝的苔藓球，德里斯纳多殖民地，美国政府希望软体动物失去，一次乱七八糟的苔藓球。

Zebra mussels, named for the zigs and zags on their almond-sized shells, are native to Eurasia, and are thought to have first arrived in North America in the late 1980s as stowaways on ships lumbering across the Great Lakes. The mussels probably sloshed around in ballast water, stored to stabilize an empty ship and released before it’s loaded with cargo.

Zebra Mussels在他们的杏仁大小的贝壳上命名为Zigs和Zags，原产于欧亚大陆，并且被认为是在20世纪80年代后期曾经抵达北美，这是在大湖泊的船上拖欠船只。贻贝可能在压载水中粘在镇流器中，储存以稳定空船并在装载货物之前释放。

Once the mussels enter a waterway, they have no trouble moving around it. Sperm and eggs drift freely, and females can lay more than a million in a single season. Larvae, known as veligers, are visible only under magnification. As adults, they tussle for space, encrusting water pipes and the hulls of boats, and glomming on to their neighbors. In lakes and rivers, zebra mussels filter vast quantities of water and siphon the nutrients for themselves. Several hundred thousand can crowd a single square meter, and they’re persistent: They hide in tangles of aquatic plants snagged on motors, can colonize essentially any hard surface, and are able to survive outside the water for several days.

一旦贻贝进入水道，他们就不会遇到麻烦。精子和鸡蛋自由漂移，女性可以在一个季节中铺设超过一百万。幼虫，称为纯粹，仅在放大率下可见。作为成年人，他们争夺空间，镶嵌水管和船上的船体，并掌握到邻居。在湖泊和河流中，Zebra Mussels过滤了大量的水和虹吸营养物质。数十万人们可以挤满单平方米，它们持久性：它们隐藏在水生植物的缠结在电机上，可以基本上殖民地殖民地，并且能够在水外生存几天。

Zebra mussels are “pretty much bad news all around,” says Ceci Weibert, an aquatic invasive species senior program specialist with the Great Lakes Commission. “They filled this niche and they don’t have any predators. They’re taking something out of the food web, and not putting anything back in.”

Zebra Mussels是“周围全部不好的消息”，Ceci Weibert，一家大湖委员会的水生侵入性物种高级方案专家Ceci Weibert说。 “他们填满了这个利基，他们没有任何掠夺者。他们把东西从食物网上拿出来，而不是把任何东西放回。“

By 1990, notes United States Geological Survey (USGS) fishery biologist Amy Benson in a report, zebra mussels had been spotted in all five Great Lakes. By 2020, the mussels had been seen in more than 600 lakes and reservoirs around the country, according to the U.S. Fish and Wildlife Service, one of several agencies that has tried for decades to stop the spread. Around 30 years ago, to keep track of sightings of zebra mussels and other introduced species, the USGS set up the Nonindigenous Aquatic Species Database. That’s how Wesley Daniel, the database coordinator and a fisheries biologist at the USGS, recently learned that a zebra mussel had turned up on an imported moss ball in a Seattle Petco.

到1990年，美国地质调查（USGS）渔业生物学家Amy Benson在一份报告中，Zebra Mussels已经发现了所有五大湖泊。根据美国鱼类和野生动物服务，在全国各地超过600多个湖泊和水库中，贻贝已经看到了2020年代，这是几十年的几个机构中的几个机构之一。大约30年前，为了跟踪斑马贻贝和其他引入的物种的目击，USGS建立了非洲水生物种数据库。这就是韦斯利丹尼尔，数据库协调员和渔业生物学家在USGS的方式，最近了解到Zebra Mussel在西雅图Petco的进口苔球上。

“I was shocked,” Daniel says. Other non-native species have been known to hitchhike on plants—salamanders from the northwest, for instance, have been ushered eastward on Christmas trees—but “we never expected zebra mussels to travel through aquarium plants,” Daniel says. “It’s not a pathway we had ever considered.”

“我感到震惊，”丹尼尔说。例如，他已知其他非本地物种在西北部的植物 - 蝾螈上搭便车，例如，在圣诞树上迎来了向东 - 但“我们从未预期的斑马贻贝通过水族馆植物旅行”，“丹尼尔说。 “这不是我们曾经考虑过的途径。”

An employee at the Seattle store uploaded the sighting February 25. By March 2, Daniel had learned about the post, reviewed some photos, rallied colleagues at the state and federal level, and canvassed for the little green blobs in Gainesville, Florida, where he lives. At the first pet store he surveyed, Daniel found an adult zebra mussel clinging to a moss ball. That made him realize the Seattle sighting wasn’t “a weird fluke,” he says. “If they’re in Washington and Florida, I’m assuming they’re going to be distributed everywhere in between.”

西雅图商店的一名员工上传了2月25日。到3月2日，丹尼尔已经了解了该职位，审查了一些照片，在州和联邦级别的一张照片，逢高的同事，以及佛罗里达州盖斯维尔的小绿色斑点，他生命。在他调查的第一个宠物商店，丹尼尔发现了一名成年斑马贻贝，紧贴着苔藓球。他说，这让他意识到西雅图瞄准并不是“一个奇怪的侥幸”。 “如果他们在华盛顿和佛罗里达州，我假设他们将在其间分发。”

Daniel showed the pet store manager how to kill the freshwater interloper by dunking it in super-salty water, and they removed the moss balls from the shelves. Still, by March 8, the USGS reported mussel sightings in moss balls in Alaska, California, Colorado, Florida, Georgia, Iowa, Massachusetts, Michigan, Montana, Nebraska, Nevada, New Mexico, North Dakota, Oklahoma, Oregon, Tennessee, Vermont, Virginia, Wisconsin, Washington, and Wyoming—with more states added since. Officials are investigating what Daniel refers to as the “chain of custody,” trying to determine whether the moss balls all came from a single producer, likely a wholesaler based in Ukraine, or from several sources. Meanwhile, with the U.S. Fish and Wildlife Service and other partners, the USGS issued guidance for going ham on the moss balls to quash the new invasion—that’s where all the bleaching, boiling, freezing, and other pummeling comes in. Afterwards, the brutalized ball should end up in the trash, in a sealed bag.

丹尼尔展示了宠物商店经理如何通过在超级咸水中扣押粉碎淡水接线线，然后从货架上取下苔藓球。仍然，到3月8日，USGS报告了在阿拉斯加，加州，科罗拉多州，佛罗里达州莫斯尔球的贻贝瞄准器，格鲁吉亚，爱荷华州，马萨诸塞州，密歇根州，蒙大拿，内布拉斯加州，内华达州，新墨西哥州，北达科他州，俄克拉荷马州，俄勒冈州，田纳西州，佛蒙特州，弗吉尼亚州，威斯康星州，华盛顿和怀俄明州 - 与更多的国家以来增加。官员正在调查Daniel指的是作为“拘留链”，试图确定苔藓球是否来自单一的生产者，可能是基于乌克兰的批发商，或来自几个来源。同时，与美国鱼类和野生动物服务和其他合作伙伴，USGS在苔藓球上发布了火腿的指导，以挤出新的入侵 - 这就是所有漂白，沸腾，冻结和其他击打的地方。之后，野蛮的球应该最终在垃圾桶里，在密封袋中。

Introductions from home aquaria aren’t the most common vector for species entering waterways such as the Great Lakes, but “they do happen,” says Christine Mayer, an aquatic ecologist at the University of Toledo. “Especially with fish—people don’t want to kill them. People don’t feel so bad about putting a plant in the compost, but they’re not sure they know how to humanely euthanize a fish.” Mayer says that ecologists sampling in the Great Lakes will sometimes come across a goldfish, either freed from a fishbowl or descended from others gone feral.

家用水族馆的介绍不是进入水道等物种的最常见的载体，如伟大的湖泊，但“他们确实发生了”，托莱多大学的水生生态学家Christine Mayer说。 “特别是鱼民不想杀死他们。人们对堆肥的植物感觉不那么糟糕，但他们不确定他们知道如何人性化安乐死。“ Mayer说，生态学家在大湖中抽样将有时会遇到一条金鱼，无论是从鱼缸里释放，还是从野生野外的其他人下降。

A preemptive strike is the best way to keep zebra mussel populations small, because established communities are incredibly difficult to eradicate. “Everyone who works with invasive species says prevention is better than cure,” Mayer says. “Keeping things out is cheaper, easier, and better than trying to kill them once they’re there.”

先发制人的罢工是保持斑马贻贝人群的最佳方式，因为成立的社区非常难以消除。 “与侵入性物种合作的每个人都说，预防比治愈更好，”Mayer说。 “让事情保持在更便宜，更容易，而不是试图杀死他们一旦他们在那里杀死它们。”

Introductions from large ships aren’t such a major issue anymore, because there are now procedures about how, where, and when ballast water can be dumped, says Eva Enders, a research scientist at Fisheries and Oceans Canada who has studied the mussels’ muscling through Lake Winnipeg, in Manitoba. Now, Enders says, “the risk that remains is inland—the transport by small leisure or recreational boats from lake to lake, river to river.” To aid in prevention, ecologists, naturalists, and others around the Great Lakes region have encouraged boaters to inspect their hulls and motors and thoroughly drain and dry water-holding compartments between outings.

大型船舶的介绍不再是这一重大问题，因为现在研究了如何倾倒镇流器水，渔业和海洋的研究科学家，现在有关于如何倾倒的程序，以及镇流器水的程序穿过温尼伯湖，在曼尼托巴。现在，方士说：“遗体仍然是内陆 - 通过湖到河到河流的小休闲或休闲船的运输。”为了帮助预防，生态学家，自然主义者和大湖区周围的其他人鼓励船员检查船体和电机，并在郊游之间彻底排出和干水持有舱室。

It’s hard to picture a future in which waterways that are suffused with the mussels are ever fully free of them. “The Great Lakes are kinda…they’re already a thing,” Mayer says. That’s not the case everywhere, though, and more recent efforts have focused on stifling invasive zebra mussels in the western U.S., where they haven’t yet claimed as much turf. Groups such as the Invasive Mussel Collaborative, which works in partnership with the Great Lakes Commission, USGS, National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration, and others, share tactics for snuffing out small populations. In New York’s Lake George, for example, a team successfully evicted a fledgling mussel population by quarantining the entire lake and dispatching divers to remove them manually.

很难想象一个与贻贝挤压的未来完全没有自由的未来。 “伟大的湖泊有点......他们已经是一件事，”Mayer说。然而，这不是无处不在的情况，最近的努力都集中在美国西部的腐烂斑马贻贝上，他们尚未声称这一目标。诸如侵入式贻贝协作等团体与大湖委员会，USGS，国家海洋和大气管理和其他人合作，分享了扼杀小人口的策略。例如，在纽约湖乔治湖中，一支球队通过隔离整个湖泊和调度潜水员成功地驱逐了一只漂浮的贻贝人口，以便手动删除它们。

Other strategies include pumping carbon dioxide into the water column, which suffocates the mussels inside it, and laying tarp-like benthic mats on mussel-studded bottoms, barricading the creatures from oxygen, light, and food. Several states have “decontamination stations,” where boats are inspected and cleaned, Weibert says. In Alberta, Canada, mussel-sniffing dogs are trotted out at highway checkpoints, Enders notes. USGS staff are currently adapting a test, initially developed for invasive carp, to suss out genetic evidence of zebra mussels in the water; Daniel thinks it could be up and running in the next several months.

其他策略包括将二氧化碳泵送到水柱中，该柱将贻贝窒息，并在贻贝填充底部铺设篷布的底层垫，将生物从氧气，光和食物中偏移。 Weibert说，几个州有“净化站”，其中船只被检查和清洁船只。在加拿大艾伯塔省，贻贝嗅狗在高速公路检查站时出局，方位票据。 USGS员工目前正在调整测试，最初为侵入性鲤鱼开发，以抑制水中斑马贻贝的遗传证据;丹尼尔认为它可能会在未来几个月内运行。

But for now, ecologists really want your help preventing new spreads—and that involves unleashing some hellfire fury on moss balls.

但是，现在，生态学家真的希望你的帮助防止新的差异 - 这涉及释放一些地狱火狂怒的苔藓球。

Parks Associates: 20% of US broadband households currently use ad-supported streaming services and 15% use a freemium service, like Peacock
Parks Associates：目前，美国20％的宽带家庭使用广告支持的流媒体服务，而15％的用户使用免费增值服务，例如孔雀

2021-01-03 17:07:39

Twenty percent of U.S.broadband households currently use an ad-supported OTT service, and 15% use a “freemium” service, according to Parks Associates research.

根据Parks Associates的研究，目前有20％的美国宽带家庭使用广告支持的OTT服务，而15％的家庭使用“免费增值”服务。

The research confirms the growingpopularity of so-called freemium services — hybrid business models that combine free, ad-supported content with a premium subscription tier.

该研究证实了所谓的免费增值服务日益普及，这种增值服务将免费广告支持的内容与高级订阅层相结合。

Currently, there is no clear marketleader in the ad-supported and freemium OTT spaces: Pluto TV, The Roku Channel, Tubi TV, Peacock and Crackle all score relatively similar adoption rates, reports Parks' Research Director, SteveNason.

目前，在广告支持和免费增值的OTT领域尚无明确的市场领导者：Pluto TV，Roku Channel，Tubi TV，Peacock和Crackle的采用率都相对较高，据Parks＆＃39; SteveNason研究总监。

“The newest offering, NBCUniversal’s Peacock, does have the reach, content and profile to disrupt this area, which could further boost usage of ad-based and freemium OTTamong U.S. households,” he adds.

他补充说：“最新的产品，NBCUniversal的孔雀，确实具有破坏该地区的影响力，内容和形象，这可能会进一步推动基于广告和免费增值的OTTamong美国家庭的使用。”

Consumer spending was shifting toward OTT services even before the coronavirus pandemic hit in March 2020, as more households continue to cut or trimtheir pay-TV services.

甚至在2020年3月冠状病毒大流行之前，随着越来越多的家庭继续削减或削减其付费电视服务，消费者的消费已转向OTT服务。

U.S. broadband households reported spending an average of $16 per month on OTT video service subscriptions in Q1 2020, behind $89 per month on pay-TV services, Parksresearch showed.

Parksresearch研究显示，美国宽带家庭报告称，2020年第一季度每月平均在OTT视频服务订阅上花费16美元，仅次于付费电视服务每月89美元。

“A prolonged economic contraction could drive households to reduce pay-TV spending more, while also scrutinizing their OTT service stacks,” pointing to continuedgrowing importance for ad-based services in the months ahead, notes Nason.

内森指出：“长期的经济萎缩可能会促使家庭减少更多的付费电视支出，同时还要审查他们的OTT服务组合，”这表明在未来几个月中，基于广告的服务的重要性将继续增长。

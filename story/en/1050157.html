A Man That Died Replicating Ben Franklin's Experiments
一个死于复制本·富兰克林实验的人

2021-03-01 23:51:26

“The new Doctrine of Lightning is, however, confirm’d by this unhappy Accident…M. Richmann being about to make Experiments on the Matter of Lightning, had supported his Rod and Wires by Electrics per se, which cut off their Communication with the Earth; and himself standing too near where the Wire terminated, help’d with his Body to compleat that Communication. It is plain the Wire conducted the Lightning to him thro’ the whole Length of the Gallery…”

“然而，新的闪电学说被这一不幸的事故……M所证实。里奇曼正准备在“闪电问题”上进行实验，他自己用电子学支持了他的“电线杆和电线”，从而切断了它们与地球的交流。并且他自己站在电线终止的地方附近，帮助他的身体完成了这种交流。很明显，电线从画廊的整个长度向他传导了闪电……”

We live with a powerful force of nature all around us, which we harness without a worry.

我们生活在我们周围强大的自然力量中，我们可以毫无忧虑地利用自然力量。

The electricity in your house is a much tamer version of what you see in the skies during a thunderstorm. Think house cat versus Bengal tiger. However, this cat wasn’t always quite so tame when mankind first began playing with electricity. And “playing” is the perfect word for it.

您家中的电力比雷雨期间在天空中看到的更为温和。想想家猫与孟加拉虎。但是，当人类刚开始玩电时，这只猫并不总是那么驯服。 “玩”是完美的词。

Georg Wilhelm Richmann learned this firsthand as he got an unforgiving kiss from a bolt of electricity. However, before we can get to his story, we have to set the stage in the saga of electricity.

乔治·威廉·里奇曼（Georg Wilhelm Richmann）从电击中得到了一个宽容的吻，这是他第一手学到的。但是，在我们了解他的故事之前，我们必须为电的传奇故事做好准备。

Pioneers in this field had no training per se. There were no electricians, or schools of electricity — just novices who experimented. Their early experiments were parlor tricks. Furthermore, Founding Father Benjamin Franklin gave up his printing business just to play with his new toy in 1745.

该领域的先驱者本身没有受过任何训练。没有电工或电力学校，只有实验的新手。他们的早期实验是客厅的把戏。此外，开国元勋本杰明·富兰克林（Benjamin Franklin）放弃了印刷业务，只是为了在1745年与他的新玩具一起玩。

While Ben did serious science, he also had a lot of fun. In fact, if he was alive today, he might have been a YouTube celebrity, or easily gotten sued. You can say he walked the line.

Ben做严肃的科学时，他也玩得很开心。实际上，如果他今天还活着，那么他可能是YouTube名人，或者很容易被起诉。可以说他走了。

The American Physical Society tells the story of Franklin hosting a barbeque in which he killed and fried a turkey with electricity. He also designed a game called “treason” where an electrified crown was hung on a picture of the king. Furthermore, the attendants also drank from electrified glasses of wine and got to watch Franklin shoot sparks across the river.

美国物理学会讲述了富兰克林主持烧烤活动的故事，在烧烤中他用电杀死并炸了一只火鸡。他还设计了一款名为“叛国”的游戏，在该游戏中，国王的头像上挂着一个带电的王冠。此外，服务人员还喝了带电的葡萄酒，并观看了富兰克林在河对岸拍摄火花。

Although there was a bit of a mishap; Franklin got a good shock when cooking his meal. It was an early reminder he was playing with more of a tiger and less of a house cat.

尽管有一些不幸；富兰克林在做饭时感到非常震惊。这是一个早期的提醒，他在玩的更多的是老虎，而更少的是家猫。

While it may seem old Ben was just screwing around, he did discover some interesting things.

尽管看上去本来只是个老家伙，但他确实发现了一些有趣的东西。

From 1747 to 1750 he sent correspondences to a member of the Royal Society of London about his experiments according to Physics Today. His first major discovery is what he called the “wonderful Effect of Points”.

从1747年到1750年，他根据《今日物理学》向伦敦皇家学会成员发送了有关他的实验的书信。他的第一个主要发现就是他所说的“积分的奇妙效果”。

Franklin found that sharp points appeared to attract electricity and throw it off better then dull ones. Plus, metal was a better conductor than wood. He also explained the idea of “grounding” an object. In other letters he described experiments where he “charged” primitive capacitors and how metal points could send charges over distances.

富兰克林（Franklin）发现，尖锐的点似乎可以吸引电，并且比钝的电更好地将其抛弃。另外，金属是比木材更好的导体。他还解释了将对象“接地”的想法。他在其他信件中描述了实验，在该实验中他为原始电容器“充电”，以及金属点如何在远处发送电荷。

“I say, if these Things are so, may not the Knowledge of this Power of Points be of Use to Mankind; in preserving Houses, Churches, Ships, etc. from the Stroke of Lightning; by Directing us to fix on the highest Parts of those Edifices upright Rods of Iron…and from the Foot of those Rods a Wire down the outside of the Building into the Ground; or down round one of the Shrouds of a Ship and down her Side, till it reaches the Water? Would not these pointed Rods probably draw the Electrical Fire silently out of a Cloud before it came nigh enough to strike, and thereby secure us from that most sudden and terrible Mischief!”

“我说，如果这些事情如此，那么对点的这种力量的认识可能对人类没有用；保护雷击中的房屋，教堂，轮船等；指示我们将直立的铁棍固定在大厦的最高部位……并从这些铁棍的脚下将电线从建筑物的外部向下插入地面；还是顺着一艘船的裹尸布顺着她的侧面走，直到到达水面？这些尖锐的竿可能不会在电云到来之前就悄无声息地从云中引出电火，从而使我们免受最突然和最可怕的恶作剧！”

He suggested an experiment be conducted where one of these iron rods was put on top of a large building and grounded by wire to the earth below. However, it appears Ben was not the first to try this particular experiment.

他建议进行一项实验，将其中一根铁棒放在一幢大建筑物的顶部，并通过电线接地到下面的大地。但是，看来Ben不是第一个尝试此特定实验的人。

According to the Founders Online, an acquaintance of Franklin, Joseph Priestley, said Philadelphia didn’t have a tower high enough in this time period. But Franklin’s idea did travel across the oceans and his description of the experiment landed on the shores of France.

据创办人在线（Founders Online）报道，富兰克林（Franklin）的熟人约瑟夫·普里斯特利（Joseph Priestley）表示，费城在此期间的塔楼不够高。但是富兰克林的想法确实横渡了大洋，他对实验的描述落在了法国的海岸上。

In 1752 two Frenchmen, Dalibard and Delor, tried Franklin’s “Philadelphia experiments” in front of King Louis XV. After the successful experiments, the King sent congratulatory letters to Franklin and the Royal Society of London. Afterwards, others tried the experiment successfully, however, there was one major disaster.

1752年，两名法国人达利巴德和德洛在路易十五国王面前尝试了富兰克林的“费城实验”。在成功的实验之后，国王向富兰克林和伦敦皇家学会发了贺信。之后，其他人成功地尝试了该实验，但是，发生了一场重大灾难。

In early August of 1753, a Swedish scientist Georg Wilhelm Richmann planned to try Franklin’s experiment himself.

1753年8月上旬，瑞典科学家乔治·威廉·里希曼（Georg Wilhelm Richmann）计划亲自尝试富兰克林的实验。

He invited a local engraver, M. Sokolaw, to watch as a storm could be heard in the distance. The test itself was performed at a building in Russia that resembled a gallery.

他邀请当地的雕刻师M. Sokolaw观看在远处听到的暴风雨。测试本身是在俄罗斯一间类似美术馆的建筑物上进行的。

Richmann had placed a glass jar on a cupboard by a window. In the jar was water with brass filings. An iron bar about an inch thick and a foot long stuck out of the jar, attached to a wire coming down from the roof. As the storm could be heard coming in, he told the engraver there was nothing to fear for the moment.

里奇曼在窗户旁边的橱柜上放了一个玻璃罐。罐子里放着水和黄铜屑。一根大约一英寸厚，一英尺长的铁棒从广口瓶中伸出，并固定在从屋顶上掉下来的一根电线上。当听到暴风雨来临时，他告诉雕刻师，目前没有什么可担心的。

The Swede stood about a foot away from the glass jar watching intently. Sokolaw suddenly saw a ball of blue and white fire come from the bar and hit Richmann in the forehead. This was followed by an explosion which knocked both men backwards. The gallery filled with smoke, and its door was knocked off the hinges. While Sokolaw lived, Richmann could not be revived.

瑞典人站在玻璃罐子旁边大约一英尺处，专心地看着。索科劳突然发现酒吧里射出一团蓝白色的火球，砸在额头上的里奇曼身上。随后发生爆炸，将两个人都击倒。画廊里弥漫着烟雾，它的门从铰链上敲了下来。索科劳生活期间，里奇曼无法复活。

A doctor examining his body, opened a vein, from which no blood came out. Aside from what looked like gun powder burns, the only mark on Richmann’s body was a red mark on his forehead.

医生检查他的身体，张开了静脉，没有血液流出。除了看似枪粉燃烧外，Richmann身上唯一的痕迹是额头上有红色痕迹。

News of Richmann’s death while attempting to replicate Franklin’s experiment spread across the globe.

里希曼（Richmann）试图复制富兰克林（Franklin）实验的消息传遍了全球。

Another French scientist, Abbé Jean-Antoine Nollet, upset by the success of his piers attacked Franklin’s grounded rod idea. Of course, he used Richmann’s death as ammunition. However, London’s Royal Society awarded the Philadelphian their highest scientific honor anyway and these grounded rod experiments continued across the world.

另一位法国科学家阿贝·让·安托万·诺莱特（AbbéJean-Antoine Nollet）对码头的成功感到沮丧，这袭击了富兰克林的扎根标杆理念。当然，他用里奇曼的死作为弹药。然而，无论如何，伦敦皇家学会还是向费城人授予了最高的科学荣誉，这些扎根的杆实验在全世界范围内继续进行。

It appears Franklin himself addressed the incident with Richmann as well. The opening paragraph in this article from the Pennsylvania Gazette examining the deadly experiment is thought to be written by old Ben himself due to its extensive explanation of electricity.

看来富兰克林本人也曾同里奇曼一起处理过这一事件。宾夕法尼亚州宪报对致命实验进行了研究的这篇文章的开篇被认为是由本·本本写的，因为它对电有广泛的解释。

Richmann apparently forgot something Franklin learned about firsthand while cooking his turkey — electricity must be grounded properly. The Swede’s wire ended in the glass container, instead of running to earth. It appears the unlucky scientist’s body became a conductor itself and sent the charge across the room.

里奇曼显然忘记了富兰克林在煮火鸡时直接学到的东西-必须正确接地。瑞典人的电线端在玻璃容器中，而不是接地。不幸的是，这位科学家的尸体本身变成了导体，并把电荷传送到整个房间。

Although in Richmann’s defense, Franklin likely never had the instance of a rogue globe of ball lightening to deal with.

尽管在里奇曼的防守下，富兰克林可能从未遇到过像闪电球这样的流氓地球。

Despite the death, it didn’t slow down the spark-loving Founding Father. He had done more dangerous experiments with electricity than the lightening rod test. The year prior to Richmann’s death, Franklin sent a silk kite into a thunderstorm armed with a metal rod with a point attached to it.

尽管死了，但它并没有减慢爱好火花的开国元勋。与避雷针测试相比，他用电进行了更危险的实验。里奇曼（Richmann）死前一年，富兰克林（Franklin）用金属棒将风筝送入雷暴，并附有金属棒。

All of the tinkering with electricity paid off and Franklin knew enough about the nascent science not to get himself killed. Unfortunately, the same couldn’t be said for Richmann.

所有的修修补补都得到了回报，富兰克林对这种新兴的科学知识了如指掌，不会被杀死。不幸的是，对于里奇曼而言，情况并非如此。

Today we’re spoiled by our friendly house cat. We flick on light switches and the world illuminates; power is usually easy and safe. However, this knowledge came with a cost and its pioneers made discoveries through tinkering and odd mishaps.

今天，我们被友好的家猫宠坏了。我们轻按电灯开关，世界便照亮了。电源通常是简单安全的。但是，这种知识是有代价的，其先驱者通过修补和奇特的不幸事件发现了问题。

While Ben Franklin’s early exploits may have looked like tricks to entertain himself and his neighbors, they gave him a strong understanding of electricity. This knowledge pushed him forward and benefited humanity. Richmann’s death only reminds us of how dangerous this experimentation actually was.

尽管本·富兰克林（Ben Franklin）的早期功绩看起来像是娱乐自己和邻居的s俩，但它们却使他对电有了深刻的了解。这些知识使他前进并造福了人类。里奇曼的死只让我们想起这项实验实际上是多么危险。

One could only imagine how brave Franklin was holding that silk kite string in his hand. Despite his knowledge of electricity, there had to be some lingering doubt. I’m sure even with our modern knowledge of science, you’d be hard pressed to find many willing to try Franklin’s kite escapade.

谁能想象富兰克林手里握着那根丝风筝线多么勇敢。尽管他了解电，但仍存在一些挥之不去的疑问。我敢肯定，即使我们拥有现代的科学知识，也很难找到许多愿意尝试富兰克林风筝逃逸的人。

The early study of electricity was like that analogy of building a plane as you’re flying it. The only way to learn was by getting shocked occasionally and playing with things yourself. It all worked to tame the tiger and provide us with the quick and safe power source mankind is addicted to today.

早期的电力研究就像在飞行时搭飞机一样。学习的唯一方法是偶尔感到震惊并自己玩东西。这一切都是为了驯服老虎，并为我们提供了当今人类沉迷的快速，安全的动力源。

Strangely, some of the progress took the form of cooking a turkey with a battery while neighbors cheered. Some might have called it idiocy, Franklin and his colleagues called it research.

奇怪的是，一些进展采取了用电池煮火鸡的方式，而邻居们却欢呼雀跃。富兰克林和他的同事称某些研究为愚蠢的研究。

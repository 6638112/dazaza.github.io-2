AMD Ryzen 5000G Series Launches With Integrated Graphics At Value Price Points
AMD 锐龙 5000G 系列以超值价格推出集成显卡

2021-08-05 21:28:06

AMD is officially taking the wraps off of its latest APUs, the Ryzen 7 5700G and the Ryzen 5 5600G. As their branding suggests, these new products are based on the same technology as Ryzen 5000 series processors, namely AMD’s excellent Zen 3 core architecture, but with integrated graphics capabilities as well, hence the “G” designation. These particular processors, however, mark somewhat of a departure from similar, previous-gen AMD products. While Ryzen 3000G series APUs were relegated to entry-level systems with a maximum of 4 cores and somewhat tame CPU and GPU clock speeds, Ryzen 5000G APUs crank things up a few notches by offering up to 8 cores, with max CPU boost clocks exceeding 4.4GHz, faster memory support, and significantly higher GPU clock speeds as well. The on-die graphics, however, are still based on AMD’s Vega GPU architecture. Of course, AMD did technically release Ryzen and Ryzen PRO 4000G series processors with higher clocks and core counts too, but those were OEM-only processors and weren't meant to be sold at retail. AMD is targeting more mainstream applications with the Ryzen 7 5700G and the Ryzen 5 5600G versus standard Ryzen 5000 series processors. Both chips feature a 65W TDP and slot right into AMD’s socket AM4 platform. These processors will essentially work with any 400 or 500 series chipset-based motherboards, provided the manufacture has updated the board's BIOS with the necessary AGESA. AGESA 1.1.8.0 is required to POST/boot these APUs, but AGESA 1.2.0.3b (or newer) is recommended for optimal performance. The Ryzen 7 5700G is obviously the higher-end model of these two CPUs. It is an 8-core / 16-thread processor with 4MB of L2 cache and 16MB of L3. Those CPU cores are mated to an 8 CU Radeon Vega graphics engine, and it has 24 lanes of PCIe Gen 3 connectivity. The 5700G’s base CPU clock is 3.8GHz, with a maximum boost clock of 4.6GHz. The on-processor GPU can boost up to 2GHz, which is a massive uptick from the 1.4GHz of previous-gen 3000-series APUs. The Ryzen 5 5600G takes things down a notch with 6 CPU cores (12 threads), and a commensurately smaller 3MB of L2 cache; the L3 cache size remains unchanged, though. The iGPU is scaled down slightly as well, and has only 7 CUs. At 3.9GHz, the 5600G’s base clock is 100MHz higher than the 5700G’s, but its max boost lands at 4.4GHz. The iGPU has a slightly lower 1.9GHz maximum clock speed too. AMD is positioning these two APUs against the Intel Core i7-11700 and Core i6-11600. The Ryzen 7 5700G’s suggested price is $359 and the Ryzen 5 5600G is a full $100 less at $259. Both of those prices are exactly in-line with Intel’s competitive offerings. Looking at current pricing for the Ryzen 7 5800X and Ryzen 5 5600X, however, these new APUs arrive at significantly lower pricing, which is great considering their similar CPU core counts with essentially a “free” GPU thrown in. The key ingredient that made the Ryzen 7 5700G and the Ryzen 5 5600G possible is AMD’s Infinity Fabric. The flexibility of the Infinity Fabric to link various IP on-die, in conjunction with AMD’s current CPU architecture, essentially allowed AMD to drop Zen 3 into the design as a replacement to the previous-gen Zen 2 architecture. With these chips, an up-to 8-core / 16-thread Zen 3 CCD is linked to an up-to 8 Compute Unit Radeon Vega iGPU and adjacent multi-media engines. The chips are produced on TSMC’s 7nm node, and the processors drop right into AMD’s existing socket AM4 ecosystem – again, assuming the motherboard has been updated with the necessary AGESA (which is probably has been). From the outside the Ryzen 7 5700G and Ryzen 5 5600G look just like all of AMD’s current mainstream processors. The packaging, pin-out, integrated heat-spreaders and branding are all the same. It’s only the model number emblazoned on top that changes. Firing up the latest edition of CPU-Z gives a glimpse into the configuration of these new Ryzen 5000G-series processors. There’s 64KB (32KB data + 32KB instructions) of L1 cache per core, 512KB of L2 cache per core, and a single pool of 16MB of L3 per CCD on board. Clocks and voltages will vary based on load (more on this later in the overclocking section), and the family, model, stepping and revision are identical between the 5700G and 5600G. With all of that out of the way, let’s see how these latest APUs perform, shall we?

AMD 正式揭开了其最新的 APU，Ryzen 7 5700G 和 Ryzen 5 5600G 的面纱。正如他们的品牌所暗示的那样，这些新产品基于与锐龙 5000 系列处理器相同的技术，即 AMD 出色的 Zen 3 核心架构，但也具有集成图形功能，因此被称为“G”。然而，这些特定的处理器标志着与类似的上一代 AMD 产品有所不同。虽然 Ryzen 3000G 系列 APU 被降级为最多 4 个内核且 CPU 和 GPU 时钟速度有些慢的入门级系统，但 Ryzen 5000G APU 通过提供多达 8 个内核，最大 CPU 加速时钟超过 4.4 GHz，更快的内存支持，以及显着更高的 GPU 时钟速度。然而，片上图形仍然基于 AMD 的 Vega GPU 架构。当然，AMD 在技术上确实发布了具有更高时钟和核心数的 Ryzen 和 Ryzen PRO 4000G 系列处理器，但这些都是 OEM 专用处理器，并不打算以零售方式出售。与标准的 Ryzen 5000 系列处理器相比，AMD 通过 Ryzen 7 5700G 和 Ryzen 5 5600G 瞄准更多主流应用。两款芯片均具有 65W TDP 和插槽，可直接插入 AMD 的插槽 AM4 平台。这些处理器基本上适用于任何基于 400 或 500 系列芯片组的主板，前提是制造商已使用必要的 AGESA 更新了主板的 BIOS。 POST/启动这些 APU 需要 AGESA 1.1.8.0，但建议使用 AGESA 1.2.0.3b（或更新版本）以获得最佳性能。锐龙7 5700G显然是这两款CPU的高端机型。它是一个 8 核/16 线程处理器，具有 4MB 的 L2 缓存和 16MB 的 L3。这些 CPU 内核与 8 CU Radeon Vega 图形引擎配合使用，并具有 24 条 PCIe Gen 3 连接通道。 5700G 的基本 CPU 时钟为 3.8GHz，最大升压时钟为 4.6GHz。处理器上的 GPU 可提升至 2GHz，与上一代 3000 系列 APU 的 1.4GHz 相比有了大幅提升。 Ryzen 5 5600G 具有 6 个 CPU 内核（12 个线程）和相应较小的 3MB L2 缓存，将性能降低了一个档次；但是，L3 缓存大小保持不变。 iGPU 也略有缩小，只有 7 个 CU。在 3.9GHz 时，5600G 的基本时钟比 5700G 高 100MHz，但其最大提升频率为 4.4GHz。 iGPU 的最大时钟速度也略低于 1.9GHz。 AMD 将这两款 APU 定位于 Intel Core i7-11700 和 Core i6-11600。 Ryzen 7 5700G 的建议价格为 359 美元，Ryzen 5 5600G 的建议价格为 259 美元，整整少了 100 美元。这两个价格都与英特尔的竞争产品完全一致。然而，从 Ryzen 7 5800X 和 Ryzen 5 5600X 的当前定价来看，这些新的 APU 的定价要低得多，考虑到它们的 CPU 内核数量相似，而且基本上是“免费”的 GPU，这很好。 Ryzen 7 5700G 和 Ryzen 5 5600G 可能是 AMD 的 Infinity Fabric。 Infinity Fabric 可以灵活地链接各种芯片上的 IP，结合 AMD 当前的 CPU 架构，基本上允许 AMD 将 Zen 3 放入设计中，作为上一代 Zen 2 架构的替代品。使用这些芯片，最多 8 核/16 线程 Zen 3 CCD 可连接到最多 8 个计算单元 Radeon Vega iGPU 和相邻的多媒体引擎。这些芯片是在台积电的 7nm 节点上生产的，处理器直接进入 AMD 现有的插槽 AM4 生态系统——同样，假设主板已经更新了必要的 AGESA（可能已经更新）。从外观上看，锐龙 7 5700G 和锐龙 5 5600G 与 AMD 目前所有的主流处理器没什么两样。包装、引脚、集成散热器和品牌都是一样的。只有印在顶部的型号发生了变化。启动最新版本的 CPU-Z 可以一瞥这些新的 Ryzen 5000G 系列处理器的配置。每个内核有 64KB（32KB 数据 + 32KB 指令）的 L1 缓存，每个内核有 512KB 的 L2 缓存，板载每个 CCD 有一个 16MB 的 L3 池。时钟和电压会根据负载而变化（稍后将在超频部分详细介绍），并且 5700G 和 5600G 之间的系列、型号、步进和版本是相同的。抛开所有这些，让我们看看这些最新的 APU 表现如何，好吗？

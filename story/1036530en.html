South Korean watchdog fines Facebook $6.1 million for sharing user info without consent
韩国监管机构未经同意就分享用户信息对Facebook罚款610万美元

2020-11-25 22:29:21

SEOUL (Reuters) - A South Korean agency for protecting personal information on Wednesday fined Facebook Inc 6.7 billion won ($6.06 million) and sought a criminal investigation for providing users’ personal information to other operators without consent.

首尔（路透社）-韩国一家保护个人信息的机构周三对Facebook Inc处以67亿韩元（约合606万美元）的罚款，并要求未经其同意将用户的个人信息提供给其他运营商的行为进行刑事调查。

The country’s Personal Information Protection Commission, launched in August this year, said in a statement it fined Facebook after a probe found that the personal information of least 3.3 million of the 18 million Facebook users in Korea were provided to operators other than Facebook without their knowledge, from May 2012 to June 2018.

该国于今年8月成立的个人信息保护委员会在一份声明中表示，在调查发现韩国1800万Facebook用户中，至少有330万个人信息是在不知情的情况下提供给了Facebook以外的运营商的，因此对Facebook处以罚款。 ，从2012年5月到2018年6月。

When someone uses another operator’s service through Facebook’s log-in, the personal information of the user’s Facebook friends were provided to other operators without their consent, the commission said.

该委员会表示，当某人通过Facebook登录使用其他运营商的服务时，该用户的Facebook朋友的个人信息会未经他们的同意而提供给其他运营商。

The commission said it will refer Facebook Ireland Ltd, the recipient of the fine, to the country’s prosecution for a criminal investigation.

该委员会表示，将把罚款接收人Facebook Ireland Ltd.移交给该国的检方，以进行刑事调查。

“We have cooperating as much as possible throughout the investigation process, we regret that the Personal Information Protection Commission has sought a criminal investigation,” a Seoul-based Facebook spokeswoman said in a statement, declining further comment as Facebook hasn’t yet fully reviewed the details of the decision.

总部位于首尔的Facebook发言人在一份声明中说：“我们在整个调查过程中进行了尽可能多的合作，我们很遗憾个人信息保护委员会已经进行了刑事调查。”由于Facebook尚未完全审查，该发言人拒绝发表进一步评论。决定的细节。

NYC Mayor demands CEOs end work from home as economy struggles
纽约市长要求首席执行官们在经济困难时结束在家工作

2022-02-20 16:05:47

New York City Mayor Eric Adams met with 100 chief executive officers, in an effort to cajole them “to get their workers back into the office to stimulate the city’s economy.”

纽约市市长埃里克·亚当斯（Eric Adams）会见了100名首席执行官，试图说服他们“让他们的员工重返办公室，以刺激纽约市的经济”

He told the business leaders it was time to get their workers back in offices, stating that the lack of people commuting into the Big Apple hurts the city’s economy. The mayor said, “We can’t send mixed messages,” by delaying the return to work dates. “We can’t keep kicking the can down the road.”

他告诉商界领袖，是时候让他们的员工回到办公室了，他说，没有人通勤到纽约会损害纽约市的经济。市长说，“我们不能发出混杂的信息”，推迟了返回工作的日期。“我们不能一直把罐子踢下去。”

"Let's start out with a three-day workweek, to let people see how safe it is to come back to work, then we cycle back into a five-day week," Adams said. He added, “Now is the time for us to get back. I’m hoping within the next few weeks the CEOs map out a real plan of  ‘this is when you need to come back.’”

"让'；让我们从一周工作三天开始，让人们看到重返工作岗位是多么安全，然后再回到五天工作周；亚当斯说。他补充道：“现在是我们回去的时候了。我希望在未来几周内，首席执行官们能制定出一个真正的计划，‘这是你需要回来的时候。’”

Adams explained his thought process. The local economy depends upon people coming into New York. "That accountant from a bank that sits in an office, it's not only him. It feeds our financial ecosystem. He goes to the cleaners to get his suits cleaned. He goes to the restaurant. He brings in a business traveler, which is 70% of our hotel occupancy. He buys a hot dog on our streets—I hope a vegan hot dog—but he participates in the economy."

亚当斯解释了他的思维过程。当地的经济依赖于进入纽约的人"一家银行的会计坐在办公室里，它'；不仅仅是他。它养活了我们的金融生态系统。他去洗衣店洗衣服。他去餐馆。他带来了一位商务旅行者，占我们酒店入住率的70%。他在我们的街道上买了一个热狗——我希望是纯素热狗，但他参与了经济"

Stephen Goldsmith, a former mayor of Indianapolis and deputy mayor of New York City, pointed out, “Anyone working in a city’s downtown can attest that on the rare days they actually venture to the office, the number of closed retailers they see and the absence of the previously familiar restaurant worker who provided them morning coffee or noon lunch service.” Goldsmith added, “When these office workers stay home in large numbers, their previous discretionary spending on food, dry cleaning, supplies and the like disappears from the urban core, as do the jobs of many.”

印第安纳波利斯前市长、纽约市副市长斯蒂芬·戈德史密斯指出，“任何在城市中心区工作的人都可以证明，在难得的几天里，他们真的去办公室，他们看到的关闭的零售商的数量，以及没有以前熟悉的餐厅工作人员为他们提供上午咖啡或中午午餐服务。”戈德史密斯补充道，“当这些办公室职员大量待在家中时，他们以前在食品、干洗、用品等方面的可自由支配开支，以及许多人的工作，都会从城市核心区消失。”

“It’s time to get back to work,” Adams said. “The time frame is now.” The challenge for him is that less than 30% of workers are returning to their offices, according to  Kastle Systems, a data security company. It's not going to be easy for Adams to get people to return.

“是时候回去工作了，”亚当斯说。“时间框架是现在。”据数据安全公司Kastle Systems称，他面临的挑战是，只有不到30%的员工返回办公室。它'；要亚当斯让人们回来并不容易。

We are in a blazing hot, tight job market. Companies can’t find enough people to fill their open headcount. Remote jobs are plentiful, according to LinkedIn’s Global Talent Trends Report. Check any job board and you’ll see “work from home” or “work from anywhere you’d like” opportunities dominating the listings. Many organizations are paying employees the same compensation—whether they continue to reside in New York or decide to leave for a better life in a state with lower taxes, affordable homes, sunny skies and quality public schools that aren’t forced to close down.

我们正处在一个火热、紧张的就业市场。公司找不到足够的人来填补空缺。根据LinkedIn的全球人才趋势报告，远程工作机会很多。查看任何工作板，你都会看到“在家工作”或“在任何你想在的地方工作”的机会在列表中占据主导地位。无论员工是否继续居住在纽约，还是决定离开纽约，在一个税收较低、住房负担得起、阳光明媚、公立学校质量良好、不会被迫关闭的州过上更好的生活，许多组织都在向员工支付同样的补偿。

Adams' goal makes sense. There is a big incentive for politicians, landlords, corporate executives and business owners to get commuters to return. Consider what would happen if workers don’t return to the Big Apple. It could cause a cascade of business closures. Without the throngs of workers commuting into New York, restaurants, bars, gyms, shops and stores may be forced to close down due to the lack of customers. The deserted landscape could create a vacuum in which crime, violence and open drug usage worsens. This would discourage people who were considering going to the office or tourists coming into town to enjoy a Broadway show and dinner.

亚当斯和#39；目标是有意义的。政客、房东、企业高管和企业主有很大的动力让通勤者返回。想想如果工人们不回到大苹果会发生什么事。这可能会导致一连串的企业倒闭。如果没有大批工人通勤进入纽约，餐馆、酒吧、健身房、商店和商店可能会因为缺少顾客而被迫关闭。这片荒芜的土地可能会造成一个真空，犯罪、暴力和公开吸毒会在其中恶化。这会让那些考虑去办公室的人或是来镇上享受百老汇演出和晚餐的游客望而却步。

The tax revenue will fall, which means that city officials will be forced to cut both public services and municipal workers. With fewer garbage collectors, firefighters, police officers, teachers and hospital personnel, the quality of life would suffer. The prior decisions made by Goldman Sachs, JPMorgan and Morgan Stanley telling their employees to return to the office may have also been prompted by a need to save the city from demise.

税收收入将下降，这意味着市政官员将被迫削减公共服务和市政工人。随着垃圾收集者、消防员、警察、教师和医院工作人员的减少，生活质量将受到影响。高盛（Goldman Sachs）、摩根大通（JPMorgan）和摩根士丹利（Morgan Stanley）此前做出的让员工重返办公室的决定，可能也是出于拯救这座城市免于灭亡的需要。

We’ve already seen an alarming increase in crime and violence in the City. Wall Street executives told younger bankers to return to the office, but they should be vigilant and careful. Bank of America suggested that bankers, brokers, traders and other personnel should keep a low profile. By avoiding dressing too fancy, wearing expensive jewelry and watches or prominently displaying the bank’s logo, they may go undetected and not catch the eye of a possible assailant. There have been horrendous acts of senseless violence perpetrated in the train stations, as well as the streets of the Big Apple.

我们已经看到这个城市的犯罪和暴力活动惊人地增加。华尔街高管告诉年轻的银行家们回到办公室，但他们应该保持警惕和谨慎。美国银行建议银行家、经纪人、交易员和其他人员保持低调。通过避免穿得太花哨，佩戴昂贵的珠宝和手表，或显著地展示银行的标识，他们可能会被发现，也不会引起可能的攻击者的注意。在火车站和纽约的街道上发生了骇人听闻的毫无意义的暴力行为。

If people don’t return en masse, Adams will have to figure out other ways to revitalize the city. If not, it could become a downward cycle of small business closures and growth in crime and violence, making potential returnees reconsider their options and seek out remote roles, so they don’t have to deal with these issues.

如果人们不集体返回，亚当斯将不得不想出其他方法来振兴这座城市。否则，它可能会成为一个小企业倒闭、犯罪和暴力增长的下行循环，让潜在的回国者重新考虑自己的选择，寻找遥远的角色，这样他们就不必处理这些问题。

On the positive side, New York City has faced serious problems in the past. In 1975, the City was heading toward bankruptcy. When asked for financial assistance, President Gerald Ford declared that he would veto any bill calling for "a federal bail-out of New York City" and instead proposed legislation that would make it easier for the city to go into bankruptcy. Famously, the New York Daily News ran a headline “ Ford to City: Drop Dead in 1975.” It took a while, but the  City ultimately rebounded. Hopefully, this will happen once again.

从积极的方面来看，纽约市过去面临着严重的问题。1975年，这座城市濒临破产。当被要求提供财政援助时，杰拉尔德·福特总统宣布他将否决任何要求"；纽约市的联邦纾困计划#34；相反，他们提出了一项立法，让这座城市更容易破产。著名的是，《纽约每日新闻》的标题是“福特到城市：1975年倒毙”这花了一段时间，但这座城市最终反弹了。希望这种情况会再次发生。

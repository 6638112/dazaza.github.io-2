俄勒冈州投票将所有毒品合法化

2020-11-04 18:57:01

Oregon became the first state in the nation to decriminalize the possession of all illegal drugs and also legalize the use of psilocybin—the active ingredient in hallucinogenic mushrooms—for mental health treatment, after voters passed a pair of ballot measures this week.

在选民本周通过了两项投票措施后，俄勒冈州成为美国第一个将持有所有非法药物合法化，并将裸盖菇素(致幻蘑菇中的活性成分)用于精神健康治疗合法化的州。

Both are the first of their kind in any U.S. state and represent the next frontier in the relaxation of drug laws beyond marijuana.

这两个州都是美国各州中第一个这样的州，代表着放宽毒品法律的下一个前沿，超越了大麻。

With results from 76% of precincts reporting early Wednesday morning, 59% of Oregonians approved Measure 110, the drug decriminalization referendum, and 56% voted for Measure 109 on psilocybin therapy, according to the Associated Press.

据美联社(The Associated Press)报道，周三清晨，76%的选区公布了结果，59%的俄勒冈州人赞成措施110，即毒品非刑事化公投，56%的人投票支持关于裸盖菇素治疗的措施109。

“There are a lot of parallels between what Oregon is trying to do now and what states were doing with marijuana 40 years ago,” said Robert Mikos, a law professor at Vanderbilt University who has written about marijuana legalization. “As long as the sky doesn’t fall in Oregon, some other states might follow suit.”

范德比尔特大学(Vanderbilt University)法学教授罗伯特·米科斯(Robert Mikos)写过一篇关于大麻合法化的文章，他说，“俄勒冈州现在试图做的事情，与40年前各州对大麻所做的事情有很多相似之处。”“只要俄勒冈州的天不塌下来，其他一些州可能会效仿。”

Also Tuesday, voters in the District of Columbia approved a measure decriminalizing psilocybin. With 61% of precincts there reporting, support was at 76%, according to the AP. Several cities previously passed similar measures, including Oakland and Santa Cruz in California, as well as Denver.

同样在周二，哥伦比亚特区的选民批准了一项将裸盖菇素合法化的措施。根据美联社的数据，那里61%的选区报告说，支持率为76%。之前有几个城市通过了类似的措施，包括加利福尼亚州的奥克兰和圣克鲁兹，以及丹佛。

Measures legalizing possession of marijuana for recreational or medical reasons passed in Arizona, Montana, New Jersey and South Dakota according to the AP.

据美联社报道，亚利桑那州、蒙大拿州、新泽西州和南达科他州通过了将出于娱乐或医疗原因持有大麻合法化的措施。

Oregon’s Measure 110 makes possession of any controlled substance, including heroin, cocaine or methamphetamines, a violation punishable by a maximum fine of $100 or a completed health assessment. It also establishes a new statewide drug treatment system funded in part by tax revenue from marijuana sales and state prison savings.

俄勒冈州的110号措施规定，拥有任何受控物质，包括海洛因、可卡因或冰毒，违反规定最高可被罚款100美元或完成健康评估。它还建立了一个新的全州戒毒系统，部分资金来自大麻销售的税收和州监狱储蓄。

The campaign in favor of Measure 110 raised $4.5 million and the one for Measure 109 raised $5.7 million, while opposing campaigns raised virtually nothing, according to campaign finance reports.

根据竞选财务报告，支持措施110的竞选活动筹集了450万美元，支持措施109的竞选活动筹集了570万美元，而反对的竞选活动几乎没有筹集到任何资金。

Proponents of Measure 110, including the Democratic Party of Oregon and the American Civil Liberties Union of Oregon, said people suffering with addiction should have greater access to rehabilitation and not face criminal punishment.

措施110的支持者，包括俄勒冈州民主党和俄勒冈州美国公民自由联盟(American Civil Liberties Union Of俄勒冈州)表示，患有毒瘾的人应该有更多的机会接受康复，而不是面临刑事处罚。

“We don’t have enough tools in our toolbox, there’s just no question about that,” said Democratic State Sen. Elizabeth Steiner Hayward, from Portland.

“我们的工具箱里没有足够的工具，这是毫无疑问的，”来自波特兰的民主党州参议员伊丽莎白·施泰纳·海沃德(Elizabeth Steiner Hayward)说。

Oregon was the first state to decriminalize the possession of cannabis, for similar reasons, in 1973.

1973年，出于类似的原因，俄勒冈州是第一个将持有大麻合法化的州。

Many of the state’s district attorneys and Republican politicians said the new measure was counterproductive because the threat of criminal penalties helped to push some addicts to accept treatment.

该州的许多地区检察官和共和党政客表示，新措施适得其反，因为刑事处罚的威胁帮助推动了一些吸毒者接受治疗。

Measure 109, meanwhile, gives the state public health department two years to develop regulations and create a program responsible for manufacturing and dispensing psilocybin to adults aged 21 or older seeking therapy with a trained facilitator for everything from post-traumatic stress disorder to nicotine dependence.

与此同时，第109号措施给了州公共卫生部两年的时间来制定法规，并创建一个项目，负责制造和分发裸盖菇素给21岁或21岁以上的寻求治疗的21岁或21岁以上的成年人，治疗从创伤后应激障碍到尼古丁依赖等各种问题都有训练有素的促进员。

“We’re going to take our time and do it right,” said Tom Eckert, a psychotherapist who was a chief sponsor of the bill along with his psychotherapist wife Sheri. “We’re in a mental health crisis and we need new treatment options.”

心理治疗师汤姆·埃克特(Tom Eckert)和他的心理治疗师妻子雪莉(Sheri)是该法案的主要发起人，他说：“我们会从容不迫地做好这件事。”“我们正处于精神健康危机之中，我们需要新的治疗选择。”

Like cannabis, the Drug Enforcement Administration classifies psilocybin as a Schedule 1 drug, meaning it isn’t approved for medical use and there’s a high potential for abuse and addiction.

和大麻一样，药品监督管理局将裸盖菇素归类为附表1药物，这意味着它没有被批准用于医疗用途，有很高的滥用和成瘾的可能性。

Psilocybin is a naturally occurring chemical in more than 200 species of fungi that can cause visual and auditory hallucinations and an inability to discern fantasy from reality.

裸盖菇素是一种天然存在于200多种真菌中的化学物质，可导致视觉和听觉幻觉，并无法区分幻想和现实。

Research has shown that it can reduce anxiety and stress among cancer patients and people diagnosed with PTSD. Some supporters of its medical use also say it can be helpful for anyone looking for help with a persistent mental health issue.

研究表明，它可以减轻癌症患者和被诊断为创伤后应激障碍的人的焦虑和压力。一些医疗用途的支持者也表示，它可以帮助任何寻求帮助的人解决持续的精神健康问题。

But the Oregon Psychiatric Physicians Association and the American Psychiatric Association opposed the measure, arguing that there isn’t enough evidence to support psilocybin’s use in mental health treatment.

但俄勒冈州精神科医生协会和美国精神病学协会反对这项措施，认为没有足够的证据支持裸盖菇素在精神健康治疗中的使用。

“Psilocybin research trials are in the early stages,” said OPPA President Nicole Cirino. “We need to know what type of mental illness it actually treats and what safety precautions, doses, follow up measures we need in place to use it safely and effectively.

“裸盖菇素的研究试验还处于早期阶段，”OPPA主席尼科尔·西里诺(Nicole Cirino)说。“我们需要知道它实际治疗的是什么类型的精神疾病，以及我们需要采取什么安全预防措施、剂量和后续措施来安全有效地使用它。

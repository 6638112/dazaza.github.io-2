How to write a resume that converts
如何编写转换的简历

2021-05-11 09:28:55

What does a great resume look like? How do you stand out? I’ve reviewed thousands of product manager resumes. Here’s the sad truth: most of them go straight to the shredder. To write a resume that converts, it helps to understand the psychology of your audience. Their #1 concern: can you do the job well?  Every step in the hiring process is engineered to de-risk you. There are two proven ways to do this starting with your resume:

什么伟大的简历是什么样的？你怎么脱颖而出？我已经审查了数千个产品经理恢复。这是悲伤的真理：他们中的大多数都是直接去碎纸机。要写转换的简历，它有助于了解观众的心理。他们的＃1涉及：你能做得好吗？招聘流程中的每一步都被设计成危险您。从您的简历开始，有两种经过两种方法可以执行此操作：

Ironically, the roles you want are rarely the ones you’re perfectly qualified for. The good news is that “qualify” is subjective and can be boosted with proven tactics. ‍

具有讽刺意味的是，您想要的角色很少是您完全合格的角色。好消息是“资格化”是主观的，可以通过经过验证的战术提升。

Mediocre resume is a laundry list of accomplishments, while a great resume only picks the all-time hits. It’s a highlight reel best contained to one page. If your audience can’t figure out if you qualify within 3 seconds, the answer defaults to no. Easy-to-skim resume respects their time, but also shows that you can identify what’s most important — a promising sign that you’ll be effective on the job. There are two types of experiences worth highlighting:

平庸简历是一份洗衣清单的成就，而伟大的简历只能选择历史胜利。它是一个最佳包含在一个页面的explight reel。如果您的观众无法弄清楚，如果您在3秒内符合资格，则答案默认为否。易于脱脂的简历尊重他们的时间，而且还表明你可以识别最重要的是什么 - 这是一个有效的迹象，你将对工作有效。有两种类型的体验值得突出显示：

Once you’ve selected the right raw materials, it’s time to refine. ‍

一旦你选择了正确的原材料，就是时候精炼了。

Take the verbs in the job description, and work it into your experience. Examples for product-related roles: define requirements, develop roadmap, analyze metrics, etc. Many verbs are cross-disciplinary, which means you have relevant experience even if you don’t have the title yet. Before I was a PM, I analyzed numbers and developed narratives which helped me get adjacent roles in user research and analytics. Even better, tie verbs to outcomes.  ‍

在职位描述中夺取动词，并将其融入您的体验。与产品相关角色的示例：定义要求，开发路线图，分析度量等。许多动词是跨学科，这意味着即使您还没有标题，您也有相关的经验。在我下午之前，我分析了数字和发达的叙述，帮助我在用户研究和分析中获得邻近角色。甚至更好，将动词绑在结果中。

What happened to the requirements you defined? What value did your analysis deliver? Quantify wherever possible. Examples of how to do this:

您定义的要求发生了什么？您的分析提供了哪些值？尽可能地量化。如何执行此操作：

Not everything has a quantifiable outcome, but the ones that do (or can be estimated) are more powerful on a resume because they prove that you didn’t just work, you created value. Here’s the underlying theme: showing beats telling. Most resumes lean too heavily on telling (“experienced professional”, “strategic thinker”, “resourceful self-starter”), but talk is cheap. Actions and outcomes are rare, so they help you stand out. The best way to show that you are “strategic” and “experienced” is to... ‍

并非一切都具有可量化的结果，但是（或可以估计）在恢复上更强大的是，因为他们证明了你不仅仅是工作，你创造了价值。这是潜在的主题：显示击败告诉。大多数恢复依赖于讲述（“经验丰富的专业”，“战略思想家”，“资源丰富的自我启动”），但谈话很便宜。行动和结果很少见，所以他们帮助你脱颖而出。表明你是“战略”和“经验丰富的”的最佳方式是......

Culprit #2 is long explanations about some unknown company. Instead, share brief context as it relates to your contribution. Example: Led retention for XYZ-backed consumer subscription startup. Focus on what you did, not what the company does (even if you’re the founder)! Culprit #3 is wasted real estate on irrelevant experience. Instead, either delete entirely (especially if it was <1 year) or stick to the bare essentials (your role + time you spent). One line should suffice, enough to pre-empt questions about where the time went. ‍

Culprit＃2是关于一些未知公司的长途解释。相反，在与您的贡献相关的情况下分享简要的背景。示例：LED保留XYZ支持的消费者订阅启动。专注于你所做的，而不是公司所做的（即使你是创始人）！ Culprit＃3是浪费的无关经验的房地产。相反，完全删除（特别是如果它是＆lt; 1年）或坚持裸露的必需品（你的角色+时间）。一条线应该足够，足以预先抢占时间的问题。

A strong track record gives people a reason to bet on you even if you lack relevant experience. Promising signs:

一个强大的轨道记录，即使您缺乏相关的经验，人们即使缺乏相关的经验也会有理由下注您。有前途的迹象：

This is why it’s best to not linger too long at a place you know is a dead-end. Besides draining your energy, it also starts dragging down your perceived momentum which unfairly affects your future trajectory. Your work and colleagues today become the lego blocks for your future. It’s no coincidence that employee referrals is a preferred hiring channel, or that most startups begin with people who worked with each other before (Google + Stanford PhD, Netflix + Pure Software, Twitter + Odeo). De-risked relationships make everything easier. ‍

这就是为什么最好不要在你知道的地方徘徊太长时间是死者。除了排出你的能量外，它还开始拖累你的感知动力，这是不公平地影响你未来的轨迹。今天你的工作和同事成为你未来的乐高街区。员工推荐是一个首选的招聘渠道并不巧合，或者大多数初创公司开始于之前的人（Google + Stanford Phd，Netflix + Pure软件，Twitter + Odeo）。失败的关系使一切都更加容易。

Polishing your resume is useful, but needing a resume is a rookie problem. With time, your resume should wane in importance compared to your growing body of work and reputation. The latter eventually creates more opportunities than you can say yes to. Years ago, nobody would’ve talked to me without a resume. I had no track record! These days, my resume is a formality, if it’s used at all. Advice when you’re risky and unproven:

抛光简历是有用的，但需要简历是一个新秀问题。随着时间的推移，与您不断增长的工作和声誉相比，您的简历应该在重要的同时。后者最终会产生更多的机会，比你对“是的。多年前，没有简历，没有人会跟我说话。我没有跟踪记录！这些天，我的简历是一种形式，如果它全部使用。当您有风险和未经证实时的建议：

Your energy precedes anything you do - try a lot of things and take note of what you like and what comes naturally to you

你的能量在你做的任何事情之前 - 尝试很多事情，并注意你喜欢什么，自然是什么

Your resume is only as strong as your outcomes, which is directly correlated with doing things repeatedly and learning from mistakes

您的简历只与您的结果一样强烈，这与反复做事直接相关，并从错误中学习

Cut the fluff, and focus on how you’ve solved similar problems, or have a strong track record of learning fast, ideally both!

切断绒毛，专注于您如何解决类似的问题，或者快速学习的强大记录，理想情况下！

New Online Tool Reveals Raphael’s Sistine Chapel Cartoons in Stunning Detail
新的在线工具以惊人的细节展示了拉斐尔的西斯廷教堂卡通

2021-01-29 05:57:19

Michelangelo’s Sistine Chapel ceiling frescoes rank among the world’s most instantly recognizable artworks. But few know that 500 years ago, a cycle of tapestries by an equally renowned Renaissance master also adorned the Vatican City chapel’s walls. Raphael designed the works, which were created in Pieter van Aelst’s Brussels workshop between 1515 and 1521; woven with silver and gold threads, they tell stories from the lives of Saints Peter and Paul, two founding fathers of the Roman Catholic Church.

米开朗基罗（Michelangelo）的西斯廷教堂（Sistine Chapel）天花板壁画是世界上最易辨认的艺术品之一。但是，很少有人知道500年前，同样著名的文艺复兴时期的大师还用挂毯装饰了梵蒂冈教堂的墙壁。拉斐尔（Raphael）设计了这些作品，这些作品是1515年至1521年之间在彼得·范·阿尔斯特（Pieter van Aelst）在布鲁塞尔的工作室中创作的；他们编织着金线和银线，讲述了罗马天主教会的两位开国元勋圣彼得和保罗的生平。

Raphael, who died in 1520 at just 37 years old, may have never seen all the completed tapestries in person. But the painter would have been intimately familiar with their “ cartoons,” or preparatory drawings. As Hannah McGovern notes for the Art Newspaper, Raphael revised and finalized his designs for the tapestries on roughly 16.5- by 11.5-foot canvases, each comprised of more than 200 pieces of paper glued together.

拉斐尔（Raphael）于1520年去世，享年37岁，他可能从未亲眼目睹所有完成的挂毯。但是画家本来应该非常熟悉他们的“卡通”或预备图。正如汉娜·麦戈文（Hannah McGovern）在《艺术报》上所指出的那样，拉斐尔（Raphael）修改并最终确定了他在大约16.5 x 11.5英尺画布上的挂毯设计，每个画布由200多张粘在一起的纸组成。

During the 16th century, the Sistine Chapel cartoons were cut into strips and traded around Europe. The future Charles I purchased the drawings in 1623 and brought them back to his home country of England. In 1865, Queen Victoria loaned the drawings to the South Kensington Museum, now the Victoria and Albert (V&A), where they have remained ever since, reported Mark Brown for the Guardian in 2019.

在16世纪，西斯廷教堂的漫画被切成条状并在欧洲各地交易。未来的查尔斯一世在1623年购买了这些图纸，并将它们带回他的祖国英格兰。 1865年，维多利亚女王（Victoria Queen）将图纸借给了南肯辛顿博物馆（South Kensington Museum），现在是维多利亚和阿尔伯特（V＆amp; A），此后一直保存在那里，据马克·布朗（Mark Brown）在2019年为《卫报》报道。

Though the London museum is currently closed due to Covid-19, art enthusiasts can now explore Raphael’s cartoons in-depth through an online V&A project, Explore the Raphael Cartoons. Complete with an essay on the works’ long history and high-resolution, interactive scans of the cartoons, the hub allows viewers to see hidden details in Raphael’s masterpieces up close.

尽管由于Covid-19，伦敦博物馆目前已关闭，但艺术爱好者现在可以通过在线V＆amp; A项目“探索拉斐尔卡通”深入探索拉斐尔的卡通。该中心提供有关作品的悠久历史和高分辨率，动画交互式扫描的文章，使观众可以近距离观看Raphael杰作中的隐藏细节。

The V&A partnered with the Factum Foundation to create the high-resolution color, infrared and 3-D scans in 2019. And last year, in honor of the 500th anniversary of Raphael’s death, the museum refurbished the cartoons’ gallery, known as the Raphael Court, by repainting the walls, replacing light fixtures and taking other steps to make the cartoons “more visible and legible to in-person visitors,” as project curator Ana Debenedetti tells the Art Newspaper. (When the V&A reopens, viewers will be able to scan QR codes in the gallery to access a number of interactive features on their phones.)

V＆amp; A与Factum Foundation合作，于2019年创建了高分辨率的彩色，红外和3D扫描。去年，为了纪念拉斐尔（Raphael）逝世500周年，该博物馆对卡通画廊进行了翻新，如项目策展人安娜·德贝内代蒂（Ana Debenedetti）告诉《艺术报》，拉斐尔法院通过重新粉刷墙壁，更换灯具并采取其他步骤使这些动画片“对现场参观者更清晰可见”。 （当V＆amp; A重新打开时，观众将能够扫描图库中的QR码以访问其手机上的许多交互式功能。）

Some of the scans revealed new information about the works. Compared with previous, smaller drafts of The Miraculous Draught of Fishes, for instance, new images revealed that Raphael made slight adjustments to Jesus’ forehead and eyes in his final underdrawing. The design shows Simon—who would later be renamed Peter—wearing a blue tunic and kneeling before Jesus, who has just performed a miracle.

一些扫描揭示了有关作品的新信息。例如，与以前的《鱼的奇迹草稿》较小的草稿相比，新的图像显示，拉斐尔在最后画画时对耶稣的额头和眼睛做了些微调整。设计显示，西蒙（后来改名为彼得）穿着蓝色上衣，跪在刚刚表现出奇迹的耶稣面前。

Pope Leo X commissioned Raphael to design the silk and wool tapestries in 1515. The painter and his studio worked quickly to complete the ten cartoons, each of which depicts a different biblical scene, in just 18 months, according to the Art Newspaper.

教皇利奥十世（Pope Leo X）于1515年委托拉斐尔（Raphael）设计丝绸和羊毛挂毯。根据《艺术报》的报道，画家和他的工作室很快完成了十幅漫画，每幅漫画在18个月内描绘了不同的圣经场景。

Each tapestry design “is remarkable for the way it synthesizes a complex message, chosen to bolster papal authority, into a clear, harmonious ensemble—which Raphael had to design in reverse, as though looking at a mirror, because he knew it would be flipped on the weaver’s loom,” writes Alastair Sooke for the Telegraph.

每个挂毯设计“对于将一个复杂的信息（选择用来增强罗马教皇的权威）综合为一个清晰，和谐的合奏的方式来说都是非同寻常的-拉斐尔不得不反向设计，就像看着镜子一样，因为他知道镜子会被翻转在织布机上”，Alastair Sooke在《电讯报》上写道。

Thanks to the online tool, viewers can zoom in on individual brushstrokes, cracks and wrinkles in the paper, charcoal underdrawings, and tiny pinpricks around the outlines of figures. Weavers made these small holes as they worked to translate Raphael’s painting into the medium of tapestry. When he was designing the work, Raphael “challenged the weavers in Brussels to, as it were, paint with threads,” says Debenedetti to the Telegraph.

借助在线工具，观众可以放大各个笔触，纸张上的裂缝和皱纹，木炭底纹以及人物轮廓周围的细小针刺。织布工在将Raphael的绘画转变为挂毯的过程中做了这些小孔。 Dephaedetti在接受《电讯报》采访时说，当他设计作品时，拉斐尔“挑战了布鲁塞尔的织布匠，使之成为带有线漆的地方。”

The 3-D scans of the cartoons’ surfaces “take you back 500 years, when the last people to see that were Raphael and his team of apprentices,” Debenedetti tells the Art Newspaper.

Debenedetti告诉《艺术报》，对动画片表面进行3D扫描“将您带回500年前，当时最后一次看到的人是拉斐尔和他的徒弟。”

She adds, “Emotionally, it’s something we’ve never been able to offer visitors before.”

她补充说：“从情感上讲，这是我们之前从未提供给游客的东西。”

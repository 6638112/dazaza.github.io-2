Six weeks after Facebook vowed to ban Holocaust denial, it’s still easy to find: Anti-Semitic content widely available on social media platform, in contrast to QAnon conspiracy pages, which were removed; company says vast majority of anti-Jewish pages censored
Facebook发誓要禁止否认大屠杀六周后，仍然很容易找到：反犹太内容在社交媒体平台上广泛存在，与QAnon阴谋页面相比已被删除；公司说，绝大多数反犹太人的页面都受到审查

2020-11-29 15:31:20

JTA — As of Wednesday afternoon, one of the first results in a Facebook search for “Holohoax” — a term popular with Holocaust deniers — was a post decrying “Zionist White Jewish Supremacist Child murdering Apartheid State, Talmudic Satanic Holohoax promoters.” Right below it was a video, posted by a group with more than 6,000 followers, captioned “Research: Holohoax and Jew world order.” These results showed up six weeks after Facebook announced that it was banning Holocaust denial and distortion across its platforms, including the hugely popular Instagram social media platform. When it made the announcement, the company pledged that it would direct users to resources that provide credible information about the Holocaust. Those resources have yet to appear on the site. In contrast, similar resources do appear on searches for the QAnon conspiracy theory, which Facebook announced it would ban approximately one week before announcing the ban on Holocaust denial. And QAnon groups appear to have been removed after that announcement. It’s not just “Holohoax.” A page for the Institute for Historical Review, a Holocaust-denying organization masquerading as an academic center, is active and has more than 1,000 likes as of Wednesday afternoon. A recent post on the page links to an article titled, “Israel’s power is unlimited.” A post from earlier this month laments the “great loss of life and terrible suffering endured by the German people during the Second World War.” A box on the side of that page labeled “Related Pages” directs users to several pages that either deny the Holocaust or spew blatantly anti-Semitic rhetoric. The Institute for Historical Review group and its related pages were first reported by the tech publication The Markup. One, with 531 likes, is called “Goy Lives Matter” and features a stream of blatantly anti-Semitic posts and links. One example is a link to a website purporting to give information about the “The Jewish Ethnic Cleansing Of Europeans.” “Goy,” a Hebrew term that means non-Jew, has been appropriated by white supremacists. Another far more popular page, with more than 11,000 likes, is called “Open Borders for Israel,” a trolling phrase that has become a slogan among white supremacists. The slogan implies that Jews are hypocritical because white supremacists believe Jews are trying to flood America with non-white immigrants, but Israel does not have a liberal immigration policy. A version of the page’s logo includes Pepe the Frog, a cartoon appropriated by white supremacists. One of its posts is a meme featuring a visibly Jewish man saying “America is a nation of immigrants” and then, when someone asks him why Israel doesn’t absorb refugees, responding, “You’re being antisemitic.” Another post featuring a picture of Harvey Weinstein saying, “Israel won’t need to open its borders to the world to let Weinstein in. It only lets Jews in.” The pages and posts — and others like them — show that more than a month after it pledged to remove content that denies or distorts the Holocaust, Facebook has yet to do so. The persistence of Holocaust denial on Facebook also underscores the challenge social media giants face as they try to fulfill recent pledges to root out hate and misinformation from their platforms.

JTA-截至星期三下午，在Facebook上搜索“ Holohoax”（此词在大屠杀否认者中很流行）的首批结果之一，是在谴责“犹太复国主义白人犹太至上主义儿童谋杀种族隔离国家，Talmudic Satanic Holohoax发起人”。在该视频的正下方是一个视频，该视频由一个拥有6,000多个关注者的小组发布，标题为“研究：头颈部和犹太人的世界秩序”。这些结果在Facebook宣布禁止其平台（包括广受欢迎的Instagram社交媒体平台）上的大屠杀否认和歪曲之后的六周。在宣布这一消息时，该公司承诺将引导用户使用能够提供有关大屠杀的可靠信息的资源。这些资源尚未出现在网站上。相比之下，在搜寻QAnon阴谋论时确实出现了类似的资源，Facebook宣布将在宣布禁止大屠杀的禁令之前大约一周禁止其进行。该公告发布后，QAnon组似乎已被删除。不只是“恶作剧”。伪装成学术中心的否认大屠杀的组织历史回顾研究所的页面活跃，截至周三下午，有1000多个赞。页面上的最新帖子链接到标题为“以色列的力量是无限的”的文章。本月早些时候的一篇帖子感叹“第二次世界大战期间德国人民遭受的巨大生命损失和可怕痛苦”。该页面侧面的框标有“相关页面”，可将用户定向到拒绝否认大屠杀或大肆宣扬反犹太言论的几页。历史回顾研究所小组及其相关页面首先由科技出版物The Markup报道。其中有531个赞，被称为“ Goy Lives Matter”，其中包含一系列公然反犹太主义的帖子和链接。一个示例是指向一个网站的链接，该网站旨在提供有关“欧洲犹太人清洗”的信息。白人至上主义者将“ Goy”（希伯来语）这个词意为非犹太人。另一个受欢迎程度更高的页面，有超过11,000个赞，被称为“以色列的开放边界”，这个通俗易懂的短语已成为白人至上主义者的口号。该口号暗示犹太人是虚伪的，因为白人至上主义者认为犹太人正试图向非白人移民充斥美国，但以色列没有宽松的移民政策。该页面徽标的一个版本包括“白人青蛙”（Pepe the Frog），这是白人至上主义者所使用的动画片。它的一个帖子是一个模因，上面有一个明显的犹太男子说：“美国是一个移民国家”，然后，当有人问他为什么以色列不吸收难民时，回答：“你在反犹太。”另一张贴着哈维·温斯坦（Harvey Weinstein）的照片的帖子说：“以色列不需要向世界开放边界就可以让温斯坦进入。它只允许犹太人进入。”这些页面和帖子以及其他类似的内容表明，在承诺删除否认或歪曲大屠杀内容的一个多月后，Facebook仍未这样做。大屠杀否认在Facebook上的持续存在也突显了社交媒体巨头在努力履行最近的承诺以从其平台中消除仇恨和错误信息时面临的挑战。

Spokespeople for Facebook, as well as a Jewish organization that’s working with it on these issues, say the social media giant is working hard to implement the policy. But they caution that it will take more time to refine artificial intelligence tools and create training materials for human moderators that can assist in recognizing Holocaust denial.

Facebook以及与之合作处理这些问题的犹太人组织的发言人表示，这家社交媒体巨头正在努力实施这项政策。但是他们警告说，将需要更多时间来完善人工智能工具，并为人类主持人创建培训材料，以帮助人们认识到对大屠杀的否认。

A spokesperson for Facebook said the company catches the vast majority of hate speech on the platform. The spokesperson told JTA that the company has 15,000 people reviewing content to monitor for hate speech and other violations. According to the company’s data, Facebook detects 95 percent of hate speech posts before users report them. And another recent report by the company says that from July to September, hate speech accounted for only 10 or 11 out of every 10,000 times someone viewed content on Facebook. (Given the billions of users Facebook has, that still results in a potentially enormous number of people viewing hate speech.)

Facebook的一位发言人说，该公司在该平台上捕获了绝大多数仇恨言论。该发言人告诉JTA，该公司有15,000人正在审核内容，以监视仇恨言论和其他违法行为。根据该公司的数据，Facebook在用户举报仇恨言论之前将其检测到95％。该公司最近的另一份报告说，从7月到9月，仇恨言论仅占人们浏览Facebook内容的10,000次的10或11。 （鉴于Facebook有数十亿用户，这仍然导致潜在的大量用户查看仇恨言论。）

“Detecting hate speech is not only a difficult challenge, it’s an evolving one,” the spokesperson wrote. “A new piece of hate speech might not resemble previous examples because it references a new trend or some new news story. We’ve built new tools so they can scale to future challenges as well as present ones. With more flexible and adaptive [artificial intelligence], we’re confident we can continue to make real progress.”

发言人写道：“检测仇恨言论不仅是一项艰巨的挑战，而且是一个不断发展的挑战。” “新的仇恨言论可能与以前的示例不同，因为它引用了新趋势或新新闻。我们已经构建了新工具，以便它们可以应对未来以及当前的挑战。有了更灵活，更具适应性的[人工智能]，我们相信我们将继续取得真正的进步。”

The spokesperson said the company has no further information on when or how it will direct users to credible external materials about the Holocaust. Facebook likewise said it would not detail its training materials or criteria for determining what constitutes Holocaust denial, out of concern that that would allow bigots to “game the system.” Facebook’s “Community Standards” likewise do not specify what counts as Holocaust denial or distortion.

发言人说，该公司没有进一步的信息，关于何时，如何将用户引导到有关大屠杀的可靠外部材料。 Facebook同样表示，出于担心将使顽固分子“玩弄系统”的担忧，将不会详述其培训材料或标准来确定什么构成大屠杀否认。 Facebook的“社区标准”同样未指定被视为大屠杀的否认或歪曲。

Facebook has been more actively enforcing at least one other recently announced policy. On October 6, six days before it banned Holocaust denial, Facebook banned groups and pages promoting QAnon, the pro-Trump conspiracy theory with anti-Semitic overtones. Just 15 days later, the company announced that it had put links in place to credible information that users now see when they search for QAnon. No such links exist, as of yet, regarding Holocaust denial.

Facebook一直在积极地执行至少另一项最近宣布的政策。 10月6日，在禁止否认大屠杀的六天前，Facebook禁止宣传QAnon的团体和页面，QAnon是特朗普的反犹太主义阴谋论。仅仅15天后，该公司宣布已将指向可信信息的链接放置到位，这些信息现在用户在搜索QAnon时便会看到。迄今为止，还没有关于否认大屠杀的这种联系。

That Facebook is banning Holocaust denial at all is itself a major shift. Two years ago, Mark Zuckerberg told the tech publication Recode that he saw Holocaust denial as a lack of knowledge as opposed to an intentional expression of anti-Semitism. “I don’t believe that our platform should take that down because I think there are things that different people get wrong,” he said.

Facebook完全禁止否认大屠杀本身就是一个重大转变。两年前，马克·扎克伯格（Mark Zuckerberg）告诉科技出版物《 Recode》，他认为否认大屠杀是因为缺乏知识，而不是有意表达反犹太主义。他说：“我不认为我们的平台应该降低这一水平，因为我认为有些事情会导致不同的人弄错。”

Zuckerberg’s statement sparked an outcry among Holocaust scholars. Earlier this year, a coalition of civil rights groups led by the Anti-Defamation League and NAACP organized a boycott of Facebook ads that grew to include more than 1,000 companies.

扎克伯格的声明引起了大屠杀学者的强烈抗议。今年早些时候，由反诽谤联盟和美国全国有色人种协进会领导的民权组织组成的联合组织抵制了Facebook广告，该广告已发展到包括1000多家公司。

Now, in the face of that activism as well as studies showing that anti-Semitism is rising while knowledge of the Holocaust is decreasing, Facebook has done an about-face. The company now views Holocaust denial as willful anti-Semitism, and before announcing the ban on Holocaust denial, Facebook said it had banned more than 250 white supremacist groups.

现在，面对这种激进主义以及研究表明反犹太主义在增加，而对大屠杀的了解却在减少，Facebook做了一个转折。该公司现在将否认大屠杀定为故意的反犹太主义，在宣布禁止大屠杀之前，Facebook表示已禁止250多个白人至上主义组织。

“Denying the Holocaust is not just getting your facts wrong,” Monika Bickert, Facebook’s head of global policy management, said in a video interview last month with the World Jewish Congress. “We know that Holocaust denial is used as a way to actually attack and stoke hatred against Jewish people.”

Facebook全球政策管理负责人莫妮卡·比克特（Monika Bickert）在上个月接受世界犹太人大会的视频采访中说：“否认大屠杀不仅是在弄错你的事实，” “我们知道，否认大屠杀是一种对犹太人进行攻击和煽动仇恨的方式。”

Yfat Barak-Cheney, an official at the World Jewish Congress who has worked closely with Facebook on the Holocaust denial policy, said she believes the company is sincere about banning Holocaust denial. She expects the policy to be fleshed out, at the soonest, in a few months, and says her organization is in near-daily consultation with Facebook about it.

世界犹太人大会的官员伊法特·巴拉克·切尼（Yfat Barak-Cheney）与Facebook紧密合作，共同应对大屠杀否认政策。她说，她认为该公司对禁止大屠杀否认有诚意。她希望该政策能在几个月内尽快充实，并表示她的组织正在与Facebook每天就此进行磋商。

“I think it’s a bit early to make these reports and look into this,” said Barak-Cheney, the World Jewish Congress’ director of international affairs. “The announcement is there. It’s not something they are trying to avoid doing. They are very serious about it. They’re definitely dedicating resources to it.”

世界犹太人大会国际事务主任巴拉克-切尼说：“我认为现在做出这些报告并进行调查还为时过早。” “公告在那里。他们并不是要避免这样做。他们对此非常认真。他们肯定会为此投入资源。”

As the election season heated up earlier this year and public pressure mounted on tech companies to confront hate and misinformation, several large social media sites announced bans on conspiracy theories or different forms of bigotry.

随着今年初选举季节的升温以及科技公司面对仇恨和错误信息的压力越来越大，一些大型社交媒体网站宣布禁止阴谋论或不同形式的偏执狂。

Earlier in the year, along with banning QAnon, Facebook also said it was cracking down on other anti-Semitic conspiracy theories. TikTok banned white supremacist content in October. YouTube banned QAnon in October as well. In response to the stepped-up action against hate groups, many far-right activists moved their center of activity to Telegram, an encrypted messaging and social media app, and more recently to Parler, which bills itself as a social network without restrictions on speech.

今年早些时候，在禁止QAnon的同时，Facebook还表示，正在打击其他反犹太阴谋论。 TikTok在10月禁止白人至上主义者的内容。 YouTube也在10月份禁止了QAnon。为了应对针对仇恨团体的加紧行动，许多极右翼的活动家将其活动中心移至加密的消息和社交媒体应用Telegram，最近又移至Parler，后者将自己标记为不受限制的社交网络。

In October, Twitter also said it would be banning Holocaust denial — though two weeks later, Twitter CEO Jack Dorsey told a Senate hearing, regarding Holocaust denial, that “we don’t have a policy against that type of misleading information.”

10月，Twitter还表示将禁止否认大屠杀-尽管两周后，Twitter CEO杰克·多尔西（Jack Dorsey）在参议院听证会上就否认大屠杀问题表示：“我们没有针对此类误导性信息的政策。”

And Facebook’s policies don’t always result in blocking haters from the site. According to a recent report by two groups that monitor extremism, Facebook and Instagram “directly host neo-Nazi networks with over 80,000 followers,” some of whom use the site to sell merchandise. After the report was covered in the Guardian, Facebook said it removed the neo-Nazis.

而且，Facebook的政策并不总是会导致阻止该网站的仇恨者。根据两个监测极端主义的团体的最新报告，Facebook和Instagram“直接托管着拥有80,000多个追随者的新纳粹网络”，其中一些人使用该网站销售商品。在《卫报》报道该报道后，Facebook说已删除了新纳粹分子。

“For too long Facebook has chosen to respond to hate on their platform only when there is enough public outcry over it,” Anti-Defamation League CEO Jonathan Greenblatt tweeted about the report. “Case in point: Facebook knowingly hosted this neo-Nazi network for years.”

“很长一段时间以来，Facebook选择在平台上引起公众强烈抗议时才对平台上的仇恨做出回应，”反诽谤联盟首席执行官乔纳森·格林布拉特（Jonathan Greenblatt）在推特上发布了该报告。 “恰当的例子：Facebook有意主持了这个新纳粹网络多年。”

But Barak-Cheney said that the problem was one she expected Facebook to tackle. While she doubts that Facebook will be able to detect all Holocaust denial, she expects the platform to find and prohibit “most of it.”

但是巴拉克-切尼说，这个问题是她期望Facebook解决的问题。尽管她怀疑Facebook是否能够检测到所有的大屠杀否认，但她希望该平台能够发现并禁止“大部分”。

“It’s hard to detect, and it changes all the time,” she said. “All of it is going to be hard, but it’s not going to be a static policy.”

她说：“很难检测到，并且一直在变化。” “所有这一切都会很艰难，但这并不是一个静态的政策。”

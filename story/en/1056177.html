Trump used dark patterns to trick supporters into donating millions
特朗普使用黑暗模式来欺骗支持者捐赠数百万

2021-04-05 07:16:53

When Donald Trump won the 2016 presidential election, he didn’t stop asking his supporters for money. The emails kept coming in. But by the end of his four years in office, according to a new report in The New York Times, they had begun to look an awful lot like a scam.

当唐纳德特朗普赢得2016年总统选举时，他并没有停止向他的支持者询问金钱。根据纽约时报的一份新报告，通过在办公室的四年结束时，电子邮件进来。根据纽约时报的一份新报告，他们已经开始看起来像骗局那样真正的地段。

By June 2020, the Trump campaign had begun using dark patterns, computer interfaces designed to trick users, to automatically sign up campaign contributors to donate far more money than they had intended — recurring monthly donations, recurring weekly donations, even a one-time surprise “money bomb” per month — by pre-checking the checkboxes for each option, burying the fine print under paragraphs of bold text, and forcing his supporters to wade through it all and opt out if they wanted to make a simple donation.

到2020年6月，特朗普竞选已经开始使用黑暗的模式，旨在欺骗用户的计算机接口，自动注册比赛的捐助者的捐助者，而不是他们预期的每月捐款，经常运转的每周捐款，即使是一次性惊喜“金钱炸弹”每月 - 通过预先检查每个选项的复选框，根据大胆的文本段落，并强迫他的支持者浏览它，并选择他们想要简单的捐款。

One Kansas City supporter who donated $500 saw his account tapped for $3,000 that month. Another realized their $990 donation had become $8,000. Banks and a major credit card company told the NYT that dealing with fraud claims for WinRed, the conservative fundraising site processing these payments, had started to become a significant percentage of their daily workload. A Trump spokeperson admitted at least $19.7 million worth of its transactions had been disputed. And those are just from people who bothered to dispute the charges.

一个堪萨斯城的支持者捐赠了500美元的账户，以3000美元的价格看到他的账户。另一个意识到他们的990美元捐款已成为8,000美元。银行和一名主要信用卡公司告诉NYT，处理欺诈索赔，保守筹款现场加工这些付款，已开始成为其日常工作量的显着比例。特朗普吐具委员会承认其价值至少为1970万美元的交易。那些就是那些困扰争执的人。

There’s far more in the full story, like how WinRed pocketed $5 million worth of payment processing fees from those who asked for refunds, but the good news is that maybe, just maybe, people are finally waking up to the dangers that dark patterns pose.

在完整的故事中，比那些要求退款的人的胜利的支付处理费用更加困扰，但好消息是可能，只许是人们终于唤醒了黑暗模式姿势的危险。

California just passed a landmark privacy bill banning dark patterns last month. Washington state is attempting one as well. Sens. Mark Warner (D-VA), Deb Fischer (R-NE) and Rep. Lisa Blunt Rochester (D-DE) are planning to reintroduce the DETOUR Act in the next congressional session, according to our sister site Recode — and Recode spoke to acting FTC chair Rebecca Slaughter about her plans to address dark patterns as well.

加利福尼亚刚刚通过了上个月禁止禁止黑暗模式的地标隐私法案。华盛顿州也在尝试一个。根据我们的姐妹网站重新编码的情况谈到代理FTC主席抢劫屠杀，关于她的计划也解决了黑暗模式。

In the meanwhile, you’ll find dark patterns wherever you look on the web, perhaps nowhere more prominently than the privacy warnings you’ll find on every website you visit these days that warn you about the cookies they collect, in order to comply with EU privacy laws. In far too many cases, there’s no obvious way to opt out of that data collection — instead, there’s a nice bright button that you can press to just give up your data and move on.

与此同时，无论您在哪里都在何处，您都会发现黑暗的模式，也许在您访问的每个网站上都会更加突出地欣赏您访问的每个网站，以便您向他们收集的饼干发出警告，以便遵守欧盟隐私法。在太多情况下，没有明显的方式选择退出该数据收集 - 相反，有一个很好的明亮按钮，您可以按下只是放弃数据并继续前进。

‘Eliminate Every Superfluous Word’: The New Documentary, Hemingway
“消除每个多余的词”：新纪录片，海明威

2021-04-04 18:21:38

His poor head, you keep thinking as you watch Ken Burns and Lynn Novick’s Hemingway. His poor bloody head. Even the corroded voice of Peter Coyote, whose narration is a study in American understatement, goes up a semitone as Hemingway—in a plane crash in Uganda, this time—sustains “yet another major head trauma.” Nine in total, spaced throughout his adult life. And that doesn’t include whatever shots he may have taken while he was boxing or drunkenly fistfighting. War to war, wife to wife, novel to novel, in all of his adventurings and embracings and abandonings, his most constant mistress may have been concussion.

他贫穷的头，你在看肯烧和林恩诺克的海明威时，你一直在思考。他可怜的血腥头。即使是彼得·土狼的腐蚀声音，其叙述是美国轻描淡写的一项研究，在乌干达的飞机崩溃中，在海明道上升，在乌干达的飞机崩溃中，这次 - 维持“另一个主要的头部创伤”。总共九个，在他的成年生活中间间隔开。这并不包括他在拳击或醉酒的拳击时所采取的任何镜头。战争战争，妻子对妻子，小说到小说，在他所有的冒险和拥抱和放弃，他最不变的情妇可能一直脑震荡。

Hemingway doesn’t include my favorite Hemingway story: Paris, mid-1920s, he’s out drinking with James Joyce, and the great Irishman—frail and half-blind but obstreperous in his cups—starts fights he can’t finish. “Deal with him, Hemingway! Deal with him!” (Hemingway himself told that one, so it may well not be true.) But there are plenty of other vignettes to chew on across the six hours of Burns and Novick’s documentary; the montage of Hemingway-ness is not exactly lacking in incident. Grandly shirtless, he’s hauling tuna from the Gulf of Mexico; in Paris, he’s test-tubing his prose in the aesthetic laboratories of Ezra Pound (“Make it new!”) and Gertrude Stein (“A rose is a rose is a rose is a rose”); in Madrid, he’s breakfasting under artillery fire at the Hotel Florida; and in 1944, he’s glimpsed loping through the death mill of the Hürtgen Forest, weapon in hand, after three wars finally and with grim joy an (unofficial) combatant for the first time.

海明威不包括我最喜欢的血缘道故事：巴黎，20世纪20年代中期，他与詹姆斯乔伊斯喝酒，伟大的爱尔兰人 - 虚弱，半盲，但在他的杯子里，他的杯子开始争夺他无法完成。 “处理他，海明威！处理他！“ （海明威本人告诉那个，所以它可能不是真的。）但是有很多其他的小插图咀嚼六个小时的烧伤和诺克斯的纪录片;海明威的蒙太奇在事件中并不完全缺乏。令人赤身赤膊上身，他是墨西哥湾的拖运金枪鱼;在巴黎，他在伊兹拉省审美实验室的散文中进行了散文（“使其新！”）和格特鲁德斯坦（“玫瑰是玫瑰是玫瑰是玫瑰”）;在马德里，他在佛罗里达州Hotel的炮兵火灾下吃早餐;在1944年，他瞥了一眼，通过Hürtgen森林，武器的死亡磨，在手中的死亡磨坊，在三次战争之后，最终并在第一次严峻的喜悦（非官方）战斗人员。

The revolution of his style is hard to discern, because to a degree we’re still in it. “Use vigorous English,” counseled the copy style sheet of the Kansas City Star, his first home as a journalist. “Be positive, not negative. … Eliminate every superfluous word.” Hemingway’s voice distilled itself with miraculous speed, a fusion of telegrammatic urgency and high modernist impersonality, with counterpoint learned from Bach and rhythms located profoundly in his own neurology; entire species of literature went extinct overnight. In the most beautiful way, it was anti-writing:

他的风格的革命很难辨别，因为到了一定程度，我们仍然存在。 “使用剧烈的英语”，咨询堪萨斯城明星的副本样式表，他是一名记者的第一家。 “是积极的，而不是消极的。 ...消除每个多余的词。“ Hemingway的声音用神奇的速度蒸馏出来，电报紧迫性和高现代主义者的融合，逆行者从他自己的神经学中的巴赫和节奏从巴赫和节奏中学到;整个文献物种过夜灭绝。以最美丽的方式，它是反作写：

Troops went by the house and down the road and the dust they raised powdered the leaves of the trees. The trunks of the trees too were dusty and the leaves fell early that year and we saw the troops marching along the road and the dust rising and leaves, stirred by the breeze, falling and the soldiers marching and afterwards the road bare and white except for the leaves.

军队在房子里和沿着道路和尘埃抚养了粉末的粉末。树干也是尘土飞扬，叶子早些时候下降，我们看到沿着道路行进的部队和尘埃上升和叶子，被微风，坠落和士兵行进，之后的道路裸露和白色叶子。

That’s from the legendary opening of A Farewell to Arms. Leaves, four times, each time a different vibration. A rose is a rose is a rose is a rose. “I read that paragraph and I want to cry,” confesses a literary scholar in Hemingway.

这是来自传奇开放的武器。叶子，四次，每次都有不同的振动。玫瑰是一朵玫瑰是玫瑰是一朵玫瑰。 “我读过那段，我想哭，”在海明威进行了一个文学学校。

Let’s talk about the Burns Method: the frowning pan across the blotchy manuscript page, the dreamy plunge into the old photograph, the smatters of ambient sound, the talking head who is not so much a talking head as a deeply invested witness. How do you dramatize the interior life? How do you dramatize writing? If you’re Ken Burns, by talking to writers, by watching their faces and bodies register the Hemingway-shocks. “The value of the American declarative sentence, right?” says Tobias Wolff, pulsing with admiration. Edna O’Brien, magically hushed and priestess-like, reads aloud from his date-rape story, “Up In Michigan”—“She was cold, and miserable, and everything felt gone”—and asks: “Could you, in all honor, say that this was a writer who didn’t understand women’s emotions, and who hated women? You couldn’t. Nobody could.”

让我们谈谈烧伤方法：炸的潘横跨斑点稿件页面，梦幻般的流入旧照片，环境声音的笑声，说话的头部并不是那么多说话的头部作为深度投资的见证。你如何戏剧化内部生活？你是如何戏剧性的写作？如果你是肯燃烧，通过与作家交谈，通过观察他们的脸部和身体登记血缘道冲击。 “美国宣言句的价值，对吧？”说Tobias Wolff，令人钦佩。埃德娜·奥布莱恩，神奇地喘气和女祭司就像他的日期 - 强奸故事朗读，“在密歇根州” - “她感冒了，悲惨，一切都感到沮丧” - 你能问：“能否问：”你能问：“我能问：”我能否问：“你能问：”我能否问：“你能问：”我能问：“我能否问：”我能问：“所有人都可以荣誉说，这是一位作家，他不懂女性的情绪，谁恨女人？你不能。没人能。“

Then there are the voice-overs: Jeff Daniels doing Hemingway, Meryl Streep doing the ass-kicking Martha Gellhorn (who says that Hemingway, while writing, is “about as much use as a stuffed squirrel”), and—with wonderful warmth—Keri Russell as Hemingway’s first wife, the passionate Hadley. “Oh Mr. Hemingway, how I love you. … Your flannel shirt seems a strangely beautiful thing, and it smells so good besides. Some day, if I don’t watch out, there’ll be a poem on the smell of a clean white shirt that’ll raise up the hair on the dead.”

然后有声音说：杰夫丹尼尔斯在做了梅林威尔，梅丽斯特雷德做屁股玛莎·格洛恩（谁说星缘，写作，是“与塞满面的松鼠一样多的用途”，而且 - 与美妙的温暖 - Keri Russell成为海明威的第一任妻子，这是热情的哈德利。 “哦，海明威先生，我怎么爱你。 ......你的法兰绒衬衫似乎是一个奇怪的东西，除了它的味道很好。有一天，如果我不介意，那些干净的白色衬衫的气味会有一首诗，会抬起死者的头发。“

Death In the Afternoon, his sprawling, hybrid book about the bullfight, was my Hemingway text. A manual, a memoir, a manifesto, a poetic anthropology: As a bullfight-obsessed schoolboy, I inhaled it, relishing without quite understanding its distinctness. There are black-and-white photographs in the book, one of which features a sheeted corpse on a slab, surrounded by 16 well-dressed men. “Granero dead in the infirmary,” reads Hemingway’s caption. “Only two in the crowd are thinking about Granero. The others are all intent on how they will look in the photograph.”

下午死亡，他庞大的混合书关于斗牛，是我的海明威文本。手册，一个回忆录，宣言，诗意的人类学：作为一个淫荡的小学生，我吸入它，令人愉快，而不是非常了解它的明显。这本书中有黑白照片，其中一家在板坯上设有一块板材，周围环绕着16件良好的男子。 “格拉梅在医务室死了，”读了海明威的标题。 “人群中只有两个正在考虑格兰罗。其他人都意图是他们将如何看待照片。“

Fascinated, you scan the picture. Two men are looking down at the dead matador; the rest are arranging themselves in various ways for the camera. One of the men looking down is sweating, pop-eyed, in a state of naked distress. We can be satisfied that he is thinking about Granero. The other, with a well-groomed mustache, looks impressively grave. Too impressively grave. Sort of lacquered with solemnity. He’s posing, surely. He’s thinking about himself. Has Hemingway been taken in? Then, at the side of the picture, almost squeezed out, you see another man: He too is looking down. His face is beyond grief; it holds nothing, only a kind of animal respect, emptied out in the presence of death. This is the second man who is thinking about Granero. Who but Hemingway would have zeroed in like this?

迷住，你扫描图片。两名男子正俯视死斗士;其余的是以各种方式安排相机。往下看的男人之一是出汗，爆发，处于裸体困境状态。我们可以满足他正在考虑Garmero。另一种情况下，有一个良好的髭，看起来令人印象深刻。太令人印象深刻的坟墓。有庄严的漆。他肯定地摆姿势了。他正在考虑自己。海明威已被采取？然后，在图片的一侧，几乎被挤出，你看到另一个男人：他也在俯视。他的脸超越了悲伤;它没有任何东西，只有一种动物尊重，在死亡的情况下倒空。这是一名正在考虑格兰罗的第二个男人。谁但海明威在这样的情况下会归零？

Death—the sight of it, the smell of it, the immanence of it—was his fetish. He stalked it in battles, bullfights, big-game hunts. And it stalked him back: Should he kill himself? His father did, with his father’s Civil War revolver. By disappearing briefly after that plane crash in Uganda, presumed dead by the world’s media, Hemingway achieved every writer’s dream: He got to read his own obituaries. And indeed there was a strange already-deadness to Hemingway as he battered his way through life. A kind of radiant nihilism, most un-American, diffused its halo around him. “Hail nothing full of nothing, nothing is with thee.” It made him wildly brave.

死亡 - 看到它，它的气味，它的内在 - 是他的恋物癖。他在战斗，斗牛，大游戏狩猎中跟踪它。它背着他回来了：他应该杀死自己吗？他的父亲与父亲的内战左轮手枪做过。通过在乌干达的飞机崩溃之后简要消失，由世界媒体推出死亡，海明威取得了每位作家的梦想：他必须阅读自己的仇恨。事实上，他的生活袭击了他的方式，已经有一个奇怪的已经死亡。一种辐射的虚无主义，大多数人，遍布他的光环。 “冰雹什么都没有，没有什么是与你在一起。”它让他疯狂地勇敢。

At a some point in Hemingway, it’s hard to say exactly when, the light in his eyes goes out. The hard, vulnerable, darkly shining gaze of the early photographs is sealed over by something else. The eyes, in the bashed-up face, become unreadable crescents of shadow. He looks sad and mean. Decades of booze, for sure, but also what the forensic psychiatrist Andrew Farah—the author of Hemingway’s Brain and one of Burns and Novick’s interviewees—has straightforwardly diagnosed as a case of brain damage. The talent degenerates. The talent dies before the man. Trying to write, Hemingway keeps arriving at “the complete blankness.”

在海明威的一点，难以准确地说，他眼中的光线熄灭。早期照片的艰难，脆弱的黑暗闪亮的凝视被其他东西密封。眼睛，在抨击的脸上，变得不可读的阴影新月。他看起来很伤心和意味着。肯定的，但肯定的是，还有法义精神病学家安德鲁法拉 - 海明威的大脑和烧伤之一的作者以及渴望的受访者 - 作为脑损伤的情况，直接被诊断出来。人才退化。人才死在男人面前。试图写作，海明威继续抵达“完整的空白”。

The writer “does his work alone,” Hemingway had written in his 1954 Nobel Prize acceptance speech, “and if he is a good enough writer he must face eternity, or the lack of it, each day.” This impossible standard, this merciless knowledge. It was his curse, in a way: to be good enough to know when he was done.

作者“他的工作独自一人，”海明威书面编写在1954年诺贝尔奖励讲话中，“如果他是一个很好的作家，他必须面对永恒，或者每天都缺乏它。”这种不可能的标准，这种无情的知识。这是他的诅咒，在某种程度上是：足以知道他完成后的好处。

Imagine a world where you could buy stocks, bonds, derivatives, cryptocurrencies or even pieces of art, all on one exchange, 24 hours a day, seven days a week, from anywhere in the world.

想象一下，您可以在世界上，您可以购买股票，债券，衍生品，加密货币甚至艺术件，七天，从世界上任何地方每周24小时。

On this exchange, trades occur directly between two investors instead of through a complex latticework of brokers, clearinghouses and other middlemen and gatekeepers. They settle, or close, almost instantly, instead of taking up to two days. The system is cheaper, more transparent and ostensibly more open. It is also potentially more volatile and risky for investors; profits can turn into losses in the wink of an eye at any time of the day or night.

在这次交流中，交易直接发生在两位投资者之间，而不是通过复杂的经纪人，清算馆和其他中间人和守门人。他们几乎立即定居或关闭，而不是长达两天。该系统更便宜，更透明，更透明，更开放。投资者也可能更有挥发性和危险;在白天或黑夜的任何时候，利润可以在眼睛的眨眼中变成损失。

Entrepreneurs have for years dreamed of using blockchain technology, the concepts and software underpinning bitcoin, to enable digital trading of virtually any asset. Today the idea seems less far-fetched than ever.

企业家多年来梦想使用区块链技术，概念和软件支撑比特币，以实现几乎任何资产的数字交易。今天，这个想法似乎不如以往任何时候都毫无遥。

When trading of videogame retailer GameStop exploded earlier this year, it illustrated just how fragile today’s markets can be. It also showed that a new generation of stock traders operates much like crypto traders: Flash mobs of retail traders gathering on social media and targeting an asset for a mass-buy has been a hallmark of cryptocurrency trading for years.

当视频游戏零售商Gamestop的交易今年早些时候爆炸时，它只是脆弱的市场可能是多么脆弱。它还表明，新一代股票交易员与加密贸易商相似，零售交易员的Flash Foot of AcioM Media在社交媒体上聚集并针对质量购买的资产一直是加密货币交易的标志。

The current capital markets aren’t built for that kind of trading, but a number of pilot programs and other experiments are investigating how to create digitized markets that can keep up with changing times. “We’re seeing a tectonic shift start to happen,” says Jeffrey Schumacher, the founder of New Asset Exchange, a Manhattan Beach, Calif.-based startup that aims to help companies create and sell digital securities.

目前的资本市场不是为这种交易而建造的，但许多试点计划和其他实验正在调查如何创建可以跟上更改时间的数字化市场。 “我们看到了一个构造的转变开始发生，”杰弗里·舒马赫，新资产交易所，曼哈顿海滩，加利福尼亚州曼哈顿海滩的创业公司，旨在帮助公司创造和销售数字证券。

London-based Blockchain.com has already built the infrastructure needed. The company, founded in 2011, began as a “wallet” service, a piece of software used to store bitcoins. As it attempted to grow, it was stymied because the existing financial industry wouldn’t extend basic services to crypto companies, says Peter Smith, the chief executive of London-based Blockchain.com. They had to build the services themselves.

基于伦敦的BlockChain.com已经建立了所需的基础架构。公司成立于2011年，开始作为“钱包”服务，一块软件用于储存比特币。基于伦敦的BlockChain.com的首席执行官Peter Smith表示，它被迟钝了，因为现有的金融业不会向加密公司扩展到加密公司。他们必须自己建立服务。

Today, Blockchain.com offers trading, custody, clearing and settlement services to retail and institutional clients trading crypto. The company can now replicate every service in the traditional capital markets, Mr. Smith says.

今天，BlockChain.com为零售和机构客户提供交易加密提供的交易，监护，清算和结算服务。史密斯先生说，该公司现在可以复制传统资本市场的每一项服务。

By the beginning of next year, Mr. Smith expects Blockchain.com to begin listing assets apart from cryptocurrencies on its exchange, and plans to “aggressively move into asset classes one by one,” he says. By the end of 2024, he says, he wants to be able to have cryptocurrencies, foreign currency exchange, options and other investment products, and corporate equities available on his platform.

在明年年初，史密斯先生预计会议综合征在其交易所与加密货币上开始列出资产，并计划“逐一地进入资产课程”，“他说。他说，到2024年底，他希望能够拥有加密货币，外币兑换，选项和其他投资产品，以及他的平台上提供的公司股票。

“Our thesis is pretty simple,” he says. “The internet is going to be the biggest economy in the world by 2030, and when it is there’s going to be a financial system built for the internet. Everything in crypto is built for that.”

“我们的论文很简单，”他说。 “互联网将成为2030年世界上最大的经济体，而当它将成为互联网建造的金融体系。 Crypto中的一切都是为此而建造的。“

On a digital exchange, investors submit asset orders on the exchange, which matches buyers and sellers directly with each other. No outside broker is needed to facilitate the trade.

在数字交易所，投资者提交交易所的资产订单，将买家和卖家彼此直接匹配。不需要外面的经纪人来促进交易。

Today, when investors buy stock, they use a broker, who holds the client’s assets at a custody bank. The trade is processed by an exchange. A clearinghouse called the Depository Trust & Clearing Corp. is the actual holder of every publicly issued stock in the U.S. Ultimately, every trade is processed by the DTCC. The amount of time it takes a stock trade to wind its way through that pipeline is about two days. Every financial intermediary maintains their own set of records, and “back offices” on Wall Street and around the world spend a lot of time and effort reconciling different record books.

今天，当投资者购买股票时，他们使用持有客户资产的经纪人在托管银行。交易由交易所处理。一个叫做存款信托＆amp的清算房间;结算公司是美国每一股公开发行股票的实际持有人最终，每次贸易都是由DTCC处理的。股票交易通过该管道蜿蜒蜿蜒的时间约为两天。每个金融中介都维护自己的一系列记录，并在华尔街和世界各地的“后办事处”花了很多时间和精力协调不同的记录书籍。

The blockchain model, in which a network of linked but independent computers all run copies of the same software program, is a way around that. On a blockchain-based system, all those intermediaries would be running the same software. Transactions would get recorded on an open ledger, of which everyone maintains an identical copy. Identities can be shielded, but the transactions themselves are visible to all. The process of clearing, settling and reconciling gets automated into one process that combines all those back-office functions. The biggest bonus? Because it’s an automated piece of software, a transaction goes from initiated to settled in minutes.

区块链模型，其中链接但独立计算机的网络全部运行相同软件程序的副本是一种方式。在基于区块链系统上，所有这些中介都将运行相同的软件。交易将在开放式分类帐上录制，其中每个人都保持相同的副本。可以屏蔽身份，但交易本身对所有人都可以看到。清除，结算和调和的过程可自动化为一个与所有这些背面函数的进程进行自动化。最大的奖金？因为它是一个自动化的软件，事务从发起以在几分钟内结算。

It’s a big opportunity for traditional banks as well. Anywhere from 50-80% of a bank’s costs come from back office functions, says Shanker Ramamurthy, global managing partner of banking at International Business Machines Meanwhile, the “front office” operations have a higher return on investment. Cutting out the back-office expenses will allow banks to shift resources to the front-office operations.

这对传统银行来说也是一个很大的机会。 50-80％的银行成本的任何地方来自后台职能，Shanker Ramamurthy表示，国际商业机器的全球管理合作伙伴同时，“前台”运营具有更高的投资回报。削减后台费用将允许银行将资源转移到前台运营。

Which is why traditional financial players are exploring such a system. The DTCC is running a pilot program using a blockchain-based market for trading private equity shares called Project Whitney, which it expects will form the basis for new platforms in the near future. The Swiss National Bank and the Bank for International Settlements are operating Project Helvetia, looking at the feasibility of integrating digitized trading within a central bank’s monetary infrastructure. The Australian Securities Exchange is expected to replace its existing infrastructure with a blockchain-based system some time in 2023. There are myriad others.

这就是为什么传统金融参与者正在探索这样的系统。 DTCC正在运营飞行员计划，该计划使用基于区块链的市场进行私募股票，称为项目惠特尼，预计将在不久的将来形成新平台的基础。瑞士国家银行和国际定居点银行正在运营Helvetia项目，研究了在央行货币基础设施内整合数字化交易的可行性。预计澳大利亚证券交易会预计将在2023年的一段时间内用基于区块链的系统取代其现有的基础设施。有无数的其他人。

“Clearly there’s a lot of interest in it,” says Nandini Sukumar, the chief executive of the World Federation of Exchanges, a trade group that represents public exchanges.

“显然对其进行了很多兴趣，”世界交易所全球交易所首席执行官Nandini Sukumar说，该贸易小组代表公众交流。

The exchanges see blockchain as a way to improve efficiency, speed up settlement times and cut costs, Ms. Sukumar says. But she doesn’t expect it will replace everything exchanges do, and she sees a future where both kinds of exchanges operate next to each other and even compete with one another.

Sukumar女士说，交易所认为区块链作为提高效率，加快结算时间和降低成本的方式。但她不指望它会取代一切交流，她看到了两种交流的未来，彼此相邻，甚至彼此竞争。

A decade’s worth of evidence shows the risks of trading in an always-on environment. Bitcoin is notoriously volatile, and most exchanges operate without trading curbs like closing bells or circuit breakers that halt exceedingly volatile trading. There is no way to slow down even the most violent selloff.

十年的证据表明，在始终如一的环境中交易的风险。比特币是臭名昭着的，大多数交换机都在没有交易条目的情况下运作，如关闭钟声或断路器，持续非常挥发的交易。即使是最暴力的卖出也没有办法放缓。

When GameStop trading was at its most volatile, it paused when the exchanges closed. A “full-stack” crypto exchange wouldn’t be beholden to any counterparties, Mr. Smith says. In other words, nobody could force it to shut down trading of an asset, no matter the circumstances.

当GameStop交易最挥发的时候，交易所关闭时它会停下来。史密斯先生说，“全堆栈”加密交易所不会被对手对手的展望。换句话说，无论情况如何，没有人能够迫使它关闭资产交易。

“It’s not like there’s any less risk,” Mr. Smith says, “there’s just less excuses and more transparency.”

“这不像有任何风险，”史密斯先生说，“借口较少，透明度更少。”

To be sure, the capital markets today work. Trillions of dollars worth of assets are exchanged every day on markets around the world. Most of the time, there isn’t any problem. At the extremes, though, cracks appear.

可以肯定的是，今天的资本市场工作。每天都在世界各地的市场交换了数万亿美元资产。大多数时候，没有任何问题。但是，在极端，出现裂缝。

With the surge of GameStop trading, the securities-trading app Robinhood Markets Inc. was unable to guarantee the trades. When it couldn’t meet a $3 billion margin call from the DTCC, it had to temporarily shut down trading.

随着GameStop Trading的激增，证券交易App Robinhood Markets Inc.无法保证交易。当它无法满足来自DTCC的30亿美元的保证金呼叫时，它必须暂时关闭交易。

“The GameStop-Robinhood thing definitely put a magnifying glass on a lot of the issues,” says Saum Noursalehi, the president of tZero, another company building a platform for trading digital securities.

“GameStop-Robinhood Temple肯定会在很多问题上放大了放大镜，”TZERO总裁Saum Noursalehi说，这是一家交易数字证券交易平台的公司。

In a rewired capital market, it will be possible for a company to incorporate, create a class of stock, issue that stock and track it on a blockchain. At least, that’s the vision in Wyoming. The Cowboy State has been revamping its laws to attract crypto businesses, and this year lawmakers introduced a bill that would upgrade its current corporate registry to a blockchain-based system.

在一个重温的资本市场中，公司可以纳入，创建一类股票，发出库存并在区块链上跟踪它。至少，这是怀俄明州的愿景。牛仔州一直在修改其法律，以吸引加密企业，今年立法者介绍了一项升级其当前公司登记处的法案，以将其基于区块的系统升级。

The added transparency of such a system would make it easier to track stocks. The lack of transparency has been a problem in shareholder votes and in the market for shorting stocks, with many instances when the number of a company’s shares outstanding don’t match the number of shares voted or being used for shorting.

这种系统的额外透明度将使履行股票更容易。缺乏透明度在股东投票和股票市场的市场上是一个问题，许多情况下，当公司的股票的数量不符合投票或用于短路的股票数量时。

Losing track of who owns which shares, or which have been pledged to counterparties as collateral, is more common than most people realize, says Avanti Financial Group founder Caitlin Long, who was one of the key drivers of the effort in Wyoming. While a blockchain isn’t the only way to achieve a higher level of transparency, it is more efficient than current methods, she says. “It’s all about accuracy of the ledger, and the defense of property rights,” she says.

Avanti金融集团创始人Caitlin Long表示，驾失谁拥有哪些股份或者已被承诺向交易对手抵押为抵押品的股票，这是比大多数人都在意识到的普遍存在。她说，虽然区块链不是实现更高水平透明度水平的唯一方法，但它比当前方法更有效。 “这完全是关于分类账的准确性以及物业的辩护，”她说。

Another experiment in digitizing the stock market is being run by New York-based Paxos. The crypto-services firm is in the middle of a two-year pilot program for a blockchain-based, operational settlement service. The test run includes only five firms with strict limits on how much they can trade and what equities are involved. But it is using real stocks like Morgan Stanley Kohl’s and Intel , and the participants include big Wall Street firms like Credit Suisse Société Générale and Instinet.

另一个在数字化股票市场的实验是由纽约的帕克斯经营。密码服务公司是一项基于区块链，业务结算服务的两年试点计划的中间。测试运行仅包括五家公司，严格限制他们可以交易多少以及涉及的股票。但它正在使用像摩根斯坦利科尔和英特尔这样的真实股票，参与者包括Big Wall Street公司，如Credit SuisseSociétéGénérale和Instinet。

“This doesn’t just disintermediate the players,” says Chad Cascarilla, the founder and chief executive of Paxos. “What it does is create a much safer and fairer system.”

“这不仅仅是宣称球员，”Paxos的创始人和首席执行官Chad Cascarilla说。 “它所做的是创造一个更安全和更公平的系统。”

Satellite networks could represent the next technological leap forward for a business where every millisecond counts.

卫星网络可以代表每毫秒计数的业务的下一个技术跃迁。

Investors may want to take a closer look at these four markets on the cusp of going mainstream.

投资者可能希望仔细看看这四个市场的主流的尖端。

随着从卡车到敬酒者的一切都上线，连接设备的下一步是为他们的人类用户购买和销售商品和服务的能力。

Jump to navigation Jump to search Silphium (also known as silphion, laserwort, or laser) is an unidentified plant that was used in classical antiquity as a seasoning, perfume, aphrodisiac, and medicine. [1] [2] It also was used as a contraceptive by ancient Greeks and Romans. [3] It was the essential item of trade from the ancient North African city of Cyrene, and was so critical to the Cyrenian economy that most of their coins bore a picture of the plant. The valuable product was the plant's resin ( laser, laserpicium, or lasarpicium).

Silphium was an important species in classical antiquity, as evidenced by the Egyptians and Knossos Minoans developing a specific glyph to represent the silphium plant. [4] It was used widely by most ancient Mediterranean cultures; the Romans who mentioned the plant in poems or songs, considered it "worth its weight in denarii" ( silver coins), or even gold. [2] Legend said that it was a gift from the god Apollo.

Silphium是古典古代的重要物种，埃及人和Knossos Minoans显现为代表硅植物的特定形状。 [4]它被大多数古代地中海文化广泛使用;在诗歌或歌曲中提到植物的罗马人，考虑到它＆＃34;在Denarii＆＃34中值得体重; （银币），甚至金。 [2]传说说，这是来自神阿波罗的礼物。

The exact identity of silphium is unclear. It is commonly believed to be a now- extinct plant of the genus Ferula, [1] perhaps a variety of " giant fennel". The still-extant plants Margotia gummifera  [ pt] [5] and Ferula tingitana [6] have been suggested as other possibilities. Another plant, asafoetida, was used as a cheaper substitute for silphium, and had similar enough qualities that Romans, including the geographer Strabo, used the same word to describe both. [7]

胫骨的确切身份尚不清楚。通常被认为是Ferula属的现在植物，[1]也许是各种＆＃34;巨型茴香＆＃34;已经提出了仍然存在的植物Margotia gummifera [pt] [pt] [5]和Ferula Tingitana [6]作为其他可能性。另一种植物Asafoetida被用作硅磷替代品更便宜，并且具有与罗马人（包括地理学手Strabo）的足够类似的品质，使用相同的词来描述两者。 [7]

The identity of silphium is highly debated. It is generally considered to belong to the genus Ferula, probably as an extinct species (although the currently extant plants Margotia gummifera  [ pt], [5] Ferula tingitana, Ferula narthex, and Thapsia garganica have historically been suggested as possible identities). [1] [6] [8] [9] Theophrastus mentioned Silphium as having thick roots covered in black bark, about 48 centimeters long, or one cubit, with a hollow stalk, similar to fennel, and golden leaves, like celery. [2]

SILPHIUM的身份高度辩论。它通常被认为属于属Ferula，可能是一种灭绝的物种（尽管目前的现存植物Margotia gummifera [Pt]，[5] Ferula Tingitana，Ferula Narthex和Thapsia Gargonica在历史上被视为可能的身份）。 [1] [6] [8] [9] [9]胫骨上提到的粘性物质覆盖黑树皮，长约48厘米长，或一个肘，与茴香，和金叶相似，像芹菜一样。 [2]

The cause of silphium's supposed extinction is not entirely known. The plant grew along a narrow coastal area, about 125 by 35 miles (201 by 56 km), in Cyrenaica (in present-day Libya). [10] Much of the speculation about the cause of its extinction rests on a sudden demand for animals that grazed on the plant, for some supposed effect on the quality of the meat. Overgrazing combined with overharvesting may have led to its extinction. [11] Demand for its contraceptive use was reported to have led to its extinction in the third or second century BCE. [12] The climate of the Maghreb has been drying over the millennia, and desertification may also have been a factor.

Silphium＆＃39; S假释灭绝的原因并不完全已知。该植物沿着狭窄的沿海地区成长，约125英里（201达56公里），在Cyrenaica（当天利比亚）。 [10]关于其灭绝原因的大部分猜测突然需要对在植物上放牧的动物的突然需求，对于一些对肉质的质量影响。过度垂涎的结合举起可能导致其灭绝。 [11]据报道，对其避孕药的需求导致其在BCE的第三或第二世纪灭绝。 [12] Maghreb的气候已经在千年中干燥，荒漠化也可能是一个因素。

Another theory is that when Roman provincial governors took over power from Greek colonists, they over-farmed silphium and rendered the soil unable to yield the type that was said to be of such medicinal value. Theophrastus wrote in Enquiry into Plants that the type of ferula specifically referred to as "silphium" was odd in that it could not be cultivated. [13] He reports inconsistencies in the information he received about this, however. [14] This could suggest the plant is similarly sensitive to soil chemistry as huckleberries are, which when grown from seed are devoid of fruit. [2]

另一个理论是，当罗马省长从希腊殖民者那里夺取权力时，他们过度养殖的胫骨并使土壤无法产生据说是这种药物价值的类型。 Theophrastus在询问中写入植物，植物是特异性称为＆＃34的植物; silphium＆＃34;奇怪的是，它无法耕种。 [13]他报告了他收到的信息中的不一致。 [14]这可能表明该植物与哈克伯利斯的土壤化学相似敏感，因为哈克布里斯从种子种植时缺乏水果。 [2]

Similar to the soil theory, another theory holds that the plant was a hybrid, which often results in very desired traits in the first generation, but second-generation can yield very unpredictable outcomes. This could have resulted in plants without fruits, when planted from seeds, instead asexually reproducing through their roots. [2]

类似于土壤理论，另一个理论认为该植物是一种杂种，其往往导致第一代中的非常需要的特征，但第二代可以产生非常不可预测的结果。当从种子种植时，这可能导致没有水果的植物，而是通过它们的根部进行繁体再现。 [2]

Pliny reported that the last known stalk of silphium found in Cyrenaica was given to the Emperor Nero "as a curiosity". [11]

普罗尼报道称，在Cyrenaica中发现的最后一个已知的硅氏茎被送给皇帝Nero＆＃34;作为一种好奇心＆＃34; [11]

Many medical uses were ascribed to the plant. [15] It was said that it could be used to treat cough, sore throat, fever, indigestion, aches and pains, warts, and all kinds of maladies. Hippocrates wrote: [16]

许多医疗用途均归于植物。 [15]据说它可用于治疗咳嗽，喉咙痛，发烧，消化不良，疼痛和痛苦，疣和各种疾病。希波克拉底写道：[16]

When the gut protrudes and will not remain in its place, scrape the finest and most compact silphium into small pieces and apply as a cataplasm.

当肠道突出并且不会留在其位置时，将最优质和最紧凑的硅灰刮成小块并作为涂鸦涂抹。

The plant may also have functioned as a contraceptive and abortifacient. [6] [17] [18] [19] Many species in the parsley family have estrogenic properties, and some, such as wild carrot, are known to act as abortifacients. [17]

该植物还可作为避孕和脂肪酸件起作用。 [6] [17] [18] [19]欧芹家族中的许多物种具有雌激素性质，并且已知一些（例如野胡萝卜）作为脱毛剂。 [17]

Long after its extinction, silphium continued to be mentioned in lists of aromatics copied one from another, until it makes perhaps its last appearance in the list of spices that the Carolingian cook should have at hand— Brevis pimentorum que in domo esse debeant ("A short list of condiments that should be in the home")—by a certain " Vinidarius", whose excerpts of Apicius [20] survive in one eighth-century uncial manuscript. Vinidarius's dates may not be much earlier. [21]

There has been some speculation about the connection between silphium and the traditional heart shape ( ♥). [22] Silver coins from Cyrene of the 6th–5th century BCE bear a similar design, sometimes accompanied by a silphium plant, and is understood to represent its seed or fruit. [23] Some plants in the family Apiaceae, such as Heracleum sphondylium, have heart-shaped indehiscent mericarps (a type of fruit).

有些关于硅藻土与传统心形之间的连接（♥）的猜测。 [22]来自6世纪的西兰的银币BCE存在类似的设计，有时伴有硅植物，并且被理解为代表其种子或水果。 [23]家庭苹果酱等一些植物，如海氏散峰，具有心形的肌肉肌肉（一种水果）。

Contemporary writings help tie silphium to sexuality and love. Silphium appears in Pausanias' Description of Greece in a story of the Dioscuri staying at a house belonging to Phormion, a Spartan, "For it so happened that his maiden daughter was living in it. By the next day this maiden and all her girlish apparel had disappeared, and in the room were found images of the Dioscuri, a table, and silphium upon it." [24]Silphium as Laserpicium makes an appearance in a poem ( Catullus 7) of Catullus to his lover Lesbia (though others have suggested that the reference here is instead to silphium's use as a treatment for mental illness, tying it to the "madness" of love [25]).

当代作品有助于巩膜与性爱和爱。 SILPHIUM出现在Pausanias＆＃39;希腊描述在居住在属于班莫翁，斯巴达，＆＃34的房子的故事中的故事;因为它发生了他的少女女儿生活在它。到第二天，这位少女和她所有的少女服装都消失了，在房间里被发现了Dioscuri，一张桌子和胫骨的图像。＆＃34;作为激光泡泡的硅磷在赛伐他斯皮斯的一首诗（catullus 7）的外观，虽然其他人表明这里的参考是硅磷酸，而是用作精神疾病的治疗，将其绑定到＆＃34;疯狂＆＃34;爱[25]）。

In the Italian military heraldry, Il silfio d’oro reciso di Cirenaica (Silphium of Cyrenaica, smoothly cut and printed in gold; in blazon: silphium couped or of Cyrenaica) is the symbol granted to units that distinguished themselves in the Western Desert Campaign in North Africa during World War II. [26]

在意大利军事纹章中，IL Silfio d'Oro Reciso di Cirenaica（Cyrenaica的Silphium，用金色平稳地切割和印刷）;在布拉泽：Couped或Cyrenaica的Silchium是授予西部沙漠运动的单位授予的符号北非在第二次世界大战期间。 [26]

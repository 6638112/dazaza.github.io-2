Facebook misled investors on scope of misinformation problems, whistleblower says
告密者说，Facebook在虚假信息问题的范围上误导了投资者

2022-02-19 18:58:13

Since filing whistleblower complaints against Facebook last year, Frances Haugen hasn’t been sitting still. A report today says the Facebook (now Meta) whistleblower has filed two new complaints with the Securities and Exchange Commission that allege the company internally acknowledged it was struggling with misinformation even while telling investors it had a handle on the problem.

自从去年对Facebook提起检举人投诉以来，弗朗西斯·豪根（Frances Haugen）一直坐着不动。今天的一份报告称，Facebook（现在的Meta）举报人向美国证券交易委员会（Securities and Exchange Commission）提交了两份新的投诉，指控该公司在内部承认自己在与错误信息作斗争，尽管它告诉投资者自己已经处理了这个问题。

Meta made “material misrepresentations and omissions in statements to investors” regarding its attempts to fight misinformation on its platforms, according to redacted complaints that a congressional staffer shared with The Washington Post and other news outlets. “Some investors simply will not want to invest in a company that fails to adequately address such misinformation and then engages in misstatements and omissions on the topic,” one complaint says.

根据一名国会工作人员与《华盛顿邮报》和其他新闻媒体分享的经过编辑的投诉，Meta就其在平台上打击虚假信息的尝试做出了“对投资者的声明中的重大失实陈述和遗漏”。“一些投资者根本不想投资一家未能充分处理此类错误信息，然后在该主题上进行错误陈述和遗漏的公司，”一份投诉书说。

Haugen’s new complaints say that while Facebook/Meta executives trumpeted their efforts to tamp down misinformation about climate change and COVID in earnings calls and elsewhere, internally, the company knew it was falling short.

豪根的新投诉称，虽然Facebook/Meta高管在财报电话和其他地方大力宣传他们为压制有关气候变化和新冠肺炎的错误信息所做的努力，但该公司内部知道自己做得不够。

As recently as the first quarter of 2021, Facebook employees discovered that climate misinformation ranked high when they searched for “climate change” in the Watch tab on Facebook. The second result was “climate misinfo,” which had been viewed more than 6.6 million times, an employee wrote.

就在2021年初的第一季度，脸谱网员工发现，当脸谱网在手表标签上搜索“气候变化”时，气候错误信息排名很高。第二个结果是“气候错误信息”，一名员工写道，该信息已被浏览660多万次。

Facebook also allegedly knew that its own heavily promoted Climate Science Information Center was a flop despite a slew of press releases and money thrown at it. Even among people who had visited the page, internal documents said that awareness of it was “very low,” according to the complaint.

据称，Facebook也知道自己大力推广的气候科学信息中心是一个失败，尽管有大量新闻稿和资金投入。据投诉称，即使是访问过该页面的人，内部文件也表示，他们对该页面的认识“非常低”。

Perhaps that’s because the company doesn’t seem to follow its own best practices when managing the page. News links are only updated every few days and are buried over halfway down the page. Plus, navigation is done through a clunky horizontal scroll instead of an infinite vertical feed like the rest of the site. The page has no “about” section or details to suggest that Facebook itself is running the page. Even the title is awkwardly stylized as the “Climate science center.” (Based on that alone, if I had stumbled across the page—and I haven’t—I would have thought it was a one-off effort by some crusading individual.) The page also doesn’t show a public follower count, and Facebook hasn’t even bothered to give it a header image. The page was officially renamed the Climate Science Center in September 2021. At the time, it had 3.8 million followers. While almost 4 million followers would make many brands happy, for a company the size of Meta, that's peanuts. Companies generally don’t rename products that are wildly popular.

也许这是因为该公司在管理页面时似乎没有遵循自己的最佳实践。新闻链接只会每隔几天更新一次，并隐藏在页面的一半。此外，导航是通过笨重的水平滚动来完成的，而不是像网站的其他部分那样通过无限的垂直提要。该页面没有“关于”部分或细节，表明Facebook本身正在运行该页面。甚至这个标题也被笨拙地称为“气候科学中心”（仅基于这一点，如果我偶然发现了这一页，而我没有——我会认为这是某个十字军个人的一次性努力。）该页面也没有显示公众关注人数，Facebook甚至懒得给它一个标题图片。该网页于2021年9月正式更名为气候科学中心。当时，它有380万粉丝。虽然近400万粉丝会让许多品牌感到高兴，但对于一家像Meta这样规模的公司来说，这'；这是花生。公司通常不会为广受欢迎的产品重命名。

Facebook does place labels on climate change content and reduces the distribution of content that its fact-checking partners deem false. But it typically doesn’t remove posts, as it does with COVID misinformation.

Facebook确实在气候变化内容上贴上标签，并减少其事实核查合作伙伴认为虚假的内容的传播。但它通常不会删除帖子，因为它会删除新冠病毒的错误信息。

Yet despite removing some COVID-related misinformation, the whistleblower complaints allege that Facebook/Meta hasn’t lived up to its public statements there, either. An internal Facebook survey found that a third of people in the US said they saw misinformation related to COVID and voting, the complaint says, and an April 2020 spike in misinformation reports from users was traced to the topic of COVID.

然而，尽管消除了一些与新冠病毒有关的错误信息，举报者的投诉称，Facebook/Meta也没有兑现其在那里的公开声明。诉状称，Facebook的一项内部调查发现，三分之一的美国人表示，他们看到了与新冠病毒和投票有关的错误信息，2020年4月用户错误信息报告的激增可追溯到新冠病毒的话题。

Using whistleblower complaints to address the misinformation problem is “creative,” Nathaniel Persily, a professor at Stanford Law School and director of the Stanford Cyber Policy Center, told The Washington Post. “You cannot pass a law in the US banning disinformation,” he said. “So what can you do? You can hold the platforms accountable to promises they make. Those promises could be made to users, to the government, to shareholders.” The strategy could work, given many investors’ appetites for focusing on environmental, social, and governance investment strategies (so-called ESG investors).

斯坦福大学法学院教授、斯坦福大学网络政策中心主任纳撒尼尔·佩斯利（Nathaniel Persly）告诉《华盛顿邮报》，利用举报者的投诉来解决错误信息问题是“创造性的”。“美国不能通过一项禁止虚假信息的法律，”他说。“那么你能做什么呢？你可以让平台对他们做出的承诺负责。这些承诺可以向用户、政府和股东做出。”考虑到许多投资者对关注环境、社会和治理投资策略（即所谓的ESG投资者）的兴趣，该策略可能会奏效。

For years, the SEC has told publicly traded companies that they need to make clear and accurate disclosures, Jane Norberg, a partner at Arnold & Porter who recently ran the SEC’s whistleblower program, told the Post. “If the company says one thing to investors but internal documents show that what they were saying is untrue, that could be something the SEC would look at,” she said.

Arnold&；最近负责SEC举报程序的波特告诉《华盛顿邮报》。她说：“如果公司向投资者说了一件事，但内部文件显示他们所说的是不真实的，那可能是SEC会考虑的。”。

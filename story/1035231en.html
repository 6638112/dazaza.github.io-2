吉他中心将根据破产法第11章申请破产保护

2020-11-15 12:49:36

Musical instrument retailer Guitar Center plans to file for Chapter 11 bankruptcy protection after negotiating a debt-cutting deal with key investors and lenders.

乐器零售商吉他中心(Guitar Center)计划在与关键投资者和贷款人谈判债务削减协议后，根据破产法第11章申请破产保护。

The company, which has been struggling with excessive debt for years, said late Friday that it plans to stay in business.

该公司多年来一直在为过度负债而苦苦挣扎，上周五晚些时候表示，计划继续经营下去。

The Westlake Village, Calif.-based retailer said it's "pleased with its overall store footprint," which includes nearly 300 Guitar Center stores and more than 200 Music & Arts stores.

这家总部位于加利福尼亚州西湖村的零售商表示，它对其整体门店面积感到满意，其中包括近300家吉他中心门店和200多家音乐与艺术门店。

But the retailer said it had hired a real estate firm "to explore opportunities to optimize its real estate portfolio and other agreements to focus on investments that best position the Company to return to its growth trajectory prior to COVID-19."

但该零售商表示，它已经聘请了一家房地产公司，以探索优化其房地产投资组合的机会，并达成其他协议，专注于最有利于公司回到新冠肺炎之前的增长轨道的投资。

Guitar Center sells and rents instruments, provides lessons and handles repairs. The company's other brands include Musician’s Friend, Woodwind Brasswind and AVDG.

吉他中心出售和租赁乐器，提供课程和维修服务。该公司的其他品牌包括音乐家的朋友，木管铜管和AVDG。

Like most non-essential retailers, Guitar Center was forced to temporarily close its stores in the early days of the pandemic. While its online revenue jumped during that period, "we expect revenues generated will not cover the sales that would have occurred through the stores," S&P Global Ratings wrote in a research note in late May.

像大多数非必需品零售商一样，吉他中心在疫情爆发初期被迫暂时关闭了门店。标普全球评级(S&P Global Ratings)在5月下旬的一份研究报告中写道：尽管该公司在此期间的在线收入大幅增长，但我们预计所产生的收入将不足以弥补通过门店实现的销售收入，标普全球评级公司(S&P Global Ratings)在5月下旬的一份研究报告中写道。

Guitar Center said Friday it had reached a restructuring deal with its controlling owner, a fund managed by private equity firm Ares Management, which acquired its stake in Guitar Center in 2014. The company said it also got support for its deal from new investors Brigade Capital Management and a fund managed by The Carlyle Group and from various lenders.

吉他中心周五表示，已与控股所有者达成重组协议，后者是一家由私募股权公司Ares Management管理的基金，后者在2014年收购了吉他中心的股份。该公司表示，它的交易还得到了新投资者Brigade Capital Management和凯雷集团(Carlyle Group)管理的一只基金以及多家贷款机构的支持。

“This agreement will allow us to significantly reduce our debt and reinvest in our business in order to better serve our customers and deliver on our mission of putting more music in the world," Guitar Center CEO Ron Japinga said in a statement.

吉他中心首席执行官罗恩·贾平加在一份声明中表示：“这项协议将使我们能够大幅削减债务，并对我们的业务进行再投资，以便更好地服务我们的客户，实现我们将更多音乐推向世界的使命，”吉他中心首席执行官罗恩·贾平加在一份声明中表示。

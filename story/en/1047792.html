Always Bet on Text
总是投注文字

2021-02-17 18:31:27

graydon2 I figured I should just post this somewhere so I can make future reference to how I feel about the matter, anytime someone asks me about such-and-such video, 3D, game or "dynamic" multimedia system. Don't get me wrong, I like me some illustrations, photos, movies and music. But text wins by a mile. Text is everything. My thoughts on this are quite absolute: text is the most powerful, useful, effective communication technology ever, period. Text is the oldest and most stable communication technology (assuming we treat speech/signing as natural phenomenon -- there are no human societies without it -- whereas textual capability has to be transmitted, taught, acquired) and it's incredibly durable. We can read texts from five thousand years ago, almost the moment they started being produced. It's (literally) "rock solid" -- you can readily inscribe it in granite that will likely outlast the human species.

我认为我应该将其发布在某个地方，以便将来在有人问我有关某某视频，3D，游戏或“动态”的情况时，可以参考我对此事的看法。多媒体系统。不要误会我的意思，我喜欢一些插图，照片，电影和音乐。但是，文字赢得了一英里。文字就是一切。我对此的想法是绝对的：文本是有史以来最强大，最有用，最有效的通信技术。文本是最古老，最稳定的通信技术（假定我们将语音/签名视为自然现象-没有人类社会就没有它-而文本的能力必须被传递，传授，获取），并且它的持久性令人难以置信。我们可以阅读五千年前的文本，几乎是在文本开始制作的那一刻。 （字面意思）是坚如磐石的-您可以轻松地将其刻在花岗岩上，而花岗岩的寿命可能会超过人类的寿命。

Text is the most flexible communication technology. Pictures may be worth a thousand words, when there's a picture to match what you're trying to say. But let's hit the random button on wikipedia and pick a sentence, see if you can draw a picture to convey it, mm? Here: "Human rights are moral principles or norms that describe certain standards of human behaviour, and are regularly protected as legal rights in national and international law."

文本是最灵活的通信技术。当图片与您要说的内容相匹配时，图片可能值一千个字。但是，让我们点击维基百科上的随机按钮并选择一个句子，看看是否可以画出图片来传达它，嗯？在这里：人权是描述某些人类行为标准的道德原则或规范，在国内和国际法中经常受到法律保护。

Not a chance. Text can convey ideas with a precisely controlled level of ambiguity and precision, implied context and elaborated content, unmatched by anything else. It is not a coincidence that all of literature and poetry, history and philosophy, mathematics, logic, programming and engineering rely on textual encodings for their ideas.

没有机会。文本可以以精确控制的歧义和精确度，隐含的上下文和精心设计的内容来传达思想，这是其他任何事物都无法比拟的。所有文学和诗歌，历史和哲学，数学，逻辑，程序设计和工程学都依靠文本编码来表达思想，这并非巧合。

Text is the most efficient communication technology. By orders of magnitude. This blog post is likely to take perhaps 5000 bytes of storage, and could compress down to maybe 2000; by comparison the following 20-pixel-square image of the silhouette of a tweeting bird takes 4000 bytes: . At every step of communication technology, textual encoding comes first, everything else after. Because it's vastly cheaper on a symbol-by-symbol basis. You have a working optical telegraph network running in 1790 in France. You the better part of a century of electrical telegraphy, trans-oceanic cables and everything, before anyone bothers with trying to carry voice. You have decades of teleprinter and text-only computer networking, mail and news, chat and publishing, editing and diagnostics, before bandwidth gets cheap enough for images, voice and video. You have pagers, SMS, WAP, USSD and blackberries before iPhones. You have Teletext and BBSs, netnews and gopher before the web. And today many of the best, and certainly the most efficient parts of the web remain text-centric. I can download all of wikipedia and carry it around on the average smartphone.

文本是最有效的通信技术。按数量级。这篇博文可能占用了5000个字节的存储空间，并可能压缩到2000个压缩空间。相比之下，以下20像素见方的鸣叫鸟剪影图像需要4000字节：。在通信技术的每一步中，文本编码都是第一位的，之后是所有其他内容。因为它在逐个符号的基础上便宜得多。您有一个在1790年在法国运行的光学电报网络。在任何人尝试传递声音之前，您都是21世纪电报，跨洋电缆以及所有事物的美好时光。您拥有数十年的电传打印机和纯文本计算机网络，邮件和新闻，聊天和发布，编辑和诊断功能，然后带宽才足够便宜以用于图像，语音和视频。 iPhone之前有寻呼机，SMS，WAP，USSD和黑莓。在网络发布之前，您已经拥有图文电视和BBS，netnews和gopher。今天，网络上许多最好的，当然也是最有效的部分仍然以文本为中心。我可以下载所有维基百科，并在普通的智能手机上随身携带。

Text is the most socially useful communication technology. It works well in 1:1, 1:N, and M:N modes. It can be indexed and searched efficiently, even by hand. It can be translated. It can be produced and consumed at variable speeds. It is asynchronous. It can be compared, diffed, clustered, corrected, summarized and filtered algorithmically. It permits multiparty editing. It permits branching conversations, lurking, annotation, quoting, reviewing, summarizing, structured responses, exegesis, even fan fic. The breadth, scale and depth of ways people use text is unmatched by anything. There is no equivalent in any other communication technology for the social, communicative, cognitive and reflective complexity of a library full of books or an internet full of postings. Nothing else comes close.

文本是对社会最有用的通信技术。它在1：1、1：N和M：N模式下效果很好。即使是手工操作，也可以对其进行有效索引和搜索。可以翻译。它可以以可变的速度生产和消费。它是异步的。它可以通过算法进行比较，扩散，聚类，校正，汇总和过滤。它允许多方编辑。它允许分支对话，潜伏，注解，引用，审阅，总结，结构化响应，释经，甚至是同志。人们使用文本的方式的广度，规模和深度是任何事物所无法比拟的。在充满书籍的图书馆或充满张贴的互联网的社交，交流，认知和反思复杂性方面，任何其他通信技术都无法与之匹敌。没有什么比这更接近了。

So this is my stance on text: always pick text first. As my old boss might have said: always bet on text. If you can use text for something, use it. It will very seldom let you down.

所以这是我对文本的立场：始终先选择文本。就像我的前任老板可能说过的：总是赌文字。如果您可以将文字用于某物，请使用它。它很少会让你失望的。

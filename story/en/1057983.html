After first appearing in the mobile app last month, Photos on the web now supports copying text from images using optical character recognition (or OCR), as first spotted by 9to5Google. Though users could already export text from a phone to a computer via their Google account, accessing Lens directly on the web removes a step in the process altogether.

在首次出现在上个月的移动应用程序后，Web上的照片现在支持使用光字符识别（或OCR）从图像中复制文本，如9to5google的第一个发现。虽然用户可以通过他们的Google帐户将文本从手机导出到计算机，但是直接在Web上访问镜头，删除了完全进程的步骤。

To use OCR in Photos on the web, look for the "Copy text from image" button along the top of any word-heavy photo. Once active, you can drag your cursor to highlight the portion of words you want to export, then click "Copy text" from the menu to the right of your image. Unlike on phones, there's no dedicated way to activate selection in the web client yet, so if your photo is missing the "Copy text" suggestion, you're out of luck.

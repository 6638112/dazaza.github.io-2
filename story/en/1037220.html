William S Burroughs and the Cult of Rock’n’Roll by Casey Rae Review
威廉·S·巴勒斯（William S Burroughs）和《摇滚迷》（Cult of Rock'n’Roll）

2020-11-30 09:38:40

The writer William Burroughs was a fringe figure until his 50s, too weird for popular tastes. Part of the original trio of Beat writers alongside Jack Kerouac and Allen Ginsberg, he was at once feted and condemned for his work – his 1959 novel Naked Lunch was briefly banned by the city of Boston following an obscenity trial; Norman Mailer testified in its defence. But in the late 60s and 70s, a new generation of musicians turned him into a countercultural hero, among them Paul McCartney, Lou Reed, David Bowie, Richard Hell, Jimmy Page and Patti Smith. Later on, Michael Stipe, Kurt Cobain and Sonic Youth’s Thurston Moore would all declare themselves in Burroughs’ thrall.

作家威廉·伯劳斯（William Burroughs）直到50年代都是个流浪汉，对流行的口味也太奇怪了。他是Beat作家最初三人的一员，与Jack Kerouac和Allen Ginsberg一起，因他的作品而受到赞誉和谴责。他1959年的小说《 Naked Lunch》在一次ob亵审判后被波士顿市短暂禁止；诺曼·梅勒（Norman Mailer）作了辩护。但是在60年代和70年代末期，新一代的音乐家使他成为反文化英雄，其中包括保罗·麦卡特尼，娄·里德，大卫·鲍伊，理查·地狱，吉米·佩奇和帕蒂·史密斯。后来，迈克尔·施蒂普（Michael Stipe），库尔特·科本（Kurt Cobain）和音速青年乐队（Sonic Youth）的瑟斯顿·摩尔（Thurston Moore）都将自己宣布为Burroughs的奴隶。

Casey Rae surveys his impact on musicians and how his thumbprints can be found all over popular culture. The film Blade Runner took its title from a Burroughs novella, and the band Steely Dan was named after a dildo in Naked Lunch. The term “heavy metal” was taken from The Soft Machine, Burroughs’s 1961 book that also provided a jazz-folk fusion band from Canterbury with their name. His face looks stonily from the cover of the Beatles’ Sgt Pepper’s Lonely Hearts Club Band, and he lent his creaky drawl to albums by Laurie Anderson, Tom Waits and the Disposable Heroes of Hiphoprisy. Elsewhere, Burroughs’ “cut-up” methods – a literary technique where the text is randomly rearranged – were adopted by Bowie, McCartney and the Rolling Stones guitarist Brian Jones.

凯西·雷（Casey Rae）调查了他对音乐家的影响以及如何在整个流行文化中找到他的指纹。电影《银翼杀手》（Blade Runner）取自Burroughs中篇小说的称号，而Steely Dan乐队则以“裸午餐”中的假阳具而得名。 “重金属”一词取自Burroughs 1961年出版的《软机》一书，该书还提供了坎特伯雷的一支爵士民谣融合乐队，并命名。在披头士乐队的Sgt Pepper的Lonely Hearts Club乐队的封面上，他的脸显得呆滞，他将令人毛骨悚然的画图借给了Laurie Anderson，Tom Waits和Disposable Heroes of Hiphoprisy的专辑。在其他地方，鲍伊（Bowie），麦卡尼（McCartney）和滚石乐队（Rolling Stones）吉他手布莱恩·琼斯（Brian Jones）采用了Burroughs的“切分”方法（一种将文字随机重新排列的文学技巧）。

Burroughs, who died in 1997, could be irascible and reclusive, but he was generous with younger artists. Wherever he went, it wouldn’t be long before a musician pitched up at his door to pay homage. While some, such as Reed and Dylan, were granted a single audience, others were welcomed into his inner sanctum and became confidants. He bonded with Bowie, who had just killed off Ziggy Stardust when they met, and was close friends with Patti Smith, who worshipped the Beat writers and said of Burroughs: “He’s up there with the Pope.” After meeting Cobain, with whom he collaborated on the 1993 single “The ‘Priest’ They Called Him ”, Burroughs told the singer’s tour manager: “Your friend hasn’t learned his limitations, and he’s not going to make it if he continues.”

Burroughs于1997年去世，他可能会变得易怒和隐居，但他对年轻艺术家很慷慨。无论他走到哪里，都很快就会有音乐家在他家门口向他致敬。尽管有些人（例如里德和迪伦）被授予了单一的听众，但其他人却受到欢迎进入他的内室并成为知己。他与鲍伊（Bouie）交往，鲍伊（Bouie）在见面时就杀死了Ziggy Stardust，并且与帕蒂·史密斯（Patti Smith）成为密友，帕蒂·史密斯（Patti Smith）敬拜Beat作家并谈到巴勒斯：遇见科本之后，他与他合作创作了1993年的单曲“他们叫他的'牧师'”，伯劳斯对这位歌手的旅游经理说：“您的朋友尚未了解他的局限性，如果继续下去，他也不会做到。 ”

Perhaps surprisingly Burroughs didn’t care much for music, though he had other things in common with these young upstarts. Like them, he had attracted opprobrium for his allegedly dangerous influence on young people. He also enjoyed their disruptive, anti-establishment spirit, and could relate to those, such as Cobain, whose only method of coping with the demands of the outside world was through copious drug use.

也许令人惊讶的是，尽管Burroughs与这些年轻的暴发户有其他共同点，但他并不十分喜欢音乐。像他们一样，他因对年轻人的危险影响而受到谴责。他还喜欢他们的破坏性和反建制精神，并且可以和诸如Cobain这样的人联系起来，例如，应对大量外界毒品的唯一方法就是通过大量吸毒。

Along with those Burroughs influenced, the book also tells the story of the man himself, from his upper-middle-class childhood soured by an incident with a nanny that may or may not have involved sexual abuse (Burroughs never told the full story), to his years travelling the world and living variously in London, Paris, New York, Mexico City and Tangier. Rae’s account is compelling, capturing the strangeness of Burroughs’s itinerant lifestyle, his bizarre obsessions (guns and the occult, mostly) and his Herculean appetite for drugs.

该书还与那些受Burroughs影响的人一起，讲述了他本人的故事，从他的上层中产阶级童年时期开始，发生在与一名可能涉及或未涉及性虐待的保姆一起的事件中（Burroughs从未讲过完整的故事），他环游世界，在伦敦，巴黎，纽约，墨西哥城和丹吉尔生活的各种经历。 Rae的叙述引人入胜，捕捉到Burroughs漫不经心的生活方式，对怪异事物的迷恋（主要是枪支和神秘主义）以及对毒品的强烈需求。

Rae makes no secret of his admiration for the writer, noting that he carries “the Burroughs gene … I remain drawn to his fierce intellect and black humour, as well as his refusal to conform.” As such, he is keen to highlight Burroughs’s kindnesses, and has a tendency to play down his more troubling aspects, most notably his misogyny (Burroughs notoriously said he believed women were a biological mistake). He breezes swiftly through the matter of the writer having accidentally killed his wife, Joan Vollmer, as part of what they called their “William Tell routine”, in which Vollmer would place a shot glass on top of her head for Burroughs to shoot – only this time he missed and shot her in the forehead. Describing this fatal party piece, Rae notes Vollmer’s “unkempt” and “thinning” hair, a result of her various addictions and a bout of polio, and goes on to observe how her “once alluring face [had] aged well beyond her twenty-seven years”. Whether, after years of drinking and heroin abuse, Burroughs remained fresh-faced and youthful is not mentioned.

Rae毫不掩饰对作家的钦佩之情，并指出他带有“ Burroughs基因……我仍然被他敏锐的才智和黑色幽默以及他拒绝服从所吸引。”因此，他热衷于强调Burroughs的善良，并且倾向于淡化他更为烦恼的方面，尤其是他的厌女症（Burroughs臭名昭著地表示，他认为女性是生物学上的错误）。他迅速微风轻拂，因为作家意外杀死了他的妻子琼·沃尔默（Joan Vollmer），这是他们所谓的“威廉·泰尔（William Tell）常规”的一部分，沃尔默将玻璃杯放在头顶供Burroughs拍摄-仅这次他想念她并且在额头上开枪。在描述这件致命的派对作品时，雷（Rae）注意到了沃尔默（Vollmer）的“蓬乱”和“稀疏”的头发，这是她的各种成瘾和小儿麻痹症的结果，并继续观察她的“一次诱人的脸”是如何远远超过她二十岁的。 7年”。经过多年的饮酒和滥用海洛因后，Burroughs是否仍然保持新鲜和年轻。

Rae shows little interest in disrupting the legend of the writer as a charismatic outlaw. Clearly, he is not the first person to give Burroughs a pass. The liberal-leaning, peace-loving musicians who adored him similarly glossed over his enthusiasm for guns, which didn’t wane after he shot Vollmer. After his death, Burroughs was buried with a ballpoint pen, a wrap of heroin and his beloved .38 Special snubnose revolver. It was, in accordance with his wishes, fully loaded.

Rae对于破坏作家的传奇作为超凡魅力的定律几乎没有兴趣。显然，他不是第一个通过Burroughs的人。崇拜他的自由主义者，爱好和平的音乐家同样对他的枪支热情有所掩饰，枪支热情在他向Vollmer开枪后并没有消失。死后，巴勒斯用一支圆珠笔，一包海洛因和他心爱的.38特制鼻甲左轮手枪埋葬。按照他的意愿，这是满载的。

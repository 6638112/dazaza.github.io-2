Airbnb Denies Refunds for Cancellations Based on Pandemic Stay at Home Orders
Airbnb拒绝大流行居家定单取消退款

2020-12-20 14:46:13

When the state’s stay-at-home order hit Lake Tahoe on Dec. 11, Silvia Wasjutin immediately went to cancel her Airbnb booking for Christmas in Tahoe City at Christmas. The region was essentially shut down, and traveling was discouraged.

12月11日，该州的全屋服务订单打到太浩湖时，西尔维亚·瓦斯汀（Silvia Wasjutin）立即在圣诞节取消了在太浩市圣诞节预订Airbnb的预订。该地区基本上被关闭，并且不鼓励旅行。

Deciding to cancel the trip was heartbreaking. It’s the only time Wasjutin, her six adult children and her half-sister get together as a family. But surely, Wasjutin thought, given the suddenness of the shutdown order and its widespread effects on travelers and lodging providers, her host would agree to refund the full $6,600 cost of the reservation.

决定取消这次旅行令人心碎。这是Wasjutin，她的六个成年子女和同父异母的妹妹唯一一家团聚。但肯定的是，Wasjutin认为，鉴于停工令的突然发生及其对旅客和住宿提供者的广泛影响，她的房东将同意退还预订的全部6,600美元费用。

The host, a woman in her 50s who asked to be anonymous over concern about reprisals from her community, told The Chronicle that guests should know the risks of booking during the pandemic and that Airbnb’s cancellation policy allows her to keep some of the cost on late cancellations. She told Wasjutin that tourists had come to Tahoe throughout the pandemic, regardless of health orders. But Wasjutin wasn’t comfortable defying the new order and perhaps spreading the coronavirus.

房东是一名五十多岁的女性，她因对社区报复的担忧而要求匿名，她告诉《纪事报》，客人应该知道大流行期间的预订风险，而且Airbnb的取消政策允许她将部分费用保留在后期。取消。她告诉Wasjutin，在大流行期间，无论健康状况如何，游客都来了塔霍。但是Wasjutin不敢抗拒新秩序，甚至可能传播冠状病毒。

After exchanges through Airbnb’s messaging service, Wasjutin canceled and the host refunded $3,000. The two have a stalemate over the remaining $3,600.

通过Airbnb的信息服务进行交易后，Wasjutin取消了房东，并退还了$ 3,000。这两人在剩余的3600美元中处于僵持状态。

With new shutdown orders sweeping across California, many people in the Bay Area are in similar situations. Hotels and property managers in Tahoe are clearing bookings for December and giving refunds. Meanwhile, Airbnb — which raised billions in an initial public offering this month — has avoided mediating disagreements even as they become more common during the pandemic.

随着新的停工令席卷整个加利福尼亚，湾区的许多人处于类似情况。塔霍（Tahoe）的酒店和物业经理将清理12月份的预订并退款。同时，Airbnb（本月在首次公开募股中筹集了数十亿美元）避免了调解分歧，即使这种分歧在大流行期间变得越来越普遍。

Wasjutin hoped explaining her circumstances to an actual human at Airbnb would help. But she struggled to get one on the phone.

Wasjutin希望向Airbnb的真实人解释她的情况会有所帮助。但是她努力地打电话给她。

“When I finally talked to a person, they had no clue about what’s happening in California,” Wasjutin said.

Wasjutin说：“当我最终与某人交谈时，他们对加利福尼亚发生的事情一无所知。”

The pandemic presents a stress test of Airbnb’s peer-to-peer marketplace model, in which guests and hosts around the world are left to navigate unprecedented uncertainty on their own. Financial data the company disclosed while going public suggests the pandemic has been a boon. It lost billions of dollars in revenue from April to June as travel cratered, but bookings bounced back the following three months. Travelers have opted for long-term, self-contained lodging, said Makarand Mody, who teaches hospitality marketing at Boston University and studies Airbnb rentals.

这场流行病对Airbnb的点对点市场模型进行了压力测试，在这个模型中，世界各地的宾客和房东只能独自应对前所未有的不确定性。该公司在上市时披露的财务数据表明，这种流行病是一个福音。从四月到六月，由于火山口，它损失了数十亿美元的收入，但随后的三个月预订量又恢复了。波士顿大学教授酒店营销和研究Airbnb租金的马卡兰德·莫迪（Makarand Mody）说，旅行者选择了长期的独立住宿。

There are no signs the company will address challenges at high-demand locations like Lake Tahoe, where contention over short-term rentals has led communities to clamp down.

没有迹象表明该公司将在太浩湖等高需求地区解决挑战，在那里对短期租金的争执导致社区采取镇压措施。

“If they do something new in Tahoe, they might have to do that across the country,” Mody said. “So I think they’re hesitant about making any moves that might hurt their business in any way.”

莫迪说：“如果他们在塔霍（Tahoe）做新的事情，他们可能必须在全国范围内这样做。” “因此，我认为他们不愿采取任何可能以任何方式损害其业务的措施。”

Bay Area guests who spoke with The Chronicle said Tahoe hosts were sympathetic and offered full refunds on last-minute cancellations.

与《纪事报》交谈的湾区客人说，塔霍（Tahoe）房东很同情，并在最后一刻取消预订时全额退款。

For hosts, even issuing cancellations can be complicated. Some in Tahoe say removing their listings during shutdown periods has been nearly impossible.

对于主机，甚至发出取消通知也可能很复杂。塔霍（Tahoe）的一些人说，在停工期间删除其房源几乎是不可能的。

Julie Weed, president and broker at Westlake Properties in Tahoe City, manages about 65 properties on Airbnb. When the shutdown was announced, she couldn’t cancel bookings fast enough to pull her listings off the website, she said. There’s too much demand to travel to Tahoe, even during the stay-at-home order.

塔霍市西湖房地产公司的总裁兼经纪人朱莉·韦德（Julie Weed）管理着Airbnb上约65家房地产。她说，当宣布关闭时，她无法以足够快的速度取消预订以将其商品清单从网站上撤下。即使是在家定购，对太浩的需求也太多了。

“As soon as I’d cancel a booking request, I’d immediately get another,” Weed said. “I called Airbnb and said, ‘Can you just shut down Tahoe across the board?’ They said no, it’s up to the hosts to manage on their own.”

“一旦取消预订请求，我会立即收到另一个请求，”韦德说。 “我给Airbnb打电话说，‘你能全盘关闭Tahoe吗？’他们说不，这取决于房东自行管理。”

While many travel operators offer more flexible booking policies during the pandemic, Airbnb remains an outlier in the hospitality industry, Mody said.

莫迪说，尽管许多旅行社在大流行期间提供了更为灵活的预订政策，但爱彼迎在酒店业仍然是一个例外。

“In COVID, consumers’ expectations have changed,” she said. “We expect everyone in the hospitality industry to be more flexible to our needs. Airlines and hotels are doing it, where you can cancel pretty much last-minute without any charges. But Airbnb hasn’t really followed suit.”

她说：“在COVID中，消费者的期望已经改变。” “我们希望酒店业的每个人都能更加灵活地满足我们的需求。航空公司和酒店都在这样做，您可以在最后一刻取消几乎任何费用。但是，Airbnb并没有真正效仿。”

Instead, the company directs guests to its extenuating circumstances policy, which says it does not cover trip changes related to COVID orders or travel advisories. In effect, that leaves hosts and guests to negotiate refunds among themselves.

取而代之的是，该公司将客人引导至其“情节减轻”政策，该政策不涵盖与COVID订单或旅行咨询相关的旅行变更。实际上，这使房东和客人之间可以协商退款。

Wasjutin’s host said her income took a big hit in March when Airbnb mandated cancellations and refunds. She now needs the money for her mortgage and the needs of two children.

Wasjutin的房东说，3月份，当Airbnb要求取消和退款时，她的收入受到了重创。她现在需要抵押贷款的钱和两个孩子的需要。

“This is my only income,” the host said. “My husband died a couple Thanksgivings ago and this is the only way I can feed my family.”

主持人说：“这是我唯一的收入。” “我的丈夫在感恩节那天过世了，这是我养家糊口的唯一途径。”

“As long as they provide the information to customers and hosts, they see their legal responsibility as ending there,” Mody said. “They’ve been able to get away with it because there’s not a government structure in place to hold them accountable.”

莫迪说：“只要他们向客户和主机提供信息，他们就会认为自己的法律责任就此终止。” “他们之所以能够摆脱困境，是因为没有适当的政府机构来追究他们的责任。”

Short-term rental regulations vary, sometimes dramatically, from city to city. San Francisco’s rules are strict, while other vacation destinations have looser rules. Airbnb links to local regulations, but its enforcement of the rules varies, and jurisdictions have sued to force the company to act.

各个城市的短期租赁法规有时甚至相差很大。旧金山的规定很严格，而其他度假胜地的规定则较为宽松。 Airbnb与当地法规有联系，但其对法规的执行情况有所不同，司法管辖区已起诉迫使该公司采取行动。

Many Tahoe areas are trying to regulate solutions. Tahoe has long been a hotbed of short-term rental properties like ski cabins and lakeside lodges, but in the past five years, rentals have become a headache for locals who reside in the lake’s small communities year-round. Many say their home has swung too far in the direction of a playground for rude tourists and wealthy second-homeowners.

塔霍（Tahoe）的许多地区都在尝试规范解决方案。塔霍（Tahoe）长期以来一直是诸如滑雪小屋和湖边小屋之类的短期出租物业的温床，但是在过去的五年中，租金一直困扰着常年居住在该湖小社区中的当地人。许多人说，对于粗鲁的游客和有钱的第二房主来说，他们的家朝操场的方向倾斜得太远了。

To combat it, South Lake Tahoe voters passed a measure two years ago to phase out most short-term rentals by 2022. Placer County, including Tahoe’s West Shore, added a registration system this year for short-term rentals, setting quiet hours and occupancy limits. A similar ordinance in Truckee takes effect in January.

为了与之抗衡，南太浩湖选民两年前通过了一项措施，到2022年逐步淘汰大多数短期租金。包括塔霍（Tahoe）的西岸（West Shore）在内的普莱瑟县今年增加了短期租金登记制度，设定了安静的时间和占用率限制。特拉基的一项类似条例将于1月生效。

Those ordinances won’t give Tahoe authorities direct control over Airbnbs. Compliance still will be largely left up to hosts, who could face fines.

这些条例不会让塔霍当局直接控制Airbnb。依从性仍将主要取决于主机，主机可能会面临罚款。

Wasjutin’s host said Friday that she will consider issuing Wasjutin a full refund on her cancellation if the property is rebooked. Wasjutin said she’s skeptical, but has made up her mind about Airbnb.

Wasjutin的房东周五表示，如果重新预订该物业，她将考虑向Wasjutin退还全额退款。 Wasjutin说她对此表示怀疑，但已经决定对Airbnb做出决定。

“Honestly,” she said, “I don’t think I’d do it again.”

“说实话，”她说，“我不认为我会再做一次。”

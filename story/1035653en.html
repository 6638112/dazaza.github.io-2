Field geology at Mars' equator points to ancient megaflood
火星赤道的野外地质学指向古老的特大洪水

2020-11-21 22:37:46

Floods of unimaginable magnitude once washed through Gale Crater on Mars' equator around 4 billion years ago—a finding that hints at the possibility that life may have existed there, according to data collected by NASA's Curiosity rover and analyzed in joint project by scientists from Jackson State University, Cornell University, the Jet Propulsion Laboratory and the University of Hawaii. The research, "Deposits from Giant Floods in Gale Crater and Their Implications for the Climate of Early Mars," was published Nov. 5 in Scientific Reports.

大约40亿年前，巨大的洪水曾经通过火星赤道上的Gale陨石坑被冲走-根据美国宇航局好奇号漫游车收集的数据，并根据杰克逊科学家在联合项目中进行的分析，这一发现暗示那里可能存在生命州立大学，康奈尔大学，喷气推进实验室和夏威夷大学。这项名为“大风火山口大洪水的沉积及其对火星早期气候的影响”的研究于11月5日发表在《科学报告》上。

The raging megaflood—likely touched off by the heat of a meteoritic impact, which unleashed ice stored on the Martian surface—set up gigantic ripples that are tell-tale geologic structures familiar to scientists on Earth.

汹涌的巨型洪水（很可能是由陨石撞击的热量触发的，它释放了火星表面储存的冰）形成了巨大的涟漪，这是地球上科学家们所熟悉的具有说服力的地质构造。

"We identified megafloods for the first time using detailed sedimentological data observed by the rover Curiosity," said co-author Alberto G. Fairén, a visiting astrobiologist in the College of Arts and Sciences. "Deposits left behind by megafloods had not been previously identified with orbiter data."

“我们使用流动站好奇号观测到的详细的沉积学数据首次发现了特大洪水，”合著者，艺术与科学学院的客座生物学家Alberto G.Fairén说。 “以前没有用轨道器数据识别出特大洪水留下的沉积物。”

As is the case on Earth, including the work of and wind have been frozen in time on Mars for about 4 billion years. These features convey processes that shaped the surface of both planets in the past.

与地球一样，包括火星和风在内的时间在火星上被冻结了约40亿年。这些特征传达了过去塑造两个行星表面的过程。

This case includes the occurrence of giant wave-shaped features in sedimentary layers of Gale , often called "megaripples" or antidunes that are about 30-feet high and spaced about 450 feet apart, according to lead author Ezat Heydari, a professor of physics at Jackson State University.

该研究的主要作者之一，埃塞特•海达里（Ezat Heydari）说，这种情况包括在大风的沉积层中出现巨大的波浪形特征，通常被称为“巨波纹”或反沙丘，高约30英尺，相距约450英尺。杰克逊州立大学。

The antidunes are indicative of flowing megafloods at the bottom of Mars' Gale Crater about 4 billion years ago, which are identical to the features formed by melting ice on Earth about 2 million years ago, Heydari said.

Heydari说，这些反沙丘表明大约40亿年前火星大风火山口底部有特大洪水在流动，这与大约200万年前地球上的冰融化形成的特征相同。

The most likely cause of the Mars flooding was the melting of ice from heat generated by a large impact, which released carbon dioxide and methane from the planet's frozen reservoirs. The and release of gases combined to produce a short period of warm and wet conditions on the red planet.

火星洪水的最可能原因是大撞击产生的热量使冰融化，从冰冻的储层中释放出二氧化碳和甲烷。气体的释放与释放结合在一起，在红色星球上产生了短暂的暖湿条件。

Condensation formed water vapor clouds, which in turn created torrential rain, possibly planetwide. That water entered Gale Crater, then combined with water coming down from Mount Sharp (in Gale Crater) to produce gigantic flash floods that deposited the gravel ridges in the Hummocky Plains Unit and the ridge-and-trough band formations in the Striated Unit.

凝结形成水蒸气云，这又产生了可能遍布全球的暴雨。那些水进入大风火山口，然后与从夏普山（大风火山口）流出的水结合起来，产生了巨大的山洪暴发，沉积了汉莫基平原单元的砾石山脊和横纹单元的山脊和低谷带构造。

The Curiosity rover science team has already established that Gale Crater once had persistent lakes and streams in the ancient past. These long-lived bodies of water are good indicators that the crater, as well as Mount Sharp within it, were capable of supporting microbial life.

好奇号流浪者科学团队已经确定，大风坑曾经在古代拥有持久的湖泊和溪流。这些长寿的水体很好地表明了火山口以及其中的夏普山能够支撑微生物的生命。

"Early Mars was an extremely active planet from a geological point of view," Fairén said. "The planet had the conditions needed to support the presence of liquid water on the surface—and on Earth, where there's water, there's life.

Fairén说：“从地质学的角度来看，早期的火星是一个非常活跃的星球。” “地球具有支持液态水在地表以及地球上存在水和生命的环境所需的条件。

"So early Mars was a habitable planet," he said. "Was it inhabited? That's a question that the next rover Perseverance ... will help to answer."

他说：“所以早期的火星是一个宜居的星球。” “有人居住吗？这是下一个漫游者的毅力……将有助于回答的问题。”

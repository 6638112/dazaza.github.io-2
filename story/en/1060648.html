When the WNBA went into a “bubble” last July to play its full, shortened 2020 season, players, coaches and staff were offered a ring by Finland-founded company Oura. The approximate size of a wedding band, the smart ring could measure heart rate and body temperature, signals of an athlete’s overall health and recovery. And during the Covid-19 pandemic, it offered an added benefit: reassurance that a player wasn’t getting sick.

当何时七月去年7月进入“泡沫”来发挥它的完整，缩短2020赛季，球员，教练和工作人员被芬兰成立的欧洲公司提供了一枚戒指。婚礼乐队的近似大小，智能环可以测量心率和体温，运动员整体健康和恢复的信号。在Covid-19大流行期间，它提供了一个额外的好处：保证球员不会生病。

The women’s basketball athletes were one of several leagues to work with Oura, alongside the NBA, Nascar, UFC and Red Bull Racing in Formula One. But while other wearable companies have gone to market — and gained buzz — by monitoring activity, Oura,  founded in 2013 in Finland and relying on the crowd-funding site Kickstarter to fund its first product, focused on  measuring sleep. “You're going to do it every day, no matter what. 99.9% of people will likely try to sleep tonight,” says Harpreet Singh Rai, Oura’s CEO. “We believe it's perhaps one of the most impactful things to your health that’s being under-looked.”

妇女的篮球运动员是与ORA合作的几个联赛之一，伴随着一级方程式的NBA，NASCAR，UFC和红牛赛车。但是，虽然其他可穿戴公司已经到了市场 - 并通过监测活动，欧洲行动，在2013年在芬兰成立，依靠人群资助的网站Kickstarter来资助其第一个产品，专注于测量睡眠。 “你＆＃39;无论如何，每天都会去做。 99.9％的人今晚可能会努力睡觉，“欧洲首席执行官Harpreet Singh Rai说。 “我们相信它，也许是你身体健康的最有影响力的事情之一。”

Today, Oura’s more like a typical high-growth tech business. Singh Rai lives in the San Francisco area, along with about half the company’s staff. Sales of about $30 million in 2019, per a regulatory filing in Finland, doubled year over year in 2020, Oura says, while achieving (at least temporary) profitability. And its signature smart ring has expanded beyond sleep and into measuring other vital signs.

今天，欧洲人更像是一个典型的高增长科技业。辛格莱住在旧金山地区，以及一半的公司员工。 2019年，销量约为3000万美元，根据芬兰的监管申请，在2020年的同一年的年度增加一倍，同时达到（至少临时）盈利能力。它的签名智能环已经扩大到睡眠之外，并衡量其他生命体征。

Investors are now pouring funds into the startup. The latest: a $100 million Series C funding round led by Temasek, Jazz Venture Partners, The Chernin Group, Bedford Ridge Capital, Japan’s One Capital and Elysian Park, a fund affiliated with the Los Angeles Dodgers baseball team. Existing investors Forerunner Ventures, Square, MSD Capital, Lifeline Ventures, Metaplanet Holdings, Next Ventures and Salesforce CEO Marc Benioff participated.

Singh Rai says Oura plans to invest the new funds in its research and development and product, as well as build out its marketing arm. The company is also open to acquisitions, its CEO says.

Launched out of Finland by founders Kari Kivela, Markku Koskela and Petteri Lahtela, Oura introduced its vision of a sleep-tracking smart ring in 2015, well exceeding its goals on Kickstarter before raising $5.3 million in first venture capital in 2016. That same year, Oura’s rings, originally estimated to ship by the end of 2015, arrived with customers including Singh Rai, an electrical engineering major at the University of Michigan who managed tech investments for a hedge fund. After investing in the business that September and joining its board of directors, Singh Rai became president in May 2017, relocating from New York to San Francisco, then assumed the CEO title the following year. That year, 2018, Oura shipped its second version of its product.

Oura于2015年推出了芬兰的芬兰Kari Kivela，Markku Koskela和Petteri Lahtela，在2016年在筹集了530万美元的第一次风险投资之前，它介绍了睡眠跟踪智能环的愿景。同年，欧洲的戒指最初估计到2015年底船舶，抵达了包括Michigan大学的电气工程专业在内的客户，他们管理了对冲基金的技术投资。在投资于9月份并加入其董事会的业务后，辛格莱于2017年5月成为总统，从纽约迁至旧金山，然后举行了首席执行官冠军。那年度，2018年，欧洲国家发运了其产品的第二个版本。

Oura took more than a year to reach 10 U.S. employees. Today, more than half are based in the country, with headcount increasing over the past year from about 100 to 250. Sales of Oura units, now in their less-bulky second generation, took off over the same period: Oura says it’s sold 500,000 units to date, up from 150,000 about a year ago. (The rings start at $299 and go up from there.)

Like many competitors from Apple Watch to Fitbit, Oura doesn’t have regulatory approval as a medical device, but markets itself as a crossover between a direct-to-consumer product and the regulated health space. “I don't think wearables can diagnose or treat yet, but the idea is of an early warning light to go see a medical professional if something is wrong,” says Singh Rai.

与Apple Watch的许多竞争对手一样对Fitbit，ORA没有作为医疗设备的监管批准，但市场本身就像直接消费产品和受管制的健康空间之间的交叉一样。 “我不认为可穿戴物可以诊断或治疗，但如果出现问题，这个想法是一个早期的警告灯，如果出现问题，请看看医疗专业人士，”Singh Rai说。

To be more than just a tracker, Oura’s consumer app must be able to learn, and set baselines, from the data it collects. Right now it provides three daily scores for wearers: sleep, activity and readiness. Among players in the WNBA, that data helped provide peace of mind that athletes weren’t getting sick last season, says Terri Jackson, executive director of the league’s players association, the WNBPA. For at least one player, that included confirming nothing looked unnatural in the app’s data after a positive Covid test, which later proved a false positive.

要不仅仅是一个跟踪器，我们的消费者应用程序必须能够从收集的数据中学习和设置基线。现在它为佩戴者提供了三个每日分数：睡眠，活动和准备。在联盟的球员协会执行董事，WNBPA的执行董事Terri Jackson表示，在WNBA中的员工人员有助于安心，以至于，运动员在赛前赛者协会执行董事Terri Jackson表示。对于至少一个玩家，其中包括确认在积极的Covid测试之后在应用程序的数据中没有看出任何不自然，后来证明了一个假阳性。

Jackson, an avid user, gave Oura rings to herself and her husband for their anniversary before going into the bubble, where she also offered rings to housekeeping, meal preparation and other staff. (She also ensures her son, NBA player Jaren Jackson, Jr., wears his.) According to Jackson, WNBA players embraced the device after confirming that their data would only be accessible to three people, including Jackson, and anonymized. “Once players understood the data that was being shared and who had access to it, they felt more comfortable about it,” she says. “The players wanted to see sleep information and related recovery data — they were keying in on it.”

杰克逊是一个狂热的用户，在进入泡泡之前给了自己和她的丈夫，她的丈夫为他们的丈夫，她还为家政，餐饮和其他工作人员提供戒指。 （她还确保她的儿子NBA球员Jaren Jackson，JR.，穿着他的。）根据杰克逊的说法，WNBA玩家在确认他们的数据只能访问包括三个人，包括杰克逊和匿名的数据后拥抱该设备。 “一旦玩家理解正在分享的数据并且谁可以访问它，他们对此感到更加舒服，”她说。 “玩家希望看到睡眠信息和相关的恢复数据 - 他们正在键入它。”

Signs of limited recovery can be useful — the WNBPA, for example, has pointed to it to argue for more rest days with the league for its upcoming season — but Oura’s ability to help users act on their data is limited for now. Singh Rai says the company plans to use some of the funding to build out an “insight engine,” which he likens to a “personalized health coach'' to improve user’s health. Such artificial intelligence-driven nudges featured heavily in the success of the chronic health management company Livongo, which was acquired by Teladoc Health for $18.5 billion last year. “We want to validate and prove our research first,” says Singh Rai. “We then want to go ahead and see where the industry goes, and we anticipate it moving more towards medically regulated devices.”

恢复有限的迹象可能是有用的 - 例如，WNBPA指出，它指出了与联盟即将到来的季节与联盟的更多休息日 - 但我们的帮助用户对用户行事的能力仅限于现在。 Singh Rai表示，该公司计划使用一些资金来建立一个“Insight引擎”，他比这是一个“个性化健康教练”和＃39;＆＃39;改善用户的健康状况。这些人工智能推动的讽刺旨在严重的是，在慢性健康管理公司Livongo成功，该公司去年由Teladoc Health获得185亿美元的收购。 “我们希望首先验证并证明我们的研究，”辛格莱说。 “我们希望继续前进，看看行业在哪里，我们预计它就会向医学监管的设备移动。”

To this end, Oura’s accuracy is being evaluated by independent researchers. Joshua Hagen, an assistant professor and director of the Human Performance Innovation Center at West Virginia University, has spent a decade studying wearables as a way to optimize military and athletic performance. Hagen tested four commercially available wearables against a clinical grade electrocardiogram, which measures heart rate. This pitted Oura, which sits on a finger, with other devices that were strapped across the chest. “My hypothesis was anything that's not a chest strap was going to be much less accurate,” says Hagen. “Oura was the outlier.” It had the second highest accuracy among the devices.

为此，欧洲的准确性正在由独立的研究人员进行评估。 Joshua Hagen是西弗吉尼亚大学人类绩效创新中心的助理教授和主任，已经花了十年的学习佩戴性，作为优化军事和运动表现的一种方式。 Hagen测试了四个可用于临床等级心电图的可商购的可穿戴性，测量心率。这是坐在手指上的灰色，其他设备绑在胸前。 “我的假设是＆＃39;没有胸带的任何东西都将不那么准确，”Hagen说。 “欧洲是异常值。”它在设备之间具有第二个最高精度。

Hagen conducted another study looking at how wearables measure sleep relative to the electrodes that measures brainwaves in a sleep lab. Oura performed well as far as measuring how much time the user spent asleep and awake, though, like all the devices tested, had challenges accurately predicting how much time a user spent within a specific sleep cycle, such as light, deep or REM sleep.

Hagen进行了另一项研究，研究了可穿戴物品如何相对于睡眠实验室中造成脑力的电极睡眠。 ORA表现得很好，但衡量用户睡着了多少时间，尽管与所有测试的所有设备一样，具有挑战，请准确预测用户在特定睡眠周期内花费的时间，例如光，深或REM睡眠。

When the Covid-19 pandemic hit, Hagen and his team started looking for clues in user data to predict illness before the person showed symptoms. What his group found is that by retroactively applying an algorithm to Oura user data, they could identify early warning signs three days in advance. That study has yet to be published. A separate team from the University of California San Francisco is running a study called TemPredict, which published a proof-of-concept study showing Oura and other wearables can detect the onset of fever.

当Covid-19大流行击中时，Hagen和他的团队开始寻找用户数据中的线索，以预测疾病之前的症状。他的小组发现是，通过追溯将算法应用于我们的用户数据，他们可以提前三天识别预警标志。该研究尚未发表。来自加州大学旧金山的独立团队正在运行一项名为Tempredict的研究，该研究发表了一个概念证据，显示ORA和其他可穿戴物可以检测发烧的发烧。

Even with such promising results, Oura faces a stable of competition with its own powerful backers and advocates. Whoop, a Boston-based startup that tracks activity through a wrist device, raised $100 million at a higher $1.2 billion valuation last October. And in January, Google completed a $2.1 billion acquisition of Fitbit, which claims to have sold more than 120 million devices to date.

即使有了如此有希望的结果，欧洲也面临着与自己强大的支持者和倡导者的稳定竞争。 WHOOP是一家基于波士顿的创业公司，可通过手腕设备追踪活动，筹集了1亿美元，以至于去年10月较高的12亿美元估值。 1月份，谷歌完成了21亿美元收购Fitbit，据声称迄今已售出超过1.0多万设备。

At Forerunner Ventures, which led Oura’s $28 million Series B funding round last March, investor Eurie Kim says that most of Oura’s backers are avid users themselves. The “stickiness” of Oura’s sleep focus — a daily activity regardless of whether the user is in shape or injured — gives it the potential to outlast competitors, Kim argues. Non-users simply can’t fully understand, she adds. “If you changed something in your life, improved your health with Oura, you would understand why this round was an obvious win for all the people that invested, and that it’s only the beginning,” Kim says.

投资者Eurie Kim在去年3月份迈出了Orerunner Ventures，其中LED ORA的2800万美元Funding Bulding round圆形的资金。我们的大部分支持者都是Avid用户自己。苏拉睡眠重点的“粘性” - 无论用户是否形状或受伤，都有日常活动 - 使竞争对手的潜力可能是竞争对手。她补充说，非用户根本无法完全理解。 “如果你在生活中改变了一些东西，就会改善你的健康，你会理解为什么这一轮对投资的所有人来说是一个明显的胜利，而且它只是一个开始，这只是一个开始，”这是刚刚开始的，“这是唯一的，”金说。

Oura’s CEO says the wearables market is still small, with plenty of room for competitors to coexist. Fitbit adding sleep tracking boosted Oura sales, he claims, instead of hurting them. Over time, Singh Rai expects the entire industry to settle on a few best practices, though, including the combination of hardware with a paid content subscription, much like Peloton, he says: “I think you can think about the future of the wearable industry, including us, the same way.”

欧洲首席执行官表示，可佩戴性市场仍然很小，有足够的竞争对手的空间共存。 Fitbit添加睡眠跟踪推动了我们的销售，他声称，而不是伤害他们。随着时间的推移，辛格rai期望整个行业解决了几个最佳实践，包括硬件与付费内容订阅的结合，就像Peloton一样，他说：“我认为你可以考虑穿戴行业的未来，包括美国，同样的方式。“

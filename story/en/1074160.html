2-13-22 When I was ten, a relative gave me a Gilbert chemistry set that he had found at a local garage sale. I do not know when this particular set was manufactured. Based on the construction of the metal case and the fact that the set contained no plastic whatsoever, my guess is the late 1950's or early 1960's. I loved that chemistry set. It contained about a dozen test tubes, a 50 ml beaker, a 50 ml Erlenmeyer flask, litmus paper, several glass tubes, an assortment of corks, rubber tubing, an alcohol burner, test tube tongs, copper and zinc plates, and various assorted chemicals in glass bottles with steel screw-on tops. This was my introduction to Chemistry.

My favorite item was the booklet of perhaps 250 experiments. I could not perform many of them, because the required chemicals were no longer present, but I spent weeks reading that booklet over and over. Its theory section and experiments explained many things of practical value. They taught how to heat the glass tubes with the alcohol burner and bend them into needed shapes for various experiments. They explained basic safety precautions. They summarized how to use litmus paper and how to perform titrations. They revealed underlying principles of acids, bases, and buffers. They taught about oxidation and reduction and exothermic and endothermic reactions. This is where I first learned about atomic valences, the theory that explains, for example, the existence of the two oxidation states of iron as ferric oxide and ferrous oxide.

我最喜欢的东西是一本大约有250个实验的小册子。我不能执行很多操作，因为所需的化学物质不再存在，但我花了数周时间反复阅读这本小册子。它的理论部分和实验解释了许多有实用价值的东西。他们教如何用酒精燃烧器加热玻璃管，并将其弯曲成各种实验所需的形状。他们解释了基本的安全预防措施。他们总结了如何使用石蕊试纸以及如何进行滴定。他们揭示了酸、碱和缓冲剂的基本原理。他们教授氧化和还原以及放热和吸热反应。这是我第一次了解原子价的地方，这个理论解释了，例如，铁的两种氧化态，氧化铁和氧化亚铁的存在。

This chemistry set planted in me the seeds from which grew a fascination for the subject and an eagerness to learn everything I could about it. Later, I took two chemistry classes in high school, which were may favorite classes of all time. I loved them even more than my high school computer class. I went on to study Chemistry for a time in college. My final senior-level college Chemistry class in quantum mechanics was the culmination of my formal Chemistry education. Ultimately, I realized that--not despite, but because of my of love the subject--my destiny lay in engineering. That decision was made only with a great deal of soul searching and sadness. I knew myself well enough to realize that working in industry as a chemist would have brought only endless frustration and ultimately kill my love for a subject that I could not bear to devalue in that way.

这种化学在我身上播下了种子，从中我对这门学科产生了一种迷恋，并渴望尽我所能了解它的一切。后来，我在高中上了两门化学课，这可能是我最喜欢的课程。我甚至比我的高中计算机课更爱他们。我在大学里学了一段时间化学。我的最后一堂量子力学高级大学化学课是我正规化学教育的高潮。最终，我意识到——不是尽管如此，而是因为我对这门学科的热爱——我的命运在于工程学。这个决定是在大量的反省和悲伤中做出的。我很了解自己，意识到在工业界当化学家只会带来无尽的挫败感，最终扼杀我对一个我无法忍受以这种方式贬值的主题的热爱。

When I was perhaps twelve, another relative gave me another chemistry set that was manufactured in the 1970's. This set was nothing like my Gilbert set. In place of the amazing experiment booklet, it contained a few sheets of paper that gave step-by-step instructions for mixing chemicals and told the resulting color changes to expect. It gave no explanation about why the color change occurred. The chemicals did not even come in bottles. They came in paper packets, so I could only perform a particular experiment once. This chemistry set contained no alcohol burner, no glass or rubber tubes. Half a dozen test tubes were its only glassware.

当我大概12岁的时候，另一位亲戚给了我另一套1970年生产的化学仪器'；s、 这一套跟我的吉尔伯特一点也不像。除了这本神奇的实验小册子之外，它还包含了几张纸，这些纸给出了混合化学物质的逐步说明，并告诉了预期的颜色变化。它没有解释为什么会发生颜色变化。这些化学物质甚至不是瓶装的。它们装在纸袋里，所以我只能做一次特定的实验。这套化学装置没有酒精燃烧器，没有玻璃管或橡胶管。六个试管是它唯一的玻璃器皿。

I later learned the reason for the difference between the two chemistry sets. The Gilbert set was manufactured before lawyers managed to convince courts in the United States that giving a child an alcohol burner and chemicals with which he could accidentally harm himself was grounds for a law suit. In the 1970's, chemistry sets became so stripped down that children could no longer do anything interesting with them. The courts had decided that if a twelve-year-old wondered what cobalt chloride tasted like, was stupid enough to find out, and poison himself in the process, the manufacturer of the Chemistry set was at fault. If a child burned his fingers while heating chemicals in a test tube, because he did not think the explained safety procedures were important, the manufacturer had to pay.

后来我了解了这两组化学成分之间差异的原因。吉尔伯特套装是在律师们试图说服美国法院，让一个孩子使用酒精燃烧器和可能会意外伤害自己的化学品是起诉的理由之前制造的。1970年'；s、 化学装置变得如此简陋，以至于孩子们再也不能用它们做任何有趣的事情了。法院裁定，如果一个12岁的孩子想知道氯化钴的味道是什么，愚蠢到发现了，并在这个过程中毒死了自己，那么化学试剂盒的制造商就有错。如果一个孩子在试管中加热化学品时烧伤了手指，因为他认为解释的安全程序并不重要，制造商必须支付费用。

The result was that virtually no child who grew up in the US in the 1970's or 1980's could have the same experience that I had had as a child. The only exceptions were the few fortunate children like me with relatives who happened to stumble upon an old chemistry set in a garage sale and understood that the value of the learning experience as worth the slight safety risk. Thank God I had such a relative when I was ten.

结果是，几乎没有一个1970年在美国长大的孩子'；或1980年'；s可以有和我小时候一样的经历。只有少数几个像我这样幸运的孩子例外，他们的亲戚碰巧在车库大甩卖中偶然发现了一种旧的化学物质，他们明白学习经验的价值值得冒一点安全风险。谢天谢地，我十岁时就有了这样一位亲戚。

We now live in a world run by lawyers. This is a world that has nearly ended the manufacture of private aircraft that any individual with a private pilot's license and enough money can purchase. This is a world that feels compelled to pass laws requiring the use of seat belts and motorcycle helmets in the hope of protecting people who are too stupid to wear them otherwise. If your car runs out of gasoline and you manage to walk to a gas station, you cannot buy gasoline in anything other than an government approved container. The fact that you are stranded makes no difference. If someone falls off a ladder while painting your house, they can sue you and take your house. This is a world that no longer allows you to purchase hydrogen peroxide above a certain concentration for fear you will use it to make a bomb. This is a world in which we have lost sight of the fact that constraining ourselves with ridiculous laws to protect us from anything that might conceivable harm us relegates us to the role of toddlers who don't know enough not to touch something hot. The god of safety has been placed above the individual's basic intelligence, creativity, and pursuit of knowledge. This is a world that uses the pretense of protecting us from ourselves to take away our basic freedoms. It says to us, "Your are too stupid to make your own decisions, so we will make them for you.

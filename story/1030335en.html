I’ve been wanting to try my hand at decapping ICs for years, for no other purposethan to satiate my curiosity. I’ve finally had the time to get to it, so thispost will describe the methods I tried and the hurdles I encountered.

The biggest factor to decide which method is best for your project is whetheror not you need the chip to still work after it’s been decapsulated. That meansnot destroying or disconnecting the die, bonding wires, external contact points,etc. so you can still use the chip after the process is complete.

决定哪种方法最适合你的项目的最大因素是，你是否需要芯片在被解封后仍能工作。这意味着不会损坏或断开芯片、焊接线、外部接触点等，因此在工艺完成后仍可使用芯片。

Full wet chemical decapsulation: Bath the entire chip in acid to remove all encapsulation, leads, bonds, etc.

全湿化学解封：将整个芯片浸泡在酸中，以去除所有封装、引线、粘合等。

Selective wet chemical decapsulation: Manual acid deposition:Repeatedly apply drops of acid on the IC’s target area, and rinse the acidresidue and weakened encapsulation

选择性湿化学解封装：手动酸沉积：在IC的靶区反复涂抹酸液，冲洗酸渣和弱化的封装。

Plasma etchinguses expensive equipment to create plasma, make it react with the encapsulation,and drain it away

等离子体蚀刻使用昂贵的设备来产生等离子体，使其与封装发生反应，然后将其排出。

In this post we’re gonna focus on the manual acid deposition method, to achievenon-destructive decapsulation at a reasonable cost.

在这篇文章中，我们将重点介绍人工酸沉淀法，以合理的成本实现无损的解封。

Drill a pocket on the top of the epoxy package so the acid does not spill overto the leads.

在环氧树脂包装的顶部钻一个口袋，这样酸就不会溢出到引线上。

当温度合适时(100摄氏度左右)，在我们刚钻好的环氧树脂口袋里滴上一滴或两滴酸。等待，直到没有更多的酸，并在发生这种情况时继续应用酸。

每隔一段时间，用塑料镊子抓住IC，将其浸泡在丙酮中，并将其四处移动，以去除反应的环氧树脂。

Reacted epoxy had a similar consistency to wet charcoal, so I could easilyremove it with the tip of my tweezers. In a second test, that worked fine for abit; until I encountered the die and ripped all the bonding wires right off it.

After the previous failure, it seemed obvious I needed a method to remove moreof the reacted epoxy without using a hard tool to manually extract it. I decidedto push a stream of acetone aimed directly at the target area.

在之前的失败之后，似乎很明显我需要一种方法来移除更多的反应环氧树脂，而不需要使用硬工具手动提取它。我决定把一股丙酮流直接对准目标区域。

The process was significantly more effective, removing epoxy more precisely andat a much quicker rate. It still took a long time, but the syringe was a bittoo effective in a sense…

这一过程明显更有效，更准确、更快地去除了环氧树脂。这仍然花了很长时间，但注射器在某种意义上是有效的(…。

The high-pressure stream pulled too much reacted epoxy off the IC, creating awider, deeper hole much more rapidly. Soon enough, I was able to reach the topof the bond wires, hence locating the die. But the bond wires are akin to steelrods in reinforced concrete: they improve the robustness of the epoxy area atopof the silicon die.

By attempting to use the syringe method to extract the last area of epoxy over the die,it’s easy to first expose the internal parts of the leads surrounding it. Oncethat happens, and we apply more nitric acid, it will dissolve the leads faster thanit weakens the epoxy atop of the die. We need to figure out a better, gentler way to remove the last layer of epoxy andclean up residue off the silicon die without damaging the bonds.

使用硝酸沉积+丙酮注射器提取的方法挖穿环氧乙烷，直到键合导线的顶部暴露出来。

Once the bond wires are found and we’re about to reach the die, it’s time tostop using the acetone syringe method to extract reacted epoxy. Instead, submergethe IC in the ultrasonic acetone bath

一旦找到了粘合线，我们即将到达芯片，就该停止使用丙酮注射器的方法来提取反应的环氧树脂了。取而代之的是，将IC浸入超声丙酮浴中。

Repeat the acid deposition + acetone ultrasonic bath until the entire silicondie is exposed. If the ultrasonic bath is unable to get any particular chunk ofepoxy, use a thin syringe to apply light pressure over that area.

重复酸沉积+丙酮超声浴，直到整个硅片裸露。如果超声浴无法获得任何特定的环氧树脂块，请使用薄注射器对该区域施加光压力。

After all the previous attempts, this methodology was finally enoughto non-destructively expose the entire silicon die. It worked on the first attempt,and the surface of the die was pristine right away. No messy residue obscuring theview under a microscope.

在所有之前的尝试之后，这种方法最终足以非破坏性地暴露整个硅晶片。它在第一次尝试时就起作用了，模具的表面立刻变得一尘不染。显微镜下没有杂乱的残留物模糊了视野。

Taking great pictures under the microscope is not easywithout expensive, specialized cameras. Getting the focus and lighting rightthroughout the sample is tricky, which makes it hard to get decent results fromimage stitching software. More info on imaging later.

没有昂贵的专用相机，在显微镜下拍出好照片并非易事。在整个样品中获得正确的对焦和照明是一件棘手的事情，这使得图像拼接软件很难获得像样的结果。稍后将提供更多有关成像的信息。

Still, for the sake of gaining some more detail in the overall picture, here’s acomposite image created from higher magnification pictures:

不过，为了在整体画面中获得更多细节，以下是由放大倍数较高的图片创建的合成图像：

Attempt 6: Dremel + 69% Nitric Acid + Room temp Nitric Acid + Acetone Syringe + Ultrasonic Acetone Cleanup

尝试6：Dremel+69%硝酸+室温硝酸+丙酮注射器+超声波丙酮清洗。

I’ve seen different sources recommend a room temp nitric acid bath after theetching process. IIRC, the goal is to improve the uniformity of the etched area.

我看到不同的消息来源建议在蚀刻过程后使用室温硝酸浴。IIRC，目标是提高蚀刻区域的均匀性。

I tested it, and it didn’t make much of a difference. Perhaps it’s not that usefulfor manual deposition? I’m not sure, but given the lack of discernible differences,there’s no point for me to document it any further.

我测试了一下，结果没什么不同。或许这对手工取证没什么用处？我不确定，但考虑到缺乏明显的区别，我没有必要进一步记录。

Attempt 7: Dremel + 98% Sulfuric Acid + 69% Nitric Acid + Ultrasonic Acetone Bath

尝试7：Dremel+98%硫酸+69%硝酸+超声丙酮浴。

I’ve seen a mix of sulfuric and nitric acid recommended in some literature.I did try it a couple of times, and it made no significant difference. It might helpwith different encapsulation or bond wire materials, but it was pretty much pointlessin my tests.

我在一些文献中看到过推荐的硫酸和硝酸的混合物，我确实试过几次，但没有明显的区别。它可能对不同的封装或粘结线材料有帮助，但在我的测试中几乎没有意义。

I’d rather not deal with mixing dangerous acids, or the extra fuming it entails,so I gave up trying and would not recommend it unless pure Nitric is not doingthe job.

我宁愿不去处理混合危险的酸，或它带来的额外烟雾，所以我放弃了尝试，除非纯粹的硝酸不起作用，否则我不会推荐它。

Still, here’s one of the tests I ran. The methodology is identical to the previousattempt, only I mixed about 5ml of 98% Sulfuric Acid and 6.6ml of 69% Nitric Acid.That results in an approximate 50/50 mix of the active chemicals, accounting forthe difference in purity.

不过，这是我做过的一项测试。方法与之前的尝试相同，只是我混合了大约5ml的98%的硫酸和6.6ml的69%的硝酸，这导致了活性化学物质的大约50/50的混合，这说明了纯度的不同。

One of the tests I ran was on ADXL345 accelerometers, since I wanted to take agood look at a MicroElectroMechanical System (MEMS) IC. The IC itself is so small,I did not remove it from its development PCB so I could move it around more easily.

我进行的其中一项测试是在ADXL345加速计上进行的，因为我想更好地观察微机电系统(MEMS)IC。集成电路本身很小，我没有把它从它的开发PCB上移走，这样我就可以更容易地移动它。

MEMS devices use micro-scale moving pieces, so they need to build differently.Let’s take a look…

MEMS器件使用微型移动部件，因此它们需要不同的制造。让我们来看看…。

Here we can see that the circuit’s internals are covered by a metal lid, so theepoxy does not glue the moving pieces together.

在这里我们可以看到电路的内部被一个金属盖所覆盖，因此环氧树脂不会将移动部件粘合在一起。

I first tried to remove the lid by pushing thin metallic tweezers from its side.In the process, I destroyed the upper layer of the MEMS:

我首先试着把薄金属镊子从盖子的侧面推开，在这个过程中，我破坏了MEMS的上层：

I decapped another ADXL345, loaded a brand new blade on my X-Acto knife, andcarefully cut through the lid’s edges using a USB “microscope” tosee what I was doing. I cut through the wire bonds in the process, because I justcared about seeing the MEMS; not being non-destructive. Here’s the undamaged toplayer of the accelerometer:

我拆下另一台ADXL345，在我的X-Acto刀上装上一个全新的刀片，然后用USB“显微镜”仔细地切开盖子的边缘，看看我在做什么。我在这个过程中切断了电线连接，因为我只关心看到MEMS，而不是无损的。这是完好无损的加速度计播放器：

Looks great! I did not remove the residue for these pictures to avoiddamaging the very delicate MEMS parts.

真不错!。我没有清除这些图片的残留物，以避免损坏非常精致的MEMS部件。

The ideal way to take pictures under the microscope would be to use a cameradesigned to fit into the microscope. I tried using a cheapish one included withthe microscope I bought, but the results were pretty terrible; probably becauseof lighting completely opaque samples like these is hard and imperfect in amicroscope like mine.

在显微镜下拍照的理想方式是使用设计成适合显微镜的相机。我试着用我买的显微镜附带的便宜的，但效果相当糟糕；可能是因为照明完全不透明，像这样的样品在我这样的显微镜下很难操作，也不完美。

A good smartphone’s camera is better at handling poor/irregular lighting conditions,but aligning it to the eyepiece is terribly annoying and imprecise.

一部好的智能手机的摄像头更善于处理糟糕/不规则的照明条件，但将其对准目镜是非常恼人的，而且不精确。

My solution was to design and 3D print an adapter to hold my old iPhone 6 directlyaligned to the microscope’s lens. It’s not perfect, but it’s immensely betterthan everything else I tried.

我的解决方案是设计并3D打印一个适配器，将我的旧iPhone6直接对准显微镜的镜头。它不是完美的，但比我试过的任何其他东西都要好得多。

Using this method, taking one decent picture of the IC is easy enough. But ifwe want a complete picture of the IC under higher magnification, we need to takemultiple pictures and stitch them together. For that, I used Image Composite Editor,created by the Microsoft Research Computational Photography Group. Getting it tocreate good results was not without headaches and tediousness, but once it works,it does create pretty amazing results. I’d definitely recommend it.

使用这种方法，给IC拍一张像样的照片是很容易的。但是如果我们想要在更高的倍率下获得集成电路的完整照片，我们需要拍摄多张照片并将它们拼接在一起。为此，我使用了由微软研究计算摄影小组创建的图像合成编辑器。让它创造好的结果并不是没有令人头疼和乏味的事情，但一旦它奏效，它确实会创造出相当令人惊叹的结果。我一定会推荐的。

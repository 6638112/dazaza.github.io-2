On a roll: skating booms in lockdown London
在滚动：在锁定伦敦滑冰臂

2021-04-03 10:51:03

O n Sunday, Shez Blake, 40, from south London , will have been roller skating for a month. She has been skating with her six-year-old son and excitedly talks about this landmark date because of the joy the sport has brought. “Skating gives you that sense of freedom … it’s like flying in the wind,” she says.

o N N星期日，Shez Blake，40，来自南伦敦，将一月溜冰。由于运动带来的快乐，她一直在滑冰与她的六岁儿子和六岁的儿子兴奋地谈论这个地标日。 “滑冰让你感到这种自由感......这就像在风中飞翔，”她说。

After struggling during the pandemic, skating has helped lift her mood. “There is such a sense of community. You are immediately welcomed.”

在大流行期间挣扎后，滑冰已经帮助提升了她的心情。 “有这样的社区感。你立刻欢迎。“

Blake is among a rising number of people who have started skating amid repeated lockdowns, but the growing community of skaters in London are calling for better spaces to go to as they are currently using tennis courts that have been shut as part of Covid-19 measures and scrambling for other flat spaces to use in parks or streets.

Blake是在重复锁定中开始滑冰的人数升起的人之一，但伦敦的越来越多的溜冰场呼吁更好的空间，因为他们目前正在使用的网球场被关闭作为Covid-19措施的一部分。并争先恐后地用于公园或街道。

“It is fundamental to mental health. Even where I skate in Burgess Park it is not the best surface at all but it is the only space we can use. My son is only six and it is gravelly so when he falls over he gets gravel on his hands,” says Shez.

“这是心理健康的基础。即使我在Burgess Park滑冰时也不是最好的表面，但它是我们可以使用的唯一空间。我的儿子只有六个，它是砾石的，所以当他摔倒时，他手上的砾石，“谢兹说。

While figures from retailers such as Locoskates and Slick Willies highlight just how popular skating has become during the pandemic – the companies have seen sales grow between 500 and 800% in the last 12 months – finding somewhere to skate is more difficult.

虽然零售商如Locoskates和Slick Willies的数字突出了滑冰在大流行过程中的流行度过了流行的，但在过去的12个月中，这些公司在过去的12个月中看到的销售增长了500％到800％ - 在某处寻找滑冰是更困难的。

Many, such as 25-year-old Amir Bacchus-Marquis – who has been skating since he was eight and started in a “shabby hall rented out by someone” in Tottenham – have been campaigning for more space for years. They have battled against constantly being moved on from car parks and parks; some areas are even laying down gravel to stop skaters, he says.

许多人，如25岁的Amir Bacchus-Marquis - 自从他八岁以来一直在滑冰，并且在托特纳姆租用的“破旧大厅”中开始 - 多年来一直在竞选更多空间。他们已经争夺了停车场和公园的不断移动;他说，有些地区甚至铺设了砾石来阻止溜冰者。

But he says that it is difficult to get people to listen or engage with officials, an issue he partly puts down to the roots of the skating culture being inherently black in the UK.

但他说，很难让人倾听或与官员倾听或聘用，这是他部分地归结为冰雪文化的根源在英国的根源。

“Skating, especially in the US is deeply embedded in black culture. It came over to the UK as a kind of underground scene for black disfranchised people who used skating to meet and congregate and have good food,” he says.

“滑冰，特别是在美国，深深地嵌入黑色文化中。他称之为使用滑冰和聚集在一起的黑人被解雇的人的地下景象，并拥有良好的食物，“他说。

“I think because the community has always been very black there is lots of trauma when it comes to speaking to or getting in contact with government officials, people who can make a difference and support the community and what we are doing,” says Bacchus-Marquis.

“我认为，因为社区一直非常黑了，有很多创伤，当谈到与政府官员接触或接触政府官员，那些能够差异和支持社区以及我们所做的事情，”布法库斯 - 侯爵。

He adds: “Even those who have tried to broach it have not been taken seriously as someone else might.”

他补充说：“即使是那些试图拉机的人也没有像别人那样认真对待。”

Bacchus-Marquis says they organise through word of mouth because the community is “so huge” and they also use social media. “The community is massive so it is easy to tell someone let’s meet Tuesday at 9am we will be here. It reverberates around,” he says.

Bacchus-Marquis说，他们通过口碑组织，因为社区是“如此巨大”，他们也使用社交媒体。 “社区是大规模的，所以很容易告诉别人让我们在上午9点举行会见我们将在这里。它回荡，“他说。

He started skating in Stratford Mall in east London until it was no longer allowed. “We moved to car parks,” he says, adding that he is constantly being kicked out of areas and chased away.

他开始在东伦敦的Stratford Mall滑冰，直到不再允许。 “我们搬到了停车场，”他说，补充说，他不断被赶出地区并追逐。

“I have experienced little bits of violence and really bad and nasty people in terms of people seeing what we are doing and thinking we are trying to be awkward about it,” he says, adding that it’s a way for young people to “keep their heads straight”. “We have nowhere safe to go after dark,” he says.

“在看到我们正在做的事情和思考我们正试图尴尬的人方面，我经历了很少的暴力和讨厌的人，”他说，“他说，这是一个年轻人”保持他们的人“抬头“。 “在黑暗之后，我们无处可行，”他说。

Bacchus-Marquis says skating is about freedom and mental health. He says he used skating as a tool to keep him sane when his sister passed away when he was young. “It’s always been that for me,” he says.

Bacchus-Marquis说滑冰是关于自由和心理健康的。他说他用滑冰作为一种工具，让他姐姐在他年轻的时候让他保持理智。 “这一直是我的，”他说。

Elizabeth Melinek has launched a campaign in Hackney, east London, to get the council to use some of their redevelopment budgets to create a multipurpose flat, level skate space and it has had over 1,000 signatures. As a mother of two boys, she says she has “seen the impact of lockdown on teenagers” and feels it is “important to create free social facilities for young people”.

Elizabeth Melinek在东伦敦哈克尼推出了一项竞选活动，让理事会使用一些重建预算来创造多用途平板，水平滑板空间，它有超过1000个签名。作为两个男孩的母亲，她说她“看到了锁定对青少年的影响”，并且觉得为年轻人创造免费的社会设施很重要“。

“There are seven tennis courts in Clissold Park. During lockdown they were being used by over 100 socially distanced people as opposed to the 12 people that normally use them … I am not anti-tennis or anti-golf but they are sports traditionally associated with the elite. Roller sports, including skateboards, are accessible to everyone and are now widely enjoyed by a hugely, diverse range of people who all need space to practise,” she says.

“克里斯德公园有七个网球场。在锁定期间，他们被超过100个社交距离的人使用，而不是12人，而不是通常使用它们的人......我不是反网球或反高尔夫，但他们是传统上与精英相关的运动。滚轮运动，包括滑板，每个人都可以访问，现在广泛享受一系列各种各样的人，他们都需要练习空间，“她说。

She adds: “It’s really important to note that roller skating has a long, rich history in the black community too, and it is primarily black skaters and skaters from working-class backgrounds who have been keeping the activity alive and fighting for increasingly diminishing space.”

她补充说：“非常重要的是要注意的那个旱冰鞋也在黑人社区拥有漫长，丰富的历史，主要是来自工作舱背景的黑色滑冰者和溜冰者，他们一直保持活动和争夺越来越越来越越来越越来越越来越越来越越来越少的空间。“

Lenka Kopecka, who has been skating for 15 years, also thinks skating is a huge mental health booster. “The growth in interest during lockdown is because people are thinking: how do I keep busy? It’s a healthy option. I am a psychotherapist and have lots of clients who do not know what to do with themselves.”

Lenka Kopecka一直在滑冰15年，也认为滑冰是一个巨大的心理健康助推器。 “锁定期间兴趣的增长是因为人们在思考：我如何忙碌？这是一个健康的选择。我是一个心理治疗师，有很多不知道该如何与自己有关的客户。“

“When you look at those who skate, there is immediate positive energy compared to other sports. Everyone is always smiling.” Nikki and Andrew Brandt, aged 50 and 54, skated as teenagers in south London. “It’s completely inclusive, you have got people of all ages including kids, and all sorts of races and backgrounds doing it ... People mix really easily,” says Nikki.

“当你看看那些滑冰的人时，与其他运动相比，就有立即积极的能量。每个人都始终微笑着。“ Nikki和Andrew Brandt，50岁和54岁，作为南伦敦的青少年滑冰。 “这是完全包容的，你有人拥有所有年龄段的人，包括孩子，以及各种各样的种族和背景......人们真的很容易混合，”Nikki说。

Her partner, Andrew, adds: “I have been roller skating for probably over 30 years off and on I used to play roller hockey when I was younger and then I used to street skate.”

她的伴侣，安德鲁，补充说：“当我年轻的时候，我一直在30多年的时间超过30年，我曾经玩过滚轮曲棍球，然后我曾经街道滑冰。”

They both think that given the freedom the sport brings, the rise in its growth will not go away, so a solution to the lack of space is needed. “There were loads of new skaters last year and that continued through the winter and it has not stopped,” says Andrew. “It’s therapeutic and fun and good for weight loss and mental health. It will get pretty popular again this summer.”

他们都认为，鉴于这种运动带来的自由，其增长的增加不会消失，因此需要解决空间缺乏的解决方案。 “去年有大量的新滑冰者，并在冬天继续，它没有停止，”安德鲁说。 “这是治疗性和乐趣，对减肥和心理健康有益。今年夏天，它会再次受欢迎。“

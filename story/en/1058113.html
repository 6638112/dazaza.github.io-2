Last year's new consoles, the Xbox Series X/S and PlayStation 5, have a ton in common as far as hardware is concerned. As just one example, they lean on a new generation of internal storage—rated PCIe 4.0 and connected via the NVMe protocol. With this jump, read and write speeds increase dramatically, and that's been part of the latest next-gen gaming sales pitch. The trouble begins once you install games on these new consoles, at which point you'll learn that the NVMe storage size listed on their boxes isn't quite accurate.

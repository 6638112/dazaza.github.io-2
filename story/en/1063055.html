Don’t eat cicadas if you’re allergic to seafood, the FDA warns
如果您对海鲜过敏，FDA警告，不要吃蝉

2021-06-03 23:17:20

Weeks after the arrival of Brood X, the buzzy swarm of cicadas that emerge from the ground every 17 years to cover trees and sidewalks across a swath of the country, federal health officials have a new warning: People with seafood allergies shouldn’t eat the insects.

育雏X的到来后几周，蝉的狂欢群，每17年出现在地面上，覆盖了这个国家的一流的树木和人行道，联邦卫生官员有一个新的警告：海鲜过敏的人不应该吃掉昆虫。

The Food and Drug Administration on Wednesday issued the advice to would-be bug-munchers via Twitter, noting that the cicadas “share a family relation to shrimp and lobsters.”

周三的食品和药物管理局通过Twitter向Wabl-Munchers发出了建议，并指出蝉“与虾和龙虾分享家庭关系。”

Many chefs and home cooks have been experimenting with recipes incorporating the plentiful cicadas since their arrival in mid-May, wrapping them in rice like sushi, filling tacos with them or using them to garnish chocolate cookies. For the first time in the brood’s long history, they are meeting their ultimate fate in air fryers, the currently voguish cooking appliance.

许多厨师和家庭厨师一直在尝试加入丰富的蝉以来，自5月中旬以来，将它们缠绕在寿司如寿司，与他们填充炸玉米饼或使用它们装饰巧克力饼干。这是在毕业的历史上第一次，他们正在满足他们在空气炸锅中的终极命运，目前的莫斯特烹饪器具。

Our colleagues Kari Sonde and Ann Maloney recently offered a recipe for Spicy Popcorn Cicadas, adapted from the “Cicada-Licious” cookbook by evolutionary biologist and ecologist Jenna Jadin, in which the bugs are marinated, dipped in a peppery batter and pan-fried. Cicada dishes have made appearances on many a local newscast; CNN anchor Brianna Keilar even sampled some on air this week.

我们的同事Kari Sonde和Ann Maloney最近为辣爆米花牌提供了一份食谱，由进化生物学家和生态学家珍珠·贾尼丁的“蝉生物”食谱改编，其中臭虫腌制，蘸上佩戴的击球手和煎炸。蝉菜在许多本地新闻播放方面取得了外观; CNN Anchor Brianna Keill甚至在本周在空中取样了一些。

Environmental advocates have long promoted eating insects as a sustainable, inexpensive protein source — a practice not traditional in much of the United States — because of their wide availability and the ease of harvesting them. And many experts have likened cicadas to their seafaring counterparts, shrimp, as a means of making them seem less intimidating to wary diners, but that similarity apparently cuts both ways.

环境倡导者长期以来促进吃昆虫作为可持续，廉价的蛋白质来源 - 在美国大部分地区的一种做法，而不是传统的 - 因为它们是广泛的可用性和易于收获它们。许多专家都将蝉变得在海上同行，虾，因为一种使它们似乎不太令人生畏的食客，但相似之处显然削减了两种方式。

All insects belong to the phylum Arthropoda, which refers to their “jointed appendages,” and so do shrimp, lobsters and crayfish, noted Jerome Grant, etymology professor at the University of Tennessee’s Institute of Agriculture. He said he is glad the FDA put out a warning. It’s similar to the ones he posts at his freshman class’s annual “Buggy Buffet,” when all manner of insects are served as a way of calling attention to how important (and delicious) a food source bugs can be.

所有昆虫属于节肢动物，这是指他们的“联合阑尾”，虾仁龙虾，田纳西大学农业研究所的杰罗姆·格兰特，杰罗姆·格兰特（Nerymology）教授。他说他很高兴FDA发出警告。它类似于他在新的班级的年度“越野车的自助餐”中的帖子，当时所有的昆虫都是一种呼吁关注（和美味）食物来源错误的方式。

“If you’re allergic to one, there’s a chance you can be allergic to both,” Grant said. “It’s better to be safe.”

“如果你对一个过敏，那么你可能对两者都过敏，”肯定说。 “这是最好的安全。”

David Stukus, a member of the Asthma and Allergy Foundation of America’s Medical Scientific Council, agreed that people with shellfish allergies should take a better-safe-than-sorry approach. “There’s no good clinical research evaluating the risk of ingesting cicadas for people with shellfish allergies,” he said, “so we are extrapolating.”

David Stukus是美国医学科学委员会哮喘和过敏基金会的成员，同意贝类过敏的人应该采取更好的安全性比不良的方法。 “没有良好的临床研究，评估贝类过敏的人摄取蝉的风险，”他说，“所以我们正在推断。”

Stukus said the allergic reaction people get from eating shellfish happens after they ingest a muscle protein called tropomyosin, which is found not just in shrimp and lobster but in some insects and even in dust mites. To have an allergic reaction to a cicada, a person would have to actually eat it; handling the bugs and their skeletons is fine, even for people with shellfish allergies, he noted.

史库塔斯表示，在摄取肌肉蛋白质之后，患有肌肉蛋白质的肌肉蛋白质不仅仅是在虾和龙虾，而且在一些昆虫，甚至在某些昆虫甚至在尘螨中发现，就会发生过敏反应。对蝉过敏反应，一个人必须实际吃它;他指出，处理虫子和他们的骨架很好，即使是贝类过敏的人。

A report by the U.N. Food and Agriculture Organization about edible insects called for “further investigation” of insect allergies. “Individuals already allergic to crustaceans are particularly vulnerable to developing allergic reactions to edible insects, due to allergen cross-reactivity,” the report said.

U.N.食品和农业组织关于食用昆虫的报告呼吁“进一步调查”昆虫过敏。报告称，“由于过敏原的交叉反应性，”对甲壳类动物过敏的人对甲壳类动物过敏的人特别容易受到可食用昆虫的过敏反应“。

And Grant noted that people should also generally avoid eating cicadas raw. “There’s a lot more risk in eating anything raw,” he said.

并指出人们还应该避免吃蝉生。 “吃东西有更多的风险，”他说。

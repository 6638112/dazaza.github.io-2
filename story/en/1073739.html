Giving discounts will bite ya — use them with caution
打折会让你痛苦——小心使用

2022-02-17 07:16:55

People love a discount; and if you’re a customer, it looks so simple — the price goes down by 20%, and you get a good deal. As a retailer or startup, however, the math ain’t in your favor. Let’s walk through it.

人们喜欢打折；如果你是一个客户，它看起来很简单——价格下降了20%，你得到了一个很好的交易。然而，作为一家零售商或初创企业，这个数字并不适合你。让我们走一遍。

Imagine you’re running a small import business. You buy cheap widgets, and you slap a fancy brand on them before attracting customers via Instagram ads and funky lifestyle images. When you buy 5,000 of these units, you can buy ’em at $9 each from Alibaba — and that’s a bargain. Well done you.

想象一下，你正在经营一家小型进口企业。你买了便宜的小部件，在通过Instagram广告和时髦的生活方式图片吸引顾客之前，你会给它们打上一个别致的品牌。当你购买5000台这样的设备时，你可以从阿里巴巴以每台9美元的价格购买它们——这是一笔划算的交易。干得好。

You order 5,000 units, and you import them. You’ve now spent $45,000 on them, you spent another $1,500 on shipping, 10% on import taxes, and you discover that 9% of the units don’t work (this is Alibaba, after all). Luckily, as if by magic, you discover this without shipping them to the end customers and having to take them in return for an exchange or a refund.

你订购5000台，然后进口。你现在花了45000美元买它们，又花了1500美元买运输，10%买进口税，然后你发现有9%的设备坏了（毕竟这是阿里巴巴）。幸运的是，就像魔术一样，你发现了这一点，而无需将它们运送给最终客户，也无需将它们作为交换或退款的回报。

In any case, taking off the units that didn’t work, and the total spend of $51,000, each of the working 4,550 units cost you $11.21 each. Let’s take a look at the cost breakdown:

在任何情况下，去掉那些不起作用的单元，总共花费51000美元，每个4550个单元的工作成本是11.21美元。让我们看看成本明细：

You think you can sell the products at $19.99, which gives you a hella healthy profit margin; now all you need to do is sell the ever-living hell out of these things. For every widget you sell, you make a $8.78 profit. Impressive; congrats, pat yourself on the back:

你认为你可以以19.99美元的价格出售这些产品，这给了你一个健康的利润率；现在你需要做的就是把这些东西卖出去。每卖出一个小部件，你就能获得8.78美元的利润。给人印象深刻的恭喜，拍拍自己的背：

Of course, you’re gonna have to do some advertising, too. You figured out how to acquire a new customer for $3. That’s really impressive, and of course that’ll eat into your profit margin, too:

当然，你也得做一些广告。你想出了用3美元获得新客户的办法。这真的令人印象深刻，当然这也会侵蚀你的利润率：

After a few months, sales are slowing significantly, because there’s other people entering the market.  Not to worry, you think.  I have a couple of tricks up my sleeve. And you break out the discounting knife. At first, you try a 10% discount. That works pretty well. You’re making mad dollar here. But of course, the competitors are still at your heels, so you decide to really go for it. First with a 25% discount. Then with a 40% discount.  That’ll show them…

几个月后，销售明显放缓，因为还有其他人进入市场。你觉得不用担心。我有两个秘密。然后你拿出打折刀。首先，你试着打九折。这很有效。你在这里赚大钱。但当然，竞争对手仍然紧跟着你，所以你决定真的去做。首先是25%的折扣。然后打40%的折扣。这会让他们看到…

From the customer’s point of view, they are happy. This is all  they see:

从客户的角度来看，他们很高兴。这就是他们所看到的：

The customers are noticing that the prices are going down. Awesome! They are buying like crazy.

顾客们注意到价格正在下降。令人惊叹的他们疯狂地购买。

At some point, you’re noticing something: You’ve only reduced your price by a little bit, but you’re not making nearly as much money. What happened? Well… The thing to remember is that you’re giving discounts not on the sales price; you’re giving your discounts out of your profits. All the other costs remain the same.

在某个时刻，你会注意到一些事情：你只降低了一点价格，但你赚的钱却没有那么多。怎么搞的？嗯……要记住的是，你给的是折扣，而不是销售价格；你在从利润中扣除折扣。所有其他成本保持不变。

As soon as you start looking at the numbers more carefully, you see why perhaps the discounting wasn’t such a great idea. Sure, dropping the price from $19.99 to $14.99 is only a $5.00 difference… but your profit has taken a nose dive from $5.78 per unit to $0.78. That’s… not a business.

一旦你开始更仔细地看这些数字，你就会明白为什么折扣不是一个好主意。当然，把价格从19.99美元降到14.99美元只差5.00美元……但是你的利润已经从每单位5.78美元暴跌到了0.78美元。那不是生意。

Of course, if you don’t have physical product logistics, the unit cost is probably less dramatic than these charts — but even in a SaaS business you will have costs; customer support, server costs, etc. Ensure you know what your cost of goods sold (COGS) is before you start slicing your prices.

当然，如果你没有实物产品物流，单位成本可能没有这些图表那么显著——但即使在SaaS业务中，你也会有成本；客户支持、服务器成本等。在开始定价之前，确保您知道自己的销售成本（COGS）。

Also, all of this doesn’t mean you shouldn’t ever do discounts; if you have items in your warehouse and a bill coming up, liquidating it and paying your bills makes more sense than going out of business. Perhaps you want to clear out old stock, maybe you want to reward new customers or perhaps you are trying to entice new customers into the fold. It all makes sense — but bear in mind what it means to your bottom line to give a discount. Break out the spreadsheets, do the math and don’t accidentally end up selling your items at a margin where it doesn’t make sense.

而且，所有这些并不意味着你永远不应该打折；如果你的仓库里有物品，而且账单即将到期，清算它并支付账单比停业更有意义。也许你想清空旧库存，也许你想奖励新客户，也许你正试图吸引新客户加入。这一切都是有道理的——但要记住，打折对你的底线意味着什么。拿出电子表格，计算一下，不要意外地以毫无意义的利润率销售你的商品。

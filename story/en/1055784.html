Are Hackers the New Luddites?
黑客是新的luddites吗？

2021-04-03 01:20:17

At the birth of industry in England, a movement of weavers gathered together to resist the imposition of technologies which threatened their way of life. Their actions – which ranged from petitioning government to direct sabotage and destruction of the new weaving frames – were done in the name of their fictional leader, the ‘mass pseudonym’ Ned Ludd. The Luddites became a byword for a pointless struggle against the inevitable tide of technological progress, but perhaps they now look prophetic. In fact, claims Gavin Mueller, the Luddite rebellions set the pattern for repeated acts of workplace and community rebellion against the imposition of technologies which dispossess workers and rob us all of our freedom. ‘To be a good Marxist’, he claims, ‘is to be a Luddite.’ He joins James Butler to trace a surprising history of Luddite rebellion stretching from pre-industrial England to the hacker culture of the early internet, and the ecologically-oriented movements of salvage and repair springing up today.

在英格兰的行业诞生时，编织者的运动聚集在一起，抵制威胁其生活方式的技术。他们的行为 - 从请愿政府直接破坏和破坏新的编织框架 - 以他们的虚构领导者的名义，“大众假名”的LUDD。 Luddites成为一个毫无疑惑的斗争逐个斗争，而不是技术进步的必然潮流，但也许他们现在看起来是预言的。事实上，Luddite叛乱者的索赔叛乱设定了反复工作场所和社区叛乱的模式，以防止剥夺工人并抢劫我们所有自由的技术。他声称“成为一个好的马克思主义者”，“是成为一个淫荡。”他加入詹姆斯巴特勒，追查从工业前英格兰延伸到早期互联网的黑客文化的Luddite叛乱的令人惊讶的历史，以及生态学 - 今天的救助和修复的导向运动。

Gavin Mueller’s Breaking Things at Work: The Luddites Were Right About Why You Hate Your Job is available now from Verso Books.

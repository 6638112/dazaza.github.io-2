Organic meats found to have the same greenhouse impact as regular meats
发现有机肉具有与普通肉相同的温室效应

2020-12-31 21:15:24

A trio of researchers from the Technical University of Munich, the University of Greifswald and the University of Augsburg have found that the meat production process for organic meats produces approximately the same amounts of greenhouse gases as does the conventional meat production process. In their paper published in the journal Nature Communications, Maximilian Pieper, Amelie Michalke and Tobias Gaugler describe their study of the impact of global food production on climate change and what they found. As the planet continues to warm, researchers continue working to better understand the sources of . In this new effort, the researchers looked at greenhouse emissions related to food production.

来自慕尼黑工业大学，格赖夫斯瓦尔德大学和奥格斯堡大学的三位研究人员发现，有机肉的肉生产过程产生的温室气体排放量与常规肉生产过程大致相同。 Maximilian Pieper，Amelie Michalke和Tobias Gaugler在《自然通讯》杂志上发表的论文中描述了他们对全球粮食生产对气候变化的影响及其发现的研究。随着地球继续变暖，研究人员将继续努力以更好地了解的来源。在这项新工作中，研究人员研究了与粮食生产有关的温室气体排放。

In looking at food production, the researchers placed into three main categories: conventional meat production, organic meat production and plant-based food production. They also took into account the emissions produced during different stages of the production process—emissions produced while growing and processing feed and fertilizer, for example, and methane released by animals and from their manure.

在研究食品生产时，研究人员将其分为三个主要类别：常规肉类生产，有机肉类生产和植物性食品生产。他们还考虑了在生产过程的不同阶段产生的排放物，例如在生长和加工饲料和肥料时产生的排放物，以及动物及其粪便释放的甲烷。

The data revealed little difference in from conventional meat production and that grown organically. They found that by organically grown animals (in which fertilizer is not used to produce feed) were often offset by increases in methane released due to slower growth rates and the need to raise more animals, as organically fed animals tend to produce less meat. More specifically, they found very little difference in emissions between conventionally produced beef and beef grown organically. They also found that organically grown chickens produced slightly more emissions than those grown conventionally, and that organic pork produced fewer emissions than conventional pork.

数据显示与常规肉类生产和有机肉类生产几乎没有区别。他们发现，有机生长的动物（其中不使用肥料来生产饲料）通常由于生长速度较慢和需要饲养更多动物而被甲烷释放增加所抵消，因为有机喂养的动物往往产生较少的肉。更具体地说，他们发现传统生产的牛肉和有机种植的牛肉之间的排放差异很小。他们还发现，有机种植的鸡肉产生的排放量比常规种植的鸡肉略多，有机猪肉的排放量低于传统猪肉。

The researchers suggest the need for taxes that reflect the environmental cost of their production. They calculated such a tax for conventional beef would raise its price by approximately 40% while organic beef would see a price increase of just 25% (because it is already more expensive than regular beef). Prices for animal-related products, such as cheese or milk, would also rise. Prices for food plants, on the other hand, would remain nearly the same. More information: Maximilian Pieper et al. Calculation of external climate costs for food highlights inadequate pricing of animal products, Nature Communications (2020). DOI: 10.1038/s41467-020-19474-6

研究人员建议需要反映其生产环境成本的税收。他们计算出对常规牛肉征收的这种税将使它的价格上涨约40％，而有机牛肉的价格将仅上涨25％（因为它已经比普通牛肉贵了）。与动物有关的产品（例如奶酪或牛奶）的价格也将上涨。另一方面，食用植物的价格将保持几乎不变。更多信息：Maximilian Pieper等。食品外部气候成本的计算突出了动物产品的定价不足，《自然通讯》（2020年）。 DOI：10.1038 / s41467-020-19474-6

Cashless society draws closer with only one in six payments now in cash
无现金的无现金的六项付款

2021-06-19 17:38:29

UK Finance study records a 35% drop in cash transactions in 2020 as Covid crisis turbo-charges change in spending habits

英国财务研究记录了2020年的现金交易下降了35％，因为Covid危机涡轮机 - 费用在消费习惯中发生变化

The UK has moved a big step closer to becoming a cashless society after official data showed that the number of payments made using notes and coins fell by 35% in 2020.

在官方数据显示，在2020年，在2020年，在官方数据显示后，英国越来越越来越近成为无现金社会。

Changes in spending habits have been dramatically accelerated by the coronavirus pandemic, with 13.7 million people leading a “cashless life” last year – almost double the 7.4 million figure in 2019. Five in six payments now involve no notes or coins, compared with half of all transactions a decade ago.

Coronavirus大流行的消费习惯的变化已经大大加速，1370万人领导了“无现实的生命生活”，2019年的740万图几乎加倍。六项付款中的五个涉及的票据或硬币涉及一半十年前的所有交易。

A growing number of businesses both big and small now refuse cash, with many having opted to go card-only over the past year.

越来越多的企业现在拒绝现金，许多人在过去的一年中选择了众多卡片。

The retailer Ikea says on its website that “for now” it is not accepting cash payments in its UK stores. Similarly, the restaurant chain Nando’s says customers cannot pay with cash, the Japanese food chain Itsu talks about being card-only in-store and the craft beer firm BrewDog is among the hospitality businesses that are now cashless.

零售商宜家在其网站上说，“现在”它不接受其英国商店的现金支付。同样，餐馆链条Nando表示，客户无法支付现金，日本食品链ITSU关于被卡片的谈判，工艺啤酒公司Brewdog是现有现金的酒店业务之一。

Others such as Birds Bakery are discouraging the use of physical money. The Midlands chain, which was at the centre of a recent row about only accepting card payments in-store, says it “would prefer to take card payments where possible”.

鸟类面包店等其他人正在劝阻使用物理资金。米德兰兹链是近期仅接受店内的卡支付的中心，表示，它“愿意在可能的情况下拿卡付款”。

“There has been a significant fall in cash use by consumers in a relatively short period of time,” said the banking trade body UK Finance, which issued the figures. “Since 2017, cash use had been declining by around 15% each year, so 2020 represented an acceleration of this decline.”

“消费者在相对较短的一段时间内有一个重要的消费者，”银行贸易机构英国财务表示，颁发了这些数字。 “自2017年以来，现金用途每年跌幅下降约15％，所以2020年代表了这种下降的加速。”

The trend reflects the fact that many places where people commonly use cash, such as pubs and cafes, were shut for chunks of last year, while the lockdowns prompted a surge in the numbers shopping online. While a string of businesses have temporarily or permanently gone card-only, some consumers have been avoiding touching banknotes and coins because of Covid transmission worries.

这一趋势反映了许多人常用现金，如酒吧和咖啡馆的许多地方都关闭了去年的块，而锁定促使数字在线购物的浪涌。虽然一系列企业暂时或永久地消失了卡片，但由于Covid传输担忧，一些消费者已经避免了触摸纸币和硬币。

Nevertheless, the findings are likely to fuel concern about millions of people potentially being left behind as the shift to a cashless society speeds up. About 1.2 million consumers still mainly used cash for their day-to-day spending during 2020, down from 2.1 million people in 2019.

尽管如此，调查结果可能会促使数百万人担心，因为转向无现金社会的转变速度。大约120万消费者仍然主要用现金为2020年的日常支出，2019年的210万人下降。

The independent Access to Cash Review said there were more than 5 million people in the UK still “heavily reliant” on notes and coins, while the consumer body Which? said those who still depended on cash to pay for everyday essentials “must not be forgotten”.

自主获取现金审查表示，英国有超过500万人仍然“严重依赖”笔记和硬币，而消费者体验？说那些仍然取决于现金支付日常必需品的人“一定不被遗忘”。

The number of contactless payments rose by 12% during 2020, and overall they accounted for more than a quarter (27%) of all payments. As recently as 2016, the figure stood at 7%.

2020年期间非接触式支付数量上升了12％，总体而言，他们占所有付款的四分之一以上（27％）。最近作为2016年，该数字价格为7％。

This has been driven by a number of factors, including the decision to increase the upper limit for tap and pay from £30 to £45 in April 2020 – a further rise to £100 is planned – and a belief that contactless is more hygienic than handling cash.

这是由许多因素驱动的，包括决定增加水龙头的上限，并于2020年4月从30英镑到£45支付 - 计划进一步增加至100英镑 - 并相信非接触更卫生的信念处理现金。

About 83% of people in the UK now use contactless, with no age group or region falling below 75%.

英国约有83％的人现在使用非接触式，没有年龄组或地区低于75％。

Cash was used for 17% of all payments in 2020 – down from 45% in 2015 and 56% in 2010.

现金用于2020年的所有付款的17％ - 2015年的45％，2010年的56％。

Despite this, it remains the second most frequently used payment method behind debit cards, and next week a new £50 note featuring the scientist Alan Turing will go into circulation. UK Finance said it was entirely possible that cash may “bounce back” later this year or next year.

尽管如此，它仍然是借记卡背后的第二次最常用的付款方式，下周新的50英镑的注释，以科学家Alan Turing将进入流通。英国财政表示，这完全可能会在今年晚些时候或明年晚些时候“反弹”。

During 2020 there were 13.7 million consumers who either did not use notes and coins at all, or only once a month. This number has grown rapidly: in 2018 it was 5.4 million people, in 2017 it was 3.4 million and in 2016 it was 2.9 million.

在2020年期间，有1370万消费者，他们要么没有使用笔记和硬币，或者只有一次每月一次。这个数字已经发展迅速：2018年，2017年为540万人，2016年为340万，而2016年为290万。

However, with large parts of the economy closed for some of last year, UK Finance said this was not necessarily a measure of people who had chosen to live a cashless life.

然而，随着去年的一些经济的大部分，英国财政表示这一定不一定是选择过现行无产的人的衡量标准。

In the 2020 budget, ministers announced plans to introduce legislation to protect access to cash for those who need it. Gareth Shaw, the head of Which? Money, said the government must make progress on this to ensure that cash remained a viable payment option for as long as it was needed.

在2020年预算中，部长们宣布计划介绍立法，以保护对需要它的人的现金。加勒希肖，哪个头？金钱表示，政府必须取得进展，以确保现金仍然是一个可行的付款方式，只要需要它。

The Access to Cash Review has previously warned that the country’s “cash infrastructure” – from ATMs to cash-sorting centres – could be in danger of collapsing because while the costs are largely fixed, income is declining quickly. Natalie Ceeney, its chair, said that as cash use declined, “there is an increasing risk that people won’t be able to access it or that shops won’t accept it. It’s not a case of waiting for people to learn to use digital.”

此前检测到现金审查先前警告说，该国的“现金基础设施” - 从ATM到现金分拣中心 - 可能存在崩溃的危险，因为虽然成本在很大程度上是固定的，但收入正在迅速下降。泰达利卡伊，其主席表示，随着现金用途的拒绝，“人们将无法访问它或者商店不会接受它的风险越来越大。等待人们学会使用数字并非如此。“这不是一个案例。”

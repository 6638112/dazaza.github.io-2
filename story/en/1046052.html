你能永远被Facebook禁止吗？答案肯定是肯定的。

But is there any way back into the Facebook community after you’ve been banned? (last update Jan. 2021)

但是，被禁止后，有什么办法可以回到Facebook社区吗？ （最新更新于2021年1月）

We know from the plethora of requests for help that we receive about locked accounts that Facebook is more willing than ever to ban users who don’t follow their rules.

从收到的有关锁定帐户的大量请求中我们知道，Facebook比以往任何时候都更愿意禁止不遵守规则的用户。

These former Facebookers who contact us all want the same thing: to reclaim their accounts and return to the popular virtual community. Unfortunately for them, this goal may be impossible. So it’s crucial that users acquaint themselves with Facebook’s community standards — before they end up on the outside looking in. Because as we saw this week, no one is immune to a Facebook banishment — not even the President of the United States.

与我们联系的这些前Facebooker都希望做同样的事情：收回他们的帐户并返回流行的虚拟社区。对于他们来说不幸的是，这个目标可能是不可能的。因此，至关重要的是，用户必须先了解Facebook的社区标准-才能最终了解外界。因为正如我们在本周看到的那样，没有人能幸免于Facebook的放逐-甚至美国总统也是如此。

Birch says that his unexpected Facebook troubles began one evening when he was using Facebook Messenger.

伯奇说，他意外的Facebook麻烦始于一个晚上，当时他正在使用Facebook Messenger。

“After a concert, I logged onto Facebook and was talking to a friend,” Birch recalled. “I was on my laptop and I accidentally sent a partial nude photo. A few minutes later, I received an alert that Facebook blocked me.”

伯奇回忆说：“音乐会结束后，我登录Facebook并在和朋友聊天。” “我当时在笔记本电脑上，不小心发送了一张裸照。几分钟后，我收到了Facebook阻止我的警报。”

From that moment last summer, Birch has been on an aggressive crusade to get his Facebook account reactivated.

从去年夏天的那一刻起，伯奇就一直在积极地努力恢复他的Facebook帐户。

He’s sent numerous emails to the Facebook team asking, then begging, them to forgive him and reactivate his account. He insists that uploading the “partial nude” was a mistake and he thought Facebook Messenger was private.

他向Facebook团队发送了许多电子邮件，要求他们原谅他并重新激活他的帐户，然后恳求他们。他坚持认为上传“部分裸照”是错误的，他认为Facebook Messenger是私人的。

“I sent this photo through a private Facebook message. I did not post it on Facebook,” Birch pleaded. “The Facebook scanner detected it was inappropriate. I was banned from Facebook immediately. Please, I just want my account back.”

“我通过私人Facebook消息发送了这张照片。我没有将其发布在Facebook上，”伯奇恳求道。 “ Facebook扫描仪检测到它不合适。我立即被Facebook禁止。拜托，我只想找回我的帐户。”

Throughout the next several months, Birch continued to send a steady stream of emails to the Facebook team.

在接下来的几个月中，Birch继续向Facebook团队不断发送电子邮件。

I don’t use Facebook for porn. All I use it for is to keep in contact with friends and family. I’ve had this account since my teen years and want to get all my photos and memories back. I also have several family members that I can only get in contact with through Facebook. This has been such a stressful five months. It was an accident and it won’t happen again. I never meant to send that photo. I miss my family and friends on Facebook. Please unblock my account.

我不使用Facebook进行色情。我使用它的唯一目的就是与朋友和家人保持联系。我从十几岁起就拥有这个帐户，并且想找回所有照片和回忆。我还有几个家庭成员，我只能通过Facebook与他们联系。五个月来真是压力很大。这是一次意外，不会再发生。我从没打算寄那张照片。我在Facebook上想念我的家人和朋友。请取消阻止我的帐户。

Although initially Birch’s paper trail shows what appears to be auto-generated responses to his pleas, Facebook soon sent a definitive answer.

尽管最初Birch的论文记录显示出似乎是对他的请求自动生成的回应，但Facebook很快就给出了明确的答案。

We’ve determined that you are ineligible to use Facebook. To learn more about Facebook’s policies, please review the Facebook Statement of Rights and Responsibilities:

我们确定您没有使用Facebook的资格。要了解有关Facebook政策的更多信息，请查看Facebook权利与责任声明：

Unfortunately, for safety and security reasons, we can’t provide additional information as to why your account was disabled. We appreciate your understanding, as this decision is final. (Facebook team)

很遗憾，出于安全考虑，我们无法提供有关您的帐户被停用原因的其他信息。感谢您的谅解，因为此决定是最终决定。 （Facebook团队）

What kind of %#*& customer service is this that it takes over five months to even try to get ahold of a real human being to help with a problem!!!! This is horrible.

哪种％＃*＆amp;客户服务就是这样，甚至要花五个月的时间才能让真正的人帮助解决问题！！！这太可怕了。

Not knowing where else to turn, his next email was to the Elliott Advocacy team.

他不知道该去哪里，他的下一封电子邮件是给Elliott倡导团队的。

When I received Birch’s request for help, I reviewed his giant paper trail. Unfortunately, I knew that his case was likely one that we could not successfully advocate.

当我收到伯奇的帮助请求时，我查看了他的巨著。不幸的是，我知道他的案子很可能是我们无法成功提起的。

Although we receive many requests for help concerning banned Facebook accounts, our track record for resolving these cases is zero.

尽管我们收到许多有关被禁止的Facebook帐户的帮助请求，但我们解决这些案件的记录为零。

In fact, Facebook, like other giant companies such as Amazon and eBay, typically ignores these types of inquiries from our team:

实际上，Facebook与其他大型公司（例如Amazon和eBay）一样，通常会忽略来自我们团队的以下类型的查询：

It would seem that after these companies take the drastic step of banning a user, there is little to no appeal process.

在这些公司采取了禁止用户的严厉措施之后，似乎没有任何上诉程序。

Facebook is a free service. Users are not customers. And although Birch was outraged by the “terrible customer service” he received from Facebook, he shouldn’t have been. Facebook is providing an online social community free of charge to the user. The Facebook Team is not a “customer service team,” and users should not expect the type of personalized attention a complaint would receive from a company of which they are actually paying customers.

Facebook是一项免费服务。用户不是客户。尽管Birch对他从Facebook获得的“糟糕的客户服务”感到不满，但他本来不应该这样做。 Facebook正在向用户免费提供在线社交社区。 Facebook团队不是“客户服务团队”，用户不应期望投诉会从他们实际上向其付款的公司那里得到个性化关注。

When you sign up with Facebook, you agree to its terms and conditions. If you violate any of those terms, you can get banned — with no clear-cut methods of appealing the decision.

当您注册Facebook时，您同意其条款和条件。如果您违反了这些条款中的任何一项，您将被禁止使用-没有明确的上诉决定方法。

Facebook doesn’t owe you an account. There are no current laws that require Facebook to allow anyone to participate.

Facebook没有欠您一个帐户。当前没有法律要求Facebook允许任何人参与。

So there is very little on which our team could base a mediation attempt over a banned Facebook account. And, unfortunately, by Birch’s own admission, he had violated one of Facebook’s terms and conditions.

因此，我们的团队几乎没有什么可以基于被禁止的Facebook帐户进行调解的。而且，不幸的是，伯奇自己承认，他违反了Facebook的条款和条件之一。

But I sent Birch’s case over to the Facebook Team and asked them if they could review it. Based on his lengthy paper trail, it was clear that this banishment was consuming his time and attention. I thought if Facebook could give him a response that left no room for interpretation, he might be able to move on without Facebook.

但是我将伯奇的案件转交给了Facebook团队，并询问他们是否可以审查。根据他冗长的论文记录，很明显，这种放逐正在浪费他的时间和精力。我以为，如果Facebook可以给他答复，而没有留下任何解释的余地​​，那么他也许可以在没有Facebook的情况下继续前进。

As I expected, several weeks went by, and all I received from the Facebook Team was an assurance that they would get back to me soon.

正如我预期的那样，已经过去了几个星期，我从Facebook团队收到的所有信息都保证他们会尽快与我联系。

In the meantime, Birch had continued on with his mission. Not  following the helpful self-advocacy guidance Christopher Elliott gives in his article on the topic, Birch bombarded Facebook with more requests to have the ban lifted. Inexplicably, he even sent the “partial nude” photo he thought got him banned back to Facebook to tell the team he learned a lesson.

同时，伯奇（Birch）继续执行自己的任务。伯奇没有遵循克里斯托弗·埃利奥特（Christopher Elliott）在有关该主题的文章中提供的有用的自我宣传指导，而是用更多的要求取消禁令轰炸了Facebook。莫名其妙地，他甚至将他认为被禁止的“部分裸照”照片发回了Facebook，告诉团队他吸取了教训。

Dec 20, 2018 I am rather upset that Facebook disabled my account for this long for an accidental partial nude photo! I just talked to one of my friends and she said she had received [frontal nudity pictures] through Facebook and nothing happened to the other person’s account!! So why on earth did Facebook disable mine, for one, an artistic photo, and two an accidental click/upload on it?!

2018年12月20日，我很沮丧的是Facebook禁用了我的账户这么长时间，以获取偶然的局部裸照！我刚刚和我的一个朋友交谈，她说她已经通过Facebook收到了[额头裸露照片]，而对方的帐户没有任何反应！那么，到底为什么Facebook禁用了我的照片，其中一张是艺术照片，而另外两个却是意外单击/上传呢？

Dec 25, 2018 Can someone unlock my account? It’s Christmas. Facebook has punished me for far too long.

2018年12月25日，有人可以解锁我的帐户吗？是圣诞节Facebook惩罚我太久了。

Dec 28, 2018 Please someone, how long until I get my account back? Jan 2 Well, happy new year. Can someone on the Facebook team help me get my account back, please? Jan 2 All this over an accidental photo. It’s rather upsetting. Jan 6 Why are you guys refusing to help me?! I feel like I’m being discriminated against as a gay man who made a mistake!

2018年12月28日，请有人，我要等多久才能收回我的帐户？ 1月2日好，新年快乐。 Facebook团队中的某人可以帮我找回我的帐户吗？ 1月2日这是一张偶然的照片。真是令人沮丧。 1月6日你们为什么拒绝帮助我？我觉得自己是一个犯错的同性恋者而受到歧视！

Then Birch sent additional letters to Mark Zuckerberg and Sheryl Sandberg. Not surprisingly, he didn’t receive a response to those messages.

然后，伯奇又给马克·扎克伯格和谢丽尔·桑德伯格发了信。毫不奇怪，他没有收到这些邮件的回复。

Finally, Birch did receive a crystal clear message from a first-name-only Facebook team member. “Maxie” sent what should have provided the closure Birch needed — his Facebook ban is permanent.

最终，伯奇的确从一位名叫Facebook的团队成员那里获得了明确的信息。 “马克西”（Maxie）发送了应该提供桦木关闭服务的信息-他的Facebook禁令是永久性的。

Your account has been permanently disabled for not following the Facebook Community Standards. Unfortunately, we won’t be able to reactivate it for any reason.

您的帐户因未遵守Facebook社区标准而被永久禁用。很遗憾，由于任何原因，我们将无法重新激活它。

This will be our last email regarding your account. For more information about our policies, please review the Facebook Community Standards:

这是我们关于您帐户的最后一封电子邮件。有关我们政策的更多信息，请查看Facebook社区标准：

However, Birch still wasn’t willing to give up. When I suggested to Birch that it was time to let this battle go, he told me he will not give up. And he insists that he’s not going to stop trying to get his Facebook life back.

但是，伯奇仍然不愿意放弃。当我建议伯希（Birch）放弃这场战斗的时候，他告诉我他不会放弃。而且他坚称自己不会停止试图恢复他的Facebook生活。

Facebook’s Community Standards page is where users can learn how to stay a Facebook member in good standing.

用户可以在Facebook的“社区标准”页面上学习如何保持Facebook成员的良好信誉。

Beyond the obvious ways to get banned from Facebook, there are a variety of more subtle things that we know can end in the disabling of a user account:

除了从Facebook被禁止的明显方法之外，我们还知道还有许多更微妙的事情可以通过禁用用户帐户来结束：

Using a Fake name: Although it is not uncommon to see Facebook accounts in nicknames or self-created monikers, this is something that can get an account banned. Using a name other than your legal name is a violation of Facebook’s terms and conditions.

使用假名：虽然看到Facebook帐户使用昵称或自己创建的绰号的情况并不罕见，但这可能会导致帐户被禁止。使用除法定名称之外的其他名称违反了Facebook的条款和条件。

Creating multiple Facebook accounts: If you create more than one Facebook account, you might suddenly find yourself with zero. Facebook allows just one account per person.

创建多个Facebook帐户：如果创建多个Facebook帐户，您可能会突然发现自己的零。 Facebook每人仅允许一个帐户。

“Friending” too many people in a short period:  Facebook is always scanning for fake accounts. If your account mimics the patterns of a fake account, you could get banned right along with the phony ones. Friending large numbers of people in a short period can end with your account being shut down.

在短期内将太多人``友善''：Facebook一直在扫描虚假账户。如果您的帐户模仿假帐户的模式，则可能会与假冒帐户一起被禁止。在短时间内与大量朋友交往可能会因为您的帐户被关闭而结束。

Liking too many things: This is another signal that an account may not be real and Facebook might disable it.

喜欢太多事情：这是另一个信号，表明帐户可能不是真实帐户，Facebook可能会禁用它。

Posting spammy-type posts: No one likes spam in their newsfeed. If you’re posting it, you’re at risk of having your account flagged.

发布垃圾邮件类型的帖子：没有人喜欢其新闻源中的垃圾邮件。如果您要发布它，则有被标记帐户的风险。

Posting fake news/ untrue information: We all have friends who repost alerts and memes that contain urban legends or completely untrue information. This behavior can also lead to a Facebook account getting disabled. All Facebook users should fact check before resharing any posts.

发布虚假新闻/不真实信息：我们都有一些朋友，他们会重新发布包含城市传说或完全不真实信息的警报和模因。此行为也可能导致Facebook帐户被禁用。所有Facebook用户都应在转载任何帖子之前先进行事实检查。

Posting offensive photos or memes: Remember, this also includes posting things through Facebook Messenger. As we’ve seen in this case, the same rules that apply to your public Facebook page apply to anything you send via messenger as well. Facebook monitors it all.

发布令人反感的照片或模因：请记住，这还包括通过Facebook Messenger发布信息。正如我们在这种情况下所看到的，适用于您的公开Facebook页面的规则也适用于您通过Messenger发送的任何内容。 Facebook对此进行监控。

Facebook is not a photo storage service. Don’t rely on Facebook to store your memories. Facebook makes no guarantees that it is safely storing your photos on its site. And if you should find your account disabled, as Birch and others have, you won’t be able to get your photos back. Without fail, the consumers who have contacted us about banned accounts are most disturbed by the loss of their pictures. So you can avoid finding yourself in this situation, make sure that you’ve backed up your photos outside of the Facebook platform.

Facebook不是照片存储服务。不要依靠Facebook来保存您的回忆。 Facebook不保证它将在您的网站上安全地存储您的照片。而且，如Birch和其他人一样，如果您发现自己的帐户已被禁用，则将无法找回您的照片。毫无疑问，与我们联系以了解违禁帐户的消费者最受图片丢失的困扰。因此，您可以避免在这种情况下发现自己，请确保已将照片备份到Facebook平台之外。

Facebook is not your Rolodex. Don’t rely on Facebook to store your personal contacts. Birch, like others, complained that he lost touch with friends and family after Facebook disabled his account.  Make sure to keep the contact information of treasured friends and family stored offline. Having no way outside of a virtual community to reach a valued “friend” is always a mistake.

Facebook不是您的Rolodex。不要依靠Facebook来存储您的个人联系人。伯奇和其他人一样，抱怨Facebook禁用帐户后，他与朋友和家人失去联系。确保将珍贵的朋友和家人的联系信息离线保存。在虚拟社区之外无法与有价值的“朋友”交往总是一个错误。

Facebook is not Amazon. Many consumers contact our team after they’ve ordered products from an ad they’ve seen on Facebook — and things have gone wrong. Shopping on Facebook is not the same as shopping on Amazon. Keep in mind the lack of customer service provided by Facebook should you decide to go shopping there. You’ll want to research the merchant and make sure it’s reputable before handing over your credit card.

Facebook不是亚马逊。许多消费者订购了在Facebook上看到的广告中的产品后，便与我们的团队联系-事情出了问题。在Facebook上购物与在Amazon上购物不同。请记住，如果您决定去那里购物，Facebook会缺少客户服务。您需要先调查商家，并确保信誉良好，然后再交付信用卡。

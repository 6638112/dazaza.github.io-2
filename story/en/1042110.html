A Vagus Nerve Survival Guide to Combat Fight-or-Flight Urges (2017)
对抗战斗或逃跑的迷走神经生存指南（2017）

All too often, the readily accessible power of the vagus nerve to lower anxiety and reduce inflammation is overlooked and underestimated. I’ve written posts here that highlight practical ways to tap into the ability of your vagus nerve to combat the cortisol-producing stress response of fight-or-flight.

迷走神经降低焦虑和减轻炎症的容易获得的力量常常被忽视和低估。我在这里写了一些帖子，重点介绍了利用迷走神经抵抗战斗或逃跑产生皮质醇的压力反应的能力的实用方法。

In 1921, Nobel Prize-winning physiologist, Otto Loewi discovered that stimulating the vagus nerve caused a reduction in heart rate by triggering the release of a substance he coined Vagusstoff (German for “vagus substance”). This substance was later identified as acetylcholine and became the first neurotransmitter ever identified by scientists.

1921年，诺贝尔物理学奖获得者奥托·洛维（Otto Loewi）发现，刺激迷走神经会触发释放他创造的Vagusstoff物质（德语为“迷走神经物质”），从而导致心率降低。该物质后来被鉴定为乙酰胆碱，并成为科学家有史以来鉴定的第一种神经递质。

The vagus nerve is the prime driving force of the parasympathetic nervous system which regulates our “rest-and-digest” or “tend-and-befriend” responses. On the flip side, to maintain homeostasis, the sympathetic nervous system drives the “fight-or-flight” response. Ideally, within your autonomic nervous system, the tug of war between these two polar opposite mechanisms creates a "yin-yang" type of harmony marked by homeostatic balance.

迷走神经是副交感神经系统的主要驱动力，它调节着我们的``休息和消化''或``趋于友好''反应。另一方面，为维持体内平衡，交感神经系统驱动“战斗或逃跑”反应。理想情况下，在您的自主神经系统中，这两个相反的机制之间的拔河会产生阴阳关系。以稳态平衡为特征的和谐类型。

From a simplified evolutionary perspective, one could speculate that our ancestors relied on the sympathetic nervous system to kickstart neurobiological responses needed to hunt, gather, and ward off enemies. Conversely, the parasympathetic nervous system probably fortified our innate drive to nurture close-knit human bonds, procreate, and build survival-based cooperative and supportive communities.

从简化的进化角度来看，可以推测我们的祖先依靠交感神经系统来启动狩猎，聚集和避开敌人所需的神经生物学反应。相反，副交感神经系统可能加强了我们与生俱来的动力，以培育紧密的人类纽带，繁殖并建立以生存为基础的合作和支持社区。

Unfortunately, the Toffleresque “ future shock” of the 21st-century digital age, marked by too much change in too short a time, is causing our evolutionary biology to short circuit by throwing our individual and collective nervous systems out of balance.

不幸的是，21世纪数字时代的托夫勒式的“未来冲击”以在短时间内发生太多变化为标志，通过使我们的个体和集体神经系统失衡，导致我们的进化生物学短路。

Recent studies show that all too often, social media and other modern-day factors exacerbate perceived social isolation and feelings of being unworthy of love and belonging. Additionally, the individualistic "every man for himself" zeitgeist undermines collective “tending-and-befriending” and can put someone's fight-or-flight response in constant hyperdrive with no reprieve.

最近的研究表明，社交媒体和其他现代因素经常会加剧人们对社交的孤立感以及不值得拥有爱和归属感的感觉。此外，个人主义的“每个人都适合自己”时代精神破坏了集体的“趋向与交友”，可以使某人的战斗或逃避反应持续不断地超速行驶，而不会有所缓和。

Luckily, there are some easily accessible and highly effective drug-free ways for you to activate the vagusstuff-producing power of your parasympathetic nervous system by stimulating your vagus nerve.

幸运的是，通过刺激迷走神经，您可以通过一些容易获得且非常有效的无毒品方式来激活副交感神经系统产生迷走神经的力量。

As an ultra-endurance athlete, I spent countless hours monitoring my heart rate variability (HRV) and the robustness of my parasympathetic nervous system and vagal tone to make sure that I wasn’t overtraining. That said, until recently, I didn’t realize that this knowledge of HRV feedback could be applied as a public health tool.

作为一名超耐力运动员，我花了无数小时来监测我的心率变异性（HRV），副交感神经系统的健壮性和迷走神经张力，以确保我没有过度训练。就是说，直到最近，我才意识到这种对HRV反馈的了解可以用作公共卫生工具。

A few days ago, I read about a new study by Kyle Bourassa and colleagues at the University of Arizona, " The Impact of Narrative Expressive Writing on Heart Rate, Heart Rate Variability, and Blood Pressure Following Marital Separation." The findings were published May 8 in Psychosomatic Medicine: Journal of Biobehavioral Medicine.

几天前，我读到Kyle Bourassa及其同事在亚利桑那大学（＆＃34;叙事表达性写作对夫妻分居后心率，心率变异性和血压的影响。＆＃34;研究结果于5月8日发表在《心身医学：生物行为医学杂志》上。

The researchers found that a specific style of journal writing called “narrative expressive writing” helped reduce the harmful cardiovascular effects of going through a divorce. In a statement, Bourassa described the findings:

研究人员发现，一种称为“叙事表达性写作”的特殊日记形式有助于减少离婚对心血管的有害影响。 Bourassa在一份声明中描述了调查结果：

“The results suggest that the ability to create a structured narrative—not just re-experiencing emotions but making meaning out of them—allows people to process their feelings in a more adaptive way, which may in turn help improve their cardiovascular health.”

“结果表明，创造结构化叙事的能力-不仅可以重新体验情感，还可以使情感变有意义，这使人们能够以一种更具适应性的方式来处理自己的感觉，这反过来又可以帮助改善他们的心血管健康。”

This study piqued my curiosity. I wanted to dig deeper and I was perplexed because, as far as I could tell, the Bourassa study didn’t discuss vagus nerve or vagal tone specifically. I’ve always associated HRV, the parasympathetic nervous system, and vagal tone as a triad that is intertwined. That said, adding a specific type of "expressive narrative writing" into this mix was a refreshing new angle with easy-to-apply prescriptive possibilities.

这项研究激起了我的好奇心。我想更深入地研究，我感到困惑，因为据我所知，Bourassa研究并未专门讨论迷走神经或迷走神经张力。我一直将HRV，副交感神经系统和迷走神经音联系在一起，将其与三联症联系在一起。也就是说，添加了一种特定类型的＆＃34;富有表现力的叙事写作＆＃34;混合在一起是一个令人耳目一新的新角度，具有易于应用的说明性可能性。

My initial attempt to unearth something fresh or useful that would complement Bourassa's findings was fruitless. Nonetheless, this study kept nagging at me. I found myself ruminating about a possible link between narrative expressive writing and the vagus nerve. One night, I tossed and turned because something in the back of my brain was trying to connect the dots of the Bourassa study with other research I’ve seen. Subconsciously, I knew there was some connection. Finally, this morning, on a long sunrise jog, I realized that the new study by Bourassa et al. shares some important similarities to the well-known research by Fredrickson et al. on loving-kindness meditation (LKM), prosocial behaviors, HRV, and the vagus nerve.

我最初试图发掘出一些新鲜或有用的东西来补充Bourassa的发现是徒劳的。但是，这项研究一直困扰着我。我发现自己对叙事表达性写作和迷走神经之间可能存在的联系进行了反思。一天晚上，我辗转反侧，因为大脑后方的某些东西试图将Bourassa研究的点点滴滴与我见过的其他研究联系起来。下意识地，我知道有某种联系。终于，今天早晨，在漫长的日出慢跑中，我意识到Bourassa等人的新研究。与Fredrickson等人的著名研究有一些重要的相似之处。关于爱心冥想（LKM），亲社会行为，HRV和迷走神经。

The 2013 study by Barbara Fredrickson and Bethany Kok of the University of North Carolina, “ How Positive Emotions Build Physical Health: Perceived Positive Social Connections Account for the Upward Spiral Between Positive Emotions and Vagal Tone,” was published in  Psychological Science. This groundbreaking research honed in on the vagus nerve and discovered that a high vagal tone index was part of a feedback loop between positive emotions, physical health, and positive social connections.

北卡罗来纳大学的Barbara Fredrickson和Bethany Kok在2013年进行的研究《心理科学如何建立积极的身体关系：感知到的积极社交关系解释了积极情绪和迷走神经张力之间呈螺旋式上升》。这项开创性的研究在迷走神经上进行了磨练，发现高迷走神经张力指数是积极情绪，身体健康和积极社交关系之间反馈回路的一部分。

Identifying this correlation prompted me to stop in my tracks. I did a simple search for “HRV loving kindness vagus nerve” on my phone.  Voila: A holy-grail type of study from 2016 by Igor Grossmann that had completely gone under my radar, “ A Heart and A Mind: Self-Distancing Facilitates the Association Between Heart Rate Variability and Wise Reasoning.” (I will explore Grossmann's hypotheses in a future post.)

识别这种相关性促使我停下来。我在手机上简单搜索了“ HRV热爱迷走神经”。 Voila：Igor Grossmann在2016年进行的一项圣杯式研究完全落入了我的视野：“一颗心与思想：自我区分有助于心率变异性与明智推理之间的联系。” （我将在以后的文章中探讨格罗斯曼的假设。）

The reason I’m telling you all of this is twofold. First, after skimming Grossmann's research on HRV, the parasympathetic nervous system, egocentric impulses, self-distancing, and the vagus nerve on the jogging trail this morning I had a mini “ Eureka!” moment. I made a quick mental list of nine vagal maneuvers to optimize heart rate variability and parasympathetic nervous system. Second, the aforementioned problem-solving experience I had over the past few days reaffirms one of my favorite quotations about the creative process, by Arthur Koestler:

我要告诉你所有这些的原因是双重的。首先，在今天早上对格罗斯曼（Grossmann）对HRV的研究进行了略读之后，副交感神经系统，以自我为中心的冲动，自我疏散和迷走神经在慢跑路线上走了，我得到了一个迷你的``尤里卡！时刻。我快速列出了九种迷走神经动作，以优化心率变异性和副交感神经系统。其次，我过去几天来解决问题的经验再次证实了我最喜欢的关于创作过程的引文之一，作者是亚瑟·科斯特勒（Arthur Koestler）：

“The moment of truth, the sudden emergence of a new insight, is an act of intuition. Such intuitions give the appearance of miraculous flashes, or short-circuits of reasoning. In fact they may be likened to an immersed chain, of which only the beginning and the end are visible above the surface of consciousness. The diver vanishes at one end of the chain and comes up at the other end, guided by invisible links.”

“关键时刻，即新见解的突然出现，是一种直觉。这样的直觉使人产生了奇迹般的闪烁，或推理的短路。实际上，它们可以比喻为沉浸式的链条，其中只有起点和终点在意识表面上方可见。潜水员在链条的一端消失，而在看不见的链接的引导下，在另一端出现。”

Anecdotally, I can reaffirm Koestler’s description of what it feels like to have a fresh insight, because I just had one. I’ve found that after fastidiously gathering a lot of crystallized knowledge and filling my head with tons of empirical evidence, an “ Aha!” moment ultimately requires an incubation period that integrates fluid intelligence and cognitive flexibility.

有趣的是，我可以重申科斯特勒对描述的理解，因为我只有一个。我发现，在认真收集了许多明确的知识并用大量的经验证据充实了我的头脑之后，“啊哈！”此刻最终需要一个结合了流体智力和认知灵活性的潜伏期。

From a neuroscientific perspective, I have a hunch that the process Koestler describes might rely on "unclamping” cerebral control of the prefrontal cortex and other cortical brain areas by allowing ideas to bubble up into consciousness from subcortical brain regions such as the basal ganglia and cerebellum.

从神经科学的角度来看，我有种预感，科斯特勒描述的过程可能依赖于通过松散大脑皮层下的大脑区域（例如基底层）使意识bubble散到大脑，从而放松对前额叶皮层和其他皮质脑区域的大脑控制。神经节和小脑。

I know my compilation of nine vagal maneuvers to stimulate the vagus nerve may not be earth-shattering. Nevertheless, it felt “miraculous” after days of wrestling with free-floating and dissociated ideas about the vagus nerve to finally connect the dots in a new and hopefully useful way.

我知道我为刺激迷走神经而编写的九种迷走神经动作的汇编可能不会使人震惊。然而，经过数天关于迷走神经的自由浮动和分离的想法的搏斗，终于以一种新的，希望有用的方式将这些点连接起来，这感觉是``神奇的''。

Over the next few weeks, I will tackle each of these nine vagal maneuvers in a separate post.

Green is the fashion flavor of the week with Rubi Labs’ materials made from captured CO2
绿色是本周的时尚风格，Rubi Labs的材料由捕获的二氧化碳制成

2022-02-17 07:24:58

Not a moment too soon, either — the fashion industry emits more carbon than international flights and shipping combined, representing around 10% of greenhouse gases every year, the BBC reports. While it’s laudable to make the industry greener, I’d argue that the problem may actually be with the fashion industry itself; in a world where an item of clothing is worn an average of seven times before it is discarded, it seems like the “ reduce, reuse, recycle” mantra of greener living falls short on all three counts, as far as fashion is concerned. Still; people are gonna people, fashion isn’t going to go away in a hurry, and perhaps it’s better if a garment worn twice rots away and fades from memory in a landfill faster rather than slower.

英国广播公司报道，时尚业排放的碳比国际航班和航运的总和还要多，约占每年温室气体排放量的10%。虽然让这个行业变得更环保是值得称赞的，但我认为问题其实可能出在时尚行业本身；在这样一个世界里，一件衣服在被丢弃之前平均要穿七次，就时尚而言，绿色生活的“减少、再利用、回收”口号似乎在这三个方面都不够。还人终将是人，时尚不会很快消失，如果一件穿了两次的衣服在垃圾堆中腐烂并从记忆中消失，那也许会更好，更快，而不是更慢。

Against that backdrop, Rubi Laboratories is flying a green banner over the industry with “carbon-negative cellulosic textiles.” The company, founded by the nieces of the founder at the Bebe fashion brand ( with a market cap north of $100 million as I’m writing this), announced it raised a $4.5 million seed funding round from Talis Capital and Necessary Ventures, and a fistful of additional institutional investors (the company’s press release lists Climactic, Collaborative Fund, Plug and Play, Incite Ventures, Darco Capital, Cayuse Partners, Axial VC, Climate Capital Collective and CapitalX) and a slew of angel investors (including James Reinhart, CEO and founder of thredUP; Manny Mashouf, CEO and founder of Bebe Stores; Nicolaj Reffstrup, founder of GANNI; Alexander Lorestani, CEO and co-founder of Geltor; and Rei Wang, co-founder of The Grand and former CEO of Dorm Room Fund). On top of that, the funding round also includes a $250,000 grant from the National Science Foundation.

在这样的背景下，鲁比实验室（Rubi Laboratories）正以“碳负纤维纺织品”在该行业高举绿色旗帜该公司由Bebe时尚品牌创始人的侄女创办（在我写这篇文章时，市值为1亿美元），宣布从塔利斯资本和必要的风险投资公司筹集了450万美元的种子资金，以及一大批额外的机构投资者（该公司的新闻稿列出了Climatic、Collaborative Fund、即插即用、煽动风投、Darco Capital、Cayuse Partners、Axial VC、Climate Capital Collective和CapitalX）和一批天使投资者（包括thredUP首席执行官兼创始人詹姆斯·莱因哈特、Bebe Stores首席执行官兼创始人曼尼·马苏夫、甘尼创始人尼古拉·雷夫斯特鲁普、Geltor首席执行官兼联合创始人亚历山大·洛雷斯塔尼、Grand联合创始人兼宿舍基金前首席执行官王丽）。此外，资助轮还包括来自国家科学基金会的250000美元赠款。

“I’ve always been passionate about sustainability and climate. When we founded Rubi, it all just clicked together. Starting when I was 15 years old, I published my first paper on artificial photosynthesis at the Lawrence Berkeley National Lab,” explains Neeka Mashouf, CEO at Rubi Laboratories. “Since then, I have been really focused on sustainable materials research. I studied materials engineering and business at UC Berkeley, and then dabbled in launching ventures around sustainability.”

鲁比实验室首席执行官尼卡·马苏夫解释说：“我一直对可持续性和气候充满热情。我们创立鲁比时，一切都很顺利。从我15岁开始，我在劳伦斯伯克利国家实验室发表了第一篇关于人工光合作用的论文。”。“从那以后，我一直非常专注于可持续材料研究。我在加州大学伯克利分校学习材料工程和商业，然后涉足围绕可持续性的创业。”

For Rubi Labs, the duo developed technologies and filed a number of patents. Its first product is a cell-free biocatalytic process that resulted in viscose — also known as Rayon — the third-most used textile fiber by the world. It is used as a cheaper and more durable alternative to silk and synthetic velvet. It is typically made by taking wood pulp, dissolving it in chemicals and spinning it into fibers that can be turned into threads. Threads make fabrics, fabrics make clothes, you get the picture.

在Rubi实验室，两人开发了技术并申请了多项专利。它的第一个产品是一种无细胞生物催化过程，它产生了粘胶纤维，也被称为人造丝，是世界上使用量第三大的纺织纤维。它被用作丝绸和合成天鹅绒的更便宜、更耐用的替代品。它通常是通过提取木浆，将其溶解在化学物质中，然后将其纺成纤维，然后变成线。线制造布料，布料制造衣服，你明白了。

“I found myself really wanting to understand the biological systems that evolved to build carbon-based life, and how you could take inspiration across nature and engineer intelligent systems of biology that could solve human problems that evolution itself was not necessarily solving for. I’ve worked in bioengineering research labs since I was around 15, as well, leading projects from ideation and execution and transfer to clinical trials, mostly focused on solving one of the arguably most difficult-to-treat diseases in medicine: brain cancer,” says Leila Mashouf, CTO at Rubi Laboratories. “And that work led me to medical school at Harvard Medical School, where I was exposed to so many different speakers who came in, who talked a lot about climate change and the threat to human health that climate change posed.”

“我发现自己真的很想了解进化来构建碳基生命的生物系统，以及你如何在自然界中获得灵感，并设计出能够解决进化本身不一定能解决的人类问题的生物智能系统。从我小时候起，我就在生物工程研究实验室工作鲁比实验室的首席技术官莱拉·马苏夫说：“此外，我们还领导着从构思、执行到临床试验的项目，主要集中在解决医学上最难治疗的疾病之一：脑癌。”这项工作让我进入了哈佛医学院的医学院，在那里我接触了很多不同的演讲者，他们谈到了气候变化以及气候变化对人类健康构成的威胁。"

To fulfill its goals, Rubi captures CO 2 from the waste streams of manufacturing facilities using its proprietary enzyme system. It is able to capture and convert CO 2 from a gas input at any concentration.

为了实现其目标，鲁比使用其专有的酶系统从生产设施的废物流中捕获二氧化碳。它能够从任何浓度的气体输入中捕获和转换二氧化碳。

“What’s exciting is our technology is actually really flexible on the source of CO 2. We’ve tested and proven that it can work even on direct air capture, which is very low levels of CO 2,” explains Leila Mashouf. She adds that it makes even more sense to capture CO 2 from sources directly related to textile production. “We like to use concentrated sources of CO 2, like flue gas from a factory, or an industrial source.”

莱拉·马苏夫解释说：“令人兴奋的是，我们的技术实际上在二氧化碳来源方面非常灵活。我们已经测试并证明，它甚至可以用于直接空气捕捉，这是非常低的二氧化碳水平。”。她补充说，从与纺织品生产直接相关的来源捕获二氧化碳更有意义。“我们喜欢使用二氧化碳的集中来源，比如工厂的烟气或工业来源。”

Once captured from whatever source is available, CO 2 is then converted into cellulose, which can then be used to create viscose-based yarn. By utilizing enzymes as the catalyst, Rubi claims it is able to turn 100% of CO 2 input into the reactors into an end product, all with zero waste. If at some point the company is able to replace all of the viscose used in the fashion industry, the product is widely used in other industries, too, such as automotive tires, food, packaging and building materials.

一旦从任何可用的来源捕获，二氧化碳就会转化为纤维素，然后可以用来制造粘胶基纱线。Rubi声称，通过利用酶作为催化剂，它能够将进入反应器的100%的二氧化碳转化为最终产品，所有这些都是零废物。如果在某个时候，该公司能够取代时尚行业中使用的所有粘胶，那么该产品也广泛应用于其他行业，如汽车轮胎、食品、包装和建筑材料。

As mentioned, the company raised $4.5 million, which is largely earmarked to develop the product from the concept and sample-scale to launch commercialization.

如前所述，该公司筹集了450万美元，主要用于从概念和样本规模开发产品，以启动商业化。

“We were really looking for investors who could see this sustainable symbiotic future that we see as possible, and who were willing to take the risks that are part of the journey, and who believe in us as founders. I think we really found that in our investors,” says Neeka Mashouf. “We’ve found such visionary, inspiring and supportive investors like Talis and Necessary Ventures. I think it’s like the perfect team to make this happen.”

Neeka Mashouf说：“我们真的在寻找能够看到我们所看到的可持续共生未来的投资者，他们愿意承担旅程中的风险，并且相信我们是创始人。我认为我们真的在我们的投资者身上找到了这一点。”。“我们已经找到了像塔利斯和必经之路这样有远见、鼓舞人心和支持性的投资者。我认为这是实现这一目标的完美团队。”

From an investors’ point of view, Talis saw an enormous opportunity to shake up the textile industry.

从投资者的角度来看，塔利斯看到了一个撼动纺织行业的巨大机会。

“When we think about where [Talis Capital] likes to invest, materials was always a big one. In the next decade or so, we really need to rethink everything around us, from chemicals to building materials to packaging. Textiles is one of them, too. I have spent a lot of time in the fashion space, and we are acutely aware of the problem the industry has from a supply chain perspective. What we really liked about Rubi was that if we look at the textile space, there’s cotton as the most-used material, but it’s really hard to remake that with synthetic biology. Then there’s polyester, which is a great material, but it’s a kind of plastic and a fossil fuel-based material,” Cecilia Manduca, associate at Talis Capital explains. “And then finally there’s viscose, which is the third-largest material. It comes from natural base materials but has a lot of production problems. But if you can clean those up, you can have a massive impact in the space. We started to look there, and we liked the look of it from a returns potential and impact perspective. We found Rubi, and we love their CO 2 approach. It fits perfectly.”

“当我们想到[塔利斯资本]喜欢投资，材料总是一个大问题。在未来十年左右，我们真的需要重新思考我们周围的一切，从化学品到建筑材料再到包装。纺织品也是其中之一。我在时尚领域花了很多时间，我们从供应链的角度敏锐地意识到了这个行业存在的问题。我们真正喜欢Rubi的地方是，如果我们看看纺织领域，棉花是最常用的材料，但用合成生物学来改造它确实很困难。还有聚酯，这是一种很好的材料，但它是一种塑料和化石燃料基材料，”塔利斯资本公司的助理塞西莉亚·曼杜卡解释道最后还有粘胶，它是第三大材料。它来自天然基材，但有很多生产问题。但是如果你能清理这些，你可以在太空中产生巨大的影响。我们开始关注它，从回报潜力和影响的角度来看，我们喜欢它的外观。我们找到了鲁比，我们喜欢他们的二氧化碳方法。很合身。"

Rubi’s first textile samples are expected to be available in February 2022. Rubi has validated its technology by creating a successful prototype and claims it has developed test plans with numerous top-tier global retail and fashion brands. Rubi is also in discussions with various multinational energy and manufacturing companies to provide CO 2 to scale up production.

鲁比的第一批纺织品样品预计将于2022年2月上市。Rubi通过创建一个成功的原型来验证其技术，并声称已与众多顶级全球零售和时尚品牌制定了测试计划。鲁比还在与多家跨国能源和制造公司讨论提供二氧化碳以扩大生产。

For now, the company targets the fashion industry, as its product comes at quite a premium compared to the existing fabrics that are available; but as the technology improves and scale increases, the company is hoping to drive prices down, too.

目前，该公司的目标是时装行业，因为与现有的面料相比，其产品具有相当高的价格；但随着技术的进步和规模的扩大，该公司也希望压低价格。

“Our goal is to reach price parity with standard viscose. That really unlocks [our product], because viscose is a really common material both in fast fashion and in higher-scale designer fashion too,” explains Neeka Mashouf. “Being able to be price-competitive with the standard textiles on the market means that the playing field has been leveled.”

Neeka Mashouf解释说：“我们的目标是达到与标准粘胶的价格平价。这真的解锁了（我们的产品），因为粘胶在快速时尚和更大规模的设计师时尚中也是一种非常常见的材料。”。“能够在价格上与市场上的标准纺织品竞争，意味着竞争环境已经变得平等。”

“Our vision is a world where human prosperity and economic growth is planet positive. And we really see this technology reaching that vision by being a platform technology,” says Neeka Mashouf. “We’re rethinking the way that we produce materials starting with textiles, but also extensible to other things, like building materials, packaging, food and so much more. We can achieve this vision that’s symbiotic with the planet using CO 2 to make critical materials in a way that is water and land-neutral, chemical-neutral, symbiotic with the planet.”

Neeka Mashouf说：“我们的愿景是一个人类繁荣和经济增长对地球有利的世界。我们真的看到这项技术通过成为平台技术来实现这一愿景。”。“我们正在重新思考我们生产材料的方式，从纺织品开始，但也可以扩展到其他东西，比如建筑材料、包装、食品等等。我们可以实现这个与地球共生的愿景，利用二氧化碳以水和土地中性、化学中性、与t他是一个星球。"

Over the past few months, music stars have been scrambling to cash in on the latest craze in collectibles by selling art, merch and even albums in the form of non-fungible tokens, or NFTs. But this week, the phenomenon became more confusing than ever for the music industry, when many of the top artists went on sale as pseudo-NFTs themselves.

在过去的几个月里，音乐明星通过销售非娱乐令牌或NFT的形式销售艺术，erc甚至相册，争夺最新的热潮中的最新热潮。但本周，当许多顶级艺术家销售伪NFTS本身时，这一现象比以往任何时候都比以往任何时候都更加混乱。

The culprit: BitClout, a new platform that promises to allow creators and musicians to monetize their celebrity. The decentralized social network and marketplace has been quietly bubbling under the radar, with backers rumored to include big-name venture capital firms. Over recent weeks, the platform had uploaded the top 15,000 influencers from Twitter and issued coins for each, allowing early site users to buy and sell them. Prices for the coins of celebs like Billie Eilish and Elon Musk soared.

罪魁祸首：Bitclout，一个新的平台，承诺让创造者和音乐家将他们的名人货币化。分散的社交网络和市场在雷达下一直悄然冒泡，背板传闻包括大型风险投资公司。最近几周，该平台从Twitter上传了来自Twitter的前15,000个影响因素，并为每个人发出硬币，允许早期的网站用户购买和销售它们。像Billie Eilish和Elon Musk这样的Conebs的价格飙升。

In a FAQ published on the site before it was taken down Friday afternoon — due to “overwhelming support,” a note on the now-empty website states — BitClout describes itself as a social network that allows users to “speculate on people and posts with real money.” BitClout claims to be a fully open-sourced project that built a custom blockchain using architecture comparable to Bitcoin with “no company behind it” that will allow users to purchase a new asset class called “Creator Coins” for accounts on the site, which includes celebrities, musicians, and influencers.

在一个常见问题解答之前发表于星期五下午的网站 - 由于“压倒性的支持”，现在空网站的说明 - BitClout将自己描述为一个社交网络，允许用户“推测人员和帖子真钱。“ BitClout声称是一个完全开放的项目，它使用与比特币相当的架构构建了自定义区块链，其中包含“缺失的公司”，这将允许用户在网站上为账户购买名为“Creator Coins”的新资产类别，其中包括名人，音乐家和影响者。

“They were on there mostly without their permission,” a source tells Billboard. Current users can buy and sell coins that will increase and decrease in value depending on the “social clout” of the user the coin is based on, or how the user behaves in public. Anyone who signs up for the site can mint their own coins, celebrity or not.

“他们主要在没有他们的许可的情况下，”一个来源告诉广告牌。当前用户可以购买和销售将增加和减少价值的硬币，这取决于用户的“社会clout”，硬币是基于的，或者用户如何在公共场合行事。任何针对该网站签署的人都可以塑造自己的硬币，名人。

The FAQ describes these “Creator Coins” as an asset “tied to the reputation of an individual, rather than to a company or commodity.” Effectively this means if a celebrity does something great and more people buy their Creator Coin, the value will go up; if they fail publicly or commit an offensive act, the value of their coin could decrease.

常见问题解答将这些“创造者硬币”描述为一个资产“与个人的声誉相关，而不是公司或商品。”有效地这意味着如果名人做出了伟大和更多人购买他们的创造者硬币，那么价值将上升;如果他们公开失败或承诺进攻行为，他们的硬币的价值可以减少。

“It’s NFTs for people,” a source close to the situation tells Billboard.

“这是人民的NFTS，”靠近情况的来源告诉广告牌。

To buy creator coins, users need to purchase BitClout cryptocurrency, which can only be acquired with Bitcoin, a move that will surely limit its initial audience. The price of BitClout “doubles for every million BitClout sold,” the paper states, a move designed to promote scarcity and increase the value of BitClout as more people buy into the influencers on the network.

要购买Creator Coins，用户需要购买位紧密电机，只能用比特币获得，这是一个肯定限制其最初受众的移动。比特琴打“每百万比特锁定的双打，”纸张国家，旨在促进稀缺，增加比特阵线的价值，因为更多人在网络上购买影响力。

Before the site was taken offline on Friday (March 12), there was a countdown timer ending at 12 p.m. PST, when BitClout was scheduled to relaunch, a plan that did not go smoothly. A source who used the network described it as “a mess, search was broken, it was incredibly buggy.” On audio-chat app Clubhouse, scores of artists, managers and executives spent the rest of the day trying to make sense of what had happened and debating whether early investors were wasting their bitcoin -- or whether the platform might hold promise for the music community.

Despite the glaring question around how BitClout will get away with using the names and likenesses of 15,000 celebrities without permission, the social network says it will allow those influencers to claim their accounts simply by tweeting their BitClout public key, which will give them full access to their accounts, as well as a “percentage of the creator coins associated with their profile,” according to the FAQ.

It’s safe to say that gamifying the social behavior of thousands of celebrities would have a massive impact on society in unpredictable ways: one act’s misguided tweet could cost its fans serious money, adding yet more pressure to the already high-stakes world of social media for those in the limelight. But for now, the biggest question is whether it is legal for a social network to monetize potential users before they even sign up.

可以安全地说，赌展成千上万名人的社会行为将以不可预测的方式对社会产生大规模影响：一个法案的误导推文可能会使其粉丝严重金钱，为已有的高赌注世界为社交媒体增加了压力那些在敏捷的人。但是，现在，最大的问题是社交网络是否合法化在他们甚至在注册之前批准潜在用户。

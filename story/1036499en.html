World's first no-kill eggs go on sale in Berlin
世界上第一个不死蛋在柏林发售

2020-11-25 16:41:40

Chick’s sex can be determined before it hatches, potentially ending culling of billions of males

小鸡的性别可以在孵出之前确定，有可能结束数十亿男性的淘汰

The world’s first ever no-kill eggs are now on sale in Berlin after German scientists found an easy way to determine a chick’s sex before it hatches, in a breakthrough that could put an end to the annual live shredding of billions of male chicks worldwide. The patented “Seleggt” process can determine the sex of a chick just nine days after an egg has been fertilised. Male eggs are processed into animal feed, leaving only female chicks to hatch at the end of a 21-day incubation period. “If you can determine the sex of a hatching egg you can entirely dispense with the culling of live male chicks,” said Seleggt managing director Dr Ludger Breloh, who spearheaded the four-year programme by German supermarket Rewe Group to make its own-brand eggs more sustainable.

在德国科学家找到了一种在孵化前确定小鸡性别的简便方法之后，世界上第一个不死蛋现已在柏林发售，这项突破可能会终止全球每年数十亿只雄性小鸡的活体粉碎。受专利保护的“ Seleggt”方法可以在卵受精后九天确定小鸡的性别。雄性卵被加工成动物饲料，在21天的潜伏期结束时仅让雌性雏鸡孵化。 Seleggt董事总经理Ludger Breloh博士说：“如果您能确定孵化卵的性别，则完全可以免除淘汰的活雄雏鸡。”鸡蛋更可持续。

“It’s not about winning or losing,” he added of the worldwide race to find a marketable solution. “We all have the same goal, which is to end the culling of chicks in the supply chain. Of course, there’s competition, but it’s positive in that it keeps us all focused on that goal.”

他在寻找可销售解决方案的全球竞赛中补充说：“这不是输赢。” “我们都有一个共同的目标，那就是结束供应链中雏鸡的淘汰。当然，这里有竞争，但这是积极的，因为它使我们所有人都专注于这一目标。”

An estimated 4-6 billion male chicks are slaughtered globally every year because they serve no economic purpose. Some are suffocated, others are fed alive into grinding or shredding machines to be processed into reptile food. The culling is a messy solution to a thorny problem of modern poultry farming. Humans have bred chickens for one of two purposes: to produce eggs, or meat. Yet half of all the animals bred for this purpose are considered useless. Male chicks lay no eggs and don’t grow fast enough to justify the cost of feeding them up for meat. So, they are simply destroyed.

据估计，全球每年有4到60亿只雄性小鸡被屠宰，因为它们没有经济目的。有些被窒息而死，另一些则被活泼地投入研磨机或切碎机中，以加工成爬行动物。剔除是现代家禽养殖棘手问题的一个混乱解决方案。人类为以下两个目的之一饲养鸡：生产鸡蛋或肉。然而，为此目的饲养的所有动物中有一半被认为是无用的。雄性雏鸡不产卵，生长速度不足以证明为它们喂肉的成本。因此，它们只是被破坏了。

Chick culling has become increasingly controversial. In 2015, a video went viral of an Israeli animal rights activist shutting down a chick shredding machine and challenging a police officer to turn it back on. Consumer kickback has prompted a global race to develop a more humane solution.

小鸡的淘汰已引起越来越多的争议。 2015年，一段录像带传播了一个以色列动物权利活动家的视频，该视频关闭了一个切碎小鸡的机器，并向警官挑战，将其重新打开。消费者的回扣促使全球范围内争相开发更加人性化的解决方案。

Breloh said his first breakthrough came when he approached scientists at the University of Leipzig where Prof Almuth Einspanier had developed a chemical marker – similar to a pregnancy test – that could detect a hormone present in high quantities in female eggs. Mixed with fluid from fertilised eggs at nine days, the marker changes blue for a male and white for a female, with a 98.5% accuracy rate.

布雷洛说，他的第一个突破是在莱比锡大学与科学家接触时，阿尔穆特·埃因斯潘尼埃教授在该处开发了一种化学标记物（类似于妊娠试验），可以检测雌卵中大量存在的激素。在第9天与来自受精卵的液体混合后，标记物的雄性变为蓝色，雌性变为白色，准确率为98.5％。

Next Breloh had to find a way of making the test easy for everyday use in hatcheries. He approached Dutch technology company HatchTech and asked them to make an automated machine to conduct Einspanier’s test from beginning to end.

Next Breloh必须找到一种使孵化场日常使用容易的方法。他与荷兰科技公司HatchTech取得了联系，并要求他们制造一台自动机器进行Einspanier的测试。

It had to be easy to use, scalable, flexible, precise, hygienic and above all, fast – the eggs couldn’t be out of the incubator for more than two hours. The biggest problem was how to extract test fluid quickly from the egg without damaging it. A needle would work, but it was invasive and also brought additional hygienic problems. Instead, a laser beam burns a 0.3mm-wide hole in the shell. Then, air pressure is applied to the shell exterior, pushing a drop of fluid out of the hole. The process takes one second per egg and enables fluid to be collected from eggs without touching them. “It worked absolutely faultlessly,” said Breloh of the test phase. “Today, female hens are laying eggs in farms in Germany that have been bred without killing any male chicks.” Earlier this year Seleggt hatched the first brood of hens bred using the method. Their eggs – the first to be sold from hens reared without killing male chicks - hit supermarket shelves in Berlin in November, bearing the seal “respeggt”. Rewe Group plan to roll out the eggs across German stores next year, while Seleggt plans to install the technology in independent hatcheries from 2020. Seleggt will require supermarkets to pay a few extra cents on every box of eggs sold with their “respeggt” seal. Eventually, the group hopes to expand the model across Europe. “With the market readiness of [this] process, Germany is a pioneer,” said German minister of food and agriculture, Julia Klöckner, whose ministry funded the project. “Once the process is made available to all and the hatcheries have implemented [it], there will be no reason and no justification for chick culling.”

它必须易于使用，可扩展，灵活，精确，卫生，最重要的是要快速-卵不能离开孵化器超过两个小时。最大的问题是如何从鸡蛋中快速提取测试液而不损坏鸡蛋。针头可以使用，但它具有侵入性，并且还带来了其他卫生问题。取而代之的是，激光束会在外壳上燃烧0.3mm宽的孔。然后，向外壳外部施加气压，将一滴流体推出孔。该过程每个鸡蛋花费一秒钟的时间，并且可以从鸡蛋中收集液体而不会碰到它们。 “它绝对完美无缺，”测试阶段的布雷洛说。 “如今，雌性母鸡正在德国的农场里产卵，它们的繁殖没有杀死任何雄性小鸡。”今年年初，塞莱格特（Seleggt）用这种方法孵化出了第一批母鸡。他们的鸡蛋是第一个在不杀死雄鸡的情况下从母鸡那里出售的鸡蛋，并于11月在柏林的超市货架上打上了“重新标明”字样。瑞威集团计划明年在德国各地的商店中推广这种鸡蛋，而塞莱格特计划从2020年开始在独立的孵化场中安装该技术。塞莱格特将要求超市为每盒带有“重新配盖”印章的鸡蛋支付额外的美分。最终，该组织希望将模型扩展到整个欧洲。 “随着（这一）过程的市场准备就绪，德国成为了先驱者，”德国粮食和农业部长朱莉娅·克洛克纳（JuliaKlöckner）表示，他的部委为该项目提供了资金。 “一旦所有人都可以使用该程序，并且孵化场已经实施，便没有理由也没有理由进行鸡淘汰。”

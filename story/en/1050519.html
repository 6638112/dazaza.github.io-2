Intel Told to Pay $2.18B After Losing Texas Patent Trial
英特尔在输掉德州专利审判后被告知要支付$ 2.18B

2021-03-03 05:52:27

Susan Decker and Matthew Bultman (Bloomberg) -- Intel Corp. was told to pay $2.18 billion after losing a patent-infringement trial over technology related to chip-making. Intel infringed two patents owned by closely held VLSI Technology LLC, a federal jury in Waco, Texas, said. The jury found $1.5 billion for infringement of one patent and $675 million for infringement of the second. Intel, the world’s largest chipmaker, denied infringing either of the patents and said one was invalid because it claimed to cover work done by Intel engineers, but the jury rejected those arguments. The patents had been owned by Dutch chipmaker NXP Semiconductors Inc., which would get a cut of any damage award, Intel lawyer William Lee of WilmerHale told jurors in closing arguments Monday. VLSI, founded four years ago, has no products and its only potential revenue is this lawsuit, he said. VLSI “took two patents off the shelf that hadn’t been used for 10 years and said, ‘We’d like $2 billion,”’ Lee told the jury. The “outrageous” demand by VLSI “would tax the true innovators.”

Susan Decker和Matthew Bultman（彭博）-英特尔公司因在一项与芯片制造相关的技术的专利侵权审判失败后被告知支付21.8亿美元。 德克萨斯州韦科市的联邦陪审团说，英特尔侵犯了紧密控股的VLSI Technology LLC拥有的两项专利。陪审团裁定，一项专利侵权的赔偿金额为15亿美元，另一项专利侵权的赔偿额为6.75亿美元。英特尔是全球最大的芯片制造商，否认侵犯了其中任何一项专利，并说一项专利无效，因为它声称涵盖了英特尔工程师所做的工作，但陪审团拒绝了这些论点。 英特尔律师威尔默·黑尔（WilmerHale）的威廉·李（William Lee）周一在闭幕辩论中对陪审团说，这些专利归荷兰芯片制造商恩智浦半导体（NXP Semiconductors Inc.）所有。他说，成立于四年前的超大规模集成电路公司尚无产品，其唯一的潜在收入就是这场官司。 Lee对陪审团说：“ VLSI取得了已有十年未使用的两项专利，并且说，‘我们想要20亿美元’。” VLSI的“残酷”需求“将使真正的创新者承受重担。”

One of the patents was originally issued in 2012 to Freescale Semiconductor Inc. and the other in 2010 to SigmaTel Inc. Freescale bought SigmaTel and was in turn  bought by NXP in 2015. The two patents in this case were transferred to VLSI in 2019, according to data compiled by Bloomberg Law.

其中一项专利最初于2012年授予飞思卡尔半导体公司，另一项专利于2010年授予SigmaTel Inc.飞思卡尔收购了SigmaTel，并于2015年又被NXP收购。在这种情况下，两项专利已于2019年转让给VLSI。彭博社法律汇编的数据。

VLSI lawyer Morgan Chu of Irell & Minella said the patents cover inventions that increase the power and speed of processors, a key issue for competition.

Vell的Irell＆amp; amp;律师摩根·朱（Morgan Chu） Minella说，这些专利涵盖了增加处理器功能和速度的发明，这是竞争的关键问题。

Federal law doesn’t require someone to know of a patent to be found to have infringed it, and Intel purposely didn’t look to see if it was using someone else’s inventions, he said. He accused the Santa Clara, California-based company of “willful blindness.” The damage request isn’t so high when the billions of chips sold by Intel are taken into account, Chu said. Intel paid MicroUnity Systems Engineering Corp. $300 million 2005 and in 2011 paid Nvidia Corp. $1.5 billion even though a  settlement in that case involved a cross license of technology, he said.

他说，联邦法律不要求知道某人知道某项专利已被侵犯，而英特尔故意不看该专利是否在使用他人的发明。他指责这家位于加利福尼亚州圣克拉拉的公司“故意失明”。 楚说，考虑到英特尔销售的数十亿芯片，对损害的要求不是很高。他说，英特尔在2005年向MicroUnity Systems Engineering Corp.支付了3亿美元，并在2011年向Nvidia Corp.支付了15亿美元，尽管在这种情况下的和解涉及技术交叉许可。

The trial is among the few in-person patent trials in recent months, with many courts pressing pause amid the coronavirus pandemic. It was delayed a week because of the winter storm that wreaked havoc across much of Texas.

该审判是近几个月来为数不多的面对面专利审判之一，在冠状病毒大流行期间，许多法院都要求暂停。由于冬季风暴在得克萨斯州的大部分地区造成了严重破坏，因此推迟了一周。

Intel had sought to postpone the case because of the pandemic, but was rejected by District Court Judge Alan Albright, a former patent litigator and magistrate who was sworn in as a federal judge in 2018 and has quickly turned his courtroom into one of the most popular for patent owners to file suit.

英特尔曾因流感大流行而试图推迟此案，但遭到地方法院法官艾伦·奥尔布赖特（Alan Albright）的拒绝，他是前专利诉讼和裁判官，他于2018年宣誓就任联邦法官，并迅速将其法庭变成了最受欢迎的法庭之一供专利所有人提起诉讼。

The case is VLSI Technology LLC v. Intel Corp., 21-57, U.S. District Court for the Western District of Texas (Waco).

Surgeons Perform First Human Tracheal Transplant Surgery
外科医生进行第一人体气管移植手术

2021-04-11 21:35:58

A team of Mount Sinai surgeons has performed the world’s first human tracheal transplant—an achievement that has the potential to save the lives of thousands of patients around the world who have tracheal birth defects, untreatable airway diseases, burns, tumors, or severe tracheal damage from intubation, including those who had been hospitalized with COVID-19 and placed on a ventilator. Until now, no long-term treatments existed for these patients with long-segment tracheal damage, and thousands of adults and children died each year as a result.

山山山队队伍已经表演了世界上第一个人类气管移植 - 这一成就，有可能挽救全世界患有气管出生缺陷，无法治疗的气道疾病，烧伤，肿瘤或严重气管损伤的患者从插管来看，包括使用Covid-19住院的人，并将其放在呼吸机上。到目前为止，这些患者没有长期治疗，这些患者患有长段气管损伤，成千上万的成年人和儿童每年都会死亡。

The trachea, also known as the windpipe, is an organ that is essential for speaking, breathing, and normal lung function. The trachea connects the larynx to the lungs and plays a critical role in normal lung function, the immune system, and breathing.

气管，也称为气管，是一种对于说话，呼吸和正常的肺功能是必不可少的器官。气管将喉部与肺部连接起来，在正常的肺功能，免疫系统和呼吸中起着关键作用。

Surgeons have been unable to transplant this organ in large part because of the complexity of providing blood flow to the donor trachea, leaving patients with long-segment tracheal disease no option for treatment. Mount Sinai’s historic procedure resulted from 30 years of research, much of it focused on how to revascularize, or provide blood flow to the trachea.

由于为供体气管提供血液流动的复杂性，外科医生无法在很大程度上移植这种器官，使患有长段气管疾病的患者无需治疗。山西山的历史程序引发了30年的研究，其中大部分都集中在如何血运重建，或为气管提供血液流动。

The 18-hour procedure took place on Wednesday, January 13, and was led by surgeon-scientist Eric M. Genden, MD, MHCA, FACS, the Isidore Friesner Professor and Chair of Otolaryngology – Head and Neck Surgery for Mount Sinai Health System and Professor of Neurosurgery, and Immunology, at the Icahn School of Medicine at Mount Sinai. The complex surgery involved a team of more than 50 specialists including surgeons, nurses, anesthesiologists, and residents.

18小时的程序于1月13日星期三进行，由Surgeon-Scients Eric M.Genden，MD，MHCA，FACS，Isidore Friesner教授和Otolaryngology教授和耳鼻喉科骑士卫生系统和颈部手术椅子领导在西奈山的Icahn医学院，神经外科教授和免疫学教授。复杂的手术涉及超过50名专家的团队，包括外科医生，护士，麻醉学家和居民。

“For the first time, we are able to offer a viable treatment option to patients with life-compromising long-segment tracheal defects, and this development will change the standard of care. It is particularly timely given the growing number of patients with extensive tracheal issues due to COVID-19 intubation. Because of both mechanical ventilation and the nature of the COVID-19-induced airway disease, tracheal airway disease is precipitously increasing, and now we have a treatment. Our trachea transplantation and revascularization protocol is reliable, reproducible, and technically straightforward,” says Dr. Genden. “For years, the medical and scientific consensus has been that trachea transplantation could not be done because the organ’s complexity made revascularization impossible, and every previous attempt to perform in-human transplantation ended in failure. This surgical achievement is not only the culmination of 30 years of research that began when I was a medical student at Mount Sinai, but was also made possible by the spirit of collaboration that exists at Mount Sinai.”

“首次，我们能够为患者危及长段气管缺陷的患者提供可行的治疗选择，这一发展将改变护理标准。鉴于Covid-19插管，尤其是较越来越多的患者越来越多的患者患有广泛的气管问题。由于机械通风和Covid-19诱导的气道疾病的性质，气管气道疾病急剧增加，现在我们有治疗。我们的气管移植和血运重建方案是可靠的，可重复的，技术直截了当，“德文博士说。 “多年来，医学和科学共识一直是无法进行气管移植，因为器官的复杂性使血运重建是不可能的，并且每一次尝试进行人类移植都会出现故障。这种外科的成就不仅是30年的高潮，即在西奈山山的医学生开始时开始的研究，而是由于西奈山山的合作精神也是可能的。“

The trachea transplant recipient is a 56-year-old female social worker from New York City. She had severe tracheal damage due to repeated intubation after an asthma attack; several failed surgical attempts to reconstruct her trachea led to even further damage. She breathed through a tracheostomy—a surgically created hole in her neck—and was at high risk of suffocation and death because of the progression of her airway disease and likelihood of her trachea collapsing. Being constantly worried she would go to sleep at night and never wake up was her main motivator to undergo the experimental procedure.

气管移植收件人是来自纽约市的56岁女性社会工作者。由于哮喘发作后，由于反复插管，她具有严重的气管损伤;重建她的气管的几次失败的手术试图导致进一步损坏。她穿过一个气管造口术呼吸 - 在她的脖子上的手术创造的洞 - 由于她的气道疾病的进展以及她气管坍塌的可能性，因此令人窒息和死亡的风险很高。不断担心她会在晚上睡觉，永不醒来是她的主要动力经历实验程序。

During the procedure, the Mount Sinai surgical team removed the trachea and the associated blood vessels from the donor. Then, the surgeons reconstructed the trachea in the recipient from the lungs to the larynx and performed a series of microvascular anastomoses, connecting the small blood vessels that nourish the donor trachea with the recipient’s blood vessels. Surgeons used a portion of the esophagus and thyroid gland to help provide blood supply to the trachea, which led to successful revascularization. Ultimately, this procedure allowed the removal of the recipient’s tracheostomy, giving her an opportunity to breathe through her mouth for the first time in six years.

在手术过程中，山山外科手术团队从供体中取出了气管和相关血管。然后，外科医生将气管从肺部从肺部重建到喉部并进行一系列微血管吻合术，将滋养供体气管的小血管连接到接受者的血管。外科医生使用了一部分食道和甲状腺，以帮助为气管​​提供血液供应，导致成功的血运重建。最终，该程序允许删除受体的气管造口术，让她有机会在六年内首次通过她的嘴呼吸。

“Despite extensive research on the vascular supply to the organ using human and animal models, there is no real way to fully prepare for conducting a first-ever in-human transplantation such as this,” Dr. Genden said. “For example, we had no guide for how well the graft would tolerate transplantation, so we worked very quickly. Eighteen hours later, it was clear we had accomplished what many said could not be done. Ultimately, everything went smoothly because we assembled a strong team with extensive surgical expertise in organ transplantation and tracheal reconstruction. Seeing the graft come alive and knowing that the organ was well vascularized was an amazing experience. Knowing that this procedure and 30 years of research will save countless lives was indescribable. It is why we do what we do, to make a difference.”

“尽管使用人类和动物模型对器官的血管供应进行了广泛的研究，但没有真正的方法可以完全准备进行第一次人类移植，例如这一点，”格登登博士说。 “例如，我们没有引导贪污会忍受移植的程度，所以我们的工作很快。十八个小时后，很明显，我们已经完成了许多人无法完成的事情。最终，一切顺利，因为我们在器官移植和气管重建中组装了一个具有广泛的外科专业知识的强大团队。看到移植物活着并知道器官很好的血管化是一种惊人的经历。知道这一程序和30年的研究将节省无数生命是难以形容的。这就是为什么我们做我们所做的事情，有所作为。“

The patient has had no complications or signs of organ rejection and doctors are monitoring her closely to assess her progress and reaction to antirejection therapy. Their observations will inform the development of Mount Sinai’s Tracheal Transplant Program, enabling Dr. Genden to offer this therapeutic approach to patients nationwide and internationally.

患者对器官拒绝的并无并发症或迹象，医生正在密切监测她，以评估她对抗反应治疗的进步和反应。他们的观察将以山上的气管移植计划的发展提供信息，使德文博士能够为全国范围内和国际的患者提供这种治疗方法。

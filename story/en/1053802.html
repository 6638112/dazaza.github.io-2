Climate fight 'is undermined by social media's toxic reports'
气候斗争'由社交媒体的有毒报告造成破坏

2021-03-21 23:21:23

Scientists warn that Nobel summit and long-term decisions to save the planet are at risk from targeted attacks online

科学家们警告说，诺贝尔峰会和拯救地球的长期决定面临着在线目标攻击的风险

Fake news on social media about climate change and biodiversity loss is having a worrying impact in the battle to halt the growing environmental threats to the planet, a group of scientists and analysts have warned.

关于气候变化和生物多样性损失的社交媒体假新闻在战斗中令人担忧地阻止了对该地球的不断增长的环境威胁，一群科学家和分析师都警告道。

In a report published by the Royal Swedish Academy of Science, they say measures needed to create a healthier, more resilient planet – by reducing fossil fuel emissions, overfishing and other threats – will be hard to enforce if they continue to suffer targeted attacks in social media. The international cooperation that is needed to halt global heating and species loss could otherwise be jeopardised, they say.

在瑞典皇家科学院发布的一份报告中，他们表示，通过减少化石燃料排放，过度捕捞和其他威胁，造成更健康，更具弹性的星球 - 如果他们继续在社交中遭受目标攻击，将难以执行的措施媒体。他们说，停止全球加热和物种损失所需的国际合作可能会受到危害。

“Social media reports have created a toxic environment where it’s now very difficult to distinguish facts from fiction,” said one author, Owen Gaffney, of the Stockholm Resilience Centre. “One of the biggest challenges now facing humanity is our inability to tell fact from fiction. This is undermining democracies, which in turn is limiting our ability to make long-term decisions needed to save the planet.”

“社交媒体报道已经创造了一个有毒环境，现在很难区分虚构的事实，”斯德哥尔摩股市中心的一位作者说，欧文加菲尼的一个作者说。 “现在面临的最大挑战之一是我们无法讲述虚构的事实。这是破坏民主国家，反过来限制了我们制定拯救地球所需的长期决定的能力。“

This view was supported by the report’s lead author, Professor Carl Folke, director of Sweden’s Beijer Institute of Ecological Economics. “Improvements are occurring – we are getting a lot of promises from big nations about tackling environmental threats – but the media still causes polarisation of views and that is not helpful. We need to tackle that.”

该观点得到了瑞典驻生生态经济学研究所主任Carl Folke教授Carl Folke教授支持。 “发生了改进 - 我们正在从关于解决环境威胁的大国家获得大量承诺 - 但媒体仍然会导致观点的极化，这并不高兴。我们需要解决这个问题。“

The group’s report is published on Monday as a background paper to the first Nobel Prize Summit, which will be held next month, on the subject “ Our Planet, Our Future”. Originally scheduled to take place in Washington last year, the meeting was postponed because of Covid-19. This time it will be held – from 26 to 28 April – as a virtual event.

本集团的报告于周一发布，作为第一个诺贝尔奖首脑会议的背景文件，将于下个月举行的“我们的星球，我们未来”的主题。最初计划去年在华盛顿举行，由于Covid-19，会议推迟了会议。这次将举行 - 4月26日至28日 - 作为虚拟事件。

Those taking part will include Nobel laureates such as Al Gore, the gene-editing pioneer Jennifer Doudna, and immunologist Peter Doherty, as well as Anthony Fauci, the chief medical adviser to US president Joe Biden, and the Dalai Lama.

其中参加的人将包括诺贝尔·洛杉矶（如戈尔），基因编辑先锋Jennifer Doudna和Immunopolard Peter Doherty，以及美国总统Joe Biden和Dalai Lama的首席医学顾问。

“The aim is to highlight ways to reduce climate change, biodiversity loss and inequalities and suggest how new technologies such as AI and synthetic biology could help save the planet,” added Gaffney, who is also one of the summit’s organisers.

“目的是突出削减气候变化，生物多样性损失和不平等的方法，并提出了艾美和合成生物学等新技术如何帮助拯救地球，”加入Gaffney，他也是峰会的组织者之一。

However, the report makes it clear that this task is a daunting one. As it points out, humanity’s dominion over nature has now reached startling levels. Three hundred years ago, there were 1 billion people on our planet. By the end of this century that figure will approach 10 billion or possibly surpass it. As a result of these dramatic increases, the totality of human beings alive today, plus the livestock that provides us with food, represent 96% of the sum weight of all mammals on Earth. The residual 4% is made of the planet’s remaining wild animals.

但是，报告明确表示这项任务是一个令人生畏的。正如它所指出的那样，人类对大自然的统治者现在已经达到了惊人的水平。三百年前，我们的星球上有10亿人。到本世纪末，这个数字将接近100亿或可能超过它。由于这些戏剧性的增加，今天的人类的总体，加上为提供我们食物的牲畜，占地球上所有哺乳动物的96％。残留的4％是由地球剩余的野生动物制成的。

Today, there is no place on our world that is untouched by homo sapiens, state the report’s authors. Three-quarters of all Earth’s ice-free land has now been directly altered by humans. Every eight days, we build the equivalent of a city the size of New York. We simplify landscapes to ensure they provide maximum economic benefits and in the process erode the biosphere’s resilience. One result is the emergence of new pathogens such as Covid-19.

今天，我们的世界没有任何一个由Homo Sapiens不受欢迎的地方，陈述该报告的作者。所有地球的冰块土地的四分之三已被人类直接改变。每八天，我们建立相当于纽约的城市的大小。我们简化了景观，以确保他们提供最大的经济效益，并在过程中侵蚀了生物圈的弹性。一个结果是新病原体等新病原体，如Covid-19。

The relatively cool years that make up the Holocene epoch, which began 11,700 years ago, have now been replaced by the Anthropocene, an epoch in which humanity is the main driver of ecological events. We are destroying rainforests that absorb carbon dioxide and are driving countless species – from insects to gorillas and chimpanzees, our closest evolutionary cousins – towards extinction. At the same time, global heating – caused by our continued burning of fossil fuels – is triggering unprecedented heatwaves, droughts, storms, floods and wildfires.

在11,000年前，弥补了全新世纪时代的相对凉爽的年度，现在已被人类人类成为生态事件主要驱动因素的人类所取代。我们正在摧毁吸收二氧化碳的热带雨林，并从昆虫到大猩猩和黑猩猩，我们最近的进化表兄弟 - 灭绝。与此同时，全球加热 - 由我们持续燃烧化石燃料 - 正在引发前所未有的散热，干旱，风暴，洪水和野火。

“Climate change impacts are now hitting people harder and sooner than was envisaged only a decade ago,” states the report, Our Future in the Anthropocene Biosphere.

“气候变化的影响现在遭到更加努力，而且只有十年前的人更迟，”我们的未来在人类化学生物圈中的未来。

Given the vast scale of the problem, the report concludes that “modest adjustments” to our current industrial and agricultural practices are now going to be insufficient. “Transformative changes are now necessary,” it concludes.

鉴于巨大的问题，报告的结论是，我们目前的工业和农业实践的“适度调整”现在将是不够的。 “现在是必要的转型变化”，“它结束了。

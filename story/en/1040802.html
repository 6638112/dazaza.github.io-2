Analyzing Minard's Visualization of Napoleon's 1812 March (2014)
分析米纳德对拿破仑1812年3月（2014）的形象化

2020-12-20 05:38:20

In The Visual Display of QuantitativeInformation, Edward Tufte calls Minard’s graphic of Napoleon inRussia one of the “best statisticaldrawings ever created.” But what makes it so good?

在“定量信息的可视显示”中，爱德华·塔夫特（Edward Tufte）将米纳德（Minard）在拿破仑（Napoleon）在俄罗斯的图形称为“有史以来最好的统计图形”之一。但是什么使它如此好呢？

The year is 1812, and Napoleon is doing pretty well for himself. He has most ofEurope under his control, except for the UK. No matter how many times he triedto invade them, he couldn’t break through their defenses. His plan was to placean embargo on them, forcing the other European countries to stop trade with theUK which would weaken them enough so that Napoleon could invade and take overeasily.

那年是1812年，拿破仑为自己做得很好。除英国外，他拥有欧洲大部分地区的控制权。无论他试图入侵他们多少次，他都无法突破他们的防御。他的计划是对它们实行禁运，迫使其他欧洲国家停止与英国的贸易，这将削弱它们的实力，以至于拿破仑可以入侵并轻易采取行动。

Czar Alexander of Russia sees that Napoleon was becoming too powerful, so herefuses to participate in this embargo. Angry at Czar Alexander’s decision,Napoleon gathers a massive army of over 400,000 to attack Russia in June of theyear 1812. While Russia’s troops are not as numerous as France’s, Russia has aplan. Russian troops keep retreating as Napoleon’s troops move forward, burningeverything they pass, ensuring that the French forces could not take anythingfrom their environment. Eventually the French army follows the Russian army allthe way to Moscow during October, suffering major losses from lack of food. Bythe time Napoleon gets to Moscow, he knows he has to retreat. As winter settlesinto Europe and the temperature drops, Napoleon’s troops suffer even morelosses, returning to France from lack of food, disease, and weather conditions.

俄罗斯的沙皇亚历山大（Alexander）认为拿破仑变得太强大了，所以这里充满了参与这项禁运的力量。拿破仑对沙皇亚历山大的决定感到生气，于是在1812年6月召集了一支超过40万人的庞大军队进攻俄罗斯。尽管俄罗斯的军队数量不及法国，但俄罗斯有计划。随着拿破仑军队的前进，俄罗斯军队不断撤退，燃烧所有经过的东西，确保法国军队不会从环境中夺取任何东西。最终，法国军队在十月期间跟随俄罗斯军队一路前往莫斯科，因缺乏食物而蒙受重大损失。拿破仑到莫斯科的时候，他知道自己必须撤退。随着冬季进入欧洲，气温下降，拿破仑的部队遭受了更大的损失，由于缺乏食物，疾病和天气条件而返回法国。

We have the numbers of Napoleon’s troops by location (longitude), organized bygroup and direction. We can plot it on line graphs like so.

我们有按位置（经度）划分的拿破仑部队人数，并按组和方向进行组织。我们可以像这样将其绘制在折线图上。

We also have the path that his troops took to and from Moscow. We can displaythis information by plotting the paths on a map.

我们也有他的部队进出莫斯科的道路。我们可以通过在地图上绘制路径来显示此信息。

We have many dimensions of data that takes several individual graphs torepresent. Minard’s graphic is quite clever because of its ability to combineall of dimensions: loss of life at a time and location, temperature, geography,historical context, into one single graphic. He shows these various detailswithout distracting text or labels as well. For example, he displays the pointswhere Napoleon’s troops divide into subgroups by breaking out the main bar intobranches. He adds thin lines to represent river crossings on the return tripthat further decimated Napoleon’s diminishing troops. And he is able to show thedrastic loss in life from Napoleon’s decision in just a single corner of thediagram.

我们有许多维度的数据，需要使用几个单独的图来表示。 Minard的图形非常聪明，因为它能够将所有维度（一次和一次的生命损失，温度，地理位置，历史背景）整合到一个图形中。他显示了这些各种细节，同时也没有分散文本或标签的注意力。例如，他显示了拿破仑的军队通过将主要的酒吧支队突围而划分成小组的要点。他在回程中添加了细线来代表河流过境点，这进一步削弱了拿破仑的兵力减少。而且他能够在图表的一个角落显示出拿破仑的决定所带来的巨大生命损失。

与显示的内容一样，未显示的内容也同样重要-这是一个发布良好的数据可视化效果的早期示例：

This graphic was created by William Playfair, largely considered to be thefather of information design, in 1786 — about 100 years before Minard’sdiagram was made. Playfair is the inventor of the pie chart, the bar graph, andthe line graph - statistic graphics we use regularly today. This graphic hasgridlines to mark the years and the number of exports. There are five colors,each color representing something different, as well as a number of specificlabels in large text. Compare this to Minard’s graphic: when he draws the map,there are no geographical borders and only very minimal geographical plotting.There are many labels describing cities and number of troops, but they are verysmall. Minard only uses two colors to represent all of the data in the graphic.Additional labels, gridlines, and geographical markings would have made thegraphic too overwhelming for the eye, which makes the impact of the data so muchstronger.

这张图片是由威廉·普莱费尔（William Playfair）于1786年创作的，该书被广泛认为是信息设计之父，比米纳德的图表制作早了100年。 Playfair是饼形图，条形图和折线图的发明者，这些是我们今天经常使用的统计图形。该图形有网格线以标记出口的年份和数量。有五种颜色，每种颜色代表不同的颜色，以及大文本中的许多特定标签。将其与Minard的图形进行比较：当他绘制地图时，没有地理边界，只有非常少的地理图。有许多标签来描述城市和部队人数，但是它们很小。 Minard仅使用两种颜色来表示图形中的所有数据。另外的标签，网格线和地理标记会使图形对眼睛来说太不堪重负，这使得数据的影响力更大。

There are some similarities to designing print data graphics and designingmodern interfaces for mobile and web. When we need to translate numbers intographics to users, we want to focus on communicating lots of information withoutoverwhelming the users with extraneous content, much like Minard with hisvisualization of Napoleon’s March. Both designers created effective graphics toturn numbers into a narrative, but Minard was able to tell a much more detailedstory with his design techniques.

设计移动电话和Web的打印数据图形和设计现代界面有一些相似之处。当我们需要将数字转换成图形给用户时，我们希望专注于交流大量信息，而又不会给用户带来过多的内容，就像Minard对拿破仑三月的可视化一样。两位设计师都创建了有效的图形以将数字转换为叙述，但是Minard能够用他的设计技巧讲述出更详细的故事。

The Laguna Garzón Bridge is a bridge famous for its unusual circular shape. It is located in Garzón, Uruguay, and was designed by renowned Uruguayan architect Rafael Viñoly. [2] [1] It is designed in a circular shape to force drivers to slow down and to allow for pedestrian access along the one-way circular route, including crosswalks that allow pedestrian access to either the inner or outer sidewalks of the circle. [3]

LagunaGarzón桥是一座以其异常圆形而闻名的桥梁。它位于乌拉圭Garzón，由着名的乌拉圭建筑师RafaelViñoly设计。 [2] [1]它以圆形设计，以迫使驱动器慢下来并允许沿单向圆形路线的行人进入，包括允许行人进入圆的内侧或外侧人行道的人行横道。 [3]

The bridge gives access across Laguna Garzón, a coastal lagoon previously served by a small 2-vehicle ferry that required daylight and good weather to operate. It was opened to vehicular traffic in December 2015, providing a crossing for pedestrians and up to approximately 1,000 vehicles a day. [4]

这座桥可以遍布LagunaGarzón，这是一座以前由一个沿海泻湖的沿海泻湖，由小型2车辆渡轮服务，需要日光和良好的天气运行。它于2015年12月开放了车辆交通，为行人提供了交叉行人，每天最多可达1000辆车。 [4]

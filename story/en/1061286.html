Reddit aims to double in size as social news site invests for growth
Reddit旨在为社会新闻网站投资增长的大小增加

2021-05-07 21:12:52

After punching below its weight for years, firm is building new tech and experimenting with charging users

在低于其重量之后多年来，公司正在建立新的技术并尝试充电用户

B y any objective measure, Reddit is an internet giant. The social news site is one of the top 20 sites in the world, with more users than Twitter. They move markets, break news and source most of the viral images that float across your Facebook page a month later. Reddit’s co-founder Alexis Ohanian is tech royalty, not to mention Mr Serena Williams.

B Y任何客观措施，Reddit都是互联网巨头。社交新闻网站是世界上20位的网站之一，用户更多的用户比推特。他们移动市场，休息新闻和源大多数病毒图像，其中大部分病毒图像在一个月后漂浮在你的Facebook上。 Reddit的联合创始人Alexis Ohanian是Tech Royaly，更不用说Serena Williams先生。

But for years, the site has punched below its weight. As a business, its valuation is a fraction of its peers, and so too is its income; as a cultural phenomenon, it’s sidelined as a geeky pursuit. But now the company has a plan to turn that around, says Jen Wong, Reddit’s chief operating officer.

但多年来，该网站在其重量之下被打了出来。作为一家企业，其估值是其同龄人的一小部分，其收入也是如此;作为一种文化现象，它是令人讨厌的追求。但现在公司有计划转向周围的计划，肯德德·雷德特的首席运营官说。

Wong, the former head of digital at the US media giant Time Inc, joined Reddit in 2018. “I think in that span we’ve also had nine different lives,” she says now. “We’re investing a lot. We raised money, and it’s great to be in investment mode. We’re growing globally, we’re building new experiences like video, audio, a virtual economy. We’re hiring a lot of people – I mean, we’re gonna be double our size by the end of the year, like 1,400 people.”

美国媒体巨头Time Inc的前数字公司前任数字公司在2018年加入Reddit。“我认为我们也有九个不同的生活，”她现在说。 “我们投资了很多。我们筹集资金，并且在投资模式中很棒。我们正在全球范围内增长，我们正在建立视频，音频，虚拟经济等新体验。我们雇用了很多人 - 我的意思是，我们将在今年年底前一倍，比如1,400人。“

After years with a mostly static design, Reddit has started to build out new tech at an impressive rate. During the pandemic, the company has trialled live video and audio offerings, joining the vast array of social networks to clone the panel discussion app Clubhouse, and hosting livestreams on site for communities, big and small, who want to replicate their offline meet-ups on the internet.

经过多年来的静态设计，Reddit已经开始以令人印象深刻的速度建造新技术。在大流行期间，该公司已试验了实时视频和音频产品，加入了大量的社交网络来克隆小组讨论应用程序俱乐部，并在现场举办Livestream，为社区，大小，他们想要复制他们的离线举措在互联网上。

It has also begun experimenting with a daring new business model for a social site: charging users. For years, the company has let users donate to the site in exchange for an ad-free experience called Reddit Gold, and users have also been able to gift Gold status to each other, as a reward for creating good content, telling a funny joke, or even just as a random act of kindness. Under Wong, that approach has been turbocharged: users can now give and receive a dizzying array of awards, all bought using real money, as well as sign up for Reddit Gold – now called Premium – for a $50 (£36) annual fee.

它还开始尝试对社交网站的大胆新的商业模式：充电用户。多年来，公司让用户捐赠给网站以换取一个叫做Reddit Gold的广告经验，用户也能够互相赠送黄金状态，作为创造良好内容的奖励，讲述一个有趣的笑话，甚至作为善意的随意行为。在黄下，这种方法已经涡轮增压：用户现在可以给予并获得令人耳目敬的奖项，所有人都购买了真正的钱，以及Reddit Gold - 现在称溢价 - 为期50美元（36英镑）的年费。

Advertising is still “the vast majority” of Reddit’s revenue, but since the company began seriously focusing on the Premium business last year, it has doubled its take. “I think it has a lot of potential,” Wong says. “What’s exciting about it is, you know, you look at the east and the behaviours in Asian apps like WeChat etc: gamification, customisation, expression, being able to pay back and forth with coins and avatars, is just a part of the experience. We have always had that behaviour on Reddit, but we hadn’t put, you know, teams behind it to make it even more fun and diverse.”

广告仍然是“绝大多数”的雷德特的收入，但由于该公司于去年开始认真地关注高级业务，因此它的占据方式翻了一番。 “我认为它有很多潜力，”王说。 “这是什么令人兴奋的是，你知道，你看看亚洲应用中的东方和行为等：游戏化，定制，表达，能够以硬币和头像来回支付，只是体验的一部分。我们一直在reddit上有这种行为，但我们没有把，你知道，它背后的团队让它更有趣和多样化。“

As well as overhauling the user experience, Reddit has also tried to deal with another troubling relic of its past: a sometimes toxic community which was allowed to thrive under the site’s permissive rules. Reddit runs on the tireless labour of volunteer moderators, who set up and shepherd communities that they run as they see fit. For the most part, the system works: each individual community can set its own rules, from r/AskHistorians (where all replies to questions are expected to be long, serious, and well-cited) to r/eeeeeeeeeee (where only the letter “e” can be posted).

除了对用户体验的彻彻过修，Reddit还试图处理其过去的另一个令人窒息的遗迹：有时有毒的群体被允许在网站的允许规则下茁壮成长。 Reddit在志愿者主持人的不知疲倦的劳动力上运行，他建立了他们所看到的牧羊犬，他们认为合适。在大多数情况下，系统工作：每个个人社区都可以从R / Askhistorians（预计对问题的所有回复都要长期，严重，并被引用）到R / EEEEEEEEE（只有这封信“e”可以发布）。

But the system breaks down when the rules that one community wants to set for itself are toxic. That, Reddit admitted in 2020, is what happened to r/The_Donald, the subreddit for fans of the former US president. “All communities on Reddit must abide by our content policy in good faith,” the founder and chief executive, Steve Huffman, said at the time. “We banned r/The_Donald because it has not done so, despite every opportunity.” A host of other communities were banned alongside it, including one for the leftwing podcast, Chapo Trap House.

但是当一个社区想要为自己设定的规则有毒时，系统会崩溃。那个，Reddit于2020年录取，是r / the_donald，前美国总统粉丝的勒德德德德·德国·德国·德国的粉丝发生了什么。 “雷德德的所有社区都必须恪守我们的内容政策，真诚，”创始人和行政长官史蒂夫·霍夫曼当时表示。 “我们禁止r / the_donald，因为它仍未这样做，尽管每一个机会。”一系列其他社区被禁止，包括一个用于左翼播客，Chapo Trap House。

Wong said: “What we’ve done over the last five years is first we invested in teams and tech to enforce our policies, like you saw in summer 2020. But the thing that really is distinctive about Reddit, is this idea of the layers of moderation. We have base layers of policy that we enforce, but then we have this really unique layer of moderation, where each community gets to write their own rules that sit on top of our policies but they enforce those rules.

王说：“我们在过去五年中做了什么，首先是我们投资于团队和技术的，就像你在2020年夏天看到的那样。但是真正对Reddit有鲜明的事情是这个层的想法适度。我们有基本层的政策，我们强制执行，但我们拥有这一真正独特的审核层，每个社区都会撰写自己的规则，坐在我们的政策之上，但他们执行这些规则。

“Our job, then, is to support our moderators and communities. And what’s amazing about that is it is a scalable model. Like, I truly don’t know how you scale anything else. There’s still more work to be done, but at the heart of it, I think that what you see is working.”

“我们的工作是，是支持我们的主持人和社区。什么是令人惊讶的是它是一个可扩展的模型。喜欢，我真的不知道你是如何缩放其他任何东西的。还有更多的工作要做，但在它的核心，我认为你所看到的是工作。“

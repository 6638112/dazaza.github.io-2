Men seen as financial providers in U.S., even as women’s contributions grow
即使妇女的贡献增长，男子被视为美国金融提供者

2021-04-24 04:41:56

In about a third of married or cohabiting couples in the United States, women bring in half or more of the earnings, a significant increase from the past.  But in most couples, men contribute more of the income, and this aligns with the fact that Americans place a higher value on a man’s role as financial provider.

在美国约有三分之一的已婚或同居夫妇中，妇女在过去的一半或更多的盈利中，与过去的重大增加。但在大多数夫妻中，男人贡献了更多的收入，这与美国人对一个人作为金融提供者的角色占据了更高价值的事实保持着。

Roughly seven-in-ten adults (71%) say it is very important for a man to be able to support a family financially to be a good husband or partner. By comparison, 32% say it’s very important for a woman to do the same to be a good wife or partner, according to a new Pew Research Center survey.

大约七十十岁的成年人（71％）表示，一个人能够支持一个家庭提供财政，成为一个好丈夫或伴侣是非常重要的。根据新的PEW研究中心调查，32％的人表示，对于一个女人做同样的妻子或合作伙伴来说，这对女性来说非常重要。

Men are especially likely to place a greater emphasis on their role as financial providers. While a nearly equal share of men and women say a man needs to be able to provide for his family to be a good husband or partner (72% and 71%, respectively), men are less likely than women to say the same about women. Just a quarter of men say this is very important for a woman to be a good wife or partner, compared with 39% of women.

男人特别可能会更加强调他们作为金融提供者的角色。虽然男人和女性的几乎平等份额表示，一个人需要能够为他的家人提供一个好丈夫或合作伙伴（分别为72％和71％），男性不太可能比女性对女性说同样的话。只有四分之一的男人说这对一个女人成为一个好妻子或合作伙伴来说非常重要，而39％的女性。

However, the importance of being the financial provider ranks behind being caring and compassionate when it comes to being a good spouse or partner, in the public’s estimation. Overwhelming majorities say it is very important for men (86%) and women (90%) to have these qualities to be good spouses or partners.

然而，当在公众估计中，成为金融提供者的重要性在成为一个好的配偶或伴侣时，落后于关心和富有同情心。绝大多数人都说这对男性（86％）和女性（90％）非常重要，使这些品质成为良好的配偶或合作伙伴。

The nationally representative survey of 4,971 adults was conducted Aug. 8-21, 2017, using Pew Research Center’s American Trends Panel.

2017年8月8日至21日，2017年8月8日至21日的国家代表调查，采用了PEW研究中心的美国趋势小组。

As women in the U.S. have increased their labor force participation and earning power, their contributions to household incomes have grown. These trends, along with the fact that women with higher levels of education and income are more likely to marry, have boosted the economic status of married households. Today, married adults are much more likely to live in upper-income households than are non-married adults.

随着美国妇女的劳动力参与和盈利权，他们对家庭收入的贡献已经增长。这些趋势以及具有更高水平教育和收入的女性更有可能结婚的事实，提高了已婚家庭的经济地位。今天，已婚成年人更有可能生活在上收入家庭而不是未婚成年人。

At the same time, income dynamics among couples have shifted. In 1980, only 13% of married women earned more than or about as much as their husbands. By 2000, the share had risen to 25%. Today, 31% of women who are married or cohabiting are contributing at least half of the couple’s total earnings (including 28% who earn more than their husband or partner and 3% who earn about the same amount). In 69% of married or cohabiting couples, the man earns more than the woman, though this is down from 87% of married couples in 1980.

与此同时，夫妻之间的收入动态转移了。 1980年，只有13％的已婚妇女赢得了超过或大约与丈夫一样多。到2000年，该股已上涨至25％。今天，31％的已婚或同居的妇女在夫妻的总收入中贡献了至少一半的盈利（包括28％的人，赢得超过丈夫或合作伙伴，3％赚取相同金额的3％）。在69％的已婚或同居夫妇中，该男子比女性赚得更多，虽然这是1980年的87％的已婚夫妇。

The relative financial contributions of men and women differ significantly by the educational attainment of each partner. In about half (49%) of couples in which the husband and wife are both at least 25 years old and the woman has more education than her male partner, she also earns at least as much as he does. In 29% of couples where both people have the same level of education, the woman earns the same as or more than the man. That share falls to 20% in couples where the man has more education than his wife or partner.

男女的相对财务贡献因每个合作伙伴的教育程度而有显着差异。大约一半（49％）夫妻俩至少25岁，女性拥有比她的男性伴侣更多的教育，她也至少尽可能多地获得。在29％的夫妻中，两个人都有相同程度的教育，那个女人比男人赚得和更多。这一份额达到了20％的夫妻，其中一个人比他的妻子或伴侣更多的教育。

Adults with lower incomes and less education are more likely to place a high value on a spouse or partner’s ability to provide for a family – whether that spouse is a man or a woman. Roughly eight-in-ten adults ages 25 and older (81%) with no education beyond high school say that, for a man to be a good husband or partner, being able to support a family financially is very important. Among those with some college experience 72% say this, and the share is smaller still among those with a four-year college degree (62%).

收入较低和较少教育的成年人更有可能对配偶或合作伙伴提供一个家庭的能力来对待高价值 - 无论是配偶是男人还是女人。大约八十十岁的成年人25岁及以上（81％）没有高中教育，因为一个人成为一个好丈夫或合作伙伴，能够支持一个家庭财务非常重要。其中一些大学经验的人在72％的人中表示，这一份额较小，仍然较小，大学学位（62％）。

The pattern is similar when it comes to a woman being a good wife or partner. Four-in-ten high school graduates say being able to financially support a family is very important, compared with 29% of those with some college and 25% of those with a bachelor’s degree or higher.

当谈到一个女人是一个好妻子或伴侣的女人时，这种模式类似。四个高中毕业生表示，能够在经济上支持一个家庭非常重要，而其中29％的学士学位和学士学位或更高学历的25％。

Adults in lower-income families are more likely than those in higher-income families to say being able to financially support a family is very important for making a man or woman a good spouse or partner. For example, 41% of adults with family incomes of less than $30,000 say this is a very important attribute for a woman to have, compared with 23% of those with incomes of $75,000 or more.

低收入家庭的成年人比那些更高收入家庭的成年人说能够在经济上支持一个家庭，这对制作一个男人或女人是一个好的配偶或伴侣非常重要。例如，41％的家庭收入的成年人低于30,000美元，这对一个女性来说是一个非常重要的属性，而一个非常重要的属性，而其中23％的收入为75,000美元或以上。

Blacks and Hispanics are more likely than whites to place a high level of importance on being able to financially support a family when it comes to being a good spouse or partner: 84% of blacks say this is very important for a man, as do 78% of Hispanics. By comparison, 67% of whites say the same. And while 52% of blacks and 40% of Hispanics say it is very important for a woman to be able to provide for a family, only 27% of whites agree.

黑人和西班牙裔人比白人更有可能在能够在成为一个好的配偶或合作伙伴时向一个家庭提供高度重要性：84％的黑人说这对一个男人来说非常重要，如78占西班牙裔的百分比。相比之下，67％的白人表示相同。而52％的黑人和40％的西班牙裔人表示，对于一个女人能够提供一个家庭来说非常重要，只有27％的白人同意。

There are age differences as well. Adults ages 65 and older, for instance, are more likely than younger age groups to say that a man should be able to provide financial support for his family. But the different expectations for men and women persist across age groups. Among adults ages 18 to 29, for example, 64% say it is very important for men to be able provide for their family, while 34% say the same about women.

有年龄差异。例如，成年人65岁及以上的年龄比较可能比较年轻的年龄组，说一个人应该能够为他的家人提供财政支持。但男性和女性的不同期望仍然存在于年龄组。例如，在18至29岁的成年人中，64％的人表示，对于人们能够为他们的家人提供，这对女性来说非常重要。

Views also differ across demographic groups when it comes to other attributes of spouses or partners. For instance, similar shares of men and women say it is very important for men to contribute to household chores to be good spouses or partners (57% vs. 58%). But men and women have somewhat different views about the importance of this attribute in women. Seven-in-ten women say contributing to household chores is very important for women, while 56% of men agree.

当涉及配偶或合作伙伴的其他属性时，观点也有所不同。例如，男女的类似股份表示，对于男性来说，为家庭家务做出良好的配偶或合作伙伴（57％vs.58％）是​​非常重要的。但男人和女性对妇女在妇女中的重要性有所不同。七十十名女性对家务造成贡献对妇女来说非常重要，而56％的男人同意。

Younger adults view contributing to chores around the house as about equally important for men and for women, but older adults are more likely to see this as an important attribute for women than for men. Among adults ages 65 and older, 65% say it is very important for a woman to contribute to household chores to be a good spouse or partner. By comparison, 52% of adults in this age group say this is very important for a man to be a good spouse or partner.

较年轻的成年人视图为家庭周围的家务提供贡献，因为对男性和女性同样重要，但老年人更有可能将其视为女性的重要属性而不是男性。在65岁及以上的成年人中，65％的人表示，一个女人为家务做出贡献是一个好的配偶或伴侣是非常重要的。相比之下，这个年龄组52％的成年人说这对一个男人成为一个好的配偶或伴侣非常重要。

As far as educational attainment, men and women overall tend to agree on the importance of a man or a woman being well educated in order to make a good spouse or partner. But college graduates are more likely than those with lower levels of education to say this is very important. Among adults ages 25 and older with a bachelor’s degree or higher, 40% say being well educated is very important for a woman to be a good wife or partner, compared with 30% of those with less education. When it comes to the importance of a man being well educated, 44% of those with a bachelor’s degree or higher say this is very important, compared with 32% of those with less education.

就教育程度而言，整体而言，男女倾向于达成一致的人或一个女人受过良好教育的重要性，以便制作好配偶或伴侣。但大学毕业生比那些较低的教育水平较低的人说这是非常重要的。在25岁及以上的成年人中，学士学位或更高年龄较大，40％的人表示受过良好教育对女性成为一个好妻子或合作伙伴的教育非常重要，而30％的受教育者则为30％。谈到一个受过良好教育的人的重要性，44％具有学士学位或更高的人表示这是非常重要的，而32％的受教育较少的人。

Blacks and Hispanics are also more likely than whites to emphasize the importance of being well educated in order for a man or a woman to be a good spouse or partner. About half of blacks (50%) and Hispanics (49%) say that this is very important for a woman, compared with 28% of whites. Similarly, 49% of Hispanics and 45% of blacks say that being well educated is very important for a man, compared with 32% of whites.

黑人和西班牙裔人比白人更有可能强调受过良好教育的重要性，以便成为一个男人或一个女人成为一个好的配偶或伴侣。大约一半的黑人（50％）和西班牙裔（49％）表示这对一个女人来说非常重要，而28％的白人。同样，49％的西班牙裔和45％的黑人表示，受过良好教育对一个人来说非常重要，而32％的白人。

Renee Stepler  is a former research analyst who focused on social and demographic trends research at Pew Research Center.

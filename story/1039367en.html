Understanding MRNA Covid-19 Vaccines
了解MRNA Covid-19疫苗

2020-12-13 07:29:35

Within the next month, messenger RNA vaccines—also called mRNA vaccines—are likely to be some of the first COVID-19 vaccines authorized for use in the United States.

在下个月之内，信使RNA疫苗（也称为mRNA疫苗）可能会成为获准在美国使用的首批COVID-19疫苗。

mRNA vaccines are a new type of vaccine to protect against infectious diseases. To trigger an immune response, many vaccines put a weakened or inactivated germ into our bodies. Not mRNA vaccines. Instead, they teach our cells how to make a protein—or even just a piece of a protein—that triggers an immune response inside our bodies. That immune response, which produces antibodies, is what protects us from getting infected if the real virus enters our bodies.

mRNA疫苗是一种新型的预防传染病的疫苗。为了触发免疫反应，许多疫苗将弱化或灭活的细菌放入我们的体内。不是mRNA疫苗。相反，他们教我们的细胞如何制造一种蛋白质，甚至只是一种蛋白质，从而触发我们体内的免疫反应。如果真正的病毒进入我们的身体，这种产生抗体的免疫反应可以保护我们免受感染。

COVID-19 mRNA vaccines give instructions for our cells to make a harmless piece of what is called the “spike protein.” The spike protein is found on the surface of the virus that causes COVID-19.

COVID-19 mRNA疫苗为我们的细胞提供了指导，使其能够制造出无害的所谓的“峰值蛋白”。在导致COVID-19的病毒表面上发现了刺突蛋白。

COVID-19 mRNA vaccines are given in the upper arm muscle. Once the instructions (mRNA) are inside the muscle cells, the cells use them to make the protein piece. After the protein piece is made, the cell breaks down the instructions and gets rid of them.

COVID-19 mRNA疫苗在上臂肌肉中给予。一旦指令（mRNA）位于肌肉细胞内部，细胞就会利用它们来制造蛋白质块。蛋白质块制成后，细胞会分解指令并摆脱它们。

Next, the cell displays the protein piece on its surface. Our immune systems recognize that the protein doesn’t belong there and begin building an immune response and making antibodies, like what happens in natural infection against COVID-19.

接下来，细胞在其表面展示蛋白质块。我们的免疫系统认识到该蛋白质不属于该蛋白质，并开始建立免疫反应并产生抗体，就像针对COVID-19的自然感染中发生的情况一样。

At the end of the process, our bodies have learned how to protect against future infection. The benefit of mRNA vaccines, like all vaccines, is those vaccinated gain this protection without ever having to risk the serious consequences of getting sick with COVID-19.

在此过程的最后，我们的身体已经学会了如何预防将来的感染。像所有疫苗一样，mRNA疫苗的好处是，那些接种疫苗的人可以获得这种保护，而不必冒险患COVID-19会导致严重的后果。

mRNA never enters the nucleus of the cell, which is where our DNA (genetic material) is kept.

mRNA永远不会进入细胞核，而细胞核就是我们的DNA（遗传物质）被保存的地方。

The cell breaks down and gets rid of the mRNA soon after it is finished using the instructions.

使用说明完成后，细胞会分解并摆脱mRNA。

mRNA vaccines are being held to the same rigorous safety and effectiveness standards external icon as all other types of vaccines in the United States. The only COVID-19 vaccines the Food and Drug Administration (FDA) will make available for use in the United States (by approval or emergency use authorization) are those that meet these standards.

mRNA疫苗与美国所有其他类型的疫苗一样，都遵循相同的严格安全性和有效性标准。美国食品药品监督管理局（FDA）将通过批准或紧急使用授权在美国使用的唯一COVID-19疫苗是符合这些标准的疫苗。

There are currently no licensed mRNA vaccines in the United States. However, researchers have been studying and working with them for decades. Interest has grown in these vaccines because they can be developed in a laboratory using readily available materials. This means the process can be standardized and scaled up, making vaccine development faster than traditional methods of making vaccines.

美国目前没有许可的mRNA疫苗。但是，研究人员已经研究和合作了数十年。人们对这些疫苗的兴趣日益浓厚，因为它们可以在实验室中使用容易获得的材料进行开发。这意味着该过程可以标准化和扩大规模，从而使疫苗开发比传统的疫苗制备方法更快。

mRNA vaccines have been studied before for flu, Zika, rabies, and cytomegalovirus (CMV). As soon as the necessary information about the virus that causes COVID-19 was available, scientists began designing the mRNA instructions for cells to build the unique spike protein into an mRNA vaccine.

以前已经针对流感，寨卡病毒，狂犬病和巨细胞病毒（CMV）研究了mRNA疫苗。一旦获得了有关引起COVID-19的病毒的必要信息，科学家便开始设计mRNA指令，使细胞将独特的刺突蛋白构建成mRNA疫苗。

Future mRNA vaccine technology may allow for one vaccine to provide protection for multiple diseases, thus decreasing the number of shots needed for protection against common vaccine-preventable diseases.

未来的mRNA疫苗技术可能允许一种疫苗为多种疾病提供保护，从而减少针对普通疫苗可预防的疾病进行保护所需的注射次数。

Beyond vaccines, cancer research has used mRNA to trigger the immune system to target specific cancer cells.

除疫苗外，癌症研究还使用mRNA来触发针对特定癌细胞的免疫系统。

Google embroiled in row over AI bias research
谷歌因人工智能偏见研究而陷入困境

2020-12-05 00:10:58

Google struggled on Thursday to limit the fallout from the departure of a top artificial intelligence researcher after the Internet group blocked the publication of a paper on an important AI ethics issue.

在互联网组织阻止发表有关重要的人工智能伦理问题的论文后，谷歌周四努力限制高级人工智能研究人员离职的影响。

Timnit Gebru, who had been co-head of AI ethics at Google, said on Twitter that she had been fired after the paper was rejected.

谷歌AI道德共同负责人蒂姆尼特·格布鲁（Timnit Gebru）在Twitter上表示，在论文被拒绝后，她被解雇了。

Jeff Dean, Google’s head of AI, defended the decision in an internal email to staff on Thursday, saying the paper “didn’t meet our bar for publication.” He also described Ms. Gebru’s departure as a resignation in response to Google’s refusal to concede to unspecified conditions she had set to stay at the company.

Google的AI负责人Jeff Dean在周四给员工的内部电子邮件中为这一决定辩护，称该论文“没有达到我们的出版标准。”他还称格布鲁女士的离职是辞职，是对Google拒绝承认她原本打算留在公司的未指定条件的回应。

The dispute has threatened to shine a harsh light on Google’s handling of internal AI research that could hurt its business, as well as the company’s long-running difficulties in trying to diversify its workforce.

这场争端威胁着对谷歌处理内部AI研究的严厉批评，这可能会损害其业务，以及该公司在努力实现员工多元化方面长期存在的困难。

Before she left, Ms. Gebru complained in an email to fellow workers that there was “zero accountability” inside Google around the company’s claims it wants to increase the proportion of women in its ranks. The email, first published on Platformer, also described the decision to block her paper as part of a process of “silencing marginalised voices.”

在离开之前，格布鲁女士在一封致同工的电子邮件中抱怨称，谷歌内部声称公司希望增加女性在女性队伍中的比例，而谷歌内部对此实行“零责任”。该电子邮件最初在Platformer上发布，还描述了将她的论文屏蔽的决定，这是“使边缘化的声音保持沉默”过程的一部分。

One person who worked closely with Ms. Gebru said that there had been tensions with Google management in the past over her activism in pushing for greater diversity. But the immediate cause of her departure was the company’s decision not to allow the publication of a research paper she had coauthored, this person added.

一位与格布鲁女士紧密合作的人说，过去谷歌管理层一直在为推动更大的多元化而采取积极行动，因此一直存在紧张关系。这位人士补充说，但她离职的直接原因是公司决定不允许发表她与他人合着的研究论文。

The paper looked at the potential bias in large-scale language models, one of the hottest new fields of natural language research. Systems like OpenAI’s GPT-3 and Google’s own system, Bert, attempt to predict the next word in any phrase or sentence—a method that has been used to produce surprisingly effective automated writing, and which Google uses to better understand complex search queries.

本文研究了自然语言研究最热门的新领域之一-大规模语言模型中的潜在偏差。诸如OpenAI的GPT-3和Google自己的系统Bert之类的系统试图预测任何短语或句子中的下一个单词-一种已被用来产生令人惊讶的有效自动书写的方法，而Google则被用来更好地理解复杂的搜索查询。

The language models are trained on vast amounts of text, usually drawn from the Internet, which has raised warnings that they could regurgitate racial and other biases that are contained in the underlying training material.

语言模型接受了大量的文本训练，这些文本通常是从Internet上提取的，这已经发出警告，警告他们可能会反省基础培训材料中包含的种族和其他偏见。

“From the outside, it looks like someone at Google decided this was harmful to their interests,” said Emily Bender, a professor of computational linguistics at the University of Washington, who co-authored the paper.

华盛顿大学计算语言学教授艾米莉·班德（Emily Bender）说：“从外部看，谷歌的某个人认为这有害于他们的利益。”

“Academic freedom is very important—there are risks when [research] is taking place in places that [doesn’t] have that academic freedom,” giving companies or governments the power to “shut down” research they don't approve of, she added.

“学术自由非常重要，[研究]在[没有]学术自由的地方进行时存在风险，”赋予公司或政府“关闭”他们不赞成的研究的权力她补充说。

Ms. Bender said the authors hoped to update the paper with newer research in time for it to be accepted at the conference to which it had already been submitted. But she added that it was common for such work to be superseded by newer research, given how quickly work in fields like this is progressing. “In the research literature, no paper is perfect.”

班德女士说，作者希望及时更新论文，以便在已经提交的会议上予以接受。但她补充说，鉴于此类领域的工作进展迅速，通常会被较新的研究取代。 “在研究文献中，没有论文是完美的。”

Julien Cornebise, a former AI researcher at DeepMind, the London-based AI group owned by Google’s parent, Alphabet, said that the dispute “shows the risks of having AI and machine learning research concentrated in the few hands of powerful industry actors, since it allows censorship of the field by deciding what gets published or not.”

谷歌的母公司Alphabet旗下的伦敦AI集团DeepMind的前AI研究人员Julien Cornebise表示，这场争端“显示出AI和机器学习研究集中在强大的行业参与者手中的风险。通过确定发布什么内容或不发布什么，从而允许对该领域进行审查。”

He added that Ms. Gebru was “extremely talented—we need researchers of her calibre, no filters, on these issues.” Ms. Gebru did not immediately respond to requests for comment.

他补充说，格布鲁女士“非常有才华-我们需要在这些问题上研究她的能力，没有过滤器。”格布鲁女士没有立即回应置评请求。

Mr. Dean said that the paper, written with three other Google researchers, as well as external collaborators, “didn’t take into account recent research to mitigate” the risk of bias. He added that the paper “talked about the environmental impact of large models, but disregarded subsequent research showing much greater efficiencies.”

迪恩先生说，与其他三名Google研究人员以及外部合作者共同撰写的论文“并未考虑到最近的研究来减轻”偏见的风险。他补充说，该论文“谈到了大型模型对环境的影响，但无视随后的研究显示出更高的效率”。

Quitting Twitter
退出Twitter

2021-02-26 04:24:35

I’ve only used Twitter for a few months and I did like a lot of it. The best part by far was following and listening to some incredible people. Patric McKenzie is one among many, but if you look at what he says on a daily basis, you’ll know what I mean. I can be endlessly entertained and learn a lot from what folks like him say.

我只使用Twitter几个月，我确实很喜欢它。到目前为止，最好的部分是跟随并听取了一些令人难以置信的人的声音。帕特里克·麦肯齐（Patric McKenzie）是其中之一，但是如果您每天看一下他所说的话，您就会明白我的意思。我可以无休止地娱乐，并从像他这样的人说的东西中学到很多东西。

There’s also the feeling of being the first one to know things. I often came across news or memes well before everyone else who wasn’t on twitter. Jan 6th, 2021 was a good example. I spent the whole day refreshing twitter, horrified by what I saw. It didn’t make a difference whether I learned of what was happening in 5 minutes or 2 hours or 2 days, but on that day I needed to be in real time.

也有成为第一个了解事物的人的感觉。我经常在其他所有不在Twitter上的人之前都看到新闻或模因。 2021年1月6日就是一个很好的例子。我整天都在刷新Twitter，这让我所看到的感到震惊。我是否知道5分钟，2小时或2天后发生的事情都没有关系，但是那天我需要实时。

As for tweeting myself, I did it a fair bit. Before twitter, I’d have this habit of wanting to share whatever interesting tidbit I found with at least one person. Something funny? To the college/school group chat it goes. Something to do with software? To my former flatmates. Something cute? To my wife. Whatever I see, I know at least one friend who’d like it. I felt happy showing it to at least one other person. But I also felt a bit guilty sending them to people on 1:1 threads. Was I being too spammy? Were my ‘jokes’ not that funny? That’s where Twitter helped. I could broadcast without worrying that I was bothering people.

至于发推文，我做了一点。在推特之前，我有一种习惯，就是想与至少一个人分享我发现的任何有趣的花絮。什么好笑的？去大学/学校群聊吧。与软件有关？对我以前的室友。可爱的东西吗？给我老婆无论我看到什么，我都知道至少有一个喜欢的朋友。我很高兴向至少另一个人展示它。但是我也感到有些内sending，以1：1线程发送给人们。我太垃圾了吗？我的“笑话”不是那么有趣吗？这就是Twitter的帮助。我可以广播而不必担心自己会打扰到别人。

I also liked sending encouragement and appreciation to small time authors and content creators. These folks have it tough, and a kind word can always help. Twitter reduces the friction to doing this to the point where there’s no reason you shouldn’t.

我也喜欢向小时间作者和内容创作者发送鼓励和赞赏。这些人很难过，善良的话总是可以帮助的。 Twitter减少了这样做的麻烦，使您毫无理由地不应该这样做。

It’s a pretty good website, as tens of millions of people around the world can attest. I would mostly agree with them. But it wasn’t for me.

这是一个非常不错的网站，全世界有成千上万的人可以证明。我大都同意他们的看法。但这不是给我的。

It feels stupid to complain about something as trivial as a lack of followers. There are ways to get around this, chiefly by putting out interesting content. I never used hashtags much, but that’s supposedly a great way too. But I never saw much engagement with what I was saying, which meant using the website felt a bit solitary. What was the point of saying things if I was the only one reading it?

抱怨缺少追随者这样琐碎的事情真是愚蠢。有一些方法可以解决此问题，主要是通过发布有趣的内容。我从未使用过井号标签，但是据说这也是一种很好的方法。但是我从没见过我所说的话，这意味着使用该网站感觉有点孤独。如果我是唯一一个读东西的人，说话的意义何在？

It’s possible that I’m just a boring knob, and that’s why no one reacted to my stuff. But that’s not been my experience elsewhere. When I use reddit or Hacker News, I find that it’s trivial to get thousands of karma and a handful of awards. Pseudo-anonymity helps too - instead of a weird Indian name I just get a nickname that doesn’t mean anything. It’s possible to get positive engagement and even just replies from people regardless of who you are. You don’t need a reputation or a following, if you said something worth reading, people will read it.

我可能只是个无聊的人，这就是为什么没人对我的东西做出反应的原因。但这不是我在其他地方的经历。当我使用reddit或Hacker News时，发现获得数千业力和少量奖励是微不足道的。伪匿名也有帮助-我不是一个奇怪的印度名字，而是一个昵称，它什么都没有。无论您是谁，都有可能获得积极的参与，甚至只是别人的回覆。您不需要声誉或追随者，只要您说了一些值得一读的内容，人们就会阅读它。

I’ll be honest - I felt jealous that people could just say “hey followers, here’s a question I could have googled. Could you google it for me?” and get a response. Or “I’m looking for a job. Any leads?” I didn’t have that. And I didn’t have a way of reaching that quickly.

老实说-我很嫉妒人们只能说“嗨，追随者，这是我可以谷歌搜索的问题。您能帮我用谷歌搜索吗？”并得到回应。或“我正在寻找工作。有线索吗？”我没有那个而且我没有办法很快达到目标。

I think Tiktok gets it really right here. No matter who the creator is, if the content is good enough it will get hundreds of thousands of views.

我认为Tiktok确实可以在这里使用它。无论创作者是谁，如果内容足够好，它都会获得成千上万的观看次数。

I followed a lot of interesting people, but these folks were also upset with many things, mainly the state of the world. Watching people snipe at each other constantly, taking their anger out on anything in sight … was understandable. You can’t tell them to react differently or to chill out, because these are reasonable responses to what they’re seeing. I can’t even disagree.

我关注了很多有趣的人，但是这些人也对很多事情感到不高兴，主要是世界状况。看着人们不断地互相sn视，将愤怒发泄在眼前的一切……是可以理解的。您不能让他们做出不同的反应或放松，因为这些是对他们所看到的内容的合理回应。我什至不能不同意。

But seeing people angry most of the time takes it’s toll too. I think it affected my mental health. Yes, I’m deeply upset with some of the things that are happening, but if I’m thinking about them 24x7 I’m not thinking about urgent things in my life that need my attention.

但是大多数时候看到人们生气会造成伤害。我认为这影响了我的心理健康。是的，我对正在发生的某些事情深感不安，但是如果我全天候24x7的思考，我就不会考虑生活中需要我关注的紧急事情。

I won’t miss people dunking on each other while also not mentioning the dunkee, piquing my curiosity to the point where I investigate who the dunkee was and why. A waste of time.

我不会错过任何人互相灌篮，同时也不会提及灌篮，这使我产生了好奇心，以至于我调查了那个灌篮是谁以及为什么。浪费时间。

Some guy with 14k followers said something. It was something like “only Indian people are rude/assholes in this particular way. Here are some anecdotes proving this”. This is a generalisation about a race of people based on anecdotes, so by definition racist. But since the person was Indian, it’s not. Don’t look at me, I don’t make the rules.

有14k追随者的家伙说了些什么。这就像“只有印度人以这种特殊方式粗鲁/混蛋。这里有一些轶事证明了这一点。这是关于基于轶事的人种的概括，因此从定义上讲是种族主义者。但由于此人是印度人，所以不是。不要看着我，我没有制定规则。

Dunking on other Indian people is a common pastime for Indian people - it makes us feel superior to our compatriots. Of course we wouldn’t do the thing that we’re talking about, we’re better than that. He wasn’t doing anything I haven’t heard a hundred others do.

与其他印度人同住是印度人民的普遍消遣，这使我们感到比同胞优越。当然，我们不会做我们正在谈论的事情，我们比那更好。他没有做我没有听说过的其他一百件事。

I commented that there could be sampling bias here. Since we’re more likely to be annoyed/embarassed by the actions of Indian people, we’re more likely to notice and remember it. I relayed an anecdote of my Indian friend being very annoyed by something an Indian did but not even remembering that a white person had done the exact same thing the previous day (making us wait while taking a photo). I thought it was ok to say this. I asked “You ever think this is sampling bias? You notice and get annoyed when Indians do it and embarrass you but don’t if it’s someone else?”

我评论说这里可能会有抽样偏差。由于我们更容易被印度人民的举动所困扰/尴尬，因此我们更有可能注意到并记住这一点。我转述了我的印度朋友的一则轶事，这件事使印度人非常恼火，但我什至不记得白人在前一天做了完全一样的事情（让我们等着拍照）。我认为可以这样说。我问“您是否认为这是抽样偏差？当印度人这样做并让您感到尴尬时，您会发现并感到烦恼，但如果是其他人，您就不会感到生气吗？”

Apparently not. He went on a massive tirade by this dude. Not content with responding rudely, he quote tweets me so he can dunk in front of all his followers. Apparently, I’m jingoistic deflector, part of the “elite” that refuses to “accept and introspect” and that I “deny deny deny” legitimate issues with India. If you’ve read my other posts, you know this isn’t true. I’m the first one to point out where my country (and me personally) can do better. ( Understanding my Privilege, Discriminating against left handers)

显然不是。他被这家伙花了大笔钱。他不满足于粗鲁的回应，他引用了一条推文给我，以便他可以在所有追随者面前灌篮。显然，我是技术主义偏转者，是“精英”的一部分，拒绝“接受和内省”，并且我“否认”否认与印度有关的合法问题。如果您阅读了我的其他文章，您就会知道这不是事实。我是第一个指出我的国家（和我个人）可以做得更好的地方的人。 （了解我的特权，歧视左撇子）

Anyway, he has 14k followers. I have 100. I wasn’t winning this one, regardless. I decided to stop responding, even though he went on and on.

无论如何，他有14k粉丝。我有100。不管怎样，我都没有赢。即使他不断，我还是决定停止回应。

The thing is, he’s actually a pretty good dude. I know, because I’ve followed him for months. But at some point he’s been exposed to so much trolling that he assumes that everyone who disagrees is a troll. It’s "#factcheckbait" according to him. He thought I was a troll and responded appropriately.

问题是，他实际上是一个非常好的家伙。我知道，因为我已经追踪了他几个月。但是在某个时候，他受到了太多的巨魔攻击，以至于他认为每个不同意的人都是巨魔。是＆＃34;＃factcheckbait＆＃34;按照他的说法。他以为我是一个巨魔，做出了适当的回应。

How does a reasonable, good person end up warped like this, where they assume that every person out there is a bad faith actor? Where there’s absolutely no scope for disagreement? My theory is that Twitter warps people in this way, by simply removing any scope for reasoned discussion. When you only meet trolls, and never have any friendly discussion with different viewpoints, you assume that a person disagreeing with you is a troll.

一个理性，善良的人如何最终像这样扭曲，他们假设每个人都有一个恶意的演员？哪里绝对没有分歧的余地？我的理论是，Twitter只是通过删除合理讨论的任何范围来以这种方式扭曲人们。当您仅遇到巨魔，而从未与其他观点进行任何友好的讨论时，您会认为一个与您意见不同的人就是一个巨魔。

I could stick around, play the game, build up by follower count to the point where I solve problem 1, prune my followee list to solve problem 2. But by then I’ve probably spent so much time there that I’ve become warped, probably without realising it - problem 3. Once I figured this out, I deactivated my account.

我可以坚持，玩游戏，按照关注者人数累积到解决问题1的程度，修剪我的关注者列表以解决问题2。但是到那时，我可能已经花了很多时间在那儿了，所以我变得扭曲了，可能是没有意识到-问题3。找到答案后，我便停用了我的帐户。

This probably isn’t permanent. The upsides of twitter still exist. I still want to read what people like Patrick McKenzie, Zach Weinersmith, John Carmack and others have to say. I still want to send encouraging messages to small time creators. But for now, I’m done.

这可能不是永久的。 Twitter的优势仍然存在。我仍然想读像Patrick McKenzie，Zach Weinersmith，John Carmack等人所说的话。我仍然想向小时间创作者发送鼓舞人心的信息。但是，到目前为止，我已经完成了。

Winner Announced In the World's First 'Quantum Chess' Tournament
获胜者宣布了世界上第一届“国际象棋”比赛

2020-12-21 07:17:12

Forget all those amusing memes of Anya Taylor-Joy's Beth Harmon from  The Queen's Gambit facing off against Spock in Star Trek's infamous 3D chess. We want to see Beth take on challengers in a quantum chess tournament. The world's first such tournament was held December 9 as part of the virtual Q2B conference on quantum computing, with Amazon's Aleksander Kubica emerging victorious, New Scientist reports.

忘掉女王的甘比特在星际迷航中臭名昭著的3D国际象棋对抗Spock的情况下，忘掉Anya Taylor-Joy's Beth Harmon的所有有趣模因。我们希望看到Beth在量子象棋锦标赛中挑战挑战者。据《新科学家》报道，作为量子计算虚拟Q2B会议的一部分，全球首个此类锦标赛于12月9日举行，其中亚马逊的亚历山大·库比卡（Aleksander Kubica）取得了胜利。

What exactly is quantum chess? It's a complicated version of regular chess that incorporates the quantum concepts of superposition, entanglement, and interference. “It’s like you’re playing in a multiverse but the different boards [in different universes] are connected to each other,” said Caltech physicist Spiros Michalakis during a livestream of the tournament. “It makes 3D chess from Star Trek look silly.”

量子象棋到底是什么？它是常规国际象棋的一个复杂版本，其中包含了叠加，纠缠和干涉的量子概念。加州理工学院的物理学家Spiros Michalakis在比赛的直播中说：“这就像是您在多元宇宙中进行游戏，但[在不同宇宙中]的不同板子彼此相连。” “它使《星际迷航》中的3D国际象棋看起来很愚蠢。”

Quantum chess (as played in the tournament) is the brainchild of Chris Cantwell of Quantum Realm Games. When he was a graduate student in quantum computing at the University of Southern California, he got the idea while working on a project for a class on creativity and invention. “My initial goal was to create a version of quantum chess that was truly quantum in nature, so you get to play with the phenomenon,” Cantwell told Gizmodo back in 2016. “I didn’t want it to just be a game that taught people quantum mechanics.” By playing the game, the player slowly develops an intuitive sense of the rules governing the quantum realm. In fact, “I feel like I’ve come to more intuitively understand quantum phenomena myself, just by making the game,” he said.

量子象棋（在锦标赛中使用）是量子领域游戏的克里斯·坎特威尔（Chris Cantwell）的创举。当他在南加州大学（University of Southern California）量子计算专业研究生时，他在从事有关创造力和发明课程的项目时就想到了这一点。坎特威尔在2016年告诉Gizmodo：“我的最初目标是创建一种本质上完全是量子的量子象棋版本，所以您就可以玩这种现象。”我不希望它只是一款具有教学意义的游戏人们量子力学。”通过玩游戏，玩家可以逐渐形成对量子域管理规则的直观认识。实际上，“我觉得我只是通过制作游戏就能更直观地了解量子现象了，”他说。

To help launch a Kickstarter campaign for a commercial version of quantum chess, Alex Winter (i.e., Bill of Bill and Ted), directed a short video, Anyone Can Quantum (embedded below), in which Paul Rudd—who went subatomic and visited the quantum realm in Ant Man—challenged the late Stephen Hawking to a game of quantum chess. The video featured not only Rudd and Hawking matching wits but also an amusing voiceover by Keanu Reeves (i.e., Ted of Bill and Ted). It made its debut in January 2016 at One Entangled Evening, a special event to kick off a Caltech conference on the future of quantum computing. The 12-minute video has since racked up more than 8 million views.

为了帮助开展针对量子国际象棋商业版本的Kickstarter活动，亚历克斯·温特（Alex Winter）（即比尔和泰德）制作了一段短片《任何人都可以量子》（嵌入下面），其中保罗·鲁德（Paul Rudd）进入了亚原子状态并参观了《蚁人》中的量子领域-使已故的斯蒂芬·霍金挑战量子棋。该视频不仅展示了陆克文和霍金相配的智慧，还展示了基努·里维斯（即比尔和泰德的泰德）的有趣配音。它于2016年1月在一个纠结的夜晚首次亮相，这是一场特别活动，旨在启动有关量子计算的未来的加州理工学院会议。自此以来，这部12分钟的视频已经获得了超过800万的观看次数。

You don't need to be a quantum physicist to play quantum chess, per Cantwell, although it does help to already know the rules of regular chess. In quantum chess, there are multiple boards on which the pieces exist, and their number is not fixed. Players can perform "quantum moves" as well as regular chess moves; players just need to indicate which type of move they're performing. Any quantum move will create a superposition of boards (doubling the number of possible boards in the superposition with each quantum move), although the player will see a single board representing all boards at the same time. And any individual move acts on all boards at the same time.

按照坎特威尔的说法，您不需要成为量子物理学家就可以玩量子象棋，尽管这确实有助于了解常规象棋的规则。在量子象棋中，存在多个棋子，并且棋子的数量不是固定的。玩家可以执行“量子移动”以及常规的棋步；玩家只需要指出他们正在执行哪种动作即可。尽管玩家会看到一个板同时代表所有板，但任何量子移动都会创建板的叠加（每次叠加都会使叠中可能的板数量加倍）。而且任何单独的举动都会同时作用于所有板上。

The teal colored “fill” of each queen shows the probability of finding the queen in that space; the same queen, existing in different locations on the board. The queen is in a superposition of being in two places at once. On their next turn, the player can choose to move any one of their pieces.

每个女王/王后的青绿色“填充”表示在该空间中找到女王的概率；同一位女王，在董事会的不同位置。女王处于一次在两个地方的叠加。在下一回合，玩家可以选择移动他们的任何一块。

Pieces can also be entangled with each other—critical to Rudd's strategy against Hawking. In order to determine where the entangled piece is actually located, a player must make a measurement. In fact, that's the only way to win a game of quantum chess, since there is no check or check mate possible. A player must capture their opponent's king as they make a quantum measurement of its location. The tournament games were timed, however, which is how Kubica eventually prevailed over his opponent, Google's Doug Strain. Strain simply ran out of time.

作品之间也可以相互纠缠，这对陆克文反对霍金的策略至关重要。为了确定纠缠的棋子的实际位置，玩家必须进行测量。实际上，这是赢得量子象棋游戏的唯一方法，因为没有检查或检查队友的可能。玩家在对对手的位置进行量子测量时必须捕获其对手的国王。然而，锦标赛比赛是定时的，这就是Kubica最终战胜了对手Google的Doug Strain的方式。劳力简直用光了时间。

And yes, Alex Winter remains a fan of quantum chess. “This idea of bridging quantum with something that’s a more understandable, practical game that people still invest a lot of thought into is, I thought that was really incredible,” he said during a surprise visit to the tournament livestream last week.

是的，亚历克斯·温特仍然是量子象棋的粉丝。他在上周意外访问比赛直播时说：“将量子与一种更易于理解，更实用的游戏联系在一起的想法，人们仍然对此进行了很多思考，这真是令人难以置信。”

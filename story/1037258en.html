New variety of Apple discovered by Wiltshire runner
威尔特郡跑步者发现了苹果的新品种

2020-11-30 18:50:58

Archie Thomas stumbled across solitary windfall fruit that could be cross between cultivated apple and European crab apple

阿奇·托马斯（Archie Thomas）偶然发现了意外收获的水果，该水果可能会在栽培苹果和欧洲山楂苹果之间杂交

A chance find of an apple on a woodland run has led one nature lover to discover a new variety that he hopes to propagate and name.

在林地上偶然发现一个苹果，使一个自然爱好者发现了一个新品种，他希望传播该品种并为其命名。

Archie Thomas, who lives in the Nadder valley in Wiltshire, stumbled across a solitary windfall apple on a wooded trackway alongside a large area of ancient woodland near his home this month.

居住在威尔特郡纳德山谷的阿奇·托马斯（Archie Thomas）这个月偶然发现了一个树木繁茂的单行道上一个孤独的暴利苹果，旁边是他家附近的一大片古老林地。

The apple, which Thomas said was “unlike any I’d seen before”, had come from a lone old apple tree in the hedgerow with a large number of fruit on it.

托马斯说，苹果“与我以前见过的苹果不同”，它来自树篱中的一棵孤独的老苹果树，上面有很多水果。

Apple trees grown from seed are all different, so cultivated varieties, or cultivars, are propagated by taking cuttings from existing trees and grafting them on to rootstock to ensure the new tree and its apples are the same. Apples have been cultivated in this or similar ways for thousands of years.

从种子生长的苹果树都是不同的，因此，通过从现有树上摘下树枝并将其嫁接到砧木上来繁殖栽培品种或栽培品种，以确保新树及其苹果相同。用这种或类似的方法栽培苹果已有数千年的历史了。

Thomas, who works for the wild plant and fungi conservation charity Plantlife, was keen to identify the unusual apple he had found in a little-visited spot to see if it was a known cultivar, or a new variety he could name himself.

托马斯（Thomas）为野生植物和真菌保护慈善机构Plantlife工作，他热衷于找出自己在一个鲜有人到访的地点发现的不寻常的苹果，看看它是不是一个已知的品种，还是他可以命名的新品种。

“While I am certainly no fruit expert it immediately struck me as highly unusual, unlike any apple I’d seen before,” he said.

他说：“虽然我当然不是水果专家，但立即使我感到与众不同，这与我以前见过的任何苹果都不一样。”

“Excited by the pale and mottled oddity, I set about trying to get it identified with a view to perhaps one day being able to name it. That was the dream but I did half suspect it would turn out to be something much less exciting than it is.”

“由于陌生的脸色苍白而杂乱无章，我着手尝试将其标识出来，以期有一天能够命名它。那是梦想，但我半信半疑，结果实在没有那么令人兴奋了。”

After what he described as a “wild apple chase”, with many fruit experts flummoxed by the find, he received help from Plantlife colleagues and was then pointed towards the Royal Horticultural Society fruit identification service at RHS Wisley.

在他称其为“野苹果追逐”之后，许多水果专家对这一发现感到困惑，他从Plantlife同事那里得到了帮助，然后被指向RHS Wisley的皇家园艺学会水果鉴定服务。

The RHS fruit specialist Jim Arbury inspected three of the apples and informed Thomas it was not a planted cultivar, but a new variety that he could propagate and name.

RHS水果专家吉姆·阿伯里（Jim Arbury）检查了三个苹果，并告知托马斯（Thomas）这不是一个种植品种，而是他可以繁殖和命名的新品种。

Arbury said it was “a very interesting apple”. It is clearly not a planted tree, but a seedling that could be a cross between a cultivated apple and a wild Malus sylvestris, a European crab apple, he said.

Arbury说这是“一个非常有趣的苹果”。他说，这显然不是种植的树，而是可能是栽培的苹果与野生的欧洲锦葵苹果（Malus sylvestris）杂交的幼苗。

“It tastes quite good. It’s a cooking apple or dual purpose, you can eat it, it’s got a bit of acidity but it’s got some flavour, and some tannin, which is what you have in cider apples,” he said, adding it could be used with other apples for cider.

“味道很好。它是煮苹果或双重用途，可以吃，有一点酸味，但有一定的风味，还有单宁，苹果酒中就有这种鞣质。”苹果酒。

He said most chance apple trees were from Bramley’s Seedling cooking apples grown in gardens or orchards, or sometimes from supermarket apples thrown out of car windows and now growing alongside roads.

他说，苹果树很有可能来自布拉姆利的苗木烹饪苹果，这些苹果是在花园或果园里种植的，有时是从超市苹果车窗外扔出的苹果，现在正沿着道路生长。

But he said the apples sent by Thomas came from a tree that could be 100 years old or more and was not the result of a dropped modern supermarket apple.

但他说，托马斯送来的苹果来自一棵可能已有100年或更久历史的树，而不是现代超市苹果掉落的结果。

Thomas admitted he may be biased, but said he thought the apples tasted great. “Tart but not wincingly-so, and with enough sweetness to eat raw … They speak of the terrain of Wiltshire; unimproved chalk grassland and chalk streams.,” he added.

托马斯承认他可能有偏见，但他说他认为苹果味道很好。 “酸但不令人吃惊-如此甜蜜，足以吃生的……他们谈到了威尔特郡的地形；未经改良的白垩草原和白垩溪。”他补充说。

As for the name, Thomas said he feft pressure to get it right: “I have too many ideas. My seven-year-old son wants me to call it Cristiano Ronaldo but that’s not happening. My wife, Hannah, is the apple of my eye, so she’s in contention.”

至于名字，托马斯说他想把它弄对就是压力：“我有太多想法。我7岁的儿子要我叫克里斯蒂亚诺·罗纳尔多（Cristiano Ronaldo），但那没有发生。我的妻子汉娜（Hannah）是我的眼中钉，所以她在争吵。”

Dr Trevor Dines, at botanical specialist at Plantlife, said: “Archie has joined a small and select group of people that have discovered something entirely new in our natural world.

Plantlife植物学专家Trevor Dines博士说：“ Archie加入了一小撮人，他们发现了我们自然世界中全新的事物。

“I absolutely adore apples and Archie’s new find is breathtaking. And what a romantic origin, unearthed deep in a wood with ancient roots. We can only speculate how it arose, but that’s the joy of botany – you never quite know what you’ll find, or how it got there. These sort of mysteries only serve to deepen our love of the countryside.”

“我绝对喜欢苹果，阿奇的新发现令人叹为观止。这是多么浪漫的起源，发掘在具有古老根源的树林深处。我们只能推测它是如何产生的，但这就是植物学的乐趣–您永远不会完全知道自己会发现什么，或者它是如何到达那里的。这些谜团只会加深我们对乡村的热爱。”

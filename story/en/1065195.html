The driest place on Earth has not seen rain for nearly 2M years
地球上最干燥的地方近2米的雨水

Jump to navigation Jump to search The McMurdo Dry Valleys are a row of largely snow-free valleys in Antarctica, located within Victoria Land west of McMurdo Sound. [1] The Dry Valleys experience extremely low humidity and surrounding mountains prevent the flow of ice from nearby glaciers. The rocks here are granites and gneisses, and glacial tills dot this bedrock landscape, with loose gravel covering the ground. It is one of the driest places on Earth and has not seen rain for nearly 2 million years. [2]

The region is one of the world's most extreme deserts, and includes many features including Lake Vida, a saline lake, and the Onyx River, a meltwater stream and Antarctica's longest river. Although no living organisms have been found in the permafrost here, endolithic photosynthetic bacteria have been found living in the relatively moist interior of rocks, and anaerobic bacteria, with a metabolism based on iron and sulfur, live under the Taylor Glacier.

该地区是世界上最极端的沙漠之一，包括许多特色，包括瓦达湖，盐湖和奥瑙河，熔融水流和南极洲＆＃39;最长的河流。虽然这里没有发现过多的生物体，但是，已经发现岩石间光合细菌在相对潮湿的岩石和厌氧细菌中，具有基于铁和硫的代谢，生活在泰勒冰川下。

The Dry Valleys are so named because of their extremely low humidity and lack of snow or ice cover. They are also dry because, in this location, the mountains are sufficiently high that they block seaward-flowing ice from the East Antarctic Ice Sheet from reaching the Ross Sea. At 4,800 square kilometres (1,900 sq mi), the valleys constitute around 0.03% of the continent and form the largest ice-free region in Antarctica. The valley floors are covered with loose gravel, in which ice wedge polygonal patterned ground may be observed. [3]

干燥的山谷是如此命名，因为它们极低的湿度和缺乏雪或冰盖。它们也是干燥的，因为在这个位置，山脉足够高，使他们从东南南极冰块到达罗斯海中的海洋流动的冰块。山谷占4800平方公里（1,900平方英尺），占大陆的0.03％，并形成南极洲最大的无冰地区。山谷地板覆盖着松散的砾石，其中可以观察到冰楔形图案地面。 [3]

Precipitation averages around 100 millimetres (3.9 in) per year over a century of records, all in the form of snow. [4] The dry wind rapidly evaporates the snow and little melts into the soil. During the summer, this process can take only hours.

降水平均每年约100毫米（3.9英寸），一流的记录，都是雪的形式。 [4]干风迅速蒸发雪，融化到土壤中。在夏季，这个过程只需几个小时。

The unique conditions in the Dry Valleys are caused, in part, by katabatic winds; these occur when cold, dense air is pulled downhill by the force of gravity. The winds can reach speeds of 320 kilometres per hour (200 mph), heating as they descend and evaporating all water, ice and snow. [5]

干谷的独特条件是由Katabatic风引起的;通过重力的力，使密集空气下坡下坡。风可以达到每小时320公里（200英里/小时）的速度，加热，因为它们下降并蒸发所有水，冰雪。 [5]

The McMurdo Oasis constitutes approximately 4,000 square kilometres (1,500 sq mi) of "deglaciated mountainous desert", according to McKelvey, bounded by the coastline of south Victoria Land and the Polar Plateau. The Taylor and Wright Valleys are major ice-free valleys within the Transantarctic Mountains. These "dry valleys" include hummocky moraines, with frozen lakes, saline ponds, sand dunes, and meltwater streams. Basement rocks include the Late Precambrian or Early Palaeozoic Skelton Group metamorphic rocks, primarily the Asgard Formation, which is a medium-high-grade marble and calc schist. The Palaeozoic Granite Harbour intrusives include granitoid plutons and dykes, which intruded into the metasedimentary Skelton Group in the Late Cambrian- Early Ordovician during the Ross orogeny. The basement complex is overlain by the Jurassic Beacon Supergroup, which is itself intruded by Ferrar Dolerite sheets and sills. The McMurdo Volcanic Group intrudes, or is interbedded with, the Taylor and Wright Valleys' moraines as basaltic cinder cones and lava flows. These basalts have ages between 2.1 and 4.4 Ma. The Dry Valley Drilling Project (1971–75) determined the Pleistocene layer within the Taylor Valley was between 137 and 275 m thick, and composed of interbedded sandstones, pebble conglomerates, and laminated silty mudstones. This Pleistocene layer disconformably overlies Pliocene and Miocene diamictites. [6] [7]

Endolithic bacteria have been found living in the Dry Valleys, sheltered from the dry air in the relatively moist interior of rocks. Summer meltwater from the glaciers provides the primary source of soil nutrients. [8] Scientists consider the Dry Valleys perhaps the closest of any terrestrial environment to the planet Mars, and thus an important source of insights into possible extraterrestrial life.

已经发现岩石的细菌生活在干燥的山谷中，从岩石的相对潮湿的内部浮出水。来自冰川的夏季熔融水提供土壤营养素的主要来源。 [8]科学家考虑干燥的山谷，也许是将任何陆地环境最接近的地球火星，从而成为可能的外星生命的重要洞察来源。

Anaerobic bacteria whose metabolism is based on iron and sulfur live in sub-freezing temperatures under the Taylor Glacier.

代谢基于铁和硫的厌氧细菌生活在泰勒冰川下的沉淀温度下。

It was previously thought that algae were staining red the ice emerging at Blood Falls but it is now known that the staining is caused by high levels of iron oxide. [9] [10]

它以前认为藻类染色在血液中出现的冰落下，但现在已知染色是由高水平的氧化铁引起的。 [9] [10]

Canadian and American researchers conducted a field expedition in 2013 to University Valley in order to examine the microbial population and to test a drill designed for sampling on Mars in the permafrost of the driest parts of the valleys, the areas most analogous to the Martian surface. They found no living organisms in the permafrost, the first location on the planet visited by humans with no active microbial life. [11]

加拿大和美国研究人员于2013年对大学谷进行了一个野外探险，以检查微生物种群，并测试谷火灾最深部分的MASS上的火星上采样的钻探，这些领域与火星表面最为类似。他们发现永久冻土中没有生物体，人类在行星上的第一个位置，没有活跃的微生物寿命。 [11]

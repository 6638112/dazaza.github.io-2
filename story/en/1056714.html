One in three survivors of severe Covid diagnosed with mental health condition
在诊断出心理健康状况的严重科迪德的三种幸存者中

2021-04-07 18:49:51

One in three people who were severely ill with coronavirus were subsequently diagnosed with a neurological or psychiatric condition within six months of infection, a study has found.

一项研究发现，随后在感染后六个月内被诊断出患有神经系统或精神病病症的三分之一。

The observational research, which is the largest of its kind, used electronic health records of 236,379 patients mostly from the US and found 34% experienced mental health and neurological conditions afterwards. The most common being anxiety, with 17% of people developing this.

观察研究，这是其最大的，二手电子健康记录为236,379名，主要来自美国，发现了34％的心理健康和神经系统条件。最常见的焦虑，17％的人发展这一点。

Experts warned that healthcare systems need to be resourced to deal with patients affected by this, which could be “substantial” given the scale of the pandemic. They anticipate that the impact could be felt on health services for many years.

专家警告说，鉴于大流行规模，医疗保健系统需要重新应对受此影响的患者，这可能是“大幅性”。他们预计可能对健康服务的影响多年来可能会受到卫生服务。

Neurological diagnoses such as stroke and dementia were rarer, but not uncommon in those who had been seriously ill during infection. Of those who had been admitted to intensive care, 7% had a stroke and almost 2% were diagnosed with dementia.

神经学诊断如中风和痴呆症是罕见的，但在感染过程中严重病害的人中并不罕见。那些被重症监护的人，7％的中风，近2％被诊断出患有痴呆症。

The study, which was published in the Lancet Psychiatry, found that these diagnoses were more common in Covid-19 patients than among those who had the flu or respiratory tract infections over the same time period.

该研究发表在柳树精神病学，发现这些诊断在Covid-19患者中更常见，而不是在同一时间段内进行流感或呼吸道感染的患者。

After taking into account underlying health characteristics, such as age, sex, ethnicity and existing health conditions, there was a 44% greater risk of neurological and mental health diagnoses after Covid-19 than after flu. Prof Paul Harrison, lead author of the study, from the University of Oxford, said: “These are real-world data from a large number of patients. They confirm the high rates of psychiatric diagnoses after Covid-19 and show that serious disorders affecting the nervous system (such as stroke and dementia) occur too. While the latter is much rarer, they are significant, especially in those who had severe Covid-19. “Although the individual risks for most disorders are small, the effect across the whole population may be substantial for health and social care systems due to the scale of the pandemic and that many of these conditions are chronic. As a result, health care systems need to be resourced to deal with the anticipated need, both within primary and secondary care services.”

考虑到潜在的健康特征，如年龄，性别，种族和现有的健康状况，在Covid-19后的神经系统和心理健康状况的风险大于流感后患有44％的风险。牛津大学的研究领先作者保罗哈里森教授说：“这些是来自大量患者的真实数据。他们在Covid-19后确认了精神病诊断的高率，并表明影响了影响神经系统（如中风和痴呆）的严重障碍。虽然后者很少，但它们很重要，特别是在那些严重Covid-19的人。 “虽然大多数障碍的个体风险都很小，但由于大流行的规模，整个人口的效果可能是卫生和社会护理系统的重要性，并且这些条件中的许多条件是慢性的。因此，在小学和二级护理服务中，需要从卫生保健系统资源处理预期的需求。“

There has been growing concern that coronavirus survivors might be at increased risk of neurological disorders. A previous observational study by the same research group reported that people are at increased risk of mood and anxiety disorders in the first three months after infection.

越来越担心冠状病毒幸存者可能会增加神经系统疾病的风险。同一研究小组的先前观察研究报告称，人们在感染后的前三个月内的情绪和焦虑障碍的风险增加。

However, until now, there have been no large-scale data examining the risks of neurological as well as psychiatric diagnoses in the six months after infection.

然而，到目前为止，在感染后六个月内没有大规模数据检查神经系统以及精神病诊断的风险。

The most common diagnoses after Covid were anxiety disorders (occurring in 17% of patients), mood disorders (14%), substance misuse disorders (7%), and insomnia (5%). The incidence of neurological outcomes was lower, including 0.6% for a brain haemorrhage and 0.7% for dementia. Dr Max Taquet, a co-author of the study, said: “We now need to see what happens beyond six months. The study cannot reveal the mechanisms involved, but does point to the need for urgent research to identify these, with a view to preventing or treating them.”

Covid后最常见的诊断是焦虑症（在17％的患者中发生），情绪障碍（14％），物质滥用障碍（7％）和失眠（5％）。神经原因的发病率较低，包括脑出血的0.6％，痴呆的0.7％。该研究的共同作者Max Taquet博士说：“我们现在需要看看超过六个月后发生了什么。该研究无法揭示所涉及的机制，但确实指出了迫切研究来识别这些，以防止或治疗它们。“

The authors note several limitations to their study including the fact many people with symptoms of the virus do not present for health care, so the people studied here are likely to have been more severely affected than most. It was noted that the severity of the neurological and psychiatric disorders is not known.

作者注意到他们的学习的几个限制，包括许多人类患有病毒症状的事实，不适用于医疗保健，因此这里学习的人可能受到大多数人的严重影响。有人指出，神经和精神病疾病的严重程度尚不清楚。

Dr Jonathan Rogers, who was not involved in the study, from University College London, said: “[This] study points us towards the future, both in its methods and implications … Sadly, many of the disorders identified in this study tend to be chronic or recurrent, so we can anticipate that the impact of Covid-19 could be with us for many years.”

来自伦敦大学学院没有参与研究的Jonathan Rogers博士说：“[本]研究我们对未来的观点，无论是在其方法和含义中......遗憾的是，这项研究中发现的许多疾病往往是慢性或反复发生，所以我们可以预测Covid-19的影响可能随时与我们同在。“

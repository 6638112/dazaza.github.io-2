As opposed to the "non-euclidean geometry" approach, we focus on hiding minute changes in the player's rotational coordinates within their regular motions in the game, trying to keep the distortion below their perceptual limits. (In other words, we don't have static distortion baked into the geometry, instead we watch what the player is doing and use heuristics to pick the best time to apply the distortions.)

与＆＃34相反;非欧几里德几何形状＆＃34;方法，我们专注于玩家的藏目微小变化，在游戏中的常规动作中旋转坐标，试图保持低于其感知限制的扭曲。 （换句话说，我们没有静态失真烘烤到几何形状中，而是观看玩家正在做什么，并使用启发式来选择应用失真的最佳时间。）

Additionally, we use some simplistic AI to predict their future motion in the game world, then map that prediction against the shape of their play space, so that we know what the next "danger spots" are for potential collisions.

此外，我们使用一些简单的ai来预测他们在游戏世界中的未来运动，然后地图对他们的播放空间的形状进行预测，以便我们知道下一个＆＃34;危险斑点＆＃34;用于潜在的碰撞。

If the engine fails at redirecting adequately, the game is interrupted, and the player is asked to move to a specific spot to reset the redirection. Our current prototype makes such interruptions very rare with a medium-sized play area (i.e. 9x9 feet)

如果发动机在充分重定向时失败，则游戏被中断，并要求玩家移动到特定点以重置重定向。我们目前的原型使得这种中断非常罕见，中等大小的游乐区（即9x9英尺）

However, we need to do a lot more end user testing to make final assessments, because there is a significant trade-off between the amount of redirection and player nausea (pretty much everything in VR needs lots of such testing for nausea lol)

但是，我们需要做更多的最终用户测试来进行最终评估，因为重定向和球员恶心之间存在重大权衡（几乎在VR中的所有内容都需要大量的恶心哈哈）

The important factor here is that the amount of space needed for nausea-free redirected walking varies greatly depending on the type of gameplay, and we're designing a game from the ground up to be the ideal gameplay for optimal redirected walking.

这里的重要因素是无恶心重定向行走所需的空间量大幅度取决于游戏类型的类型，以及我们从地上设计一个游戏，成为最佳重定向行走的理想游戏。

There are some other tricks as well, but that is the main gist of our approach.

还有一些其他技巧，但这是我们方法的主要主旨。

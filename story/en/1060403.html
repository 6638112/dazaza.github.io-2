Surprisingly little alcohol can affect heart rate variability
令人惊讶的小酒精会影响心率变异性

2021-04-28 11:17:30

Heart Rate Variability (HRV) gives us an idea about the state of our autonomic nervous system (ANS). The ANS controls the involuntary functions of our body and has effects on the function of the heart. The ANS has two main branches: parasympathetic (activated during rest and digestion) and sympathetic (activated during periods of stress and exercise).

心率变异性（HRV）为我们的自主神经系统（ANS）的状态提供了一个想法。 ANS控制我们身体的非自愿功能，并对心脏的功能产生影响。 ANS有两个主要分支：副交感神经（在休息和消化期间活化）和同情（在压力和运动期间活化）。

A healthy HRV reflects a healthy balance between sympathetic and parasympathetic stimulation. What we mean by a healthy HRV differs from person to person but we generally want HRV to be in the upper limit of normal. A constantly low HRV usually means sympathetic overstimulation on the expense of the parasympathetic stimulation.

健康的HRV反映了交感神经和副交感神经刺激之间的健康平衡。我们的意思是健康的HRV与人的人不同，但我们通常希望HRV处于正常的上限。不断低的HRV通常意味着对副交感神经刺激的费用表示同情过度刺激。

Anything that causes stress to our inner environment can cause sympathetic stimulation. Sometimes this can be due to exercise which trains our bodies to be more adaptive to stress. Other times however, it can be caused by conditions such as sleep deprivation, psychological stress and physical illness.

对内部环境产生压力的任何东西都会导致交感神经刺激。有时这可能是由于锻炼，训练我们的身体更适应压力。然而，其他时间，它可能是由睡眠剥夺，心理压力和身体疾病等条件引起的。

When stimulated, an area in the brain called the amygdala (specifically, the central nucleus of the amygdala) can cause excitation of the sympathetic pathways and inhibition of the parasympathetic pathways affecting the heart.

当刺激时，大脑中的一个区域称为杏仁达拉（特别是杏仁核的中央核）可以引起影响影响心脏的副交感神经途径的激发和抑制副交感神经途径。

Under normal circumstances, this area is being constantly suppressed by other structures in the brain. This means the sympathetic system (stress pathway) is kept at bay. In 2009, researchers Thayer and Lane proposed that specific areas in the frontal lobe of the brain are responsible for the suppression of the amygdala.

在正常情况下，大脑中的其他结构不断抑制该区域。这意味着交感神经系统（应力途径）保持在海湾。 2009年，研究人员Thayer和Lane提出了大脑前叶中的特定区域负责抑制Amygdala。

According to their model, anything that dampens activity in the neurons of the frontal lobe may lead to a reduction in the inhibitory effect on the Amygdala. Simply, it inhibits the brakes the frontal lobe naturally uses to stop over stimulation of the sympathetic pathway. This in turn means more sympathetic stimulation and less parasympathetic activity. Ultimately, this leads to a decreased HRV. Alcohol depresses the central nervous system including the frontal cortex (which is also where we make our rational decisions) which explains its effects on HRV.

根据他们的模型，在额叶的神经元中抑制活性的任何东西可能导致杏仁杆菌的抑制作用减少。简单地，它抑制了额叶天然用来停止刺激交感神经途径的制动器。这反过来意味着更加同情刺激和较少的副交感神经活动。最终，这导致HRV减少。酒精抑制了中枢神经系统，包括前皮层（这也是我们制作理性决定的地方），这解释了其对HRV的影响。

A study in 1992 evaluated the effect of drinking alcohol in healthy nonalcoholic individuals. The researchers demonstrated that a low dose of alcohol as defined as 0.3 g/kg (which is roughly equivalent to a medium glass of table wine (175 ml) in a person who weighs 75 kg) was enough to have a negative effect on HRV.

1992年的一项研究评估了饮酒在健康的非酒精中的饮酒的影响。研究人员证明，定义为0.3g / kg的低剂量醇（其大致相当于重量75 kg的人中的表葡萄酒（175ml）的中等玻璃）足以对HRV产生负面影响。

Another study in 2010 compared three scenarios: drinking one standard drink of alcohol (defined as 5 ounces of wine or 12 ounces of regular beer), or drinking two standard drinks, or drinking only water in healthy individuals. The researchers demonstrated that there is a clear dose-dependent response. Small effects on HRV started with only one drink of alcohol with no effect on heart rate but the effect was even bigger with two drinks which had a significant effect on heart rate.

2010年的另一项研究比较了三种情景：饮用一个标准的酒精（定义为5盎司的葡萄酒或12盎司常规啤酒），或饮用两种标准饮料，或仅在健康的人中饮用水。研究人员证明存在明显的剂量依赖性反应。对HRV的小效果仅启动了一杯饮酒，对心率没有影响，但效果甚至更大，两杯饮料对心率有显着影响。

A report by WHOOP in 2016 shared the analysis of data collected from 148 student-athletes during their academic and athletic year. It revealed that the effect of alcohol on heart rate and HRV was detected in some athletes as long as four days after alcohol consumption.

2016年WHOOP的报告分享了在学术和运动年度在148名学生运动员收集的数据分析。它揭示了酒精对心率和HRV对某些运动员的影响，只要酒精消耗后四天就会检测到一些运动员。

In epidemiological studies, the relationship between alcohol consumption and cardiovascular health has been described as a J-shaped curve. This means low to moderate consumption of alcohol is associated with lower mortality rates when compared to zero alcohol consumption. With heavy consumption having the worst effect on mortality.

在流行病学研究中，醇消耗和心血管健康之间的关系被描述为J形曲线。与零酒精消耗相比，这意味着低于醇的低消耗量与降低死亡率相关。沉重消费对死亡率最严重的影响。

Another point to make it is that not all types of alcohol are the same even with the same ethanol content (ethanol is the main component of alcoholic beverages). Observational studies aside, there is some evidence from RCTs (randomized controlled studies) that red wine may reduce blood pressure and have some cardio-protective and anti-oxidant effects. These are in part due to its high polyphenol content (there may be a role for ethanol as well).

另一种意义表明，即使在相同的乙醇含量（乙醇是酒精饮料的主要成分），并非所有类型的醇都相同。除了观察研究之外，来自RCT（随机对照研究）的一些证据，红葡萄酒可能会降低血压并具有一些心脏保护和抗氧化效果。由于其高多酚含量（也可能存在乙醇的作用，这些部分是部分。

The good news is, when weighing the health benefits of red wine against the negative effects of ethanol, there may be a way to get the good without the bad. Red non-alcoholic wine has a similar health profile without the ethanol content. Although, make sure to choose the less sweetened types, ideally less than 2g/100 mL of total carbs or sugars. ‍

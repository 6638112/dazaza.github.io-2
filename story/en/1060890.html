iPhones’ Night Shift feature doesn’t help you sleep better, study finds
学习发现，iPhone的夜班功能并没有帮助您睡眠更好

2021-05-06 05:31:10

Researchers at Brigham Young University conducted a study to see how much blue-light-reducing features like Apple's Night Shift improve sleep quality, and they came to a conclusion that doesn't match the marketing message behind said features: Night Shift doesn't help at all.

In the study which was published in Sleep Health, the BYU researchers assessed the sleep quality of 167 young adults, asking each to wear a wrist accelerometer before sleep. Participants were randomly assigned three conditions regarding iPhone use before bed: one group didn't use its iPhones at all, one used its iPhones without Night Shift enabled, and another used its iPhones with Night Shift enabled. "There were no significant differences in sleep outcomes across the three experimental groups," the researchers concluded.

For individuals who slept more than 6.8 hours per night, there was some improvement in sleep quality for those who did not use their smartphones at all. But Night Shift didn't have a significant impact, and there was no difference between those who used smartphones and those who didn't when the amount of sleep was less than 6.8 hours per night.

对于每晚睡觉超过6.8小时的人，对于那些没有使用智能手机的人来说，睡眠质量有一些改善。但夜班没有产生重大影响，并且那些使用智能手机的人和睡眠量的人之间没有差异，每晚睡眠量小于6.8小时。

"This suggests that when you are super tired you fall asleep no matter what you did just before bed... the sleep pressure is so high there is really no effect of what happens before bedtime," said Chad Jensen, one of the researchers.

The takeaway for users of these devices seems to be that the old adage—avoiding smartphone use before bed can improve your sleep holds up—even as the study undermines the common belief that features like Night Shift (which has its parallels in products made by other companies besides Apple, too) can mitigate the negative effects of smartphone use on sleep.

Why do characters never say bye on the phone?
为什么人物永远不会在电话上说再见？

2021-05-10 23:56:42

Watch any film or TV show and you’ll notice that nobody ever says goodbye. Are they too busy – or just rude?

观看任何电影或电视节目，你会注意到没有人说再见。他们太忙了 - 还是粗鲁？

In the Guide’s weekly Solved! column, we look into a crucial pop-culture question you’ve been burning to know the answer to – and settle it

在指导周刊上解决！专栏，我们研究了一个关键的流行文化问题，你一直在燃烧，了解答案 - 并解决它

I absolutely hate talking on the phone. I am one of those infuriating friends who’d rather have a long, winding text conversation than speak on the phone to arrange a simple plan. People under the age of 60 who still leave voicemails? Seek help. And unknown numbers? I follow Dua Lipa’s first rule (don’t pick up the phone!). But it seems as if there’s a group out there who loathe the inconvenience of talking on the phone even more than me: screenwriters.

我绝对讨厌谈电话。我是一个愤怒的朋友之一，宁愿拥有长期蜿蜒的文本谈话，而不是在手机上安排一个简单的计划。 60岁以下的人仍然留下语音邮件？寻求帮助。和未知的数字？我遵循Dua Lipa的第一个规则（不要拿起电话！）。但它似乎有一个小组在那里厌恶甚至超过手机的不便：编剧。

Watch any film or TV show and you’re likely to notice (and be very irritated by) the fact that characters hardly ever say “goodbye” at the end of phone calls. After the main part of a conversation or a salacious bombshell is dropped, it’s very common for the phone call to abruptly end. No voices clumsily saying “bye” over the top of each other, no awkward variations on “speak soon”. Just pure silence.

观看任何电影或电视节目，您可能会注意到（并且非常恼火）这个事实，角色在电话结束时几乎没有说“再见”。在谈话的主要部分或持伤害炸弹之后，这对电话突然结束是非常常见的。没有声音笨拙地说“再见”在彼此之上，没有尴尬的变化“很快就会说话”。只是纯粹的沉默。

I am not the only one who is irked by the lack of on-screen goodbyes. It does seem quite rude: after all, even the 81% of millennials who find phone calls “anxiety-inducing” still probably try their best to be polite once they’re on the phone. But is it something people actually do? Anecdotally, it seems like it’s a no, at least in the UK. A reader wrote to the Guardian to say they’d tried this bizarre Hollywood trend for themselves, only for their friends to call back immediately and ask if their “signal had cut out”.

我不是唯一受到屏幕缺乏的再见的人。它似乎非常粗鲁：毕竟，即使是千禧一代的81％，千禧一代的千禧一代仍然可能会尽力让他们在手机上有礼貌。但是，人们真的做了什么吗？轶事，至少在英国，这似乎是不是。一位读者写信给守护者说他们自己尝试过这个奇怪的好莱坞潮流，只为他们的朋友立即回电并询问他们的“信号已经被删除”。

Maybe it’s an American thing? This too seems doubtful. There is a whole thread on Reddit where Americans are insistent that not saying “goodbye” is not a part of their culture. There is also the fact that, on US reality TV, it’s very common to see stars such as the Kardashians calling each other and, unless a person is hanging up in anger, there’s usually a goodbye.

也许这是美国的东西？这似乎似乎是怀疑的。在Reddit上有一个整个线程，美国人坚持认为没有说“再见”不是他们文化的一部分。还有一个事实是，在美国现实电视上，看到诸如卡戴珊互相呼唤的星星是非常常见的，除非一个人陷入愤怒，通常会有一个再见。

So it seems as if it is an intentional decision in scripted shows and films. But why would screenwriters do this? One reason could be to add tension. If the conversation is between romantic partners, not saying “bye” could create distance between them. But goodbyes – like the ones dramatically uttered by Sex and the City’s Carrie Bradshaw, breathing down her landline to Mr Big – can be equally suspenseful.

所以它似乎是脚本表演和电影中的有意决定。但为什么编剧会这样做？一个原因可以是增加张力。如果谈话是浪漫伙伴之间，而不是说“再见”可以在它们之间产生距离。但再见 - 就像性别和城市的嘉莉布拉德肖一样戏剧性地说，呼吸着大型固定电话到大的陆地 - 可以同样悬疑。

The disappearance of phone call farewells has probably got more to do with something much more boring: time management. Most of the time pleasantries don’t significantly further a story and, particularly when TV shows and films aren’t made for streaming, writers are very pressed for time. When a show is 23 minutes long, every second is precious and getting straight to the point is essential. A quick Google of how to write dialogue shows that “avoid small talk” is on almost every how-to list.

电话告别的消失可能更多地与更无聊的东西有关：时间管理。大多数时间令人愉快的兴趣不会进一步进一步解决故事，特别是当电视节目和电影不是为了流媒体而言，作家非常按下。当一个节目是23分钟时，每秒都是珍贵，直接到这一点是必不可少的。快速谷歌如何编写对话表明，“避免小话识”几乎是几乎所有的方式列表。

Goodbyes being sidelined from our screens has become a major hang-up for detail-conscious viewers. It’s an instant, awkward reminder that the conversation we are watching is completely scripted. Looking wider, this feels like a sign that even in the streaming era, where running times are more flexible and we inhale content like tubes of paprika Pringles, keeping the pace of a story and holding our attention is more important than pretending everything is real. And that’s a bad call, if you ask me.

从我们的屏幕中缺走的再见已成为细节意识观众的重大挂断。这是一个瞬间，尴尬的提醒说我们正在观看的谈话是完全脚本的。看起来更广泛，感觉甚至在流媒体时代，运行时间更加灵活，我们吸入了辣椒粉的管道等内容，保持故事的步伐并使我们的注意力比假装一切都更重要。如果你问我，这是一个糟糕的电话。

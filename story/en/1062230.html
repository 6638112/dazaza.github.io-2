Jump to navigation Jump to search The Einstein–Szilárd letter was a letter written by Leó Szilárd and signed by Albert Einstein that was sent to the United States President Franklin D. Roosevelt on August 2, 1939. Written by Szilárd in consultation with fellow Hungarian physicists Edward Teller and Eugene Wigner, the letter warned that Germany might develop atomic bombs and suggested that the United States should start its own nuclear program. It prompted action by Roosevelt, which eventually resulted in the Manhattan Project developing the first atomic bombs.

Otto Hahn and Fritz Strassmann reported the discovery of uranium fission in the January 6, 1939 issue of Die Naturwissenschaften, and Lise Meitner identified it as nuclear fission in the February 11, 1939 issue of Nature. This generated intense interest among physicists. Danish physicist Niels Bohr brought the news to the United States, and the U.S. opened the Fifth Washington Conference on Theoretical Physics with Enrico Fermi on January 26, 1939. The results were quickly corroborated by experimental physicists, most notably Fermi and John R. Dunning at Columbia University. [1]

Otto Hahn和Fritz Strassmann报道了1939年1月6日铀裂变的发现，1939年2月11日1939年2月11日本质上将其确定为核裂变。这种生成了物理学家之间的强烈兴趣。丹麦物理学尼尔斯·博恩将新闻向美国带来了新闻，美国于1939年1月26日将第五名华盛顿理论物理学与Enrico Fermi开展了第五名华盛顿会议。结果迅速被实验物理学家迅速证明费米和约翰·鲁宁哥伦比亚大学。 [1]

Hungarian physicist Leó Szilárd was living in the United States at the time and realized that the neutron-driven fission of heavy atoms could be used to create a nuclear chain reaction which could yield vast amounts of energy for electric power generation or atomic bombs. He had first formulated such an idea in 1933 upon reading Ernest Rutherford's disparaging remarks about generating power from his team's 1932 experiment using protons to split lithium. However, Szilárd had not been able to achieve a neutron-driven chain reaction with neutron-rich light atoms. In theory, if the number of secondary neutrons produced in a neutron-driven chain reaction was greater than one, then each such reaction could trigger multiple additional reactions, producing an exponentially increasing number of reactions. [2]

匈牙利物理学家莱霍斯·斯齐拉当时居住在美国，意识到重型原子的中子驱动裂变可用于制造核链反应，该反应可以为发电或原子弹产生大量的能量。他在阅读Ernest Rutherford＆＃39;脱离言论中首先在1933年举行了关于从他的团队产生权力的贬低言论，使用质子来分裂锂。然而，Szilárd未能实现与富中子的光原子的中子驱动的链反应。理论上，如果在中子驱动链反应中产生的二级中子的数量大于1，则每个这样的反应可以引发多个另外的反应，产生指数增加的反应。 [2]

Szilárd teamed up with Fermi to build a nuclear reactor from natural uranium at Columbia University, where George B. Pegram headed the physics department. There was disagreement about whether fission was produced by uranium-235, which made up less than one percent of natural uranium, or the more abundant uranium-238 isotope, as Fermi maintained. Fermi and Szilárd conducted a series of experiments and concluded that a chain reaction in natural uranium could be possible if they could find a suitable neutron moderator. They found that the hydrogen atoms in water slowed neutrons but tended to capture them. Szilárd then suggested using carbon as a moderator. They then needed large quantities of carbon and uranium to create a reactor. Szilárd was convinced that they would succeed if they could get the materials. [3]

Szilárd与费米合作，从哥伦比亚大学的天然铀建造核反应堆，乔治B.PEGRAGE领导了物理部门。铀-235是否产生裂变的分歧，其占天然铀的百分之不到的百分之一，或铀-238同位素的百分比，作为费米维持。 Fermi和Szilárd进行了一系列实验，得出结论，如果它们可以找到合适的中子调节剂，则可能有可能在天然铀中的链反应。他们发现水中的氢原子放缓，但倾向于捕获它们。然后使用碳作为主持人来建议szilárd。然后他们需要大量的碳和铀来制造反应器。如果他们能得到材料，Szilárd确信他们会成功。 [3]

Szilárd was concerned that German scientists might also attempt this experiment. German nuclear physicist Siegfried Flügge published two influential articles on the exploitation of nuclear energy in 1939. [4] [5] After discussing this prospect with fellow Hungarian physicist Eugene Wigner, they decided that they should warn the Belgians, as the Belgian Congo was the best source of uranium ore. Wigner suggested that Albert Einstein might be a suitable person to do this, as he knew the Belgian Royal Family. [6]

Szilárd担心德国科学家也可能尝试这个实验。德国核物理学家Siegfriedflügge于1939年出版了有关核能开采的两篇有影响力的文章。[4] [5]在与匈牙利物理学家尤金瓦杰的同伴讨论这一前景后，他们决定他们应该警告比利时，因为比利时刚果是铀矿石最好的来源。 Wigner建议阿尔伯特爱因斯坦可能是一个合适的人，因为他知道比利时王室。 [6]

On July 12, 1939, Szilárd and Wigner drove in Wigner's car to Cutchogue on New York's Long Island, where Einstein was staying. [7] When they explained about the possibility of atomic bombs, Einstein replied: "Daran habe ich gar nicht gedacht" ("I did not even think about that"). [8] Szilárd dictated a letter in German to the Belgian Ambassador to the United States. Wigner wrote it down, and Einstein signed it. At Wigner's suggestion, they also prepared a letter for the State Department explaining what they were doing and why, giving it two weeks to respond if it had any objections. [7]

1939年7月12日，Szilárd和Wigner开车在Wigner＆＃39;在纽约举行的纽约和＃39;长岛上的长岛屿，在那里爱因斯坦留下来。 [7]当他们解释了原子弹的可能性，爱因斯坦回答说：＆＃34;达兰哈布·赫卡·吉西·吉西·哈德（＆＃34;我甚至没有想到那个＆＃34;）。 [8]Szilárd为比利时大使到美国的德语来说。 Wigner向下写下，而爱因斯坦签了它。在Wigner＆＃39; S的建议中，他们还为国家部门准备了一封信，解释了他们所做的事情以及为什么，如果有任何反对意见，请回应这两周。 [7]

This still left the problem of getting government support for uranium research. Another friend of Szilárd's, the Austrian economist Gustav Stolper, suggested approaching Alexander Sachs, who had access to President Franklin D. Roosevelt. Sachs told Szilárd that he had already spoken to the President about uranium, but that Fermi and Pegram had reported that the prospects for building an atomic bomb were remote. He told Szilárd that he would deliver the letter, but suggested that it come from someone more prestigious. For Szilárd, Einstein was again the obvious choice. [6] Sachs and Szilárd drafted a letter riddled with spelling errors and mailed it to Einstein. [9]

这仍然留下了政府支持铀研究的问题。 Szilárd＆＃39;奥地利经济学家Gustav Tolper的另一个朋友，建议接近亚历山大·萨克斯，他可以访问富兰克林D.罗斯福总统。萨克斯告诉Szilárd，他已经与总统关于铀谈过的，但费米和PEGRAG据报道，建立原子弹的前景是遥远的。他告诉Szilárd，他将提供这封信，但建议它来自更为耻的人。对于Szilárd，爱因斯坦再次是明显的选择。 [6]Szilárd起草了一封信，凭借拼写错误并将其邮寄给爱因斯坦。 [9]

Szilárd also set out himself for Long Island again on August 2. Wigner was unavailable, so this time Szilárd co-opted another Hungarian physicist, Edward Teller, to do the driving. After receiving the draft, Einstein dictated the letter first in German. On returning to Columbia University, Szilárd dictated the letter in English to a young departmental stenographer, Janet Coatesworth. She later recalled that when Szilárd mentioned extremely powerful bombs, she "was sure she was working for a nut". [10] Ending the letter with "Yours truly, Albert Einstein" did nothing to alter this impression. Both the English letter and a longer explanatory letter were then posted to Einstein for him to sign. [10]

Szilárd也在8月再次向长岛举行了自己的长岛2. Wigner不可用，所以这次Szilárd共同选择了另一名匈牙利物理学家Edward Teller，要做驾驶。收到草案后，爱因斯坦在德语中首先提出了这封信。在回到哥伦比亚大学时，Szilárd将英语信中的信称为年轻的部门速记员，珍妮特Coatesworth。她后来回顾说，当Szilárd提到极其强大的炸弹时，她＆＃34;确保她正在为坚果＆＃34工作; [10]结束了与＃34的信件;你真的，阿尔伯特爱因斯坦＆＃34;没有什么可以改变这种印象。然后发布英文信和更长的解释性的信件给他签署的爱因斯坦。 [10]

"In the course of the last four months it has been made probable – through the work of Joliot in France as well as Fermi and Szilárd in America – that it may become possible to set up a nuclear chain reaction in a large mass of uranium, by which vast amounts of power and large quantities of new radium-like elements would be generated. Now it appears almost certain that this could be achieved in the immediate future.

＆＃34;在过去四个月的过程中，已经取得了可能 - 通过法国朱枯的工作以及美国的费米和斯基拉德 - 可能有可能在大量质量中建立核链式反应将产生大量功率和大量新的镭的像素状元素。现在它似乎似乎肯定可以在立即实现这一点。

This new phenomenon would also lead to the construction of bombs, and it is conceivable – though much less certain – that extremely powerful bombs of a new type may thus be constructed. A single bomb of this type, carried by boat and exploded in a port, might very well destroy the whole port together with some of the surrounding territory. However, such bombs might very well prove to be too heavy for transportation by air." [11]

这种新现象也会导致炸弹的构建，可以想到 - 尽管如此，可以制造极其强大的新型炸弹。这种类型的单一炸弹，乘船携带并在港口爆炸，可能与一些周围的领域一起破坏整个港口。然而，这种炸弹可能非常妥善被证明过于沉重的空气运输。＆＃34; [11]

"I understand that Germany has actually stopped the sale of uranium from the Czechoslovakian mines which she has taken over. That she should have taken such early action might perhaps be understood on the ground that the son of the German Under-Secretary of State, von Weizsäcker, is attached to the Kaiser-Wilhelm-Institut in Berlin where some of the American work on uranium is now being repeated." [11]

＆＃34;我知道德国实际上已经停止了她接管的捷克斯洛伐克地雷的铀销售。她应该采取这种早期的行动可能会在理由上理解，德国国务卿冯威迪斯·普勒的儿子附属于柏林的Kaiser-Wilhelm-Institut，其中一些美国在铀的工作现在重复。＆＃34; [11]

At the time of the letter, the estimated material necessary for a fission chain reaction was several tons. Seven months later a breakthrough in Britain would estimate the necessary critical mass to be less than 10 kilograms, making delivery of a bomb by air a possibility. [12]

在信中，裂变链反应所需的估计材料是几吨。七个月后，英国的突破将估计必要的临界质量少于10公斤，通过空气来交付炸弹。 [12]

The Einstein–Szilárd letter was signed by Einstein and posted back to Szilárd, who received it on August 9. [10] Szilárd gave both the short and long letters, along with a letter of his own, to Sachs on August 15. Sachs asked the White House staff for an appointment to see President Roosevelt, but before one could be set up, the administration became embroiled in a crisis due to Germany's invasion of Poland, which started World War II. [13] Sachs delayed his appointment until October so that the President would give the letter due attention, securing an appointment on October 11. On that date he met with the President, the President's secretary, Brigadier General Edwin "Pa" Watson, and two ordnance experts, Army Lieutenant Colonel Keith F. Adamson and Navy Commander Gilbert C. Hoover. Roosevelt summed up the conversation as: "Alex, what you are after is to see that the Nazis don't blow us up." [14]

爱因斯坦-Szilárd信由Einstein签署，并张贴回Szilárd，他们于8月9日收到它。[10]Szilárd在8月15日向Sachs提供了短信，以及他自己的一封信。萨克斯问道白宫员工预约看罗斯福总统，但在一个可以建立之前，由于德国的入侵，政府在德国的侵犯时刺激了危机。 [13]萨克斯延迟了他的任命，直到10月，所以总统会给截至10月11日的任命给予截至的委任。在那个日期，他与总统，总统兼第三届德文兼埃德文委员会委员会委员会召开会议。 ; PA＆＃34; Watson和两个军械专家，陆军中校上校基塞F. adamson和海军指挥官Gilbert C. Hoover。罗斯福总结了谈话：＆＃34;亚历克斯，你以后的是什么，是看纳粹＆＃39;＆＃34; [14]

"I found this data of such import that I have convened a Board consisting of the head of the Bureau of Standards and a chosen representative of the Army and Navy to thoroughly investigate the possibilities of your suggestion regarding the element of uranium." [15]

＆＃34;我发现了这种进口的数据，即我召开了由标准局负责人和军和海军的选定代表组成的董事会，以彻底调查您对铀因素的建议的可能性。＆ ＃34; [15]

Einstein sent two more letters to Roosevelt, on March 7, 1940, and April 25, 1940, calling for action on nuclear research. Szilárd drafted a fourth letter for Einstein's signature that urged the President to meet with Szilárd to discuss policy on nuclear energy. Dated March 25, 1945, it did not reach Roosevelt before his death on April 12, 1945. [11]

爱因斯坦于1940年3月7日和1940年4月25日致电核查派遣两封罗斯福。 Szilárd起草了爱因斯坦的第四封信＆＃39;签署的签名，敦促总统会见Szilárd，讨论核能政策。 1945年3月25日日期日，在1945年4月12日之前没有达到罗斯福。[11]

Roosevelt decided that the letter required action, and authorized the creation of the Advisory Committee on Uranium. The committee was chaired by Lyman James Briggs, the Director of the Bureau of Standards (currently the National Institute of Standards and Technology), with Adamson and Hoover as its other members. It convened for the first time on October 21. The meeting was also attended by Fred L. Mohler from the Bureau of Standards, Richard B. Roberts of the Carnegie Institution of Washington, and Szilárd, Teller and Wigner. Adamson was skeptical about the prospect of building an atomic bomb, but was willing to authorize $6,000 ($100,000 in current USD) for the purchase of uranium and graphite for Szilárd and Fermi's experiment. [16]

罗斯福决定这封信所需行动，并授权建立铀咨询委员会。委员会由标准局（目前国家标准和技术研究所）主任Lyman James Briggs主持，亚当申和胡佛是其其他成员。它于10月21日首次召集。来自标准局的Fred L. Mohler，华盛顿卡内基机构和Szilárd，出纳员和Wigner的弗雷德L. Mohler也参加了会议。亚当森对建设原子弹的前景持怀疑态度持怀疑态度，但愿意授权购买铀和费米和费米的铀和石墨的6,000美元（目前的美元兑美元）。 [16]

The Advisory Committee on Uranium was the beginning of the US government's effort to develop an atomic bomb, but it did not vigorously pursue the development of a weapon. It was superseded by the National Defense Research Committee in 1940, [17] and then the Office of Scientific Research and Development in 1941. [18] The Frisch–Peierls memorandum and the British Maud Reports eventually prompted Roosevelt to authorize a full-scale development effort in January 1942. [19] The work of fission research was taken over by the United States Army Corps of Engineers's Manhattan District in June 1942, which directed an all-out bomb development program known as the Manhattan Project. [20]

铀咨询委员会是美国政府的开始，努力发展原子弹，但它没有大力追求武器的发展。它在1940年的国防研究委员会取代了[17]，然后在1941年的科研与发展办公室。[18]弗里奇 - 佩尔尔备忘录和英国莫德报告最终促使罗斯福授权全面发展1942年1月的努力。[19] 1942年6月由美国陆军军团的森林军团接管了裂变研究的工作，该地区曼哈顿区曼哈顿地区被称为曼哈顿项目的全面炸弹开发项目。 [20]

Einstein did not work on the Manhattan Project. The Army and Vannevar Bush denied him the work clearance needed in July 1940, saying his pacifist leanings and celebrity made him a security risk. [21] At least one source states that Einstein did clandestinely contribute some equations to the Manhattan Project. [22] Einstein was allowed to work as a consultant to the United States Navy's Bureau of Ordnance. [23] [24] He had no knowledge of the atomic bomb's development, and no influence on the decision for the bomb to be dropped. [11] [21] According to Linus Pauling, Einstein later regretted signing the letter because it led to the development and use of the atomic bomb in combat, adding that Einstein had justified his decision because of the greater danger that Nazi Germany would develop the bomb first. [25] In 1947 Einstein told Newsweek magazine that "had I known that the Germans would not succeed in developing an atomic bomb, I would have done nothing." [21] [26]

爱因斯坦没有在曼哈顿项目上工作。陆军和vannevar布什否认了他1940年7月所需的工作清关，称他的和平主义倾向和名人使他成为安全风险。 [21]至少一个来源指出，爱因斯坦确实秘密地为曼哈顿项目贡献了一些方程。 [22]爱因斯坦被允许作为美国海军委员会的顾问。 [23] [24]他没有了解原子弹和＃39的发展，对炸弹的决定没有影响。 [11] [21]据林斯·鲍林介绍，爱因斯坦后来遗憾地签署了这封信，因为它导致了战斗中原子弹的发展和利用，并补充说，因为纳粹德国将发展的危险更大，因此是他的决定首先炸弹。 [25]在1947年，爱因斯坦告诉新闻公司，＆＃34;据我所知，德国人不会成功发展原子弹，我什么都没有。＆＃34; [21] [26]

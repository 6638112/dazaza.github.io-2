Middle-Age Crisis: The Surprising Story of Medieval Science
中年危机：中世纪科学的惊人故事

2020-11-25 03:52:02

We can probably blame George and Ira Gershwin. It was that brilliant duo who, in 1937, penned the memorable lyric ‘They all laughed at Christopher Columbus when he said the world was round.’ The song has been recorded by at least 15 artists over the years, from Fred Astaire to Lady Gaga, and is embedded in the consciousness of the West.

我们可能会怪罪乔治和艾拉·格什温。正是那位出色的二人在1937年写下了令人难忘的抒情诗“当克里斯托弗·哥伦布说世界是圆的时，他们都笑了。”多年来，从弗雷德·阿斯特尔（Fred Astaire）到Lady Gaga，至少有15位艺术家录制了这首歌。 ，并嵌入在西方的意识中。

But its headline message — medieval people are stupid — is total nonsense. No one, as Professor Seb Falk points out in this brilliant study of medieval astronomy and learning, ever disbelieved the world was round, and medieval people were far cleverer than they get credit for. Half the population, for one thing, he says, was literate.

但是它的头条信息-中世纪的人们是愚蠢的-完全是胡说八道。正如Seb Falk教授在对中世纪天文学和学习的出色研究中指出的那样，没人相信世界是圆形的，而中世纪的人们却比他们应得的更加聪明。他说，一半的人口识字。

The idea that the Middle Ages was a time of superstition, brutality, short lives, nonstop dysentery and a retreat from rationality has many promoters. This slander is repeated in Pulp Fiction, when Marsellus says to his hillbilly attackers: ‘I’m gonna get medieval on yo’ ass.’ The myth has its roots in the Reformation, when Puritan reformers sought to discredit the 900 years that preceded them as a fog of ignorance. We see the same notion in the 18th century, in Pope’s celebrated couplet about Isaac Newton: ‘Nature and nature’s laws lay hid in night./ God said, Let Newton be! and all was light.’

中世纪是迷信，残暴，短暂的生命，不间断的痢疾和对理性的退缩的时代，这一观念得到了许多推动。当Marsellus对他的乡土袭击者说：“我要在中世纪的屁股上变成中世纪”时，在《低俗小说》中重复了这种诽谤。神话的根源是宗教改革，当时清教徒的改革者试图抹黑他们之前的900年。作为无知之雾。在18世纪，在教皇关于艾萨克·牛顿的著名对联中，我们看到了相同的概念：“自然与自然法则藏在深夜。” /上帝说，放牛顿！一切都很光明。’

In actual fact, as Falk points out over a riveting though occasionally knotty 400 pages, the so-called Dark Ages were characterized by intense scholarly inquiry into nature and nature’s laws. Like the ancient philosophers before them, the medievals — generally the monks, who had the time for this stuff — studied the motions of the planets and stars and moon and sun, produced highly complex tables and wrote voluminous treatises.

实际上，正如Falk指出的那样，尽管铆接有时会令人费解，但共400页，但所谓的“黑暗时代”的特点是学者对自然和自然规律进行了激烈的研究。像他们之前的古代哲学家一样，中世纪的人们（通常是僧侣，他们有时间来研究这些东西）研究行星，恒星，月亮和太阳的运动，制作出高度复杂的桌子并撰写了大量论文。

They also developed, and possibly perfected, the ancient calculating instrument, the astrolabe. Made famous by Chaucer’s work, A Treatise on the Astrolabe, astrolabes were the iPads of their day, beautiful portable devices made for measuring the heavens and taking coordinates. Fitted with a viewing tube, the astrolabe was a sort of map of the cosmos combined with an annual calendar with which you could not only tell the time but also find the position of the sun. It was this viewing tube that led to a gag in Chaucer’s ‘Shipman’s Tale’, where a lusty, astrolabe-owning monk attempts a seduction: ‘Let us dine as soon as that ye may, for by my cylinder ’tis prime of day.’

他们还开发并可能完善了古代的计算工具星盘。乔uc的作品《星盘上的论文》而闻名，星盘是当时的iPad，是用于测量天体和获取坐标的精美便携式设备。装有观察管的星盘是一种结合了年度日历的宇宙图，您不仅可以分辨出时间，还可以找到太阳的位置。正是这个观察筒导致乔uc的《船夫的故事》中发生了混乱，在那里一个有欲望，拥有星盘的和尚试图引诱他：“让我们尽快就餐，因为我的缸是最重要的。 '

The scientists of the Middle Ages, says Falk, also invented the mechanical clock. They toiled away at the idea and in 1273 seemed to have cracked it: a mechanical clock was installed at the Norwich Cathedral priory, and soon after clocks appeared in London, Westminster and Oxford. Falk reserves particular praise for a terrifically complex astronomical clock at St Albans Abbey.

福克说，中世纪的科学家们还发明了机械钟。他们辛辛苦苦地想到了这个主意，并在1273年似乎破解了这个主意：在诺里奇大教堂的小修道院安装了机械钟，不久之后钟就出现在伦敦，威斯敏斯特和牛津。福克（Falk）对圣奥尔本斯修道院（St Albans Abbey）复杂的天文钟特别赞扬。

In support of his thesis, Falk tells the story of a monk named Eilmer at Malmesbury Abbey in Wiltshire who invented the glider around 1100:

为了支持他的论点，福克讲述了威尔特郡马姆斯伯里修道院的一位名叫埃米尔的和尚的故事，他在1100年左右发明了滑翔机：

‘He fastened wings to his hands and feet and leapt from a tall tower. According to the abbey chronicle, he flew more than 100 meters, before a gust of wind caused him to fall and break his legs… Eilmer piloted an experimental glider, not wholly without success, almost 500 years before Leonardo da Vinci sketched a similar flying machine.’

‘他将翅膀固定在手和脚上，并从高塔上跳下。根据修道院纪事，他飞了100多米，然后一阵风​​使他摔倒并摔断了腿……埃米尔（Eilmer）驾驶了实验性滑翔机，并非完全没有成功，差不多是500年前，莱昂纳多·达·芬奇（Leonardo da Vinci）绘制了类似的飞行器。”

Far from being isolated in ignorant bubbles, medieval scholars from all over the known world worked with each other. The Light Ages shows how Islamic, Jewish and Christian scholars were constantly borrowing ideas from one another. One well known example is the monks’ reading of Islamic translations of Aristotle. Christians loved the ancient Greek philosopher.

来自已知世界的中世纪学者远没有被愚昧的泡沫所隔离，而是彼此合作。光明时代表明伊斯兰，犹太和基督教学者是如何不断相互借鉴思想的。一个众所周知的例子是和尚们阅读亚里士多德的伊斯兰译本。基督徒喜欢古希腊哲学家。

True, they believed in God, and science and religion were intertwined. But does that make them superstitious and ignorant? As well as the clock and the astrolabe, the medievals invented the university, where the ancient curricula of the Trivium and the Quadrivium were pursued. It was a progressive time in other ways. We might also mention the guilds, hospitals, cathedrals, light-filled art (as David Hockney has shown in his book Secret Knowledge), a short working week and a ban on usury. I agree with Falk. Dear, naive Steven Pinker, apostle of progress and disrespecter of the past, is wrong. We need to give more respect to the giants of the Middle Ages on whose shoulders we stand.

是的，他们相信上帝，科学与宗教交织在一起。但这是否会使他们迷信而无知？除了钟表和星盘外，中世纪人还发明了大学，在此大学中研究了Trivium和Quadrivium的古代课程。在其他方面，这是一个进步的时期。我们可能还会提到行会，医院，大教堂，充满光彩的艺术品（正如David Hockney在他的《秘密知识》一书中所展示的那样），短暂的工作周和禁止高利贷。我同意福克。亲爱的，天真的史蒂文·平克（Steven Pinker），进步的使者和对过去的漠视是错误的。我们需要更加尊重我们所站在的中世纪巨人。

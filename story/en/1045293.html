New Evidence Points to Riot Conspiracy
防暴阴谋的新证据

2021-01-20 13:08:28

WASHINGTON — In the days leading up to the Jan. 6 riot, Thomas Edward Caldwell, an apparent leader of the far-right Oath Keepers, had a message for the militia members he had organized to mobilize against Congress: “This kettle is set to boil.”

华盛顿—在1月6日骚乱发生之前的几天，托马斯·爱德华·卡尔德威尔（Thomas Edward Caldwell）显然是极右翼誓约者的领袖，向他组织动员起来反对国会的民兵成员传达了一条信息：“熬。”

Court documents unsealed on Tuesday said Mr. Caldwell, a 66-year-old from rural Virginia, advised the others on Dec. 31, “It begins for real Jan 5 and 6 on Washington D.C. when we mobilize in the streets. Let them try to certify some crud on capitol hill with a million or more patriots in the streets.”

周二未公开的法庭文件说，来自弗吉尼亚州农村的66岁的考德威尔先生于12月31日向其他人提供了建议，“当我们在大街上动员时，它始于1月5日和6日在华盛顿特区。让他们尝试证明在国会山上有100万或更多爱国者的小伙子。”

Mr. Caldwell and two associates from Ohio — Donovan Crowl, 50, and Jessica Watkins, 38 — were charged with conspiracy to commit federal crimes. All three had admitted to invading the Capitol to reporters and were also identifiable in videos posted on social media.

考德威尔先生和俄亥俄州的两名同伙-50岁的多诺万·克劳（Donovan Crowl）和38岁的杰西卡·沃特金斯（Jessica Watkins）被指控串谋实施联邦罪行。所有这三个人都承认侵略了记者的国会大厦，并且在社交媒体上发布的视频中也可以识别。

The case revealed the first evidence of planning among a known militia group ahead of the day of chaotic mob violence. Investigators have said they are increasingly focused on right-wing extremist groups to determine whether any plotted aspects of the attack on the Capitol in advance, even as most of the rioters spontaneously stormed it.

该案揭示了在混乱的暴民暴力发生之前一个已知民兵组织进行计划的第一个证据。调查人员说，他们越来越关注右翼极端主义团体，以便提前确定是否有任何袭击国会大厦的阴谋方面，即使大多数暴乱者自发袭击了国会大厦。

Mr. Caldwell had advised militia members to stay in a particular Comfort Inn in Washington’s suburbs, according to messages cited in court documents, advising that it offered a good base to “hunt at night” — apparently meaning looking for antifa-style left-wing protesters to fight. Ms. Watkins apparently rented a room there under an assumed name, an F.B.I. agent said.

根据法院文件中引用的消息，卡尔德威尔先生曾建议民兵留在华盛顿郊区的一家特殊的Comfort Inn酒店，并建议该基地为“夜间狩猎”提供了良好的基地-显然意味着寻找反法式的左翼抗议者打架。沃特金斯女士显然在那儿以假名FBI租了一个房间。代理商说。

Mr. Caldwell appeared virtually in federal court in Virginia on Tuesday, pledging to fight the charges against him. In arguing for release on bond, Mr. Caldwell pointed to his age as well as underlying medical issues and the threat of the coronavirus, noting that he relied on a machine at night to help with sleep apnea. A judge declined the request.

考德威尔先生周二实际上出现在弗吉尼亚州的联邦法院，誓言要对付他的指控。在争辩释放时，卡尔德威尔先生指出了他的年龄以及潜在的医疗问题和冠状病毒的威胁，并指出他在晚上依靠一台机器来帮助睡眠呼吸暂停。法官拒绝了该请求。

Federal investigators also recovered audio recordings of Ms. Watkins’ voice during the riot from the Zello cellphone app, which acts like a walkie-talkie, speaking to other people thought to be Oath Keepers. “We have a good group,” she said early in the riot, according to the charging document. “We have about 30-40 of us. We are sticking together and sticking to the plan.” An unknown male responded, “We’ll see you soon, Jess. Airborne.”

联邦调查人员还从Zello手机应用程序中恢复了骚乱期间沃特金斯女士声音的录音，该手机应用程序像对讲机，与其他被认为是誓言守护者的人交谈。根据指控文件，她在骚乱初期说：“我们有一个很好的团队。” “我们大约有30至40个人。我们团结一致并坚持计划。”一位不知名的男性回答：“我们很快会再见，杰西。空降。”

An unidentified man said later, “You are executing citizen’s arrest. Arrest this assembly; we have probable cause for acts of treason, election fraud.” Ms. Watkins responded that she and others were under the main dome of the Capitol, and another unknown male voice encouraged her to continue, saying this was what they “trained for.”

一名身份不明的男子稍后说：“您正在执行公民逮捕。逮捕该集会；我们可能有叛国，选举舞弊的原因。”沃特金斯女士回应说，她和其他人都在国会大厦的主要圆顶下，另一位不知名的男性声音鼓励她继续前进，说这是他们“所训练的”。

The group they are accused of being part of, the Oath Keepers, is a far-right militia-style organization founded by military and law enforcement veterans that professes to believe that a shadowy globalist cabal is plotting to take away Americans’ rights.

他们被指控是誓言守护者组织的成员，是一个由军事和执法经验丰富的人创立的极右翼民兵式组织，自称相信一个阴暗的全球主义者阴谋集团正在谋取剥夺美国人的权利。

Charging documents against the trio noted that amid the generally chaotic scene as the mob started to push its way into the Capitol, a group dressed in paramilitary gear and Oath Keepers paraphernalia stood out for its coordination. In one video, about 10 Oath Keepers in helmets “move in an organized and practiced fashion and force their way to the front of the crowd gathered around a door to the U.S. Capitol.”

控告三人的文件指出，在暴乱开始进入国会大厦的混乱局面中，一个穿着准军事装备的组织和誓约者用具突出了其协调性。在一个视频中，约有10名戴着头盔的誓言守卫者“以一种有组织和实践的方式移动，并挤到聚集在美国国会大厦门附近的人群中。”

Scenes of people with military training marching shoulder to shoulder have horrified Pentagon leaders, who fear that weapons and tactics training once provided by the armed forces were co-opted. At least 12 National Guard members have been removed from duties related to the inauguration, two of them for possible links to right-wing extremist movements, Defense Department officials said on Tuesday.

五角大楼的领导人震惊地接受了军事训练的场面，五角大楼的领导人感到恐惧，他们担心一旦武装部队提供武器和战术训练，就会被选择。国防部官员周二说，至少有12名国民警卫队成员被免职与就职典礼有关的职务，其中两名是可能与右翼极端主义运动有联系的。

Others arrested in the riots have been linked to the Oath Keepers, though they have not been accused of being part of an organized conspiracy.

在骚乱中被捕的其他人与宣誓者有联系，尽管他们没有被指控是有组织的阴谋的一部分。

At a hearing in Texas last week for Larry Brock, who was photographed on the Senate floor carrying flexible handcuffs, a prosecutor cited a Facebook post linking him to the Oath Keepers. Mr. Brock, a retired Air Force officer, told The New Yorker that he had found the flex cuffs on the ground and did not intend to use them.

上周在德克萨斯州举行的一次听证会上，拉里·布罗克（Larry Brock）在参议​​院的地板上被拍了一下，上面戴着柔软的手铐，检察官引用了一条Facebook帖子，将他与宣誓者联系起来。退休的空军军官布罗克先生告诉《纽约客》，他在地面上发现了可弯曲的袖带，并且不打算使用它们。

And a criminal complaint filed Saturday against Jon Ryan Schaffer, a guitarist for the heavy metal band Iced Earth, accused of being among rioters who sprayed Capitol Police with “bear spray,” said he has long held “far-right extremist views” and suggested that he is a member of the Oath Keepers.

周六，针对重金属乐队“冰山”的吉他手乔恩·瑞安·沙弗（Jon Ryan Schaffer）提起刑事诉讼，被指控是向国会大厦警察喷洒“熊喷剂”的暴徒中的一员。他是誓言守护者的成员。

The Oath Keepers — named for members’ professed intent to uphold their oaths to protect the Constitution — have no geographical center, with chapters scattered in Oregon, Montana, Florida, Texas, Arizona, Ohio, New York and elsewhere. Some of its ideology overlaps with the so-called Three Percenters, another paramilitary-style right-wing movement that seeks to attract veterans.

誓言守护者（以成员自称捍卫宪法保护宪法的誓词而得名）没有地理中心，其章节分散在俄勒冈州，蒙大拿州，佛罗里达州，德克萨斯州，亚利桑那州，俄亥俄州，纽约州和其他地方。它的某些意识形态与所谓的“三个中心”重叠，这是另一种准军事式的右翼运动，旨在吸引退伍军人。

The Oath Keepers have a more formal structure, including bylaws and dues. Although the organization has claimed to have around 35,000 members, the Anti-Defamation League, which tracks extremist groups, estimated several years ago that its membership is more like several thousand.

宣誓者有一个更正式的结构，包括章程和会费。尽管该组织声称拥有约35,000名成员，但追踪极端主义组织的反诽谤联盟在几年前估计其成员人数更是达到了几千。

The organization was founded in 2009 by Stewart Rhodes, 55, a disbarred Montana lawyer who attended Yale Law School and once worked as an aide to the former Representative Ron Paul, the Texas libertarian. Its members tend to anticipate a coming new civil war.

该组织由55岁的斯图尔特·罗兹（Stewart Rhodes）于2009年成立，他是蒙大拿州的一名律师，曾就职于耶鲁法学院，曾担任德克萨斯州自由主义者前代表罗恩·保罗的助手。其成员倾向于预料即将到来的新内战。

“It was motivated by the perception that the federal government was tyrannical and that everyday Americans needed to be ready to resist that tyrannical government when it came to the door,” said Sam Jackson, who published a book on the organization last year and is a professor at the University of Albany specializing in homeland security issues.

萨姆·杰克逊（Sam Jackson）表示：“这是因为人们认为联邦政府是专横的，而每天美国人都需要准备好抵制这个专横的政府。”奥尔巴尼大学教授，专门研究国土安全问题。

The Oath Keepers were involved in confrontations with the federal government over land issues in the West, including the showdown between the Bundy family and the federal government in Oregon in 2014.

誓约守护者参与了与联邦政府在西方土地问题上的对抗，包括邦迪家族与联邦政府在俄勒冈州之间的对决。2014年。

And during the unrest that year after the police killing of a Black man in Ferguson, Mo., heavily armed Oath Keepers patrolled the streets and claimed to protect businesses, but discontinued after criticism from local law enforcement.

在警察在密苏里州弗格森（Ferguson）杀害一名黑人的那一年，动乱期间，全副武装的誓言守卫者在街上巡逻并声称要保护企业，但因当地执法部门的批评而中止。

The group has also been among those who sent armed vigilantes to patrol the border with Mexico or promoted such efforts by others.

该团体也是派遣武装警卫人员在墨西哥边境巡逻或促进其他人努力的人之一。

In the weeks leading up to the Capitol assault, Oath Keepers issued heated calls to action. Shortly after the election, a lengthy message attributed to Mr. Rhodes circulated on far-right websites urging volunteers to converge on Washington for a “Stop the Steal” rally. Oath Keepers, it said, would provide security, including “some of our most skilled special warfare veterans standing by armed, just outside D.C.”

在国会大厦袭击前的几周内，誓约者发出了激烈的行动呼吁。选举结束后不久，罗德先生在右翼网站上散发了一条冗长的信息，敦促志愿者们聚集在华盛顿参加“制止偷窃”集会。它说，誓言守卫者将提供安全保障，包括“在华盛顿特区郊外武装的一些我们最熟练的特种作战退伍军人”。

The riot inside the U.S. Capitol on Wednesday, Jan. 6, followed a rally at which President Trump made an inflammatory speech to his supporters, questioning the results of the election. Here’s a look at what happened and the ongoing fallout:

1月6日星期三，美国国会大厦内发生的骚乱是在特朗普集会上向支持者发表煽动性讲话的集会之后，对选举结果提出了质疑。以下是发生了什么以及正在进行的后果的调查：

As this video shows, poor planning and a restive crowd encouraged by President Trump set the stage for the riot.

如该视频所示，在特朗普总统的鼓励下，糟糕的计划和宁静的人群为这场暴动奠定了基础。

Several Trump administration officials, including cabinet members Betsy DeVos and Elaine Chao, announced that they were stepping down as a result of the riot.

包括内阁成员Betsy DeVos和Elaine Chao在内的几位特朗普政府官员宣布，由于骚乱而辞职。

Federal prosecutors have charged more than 70 people, including some who appeared in viral photos and videos of the riot. Officials expect to eventually charge hundreds of others.

联邦检察官已经起诉了70多人，其中包括一些在暴动的病毒照片和录像中出现的人。官员们预计最终将向其他数百人收取费用。

The House voted to impeach the president on charges of “inciting an insurrection” that led to the rampage by his supporters.

众议院投票谴责总统以“煽动暴动”罪名指控，导致其支持者横冲直撞。

While participants were instructed to remain disciplined, the message also included what it described as advice from an unidentified “patriot from Serbia,” who said in a speech transcript that Americans must replicate the uprising in his country 20 years ago that deposed Slobodan Milosevic:

在指示参与者保持纪律的同时，该消息还包括一位身份不明的“塞尔维亚爱国者”的建议，后者在演讲稿中说，美国人必须复制自己20年前罢免斯洛博丹·米洛舍维奇的起义：

“Millions gathered in our capital. There were no barricades strong enough to stop them, nor the police determined enough to stop them. Police and Military aligned with the people after few hours of fist-fight. We stormed the Parliament. And burned down fake state Television! WE WON!”

“数以百万计的人聚集在我们的首都。没有足以阻止他们的路障，也没有警方决心阻止他们。经过数小时的战斗，警察和军方与人民结盟。我们冲进了议会。并烧毁了伪造状态电视台！我们赢了！”

By the time plans were announced for a big rally on Jan. 6, the Oath Keepers’ entreaties had taken on a tone of urgency. An appeal for volunteers framed the event as a last chance to help “President Trump’s fight to defeat the enemies foreign and domestic who are attempting a coup.”

到1月6日宣布大规模集会的计划时，誓言守护者的恳求已变得紧迫。对志愿者的呼吁使该活动成为帮助“特朗普总统打败企图发动政变的外国和国内敌人的斗争”的最后机会。

Followers were urged to “prepare yourselves for whatever may come. Prepare your mind, body, and spirit for battle, and above all else, prepare to STAND!”

敦促追随者“为可能发生的一切做好准备。为战斗做好准备的思想，身体和精神，最重要的是，准备站起来！”

Mr. Rhodes was in Washington on Jan. 6 — a distinctive figure with his eye patch because he lost an eye to a gun accident. He is not believed to have entered the Capitol himself.

1月6日，罗德斯先生在华盛顿-眼神与众不同，因为他对一次枪击事故视而不见。不认为他本人已进入国会大厦。

Charging documents said Mr. Caldwell was “believed to have a leadership role” in the group. While Mr. Crowl addressed him as “Commander” in a message cited in court documents, his precise role remains murky.

收费文件显示，考尔德威尔先生“被认为在该组织中发挥领导作用”。克罗尔先生在法庭文件中引用的讯息中称他为“司令”，但他的确切角色仍然模糊。

Mr. Caldwell, however, appeared to think that Mr. Rhodes was doing too little to organize for the day. “I don’t know if Stewie has even gotten out his call to arms but its a little friggin late,” he wrote to Mr. Crowl, according to court papers. “This is one we are doing on our own. We will link up with the north carolina crew.”

但是，考德威尔先生似乎认为罗德斯先生在当天的组织工作中做得很少。根据法庭文件，他写信给克罗尔：“我不知道史蒂文是否甚至还没有伸手去拿武器，但迟了一点。” “这是我们自己做的。我们将与北卡罗来纳州的船员联系起来。”

Other charging documents identified Ms. Watkins, a bartender from Champaign County, Ohio, who was arrested on Monday, of being affiliated with the Oath Keepers. She said at the top of her account page on Parler, a social media network that attracted a right-wing following, that she is commanding officer “of the Ohio State Regular Militia,” which the complaint identified as “a local militia organization which is a dues-paying subset of the Oath Keepers.”

其他收费文件还指出，来自俄亥俄州占比恩县的调酒师沃特金斯女士于周一被捕，他与誓言守护者有关联。她在社交媒体网络Parler的帐户页面顶部说，她是右俄亥俄州追随者，她是“俄亥俄州立民兵组织”的指挥官，申诉被确定为“当地民兵组织，誓约守护者的应缴税子集。”

The F.B.I. identified Mr. Crowl, also from Champaign County, as a member of the same Ohio militia.

联邦调查局确定了克劳普先生（同样来自香槟县）是同一俄亥俄州民兵的成员。

The F.B.I. affidavit against all three described several sightings of them in video of the Capitol riot and their own social media postings boasting of storming the building.

联邦调查局对这三人的宣誓书在国会大厦暴动的视频和他们自己的社交媒体帖子中都在吹嘘这座建筑物。

For example, the charging document said, Mr. Caldwell sent a picture of the riot that evening to someone on Facebook Messenger, adding, “Us storming the castle” and “I am such an instigator!” He also wrote, “We need to do this at the local level. Lets storm the capitol in Ohio. Tell me when!”

例如，收费文件说，考德威尔先生当天晚上向Facebook Messenger上的某人发送了骚乱的照片，并添加了“我们冲进城堡”和“我是煽动者！”他还写道：“我们需要在地方一级做到这一点。让我们猛攻俄亥俄州的国会大厦。告诉我何时！”

A search of Ms. Watkins’ house after the riot — she was not there, and investigators cited evidence that she and Mr. Crowl had gone to stay with Mr. Caldwell — revealed paramilitary equipment like that used in the riot and instructions to build a bomb using bleach as a primary ingredient, the charging document said.

骚乱后对沃特金斯女士的房子进行了搜查-她当时不在那儿，调查人员援引了证据，证明她和克劳尔先生已与卡尔德威尔先生呆在一起-揭示了骚乱中使用的准军事装备以及建造突击队的指示装弹文件说，炸弹以漂白剂为主要成分。

Other well-known defendants in the Capitol attack were released on Tuesday pending trial at a five-hour string of hearings in Washington. Most were accused of misdemeanors and federal prosecutors did not seek to keep them detained.

国会大厦袭击事件中的其他知名被告于周二在华盛顿进行了为时五个小时的听证会，已被释放，等待审判。大多数人被指控犯有轻罪，联邦检察官并未试图将他们拘留。

Among those released were Adam Johnson, 36, of Florida, who was charged with stealing Speaker Nancy Pelosi’s lectern, and Josiah Colt, 34, of Idaho, who was filmed in a viral image dangling from a balcony of the Senate chamber.

被释放的有36岁的佛罗里达州的亚当·约翰逊（Adam Johnson），他被指控偷窃发言人南希·佩洛西（Nancy Pelosi）的讲台，以及现年34岁的爱达荷州的乔赛亚·柯尔特（Josiah Colt），后者是在参议院会议室阳台上悬挂的病毒图像中拍摄的。

Two Virginia police officers accused of breaking into the Capitol, Jacob Fracker and Thomas Robertson, were released pending trial.

两名涉嫌闯入国会大厦的弗吉尼亚州警察雅各布·弗雷克（Jacob Fracker）和托马斯·罗伯森（Thomas Robertson）被释放，等待审判。

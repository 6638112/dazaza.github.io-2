Some US unemployment applicants say facial recognition models used by ID verification service ID.me are failing to identify them, locking them out of benefits
一些美国失业申请人表示身份证验证服务使用的面部识别模型id.me未能识别它们，将它们锁定摆脱福利

2021-06-19 22:38:59

People around the country are furious after being denied their unemployment benefits due to apparent problems with facial recognition technology that claims to prevent fraud.

由于面部识别技术的表观问题，在否认失业救济金后，全国各地的人们会愤怒。

Unemployment recipients have been complaining for months about the identity verification service ID.me, which uses a combination of biometric information and official documents to confirm that applicants are who they claim to be. The complaints reached another crescendo this week after Axios published a “deep dive” article about the threat of unemployment fraud based on statistics provided to the outlet by ID.me.

失业收件人对身份验证服务ID.ME的几个月抱怨，它使用生物信息和官方文件的组合来证实申请人是否声称是谁。在Axios发表了关于失业欺诈威胁的基于id.me的统计数据，本周，投诉达到了另一个Crescendo。

Some unemployment applicants have said that ID.me’s facial recognition models fail to properly identify them (generally speaking, facial recognition technology is notoriously less accurate for women and people of color). And after their applications were put on hold because their identity couldn’t be verified, many should-be beneficiaries have had to wait days or weeks to reach an ID.me “trusted referee” who could confirm what the technology couldn’t.

一些失业申请人表示，ID.Me的面部识别模式未能妥善识别它们（一般而言，面部识别技术对于妇女和颜色人民来说是众所周知的。在他们的申请被搁置之后，因为他们的身份无法核实，许多应该是受益者必须等待几天或几周才能达到id.me“可信赖的裁判”，他们可以证实技术无法做些什么。

On Twitter, there are dozens of complaints about ID.me per day, and local news articles all over the country have detailed the problem over the course of months. In California, 1.4 million unemployment beneficiary accounts were abruptly suspended on New Year’s Eve and the beneficiaries were required to re-verify their identity using ID.me, a process which many found difficult and resulted in them waiting for weeks to reactivate their accounts while they struggled to make ends meet.

在Twitter上，关于ID.Me每天有几十个投诉，而全国各地的本地新闻文章在几个月内详细介绍了问题。在加利福尼亚州，新年前夜突然暂停140万失业受益人账目，需要使用id.me重新验证其身份的受益人，这是许多人发现困难的过程，导致他们等待几周重新激活他们的帐户挣扎成结束。

In Colorado, benefit recipients who had no problem establishing their identity before ID.me took over were suddenly rejected and went months without receiving the payments they were eligible for.

在科罗拉多州，在ID.ME接管之前没有问题的福利收件人在id.me接管之前，突然被拒绝了，并且没有收到他们有资格的付款。

ID.me CEO Blake Hall told Motherboard that the company’s facial recognition technology does one-to-one matching—comparing one face against a picture of that same face (from a driver’s license, say)—whereas other applications of facial recognition attempt to find a match for a face in a large dataset of faces, known as one-to-many matching.

id.me首席执行官布莱克霍尔告诉主板，公司的面部识别技术做一对一的匹配 - 比较一张面对同一面孔的图片（从驾驶执照，比如说） - 核心面部识别试图找到的其他应用在面部大型数据集中的脸部匹配，称为一对多匹配。

“The algorithms used for Face Match operate ~99.9% efficacy,” Hall wrote in an email to Motherboard. “There is in fact no relationship between skin tone and Face Match failure on a 1:1 basis” according to a regression analysis the company performed.

“用于面部匹配的算法操作效果约为99.9％，”霍尔在电子邮件中写入主板。 “肤色与面部匹配失败的情况毫无关系A 1：1基础”根据公司执行的回归分析。

That doesn’t mesh with the experiences being shared on Twitter by people like Tim Weaver, a gig economy worker in Las Vegas who was suddenly cut off from his unemployment benefits in late March after ID.me failed to identify him.

这并不是在蒂姆维亚这样的人们在Twitter上分享的经验，拉斯维加斯的一名Gig经济职工在3月下旬突然从他的失业救济人员突然切断了Id.Me未能识别他。

Weaver told Motherboard that when he attempted to pass ID.me’s facial recognition test he held a phone in front of him in the instructed position but “it rejected it, didn’t give us a reason, just rejected it. It rejected it three times, and then it locked me out of the system.”

韦弗告诉主板，当他试图传递id.me的面部识别测试时，他在他面前举行了一部手机，在指示的位置，但“它拒绝了它，没有给我们一个理由，只是拒绝了它。它拒绝了三次，然后把我锁在系统中。“

Weaver said he attempted to contact the company’s customer support through its chat feature, which claims to provide assistance 24-hours a day, seven days a week. He tried numerous times at all hours of the day. He tried contacting the state of Nevada for help, but the employees there directed him back to ID.me.

韦弗表示，他试图通过聊天功能联系公司的客户支持，并声称每周七天提供24小时的援助。他在一天中的所有时间都尝试过多次。他试图联系内华达州以获取帮助，但员工将他返回id.me.

This went on for several weeks, Weaver said, until he tweeted a scathing criticism of the company, which then reached out and—after several more frustrating days—verified Weaver’s identity.

韦弗说，这已经过去几周了，直到他发布了对公司的紧张批评，然后达到了几个令人沮丧的日子验证了韦弗的身份。

Weaver went for three weeks without receiving his benefit. “I couldn’t pay bills,” he said. “Luckily I had enough food saved up so I didn’t have to worry about that. It’s just ridiculous.”

韦弗没有收到他的利益就去了三个星期。 “我无法支付账单，”他说。 “幸运的是，我有足够的食物，所以我不必担心这一点。这只是荒谬的。“

In his statement to Motherboard, Hall said that facial recognition failures are not a problem with the technology but with the people using it to verify their identity. “For example, if someone uploads a selfie that only shows half their face.”

在他对主板的声明中，霍尔表示，面部识别失败不是技术的问题，而是与人们使用它来验证他们的身份。 “例如，如果有人上传只有只显示他们脸部的自拍照。”

“We are unaware of eligible individuals who have not been able to verify their identity with ID.me,” he said, and “the wait time for a live video chat session right now is less than five minutes and it has been consistently under 30 minutes all week.”

“我们不知道符合条件的人，他们无法验证与id.me的身份，”他说，并“现在的等待时间现在不到五分钟，它一直不到30岁以下整个星期分钟。“

A quick search for tweets directed at ID.me shows reams of people locked out of their unemployment accounts by the company.

快速搜索id.me指示的推文显示了本公司锁定失业账户的人。

At least 21 states use ID.me to detect fraudulent benefit claims. The spread of the technology has coincided with an aggressive media blitz from Hall in which he has stoked fears about the crisis of unemployment fraud using some fluid statistics.

至少21个州使用ID.Me检测欺诈性福利索赔。该技术的传播恰逢来自大厅的积极媒体闪电师，其中他已经使用一些流体统计数据引起了对失业欺诈危机的恐惧。

In February, he told an Oregon TV station that unemployment fraud had cost the country $100 billion. Several weeks later, he told a Montana TV station that unemployment fraud had cost the country nearly $200 billion. The next month, he was telling a San Diego station that unemployment fraud would cost $300 billion. By the time ID.me reached Axios, it was $400 billion.

2月，他告诉俄勒冈州电视台，失业欺诈造成了1000亿美元的价格。几个星期后，他告诉蒙大拿电视台，失业欺诈的成本占该国近2000亿美元。下个月，他告诉圣地亚哥站，失业欺诈将花费3000亿美元。到了id.me到达Axios，这是400亿美元。

Hall told Motherboard that the Oregon TV station didn’t quote him directly—it did attribute the $100 billion estimate to him, though—and that he was consistent through January and February estimating the losses from unemployment fraud around $200 billion. “As we screened existing claimants in more states, we had more data points … In June, I estimated the loss over $400 [billion] in line with past estimates. All of those data points are consistent over time.”

霍尔告诉主板，俄勒冈电视台直接引用他 - 它确实归因于他的1000亿美元估计，而且他一直在1月和2月估计失业欺诈的损失约为2000亿美元。 “随着我们在更多州筛选现有索赔人，我们在6月份有更多的数据点......我估计超过4000万美元的损失，符合过去的估计。所有这些数据点随着时间的推移是一致的。“

Hall did not respond to Motherboard’s question about how ID.me calculated its estimates.

大厅没有回应主板关于id.me如何计算其估计的问题。

It’s possible that the rate of successful unemployment fraud is growing rapidly—hence Hall’s rising cost estimates—but ID.me also has a vested interest in depicting fraud as a major problem, which Hall has repeatedly done. It makes sense for a company that makes anti-fraud technology to study fraud; it's also fair for the general public to be skeptical of fraud statistics published by companies that would benefit from showing that fraud is a major problem.

失业欺诈的成功率迅速增长，因此大厅的成本估计 - 但id.me还对描绘欺诈作为一个主要问题的既得利益，这是一个大厅一再做的。这是一家对学习欺诈进行反欺诈技术的公司有意义;它＆＃39是公开的公开展会持怀疑态度，这些公司会受益于显示欺诈是一个主要问题。

Axios has now published two articles that have cited the company's numbers, and, on Twitter, Axios writer Felix Salmon suggested that fraud associated with expanded unemployment insurance during the pandemic has resulted in " quite possibly the largest theft of all time."

Axios现已发布了两篇文章，该文章＆＃39; S数字，以及在Twitter上，Axios作家Felix Salmon建议在大流行期间与扩大的失业保险相关联的欺诈行为导致＆＃34;很可能是所有时间的最大盗窃。＆＃34;

The Axios article has been widely criticized for using ID.me—a company with a financial incentive to inflate the rate of unemployment fraud—as its main source. And while many local news outlets have provided great coverage of the pain caused by delays due to ID.me’s system, others have run with stories similar to Axios’s, citing the company’s numbers. The Axios article was also immediately cited by Republicans in the House of Representatives as evidence of widespread defrauding of the U.S. government.

The U.S. Department of Labor says that between March and October 2020 it uncovered just $5.6 billion in potentially fraudulent unemployment payments. And while more recent data suggests the losses are actually higher, the agency estimated that they would be “into the tens of billions of dollars.”

美国工党部表示，在3月和10月20日之间，它在可能的欺诈性失业率下揭示了仅56亿美元。虽然最近的数据表明，损失实际上是更高的，但原子能机构估计他们将“进入数十亿美元。”

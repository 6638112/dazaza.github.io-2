Brexit Ruined Easter for Britain’s Chocolate Makers
Brexit毁了英国巧克力制造商的复活节

2021-04-04 04:42:04

LONDON — For the second year in a row, Easter will be a largely online affair, with socially distanced egg hunts and virtual church services. But there will be one notable difference here in the Britain. Domestic chocolate makers, who should be celebrating one of their busiest times of year, are fuming instead, and all of them cite the same cause: Brexit.

伦敦 - 连续第二年，复活节将是一个主要的在线乱纷，具有社会距离蛋狩猎和虚拟教堂服务。但英国在这里会有一个值得注意的区别。国内巧克力制造商，谁应该庆祝他们最繁忙的一年中的一个，反而，他们都引用了同样的原因：Brexit。

“We’ve lost our entire European trade,” said Aneesh Popat, the owner of The Chocolatier, which sells dark chocolate salted caramel water ganache Easter eggs and other treats out of Bedfordshire, about 50 miles north of London. “Worse than that, we’ve lost our reputation, because when we send pallets of chocolate to, say, Germany and it disappears or we can’t track it, our customers don’t blame the courier. They blame us.”

“我们已经失去了整个欧洲贸易，”巧克力里的所有者的Aneesh Popat称，巧克力盐味焦糖水Ganache复活节彩蛋和其他贝德福德郡，大约50英里的伦敦北部约50英里。 “比这更糟糕，我们失去了声誉，因为当我们送巧克力托盘时，德国和它消失或者我们无法跟踪它，我们的客户不会责怪快递员。他们责怪我们。“

The trade deal struck late last year with the European Union spared Britain from a variety of tariffs that would have inflated the prices of goods that traveled to the mainland. It has not saved British companies from a maddening, unpredictable array of time-consuming, morale-sapping procedures and from stacks of paperwork that have turned exporting to the E.U. into a sort of black-box mystery.

去年年底，欧洲联盟袭击了英国的贸易交易从各种关税中施加了夸大旅游的商品价格。它并未将英国公司从疯狂，不可预测的耗时，士气排名的程序以及从已转向E.U的文书工作中的堆栈工作。进入一种黑匣子的谜团。

Goods go in and there is no telling when they will come out. Or how much customs duties will cost the recipient. Or even where the goods will ultimately land.

货物进入，何时出来的时候没有讲述。或者海关职责是多少收件人。甚至在货物最终的地方。

“We sent a palette of drinking chocolate to a customer in Paris on Jan. 4, and it came back to our company yesterday,” Mr. Popat said on April 1. “It’s just embarrassing.

“我们在1月4日派遣了一名饮用巧克力的调色板。

“So we decided that instead of trying to explain that we have no idea where our shipments to Europe have gone, we should just stop shipping there,” he added.

“所以我们决定这一点，而不是试图解释我们没有想到我们对欧洲的出货量消失的地方，我们应该只是停止在那里发货，”他补充道。

Complaints about long and bewildering forms and wayward merchandise have been heard from industries across the country, from automakers to shellfish producers. In November the government predicted that the country would suffer the worst recession in three centuries because of the pandemic and forecast that the economy would shrink by 11.3 percent. At the same time, the Boris Johnson administration has minimized the ongoing migraine of post-Brexit trading with Europe, describing the matter as “teething problems.”

从全国各地的行业听到了关于长令人留意的形式和任性商品的投诉，从汽车制造商到贝类生产商。 11月，政府预测，由于大流行和预测，该国将在三个世纪遭受最严重的经济衰退，并预测经济将收缩11.3％。与此同时，Boris Johnson行政当局已经最大限度地减少了与欧洲的Brexit交易的持续偏头痛，将此事描述为“出牙问题”。

To chocolate makers, the issues feel more like bites that are going to leave a mark. Chocolate is the U.K.’s second-largest food and drink export, after whiskey, according to the Food and Drink Federation. Chocolate exports to all countries hit $1.1 billion last year, and Europe accounts for about 70 percent of those sales. In January, exports of British chocolate to Europe fell 68 percent compared with the same period the year before.

对于巧克力制造商来说，这些问题感觉更像是要留下标记的叮咬。据食品和饮料联合会称，巧克力是U.K.的第二大食品和饮料出口，威士忌。对所有国家的巧克力出口达到了11亿美元，欧洲占这些销售额的70％。 1月份，与前一年相比，英国巧克力的出口下降了68％。

The industry is dominated by a handful of players, including Cadbury, one of the largest candy makers in the world . But following a boom of artisanal “bean-to-bar” makers in the states, dozens of family-run companies cropped up around Britain, emphasizing ethically sourced ingredients and bespoke batches. They became big sellers in Europe but have been nearly impossible to find there since January.

该行业由少数玩家主导，包括吉伯里，世界上最大的糖果制造商之一。但在州的手工制造商的繁荣之后，在英国周围播种了几十家家庭经营公司，强调道德采购成分和定制批次。他们成为欧洲的大卖家，但自1月以来几乎不可能找到那里。

“We have customers complain to us all the time, ‘Why can’t I buy my favorite British chocolate?’” said Hishem Ferjani, the founder of Choco Dealer in Bonn, Germany, which supplies grocery stores and sells through its own website. “We have store owners with empty shelves.”

“我们让客户一直抱怨我们，”为什么我不能购买我最喜欢的英国巧克力？“”德国波恩·波恩·布诺经销商的创始人Hishemferjani说，杂货店和销售自己的网站。 “我们拥有带空架子的所有者。”

“We have to explain, it’s not our fault, it’s not the fault of the producer. It’s Brexit,” he said.

“我们必须解释一下，这不是我们的错，这不是生产者的错。这是Brexit，“他说。

Mr. Ferjani has a list of 100 customers who have asked for alerts once British chocolate starts arriving again. One of them is Simone Schlief, a fan of The Chocolate Tree, an Edinburgh maker of what the company describes as “visually stunning works of edible art crafted in small batches.” She’s especially fond of the whiskey-flavored nibs the company makes.

Ferjani先生列出了100名顾客，一旦英国巧克力再次抵达，就会要求提醒警报。其中一个是Simone Schlief，巧克力树的粉丝，这家公司描述为“在小批次制作的可食用艺术的视觉令人惊叹的作品”。她尤其喜欢公司制造的威士忌味的尖端。

“So is my mother,” she said. “I would definitely would have bought some for Easter, but I would have been buying them every month.”

“我母亲也是如此，”她说。 “我肯定会为复活节买一些东西，但我会每月买它们。”

Alastair Gower, the owner of The Chocolate Tree, is just as annoyed. He has managed to successfully send exactly one batch of product to Europe since Brexit. And there were plenty of snafus along the way.

巧克力树的主人阿拉斯泰尔嫩林，就像生气一样。他已经成功地向欧洲成功地向欧洲发送了一批产品。而且沿途有很多伤害。

The shipment of about $8,000 worth of chocolate sat in limbo for weeks on its way to Holland. Once it finally arrived, his customer was asked to pay the equivalent of about $5,000 in duties because Mr. Gower had not filled out the import statement correctly.

在前往荷兰的路上，在Limbo坐落在荷兰的偏僻周期上坐了大约8,000美元的巧克力。一旦它终于到达，他的客户被要求支付相当于约5,000美元的职责，因为Gower先生没有正确填写进口陈述。

“We’d been told the product would arrive in France, so we put Calais as the point of entry. It went to Rotterdam, where it sat for six weeks,” he said. “Chocolate. Sitting in a warehouse. For six weeks.”

“我们被告知该产品将在法国抵达，所以我们将Calais作为进入点。它去了鹿特丹，在那里坐了六个星期，“他说。 “巧克力。坐在仓库里。六周。“

Through a shipping agent, he managed to get the import duty dropped. He learned a lesson about filling out forms, but that expertise won’t help him much.

通过运输代理商，他设法删除了进口责任。他了解了填写表格的教训，但这种专业知识不会帮助他。

“It’s impossible to find shippers that will deliver to Europe,” he said, “because there’s a backlog of goods in the pipeline.”

“发现将送到欧洲的托运人是不可能的，”他说，“因为管道上有积压的商品。”

At Coco Caravan, a chocolate maker in the Cotswolds, the stasis has meant that Europe has gone from 15 percent of the company’s revenue to zero. That has caused Jacques Cop, the owner, to disappoint old customers and put off new ones. In recent months, prospective buyers in the Netherlands, France and Germany have expressed interest.

在CoTwolds的巧克力制造商Coco Caravan，Stasis的意思是欧洲已从公司的15％从零本的收入归零。这引起了雅克缔约方委员会，主人，让老客户失望并推迟了新的客户。最近几个月，荷兰，法国和德国的预期买家表示了兴趣。

“They say, ‘We found you online and love everything you do in terms of being ethically sourced and vegan, but how are you going to combat the import-export problem we will have with the European Union?’” said Mr. Cop. “We can’t give them a clear answer, other than, ‘Yes, there will be additional costs involved.’”

“他们说，'我们在线找到你在线和爱你所做的一切，而是在道德上采购和素食主义者，但你将如何打击我们将与欧盟的进出口问题进行打击？”“缔约方会议说。 “我们不能给他们一个明确的答案，除了'是的，会有额外的成本。'”

Mr. Cop is also confronting a challenge common among small chocolate makers in Britain: importing raw ingredients from Europe. He stockpiled cacao in 2020 from his source of choice in Amsterdam. Now that it is time to buy more, obstacles have emerged. Transportation costs have doubled, which is bad enough. But Mr. Cop says his shipper refuses to take new orders because of worries that a shipment will somehow get blocked between Amsterdam and Britain.

缔约方议员也面临着英国小巧克力制造商共同的挑战：从欧洲进口原料。他在阿姆斯特丹的选择来源于2020年储存了洛杉矶。现在是时候购买了更多，出现了障碍。运输成本加倍，这足够糟糕。但警察称他的托运人拒绝采取新订单，因为担心货物在阿姆斯特丹和英国之间被封锁。

“It’s to the point where I’m thinking of borrowing a Renault van and just driving to the Netherlands myself,” Mr. Cop said. “It’s a 10-hour drive each way. But I’m not sure I have another choice.”

“我正在考虑借用雷诺范，只是开车去荷兰的地步，”帕克先生说。 “这是每种方式10小时的车程。 但我不确定我还有另一种选择。“

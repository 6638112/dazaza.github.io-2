What to expect from the dramatic Mars Perseverance landing on Thursday

2021-02-18 05:36:34

Thursday's the big day. NASA's multibillion-dollar Mars rover will arrive at the red planet and almost immediately begin the process of trying to set down safely on the surface.

星期四是重要的日子。 NASA数十亿美元的火星探测器将到达这颗红色星球，几乎立即开始尝试安全降落在地面上的过程。

This is easier said then done. On approach to Mars, the Perseverance rover will shed its cruise stage, retaining only an aeroshell to protect itself and a descent stage. This slimmed-down spacecraft will hit the atmosphere traveling at about 20,000km/hour and have just 410 seconds—or nearly seven minutes—to shed this velocity and make a feathery touchdown.

说起来容易做起来容易。在驶向火星时，恒心号漫游车将离开巡航阶段，仅保留用于保护自身的机壳和下降阶段。这款瘦身的航天器将撞击以大约20,000 km / h的速度飞行的大气层，仅用410秒（或将近7分钟）即可释放出这一速度并进行羽毛状的触地。

Though the Martian atmosphere is thin, it will nonetheless provide a majority of the resistance to slow Perseverance down. Within about 80 seconds of entering the Martian atmosphere, temperatures outside the aeroshell are expected to reach 1,300° Celsius.

尽管火星的气氛很稀薄，但是它将为抵抗毅力的下降提供大部分阻力。进入火星大气层大约80秒钟之内，航空器外壳外部的温度预计将达到1300摄氏度。

About four minutes after encountering the atmosphere, the vehicle's parachutes will deploy. Shortly after, the protective aeroshell will fall away, its job done. Then, when the vehicle descends to about 4km above the planet's surface, it will activate its  Terrain Navigation System. Up until this point, Perseverance's trip will be very similar to that of the Curiosity rover, which underwent a similar "seven minutes of terror" during its successful landing in August 2012.

遇到大气约四分钟后，车辆的降落伞将展开。不久之后，保护性航空器外壳将脱落，其工作完成。然后，当车辆下降到地球表面上方约4公里时，它将激活其地形导航系统。到现在为止，毅力之旅将与好奇号漫游车非常相似，好奇号漫游车经历了类似的恐怖七分钟。在2012年8月成功登陆期间。

With Perseverance there is one key difference—whereas Curiosity sought a safe landing site in the relatively smooth terrain of the Gale crater, this spacecraft will land in a more hazardous location with boulders, the Jezero crater. This location should make for richer scientific exploration. To mitigate this risk, engineers added a new system to would allow for a more precise landing.

持之以恒有一个主要区别-好奇号在大风火山口的相对平坦的地形中寻找了一个安全的着陆点，而这艘飞船将在更危险的地方着陆，即杰泽洛火山口。此位置应进行更丰富的科学探索。为了减轻这种风险，工程师增加了一个新系统，以实现更精确的着陆。

As it descends, an onboard computer will begin to rapidly take pictures of the Martian surface, looking for features such as craters, cliffs, and large boulders to compare against previously captured orbital imagery. After the onboard computer has made 15 landmark "matches," it will switch to a higher-resolution imaging mode to fine-tune the landing positioning. Curiosity could estimate where it was on Mars to within about 3km. Perseverance will bring this error down to 40 meters.

当它下降时，车载计算机将开始迅速拍摄火星表面，寻找陨石坑，悬崖和大石块等特征，以与先前捕获的轨道图像进行比较。车载计算机完成15个标志性比赛之后，它将切换到更高分辨率的成像模式以微调着陆位置。好奇心可以估计它在火星上约3公里内的位置。坚韧不拔会使错误降低至40米。

Upon reaching an altitude of 2km, the rover will begin a powered descent, followed by the "skycrane" maneuver to set Perseverance down on Mars at a velocity of less than 1m/s.

到达2公里的高度后，流动站将开始动力下降，然后是＆＃34; skycrane＆＃34;采取措施以低于1m / s的速度降落在火星上的毅力。

When will we know Perseverance made it? Cameras on board the spacecraft will record its descent, but there will be no ability to relay this data in real time, and it will be sent a few days later. Because there is an 11-minute time delay between Earth and Mars at present, we won't know the spacecraft's fate until after it has touched down (or left a smoking hole in the surface of Mars). Touchdown is scheduled for about 3:55pm EST (20:55 UTC). With luck, a signal from the spacecraft will arrive on Earth, telling us all is well.

我们何时才能知道毅力呢？航天器上的摄像头将记录其下降情况，但是无法实时中继此数据，几天后将发送该数据。由于目前地球与火星之间的时间延迟为11分钟，因此直到它降落（或在火星表面留下烟囱）之前，我们才知道航天器的命运。着陆时间定为美国东部时间下午3:55（UTC 20:55）。幸运的是，航天器发出的信号将到达地球，告诉我们一切都很好。

NASA television will provide live coverage on all the usual places beginning at 2:15pm EST (19:15 UTC) Thursday. So many people at NASA and in industry have worked extraordinarily hard to make Thursday a success, let us hope they are rewarded.

美国国家航空航天局电视台将从周四美国东部时间下午2点15分（世界标准时间19点15分）开始对所有平常地点进行直播。 NASA和行业中的许多人为使星期四取得成功付出了极大的努力，让我们希望他们能得到回报。

Perseverance has a lot in common with Curiosity in other ways. They were built from the same design and share many common parts. But NASA learned from Curiosity about the vehicle's performance on Mars—such as the need for more durable aluminum wheels—and because of this, NASA has been able to push Perseverance further.

毅力在其他方面与好奇心有很多共同点。它们采用相同的设计构建，并具有许多共同的部分。但是NASA从好奇号中学到了汽车在火星上的性能，例如需要更耐用的铝制轮毂，因此，NASA能够进一步提高毅力。

For example, scientists believe the Jezero crater region used to be a delta and that ancient sediments have been preserved. Perseverance carries a laser-based spectroscopy system to look for past biosignatures.

例如，科学家认为Jezero火山口地区曾经是三角洲，古老的沉积物得到了保存。毅力带有基于激光的光谱系统，可以查找过去的生物特征。

The rover also contains a complex system that will collect and cache dozens of samples of Martian rock in hopes that a future NASA mission to Mars will retrieve those samples and return them to Earth. NASA and the European Space Agency are working on a joint mission that would return these rocks in 2031, but it is not fully funded.

流动站还包含一个复杂的系统，该系统将收集和缓存数十个火星岩石样本，以期未来NASA前往火星的任务将取回这些样本并将其返回地球。美国宇航局和欧洲航天局正在进行一项联合任务，该任务将在2031年归还这些岩石，但资金尚不足。

Finally, of course, the Perseverance mission will bring the small Ingenuity helicopter to the red planet. It will seek to demonstrate the first powered flight on another world. It may not work—the Martian atmosphere is very thin, and therefore producing any lift will be a challenge—but it's great to see NASA take risks like this that will only further propel our space adventures to greater heights.

最后，当然，毅力任务会将小型Ingenuity直升机带到红色星球。它将试图证明在另一个世界上的首次动力飞行。它可能无法正常工作-火星大气层非常稀薄，因此要产生任何升力将是一个挑战-但很高兴看到NASA冒这样的风险，这只会进一步推动我们的太空冒险进入更高的高度。

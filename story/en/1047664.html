The last stand for forbearance housing market crash bros?
忍耐住房市场崩溃的最后立场兄弟？

2021-02-10 03:20:05

In 2021, a lingering symptom of the economic sickness we suffered in 2020 is forbearance. Not the forbearance plans themselves, which allowed mortgage holders to delay their payments for many months, but the fact that 2.72 million homes remain in forbearance and can therefore be considered at risk. Forbearance will have to end at some point, and when it does, couldn’t all these homes flood the housing market at once, driving prices down and scaring would-be homeowners away from purchasing?

2021年，我们忍受着2020年所遭受的经济疾病的持续症状。不是因为宽容计划本身使抵押贷款持有人推迟了数月之久，而是因为有272万套房屋仍处于宽容状态，因此可以认为存在风险。忍耐必须在某个时候结束，而一旦忍受，所有这些房屋难道不会立即淹没房地产市场，压低房价并吓scar可能的购房者吗？

We know the current status of the housing market in America is vigorous, if not hot. The MBA purchase application data is growing at a trend of 12% year over year. This growth is 1% higher than the peak of what I forecasted for 2021, up until March 18. So while the housing market bubble bears predicted a crash due to the COVID crisis, the exact opposite is happening. Home price growth is accelerating above my comfort zone for nominal home price growth, which is 4.6% or lower. As I have written many times, the housing market’s current strength is not because of COVID-19, but despite it.  Demographics plus low mortgage rates serve as the one-two punch that knocked out COVID-19. In 2018/2019, when mortgage rates got to 5%, all it did was cool down price gains in the existing housing market. Real home prices went negative year over year, which I wrote back then was very healthy and what the housing market needed.

我们知道，即使不热门，美国住房市场的现状也很旺盛。 MBA购买申请数据以每年12％的速度增长。到3月18日，这一增长率比我预测的2021年的峰值高1％。因此，尽管房地产市场泡沫熊市预测由于COVID危机而崩溃，但正相反。房屋价格的增速正在加速，超过名义房屋价格增速的4.6％或更低。正如我多次写的那样，房地产市场的当前实力并不是因为COVID-19，而是尽管如此。人口统计特征和低抵押贷款利率是淘汰COVID-19的一剂两药。在2018/2019年，抵押贷款利率达到5％时，所做的一切只是降低了现有住房市场的价格上涨。实际房屋价格逐年下降，我当时写得非常健康，住房市场需要什么。

In today’s low-inventory environment, complicated by external factors such as forbearance and foreclosure moratoriums, it’s crucial for real estate agents and brokers to be proactive in order to grow their business.

在当今库存低迷的环境中，再加上诸如宽容和止赎止赎之类的外部因素，房地产经纪人和经纪人必须积极主动地发展业务，这一点至关重要。

Today, inventory levels are at all-time lows, and the purchase application data index is above 300. This means home price growth is getting too hot! Just look at the difference 2020 brought into the data lines.

如今，库存水平处于历史最低点，购买应用程序数据指数超过300。这意味着房价增长变得太热了！只需看看数据线带来的差异2020。

The question remains: will all this strength in the housing market dampen or erase the risk of having all those homes in forbearance once forbearance ends? Here are three reasons you don’t have to worry.

问题仍然存在：一旦忍耐期结束，住房市场上的所有这些力量是否会抑制或消除将所有房屋都忍受的风险？这是您无需担心的三个原因。

First, the latest chart from BlackKnight shows us that the number of homes in forbearance has been decreasing. We are well off the peak. I expect this number to decline as our employment picture improves; however, there will be a lag period for this data line to show more improvement.

首先，BlackKnight的最新图表向我们显示，忍耐的房屋数量一直在减少。我们远远没有达到顶峰。我希望随着我们就业情况的改善，这个数字将会下降。但是，此数据线会有一个滞后时间才能显示出更多的改进。

Second, and this is critical to the story, homeowners’ credit profiles, when they originated their loans, were excellent. The previous expansion had the best loan profiles I have seen in my life. These buyers, especially those who purchased from 2010-2017, have fixed low debt costs due to low mortgage rates, with rising wages and nested equity. As home prices continue to grow beyond expectations, these homeowners have added another year of gains to their nested equity.

其次，这对于故事至关重要，房主在发起贷款时的信用状况非常好。以前的扩张具有我一生中见过的最好的贷款状况。这些购买者，尤其是从2010年至2017年购买的购买者，由于抵押贷款利率低，工资上涨和股权嵌套而固定了较低的债务成本。随着房价继续超出预期，这些房主又增加了一年的套利收益。

In this way, the current housing market backdrop is unlike the housing credit bubble years when loan profiles weren’t healthy, and we had a debt leverage speculative market. Last year, I wrote about the forbearance crash bros to outline their problems with their crash thesis. Here is a link to one of those articles.

这样，当前的房地产市场背景不同于住房信贷泡沫时代，当时的贷款状况不佳，而我们的债务杠杆投机市场也是如此。去年，我写了关于宽容崩溃兄弟的文章，概述了他们崩溃问题的问题。这里是这些文章之一的链接。

And the third reason we don’t have to worry about a crash when forbearance ends is J.O.B.S.!

还有，我们不必担心在宽容期结束时会发生车祸的第三个原因是J.O.B.S.！

The primary reason I believe the crash thesis of the housing market bubble boys turned forbearance crash bros will fail is that jobs are coming back. The employment gains started last year and have continued.  We have gained 12,470,000 jobs – and that was not in the forecast of the housing bubble boys.

我认为，房地产市场泡沫男孩的崩溃论点变成忍无可忍的崩溃兄弟会失败的主要原因是，工作正在恢复。就业增长始于去年并一直持续。我们已经获得了12,470,000个工作岗位，而这并不是房地产泡沫男孩的预测。

The February 2020 nonfarm payroll data, which accounts for most workers, had roughly 152,523,000 employed workers. We got as low as 130,161,000 employed workers during the Covid crisis peak and are now back to 142,631,000. We are still short 9,892,000 jobs, which is more than the jobs lost during the great financial crisis. Sadly, but to be expected, the last two jobs report combined were negative. We will not get back to the employment level we had in February 2020 while COVID-19 is with us, which prevents some sectors from operating at full capacity.  So job growth remains limited until we get more Americans vaccinated.     Think of this period as the calm before the job storm.  And the job storm is coming. We are vaccinating people faster every week that goes by. We just need time, and then all the lost jobs will come back and then some. Even those 3.5 million permanent jobs lost will be replaced.

占大多数工人的2020年2月非农就业数据大约有152,523,000个就业工人。在Covid危机高峰期，我们的员工人数仅为130,161,000，目前已回到142,631,000。我们仍然短缺9,892,000个工作岗位，这比严重的金融危机期间失去的工作岗位还多。令人遗憾的是，但可以预见的是，最后两个工作报告的总和为负。当COVID-19出现在我们身边时，我们将无法回到2020年2月的就业水平，这将阻止某些行业满负荷运转。因此，在我们让更多的美国人接种疫苗之前，工作增长仍然受到限制。将此时期视为工作风暴之前的平静时期。工作风暴即将来临。我们每周都会给人们接种疫苗的速度更快。我们只需要时间，然后所有丢失的工作都会回来，然后再回来。即使失去的这350万个永久性工作也将被替换。

This isn’t 2008 all over again. That housing market recovery was slow, but today our demographics are better, and our household balance sheets are healthier. The fiscal and monetary assistance now is hugely improved from what we saw after 2008. We have everything we need to get America back to February 2020 jobs levels; we just need time.

这不是2008年。住房市场恢复缓慢，但是今天我们的人口统计数据有所改善，我们的家庭资产负债表更加健康。现在的财政和货币援助已比2008年后的水平有了很大改善。我们拥有使美国回到2020年2月的就业水平所需的一切；我们只需要时间。

I am convinced that the number of homes under forbearance will fall as more people gain employment. Expect the forbearance data to lag the jobs data, but they will eventually coincide.

我坚信，随着更多人获得就业，忍耐的房屋数量将会减少。期望容忍数据落后于工作数据，但最终它们会重合。

Disaster relief is coming, and then when we can walk the earth freely, look for the government to do a stimulus package to push the economy along. By Aug. 31, 2021, we will have a much different conversation about the state of U.S. economics. Hopefully, by then, the 10-year yield will have hit 1.33% and higher. Wait for it! If the jobs data continues to worsen and we decide it is too expensive to help our American citizens in this crisis, we will likely see an uptick in distress sales and forced selling, but we still would not see a bubble crash in the housing market. It may suppress home price growth, but that wouldn’t necessarily be a bad thing since my most significant concern in housing is that home prices are growing too fast.  I recently talked about it on Bloomberg Financial.

灾难救济即将来临，然后当我们可以自由行走时，请政府出台刺激计划以推动经济发展。到2021年8月31日，我们对美国经济状况的讨论将大为不同。希望到那时，十年期国债收益率将达到1.33％或更高。等一下如果就业数据继续恶化，并且我们认为在这场危机中为美国公民提供帮助的成本太高，我们可能会看到遇险销售和强迫出售有所增加，但我们仍不会看到房地产市场出现泡沫破裂。它可能会抑制房价的上涨，但这并不一定是一件坏事，因为我对住房的最大担忧是房价上涨过快。我最近在彭博财经上谈到了它。

If we are battling COVID-19 as war, would we leave any American behind? Imagine during wartime if we were told to build our tanks, rifles, and gear to fight the war without government assistance. The government can do certain things that the private sector can’t. Without COVID-19, we would still be enjoying the most prolonged economic and jobs expansion in history and have debates about what constitutes full employment. But it happened, and we have the power to leave no American behind once again.

如果我们与COVID-19作战是战争，我们会不会留下任何美国人？想象一下在战争期间，如果我们被告知在没有政府援助的情况下建造坦克，步枪和装备来进行战争。政府可以做一些私营部门做不到的事情。如果没有COVID-19，我们仍将享受历史上最长时间的经济和就业扩展，并就什么构成充分就业展开辩论。但这确实发生了，我们有权再次不让任何美国人落后。

Think about that next time you see someone hawking a housing market bubble crash thesis. All the jobs will come back in time, and we will all be walking in the sun again without a mask. Until then, we need to support government programs, like disaster relief and programs that help homeowners in forbearance get out of it, and help renters too. Let’s not leave any American behind in this war against COVID-19.

想一想下一次您看到有人摆弄房地产市场泡沫崩溃的论文。所有的工作都会及时回来，我们所有人都会戴着面具再次在阳光下行走。在此之前，我们需要支持政府计划，例如disaster灾和帮助忍受房主摆脱困境并帮助租房者的计划。在这场与COVID-19的战争中，不要让任何美国人落伍。

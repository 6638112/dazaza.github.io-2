即将到来的WFH对峙

2020-10-19 15:14:31

Covid forced us all to our houses and for a while it seemed like this would be a permanent trend, at least in the IT space. People were surprised when productivity stayed consistent or even increased. They started talking about making it permanent. Some even took the plunge (Dropbox, Twitter, Upwork, Zillow).

Covid强迫我们所有人回到自己的房子里，有一段时间，这似乎是一种永久的趋势，至少在IT领域是这样。当生产率保持稳定甚至提高时，人们会感到惊讶。他们开始讨论把它变成永久性的。一些人甚至尝试了一下(Dropbox，Twitter，Upwork，Zillow)。

But grumblings about a potentially remote-working-world are circulating as well. Particularly on social media. Doubters claim that teams cannot build trust remotely, that work relationships must be built face-to-face.

但对潜在的远程工作世界的抱怨也在流传。尤其是在社交媒体上。怀疑者声称，团队不能远程建立信任，工作关系必须面对面地建立。

These comment s, however, stand in contrast to successful companies like Hashicorp, Basecamp and Automattic that were 100% remote even before the pandemic.

然而，这些评论与哈希科普(Hashicorp)、Basecamp和Automattic等成功的公司形成了鲜明对比，这些公司甚至在大流行之前就100%远离这里。

So what is the truth? As far as I can tell, the remote work critic’s argument doesn’t center on productivity, but on relationships and may be an expression of personal preference — the classic difference between introverts and extroverts. People are different, and they need different things from their place of employment. For some, work doesn’t need to provide both coworkers and lunch companions. For others, more personal contact is crucial for effective collaboration.

那么真相是什么呢？据我所知，这位远程工作批评家的论点不是集中在生产力上，而是集中在人际关系上，可能是个人偏好的一种表达--这是内向和外向之间的经典区别。人们是不同的，他们需要不同的工作场所的东西。对一些人来说，工作不需要同时提供同事和午餐伴侣。对于其他人来说，更多的个人接触对于有效的协作至关重要。

If the need for face-to-face communication is individually determined, then we should settle on a hybrid solution that respects that difference. Not a hybrid approach where some days you are on-site and some days you are not. That only promises to make people unhappy half the time. But a hybrid solution where the employees get to choose between a remote-only or on-site team — where everyone on the team is either 100% remote or 100% on-site¹.

如果面对面交流的需求是单独确定的，那么我们应该选择尊重这种差异的混合解决方案。如果您有时在现场，有时不在现场，这不是一种混合方法。这只会让人们一半时间不开心。而是一种混合解决方案，员工可以在仅远程团队或现场团队(团队中的每个人都100%远程或100%现场)之间进行选择？

This determination needs to be team specific, because teams with only some remote workers are problematic. If building trust with remote employees is difficult now for the face-to-facers, that won’t change after they return to the office and still have to collaborate online with half their team. It also doesn’t seem practical to ask them to limit their interactions to video conferencing from their desks. If face-to-face interaction was what they were craving that scheme would only further deprive them. Remote workers also suffer in these schemes. They are likely to feel excluded, wondering what decisions are being made without them.

此确定需要特定于团队，因为只有一些远程员工的团队是有问题的。如果现在面对面的员工很难与远程员工建立信任，那么在他们回到办公室后，这一点也不会改变，他们仍然需要与一半的团队在线协作。要求他们将互动限制在办公桌上的视频会议似乎也是不切实际的。如果面对面的互动是他们所渴望的，那么这个计划只会进一步剥夺他们的权利。远程工作者也在这些计划中蒙受损失。他们可能会感到被排斥，想知道没有他们会做出什么决定。

Consider how a university is configured. There are study arrangements for all types of people. Some spend their time in study groups in shared spaces in libraries or elsewhere. Some take to a university carrel where they can be by themselves for long periods of quiet concentration. Some study in open areas like cafeterias or dormitory lobbies for the calming background noises and activity. There is something for everyone.

考虑一下大学是如何配置的。各种类型的人都有学习安排。有些人把时间花在学习小组、图书馆的共享空间或其他地方。有些人参加大学聚会，在那里他们可以长时间安静地集中精力。一些人在像自助餐厅或宿舍大堂这样的开阔区域学习，以获得平静的背景噪音和活动。每个人都有适合自己的东西。

A university caters to all personality types, and does not glorify or disparage any specific style. Work should strive to do the same. We should not project our own preferences on anyone else, but respect them and seek to accommodate them where possible. Where people choose to work has such a significant impact on happiness and productivity that it deserves particular attention.

一所大学迎合所有性格类型，不美化或贬低任何特定的风格。工作也要努力做到这一点。我们不应该把自己的喜好投射到任何人身上，而应该尊重他们，并在可能的情况下设法容纳他们。人们选择在哪里工作对幸福感和生产力有如此重大的影响，因此值得特别关注。

We just came off a decade or more where engineers were forced to work in open office plans because of a minority preference of corporate executives. Plenty of engineers complained about the distraction. It would be a mistake to force it on them again. Likewise, forcing everyone to work remotely would be the other side of the same mistake.

我们刚刚经历了十年或更长时间，工程师们被迫在开放式办公室计划中工作，因为公司高管的少数偏好。很多工程师抱怨分心。如果再把它强加给他们，那将是一个错误。同样，强迫每个人远程工作也是同样错误的另一面。

In our future working arrangements — even after covid — let’s have it both ways. It’s not that hard. No matter how much either side may try to project their own preferences on the other, let’s stay strong and remember the lessons we’ve learned. Most of all, let’s be sensitive to each other’s personal needs. This pandemic has at least taught us that much.

在我们今后的工作安排中--即使是在愉快之后--让我们双管齐下。没那么难。无论哪一方都试图将自己的喜好投射到另一方，让我们保持坚强，记住我们学到的教训。最重要的是，让我们对彼此的个人需求保持敏感。这场大流行至少教会了我们这么多。

[1] Another popular idea is for teams to all be on-site some days of the week and remote the rest. This scheme could work, but has some potential pitfalls. First, it would defeat the purpose if everyone wasn’t on the same in-office schedule— but that can be difficult to arrange. Second, over time people will naturally prefer one working mode over the other and drift towards it primarily. Then all the same failed dynamics of a half-and-half team will be present.

[1]另一个流行的想法是让团队在一周中的某些日子都在现场，其余的时间则远离现场。这一计划可能会奏效，但也有一些潜在的陷阱。首先，如果每个人的办公室日程都不一样，那就违背了目的--但这可能很难安排。其次，随着时间的推移，人们自然会喜欢一种工作模式而不是另一种，并主要倾向于这种工作模式。然后，所有同样的失败的动态，一半和一半的团队将会出现。

Avalanche Warnings Are Issued in Northwest
雪崩警告在西北发行

2021-02-25 03:16:40

Two avalanche warnings have been issued for parts of Washington and Oregon as heavier-than-usual rainfall and snowfall is expected to hit part of the West Coast through Monday.

华盛顿州和俄勒冈州的部分地区发出了两名雪崩警告，因为通常比平常的降雨量和降雪预计将于周一袭击西海岸的一部分。

One of the warnings, a Level 4 on a scale of 5, said there was a high avalanche danger for parts of North Cascades National Park by the Canadian border, extending south through Okanogan-Wenatchee National Forest and into parts of Gifford Pinchot National Forest, which is about 140 miles southeast of Seattle.

其中一个警告，一个等级为5，达到5级，北瀑布国家公园由加拿大边境部分有一个高雪崩危险，南通过Okanogan-Wenatchee国家森林延伸到Gifford Pinchot国家森林的部分地区，这是西雅图东南部约140英里。

The warning was in effect until Monday evening and also covered part of Mount Hood National Forest, which is about 70 miles east of Portland, Ore.

警告直到星期一晚上，也覆盖了奥德兰国森山的一部分，大约70英里，奥勒。

A separate, Level 5 warning, indicating extreme danger, also covered smaller parts of the Okanogan-Wenatchee National Forest, about 80 miles west of Moses Lake, Wash. That warning, which said heavy snow, strong winds and warming temperatures could create avalanche conditions, was also in effect until Monday evening.

一个单独的5级警告，表明极端危险，也覆盖了Okanogan-Wenatchee国家森林的较小部分，摩西湖西部约80英里。那个警告，该警告称，大雪，强风和温暖的温度可能会产生雪崩条件，直到星期一晚上也有效。

The warnings were issued by the Northwest Avalanche Center, which said at least 30 people in the United States had been killed in avalanches so far this season. That’s the highest number of fatalities since the 2015-16 season, according to the center.

警告由西北雪崩中心发布，该中心表示，本赛季迄今为止，美国至少有30人丧生。根据中心，这是自2015-16赛季以来的最高死亡人数。

The warnings came as parts of the Pacific Northwest braced for heavier-than-usual precipitation as a result of an “atmospheric river,” the National Weather Service said on Twitter.

警告作为太平洋西北部的部分地支撑了一个“大气河流”的较重常见的降水，国家天气服务在Twitter上表示。

That type of weather event — “a long river of moisture” that can hover over concentrated areas for a period of time — is expected to lead to very heavy rainfall or, in higher elevations, intense snowfall, said meteorologists at the Weather Service in Seattle.

这种类型的天气事件 - “一条漫长的水分河流”，可以悬停在一段时间内的集中区域 - 预计会导致雨量非常大的降雨，在西雅图的天气服务中的气象学家说。

The National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration described such events as “rivers in the sky.” This one extends about 3,000 miles, from the coast of British Columbia to the coast of Hawaii, said Dustin Guy, a Weather Service meteorologist.

国家海洋和大气管理局描述了这样的事件是“天空中的河流”。这一人们延伸到3,000英里，从不列颠哥伦比亚省海岸到夏威夷的海岸，说明了天气气象学家达斯汀人。

Though Seattle may see only about half an inch of rain, coastal areas and mountain regions can expect up to three inches, said another Weather Service meteorologist, Matthew Cullen. In high-elevation places, like the Cascade Mountains, one to two feet of snow may fall in elevations above 4,000 feet, he said.

虽然西雅图可能只看到大约半英寸的雨，沿海地区和山地地区可以预期高达三英寸，另一个天气服务气象学家马修·卡伦说。他说，在高度高度的地方，就像级联山脉一样，一到两英尺的雪可能落在4,000英尺以上。

A handful of atmospheric rivers have hit the area each season, and this one, so far, is expected to be moderate in terms of precipitation and duration, Mr. Cullen said. Still, it is coming to the region shortly after a particularly intense snowfall.

Cuplen先生说，少数大气河流袭击了每个季节的地区，而且，这是预计在降水和持续时间方面的温和。尽管如此，它仍然在特别强烈的降雪后不久来到该地区。

A total of about half an inch of snow fell in Seattle in December and January, he said. Then, nearly 12.5 inches of snow fell over a three-day period ending on Feb. 14.

他说，在西雅图和1月份总共大约半英寸的雪下降。然后，在2月14日结束的三天期间，近12.5英寸的雪下降。

“There was definitely several hours of just consistent snow on the order of about an inch an hour,” Mr. Cullen recalled.

“肯定有几个小时的雪大约一英寸的一英寸，”卡伦先生回忆道。

Auction Kennel Hit by Meteorite Sold For $44k at Christie's Auction
在佳士得拍卖会上，被陨石击中的狗舍以4.4万美元成交

2022-02-26 05:46:53

While billionaires are battling it out in a race to colonise the moon, mere stratospherically rich mortals on Earth were able to grab a small slice of space rock for themselves on Wednesday at Christie’s annual sale of rare and unusual meteorites.

虽然亿万富翁们正在争夺月球殖民地，但在周三佳士得（Christie）举行的罕见陨石年度拍卖会上，地球上只有同温层富有的人类能够为自己攫取一小块太空岩石。

Star-gazers and meteorite enthusiasts bid frantically for fragments of the “oldest matter humankind can touch” – as the auction house put it – while other objects such as a comet-cracked kennel from Costa Rica sold for tens of thousands of dollars.

正如拍卖行所说，天文爱好者和陨石爱好者疯狂地竞购“人类能触摸到的最古老物质”的碎片，而哥斯达黎加一座彗星破碎的犬舍等其他物品的价格则高达数万美元。

A 15g fragment of the Winchcombe meteorite, which briefly became Britain’s most coveted rock after the bright fireball was seen blazing across the sky over the Cotswold town last year, sold for $30,200 (£22,300), while a smaller 1.7g fragment fetched $12,600.

去年，人们看到明亮的火球在科茨沃尔德镇上空燃烧，一块15克的温克康贝陨石碎片一度成为英国最令人垂涎的岩石，售价为30200美元（22300英镑），而一块较小的1.7克陨石碎片的售价为12600美元。

According to the Christie’s catalogue of the 66-lot “Deep Impact: Martian, Lunar and other Rare Meteorites” sale, only 602g of Winchcombe was ever found – with 90% of the material now housed in the UK’s national collection, curated by London’s Natural History Museum.

根据佳士得拍卖的66幅“深度撞击：火星、月球和其他稀有陨石”的目录，只发现了602克温克康贝——其中90%的材料目前存放在英国国家收藏中，由伦敦自然历史博物馆（London’s Natural History Museum）策展。

Among the more usual items for sale was a dog house hit by a meteorite which crashed through the tin roof in April 2019 in Aguas Zarcas, Costa Rica. But while it was expected to sell for as much as $300,000, after all it boasted a seven-inch hole which “marks where the meteorite punctured the roof”, it finally sold for $44,100.

2019年4月，哥斯达黎加阿古斯·扎卡斯的一座狗屋被陨石砸穿了铁皮屋顶，这是较为常见的出售物品之一。不过，尽管预计售价高达30万美元，但毕竟它有一个7英寸的洞，“标志着陨石穿透屋顶的地方”，最终售价为4.41万美元。

Its resident, a German shepherd named Roky, survived unharmed, according to James Hyslop, head of the science and natural history department at Christie’s.

据佳士得科学与自然历史部门负责人詹姆斯·希斯洛普说，它的居民是一只名叫罗基的德国牧羊犬，安然无恙地活了下来。

“My first question when I was offered the dog shed for auction was, ‘Was Roky OK?’,” he told the BBC. “I’m pleased to report that other than now being ‘sans’ dog shed, he’s doing just fine.”

他告诉BBC：“当我被拍卖狗舍时，我的第一个问题是，‘罗基还好吗？’”。“我很高兴地报告，除了现在是‘无’狗舍，他做得还不错。”

The fragment that caused Roky to lose his home, a shard weighing 178.7g and “covered with fusion crust, the result of its fiery plunge through Earth’s atmosphere” sold for $21,420 – a snip for an item that consists of “calcium aluminium inclusions– the oldest matter humankind can touch”.

导致罗基失去家园的碎片是一块重达178.7克的碎片，“被熔合地壳覆盖，是它在地球大气层中猛烈撞击的结果”，售价21420美元——这是一块由“钙铝包裹体——人类能接触到的最古老物质”组成的物品的剪报。

Collecting fireballs from space has attracted the attention of celebrity collectors, who have pushed the price of the rocks tenfold over the past decade. Collectors are thought to include the film director Steven Spielberg; the actor Nicolas Cage; the illusionist Uri Geller; the cellist Yo-Yo Ma; and, inevitably, Elon Musk, the world’s richest person and chief executive of electric carmaker Tesla and rocket company SpaceX.

从太空收集火球引起了名人收藏家的注意，在过去十年中，他们将火球的价格推高了十倍。收藏家被认为包括电影导演史蒂文·斯皮尔伯格；演员尼古拉斯·凯奇；魔术师乌里·盖勒；大提琴家马友友；当然，埃隆·马斯克也是不可避免的，他是世界首富，也是电动汽车制造商特斯拉和火箭公司SpaceX的首席执行官。

A further lot included more than 100 tiny shards from a meteoroid that exploded over the Russian city of Chelyabinsk in 2013 with “an explosive yield estimated to rival that of an atomic bomb”, which sold for $4,788.

还有一批是2013年在俄罗斯车里雅宾斯克市上空爆炸的流星体上的100多个微小碎片，其“爆炸当量估计可与原子弹媲美”，售价为4788美元。

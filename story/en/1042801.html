The largest dam-removal in US history
美国历史上最大的水坝拆除

For over a century, one of the most important salmon runs in the United States has had to contend with historic dams – and now four of them are set to be taken down.

一个多世纪以来，美国最重要的鲑鱼养殖之一不得不与历史悠久的水坝抗衡-现在将要拆除其中的四个。

“My great uncle and my grandma and my great grandparents and, I'm sure, their great grandparents: they were all fishermen. That's just what they did – they fished and it was out of necessity to support their families. And it's because that's what we've always done and we've never known another life,” says Amy Cordalis, the general counsel of the Yurok, and a member of California’s largest indigenous tribe.

“我的曾叔叔，祖母，曾祖父母，以及我的祖父母，我敢肯定，他们都是渔民。那就是他们所做的–他们钓鱼了，没有必要养家糊口。这是因为这是我们一直以来所做的，而我们从未知道过另一种生活，”尤罗克州的总法律顾问，加利福尼亚最大的土著人之一艾米·科尔迪利斯（Amy Cordalis）说。部落。

It’s hard to overstate how important this livelihood has been to the Yurok people who have lived for millennia in rural Northern California. And yet this livelihood has been diminishing for decades after the Klamath River – which flows through the tribe’s territory – was dammed for hydroelectricity. But now, after years of painstaking negotiations, the fortunes of the Yurok could be set to change, with the largest dam removal project in US history given the green light.

很难夸大这种生计对在北加州农村生活了几千年的尤罗克人的重要性。然而，在流经部落领土的克拉马斯河因修建水力发电而被筑坝后，这种生计已经减少了数十年。但是现在，经过多年艰苦的谈判，尤罗克的命运可能会改变，美国历史上最大的水坝拆除项目获得了批准。

Although she grew up in Ashland, Oregon, Cordalis would often visit Requa, a tiny village near the mouth of the Klamath River in northern California, to see family, attend tribal ceremonies – and to fish. Her father – “the ultimate Yurok fisherman” – had four daughters and a son, and he taught all of them to fish.

尽管科达里斯在俄勒冈州的阿什兰长大，但她经常会拜访雷克（Requa），这是一个位于加利福尼亚北部克拉马斯河口附近的小村庄，去看望家人，参加部落仪式和钓鱼。她的父亲是“尤罗克族的终极渔夫”，有四个女儿和一个儿子，他教他们所有人钓鱼。

“When I was growing up, there were still decent salmon runs,” she recalls. “On good nights, you could catch 100, 200 fish. We loved it. That's when you felt like you were like being your best Yurok self: you were doing what the creator made you for. You were going to be able to fill up your smokehouse and your freezer and not only just yours, but your grandma’s, your aunties’, your cousins’ – all the people you cared about, you could give them fish so that they had food.”

她回忆说：“当我长大后，仍然有不错的鲑鱼奔跑。” “晚安，您可以钓到100到200条鱼。我们喜欢它。那就是当您感觉自己就像是自己最好的Yurok自己：您正在做创作者为您做的事情。您将能够装满您的烟熏室和冷冻室，不仅是您自己的，还有奶奶，您的阿姨，表兄弟的所有–您所关心的所有人都可以给他们钓鱼，以便他们吃东西。 ”

The money from selling the fish they didn’t need would provide money for the children’s school clothes, a fridge or a second-hand car, Cordalis says. In short, fishing was a valuable income on a reservation where the median income is only $11,000 (£8,400) a year.

Cordalis说，通过出售不需要的鱼来赚钱，可以为孩子们的学校衣服，冰箱或二手车赚钱。简而言之，在保留地上捕鱼是一笔宝贵的收入，那里的年收入中位数仅为11,000美元（8,400英镑）。

Today the fishing experience is very different, she says. Drawing upon all the techniques and skills passed down over generations is of little use, because when she goes down to the river with her own three boys, “there are no fish to be caught”.

她说，今天的捕鱼经历已经大为不同。利用几代人传承下来的所有技术和技能几乎没有用，因为当她和自己的三个男孩一起下河时，“没有鱼可钓”。

“It's like the crumbling of the way that we live. It's the crumbling of the way that we teach our kids,” says Cordalis. “It's the crumbling of how we interact with that natural environment, because there's no fish.”

“这就像我们生活方式的崩溃。这是我们教孩子的方式的崩溃。” “这是我们与自然环境互动的方式的崩溃，因为那里没有鱼。”

Research bears this out. The Klamath River, once home to the third-largest salmon runs (the migration of adult salmon upstream to spawn) in the continental United States, now has runs at a fraction of their original numbers. One of five Pacific salmon species, the spring-run Chinook salmon, which historically numbered in the hundreds of thousands, has almost entirely been wiped out: the run consisted of fewer than 700 fish last year. Another species, the Coho salmon, which grows typically to between 60cm and 76cm (24 to 30 inches) and can weigh over 5kg (11lb) in adulthood, has been designated “threatened” under the US’s Endangered Species Act.

研究证实了这一点。克拉马斯河曾经是美国第三大鲑鱼河道（成年鲑鱼向上游迁移到产卵）的家园，现在河道的水流仅为原来数量的一小部分。春季运行的奇努克鲑鱼是太平洋鲑鱼的五种之一，其历史记录数以十万计的鲑鱼已几乎全部被消灭：去年的游动鱼类少于700条。另一种名为Coho鲑鱼，通常长到60厘米至76厘米（24至30英寸）之间，成年时体重超过5千克（11磅），根据美国《濒危物种法》被指定为“受到威胁”。

The dams built on the Klamath River have been identified as one cause of the drop in salmon numbers. Eight dams were built on the river between the early 1900s and 1962 to produce hydroelectric power. The presence of dams has been linked to marked changes in salmon populations on the Klamath and elsewhere.

在克拉马斯河上建造的水坝被确定为鲑鱼数量下降的原因之一。在1900年代初期至1962年之间，在河上修建了八座水坝，以产生水力发电。水坝的存在与克拉马斯及其他地区鲑鱼种群的明显变化有关。

“Anytime you put a dam on a river, it always has profound effects: it chops the river into two pieces,” explains the Yurok tribe’s senior fisheries biologist, Michael Belchik, a tribal member who has decades of experience in fish restoration. “Rivers carry a lot more than just water. The water goes down river, fish move upriver, but not only that: there's nutrients, sediment and other organisms.”

“任何时候在河上修建水坝，都会产生深远的影响：它将河流分成两部分，” Yurok部落的高级渔业生物学家迈克尔·贝尔奇克（Michael Belchik）解释说，他是一位有着数十年鱼类修复经验的部落成员。 “河水携带的不仅仅是水。水顺着河水流下来，鱼向上流，但不仅如此：还有养分，沉积物和其他生物。”

Without flowing sediment, the river below the dams then becomes starved of it, leaving only larger rocks on the river bottom. These rocks are ideal for bristle worms, also known as polychaete worms, to cling onto. “Normally, the mobile bed of the river prevents colonies of these filter-feeding worms from taking over every square inch of the bottom of the river,” says Belchik. But now, “these worms have taken over everything”.

如果没有流动的沉积物，大坝下方的河就会饿死，河底仅留下较大的岩石。这些岩石非常适合硬毛蠕虫（也称为多毛et蠕虫）紧贴。 “通常，河流的流动床阻止了这些以过滤器为食的蠕虫的殖民地占据了河底的每平方英寸，” Belchik说。但是现在，“这些蠕虫已经占领了一切”。

Although not harmful in and of themselves, the worms are the secondary host for C. Shasta, a parasite to which juvenile chinook salmon have proven particularly vulnerable. Belchik says that the Klamath’s lowest dam, Iron Gate, has created overcrowded conditions that are ripe for the spread of C. Shasta. The salmon that don’t go into the hatchery – a man-made spawning facility – spawn just below the dam. The many juvenile salmon that then gather closely here are prone to picking up the parasite.

尽管这些蠕虫本身对人体无害，但它们是C. Shasta的次要寄主。Shasta被证明是特别易受攻击的朱诺鲑鱼的寄生虫。贝尔奇克说，克拉马斯最低的水坝铁门造成了人满为患的状况，这对于沙斯塔的传播已经成熟。不进入孵化场的鲑鱼（人工产卵设施）在水坝下方产卵。然后聚集在这里的许多幼鲑鱼很容易被寄生虫感染。

“[These] are all the ingredients necessary to put together a runaway out of control disease problem that is now wiping out of 80 or 90% of our fish,” says Belchik. “Anytime you have fish that have limited genetic diversity and limited geographic area, you invite catastrophe to take out your fish, whether it's a fish disease or a flood.”

Belchik说：“ [这些]是解决失控的疾病问题所必需的所有成分，这些疾病现在已经消灭了我们80％或90％的鱼。” “无论何时，只要您的鱼类的遗传多样性和地理区域有限，无论是鱼病还是洪水，您都会招致巨灾来夺走您的鱼。”

The reservoirs behind the dams are also responsible for a significant build-up of toxic algae – which thrives in warm, nutrient-rich stagnant water. In sufficient quantities it becomes harmful to human health. In the autumn, water containing toxic algae is released and sent downstream towards the Klamath’s mouth where the Yurok reservation is.

大坝后面的水库还导致有毒藻类大量积聚，这些藻类在温暖，营养丰富的积水中生长。足够的数量对人体健康有害。在秋天，含有毒藻类的水被释放出来，并向下游流向尤鲁克保留地的克拉马斯河口。

“We have just received our almost yearly announcement that the toxic levels of microcystin in the river and blue-green algae are now at unhealthy levels,” says Frankie Myers, vice-chairman of the Yurok tribe. This doesn’t just make fishing hazardous. “There are pieces of our culture and our spiritual practices that we cannot do now without risking the health and safety of our people,” he explains. “The place we go to pray, the place we go to heal, the place we go to do our medicine will make you sick. That has a psychological impact on our communities.”

“我们几乎每年都收到公告，说河中微囊藻毒素和蓝藻的毒性水平现已达到不健康的水平，” Yurok部落的副主席弗兰基·迈尔斯（Frankie Myers）说。这不仅使钓鱼变得危险。他解释说：“我们的文化和精神实践中的某些方面，如果不冒着人民健康和安全的风险，我们现在无法做到。” “我们去祈祷的地方，我们去医治的地方，我们去做药的地方会让您生病。这对我们的社区产生了心理影响。”

The solution that Yurok and a coalition of other tribes and environmental organisations have long advocated for is the removal of the lower four of the eight dams on the Klamath. After painstaking negotiations, this led to the signing of an agreement between PacifiCorp (which operates these dams) and 40 other signatories, including tribes and state governments in 2010.

尤罗克以及其他部落和环境组织的联盟长期以来一直主张的解决方案是，拆除克拉马斯的八个水坝中的下四个。经过艰苦的谈判，这导致PacifiCorp（经营这些水坝）与40个其他签署方（包括部落和州政府）于2010年签署了协议。

The simultaneous removal of the four dams, with a combined height of 411ft (125m), makes it the largest dam removal project in America’s history, according to the Klamath River Renewal Corporation, the nonprofit tasked with overseeing the dam removals. It is also set to be the most expensive, at a cost of almost $450m (£340m).

非营利组织负责监督大坝拆除的非营利组织克拉马斯河复兴公司表示，同时拆除这四个大坝，总高度为411英尺（125m），使其成为美国历史上最大的大坝拆除项目。它也将成为最昂贵的，成本将近4.5亿美元（3.4亿英镑）。

The result will be 400 stream-miles of restored habitat for salmon and other migratory species like steelhead trout and Pacific lamprey. Opening up previously inaccessible spawning grounds will allow for greater genetic diversity and less crowding, says Belchik, which reduces the risk of disease transmission.

结果将是鲑鱼和其他迁徙物种（如硬头鳟和太平洋七lamp鳗）的恢复栖息地达400英里。 Belchik说，开放以前无法到达的产卵场将允许更大的遗传多样性和更少的拥挤，这降低了疾病传播的风险。

“I've spent my career helping design fish restoration projects of varying types,” says Belchik. In terms of impact, “restoration of passage [projects] are always the most successful and most immediate”.

Belchik说：“我的职业生涯是帮助设计各种类型的鱼类修复项目。”就影响而言，“通过[项目]的恢复总是最成功，最直接的”。

By reconnecting springs and cold-water tributaries to the main Klamath River, Belchik says the water temperatures of the river as a whole would drop. This not only improves water quality (lower temperatures reduce the risk of algae blooms, which in turn increases dissolved oxygen and improves pH levels); it would also benefit the salmon whose spawning and migratory behaviour benefit from cooler water – and who are more vulnerable to disease when it’s warmer. The return of cooler water, fed by snowmelt, will help make the fish more resilient in the face of climate change. And the return of naturally flowing sediment and a newly mobile riverbed would drastically reduce the habitat of the polychaete worms which release the salmon-killing C. Shasta.

贝尔奇克说，通过将泉水和冷水支流重新连接至克拉马斯河主干河，整个河流的水温将下降。这不仅改善了水质（较低的温度降低了藻类大量繁殖的风险，从而增加了溶解氧并提高了pH值）；这也将使鲑鱼受益，鲑鱼的产卵和迁徙行为会因凉水而受益，而鲑鱼在变暖时更容易患病。融雪喂养的凉水的返回将有助于使鱼类在面对气候变化时更具韧性。自然流动的沉积物和新近流动的河床的回流将大大减少多沙et蠕虫的栖息地，这些多沙cha蠕虫会释放出杀死鲑鱼的沙斯塔氏菌。

With over 1,700 dams removed in the US – including 90 last year, according to American Rivers’ database – there is a growing list of examples illustrating the benefits to ecosystems, especially for imperilled fish populations. The removal of two smaller dams and fish passage improvements on the Penobscot River in Maine, which were completed in 2016, have restored 2,000 miles of habitat for Atlantic salmon and other species, compensating for lost power by improving output from other dams. The project led to a rebound in numbers returning to spawn: this year 1,426 salmon returned, compared to only 248 in 2014. Other migratory fish have benefitted too: alewife and blueback herring returns have surged to 1.9 million compared to just 2,000 in 2011.

根据美国河流组织的数据库，美国已拆除了1,700多个水坝，其中包括去年的90座。有越来越多的例子说明了对生态系统的好处，特别是对鱼类种群的危害。 2016年完成的缅因州Penobscot河上的两个小水坝的拆除和鱼类通道的改善，已为大西洋鲑鱼和其他物种恢复了2,000英里的栖息地，通过提高其他水坝的输出来弥补动力损失。该项目导致数量恢复反弹：今年返回了1,426条鲑鱼，而2014年仅为248条。其他迁徙鱼类也从中受益：阿勒维夫和蓝鲱鱼的收入激增至190万，而2011年仅为2,000。

In the Olympic National Park in Washington State, removals of two large dams on the Elwha River were completed in 2014, restoring 75% of previously inaccessible spawning habitat. Just over 1,600 chinook salmon redds (spawning nests) were spotted upriver of where the second dam used to be in 2018 – encouraging signs that the chinook are recolonising this habitat. In the same area, coho salmon smolts (young fish) have increased from 9,000 in 2014 to 17,000 in 2017. But it is perhaps the steelhead running in summer whose revival has been most dramatic.

在华盛顿州奥林匹克国家公园，Elwha河上的两个大坝于2014年完成拆除，恢复了以前无法到达的产卵栖息地的75％。在2018年第二座大坝的上游发现了超过1,600股奇努克鲑鱼变红（产卵巢）–令人鼓舞的迹象表明，奇努克人正在重新定居该栖息地。在同一地区，银大麻哈鱼鲑鱼（幼鱼）已从2014年的9,000只增加到2017年的17,000只。但是，也许是夏季运行的硬头鲑的复兴最为活跃。

“Prior to dam removal snorkel surveys of the lower Elwha (2009-11) never revealed more than one or two summer steelhead,” writes NOAA fish biologist Sarah Morley and colleagues in a May 2019 paper. “Sonar [research] estimated the 2018 summer run population to be at least 300 fish. Like the phoenix, summer runs have arisen from the ashes.”

NOAA鱼类生物学家Sarah Morley及其同事在2019年5月的一篇文章中写道：“在下Elwha（2009-11）进行大坝拆除通气管之前，从未发现过一两个夏天的夏季硬头。” “ Sonar [研究]估计，2018年夏季跑步的鱼类至少为300条。像凤凰一样，夏天从灰烬中升起。”

To remove dams as large as those on the Klamath River will be a complex operation. In the Klamath River Renewal Corporation’s plan for the removals, it will start with drawing down the water levels behind each dam wall. Demolition comes next – largely through drilling and blasting, with trucks removing the rubble. The newly exposed reservoir bed is then covered in mulch and indigenous seeds. Not only does this help restore this habitat to its natural state: both will be critical to reduce the amount of sediment washed down to the sea. In experiments conducted by Ellen Mussman and others ahead of the Elwha dam removals, plants reduced erosion by 33%, while mulch reduced it by 99%. Together, these could be a highly effective means to stop erosion, the researchers conclude.

拆除与克拉马斯河一样大的水坝将是一项复杂的工作。在克拉马斯河复兴公司的拆除计划中，将首先降低每座大坝墙后的水位。接下来是拆除工作–主要是通过钻孔和爆破，然后用卡车将碎石运走。然后，新暴露的水库床被覆盖物和本地种子覆盖。这不仅有助于将栖息地恢复到自然状态：两者对于减少冲洗到海底的沉积物的数量至关重要。在Elwha大坝拆除之前，Ellen Mussman和其他人进行的实验中，植物减少了33％的侵蚀，而覆盖减少了99％。研究人员得出结论，这些因素一起可能是阻止侵蚀的高效方法。

And while it might seem counterintuitive that a power company would be in favour of dam removals, it actually makes good business sense for PacifiCorp. This is because to renew the operating licence for these dams, its ratepayers would have to foot an approximately $400m (£308m) bill for upgrades to ensure compliance with legislation (including the installation of costly fish ladders at each dam that would enable migration).

尽管电力公司赞成拆除大坝似乎是违反直觉的，但对于PacifiCorp而言，这实际上具有良好的商业意义。这是因为要续签这些水坝的运营许可证，其纳税人将必须支付约4亿美元（3.08亿英镑）的升级费用，以确保遵守法律（包括在每个水坝上安装昂贵的鱼梯，以实现移民） 。

Removing the dams is a cheaper option: under the Klamath Hydroelectric Settlement Agreement (KHSA), customers will only have to pay $200m (£154m), with an additional $250m (£193m) coming from the State of California. The removals have been endorsed by the Public Utility Commissions of both Oregon and California as being in the interests of ratepayers. Bob Gravely, regional business manager of Pacific Power (the PacifiCorp subsidiary which runs the dams) says that the dam removals “became a better outcome for customers”.

拆除水坝是一种较便宜的选择：根据《克拉马斯水电定居协议》（KHSA），客户仅需支付2亿美元（1.54亿英镑），另外2.5亿美元（1.93亿英镑）来自加利福尼亚州。俄勒冈州和加利福尼亚州的公共事业委员会都批准了搬迁，这符合纳税人的利益。太平洋电力公司（经营水坝的PacifiCorp子公司）的区域业务经理Bob Gravely说，拆除水坝“为客户带来了更好的结果”。

Overall, little will be lost in terms of renewable energy generation: the dams represent less than 8% of PacfiCorp’s 2,208 MW current renewable generation capacity, and as of July 2020 a further 1,190 MW of renewable capacity was under construction. The utility anticipates an additional 3,743MW of renewables coming on stream by the end of 2023.

总体而言，就可再生能源发电而言，损失几乎没有：大坝仅占PacfiCorp当前2208兆瓦可再生能源发电量的不到8％，截至2020年7月，又有1190兆瓦可再生能源正在建设中。该公司预计到2023年底将有3,743MW可再生能源投入生产。

“I think one of the coolest parts about this whole project is we’re setting a precedent for the world to follow,” says Cordalis. “I think the approach of working together with the company, with states, with tribes, with environmentalists, to reach an agreement that allows these dams to be removed for the tribes and for American citizens to benefit from the restoration of this river in a way that costs less money than it would be to relicense [the dams] – that's really a model of how you might approach sustainable river restoration across the world.”

Cordalis说：“我认为整个项目中最酷的部分之一就是为世界树立了先例。” “我认为与公司，州，部落，环保主义者合作的方法达成一项协议，允许拆除这些水坝，为部落和美国公民从某种程度上从这条河的修复中受益它的费用比发给大坝的费用要少。这确实是您如何在世界范围内进行可持续河流修复的模型。”

The dam removals were slated for 2022, though with negotiations still ongoing between the company, the tribes and other stakeholders, that date is still unconfirmed. But Cordalis says she still remains hopeful. “We’re getting very close,” she says.

大坝拆除计划在2022年进行，尽管公司，部落和其他利益相关者之间仍在进行谈判，但该日期仍未确定。但是科尔达利斯说她仍然充满希望。她说：“我们之间的距离非常近。”

“I think we all understand that there is an indigenous tribe [and] a culture at stake,” says Myers. “I think it has held fast in these negotiations that these dam removal efforts are as much to remove the dams for the ecology and benefits of salmon restoration as they are to the wrongs that took place in this country for the last 150, 200 years against Native Americans.”

迈尔斯说：“我认为我们都知道，土著部落和文化处于危险之中。” “我认为，在这些谈判中，为消除鲑鱼的生态和利益而进行的水坝拆除工作与在该国过去150、200年中针对该国的过失所犯的错误同样重要美洲土著。”

For the Yurok, Myers says the dams are seen as “monuments to colonialism” and compares them to statues of Confederate generals. “These dams are statues of the war that we fought here on the Klamath River. And these statues destroy our river, the landscape, our culture. We have to deal with them every single day.” In response to this, Pacific Power’s Gravely says: “We are very pleased to be part of a settlement agreement that allows the desire of Klamath Basin Tribes and others for dam removal to move forward” while also ensuring protections for electricity customers in six states.

迈尔斯说，对于尤罗克人来说，水坝被视为“殖民主义的纪念碑”，并将其与同盟将军的雕像相提并论。这些水坝是我们在克拉马斯河上进行的战争的雕像。这些雕像破坏了我们的河流，风景和文化。我们必须每天与他们打交道。”对此，太平洋电力公司的格雷夫利说：“我们很高兴成为一项和解协议的一部分，该协议允许克拉马斯盆地部落和其他人希望拆除大坝向前发展”，同时还确保了对六个州电力用户的保护。

Myer says the treaty negotiated between Yurok and the federal government in the 1850s limited the tribe to their reservation in return for a good standard of living in perpetuity. Although, he says, the federal government failed to live up to its end of the bargain, dam removals would bring that goal closer.

迈尔（Myer）说，尤罗克（Yorok）与联邦政府在1850年代谈判达成的条约将部落限制在他们的保留范围内，以换取永久性的良好生活水平。他说，尽管联邦政府未能履行其讨价还价的条件，但拆除大坝将使这一目标更加接近。

Anticipating the return of healthy fish runs, the tribe has already built a salmon harvesting plant – both for commercial and subsistence fishing – done sustainably, just as Yurok have done for millennia.

期望恢复健康的鱼类繁殖，该部落已经建立了鲑鱼捕捞厂，无论是商业捕鱼还是自给自足捕鱼，都是可持续发展的，就像Yurok千年来所做的那样。

“We have been surviving off the river’s resources and living symbiotically with it since time immemorial,” says Cordalis. “Our creation story talks about how the creator made the river, the land, the animals, the plants, and then made the people and said to the people, ‘This will all be here for you and you won't want for anything as long as you live in a sustainable way with the natural environment, and as long as you don't take more than you need to support your family.’ That initial religious principle informs how we interact with the river, how we interact with all of its resources and the natural world.”

Cordalis说：“自远古时代以来，我们一直在河上生存，并与之共生。” “我们的创作故事讲述了创作者如何创造河流，土地，动物，植物，然后创造了人们，并对人们说：'这一切都将在这里为您服务，而您将不会想要只要您与自然环境以可持续的方式生活，并且只要您不花更多的钱养家就可以。”最初的宗教原则告诉我们如何与河流互动，与所有资源和自然世界互动。”

While the dams have increasingly threatened this symbiosis, their removal will once more enable the ancient connection between the Yurok people and the Klamath River to flourish.

尽管水坝越来越多地威胁着这种共生，但将其拆除将再次使尤罗克人与克拉马斯河之间的古老联系蓬勃发展。

On 17 November 2020, a new agreement was signed between PacifiCorp, the Yurok and other stakeholders to facilitate the dams' removals. Should federal regulators approve, the project will begin in 2022, with the demolitions slated for 2023.

2020年11月17日，PacifiCorp，Yurok和其他利益相关者签署了一项新协议，以便利大坝建设。清除。如果联邦监管机构批准，该项目将在2022年开始，拆除计划在2023年进行。

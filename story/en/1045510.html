Scientists Use Satellite Imagery To Count Elephants
科学家利用卫星图像计数大象

2021-01-21 15:12:16

Monitoring endangered species is vital to help them survive. In order to protect them from poachers or habitat destruction, conservationists need accurate monitoring.

监测濒危物种对于帮助他们生存至关重要。为了保护他们免遭偷猎者或栖息地破坏，保护主义者需要进行准确的监测。

Current methods are prone to error, which could lead to misunderstandings of animal trends and wrongly allocating resources. So a team of scientists from the University of Bath, the University of Oxford, and the University of Twente in the Netherlands, combined satellite imagery with deep learning to detect elephants from space.

当前的方法容易出错，可能导致对动物趋势的误解和错误地分配资源。因此，来自巴斯大学，牛津大学和荷兰特温特大学的一组科学家将卫星图像与深度学习相结合，可以从太空中检测大象。

The team's method proved comparable to human detection accuracy and could help solve a number of existing challenges, such as cross-border limits, and cloud coverage, among others.

事实证明，该团队的方法可与人类检测的准确性相媲美，并且可以帮助解决许多现有挑战，例如跨境限制和云覆盖等。

The team used Maxar WorldView-3 satellite imagery, which is capable of collecting more than one million acres (5,000 km2) imagery in one go in just a few minutes. This allows for fast repeat imaging when necessary, and minimizes the risk of double counting as it's so rapid.

该团队使用了Maxar WorldView-3卫星图像，该图像能够在短短几分钟内一次采集超过100万英亩（5,000平方千米）的图像。这样可以在必要时进行快速重复成像，并且将重复计数的速度降到最低，从而将其最小化。

Then the team leveraged deep learning to process the vast amount of imagery it collected from Maxar's WorldView-3 satellite. In a matter of hours, the team collected its relevant data. This process usually takes months when sorting out by hand.

然后，该团队利用深度学习处理从Maxar的WorldView-3卫星收集的大量图像。在几个小时内，团队就收集了相关数据。手工整理时，此过程通常需要几个月的时间。

On top of speed, the deep learning algorithms also provided consistent results less prone to error, as well as false negatives and false positives.

除了速度之外，深度学习算法还提供了一致的结果，不易出错，以及误报和误报。

In order to develop this method, the team created a customized training dataset of over 1,000 elephants, and then fed it into a Convolutional Neural Network (CNN). After trials, the team concluded that its CNN can detect elephants in satellite imagery with as high an accuracy as human detection capabilities.

为了开发这种方法，研究小组创建了一个定制的训练数据集，其中包含1000多头大象，然后将其输入到卷积神经网络（CNN）中。经过试验，研究小组得出结论，其CNN能够以与人类检测能力一样高的精度检测卫星图像中的大象。

" Satellite imagery resolution increases every couple of years, and with every increase, we will be able to see smaller things in greater detail," said Dr. Olga Isupova, a computer scientists at the University of Bath, adding: "Other researchers have managed to detect black albatross nests against snow. No doubt the contrast of black and white made it easier, but that doesn’t change the fact that an albatross nest is one-eleventh the size of an elephant."

Last month’s “Time Flies” event for the Apple Watch Series 6 and new iPad Air was about an hour long; this week’s “Hi, Speed” event ran just a bit longer at 70 minutes. Perhaps, if 2020 had gone has planned, all of these products would have been announced in one big two-hour event in early September. There’s a lot I miss about in-person events, 1 but in terms of dosing the news, I like the digestibility of these shorter, more focused events. There’s more than enough to process considering just the new iPhone 12 models and the dessert course (served before the entrees) of HomePod Mini.

上个月Apple Watch Series 6和新款iPad Air的“时光飞逝”活动持续了大约一个小时；本周的“嗨，速度”活动的时间稍长一些，为70分钟。也许，如果2020年按计划过去了，所有这些产品都会在9月初的一个两小时的大型活动中发布。我很怀念面对面的活动，1但就新闻的投放而言，我喜欢这些更短、更有针对性的活动的易解性。仅考虑到新的iPhone12机型和HomePod Mini的甜点套餐(在主菜之前提供)，我们就有足够的东西要处理了。

So there are four new iPhones this year. Is that confusing? I don’t think so, and if anything is confusing or complicated about this year’s lineup, it’s at the high end, with the camera system differences between the 12 Pro and 12 Pro Max. The addition of a fourth iPhone, the 12 Mini, doesn’t complicate the lineup at all, because its name completely describes everything there is to know about it.

因此，今年有四款新iPhone。这让人困惑吗？我不这么认为，如果说今年的产品阵容有什么令人困惑或复杂的地方，那就是高端产品，因为12Pro和12Pro Max之间的相机系统存在差异。第四款iPhone 12 Mini的加入丝毫不会让产品阵容变得复杂，因为它的名字完全描述了所有关于它的信息。

Really, the product names tell you how to understand the lineup. The iPhone 12 is the new iPhone — almost certainly the best choice for most people in the market for a new-model-year iPhone. Unless they really want an iPhone that is smaller, in which case they should, with no hesitation, get the iPhone 12 Mini. In terms of features and specs, it’s exactly like the iPhone 12, just smaller. It’s also $100 cheaper — but the reason to buy a 12 Mini is the size, not the price. If a lower price is more meaningful to you than device size, you should probably either get an iPhone 11 or XR and save some money, or, for a smaller device, get an iPhone SE and save a lot of money.

真的，产品名称告诉你如何理解产品阵容。IPhone12是新款iphone - ，几乎可以肯定是市场上大多数人购买新款iphone的最佳选择。除非他们真的想要一部更小的iPhone，在这种情况下，他们应该毫不犹豫地购买iPhone12 Mini。就功能和规格而言，它与iPhone12完全一样，只是更小。 - 的价格也便宜了100美元，但购买12 Mini的原因是尺寸，而不是价格。如果更低的价格对你来说比设备大小更有意义，你可能应该买一部iPhone11或XR并节省一些钱，或者，对于更小的设备，买一部iPhone SE并节省一大笔钱。

First, comparing like-to-like models year-over-year, the regular iPhone 12 is $130 more expensive than the iPhone 11 was. The fact that the 12 Mini is just $30 more than the equivalent 11 was a year ago helps assuage that, but, again, I think the 12 Mini is best thought of as a variant for people who really prefer smaller phones, not as the base model.

首先，与去年同期相比，普通iPhone12比iPhone11贵130美元。事实上，12 Mini只比一年前同等的11款高出30美元，这有助于缓解这一点，但我再次认为，12 Mini最好被认为是那些真正喜欢较小手机的人的变种，而不是作为基础机型。

Second, the Pro models are actually less expensive than they were last year. The base prices are the same, but base storage goes from 64 to 128 GB, which, to be honest, feels overdue. We can argue about how much the “Pro” in these iPhone product names means professional and how much it actually means deluxe, but 64 GB of storage in 2019 was neither professional nor deluxe. At the 256 and 512 GB tiers, prices are $50 lower this year.

其次，Pro型号实际上比去年便宜。基本价格是一样的，但基本存储空间从64 GB增加到128 GB，老实说，这让人感觉早该这么做了。我们可以争论这些iPhone产品名称中的“Pro”到底有多专业，到底有多少意味着豪华，但2019年的64 GB存储空间既不专业也不豪华。在256 GB和512 GB的层级上，今年的价格低了50美元。

But the main thing to take away is that the prices are much more continuous. Last year there was a gaping $300 chasm between the iPhone 11 and the 11 Pro; this year that difference is only $120. You don’t have to be Jeff Williams to figure out that OLED displays are expensive. 5G modems — exclusively available from Apple’s bitter frenemy Qualcomm — are probably expensive too, but with the Pro models going down in price year-over-year, it seems clear that the single biggest factor is OLED vs. LCD. When the iPhone 11 was LCD and the 11 Pros were OLED — and in 2018 when there was an LCD/OLED split between the XR and XS models — there was a gaping difference in price. This year, with all iPhone 12 models on OLED, there is no pricing gap.

但主要要考虑的是，价格的连续性要高得多。去年，iPhone11和11Pro之间存在着300美元的巨大鸿沟；今年的差距只有120美元。即使你不是杰夫·威廉姆斯(Jeff Williams)，也能明白OLED显示屏是昂贵的。5G调制解调器 - 可以从苹果苦涩的朋友高通 - 独家购买，价格可能也很贵，但随着Pro型号的价格逐年下降，似乎很明显，最大的因素是oled与lcd。当iPhone11是液晶屏，11个专业版是oled - 时，2018年，当XR和XS型号 - 的液晶屏/oled分开时，价格出现了巨大的差异。今年，所有iPhone12机型都安装了OLED，没有定价差距。

12 Pro vs. 12 Pro Max the Rather Confusing Nomenclature and Technical Differences With Their Cameras

12专业版与12专业版MAX它们的相机在名称和技术上的差异相当令人困惑。

When Apple introduced the “Max” moniker with 2018’s iPhone XS, the proposition was much like that of the 12 and 12 Mini this year: the only difference was size. That was true last year with the 11 Pro and 11 Pro Max, too. For me, someone who desired the very best camera system Apple makes but not the biggest-ass display size, that was great.

当苹果公司(Apple)在2018年的iPhone XS中引入“Max”这个绰号时，其主张与今年的12和12 Mini非常相似：唯一的区别是尺寸。去年11Pro和11Pro Max也是如此。对于我来说，对于一个渴望苹果制造的最好的相机系统，而不是最大的显示器尺寸的人来说，这是很棒的。

The 12 Pro Max has several camera advantages over the 12 Pro, harking back to the iPhone 6 / 6S / 7 era, when the Plus models had camera features their regular-sized siblings did not. 2 Herewith, I believe, is the full accounting of the differences between the 12 Pro and 12 Pro Max, camera by camera:

与12 Pro相比，12 Pro Max有几个相机优势，这让人回想起iPhone6/6S/7时代，当时Plus机型拥有普通尺寸的兄弟姐妹所没有的相机功能。2我相信，下面是对12 Pro和12 Pro Max之间的差异的全面说明(逐个摄像头)：

Front-facing (a.k.a. “TrueDepth”, a.k.a. “selfie”): Same across all iPhone 12 models.

正面(也称为。“真相深度”(TrueDepth)，又名。“自拍”)：所有iPhone12机型都是一样的。

Wide (1×): Same on iPhone 12, 12 Mini, and 12 Pro, with a new ƒ/1.6 lens that captures 27 percent more light than last year’s 1× wide lens. The 12 Pro Max has the same ƒ/1.6 lens, but also has an altogether different sensor that is 47 percent larger than the 1× camera sensor on the other models. This bigger sensor has the same number of pixels (12 MP = 4032 × 3024), but those pixels are bigger. The larger sensor combined with the new-to-all-models ƒ/1.6 lens means the 1x wide camera on the 12 Pro Max captures 87 percent more light than last year’s iPhone 11 models. And that’s not all: in addition to being bigger, the new Pro Max’s 1x camera sensor exclusively features sensor-shift OIS, stabilizing the sensor rather than the lens, which according to Apple is beneficial both for photos and video. This sensor-shift OIS is also what enables the 12 Pro Max’s ability to capture up to 2-second exposures handheld, which, if it works as Apple describes, is a breakthrough that would be impractical in non-computational photography. Bottom line: all iPhone 12 models have the same 1x camera lens, which is faster than last year’s models, but the 12 Pro Max also has a bigger sensor and sensor-shift OIS.

宽(1倍)：iPhone12、12Mini和12Pro也是如此，新的ƒ/1.6型镜头比去年的1倍宽镜头多捕捉27%的光线。12Pro Max拥有相同的ƒ/1.6型镜头，但也有一个完全不同的感应器，比其他型号的1倍摄像头感应器大47%。这个较大的传感器具有相同的像素数(12MP=4032×3024)，但这些像素更大。更大的传感器与全新的ƒ/1.6型镜头相结合，意味着12Pro Max上的1倍宽摄像头比去年的iPhone11型号多捕捉87%的光线。这还不是全部：新的Pro Max的1X相机传感器除了更大之外，还具有传感器移位OIS功能，稳定了传感器而不是镜头，据苹果公司称，这对照片和视频都有好处。这种传感器偏移的OIS也是12Pro Max能够捕捉最多2秒曝光的手持设备的原因，如果它像苹果所说的那样工作，这是一个突破，在非计算摄影中是不切实际的。一句话：所有iPhone 12机型都有相同的1倍摄像头镜头，比去年的机型速度更快，但12 Pro Max也有更大的感应器和感应器移位OIS。

Telephoto: This is the lens that the non-Pro models do not have. On the iPhone 12 Pro, it’s a 2× ƒ/2.0 lens with equivalent field of view to a 52mm lens. On the 12 Pro Max, it’s a 2.5× ƒ/2.2 lens equivalent to a 65mm lens. The sensors, apparently, are the same or effectively the same. 2.5x is “better” than 2.0x because it’s longer, offering more effective optical zoom. But ƒ/2.0 is “better” than ƒ/2.2, because it lets in more light. But whatever low-light advantage the 12 Pro’s ƒ/2.0 aperture might have over the 12 Pro Max’s ƒ/2.2 aperture, in practice this is almost certainly effectively moot, because in low-light situations the camera system probably gets better results using the faster 1× camera and digitally zooming to a 2×/2.5× crop factor.

长焦镜头：这是非专业型号没有的镜头。在iPhone12Pro上，它是一个2xƒ/2.0型镜头，视场相当于52 mm镜头。在12Pro Max上，它是一个相当于65 mm镜头的2.5×ƒ/2.2镜头。显然，这些传感器是相同的或实际上是相同的。2.5x比2.0x“更好”，因为它更长，提供了更有效的光学变焦。但是ƒ/2.0比ƒ/2.2“更好”，因为它引入了更多的光。但是，无论12Pro的ƒ/2.0光圈与12Pro MAX的ƒ/2.2光圈相比有什么微光优势，实际上这几乎肯定是没有意义的，因为在微光情况下，相机系统可能会使用速度更快的1倍相机并以数字方式缩放到2×/2.5倍的裁剪因子，从而获得更好的效果。

Apple has confused all of this by promoting “4x” (12 Pro) and “5x” (12 Pro Max) “optical zoom range”. How can you get 4x or 5x optical zoom out of 2x and 2.5x lenses? What Apple is talking about here is the full range of optical zoom from the ultra wide 0.5x lens to the telephoto 2x/2.5x lenses. I think they’re doing this because the marketing looks better to say 4x/5x rather than 2x/2.5x.

苹果通过推广“4倍”(12 Pro)和“5倍”(12 Pro Max)的“光学变焦范围”，混淆了这一切。怎样才能使2x和2.5x镜头的光学变焦达到4x或5x呢？苹果在这里谈论的是从超宽0.5x镜头到长焦2x/2.5x镜头的全系列光学变焦。我认为他们这样做是因为市场营销说4x/5x比2x/2.5x更好。

The biggest difference, most obviously, is the existence of a telephoto lens at all. Also, the existence of a lidar sensor, which the 12 Pro models use for faster autofocus in low light (6 times faster, according to Apple) and to enable Night Mode portrait shots.

最大的不同，最明显的，是长焦镜头的存在。此外，还有激光雷达传感器的存在，12Pro型号使用该传感器在微光下更快地自动对焦(据苹果公司称，速度是以前的6倍)，并启用夜间模式人像拍摄。

All iPhone 12 models support shooting Dolby Vision 10-bit HDR video. But the 12 and 12 Mini only support Dolby Vision HDR at 30 FPS — the Pro models both support up to 60 FPS.

所有iPhone 12机型均支持拍摄杜比视觉10位HDR视频。但12和12迷你只支持杜比视觉HDR在30FPS的 - 的专业型号都支持高达60FPS。

Apple’s upcoming ProRAW features — which will enable shooting RAW images using the built-in Camera app and a bunch of new APIs for third-party camera and photo-editing apps — are exclusive to the 12 Pro models. (Apple says ProRAW is coming “later this year” — I’m guessing that means iOS 14.2.)

苹果即将推出的proraw具有 - 功能，它可以使用内置相机应用程序拍摄原始图像，以及一系列针对第三方相机和照片编辑应用程序的新API， - 是12Pro机型独有的。(苹果公司表示，ProRAW将在“今年晚些时候”推出 - ，我猜这意味着iOS14.2。)。

When you consider the camera specs alone, that seems like pure marketing spite. All iPhone 12 models have the A14 SoC with the same CPU, GPU, and Neural Engine. But there might be a technical reason ProRAW is limited to the iPhone 12 Pro models: according to the latest version of Xcode, the 12 Pro models have 50 percent more RAM than the iPhone 12 and 12 Mini (6 GB vs. 4 GB). It seems reasonable to assume that ProRAW and 60 FPS Dolby Vision encoding are RAM-hungry features. But because Apple never ever talks about RAM in iOS devices, even in the small print of their advertised tech specs, this comes across as purely marketing-driven differentiation.

当你单独考虑相机的规格时，这似乎是纯粹的营销怨恨。所有iPhone12机型都采用A14 SoC，采用相同的CPU、GPU和神经引擎。但可能有一个技术原因，ProRAW仅限于iPhone 12 Pro型号：根据最新版本的Xcode，12 Pro型号的RAM比iPhone 12和12 Mini多50%(6 GB对4 GB)。似乎可以合理地假设ProRAW和60FPS杜比视觉编码是需要RAM的功能。但由于苹果从不在iOS设备中谈论RAM，即使在其广告技术规格的小字体中也是如此，这给人的印象是纯粹的营销驱动的差异化。

If you were holding out for a X-class iPhone significantly smaller than the 5.8-inch iPhone X, your patience has finally been rewarded. The iPhone 12 Mini seems like a fantastic device. You save $100 and pay no penalty in camera quality, performance, display brightness or color gamut. It’s just smaller. Battery life takes a hit compared to the regular iPhone 12, but, judging by Apple’s quoted numbers, the battery life difference seems commensurate with device volume. You can’t simultaneously clamor for a smaller device and a bigger battery.

如果你坚持要一部比5.8英寸的iPhone X小得多的X级iPhone，你的耐心终于得到了回报。IPhone12 Mini似乎是一款非常棒的设备。您可以节省100美元，并且在相机质量、性能、显示亮度或色域方面不会有任何损失。它只是更小而已。与普通的iPhone12相比，电池续航时间受到了影响，但从苹果引用的数据来判断，电池续航时间的差异似乎与设备体积相称。你不能同时叫嚣着要一个更小的设备和一个更大的电池。

So Apple now sells modern X-class iPhones in small, medium, and large sizes. But a truly complete lineup would have two additional models: a non-Pro iPhone 12 Max and an iPhone 12 Pro Mini. Given that there is now a technology gap between the regular-size 12 Pro and 12 Pro Max camera systems (see above), it seems unfair to assume a 12 Pro Mini with all the features of the regular 12 Pro is possible without undesirable tradeoffs. There may well not be room for all the 12 Pro camera system features in a Mini-sized device.

因此，苹果现在销售小号、中号和大号的现代X级iPhone。但一个真正完整的阵容应该有两个额外的型号：非专业的iPhone12Max和iPhone12Pro Mini。鉴于目前普通尺寸的12 Pro和12 Pro Max相机系统之间存在技术差距(见上文)，假设12 Pro Mini拥有常规12 Pro的所有功能而不进行不受欢迎的权衡，似乎是不公平的。迷你设备中可能没有空间容纳12Pro相机系统的所有功能。

But a non-Pro 12 Max is obviously technologically feasible. That’s a product Apple simply chooses not to make. If you want a big 6.7-inch display, you have to pay Pro prices, even if you don’t care about any of the Pro features or accoutrements (extra camera lens, lidar, premium stainless steel finish, sleeker wallpapers, etc.).

但非Pro 12Max显然在技术上是可行的。这是苹果简单地选择不生产的产品。如果你想要6.7英寸的大显示屏，你必须支付Pro的价格，即使你不关心Pro的任何功能或装备(额外的相机镜头、激光雷达、优质不锈钢表面处理、更光滑的壁纸等)。

But I think a non-Pro iPhone Max model, in particular, would be really popular, because I think a lot of people desire big-ass phones solely for the display size. And I think Apple doesn’t make it because a lot of people who really care that much about having the largest possible display will just pay the premium for the Pro Max. This product strategy is true for the iPad and MacBook lineups, too — Apple’s biggest displays are only in its “Pro” models. A 16-inch MacBook Air and 12.9-inch iPad Air would undeniably both be popular, but would cannibalize sales of the more expensive Pro models with the largest displays.

但我认为非Pro iPhone Max机型会特别受欢迎，因为我认为很多人想要的大屁股手机仅仅是为了显示屏尺寸。我认为苹果没有做到这一点，是因为很多真正关心拥有尽可能大的显示屏的人只会为Pro Max支付溢价。这一产品策略对ipad和macbook阵容也是如此，苹果最大的显示屏也只在其“ - ”机型中。不可否认，16英寸的MacBook Air和12.9英寸的iPad Air都会很受欢迎，但这会蚕食拥有最大显示屏的更昂贵的Pro机型的销售。

But, along those lines, it thus seems to me that of the two “missing” iPhones — Pro Mini and non-Pro Max — we’re more likely to someday see the one that’s more technically challenging (the Pro Mini) because it’s a product that would sell at a higher price to people whose first concern is size. But my gut says we’ll never see either of them.

但是，按照这些思路，在我看来，在两款“失踪”的 - Pro Mini和非Pro Max - 中，我们更有可能有一天看到技术上更具挑战性的一款(Pro Mini)，因为这款产品会以更高的价格卖给那些首先考虑尺寸的人。但我的直觉告诉我我们永远见不到他们中的任何一个。

People are complaining about Apple pinching pennies by no longer including the power adapter and headphones with new iPhones, but to me, the most notable omission across the board are entire products that don’t exist: non-Pro iPhones, iPads, and MacBooks with large displays.

人们抱怨苹果公司吝啬，因为新款iPhone不再包括电源适配器和耳机，但对我来说，最明显的遗漏是整个产品都不存在：非专业版iPhone、iPad和大屏幕MacBook。

I’m unequivocally in favor of no longer including headphones in the box. The move away from including power adapters is a little iffier, but I think it’s right. I think of it this way: they were never “free”. Whatever the price for the iPhone was, that price included the cost of the bundled power adapter. So if they’re included in the box, you might be (and in my case, personally, often were) paying for a power adapter and headphones you neither want or need. And Apple has lowered the prices on their own peripherals: their Lightning earbuds and 20W power adapter (which replaces the 18W adapter they included with iPhone Pros last year) are each $19; they used to cost $29.

我毫不含糊地赞成不再把耳机放在盒子里。不包括电源适配器的做法有点可疑，但我认为这是正确的。我是这样想的：他们从来都不是“自由的”。无论iPhone的价格是多少，这个价格都包括了捆绑电源适配器的成本。因此，如果包装盒中包含了它们，您可能(就我个人而言，通常是)为您既不想要也不需要的电源适配器和耳机买单。苹果已经降低了自己的外设价格：他们的闪电耳塞和20W电源适配器(取代了去年iPhone Pro附带的18W适配器)都是19美元；它们过去的价格是29美元。

Not including the power adapter in the box also clearly nudges people toward spending the $39 for the new MagSafe charger (which does not itself include a power adapter), in the same way that removing the headphone jack nudged people toward buying AirPods. Cynically, yes, such nudging is in the direction of buying more stuff from Apple. But it’s also the direction of a better user experience. The future is clearly not iPhones with USB-C ports instead of Lightning, but iPhones with no ports at all, like Apple Watch.

不包括电源适配器显然也会促使人们花39美元购买新的MagSafe充电器(本身不包括电源适配器)，就像去掉耳机插孔会促使人们购买AirPods一样。讽刺的是，是的，这样的推动是朝着从苹果购买更多东西的方向进行的。但这也是更好的用户体验的方向。未来显然不是有USB-C接口的iPhone，而不是Lightning，而是根本没有接口的iPhone，就像Apple Watch一样。

If you want to argue that it’s a silent $38 price hike, fine, it’s a $38 price hike. But by that logic, savvy buyers who don’t need wired earbuds or yet another USB-C power adapter are getting a $38 discount. As a general rule in life, it’s better to pay for what you need.

如果你想争辩说这是无声的38美元涨价，好吧，这是38美元的涨价。但按照这种逻辑，不需要有线耳塞或另一个USB-C电源适配器的精明买家将获得38美元的折扣。作为生活中的一条普遍规则，最好是为你需要的东西买单。

Did you hear about 5G and Verizon’s 5G network? If you watched the event, you certainly did. If you didn’t, here’s a supercut of every mention of “5G”.

你听说过5G和Verizon的5G网络吗？如果你看了这个活动，你肯定看了。如果你没有，这里有一个超级剪辑，每次提到“5G”。

Apple once named an iPhone after a cellular network technology upgrade and somehow this 5G stuff felt more heavy-handed. I just don’t see 5G as all that meaningful, and mentioning “5G” several dozen times throughout the event doesn’t change that. Going from 2G “EDGE” to 3G was a breakthrough in performance. 3G to LTE was significant. But today, when it comes to complaints and wishes for your phone, is “LTE is not fast enough” even on your list?

苹果曾以蜂窝网络技术升级命名iPhone，不知何故，这款5G产品感觉更重了。我只是不认为5G有那么有意义，在整个活动中提到几十次“5G”并不能改变这一点。从2G“EDGE”到3G是性能上的突破。3G到LTE意义重大。但是今天，当谈到对你手机的抱怨和愿望时，你的单子上是否甚至都有“LTE不够快”的字眼呢？

Before introducing the iPhone 12, Tim Cook handed the stage (literally — this part was shot in Apple’s Steve Jobs Theater) to Verizon CEO Hans Vestberg for four minutes. Four very long, conspicuous minutes. Vestberg was fine — this was not a Stan Sigman fiasco — but it just felt so gratuitous. I don’t know what Verizon paid Apple for this slot in the event, but it must have been a fortune. Good god would I love to be privy to the negotiations between Apple, Verizon, and AT&T for this. (I just presume T-Mobile was not in the running.)

在介绍iPhone12之前，蒂姆·库克将舞台(字面意思是 - ，这部分是在苹果的史蒂夫·乔布斯剧院拍摄的)交给威瑞森首席执行官汉斯·维斯特伯格(Hans Vestberg)四分钟。四分钟很长，很显眼。维斯特伯格很好， - ，这不是斯坦·西格曼惨败的 - ，但感觉太没必要了。我不知道Verizon为这次活动的这个位置向苹果支付了多少钱，但肯定是一大笔钱。天哪，我真希望能参与苹果、威瑞森和美国电话电报公司(AT&；T；)就此进行的谈判。(我只是假设T-Mobile不会参选。)。

Without delving deeply into details that, to be honest, I just don’t care about, there are two main types of 5G service. In Verizon’s parlance, these are “5G Nationwide” and “5G Ultra Wideband”. 5G Nationwide is best thought of as regular 5G — for most people in most places in most situations on most carriers, when their phone is getting 5G service, this is what they’re getting. 5G Ultra Wideband is faster, maybe way faster, but looking at coverage maps it’s sort of like Wi-Fi in scope — it’s only available outdoors and limited in range to specific streets. Ultra Wideband support on all iPhone 12 models is limited to the United States, and is the reason U.S. iPhone 12’s have a window on the side under the power button. (iPhone 12’s in Europe have their own ugly turds marring the side — stupid regulatory etchings that are mandatory over there.)

不深入挖掘细节，老实说，我就是不在乎，5G服务主要有两种类型。用Verizon的话说，这些是“5G全国范围”和“5G超宽带”。对于大多数地方的大多数人来说，在大多数运营商的大多数情况下，5G Nationwide最好被认为是常规的5G - ，当他们的手机获得5G服务时，这就是他们得到的。5G超宽带更快，可能要快得多，但从覆盖地图来看，它有点像Scope - 中的Wi-Fi，它只能在户外使用，范围仅限于特定的街道。所有iPhone12机型的超宽带支持仅限于美国，这也是美国iPhone12在电源按钮下的侧面有一个窗口的原因。(欧洲的iPhone12有自己的丑陋粪便，破坏了侧面 - 愚蠢的监管蚀刻，这些蚀刻在那里是强制性的。)。

Looks cool, $99 sounds right, looking forward to hearing it in action. The upcoming Intercom feature seems neat, too.

看起来很酷，99美元听起来不错，期待着在行动中听到它。即将推出的对讲机功能看起来也很棒。

As a watch nerd I get it, but it’s kind of funny that the HomePod Mini is the same price as some of Apple’s watch bands.

作为一个钟表迷，我明白这一点，但有趣的是，HomePod Mini的价格与苹果的一些表带相同。

I’m fascinated by the degree to which so many of Apple’s new products are doing cool things with magnets, of all things. iPad covers and keyboards, Apple Watch band clasps, and now MagSafe chargers and cases for iPhone.

苹果的许多新产品都在用磁铁做很酷的事情，这让我着迷。IPad保护套和键盘，Apple Watch表带扣，现在又有适用于iPhone的MagSafe充电器和保护套。

There’s a weird display brightness tech spec difference between the Pro and non-Pro models I don’t understand. On Apple’s Compare page, the display specs for the 12 and 12 Pro are identical — same size, same pixel count, same contrast ratio —  except for brightness. The 12 Pro is listed as “800 nits max brightness (typical)”; the 12 says “625 nits max brightness (typical)”. Yet both have the same max brightness (1,200 nits) for HDR content. Are these different components?

专业版和非专业版之间的显示亮度技术规格有一个奇怪的差异，我不明白。在苹果的比较页面上，12和12Pro的显示屏规格是相同的 - ，相同的尺寸，相同的像素数，相同的对比度 - ，除了亮度。12Pro被列为“800尼特最大亮度(典型)”；12表示“625尼特最大亮度(典型)”。然而，两者对于HDR内容都具有相同的最大亮度(1200尼特)。这些是不同的组件吗？

During Apple’s event, the iPhone 12 scenes were set in daytime, the 12 Pro scenes were at night. Apple’s iPhone 12 web pages are white, the 12 Pro pages black. Pro = dark mode.

在苹果发布会期间，iPhone12的场景设置在白天，而Pro的12个场景设置在晚上。苹果的iPhone12网页是白色的，12Pro网页是黑色的。PRO=暗模式。

我四处打听，一只小鸟证实丽莎·杰克逊真的去了苹果园的屋顶参加她的节目。看起来令人叹为观止。这个特技让我想起了史蒂夫·韦恩(Steve Wynn)在2005年介绍永利拉斯维加斯(他在三年后为其兄弟企业Encore重演了这一幕)(现在已经名誉扫地)。

Unsurprisingly, there’s no Touch ID in the power button like on the new iPad Air. Nor any mention of better support for face mask awareness with Face ID.

不出所料，电源按钮中没有像新iPad Air那样的Touch ID。也没有提到通过Face ID更好地支持面膜识别。

这是第一次在没有菲尔·席勒的情况下推出旗舰iPhone。事实上，席勒在史蒂夫·乔布斯病假期间主持了2009年WWDC上iPhone3GS的整个推介活动。他甚至告诉我们“S”代表什么！

说到2009年，你可能还记得苹果iPhone导游视频和广告中的HomePod Mini介绍人鲍勃·博尔彻(Bob Borcher)。

The obvious thing I miss from in-person events is hands-on time with all of the announced products. I opened with this observation in my week-on-the-wrist review of the Series 6 Apple Watch. When it comes to color, material, and device size, you really need to see and hold and feel the products. But the bigger thing, really, is in-person nuance. I miss talking to my fellow hacks in the press, getting their impressions and thoughts. And I greatly miss talking to folks from Apple in person, both formally, through the official PR channel, and informally, simply by bumping into people I know. Like any meeting, in any sphere, the official product briefings are just higher-bandwidth in person than when conducted remotely. It’s the difference between being able to chug straight from a cup and being forced to use an annoyingly narrow straw, despite being very thirsty. It sucks.

很明显，我在面对面活动中错过的一件事就是亲身体验所有发布的产品。在我对Series6 Apple Watch的一周回顾中，我以这样的观察作为开场白。当谈到颜色、材料和设备尺寸时，你真的需要看到、拿着和感受产品。但实际上，更重要的是面对面的细微差别。我很怀念与媒体上的黑客同行交谈，了解他们的印象和想法。我非常怀念与苹果公司的人面对面交谈，无论是正式的，通过官方公关渠道，还是非正式的，仅仅是通过碰见我认识的人。与任何会议一样，在任何领域，官方产品简报会都只是当面进行，而不是远程进行。对我来说，我们隔离的时间越长，Zoom/Webex/无论远程交互变得如何，其地方性缺点就越明显。这是能够直接从杯子里吸出来和被迫使用令人讨厌的狭窄吸管(尽管非常口渴)之间的区别。真没意思。

And the unofficial interactions — they just don’t really happen the same way at all if they’re not in person. I’m not talking about anyone spilling state secrets — the folks at Apple who have the most interesting things to say are also the people who are the least likely to ever reveal anything that shouldn’t be revealed.

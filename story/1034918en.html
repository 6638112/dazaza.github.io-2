工作兴趣不是工作满意度的重要预测因素

2020-11-13 19:36:53

Most people closing in on a high school or college degree, and even those casting their career fates without a diploma, at some point take an interest assessment to help determine how their interests relate to different jobs. According to the U.S. Department of Labor, The Occupational Information Network’s (O*NET) My Next Move website, which hosts the Interest Profiler, averages over 1 million site visits per month.

大多数即将获得高中或大学学位的人，甚至是那些在没有文凭的情况下决定自己职业命运的人，都会在某个时候进行兴趣评估，以帮助确定他们的兴趣与不同的工作之间的关系。根据美国劳工部的数据，职业信息网(O*Net)的My Next Move网站每月平均访问量超过100万次。

And now it can be told –  a report by a University of Houston researcher reveals that  your interest in an occupation does not  matter  as much as you  might   think   when it comes to job satisfaction.

现在可以说了--休斯顿大学(University Of Houston)一名研究人员的一份报告显示，当谈到工作满意度时，你对职业的兴趣并不像你想象的那么重要。

“Our main finding was that interest fit  significantly predict s  satisfaction, but it's not as strong of a relation as people expect, ”  reports Kevin Hoff, assistant professor of industrial-organizational psychology, in the  Journal of Vocational Behavior . “Other things that lead to satisfaction include the organization you work for, you r  supervisor, colleagues and pay.”

产业-组织心理学助理教授凯文·霍夫在《职业行为杂志》上写道：“我们的主要发现是，兴趣契合度可以显著预测人们的满意度，但这种关系并不像人们预期的那样强烈。”“其他能带来满足感的因素包括你所在的组织、你的主管、同事和薪酬。”

“ To be satisfied with a job,  y ou don’t have to worry  too  much about finding a perfect fit for  your  interests because  we know other things matter, too.  As long as  it’s something  you don’t hate doing, you may find  yourself  very  satisfied  if you have a good supervisor, like your coworkers, and are treated fairly by your organization ,” said Hoff.

霍夫说：“要想对一份工作感到满意，你就不必太担心找到一份适合你个人兴趣的完美工作，因为我们知道其他事情也很重要。只要你不讨厌做这件事，你可能会发现自己非常满意，如果你有一个好的上司，就像你的同事一样，并且受到你所在组织的公平对待。”(霍夫说，如果你有一个好的上司，就像你的同事一样，并且受到你的组织的公平对待，那么你就不必太担心找到一份与你的兴趣完全匹配的工作，因为我们知道其他事情也很重要。)。

To reach these conclusions, Hoff and team systematically reviewed data from 65 years of research  conducted  between 1949 and 2016 on the link between interest fit and job satisfaction. They examined 105 studies with 39,602 participants.

为了得出这些结论，霍夫和他的团队系统地回顾了1949年至2016年间就兴趣匹配和工作满意度之间的联系进行的65年研究的数据。他们检查了105项研究，有39602名参与者。

The research also indicates that the relationship between interest  fit  and job performance is more critical than the link to satisfaction.

研究还表明，兴趣和契合度与工作绩效之间的关系比与满意度的联系更为关键。

“Being interested in your work  seems  more important for  job  performance and  the downstream consequences  of performing well,  like raises or promotions,” said Hoff.

霍夫说：“对你的工作感兴趣似乎对你的工作表现和表现更重要，也更重要的是表现出色带来的下游后果，比如加薪或升职。”

The use of vocational interests to predict job satisfaction dates to the 1940's when interest assessments were first described as a tool to help people discover satisfying work.

利用职业兴趣来预测工作满意度可以追溯到20世纪40年代，当时兴趣评估首次被描述为帮助人们发现令人满意的工作的工具。

“In popular career guidance literature, it is widely assumed that interest fit is important for job satisfaction.  Our results  show that people who are more interested in their jobs tend to be slightly more satis fied , but  interest assessments are  more   useful for guiding people towards jobs in which they will perform better and make more money ” reports Hoff.

霍夫报道：“在流行的职业指导文献中，人们普遍认为兴趣契合度对工作满意度很重要。我们的研究结果显示，对工作更感兴趣的人往往会略微更满意，但兴趣评估在引导人们走向表现更好、收入更高的工作方面似乎更有用。”

Chelsea Song, Purdue University   co-led the study  along with Hoff;   and the research team also included   Wei Ming Jonathan Phan , California State Long Beach,   Colin  J.M.   Wee ,  and James Rounds, University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign.

普渡大学的切尔西·宋与霍夫共同领导了这项研究；研究团队还包括加州州立大学长滩的乔纳森·潘(Jonathan Phan)、伊利诺伊大学厄巴纳-香槟分校(University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign)的乔纳森·潘(Jonathan Phan)、科林(Colin)、J.M.Wee和詹姆斯·朗斯(James Runds)。

Twitter shuts down account of Sci-Hub
Twitter关闭了Sci-Hub的帐户

2021-01-15 20:08:45

Twitter last week permanently suspended the account of Sci-Hub, the website that has posted millions of freely accessible copies of scientific articles pirated from subscription journals. Twitter said Sci-Hub had violated its policy against promoting “counterfeit goods,” according to Sci-Hub’s founder, Alexandra Elbakyan.

Twitter上周永久性地暂停了Sci-Hub的帐户，该网站已发布了数百万免费订阅期刊盗版的科学文章的副本。 Twitter表示，根据Sci-Hub的创始人Alexandra Elbakyan的说法，Sci-Hub违反了其宣传“假冒商品”的政策。

The notification came shortly after a 6 January court hearing in India about a lawsuit filed by three of the world’s largest journal publishers—Elsevier, Wiley, and the American Chemical Society—which are seeking to block public access to Sci-Hub in that country because of copyright infringement. Elbakyan says Sci-Hub’s defense to the suit, filed in December 2020, will rely in part on tweets from Indian scientists who have said they support continued access to Sci-Hub because they cannot afford subscriptions to journal content.

该通知是在1月6日在印度举行的法院听证会上宣布的，该听证会涉及世界上三大期刊出版商Elsevier，Wiley和American Chemical Society提起的诉讼，它们正试图阻止该国公众访问Sci-Hub，因为侵犯版权。 Elbakyan表示，Sci-Hub对2020年12月提起的诉讼的辩护将部分取决于印度科学家的推文，这些推文称他们支持继续使用Sci-Hub，因为他们无法负担订阅期刊内容的费用。

The now-suspended Twitter account had included some tweets that linked to Sci-Hub’s website, Elbakyan wrote in an email to ScienceInsider. But the “account was used primarily for discussions around open science issues,” she wrote. “There is a clear difference between some account that shares copyrighted material and [a] discussion account such as Sci-Hub[’s]. … The ban of [the] Sci-Hub account is either an error or a censorship.”

Elbakyan在给ScienceInsider的电子邮件中写道，现已暂停的Twitter帐户包含一些链接到Sci-Hub网站的推文。但她写道：“该帐户主要用于围绕开放科学问题进行讨论”。 “共享共享材料的某些帐户与讨论帐户（例如Sci-Hub ['s）之间存在明显的区别。 …[Sci-Hub]帐户的禁令要么是错误，要么是审查制度。”

Sci-Hub started its Twitter account 9 years ago, and it had attracted more than 180,000 followers. That number pales in comparison with the more than 3 million unique internet addresses people used to download scholarly articles from Sci-Hub during a 6-month period in 2016, according to  a news article in  Science. The website now claims to offer more than 85 million papers for download—a large chunk of the world’s scientific literature.

Sci-Hub于9年前开始了其Twitter帐户，迄今已吸引了18万名粉丝。根据《科学》（Science）上的一篇新闻报道，与2016年过去6个月内从Sci-Hub下载学术文章的300万个独特互联网地址相比，这一数字相形见number。该网站现在声称提供了超过8500万篇论文供下载，这是世界科学文献的很大一部分。

The Twitter account had provided a measure of public support for Elbakyan’s service, which publishers call a criminal enterprise but she calls necessary because of the high cost of subscriptions. “Sci-Hub is really a matter of public interest, and many researchers are against” publisher efforts to shut down the site, she wrote.

Twitter帐户为Elbakyan的服务提供了一定程度的公共支持，发布商称该服务为犯罪企业，但由于订阅费用高昂，她认为这是必要的。她写道：“ Sci-Hub实际上是一个公共利益问题，许多研究人员都反对”出版商关闭该网站的努力。

Sci-Hub’s supporters have included researchers in India, and Elbakyan said she had archived many such tweets before Twitter suspended Sci-Hub’s account and now plans to cite them in court. But Sci-Hub’s argument that it is meeting a popular demand has not won it victories in copyright lawsuits brought by publishers in other countries, including the United States and Russia, where courts have issued injunctions blocking Sci-Hub from operating. Sci-Hub has circumvented some orders by moving its website to new internet addresses. The three journal publishers suing in India are asking the court to block such a loophole there by issuing a “dynamic” blanket order that could apply to multiple internet service providers through which Sci-Hub can be accessed.

Sci-Hub的支持者包括印度的研究人员，Elbakyan说，在Twitter暂停Sci-Hub的帐户并计划在法庭上引用之前，她已经存档了许多此类推文。但是，Sci-Hub关于满足大众需求的论点并未在其他国家（包括美国和俄罗斯）的出版商提起的版权诉讼中赢得胜利，在美国和俄罗斯，法院已经发布了禁止Sci-Hub运营的禁令。 Sci-Hub通过将其网站移至新的互联网地址来规避了一些订单。在印度提起诉讼的三家期刊出版商要求法院通过发布“动态”一揽子命令来阻止印度的这种漏洞，该命令可能适用于可通过其访问Sci-Hub的多个互联网服务提供商。

At a 6 January hearing, a judge delayed the proceedings until February. But the plaintiffs got some interim relief: Sci-Hub agreed not to upload any new papers from the three publishers to its website, at least until the next court hearing in the case.

在1月6日的听证会上，法官将诉讼推迟至2月。但是，原告得到了一些暂时的缓解：Sci-Hub同意不将这三个出版商的任何新论文上传到其网站，至少要等到下一次法院开庭审理之前。

How to Breathe Better
如何更好地呼吸

2021-03-22 00:21:25

“Take a deep breath.” This is some of the most common advice for dealing with stressful situations. But how exactly does taking a deep breath help us? And what really is a perfect deep breath anyway? On a recent episode of How To!, James Nestor, author of Breath: The New Science of a Lost Art, shared how we’ve got breathing all wrong. From snoring to anxiety to asthma, learning to breathe better can ameliorate so many of our bodies’ issues, and James reveals some of the key exercises to help us get there. This interview has been condensed and edited for clarity.

“深吸一口气。”这是处理压力情况的一些最常见建议。但是究竟深呼吸有多帮助我们？无论如何，真的是一个完美的深呼吸？在最近的一章如何！，James Nestor，呼吸作者：迷失艺术的新科学，共享我们如何呼吸所有错误。从打鼾到哮喘，学习更好地呼吸可以改​​善我们的尸体的问题，詹姆斯揭示了一些关键的练习，以帮助我们到达那里。此面试已被凝聚和编辑清楚起见。

James Nestor: I never set out to write a book about breath. I would have thought that that was a foolish thing to do. Breathing is something that is unconscious. It just runs in the background. Nobody ever thinks about it. That was my understanding of breath until I met these freedivers. I was sent on assignment to go to Greece and write about the World Freediving Championship, which is a competition in which different divers challenge one another to see how deep they can dive on a single breath of air and come back to the surface conscious. If that sounds crazy, it’s because it is. So I watch this guy take a single breath of air and completely disappear into the water below. The visibility was about 200 feet. He came back four minutes later and had just dived 103 meters on a single breath of air in four minutes. This completely blew me away because what these people are doing, according to several scientists years ago, was physically impossible. We shouldn’t be able to withstand such pressures. We shouldn’t be able to hold our breath for four, five, six, seven, eight minutes at a time.

詹姆斯·斯特诺特：我从未开始写一本关于呼吸的书。我会认为这是一个愚蠢的事情。呼吸是无意识的东西。它只是在后台运行。没有人想过它。这是我对呼吸的理解，直到我遇到了这些自由。我被派遣去参加希腊并写下世界自由冠军，这是一个不同潜水员挑战的竞争，彼此挑战，看看他们可以在一次呼吸中潜水，并回到表面意识。如果这听起来很疯狂，那就是因为它是。所以我看着这家伙呼吸一口气，完全消失在下水中。可见性约为200英尺。他稍后再回来四分钟，刚刚在四分钟内踩到了一口气的呼吸了103米。这完全完全吹了我，因为几年前的几个科学家介绍了这些人正在做的事情，因此是不可能的。我们不应该承受这种压力。我们不应该能够呼吸四个，五，六，七，八分钟。

I wrote the book Deep, and while writing and researching that book, I kept finding these super breathers, not just freedivers, but people who claimed to be able to heal themselves with their breath. All of this seemed totally sketchy to me. But I spent several months talking to the leaders in the field, looking at scientific studies, and I found out so many of these stories, which sounded so crazy, have been studied in controlled, randomized experiments. It’s true. We get most of our energy through our breath, and we’ve completely ignored that to our fault.

我深深地写了这本书，同时写作和研究那本书，我一直在找到这些超级呼吸，而不仅仅是自由，而且声称能够用呼吸治愈自己的人。所有这些似乎完全粗略地欺负我。但我花了几个月与该领域的领导人谈话，看着科学研究，我发现了许多这么多的故事，它似乎很疯狂，已经在受控，随机的实验中进行了研究。这是真的。我们通过呼吸获得大部分能量，我们完全忽略了我们的错。

Before we get to some of these exercises, can you describe how we have gotten to the place where most of us don’t breathe in the right way?

在我们参加其中一些练习之前，你能描述我们如何将我们大多数人都不正确呼吸的地方？

Well, some of it is anatomical. If you were to look at an ancient skull, anything older than about 500 years old, you would see straight teeth, a very broad jaw, a prognathic face, and these huge nasal apertures. You know, I had crooked teeth. I had braces, extractions, headgear, all that stuff. Everyone I knew had the same thing. And it got me wondering, like, well, why didn’t our ancestors have their wisdom teeth removed? Why didn’t they have braces? It’s because their mouths formed large enough to allow teeth to grow in straight.

好吧，其中一些是解剖学。如果你要看一个古老的头骨，那么超过500岁的东西，你会看到直的牙齿，一个非常宽阔的下巴，一种预后的面孔和这些巨大的鼻孔。你知道，我有牙齿弯曲的牙齿。我有牙套，提取，头饰，所有这些东西。我知道的每个人都有同样的事情。它让我想知道，好吧，好吧，为什么我们的祖先没有删除他们的智齿？他们为什么没有牙套？这是因为他们的嘴巴形成了足够大的，以便齿直线成长。

The change was brought on by industrialized food. A lot of people view evolution as progress, that we’re getting faster and leaner. That is complete garbage. We are developing traits right now across our species that are in no way advantageous for our long-term survival. Just look at our breathing—what happened is about 300 years ago very processed foods came into the food supply. Because this food is so soft, it’s also nutritionally deficient, but the main culprit is this chewing stress. You need that early on in life for the upper palate to spread apart, for your mouth to grow wide and for that face to be pulled outward. But if a mouth is too small, teeth have no option than to grow crooked. A smaller mouth means you have a smaller airway. That means you’re much more susceptible to respiratory problems, to obstruction, which is exactly what’s happened to us now.

通过工业化食品带来了改变。很多人认为演变是进步的，我们正在越来越瘦。这是完整的垃圾。我们正在通过我们的物种正在开发特质，这些物种绝不是我们的长期生存。只要看看我们的呼吸 - 发生了大约300年前的事情，非常加工的食物进入食品供应。因为这种食物如此柔软，所以它也营养缺乏，但主要的罪魁祸首是这种咀嚼的压力。在生活中需要早期的上层腭裂，让你的嘴宽阔，因为那张面部被向外拉动。但如果嘴巴太小，牙齿没有比种植弯曲。较小的嘴巴意味着你有一个较小的气道。这意味着你更容易受到呼吸问题的影响，阻碍，这正是现在发生在我们身上的。

If you open your mouth up, you’ll feel your tongue rocking back into your throat. When you close your mouth, you feel that tongue rocking up to your upper palate. That’s where it needs to be for that airway to be open. You can put your thumb to the roof of your mouth and if there’s a very large indentation there, that is likely causing some sort of obstruction in your nasal passages. It’s fascinating to see these ancient skulls and they all had these perfectly flat upper palates. None of them had these V-shaped palettes.

如果你张开嘴，你会觉得你的舌头摇晃回你的喉咙。当你闭上嘴时，你觉得舌头摇摆到你的上部腭。这就是它需要开放的气道所需的地方。你可以把你的拇指放在嘴的屋顶上，如果那里有一个非常大的缩进，那可能导致你的鼻腔段落中的某种阻碍。看到这些古老的头骨是令人着迷的，他们都有这些完美的平坦的上颚。他们都没有这些v形调色板。

So these maladaptations of our mouths are causing our airways to be constricted. Does this connect to the fact that many of us have problems with snoring and breathing through our mouths?

所以我们嘴的这些不适应导致我们的气道被收缩。这是否可以连接到许多人在我们的嘴里打鼾和呼吸有问题？

Yes. I was a mouth breather at night for as long as I ever remember. There are so many problems with that. When you sleep with an open mouth, you’re changing the P.H. in your mouth and making it more acidic, which it becomes a breeding ground for bad breath and cavities. Another problem is when you breathe through your mouth, you can essentially think of your lungs as an external organ. They’re exposed to all the dust, allergens, mold, pollution in the environment. Our nose is our first line of defense. It filters air so that by the time it enters our lungs they can much more efficiently extract oxygen. We get 20 percent more oxygen breathing through our nose than we do equivalent breaths through our mouth.

是的。只要我记得，我晚上都是一个嘴巴呼吸道。这有很多问题。当你用张开的嘴巴睡觉时，你正在改变p.h.在你的嘴里，使其更酸性，这成为口气不良和蛀牙的繁殖场。另一个问题是当你通过嘴巴呼吸时，你可以基本上将你的肺部视为外部器官。它们暴露于所有灰尘，过敏原，霉菌，环境中的污染。我们的鼻子是我们的第一道防线。它过滤空气，使其进入我们的肺部，它们可以更有效地提取氧气。我们通过我们的鼻子获得20％的氧气呼吸，而不是我们通过嘴巴的呼吸。

So I would suggest some advice I learned from a breathing therapist at Stanford: Take a little piece of tape about the size of a postage stamp, place it on your lips, and keep it on throughout the day for an hour or to acclimate yourself to breathe through your nose. After you’re comfortable with that, you can wear it at night. It’s going to be miserable. It’s going to be awkward. It’s a reminder so that when your muscles relax, you keep your mouth shut. If you can become a nasal breather at night, not only is that better for your lungs, but it can also significantly decrease your snoring because there’s more pressure and air is moving more slowly in through the nose than it is through the mouth right now.

所以我建议一些建议，我从斯坦福呼吸治疗师那里了解到：拿一点录像带关于邮票的大小，将它放在嘴唇上，并将其放在整天一天一小时或适应自己通过你的鼻子呼吸。在你对此感到满意之后，你可以在晚上穿它。这将是悲惨的。它会尴尬。这是一个提醒，这样当你的肌肉放松时，你会闭嘴。如果你可以在晚上成为鼻腔，不仅对你的肺部更好，但它也可以显着降低你的打鼾，因为在鼻子上的压力和空气越来越慢地移动而不是现在嘴巴。

Have you changed the way that you breathe because of what you’ve learned?

你是否改变了你所学到的东西所吸气的方式？

Yes, I took a CAT scan a year before and exactly a year after and during that year, I practiced some different exercises in my mouth. I expanded my upper palate with this weird little device, this retainer. I learned how to hold my tongue in the proper position. And through that year, I gained about in some areas of my airway about 15 to 20 percent more space. I also gained more bone in my face by doing what our ancient ancestors did: chewing a lot. The key with proper chewing stress is that it’s not that you can just grind your teeth right now because if we’re clenching both sides of our jaw that elicits a stress response. Chewing stress is on one side or the other and that elicits this parasympathetic response, which is why you accumulate saliva in your mouth, because your body is relaxing. So chewing [especially foods like carrots or gum] is very relaxing to you.

是的，我一年前拍了一只猫扫描，在那一年之后一年，我练习了一些不同的练习。我用这个奇怪的小装置扩展了我的上颚，这个保持器。我学会了如何在适当的位置握住我的舌头。到那一年，我在我的气道的一些地区获得了大约15到20％的空间。通过做我们古老的祖先所做的事情：咀嚼很多，我也在我脸上获得了更多的骨头。具有适当咀嚼压力的关键是，现在不是你现在可以磨牙，因为如果我们握紧我们的下巴的两侧，可能引起压力反应。咀嚼压力在一边或另一方，引发这种副交感神经反应，这就是你患上嘴里积累唾液的原因，因为你的身体正在放松。所以咀嚼[特别是食物，如胡萝卜或胶]对你来说非常放松。

Hold your breath while pinching your nostrils. Take an easy breath in and then exhale very soft breath and pinch your nostrils. Now hold your breath and rock your head up and down, back and forth. Whenever you feel a pronounced need to breathe, I want you to breathe through your nose very softly about 50 percent of the breath you would regularly take. Whenever you feel that need to breathe, just release your nostrils and breathe very softly. After about 30 to 40 seconds or so, do that exact same thing. This can really help to open up the nose and allow you to breathe through it much more easily.

捏你的鼻孔时屏住呼吸。轻松呼吸，然后呼气非常呼吸，捏住鼻孔。现在握住你的呼吸并来到和下来摇滚。每当你觉得呼吸呼吸时，我希望你能够非常轻柔地呼吸你的鼻子，你会定期呼吸约50％。每当你觉得需要呼吸时，只需释放你的鼻孔并呼吸很轻柔。大约30到40秒左右，做得完全相同。这真的可以帮助打开鼻子，让你更容易呼吸它。

The problem is today is many of us have such a low threshold of fear that we are constantly stressed out. We open our inbox. We see 30 messages our boss just wrote. We get that squirt of cortisol and we become stressed out. So breathing is one of the quickest ways of getting a hold of this. Another really healthy way to breathe is to breathe in to a count of about five to six seconds and out to a count of about five to six seconds. The systems of your body are going to enter the state of what they call resonance, where everything’s working at peak efficiency.

这个问题是今天的许多人都有这样一个低门槛，恐惧我们不断强调。我们打开收件箱。我们看到我们的老板刚写的30条消息。我们得到了皮质醇的喷射，我们受到压力。所以呼吸是抓住这个的最快方式之一。另一种非常健康的呼吸方式是呼吸约五到六秒钟的数量，达到大约五到六秒钟。您的身体系统将进入所谓的谐振的状态，一切都以峰值效率为工作。

I’ve found, especially when you’re anxious, or about to have a panic attack or asthma attack, that’s the worst advice in the world. What’s happening at these stages of panic is people start to breathe. Asthma attacks offload too much CO2, they cause constriction and exacerbate and trigger an attack. If someone’s having a heart attack and they’re given a paper bag, that is a really bad thing. An easier way that’s much healthier for you is to just slow down your breathing. You can put your hands in front of your face to mimic the feeling of a paper bag. Instead of breathing too much in those situations, breathe less to get your CO2 up. This is a very effective way of not only staving off an attack, but also inhibiting those attacks in the future.

我发现了，特别是当你担心或即将有恐慌或哮喘袭击时，这是世界上最糟糕的建议。在这些恐慌阶段发生了什么是人们开始呼吸。哮喘攻击卸载过多的二氧化碳，它们导致收缩和加剧并引发攻击。如果有人心脏病发作，他们给了一个纸袋，那就是一件非常糟糕的事情。一种更简单的方式，对你来说更健康，只是放慢你的呼吸。你可以把手放在你的脸前，以模仿纸袋的感觉。而不是在那些情况下呼吸太多，而不是呼吸少呼吸，以获得你的二氧化碳。这是一种非常有效的方式，不仅是攻击攻击，而且还抑制了未来的袭击。

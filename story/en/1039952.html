California fines Uber $59M for defending sexual assault victims’ privacy
加州因捍卫性侵犯受害者的隐私而对Uber罚款5900万美元

2020-12-16 06:05:45

The California Public Utility Commission has slapped Uber with a $59 million fine for refusing to hand over detailed records about more than 1,200 alleged sexual assaults involving Uber drivers in California between 2017 and 2019.

加州公共事业委员会因拒绝在2017年至2019年之间拒绝移交涉及Uber司机的1200多起涉嫌性侵犯的详细记录，对Uber处以5900万美元的罚款。

"The CPUC has been insistent in its demands that we release the full names and contact information of sexual assault survivors without their consent," Uber said in a Monday statement. "We opposed this shocking violation of privacy, alongside many victims’ rights advocates."

Uber disclosed the existence of thousands of sexual assaults nationwide in its 2019 safety report. Afterwards, the CPUC demanded detailed information about cases that occurred in California—including the time and place where assaults happened and names and contact information of witnesses. The CPUC order doesn't specifically ask for the names of victims. However, in many cases the victim would be the only witness, so CPUC was essentially seeking to unmask hundreds of sexual assault victims.

优步在其2019年安全报告中披露了全国范围内数千起性侵犯。之后，CPUC要求提供有关在加利福尼亚发生的案件的详细信息，包括袭击发生的时间和地点以及证人的姓名和联系方式。 CPUC命令没有具体要求受害者的名字。但是，在许多情况下，受害者将是唯一的证人，因此，CPUC实质上是在寻求掩盖数百名性侵犯受害者的信息。

Uber objected, noting that most of the victims had not consented to have their identities or stories shared with third parties. Even if the records were kept confidential, Uber argued, a CPUC investigation of these cases could force victims to revisit one of the most traumatic moments in their lives.

优步反对，并指出大多数受害者不同意与第三方分享自己的身份或故事。 Uber认为，即使对记录进行保密，CPUC对这些案件的调查也可能迫使受害者重新审视他们一生中最痛苦的时刻。

In a revised order, the CPUC allowed Uber to submit the information under seal to ensure that the data did not become public. But beyond that the CPUC refused to budge, issuing a second order in January demanding that Uber turn over the data.

按照修订后的顺序，CPUC允许Uber提交带有盖章的信息，以确保数据不会公开。但除此之外，CPUC拒绝让步，于1月发布了第二份订单，要求Uber交出数据。

Victims' rights groups like the Rape Abuse Incest National Network (RAINN) rallied to Uber's defense. "RAINN is exceedingly concerned that the Commission's ruling" directs Uber to "share a list of victim names, contact information, and additional incident details with the Commission, without the consent of the victims," the group wrote in an August filing with the CPUC.

受害者诸如强奸虐待乱伦国家网络（RAINN）之类的人权组织集会起来，对Uber的辩护。 RAINN非常担心委员会的裁决。指示Uber未经受害者同意，与委员会共享受害者姓名，联系信息和其他事件详细信息列表，＆＃34;该小组在8月向CPUC提交的文件中写道。

"The vast majority of sexual assault victims choose not to report to police," RAINN added. "Surely, they never envisioned that a state regulatory commission would require disclosure of information that they, themselves, have explicitly decided to not share with the state."

＆＃34;绝大多数性侵犯受害者选择不向警察报告，＆＃34;已添加RAINN。 ＆＃34;当然，他们从未想到过国家监管委员会会要求披露他们自己已明确决定不与国家共享的信息。

RAINN argued that enforcement of the CPUC's demands would "nullify each victim's right to decide when, and to whom, to disclose their assault" and would "risk re-traumatization of victims."

RAINN认为，执行CPUC的要求将使每个受害人有权决定何时以及向谁披露其攻击行为。并会再次对受害者造成伤害。

But the CPUC seemed unmoved by these arguments, whether they came from Uber or from groups like RAINN. In a Monday ruling, the agency held that "this decision does not require the public disclosure of such information that could potentially traumatize the victims a second time." Instead, the agency reasoned, the rules "require only that the information regarding sexual assaults and sexual harassment be submitted to the Commission under seal."

但是，无论是来自Uber还是来自RAINN等组织，CPUC似乎都对这些论点毫不动摇。该机构在周一的裁决中裁定＆＃34;该决定不要求公开披露可能再次伤害受害者的信息。相反，该机构认为，该规则仅要求将有关性侵犯和性骚扰的信息盖章提交给委员会。

CPUC concluded that Uber had "refused, without any legitimate legal or factual grounds, to comply" with the data request. The agency held that Uber's actions were particularly severe because they had "harmed the regulatory process."

CPUC得出的结论是，Uber在没有任何合法法律或事实依据的情况下拒绝了遵守。与数据请求。该机构认为，Uber的行为特别严重，因为它们损害了监管程序。

In setting a fine, the CPUC is supposed to consider whether a party's actions were in the public interest. But in Uber's case, the agency concluded that "there are no facts to mitigate the degree of Uber's wrongdoing." So the CPUC slapped Uber with a harsh $59 million penalty.

在设置罚款时，CPUC应该考虑当事方的行为是否符合公共利益。但是在Uber案中，该机构得出结论认为，没有任何事实可以减轻Uber的不法行为程度。因此，CPUC以5900万美元的严厉罚款打败了Uber。

Ironically, after hitting Uber with a massive fine for refusing to name sexual assault victims, the ruling allows Uber to submit its data in anonymized form.

具有讽刺意味的是，在拒绝给性侵犯受害者的名字而对Uber处以巨额罚款之后，该裁决允许Uber以匿名形式提交其数据。

"Uber shall work with the Commission's staff to develop a code or numbering system as a substitute for the actual names and other personally identifiable information" of victims and witnesses to assault cases, the ruling said.

＆＃34; Uber应与委员会工作人员合作，开发代码或编号系统，以代替实际姓名和其他个人身份信息。裁定说，该案涉及受害者和目击者的证人。

Uber seems to be the only taxi or ride-hailing service that has faced these kinds of questions from the CPUC. The company's decision to publish statistics about sexual assault and other crimes against its passengers set a new standard for transparency, but the report seems to have attracted extra scrutiny from California regulators.

"These punitive and confusing actions will do nothing to improve public safety and will only create a chilling effect as other companies consider releasing their own reports," Uber said in its statement. "Transparency should be encouraged, not punished."

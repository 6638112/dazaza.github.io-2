An Israeli Startup Is 3D Printing Cultured Ribeye Steaks
一家以色列初创公司是3D打印培养的眼扒牛排

2021-02-19 07:06:29

The market for meat alternatives is booming, but s o far most p roducts are only able to replicate the formless ground meat found in burgers, sausages, and nuggets. The world’s first 3D printed steak may be set to change that, opening the door to replicating any cut of meat we want.

肉类替代品的市场正在蓬勃发展，但到目前为止，大多数产品只能复制汉堡，香肠和金块中发现的无形的碎肉。世界上第一个3D打印牛排可能会改变这一现状，从而为复制我们想要的任何切肉打开了大门。

Growing sensitivity to the environmental and ethical impacts of industrial animal agriculture has seen interest in plant-based diets boom. But companies have been quick to spot a major gap in the market: the morally conflicted carnivore who is swayed by the vegan’s argument but can’t imagine ever completely giving up on their favorite food.

对工业动物农业对环境和道德影响的敏感性日益提高，人们对以植物为基础的饮食的兴起引起了兴趣。但是公司很快发现了市场上的一个重大缺口：道德上矛盾的食肉动物受到纯素食主义者的争论所左右，但无法想象会完全放弃自己喜欢的食物。

This is leading to an explosion in companies looking to fill that gap with a variety of faux meats made from plant material or slaughter-free meat that’s grown in a lab rather than being cut from a carcass.

这导致了公司的爆炸式增长，这些公司希望用在实验室中生长而不是从car体上切下的各种植物材料或无屠宰肉制成的人造肉来填补这一空白。

Some of these companies have already crossed over to the big time. Impossible Foods’ plant-based burgers are available at White Castle and Burger King, while Beyond Meat’s plant-based chicken is featured at KFC.

其中一些公司已经跨入了重要的时期。怀特城堡（White Castle）和汉堡王（Burger King）出售Impossible Foods的植物性汉堡，肯德基则以Beyond Meat的植物性鸡肉为特色。

So-called “cultured meat” is lagging slightly behind, with the first commercial sale coming only in December, when Eat Just’s “chicken bites” were sold in a Singapore restaurant for $23. But money has been pouring into the sector, and a number of startups are planning to enter the market by 2022.

所谓的“养殖肉”略有滞后，仅在12月才进行首次商业销售，当时Eat Just的“鸡肉叮咬”在新加坡一家餐馆以23美元的价格出售。但是资金已经涌入该领域，许多初创公司计划在2022年进入市场。

But for both approaches, there’s still a major hurdle to fully replicating the gastronomic experience of eating meat. While cultured methods work with the same raw ingredients and plant-based ones have found clever workarounds to mimic the taste of meat, they have struggled to create products that can replicate the complex networks of muscle, fat, and connective tissue that give whole cuts of meat their distinctive texture and flavor. And this is where the real money is. According to Business Insider , whole cuts like steak have the highest profit margins in the meat industry.

但是，对于这两种方法，要完全复制吃肉的美食体验仍然存在很大的障碍。虽然培养方法使用相同的原料，而基于植物的方法已经找到了模仿肉类味道的巧妙解决方法，但他们努力创造出可以复制复杂的肌肉，脂肪和结缔组织网络的产品，从而可以完整地切割肉。肉具有独特的质地和风味。这就是真正的钱所在。据Business Insider称，像牛排这样的整体切块在肉类行业中具有最高的利润率。

Advances in 3D printing may be set to change that, though. Last week, Israeli startup Aleph Farms unveiled the first lab-grown ribeye steak using their proprietary bio-printing process, which they say will eventually allow them to recreate any cut of meat.

不过，可以设置3D打印的进步来改变这种状况。上周，以色列初创公司Aleph Farms使用其专有的生物印刷工艺揭开了第一批实验室种植的肋眼牛排的面纱，他们说最终将使他们能够重制任何肉块。

The technology, developed in tandem with Isra e l’s Technion University, is similar to that being used in medical research to print “organoids” for drug testing, which one day could let us regrow entire organs from human cells. Using a device similar to an inkjet printer, the company lays down layers of support cells, fat cells, blood cells, and muscle cells that are then placed in an incubator to grow into the finished steak.

该技术是与以色列理工大学共同开发的，类似于医学研究中用于打印“类器官”以进行药物测试的技术，有朝一日可以让我们从人体细胞中再生出整个器官。该公司使用类似于喷墨打印机的设备，铺设了支持细胞，脂肪细胞，血细胞和肌肉细胞的层，然后将它们放置在培养箱中以成长为成品牛排。

The secret behind creating a cut as complex as a ribeye is finding a solution to an issue that has plagued medical bio-printing as well. Building bulk tissue using a bio-printer isn’t that tough, but creating the fine network of blood vessels that ferry nutrients and waste products in and out of cells is much harder.

创建像肋眼一样复杂的切口背后的秘密是找到解决问题的解决方案，该问题也困扰着医学生物印刷。使用生物打印机构建大块组织并不是那么困难，但是要创建一个精细的血管网络来运送营养物质和废物到细胞内或细胞外，则要困难得多。

While the company isn’t giving away any details, they say they have created a proprietary process that acts similarly to a natural vascular system, help ing nutrients across thicker sections of tissue during growth and maintain ing a natural shape and structure before and after cooking.

尽管该公司没有透露任何细节，但他们表示他们已经创建了一种专有程序，该程序的作用类似于天然血管系统，有助于在生长过程中吸收较厚组织的营养，并在烹饪前后保持自然的形状和结构。

It could take some time before you see these steaks in your local supermarket. The company doesn’t say how much it cost to make th e bespoke steak, but judging by the price of other cultured meats, it won’t be cheap. And so far the only country to approve cultured meat for sale is Singapore, though regulators in the US have started the policymaking process.

您可能需要一段时间才能在当地的超市看到这些牛排。该公司没有说制作定制牛排要多少钱，但是从其他养殖肉的价格来看，它并不便宜。到目前为止，唯一允许出售养殖肉的国家是新加坡，尽管美国的监管机构已经开始制定政策。

The company also isn’t the only one relying on 3D printing to disrupt the meat industry. This week, another Israeli startup called Redefine Meat raised $29 million to build a large-scale pilot factory to start pumping out 3D printed plant-based steaks.

该公司也不是唯一一家依靠3D打印来破坏肉类行业的公司。本周，另一家名为Redefine Meat的以色列创业公司筹集了2900万美元，建立了一个大型试点工厂，开始抽出3D打印植物性牛排。

Rather than laying down layers of cells that then have to be incubated, they are using vegan-friendly mockups of muscle, fat, and blood to create complex patterns that mimic a well-marbled steak.

他们使用的是素食主义者友好的肌肉，脂肪和血液模型，而不是放下必须进行孵育的细胞层，以模仿复杂的图案，仿制出一块大理石大理石。

Whether the plant-based approach will be able to truly replicat e the experience of biting into a juicy steak or the cultured meat industry will make cuts cheap enough for most of us remains to be seen. But it may not be long until your local butcher is hanging up his knives in favor of a 3D printer.

基于植物的方法是否能够真正复制叮咬多汁的牛排的经验，还是养殖肉类行业将使切菜便宜到我们大多数人尚待观察。但这可能不会很快，直到您当地的肉店老板挂起刀来支持3D打印机。

Cyberpunk 2077 developer hit with ransomware attack
赛博朋克2077开发人员遭受勒索软件攻击

2021-02-09 23:42:42

Cyberpunk 2077 developer CD Projekt Red announced that it has "become the victim of a targeted cyberattack" that allegedly exposed the source code for many of its games.

赛博朋克2077开发人员CD Projekt Red宣布已成为目标网络攻击的受害者＆＃34;据称暴露了其许多游戏的源代码。

In a message the developer shared on Twitter Tuesday morning, the hackers allege that they stole the closely guarded source code for Cyberpunk 2077, Gwent, and The Witcher 3 (including an unreleased prototype of the last). Documents "relating to accounting, administration, legal, HR, investors relations and more" were also allegedly compromised.

在开发人员周二早上在Twitter上分享的消息中，黑客声称他们窃取了Cyber​​punk 2077，Gwent和The Witcher 3（包括未发布的最后一个原型）的受严格保护的源代码。与会计，行政，法律，人力资源，投资者关系等相关的文件＆＃34;据称也受到了损害。

While the hackers apparently used ransomware software to lock CDPR out of certain parts of its systems, the company says it should be able to restore access to the data through backups. The company also remained defiant in the face of a ransom demand of an unspecified amount, saying it wouldn't negotiate, despite a threat to release the stolen data. "We will not give in to the demands nor negotiate with the actor, being aware that this may eventually lead to the release of the compromised data," the company wrote. The demand came with a 48-hour deadline. CDPR says that "to the best of our knowledge, the compromised systems did not contain any personal data of our players or users of our services." That sets this attack apart from a recent ransomware attack against Capcom, in which Capcom store customers, employees, and esports team members were among the groups that may have had their personal information revealed to hackers.

The raw source code for a game, which is used to create the executable files distributed to players, is usually considered to be among a developer's most valuable trade secrets. Back in 2003, the leak of source code for Valve's then-unreleased Half-Life 2 led to the arrest of a German hacker. More recently, a vast tranche of source code for classic Nintendo games was released online as part of a so-called "Gigaleak." A recent report by cybersecurity analysis firm Coveware found that total ransomware attack payments dipped slightly in the fourth quarter of 2020, after rising steadily for years prior, as more companies refuse to pay. An increasing number of those attacks now include threats to leak data online, Coveware found, and hackers often release that data even after the desired ransom is paid.

通常将游戏的原始源代码（用于创建分发给玩家的可执行文件）视为开发人员最有价值的商业秘密之一。早在2003年，Valve当时未发布的Half-Life 2的源代码泄漏导致一名德国黑客被捕。最近，作为所谓的“ Gigaleak”的一部分，在线发布了许多经典任天堂游戏的源代码。网络安全分析公司Coveware的最新报告发现，随着越来越多的公司拒绝付款，勒索软件攻击的总支出在经过数年稳步增长之后，在2020年第四季度略有下降。 Coveware发现，如今越来越多的这类攻击包括威胁在线数据泄漏，即使支付了所需的赎金，黑客也经常释放这些数据。

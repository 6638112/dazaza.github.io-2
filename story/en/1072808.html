Mozilla and Meta (FB) are working together on “privacy” ads
Mozilla和Meta（FB）正在合作制作“隐私”广告

2022-02-13 09:40:37

Mozilla, owner and developer of the Firefox browser, has attacked Facebook (now Meta) many times over the years for the company’s disastrous record on privacy and security. However, the two companies are now working together on a proposal for slightly more private online advertising, which is already drawing criticism from long-time fans of Mozilla.

Firefox浏览器的所有者和开发者Mozilla多年来多次攻击Facebook（现在是Meta），因为该公司在隐私和安全方面的灾难性记录。然而，这两家公司目前正在合作制定一项略为私人化的在线广告提案，该提案已经招致Mozilla长期粉丝的批评。

Mozilla revealed in a blog post on Tuesday, “For the last few months we have been working with a team from Meta (formerly Facebook) on a new proposal that aims to enable conversion measurement – or attribution – for advertising called Interoperable Private Attribution, or IPA.” The project aims to allow advertisers to measure the success rate of online ads, while being more privacy-respecting than existing online ads.

Mozilla在周二的一篇博文中透露，“过去几个月，我们一直在与Meta（前Facebook）的一个团队合作，制定一项新提案，旨在为名为“可互操作私人归因”（Interoperable Private attribution，简称IPA）的广告启用转换度量或归因。”该项目旨在让广告商衡量在线广告的成功率，同时比现有的在线广告更尊重隐私。

The core concept, as explained in the proposal draft, is to replace per-action ad reporting (e.g. the browser sending data to an advertising group when you click on an ad) with aggregated reports for batches of events. Websites can create a “match key” connected to your account or device, which is apparently only accessible by the browser to avoid fingerprinting. There are also a few functions in place intended to make it difficult for anyone (including the companies or advertisers collecting data) to identify people interacting with ads. It’s similar to Prio, the technology Mozilla developed a few years ago to analyze how people use Firefox.

正如提案草案中所解释的那样，核心概念是用批量事件的聚合报告取代按行动的广告报告（例如，当你点击广告时，浏览器向广告组发送数据）。网站可以创建一个连接到您的帐户或设备的“匹配密钥”，显然只有通过浏览器才能访问该密钥，以避免指纹识别。此外，还有一些功能旨在让任何人（包括收集数据的公司或广告商）难以识别与广告互动的人。这与Prio类似，Prio是Mozilla几年前开发的一种分析人们如何使用Firefox的技术。

Even though the proposal seems solid, the partnership is rather surprising. Mozilla just began a trial study last month in partnership with The Markup that aimed to identify how Meta/Facebook was using tracking pixels across the web to record web activity. Mozilla said the goal of the study was to “report on where Facebook is tracking you and what kind of information they are collecting.” The group has also rallied against Facebook many times in recent history, and less than a year ago, started publishing ads on Meta’s platforms that called out the company’s creepy ad targeting abilities.

尽管这个提议看起来很可靠，但这种合作关系还是相当令人惊讶的。Mozilla上个月刚刚开始与Markup合作进行一项试验性研究，旨在确定Meta/Facebook是如何通过追踪网络上的像素来记录网络活动的。Mozilla表示，这项研究的目的是“报告Facebook在哪里跟踪你，以及他们在收集什么样的信息。”在最近的历史中，该组织还多次与Facebook对抗，不到一年前，该组织开始在Meta的平台上发布广告，展示该公司令人毛骨悚然的广告定位能力。

The blog post has not been shared on the official Twitter accounts for Firefox or Mozilla, unlike nearly every other article the company publishes. Criticism has been limited so far, possible due to the low-key announcement (it only appeared on the Firefox subreddit on Friday, for example), but overall feedback at the moment is mostly negative.

与该公司发布的几乎所有其他文章不同，Firefox或Mozilla的官方Twitter账户上没有分享这篇博文。到目前为止，批评是有限的，可能是因为低调的声明（例如，它只出现在周五的Firefox subreddit上），但目前总体反馈大多是负面的。

European Commission Fines Valve, Publishers for Regional Pricing and GeoBlocking
欧盟委员会对阀门，区域定价和地理封锁发布者处以罚款

2021-01-21 02:39:48

The European Commission has issued a fine to Valve and five game publishers (Bandai Namco, Focus Home Interactive, Capcom, Koch Media, and ZeniMax after an antitrust probe decided the companies violated EU antitrust laws. The Commission investigated regional pricing and geo-blocking practices that were put in place by valve and the hitherto fined publishers, and says that these rule-breaking pricing manipulations resulted from bilateral agreements between game publishers and Valve between 2010 and 2015, as well as from purpose-built licensing and distribution licenses between four out of five fined publishers between 2007 and 2018. The European Commission found these practices to relieve "European consumers of the benefits of the EU Digital Single Market and of the opportunity to shop around for the most suitable offer in the EU". In particular, the offendants sold video game licenses relative to some 100 different titles at lower pricing in certain Eastern Europe countries, and which could not be activated in Western Europe. The fine's total amount of €7.8 million is shared between Valve (€1,624,000); Bandai Namco (10% fine reduction for cooperation, €340,000); Capcom (15% fine reduction for cooperation, €396,000); Focus Home Interactive (10% fine reduction for cooperation, €2,888,000); Koch Media (10% fine reduction for cooperation, €977,000), and ZeniMax (10% fine reduction for cooperation, €1,664,000). Valve didn't receive any fine reduction because the company elected not to cooperate with the Commission. Executive Vice-President Margrethe Vestager, in charge of competition policy, said in a statement: More than 50% of all Europeans play video games. The videogame industry in Europe is thriving and it is now worth over € 17 billion. Today's sanctions against the "geo-blocking" practices of Valve and five PC video game publishers serve as a reminder that under EU competition law, companies are prohibited from contractually restricting cross-border sales. Such practices deprive European consumers of the benefits of the EU Digital Single Market and of the opportunity to shop around for the most suitable offer in the EU.

在反托拉斯调查确定两家公司违反了欧盟反托拉斯法之后，欧盟委员会对Valve和五家游戏发行商（Bandai Namco，Focus Home Interactive，Capcom，Koch Media和ZeniMax）处以罚款。委员会调查了区域定价和地理封锁做法由Valve和迄今被罚款的发行商实施，并说这些违反规则的价格操纵是由于游戏发行商与Valve在2010年至2015年之间达成的双边协议，以及四分之三的特制许可和发行许可在2007年至2018年之间有五家被罚款的发行商。 欧盟委员会发现这些做法可以使欧洲消费者从欧盟数字单一市场的利益中解放出来，并有机会货比三家，寻找欧盟最合适的产品。尤其是，在某些东欧国家，违法者以较低的价格出售了与约100种不同游戏相关的视频游戏许可，而在西欧则无法激活。总计780万欧元的罚款由Valve公司分摊（1,624,000欧元）； Bandai Namco（为合作减少10％的罚款，340,000欧元）； Capcom（为合作减少15％的罚款，396,000欧元）; Focus Home Interactive（合作减少10％罚款，2,888,000欧元）; Koch Media（合作伙伴可被罚款10％，减少977,000欧元）和ZeniMax（合作伙伴可被罚款10％，1,664,000欧元）。阀门没有因为公司选择不与委员会合作而被罚款。负责竞争政策的执行副总裁玛格丽特·韦斯特格（Margrethe Vestager）在一份声明中说：所有欧洲人中有50％以上在玩电子游戏。欧洲的视频游戏产业正在蓬勃发展，目前价值超过170亿欧元。当今针对“地道封锁”的制裁Valve和五家PC视频游戏发行商的做法提醒我们，根据欧盟竞争法，禁止公司通过合同限制跨境销售。这样的做法剥夺了欧洲消费者从欧盟数字单一市场中获得的利益，也剥夺了在欧盟范围内货比三家的机会。

Of course, while this money will come directly out of pocket from the convicted companies, what this does in practice is that these companies will likely no longer provide videogames at discounted prices for countries with lower purchasing power (and, one might argue, at pricing that better reflects those citizen's purchasing power, which as we all know, vary from country to country). If history has shown us anything is that companies will almost never take the road of lowering the purchase price towards the lowest bar, but the opposite, thus potentially turning games into luxury commodities that are only reserved for the few lucky enough to have been born in a country with a higher minimum wage.

当然，虽然这笔钱将直接从被定罪的公司中掏钱，但实际上，这些公司可能不再为那些购买力较低的国家提供打折的价格的电子游戏（而且有人可能会争论说，在定价上更好地反映了公民的购买力，众所周知，购买力因国家/地区而异。如果历史向我们展示了什么，那就是公司几乎永远不会走降低购买价格到最低门槛的道路，而恰恰相反，因此有可能将游戏变成奢侈品，只为少数幸运的人保留。一个最低工资更高的国家

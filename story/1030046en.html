伽利略的地面段问题

2020-10-20 22:39:10

The European Global Navigation Satellite System Galileo has recently beensuffering from frequent “almost disruptions”, most likely partially due toCorona, and possibly also due to Brexit, but in any case due to somehistorical stinginess.

欧洲全球导航卫星系统伽利略(Galileo)最近频繁遭受“几乎中断”，很可能是由于日冕，也可能是由于英国退欧，但无论如何都是由于历史上的一些吝啬。

Before I explain what is going on, I should hasten to say that most days ofthe week, Galileo is working well for most users. But for example, Mondaythe 28th of September, one satellite was off by a stunning 5meters. For asatellite system claiming 1 meter accuracy, that is rather dire.

在我解释发生了什么之前，我应该匆忙地说，一周中的大部分时间，伽利略对大多数用户来说都运行得很好。但例如，9月28日星期一，一颗卫星偏离了惊人的5米。对于声称1米精度的卫星系统来说，这是相当可怕的。

A Global Navigation Satellite System (GNSS) helps us determine our position,velocity and time. All three can be very important. For example, the timesignal is used to synchronise power production, radio networks and globaldatabases.

全球导航卫星系统(GNSS)帮助我们确定我们的位置、速度和时间。这三点都可能非常重要。例如，时间信号用于同步电力生产、无线电网络和全球数据库。

For our phone to know where it is, it needs to know two things: where theGNSS satellites are, and the exact status of the clocks on board. Byclocking the signals received from space, a GNSS receiver knows exactly how far it isaway from the satellites. And from there, it can calculate position,velocity and time on the ground.

为了让我们的手机知道它在哪里，它需要知道两件事：GNSS卫星在哪里，以及机上时钟的确切状态。通过对从太空接收到的信号进行计时，GNSS接收器确切地知道它离卫星有多远。从那里，它可以计算出地面上的位置、速度和时间。

Crucially, the orbits in space and the atomic clocks are not entirelyregular. To determine our location with the desired precision, we need anupdate on the “ephemeris” (orbit) and clock around once an hour.

最重要的是，空间的轨道和原子钟并不是完全规则的。为了以期望的精度确定我们的位置，我们需要更新“星历”(轨道)，每小时运行一次。

And for the past months, Galileo has struggled to determine and disseminatesuch orbit corrections in a timely fashion. Typically this problem happensone or two days a week, and so far the satellites have been regular enoughthis has not degraded precision a lot. Except on the 25th of September.

在过去的几个月里，伽利略一直在努力及时确定和传播这样的轨道修正。通常情况下，这个问题每周发生一到两天，到目前为止，卫星已经足够正常，这并没有太大地降低精度。除了9月25日。

Monitoring stations all over the world work together to measure the preciseorbit of the Galileo satellites, and to determine how far ahead or behindthe on-board clocks are.

世界各地的监测站一起工作，测量伽利略卫星的精确度，并确定星载时钟领先或落后多远。

This information is combined into a fresh “ephemeris” every 10 minutes.This happens for each individual satellite (there are 22 + 2 testing). Theephemeris is then uplinked to the right satellite, which then broadcasts itto users around the world. Because receivers get frequent updates on theorbits and clocks, they can do their work accurately.

这些信息每10分钟被合并到一个新的“星历”中，每颗单独的卫星都会发生这种情况(有22+2次测试)。然后，星历被上传到正确的卫星，然后卫星将其广播给世界各地的用户。因为接收器经常得到理论轨道和时钟的更新，所以他们可以准确地完成他们的工作。

Every satellite in space is equipped with four clocks, multiple power supplies,multiple computers, multiple everything, just to make sure there is enough sparecapacity to keep things working 24 hours a day for 10 years.

太空中的每颗卫星都配备了四个时钟，多个电源，多台计算机，多个东西，只是为了确保有足够的空间容量来保持事物在10年内每天24小时工作。

It would then stand to reason to have more than 24 dishes available to talkto the 24 Galileo satellites. From an engineering perspective, it wouldeven make sense to have a large overcapacity in the ground segment. Comparedto the cost of building and launching a satellite, this would seem like areasonable investment to make.

那么，它将有超过24个碟子可供与24颗伽利略卫星通话，这是合情合理的。从工程的角度来看，在地面部分出现大量产能过剩甚至是有意义的。与建造和发射一颗卫星的成本相比，这似乎是一项合理的投资。

Galileo is evolving and will soon (?) be delivering two new services: the High Accuracy Service whichpromises realtime updates enabling decimeter level accuracy, and OpenService Network MessageAuthentication,which adds cryptographic signatures to the navigation messages.

伽利略正在进化，很快就会(？)。将提供两项新服务：高精度服务(High Accuracy Service)和OpenService网络消息身份验证(OpenService Network MessageAuthentication)，前者承诺实现分米级精度的实时更新，后者为导航消息添加加密签名。

In addition, early 2021 will see the launch of two new Galileo Satellites,and these will of course also demand attention from the ground segment.

此外，2021年初将发射两颗新的伽利略卫星，这些卫星当然也需要地面部分的关注。

Talking to a Galileo satellite requires a moderately sized dish.A Galileo satellite zips along at 3 kilometers per second, so the dish needsto track the satellite as it moves across the sky.

与伽利略卫星交谈需要一个中等大小的碟子，伽利略卫星以每秒3公里的速度飞驰，所以碟子需要跟踪卫星在天空中移动的轨迹。

Because the Earth is round, there can’t just be a single big ground stationin Europe somewhere - the uplink facilities need to be spread around theworld.

因为地球是圆的，欧洲某个地方不可能只有一个大型地面站--上行链路设施需要分布在世界各地。

Although over 10 billion euros have been invested in Galileo, for somereason, the uplink capacity has always been limited. Most of the time, onlya subset of satellites is in active ground communications. For the past few years,the goal has been that every satellite is in touch with the ground onceevery 100 minutes at least.

虽然伽利略已经投入了超过100亿欧元，但由于季节的原因，上行能力一直是有限的。大多数时候，只有一部分卫星在进行活跃的地面通信。在过去的几年里，目标一直是每颗卫星至少每100分钟与地面联系一次。

This means that the existing satellite dish capacity moves from onesatellite to the other to make sure every Galileo satellite gets attentionat least once an hour or so.

这意味着现有的卫星天线容量将从一颗卫星转移到另一颗卫星，以确保每颗伽利略卫星至少每小时一次受到关注。

So this is a very parsimonious design already. From what I read, the goal isthat at best, 20 out of 24 satellites can be contacted simultaneously, if everything isworking.

所以这已经是一个非常节俭的设计了。据我所知，我们的目标是，如果一切正常，最多也就是24颗卫星中的20颗可以同时联系到。

The nominal capacity is already not ‘100%’, but frequently things are notnominal. In a typical week, at least one or two days see Galileo satellites notbeing provisioned with new information on time.

名义产能已经不是“100%”，但通常情况下，情况并不是名义的。在典型的一周中，至少有一两天的伽利略卫星没有及时获得新的信息。

A cursory look at update frequencies teaches us that often only 13 dishesare talking to the Galileo satellites.

粗略地看一下更新频率就会发现，通常只有13个磁盘在与伽利略卫星通话。

The causes may be diverse - for various reasons, many uplink stations arehosted on remote islands or Nordic archipelagos. Given COVID-19, it is notalways easy to reach these islands to do maintenance or expansion work.

原因可能是多种多样的-由于各种原因，许多上行电台都设在偏远的岛屿或北欧群岛上。考虑到新冠肺炎，要到达这些岛屿做维修或扩建工作并不容易。

In addition, some monitoring stations were hosted on UK territory, and itseems this may no longer be working out post-Brexit.

此外，一些监测站设在英国领土上，似乎这在英国退欧后可能不再奏效。

Due to further technical reasons, dishes that are not available have aknock-on effect on reincorporating satellites into the network. The resultsof uplink station problems can therefore last long after the initial problem wasfixed.

由于更多的技术原因，不可用的碟子会对卫星重新加入网络产生不确定的影响。因此，在修复初始问题之后，上行站问题的结果可以持续很长时间。

Galileo should be flying 24 operational satellites - but it is not, thereare 22 that work. This number was only reached by keeping engineeringprototypes in production, which is great when it works. Galileo tries realhard to keep 22 satellites operational.

伽利略应该飞行24颗运行中的卫星-但事实并非如此，有22颗正在运行。这个数字只有通过保持工程原型在生产中才能达到，这在它工作的时候是很棒的。伽利略号竭尽全力让22颗卫星保持运转。

Over time, atomic clocks degrade and can become unruly. The oldest currentlyfunctioning Galileo satellite (E11) has such an unruly clock. This is not aproblem if corrections are received every 10 minutes, as is the nominalschedule.

随着时间的推移，原子钟会退化，变得难以驾驭。目前正在运行的最古老的伽利略卫星(E11)就有这样一个不守规矩的时钟。如果按照名义计划，每10分钟收到一次更正，这不是问题。

Late September 2020, communications broke down and E11 continued to functionwithout feedback, leading its clock to accumulate a 5+ meter equivalenterror, twice - but not broadcasting a warning about that.

2020年9月下旬，通讯中断，E11在没有反馈的情况下继续工作，导致它的时钟积累了两次5米以上的当量误差-但没有就此发出警告。

Galileo satellites are very dependable but also not very autonomous - onceout of reach from the ground, for a very long time a satellite will doexactly what it was told. In this case however, E11 should copy a trick fromsome other Galileo satellites, and learn how to declare itself unreliable(‘working without guarantee’) if it hasn’t heard from the ground recently.

伽利略卫星是非常可靠的，但也不是很自主-一旦从地面够不到，在很长一段时间内，卫星都会完全按照命令行事。然而，在这种情况下，E11应该复制一些其他伽利略卫星的诀窍，并学习如果最近没有收到地面的消息，如何宣布自己不可靠(“在没有保证的情况下工作”)。

Because as it stands, most Galileo satellites will happily continuebroadcasting a healthy status for 24 hours even if they haven’t been inground contact. For some satellites this is a reasonable configuration - butfor others, it is very much not.

因为就目前情况而言，大多数伽利略卫星将愉快地持续24小时广播健康状态，即使它们没有进行过全面接触。对于一些卫星来说，这是一个合理的配置，但对另一些卫星来说，情况就大相径庭了。

I am not quite sure. Galileo is famously a 10 billion euro project. You’dthink there would be enough money to buy and operate 40 moderately sizedsatellite dishes.

我不太确定。众所周知，伽利略计划是一个100亿欧元的项目。你会认为有足够的钱购买和操作40个中等大小的卫星天线。

Ground stations are expensive, although it is not always immediately clearwhy. A “Galileo-grade” uplink dish plus steering & other hardware shouldcost around 150k euros, I am told. I’m sure the Galileo-specific bits willdouble that, but it is still a doable amount of money compared to the costof a single satellite (65 million euros, excluding launch).

地面站价格昂贵，尽管原因并不总是一目了然。有人告诉我，一个“伽利略级”的上行链路盘加上方向盘和其他硬件应该要15万欧元左右。我相信伽利略专用的部分将会翻一番，但与一颗卫星的成本(6500万欧元，不包括发射)相比，这仍然是一个可行的金额。

Much like you can hire a consultant and pay 2500 euros a day, but thatconsultant actually makes nowhere near that kind of money, it seems plausible thatindividual Galileo uplink dishes are billed at “millions/year” rate, once youorder them over at Thales Alenia Space.

就像你可以雇佣一名顾问，每天支付2500欧元，但那个顾问实际上赚不到那么多钱，似乎有道理，一旦你在泰利斯·阿莱尼亚空间公司(Thales Alenia Space)订购了单个伽利略上行菜肴，就会以“数百万/年”的费率收费。

It is also entirely possible that the ‘uplink facility’ was tendered in sucha way that additional capacity was priced to compensate for lower initialtender prices. This is very common when doing large scale procurement -trading a short term saving for a longer term steeper price.

此外，“上行设施”的投标方式亦完全有可能导致额外容量的定价，以补偿较低的初始投标价格。这在进行大规模采购时非常常见-以短期节省换取长期更高的价格。

People with time on their hands might be able to figure this out by trawlingthrough the European Union’s tenderingsite.

手头有时间的人或许可以通过拖网浏览欧盟的招标地点来弄清楚这一点。

It seems that this problem has been around for a very long time, so itlikely can’t be solved easily. From what I understand, work is inprogress to deliver at least the nominal 20 dishes, and this is very good.

这个问题似乎已经存在很长时间了，所以很可能不容易解决。据我了解，目前正在进行至少提供20道名义菜肴的工作，这是非常好的。

Meanwhile, it might be a stellar idea to copy the ‘Working WithoutGuarantee’ functionality from the two ‘testing’ vehicles to all otherGalileo satellites. This would at least enable Galileo to be a “good GNSScitizen” by configuring satellites to mark themselves faulty if they aren’tsure they are doing a good job. There’s always GPS to fall back on!

同时，将“无担保工作”功能从两个“测试”飞行器复制到所有其他伽利略卫星上可能是一个绝妙的想法。这至少会使伽利略成为一个“好的GNSScience用户”，如果他们不确定自己做得很好，可以配置卫星来标记自己有故障。总有GPS可以依靠！

And for the longer term.. people might start asking questions why the groundsegment is so anemic compared to the glorious satellites that fly in space,and why this makes sense.

从长远来看..。人们可能会开始问，为什么与在太空中飞行的辉煌的卫星相比，地面段如此贫乏，为什么这是有意义的。

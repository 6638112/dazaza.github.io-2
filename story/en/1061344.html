Microsoft is changing its default font
Microsoft正在更改其默认字体

2021-05-08 22:45:33

For almost 15 years, Calibri has reigned as the default and therefore dominant font choice for Microsoft systems. It has appeared countless times in unformatted Word documents, PowerPoint presentations, and Excel spreadsheets, a typographical reprieve for the decision-paralyzed. But now there’s a new sans serif in town. Actually, five of them: Microsoft announced that it plans to replace Calibri as the default font with one of five new typefaces it released this week.

近15年来，Calibri已统治为默认值，因此是Microsoft系统的主导字体选择。它在未格式化的Word文档，PowerPoint演示文稿和Excel电子表格中出现了无数次，对决策瘫痪的印刷缓刑。但现在镇上有一个新的Sans Serif。实际上，其中五个：微软宣布它计划将Calibri替换为本周发布的五个新字体之一的默认字体。

It’s the end of an era, but Calibri’s designer, Lucas de Groot, has no qualms about letting his typeface rest for a bit. “It’s a relief,” he says.

这是一个时代结束，但Calibri的设计师Lucas de Groot，没有Qualms，让他的字体休息一下。 “这是一个救济，”他说。

De Groot created Calibri in the early 2000s, as part of a collection of fonts for enhanced screen reading. “I designed it in quite a hurry,” he says. “I had some sketches already, so I adapted those and added these rounded corners to get some design feeling in it.” For a long time, computer displays lacked the pixel density to faithfully render all fonts; rounded corners appeared not as an arch but a stair. That changed in 2000 with Microsoft’s new ClearType technology, which optimized the resolution on LCD screens and made fonts like de Groot’s easier to read. The company liked Calibri enough to make it the default for Windows Vista in 2007.

De Groot在2000年代初创建了Calibri，作为增强屏幕阅读的字体集合的一部分。 “我匆忙设计了它，”他说。 “我已经有一些草图，所以我改编了那些，并加入了这些圆角，以获得一些设计感觉。”很长一段时间，计算机显示屏缺少像素密度忠实地呈现所有字体;圆角出现不像拱门，而是一个楼梯。 2000年改变了Microsoft的新的ClearType技术，该技术优化了LCD屏幕上的分辨率，并使De Goot更容易阅读字体。该公司喜欢Calibri，足以使其成为2007年Windows Vista的默认值。

Since then, Calibri has performed its duties with absolute modesty. It never became a typographical darling like Helvetica, but it didn’t create many enemies, either. “We’re not seeing customers turn against it, which does happen with fonts,” says Simon Daniels, the principal program manager at Microsoft Office Design. Nothing is wrong with Calibri. It’s simply that after almost two decades, Daniels figured it might be time to try something new.

从那时起，Calibri已经以绝对的谦虚表现得其职责。它从来没有像Helvetica那样成为印刷的亲爱的，但它也没有创造许多敌人。 “我们没有看到客户转向它，这确实存在于字体上，”Microsoft Office Design的主要计划经理Simon Daniels说。 Calibri没有什么是错的。这只是，在近二十年后，丹尼尔斯想到了它可能是时候尝试一些新东西。

“I often think of this Roger Black quote, which says that fonts are basically like clothing for your ideas,” says Daniels. “So what we’re saying is that Calibri has gone out of fashion.”

“我经常想到这个罗杰黑人报价，这表示字体基本上就像服装的想法，”丹尼尔斯说。 “所以我们所说的是卡利奇已经摆脱了时尚。”

Rather than settle into a new look right away, though, Microsoft is giving itself some time to consider the options. Daniels commissioned five new fonts from leading type designers, each one bringing a fresh take on what a default font could be: Tenorite is crisp and circular, with round punctuation marks. Bierstadt is more restrained, paying homage to mid-century Swiss typography. Skeena is a “humanist” sans serif; Grandview, an “industrial” one. Seaford takes inspiration from the shape of armchairs: comfortable but ergonomic.

然而，Microsoft of Microsoft而不是定时进入新的外观，而不是给自己提供一些时间来考虑选项。丹尼尔斯从领先的设计师委托五个新字体，每一个都会带来一个新鲜的默认字体：代理人是清脆的，圆形的，圆形标点标记。 Bierstadt更克制，向中世纪瑞士排版支付致敬。 Skeena是一个“人文主义”SANS; Grandview，一个“工业”。 Seaford从扶手椅的形状中获取灵感：舒适但符合人体工学。

All five fonts are now available to use on Microsoft products that are connected to the cloud, and the company is inviting people to give feedback on which they like best. It will announce its selection of the new default later this year. Daniels can’t recall another time that Microsoft has crowd-tested its typefaces this way, but he believes it will lead to a better decision. Plus, providing people with options minimizes some of the pressure. “You give somebody one, then there’s a good chance it becomes polarizing,” Daniels says. “But if you give people five, almost everyone will have a favorite.”

所有五个字体现在都可以在连接到云的Microsoft产品上使用，公司邀请人们提供最佳反馈。它将在今年晚些时候宣布选择新的默认值。丹尼尔斯不能再回想起微软在这种方式测试了其字体的时间，但他认为它会导致更好的决定。此外，提供有选择的人最小化一些压力。 “你给了一个人，那么就会有一个很好的机会变得极化，”丹尼尔斯说。 “但如果你给人们五，几乎每个人都会有一个最爱。”

And make no mistake, the public has feelings about fonts. People have already begun weighing in on Twitter, with strong opinions about nearly every option: “The G on Grandview is awesome.” “Maybe not Grandview?” “Awful kerning for Bierstadt.” “Tenorite is too blocky.” Several people raised specific concerns about legibility for people with poor vision or dyslexia. Others wondered why the big fuss over what the text looks like on Microsoft products: “What's wrong with Calibri? It looks good and works just fine.”

并没有错误，公众有关于字体的感受。人们已经开始在推特上称重，对几乎每个选项的强烈意见：“Grandview上的G很棒。” “也许不是Grandview？” “糟糕的Biersning Kerning。” “代理人太矮了。”有几个人对视力差或诵读障碍物或诵读障碍较差的人的易读性提出了特定的担忧。其他人想知道为什么大惊小怪的文字在微软产品上看起来像什么：“Calibri的错误＆＃39;错误？它看起来很好，工作很好。“

“What’s wrong with Calibri? Nothing,” says Gail Anderson, a designer at the School of Visual Arts. “It’s not a fancy-dress typeface. You don’t need to check the mirror before leaving the house when you’re using Calibri.” The font, she says, doesn’t offend her. “It’s probably just not my first choice.” As for which of the new typefaces she would use instead, she diplomatically declined to choose a favorite.

“Calibri有什么问题？没有，“Visual Arts学校的设计师Gail Anderson说。 “这不是一个花哨的字体。当您使用Calibri时，您不需要在离开房子之前检查镜子。“她说，字体并没有冒犯她。 “这可能只是我的第一选择。”至于她将使用的新字体中的哪一个，她外交拒绝选择最喜欢的。

Other designers say that Calibri worked well in the context for which it was designed—but now that screen pixel density is no longer an issue, a default font can take more liberties. “Calibri, I think, can be overly dense,” says Tobias Frere-Jones, the design director at Frere-Jones Type, which created Seaford. His new font has wider spacing and more accentuated shapes: The round letters are more round, the square letters more square, so that each letter appears more distinctly in a word or a sentence. “It’s very effective in making word shapes more intelligible,” he says. “And in the last year, as screens became more and more the place where we live, it seemed more urgent to make something that was kinder to our eyes.”

其他设计人员说，Calibri在设计的上下文中运作良好 - 但现在屏幕像素密度不再是一个问题，默认字体可以采取更多的自由。 “Calibri，我认为，可以过于密集，”Tobias Berere-Jones，Brier-Jones类型的设计总监，它创造了Seaford。他的新字体具有更宽的间距和更强调的形状：圆形字母更圆，方形字母更加正方形，使每个字母在一个单词或句子中更清晰。 “在制作单词形状更加可理解时，这是非常有效的，”他说。 “在去年，随着屏幕变得越来越多的地方，我们生活的地方似乎更加迫切，让我们的眼睛更善良。”

De Groot was surprised by some of the new designs Microsoft chose, which he criticized as chasing typographical fashion trends. “My absolute favorite is Seaford,” he says. “It has a strong voice, which I love. But of course, a strong design voice might also pose a danger. One of the things I tried to do with Calibri is make it kind of neutral.”

除了微软选择的一些新设计感到惊讶的是，他批评为追逐印刷时尚潮流。 “我绝对最喜欢的是Seaford，”他说。 “它具有强烈的声音，我喜欢。但当然，强大的设计声音也可能构成危险。我用Calibri尝试做的事情之一是使它成为中立的。“

The average person might not pick up on that “voice,” or even notice that their default font has changed at all. (On Twitter, a few people replied to Microsoft’s announcement saying they couldn’t tell the difference between the five new fonts.) But designers say that each carefully designed shape, with its precise curves and kerning, makes a difference in how people communicate online. “What we make is the vehicle that people’s thoughts will ride on,” says Frere-Jones. Now, the only thing left to do is to choose which of the five new fonts sends the right message.

普通人可能不会接受“语音”，甚至会注意到他们的默认字体完全更改。 （在Twitter上，一些人回答了微软的公告，说他们无法讲述五个新字体之间的差异。）但是设计师表示，每个精心设计的造型都具有精确的曲线和克宁，有所不同地在线沟通。 “我们制作的是人们的想法将骑行的车辆，”琼斯韦尔说。现在，唯一剩下的是选择五个新字体中的哪一个发送正确的消息。

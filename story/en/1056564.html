NYPD Has Misled the Public About Its Use of Facial Recognition Tool Clearview AI
NYPD已经误导了公众使用面部识别工具Clearive AI

2021-04-07 01:28:27

Despite statements from the NYPD in early 2020 stating that it had no relationship with the facial recognition software company Clearview AI, documents from a new public records request reveal that the company was an acknowledged vendor to the department from as early as 2018, and that it continued to enjoy a congenial relationship with officers on the force long after.

尽管2020年初来自NYPD的陈述，但它与面部识别软件公司明确观察AI没有关系，新的公共记录请求的文件显示，该公司于2018年作为本署的承认供应商，以及它继续享受与武力的官员的共同关系。

The NYPD told BuzzFeed News in February 2020 that it “does not have any contract, agreement, or relationship with Clearview AI, formally or informally.” It also told BuzzFeed News in January 2020 that it has “no institutional relationship” with the company. However, documents obtained through a public record request submitted under the name Rachel Richards contradict these claims.

NYPD于二月二十日告诉Buzzfeed新闻，它“没有任何合同，协议或与ClearView AI的关系，正式或非正式地。”它还在1月2020年1月讲述了Buzzfeed新闻，它与公司有“没有制度关系”。但是，通过根据“瑞珊理查德”名称提交的公共记录请求获得的文件与这些索赔相矛盾。

These documents, which include emails and official contracts, are signed by Clearview cofounder and CEO Hoan Ton-That, and authorize a Clearview trial that ran from December 2018 to March 2019. They show that the New York City Police Department, the largest police department in the country, with about 36,000 officers, has had a close and previously unknown relationship with Clearview that included in-person meetings and customer support from Ton-That. BuzzFeed News was unsuccessful in multiple attempts to reach Rachel Richards, the person listed as filing the public records request.

这些文件包括电子邮件和官方合同，由Clearview Cofounder和Ceo Hoan Ton签署，并授权从2018年12月到2019年3月举行的明确审判。他们表明纽约市警察局是最大的警察局在该国，大约36,000名官员，与ClearView进行了密切，先前未知的关系，其中包括吨位和客户支持。 Buzzfeed新闻在多次尝试到达Rachel Richards的尝试中是不成功的，该人被列为提交公共记录请求。

On Monday, an NYPD spokesperson did not answer questions about the emails or the agency’s interactions with Clearview during the period they were exchanged. They described facial recognition as “a limited investigative tool” that compares a “still image from a surveillance video to a pool of lawfully possessed arrest photos.” The statement was identical to one that was provided to BuzzFeed News for a previous story.

星期一，NYPD发言人在他们交换期间没有回答关于电子邮件或机构与ClearView的互动的问题。他们将面部识别描述为“有限的调查工具”，该工具将“静止图像从监视视频到合法拥有的逮捕照片池”。该声明与以前故事提供给Buzzfeed新闻的声明相同。

Clearview AI, known for having scraped 3 billion photos without permission from platforms such as Facebook, Instagram, and LinkedIn, has distributed its software to thousands of police officers around the country, as BuzzFeed News recently detailed in a groundbreaking investigation.

ClearView AI已知已在未经Facebook，Instagram和LinkedIn等平台允许的平台上刮擦了30亿张照片，已将其软件分发到全国各地的数千名警察，因为最近在突破性调查中详述的Buzzfeed新闻。

But from March 2019 through at least February 2020, officers continued to sign up for Clearview through its free trial program, which gives people an unlimited amount of Clearview searches for at least 30 days, unless Clearview extends it. The emails also reveal that the NYPD tested Clearview on one of its own officers — a use of the software that Clearview has explicitly encouraged in the past, asking users to search for their friends.

但2019年3月至2020年2月，官员继续通过其免费试用计划报名参加ClarView，这给人们提供了无限数量的ClearView搜索，除非ClearView扩展了30天。电子邮件还揭示了NYPD测试了一个自己的官员的清除视图 - 使用清除视图的软件在过去明确鼓励的情况下，要求用户寻找他们的朋友。

“Take a selfie with Clearview or search a celebrity to see how powerful the technology can be,” a January 2020 email from Clearview to an NYPD officer reads.

“用ClearView拍摄Selfie或搜索名人，看看该技术有多强大，1月2020 2020从ClearView到NYPD官员阅读。

Moreover, the emails show that the NYPD had a much more intimate relationship with Clearview than previously reported. CEO Ton-That, for example, corresponded directly with several NYPD officers throughout the trial period, seeking feedback on the tool.

此外，电子邮件表明，NYPD与明确观察的关系比以前报告的更加亲密关系。首席执行官吨 - 例如，在整个试用期内直接与几名NYPD官员相符合，寻求对工具的反馈。

“We have the greatest respect and admiration for the NYPD, which protects those of us who live and work in New York City,” Ton-That said in a statement to BuzzFeed News. “We were honored to provide our service to the NYPD on a trial basis. We are always here to assist should the need arise.”

“我们对NYPD拥有最大的尊重和钦佩，这些尊敬和钦佩的人可以保护我们在纽约市生活和工作的人，”吨 - 在宣布Buzzfeed新闻中表示。 “我们很荣幸能够在审判的基础上向NYPD提供服务。我们总是在这里有助于援助。“

The relationship between the NYPD and Clearview appears to have begun in October 2018, when Clearview cofounder Richard Schwartz emailed NYPD Lieutenant Gregory Besson, whom he had just met. Emails show that Besson connected him with Deputy Inspector Chris Flanagan, who “creates tons of wanted photographs” on the Financial Crimes Task Force, during the week of October 15.

NYPD和ClearView之间的关系似乎在2018年10月开始，当ClearView Cofounder Richard Schwartz电子邮件向我们刚刚遇到的NYPD中尉Gregory Besson时。电子邮件显示，Besson与副督察Chris Flanagan联系在一起，他在10月15日的金融犯罪工作队上“创造了吨想要的照片”。

Schwartz told Besson that he was “personally highly committed to the success of the NYPD,” since he “served as Mayor Giuliani’s Senior Advisor and introduced him to Bill Bratton and Jack Maple.”

施瓦茨告诉贝尔松，他“个人高度致力于诺维德的成功”，因为他“曾担任市长朱利亚尼的高级顾问，并将他介绍给比尔布拉顿和杰克枫树。”

Days later, Flanagan emailed Ton-That saying some officers were already “using Clearview on a daily basis.”

几天后，弗拉诺通过电子邮件发送给吨 - 说某些人员已经“每天使用ClearView”。

Ton-That soon began corresponding with officers, at times directly reminding them of their login credentials or resetting their passwords via email. On at least one occasion, an NYPD officer sent Ton-That pictures of three individuals who appear to have allegedly committed a crime at an ATM.

吨 - 很快就开始与官员相对应，有时直接提醒他们登录凭据或通过电子邮件重置密码。在至少有一次，一个诺维德官员发了吨 - 似乎据称在ATM上犯下犯罪的三个人的照片。

In December 2018, Flanagan told Ton-That and Schwartz that officers working with the Real Time Crime Center — a data hub that pulls from multiple surveillance sources operated by the NYPD — were “very impressed with Clearview’s capabilities.” But there were also some issues, like being unable to search for mugshots and Clearview’s lack of a search history.

2018年12月，弗拉纳曼告诉吨 - 与实时犯罪中心合作的施瓦茨（Schwartz）讲述了实时犯罪中心的官员 - 从NYPD运营的多个监控来源中拉出的数据集线器 - “ClearView的能力非常深刻”。但也有一些问题，就像无法搜索Mugshots和ClearView缺乏搜索历史记录一样。

“We would also need each search logged with our own case numbers and who conducted the searches to avoid misuse on our end,” Flanagan said.

“我们还需要每次搜索使用自己的案例编号，并在我们的目前进行搜索以避免滥用，”弗拉纳纳说。

On December 6, two days after Flanagan’s email, Clearview signed a nondisclosure agreement with the NYPD and filled out a vendor contract. The vendor contract stipulates that the NYPD is “only interested in learning about the products and/or services” offered by Clearview and does not guarantee an eventual purchase order. The testing period was stated to run from December 6, 2018, to March 6, 2019.

5月6日，弗拉纳曼的电子邮件后两天，ClearView与NYPD签署了一个非歧视协议，并填写了供应商合同。供应商合同规定，NYPD是“对学习ClearView提供的产品和/或服务才有兴趣，并且不保证最终的采购订单。测试期限于2018年12月6日至2019年3月6日起营业。

During the 2018 to 2019 testing period, officers from the NYPD’s Financial Crimes Task Force, Grand Larceny Division, Facial Identification Section, and a task force for the Department of Homeland Security focused on crimes against children used Clearview, according to the emails. Ton-That also traded personal messages with several of them.

根据电子邮件，2018年至2019年2019年测试期间，NYPD的金融犯罪工作队伍，大型盗窃案，面部识别科，面部识别科，以及国土安全部的工作队，专注于对儿童的罪行，根据电子邮件。吨 - 也与其中几个交易个人信息。

“How are the searches going? It’s fun,” Ton-That asked Michael King, the deputy inspector of the FBI’s New York Joint Terrorism Task Force.

“搜索怎么样？这是有趣的，“吨 - 就联邦调查局纽约共同恐怖主义工作组的副督察迈克尔·王问道。

When an officer asked Ton-That if they could register for Clearview with a personal email account, Ton-That obliged. (Typically, officers sign up for Clearview using their work emails.) He gave advice to an NYPD officer who wanted to “streamline” a search of a “large number of images” related to an identity theft investigation, explaining that searching for many people can be done “easily and remotely.”

当一名官员询问吨 - 如果他们可以通过个人电子邮件帐户注册ClearView，吨 - 有义务。 （通常，官员使用他们的工作电子邮件注册ClearView。）他向一个诺维士官员提供了建议，该官员希望“简化”搜索与身份盗窃调查相关的“大量图像”，解释了寻找许多人可以“轻松和远程”完成。

A February 2019 email includes a picture of an NYPD officer taken at a slanted angle, seemingly from a train.

2019年2月电子邮件包括一张以倾斜的角度拍摄的NYPD官员的图片，似乎来自火车。

“As per commissioner Reznick can you please identify the Member of the service in the photos,” NYPD Detective Alfredo Torres asked King.

“根据委员会reznick可以在照片中确定服务的成员，”Nypd侦探Alfredo Torres问国王。

King promptly forwarded the email to Ton-That. Clearview did not respond to questions about this exchange.

国王及时将电子邮件转发给吨 - 那个。 ClearView没有回答有关此交换的问题。

On March 6, 2019, the Clearview testing period ended. The emails indicate that the NYPD did not sign a paid contract with Clearview AI after that 90-day period, although several officers appeared to like the tool. In March 2019, detective Michael Furia from the Facial Recognition unit said he would “do all I can in helping the NYPD take signing on with Clearview as I am a big supporter.” The following month, when Ton-That asked Sergeant Sara Morris if she had any Clearview “success stories,” she said, “we continue to receive positive results that we confirm and [go on] to make arrests.”

2019年3月6日，明确检测期结束。电子邮件表明，在90天的时间之后，NYPD没有通过ClearView AI签署付费合同，尽管若干人员似乎喜欢该工具。 2019年3月，来自面部识别统一委员会的侦探迈克尔富亚表示，他将“尽我所能在帮助NYPD签署清晰度，因为我是一个重要的支持者。”第二个月，当吨 - 问警长萨拉莫里斯，如果她有任何明确的观点“成功案例”，她说，“我们继续接受我们确认和[继续]进行逮捕的积极成果。”

Despite not signing a contract, many officers continued to use Clearview AI through at least February 26, 2020, the date of the last of the responsive emails in the document.

尽管没有签订合同，但许多人员继续使用ClearView AI，至少2月26,2022022，该文件的最后一个响应电子邮件的日期。

Although Clearview had supporters within the NYPD, emails show that it did not always return matches for faces searched.

虽然ClearView在NYPD中有支持者，但是电子邮件表明它并没有总是搜索面部的返回匹配。

A series of emails sent from the Clearview help account to three different NYPD officers appear to show the number of searches the NYPD ran on Clearview each day and the “hit rate,” or, presumably, the percentage of those searches that came up with at least one match. Between March 18, 2019, and April 4, 2019, Clearview said the NYPD ran between 15 and 87 searches per day, with an average of 45 searches per day. The hit rate, meanwhile, varied between 32% and 59%, with an average of 48%.

从ClearView帮助帐户发送到三个不同的NYPD官员的一系列电子邮件似乎显示NYPD在ClearView上的搜索数量和“命中率”，或者可能是那些提出的那些搜索的百分比至少一场比赛。 ClearView表示，2019年3月18日至4月4日至4月4日，诺维德在每天15到87岁之间搜索，平均每天45次搜索。同时，命中率在32％和59％之间变化，平均为48％。

Essentially, over 18 days, on average, Clearview got a positive match less than half the time.

本质上，超过18天，平均而言，ClearView的积极比赛不到一半。

Through 2019, officers continued to sign up for Clearview. In a “tips” document sent to NYPD officer Michael Rizo in July of that year, the company said that it has “a 30-60% match rate,” which appears to be consistent with the NYPD’s experience.

到2019年，官员继续注册清除视图。在该年7月送到NYPD官员迈克尔·罗兹的“提示”文件中，该公司表示，它具有“30-60％的比赛率”，似乎与NYPD的经验一致。

The email notes that it is “adding hundreds of millions of new faces every month and we expect to get to 80% by the end of this year.”

电子邮件指出，它每月增加数亿新面孔，我们预计到今年年底将获得80％。“

“If you get no results for a photo, first try another photo of your suspect, if you have one available,” the email also says. “If you still don’t get anything, try the same photos again in a week, or two weeks, or in a month. Since we’re adding millions of new photos a day, you might get a match in a few weeks even if you don’t right now.”

“如果你没有出现照片的结果，请先尝试您的嫌疑人的另一张照片，如果您有一个可用，则说：”电子邮件也表示。 “如果你仍然没有得到任何东西，请在一周内再次尝试同一个照片，或两周或一个月。由于我们每天添加数百万新照片，即使您现在不在，您可能会在几周内获得比赛。“

It’s unclear what Clearview’s accuracy and match rates are as of 2021.

目前尚不清楚清晰度的准确性和匹配率达到2021。

Officers continued to use Clearview AI through at least February 26, 2020, the date of the last of the responsive emails in the document.

官员继续使用ClearView AI，至少2月26日，2020年2月26日，该文件的最后一个响应电子邮件的日期。

It’s unclear if NYPD officers are still using Clearview. When reached for comment by BuzzFeed News, an NYPD spokesperson declined to answer specific questions. However, the relationship between Clearview and the NYPD appears to have remained friendly through at least February 2020.

如果NYPD官员仍然使用ClearView，则目前尚不清楚。当BuzzFeed新闻达成评论时，NYPD发言人拒绝回答具体问题。但是，ClearView与NYPD之间的关系似乎至少在2020年2月仍然仍然友好。

In January 2020, NYPD officer Damon Gregar sent a copy of his own resume to Ton-That.

2020年1月，NYPD官员达蒙格雷格派遣了自己简历的副本到吨 - 那。

“Hi, Hoan! Let me know if you receive this and what you think. Thanks so much,” Gregar wrote.

“嗨，霍恩！如果您收到此问题以及您的想法，请告诉我。非常感谢“格雷格写道。

That same month, officer Emilio Gomez asked Clearview, “what are the fees to have full access to your software?”

同月，艾米利奥戈麦斯官员询问ClearView，“有什么费用可以完全访问您的软件？”

Want to offset your carbon footprint? Here’s what you need to know
想抵消您的碳足迹吗？这是你需要知道的

2020-11-25 22:34:28

In the Middle Ages, the Catholic Church offered indulgences, letting people exchange donations for slips of paper that promised reduced time in purgatory after their death. Less controversially, today someone who over-indulges in the office tea supply might feel obliged to pay for a replacement box. Do carbon offsets more closely resemble the former or the latter?

在中世纪，天主教会提供了放纵，让人们用捐款交换纸条，承诺减少死后炼狱的时间。毫无争议的是，今天有人过度沉迷于办公室的茶水供应，可能会觉得有义务为更换盒子付费。碳补偿是否更接近前者或后者？

There are many reasons why you might seek to offset part of your carbon footprint, whether it’s to assuage a general feeling of guilt for your lifestyle, to precisely cover the estimated emissions of a flight, or just to do something beneficial for the environment. Regardless of motivation, all these efforts are predicated on the belief that the money you paid truly will result in the removal of the promised amount of CO 2 from the atmosphere. Otherwise, you’re paying for a lie—or at least getting a smaller box of tea than you ordered.

您可能有很多理由想要抵消部分碳足迹，无论是为了减轻自己对生活方式的一般内feeling感，还是为了准确地涵盖航班的估计排放量，或者只是为了做对环境有益的事情。无论出于何种动机，所有这些努力都基于这样一个信念，即您真正支付的钱将导致从大气中清除承诺的CO 2量。否则，您要撒谎，或者至少要得到比预定少的茶。

Finding out whether you've been lied to is genuinely difficult. Here’s what you need to know.

找出您是否被骗是非常困难的。这是您需要知道的。

Carbon dioxide in the atmosphere is part of a bio-geo-chemical dance called the carbon cycle. Carbon regularly changes form and area code, cycling among the atmosphere, oceans, ecosystems, and even bedrock. Plants take carbon atoms from CO 2 in the air to grow. Herbivores consume plants and exhale the carbon as CO 2 again. Weathering rocks pull CO 2 out of the air. Volcanoes release it into the atmosphere. These are just some of many exchanges in the carbon cycle.

大气中的二氧化碳是生物地球化学舞的一部分，称为碳循环。碳有规律地改变形式和区域代码，在大气，海洋，生态系统甚至基岩之间循环。植物从空气中的CO 2中吸收碳原子进行生长。草食动物消耗植物并再次呼出碳作为CO 2。风化岩石将CO 2排出空气。火山将其释放到大气中。这些只是碳循环中许多交换的一部分。

Before the Industrial Revolution, these flows were in balance, maintaining a stable concentration of CO 2 in the atmosphere. But then we discovered fossil fuels and ways of lighting them on fire for fun and profit. This increasingly took carbon that had spent many millions of years locked up deep underground and liberated it into the atmosphere. Some of that carbon (a little over half, actually) was taken up by the oceans and land ecosystems. But the rest accumulated in the atmosphere, pushing greenhouse gas concentrations higher and higher.

在工业革命之前，这些流量是平衡的，从而使大气中的CO 2浓度保持稳定。但是后来我们发现了化石燃料，以及点燃它们的方式来取乐和牟利。越来越多的碳被消耗了，这些碳已经花费了数百万年的时间锁定在地下深处并释放到大气中。其中一些碳（实际上是一半多一点）被海洋和陆地生态系统吸收了。但是其余的气体则聚集在大气中，使温室气体的浓度越来越高。

To stop the rise in this greenhouse gas, our net total emissions of CO 2 must reach zero. It’s that simple. We can get there in part by eliminating the emissions caused by some of our activities. But we'll probably also have to compensate for ongoing emissions to reach net zero.

为了阻止这种温室气体的增加，我们的CO 2净总排放量必须达到零。就这么简单。我们可以通过消除某些活动造成的排放来部分达到目标。但是我们可能还必须补偿持续的排放以达到净零。

Some carbon offsets projects help with this goal by preventing emissions that would otherwise have occurred. Others promise to preserve something that in carbon cycle terms is known as a “sink”—a durable form of carbon like forests or even mineralized carbonate in the ground. These offsets aren’t always identical to prevented emissions, as they can have climate effects separate from carbon. Forests, for example, can be darker than their surroundings, so their expansion results in a local warming effect as more sunlight is absorbed. But reforestation also comes with the potential to provide other ecosystem services like animal habitats. So there are a lot of factors beyond carbon to consider.

一些碳补偿项目通过防止原本会发生的排放来帮助实现这一目标。其他人则承诺保留某种在碳循环方面被称为“汇”的东西—一种持久的碳形式，例如森林，甚至是地下的矿化碳酸盐。这些补偿并不总是与预防性排放相同，因为它们可以使气候影响与碳隔离。例如，森林可能比周围环境更暗，因此森林的扩张会导致吸收更多的阳光，从而导致局部变暖。但是，重新造林还具有提供其他生态系统服务（如动物栖息地）的潜力。因此，除了碳之外，还有许多其他因素需要考虑。

Planting trees is a tantalizingly simple response to climate change. (For evidence, see: knee-jerk Internet comments.) But our capacity to store carbon this way is finite. We could undo the emissions caused by past deforestation, but we can never plant enough to cover rampant fossil fuel use. But, even if you accept that limitation, a lot can go wrong after your seedlings get tucked into the dirt.

植树是对气候变化的诱人的简单反应。 （有关证据，请参阅：下意识的互联网评论。）但是我们以这种方式存储碳的能力是有限的。我们可以消除过去森林砍伐造成的排放，但是我们永远无法种植足够的土地来弥补化石燃料的大量使用。但是，即使您接受该限制，在您的幼苗被塞进泥土后，很多事情还是会出错。

Assuming that you find a program you can trust to actually plant the promised trees, many things will influence their carbon cycle impact over time. The first is the way that species of tree grows. Some grow quickly, accumulating a lot of carbon per acre over the last decade or so, which provides immediate help to our carbon emissions balance sheet. But fast growing trees are typically less dense than slower-growing species, so the ultimate amount of carbon stored per acre is lower.

假设您找到可以信赖的程序来实际种植承诺的树木，那么随着时间的流逝，许多事情都会影响其碳循环的影响。首先是树种的生长方式。有些迅速增长，在过去十年左右的时间里，每英亩积累了大量的碳，这立即为我们的碳排放平衡表提供了帮助。但是，生长较快的树木通常比生长较慢的树木密度较低，因此每英亩的最终碳存储量较低。

Faster-growing species also tend to be shorter-lived. If trees in this plantation are being managed for harvesting, end-of-life will happen sooner. Then where does the carbon go? 生长快的物种也往往寿命较短。如果对该种植园中的树木进行采伐管理，则使用寿命将更早发生。那碳去了哪里呢？有些可以变成木材，这时它的碳汇价值取决于用该木材建造的东西能持续多久。剩余的植物材料会腐烂或燃烧，将碳释放回大气中，从而结束了它的时间。水槽，

Even if the planted trees aren’t ever harvested, their carbon could be liberated in other ways. Old trees die and rot, of course. Bug infestations can rip through the forests, killing trees en masse. Wildfires can do the same. Some risks like these are increasing due to climate change, meaning that forest carbon storage is becoming less and less safe over time in many regions.

即使从未收获过树木，也可以通过其他方式释放它们的碳。当然，老树会枯萎烂掉。虫虫侵扰会在森林中蔓延，大量杀死树木。野火也可以这样做。由于气候变化，诸如此类的一些风险正在增加，这意味着随着时间的流逝，许多地区的森林碳储存变得越来越安全。

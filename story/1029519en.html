鲍莫尔效应

2020-10-18 07:13:47

Jump to navigation Jump to search Baumol's cost disease (or the Baumol effect) is the rise of salaries in jobs that have experienced no or low increase of labor productivity, in response to rising salaries in other jobs that have experienced higher labor productivity growth. This pattern seemingly goes against the theory in classical economics in which real wage growth is closely tied to labor productivity changes. The phenomenon was described by William J. Baumol and William G. Bowen in the 1960s. [1]

跳转到导航跳转搜索鲍莫尔的成本病(或鲍莫尔效应)是指劳动生产率没有增长或增长较低的工作岗位的工资上涨，以应对其他劳动生产率增长较快的工作岗位的工资上涨。这种模式似乎与古典经济学中的理论背道而驰，在古典经济学中，实际工资增长与劳动生产率的变化密切相关。威廉·J·鲍默和威廉·G·鲍恩在20世纪60年代描述了这一现象。[1]。

The rise of wages in jobs without productivity gains derives from the requirement to compete for employees with jobs that have experienced gains and so can naturally pay higher salaries, just as classical economics predicts. For instance, if the retail sector pays its managers 19th-century-style salaries, the managers may decide to quit to get a job at an automobile factory, where salaries are higher because of high labor productivity. Thus, managers' salaries are increased not by labor productivity increases in the retail sector but by productivity and corresponding wage increases in other industries.

没有生产率提高的工作的工资增长源于要求与经历了增长的工作争夺员工，因此自然可以支付更高的工资，正如经典经济学预测的那样。例如，如果零售业向经理支付19世纪式的工资，经理们可能会决定辞职，在一家汽车工厂找到一份工作，那里的工资更高，因为劳动生产率高。因此，经理工资的增长不是由零售业劳动生产率的提高，而是由其他行业的生产率和相应的工资增长来实现的。

The original study was conducted for the performing arts sector. [1] Baumol and Bowen pointed out that the same number of musicians is needed to play a Beethoven string quartet today as was needed in the 19th century; the productivity of classical music performance has not increased. On the other hand, the real wages of musicians (as in all other professions) have increased greatly since the 19th century.

原来的研究是为演艺界进行的。[1]鲍莫和鲍文指出，今天演奏贝多芬弦乐四重奏所需要的音乐家人数与19世纪时一样多；古典音乐演奏的生产力并没有增加。另一方面，自19世纪以来，音乐家(就像所有其他职业一样)的实际工资有了很大提高。

Baumol's cost disease is often used to describe consequences of the lack of growth in productivity in the quaternary sector of the economy and public services, such as public hospitals and state colleges. Labor-intensive sectors that rely heavily on non-routine human interaction or activities, such as health care, education, or the performing arts, have had less growth in productivity over time. As with the string quartet example, it takes nurses the same amount of time to change a bandage or college professors the same amount of time to mark an essay today as it did in 1966, as those types of activities rely on the movements of the human body, which cannot yet be engineered to perform more quickly, accurately, or efficiently in the same way that a machine, such as a computer, have. In contrast, goods-producing industries, such as the car manufacturing sector and other activities that involve routine tasks, workers are continually becoming more productive by technological innovations to their tools and equipment. [2]

鲍莫尔的成本病经常被用来描述第四经济部门和公共服务部门(如公立医院和州立大学)生产率增长乏力的后果。严重依赖非常规人类互动或活动的劳动密集型部门，如医疗保健、教育或表演艺术，随着时间的推移，生产率增长较少。就像弦乐四重奏的例子一样，护士换绷带或大学教授今天批改一篇作文所需的时间与1966年一样多，因为这些类型的活动依赖于人体的运动，而人体的运动还不能被设计成像计算机这样的机器那样更快、更准确、更高效地执行任务。相比之下，商品生产行业，如汽车制造业和其他涉及日常任务的活动，工人通过工具和设备的技术创新不断提高生产力。[2]。

The concept was alluded to as early as 1776 by Adam Smith in The Wealth of Nations: [3]

早在1776年，亚当·斯密在“国富论”中就提到了这个概念：[3]。

Let us suppose, for example, that in the greater part of employments the productive powers of labour had been improved to tenfold, or that a day’s labour could produce ten times the quantity of work which it had done originally; but that in a particular employment they had been improved only to double, or that a day’s labour could produce only twice the quantity of work which it had done before. In exchanging the produce of a day’s labour in the greater part of employments for that of a day’s labour in this particular one, ten times the original quantity of work in them would purchase only twice the original quantity in it. Any particular quantity in it, therefore, a pound weight, for example, would appear to be five times dearer than before. In reality, however, it would be twice as cheap.

例如，让我们假设，在大部分的就业中，劳动的生产力已经提高到原来的十倍，或者一天的劳动可以产生原来的十倍的工作量，但是在某一特定的就业中，劳动生产力只提高到原来的两倍，或者一天的劳动只能产生原来的两倍的工作量。如果把大部分就业岗位上的一天劳动成果换成这一特殊岗位上的一天劳动成果，十倍于原劳动数量的劳动，只会买到原来数量的两倍。因此，它里面的任何特定数量，例如一磅重，都会显得比以前贵五倍。然而，在现实中，它的价格会是现在的两倍。

In the case of education, the Baumol effect has been used as at least partial justification for the fact that, in recent decades, the price of college tuition has risen faster than the general rate of inflation. [4]

就教育而言，近几十年来，大学学费价格的上涨速度快于一般通货膨胀率，鲍莫尔效应至少被用来部分解释这一事实。[4]。

The reported productivity gains of the service industry in the late 1990s are largely attributable to total factor productivity. [5] Providers decreased the cost of ancillary labor through outsourcing or technology. Examples include offshoring data entry and bookkeeping for health care providers and replacing manually-marked essays in educational assessment with multiple choice tests that can be automatically marked.

据报道，20世纪90年代末服务业生产率的提高主要归功于全要素生产率。[5]供应商通过外包或技术降低了辅助劳动力的成本。例如，将医疗保健提供者的数据录入和簿记离岸，以及将教育评估中手动批改的作文替换为可以自动批改的多项选择考试。

The total factor productivity treatment is not available to the performing arts sector, as the consumable good is the labor itself. Instead, it has been observed that increases in price of the performing arts has been offset by increases in standard of living and entertainment spending by consumers. [6] The extent to which other treatments have been employed is subjective.

全要素生产率待遇对表演艺术部门是不可用的，因为消耗品就是劳动力本身。相反，据观察，表演艺术价格的上涨已被消费者生活水平和娱乐支出的提高所抵消。[6]采用其他治疗的程度是主观的。

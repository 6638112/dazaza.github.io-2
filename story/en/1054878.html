Most couples may have to use assisted reproduction by 2045
大多数夫妻可能必须在2045年使用辅助再现

2021-03-28 22:04:29

The professor of environmental medicine explains how chemicals in plastics are causing our fertility to decline – and what we can do about it

环境医学教授解释了塑料中的化学物质如何导致我们的生育能力下降 - 以及我们可以做的事情

Shanna Swan is a professor of environmental medicine and public health at Mount Sinai school of medicine in New York City, studying fertility trends. In 2017 she documented how average sperm counts among western men have more than halved in the past 40 years. Count Down is her new book.

苏珊娜天鹅是纽约市山上西奈山医学院的环境医学和公共卫生教授，研究生育趋势。 2017年，她记录了在过去的40年中，西方男性的平均精子数量在减半。倒数是她的新书。

You’ve spent more than 20 years examining the effects of hormone disrupting chemicals on reproductive health. Are you now sounding the alarm? I am directly speaking to this hidden problem people don’t like to talk about, which is their sub-fertility or reproductive problems, and how that is tied to the environment. People are recognising we have a reproductive health crisis, but they say it’s because of delayed childbearing, choice or lifestyle – it can’t be chemical. I want people to recognise it can. I am not saying other factors aren’t involved. But I am saying chemicals play a major causal role. It is difficult to use that word, “cause”, but it’s a body of evidence. We have mechanisms, animal studies, and multiple human studies.

您已经花了20多年的时间检查激素中扰动化学品对生殖健康的影响。你现在发表闹钟吗？我直接与这个隐藏的问题谈到，人们不喜欢谈论，这是他们的子生育力或生殖问题，以及如何与环境相关联。人们认识到我们有生殖健康危机，但他们说这是因为延迟生育，选择或生活方式 - 它不能是化学品。我希望人们认识到它可以。我不是说不参与其他因素。但我说化学品发挥着重大因果作用。很难使用这个词，“原因”，但这是一系列证据。我们有机制，动物研究和多重人类研究。

Female fertility declines rapidly after about 35. Isn’t that why so many people are turning to IVF? It’s not that simple. When a colleague and I looked at the change in impaired fecundity [the ability to have children] we were surprised to see younger women had experienced a bigger increase than older age groups. This suggests that something besides ageing and delayed childbearing is affecting fertility.

女性生育能力在大约35后在迅速下降。不是为什么这么多人转向IVF？这不是那么简单。当一位同事和我看着益处理的变化[有孩子的能力]，我们惊讶地看到年轻的女性比年龄较大的年龄群体更大的增加。这表明除了老龄化和延迟生育之外，还会影响生育能力。

Moreover, there’s compelling evidence that the risk of miscarriage has been rising among women of all ages.

此外，有令人信服的证据表明流产的风险在所有年龄段的女性中都在上升。

Which chemicals are the most worrying for reproductive health and how do they work? Those that can interfere with or mimic the body’s sex hormones – such as testosterone and oestrogen – because these make reproduction possible. They can make the body think it has enough of a particular hormone and it doesn’t need to make any more, so production goes down.

哪些化学品最令人担忧的生殖健康以及它们如何工作？那些可以干扰或模仿身体的性激素 - 例如睾酮和雌激素的人 - 因为这些使得可以再生产。他们可以让身体思考它有足够的特定激素，因此不需要做得更多，因此生产下降。

Phthalates, used to make plastic soft and flexible, are of paramount concern. They are in everybody and we are probably primarily exposed through food as we use soft plastic in food manufacture, processing and packaging. They lower testosterone and so have the strongest influences on the male side, for example diminishing sperm count, though they are bad for women, too, shown to decrease libido and increase risk of early puberty, premature ovarian failure, miscarriage and premature birth.

邻苯二甲酸盐，用于制造塑料软和灵活，是最重要的担忧。他们在每个人身上，我们可能主要通过食品暴露，因为我们在食品制造，加工和包装中使用软塑料。它们降低睾酮，因此对男性侧的影响最强，例如递减精子数量，尽管它们对女性也很糟糕，但显然会降低性欲，增加早期青春期，过早卵巢衰竭，流产和早产的风险。

Bisphenol A (BPA), used to harden plastic and found in cash-register receipts and the lining of some canned-food containers, is another. It is oestrogen mimicking and so is a particularly bad actor on the female side, increasing risks of fertility challenges, but likewise it can affect men. Men occupationally exposed to BPA have shown decreased sperm quality, reduced libido and higher rates of erectile dysfunction. Other chemicals of concern include flame retardants and certain pesticides such as atrazine.

双酚A（BPA），用于硬化塑料，并在现金登记收据中找到，是一些罐头食品容器的衬里，是另一个。它是雌激素模仿，也是女性方面特别糟糕的演员，增加了生育挑战的风险，但同样会影响男性。职业暴露于BPA的男性表现出了降低的精子质量，降低了性欲和更高的勃起功能障碍。其他令人担忧的化学品包括阻燃剂和某些农药如尿嘧啶。

When is the most damage done? A lot of the exposure that causes these changes occurs in utero when the foetus is first forming. These rapidly dividing cells are the most sensitive. The hits then continue through childhood, adolescence and adulthood. There is a cumulative nature to this. And we can pass these effects on. The simplest way is by direct exposure. A female foetus, in utero, is growing the eggs that she will use to have her own children. These chemicals can make their way to those germ cells, too.

什么时候做出最大的伤害？当胎儿首先形成时，在子宫内发生了许多导致这些变化的曝光。这些快速分裂的细胞是最敏感的。然后击中，然后继续穿过童年，青春期和成年。这有一个累积的性质。我们可以通过这些影响。最简单的方式是直接曝光。在子宫中的雌性胎儿正在生长鸡蛋，她将用来拥有自己的孩子。这些化学品也可以向那些生殖细胞进行途径。

What is the anogenital distance (AGD) and why does it matter? It is the distance from the anus to the genitals and it is usually far longer in males than females. It is a indicator of how much androgen [male sex hormones including testosterone] an infant was exposed to during early pregnancy. AGD is such an important marker of reproductive health and endocrine disruption – if we measured it for every infant, we would know something about their future fertility. Shorter AGDs in males and longer AGDs in females indicate less reproductive success.

什么是胃部距离（AGD），为什么重要？它是与肛门对生殖器的距离，并且通常比女性更长。这是一个雄激素[男性性激素，包括睾酮]婴儿在妊娠期间暴露多少腺体的指标。 AGD是生殖健康和内分泌中断的重要标志 - 如果我们为每个婴儿测量它，我们会对他们未来的生育能力达到一些问题。男性中的较短agds和女性中的较长agds表明较少的生殖成功。

You’ve used AGD to show phthalates cause low sperm count in men. How? When I started looking at phthalates, around 2000, phthalate syndrome had been shown experimentally in rodents but not in humans. Mother rats given phthalates had male babies with a smaller penis and scrotum, their sperm counts were lower and their AGD was shorter. I ran a study where we measured the stored urine of pregnant women for phthalates and we measured their male babies’ and toddlers’ AGDs – a first – along with some other genital measurements. We found exactly what had been found in rodents: phthalate syndrome. I then did a study of college-age men, where we got them to give a semen sample and measured their AGD, and we showed that the shorter the AGD, the lower the sperm count. This is why I am confident: we have a direct link from phthalates to short AGD and then from short AGD to low sperm count, and those results have been confirmed by subsequent studies.

您使用AGD显示邻苯二甲酸盐导致男性的低精子计数。如何？当我开始看邻苯二甲酸盐，大约2000年，邻苯二甲酸综合征在啮齿动物中被实验显示，但不是在人类中。鉴于邻苯二甲酸酯的母亲鼠患有较小的阴茎和阴囊的雄性婴儿，它们的精子计数较低，其agd较短。我跑了一项研究，在那里，我们测量了蛋白酶的孕妇尿液，我们测量了他们的男性婴儿和幼儿的agds - 以及一些其他生殖器测量。我们确切地发现了啮齿动物中发现的内容：邻苯二甲酸肽综合症。然后，我对大学男性进行了研究，在那里我们让他们给出一个精液样本并测量他们的agd，我们表明agd越短，精子数量越低。这就是为什么我有信心：我们与邻苯二甲酸盐的直接链接到短片，然后从短暂的agd到低精子计数，并通过随后的研究证实了这些结果。

How dire is the reproductive crisis? You’ve said we are on course for an infertile world by 2045… It is serious. If you follow the curve from the 2017 sperm-decline meta-analysis, it predicts that by 2045 we will have a median sperm count of zero. It is speculative to extrapolate, but there is also no evidence that it is tapering off. This means that most couples may have to use assisted reproduction.

reire是如何生殖危机？你说我们在2045年到2045年的不育的世界。这是严肃的。如果您从2017年精液下降荟萃分析中遵循曲线，则预测到2045年，我们将具有零中位的精子计数。推动外推，但也没有证据表明它是逐渐减少的。这意味着大多数夫妻可能必须使用辅助再现。

Drugs are monitored for safety. What is the regulatory situation for these chemicals? In the US, most chemicals have not been tested and are assumed to be safe. There has been better progress in Europe. Though imperfect, the EU’s Reach (Registration, Evaluation, Authorisation and Restriction of Chemicals) regulation was a big step forward. There are huge economic interests preventing tougher regulation.

为安全监测药物。这些化学品的监管局势是多少？在美国，大多数化学物质尚未进行测试，并被认为是安全的。在欧洲取得了更好的进展。虽然不完善，欧盟的覆盖范围（注册，评估，授权和化学品的限制）规例向前迈出了一大步。有巨大的经济利益，防止了更加艰难的监管。

What needs to happen? The chemical industry must start producing chemicals that can be used in everyday products that are non-hormonally active. Regrettable substitution – where one harmful chemical is replaced by another untested one, which then turns out to have the same risks – must also stop. It has happened with different phthalates, BPA and flame retardants and it is unacceptable. And we need to test the chemicals we are currently using – and not just at high doses and not just one at a time, because we are being exposed to a large number.

需要发生什么？化学工业必须开始生产可用于非激素活跃的日常产品的化学品。令人遗憾的替代 - 其中一个有害化学物质被另一个未经发现的化学物品取代，然后拒绝具有相同的风险 - 也必须停止。它已经发生在不同的邻苯二甲酸盐，BPA和阻燃剂，并且是不可接受的。我们需要测试我们目前使用的化学品 - 而不仅仅是高剂量，而不是一次，因为我们被暴露在大量大量。

Should we believe packaging that says BPA or phthalate free? If it says “BPA free” it probably doesn’t have BPA. But notice that it doesn’t say bisphenol free so you could still be getting bisphenol S or F, which are regrettable substitutes. “Phthalate free” I would also be suspicious of. While it might be free of the old, well-known actors, it may not be free of newer ones. People should feel angry about potentially being tricked this way and press for change.

我们是否相信包装，表明BPA或邻苯二甲酸酯？如果它说“BPA免费”，它可能没有BPA。但请注意，它没有说双苯酚，所以你仍然可以获得双酚或F，这是令人遗憾的替代品。 “邻苯二甲酸酯免费”我也会怀疑。虽然它可能是没有旧的，众所周知的演员，但它可能没有较新的演员。人们应该感到生气，可能是这种方式被欺骗，然后按下改变。

What should we be doing to cut down our exposure? People of reproductive age, particularly those planning pregnancy or pregnant, should be aware that everything they bring into their home has the potential to contain these chemicals. To the extent possible, eat unprocessed foods – a bunch of carrots, potatoes that you cook yourself – as this should reduce exposure through plastic. Also, when cooking, don’t use Teflon or anything coated and don’t microwave in plastic. For personal care and household products use a minimum of simple products and try to avoid those that are scented; phthalates are added to hold scent. The non-profit Environmental Working Group has free consumer guides that give information about specific products.

我们应该做些什么来减少我们的曝光？生殖年龄，特别是那些规划怀孕或怀孕的人，应该意识到他们带入他们家的一切都有可能遏制这些化学品。尽可能多，吃未加工的食物 - 一堆胡萝卜，你自己做饭的土豆 - 因为这应该通过塑料减少暴露。此外，当烹饪时，不要使用Teflon或任何涂层，并不在塑料中微波。对于个人护理和家庭产品，使用最少的简单产品，并尽量避免那些受到香味的产品;加入邻苯二甲酸酯以保持气味。非营利性环境工作组有免费的消费者指南，提供有关特定产品的信息。

Can we can turn things around? I think we can. We have the ingenuity and the resources to do it. But we need a recognition of the problem and the will to change.

我们可以转过身来吗？我想我们可以。我们有聪明才智和资源来做。但我们需要一个问题的认可，而且将改变的意志。

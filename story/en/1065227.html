Apple turned over the data of two lawmakers to Trump Justice Dept
Apple转过了两个立法者的数据，以特朗普司法部

2021-06-12 07:43:13

On Feb. 6, 2018, Apple received a grand jury subpoena for the names and phone records connected to 109 email addresses and phone numbers. It was one of the more than 250 data requests that the company received on average from U.S. law enforcement each week at the time. An Apple paralegal complied and provided the information.

2018年2月6日，Apple收到了一个大型陪审团陪审团，用于连接到109个电子邮件地址和电话号码的名称和电话记录。这是每周从美国执法人员收到的250多个数据请求之一。苹果律师亚加哥蛋白酶遵守并提供了信息。

This year, a gag order on the subpoena expired. Apple said it alerted the people who were the subjects of the subpoena, just as it does with dozens of customers each day.

今年，传票中的堵嘴订单已过期。 Apple表示，它提醒了那些作为传票的主题的人，每天都有数十家客户。

Without knowing it, Apple said, it had handed over the data of congressional staffers, their families and at least two members of Congress, including Representative Adam B. Schiff of California, then the House Intelligence Committee’s top Democrat and now its chairman. It turned out the subpoena was part of a wide-ranging investigation by the Trump administration into leaks of classified information.

如果不知道它，Apple表示，它已经交出了国会员工，家庭和至少两个国会成员的数据，包括亚当··舍夫的加州席族，然后是家庭情报委员会的民主党人，现在是其主席。事实证明，传票是特朗普政府泄露分类信息的广泛调查的一部分。

The revelations have now plunged Apple into the middle of a firestorm over the Trump administration’s efforts to find the sources of news stories, and the handling underscores the flood of law enforcement requests that tech companies increasingly contend with. The number of these requests has soared in recent years to thousands a week, putting Apple and other tech giants like Google and Microsoft in an uncomfortable position between law enforcement, the courts and the customers whose privacy they have promised to protect.

现在，启示录现在陷入了特朗普政府的努力，找到了新闻故事的努力，揭开了凤凰风暴的中间，处理强调了技术公司越来越争辩的执法要求。这些请求的数量近年来飙升至每周达到数千个，将苹果和其他科技巨头像谷歌和微软一样，在执法，法院和他们承诺保护的隐私之间的客户之间存在不舒服的立场。

The companies regularly comply with the requests because they are legally required to do so. The subpoenas can be vague, so Apple, Google and others are often unclear on the nature or subject of an investigation. They can challenge some of subpoenas if they are too broad or if they relate to a corporate client. In the first six months of 2020, Apple challenged 238 demands from the government for its customers’ account data, or 4 percent of such requests.

这些公司定期遵守该请求，因为它们是合法要求的。子公司可能是模糊的，所以苹果，谷歌和其他人往往不清楚调查的性质或主题。如果他们过于广泛或与公司客户有关，他们可以挑战一些子公司。在2020年的前六个月，Apple对其客户的账户数据或4％的此类请求中的4％挑战了238份要求。

As part of the same leak investigation by the Trump administration, Google fought a gag order this year on a subpoena to turn over data on the emails of four New York Times reporters. Google argued that its contract as The Times’s corporate email provider required it to inform the newspaper of any government requests for its emails, said Ted Boutrous, an outside lawyer for The Times.

作为特朗普管理局的相同泄露调查的一部分，谷歌今年在传票中讨论了一个GAG订单，以翻入四个纽约时报记者的电子邮件数据。谷歌认为，它的合同作为时代的公司电子邮件提供商需要它向报纸通知任何政府要求的电子邮件，这是泰晤士报的外部律师。

But more frequently than not, the companies comply with law enforcement demands. And that underlines an awkward truth: As their products become more central to people’s lives, the world’s largest tech companies have become surveillance intermediaries and crucial partners to authorities, with the power to arbitrate which requests to honor and which to reject.

但更频繁的是，公司遵守执法需求。并且强调了一个尴尬的真理：随着他们的产品对人们的生活变得更加核心，世界上最大的科技公司已成为监测中介机构和对当局的关键合作伙伴，仲裁要求尊重纪念并拒绝的权力。

“There is definitely tension,” said Alan Z. Rozenshtein, an associate professor at the University of Minnesota’s law school and a former Justice Department lawyer. He said given the “insane amount of data these companies have” and how everyone has a smartphone, most law enforcement investigations “at some point involves these companies.”

“肯定是紧张的，”明尼苏达大学法学院和前司法部律师的副教授Alan Z. Rozenshtein说。他说，鉴于“这些公司有疯了数量”以及每个人的智能手机，大多数执法调查的“某些时候都涉及这些公司”。

On Friday, the Justice Department’s independent inspector general opened an investigation into the decision by federal prosecutors to secretly seize the data of House Democrats and reporters. Top Senate Democrats also demanded that the former attorneys general William P. Barr and Jeff Sessions testify before Congress about the leak investigations, specifically about the subpoena issued to Apple and another to Microsoft.

星期五，司法部的独立检验员将军开启了联邦检察官秘密抓住房屋民主党和记者数据的决定的调查。最佳参议院民主党还要求前律师将军威廉佩尔和杰夫会议在国会之前作证了关于泄露调查的大会，特别是关于向苹果发给苹果和另一个向微软发出的传票。

Fred Sainz, an Apple spokesman, said in a statement that the company regularly challenges government data requests and informs affected customers as soon as it legally can.

苹果发言人Fred Sainz表示，该公司定期挑战政府资料请求，并在合法地挑战受影响的客户。

“In this case, the subpoena, which was issued by a federal grand jury and included a nondisclosure order signed by a federal magistrate judge, provided no information on the nature of the investigation and it would have been virtually impossible for Apple to understand the intent of the desired information without digging through users’ accounts,” he said. “Consistent with the request, Apple limited the information it provided to account subscriber information and did not provide any content such as emails or pictures.”

“在这种情况下，由联邦盛大陪审团发布的传票，并包括联邦裁判法官签署的非学位令，没有关于调查的性质的信息，苹果几乎不可能了解意图没有通过用户的账户挖掘所需信息，“他说。 “与请求一致，Apple限制了它提供给帐户订户信息的信息，并且没有提供任何内容，例如电子邮件或图片。”

In a statement, Microsoft said it received a subpoena in 2017 related to a personal email account. It said it notified the customer after the gag order expired and learned that the person was a congressional staff member. “We will continue to aggressively seek reform that imposes reasonable limits on government secrecy in cases like this,” the company said.

在一份声明中，微软表示，它在2017年收到了一个包含个人电子邮件帐户的传票。它表示，在GAG订单过期后，客户通知客户并了解到该人是国会工作人员。 “我们将继续积极寻求改革，以这种情况在这样的情况下对政府秘密施加合理限制，”该公司表示。

With Apple’s latest mobile software update, we can decide whether apps monitor and share our activities with others. Here’s what to know.

通过Apple的最新移动软件更新，我们可以决定应用程序是否监控并与他人分享我们的活动。这是要知道的。

A little maintenance on your devices and accounts can go a long way in maintaining your security against outside parties’ unwanted attempts to access your data. Here’s a guide to the few simple changes you can make to protect yourself and your information online.

对您的设备和帐户的一点维护可以在维护您的外部缔约方的不需要的尝试访问数据时进行维护。以下是您可以在线保护自己和您的信息的几个简单变化的指南。

Google declined to comment on whether it received a subpoena related to the investigation on the House Intelligence committee.

谷歌拒绝发表评论是否收到与房屋情报委员会调查有关的传票。

The Justice Department has not commented publicly on Apple turning over House Intelligence Committee records. In congressional testimony this week, Attorney General Merrick B. Garland sidestepped criticism of the Trump administration’s decisions and said the seizure of records was made “under a set of policies that have existed for decades.”

司法部没有公开发表评论苹果，转向房屋情报委员会记录。在本周的国会证词中，律师Merrick B. Garland批评了特朗普政府的决定，并表示在几十年存在的一系列政策下制定了缉获记录。“

In the Justice Department’s leak investigation, Apple and Microsoft turned over so-called metadata of people who worked in Congress, including phone records, device information, and addresses. It is not unusual for the Justice Department to subpoena such metadata, because the information can be used to establish whether someone had contact with a member of the media or whether their work or home accounts were tied to anonymous accounts that were used to disseminate classified information.

在司法部的泄露调查中，Apple和Microsoft转过了在国会工作的人的所谓元数据，包括电话记录，设备信息和地址。司法部向这些元数据传唤并不罕见，因为信息可用于确定有人是否与媒体成员联系或他们的工作或家庭账户是否与用于传播分类信息的匿名账户相关联。

Under the gag orders that authorities placed on the subpoenas, Apple and Microsoft also agreed to not tell those people whose information was being demanded. In Apple’s case, a yearlong gag order was renewed three separate times. That contrasted with Google, which resisted the gag order on a subpoena to turn over data on the four Times reporters.

根据GAG订单，该当局在SubpoenaS，Apple和Microsoft上也同意不告诉那些信息所需的人。在苹果的案例中，一年长的GAG订单被重新续订了三次。与谷歌形成鲜明对比，抵制了在传票中的GAG顺序，以转向四次记者的数据。

The differing responses are largely explained by the different relationships the companies had with their customers in the case. Apple and Microsoft were ordered to hand over data related to individual accounts, while the subpoena to Google affected a corporate customer, which was governed by a contract. That contract gave Google a more specific basis on which to challenge the gag order, lawyers said.

不同的反应主要由公司在案件中与客户的不同关系解释。 Apple和Microsoft被命令处理与个别账户相关的数据，而传票给谷歌影响了一名公司客户，该公司由合同管辖。律师说，该合同给谷歌更具体的基础挑战了牙科秩序。

The subpoena to Apple was also more opaque — it simply asked for information about a series of email addresses and phone numbers — and the company said it did not know it related to an investigation into Congress. For Google, it was clear that the Justice Department sought records from The Times because the email addresses were clearly those of Times reporters.

苹果的传票也更为不透明 - 它简单地要求有关一系列电子邮件地址和电话号码的信息 - 公司表示，它不知道它与对国会调查有关。对于谷歌，很明显，司法部从时代寻求记录，因为电子邮件地址显然是时代记者。

Google said it generally does not handle requests for customer information differently for individual accounts and corporate customers. But the company has a strong argument to redirect requests for data of corporate customers based on the Justice Department’s own recommendations.

谷歌表示，对于个别账户和企业客户，它通常不处理客户信息的请求。但该公司对基于司法部的建议重定向公司客户数据请求的强大争论。

In guidelines released in 2017, the Justice Department urged prosecutors to “seek data directly” from companies instead of going through a technology provider, unless doing so was impractical or would compromise the investigation. By going to Google to seize information about the reporters, the Justice Department sought to circumvent The Times. Google declined to say whether it used the Justice Department guidelines to fight the gag order.

在2017年发布的指导方针中，司法部敦促检察官从公司“直接寻求数据”而不是经过技术提供者，除非这样做是不切实际或会妥协调查。通过去谷歌扣押有关记者的信息，司法部试图绕过时代。谷歌拒绝说它是否利用司法部指导指南争取堵嘴秩序。

Google said it produced some data in 83 percent of the nearly 40,000 requests for information from U.S. government agencies it received in the first half of 2020. By comparison, it produced some data in 39 percent of requests for information on 398 paying corporate customers of Google Cloud, including its email and web-hosting offerings, during the same time period.

谷歌表示，它在2020年上半年收到的美国政府机构的信息中有近40,000个近40,000个请求产生了一些数据。相比之下，它在398次谷歌支付公司客户的398份信息请求中产生了一些数据云，包括其电子邮件和网络托管产品，同时。

Law enforcement requests for data from American tech companies have more than doubled in recent years. Facebook said it received nearly 123,000 data requests from the U.S. government last year, up from 37,000 in 2015.

近年来，美国科技公司的数据的法律执法请求增加了一倍多。 Facebook表示，它去年从美国政府获得了近123,000个数据请求，2015年的37,000。

Apple said that in the first half of 2020, it received an average of 400 requests a week for customer data from U.S. law enforcement, more than double the rate five years prior. The company’s compliance rate has remained roughly between 80 percent and 85 percent for years.

Apple表示，在2020年的上半年，它平均每周收到400个请求，为客户资料从美国执法部门进行两次，比率为五年前的两倍。多年来，该公司的合规性率大约为80％至85％。

Authorities are also demanding information about more accounts in each request. In the first half of 2020, each U.S. government subpoena or warrant to Apple requested data for 11 accounts or devices on average, up from less than three accounts or devices in the first half of 2015, the company said.

当局还要求有关每个请求中更多帐户的信息。该公司表示，在2020年上半年，每个美国政府召集或逮捕苹果公司要求的11个账户或设备的数据，从少于三年的账户或设备上升到2015年。

Apple said that after the government began including more than 100 accounts in some subpoenas, as it did in the leak investigation in 2018, it asked law enforcement to limit requests to 25 accounts each. Police did not always comply, the company said.

Apple表示，在政府开始在某些子公司中占100多个账户后，在2018年的泄露调查中，它要求执法限制每项申请25个账户。该公司表示，警方并不总是遵守。

Apple has often challenged subpoenas that included so many accounts because they were too broad, said a former senior lawyer for the company, who spoke on the condition of confidentiality. This person said that it would not have been surprising for Apple to challenge the 2018 Justice Department subpoena but that whether a request is challenged often depends on whether a paralegal handling the subpoena elevates it to more senior lawyers.

苹果公司经常挑战子公司，其中包括这么多的账户，因为他们太广泛了，这是该公司的前高级律师，他谈到机密性条件。这个人表示，苹果挑战2018年司法部的传票并不令人惊讶，但是一个要求是否受到挑战，往往取决于律师签名是否会将其提升到更多高级律师。

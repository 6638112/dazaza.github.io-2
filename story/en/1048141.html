Steam developer gets banned for “Very Positive” review trickery
Steam开发人员因“非常积极”的审查技巧而被禁止

2021-02-18 06:35:37

Here at Ars, we've covered Valve banning Steam game developers for everything from sexual content and gratuitous ultraviolence to ill-defined "trolling". But we've never before seen a case where a developer got kicked off of Steam just because of its (non-infringing) name. That's just what happened to Emoji Evolution developer Very Positive, which said on Twitter Saturday that its developer account had been banned for "review manipulations." Unlike other prominent examples of Steam user-review manipulation, though, Very Positive didn't do anything to unduly skew the reviews players posted for its games.

在Ars，我们涵盖了Valve禁止Steam游戏开发人员进行的一切活动，从色情内容和免费的超暴力行为到定义不明确的＆＃34;拖钓＆＃34;。但是我们之前从未见过这样的情况：开发人员因为其（非侵权）名称而被Steam开除。这就是Emoji Evolution开发人员Very Positive发生的事情，该公司周六在Twitter上表示，其开发人员帐户已被禁止进行审查操作。但是，与Steam使用者评论操纵的其他突出示例不同，“非常肯定”并没有采取任何措施来过分歪曲玩家为其游戏发布的评论。

Instead, Very Positive exploited a vagary of the Steam store's user interface. That interface displays a game's developer and publisher name in the same font, color, and general area as the written summary of that game's overall user review summary (e.g., "Overwhelmingly Positive," "Mixed," "Mostly Negative," etc.) Thus, it was hard for users to distinguish at a glance that the "Very Positive" developer name wasn't an accurate summary of  Emoji Evolution's actual user reviews (which ranged from "Mixed" to "Mostly Positive" according to screenshots). Simon Carless was among the first to notice this bit of trickery, writing about it in his GameDiscoverCo newsletter on February 8. Over the next few days, word of Very Positive's existence and actions spread among gaming news sites and social media. By February 12, the developer had been banned from Steam.

取而代之的是，Very Positive利用了Steam商店用户界面的一个变体。该界面以与该游戏总体用户评论摘要的书面摘要相同的字体，颜色和一般区域显示游戏的开发者和发行者名称（例如，压倒性的肯定， 34;＆＃34;＆＃34;＆＃34;＆＃34;＆＃34;＆＃34;等。）因此，用户很难一眼就分辨出＆＃34;非常积极＆＃34;开发者名称不是Emoji Evolution实际用户评论的准确摘要（根据屏幕截图，其范围从``混合''到``最积极''）。西蒙·卡利斯（Simon Carless）是最早注意到这种骗术的人之一，他在2月8日的GameDiscoverCo时事通讯中对此进行了报道。在接下来的几天中，非常积极的存在和行动的消息在游戏新闻网站和社交媒体中流传开来。到2月12日，开发人员已被禁止使用Steam。

"I knew that reviews have a huge impact on the customer's decision," the coder behind Very Positive (who goes by the pseudonym Mike) told Vice. "I noticed that the publisher/developer name is located really close to the reviews and has the same color, and I decided to use it for my purposes." Steam users, Mike said, "make conclusions about information when seeing familiar words and don't spend much time reading all the words."

我知道评论会对客户的决定产生重大影响，非常肯定（由化名麦克带走）背后的编码员告诉Vice。 ＆＃34;我注意到发布者/开发者的名字确实位于评论附近，并且具有相同的颜色，因此我决定将其用于我的目的。迈克说，Steam用户在看到熟悉的单词时可以得出有关信息的结论，而不必花费大量时间阅读所有单词。

Mike seems to be taking the ban in stride, promoting memes and jokey polls about the saga on Twitter. Even before the Steam ban, the Very Positive account  tweeted that "to be honest the Developer and Publisher name is the best thing in the whole Emoji Evolution project," acknowledging the bargain-basement simplicity of the game itself.

迈克似乎正在大步走下禁令，在Twitter上宣传有关传奇的模因和笑话。甚至在Steam禁令发布之前，Very Positive帐户就发推文说，老实说，开发人员和发行人的名字是整个Emoji Evolution项目中最好的事情，＆＃34;承认游戏本身的廉价基础。

"I've made a really bad game - this is the only thing I'm guilty of," Very Positive cheekily tweeted on Wednesday. "If making awful games is not allowed on Steam, why haven't they already suspended the CDPR account?" ( Zing!)

我做了一个非常糟糕的游戏-这是我唯一有罪的事情，＆＃34;非常正面周三发表推文。 ＆＃34;如果Steam上不允许制作糟糕的游戏，为什么他们还没有暂停CDPR帐户？ （Z！）

In the end, Very Positive tried to exploit a small attention glitch in Steam's byzantine store system and failed in part because too many people gave it attention. Going forward, though, we can't help but think a UI change on Valve's part might be more effective (and easy to implement) than policing individual developer names for looking like "fake" review summaries. Or, as Twitter user DoctorWyrm put it (in a tweet retweeted by Very Positive), "Maybe Valve should fix their easily exploitable review system instead of just banning developers."

最后，Very Positive试图在Steam的拜占庭式商店系统中利用很小的注意力毛刺，但失败的部分原因是太多的人给予了它注意力。不过，展望未来，我们不禁要想，在Valve的角色上进行UI更改可能比管制单个开发人员的姓名看起来像假的更有效（且易于实现）。查看摘要。或者，就像Twitter用户DoctorWyrm所说的那样（在Very Positive转推的推文中），也许Valve应该修复他们易于利用的审查系统，而不是仅仅禁止开发者。

Norman Abramson, Pioneer Behind Wireless Networks, Dies At 88
无线网络的先驱Norman Abramson去世，享年88岁

2020-12-12 09:24:33

Norman Abramson, the leader of a group of scientists and engineers who pioneered the development of wireless computer networks, died on Dec. 1 at his home in San Francisco. He was 88.

诺曼·艾布拉姆森（Norman Abramson）是率先开发无线计算机网络的一组科学家和工程师的领导人，他于12月1日在他位于旧金山的家中去世。他当时88岁。

The cause was skin cancer that had metastasized to his lungs, his son, Mark, said.

他的儿子马克说，原因是皮肤癌转移到了肺部。

Professor Abramson’s project at the University of Hawaii was originally designed to transmit data to schools on the far-flung Hawaiian islands by means of a radio channel. But the solution he and his group devised in the late 1960s and early ’70s would prove widely applicable; some of their technology is still in use in today’s smartphones, satellites and home WiFi networks.

夏威夷大学的艾布拉姆森教授的项目最初旨在通过无线电信道将数据传输到遥远的夏威夷群岛上的学校。但是，他和他的小组在1960年代末和70年代初设计的解决方案将被广泛应用。他们的某些技术仍在当今的智能手机，卫星和家庭WiFi网络中使用。

The technology they created allowed many digital devices to send and receive data over that shared radio channel. It was a simple approach that did not require complex scheduling of when each packet of data would be sent. If a data packet was not received, it was simply sent again. The approach was a departure from telecommunications practices at the time, but it worked.

他们创建的技术允许许多数字设备通过该共享无线电信道发送和接收数据。这是一种简单的方法，不需要复杂的计划何时发送每个数据包。如果未收到数据包，则只需再次发送即可。该方法当时与电信实践背道而驰，但行之有效。

“It was an incredibly audacious idea, real out-of-the box engineering,” said Vinton Cerf, a computer scientist at Google and the co-author, with Robert Kahn, of the technical standards for linking computer networks on the internet.

谷歌的计算机科学家，与罗伯特·卡恩（Robert Kahn）合着的关于互联网上连接计算机网络的技术标准说：“这是一个非常大胆的想法，是真正的开箱即用的工程。”

The wireless network in Hawaii, which began operating in 1971, was called ALOHAnet, embracing the Hawaiian salutation for greeting or parting. It was a smaller, wireless version of the better known ARPAnet, the precursor to the internet, which allowed researchers at universities to share a network and send messages over landlines. The ARPAnet was led by the Pentagon’s Advanced Research Projects Agency, which also funded the ALOHAnet.

夏威夷的无线网络于1971年开始运行，被称为ALOHAnet，它拥抱夏威夷的问候语或问候语。它是众所周知的ARPAnet（互联网的前身）的较小的无线版本，它使大学的研究人员可以共享网络并通过固定电话发送消息。五角大楼的高级研究计划局（ARPAnet）由五角大楼的高级研究计划局领导，该机构也资助了ALOHAnet。

“The early wireless work in Hawaii is vastly underappreciated,” said Marc Weber, an internet historian at the Computer History Museum in Mountain View, Calif. “Every modern form of wireless data networking, from WiFi to your cellphone, goes back to the ALOHAnet.”

加利福尼亚山景城计算机历史博物馆的互联网历史学家马克·韦伯说：“夏威夷的早期无线工作被大大低估了。从WiFi到手机的各种现代形式的无线数据网络都可以追溯到ALOHAnet 。”

Professor Abramson has been called the father of wireless networking. But it was a shared paternity. The project included graduate students and several faculty members, notably Frank Kuo, a former Bell Labs scientist who came to the University of Hawaii in 1966, the same year Professor Abramson arrived.

艾布拉姆森教授被称为无线网络之父。但这是一个共同的父子关系。该项目包括研究生和几位教职员工，特别是前贝尔实验室的科学家弗兰克·郭（Frank Kuo），他于1966年来到夏威夷大学，同年艾布拉姆森教授到达。

His deepest expertise was in communication theory, the subject of his Ph.D. thesis at Stanford University. The fundamental design ideas behind ALOHAnet were his. In a 2018 oral history interview for the Computer History Museum, Professor Kuo recalled, “Norm was the theory and I was the implementer, and so we worked together pretty well.”

他最擅长的领域是通讯理论，即博士学位。斯坦福大学的论文。 ALOHAnet背后的基本设计思想是他的。郭教授在2018年对计算机历史博物馆的口述历史采访中回忆说：“规范是理论，我是实施者，因此我们在一起工作得很好。”

ALOHAnet owed a lot to surfing. Professor Abramson was presenting a paper at an academic conference in Tokyo in the days when flights from San Francisco to Tokyo had to stop midway in Honolulu. Professor Abramson, who was raised in Boston, had not been to Hawaii before and decided to spend a few days there on the way home.

ALOHAnet归功于冲浪。从旧金山到东京的航班不得不在火奴鲁鲁中途停止的那几天，艾布拉姆森教授在东京的一次学术会议上发表论文。在波士顿长大的艾布拉姆森教授以前没去过夏威夷，因此决定在那儿待几天。

He rented a surfboard. “I got on, I learned how to surf, and I said, Boy, I could stand some of this,” he recalled in 2013 in an oral history interview with the Computer History Museum.

他租了一个冲浪板。 “我上了车，学会了冲浪，然后我说，男孩，我能忍受其中的一部分，”他在2013年对计算机历史博物馆的一次口述历史采访中回忆道。

Within a year, after the University of Hawaii offered him a tenured professorship, he and his family moved to Hawaii. “My father was really wrapped up in his work, but he surfed nearly every day,” Mark Abramson said.

在不到一年的时间内，夏威夷大学为他提供了终身教授职位，他和他的家人搬到了夏威夷。马克·艾布拉姆森（Mark Abramson）说：“我父亲的工作真的很忙，但他几乎每天都冲浪。”

That the ALOHAnet technology became so widely used was partly because Professor Abramson and his team had shared it freely and welcomed other scientists to Hawaii.

ALOHAnet技术得到如此广泛的应用，部分原因是Abramson教授及其团队免费共享了该技术，并欢迎其他科学家来到夏威夷。

“We had done no patenting, and ALOHA was published in scientific papers,” putting their work in the public domain, Professor Abramson said in the oral history, adding: “And that was fine with me. I was too busy surfing to worry about that sort of thing.”

艾布拉姆森教授在口述历史中说：“我们没有申请专利，并且ALOHA已发表在科学论文上，”他们的工作进入了公共领域，并补充说：“这对我来说很好。我太忙于上网，不必担心这种事情。”

Norman Manuel Abramson was born in Boston on April 1, 1932, to Edward and Esther Abramson. His father was a commercial photographer, his mother a homemaker. Norman and his sister, Harriet, grew up in the Dorchester neighborhood, home to mostly Jewish immigrants, like his parents, at the time. His father was from Lithuania, his mother from Ukraine.

诺曼·曼努埃尔·艾布拉姆森（Norman Manuel Abramson）于1932年4月1日出生于波士顿，分别是爱德华和埃丝特·艾布拉姆森。父亲是商业摄影师，母亲是家庭主妇。诺曼和他的妹妹哈丽雅特（Harriet）在多切斯特（Dorchester）附近长大，当时是大多数犹太移民的家园，就像他的父母一样。他的父亲来自立陶宛，母亲来自乌克兰。

Norman was educated in Boston’s public schools, at the elite Boston Latin School and the English High School, where he excelled in math and science. He went on to Harvard University, where he took a course taught by Howard Aiken, a mathematician and early pioneer in computing. It was a computer course long before the discipline of computer science existed, and he enjoyed his first taste of programming.

诺曼在波士顿的公立学校，波士顿精英拉丁学校和英语高中接受教育，在那里他在数学和科学方面表现出色。他进入了哈佛大学，在那里他参加了由数学家和早期计算先驱Howard Aiken教授的课程。这是一门计算机课程，远远早于计算机科学学科的存在，他喜欢编程的初衷。

Professor Abramson majored in physics at Harvard, then earned a master’s degree in physics from the University of California, Los Angeles, and his doctorate in electrical engineering from Stanford, in 1958. He briefly worked in industry and had postdoctoral teaching stints before he went to Hawaii. He retired from the University of Hawaii in 1994.

艾布拉姆森教授在哈佛大学主修物理学，然后于1958年在加利福尼亚大学洛杉矶分校获得物理学硕士学位，并在斯坦福大学获得了电气工程博士学位。在进入工业界之前，他曾短暂地从事过博士后的研究工作。夏威夷他于1994年从夏威夷大学退休。

In addition to his son, Mark, he is survived by his wife, Joan Abramson; his sister, Harriet Schannon; and three grandchildren. His daughter, Carin Wethington, died in 2014.

除儿子马克外，他的妻子琼·艾伯拉姆森还活着。他的妹妹哈丽雅特·尚农（Harriet Schannon）；和三个孙子。他的女儿卡林·威辛顿（Carin Wethington）于2014年去世。

Some of the data-networking techniques developed by Professor Abramson and his Hawaii team proved valuable not only in wireless communications but also in wired networks. One heir to his work was Robert Metcalfe, who in 1973 was a young computer scientist working at Xerox PARC, a Silicon Valley research laboratory that had become a fount of personal computer innovations.

艾布拉姆森教授和他的夏威夷团队开发的一些数据网络技术不仅在无线通信中而且在有线网络中都被证明是有价值的。罗伯特·梅特卡夫（Robert Metcalfe）是他工作的继承人，他于1973年是硅谷研究实验室Xerox PARC的年轻计算机科学家，该实验室已成为个人计算机创新的源头。

Mr. Metcalfe was working on how to enable personal computers to share data over wired office networks. He had read a 1970 paper, written by Professor Abramson, describing ALOHAnet’s method for transmitting and resending data over a network.

梅特卡夫先生正在研究如何使个人计算机能够通过有线办公网络共享数据。他阅读了Abramson教授在1970年发表的一篇论文，该论文描述了ALOHAnet通过网络传输和重新发送数据的方法。

“Norm kindly invited me to spend a month with him at the University of Hawaii to study ALOHAnet,” Mr. Metcalfe recalled in an email.

梅特卡夫在一封电子邮件中回忆说：“诺姆（Norm）诚挚地邀请我在夏威夷大学与他一起学习ALOHAnet。”

Mr. Metcalfe and his colleagues at Xerox PARC adopted and tweaked the ALOHAnet technology in creating Ethernet office networking. Later, Mr. Metcalfe founded an Ethernet company, 3Com, which thrived as the personal computer industry grew.

Xerox PARC的Metcalfe先生和他的同事采用并调整了ALOHAnet技术来创建以太网办公网络。 后来，梅特卡夫先生创立了一家以太网公司3Com，该公司随着个人计算机行业的发展而蓬勃发展。

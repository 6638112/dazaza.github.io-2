跨舒曼主义与增强现实--一个勇敢的新增强世界

2020-10-29 10:28:02

This post is a bundle of thoughts on the near future of Augmented Reality implementations, ways of integration, and introducing new layers of cognition. We are going to talk about AR as a way of enabling transhumanism by broadening the human cognitive and sensory toolbox. We are not going to be concerned with what is and what is not technologically possible at the moment.

这篇文章是对近期增强现实实现、集成方式以及引入新认知层次的一系列思考。我们将讨论AR作为一种通过拓宽人类认知和感官工具箱来实现跨舒曼主义的方式。我们目前不会关心什么是技术上可能的，什么是不可能的。

Have you ever taken a walk and thought about all the accessible information on animals, plants, objects, or even people? How much information is available on the internet? How many facts have you encountered and already forgotten?

你有没有散步过，想过所有关于动物、植物、物体甚至是人的可获取的信息？互联网上有多少信息？你遇到过多少已经忘记的事实？

Ancient Greeks put an active effort into training and maintaining their memorizing capabilities, and already at this stage of progress, human memory is shifting – or has shifted from remembering facts to remembering how and where to find facts. We are keeping less and less information hardcoded in our permanent memory and are in turn learning how to consume, process, and find useful data in an endless stream of information and misinformation.

古希腊人积极训练和保持他们的记忆能力，在这个进步的阶段，人类的记忆已经在转变-或者说已经从记住事实转向记住如何以及在哪里找到事实。我们在永久记忆中保存的信息越来越少，反过来，我们也在学习如何在源源不断的信息和错误信息中消费、处理和寻找有用的数据。

A technologically proficient user has access to a vast volume and spectrum of data at his/her disposal, sitting right in the pocket. The human approach to holding knowledge is evolving as we speak. Heck, it started evolving with the invention of speech. But another significant change is on the cusp of happening. Our mobile phone interfaces, while serving all the information we need, are still detached from us, having considerable latency when we need to find, show, or input data. With the introduction of AR, AI, and human-computer interfaces, everything is about to change. AR or human augmentation in general will make that data more accessible and drastically lessen the latency needed for the interaction, making the tools feel more and more as part of ourselves.

精通技术的用户可以访问他/她可以支配的海量和频谱数据，就在口袋里。就在我们说话的时候，人类持有知识的方式正在演变。见鬼，它是随着语音的发明而开始进化的。但另一个重大变化即将发生。我们的手机界面在提供我们需要的所有信息的同时，仍然与我们分离，当我们需要查找、显示或输入数据时，会有相当长的延迟。随着AR、AI和人机界面的引入，一切都将改变。一般说来，AR或人工增强将使数据更容易访问，并极大地缩短交互所需的延迟，使工具感觉越来越像我们自己的一部分。

Imagine walking down the street. You look around to observe people on the street and notifications start popping up alongside people’s heads. For one person the message says: "You have seen this person three times this week already"; for another one it states: "You have five mutual connections and common interests". Those notifications could also tell you if the person is willing to make new connections, based on previously shared data or facial microexpression analysis. Maybe we have gone too far down the rabbit hole for now, but you get the idea.

想象一下走在街上的情景。你环顾四周，观察街上的人们，通知开始在人们的头部旁边弹出。对于一个人，这条信息说：你本周已经见过这个人三次了；对于另一个人，它说：你们有五个相互联系和共同的兴趣。根据之前共享的数据或面部微表情分析，这些通知还可以告诉你这个人是否愿意建立新的联系。也许我们现在已经走得太远了，但你明白我的意思。

Merriam-Webster defines Augmented Reality as "an enhanced version of reality created by the use of technology to overlay digital information on an image of something being viewed through a device (such as a smartphone camera)". For the purposes of this post, I would like us not to limit reality to human vision alone.

梅里亚姆-韦伯斯特公司将增强现实定义为增强版的现实，即利用技术将数字信息叠加在通过设备(如智能手机摄像头)观看的某物的图像上，从而创造出增强版的现实。为了这篇文章的目的，我希望我们不要把现实仅仅局限于人类的视觉。

Merriam-Webster has no definition for Transhumanism, but Wikipedia defines it as a philosophical movement that advocates for the transformation of the human condition by developing and making widely available sophisticated technologies to greatly enhance human intellect and physiology.

梅里亚姆-韦伯斯特没有对跨舒马主义下定义，但维基百科将其定义为一场哲学运动，主张通过开发和广泛使用尖端技术来极大地提高人类的智力和生理能力，从而改变人类的状况。

The two are getting more and more intertwined as technology advances, so I would like us to redefine AR, if only for this post, as "any technology that enhances or expands the human experience of reality", or maybe even as "practical transhumanism".

随着技术的进步，这两者正变得越来越交织在一起，所以我希望我们将AR重新定义为任何增强或扩展人类对现实的体验的技术，甚至可以定义为实用的跨舒马主义，哪怕只是就这篇文章而言。

For the time being, and at least until we master the LVL 4 milestone described above, we are not creating new sensory or actuation systems, we are only hijacking the existing ones. Hi, Neuralink ;). This means that with every bit of information we gain, we are blocking the bandwidth and use for our existing IO mechanisms. We still have accidents while using mobile phones or headphones, so the question arises: how could we manage a society full of people that overlap and fully cover their field of vision and hearing? At some point, AR should enable access to data from the internet, which should be retrieved and presented without too much user effort, based on surrounding situations and context. Also, the useful data should be categorized and stored for retrieval at a later time. The interface should be able to combine relevant data and present solutions, statistics, and probabilities.

在某种程度上，AR应该能够访问来自互联网的数据，根据周围的情况和上下文，这些数据应该在不需要太多用户努力的情况下检索和呈现。此外，还应对有用的数据进行分类和存储，以备以后检索。该界面应该能够结合相关数据并提供解决方案、统计数据和概率。

This could also enable saving verifiable event recordings, snapshots, and transcripts, real-time language translation, or seeing blueprints and instructions while fixing devices.

这还可以保存可验证的事件记录、快照和文字记录、实时语言翻译，或者在修复设备时查看蓝图和说明。

The human sensory reach is already drastically extended with the use of IoT and the internet in general, but there is so much more of existing tech to be integrated into an AR system to extend human senses. Users could see and feel if their home or possessions are safe, or more generally, sense physical-world information streams in real-time. For example, physical-world data can be processed in parallel with the user experiencing the immediate surroundings. Thus, the user would, for instance, return home and detect certain objects have been moved, etc.

总体上，随着物联网和互联网的使用，人类的感觉范围已经大幅扩展，但有如此多的现有技术需要整合到AR系统中，以扩展人类的感觉。用户可以看到并感觉到他们的家或财产是否安全，或者更广泛地说，可以实时感知物理世界的信息流。例如，物理世界的数据可以与体验直接环境的用户并行处理。因此，用户将例如返回家中并检测到某些对象已被移动等。

Besides our physical reach, most of us have already experienced controlling or affecting the physical-world at a distance. AR could help make the world around us feel more like an extension of our physical bodies. We could run automated processes upon visual triggers, i.e. unlock the front door without thinking, or control devices, drones, and robotics with ease.

除了我们的身体范围之外，我们中的大多数人都已经经历过远距离控制或影响物质世界的经历。增强现实可以让我们周围的世界感觉更像是我们身体的延伸。我们可以在视觉触发器上运行自动化过程，比如不假思索地打开前门，或者轻松地控制设备、无人机和机器人。

We could socialize, play games, or work like never before: imagine coding while sharing your thought process with a colleague, and also having an "AI" assisted IDE.

我们可以以前所未有的方式进行社交、玩游戏或工作：想象一下，一边编码，一边与同事分享您的思维过程，同时还拥有一个人工智能辅助的集成开发环境(IDE)。

人工智能或通用人工智能，如果可用，也可以用作可用的认知工具箱的中介，为最终用户提供必要的工具和结果。

I won’t go into great detail here, VR games are already immersive and fun, but try to imagine what they would be like if some freedom is added to the mix. As games and entertainment are presumably most likely to require as much immersion as possible, it will be necessary to consider immersion safety.

我不会在这里详述，VR游戏已经让人身临其境，而且很有趣，但试着想象一下，如果在组合中增加一些自由，它们会是什么样子。由于游戏和娱乐想必最有可能需要尽可能多的沉浸，因此有必要考虑沉浸安全。

Cars, stairs, sudden drops, and obstacles are here to stay, so for any device that can write over your sensory input, a way to risk mitigation must be in place.

汽车、楼梯、突然坠落和障碍物都会留在这里，所以对于任何可以覆盖你的感官输入的设备来说，必须有一种降低风险的方法。

Imagine you want to play a full-on VR game somewhere. How could you stay safe and not trip over something or fall over some curb? It is simple, you could just walk around your playground to define its contours and limits. A certain amount of space can even be taken out of the outer limits of the playground, any obstacles are reproduced in-game so you can navigate past them. You start the game and play, and in case you move to the outer limits, the game simply starts to fade away.

想象一下，你想在某个地方玩一款全功能的虚拟现实游戏。你怎么能保证安全而不被什么东西绊倒或者从路边跌倒呢？这很简单，你只需绕着你的操场走一圈，就可以定义它的轮廓和界限。一定的空间甚至可以从操场的外部界限中移出，任何障碍物都会在游戏中重现，这样你就可以导航通过它们。你开始玩游戏，如果你移动到外部极限，游戏就会开始淡出。

OK, but where else could you use 100% augmentation opacity in the real world? The first thing that comes to mind as safe would be walls. You can project stuff in front of almost any wall and be sure that nothing you can’t see will hit you from that direction, or at least as sure as you are now.

好的，但是在现实世界中，你还能在哪里使用100%的增强不透明度呢？首先想到的安全的东西是墙。你可以将东西投射到几乎任何一面墙的前面，并确保没有什么你看不到的东西会从那个方向击中你，或者至少像你现在这样确定。

Another useful feature would be to have the immersive content stop when quiet time or rest is needed, as well as if stress levels are critical.

另一个有用的功能是，当需要安静时间或休息，以及压力水平非常严重时，让身临其境的内容停止。

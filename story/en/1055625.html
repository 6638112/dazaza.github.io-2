Geoengineering Researchers Have Halted Plans For a Balloon Launch in Sweden
Geoengineering研究人员已经停止了瑞典气球推出的计划

2021-04-02 04:42:42

In an unexpected move, the advisory committee for a Harvard University geoengineering research project is recommending that the team suspend plans for its first balloon flight in Sweden this summer.

在一个意外的举动中，哈佛大学地理工程研究项目的咨询委员会推荐该团队今年夏天暂停其在瑞典的第一个气球航班的计划。

The purpose of that initial flight was to evaluate the propelled balloon's equipment and software in the stratosphere. In subsequent launches, the researchers hope to release small amounts of particles to better understand the risks and potential of solar geoengineering, the controversial concept of spraying sulfates, calcium carbonate or other compounds above the Earth to scatter sunlight and ease global warming. These would mark the first geoengineering-related experiments conducted in the stratosphere.

最初的航班的目的是评估流层中推进的气球和＃39;设备和软件。在随后的推出中，研究人员希望释放少量粒子以更好地了解太阳能地理工程的风险和潜力，喷洒硫酸盐，碳酸钙或地球上方其他化合物的争议概念散射阳光，缓解全球变暖。这些将标志着在平流层中进行的第一个与地理化相关的实验。

But the committee has determined that the researchers should hold off on even the preliminary equipment tests until they’ve held discussions with members of the public in Sweden. David Keith, a Harvard climate scientist and member of the research team, said they will abide by the recommendations.

但委员会确定，即使在瑞典与公众成员讨论之前，研究人员也应该暂停初步装备测试。哈佛气候科学家和研究团队成员的大卫基特表示，他们将遵守该建议。

The decision is likely to push the launch into 2022, further delaying a project initially slated to begin as early as 2018. It also opens up the possibility that the initial flights will occur elsewhere, as the researchers had selected the Esrange Space Center in Kiruna, Sweden in part because the Swedish Space Corporation could accommodate a launch this year.

该决定可能会将发射推向2022，进一步延迟一个最初的项目早期开始于2018年开始。它还开辟了最初航班的可能性，因为研究人员选择了Kiruna的Esrange Space Center，瑞典部分原因是瑞典航天公司今年可以容纳推出。

Moreover, that company said in its own statement that it decided not to conduct the flights as well, following recent conversations with geoengineering experts, the advisory board and other stakeholders.

此外，该公司在其自己的声明中表示，它在最近与地理工程专家，咨询委员会和其他利益攸关方的谈话之后，它也决定不进行航班。

Harvard set up the advisory committee in 2019 to review the proposed experiments and ensure the researchers take appropriate steps to limit risks, seek outside input, and operate in a transparent manner.

哈佛在2019年建立了咨询委员会，审查拟议的实验，并确保研究人员采取适当的措施来限制风险，寻求外部投入，以透明的方式运作。

In a statement, the committee said it has begun the process of working with public engagement specialists in Sweden and looking for organizations to host conversations.

在一份声明中，委员会表示，它已经开始与瑞典公开订婚专家合作，并寻找组织进行讨论。

“This engagement would help the committee understand Swedish and Indigenous perspectives and make an informed and responsive recommendation about the equipment test flights in Sweden,” the committee said. “The engagement in Sweden would also contribute to the committee’s deliberations regarding the proposed particle release flights and contribute to a growing body of research and practice about public governance of geoscience research.”

委员会说：“这笔订婚将有助于委员会了解瑞典和土着观点，并对有关瑞典设备测试航班进行知情和敏感的建议。” “瑞典的参与也会有助于委员会关于拟议的粒子发布航班的审议，并有助于对地球科学研究的公共治理的越来越多的研究和实践。”

Frank Keutsch, principal investigator on the research project, said in a statement that the team: "fully supports the Advisory Committee's recommendation that any equipment test flights in Sweden need to be suspended until the committee can make a final recommendation about those flights based on robust public engagement in Sweden that is broadly inclusive of indigenous populations. Our research team intends to listen closely to this public engagement process to inform the experiment moving forward."

In recent weeks, several environmental groups and geoengineering critics have called on Swedish government officials and the heads of the Swedish Space Corporation to halt the project.

最近几周，瑞典政府官员和瑞典航天公司的负责人呼吁瑞典政府官员和瑞典航天公司的首脑停止该项目。

Solar geoengineering “is a technology with the potential for extreme consequences, and stands out as dangerous, unpredictable, and unmanageable,” read a letter issued by Greenpeace Sweden, Biofuelwatch, and other organizations. “There is no justification for testing and experimenting with technology that seems to be too dangerous to ever be used.”

太阳能GeoEngineering“是一种具有极端后果潜力的技术，并脱颖而出，危险，不可预测和无法管理，”阅读由GreenPeace Sweden，BiofuelWatch和其他组织发出的信件。 “没有理由测试和试验技术似乎无法使用太危险。”

In February, I published a feature story exploring what the Harvard researchers hope to learn from the experiments.

“My view is actually very strongly that I seriously hope we’ll never get in a situation where this actually has to be done, because I still think this is a very scary concept and something will go wrong," Keutsch told me.

“我的观点实际上非常强烈，我认真希望我们永远不会在实际完成的情况下进入这种情况，因为我仍然认为这是一个非常可怕的概念，有些东西会出错，＆＃34; Keutsch告诉我。

“But at the same time, I think better understanding what the risks may be is very important,” he added. “And I think for the direct research I’m most interested in, if there is a type of material that can significantly reduce [climate change] risks, I do think we should know about this.”

“但与此同时，我认为更好地了解风险可能是非常重要的，”他补充道。 “我认为对于直接研究我最感兴趣，如果有一种可以显着减少[气候变化]风险的材料，我认为我们应该了解这一点。”

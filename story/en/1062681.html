When MTV debuted 40 years ago, everyone thought it would fail
当MTV在40年前借鉴时，每个人都认为它会失败

2021-05-14 23:56:18

Some products, in hindsight, seem like they were immediately destined for success. Take dishwashers, automobiles, or light bulbs—each created a demand for a product that hadn’t existed before, except in inferior incarnations. Once people knew about these significant upgrades in convenience, speed, or reliability, success and profit was assured.

一些产品，在后似乎似乎是他们立即注定成功。采取洗碗机，汽车或灯泡 - 每次都会为之前的产品创造了一个产品，除了较差的化身。一旦人们在方便，速度或可靠性方面知道这些重大升级，确保了成功和利润。

On August 1, MTV celebrates the 40th anniversary of its launch, and given the channel’s worldwide brand ubiquity and its history of making stars of anyone from to Cyndi Lauper to Puck to Rob Dyrdek, it’s easy to imagine the channel had a liquid-hydrogen-fueled launch attended by celebs, photographed by paparazzi, and hailed by Wall Street.

在8月1日，MTV庆祝其发射40周年，鉴于渠道的全球品牌普遍存在及其历史，使任何人的星星从Cyndi Lauper发布到罗伯德德克，很容易想象通道有液体氢气 - Celebs的推动推动推出的发射，由Paparazzi拍摄，并被华尔街所在。

But MTV’s creation isn’t a Social Network tale of Ivy League boffins on track for greatness; it’s more like The Bad News Bears, a tale of scrappy underdogs. MTV’s launch prompted skepticism, if not indifference or mockery, from the music, advertising, and television industries. Prior to the launch, only a few dozen people believed the network would succeed, and all of them worked there.

但是MTV的创作不是常春藤联盟Boffins的社交网络故事，以追踪伟大;它更像是坏消息熊，这是一个陷入困境的失败者。 MTV的发射促使怀疑主义，如果不是漠不关心或嘲弄，来自音乐，广告和电视行业。在发布之前，只有几十人相信网络会成功，所有这些都在那里工作。

MTV usually doesn’t acknowledge its own anniversaries, because like a middle-aged actor, it doesn’t want to admit its age. But for lots of people born between 1960 and 1980, a 40th anniversary brings back delightful memories of ridiculous haircuts, tight spandex, and outrageous spectacle. The Grammy Museum Mississippi is even marking the anniversary with an exhibit, “MTV Turns Forty: I Still Want My MTV,” that opens May 14, continues through the summer of 2022, and includes such mementos as Madonna’s dress in the “Vogue” video, Michael Jackson’s “Dirty Diana” leather suit, and Bret Michaels’s acoustic guitar from “Every Rose Has Its Thorn.”

MTV通常不会承认自己的周年纪念日，因为就像一个中年演员一样，它不想承认其年龄。但对于1960年至1980年间出生的很多人来说，40周年纪念日带回了荒谬的发型，紧张的氨纶和令人愤慨的奇观的令人愉快的回忆。 Mississippi格莱美博物馆甚至标志着展览的周年纪念日，“MTV变成四十：我仍然想要我的MTV，”开放5月14日，在2022年夏天继续，并包括麦当娜的衣服在“Vogue”视频中的纪念品迈克尔杰克逊的“肮脏的戴安娜”皮革套装，以及“每朵玫瑰的巨型声学吉他有它的荆棘。”

MTV’s startup staff were mostly young misfits with little or no TV experience. The senior staff included two one-eyed executives who were later hailed as visionaries, and another who’d previously run nothing except an import-export business in Afghanistan and India. The junior staff were given responsibilities they weren’t nearly qualified for, told to figure it out, and succeeded by not knowing the rules that supposedly constrained them.

MTV的初创员工大多是年轻人的不足，很少或没有电视体验。高级员工包括两个以后被誉为Visionaries的一个单眼高管，另一个在阿富汗和印度的进出口业务以外的另一个人以前没有任何运作。初级工作人员有责任，他们并没有近乎合格，告诉据说，并不知道据说限制了他们的规则。

“We were lean and mean and didn’t know what the hell we were doing,” Tom Freston said in I Want My MTV: The Uncensored Story of the Music Video Revolution, a 2011 book I wrote with Craig Marks. The staff worked out of cramped rooms at the Sheraton in midtown Manhattan, which smelled like pastrami, because they were above the Carnegie Deli. “My first office,” said Freston, the former import-export mogul who would go on to become the CEO of Viacom, “was a soda storeroom.”

“我们是瘦，卑鄙，不知道我们所做的到底是什么，”汤姆·弗雷特在我想要我的MTV：2011年关于我用克拉格的书写的音乐视频革命的未经审查的故事。工作人员在曼哈顿中城的喜来登横渡的房间里闻起来，闻起来像Pastami，因为他们在卡内基熟食店之上。 “我的第一个办公室，”佩雷斯顿说，前进出口莫尔·佩雷尔·普罗姆，“是苏打储藏室的首席执行官。”

MTV employees had to shlep across the George Washington Bridge to Fort Lee, New Jersey, to see the midnight launch, because no cable operator in Manhattan would agree to carry MTV. The network had somewhere between 150 and 250 videos—no one remembers exactly—and with 24 hours to fill every day because they had no advertisers, the channel had no choice but to play a few of the more popular videos (including such non-household names as Bootcamp, Shoes, Rupert Hine, Fischer-Z, jazz guitarist Lee Ritenour, and Blotto, a band who were all but unknown outside their native Albany, New York) every few hours. This was great news if you were a fan of Phil Collins’s “In the Air Tonight” or “Just Between You and Me” by April Wine. More than 250 videos existed, but several record labels, including PolyGram, MCA, and Arista, refused to give copies of them to MTV—what was the point of giving away, for free, content for a network that was on a fast train to failure?

MTV员工不得不穿过乔治华盛顿桥上的新泽西州乔治华盛顿大桥，看看午夜推出，因为曼哈顿没有有线运营商会同意携带MTV。网络在150到250个视频之间进行了某处 - 没有人确切地记得 - 并且每天都有24小时填写，因为他们没有广告商，频道别无选择，只能扮演一些更流行的视频（包括这样的非家庭名称作为训练营，鞋子，rupert hine，fischer-z，爵士吉他手lee ritenour和blotto每隔几个小时都在纽约州原住民外的乐队。如果你是4月葡萄酒的菲尔柯林斯的粉丝，这是一个很棒的消息，如果你是菲尔柯林斯的“在空中”或“在你和我之间”。存在超过250个视频，但几个记录标签，包括Polygram，MCA和Arista，拒绝将其中的副本送到MTV - 免费赠送的点，以便免费赠送到快车上的网络的内容失败？

Over the years, people have claimed they saw the first hour of MTV; Pat Benatar and Chynna Phillips both told us they’d seen the first few minutes of the debut. This is likely a false memory—MTV has frequently rebroadcast the first hour, so lots of people have seen it in the last 40 years, as a repeat—because the network’s national distribution was so bad. It’s much more likely that outside of Fort Lee, New Jersey, no one at all was watching.

多年来，人们声称他们看到了MTV的第一个小时; Pat Benatar和Chynna Phillips都告诉我们他们看到了首次亮相的前几分钟。这可能是一个假的内存 - MTV经常重新广播第一个小时，所以在过去的40年里，很多人都看到了它，因为网络的国家分配太糟糕了。在李堡，新泽西州堡，没有人看起来更有可能看。

The few reporters who paid attention struggled to explain MTV in print, because the phrase music video didn’t exist. “The main ingredients in MTV’s programming are ‘video records’ or ‘videos’: current recordings illustrated by 3- or 4-minute videotapes,” Time magazine said, a sentence that reads as though it was run through Google Translate.

少数报告者努力努力解释MTV打印，因为短语音乐视频不存在。 “MTV编程中的主要成分是”视频记录“或”视频“：3分钟或4分钟的录像带所示的当前录像，”时间杂志表示，一个读取的句子，好像它通过谷歌翻译运行。

MTV’s business model was to sell advertising for a product someone else made and financed. It was brilliant. But they needed viewers to make it work. They sold only $500,000 of advertising in their first year, lost about $50 million, and had the temerity to ask for a lot more. Jack Schneider, the president and CEO of parent company WASEC (a joint venture between American Express and Warner Communications), gave them only $2 million, and made it clear he was running out of patience. MTV was nearly kaput.

MTV的商业模式是为其他人提供和融资的产品销售广告。那真是太棒了。但他们需要观众来使它工作。他们在第一年销售了500,000美元的广告，损失了约5000万美元，并有很大的努力要求更多。普通母公司普通士（美国快递与华纳通讯的合资企业）总裁兼首席执行官杰克·施耐德（American Contruct and Warner Communications）只给了他们200万美元，并明确了他的耐心。 MTV几乎是Kaput。

That $2 million went into an ad campaign Bob Pittman told colleagues was “our last chance”— Mick Jagger, Pete Townshend, David Bowie, and other stars appeared in a direct-to-consumer ad, bellowing, “Call your cable operator and demand your MTV.” Which kids did in such volume that even the stoniest, most conservative cable operators buckled.

这是200万美元进入AD Campaign Bob Pittman告诉同事是“我们的最后一次机会” - Mick Jagger，Pete Townshend，David Bowie和其他明星出现在直接消费广告，吼叫，“呼叫您的电缆运营商和需求。你的MTV。“哪些孩子在这样的卷中表现出最稳定，最保守的电缆运营商勾结。

That brought in viewers, which led to more advertising and fresh videos from record companies who were willing to foot the bill because airplay on MTV guaranteed album sales. The network’s timing was charmed; 1984 was one of the greatest years in pop-music history, and nearly every smash had a video. Budgets skyrocketed (the line item for cocaine was usually hidden as a handwritten catering bill), production quality improved, and clichés emerged: spaceships, motorcycles, bands posturing in brick-wall alleyways, Mad Max references, and so, so, so many dwarves. Videos spread nonnormative ideas about sex, gender, and eye makeup across the country, giving solace to gay, trans, or art school teens who didn’t see themselves represented on CNN or CBS.

这带来了观众，它导致了愿意徒步票据的纪录公司的广告和新的视频，因为MTV保证专辑销售的Airplay。网络的时机是迷人的; 1984年是流行音乐历史上最伟大的岁月之一，几乎每个粉碎都有视频。预算飙升（可卡因的线条物品通常被隐藏为手写的餐饮费用），生产质量有所改善，陈词滥调出现：宇宙飞船，摩托车，在砖墙巷道，疯狂的最大参考资料中张力，所以，如此多的矮人。视频传播了全国各地的性别，性别和眼睛构成的非动态思想，给同性恋，跨境或艺术学校青少年，他们没有看到自己代表在CNN或CBS上。

But the person who deserves the most credit for turning MTV from a stable business to a dominant one is a singer who didn’t fit the network’s format: Michael Jackson.

但是那些值得将MTV从稳定业务转向主导地位的人是一个不适合网络格式的歌手：迈克尔·杰克逊。

Decades later, MTV executives are still rankled by accusations from Rick James, David Bowie, and others that their format was racist. They insist that as soon as they looked at Jackson’s fantastic video for “Billie Jean,” they added him to the format. Jackson’s team says MTV rejected the video and played it only after his record label threatened to pull all their other videos. One fact is clear: The song had been out for two months and had reached number one on the Billboard charts before MTV began playing the video.

几十年后，MTV高管仍由Rick James，David Bowie等人的指控，以及他们的格式是种族主义的指责。他们坚持认为，一旦他们看着杰克逊的梦幻般的“Billie Jean”，他们将他添加到了格式。杰克逊的团队说，MTV拒绝了视频，只有在他的记录标签威胁到所有其他视频之后才会播放它。一个事实很清楚：这首歌已经出了两个月，在MTV开始播放视频之前，在广告牌图表上达到了第一款。

For several weeks, “Billie Jean” and “Beat It” were both in heavy rotation. Then came “Thriller,” the most elaborate (and expensive) video ever made. MTV played it every hour, and with each repeat, ratings spiked. “Michael Jackson was the reason MTV went from big to huge,” John Sykes, one of the network’s founding executives, told us.

几个星期，“Billie Jean”和“击败它”都沉重旋转。然后来“惊悚片”，最精心的（和昂贵的）视频。 MTV每小时发挥它，每次重复，评分飙升。 “迈克尔·杰克逊是MTV从大到巨大的原因，”约翰斯克斯是一家网络的创始高管告诉我们。

The monoculture was dying, fractured by an explosion in cable TV. The February 1983 finale of M*A*S*H, which drew more than 106 million viewers, was the last gasp of network TV’s hold on America. But in a landscape of reduced audiences, MTV was unique: Thanks largely to Michael Jackson, it became the singularity. Everyone watched, and the ramifications spread quickly through the record business, Hollywood, advertising, and fashion. Everything became MTV-ized.

单一型材垂死，通过有线电视的爆炸破裂。 1983年2月的M * A * S * H的决赛，它吸引了超过1.06亿观众，是网络电视持有美国的最后一个喘息。但在减少受众的景观中，MTV是独一无二的：很大程度上归人对迈克尔杰克逊，它成为奇点。每个人都在观看，并通过记录的业务，好莱坞，广告和时尚迅速传播。一切都变得了MTV-IZED。

The business model couldn’t survive: Record labels began to resent giving MTV free content, competing networks and video shows emerged, and MTV discovered that relying on Michael Jackson (or Madonna or Van Halen or whomever) to make great videos created volatility in ratings when those artists didn’t have new singles. The all-video format often put off viewers; would a Bon Jovi fan sit through a Run-DMC video, and vice versa? MTV decided they needed their programming to be stickier, which meant actual shows, not just videos: Yo! MTV Raps, the game show Remote Control, and the reality show whose success brought down the curtain on the golden age of MTV, The Real World. If MTV didn’t invent reality TV, it certainly pushed it into the mainstream, and the network quickly replaced videos with reality shows. Instead of Cyndi Lauper, we got Snooki; Michael Jackson was replaced by the Osbournes. By the early ’90s, MTV’s effect had spread so far and so thoroughly that it was no longer unique.

业务模式无法生存：录制标签开始怨恨提供MTV免费内容，竞争网络和视频节目出现，而MTV发现依靠Michael Jackson（或麦当娜或梵顿或任何人），以使巨大的视频在评级中创造了巨大的视频当那些艺术家没有新的单打。全视频格式经常推迟观众; Bon Jovi Fan会坐在运行DMC视频中，反之亦然吗？ MTV决定他们需要他们的编程是贴上的，这意味着实际显示，而不仅仅是视频：哟！ MTV Raps，游戏展示遥控器，与现实表演的成功在于MTV的黄金时代，现实世界取得了成功。如果MTV没有发明现实电视，那么它当然将其推入主流，网络快速更换了现实展示的视频。我们没有Cyndi Lauper，我们得到了Snooki;迈克尔杰克逊被奥巴府所取代。到了90年代初，MTV的效果到目前为止，彻底遍布，这不再是独一无二的。

TikTok reproduces MTV’s model of letting others create their programming, rewards a similar quick-cut visual energy, and like MTV, is often dismissed as a fad that’s bad for music creativity. Record labels court TikTok, as they did MTV in the mid-’80s, though they do seem to have learned their lesson, and now charge for the content. You can’t break a new artist these days, whether pop, rap, or country, without TikTok support.

Tiktok再现MTV让其他人创造他们的编程，奖励类似的快速视觉能量，就像MTV一样，通常被解雇为一个不对的音乐创造力。记录标签法庭Tiktok，因为他们在80年代中期做了MTV，尽管他们似乎已经了解到了他们的课程，现在为内容收费。这些天你不能打破一个新的艺术家，无论是流行音乐，说唱还是国家，没有Tiktok支持。

Last year, a Yahoo news headline shouted, “TikTok Is the New MTV, and It’s Time We All Get on Board.” Billie Eilish and TikTok did for each other what Michael Jackson and MTV once did. And there’s a larger, transformative similarity: The network marked the first time in decades that a new consumer product became a rock star, a development that is now common. Apple has sold more than 400 million iPods and almost 2 billion iPhones, which dwarfs the 183 million RIAA-certified albums sales of the Beatles, rock's most popular band.

去年，雅虎新闻标题大声喊道，“Tiktok是新的MTV，是我们都上船上的时候了。” Billie Eilish和Tiktok为彼此做了迈克尔·杰克逊和MTV曾经做过的事情。并且有一个更大，变革的相似性：网络三十年来第一次标志着，一个新的消费产品成为摇滚明星，这是一个现在普遍的发展。 Apple已售出超过4亿个iPod和近20亿个iPhone，其中矮人是披头士乐队的18300万Riaa认证的专辑销售，摇滚乐和＃39;最受欢迎的乐队。

But at various times, pundits have crowned MySpace, ESPN, Roblox, Rock Band, Vine, Funny or Die, and Friendster as “the new MTV,” and look at how that worked out. Bob Pittman now runs a media empire focused on a technology even older than music videos: terrestrial radio. Even Facebook, with its billions of users, could fall prey to government regulation or anti-monopoly lawsuits.

但是，在不同的时候，Pundits已经加冕了Myspace，ESPN，Roblox，摇滚乐队，葡萄，有趣或死亡，以及Friendster作为“新的MTV”，看看它是如何解决的。 Bob Pittman现在运行媒体帝国专注于甚至比音乐视频更旧的技术：地面无线电。即使是Facebook，凭借数十亿用户，可能会牺牲政府监管或反垄断诉讼。

As a wise man once reminded us, video killed the radio star. No matter how popular it is, no medium is stable or permanent. Radio was ubiquitous until TV came along. FM radio was hip until satellite radio emerged. CDs replaced vinyl, then streaming replaced CDs, then albums started to make a comeback. Snooki and the gang are returning to MTV, which is evidently out of ideas, for a new series of Jersey Shore Family Vacation in June. The half-life of a popular medium is shorter than ever these days.

作为一个聪明人曾经提醒我们，视频杀死了无线电星星。无论是多么受欢迎，没有培养基稳定或永久。广播无处不在，直到电视发布。 FM收音机是臀部，直到出现卫星收音机。 CD替换乙烯基，然后流替换CD，然后相册开始卷土重来。 Snooki和Gang正在返回MTV，这显然是在6月份的新系列泽西海岸家庭度假的想法。这些日子的流行介质的半衰期短。

And certainly, MTV isn’t “the new MTV.” Viacom (the media company that bought MTV in 1985) failed to transition to the internet era and lost control of the zeitgeist. The channel, now visually bland, seems to play an endless loop of Ridiculousness episodes. A few years ago, Tom Freston said “It breaks my heart” to look at the current state of MTV.

当然，MTV不是“新的MTV”。维亚康姆（1985年购买MTV的媒体公司）未能转向互联网时代并失去对Zeitgeist的控制。该频道现在视觉平淡，似乎扮演了一个无穷无尽的荒谬剧集。几年前，汤姆弗雷斯顿说：“它打破了我的心”来看看现行的MTV状态。

Nothing can be “the new MTV,” because 2021 is as different from 1981 as 1981 was from 1901. If there is a new singularity, it’s the Internet itself, a rabbit hole big enough for all mankind. Mass media has been replaced by niche media, and music platforms, like most of pop culture, are stratified. Only technology unites us, much as it did in a 10-year period beginning in 1981.

没有什么可以是“新的MTV”，因为2021与1981年不同，因为1981年从1901年起，如果有一个新的奇点，这是互联网本身，足以让所有人都足够大的腹部。大众媒体已被利基媒体所取代，以及像大多数流行文化一样的音乐平台都分层。只有技术界，就像在1981年开始的10年期间一样。

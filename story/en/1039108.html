A State Scientist Questioned Florida’s Virus Data. Now Her Home’s Been Raided
一位州立科学家质疑佛罗里达的病毒数据。 现在她家被突袭了

2020-12-12 00:13:48

MIAMI — The complicated story of how a Florida data scientist responsible for managing the state’s coronavirus numbers wound up with state police agents brandishing guns in her house this week began seven long months ago, when the scientist, Rebekah D. Jones, was removed from her post at the Florida Department of Health.

迈阿密-佛罗里达州数据科学家负责管理本州冠状病毒数量的复杂故事是如何发生的，这是七个月前的事，这是七个月前的事，当时这位科学家丽贝卡·D·琼斯（Rebekah D. Jones）被从她的家中搬走了，州警察在她家中挥舞着枪。在佛罗里达州卫生署任职。

Ms. Jones had helped build the statistics dashboard showing how the virus was rapidly spreading in a state that had been hesitant to mandate broad restrictions.

琼斯女士帮助建立了统计仪表板，以显示该病毒如何在一直不愿实行广泛限制的状态下迅速传播。

Two months in, Ms. Jones was sidelined and then fired for insubordination, a conflict that she said came to a head when she refused to manipulate data to show that rural counties were ready to reopen from coronavirus lockdowns. The specter of possible censorship by the administration of Gov. Ron DeSantis, a Republican allied with President Trump, exploded into the frenetic pandemic news cycle, and Ms. Jones’s defiance came to symbolize the growing questions over Florida’s handling of the pandemic.

两个月后，琼斯女士被裁员，然后因不服从被解雇。当她拒绝操纵数据以表明农村县已准备好从冠状病毒封锁中重新开放时，她说这是一个冲突。与特朗普总统结盟的共和党州长罗恩·德桑蒂斯（Ron DeSantis）的政府可能会对政府进行审查，这使该新闻进入了狂热的大流行新闻周期，琼斯（Jones）女士的蔑视开始象征着人们对佛罗里达州如何处理这一大流行病的质疑日益增加。

The arrival of state agents at her home in Tallahassee on Monday to execute a search warrant in a criminal investigation marked a new, dramatic chapter in Ms. Jones’s saga, which at its core has always returned to the same basic question: Can Floridians, who are now in the midst of another alarming rise in coronavirus infections and deaths, trust the state’s data?

周一，特工到达她在塔拉哈西的家中执行刑事调查的搜查令，标志着琼斯女士传奇中一个崭新的戏剧性篇章，而这部小说的核心始终是一个基本问题：可佛罗里达人，相信该州的数据，现在正处于冠状病毒感染和死亡人数又一次惊人的上升之中吗？

“This isn’t really unexpected,” she said of this week’s raid. “You take down a governor, he’s going to come for you. Six months ago, I was just a scientist trying to do my job.”

她在谈到本周突袭时说：“这并不是真的出乎意料。” “您罢免总督，他会为您服务。六个月前，我只是一个试图做我的工作的科学家。”

Ms. Jones’s firing in May became a national flash point as Mr. DeSantis touted Florida’s early success in battling the virus — a victory lap that turned out to be premature, given that infections and deaths later surged over the summer, and are rising again. Mr. DeSantis cast Ms. Jones as a disgruntled ex-employee who is not an epidemiologist and whose claims about a lack of data transparency were unfounded.

琼斯女士5月份的解雇事件成为全国性的热点，因为德桑蒂斯（DeSantis）吹捧佛罗里达州在对抗这种病毒方面的早期成功-鉴于感染和死亡后来在夏季激增并再次上升，因此胜利圈还为时过早。 DeSantis先生将琼斯女士裁定为心怀不满的前雇员，他不是流行病学家，并且声称缺乏数据透明性是没有根据的。

The tiff with the governor turned Ms. Jones, 31, into a cause célèbre. By June, she had built her own dashboard to rival the state’s, funded in part by donations from hundreds of thousands of newfound followers on social media.

与州长的昧使31岁的琼斯女士成为了事业。到6月，她已经建立了自己的仪表板来与州政府抗衡，其中部分资金来自社交媒体上成千上万新追随者的捐款。

Ms. Jones has spent months publicly urging health department employees to denounce what she says has been the manipulation and obfuscation of virus data to make Florida look better off than it really is. In July, she filed a formal whistle-blower complaint.

琼斯女士花了数月时间公开敦促卫生部门的员工谴责她所说的对病毒数据的处理和混淆，以使佛罗里达州的状况比实际情况更好。 7月，她提出了正式的举报人投诉。

But questions remain about why she was fired. State officials insist that her claims about hiding virus data are false. She was dismissed, they said, because she made unilateral decisions to modify the virus dashboard without approval.

但是关于她为什么被解雇的问题仍然存在。州官员坚持认为她关于隐藏病毒数据的说法是错误的。他们说，她被解雇了，因为她单方面决定未经批准就修改病毒仪表板。

“Our data is available,” Mr. DeSantis said when Ms. Jones was fired. “Our data is transparent. In fact, Dr. Birx has talked multiple times about how Florida has the absolute best data,” he added, referring to Dr. Deborah L. Birx, the head of the White House’s coronavirus response, who had praised the state dashboard when Ms. Jones was overseeing it.

“我们的数据可用，”琼斯女士被解雇时，德桑蒂斯先生说。 “我们的数据是透明的。实际上，伯克斯博士曾多次谈论佛罗里达州如何拥有绝对最佳的数据，”他补充说，指的是白宫冠状病毒应对措施负责人黛博拉·伯克斯博士（Deborah L. Birx）。琼斯正在监督。

“So any insinuation otherwise is just typical partisan narrative trying to be spun,” Mr. DeSantis concluded.

迪桑蒂斯总结说：“因此，任何暗示都是试图旋转的典型的党派叙述。”

The new dashboard Ms. Jones set up surfaces some data about virus cases that had been buried deep in PDF files on the state website and generally shows a higher number of cases than the number reported by the state. It also includes information from other agencies, such as hospitalization rates from the Agency for Health Care Administration that are not on the state dashboard.

新的仪表板琼斯女士设置了一些有关病毒案件的数据，这些数据已被该州网站上的PDF文件深埋，并且通常显示的案件数量比该州所报告的数量要多。它还包括来自其他机构的信息，例如不在州面板上的卫生保健管理局的住院率。

But hers has remained a mostly one-woman operation. Though several of her former co-workers have recently left the department, they have not spoken publicly about why they stopped working there, nor have they joined Ms. Jones’s cause.

但是她的手术仍然主要是单人手术。尽管她的几位前同事最近已离开部门，但他们尚未公开谈论为何停止在该部门工作，也未加入琼斯女士的事业。

The story took a surprising new turn on Monday morning, when agents from the Florida Department of Law Enforcement appeared at the door of Ms. Jones’s townhome.

周一早上，这个故事发生了令人惊讶的新变化，佛罗里达州执法部门的特工出现在琼斯女士的住所门口。

“Come outside!” one of the agents barked as she stepped out and put her hands up. “Outside!”

“到外面来！”当她走出并举起双手时，其中一名特工咆哮。 “外！”

The first agent stepped in and ordered Ms. Jones’s husband to come downstairs. Two other agents followed with guns drawn. One pointed his weapon upstairs, where Ms. Jones’s 11-year-old son and 2-year-old daughter had been sleeping.

第一位特工介入，命令琼斯女士的丈夫下楼。其他两名特工随后开枪。有人指出他的武器在楼上，琼斯女士11岁的儿子和2岁的女儿一直在睡觉。

The agents had come to serve a search warrant for Ms. Jones’s computers and other electronics. Ms. Jones posted a 31-second video clip of the agents’ arrival to Twitter on Monday afternoon, creating new outrage against Mr. DeSantis, who in the eyes of her supporters has continued to try to downplay the virus.

这些代理商已开始为琼斯女士的计算机和其他电子设备提供搜查令。琼斯女士在周一下午发布了特工到达Twitter的31秒视频片段，这引起了对德桑蒂斯先生的新愤慨，德桑蒂斯在支持者的眼中一直在继续淡化这种病毒。

The search was part of a criminal investigation into unauthorized messages sent last month to a group of health department employees using an internal emergency alert system.

搜查是对上个月使用内部紧急警报系统发送给一群卫生部门员工的未经授权消息进行刑事调查的一部分。

“It’s time to speak up before another 17,000 people are dead,” read the messages — approximately 1,750 of them — sent on Nov. 10. “You know this is wrong. You don’t have to be part of this. Be a hero. Speak out before it’s too late.”

11月10日发送的消息（其中约有1,750人）说：“是时候再说17,000人死亡了。”您不必参与其中。成为英雄。在为时已晚之前说出来。”

According to an affidavit supporting the search of the townhome, investigators traced the messages to Ms. Jones’s internet protocol address, a unique number sequence assigned to each computer connected to the web.

根据支持搜索联排别墅的誓章，调查人员将消息追踪到琼斯女士的互联网协议地址，该地址是分配给连接到网络的每台计算机的唯一编号序列。

She denied having anything to do with the messages. Florida had reported 17,460 coronavirus deaths at the time, and she said she would never have rounded that number down.

她否认与这些消息有任何关系。佛罗里达当时报告了17,460例冠状病毒死亡，她说她永远不会把这个数字四舍五入。

According to the affidavit, the users on the emergency alert group account shared the same username and password, which cybersecurity experts said left the system vulnerable to a breach that could be difficult to trace.

根据宣誓书，紧急警报组帐户上的用户共享相同的用户名和密码，网络安全专家说，该系统使该系统容易受到可能难以追踪的漏洞的攻击。

“That’s textbook bad security practice, and this is an example of why — it’s cumbersome to revoke access and hard to attribute actions to the responsible people,” said J. Alex Halderman, a computer science and engineering professor at the University of Michigan.

密歇根大学计算机科学与工程学教授J. Alex Halderman说：“这是教科书上不良的安全做法，这是为什么的一个例子-撤销访问权限并且很难将责任归咎于负责人。”

Stephen S. Dobson III, Ms. Jones’s criminal defense attorney, said the password to the system was written on at least three boards at the Department of Health. He suggested that Ms. Jones’s I.P. address was “spoofed,” or impersonated by someone else.

琼斯女士的刑事辩护律师斯蒂芬·多布森（Stephen S. Dobson III）说，该系统的密码至少在卫生部的三个委员会上都已写上。他建议琼斯女士的I.P.该地址被“欺骗”或被其他人冒充。

“And it is just complete overreach for F.D.L.E. to come in with guns out, pointing guns at people,” he said.

“这对F.D.L.E.拿出枪来，将枪对准人。”他说。

Within the seized electronics, which included computers, cellphones and thumb drives, was information from contacts inside the health department to whom Ms. Jones had promised confidentiality, she said.

她说，在被没收的电子设备中，包括计算机，手机和拇指驱动器，是卫生部门内部联系人提供的信息，琼斯女士曾承诺要对此保密。

The Department of Law Enforcement denied that agents pointed their weapons at anybody and released body camera footage on Thursday showing how agents repeatedly knocked on Ms. Jones’s door and called her cellphone to announce the search warrant. They wondered at first if no one was home and then, after seeing people inside, whether they would have to break the door to enter. Ms. Jones came to the door about 23 minutes after the agents arrived, they said. (Ms. Jones said it was not clear from the start whether they had a search warrant.)

执法部否认特工将武器对准任何人，并在周四发布了人体摄像机录像，显示特工如何反复敲开琼斯女士的门，并打电话给她的手机宣布搜查令。他们首先想知道是否没有人在家，然后在看到里面的人之后，是否必须打破大门才能进入。他们说，特工到达后约23分钟，琼斯女士来到门口。 （琼斯女士说，从一开始就不清楚他们是否有搜查令。）

“Agents afforded Ms. Jones ample time to come to the door and resolve this matter in a civil and professional manner,” Commissioner Richard L. Swearingen of the Department of Law Enforcement said in a statement.

执法部专员理查德·Swearingen在一份声明中说：“特工为琼斯女士提供了足够的时间来以民事和专业的方式来解决这一问题。”

A spokesman for Mr. DeSantis said the governor had no knowledge of the investigation until Ms. Jones publicized the raid.

迪桑蒂斯（DeSantis）的发言人表示，直到琼斯（Jones）女士宣布突击行动之前，州长才对调查进行了了解。

Florida publishes quite a bit of coronavirus data, but Mr. DeSantis and his administration have also contributed to the perception that the state has not been fully transparent.

佛罗里达州发布了很多冠状病毒数据，但是DeSantis先生和他的政府也促成了人们对该州尚未完全透明的看法。

Early on, state agencies refused to release information about the number of coronavirus hospitalizations and cases in long-term care facilities, and only provided it after news organizations threatened litigation. Florida still does not provide a comprehensive list of outbreaks at businesses and other public places.

早些时候，国家机构拒绝发布有关长期护理机构中冠状病毒住院人数和病例的信息，只有在新闻机构威胁要诉讼后才提供。佛罗里达州仍未提供企业和其他公共场所爆发的全面清单。

County health administrators have been limited in what they are allowed to say. The state surgeon general has made few public appearances since he acknowledged in April that social distancing and other measures would most likely have to be in place until the advent of a vaccine, for “probably a year, if not longer.”

县卫生行政人员的发言权受到限制。自从他在四月承认，在疫苗出现之前，很可能必须采取社会隔离措施和其他措施，“可能大约一年，甚至更长的时间”以来，这位州外科医生几乎没有公开露面。

But Ms. Jones has not been universally embraced as a whistle-blower. Some critics have dismissed her lack of public health training. Others have been made uncomfortable by the attention she has sought — sometimes tagging the late-night host Stephen Colbert, for example, in her Twitter posts.

但是琼斯女士并没有被普遍认为是举报人。一些批评家认为她缺乏公共卫生培训。她所寻求的关注使其他人感到不舒服-例如，有时在她的Twitter帖子中标记了深夜主持人Stephen Colbert。

Her dashboard shows a higher total number of cases than the state’s official records because it includes the number of positive antibody tests, something the Department of Health and outside epidemiologists do not recommend. Ms. Jones has reacted defensively to some of their criticism on Twitter.

她的仪表盘显示的病例总数高于该州的官方记录，因为它包括抗体阳性检测的数量，而卫生部和外部流行病学家不建议这样做。琼斯女士在推特上对他们的一些批评采取了防御性的反应。

The search warrant served this week did not represent Ms. Jones’s first brush with the law. She faced a number of criminal charges in Florida, including criminal mischief and violation of a domestic violence injunction, all involving a relationship with one of her students when she was a graduate assistant at Florida State University. None of them resulted in a conviction, but last year, Tallahassee prosecutors charged Ms. Jones with cyberstalking the man.

本周送达的搜查令并不代表琼斯女士对该法律的首次抨击。她在佛罗里达州面临多项刑事指控，包括刑事恶作剧和违反家庭暴力禁令，所有这些都涉及她在佛罗里达州立大学读研究生时与一名学生的关系。他们均未定罪，但去年，塔拉哈西检察官指控琼斯女士对该男子进行网络跟踪。

Her lawyer wrote in a court filing this week that Ms. Jones had agreed to a plea deal in that case, but prosecutors rescinded it after Monday’s raid.

她的律师在本周的法庭文件中写道，琼斯女士已经同意了该案的认罪协议，但检察官在周一的突袭行动后撤消了这一请求。

At the state health department, Ms. Jones was earning a salary of $47,999. Since her termination, she has raised more than $250,000 in a GoFundMe account created by her sister, and also founded a for-profit company that accepts undisclosed donations.

在州卫生部门，琼斯女士的薪水为47,999美元。自从她辞职以来，她已经通过姐姐创建的GoFundMe帐户筹集了超过25万美元，还成立了一家营利性公司，接受未公开的捐款。

This week, she opened a new GoFundMe account, this time to fund her legal defense. Her video of the raid has been shared more than 137,000 times, including by actors, scientists and members of Congress.

本周，她开设了一个新的GoFundMe帐户，这次是为她的法律辩护提供资金。她的突袭录像已被演员，科学家和国会议员们分享了137,000次以上。

The fund-raising appeal quickly surpassed her initial $150,000 goal. She raised her target to $1 million, then scaled it back to $500,000, saying she would need the money for bail, if she were arrested, and to take care of her family.

筹款呼吁很快超过了她最初的目标150,000美元。她将目标提高到100万美元，然后又将其缩小到50万美元，并说如果她被捕并需要保释金和照顾家人，她将需要这笔钱。

“It will also go to finding a new place to live outside of Florida so I can continue to do this important reach out of Ron DeSantis’ administration,” she said in a text message. “I can do this work from anywhere.”

她在一条短信中说：“这还将找到在佛罗里达州以外居住的新地方，这样我就可以继续在罗恩·德桑蒂斯政府的领导下取得这一重要成就。” “我可以在任何地方进行这项工作。”

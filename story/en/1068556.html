Neeva, a $4.95/month ad-free search engine from two ex-Google executives, launches after raising $77.5M
Neeva，来自两个前谷歌高管的免费搜索引擎，从两家前谷歌高管提供14.95美元/月，在筹集77.5米后发布

At first blush, it may seem like a textbook example of a startup idea destined never to get anywhere. By definition, any new search engine competes with Google, whose 90 percent-plus market share leaves little oxygen for other players. And we’ve been accustomed to getting our search for free since well before there was a Google—which might make paying for it sound like being expected to purchase a phone book.

起初暗示，它看起来像一个初学者的课本例子，它目的是从未到达任何地方的初创想法。根据定义，任何新的搜索引擎与谷歌竞争，其90％加上市场份额为其他玩家留下了很少的氧气。我们一直习惯于在有谷歌之前免费获得我们的搜索 - 这可能会为您的声音付出代价，就像预计购买电话簿一样。

But Neeva is indeed a new search engine, officially launching today, that carries a subscription fee. Though it’s extremely similar to Google in many respects—with a few twists of its own—it dumps the web giant’s venerable ad-based business model in the interest of avoiding distractions, privacy quandaries, and other compromises. It’s free for three months—long enough for users to grow accustomed to it without obligation—and $4.95 a month thereafter. Apps for iPhones and iPads, and browser extensions for Chrome, Firefox, Safari, Edge, and Brave, are part of the deal.

但Neeva确实是新的搜索引擎，今天正式启动，即提供订阅费。虽然在许多方面非常类似于谷歌 - 但是由于它自己的一些曲折，它会使网络巨头的尊重广告的业务模式避免分心，隐私酶促和其他妥协。它是免费的三个月 - 足够长的用户，以便用户习惯于毫无义务 - 此后每月4.95美元。 iPhone和iPad的应用程序以及Chrome，Firefox，Safari，Edge和Brave的浏览器扩展，是交易的一部分。

Neeva may have a certain whiff of improbability about it, but its cofounders, Sridhar Ramaswamy and Vivek Raghunathan, are the furthest thing from naïfs. Two long-time Google executives with more than a quarter-century of experience at the web giant between them, they have an insider’s understanding of how it operates. Moreover, about 30 percent of the roughly 60-person staff they’ve assembled at Neeva consists of ex-Googlers, including Hall-of-Famers such as Udi Manber (a former head of Google search) and Darin Fisher (one of the inventors of Chrome). They’ve also secured $77.5 million in funding, including investments from venture-capital titans Greylock and Sequoia.

Neeva可能对此有一定的不可能性，但它的Cofounders，Sridhar ramaswamy和Vivek Raghunathan是Naïfs最远的东西。两位长期谷歌高管在他们之间拥有超过四分之一世纪的经验，他们有一个内在人们对其运作方式的了解。此外，在NEEVA组装的大约60人工作人员中约有30％由前陀螺组成，包括来自乌迪曼伯（谷歌搜索的前负责人）和Darin Fisher（其中一位发明者之一） Chrome）。他们还获得了7750万美元的资金，包括福利 - 首都泰坦Greylock和LemoIa的投资。

At its highest level, Neeva represents a bet that the way Google monetizes search and other services through advertising—as it’s done for more than two decades to wildly profitable effect—has hampered its user experience, thereby opening up an opportunity. “I tell people that Neeva is as much a social experiment as it is a technological experiment,” says Ramaswamy, the company’s CEO. “It’s looking for the answer to the question, ‘If there was a high-quality product that clearly benefits you in multiple ways, would you pay for it as opposed to having it be free, supported by ads?'”

NEEVA在最高级别，NEEVA代表了谷歌通过广告赚取搜索和其他服务的赌注 - 因为它已经完成了超过二十年的盈利效果 - 阻碍了其用户体验，从而开放了一个机会。 “我告诉人们Neeva是一个社会实验，因为它是一种技术实验，”公司的首席执行官Ramaswamy说。 “它正在寻找问题的答案，”如果有一种高质量的产品，那么在多种方面明确地利益，你会为它付出代价，而不是因为它是免费的，由广告支持？＆＃39;“

Whatever the answer to that question, Neeva’s creators understand what they’re getting into. “Sridhar and Vivek, with their depth of knowledge on everything from technology to what people actually need and do, are probably the only people in the world where I would go, ‘Okay, I’ll go on this journey with you, because you know how to go on this journey,'” says Greylock partner and LinkedIn cofounder Reid Hoffman.

无论对这个问题的答案如何，Neeva的创造者都明白他们进入了什么。 “Sridhar和Vivek，他们对从技术到实际需要和做的事情的一切知识的深度，可能是世界上唯一的人，我会去的地方，”好的，我会和你一起去旅行，因为你知道如何继续这个旅程，＆＃39;“ Greylock Partner和Linkedin Cofounder Reid Hoffman说。

(Which is not to say there aren’t other ambitious privacy-centric search engines on journeys at least somewhat similar to Neeva’s. DuckDuckGo has been on its own for 13 years; once a one-man operation, it now has 129 employees and $100 million in annual revenue from ads that don’t involve tracking individual users. And Brave, the browser company founded by web pioneer Brendan Eich, is beta-testing its own privacy-first search engine and says free and for-pay versions will be available.)

（这并不是说没有其他雄心勃勃的隐私中心搜索引擎至少有些类似于Neeva的搜索引擎。Duckduckgo一直在自己的13年;一旦一个人的操作，它现在有129名员工和100美元来自广告的年收入中的百万，不涉及跟踪个别用户。并勇敢的是由Web先驱Brendan Eich创立的浏览器公司，是测试自己的隐私第一搜索引擎，并提供免费和付费版本。）

Looming antitrust case aside, it’s not that Google is all that obviously vulnerable. Over the years, unlike some products with monopolistic market share— Microsoft’s Internet Explorer comes to mind—the search behemoth hasn’t calcified through lack of competition. Instead, it remains the category’s  leading engine of innovation: Google says that it rolled out 4,500 improvements last year alone.

抛弃反托拉斯案，除了谷歌是明显脆弱的。多年来，与垄断市场份额的一些产品不同，微软的互联网探险家来到思想 - 搜索Beemoth没有通过缺乏竞争来钙化。相反，它仍然是该类别的创新引擎：谷歌表示，它去年单独推出4,500个改进。

But some of the downsides of Google’s dependence on ad revenues are right before our eyes. Once famously unobtrusive, the ads displayed with search results have gradually migrated from the right column to the top of search results, multiplied in number and size, and lost the shaded box that isolated them from organic results. Today, for many searches, ads all but crowd the organic results off the screen until you scroll downwards. For others, links to Google’s own money-making services, such as Google Flights, get prime real estate.

但谷歌对广告收入的一些缺点是我们的眼前。一旦着名不引人注目，搜索结果显示的广告已从右栏逐渐迁移到搜索结果的顶部，乘以数量和尺寸，并丢失了从有机结果隔离的阴影框。今天，对于许多搜索，Al Al Al Al Al Al But But But All，在屏幕上将有机成果从屏幕上向下滚动。对于其他人来说，与谷歌自己的赚钱服务有关，例如Google航班，获得Prime Real Estate。

Then there’s the fact that Google builds profiles of its users based on their online activity, the better to precisely target them with advertising not only at its own sites but all the other ones across the web whose ads are powered by Google. With no ads to serve up, Neeva shouldn’t leave privacy-conscious types feeling like they’re being monitored for ulterior purposes. (By default, Neeva does hold onto your searches for 90 days to improve the quality of features such as autosuggestions, but you can erase this log or tell the service you don’t want it to keep it in the first place.)

然后，谷歌基于在线活动的基础上构建了其用户的简档，更好地迫切地针对他们的广告，而不是在自己的网站上进行广告，而是通过谷歌推广的所有其他网络。没有广告即可为UP服务，NEEVA不应该留下隐私的类型，感觉像他们被监视的尺子目的。 （默认情况下，NEEVA确实抓住了90天的搜索，以提高自动发言等功能的质量，但您可以删除此日志或告诉服务您不希望将其保持在第一个位置。）

In another break from search-engine tradition, Neeva says that it will turn at least 20 percent of its top-line revenue over to publishing partners, including the first two it’s announced, Quora and Medium. Though the details of where this could lead remain vague, it’s another attempt to set Neeva apart from Google, which has often been accused of benefiting from media outlets’ content without adequate compensation, a long-simmering dispute that has led to lawsuits and legislation. (After years of controversy, Google can at least point to the fact that it’s spending $1 billion on programs involving giving money to news outlets.)

在搜索引擎传统的另一个休息中，NEEVA表示，它将至少将其最具额外的伙伴的最终收入的20％，包括前两个宣布，Quora和Medium。虽然这可能导致的地方仍然含糊，但它是另一次尝试将NEEVA与谷歌分开，这通常被指控从媒体网点的内容中受益，而没有充分的赔偿，这是一项导致诉讼和立法的长期酝酿的争议。 （经过多年的争议，谷歌至少可以指出它在涉及给新闻网点捐款的方案上花费了10亿美元。）

Ramaswamy and Raghunathan tend to frame their discomfort about Google’s business model in polite terms. One of Neeva’s investors—NYU professor, author, and entrepreneur Scott Galloway—isn’t so circumspect. As you might expect of someone who has called for the breakup of Google and other tech giants, he’s withering in his analysis of Google and enthusiastic about the possibility of an upstart like Neeva chipping away at its pervasiveness.

Ramaswamy和Raghunathan倾向于在礼貌的条件下造成谷歌的商业模式的不适。 Neeva的投资者之一 - Nyu教授，作者和企业家斯科特加洛韦 - 不是那么谨慎的。正如您可能希望呼吁谷歌和其他科技巨头分手的人，他在他对谷歌和热情的分析中积累了对待Neeva在其普遍存在中的Neeva削减的可能性。

“The addictive nature of search, of getting answers at your fingertips or connecting with people, that’s sort of the nicotine,” says Galloway. “It’s bad for you, it’s addictive, but it doesn’t give you cancer. The thing that gives you cancer, the tobacco, if you will, is the ad model. Because ultimately, over time, all you’re focused on is gathering more data on the consumer so that you can further monetize him or her. And where it ends up is where you are now with Google, where Google no longer takes you to the best place based on your query, but to another place they can further monetize.”

“搜索的上瘾性质，让你的指尖或与人们联系的答案，这是尼古丁的答案，”洛克莱说。 “这对你来说很糟糕，这是令人上瘾的，但它没有给你癌症。为您提供癌症的东西，烟草，如果您愿意，是广告模型。因为最终，随着时间的推移，你专注于正在收集更多关于消费者的数据，以便进一步将他或她货币化。最终的位置是您现在的谷歌，谷歌不再将您带到您的查询，但到另一个地方可以进一步货币化。“

Galloway emphasizes that he put money into Neeva mostly because he thinks it’s a smart investment. At the same time, he adds, “I want to be a small part of the effort to try and release the stranglehold that Google has on content discovery. I think it leads to very bad places.”

加洛韦强调他主要将钱放入Neeva，因为他认为这是一个聪明的投资。与此同时，他补充道，“我想成为尝试和释放谷歌对内容发现的施行的努力的一小部分。我认为它会导致非常糟糕的地方。“

Even if you’re nowhere near as agitated over Google’s dominance and practices as Galloway is, you might find yourself rooting for for what Neeva is trying to accomplish. Search “is a phenomenal daily habit that has transformed how we live,” says Neeva board member Margo Georgiadis, herself a former high-ranking Googler who ran the company’s commercial and ad operations in the U.S., Canada, and Latin America. “But there’s an opportunity with how we’ve evolved the way we live our lives and use technology to make it much, much better if your business model is aligned 100 percent with the consumer.”

即使您无处可见，在谷歌的主导地位和实践中令人振奋的是加洛韦，您也可能会发现自己为Neeva正在努力实现的目标。搜索“是一种改变我们生活的现象日常习惯，”NEEVA董事会成员MARGO GEORGIADIS说，他是一家前高级吉隆勒，他在美国，加拿大和拉丁美洲运行了公司的商业和广告业务。 “但是有机会与我们生活的生活方式发挥作用，如果您的商业模式与消费者对齐100％，我们会更好地发展到我们的生活方式。”

It’s hard to bring up ad-subsidized services from big tech companies without someone helpfully reminding you that if you’re not paying for the product, you are the product. With Neeva’s subscription-based model, the product is unquestionably the product. It just needs to be a compelling one—even though its team is small and the competition has a 23-year head start.

很难从大型技术公司提出广告补贴服务，如果您没有为您支付产品，那么如果您没有为产品支付，您就是产品。通过NEEVA的基于订阅的模型，产品毫无疑问的产品。它只需要一个引人注目的 - 即使它的团队很小，比赛有23年的脑袋。

In a way, the Neeva story begins in 2003. That’s when Ramaswamy joined Google and was put to work on its still-nascent advertising team. Placing targeted text ads next to search results was already proving to be a powerful idea, but “little did I, or most others, realize that this would be one of the largest businesses ever,” he remembers. He devoted the next fifteen years to the engineering side of that growth, eventually overseeing 10,000 Googlers as senior VP of ads and commerce.

在某种程度上，NEEVA的故事始于2003年。那是Ramaswamy加入谷歌的时候，并在其静止的广告团队上工作。将有针对性的文本广告放在搜索结果旁边已经证明是一个强大的想法，但“我的小孩或大多数其他人，意识到这将是有史以来最大的企业之一，”他记得。他将近十五年致力于该增长的工程方面，最终监督10,000名陀螺歌手作为广告和商业的高级副总裁。

But by the latter part of that time, he was developing qualms about the whole proposition of monetizing eyeballs through targeted ads all around the web, as Google was doing. “One of the things that I paid a lot of attention to is how data was separated between off-Google experiences and on-Google experiences,” he explains. “To me, keeping that data separate was an important part of the promise that Google implicitly made to its users.” Increasingly, however, he found himself having to consent to compromises such as users’ Google searches influencing the ads they saw on YouTube.

但是，由于当时的后半部分，他正在通过在网上全部围绕网络通过目标广告来制定QUARMALLY通过针对性的广告来破坏眼球的主张。 “我支付了很多关注的事情之一是数据如何在除了谷歌体验和谷歌体验之间分开，”他解释道。 “对我来说，保持数据分开是谷歌隐式对其用户所做的承诺的重要组成部分。”然而，越来越多地发现他自己必须同意妥协，例如用户的谷歌搜索影响他们在YouTube上看到的广告。

“We tried to be thoughtful about how information flowed around, but it was also very clear where things were pointed at,” Ramaswamy says. Having had his fill of the dilemmas associated with the ad business, he explored the possibility of pivoting to a different role within Google. Ultimately, “I decided that a clean start was what I needed,’ he says.

“我们试图深思地了解信息如何流动，但它也很清楚事情所指出的地方，”雷马西说。他填补了与广告业务相关的困境，他探讨了在谷歌中枢转到不同的角色。最终，“我决定一个干净的开始就是我需要的，”他说。

After quitting Google in the fall of 2018, he thought he’d found that clean start as a venture capitalist at Greylock. The conflict between ad-based business models and serving users remained on his mind, though. Thinking about it took him down the path that would lead to Neeva.

在2018年秋季退出谷歌后，他认为他发现在Greylock的风险资本家中发现干净的开始。不过，广告业务模式与服务用户之间的冲突仍然在他的脑海中。考虑到他把他带到了会导致Neeva的道路上。

Soon, he was joined in this exploration by Raghunathan, who’d had an impressive Google career of his own. In the middle of it, he’d worked on Google Now, an initiative to make search more anticipatory that later evolved into the Google Assistant. He says that he found shaping the future of search to be exhilarating, but nonetheless then “went over to the dark side, and I monetized YouTube.”

很快，他加入了拉格努纳森的这一探索，他曾令人印象深刻的谷歌职业。在其中的中间，他现在在谷歌上工作了，这是一项关于搜索更多预期的倡议，后来演变为谷歌助理。他说，他发现塑造了搜索的未来令人振奋，但仍然“走到黑暗的一面，我将你被货币化的YouTube。”

Like Ramaswamy, Raghunathan wrestled with the effect that business pressures had on user experiences. Marketers love to reinforce their messaging by exposing consumers to the same ad over and over and over. YouTube fans rankle at such repetition. Guess whose interests won out? “We’d have these well-intentioned conversations around trading off users’ happiness for customer value,” Raghunathan recounts.

像Ramaswamy一样，Raghunathan摔断了商业压力对用户体验的影响。营销人员喜欢通过将消费者暴露在同一广告中通过一遍又一遍地加强他们的消息。 YouTube粉丝在这样的重复中粉丝。猜猜谁的利益赢了？ “我们会在交易用户幸福的情况下，有这些良好的谈话，以满足客户价值，”拉吉坦坦召开。

Still at Google, Raghunathan chatted with Ramaswamy about the startup idea the latter was formulating. “I remember him telling me this insight, this string he was pulling on,” says Raghunathan. “That you could not reimagine search the product without reimagining the business model that underpins search.” Eager to join that effort, Raghunathan left Google after almost 12 years to become Neeva’s cofounder.

仍然在谷歌，拉格森纳用ramaswamy追逐后者正在制定的启动思想。 “我记得他告诉我这个洞察力，他正在拉的这个字符串，”拉格森纳森说。 “如果您无法重新旨在搜索产品，而无需重新称解基于基本搜索的商业模式。”渴望加入这一努力，拉格村在近12年后离开了谷歌成为Neeva的Cofounder。

(Why the name “Neeva?” Raghunathan cheerfully admits that they chose it, then backfilled in a rationale: “In hindsight, there is a story we can tell about Neeva, in Sanskrit, meaning ‘fundamentals’ or ‘the basics.’ It actually means something that is very core to our mission. But I’d be lying if I told you we knew that.”)

（为什么姓名“Neeva？”Raghunathan高兴地承认他们选择了它，然后在理由中回填：“在后古，有一个故事我们可以讲述Neeva，在梵语，意思是”基础知识“或”基础知识“。它实际上意味着我们使命是非常核心的东西。但如果我告诉你我们知道这一点，我会撒谎。“）

As Ramaswamy and Raghunathan recruited a team, they found that other Googlers past and present were eager to take a fresh look at search, including some whom they hadn’t known at their old company. “The kind of ideas I heard from Sridhar and Vivek really excited me,” says Bindu Pucha, who was once an IBM Research staffer, spent five years at Google, and now works on infrastructure technology for Neeva.

正如Ramaswamy和Raghunathan招募一个团队一样，他们发现其他陀螺队过去和目前渴望进行新的搜索，包括他们在旧公司尚未知道的人。 “我听到的那种想法来自Sridhar和Vivek真的很激动我，”Bindu Pucha说，曾经是IBM研究人员，在谷歌五年内花了五年，现在在Neeva的基础设施技术上工作。

For Udi Manber, what Neeva was up to was compelling enough to lure him back into search, an area he’d left behind in 2015 to focus on health-related technologies at the National Institutes of Health and elsewhere. Earlier this year, he joined Neeva part-time, spending two or three days a week on research efforts.

对于UDI Manber，Neeva令人兴奋地引起了足够的兴奋，让他回到搜索，这是他在2015年留下的一个地区，专注于国家卫生研究院和其他地方的健康有关的技术。今年早些时候，他加入了Neeva兼职，每周花费两三天，并在研究努力上。

A legendary computer scientist who held top jobs at Yahoo and Amazon as well as Google, Manber was another key Googler who had his frustrations with the dynamics of ad-subsidized search. “I was one of the biggest advocates of privacy at Google, and I pushed for it a long time,” he says. “I won some fights. I lost many others.” The fact that Neeva is tiny is liberating, he argues: “Innovation is hard for every company, but I think small companies are better at it than large companies overall.”

一个传奇的计算机科学家，在雅虎和亚马逊以及谷歌举行了顶级工作，Manber是另一个与广告补贴搜索的动态的挫折。 “我是谷歌的最大倡导者之一，我很长一段时间推动了它，”他说。 “我赢了一些战斗。我失去了许多人。“ Neeva很小的事实是解放的，他辩称：“创新对每个公司来说都很难，但我认为小公司总体而言比大公司更好。”

To say that Neeva is in a position to rethink search is not to suggest that it’s trying to change everything. What it’s launching is not the iPhone to Google’s BlackBerry. Indeed, much of the user experience can fairly be described as “Google-like.”

要说NEEVA处于重新思考的位置，搜索不是表明它试图改变一切。它启动的不是iPhone到谷歌的黑莓手机。实际上，大部分用户体验都可以被公平地描述为“谷歌类似”。

That’s not a bad thing. “The paradox and the challenge here is that it has to be different enough from what already exists to get attention and to be engaging, but not so radically different that it’s disorienting to people,” says Greg Sterling, a long-time observer of the search business and VP of market insights at location marketing company Uberall. “There’s a weird tight rope that I think they’re trying to walk.”

那不是坏事。 “悖论以及这里的挑战是它必须与已经存在的东西有所不同，而且要引人注目，但并不是那么根本地不同，这对人们迷失方向，”长期观察者搜索业务和市场洞察副总裁Uberall。 “有一个奇怪的紧绳，我认为他们试图走路。”

They’re not doing it alone. Neeva has indexed several billion web pages on its own but also credits Apple, Bing, and Yelp at the bottom of search results; other information providers include Intrinio, Weather.com, and Xignite. Even Google is an ingredient, with Google Maps embedded in search results.

他们不是一个人这样做。 NEEVA在搜索结果的底部索引了几亿个网页，而且还在苹果，Bing和Yelp上索引;其他信息提供商包括Intrinio，Weather.com和XIgnite。甚至谷歌是一种成分，谷歌地图嵌入了搜索结果。

“Wherever we can build, we will build,” declares Raghunathan. “Where we can license or buy, we will. Users don’t care. And so we will do what makes sense for users to build a maximally differentiated, maximally useful, maximally, delightful product.”

“无论我们建立在哪里，我们都会建立，”raghunathan宣布。 “我们可以在哪里许可或购买，我们会。用户不在乎。因此，我们将对用户建立最大化，最大限度的，最大，令人愉快的产品有意义。“

Nothing about the end result is likely to discombobulate the Googling masses. There’s a search field that looks just like Google’s search field—except that it sports a rotating set of taglines such as “No ads here” and “You are not the product.” There are autosuggestions as you type, plus links to related searches and “People Also Asked” queries. Rows of videos and news items are interspersed throughout the results, and the right column often includes a block of facts on whatever you searched for based on sources such as Wikipedia.

关于最终结果的任何内容都可能会发现谷歌群众。有一个搜索字段看起来就像谷歌的搜索字段一样 - 除了它体育一套旋转的标记，如“这里没有广告”和“你不是产品”。与您键入的自动发言，加上与相关搜索的链接以及“人们也要求”查询。在整个结果中遍历视频和新闻项目的行，并且右栏通常包括基于维基百科等来源搜索的任何事实块。

Dig into the same results on Neeva and Google, however, and you’ll notice some differences. For instance, while both search engines respond to searches such as “best laptops” with tidy carousels summarizing expert reviews, they’re more prominent on Neeva, while Google puts a similar-looking carousel of paid links at the top. You can also expand Neeva’s summaries into more detailed previews without leaving the search results, a boon for comparison shopping. (The service takes a similar approach with recipes.)

然而，在NEEVA和Google上挖掘相同的结果，您将注意到一些差异。例如，虽然搜索引擎的响应了与整洁的旋转木马求出的搜索，但它们在Neeva上更加突出，而谷歌在顶部展示了类似的付费链接旋转木马。您还可以将Neeva的摘要扩展到更详细的预览中，而无需离开搜索结果，是一个用于比较购物的福音。 （该服务采用了类似的方法。）

The Google home page consists of a search field, “Google Search” and “I’m Feeling Lucky” buttons, and an expanse of white space—remarkably unchanged from its original look back in 1998. Neeva, by contrast, fills its home page with personalizable widgets. They include ones for local weather, stock prices of your choice, and news. That last widget consists entirely of stories from name-brand media outlets—from The Daily Beast to Fox News—and you can customize it by specifying which sources you want to see more often or less often.

Google主页由搜索字段组成，“Google Search”和“我感到幸运”按钮，并且从1998年的原始环保的白色空间显着不变。通过对比度，Neeva填补了其主页使用可个性化小部件。它们包括当地天气，您选择的股票价格和新闻。最后一个小部件完全由名称 - 品牌媒体网点的故事组成 - 从每日野兽到福克斯新闻 - 您可以通过指定您想要更常见或更少的源来定制它。

Still another distinguishing characteristic is Neeva’s integrations with third-party services. You can hook the service up with information sources such as your Gmail or Outlook email and calendar, Google Drive or Dropbox storage, and/or Slack or Jira account. Neeva will then index them and intersperse material from them in your search results as well as presenting it in a dedicated “Personal” tab. Google has experimented with similar concepts in the past—it once mashed up Google search and Gmail—but seems to have moved on from the concept.

另一个有区别的特征是NEEVA与第三方服务的一体化。您可以将服务与信息源（如Gmail或Outlook电子邮件）和日历，Google Drive或Dropbox存储以及/或Slack或Jira帐户提供联系。然后，NEEVA将在搜索结果中向其中索引它们和漫步材料，并在专用的“个人”选项卡中将其呈现。谷歌已经尝试了过去的类似概念 - 曾经用过谷歌搜索和Gmail - 但似乎已经从这个概念上移动。

For all that’s nice about Neeva—starting with its organic results sitting at the top of the page rather than below a payload of advertising—it hasn’t done the impossible by reaching Google-like sophistication right out of the gate. Those product-oriented results for searches such as “best laptops” are well packaged, but the ones I checked out were thin on sources, with PC Mag dominating the review links to such an extent that I wondered if it was through some partnership deal. (It isn’t.)

对于所有这一切都很好，关于NEEVA-over坐在页面顶部的有机结果，而不是在广告的有效载荷之下 - 它通过在门外达到Google的复杂性而无法完成不可能的。这些以产品为导向的搜索结果，如“最佳笔记本电脑”，包装良好，但我被检查出来的源头稀薄，PC Mag占据了审查链接，这在某种程度上我想知道它是通过一些伙伴关系交易。 （它不是。）

Neeva also doesn’t do local information anywhere near as well as Google: It knows my Zip Code, but when I searched for a local pizza chain, it gave me a map for the branch 28 miles away, not the one within walking distance. It can answer questions such as “What time is it in Rio?” and “How tall is Joe Biden?” with facts that appear on the results page itself, but it doesn’t do so for as dizzying an array of queries as Google does. When I used the pre-release site, I also encountered the occasional out-and-out glitch, like a thumbnail map of San Francisco that turned into Indianapolis when I clicked on it.

Neeva也没有在附近的任何地方以及谷歌的地方信息：它知道我的邮政编码，但是当我搜索当地的披萨链时，它给了我一个28英里的分支的地图，而不是步行距离之内。它可以回答问题，例如“在里约在什么时候？” “乔拜登有多高？”与结果页面本身出现的事实，但它没有这样做，因为谷歌这样的查询数组。当我使用预发布的网站时，我也遇到了偶尔的出口故障，就像旧金山的缩略图地图，当我点击它时，旧金山的缩略图地图变成了印第安纳波利斯。

Ramaswamy acknowledges that in its current state, Neeva is something of a rough draft: “We have several large launches planned for next month, on the search quality side, after being publicly available,” he says. It will be interesting to see where it gets by the fall, when the people now signing up conclude their three-month free trials and choose whether to become paying customers.

Ramaswamy承认，在目前的国家，Neeva是一个粗略的草案：“在公开可用之后，我们在搜索质量方面有几个大型推出，在搜索质量方面，”他说。看到秋天的地方会有很有趣的是，当时的人们签名结束他们的三个月免费试验并选择是否成为支付客户。

Where it could go after that is hard to say, but it’s not like search has no remaining problems to solve.  When I asked Manber about what he was working on, he wouldn’t tell me: “I learned a long time ago to talk about things only after I’ve done them, not when I’m just planning to do them.”

在那里可以追求很难说，但它不像搜索没有剩下的问题要解决。当我问一个人的努力时，他不会告诉我：“我很久以前学到了，只有在我完成了他们之后才能谈论事情，而不是我打算做他们的时候。”

As I began working on this article and got pre-release access to Neeva, I wanted to use it all the time. To ensure that I didn’t reflexively Google for anything, I went into Safari’s settings to change my default search engine to Neeva. It was only then that I realized I couldn’t. Most major browsers make you pick from a few pre-selected search engines, not (yet) including Neeva; only Chrome allows you to choose one that’s not on its canned list.

正如我开始在这篇文章上工作并获得了对NEEVA的预发布访问，我想一直使用它。为了确保我没有反驳谷歌，我进入了Safari的设置，将我的默认搜索引擎更改为NEEVA。那么我意识到我不能。大多数主要浏览器让您从一些预先选择的搜索引擎中挑选，而不是（尚未）包括NEEVA;只有Chrome允许您选择一个不在其罐头列表中的一个。

That experience underlined that Neeva is entering a world that isn’t exactly wired to give a new stand-alone search engine its best shot at success. To control its destiny, a search company really needs to write at least some of its own software; it’s not a coincidence that everyone from Google to DuckDuckGo has come out with a browser. And now so has Neeva, with its iPhone and iPadOS browsers as well as extensions that embed similar functionality in other browsers.

这种经验强调了Neeva正在进入一个并不完全有线的世界，以便为新的独立搜索引擎提供最佳的成功射击。为了控制其命运，搜索公司真的需要至少写一些自己的软件;从谷歌到Duckduckgo的每个人都没有浏览器并不是巧合。现在所以拥有NEEVA，具有IPHOD和iPadoS浏览器以及嵌入其他浏览器中的类似功能的扩展。

“The browser and search are just so intertwined, right?” says Neeva software engineer Darin Fisher. “Search is such an important part of the UI of the browser.” Fisher knows whereof he speaks: He’s a 21-year veteran of the browser wars whose résumé includes Netscape and Firefox as well as Chrome from its inception until earlier this year.

“浏览器和搜索只是如此交织在一起，对吧？” Neeva软件工程师Darin Fisher说。 “搜索是浏览器UI的如此重要的部分。”费舍尔知道他讲的地方：他是一个21年的浏览器战争的老兵，他的Résumé包括Netscape和Firefox以及今年早些时候的铬。

According to Fisher, the symbiotic nature of browsing and searching can create design conundrums that put a browser/search engine company at odds with its users. For example, if you type the name of a site into the browser’s address bar, will it send you right there? Or will it err on the side of directing you to a page of search results it can monetize through ads?

根据Fisher，浏览和搜索的共生性质可以创建设计难题，将浏览器/搜索引擎公司与其用户的赔率。例如，如果您在浏览器的地址栏中键入站点的名称，它会在那里发送你吗？或者它会在将您指导到搜索结果的页面方面，它可以通过广告批准？

With no ads, Neeva can resist such temptations. “When I think about Neeva and that simple business model that it has—if people are happy, they’re paying—it opens the door to not having that tension,” says Fisher.

没有广告，Neeva可以抵制这种诱惑。 “当我想到Neeva和那个简单的商业模式时 - 如果人们幸福，他们付钱 - 它打开了没有那张紧张的门，”Fisher说。

Along with putting the Neeva search engine front and center, the company’s apps and extensions allow it to implement privacy-related features it couldn’t implement purely as web services. While browsing a website, you can click on an icon near its address to get a summary of the trackers it contains that monitor your browsing for advertising and other purposes; you can also turn on a feature called Tracking Prevention, which blocks such surveillance and speeds up page rendering.

随着NEEVA搜索引擎前端和中心，公司的应用和扩展允许它实现与隐私相关的功能，它无法纯粹地实现为Web服务。在浏览网站时，您可以点击其地址附近的图标，以获取其包含它包含的跟踪器的摘要，该序列监控广告和其他目的的浏览;您还可以打开称为跟踪预防的功能，这会阻止此类监视并加快页面呈现。

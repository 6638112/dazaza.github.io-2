The first thing you should know about the history of energy in the United States is that, about 150 years ago, the horses got absolutely ripped.

关于美国的能源历史，您应该了解的第一件事是，大约在150年前，马匹被彻底撕破了。

“In the mid-19th century, you have a massive expansion of the horse body. They become 50 percent more powerful,” Robert Suits, a historian at the University of Chicago, told me.

“在19世纪中叶，您的马身大量扩张。它们变得更强大了50％，”芝加哥大学的历史学家罗伯特·西茨（Robert Suits）告诉我。

This matters to the history of energy because, back then, horses were a primary form of transportation. (As Jason Torchinsky has written, horses were the world’s first semiautonomous vehicle.) It matters because of how the change happened at all—a massive program of selective breeding that was not possible, Suits said, until railroads existed to ferry promising horses over long distances.

这对能源的历史很重要，因为那时马是运输的主要形式。 （正如杰森·托尔钦斯基（Jason Torchinsky）所写，马是世界上第一个半自动车辆。）这很重要，因为这种变化是如何发生的，西斯说，这是不可能实现的大规模的选择性育种计划，直到有铁路能够长期运送有希望的马。距离。

And it matters, finally, because you must understand how yoked horses are to figure out how much energy the U.S. economy used to use. In the 1810s, the average American horse ate 25,000 to 30,000 calories a day, Suits said. By 1900, each ate 35,000 to 40,000 calories a day. That translates to far more hay and oats.

最后，这很重要，因为您必须了解如何利用马来弄清楚美国经济过去使用了多少能源。考茨说，在1810年代，美国人平均每天吃掉25,000至30,000卡路里的热量。到1900年，每人每天吃掉35,000至40,000卡路里。这意味着更多的干草和燕麦。

That tidbit is one of many things I learned from an astonishing new research project from Suits and his colleagues. It’s a history of the American energy system in chart form, from 1800 to 2019. It was published today:

这个花絮是我从西服和他的同事们从一个惊人的新研究项目中学到的东西之一。它以图表的形式记录了美国能源系统的历史，从1800年到2019年。今天出版：

This type of chart is called a Sankey diagram, which shows the relative size of flows in and out of a system. This particular Sankey diagram shows the inputs and outputs for the U.S. energy system, measured in watts per capita. The left side of the chart shows where energy is coming from (coal, natural gas, or petroleum) and the right side shows what it’s being used for (transportation, agriculture, or home lighting and heating).

这种类型的图表称为Sankey图，它显示了流入和流出系统的相对大小。此特定的Sankey图显示了美国能源系统的输入和输出，以人均瓦特为单位。图表的左侧显示了能源的来源（煤炭，天然气或石油），右侧显示了能源的用途（运输，农业或家庭照明和供暖）。

You can find a full interactive version of their chart online. Honestly, I’ve featured this chart here in the hopes that you’ll go to their website and play around with it. There is … a lot going on in it. As far as I’m aware, this is the first attempt to put so much information about U.S. energy history in one place. “You could write a book from that Sankey alone,” Apratim Sahay, an energy consultant based in Boston, told me.

您可以在线找到其图表的完整交互式版本。老实说，我在此列出了这张图表，希望您能访问他们的网站并继续使用它。里面有很多事情。据我所知，这是将如此多有关美国能源历史的信息放在一个地方的第一次尝试。 “您可以单独从Sankey写一本书，”驻波士顿的能源顾问Apratim Sahay告诉我。

So click around! Look at what’s happening in the year you were born versus what’s happening now. Look at what the energy system was like 100 years before you were born. It will help you think more keenly about climate change.

因此，请点击周围！看看出生那年发生了什么与现在发生了什么。看一下出生前100年的能源系统是什么样的。这将帮助您更敏锐地思考气候变化。

In a broader sense, I’m featuring this project because it has a lot to teach us about how the energy system got to be the way it is today—and how it might change, and be made to change, in the future.

从广义上讲，我之所以选择这个项目，是因为它有很多要教我们的知识，那就是能源系统如何成为今天的样子，以及将来如何改变和改变。

First, you can glean the big eras in American energy use from this chart. The half century from 1800 to 1850 saw the country devour biomass, most of it in the form of firewood and animal feed. In the 1870s, biomass gave way to the first fossil fuels: coal and, to a lesser extent, petroleum. (Petroleum initially dominated as fuel for lamps, not transportation.)

首先，您可以从此图表中了解美国能源使用的大时代。从1800年到1850年的半个世纪，该国吞噬了生物质，其中大部分以柴火和动物饲料的形式存在。在1870年代，生物质被第一批化石燃料所取代：煤炭，以及程度较小的石油。 （最初，石油主要用作灯的燃料，而不是运输的燃料。）

By the 1910s, coal was dominant. “Coal was basically doing everything in the economy except electricity,” Suits said. It heated homes, powered railroads, and helped forge steel. But it was dirty and frustrating to move, and its price was volatile. In the 1920s, it began to fade from the economy, replaced by natural gas, electricity, and—in the transportation sector—petroleum (in the form of gasoline).

到1910年代，煤炭已占主导地位。西斯说：“煤炭基本上是经济中除电力以外的所有事务。”它给房屋供暖，为铁路供电，并帮助锻造钢铁。但是它很脏而且令人沮丧，而且价格波动很大。在1920年代，它开始从经济中消失，取而代之的是天然气，电力，以及在运输领域中的石油（以汽油的形式）。

This was the age of cars and electrified Sun Belt suburbs—and it lasted 50 years, until the energy crisis of the 1970s arrived and capped energy use. Since 1973, per capita energy use hasn’t increased.

这是汽车时代和电气化的太阳带郊区的时代，持续了50年，直到1970年代的能源危机出现并限制了能源使用。自1973年以来，人均能源使用量一直没有增加。

In recent years, you can see natural gas driving out coal from the electricity sector. It was getting a handle on that change, actually, that led the project’s leader to start working on it in the first place. “The changes that are happening in the electricity sector now—changes that are as large as any energy transition we’ve seen—are difficult to grasp … without animating the data,” Elisabeth Moyer, an atmospheric-chemistry professor at the University of Chicago who created the project, told me.

近年来，您可以看到天然气将电力从电力部门运出。实际上，正是要抓住这种变化，才导致项目负责人从头开始着手进行这项工作。芝加哥大学大气化学教授伊丽莎白·莫耶（Elisabeth Moyer）说：“如果不对数据进行动画处理，那么电力行业正在发生的变化-就像我们已经看到的任何能量转换一样大的变化-很难掌握……”创建项目的人告诉我。

(This is as good a moment as any to mention that I am a visiting journalism fellow at the Energy Policy Institute at the University of Chicago this year. It is not a paid position, and I haven’t been able to visit since 2019, because of the pandemic. But it’s how I met Moyer.)

（这是我提到我今年是芝加哥大学能源政策研究所的访问学者的好时机。这不是有薪职位，自2019年以来我一直没有来过，因为大流行。但这就是我遇见Moyer的方式。）

However, this narration leaves out a lot of the surprises that make the chart wonderful. Take 1800, for instance.

但是，这种叙述没有使图表变得令人惊奇的许多惊喜。以1800为例。

That thread of wind energy going to transportation? That’s wind filling the sails of schooners. The hydro going to industry is from rivers spinning gristmills.

那条风能将用于运输吗？狂风笼罩着大篷车的帆。走向工业的水电来自河流旋转的磨坊。

Another observation: Everything happens in energy history later than I expect. The first commercial coal mines operated in America in the 18th century, and the first oil well was drilled in 1859. But the amount of coal energy didn’t exceed the amount of biomass energy until 1888. And because Americans used so much firewood to heat their homes, that’s also about when the energy used in the economy exceeded the amount of energy used at home.

另一个观察结果：能源历史上发生的一切都比我预期的晚。 18世纪，美国第一家商业煤矿开始运营，1859年钻出了第一口油井。但是直到1888年，煤炭能源的数量才超过生物质能源的数量。而且由于美国人使用了如此多的柴火来加热他们的房屋，也就是经济中使用的能源超过家用能源的时间。

The same is true with electricity. Electricity started to take off only after World War II, but not until 1959 did Americans consume more electricity than coal at home.

电力也是如此。电力只是在第二次世界大战之后才开始腾飞，但是直到1959年，美国人在家中消耗的电能才超过煤炭。

By the same token, every battle in energy seems to have happened before. Look at coal’s struggle in the 20th century—at its peak in 1918, it powered every part of the economy except agriculture. Then it declined so quickly that, by the 1960s, it seemed destined for eradication. If you had looked around then, you would’ve assumed coal had only a few short years left, Moyer and her colleagues write.

同样，能源方面的每场战斗似乎都曾经发生过。看看20世纪的煤炭斗争-在1918年的顶峰时期，煤炭为除农业之外的所有经济领域提供了动力。然后它迅速下降，以至于在1960年代，它注定要根除。莫耶和她的同事们写道，如果环顾四周，您会以为煤炭只剩下了短短的几年。

Instead, the oil crisis struck during the 1970s, and coal found a new niche. It became a fuel primarily used for generating electricity, in part because its domestic origin made it especially attractive and reliable-seeming to policy makers. Then it rallied again. It remained a core part of the energy mix until the 2010s, when it was priced out by natural gas:

相反，石油危机在1970年代爆发，煤炭找到了新的利基市场。它成为主要用于发电的燃料，部分原因是由于其国内来源对决策者而言特别有吸引力且可靠。然后它再次反弹。直到2010年代，它仍然是能源结构的核心部分，当时它被天然气定价：

One more takeaway: It’s really, really hard to decrease the amount of raw fuel used in the economy. Remember, this Sankey diagram is in per capita terms. But because the country’s population is almost always growing, a decline in per capita use doesn’t translate to a decline in absolute use. In fact, since 1800, the only type of energy source to decline in absolute terms is coal, represented as black in this chart:

另外一点：减少经济中使用的原始燃料的数量确实非常非常困难。请记住，此Sankey图按人均计算。但是，由于该国的人口几乎总是在增长，因此人均使用量的减少并不会转化为绝对使用量的减少。实际上，自1800年以来，绝对值唯一下降的能源类型是煤炭，在此图表中以黑色表示：

Look at that green sliver at the bottom of the chart. It’s biomass. Even in our petroleum-powered age, the U.S. uses more biomass today (about 165 gigawatts a year) than it did at any point during the 19th century. But whereas biomass became animal feed and firewood back then, today it is mostly corn used for ethanol production—and also firewood, actually.

查看图表底部的绿色条子。它是生物质。即使在我们以石油为动力的时代，美国今天使用的生物质能（每年约165吉瓦）也比19世纪任何时候都要多。但是，尽管生物质在那时成为动物饲料和木柴，但今天它主要是用于生产乙醇的玉米，实际上也是木柴。

“Historians have a habit of emphasizing firsts . When is the first viable automobile? When is the first steam engine in America? ” Suits said. “But if you look at the economy, steam engines straight-up don’t matter until the 1860s or ’70s. You really start to miss that the bulk of any given transformation has to have this layer of infrastructure that enables scaling up.”

“历史学家习惯于强调第一。什么时候第一辆可行的汽车？什么时候是美国第一台蒸汽机？西服说。 “但是，从经济的角度来看，直到1860或70年代，蒸汽机的直线加速才变得重要。您真的开始错过任何给定转换的大部分都必须具有可扩展的基础架构层。”

That is, indeed, Suits and Moyer’s biggest takeaway from working on this project. Energy transitions tend to move faster when they’re able to coast on infrastructure that was built for an earlier purpose. So coal was able to power so much of the economy, so quickly, because it traveled easily by railroad. Petroleum and natural gas became dominant in the 20th century after the pipeline infrastructure was built.

的确，这是Suits和Moyer从事此项目的最大收获。能源过渡能够在较早建立的基础设施上顺利进行时，其移动速度往往会更快。因此，煤炭能够以如此之快的速度为整个经济提供动力，因为煤炭很容易通过铁路运输。管道基础设施建成后，石油和天然气在20世纪占据主导地位。

Likewise, Moyer said, energy transitions don’t tend to slow down because of the kinds of technologies that consumers are using. Households don’t seem to mind throwing out a coal furnace in their house in exchange for a natural-gas one, she said. Instead, it’s the lack of supply and distribution technology—such as a pipeline or the power grid—that tend to drag transitions down. And likewise, transitions can work more quickly when they’re able to use preexisting infrastructure. So once electric cars have slightly better battery life, she said, their basic obstacle to mass adoption is likely to be the lack of charging infrastructure, not the fact that many people will already own a gas-powered car.

同样，Moyer说，由于消费者使用的技术种类繁多，能源转换不会趋于放缓。她说，家庭似乎并不介意在家里扔掉一个煤炉来换取天然气。相反，缺少供应和分配技术（例如管道或电网）会拖累过渡。同样，当过渡能够使用现有的基础结构时，过渡可以更快地工作。她说，因此，一旦电动汽车的电池寿命稍长一点，它们普及的基本障碍就可能是缺乏充电基础设施，而不是许多人已经拥有汽油动力汽车这一事实。

I sent the chart to two other energy experts to get their thoughts. Emily Grubert, an engineering professor at Georgia Tech, noted that nearly all of the transitions depicted were accidental or the result of market forces. It’s possible that the transition to zero-carbon energy could be faster, she said, because it will be intentional.

我将图表发送给其他两名能源专家，以获取他们的想法。乔治亚理工学院的工程学教授埃米莉·格鲁伯特（Emily Grubert）指出，几乎所有描述的转变都是偶然的或市场力量的结果。她说，向零碳能源的过渡可能会更快，因为这是故意的。

Sahay, the energy consultant, noticed other details. Electrification’s growth after 1932 was inseparable from the Roosevelt administration’s program of rural electrification, he said. “The Bureau of Reclamation would build dams on river valleys, and the federal Power Marketing Administrations would sell electricity to reclaim costs,” he said. “These projects not only reduced urban-rural disparity but structurally transformed jobs in the economy from agriculture towards manufacturing and services.” They also made farms more productive. We should use a similar scheme now to electrify the rest of the economy, he said—and, in fact, that’s what Senator Bernie Sanders proposed during the presidential primary.

能源顾问Sahay注意到了其他细节。他说，1932年后电气化的增长与罗斯福政府的农村电气化计划密不可分。他说：“垦殖局将在河谷上建造水坝，联邦电力销售管理局将出售电力以收回成本。” “这些项目不仅减少了城乡差距，而且在经济上从农业向制造业和服务业转变了工作岗位。”他们还提高了农场的生产力。他说，我们现在应该使用类似的计划来使其余经济体电气化-实际上，这就是参议员伯尼·桑德斯在总统初选中提出的建议。

So what does this chart have to do with climate change? The short and glib answer is that this chart is climate change. America has emitted a quarter of the world’s total historic CO₂ pollution, 400 billion tons, more than any other country. That is what you’re looking at in this chart, in the jagged slopes of coal power and the unquenched demand for petroleum.

那么，这张图与气候变化有什么关系？简短而轻松的回答是，这张图是气候变化。美国的二氧化碳排放量占全球历史性二氧化碳排放总量的四分之一，为4000亿吨，超过任何其他国家。这就是您在这张图表中看到的情况，即煤电的锯齿状斜坡和石油需求的旺盛。

The second and maybe just as obvious point is that the U.S. and the entire world need to pull off an energy transition that rivals anything seen in the past two centuries. If you look at the 2019 version of the Sankey diagram, you’ll see the two largest blocks of color represent natural gas and petroleum. “The stuff we talk about wanting to do isn’t even visible on this chart,” Grubert said. Solar and wind energy are represented by lines only a few pixels wide. “We have to turn those pencil lines into boxes,” she said.

第二个也许也是显而易见的一点是，美国和整个世界都需要实现与过去两个世纪以来任何事物都无法匹敌的能源转型。如果您查看2019年版的Sankey图，您会看到两个最大的色块分别代表天然气和石油。格鲁伯特说：“我们谈论要做的事情在这张图表上甚至都看不到。”太阳能和风能由仅几像素宽的线表示。她说：“我们必须将那些铅笔线变成盒子。”

But finally, consider the horses. They were able to get so beefy in the 19th century not because the corn or oats improved, but because selective breeding was suddenly possible. It was possible because railroads existed—which, in turn, ran on coal. That means that horses today are biologically inseparable from how fossil fuels historically transformed them. So are we.

但最后，考虑一下马匹。他们之所以能够在19世纪变得如此强大，不是因为玉米或燕麦有所改良，而是因为突然有可能进行选择性育种。这是可能的，因为存在铁路，而铁路又是依靠煤炭。这意味着当今的马在生物学上与历史上化石燃料如何转化它们密不可分。我们也是。

数百份美洲原住民条约首次数字化

2020-10-20 10:58:31

For many Native American tribes, historical treaties are a fraught reminder of promises made—and broken—by the United States government over centuries of colonial expansion and exploitation. The documents are also of paramount importance today, as tribes and activists point to them as binding agreements in legal battles for land and resources.

对于许多美洲原住民部落来说，历史条约令人担忧地提醒人们，美国政府在几个世纪的殖民扩张和剥削中做出了承诺，也违背了承诺。这些文件在今天也是至关重要的，因为部落和活动人士指出，它们是土地和资源法律战中具有约束力的协议。

Thanks to a newly completed digitization effort by the U.S. National Archives and the Museum of Indian Arts and Culture (MIAC) in Santa Fe, researchers and the public now have unprecedented access to hundreds of these critical agreements.

由于美国国家档案馆(U.S.National Archives)和圣达菲的印度艺术与文化博物馆(MIAC)最近完成的数字化工作，研究人员和公众现在可以史无前例地接触到数百份这样的关键协议。

The online collection features 374 ratified Indian treaties from the archives’ holdings. According to a blog post, these documents are housed in a specially protected area of the National Archives building and are unavailable for use in the Central Research Room due to their fragility and significance. More than 50 of the treaties are written on large sheets of parchment; several contain drawings, maps and wampum, or decorative beads used as currency in some Native American tribes.

在线收藏从档案馆的藏品中收集了374份批准的印度条约。根据一篇博客文章，这些文件存放在国家档案馆大楼的一个特别保护的区域，由于它们的脆弱性和重要性，无法在中央研究室使用。其中50多个条约写在大张羊皮纸上；其中几个条约包含绘画、地图和wampum，或者在一些美洲原住民部落用作货币的装饰珠子。

Those hoping to delve into the trove can use Indigenous Digital Archive (IDA) Treaties Explorer, a free tool optimized for easily searching and studying the documents. In addition to providing a framework for research, the portal offers maps of different treaty land designations, as well as extensive historical and contextual information.

那些希望深入研究这些宝藏的人可以使用土著数字档案馆(IDA)条约资源管理器，这是一个免费的工具，经过优化，可以轻松地搜索和研究这些文件。除了提供研究框架外，该门户网站还提供不同条约土地指定的地图，以及广泛的历史和背景信息。

“The treaties between the U.S. and Native nations are relevant, and few people have had access to know about treaties that are related to where they live,” Della Warrior, MIAC’s director and a member of the Otoe-Missouria tribe, tells the Albuquerque Journal’s Adrian Gomez. “MIAC is pleased to be able to provide this online resource that we all can use to explore our relationships using maps and a carefully curated set of historical documents from the National Archives, the Library of Congress, the Smithsonian, and other sources.”

MIAC主任、奥托-密苏里部落成员德拉·瓦雷尔(Della Warrior)告诉阿尔伯克基日报的阿德里安·戈麦斯(Adrian Gomez)：“美国和土著国家之间的条约是相关的，很少有人有机会了解与他们居住的地方有关的条约。”MIAC很高兴能够提供这一在线资源，我们都可以使用来自国家档案馆、国会图书馆、史密森学会和其他来源的地图和一套精心策划的历史文件来探索我们的关系。“。

In addition to debuting the new resource, MIAC recently hosted a series of Facebook workshops on treaty law. Led by Sherri Thomas, assistant director of the University of New Mexico Law Library, the recorded sessions are now available to view online.

除了推出新资源外，MIAC最近还在Facebook上举办了一系列关于条约法的研讨会。在新墨西哥大学法律图书馆助理馆长雪莉·托马斯(Sherri Thomas)的带领下，录制的会议现在可以在网上观看。

The digitization project was completed with funding from an anonymous donor, says David S. Ferriero, archivist of the United States, in a statement.

美国档案馆馆长大卫·S·费列罗(David S.Ferriero)在一份声明中表示，数字化项目是在一位匿名捐赠者的资助下完成的。

“Of all the things we have custody of and are responsible for—even the Charters of Freedom—I believe the Indian treaties are the most valuable documents in terms of reading the original language and the government promises, and realizing what was never delivered,” he adds. “Now, many more descendants of the original peoples can examine the names and seals and read the words set down by their ancestors so long ago.”

他补充道：“在我们保管和负责的所有东西中--甚至是”自由宪章“--我认为，就阅读原文和政府承诺、实现从未兑现的东西而言，印度条约是最有价值的文件。”现在，更多的原始民族的后代可以检查名字和印章，阅读祖先很久以前写下的文字。

As Kimbra Cutlip reported for Smithsonian magazine in 2018, a group of Native American tribes has long contested its right to land in South Dakota’s Black Hills. The tribes’ argument hinges on the Fort Laramie Treaty, an 1868 legal document forged between a collective of Native American bands—including the Dakota, Lakota, Nakota and Arapaho—and the U.S. government. Though officials had initially designated the Black Hills as land reserved for Native use, they reneged on the treaty when gold was discovered in the region less than a decade later.

正如Kimbra Cutlip在2018年为Smithsonian杂志报道的那样，一群美洲原住民部落长期以来一直在争夺其在南达科他州黑山的登陆权。部落的争论基于“拉勒米堡条约”(Fort Laramie Treaty)，这是一份1868年在包括达科他州、拉科塔、纳科塔和阿拉帕霍在内的美国原住民乐队集体与美国政府之间达成的法律文件。尽管官员们最初将黑山指定为保留给土著使用的土地，但不到十年后，当该地区发现黄金时，他们违背了条约。

The importance of historic Native American treaties once again surfaced in the news in July, when the Supreme Court ruled that much of the eastern half of Oklahoma falls within Native American territory. Though no land changed hands, the McGirt v. Oklahoma decision was hailed as a win for tribal sovereignty because it demonstrated that Native reservations established by treaties with the U.S. in the 1830s were still valid despite the fact that Oklahoma became a state in 1907.

7月份，当最高法院裁定俄克拉荷马州东半部的大部分地区属于美洲原住民领土时，历史性的美洲原住民条约的重要性再次出现在新闻中。虽然没有土地易手，但McGirt诉俄克拉荷马州的裁决被誉为部落主权的胜利，因为它表明，19世纪30年代与美国签订的条约建立的土著保留地仍然有效，尽管俄克拉何马州于1907年成为一个州。

“This is a historic day,” Creek Nation Principal Chief David Hill told the New York Times’ Jack Healy and Adam Liptak shortly after the announcement. “This is amazing. It’s never too late to make things right.”

“这是历史性的一天，”克里克国家首席执行官大卫·希尔在宣布这一消息后不久告诉“纽约时报”的杰克·希利和亚当·利普塔克(Jack Healy And Adam Liptak)。“这真是太棒了。把事情做好永远不会太晚。“

Lost Alaskan Indigenous fort rediscovered after 200 years
200年后重新发现失落的阿拉斯加土著堡垒

2021-02-06 21:02:11

In 1804, Tlingit warriors sheltered behind the walls of a wooden fort on a peninsula in southeastern Alaska, preparing to repel a Russian amphibious assault. An archaeological survey near the modern community of Sitka recently revealed the hidden outline of the now-legendary fort, whose exact location had been lost to history since shortly after the battle.

1804年，特林吉特（Tlingit）战士躲在阿拉斯加东南部半岛一个木制堡垒的墙后面，准备击退俄国的两栖攻击。最近在锡特卡（Sitka）现代社区附近进行的一项考古调查显示，这座如今已成为传奇的堡垒的隐蔽轮廓，自从战后不久以来，其确切位置就已不复存在。

The Tlingit had already sent Russia packing once, in 1802, after three years of mounting tensions over the Russian-American Trading Company (a venture akin to the better-known British East India Company), which had a presence on what is now called Baranof Island. Because the Tlingit elders—especially a shaman named Stoonook—suspected that the Russian troops would soon be back in greater numbers, they organized construction of a fort at the mouth of the Kaasdaheen River to help defend the area against assault from the sea.

在俄美贸易公司（类似于知名的英国东印度公司的合资企业）三年来不断加剧的紧张局势之后，特林吉特已经在1802年向俄罗斯发送了一次装箱通知，该公司存在于现在称为Baranof岛。由于特林吉特长老（尤其是萨满教徒萨通克）的怀疑，是俄罗斯军队不久后会复员，因此他们在卡斯达黑河河口组织了一座堡垒，以保护该地区免受海上袭击。

By 1804, the Tlingit had procured firearms, shot, gunpowder, and even cannons from American and British traders. They had also built a trapezoid-shaped palisade, 75 meters long and 30 meters wide, out of young spruce logs, which sheltered more than a dozen log buildings. The Tlingit dubbed it Shis’gi Noow—the Sapling Fort.

到1804年，特林吉特人从美国和英国商人那里购买了枪支，子弹，火药甚至大炮。他们还用年轻的云杉原木建造了一个长75米，宽30米的梯形栅栏，掩盖了十多个原木建筑。特林吉特人将其称为“ Shisgi Noow”（树苗堡）。

The Tlingit had built fortifications before; in 1799, the Russians set up their trading post practically in the shadow of a hilltop fortification called Noow Tlein, or Big Fort. But this time, Stoonook and his people had engineered their fort specifically to counter the threat of naval guns.

特林吉特人以前曾筑过防御工事。 1799年，俄国人实际上是在名为Noow Tlein或Big Fort的山顶防御工事的阴影下建立了贸易站。但是这次，斯托努克和他的人民专门设计了自己的堡垒，以抵抗海军枪支的威胁。

“This fort is different than other Tlingit forts, and may have been adapted in response to an expected naval attack,” Cornell University archaeologist Thomas Urban told Ars. The log palisade was meant to be sturdy enough to repel cannon projectiles, and the strange trapezoidal shape was “probably a function of the anticipated directions of naval cannon fire, but not known for sure,” according to Urban.

康奈尔大学考古学家托马斯·厄本（Thomas Urban）告诉Ars：“该堡垒与其他特林吉特堡垒不同，并且可能已针对预期的海军袭击进行了调整。”原木栅栏的坚固性足以击退加农炮弹，奇特的梯形形状“可能是预期的海军加农炮发射方向的函数，但不确定，” Urban表示。

When the Russian sloop-of-war (a small warship) Neva arrived in September 1804, the ship’s guns couldn’t even put a dent in the sturdy wooden palisade. (In fairness to the Russians, a sloop-of-war doesn’t pack much of a punch, relatively speaking.) “It was constructed of wood, so thick and strong, that the shot from my guns could not penetrate it at the short distance of a cable's length (about 185 meters),” wrote Lt. Cmdr. Yuri Feodorovich Lisyansky, Neva’s commanding officer.

1804年9月，俄国涅瓦河（一艘小型军舰）到达时，船上的枪支甚至无法在坚固的木栅栏上留下凹痕。 （相对于俄罗斯人来说，公平地说，战争的一环并没有太大的麻烦。）``它是用木头建造的，又厚又结实，以至于我的枪支无法穿透它。 Cmdr中校写道：“电缆距离很短（约185米）。”内娃的指挥官尤里·费奥多罗维奇·里扬斯基。

The Russians sent 150 men ashore, mostly Aleut warriors fighting on behalf of their Russian allies. But the landing force found itself facing a hail of gunfire and a savvy two-pronged attack by the Tlingit. While half the Tlingit force emerged from the fort to engage the enemy head-on, the other half waited in the woods to attack the Russians' and Aleuts’ exposed flank. The would-be invaders beat a disorganized retreat back to Neva under the dubious cover of the sloop’s guns.

俄国人派遣了150名士兵上岸，其中大部分是阿留特（Aleut）战士代表他们的俄国盟友作战。但是，登陆部队发现自己面对着枪击和特林吉特人敏锐的两管齐下的攻击。特林吉特部队的一半从堡垒中出来与敌人正面交战，另一半则在树林中等待攻击俄国人。和阿留斯的裸露侧面。潜在的入侵者在单桅帆船的可疑掩护下打败了一个混乱的撤退，回到了涅瓦河。

For the modern Tlingit, “the fort is a cultural symbol of resistance to colonization, and the location is considered sacred by many community members,” Urban told Ars. But for the last 200 years, only its approximate location has been known. A recent geophysical survey of the peninsula revealed the ghostly outline of the fort, long hidden beneath the ground.

对于现代的Tlingit来说，“堡垒是抵抗殖民的文化象征，许多社区成员都认为该城堡是神圣的，” Urban告诉Ars。但是在过去的200年中，仅知道其大概位置。最近对该半岛进行的地球物理调查显示，该堡垒的幽灵轮廓长期隐藏在地下。

Urban and his colleagues used ground-penetrating radar and a technique called electromagnetic induction, which measures differences in how the soil conducts an electric current, to search a 17-hectare swath of the Alaskan coast for traces of the past. Both surveys revealed the buried foundations of walls forming the shape of a trapezoid roughly 75 meters long and 30 meters wide, exactly like the fort described in Tlingit oral history and Russian writings. It could only be the Sapling Fort.

Urban和他的同事使用了探地雷达和一种称为电磁感应的技术，该技术可以测量土壤传导电流的方式之间的差异，从而在阿拉斯加海岸17公顷的土地上搜寻过去的痕迹。两项调查均显示出长约75米，宽30米的梯形墙壁的地下埋藏地基，与特林吉特口述历史和俄罗斯著作中描述的堡垒完全一样。它只能是树苗堡。

“The location and protection of the fort site and broader battlefield has long been of significant interest to the Sheetka Kwaan Tlingit, especially those of the Kiks.adi Clan,” wrote Urban and his colleagues. The Kiks.adi clan built and defended the Sapling Fort under the leadership of war chief Ḵ’alyaan.

Urban和他的同事写道：“要塞区（Sheetka Kwaan Tlingit），尤其是Kiks.adi氏族的堡垒，长期以来一直对要塞地点和更广阔的战场的位置和保护产生重大兴趣，” Kiks.adi家族在战争首领Ḵ’alyaan的领导下建造并保卫了树苗堡。

Archaeologists had been trying to pinpoint the exact site of the Sapling Fort since 1910, when then-President William Taft declared the site a national monument (now Sitka National Historic Park). Several digs found compelling hints about the fort’s location, like cannonballs and possible fragments of buried walls. With the available evidence, the National Park Service could only point to a cleared area on the otherwise forested peninsula. Meanwhile, archaeologists and historians kept suggesting alternative sites for the fort.

自1910年以来，当时的总统威廉·塔夫脱（William Taft）宣布该遗址为国家历史遗迹（现为锡特卡国家历史公园），考古学家一直在试图确定萨普林堡的确切遗址。多次挖掘发现了有关堡垒位置的令人信服的暗示，例如炮弹和可能的掩埋碎片。根据现有证据，国家公园管理局只能指向原本森林茂密的半岛上的一片空地。同时，考古学家和历史学家不断提出要塞的替代地点。

The recent geophysical survey seems to have settled the question. It lines up well with the results of a 1958 survey, which claimed to have excavated parts of the southern and western walls of the Sapling Fort. Electromagnetic induction also found some buried metal debris in the northwest corner of the fort, near where an earlier survey had found cannonballs.

最近的地球物理调查似乎已经解决了这个问题。它与1958年的一项调查结果非常吻合，该调查声称已挖掘了树苗堡的南墙和西墙的一部分。电磁感应还在堡垒的西北角发现了一些埋藏的金属碎片，这是较早的调查发现炮弹的地方附近。

But why was the fort lost for 200 years? The Russians burned it to the ground a few days after the Tlingit repelled their amphibious assault.

但是为什么要塞失去了200年呢？特林吉特人击退了两栖攻击之后的几天，俄国人将其焚毁在地上。

The Tlingit had stashed their supply of gunpowder across Sitka Sound, perhaps to prevent it from accidentally blowing up the fort if a stray cannonball landed in the wrong place. Early in the siege, Russian cannon sank the Tlingit canoe making its way back to the Sapling Fort with a load of powder. The resulting explosion cut off the Tlingit powder supply and killed a respected elder and several high-ranking young members of the Kiks.adi clan.

特林吉特人（Tlingit）在锡特卡海湾（Sitka Sound）掩藏了火药的供应，也许是为了防止如果散落的炮弹落在错误的地方时火药意外炸毁了堡垒。在攻城初期，俄罗斯大炮沉没了特林吉特独木舟，并带着大量粉末回到了树苗堡。由此产生的爆炸切断了Tlingit粉末的供应，并杀死了Kiks.adi家族中一位受尊敬的老年人和几名高级青年。

Despite the setback, the Tlingit held off the Russian and Aleut advance for several more days. The delaying action bought the clan’s civilians time to flee to safer (and less contested) ground. In what is now called the Sitka Kiks.ádi Survival March, the women and children hiked north across Baranof Island, then rowed canoes across a strait to Chichagof Island (both islands are now part of the modern city of Sitka, Alaska).

尽管遭遇挫折，特林吉特人还是推迟了俄罗斯和阿留特的前进数日。拖延行动为氏族平民腾出了时间逃往更安全（竞争较少）的地区。在现在称为SitkaKiks.ádiSurvival March的游行中，妇女和儿童向北徒步穿越Baranof岛，然后划着独木舟越过海峡到达Chichagof岛（这两个岛屿现在都是现代城市Sitka，阿拉斯加的一部分）。

Meanwhile, the fort’s defenders even managed to pull off a stealthy, organized retreat under cover of darkness in order to provide a rearguard for the fleeing Tlingit civilians. The next morning, several days after the first landing, Cmdr. Lisyansky and his Aleut allies walked into the empty fort. They sketched it in detail before burning it to the ground.

同时，堡垒的防御者甚至设法在黑暗的掩护下进行了一次隐蔽的有组织的撤退，以便为逃离的特林吉特（Tlingit）平民提供后卫。第二天早上，在第一次着陆后的几天，Cmdr。 Lisyansky和他的Aleut盟友走进了空旷的堡垒。他们在将其烧至地面之前详细绘制了草图。

“Perhaps the battle could have gone in a different direction [if the Tlingit hadn’t run out of gunpowder],” Urban told Ars. “But ultimately Russia had more resources with which to wage war, so a different outcome for the battle might not have necessarily meant a different long-term outcome.”

乌尔班告诉Ars：“也许战斗可能会朝着不同的方向发展（如果特林吉特人还没有用完火药的话）。” “但是最终俄罗斯将拥有更多的资源来发动战争，因此战斗的不同结果不一定意味着长期的结果。”

But the people of the Kiks.adi clan had pulled off no small feat; they had bloodied the invaders, held them off for several days, and gotten the clan’s people to safety—all without the help of other clans whose alliance they had spent two years requesting. The Battle of Sitka, as it’s now known, is a proud piece of history for the modern Tlingit.

但是基克斯。阿迪家族的人民取得了不小的成就。他们为入侵者流血，将他们拘留了几天，并使氏族的人民安全起来，而这一切都没有他们花费了两年时间要求的其他氏族的帮助。众所周知，锡特卡战役是现代特林吉特人的骄傲历史。

“Tribal members have maintained ongoing involvement and interest in archaeological work within the park, and representatives of the Sitka Tribe of Alaska have responded favorably to the new findings,” wrote Urban and his colleagues.

“部落成员一直对公园内的考古工作保持不断的参与和兴趣，阿拉斯加的锡特卡部落的代表对新发现做出了积极回应，”厄本及其同事写道。

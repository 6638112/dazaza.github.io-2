Paper Aeroplane Wind Tunnel
纸飞机风洞

2021-05-15 07:24:59

For a while now Mark and I have been wanting to do something with paper aeroplanes, so when looking for a more interactive Maker Faire project we decided to go big and make a wind tunnel with a working section fit for A4 aerodynamics.

有一段时间暂时标记，我一直想用纸飞机做点什么，所以在寻找更具互动的制造商童话项目时，我们决定做大量，并制作一个带有工作台的风洞，适合A4空气动力学。

Why paper aeroplanes? Well, first of all we are both engineers so have been making paper planes for as long as we can remember, but there’s more than that; paper planes are an evocative symbol of frivolity that strikes a chord with many. You take a piece of paper that could oh so easily become the base for recording meeting minutes or some other such boring dreariness, and instead you turn it into something aspirational, something that can defy gravity and achieve flight, not to mention catch the back of an unsuspecting colleague’s head by surprise.

为什么纸飞机？好吧，首先，我们都是工程师所以只要我们能记住，我们都是制作纸张飞机，但已经有更多;纸飞机是一种令人兴奋的象征，使与许多人罢工的和弦。你拿一张纸可以很容易地成为录制会议分钟或其他一些这种无聊的沉闷的基础，而是将它变成抱负的东西，可以蔑视重力和实现飞行的东西，更不用说抓住了一个毫无戒心的同事令人惊讶的是。

You can wow the masses with the pin straight stable flight you can achieve or with your best triple loop-the-loops. Ok… they might not admit to being impressed but, you know, they’re just jealous of your aerodynamic stationery skills.

你可以用别针直线稳定飞行哇哇哇哇哇哇哇哇哇哇哇哇哇哇哇哇哇哇哇哇哇哇哇哇哇哇哇哇哇哇哇哇哇哇哇哇好的......他们可能不承认印象深刻，但是，你知道，他们只是嫉妒你的空气动力学文具技能。

They are custom and individual with infinite scope for creativity of design both by folds and by felt-tips.

它们是习俗和个人，具有无限的设计，可通过折叠和毛毡提示设计。

The simplest are so easy to make a six year old can do it, thereby starting a life time of creating minor irritation by letter-headed aerial bombardment

最简单的是让六岁的历史可以做到这一点，从而开始通过字母头空中轰炸产生轻微刺激的寿命

If you aren’t familiar with wind tunnels, I suggest a quick wiki on the topic.

如果您不熟悉风隧道，我建议在主题上快速维基。

Wind tunnels are a useful tool for development of high speed cars and aircraft, but we’re a little short of projects that big; in this case we decided to focus on letting people see the aerodynamics for themselves. Most people without an engineering background have never had an opportunity to see how air flow forms around objects, and given the chance find the results both interesting and often quite pretty. Using the tunnel to explain how paper aeroplanes generate lift gave us purpose, gave the young kids something to do to get involved and hopefully may have got someone a little more interested in aerodynamics or technology. It also allowed us to demo the wind tunnel to show people that they can make a working wind tunnel without much expense or difficulty.

风隧道是开发高速汽车和飞机的有用工具，但我们很短的项目很短;在这种情况下，我们决定专注于让人们自己看到空气动力学。大多数没有工程背景的人从未有机会看到空气流动如何形成物体，并且鉴于机会发现结果既有趣，经常相当漂亮。使用隧道解释纸飞机如何生成升力给了我们的目的，给了年轻的孩子要做的事情要做，希望可能有一个对空气动力学或技术感兴趣的人。它还允许我们演示风洞，以显示他们可以在没有多少费用或困难的情况下制作一个工作风洞。

With most of our projects we try to make our builds accessible and inexpensive so in this case we have built our wind tunnel mostly from cardboard and scrap:

随着我们的大部分项目，我们尝试使我们的构建能够且价格便宜，所以在这种情况下，我们主要从纸板和废料中建立了风洞：

This project has seen some keen skip-diving, including the acquisition of our flow straightener. Our first thought was some honeycomb composite core material, but the price turned our stomachs a little. We then looked into the more usual drinking straw approach. While cutting down hundreds of straws to inch long sections and gluing them together works well, I jumped at the opportunity to avoid the hassle when I saw a skip full of office lighting diffuser grids of the appropriate dimensions. Taping three grids together in series seems to work well for straightening the air entering the tunnel.

这个项目已经看到一些敏锐的跳跃潜水，包括获取我们的流动矫直机。我们的第一个思想是一些蜂窝复合核心材料，但价格让我们的肚子变得有点。然后我们看着更常见的饮用秸秆方法。在将数百个吸管切割成英寸长的部分并将它们粘在一起效果良好，我跳起来有机会避免麻烦，当我看到充满办公室照明扩散器网格的适当尺寸时。串联三个网格似乎很好地矫正进入隧道的空气。

In an initial build we guessed a suitable shape for the collector, thinking we would remake it. In further research I found a NASA wind tunnel design aimed for schools to build that was almost identical, so we decided to leave it as it was.

在初始构建中，我们猜到了收集器的合适形状，认为我们会重塑它。在进一步的研究中，我发现一个美国宇航局风洞设计，旨在为学校建造几乎相同，所以我们决定将其留下。

Our working section is the only part not built from scrap. The body of this section was laser cut from plywood, and the screens were cut from new acrylic for clear viewing. The clips used to mount the paper plane were 3D printed on an PP3DP Up! 2. The base of the clip has a neodymium magnet push fit inside for easy swapping of planes. The clip magnets were stuck onto a steel rule taped to the bottom of the working section allowing easy positioning.

我们的工作部分是唯一没有从废料构建的部分。该部分的主体是从胶合板切割的激光，并从新的丙烯酸切割筛网以清晰的观察。用于安装纸平面的夹子在PP3DP上打印3D！ 2.夹子的底座具有内部钕磁铁推动配合，以便于平面易于交换。夹子磁铁卡上粘贴到粘贴到工作部分底部的钢结构上，允许易于定位。

To visualise flow within the working section we used smoke trails introduced by another 3D printed part, an aerodynamically streamlined splitter nozzle. The smoke itself is actually a non-toxic vapour generated by heating a  water/ glycerine/propylene  glycol  mix. To do this safely we used a ‘Zero Blaster’ toy smoke ring gun with 3D printed adapters (you may have guessed I recently got access to a new Up! 3D printer at work), with a small fan to blow the smoke out of the chamber through a straw.  LED strip lighting illuminated the smoke trails, although while the smoke was visible at low speeds it was very faint as the fan speed increased.

为了在工作部分内可视化流动，我们使用由另一个3D印刷部分引入的烟雾跟踪，空气动力学流线型分离喷嘴。烟雾本身实际上是通过加热水/甘油/丙二醇混合物产生的无毒蒸气。要安全地做到这一点，我们使用了一个带有3D印刷适配器的“零弹簧的玩具烟圈枪（你可能已经猜到了我最近访问了一个新的！3D打印机在工作中），带有一个小风扇吹出烟雾房间通过稻草。 LED剥离照明照亮了烟雾跟踪，虽然烟雾以低速可见，但由于风扇速度的增加是非常微弱的。

Our original build saw an ugly square to hexagon to circle diffuser, but on discovering the NASA plans linked above we remade it to a design that was   similar   albeit of cardboard and gaffa tape construction.

我们的原始构建将一个丑陋的广场呈六角形到圈子扩散器，但是在发现上面相关的美国宇航局计划，我们将其重塑到类似纸板和面包胶带施工的设计。

When we started out we were worried that a standard pedestal fan wouldn’t have the beans to create the required airflow, but a quick pre-build build showed that my living room fan could easily kite my favourite paper plane design – the most stable design I know – on a short tether from a crocodile clip. Call me psychic, but I soon sensed that filling our living room with a very untidy cardboard wind tunnel taped to our only fan the very day the UK plunged into a 35°C heatwave was not the romantic gesture my relationship needed. The obvious solution was skip diving (of course). Mark and I liberated a pedestal fan with a broken pedestal from a nearby skip and the tunnel relocated to my garage. Problem solved.

当我们开始时，我们担心标准的基座风扇不会让豆类创造所需的气流，但快速的预制构建显示，我的起居室风扇可以轻松地风筝是我最喜欢的纸飞机设计 - 最稳定的设计我知道 - 从鳄鱼夹的短系绳上。叫我心理，但我很快就感到了填充我们的起居室，带着一个非常不整洁的纸板风洞贴在我们唯一的粉丝中，英国陷入了35°C的热浪不是我所需要的关系的浪漫姿态。明显的解决方案是跳过潜水（当然）。 Mark和我用从附近的跳过和隧道搬到我的车库的隧道上用一个破碎的基座来解放了一个基座风扇。问题解决了。

Possibly the most unexpectedly popular feature of the wind tunnel was the big green button we used to activate the fan. Many kids seemed to have an unstoppable urge to press the button without even knowing what it did. It was pressed… a lot…

可能是风洞最常见的流行功能是我们用于激活风扇的大绿色按钮。许多孩子似乎都有一个不可阻挡的冲动，甚至不知道它所做的内容就不达到了按钮。它被按下......很多......

It was a green arcade button that Mark wired to a timed relay and put in a really nice laser cut plywood box.

这是一个绿色街机按钮，标记到定时继电器​​并放入一个非常好的激光切割胶合板盒。

Aside from that, adding a way to measure lift/drag ratio might be a nice way to add a competitive edge to the plane testing (for the older participants to enjoy;)

除此之外，增加了测量升力/阻力比的方法可能是向平面测试添加竞争优势的好方法（对于旧的参与者享受;）

Perhaps a linkage covering an arc with a pointer, or a couple of microbalances, but that is for later…

也许是一种覆盖带指针的弧的连杆，或者几个微稳定，但这是以后的......

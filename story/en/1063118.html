Google Joins Apple to Opt-out of Mobile Advertising IDs
谷歌加入Apple解Ot退出移动广告ID

2021-06-03 23:43:07

Facebook has a market evaluation of over 930 billion dollars today. That’s billion with a B — and just shy of becoming a trillion-dollar company.

Facebook今天的市场评估超过9300亿美元。这是一个B - 而且只是害羞成为万亿美元的公司。

Through shrewd acquisitions, blatantly copying the exclusivity of rivals (read: Snapchat, TikTok), Facebook managed to become an integral part of our lives in less than a decade.

通过精明的收购，公布抄袭竞争对手的排他性（阅读：Snapchat，Tiktok），Facebook设法在不到十年内成为我们生活中的一个组成部分。

We all use their free services like Instagram and WhatsApp almost every day. Even if try staying at an arm’s length from the Facebook family of apps, be it by uninstalling, deactivating your profile, the company still knows who you are and continues to build a shadow profile.

我们都像Instagram一样使用自由服务，几乎每天都在Whatsapp。即使尝试从Facebook系列的Apps的ARM的长度停留，通过卸载，取消激活您的个人资料，该公司仍然知道您是谁，并继续构建影子配置文件。

Some might wonder how’s that possible? Especially since they’ve not on the app. Others’ would ponder over Facebook’s evergrowing market valuation and how it’s even justified? More so, since the social media giant doesn’t charge a penny.

有些人可能想知道怎么可能了？特别是因为他们没有在应用程序上。其他人会思考Facebook的Evergring市场估值以及甚至是合理的？因此，由于社交媒体巨头不收取一分钱。

A few years ago in an anti-trust court hearing, Mark Zuckerberg had put Facebook’s business revenue mysteries to rest.

几年前，在反信托法院听证会上，Mark Zuckerberg将Facebook的商业收入奥秘休息。

“Senator, We Run Ads” was the iconic response from Facebook’s CEO back then.

“参议员，我们运行广告”是Facebook首席执行官的标志性反应。

And that’s how Facebook still manages to stay free of cost today. It’s also the same reason why the company manages to track you even if you aren’t using their apps.

这就是Facebook今天仍然可以携带的费用。它也是公司的原因，即使您不使用其应用程序也是为了跟踪您的原因。

For instance, if you’ve uploaded a photo to Facebook, the company can figure the metadata. No wonder, my newsfeed was flooded with weight gain product ads when I was thin (and beard-grooming products during lockdowns).

例如，如果您已将照片上载到Facebook，该公司可以铭记元数据。难怪，当我薄（锁定期间的胡须制作产品）时，我的新闻充斥着重量增益产品广告。

Similarly, when walking in a mall or supermarket, you’d probably see a bunch of ads about good deals nearby. This is because Facebook could secretly track your location and run targeted campaigns. Despite enhanced location permissions over the years, Facebook had a way around it too. They could leverage Bluetooth tracking to identify people around and deduce your relationships with them.

同样，在商场或超市行走时，您可能会看到关于附近的好交易的广告。这是因为Facebook可以秘密跟踪您的位置并运行目标广告系列。尽管多年来，尽管存在增强的位置权限，但Facebook也有办法。他们可以利用蓝牙跟踪来识别周围的人并与他们推断你的关系。

Worse, even if you did ditch the Bluetooth and deleted the Facebook family of apps, they could still track and understand your behavior through deep links.

更糟糕的是，即使你这样做了蓝牙并删除了​​Facebook系列的应用程序，他们仍然可以通过深度链接跟踪并理解您的行为。

For those who’re new, deep links are web URLs that help redirect mobile users to the app directly instead of a website. Most of the third-party apps today integrate Facebook SDK to add deep links. So, Facebook could easily collect your Advertiser ID every time you clicked a link to install a new app.

对于那些新的，深度链接是网络URL，它可以直接将移动用户重定向到应用而不是网站。今天的大多数第三方应用程序集成了Facebook SDK以添加深度链接。因此，Facebook可以在每次单击链接以安装新应用程序时轻松收集您的广告商ID。

As a result, they could easily match you with advertisers, who would then run targeted campaigns on you, your Facebook friends to entice more ad impressions.

因此，他们可以轻松地与广告商匹配，然后将有针对性的广告系列，您的Facebook好友运行有针对性的活动，以吸引更多的广告印象。

Howsoever cliche it sounds, when a service is free of cost, you’d probably know whose the product.

无论陈词滥调它听起来，当服务没有成本时，你可能会知道其产品。

Facebook manages to collect terabytes of first-party data from mobile and web users and subsequently became the king of personalized ads. Unsurprisingly, companies started dedicating a part of their market budget towards Facebook to help increase their exposure.

Facebook管理从移动设备和网络用户收集第一方数据的Terabytes，随后成为个性化广告之王。不出所料，公司开始将其市场预算的一部分致力于Facebook，以帮助增加其曝光率。

And soon, Facebook set up an Audience Network and created a billion-dollar business out of in-app ads.

很快，Facebook设置了一个受众网络，并在应用内广告中创建了一亿美元的业务。

However, technology has started to undergo enhancements in the last few years. Google and Apple’s focus on privacy has ensured Facebook’s road to a trillion won’t be a cakewalk. Facebook won’t stay a trillion-dollar business for long.

但是，技术已经开始在过去几年中进行增强。谷歌和苹果专注于隐私，确保了Facebook的一万亿的道路不会成为Cakewalk。 Facebook不会长时间留下万亿美元的业务。

Android and iOS occupy the majority share of the mobile operating systems. Together, Google and Apple have eliminated all ways to track a user’s advertisement ID and limit cross-app tracking.

Android和iOS占据移动操作系统的大多数份额。一起，谷歌和苹果公司已经消除了跟踪用户的广告ID并限制交叉应用跟踪的所有方式。

Apple’s iOS 14 App Tracking Transparency Drew the First Blood in the Ad-Wars

Apple的iOS 14应用程序跟踪透明度在广告战争中制作了第一个血液

Facebook and Apple have a strained history that encompasses a decade. But, it’s the iPhone maker’s recent push towards privacy that’s beefed up their feud.

Facebook和Apple有一个紧张的历史，包括十年。但是，这是iPhone制造商最近推动隐私，这是他们的仇恨。

Apple first embarrassed Facebook by including a Bluetooth permission popup when users launch the app for the first time. They further rubbed salt in the wounds by limiting background location tracking and giving users a choice to use approximate location tracking.

当用户第一次启动应用程序时，Apple首次尴尬Facebook通过蓝牙权限弹出。它们通过限制背景位置跟踪并为用户提供使用近似位置跟踪来进一步摩擦伤口中的盐。

Yet, it was the iOS 14 update that made Facebook more worried. For the uninitiated, Apple released a new opt-in ad tracking popup that lets the user decide whether to give apps access to their device-specific advertising ID (IDFA) or not.

然而，它是iOS 14更新，使Facebook更担心。对于uninItitiveation，Apple发布了一个新的选择in AD跟踪弹出窗口，让用户决定是否为其提供特定于设备的广告ID（IDFA）的应用程序。

Unanimously, users would opt out of cross-app tracking by choosing “Ask App Not To Track” thereby limiting Facebook’s ability to track our behavior, reducing their reach, and ways of monetization on the smartphone.

一致，用户将通过选择“询问应用程序不跟踪”来选择跨应用跟踪，从而限制了Facebook追踪我们行为的能力，降低了他们的范围，以及智能手机上的货币化方式。

Facebook and advertisers' loud outcry succeeded in convincing Apple to postpone that privacy update. But despite best efforts, iOS 14.5 finally launched App Tracking Transparency and got a positive response from the iPhone users.

Facebook和广告商＆＃39;响亮的屈尊伴随着说服苹果推迟了隐私更新。但尽管尽力努力，iOS 14.5最终推出了应用跟踪透明度，并从iPhone用户获得了积极的回复。

Last year, I poured my thoughts on the impact Apple’s iOS 14 privacy update would have on the whole smartphone ecosystem.

去年，我倾注了我对苹果的影响力的IOS 14隐私更新将在整个智能手机生态系统上进行思考。

My prediction of Facebook being the biggest loser was accurate. I also thought Google would roll out a similar feature with the Android 12 this year.

我对Facebook的预测是最大的失败者是准确的。我也想到谷歌将在今年与Android 12推出类似的功能。

Android 12 got the biggest privacy boost in recent years. Most were analogous to iOS 14's privacy features with an exception in Bluetooth permission. By removing access to  location services from Bluetooth, Google managed to weaken Facebook’s location-based advertisement business.

Android 12近年来获得了最大的隐私提升。大多数类似于iOS 14＆＃39;在蓝牙许可中的隐私功能。通过从蓝牙中删除对位置服务的访问，谷歌设法削弱了Facebook的基于位置的广告业务。

However, the absence of a similar feature like App Tracking Transparency from Google I/O 2021 was a bummer. Gladly, though, our disappointment was short-lived.

但是，缺少Google I / O 2021的应用跟踪透明度等类似的特征是一个bummer。但很乐意，我们的失望是短暂的。

Only yesterday, Google announced its plans to make advertising ID an opt-in feature. Here’s an excerpt from their  support page:

只有昨天，谷歌宣布其计划使广告ID成为一个选择的功能。以下是他们的支持页面摘录：

Starting in late 2021, when a user opts out of interest-based advertising or ads personalization, the advertising identifier will not be available. You will receive a string of zeros in place of the identifier.

从2021年底开始，当用户选择超出基于兴趣的广告或广告个性化时，广告标识符将无法使用。您将收到一串Zeros代替标识符。

To put it straight, Google’s advertising ID (GAID) can no longer be accessed by advertisers if the user disables it from their settings.

要将其直接，如果用户从其设置禁用它，则无法再访问谷歌的广告ID（GAID）。

GAID is similar to Apple’s IDFA in the aspect that both are used to monetize off users’ interests.

GAIA类似于Apple的IDFA，两者都用于将用户兴趣批准。

Now that Google is pulling the plug by making GAID almost obsolete, Facebook is about to stare at a bigger loss. If their loss from iOS 14 was in the range of $80bn, Android the more dominant platform will only increase their losses and woes.

现在，谷歌正在通过几乎已经过时地拉动插头，Facebook即将盯着更大的损失。如果他们从iOS 14的损失在80亿美元的范围内，Android将更多的占优势平台只会增加他们的损失和困境。

Google’s opt-out ad-tracking popup isn’t on the forefront like in iOS. Instead, it’s buried deep in the Google Play Service settings — which could be difficult for the non-tech savvy user to find out.

谷歌的退出广告跟踪弹出窗口不是在iOS中的最前沿。相反，它在谷歌游戏服务设置中深入掩盖 - 这可能很难找到难以找到的。

Regardless, the move by the search engine giant is surprising. Much like Facebook, advertisements are a major chunk of Google’s business as well.

无论如何，搜索引擎巨头的移动令人惊讶。就像Facebook一样，广告也是谷歌业务的主要块。

Maybe, they don’t mind losing out on ads given they already have so much first-party data from Android and Google’s suite of apps?

也许，他们不介意在鉴于Android和Google的Apps套件中拥有如此多的第一方数据

When Facebook launched an all-out battle against Apple last year, little did they know that the war would be two-front.

当Facebook去年对Apple发出全方位的战斗时，他们很少知道战争将是两端的。

Clearly, this chain of events is about to trigger an irreversible slump in Facebook’s advertisement business — making their ambitions of becoming the fastest trillion-dollar tech firm a far-fetched dream.

显然，这种事件链即将触发Facebook的广告业务的不可逆转衰退 - 使他们成为最快的万亿美元的技术公司成为一个偏远的梦想。

All is not over for Facebook though. They have AR/VR social media plans in the pipeline.

虽然，所有人都没有结束。他们在管道中拥有AR / VR社交媒体计划。

However, if Mark Zuckerberg goes back on the drawing board to figure what went wrong, he’ll probably realize that data breaches and privacy awareness aren’t the causes of an impending downfall.

然而，如果Mark Zuckerberg回到绘图板上来弄错了出现问题，他可能会意识到数据违规和隐私意识并不是即将垮台的原因。

Instead, a lack of effort in developing their own operating system a decade ago might be Facebook’s biggest regret. And this has come back to bite them today.

相反，十年前在开发自己的操作系统方面缺乏努力可能是Facebook最大的遗憾。这回到了今天咬他们的。

In the end, the battle is about privacy, but it’s also about collecting first-party data. Facebook has a lot, but they’re heavily reliant on Google and Apple — both of whom own the popular operating systems and browsers, which gives them leverage.

最后，战斗是关于隐私，但它也是关于收集第一方的数据。 Facebook有很多，但他们在谷歌和苹果中依赖依赖 - 两个人拥有流行的操作系统和浏览器，这让他们杠杆作用。

Astronomers Discover New 'Fossil Galaxy' Buried Deep Within the Milky Way
天文学家发现银河系深处埋藏的新“化石星系”

2020-11-22 16:37:12

Scientists working with data from the Sloan Digital Sky Surveys' Apache Point Observatory Galactic Evolution Experiment (APOGEE) have discovered a "fossil galaxy" hidden in the depths of our own Milky Way. This result, published today in Monthly Notices of the Royal Astronomical Society, may shake up our understanding of how the Milky Way grew into the galaxy we see today.

斯隆数字天空调查的阿帕奇点天文台银河演化实验（APOGEE）的数据科学家们发现了一个隐藏在我们银河系深处的“化石星系”。今天发表在《皇家天文学会月刊》上的这一结果可能会动摇我们对银河系如何成长为我们今天所看到的银河系的理解。

The proposed fossil galaxy may have collided with the Milky Way ten billion years ago, when our galaxy was still in its infancy. Astronomers named it Heracles, after the ancient Greek hero who received the gift of immortality when the Milky Way was created.

提议的化石星系可能在100亿年前与银河系相撞，那时我们的银河系还处于起步阶段。天文学家将其命名为Heracles，以古希腊英雄的名字命名，该英雄在银河系诞生时就获得了永生的礼物。

The remnants of Heracles account for about one third of the Milky Way's spherical halo. But if stars and gas from Heracles make up such a large percentage of the galactic halo, why didn't we see it before? The answer lies in its location deep inside the Milky Way.

赫拉克勒斯残余物约占银河系球形晕圈的三分之一。但是，如果来自Heracles的恒星和气体占银河晕的很大比例，为什么我们以前没有看到它呢？答案在于它在银河系深处的位置。

"To find a fossil galaxy like this one, we had to look at the detailed chemical makeup and motions of tens of thousands of stars," says Ricardo Schiavon from Liverpool John Moores University (LJMU) in the UK, a key member of the research team. "That is especially hard to do for stars in the center of the Milky Way, because they are hidden from view by clouds of interstellar dust. APOGEE lets us pierce through that dust and see deeper into the heart of the Milky Way than ever before."

英国利物浦约翰摩尔大学（LJMU）的里卡多·席亚文（Ricardo Schiavon）说：“要找到像这样的化石星系，我们必须研究成千上万颗恒星的详细化学组成和运动。”球队。 “对于银河系中心的恒星来说，这样做尤其困难，因为它们被星际尘埃云遮住了视线。APOGEE让我们穿透了尘埃，比以往任何时候都更深入银河系的中心。 ”

APOGEE does this by taking spectra of stars in near- , instead of visible light, which gets obscured by dust. Over its ten-year observational life, APOGEE has measured spectra for more than half a million stars all across the Milky Way, including its previously dust-obscured core.

APOGEE通过获取近处的恒星光谱而非可见光来做到这一点，可见光被尘埃遮盖。在其十年的观测寿命中，APOGEE已测量了整个银河系中超过一百万颗恒星的光谱，包括先前被尘埃遮盖的核心。

Graduate student Danny Horta from LJMU, the lead author of the paper announcing the result, explains, "examining such a large number of stars is necessary to find unusual stars in the densely-populated heart of the Milky Way, which is like finding needles in a haystack."

宣布结果的论文的主要作者，来自LJMU的研究生Danny Horta解释说：“检查如此多的恒星对于在银河系人口稠密的心脏中发现不寻常的恒星是必要的，就像在恒河中寻找针头一样。大海捞针。”

To separate stars belonging to Heracles from those of the original Milky Way, the team made use of both chemical compositions and velocities of stars measured by the APOGEE instrument.

为了将属于Heracles的恒星与原始银河系的恒星分开，研究小组利用了APOGEE仪器测量的恒星的化学成分和速度。

"Of the tens of thousands of stars we looked at, a few hundred had strikingly different chemical compositions and velocities," Horta said. "These stars are so different that they could only have come from another galaxy. By studying them in detail, we could trace out the precise location and history of this fossil galaxy."

奥尔塔说：“在我们观察的数万颗恒星中，有数百颗的化学组成和速度截然不同。” “这些恒星是如此不同，以致它们只能来自另一个星系。通过对其进行详细研究，我们可以找到该化石星系的确切位置和历史。”

Because galaxies are built through mergers of smaller galaxies across time, the remnants of older galaxies are often spotted in the outer halo of the Milky Way, a huge but very sparse cloud of stars enveloping the main galaxy. But since our galaxy built up from the inside out, finding the earliest mergers requires looking at the most central parts of the Milky Way's halo, which are buried deep within the disc and bulge.

由于星系是通过较小的星系合并而成的，因此，较老的星系的残留物通常被发现在银河系的外环，这是一个巨大但稀疏的星云，包围着主要星系。但是，由于我们的银河系是由内而外建立的，要找到最早的合并，就需要研究银河系光环的最中央部分，这些部分深埋在圆盘和凸起内。

Stars originally belonging to Heracles account for roughly one third of the mass of the entire Milky Way halo today—meaning that this newly-discovered ancient collision must have been a major event in the history of our galaxy. That suggests that our galaxy may be unusual, since most similar massive spiral had much calmer early lives.

最初属于Heracles的恒星约占今天整个银河系光晕质量的三分之一，这意味着这种新近发现的古代碰撞一定是我们银河系历史上的重大事件。这表明我们的银河系可能是不寻常的，因为大多数类似的大螺旋具有更平静的早期生活。

"As our cosmic home, the Milky Way is already special to us, but this ancient galaxy buried within makes it even more special," Schiavon says.

Schiavon说：“作为我们的宇宙之家，银河系对我们来说已经很特别了，但是埋藏在其中的古老星系使其变得更加特别。”

Karen Masters, the Spokesperson for SDSS-IV comments, "APOGEE is one of the flagship surveys of the fourth phase of SDSS, and this result is an example of the amazing science that anyone can do, now that we have almost completed our ten-year mission."

SDSS-IV发言人Karen Masters表示：“ APOGEE是SDSS第四阶段的旗舰调查之一，由于我们几乎已经完成了十项研究，年任务。”

And this new age of discovery will not end with the completion of APOGEE observations. The fifth phase of the SDSS has already begun taking data, and its "Milky Way Mapper" will build on the success of APOGEE to measure spectra for ten times as many in all parts of the Milky Way, using near-infrared light, visible light, and sometimes both. More information: "Evidence from APOGEE for the Presence of a Major Building Block of the Halo Buried in the Inner Galaxy," Danny Horta et al., 2020 Nov. 20, Monthly Notices of the Royal Astronomical Society: arxiv.org/abs/2007.10374].

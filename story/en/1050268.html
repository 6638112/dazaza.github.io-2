New EU ‘right to repair’ laws require technology to last for a decade - New devices will also have to come with repair manuals and be made in such a way that they can be dismantled using conventional tools
欧盟新的“维修权”法律要求技术必须持续十年-新设备还必须随附维修手册，并且必须以可以使用传统工具拆除的方式进行制造

2021-03-02 06:54:49

Companies that sell consumer electronics such as refrigerators, washers, hairdryers, or TVs in the European Union - and in the UK - will need to ensure those goods can be repaired for up to 10 years.

在欧盟以及英国，销售诸如冰箱，洗衣机，吹风机或电视之类的消费电子产品的公司将需要确保这些产品可以维修长达10年。

The change comes as a result of legislation from the European Parliament, which recently voted in favour of establishing stronger “right to repair” rules. These rules should help reduce electrical waste, which has been increasing due to greater manufacturing.

这种变化是欧洲议会立法的结果，欧洲议会最近投票赞成建立更强大的“维修权”规则。这些规则应有助于减少电气浪费，这种电气浪费由于更高的制造水平而不断增加。

While the UK has left the European Union, the UK’s manufacturing standards will necessarily have to match those of the 27 nation bloc, in order for any trade to continue. The rule comes into effect today.

尽管英国已经退出欧盟，但为了继续进行任何贸易，英国的制造标准将必须与27国集团的制造标准相匹配。该规则从今天开始生效。

"This is a really big step in the right direction" said Daniel Affelt of the environmental group BUND-Berlin, which runs several "repair cafes" where people can bring in their broken appliances and get help fixing them up again.

＆＃34;这是朝正确方向迈出的非常重要的一步＆＃34;环保组织BUND-Berlin的Daniel Affelt说，该组织经营着几家“维修咖啡厅”。人们可以带回他们损坏的电器，并获得帮助以再次修复它们。

Modern appliances are often glued or riveted together, he said. "If you need specialist tools or have to break open the device, then you can't repair it."

他说，现代家用电器经常被粘在一起或铆接在一起。 ＆＃34;如果您需要专业工具或必须拆开设备，则无法维修。

Lack of spare parts is another problem, campaigners say. Sometimes a single broken tooth on a tiny plastic sprocket can throw a proverbial wrench in the works.

活动家说，缺少零件是另一个问题。有时，一个小的塑料链轮上的单个断齿会在工作中抛出一个众所周知的扳手。

"People want to repair their appliances," Affelt said. "When you tell them that there are no spare parts for a device that's only a couple of years old then they are obviously really frustrated by that."

人们想修理他们的电器，阿弗尔特说。当您告诉他们只有两年之久的设备没有备件时，他们显然对此感到沮丧。

Under the new EU rules, manufacturers will have to ensure parts are available for up to a decade, though some will only be provided to professional repair companies to ensure they are installed correctly.

根据新的欧盟规定，制造商将必须确保零件的使用期限长达十年，尽管某些零件只会提供给专业维修公司以确保正确安装。

New devices will also have to come with repair manuals and be made in such a way that they can be dismantled using conventional tools when they really can't be fixed anymore, to improve recycling.

新设备还必须随附维修手册，并以这样的方式进行制造：在确实无法修复时，可以使用常规工具将其拆除，以提高回收率。

Each year, Europeans produce more than 16 kilograms of electrical waste per person. About half of that junk is due to broken household appliances, and the EU recycles only about 40 per cent of it, leaving behind huge amounts of potentially hazardous material.

每年，欧洲人平均每人产生超过16千克的电气废物。大约一半的垃圾是由于家用电器损坏造成的，欧盟仅回收了其中的40％，留下了大量潜在的危险材料。

German Environment Minister Svenja Schulze said that in a next step, manufacturers should have to state how long a product is expected to work for and repair it if it breaks down earlier. This would encourage companies to build more durable products, she said.

德国环境部长Svenja Schulze表示，下一步，制造商应说明产品预期使用多长时间，如果产品较早出现故障，应进行维修。她说，这将鼓励公司制造更耐用的产品。

"In the repair cafes we see a lot of devices that broke shortly after the warranty expired," said Affelt — a phenomenon that has prompted some environmentalists to accuse manufacturers of designing their devices with planned obsolescence.

＆＃34;在维修网吧中，我们看到许多设备在保修到期后不久就坏了，＆＃34; Affelt说，这种现象促使一些环保主义者指责制造商在计划过时的情况下设计其设备。

Knowing an appliance will really last for a decade might prompt consumers to choose products that are more durable or can be easily fixed, he said.

他说，知道一种设备确实可以使用十年，可能会促使消费者选择更耐用或易于固定的产品。

"For the vast majority of devices, repair is the right choice," said Affelt, adding that the exception might be old, inefficient refrigerators that can contain powerful greenhouse gases which fuel climate change.

对于绝大多数设备，维修是正确的选择， Affelt说，并补充说例外可能是老式的，效率低下的冰箱，其中可能包含导致气候变化的强大温室气体。

In a next step, environmentalists and consumer rights groups want the "right to repair" expanded to include smartphones, laptops and other small electrical devices.

下一步，环保主义者和消费者权益组织希望获得“维修权”。扩展到包括智能手机，笔记本电脑和其他小型电气设备。

Responding to growing demand, Apple last year announced it would start providing training and spare parts to certified independent repair stores fixing Mac computers, not just iPhones.

为了应对不断增长的需求，苹果公司去年宣布将开始为经过认证的独立维修店提供培训和备件，这些维修店修理Mac电脑，而不仅仅是iPhone。

Right to repair bills have been introduced in several US state legislatures, attracting bipartisan support, though as yet there is no nationwide measure in force.

虽然美国尚无有效的全国性措施，但美国的几个州立法机构已经引入了维修权法案，得到了两党的支持。

Sweden has gone further than most of the EU, making repairs and spare parts subject to lower value-added tax.

瑞典比欧盟大多数国家走得更远，使维修和备件应缴纳较低的增值税。

The bloc's ecological design directive — of which the right to repair requirement is a part — will also revise existing energy labels that describe how much electricity washers and other household devices consume. The new seven-step scale from A to G will be complemented by a QR code that provides consumers with further information, such as how loud the devices are.

欧盟的生态设计指令（其中要求维修的权利是其中的一部分）也将修订现有的能源标签，以描述洗衣机和其他家用设备的消耗量。从A到G的新的7步刻度将由QR码补充，该QR码可为消费者提供更多信息，例如设备的声音大小。

Right to repair laws have also seen some traction across the Atlantic; in 2019, California became the 18th state to propose similar laws in order to make companies like Apple easier for users to fix should something go wrong.

维修权法也已经在整个大西洋引起了一定的关注。在2019年，加利福尼亚州成为第18个提出类似法律的州，以使像Apple这样的公司在出现问题时更易于用户修复。

由于缺乏单一的全国性应用程序，美国的联系人追踪一直受到阻碍，10个州和华盛顿特区创建了自己的应用程序，而大多数州根本没有应用程序

2020-10-22 18:38:42

Months after a burst of optimism about the potential for technology to help track the spread of Covid-19, a hodgepodge of mobile phone apps around the U.S. has yielded unclear results amid inconsistent policies and usage.

几个月前，人们对技术帮助追踪新冠肺炎传播的潜力感到乐观，但由于政策和使用情况不一致，美国各地的手机应用大杂烩产生了不明朗的结果。

Ten states and Washington, D.C., have launched apps that notify users of exposure to infected people through a development framework built by Alphabet Google and Apple Inc. Eleven more are either piloting or building such tools.

十个州和华盛顿特区已经推出了应用程序，通过Alphabet Google和苹果公司(Apple Inc.)建立的开发框架通知用户接触感染者。另有11家公司正在试验或建造这样的工具。

But the lack of a national strategy—unlike in many European countries that have adopted such apps—adds a hurdle to making sure the tools work across state lines as case counts tick upward, tech and public health experts say.

但科技和公共卫生专家表示，与许多采用此类应用的欧洲国家不同，缺乏国家战略为确保这些工具在病例数量上升时跨州工作增加了障碍。

“If you’re going to have a lot of jurisdictional boundaries that cut through this, it will naturally make the system less effective,” said Eric Rescorla, chief technology officer of Mozilla Corp.’s Firefox web browser.

Mozilla Corp.的Firefox网络浏览器的首席技术官埃里克·雷斯考拉(Eric Rescorla)说，“如果你要有大量的管辖边界来突破这一点，自然会降低系统的效率。”

Advances in the Bluetooth-based Google-Apple system in recent months have reduced the cost for states to opt in, and created a central server to store anonymized markers of positive tests for when phones cross state lines.

近几个月来，基于蓝牙的谷歌-苹果系统的进步降低了各州选择加入的成本，并创建了一个中央服务器，用于存储手机跨越州线时的匿名阳性检测标记。

Some state governments also are collaborating to roll out tools they see as a useful addition to a disease-fighting tool kit that includes mask wearing, social distancing and traditional contact tracing through phone calls and other manual outreach.

一些州政府还在合作推出他们认为是抗击疾病工具包的有用补充工具，其中包括戴口罩、社交距离，以及通过电话和其他手动外联追踪传统接触者。

California, Oregon and Washington last month announced pilot programs for apps on the Google-Apple system. Pennsylvania and Delaware last month launched apps created by Irish developer NearForm Ltd., which has built tools for several European countries. New York and New Jersey unveiled NearForm-made apps this month.

加利福尼亚州、俄勒冈州和华盛顿州上个月宣布了谷歌-苹果系统上应用程序的试点计划。宾夕法尼亚州和特拉华州上个月推出了爱尔兰开发商NearForm Ltd.开发的应用程序，该公司已经为几个欧洲国家开发了工具。纽约和新泽西本月推出了NearForm制造的应用程序。

“You have a lot of cross-border travel,” said Larry Schwartz, who led New York’s app push on the state’s Covid-19 task force. “We have people from New Jersey who work in New York, and vice versa.”

“你有很多跨境旅行，”拉里·施瓦茨(Larry Schwartz)说，他领导了纽约州新冠肺炎特别工作组在纽约的APP推广活动。“我们有来自新泽西的人在纽约工作，反之亦然。”

About 1.2 million people have downloaded the NearForm apps across New York, Pennsylvania, New Jersey and Delaware, according to the company and state officials.

据NearForm公司和州官员称，纽约、宾夕法尼亚州、新泽西州和特拉华州约有120万人下载了NearForm应用程序。

In Delaware, about 10% of people who test positive in the state report that they have downloaded its app, said Molly Magarik, Department of Health and Social Services secretary. More than 80% of those users say they accepted a six-digit code that verified their test result and allowed their phone to send notifications to other devices, she said.

在特拉华州，大约10%的州检测呈阳性的人报告说，他们已经下载了它的应用程序，卫生和社会服务部部长莫莉·马加里克(Molly Magarik)说。她说，这些用户中超过80%的人说，他们接受了一个六位数的代码，该代码验证了他们的测试结果，并允许他们的手机向其他设备发送通知。

Still, some public health experts remain skeptical that the apps will push users to isolate or get tested.

尽管如此，一些公共卫生专家仍然怀疑这些应用程序是否会推动用户隔离或接受检测。

“What evidence do we have that these make a difference?” said James Thomas, a professor of epidemiology at the University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill. “Is it a placebo for us to feel better about what we’re doing—and feel like we have a high-tech response—or does it make a dent in the number of cases we’re seeing?”

“我们有什么证据证明这些能起到作用呢？”北卡罗来纳大学教堂山分校的流行病学教授詹姆斯·托马斯说。“对我们来说，这是一种安慰剂，让我们对正在做的事情感觉更好--感觉我们得到了高科技的回应--还是减少了我们看到的病例数量？”

In some cases, the lack of evidence is by design, said Joanna Masel, a University of Arizona biology professor who is helping to lead the state’s app pilot program at her school and Northern Arizona University. About 33,000 people have downloaded the app around the university campuses, Ms. Masel said, and those who tested positive for Covid-19 increasingly are entering verification codes on the app. But she declined to provide specific numbers for the latter metric.

亚利桑那大学生物学教授乔安娜·马塞尔(Joanna Masel)说，在某些情况下，缺乏证据是故意的。她正在帮助领导该州在她所在的学校和北亚利桑那大学的APP试点项目。马塞尔说，大学校园里大约有3.3万人下载了这款应用，那些新冠肺炎检测呈阳性的人越来越多地在应用上输入验证码。但她拒绝提供后一项指标的具体数字。

“The reason we’re not sharing information is we’re really concerned people will think it’s not private,” she said.

“我们不分享信息的原因是我们真的很担心人们会认为这不是隐私，”她说。

While some public health experts prefer Global Positioning System data to aid traditional contact tracing, which tracks people’s interactions and movements, privacy experts warn it could lead to surveillance.

虽然一些公共卫生专家更喜欢全球定位系统(Global Positioning System)的数据来帮助传统的接触者追踪，后者追踪人们的互动和行动，但隐私专家警告称，这可能会导致监控。

The Arizona project relies on a blueprint unveiled by Google and Apple in May using only Bluetooth-enabled alerts of users’ proximity rather than collecting GPS location data.

亚利桑那州的项目依赖于谷歌和苹果在5月份公布的一份蓝图，该蓝图只使用了蓝牙功能的用户接近警报，而不是收集GPS位置数据。

Apple last month rolled out an upgrade that allows iPhone users in participating states, like Colorado, to opt into the network without a dedicated app. The Association of Public Health Laboratories, a trade group, also has partnered with the two companies, as well as Microsoft , to develop a central server that stores anonymized verification codes for infected people across the Google-Apple system. Eight states with statewide apps and two with pilot programs have opted in so far, according to the trade group’s website.

苹果上个月推出了一项升级，允许科罗拉多州等参与州的iPhone用户选择加入网络，而不需要专用的应用程序。行业组织公共卫生实验室协会(Association Of Public Health Laboratory)也与这两家公司以及微软合作，开发了一种中央服务器，用于存储整个谷歌-苹果系统中感染者的匿名验证码。根据该行业组织的网站，到目前为止，已经有八个州拥有全州范围的应用程序，两个州拥有试点项目。

Still, 27 states haven’t launched an app or aren’t actively developing one, public health officials said. Many cited concerns about utility, in addition to real or perceived privacy fears.

公共卫生官员说，尽管如此，仍有27个州没有推出应用程序，或者没有积极开发应用程序。许多人提到了对公用事业的担忧，以及真实或感知的隐私担忧。

“There is much distrust around contact tracing as it is,” said Nancy Nydam, spokeswoman for the Georgia Department of Public Health.

佐治亚州公共卫生部发言人南希·尼达姆(Nancy Nydam)说：“人们对接触者追踪有很大的不信任。”

Attempts to use apps stalled in at least two states after they launched GPS-based tools from local developers in the early months of the crisis.

在危机的头几个月，至少有两个州推出了当地开发者提供的基于GPS的工具后，使用应用程序的尝试陷入停滞。

In April, South Dakota adopted the Care19 app built by a developer best known for an app that connects North Dakota State University football fans during the team’s frequent national championship runs. The state hasn’t moved forward with a tool on the Google-Apple system.

今年4月，南达科他州采用了由一位开发者开发的Care19应用程序，该应用程序最出名的是一款应用程序，在球队频繁参加全国锦标赛期间，该应用程序可以连接北达科他州州立大学的球迷。该州还没有在谷歌-苹果系统上推出任何工具。

Similarly, in Utah, the Healthy Together app disabled its location-tracking feature in July, said Jared Allgood, co-founder of Twenty Holdings Inc., the app’s developer. The company, which previously built an app called Twenty marketed as a tool to help “real friends” meet up in “real life,” remains in talks with Google and Apple to jump to their system, a spokeswoman said.

同样，在犹他州，Healthy Together应用的联合创始人贾里德·奥尔古德(Jared Allgood)说，该应用在7月份关闭了位置跟踪功能。20家控股公司是该应用的开发商。该公司的一位女发言人表示，该公司仍在与谷歌和苹果就跳转到他们的系统进行谈判。该公司之前开发了一款名为“20”的应用程序，作为一种工具来帮助“真正的朋友”在“现实生活”中相遇。

Healthy Together, which has more than 94,000 users in Utah, has shifted away from trying to track the virus and toward surveying symptoms and relaying test results, Mr. Allgood said.

奥尔古德说，Healthy Together在犹他州拥有超过9.4万名用户，它已经从试图追踪病毒转向调查症状和传递检测结果。

“We were early,” he added. “For everybody, it was their first pandemic.”

“我们来得很早，”他补充说。“对每个人来说，这都是他们第一次大流行。”

Why autoclaves operate at 121 degrees Celcius
为什么高压釜在121摄氏度下运行

2021-01-04 20:25:29

$\begingroup$ Let me start by saying I know why, but I am inquiring more about the origin.

$ \ begingroup $让我首先说我知道原因，但我在询问更多有关起源的信息。

My question is more related to literature that I cannot seem to find.I've found some helpful papers and information on the origin of the using 121°C for sterilizing equipment, and killing bacteria but I wanted to ask the question anyways and see if anyone knew something more about it.

我的问题与我似乎找不到的文献更相关。我找到了一些有用的论文和信息，涉及使用121°C进行设备消毒和杀死细菌的起源，但无论如何我想问这个问题，看看是否有人对此有更多了解。

So to restate my question, I want to see data as to why 121°C and not 120°C?

因此，重申一下我的问题，我想查看有关为何为何在121°C而不是120°C的数据？

Originally I though it is because the experiments might've been conducted in F and the inter-conversion from F to C yielded 121°C which is an odd number.

最初我是因为这可能是因为实验可能是在F中进行的，并且从F到C的相互转化产生121°C，这是一个奇数。

From what I could gather it started in the food industry with a company that made canned products and their canned products were exploding. This company approached MIT and after some research they found that at 121°C (250F) for 60 min in retort would kill the spores surviving the previous processing. (further exp. reduced the time to 10 min, for canned clams)

据我所知，它始于食品工业中的一家罐头产品制造公司，其罐头产品正在爆炸式增长。该公司与麻省理工学院联系，经过一番研究，他们发现在121°C（250°F）的温度下蒸煮60分钟会杀死在先前加工过程中存活的孢子。 （对于罐装蛤lam，进一步的实验将时间减少到10分钟）

The autoclave was invented in 1879 (18 years prior to the publication).At the end of the publication it says:

高压釜是在1879年发明的（出版前18年），在出版结束时说：

Detailed descriptions of the organisms and of many more experiments will be given in our full paper on this subject to appear in a forthcoming number of the Technology Quarterly

有关该生物的详细说明和更多实验将在我们关于该主题的全文中给出，并出现在即将出版的《技术季刊》上。

As we have shown in our previous paper, in order to insuresterilization in practice, it is necessary to obtain and maintain atemperature in excess of 121°C (212F) throughout the contents of thecan. Intermittent sterilization may be employed, but is less efficientand is not practicable upon a large or commercial scale. We have foundby experiment that sixty minutes at 121°C (250F) is sufficient time forsterilizing corn, and it seems probable that this can be shortenedsomewhat or the temperature reduced.

正如我们在前一篇论文中所表明的那样，为了在实践中确保灭菌，有必要在整个罐头内容物中获得并保持超过121摄氏度（212华氏度）的温度。可以采用间歇消毒，但是效率较低，并且在大规模或商业规模上都不可行。通过实验我们发现，在121°C（250°F）的温度下60分钟足以对玉米进行灭菌，并且似乎可以将其缩短一点或降低温度。

They are using °C, but since it's 1890's retorts (autoclaves) used in food industry might be F and the authors probably converted retort eqipment readings to °C for scientific community.

他们使用的是°C，但由于食品工业中使用的1890蒸馏罐（高压灭菌器）可能为F，因此作者可能会将蒸馏罐设备的读数转换为°C，以用于科学界。

Also, this was what pioneered thermal death study and if you look at the temperature that they used for the study they are kind of sporadic. Probably because they wanted to see what temperature + time would reach the center of the can. The length of heating, or processing, and the pressure which is given vary somewhat in different factories and that might’ve been what led them to the 121°C and not 120°C.

同样，这也是开创性热死研究的先驱，如果您查看他们用于研究的温度，那是零星的。可能是因为他们想知道什么温度+时间将达到罐的中心。加热或处理的时间长度以及所施加的压力在不同的工厂中有所不同，这可能是导致它们达到121°C而不是120°C的原因。

Further research was done in this field:In 1956 and 1958 F. H. Deindoerfer & A. E. Humhrey found that 250F (121C) is the optimal temperature to avoid damage nutrient media. They also discussed analytical methods for heat sterilization times.

在该领域进行了进一步的研究：1956年和1958年F. H. Deindoerfer＆amp; A. A. E. Humhrey发现250F（121C）是避免破坏营养培养基的最佳温度。他们还讨论了热灭菌时间的分析方法。

What I've been specifically looking for is the first thermal death study with D&Z values that led to the widespread usage of 121°C. Andy why the time and psi required for sterilizing biological samples. I know and found papers on what is used to validate Autoclaves.

我一直在特别寻找的是第一项具有D＆amp; Z值的热死亡研究，该研究导致121°C的广泛使用。安迪（Andy）为什么要对生物样品进行灭菌需要时间和psi。我知道并找到有关用于验证高压灭菌器的论文。

Geobacillus sterothermophilus (known as Bacillus sterothermophilus also) is used for validating autoclaves and that is due to it being very heat resistant, making a good biological indicator of microbial life post sterilization. The one paper I found which had some graphical data about its thermal death rate uses °C instead of F. I cannot seem find any information on the bacterial Log reduction at 120°C comparing it to 121°C. This answer might as well as be a mathematical equation leading to the that temperature, but that is only venue I haven't explored

嗜热热土芽孢杆菌（也称为杀菌热芽孢杆菌）用于验证高压灭菌器，这是因为它具有很高的耐热性，是灭菌后微生物寿命的良好生物学指标。我发现的一篇论文有一些关于其热死率的图形数据，使用的是°C而不是F。我似乎找不到关于120°C与121°C相比细菌对数减少的任何信息。这个答案可能也是导致该温度的数学方程式，但这只是我没有探索过的场所

If anyone can find that Technology Quarterly paper I mentioned at the start that'd be great.

如果有人能找到我一开始提到的《技术季刊》，那将是很棒的。

$\begingroup$ The reason for 121C and not 120C is due to how autoclaves work and how they were developed. They sterilize with saturated steam under pressure. Historically, we measure the pressure generated by the steam. Which leads directly to the answer.

$ \ begingroup $之所以选择121C而不是120C是因为高压釜的工作原理和开发方式。它们在压力下用饱和蒸汽灭菌。从历史上看，我们测量蒸汽产生的压力。这直接导致了答案。

Steam power was the basis of industrial process in the 19th century, and to use it safely required monitoring and controlling boiler pressure. Boiler explosions were not uncommon in the early days of steam power. The Bourdon pressure gauge was invented in 1849 and was a robust technology that yielded reliable and accurate measurement of steam pressure. https://journals.sagepub.com/doi/pdf/10.1177/002029408702000803

蒸汽动力是19世纪工业过程的基础，要安全使用蒸汽，就需要监测和控制锅炉压力。在蒸汽发电的早期，锅炉爆炸并不罕见。 Bourdon压力表是1849年发明的，是一项可靠的技术，可对蒸汽压力进行可靠而准确的测量。 https://journals.sagepub.com/doi/pdf/10.1177/002029408702000803

When Chamberland invented the autoclave in 1879 from Papain's earlier pressure cooker, it relied exclusively on pressure measurement for control. Not until 1933 were autoclaves produced that monitored temperature at the drain line to confirm that air was exhausted and the steam was truly saturated. https://www.steris.com/healthcare/knowledge-center/sterile-processing/everything-about-autoclaves

1879年，钱伯兰（Chamberland）从帕帕因（Papain）的早期压力锅发明高压釜时，它完全依靠压力测量来进行控制。直到1933年，才生产出高压灭菌器，以监测排水管的温度，以确认空气已耗尽并且蒸汽真正饱和。 https://www.steris.com/healthcare/knowledge-center/sterile-processing/everything-about-autoclaves

Study of steam engines was the basis for the development of thermodynamics and the kinetic molecular theory of gasses. Although steam is far from an ideal gas, there is a predictable relationship between temperature and pressure in saturated steam as well as well established properties of heat transfer. These can be found on standard engineering steam tables. Examples: https://www.engineersedge.com/thermodynamics/steam_tables.htm

蒸汽机的研究是发展气体热力学和动力学分子理论的基础。尽管蒸汽远不是理想的气体，但是饱和蒸汽中的温度和压力以及传热的良好特性之间存在可预测的关系。这些可以在标准工程蒸汽表上找到。示例：https：//www.engineersedge.com/thermodynamics/steam_tables.htm

Saturated steam is very efficient in transferring heat. This is why scald injuries are so severe compared to simple burns at the same temperatures. And,it is how saturated steam kills even heat resistant microorganisms in an autoclave. https://learnaboutgmp.com/good-manufacturing-practices-cgmp/saturated-steam-efficient-sterilization/

饱和蒸汽在传热方面非常有效。这就是为什么与在相同温度下简单灼伤相比，烫伤很严重的原因。并且，这就是饱和蒸汽如何杀死高压釜中的耐热微生物。 https://learnaboutgmp.com/good-manufacturing-practices-cgmp/saturated-steam-ficient-sterilization/

One earth atmosphere of pressure at sea level is defined a 14.69psi, though in reality it varies a little bit due to temperature and weather, Because of this variation and because humans using arabic numerals, arithmetic and algebra like even integer units in 5's; engineers often think of 1 atm as 15psi. This amount of saturated steam pressure is well within the capacities of typical steam boilers. This nice round 15psi of saturated steam will kill even highly resistant bacterial endospores in about 15 minutes. A very reasonable time frame, and a nice mnemonic: 15psi for 15 minutes.

地球的一个海平面大气压力定义为14.69psi，尽管实际上它会因温度和天气而略有变化，因为这种变化以及人类使用阿拉伯数字，算术和代数，甚至是5＆＃39中的整数单位; s;工程师通常认为1 atm为15psi。该饱和蒸汽压力的量完全在典型蒸汽锅炉的能力范围内。这种约15psi的饱和蒸汽饱和度将在约15分钟内杀死甚至高抵抗力的细菌内生孢子。一个非常合理的时间范围，以及一个漂亮的助记符：15 psi，持续15分钟。

And that is why we use a very unusual 121C for typical autoclaving. It's because we are really using a rounded one earth atmosphere of steam pressure. And we did so without concern for direct temperature measurement for the first 50 years of autoclave use. In many ways the temperature of the autoclave was of secondary importance.

这就是为什么我们将非常不寻常的121C用于典型的高压灭菌。这是因为我们实际上使用的是蒸汽压力的圆形地球大气。而且，我们这样做无需担心高压釜使用后头50年的直接温度测量。在许多方面，高压釜的温度是次要的。

Broadband, like cable TV, electricity, gas, and railroad tracks, are a "natural monopoly" [https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Natural_monopoly]. The largest cost is the upfront capital expense of building the infrastructure, with relatively low ongoing costs from there. As long as there is a single infrastructure provider, they can collect extraordinary profits (monopoly rents). The result is that there is very low competition. Second and later competitors duplicate the high capital cost, but then competition destroys profit margins. As a result, there's almost always only one provider. The best way to approach situations of natural monopolies, where the competitive free market can't work its magic because there tends to be no competition, is through tight regulation and/or non-profit quasi-governmental ownership of the infrastructure. So, for example, the government owns the interstate highways, while it's fairly wide open competition in the trucking industry. For centuries we have had common carrier regulation of railroads and ferries. And now, with broadband, we should have the government and non-profits control a broad network of fiber optic cable, but then let ISPs slug it out in a competitive free for all to deliver services on those public networks, subject only to light regulation of transparency in pricing and similar pro-competitive regulation.

宽带，如有线电视，电力，天然气和铁轨，是A＆＃34;自然垄断＆＃34; [https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/natural_monopoly]。最大的成本是建立基础设施的前期资本费用，从那里的持续成本相对较低。只要有一个基础设施提供商，他们就可以收集非凡的利润（垄断租金）。结果是竞争很低。第二和后期竞争对手重复了高资本成本，但随后竞争摧毁了利润率。结果，有几乎总是只有一个提供商。接近自然垄断情况的最佳方式，竞争自由市场可以＆＃39; T工作魔法，因为往往没有竞争，是通过紧张的监管和/或非营利性准则基础设施的所有权。因此，例如，政府拥有高州际公路，而它在卡车运输业中相当广阔的开放式竞争。几个世纪以来，我们有共同的载体调节铁路和渡轮。目前，随着宽带，我们应该让政府和非营利利润控制广泛的光纤电缆网络，但随后让ISPS在竞争力中解放出来，为这些公共网络提供服务，仅限灯具监管定价透明度与竞争监管。

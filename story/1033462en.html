火箭实验室即将尝试第一级回收

2020-11-06 07:44:38

Rocket Lab said Thursday it will attempt to recover the first stage of its Electron rocket for the first time with its next mission, scheduled for liftoff in mid-November.

火箭实验室周四表示，它将首次尝试回收其电子火箭的第一级，下一次任务定于11月中旬发射升空。

This step follows a series of tests during which Rocket Lab has been building toward a full stage recovery. During these earlier tests, engineers have deployed parachutes from the first stage at maximum loads and collected data as two earlier rockets returned through "the wall," which is what the company calls the high heat and pressure of reentry at supersonic velocity.

这一步骤是在火箭实验室进行了一系列测试之后进行的，在这些测试期间，火箭实验室一直在朝着全面恢复的方向发展。在早些时候的测试中，工程师们在最大载荷下展开了第一级的降落伞，并收集了早些时候两枚火箭返回墙体时的数据，这就是该公司所说的以超音速再入大气层的高热量和高压力。

The company's founder, Peter Beck, said during a conference call with reporters that he was not sure what the company would fish out of the ocean. It could be a nearly intact first stage or, he admitted, "a smoldering stump." The key with this test, he said, was to gather data about the parachute system.

该公司创始人彼得·贝克(Peter Beck)在与记者的电话会议上表示，他不确定公司将从海洋中捞到什么。他承认，这可能是一个几乎完好无损的第一阶段，也可能是一个冒烟的树桩。他说，这次测试的关键是收集有关降落伞系统的数据。

Beck announced the company's plans to begin using its small Electron rocket a little more than a year ago, saying the goal was to effectively double production of the vehicle in the face of customer demand. "We're seeing the production delivery times get smaller, and a stage is rolling out of the factory every 30 days now," he said. "But really, we're just nowhere close to keeping up with the demand from our customers."

一年多前，贝克宣布了该公司开始使用小型电子火箭的计划，称其目标是在面对客户需求的情况下，有效地将这种火箭的产量提高一倍。他说：我们看到生产交货时间越来越短，现在每30天就有一个舞台从工厂出来。但说真的，我们远远跟不上客户的需求。

His engineers developed a plan whereby the first stage would launch and separate from the Electron second stage, as normal, at an altitude of 80km and then use reaction control system thrusters to reorient the vehicle for reentry, with the engine section leading the way. Unlike with SpaceX's Falcon 9 rocket, the nine first-stage engines on Electron will not fire. (SpaceX is the only company to successfully recover a first stage.)

他的工程师们制定了一项计划，按照计划，第一级将像往常一样在80公里的高空发射，并与电子第二级分离，然后使用反作用控制系统推进器重新调整飞行器的方向，以便重返大气层，发动机部分领先。与SpaceX的猎鹰9号火箭不同，电子火箭的9个第一级发动机不会点火。(SpaceX是唯一一家成功恢复第一阶段的公司。)。

As the Electron rocket descends lower into the atmosphere, a drogue parachute will deploy, followed by a main parachute. If all goes well, the rocket should splash into the ocean traveling less than 10 meters per second.

当电子火箭下降到大气层以下时，一个减速伞将展开，随后是一个主降落伞。如果一切顺利，火箭应该会以低于10米每秒的速度坠入海洋。

That is the plan, at least. With the upcoming mission, named "Return to Sender," the company will then recover the vehicle from the ocean and return it to its factory in New Zealand to see what condition the first stage is in.

至少这是我们的计划。即将到来的任务名为“返回发送者”，该公司随后将从海洋中打捞出这辆车，并将其送回其在新西兰的工厂，看看第一阶段处于什么状态。

"Parachutes are not trivial things to get right," Beck said. "Whenever you throw out a piece of fabric at just slightly under the speed of sound, it's always a little bit interesting. I'll stop being nervous once we get it back in the factory."

贝克说，降落伞不是做对的小事。每当你以略低于音速的速度扔掉一块布料时，它总是有点有趣。一旦我们把它带回工厂，我就不会再紧张了。

The company will likely have to fine-tune Electron's hardware and software, Beck said, and this should improve future recovery efforts. Once the company is able to bring the first stage back whole, the final step will be catching the vehicle mid-air, using a helicopter—this will avoid water damage to the vehicle from the ocean. The company has already successfully practiced this maneuver by capturing a mock rocket stage with a helicopter.

贝克说，该公司可能不得不对Electron的硬件和软件进行微调，这应该会改善未来的恢复工作。一旦该公司能够将第一级带回完整，最后一步将是使用直升机在空中抓住车辆-这将避免海洋对车辆的水损害。该公司已经成功地练习了这一动作，用直升机捕获了一个模拟火箭级。

"I'm sure there's going to be a ton of work on heat shields and on various bits and pieces that we'll need to get right," he said. "When we've reached the point where we have something that's in a condition that we actually care that it doesn't get wet, we'll start bringing the helicopter in."

他说：我敢肯定，在隔热罩和各种零碎的东西上会有大量的工作要做，我们需要把这些东西做好，才能把它做好，我敢肯定，会有大量的工作要花在隔热罩上，以及各种零碎的东西上，而这些都是我们需要做好的。当我们拿到的东西处于我们真正关心的不会被淋湿的情况下时，我们会开始把直升机带进来。

This may take several flights, but the company has plenty of commercial missions upon which to test this technology. Beck said the cost to Electron's payload capacity to incorporate a parachute, heat shielding, reaction control systems, and other measures is only about 15 kilograms.

这可能需要几次飞行，但该公司有很多商业任务可以测试这项技术。贝克说，电子公司安装降落伞、隔热、反应控制系统和其他措施的有效载荷能力的成本只有15公斤左右。

The "Return to Sender" mission will deliver 30 small satellites into various orbits, and the two-week launch window opens on November 15. The down-range recovery attempt will take place about 400km from the launch site, in the Pacific Ocean.

返回发送者号任务将把30颗小卫星送入不同轨道，为期两周的发射窗口将于11月15日开启。近距离回收尝试将在距离发射场约400公里的太平洋进行。

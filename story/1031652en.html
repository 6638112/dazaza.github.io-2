温莎城堡：更换数百个皇家钟表

2020-10-28 07:23:31

As the clocks go back this autumn, the horological conservator at Windsor Castle will be busy changing more than 400 clocks from the Royal Collection Trust.

随着时钟在今年秋天倒流，温莎城堡的钟表保管员将忙于更换皇家收藏信托基金(Royal Collection Trust)的400多个时钟。

For those of us with smartphones and smartwatches, the change to Greenwich Mean Time (GMT) on Sunday 25 October will happen automatically whilst we sleep.

对于我们这些拥有智能手机和智能手表的人来说，10月25日周日格林威治标准时间(GMT)的更改将在我们睡觉时自动进行。

But for the hundreds of clocks in the Royal Collection at Windsor Castle, each one will need to be changed by hand to ensure accurate timekeeping for visitors and residents.

但对于温莎城堡皇家收藏品中的数百座钟来说，每一座都需要手工更换，以确保游客和居民的准确计时。

The changing of the clocks this autumn will be Fjodor's first since taking on the role.

今年秋天的时钟调整将是Fjodor上任以来的第一次。

He plans to spend about 16 hours over the weekend changing all 400 clocks on the Windsor estate, including about 250 in the castle itself, along with seven tower clocks.

他计划在周末花大约16个小时更换温莎庄园的所有400个钟，其中包括城堡本身的大约250个钟，以及7个塔钟。

"It's just myself, and I have one colleague at Buckingham Palace who changes all the clocks there," Fjodor told the BBC.

弗约多告诉BBC，只有我一个人，我在白金汉宫的一位同事把那里所有的钟都调了。

"People are still amazed that at Windsor Castle and Buckingham Palace there is a small time zone in the kitchens, where the clocks are always five minutes fast.

人们仍然感到惊讶的是，在温莎城堡和白金汉宫，厨房里有一个很小的时区，那里的时钟总是快五分钟。

"This is so that the food arrives on time... it's a constant reminder that this is important."

这是为了让食物准时送到……。它不断提醒我们，这很重要。

When he's not changing the time, Fjodor's job includes spending one full day a week winding up the mechanical clocks to keep their pendulums swinging.

当他不改变时间时，Fjodor的工作包括每周花一整天的时间给机械钟上发条，以保持钟摆的摆动。

On a typical day of walking around the castle and winding clocks, the step counter on his watch will record about 16,000 steps.

在一个典型的绕着城堡散步和给钟上发条的日子里，他手表上的计步器将记录下大约16,000步。

"I check all the clocks in the state apartments before the public arrive to make sure they're on time."

在公众到来之前，我会检查州立公寓里的所有钟，以确保它们准时。

The state apartments are where the Queen hosts official visits by heads of state from other countries, investitures and awards ceremonies.

国家公寓是女王接待来自其他国家的国家元首正式访问、授勋和颁奖仪式的地方。

"Most of the clocks are quite accurate but every now and then, for no reason, they will suddenly start losing or gaining time - something which I've just started calling 'life'.

大多数钟都相当准确，但时不时地，它们会无缘无故地突然开始失去或赢得时间--这是我刚刚开始称之为“生活”的东西。

The rest of his week is spent in his workshop servicing and repairing clocks, many of which are 200 to 300 years old.

他一周剩下的时间都花在他的车间里，维修钟表，其中许多都有200到300年的历史了。

When a part breaks or wears out, Fjodor makes a replacement using a lathe, a milling machine and lots of hand tools.

当零件损坏或磨损时，Fjodor使用车床、铣床和大量手动工具进行更换。

"[It has been said that] clocks were a way to bring God into your house, because God made time, and man made a machine to capture time," explains Fjodor.

[有人说]钟是把上帝带进你家的一种方式，因为上帝创造了时间，而人类制造了一台机器来捕捉时间，弗约多解释说。

"Now we buy a really modern smartphone, but back then if you wanted to show off you bought the latest clock."

现在我们买了一部非常现代的智能手机，但那时候如果你想炫耀一下，你就买了最新的钟。

One of the clocks Fjodor looks after is an ornate French clock in the State Dining Room.

弗约多看管的其中一座钟是国家餐厅里的一座华丽的法式钟。

Said to have been Queen Victoria's favourite, the clock was presented to the monarch in 1844 by King Louis-Philippe of France.

据说这座钟是维多利亚女王的最爱，1844年由法国国王路易斯-菲利普赠送给女王。

The clock features three paintings that represent the three ages of horology (the art of making clocks).

这座钟以三幅画为特色，代表了钟表(钟表制造的艺术)的三个时代。

On the front there is the first astronomical clock in the town hall of Padua, Italy, in 1364.

前面是1364年意大利帕多瓦市政厅的第一座天文钟。

On the left side the Dutch physicist Christian Huyghens is depicted demonstrating the first pendulum clock, which he invented in 1656, and on the right side there is a Roman senator holding a water clock.

左边是荷兰物理学家克里斯蒂安·惠金斯(Christian Huyghens)展示了他在1656年发明的第一个摆钟，右边是一位罗马参议员拿着水钟。

The rest of the case is decorated with leaf wreaths, medallions and figures of famous clockmakers.

盒子的其余部分装饰着树叶花环、奖章和著名钟表匠的雕像。

The porcelain inlays around the paintings were made in the famous Sèvres porcelain factory in Paris, a leading producer in Europe.

油画周围的瓷器镶嵌是在欧洲领先的生产商巴黎著名的塞夫尔瓷器厂制造的。

Another clock linked to Queen Victoria's reign is a large organ clock in the King's Drawing Room, which houses a Bible placed by the monarch inside a rock crystal casket.

另一个与维多利亚女王统治有关的钟是国王客厅里的一个大型风琴钟，里面放着国王放在一个岩石水晶棺材里的一本圣经。

Measuring about 6ft (1.8m) in height, the organ clock was made by Charles Clay in 1740 and plays melodies by Handel, four of which were composed just for the clock.

风琴钟高约6英尺(1.8米)，由查尔斯·克莱于1740年制造，演奏亨德尔的旋律，其中四首是专门为钟创作的。

Clay and Handel often worked together in creating organ clocks, a number of which went to royal families in Europe.

克莱和汉德尔经常合作制作风琴钟，其中一些送给了欧洲的皇室。

"This is more an instrument than a timepiece, it was meant to be heard," says Fjodor.

弗约多说，与其说这是一块钟表，不如说它是一件乐器，它本来是要被听到的。

Inside the clock base is the brass drum and pins of the organ, along with bellows that blow air through the flutes.

在钟座内部是管风琴的铜鼓和针脚，还有通过长笛吹气的风箱。

The Bible in the casket was owned by General Gordon, who was killed at Khartoum in 1885.

棺材里的圣经归戈登将军所有，他于1885年在喀土穆遇害。

The casket was made by Melchior Baumgartner, who wrote in German on a panel inside: "[I] made this casket in Augsburg and covered it with silver in the year 1664."

这个棺材是梅尔基奥尔·鲍姆加特纳做的，他用德文在里面的一块面板上写道：“我在奥格斯堡制作了这个棺材，并在1664年用银覆盖了它。"；[我]在1664年在奥格斯堡制作了这个棺材，并用银覆盖了它。"；

The rock crystal panels are engraved with land and seascapes, and surrounded by floral-painted enamel.

岩石水晶板上雕刻着陆地和海景，周围环绕着花卉彩绘的珐琅。

On the very top is a depiction of St George and the Dragon by Francesco Fanelli, which was added during King George IV's reign in the 19th Century.

最上面是弗朗西斯科·法内利(Francesco Fanelli)绘制的圣乔治与龙的肖像，这幅画是在19世纪国王乔治四世统治期间添加的。

One of Fjodor's favourite items from the collection is an arresting clock that depicts the Greek mythological figures of Chronos (Father Time) and Study, with Chronos's scythe pointing to the current time on a rotating globe in the centre.

Fjodor最喜欢的藏品之一是一个引人注目的钟，它描绘了Chronos(时间之父)和书房的希腊神话人物，Chronos的镰刀指向中心旋转的地球仪上的当前时间。

"At first glance this is a statue, a piece of art, then secondly you see it's a clock."

乍一看这是一尊雕像，一件艺术品，然后你再一眼就会看到它是一座钟。

Study is holding an open book and is surrounded by objects of learning: a lyre, an artist's palette and a protractor.

书房拿着一本打开的书，周围环绕着学习的对象：一把竖琴、一张艺术家的调色板和一把量角器。

"I've seen multiple depictions of this clock and they all have a slightly different explanation behind them. Some show Study stopping Time, others are Time stopping Study."

我看过很多关于这座钟的描述，它们背后的解释都略有不同。一些显示学习停止时间，另一些显示时间停止学习。"；

The figures are made of bronze and the base is marble, with the clock weighing about 200lb (90kg).

雕像由青铜制成，底座为大理石，时钟重约200磅(90公斤)。

"We have a trolley that can lift itself to the level of the mantelpiece, you then put it on Teflon sliders to move it on to the trolley without damaging the nice marble mantelpiece.

我们有一个手推车，可以把自己抬到壁炉架的水平，然后你把它放在特氟龙滑块上，在不损坏漂亮的大理石壁炉架的情况下把它移到手推车上。

"In the 19th Century it would have taken a couple of people with strong backs to move it."

在19世纪，要移动它需要几个背部强壮的人。

The front of the clock is inscribed with the name of the French maker of the original clock movements, Charles-Guillaume Manière.

钟表的正面刻有法国钟表机芯制造商查尔斯-纪尧姆·马尼埃(Charles-Guillaume Manière)的名字。

"It's one of my favourite clocks in terms of performance, because I serviced it three months ago and since then it has kept perfect time, within a minute."

就性能而言，它是我最喜欢的钟之一，因为我三个月前维修过它，从那时起，它就一直保持着精确的计时，不到一分钟。

Another timepiece that pays homage to learning, specifically the arts, is a mantel clock in the Crimson Drawing Room.

另一款向学习致敬的钟表，特别是艺术，是深红色客厅里的壁炉架时钟。

The Greek god Apollo is seen with a sculpted head at his feet and Artium Genio written on the front, meaning the genius of arts.

希腊神阿波罗脚下有一个雕刻的头部，正面写着阿蒂姆·吉尼奥，意思是艺术的天才。

The clock was commissioned by King George IV when he was the Prince of Wales, evidenced by the three feathers emblem on the front.

这座钟是乔治四世国王在担任威尔士亲王时委托建造的，前面的三根羽毛标志证明了这一点。

The case was made by Pierre-Philippe Thomire, a Parisian bronzeur and gilder of the early 19th Century, and the inner movements were made by Benjamin Vulliamy, clockmaker to King George III from 1773.

表壳由19世纪初的巴黎青铜器和镀金大师Pierre-Philippe Thomire制作，内部机芯由1773年至国王乔治三世的钟表匠Benjamin Vulliamy制作。

The striking mantelpiece surrounding this clock was made by Vulliamy's son, George John Vulliamy, a bronze castor and architect who also made the dolphin lampposts seen along the Thames Embankment near the Palace of Westminster.

围绕这座钟的引人注目的壁炉架是由Vulliamy的儿子乔治·约翰·Vulliamy制作的，他是一名青铜铸造者和建筑师，他还制作了威斯敏斯特宫附近泰晤士河畔的海豚灯柱。

"There is the historic value, the provenance, and a lot of the clocks are one-offs, so they're priceless."

有历史价值，有出处，而且很多钟都是一次性的，所以它们是无价之宝。

Each clock will be brought in for a full service every 10 to 15 years, which sees Fjodor take it apart and clean it.

每个钟都将每10到15年进行一次全面维修，届时Fjodor将把它拆开并清洗一次。

"There will be a little wear [to parts] that will need repairing, I'll then lubricate the mechanism and put the clock back together."

会有一点磨损[零件]需要修理，然后我会给机械润滑一下，然后把钟放回原处。

There has been an in-house clockmaker at Windsor Castle since the late 1970s, with Fjodor being the fourth horological conservator.

自20世纪70年代末以来，温莎城堡(Windsor Castle)一直有一位内部钟表匠，弗约多(Fjodor)是第四位钟表保管员。

"My predecessor was here for 20 years, his predecessor was working for around 30 years, so I think it's a job for life.

我的前任在这里工作了20年，他的前任在这里工作了大约30年，所以我认为这是一份终身的工作。

"I'm thankful I've managed to land this job quite early in my career, so I do hope I'll be here for quite a while."

我很感激我在职业生涯的早期就设法找到了这份工作，所以我真的希望我能在这里呆上一段时间。

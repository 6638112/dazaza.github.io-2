奎比短暂而奇特的一生

2020-10-24 07:46:22

With that, the founding executives confirmed the rumors and put Quibi to bed, a little more than six months after launching the service.

说完，创始高管证实了传言，并在推出这项服务六个多月后让Quibi上床睡觉。

Starting a business is an impossibly difficult task under nearly any conditions, but even in a world that’s littered with high-profile failures, the streaming service’s swan song was remarkable for both its dramatically brief lifespan and the amount of money the company managed to raise (and spend) during that time.

在几乎任何情况下，创业都是一项极其困难的任务，但即使在一个充斥着高调失败的世界里，这家流媒体服务公司的告别之歌也是引人注目的，因为它的生命周期极其短暂，而且在此期间该公司设法筹集(和花费)了大量资金。

A month ahead of its commercial launch, Quibi announced that it had raised another $750 million. That second round of funding brought the yet-to-launch streaming service’s funding up to $1.75 billion — roughly the same as the gross domestic product of Belize, give or take $100 million.

在商业发布的前一个月，Quibi宣布又筹集了7.5亿美元。第二轮融资使这家尚未推出的流媒体服务的资金达到17.5亿美元-大致相当于伯利兹的国内生产总值(GDP)，上下浮动1亿美元。

“We concluded a very successful second raise which will provide Quibi with a strong cash runway,” CFO Ambereen Toubassy told the press at the time. “This round of $750 million gives us tremendous flexibility and the financial wherewithal to build content and technology that consumers embrace.”

“我们完成了非常成功的第二次加薪，这将为Quibi提供一个强大的现金跑道，”首席财务官Ambereen Toubasy当时告诉媒体。这一轮7.5亿美元的投资为我们提供了巨大的灵活性和所需的资金，以建立消费者欢迎的内容和技术。“。

From a financial perspective, Quibi had reason to be hopeful. Its fundraising ambitions were matched only by the aggressiveness with which it planned to spend that money. At the beginning of the year, Whitman touted the company’s plans to spend up to $100,000 per minute of programming — $6 million per hour. The executive proudly contrasted the jaw-dropping sum to the estimated $500 to $5,000 an hour spent by YouTube creators.

从财务角度来看，基比有理由抱有希望。它的筹款野心只与它计划花这笔钱的积极程度相匹配。今年年初，惠特曼吹捧该公司计划每分钟节目花费高达10万美元，即每小时600万美元。这位高管自豪地将这笔令人瞠目结舌的金额与YouTube创建者每小时估计花费的500至5000美元进行了对比。

For Whitman and Katzenberg — best known for their respective reigns at HP and Disney — money was key to success in an already crowded marketplace. Indeed, $1 billion was a drop in the bucket compared to the $17.3 billion Netflix was expected to spend on original content in 2020, but it was a start.

对于惠特曼和卡森伯格-他们最出名的是各自在惠普和迪士尼的统治-钱是在本已拥挤的市场上取得成功的关键。事实上，与Netflix预计在2020年原创内容上花费的173亿美元相比，10亿美元只是杯水车薪，但这只是一个开始。

Following in the footsteps of Apple, who had also recently announced plans to spend $1 billion to launch its own fledgling streaming service, the company was enlisting A-List talent, from Steven Spielberg, Guillermo del Toro and Ridley Scott to Reese Witherspoon, Jennifer Lopez and LeBron James. If your name carried any sort of clout in Hollywood boardrooms, Quibi would happily cut you a check, seemingly regardless of content specifics.

继苹果之后，苹果最近也宣布计划斥资10亿美元推出自己的羽翼未丰的流媒体服务，该公司正在招募一线人才，从史蒂文·斯皮尔伯格(Steven Spielberg)、吉列尔莫·德尔·托罗(Guillermo Del Toro)和雷德利·斯科特(Ridley Scott)到瑞茜·威瑟斯彭(Reese Witherspoon)、詹妮弗·洛佩兹(Jennifer Lopez)和勒布朗·詹姆斯(LeBron James)。如果你的名字在好莱坞的董事会中有任何影响力，Quibi会很高兴地给你开张支票，似乎不考虑内容的细节。

Quibi’s strategy primarily defined itself by its constraints. In hopes of attracting younger millennial and Gen Z viewers, the company’s content would be not just mobile-first, but mobile-only. There would be no smart TV app, no Chromecast or AirPlay compatibility. Pricing, while low compared to the competition, was similarly off-putting. After a 90-day free trial, $4.99 got you an ad-supported subscription. And boy howdy, were there ads. Ads upon ads. Ads all the way down. Paying another $3 a month would make them go away.

Quibi的战略主要是通过它的限制来定义自己的。为了吸引更年轻的千禧一代和Z世代观众，该公司的内容将不仅仅是移动优先，而是仅限移动。届时将不会有智能电视应用程序，也不会有Chromecast或AirPlay兼容性。虽然与竞争对手相比，定价较低，但同样令人不快。在90天的免费试用后，4.99美元就能让你获得广告支持的订阅。男孩你好，有没有广告。广告一条接一条。广告一直往下走。每月再付3美元就能让他们离开。

Technological constraints and Terms of Service fine print forbade screen shots — a fundamental understanding of how content goes viral in 2020 (though, to be fair, one shared with other competing streaming services). Amusingly, the inability to share content led to videos like this one of director Sam Raimi’s perplexingly earnest “The Golden Arm.”

技术限制和服务条款禁止截屏-这是对2020年内容如何传播的基本理解(不过，公平地说，是与其他竞争对手的流媒体服务共享的内容)。有趣的是，无法分享内容导致了像这样的视频，比如导演萨姆·雷米(Sam Raimi)令人费解地认真的“黄金手臂”(The Golden Arm)。

It features a built-on laugh track from viewers as Emmy winner Rachel Brosnahan lies in a hospital bed after refusing to remove a golden prosthetic. It’s an allegory, surely, but not one intentionally played for laughs. Many of the videos that did ultimately make the rounds on social media were regarded as a curiosity — strange artifacts from a nascent streaming service that made little sense on paper.

它的特色是观众内置的笑声，艾美奖获得者蕾切尔·布罗斯纳汉在拒绝移除金色假肢后躺在医院的病床上。当然，这是一个寓言故事，但不是故意为了取笑而玩的。许多最终在社交媒体上流传的视频被认为是一件稀奇古怪的东西-来自一家新生的流媒体服务的奇怪艺术品，在纸面上几乎没有意义。

Most notable of all, however, were the “quick bites” that gave the service its confusingly pronounced name. Each program would be served in 5-10 minute chunks. The list included films acquired by the service, sliced up into “chapters.” Notably, the service didn’t actually purchase the content outright; instead, rights were set to revert to their creators after seven years. Meanwhile, after two years, content partners were able to “reassemble” the chunks back into a movie for distribution.

然而，最值得注意的是“快速咀嚼”，这给这项服务起了一个发音令人困惑的名字。每个节目将以5-10分钟为单位提供服务。这份名单包括由该服务获得的电影，被分成“章节”。值得注意的是，该服务实际上并没有直接购买内容；相反，版权被设定为在七年后恢复到创作者手中。与此同时，两年后，内容合作伙伴能够将这些片段“重新组装”成一部电影进行发行。

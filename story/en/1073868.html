I collect and read old computer manuals
我收集并阅读旧电脑手册

2022-02-17 16:48:30

Jan. 22, 2022 It’s my ritual: every time I enter a secondhand bookshop, I go straight to the Sciences section and search for old computer manuals. They’re very hard to come by, as their owners tend to throw them away once they stop using a particular device or piece of software. Manuals also happen not to be the most engaging read for most people, which adds to their rarity; few want to peruse an old IBM AS/400 handbook while laying at the beach.

2022年1月22日这是我的惯例：每次我进入一家二手书店，我都会直接去理科，寻找旧的电脑手册。它们很难找到，因为一旦它们停止使用特定的设备或软件，主人往往会把它们扔掉。手册对大多数人来说也不是最吸引人的读物，这增加了手册的稀有性；躺在海滩上时，很少有人愿意阅读IBM AS/400的旧手册。

Disregarding old manuals as useless piles of paper does them a grave disservice, though. Many of them are admittedly awful or outright boring, but some are ripe with forgotten tech lore and high-quality design. The writers of old manuals often enjoyed more editorial resources than tech writers are used to today, and produced handbooks and guides with greater care, because they couldn’t afford gross inaccuracies to go to press.

然而，把旧手册当作无用的纸堆，这对它们是一种严重的伤害。诚然，它们中的许多都很糟糕或完全无聊，但有些已经成熟，已经被遗忘了技术知识和高质量的设计。旧手册的作者通常比现在的科技作家享有更多的编辑资源，并且更加谨慎地编写手册和指南，因为他们无法承受出版时出现的严重错误。

The ZX Spectrum User Guide (1984) is a colourful, tail-wagging joy of a book that guides newbies with highly visual instructions.

ZX Spectrum用户指南（1984）是一本色彩斑斓、摇尾乞怜的书，用高度直观的说明指导新手。

What makes old manuals so fascinating to me is that they document the history of applied computer science. This gives the entire field, including tech comms, the resemblance of a past. Let’s not dismiss that: in a sector where startups seem to build new things on top of blurry or nonexistent foundations, reading about old software reminds us that there’s indeed a giant upon whose shoulders we’ve climbed.

让我如此着迷的是，它们记录了应用计算机科学的历史。这让整个领域，包括科技通信，都有了过去的相似之处。我们不要忽视这一点：在一个初创企业似乎在模糊或不存在的基础上创造新事物的行业，阅读有关旧软件的文章提醒我们，确实有一个巨人，我们已经爬上了他的肩膀。

In the old days, technical writers produced documentation in near isolation, surrounded by complex and similarly isolated corporate cultures. From an anthropological perspective, old manuals are like tribal artefacts. The resulting docs show surprisingly rich design and content patterns, more refreshing than we’re used to in the age of googling for Stack Exchange questions and of bowing to Stripe docs.

在过去，技术作者几乎孤立地编写文档，周围是复杂而类似孤立的企业文化。从人类学的角度来看，旧手册就像部落的手工艺品。由此产生的文档显示出令人惊讶的丰富设计和内容模式，比谷歌搜索堆栈交换问题和向条状文档鞠躬的时代更令人耳目一新。

The original Macintosh manual (1984) uses brilliant illustrations to explain complex concepts and procedures. There’s very little text in it.

最初的Macintosh手册（1984）使用了精彩的插图来解释复杂的概念和过程。里面几乎没有文字。

Most of the tech we use today is based on technology invented in universities and research labs. For example, The C Programming Language , also known as K&R from its authors, was published in the 70s, but is still considered a prime example of technical writing. The C language, in fact, is still one of the most popular programming languages. Old manuals, in this sense, are evergreen knowledge that can prevent us from reinventing the wheel over and over again.

我们今天使用的大部分技术都是基于大学和研究实验室发明的技术。例如，C编程语言，也称为K&；《来自作者的R》发表于70年代，但仍被认为是技术写作的最佳范例。事实上，C语言仍然是最流行的编程语言之一。从这个意义上说，旧手册是常青的知识，可以阻止我们一次又一次地重新发明轮子。

K&R starts with an example right on the first page. Its succinctness and hands-on spirit has inspired several generations of tech writers.

K&；R从第一页的一个例子开始。它的简洁和动手精神启发了几代科技作家。

As old manuals couldn’t hyperlink their way out of trouble, they’re usually more self-contained than modern web documentation, which, never being complete, lives in a perennial, amorphous state of wikiness. Current docs are afraid of scaring users who land via Google and crave for solutions; old documentation has no such qualms, and happily delves into concepts and theory when the need arises. It’s an entirely different frame of mind, more akin to that of school teachers than to that of community managers.

由于旧的手册无法通过超链接解决问题，它们通常比现代网络文档更加独立，而现代网络文档永远不完整，生活在一种永久的、无定形的维基状态中。当前的文档害怕吓唬通过谷歌登陆并渴望解决方案的用户；旧文献没有这样的疑虑，当需要时，它们会乐于深入研究概念和理论。这是一种完全不同的心态，更像是学校教师的心态，而不是社区管理者的心态。

The MS-DOS 6.22 manual (1994) contains semi-humorous examples of directories named after fruits. Tongue-in-cheek jokes were common.

MS-DOS 6.22手册（1994）包含了以水果命名的目录的半幽默例子。开玩笑的笑话很常见。

There’s a calmness in old computers manuals that modern docs often lack. Old handbooks took their time and space to explain things, and did so at a pace that’s rare to find nowadays. Today’s programmers furiously Control+Fing through docs, opening tens of tabs in search for fragments of an answer. I’m not saying that the way we consume docs now is inherently worse; rather, I’m suggesting that old docs could be a beacon of quality in an age of half-baked docs made in agile shops. Shall we embrace slow docs?

在旧电脑手册中有一种现代医生常常缺乏的平静。古老的手册花时间和空间来解释事物，而且解释的速度是当今罕见的。今天的程序员通过文档疯狂地控制着+Fing，打开几十个标签来搜索答案的片段。我并不是说我们现在使用文档的方式本质上更糟；相反，我的意思是，在敏捷商店里制作的文档还不成熟的时代，旧文档可能是质量的灯塔。我们要拥抱慢医生吗？

The chapter about fonts of The Macintosh Bible, 6th Ed. (1996) is hard to forget thanks to its leisurely walk through system and third-party fonts. It’s cheeky and delightful; why are we so serious now? I don’t know.

《麦金塔圣经》（Macintosh Bible，第六版，1996年）中关于字体的章节很难忘记，这要归功于它悠闲的漫游系统和第三方字体。它厚颜无耻，令人愉快；为什么我们现在这么认真？我不知道。

For all the reasons I’ve just stated above, I think that collecting and reading old computer manuals is a good thing to do, and a must for technical writers. Reading about the software and hardware of yore not only brings me good memories of days past: it also helps me be a better technical writer.

出于上述所有原因，我认为收集和阅读旧的计算机手册是一件好事，也是技术作家必须做的事。阅读过去的软件和硬件不仅能给我带来过去美好的回忆，还能帮助我成为一名更好的技术作家。

周末亮眼：彼得森博物馆的新超级跑车展览

2020-11-08 10:36:24

Identifying the world's first supercar is harder than it sounds. The problem—to me at least—is that there's no universally agreed-upon definition of what a supercar actually is. If you'd asked me yesterday I'd have probably said Lamborghini, when it introduced the Miura in 1966.

识别世界上第一辆超级跑车比听起来要难。问题是--至少对我来说--超级跑车到底是什么，并没有一个公认的定义。如果你昨天问我，我可能会说兰博基尼(Lamborghini)，当时兰博基尼在1966年推出了Miura。

Its body was dramatically styled by Gandini, writing checks that the mid-mounted V12 could definitely cash, sending notice to establishment names like Ferrari that the game had been changed forever. But my answer might be 50 years too late, according a new exhibit at the Petersen Museum in Los Angeles.

它的车身被甘迪尼戏剧性地塑造了风格，开出了支票，证明中置的V12肯定可以变现，向法拉利(Ferrari)等建制派品牌发出了通知，游戏已经永远改变了。但根据洛杉矶彼得森博物馆(Petersen Museum)的一项新展览，我的答案可能晚了50年。

It's called Supercars: A Century of Spectacle and Speed, and our friends at the museum—which was just named Museum of the year by The Historic Motoring Awards—were kind enough to send over photos of some of the cars in the new exhibit. And that's as much excuse as I need to put together a gallery to give people something to click through this fine November Saturday.

它被称为超级汽车：一个世纪的奇观和速度，我们在博物馆的朋友们--它刚刚被历史汽车奖命名为年度博物馆--好心地寄来了新展览中一些汽车的照片。这就是我所需要的足够多的借口，我需要组织一个画廊，让人们可以点击浏览这个11月这个晴朗的星期六。

The Miura might have been the first production sports car to mount its V12 between the driver and the rear wheels, but the idea of a powerful engine, sleek body, and just two seat is almost as old as the automobile itself. Take the 1913 Mercer Raceabout, which even to these Generation X-eyes looks like an Edwardian equivalent to a McLaren. With 34hp (25kW) from its 4.9L engine the Raceabout could see the other side of 100mph (160km/h), which I have to imagine required a lot more bravery than it would in something made in 2013.

Miura可能是第一款将V12安装在驾驶员和后轮之间的量产跑车，但强大的发动机、光滑的车身和只有两个座位的想法几乎和汽车本身一样古老。以1913年的美世赛车(Mercer RaceAbout)为例，即使对这些X世代的人来说，它看起来也像爱德华七世时代的迈凯轮(McLaren)。凭借4.9L发动机34hp(25kW)的功率，RaceAbout可以看到100英里/小时(160公里/小时)的另一面，我不得不想象这比2013年生产的车型需要更多的勇气。

Or how about the Ferrari 212/224 Inter? It's one of the first Ferrari road cars, and the 1952 example on display was once owned by the Ford Motor Company, bought as research during development of the original Ford Thunderbird. The 1950s was a time when there was little difference between a supercars and a top-level race car. For example, After dominating Le Mans in 1956 with the D-Type, Jaguar slapped on a windshield and a luggage rack to create the XKSS. Jaguar only planned to build 25, and built even fewer after a fire at its factory destroyed nine of the chassis. The green one you see here was even owned by an actor called Steve McQueen for some time.

或者法拉利212/224国米怎么样？它是最早的法拉利公路车之一，展出的1952年的样车曾经属于福特汽车公司(Ford Motor Company)，在开发最初的福特雷鸟(Ford Thunderbird)时作为研究购买。上世纪50年代，超级跑车和顶级赛车几乎没有什么区别。例如，在1956年以D型车统治勒芒之后，捷豹在挡风玻璃和行李架上安装了XKSS。捷豹只计划建造25辆，在其工厂的一场大火烧毁了9辆底盘后，建造的数量甚至更少。你在这里看到的那辆绿色的甚至有一段时间是由一位名叫史蒂夫·麦昆的演员拥有的。

Joining the Miura are a couple of mid-engined 60s supercars that, like the XKSS, also began life as race cars first. The Ford GT40 Mk III—a road-legal version of the Le Mans winner—is probably easily recognized. The McLaren M6GT may be less so, as the road car project was derailed by the untimely death of team founder Bruce McLaren in 1970.

加入Miura的还有几款60年代的中置引擎超级跑车，和XKSS一样，它们最初也是作为赛车诞生的。福特GT40 Mk III-勒芒(Le Mans)获胜者的公路合法版本-可能很容易被认出。迈凯轮M6GT可能不是那么好，因为1970年车队创始人布鲁斯·麦克拉伦(Bruce McLaren)过早去世，导致公路车项目脱轨。

The 70s are represented by vehicles like the Lancia Stratos, another racer gone legal, and the BMW M1, a car that was supposed to be built for the Germans by Lamborghini until Lamborghini went broke.

70年代的代表是兰西亚·斯特拉托斯(Lancia Stratos)和宝马M1(BMW M1)等车型，前者是另一家合法的赛车手，后者本应是兰博基尼(Lamborghini)为德国人制造的，直到兰博基尼破产。

As a product of my age, it's the cars from the 1980s onwards where I really start to get interested. And I can't really quibble with any of the cars the Petersen has included that came from the final decades of the 20th century, nor the handful from the new millennium.

作为我这个年龄的产物，我真正开始感兴趣的是上世纪80年代以后的汽车。我真的不能对彼得森所包括的20世纪最后几十年的任何一辆车吹毛求疵，也不能对几辆来自新千年的车吹毛求疵。

There are the early 1980s Group B homologation twins, the Ferrari 288 GTO and Porsche 959. Late 80s yuppiedom is represented in the shape of a Lamborghini Countach and a Ferrari Testarossa. Honda's pioneering NSX deserves its place here, for proving you could build a car that looked like a Ferrari and was quick like a Ferrari but without being unreliable like a Ferrari. And then my personal favorites and harbingers of early 90s excess: the Jaguar XJ220 and McLaren F1.

还有20世纪80年代初的B组同源双胞胎，法拉利288GTO和保时捷959。80年代末雅皮士的形象表现为兰博基尼Countach和法拉利Testarossa。本田的先驱NSX值得在这里占有一席之地，因为它证明了你可以制造出一辆看起来像法拉利的车，速度像法拉利一样快，但又不像法拉利那样不可靠。然后是我个人最喜欢的，也是90年代初过度的先兆：捷豹XJ220和迈凯轮F1。

Of course, now we live in an age where an electric Volvo crossover can out-accelerate many of the supercars on display, and where the ultimate automotive excess is now characterized by hypercars that are outgrowing the roads on which they're supposed to drive. The supercar still exists though, even though these days 600hp and a top speed of 200 miles an hour is considered entry level…

当然，现在我们生活在这样一个时代，电动沃尔沃跨界车的速度可以超过许多展出的超级跑车，而超级跑车的终极超速现在的特点是，超级跑车的增长速度超过了它们应该行驶的道路。然而，超级跑车仍然存在，尽管如今600马力和每小时200英里的最高时速被认为是入门级…

Boston Dynamics' New Robot Doesn't Dance. It Has a Warehouse Job
波士顿动力学的新机器人不跳舞。 它有一个仓库工作

2021-03-30 05:03:23

It can’t do back flips like Atlas the humanoid robot, nor can it dance or open doors for its friends, like Spot the robotic dog can. Instead, Boston Dynamics’ new robot, named Stretch, is going straight to work in a warehouse. Rolling around on a wheeled base, it’s basically a large robotic arm that grabs boxes using vacuum power, and it’s designed for tasks like unloading trucks or stacking pallets.

它不能像阿特拉斯那样的人形机器人一样重新翻转，也不能为其朋友跳舞或开门，就像斑点机器人狗一样。相反，波士顿动力学的新机器人被命名的拉伸，正在直接在仓库中工作。在轮式基地上滚动，它基本上是一个大型机器人手臂，使用真空电源抓住盒子，它设计用于卸载卡车或堆叠托盘等​​任务。

If Spot and Atlas are the show-offs in the family, Stretch is the straight-up workhorse. But while these machines all look and move in wildly different ways, they actually share a lot of DNA. Stretch may seem familiar to you, because it’s a sort of descendant of another machine that debuted a few years back: Handle. That robot had a similar suction arm, but it balanced on two wheels, like a Segway scooter. Handle would grab a box, scoot backward, turn 90 degrees, and roll away to stack the box somewhere else.

如果地方和地图集是家庭的展示，延伸是直接的主主力。但是，虽然这些机器都以野外不同的方式看起来，但它们实际上分享了很多DNA。伸展可能似乎熟悉，因为这是另一台机器的后代，这是几年的推迟：处理。机器人有类似的吸入臂，但它在两个轮子上平衡，就像Segway踏板车一样。手柄会抓住一个盒子，向后脚踏，转90度，然后滚动堆放在其他地方的盒子。

It looked neat on video, but in practice the robot needed a lot of room to operate. It could manage unloading boxes from a truck, sure. “But it took a long time,” says Kevin Blankespoor, lead of warehouse robotics at Boston Dynamics. “The truck is a pretty confined space. And so for Handle, every time it grabbed the box, it would need to roll back into some space where it could rotate freely without collisions.”

它在视频上看起来很整洁，但在实践中，机器人需要大量的空间来运作。它可以从卡车中管理卸载箱，肯定。 “但花了很长时间，”凯文百彩的百彩百彩，波士顿动力学领先。 “卡车是一个非常狭窄的空间。因此，对于手柄，每次抓住盒子时，它需要回到某些空间，在没有冲突的情况下可以自由旋转。“

Which is all to say: If Handle were a human, it’d be let go. So Boston Dynamics pivoted (sorry) to a new form factor for Stretch that slapped a similar robotic vacuum arm on a base with four wheels. Each wheel can move independently, so the robot can shift side to side or forward and backward to orient itself in, say, the back of a truck.

这一切都说：如果把手是一个人，那就放手了。因此，波士顿动力学使（抱歉）旋转（抱歉）延伸的新形状因素，将类似的机器人真空臂放在带有四个轮子的底座上。每个轮子都可以独立移动，所以机器人可以侧向侧面或向前和向后移动，以便在卡车的背面定向。

This new base granted Stretch two powers. For one thing, resting on four wheels is a whole lot more energy efficient than trying to constantly balance on two. The same is true for animals: A dog or cat is inherently more stable than a human. (Stretch will get 8 hours of battery life, and clients will have the option to upgrade to a double battery that holds 16 hours of charge.) The second advantage is that Stretch’s arm can now pivot around its base, while Handle had to pivot its whole body to turn.

这个新的基础授予延伸两个力量。一方面，在四个轮子上休息的节能比试图不断平衡在两者上更大。动物也是如此：狗或猫本质上比人类更稳定。 （弹力将获得8小时的电池寿命，客户可以选择升级到一个拥有16小时充电的双电池。）第二个优点是，伸展的臂现在可以围绕其基地枢转，而手柄必须枢转整个身体转向。

That arm can lift 50 pounds yet is a quarter the weight of a typical industrial robot arm, Blankespoor says. And oddly enough, this is where it shares the most design DNA with its cousin, Spot the robot dog. “If you take a close look at the wrist joints for Stretch, they're the same as Spot’s hips,” says Blankespoor. “We use the same electric motors and gearboxes and sensors on those joints across Stretch and Spot, and we use the same software to control the joints.”

持有50磅的臂可以抬起50磅，是典型的工业机器人手臂的重量，百搭说。奇怪的是，这就是它与堂兄们分享最多设计的DNA，发现机器人狗。 “如果你仔细看看腕带的伸展，他们就像现场的臀部一样，”鲍普斯说。 “我们在横跨拉伸和点的关节上使用相同的电动机和变速箱和传感器，我们使用相同的软件来控制关节。”

Because the arm is light, it doesn’t require a hulking base to keep it steady. So Boston Dynamics engineers could pare down the base to the size of a pallet, allowing the robot to squeeze into tighter spaces around a warehouse.

因为臂很亮，所以它不需要巨大的基础来保持稳定。因此，波士顿动力学工程师可以将基座削减到托盘的大小，使机器人挤入仓库周围的更紧密的空间。

Stretch also uses a lot of the same cameras and depth sensors as Spot and Atlas and even the same control software to navigate. “It can go up and down ramps, but it's not going to go hiking with you, like Spot,” says Blankespoor. “So it’s kind of a balancing act—we don't want more complexity than we need.”

拉伸也使用大量相同的相机和深度传感器作为现场和地图集，甚至是导航的相同的控制软件。 “它可以上下坡道，但它不会与你一起徒步旅行，就像现货一样，”较空望。 “所以这是一种平衡的行为 - 我们不想要比我们所需要的更复杂。”

Its gripper is an array of vacuum-powered suction cups, which is really all the robot needs if it’s stacking boxes, which have flat surfaces that are easy to stick to. But Blankespoor says Boston Dynamics is working on a next generation of the gripper that still uses suction but will be able to grasp more irregular surfaces—think of a flat box of soda cans covered with shrink wrap.

它的夹具是一系列真空动力的吸盘，如果它的堆叠盒，那么所有机器人都是真正的机器人需要，这具有易于坚持的平坦表面。但是空中的镜片动态正上下一代仍然使用吸力的夹具，而是能够掌握更不规则的表面 - 想到一块覆盖着收缩包裹的苏打水箱。

Boston Dynamics' engineers imagine that Stretch, which will be available next year, will complement human warehouse labor, not replace it. The machine is semiautonomous: A warehouse manager would give it a specific task and the robot would take care of the heavy lifting. “You can think of it like a sophisticated power tool to help warehouse operators get their job done,” says Blankespoor.

“The good news is that automating the back-breaking task of lifting boxes might indeed improve worker safety, and Stretch is unlikely to replace humans entirely,” says MIT Media Lab robotics ethicist Kate Darling. “At the same time, I would like to see companies thinking more creatively about how technology can support their workers rather than automate them away.”

“好消息是，自动化升降箱的备用任务可能确实可以提高工人安全性，并且延伸不太可能完全取代人类，”麻省理工学院媒体实验室机器人伦理凯特·达林说。 “与此同时，我希望看到公司更加创造地思考技术如何支持他们的工人而不是自动化它们。”

How the Soviets Put Out Oil Well Fires by Using Nuclear Bombs
苏联人如何通过使用核炸弹来燃烧油井

2021-04-10 10:59:15

In the early 1960s, the two nuclear superpowers of the world—the United States of America and the Soviet Union—began looking for ways to utilize the tremendous energy released by nuclear bombs for productive purposes. Things that were discussed included blasting a new canal through the Isthmus of Panama, creating an artificial harbor in Alaska, excavating mountains, and other such projects that involved moving large quantities of earth and rocks. Under Operation Plowshare, the US carried out a few tests in the Nevada desert, the effect of which can still be seen in the crater-riddled desert floor. The largest of these craters, from the Sedan nuclear test in 1962, is now a tourist attraction.

20世纪60年代初，世界两座核超级大国 - 美利坚合众国和苏联 - 开始寻找利用核炸弹释放的巨大能源以获得生产目的的方法。讨论的事情包括通过巴拿马的斯蒂斯爆炸新运河，在阿拉斯加创造一个人工港，挖掘山脉，以及其他这样的项目，这些项目涉及大量地球和岩石。在运作普罗佐，美国在内华达沙漠中进行了几次测试，其效果仍然可以在火山口肆无忌惮的沙漠地板中看到。这些陨石坑中最大，从1962年的轿车核试验中，现在是一个旅游景点。

Oil well fires blaze out of control outside Kuwait City during Operation Desert Storm. Photo credit: David Mcleod

在手术沙漠风暴期间，石油井火灾在科威特市外面的控制失控。照片信用：David McLeod

The Soviet Union carried out more extensive tests under the “Nuclear Explosions for the National Economy” program. Using small nuclear explosions they probed the ground for oil deposits, created vast underground caverns for storing natural gas, crushed ore in open-pit mines, dug channels, and built dams. Once while exploring for oil, radioactive gases contaminated a significant portion of a densely populated region near river Volga. Another time, they blew up a river to see if they can create a reservoir. They got a reservoir all right, but it’s radioactive to this day.

苏联在“全国经济核爆炸”计划下进行了更广泛的测试。使用小核爆炸，他们探讨了油沉积物的地面，为储存天然气，碾碎的矿石，露天矿山，挖掘渠道和建筑水坝，创造了巨大的地下洞穴。一旦探索石油，放射性气体污染了伏尔加河附近的一部分密集地区的重要部分。另一个时候，他们吹了一条河，看看他们是否可以创造一个水库。他们得到了一个水库，但它的放射性至今。

In 1963, a gas well in the Urtabulak gas field in Southern Uzbekistan, about 80 km southeast of Bukhara, suffered a blowout at a depth of 2.4 km. Over the next three years the well burned steadily causing losses of more than 12 million cubic meters of gas per day—enough to supply the needs of a city the size of St. Petersburg.

1963年，乌兹别克斯坦南部乌兹别克斯坦乌兹别克斯坦的乌尔塔布拉克气田的一股煤气井，约80公里的布哈拉遭遇了2.4公里的深度。在接下来的三年里，稳定地造成了每天超过1200万立方米的燃气损失 - 足以提供城市的需求圣彼得堡的大小。

When all conventional methods of controlling the fire failed, the Soviet government turned to their nuclear scientists for help. The geologists and physicists of the nuclear program calculated that if a nuclear bomb was detonated close to the blowout, the pressure generated by the explosion cloud squeeze shut any hole within 25 to 50 meters of the explosion. The yield necessary was calculated to 30 kiloton, or about twice the power of the bomb that leveled Hiroshima.

当所有传统的控制消防方法失败时，苏联政府转向核科学家寻求帮助。核计划的地质学家和物理学家计算出，如果核弹轰炸击中井喷靠近井喷，爆炸云产生的压力挤压在爆炸的25到50米范围内。所需的收益率为30千吨，或大约两倍于炸弹的炸弹。

In the fall of 1966, two slant wells, slightly wider than a feet, were drilled simultaneously as close as possible to the oil well that was burning uncontrollably. At a depth of 1,400 meters, at an estimated distance of 35 meters from the leaking oil well, a nuclear bomb was lowered into one of the holes. The hole was then filled with cement to contain the explosion and prevent it from erupting to the surface. The bomb was then detonated.

在1966年的秋季，两个倾斜孔比脚略宽，同时钻孔，尽可能靠近燃烧不可控制的油井。深度为1,400米，距离泄漏油井35米的估计距离，将核炸弹降低到其中一个孔中。然后将该孔填充水泥以含有爆炸并防止其爆发到表面。然后炸弹爆炸了。

The Soviet newspaper Pravda Vostoka of Tashkent published an account of the experiment at the time.

塔什干苏维埃报纸Pravda vostoka在当时发表了对实验的叙述。

“On that cold autumn day in 1966, an underground tremor of unprecedented force shook the [ground] with a sparse grass cover on white sand. A dusty haze rose over the desert. The orange colored torch of the blazing well diminished, first slowly, then more rapidly, until it flickered and finally died out. For the first time in 1,064 days, quiet descended on the area. The jet like roar of the gas well had been silenced.”

“在1966年的那个寒冷的秋日，一个前所未有的力量的地下震颤震动了[地面]用稀疏的草盖在白色沙滩上。尘土飞扬的阴霾上升了沙漠。炽热的橙色火炬很好地减少了，首先慢慢地，然后更快地迅速，直到它闪烁，最终死了。在1,064天内第一次，安静地区在该地区下降。像气井一样咆哮的喷射已经沉默了。“

It took only 23 seconds for the flames to go out. Historic footage of this incredible operation shows the moment the nuclear bomb was detonated and the subsequent dying of the fire.

火焰外出花了23秒。这种令人难以置信的操作的历史镜头显示核弹被引爆的那一刻，随后的火灾死亡。

This was the first time in history when a nuclear bomb was successfully used to extinguish oil fires, and it wasn’t going to be the last.

这是核弹成功灭火时历史上的第一次，并且不会成为最后一个。

Months later another fire broke out at the nearby Pamuk gas field, and the fire spread to the surface via additional holes. This time a 47 kiloton nuclear weapon was lowered to a depth of 2,440 meters and detonated. Because the leakage was large, the flow continued for seven days before it finally died out.

几个月后，另一个火灾在附近的Pamuk气田爆发，火灾通过额外的孔蔓延到表面。这次47千吨核武器被降低到2,440米的深度并引爆。因为泄漏很大，所以流动持续七天，然后终于死亡。

The second success gave Soviet scientists great confidence in their new technique of controlling runaway gas and oil wells. In the following years, several more opportunities arose for the use of nuclear explosions to extinguish runaway gas well fires. In May 1972, a 14 kiloton explosion at a death of 1,700 meters sealed a two-year-old gas leak in the Mayskii gas field about 30 km southeast of the city of Mary in Central Asia. Then again in July the same year, another runaway gas well in Ukraine was sealed with a 3.8 kiloton nuclear explosion at a depth of more than 2.4 km. The last attempt occurred in 1981 on a runaway well in the Kumzhinskiy gas deposit in the northern coast of European Russia. A 37.6 kiloton nuclear explosion was detonated at a depth of 1,511 meters, but this time the well was not sealed because the position of the leak was not precisely known.

第二次成功使苏联科学家对其控制失控煤气和油井的新技术充满信心。在接下来的几年里，有几个机会采取了使用核爆炸以灭绝失控的气井火灾。 1972年5月，14千吨爆炸在1,700米的死亡中封锁了梅尔斯齐天然气领域的两年历史的气体泄漏，大约在中亚市玛丽市东南30公里。在同年7月再次，乌克兰的另一个失控的汽油良好地用3.8千吨核爆炸，深度超过2.4公里。最后一次尝试发生在1981年在欧洲北部北部海岸的Kumzhinskiy天然气矿床中发生艰巨。 37.6千吨核爆炸被引爆，深度为1,511米，但这次井未密封，因为泄漏的位置并未精确众所周知。

The experiments came to an end in 1989 when the Soviets suspended any kind of nuclear weapons testing.

当苏维埃暂停任何类型的核武器测试时，实验结束了1989年。

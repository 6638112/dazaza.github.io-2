Since you are reading this blog, you may be even familiar with the broader context.

由于您正在阅读此博客，因此您甚至可能熟悉更广泛的上下文。

In 1961, the MIT recieved a DEC PDP-1 computer from Digital Equipment Corporation as a donation. It was one of the very first production models and DEC’s motivation was a multiple one: First, the PDP-1 was somewhat of a commercial version of MIT’s experimental TX-0 computer , then, the donation was reinforcing ties between MIT and the DEC founders, who had been working on various experimental MIT machines in the past, and, last but not least, DEC was hoping for MIT to come up with some exciting demo applications for the new machine. — And a hell of a demo application DEC should get out of this deal, probably the killer application of interactive, visual computing of all time.

1961年，麻省理工学院收到了Digital Equipment Corporation捐赠的DEC PDP-1计算机。它是最早的生产模型之一，DEC的动机是多重的：首先，PDP-1有点像是MIT的实验TX-0计算机的商业版本，然后，捐赠加强了MIT与DEC创始人之间的联系。 ，他过去曾在各种实验性的MIT机器上工作，最后但并非最不重要的一点是，DEC希望MIT为新机器提供一些令人兴奋的演示应用程序。 — DEC演示应用程序应该彻底摆脱困境，这可能是一直以来交互式，可视化计算的杀手级应用程序。

As it happened, there was a community of students gathering around the TX-0, which sprang into action to port essential tooling software from the TX-0 to the PDP-1, earning in turn rights to some runtime on the new machine. This very group also used to socialize at MIT’s Tech Model Railroad Club (TMRC), where it picked up reinforcement in form of a grade student by the name of Steve Russell, who, while not one of the TX-0 gang, had previously implemented the very first version of LISP on MIT’s IBM 704 mainframe. These teamed up to provide a demonstration program for MIT’s next annual Open House day in May 1962, a program, which, as they consented, ought to satisfy the following criteria:

碰巧的是，有一群学生聚集在TX-0周围，他们采取了行动，将必要的工具软件从TX-0移植到PDP-1，从而获得了新机器上某些运行时的权利。这个小组还曾在麻省理工学院的技术模型铁路俱乐部（TMRC）进行社交活动，在那里以史蒂夫·罗素（Steve Russell）的名字命名了一名年级学生，他虽然不是TX-0帮派之一，但以前曾实施过这种运动MIT的IBM 704大型机上的LISP的第一个版本。他们联手为MIT在1962年5月的下一个年度开放日提供了一个示范程序，该程序在他们同意下应该满足以下条件：

It should show off as many of the computer’s resources as possible, and tax those resources to the limit;

它应该显示出尽可能多的计算机资源，并将这些资源加到极限。

Within a consistent framework, it should be interesting, which means every run should be different;

在一个一致的框架内，这应该很有趣，这意味着每次运行都应该不同。

It should involve the onlooker in a pleasurable and active way — in short, it should be a game.

它应该以愉悦和积极的方式吸引旁观者-简而言之，它应该是一种游戏。

Flavored with a bit of dedication to lowbrow science fiction and some contemporary Space Race enthusiasm this principal idea took shape as a game for two human players interacting in the virtual realms of the PDP-1’s scope, and Steve Russell eventually stepped out to put it to work. And so, what DEC got out of that deal, was Spacewar!, the very first digital video game.

出于对低俗科幻小说的某种奉献精神和一些当代太空竞赛的热情，这一主要思想逐渐形成，它是两个人类玩家在PDP-1范围的虚拟领域中进行交互的游戏，而史蒂夫·罗素（Steve Russell）最终出面将其付诸实践。工作。因此，DEC从这笔交易中脱颖而出的便是第一款数字视频游戏《 Spacewar！》。

(In case you are interested in the particulars, head over to “The Origin of Spacewar” by J. M. Graetz, 1981.)

（如果您对详细信息感兴趣，请转至J. M. Graetz于1981年出版的“太空战争的起源”。）

Both images share a date attribution of “1961 ca.” and show a computer in a deplorable state of disassembly placed at a model railway layout. The layout is the same in both images, even the trains and coaches are in the same positions, and general aspects and perspectives are similar, hinting at both photos being shot at the same occasion. Moreover, the coaches are of typical North American appearance, providing a clue for where the scene is set. Due to its form factor, the computer may be a DEC PDP-1 or DEC PDP-4 and surely, there’s also the iconic hexagonal housing of the Type 30 visual CRT display, which went with the PDP-1 and the PDP-4, to be discerned. A closer inspection of the console switches to be seen in the second photo confirms that this is, in deed, a PDP-1. The habitual appearance of the person, who appears to be operating a light pen on the first image, also seems to roughly fit the period (this may be 1960s, maybe a bit later, like around 1970). So, this is clearly the PDP-1 at the TMRC, maybe, while the TX-0 gang was porting the Macro assembler, maybe even during the development of Spacewar!. — How lucky we are to have these images, which are showing the PDP-1 in this noteworthy context!

两张图片的日期归因均为“ 1961 ca”。并显示了处于模型铁路布局中处于可拆卸状态的计算机。两张图片中的布局都相同，即使火车和长途汽车都位于相同的位置，并且总体方面和视角也相似，这暗示着两张照片是在同一时间拍摄的。此外，教练具有典型的北美风貌，为现场设置提供了线索。由于其外形尺寸，计算机可能是DEC PDP-1或DEC PDP-4，当然，还有Type 30可视CRT显示器的标志性六角形外壳，与PDP-1和PDP-4一起使用，有待观察。在第二张照片中仔细观察控制台开关，可以确认这确实是PDP-1。这个人的惯常外貌，似乎是在操作第一幅图像上的一支电笔，似乎也大致适应了这一时期（可能是1960年代，也许是稍晚一些的时期，例如1970年左右）。因此，很明显，这可能是TMRC上的PDP-1，甚至在TX-0帮派正在移植Macro汇编程序时，甚至在Spacewar！的开发过程中也是如此。 —我们很幸运有这些图像，这些图像在这种值得注意的情况下显示了PDP-1！

The timeline simply doesn’t fit. The photos couldn’t have been shot in 1961, since the display Type 30 display hadn’t yet arrived, and they couldn’t have been taken after this, since the PDP-1 was installed in Room 20-260. The timeline simply doesn’t allow for the PDP-1 having been taken to the TMRC layout.

时间轴根本不合适。这些照片无法在1961年拍摄，因为尚未显示30型显示屏，并且之后也无法拍摄照片，因为PDP-1安装在20-260室。时间轴根本不允许将PDP-1带入TMRC布局。

To put this into perspective, there were just about 55 PDP-1s ever made. Half of the PDP-1s built between 1960 and 1965 were actually packaged as International Telephone and Telegraph (ITT) Automated Data Exchange (ADX) 7300 messaging system, used for switching 256 telegraph lines each, which hadn’t a display at all. (Major ADX 7300 cutomers included Eastern Airlines in Canada, the U.S. and Mexico, Trans World Airlines, the U.S. State Department, NASA, the U.S. Air Force and the Aluminum Company of Canada.) BTW, a not that unimportant footnote to computer history, Gordon Bell deleveloped the UART (Universal Asynchronous Receiver Transmitter) while working on this project. (Hats off to Lyle Bickley for research on the ITT ADX 7300.)

从这个角度来看，曾经制造过大约55台PDP-1。实际上，在1960年至1965年之间生产的PDP-1的一半被包装为国际电话和电报（ITT）自动数据交换（ADX）7300消息系统，用于分别交换256条电报线，而根本没有显示。 （主要的ADX 7300剪切机包括加拿大，美国和墨西哥的东方航空，环球航空公司，美国国务院，美国宇航局，美国空军和加拿大铝业公司。）BTW，对于计算机历史而言，这并不是那么重要的注脚，在进行此项目时，Gordon Bell取消了UART（通用异步接收器发送器）的级别。 （前往Lyle Bickley进行ITT ADX 7300的研究。）

Also, not every of the PDP-1s actually sold as PDP-1s featured a display. A prominent example is the PDP-1D timesharing prototype built to BBN’s specifications, which never had a display. According to some sources only aboult half of the PDP-1s featured a display of some kind, while we see here a PDP-1 not only with a display, but also with a light pen attached to it. This leaves less than a few dozens of installations eligble.

而且，并非实际上所有出售为PDP-1的PDP-1都带有显示器。一个突出的例子是根据BBN的规格制造的PDP-1D分时原型，该显示器从未显示过。根据某些消息来源，只有一半以上的PDP-1具有某种类型的显示器，而我们在这里看到的PDP-1不仅具有显示器，而且还带有光笔。这样一来，只有不到几十个安装合格。

Further, while the PDP-1 was advertised as “reasonably priced”, it came at a considerable price tag. It certainly was reasonably priced, at an initial unit price of US$ 120,000 and an additional $ 14,300 for the Type 30 display, which put it more in the range of a better drum computer as compared to the $ 10,600 monthly lease for a contemporary IBM 1401 in its basic configuration. However, this is relative to the general cost of computers in the day. The $ 134,300 for a PDP-1 and a display was still 11 new family homes and a brand new average car in 1960-money’s worth. Hence, only major institutions or potent corporations entertaining a well-funded research lab at the very forefront of technecological innovation would afford one.

此外，尽管PDP-1被宣传为“价格合理”，但价格却相当可观。它的价格当然是合理的，最初的单位价格为120,000美元，而Type 30显示器的价格为14,300美元，与现代IBM的每月10,600美元的租赁费用相比，它的价格更多地属于更好的鼓式计算机范围。 1401的基本配置。但是，这与一天中计算机的总体成本有关。 PDP-1和显示器的售价为134,300美元，仍然是11栋新的家庭住宅和1960年货币的平均价格。因此，只有在技术创新最前沿的大型机构或有实力的公司在资金雄厚的研究实验室中进行娱乐活动才可以提供。

尤其是，这看起来很有趣，因为它明确引用了“ DEC Photo Archive，Ap Cards”作为来源。因此，我们知道DEC照片档案库中确实存在该特定机器的照片。它也是总共7台机器中的一台，在此列表中提到了DEC Photo Archive（没有其他与我们的名字缩写“ UM”匹配的机器。）此外，PDP-1C与这些照片上看到的机器匹配。

But there’s still hope. The list of peripherals isn’t exhaustive (as can be seen from the required Type 1578 switches missing) and the Type 340 display was only availble as late as 1964/5 and came at quite a hefty price of US$ 28,600. Chances are, this was a later acquisition, which supplanted a Type 30 display previously obtained together with the machine.

但是仍然有希望。外围设备列表并不详尽（从必填的1578型交换机中可以看出），340型显示屏直到1964/5年才可用，价格高达28,600美元。很有可能这是后来的收购，它取代了以前与机器一起获得的Type 30显示器。

I haven’t been able to find any references to a club layout at the University of Michigan at the time in question. Having a closer look at these photos, this seems to be more an 0 gauge layout than H0 scale. There are some references to recent ties between the local LMRC club and the University of Michigan to be found on the Web, but this is by no means sufficient proof.

在相关时间段，我还没有找到密歇根大学关于俱乐部布局的任何参考资料。仔细观察这些照片，这似乎更像是0标尺布局而不是H0标尺。在网上可以找到有关本地LMRC俱乐部与密歇根大学之间最近关系的一些参考，但这绝不是充分的证据。

We can’t be sure and may still entertain a reasonable amount of doubt. However, this being the University of Michigan around 1970 is still the best guess. At least, it’s my best guess. I guess, it’s close.

我们无法确定，仍然可能会引起合理的怀疑。然而，这是1970年左右的密歇根大学仍然是最好的猜测。至少，这是我最好的猜测。我想这已经很接近了。

Lumber Prices Are Falling Fast, Turning Hoarders into Sellers
木材价格迅速下降，将囤积者转向卖家

2021-06-18 09:38:28

Futures for July delivery ended Tuesday at $1,009.90 per thousand board feet, down 41% from the record of $1,711.20 reached in early May. Futures have declined 14 of the past 16 trading days.

7月交付期货周二周二以1,009.90美元的价格为每千辆，下降41％，从5月初达到1,711.20美元。期货在过去的16个交易日中有14个。

Cash lumber prices are also crashing. Pricing service Random Lengths said Friday that its framing composite index, which tracks on-the-spot sales, dropped $122 to $1,324, its biggest ever weekly decline. The pullback came just six weeks after the index rose $124 during the first week of May, its most on record. Random Lengths described a chaotic rout in which sawmill managers struggled to provide customers with price quotes. It said late Tuesday that its index had dropped another $114, to $1,210.

现金木材价格也崩溃。定价服务随机长度表示，其框架综合指数追踪现场销售额，其最大的每周下降122至1,324美元。在5月的第一周索引上涨124美元后，下跌六周是六周，最重要的是。随机长度描述了一种混沌溃败，其中锯木厂经理努力为客户提供价格报价。星期二表示，其指数下降了114美元，达到1,210美元。

Economists and investors have wondered if sky-high prices for wood products would doom the booming housing market. Builders raised home prices and many stopped selling houses before the studs were installed, lest they misjudge costs and sell too cheaply. Lumber became central to the inflation debate: whether a period of runaway inflation was afoot or high prices were temporary shocks that would ease as the economy moved further from lockdown.

经济学家和投资者想知道木材产品的天空高价格是否会使住房市场蓬勃发展。建筑商在安装铆钉之前提高了房价，许多人停止卖房，以免误导成本并畅销。木材成为通货膨胀辩论的核心：逃任通货膨胀的时期是否正在进行或高价格是暂时的冲击，因为经济从锁定进一步移动时会放松。

The rapid decline suggests a bubble that has burst and the question is how low lumber prices will fall. Even after tumbling, lumber futures remain nearly three times what is typical for this time of year. Lumber producers and traders expect that prices will remain relatively high due to the strong housing market, but that the supply bottlenecks and frenzied buying that characterized the economy’s reopening and sent prices to multiples of the old all-time highs are winding down.

迅速下降表明了一个爆发的泡沫，问题是低木材价格将如何下降。即使在翻滚后，木材期货仍然近三倍于一年中这个典型的典型。木材生产商和贸易商预计由于房地产市场强劲，价格将保持相对较高，但供应瓶颈和疯狂的购买，以为经济重新开放和向旧的历史高级倍增的倍数送达。

During the run-up, wood was hoarded by builders, retailers and others worried about running out of material during a construction season set into overdrive by low mortgage rates and federal stimulus payments.

在跑步期间，墙上的囤积者，零售商和其他人在施工赛季的施工赛期间被低抵押贷款率和联邦刺激款项预防，占据了物质。

“Everyone was buying more than they needed,” said Mike Wisnefski, a former lumber trader and chief executive of online marketplace MaterialsXchange. “There was this fear of lack of availability.”

“每个人都购买的比他们所需要的更多，”迈克·威尔纽斯基说，这是在线市场材料Xchange的前木材交易商和首席执行官。 “有这种恐惧缺乏可用性。”

Now the market is being flooded by what Mr. Wisnefski calls shadow inventory as businesses that are normally big buyers, such as home builders and companies that prefabricate the trusses that hold up roofs and floors, sell from their own stockpiles.

现在市场正在被Wisnefski先生呼叫影子库存作为通常是大买家的业务的淹没，例如家庭建筑商和预制桁架的公司，从他们自己的库存中卖出屋顶和地板。

Lumber producers say that prices should remain relatively high as home builders try to erase the country’s deficit of housing. Mortgage-finance firm Freddie Mac estimated in April that the U.S. is about 3.8 million houses shy of meeting demand, thanks largely to the lack of construction following the 2008 housing crash.

木材生产商表示，随着家庭建设者试图抹去国家的住房赤字，价格应该保持相对较高。抵押贷款金融公司Freddie Mac于4月份估计，美国约有380万房屋害羞的满足需求，主要致力于2008年住房崩溃后缺乏建设。

“I don’t think $1,000 lumber prices are the new normal,” Devin Stockfish, chief executive of lumber producer Weyerhaeuser WY -0.99% , told investors at a conference last week. “But that being said, when you think about the amount of housing that we’re going to have to build in the U.S. over the next three, five, 10 years, that’s just a significant amount of demand for wood products.”

“我认为我不需要1000美元的木材价格是新的正常新的正常，”Lumber Producer Weyerhaeuser Wy -0.99％的首席​​执行官Devin Streadfish Wy -0.99％在上周在会议上告诉投资者。 “但是说，当你想到所以在未来三个，五个，10年内，我们将要在美国那里建造的住房数量，这只是对木制品的大量需求。”

At the same conference, executives from lumber producer PotlatchDeltic PCH -0.40% said they expect lumber to trade in a range of $700 to $800 through next year. That is still more than the pre-pandemic record of $639 and is based on their estimation of the price that mills in British Columbia need to break even sawing North America’s most-expensive logs. Prices below that could prompt mills in Canada to dial back output or shut down, which would eventually force prices higher to meet demand, they said.

在同一次会议上，来自木材生产商Potlatchdeltic PCH -0.40％的高管表示，他们希望木材在700美元至下一年的范围内交易。这仍然超过了大流行前639美元的历史记录，依据他们对不列颠哥伦比亚省米尔斯的价格的估计，甚至需要打破北美最昂贵的日志。他们说，下面的价格可以提示加拿大的磨坊，以拨回产出或关闭，这最终会强迫价格更高，以满足需求。

The stock market is assuming even lower lumber prices ahead. Analysts with BMO Capital Markets recently calculated that the share prices of three Canadian firms that have become the biggest sawyers in the southern pinelands have priced in expectations of $447 lumber next year. That would be a little more expensive than normal, but more in line with historical prices.

股市仍在假设较低的木材价格。 BMO Capital Markets的分析人士最近计算出三个加拿大公司已成为南方南部最大的锯片的公司的股价已在明年的期望中定价为447美元。这将比正常贵，但更多符合历史价格。

Those firms— Interfor IFP -3.35% , Canfor CFPZF -2.97% and West Fraser Timber WFG -3.18% —along with U.S. rivals Weyerhaeuser and PotlatchDeltic have notched record profits since ramping up mills to meet an unexpected rush of demand last summer.

这些公司 - IFP-3.35％，CANFOR CFPZF -2.97％和West Fraser Timber WFG -3.18％ - 与美国竞争对手Weyerhaeuser和Potlatchdeltic有缺口创业盈利，因为去年夏天升高了磨坊以满足意外的需求。

Sawmill owners have had some of the best performing stocks of the pandemic, though all five lumber producers are down at least 9.5% over the past month.

Sawmill业主拥有大流行的一些最佳表现股票，尽管所有五个木材生产商在过去一个月内至少有9.5％。

A flurry of stock sales by forest-products executives last quarter suggests that those at the top of these firms believe peak lumber is in the rearview, said Ben Silverman, director of research at InsiderScore, which tracks executive stock sales and purchases.

上季度森林产品高管的一系列股票销售表明，这些公司顶部的人认为，历史执行股票销售和采购的研究总监Ben Silverman表示，那些在后卫中。

Executives at Weyerhaeuser sold $14.6 million worth of company shares last quarter, the most since the third quarter of 2016, according to InsiderScore. PotlatchDeltic executives sold $12.1 million during that time, the most since InsiderScore began keeping track in 2003. Timberland owner Rayonier RYN -0.99% also disclosed the heaviest selling by insiders in more than a decade. Rayonier doesn’t own any sawmills but holds timberland in New Zealand and the Pacific Northwest, where log prices have risen along with those of lumber. Spokesmen for Weyerhaeuser and Rayonier declined to comment. Representatives for PotlatchDeltic didn’t respond to requests for comment.

据Insiderscore表示，韦伊赫队的高管在2016年第三季度自2016年第三季度以来的价值为1460万美元的公司股票。 Potlatchdeltic高管在此期间销售了1210万美元，自Insiderscore于2003年开始跟踪。Timberland所有者Rayonier Ryn -0.99％亦披露了十多个十多年的内部人的最重的销售。 Rayonier不拥有任何锯木厂，而是拥有新西兰的Timberland和太平洋西北地区，日志价格与木材的日志一起上升。 Weyerhaeuser和Rayonier的发言人拒绝发表评论。 Potlatchdeltic的代表没有回应评论的要求。

“This level of selling is simply unusual and to have this type of alignment among peers like this is unusual,” Mr. Silverman said. “It shows a consensus within the group about how they are thinking about their stock prices.”

“这种销售水平只是不寻常的，并且在像这样的同行之间的这种类型的对齐是不寻常的，”Silverman先生说。 “它在本集团中显示了关于他们如何考虑其股票价格的达成共识。”

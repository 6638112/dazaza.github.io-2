Grabango, which develops computer vision-based check-out free tech for existing grocery and convenience stores, raises $39M Series B led by Commerce Ventures
Grabango为现有杂货店和便利店开发基于计算机视觉的自由理工，培养了商务风险投资390万系列

2021-06-09 11:04:22

In 2016 Will Glaser was consulting for tech companies and found himself increasingly immersed in autonomous vehicles, fascinated by how the sector utilizes computer and machine vision. The former cofounder of music streaming service Pandora and software company Hydra Systems came to the conclusion that this could be the most disruptive technological advancement of the current and next decade — but probably not because of driverless cars.

2016年将展示Glaser为科技公司咨询，并发现自己越来越沉浸在自治车辆中，令人着迷于该行业如何利用计算机和机器愿景。音乐流服务潘多拉和软件公司Hydra Systems的前联合国的结论是，这可能是当前和未来十年的最具破坏性的技术进步 - 但可能不是因为无人驾驶汽车。

Glaser realized the recent advancements in computer vision could benefit society sooner, and in a broader way, and thought what is a more universal experience than going to the grocery store and having to wait in line to checkout. “I landed on checkout-free shopping, both as being economically important, and useful to the end users, and that’s what got me excited,” Glaser tells Forbes. The ensuing company, Grabango, got to work in 2017 to design out-of-the-box checkoutless systems. The tech allows Grabango users to shop as they regularly would, at the stores they already frequent, but to skip the checkout line and just scan a barcode near the door on their way out. The service formally launched in September 2020 and counts five corporate chains as early customers, like grocer Giant Eagle, and has quickly amassed thousands of repeat customers. The startup raised its Series B round, as originally reported in the Midas Touch newsletter, to continue expansion.

Glaser意识到计算机愿景中最近的进步可以越早损益社会，并以更广泛的方式造成社会，并以更广泛的方式思考比去杂货店更具普遍的经历，并不得不等待结账。 “我降落于免费购物，无论是在经济上重要的，还有对最终用户有用，而且这就是让我兴奋的是什么，”Glaser告诉Forbes。随后的公司Grabango必须在2017年工作，设计开箱即用的检查系统。 Tech允许Grabango用户经常购物，在他们已经经常频繁的商店，而是跳过结账线，只需扫描门附近的条形码。该服务于2020年9月正式启动，并将五个企业连锁店算作早期客户，如杂货店巨型鹰，并迅速积累了数千个重复客户。该初创公司提出了其系列B轮，最初在MIDAS触控通讯中报告，继续扩展。

The Berkeley, California-based company raised a $39 million Series B round led by existing backer Commerce Ventures with participation from Founders Fund, Honeywell Ventures and Unilever Ventures, among others, including large players in the grocery sector. “When someone who you want to be a customer wants to invest you make room for the capital,” Glaser says about the oversubscribed funding. The startup has raised $71 million in total and will use the latest capital batch to expand into more stores and secure more partnerships. Matt Nichols, a partner at Commerce Ventures, says that the firm has been tracking the checkoutless retail space for a few years. Commerce originally invested in the company’s 2019 Series A and doubled down for the Series B once the startup launched into stores. “They are the only ones so far [in the U.S.] to actually launch in a real production environment by taking an existing store and making it work,” Nichols says. “Really no one else has done it before and that is what got us excited.” Israel-based Trigo has launched similar tech in stores in Europe.

基于加利福尼亚州的Berkeley公司筹集了3900万美元的B系列，由现有的Backer Commerce Ventures参与创始人资金，霍尼韦尔风险投资和联合利华企业，其中包括杂货店的大型球员。 “当你想成为客户想要投资你的资本腾出空间时，”Glaser说，关于超额认购的资金。初创公司总共筹集了7100万美元，并将使用最新的资本批次扩展到更多商店并获得更多合作伙伴关系。商务冒险的合作伙伴Matt Nichols表示，该公司一直在追踪几年的支架零售空间。商业最初投资于公司2019系列A，一旦初创公司发射到商店，就可以为B系列增加一倍。 “他们是唯一一个迄今为止[在美国]的人，实际上通过乘坐现有商店并制造它的工作来实际发射实际生产环境，”尼科尔斯说。 “真的没有其他人在之前做过，这就是让我们兴奋的东西。”以色列的Trigo在欧洲商店推出了类似的技术。

Nichols added that the system's ability to be retrofitted to existing stores was also a draw because of its potential to scale, which was intentional. The system works because of a labyrinth of ceiling sensors equipped with cameras about the same size as the ones in an iPhone. The cameras track where shoppers go, what they pick up and what they do and don’t take with them. At least two cameras can see every item in the store at any given time. This approach enables the sensors to be installed in almost any store because changes in store layout or merchandise won’t impact the system’s accuracy, which currently sits at about 99.6% Glaser says. “[Retail] is a difficult, difficult business,” Glaser says. “Our presumption is that retailers are smart and know what they are doing so we need to adapt our tech to them and not the other way around.”

尼科尔斯补充说，系统＆＃39;在现有商店被改装的能力也是一种绘制，因为它的潜力缩放，这是故意的。该系统是由于迷宫的天花板传感器，配备了与iPhone中的相同大小的相机。购物者去的相机轨道，他们接受了什么以及他们所做的事情，也不带他们。至少两个相机可以在任何给定时间在商店中看到商店中的每个项目。这种方法使传感器能够安装在几乎任何商店中，因为商店布局或商品的变化不会影响系统的准确性，这目前坐在约99.6％的Glaser上。 “[零售]是一个困难，困难的业务，”Glaser说。 “我们的推定是零售商很聪明，知道他们所做的事情，所以我们需要将我们的技术调整给他们，而不是另外的方式。”

When Grabango users go to checkout, they scan their app at the designated space and merely walk out. Glaser says the company doesn’t track or store the data its sensors collect. He adds the system deletes its data almost immediately after the shopper leaves, noting the potential liability, and that it doesn’t use facial recognition of any kind. “Privacy rights are top of mind,” Glaser says. “I sort of cringe when others are excited to sell data. We are deeply, profoundly not in the data business.”

当Grabango用户转到结账时，他们会在指定的空间扫描他们的应用程序并仅走出来。 Glaser表示，该公司不会跟踪或存储其传感器收集的数据。他添加系统几乎在购物者离开后立即删除其数据，并不潜在责任，并不使用任何类型的面部识别。 “隐私权是最重要的，”Glaser说。 “当其他人兴奋销售数据时，我有点畏缩。我们深深地，深受数据业务。“

Glaser says the company has seen strong demand for its tech thus far, and has already had to turn away potential partnerships. “We have only signed five customers, not because we have only gotten five calls, but they are the top retailers in the world,” Glaser says. “Every single one of five companies have more than $1 billion in revenue. They don’t want us to do one proof-of-concept store, they want to build out their whole fleet. Our model is not to grow as fast as we can but as wisely as we can.” Both Glaser and Nichols predict that once consumers start using this kind of tech, they won’t settle for less.

Glaser表示，该公司于到目前为止对其技术的需求很大，已经不得不拒绝潜在的伙伴关系。 “我们只签了五个客户，不是因为我们只有五个电话，但他们是世界上的顶级零售商，”Glaser说。 “五家公司中的每一个都有超过10亿美元的收入。他们不希望我们做一个概念验证商店，他们想要建造他们的整个舰队。我们的型号并不像我们尽可能聪明的那样快速增长。“两个Glaser和Nichols都预测，一旦消费者开始使用这种技术，他们就不会满足于此。

Glaser sees many avenues for Grabango to grow both in the same category through international expansion and also into different retail categories. “Computer vision is the most powerful technology of the coming decade,” he says. “The part we are focusing on is people’s interaction with things, others are focused on keeping a Tesla on the road. We’ve landed on the go-to-market strategy of starting with grocery and convenience stores because there are the greatest needs there.”

Glaser通过国际扩张以及不同的零售类别，Glabango看到许多Grabango途径在同一类别中成长。 “计算机愿景是未来十年的最强大的技术，”他说。 “我们专注的部分是人们与事物的互动，其他人专注于保持TESLA在路上。我们降落于从杂货和便利店开始的上市战略，因为那里有最大的需求。“

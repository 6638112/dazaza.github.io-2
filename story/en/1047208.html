India is restoring 4G internet services in Jammu and Kashmir, 18 months after imposing a sweeping communications blackout in the Muslim-majority region
在穆斯林占多数的地区实施全面的通信中断18个月后，印度正在恢复查Jam和克什米尔的4G互联网服务

2021-02-06 20:32:11

Rohit Kansal, principal secretary of the Jammu and Kashmir government, said 4G internet services were being restored in the entire region.

查mu和克什米尔政府首席秘书罗希特·坎萨尔（Rohit Kansal）表示，整个地区正在恢复4G互联网服务。

India partially lifted the ban on internet in two districts — out of 20 — of the state last year but maintained speed restrictions (2G internet), after a ruling from the Supreme Court that found an indefinite shutdown of the internet in the state unwarranted, and demonstrated “abuse of power” by the Prime Minister Narendra Modi-led government.

去年，印度最高州的一项裁决裁定该州无限期关闭互联网，在该州的两个地区中，印度在该州的20个地区中部分解除了互联网禁令，但仍保持了速度限制（2G互联网）。总理纳伦德拉·莫迪（Narendra Modi）领导的政府表现出“滥用权力”。

The lifting of restrictions at the time had applied to 301 “whitelisted” services that included entertainment platforms such as Netflix and Amazon and some news outlets including The New York Times. Many Indian news outlets remained blocked, along with all social media. Many residents complained that the slow 2G speeds barely allowed them to check their emails and WhatsApp messages.

当时的限制解除适用于301个“列入白名单”的服务，其中包括诸如Netflix和Amazon之类的娱乐平台以及包括《纽约时报》在内的一些新闻媒体。许多印度新闻媒体以及所有社交媒体仍然受阻。许多居民抱怨说，缓慢的2G速度几乎使他们无法查看电子邮件和WhatsApp消息。

The internet ban in Jammu and Kashmir, home to more than 13 million people, has been by far the longest by any democracy.

查mu和克什米尔地区（共有1300万人）的互联网禁令到目前为止是所有民主国家中时间最长的。

In August, New Delhi told Supreme Court that it will start offering high-speed internet in one district of Jammu and one of Kashmir Valley on a “trial basis.” Late last year, the government resumed broadband service in the region. Critics have said that ban on internet has cost citizens of Jammu and Kashmir hundreds of thousands of jobs and millions of dollars.

8月，新德里告诉最高法院，它将开始在查mu地区和克什米尔山谷地区之一提供“试用”高速互联网。去年底，政府恢复了该地区的宽带服务。批评人士说，禁止上网已经使查mu和克什米尔的公民丧生了数十万个工作和数百万美元。

Raman Jit Singh Chima, a senior international counsel and Asia Pacific Policy director at Access Now, a nonprofit internet advocacy organization, said today’s move was welcoming, but “let’s be clear — the previous shutdown was excessive, mindlessly repeated. Glad that all Jammu and Kashmir residents will soon have internet restored, denied to them in violation of their rights under the Indian Constitution.”

非营利性互联网倡导组织Access Now的高级国际顾问兼亚太地区政策总监Raman Jit Singh Chima说，今天的举动是令人欢迎的，但“请清楚，以前的关闭是过分的，无意识地重复了。很高兴所有查mu和克什米尔居民都将很快恢复互联网连接，并否认他们违反了《印度宪法》规定的权利。”

Omar Abdullah, former chief minister of Jammu and Kashmir, celebrated the news, adding “For the first time since Aug 2019 all of J&K will have 4G mobile data. Better late than never.”

占木与克什米尔邦前首席部长奥马尔·阿卜杜拉（Omar Abdullah）为这一消息庆祝。他补充说：“自2019年8月以来，所有J＆amp; K都将首次拥有4G移动数据。迟到总比不到好。”

Who makes wikiHow's bizarre art (2020)

Getting to the bottom of one of the internet’s most ridiculously drawn mysteries

进入其中一个互联网最易绘制的谜团的底部

F ounded in 2005, wikiHow.com appears to be a typical volunteer-authored wiki site that offers step-by-step guides on how to do just about anything, from how to tie your shoes to how to reverse a curse. But there’s a conspicuous difference between wikiHow and your average wiki: the site’s infamous, pallid, anime-adjacent illustrations.

FORY于2005年，WikiHow.com似乎是一个典型的志愿者撰写的Wiki网站，提供关于如何做任何事情的逐步指南，从如何将鞋子捆绑到如何扭转诅咒。但是，Wikihow和您的平均Wiki之间存在显着差异：该网站的臭名昭着，苍白，动漫相邻的插图。

Check out the eerily humanlike cat in “ How to Te ach Your Cat to Do Tricks,” the swelling airline employee explaining “ How to Visit Antarctica on a Budget,” or whatever the hell is going on in “ How to Stop Laughing at Inappropriate Times.” The site’s illustrations sometimes look like a file of clip art got shoved into a blender and then was indiscriminately pasted back together. Click the site’s random article button a few times, and you’ll likely find some head-scratching depictions of human interaction. Or, let me just draw your attention to this. Or this. Or THESE.

看看令人毛骨悚然的人类猫在“如何嘲笑你的猫，”膨胀的航空公司员工解释“如何在预算上访问南极洲”或者在“如何停止嘲笑不恰当的时间。“该网站的插图有时看起来像剪贴画的文件被推入搅拌机，然后不分青红皂白地贴在一起。单击网站的随机文章按钮几次，您可能会发现一些人为互动的头部划伤描绘。或者，让我只是引起你的注意。或这个。或者这些。

wikiHow’s anarchic aesthetic has attracted a cult following, inspiring a popular Reddit board, a lightly viral guessing game website, a currently dormant Twitter account, a frequently updated Twitter account, a surprisingly active Tumblr profile, and more than a few blogs. Still, beyond a trickle of unverifiable social chatter on Reddit, Quora, and Twitter, nobody seems to have been able to definitively answer the question “Who the heck makes this stuff?”

Wikihow的无政府主义审美吸引了邪教，激发了一个受欢迎的红线板，一个轻微的病毒猜测游戏网站，目前休眠推特账户，经常更新的Twitter帐户，一个惊人的活跃的Tumblr概况，并且不仅仅是一些博客。尽管如此，超越了在Reddit，Quora和Twitter上的无助性社会喋喋不休，似乎没有人能够明确地回答“谁是哎呀做这件事的问题？”

Peculiarly, you won’t find an artist credit under a typical wikiHow image. wikiHow’s volunteer authors use a bot called “Wikivisual” to submit requests for images to illustrate their “how-to” posts, which are then handled by what wikiHow describes only as the “Wikivisual team.” Most wikiHow graphics have a Creative Commons license that is marked only as “uploaded by Wikivisual.” The bot boasts more than 3 million “contributions” to the site, which had published 212,368 articles and 4,417,238 “images and other files” as of 2019.

特殊的，你不会在典型的Wikihow形象下找到艺术家信用。 WikiHow的志愿作者使用一个名为“wikivisual”的机器人来提交图像的请求，以说明他们的“如何到”帖子，然后由WikiHow仅描述为“Wikivisual团队”。大多数Wikihow Graphics都有一个创造性的公共许可证，只标有“由Wikivisual上传”。该网站的机器人为该网站拥有300多万“贡献”，该网站发布了212,368篇文章和4,417,238件“图像和其他文件”，截至2019年。

A 2015 Gawker article mentions wikiHow’s “famously stilted illustrations,” but doesn’t say how they are created. A 2019 feature in Wired calls the art “part of what has made Wikihow famous online” and notes “the idea to use illustrators” happened in 2011, but does not mention the process by which those commissioned drawings are made. Just a few months ago, wikiHow founder Jack Herrick himself addressed the odd visuals in an interview with The Atlantic, saying, “we’re probably the largest illustrator on the web at this point.” Though the piece noted that “millions upon millions of illustrations” had been “made by thousands of contributors,” no details were offered about who those “thousands” were.

2015年Gawker文章提到Wikihow的“着名的洞穴插图”，但并没有说他们是如何创造的。有线电视的2019年功能呼叫艺术品“部分使Wikihow在线着名的东西”，并注意到2011年发生的“使用插画师”，但没有提到那些委托图纸的过程。就在几个月前，Wikihow创始人杰克Herrick自己在接受大西洋采访时解决了奇怪的视觉，“我们可能在这一点上是网络上最大的插画家。”虽然这件作品指出，“数百万以列数百万插图”已经“成千上万的贡献者，”没有提供关于谁是谁“成千上万”。

It turns out, OneZero has found, that wikiHow sources its graphics from freelance artists around the globe, primarily concentrated in the Philippines. Freelancers told OneZero they had been recruited through contract work sites like Upwork and Indeed, and in one case described pay as low as $.40 per image. In a 2017 style guide obtained by OneZero, wikiHow instructed these freelancers on how “to create the most instructive visuals for every step of every article.” WikiHow’s vice president of operations, Chris Hadley, confirmed Wikivisual’s project structure in a phone interview with OneZero.

事实证明，onezero已经找到了，Wikihow从全球自由艺术家来源来源，主要集中在菲律宾。自由职业者告诉olezero，他们通过合同工作网站招募了像春天，实际上，并且在一个案例中被描述为每张图片的低至$ .40。在由OneZero获得的2017年风格指南中，WikiHow指示这些自由职业者在如何为“每篇文章中的每一步中创造最具指令的视觉效果”。“ WikiHow副总裁Chris Hadley，确认了Wikivisual在与Onezero的电话采访中的项目结构。

This global freelance illustration operation is likely a key part of what keeps privately owned wikiHow profitable, especially after a 2011 shift to Google’s search algorithm rewarded originally illustrated guides and dinged “content farms.” While the company develops on open-source software and offers much of its content for public reuse via Creative Commons licenses, it is not a nonprofit like, say, Wikipedia. It calls itself a “hybrid organization” — a “for-profit company focused on achieving a social good.”

这种全球自由图示的操作可能是私人拥有的WikiHow盈利的关键部分，特别是在2011年转移到谷歌的搜索算法之后，奖励最初说明的指南和“内容农场”。虽然公司在开源软件上开发，但通过Creative Commons许可证提供了大部分公共重用的内容，而且是Wikipedia的非营利组织。它呼吁自身是一个“混合组织” - 一个“营利公司专注于实现社会良好”。

In a 2015 presentation at LDV Vision Summit, Thom Scher, who, according to LinkedIn, worked at wikiHow for four years and rose from a project manager to “Senior Director Marketing & Visual Content,” describes wikiHow’s visual content strategy.

根据LinkedIn的介绍2015年的LDV Vision Summit，据Linkedin的介绍，在WikiHow工作了四年并从项目经理上升到“高级总监营销＆amp;视觉内容，“描述了WikiHow的视觉内容策略。

Scher says in the presentation that, “about four years” previous to the May 2015 keynote, wikiHow’s graphics were mostly a motley assortment of user-created art and pictures sourced from Flickr. Around that time, he continues, wikiHow “started an initiative to start producing this visual content ourselves.”

Scher在演讲中说：“大约四年”前一2015年5月主题演讲，Wikihow的图形大多是来自Flickr的商品创造的艺术和图片。在那个时候，他仍在继续，Wikihow“开始一项倡议开始生产这种视觉内容。”

The shift in strategy came the same year that Google released “Google Panda,” a major change to its search ranking algorithm, which an O’Reilly Media report describes as being released in response “to the rise of low quality ‘content farms.’”

谷歌发布了“谷歌熊猫”的同年，策略的转变是对其搜索排名算法的重大变化，O'Reilly媒体报告描述为响应的响应被释放“到低质量”内容农场。“ “

One slide showing the “early days of the wikiVisual project” illustrates the evolution of a wikiHow post about how to cook macaroni and cheese. Scher comments that it once featured a “really bad illustration of blocks of yellow being put in water.”

一个幻灯片显示“瓦基意志项目的早期”的幻灯片说明了Wikihow Post关于如何烹饪通心粉和奶酪的演变。 Scher评论曾经是一个“真正糟糕的例证的黄色块被放入水中。”

“Here’s the thing though, it still led to an increase to traffic on the page,” Scher said, referring to the clip-art style graphic. “We were being rewarded for adding images, regardless of whether or not the images were crappy.”

“这是事情，但它仍然导致页面上的流量增加，”Scher说，参考剪贴画风格的图形。 “无论图像是否糟糕，我们都被添加了添加图像。”

Scher notes during his talk that, in certain cases, wikiHow opted to commission original photography or create videos as opposed to drawings, as these often tested better for metrics like engagement, “helpfulness,” and “authority.” Regardless, the company leaned into making illustrations en masse in 2011.

在他的谈话中，在他的谈话期间，在某些情况下，Wikihow选择委托原始摄影或创造视频而不是图纸，因为这些经常为度过参与，“乐于助人”和“权威”而更好地测试。无论如何，该公司倾向于2011年制作插图en Masse。

“It was so worth the cost, we did it at scale. Over four years we ended up doing 1.4 million custom visuals, and those are live on over 150,000 of our articles. That’s a lot, and it’s taken a lot of manpower to make it happen, and we’re constantly trying to move the needle even further on that visual content.”

“这是如此值得的成本，我们在规模上做了。超过四年，我们最终完成了140万自定义视觉效果，而且这些含量超过150,000件文章。这是很多，它拍摄了很多人来实现它，我们不断尝试进一步推动针头上的视觉内容。“

“This project kind of started as, frankly, something completely experimental in 2011,” Hadley told me. “We just wanted to see if we could find people to help us create custom images for our articles.”

“这一项目的开始就像2011年完全实验的那样，”哈德利告诉我。 “我们只是想看看我们是否能找到人们帮助我们为我们的文章创建自定义图像。”

Hadley said the secrecy around wikiHow art exists partly because the system worked so well. “It was new, but we felt like we had something that was really special, so we kind of asked people to keep it a bit of a mystery,” he said. “And, over the years, kind of maintaining the enigma has been part of the fun… that mystery has been a part of why this stuff has gained traction.”

Hadley表示，维基艺术周围的保密部分存在部分，因为系统工作得很好。 “这是新的，但我们觉得我们有一些真正特别的东西，所以我们有点要求人们保持一点神秘，”他说。 “而且，多年来，维护谜的一部分是有趣的一部分......那个神秘是这一部分为什么这东西已经获得了牵引力的一部分。”

Still, traces of the operation can be found online. Dozens of professional profiles on sites like LinkedIn and Behance mention freelance illustration for wikiHow. The vast majority list locations in the Philippines, with others based in countries including Argentina, Romania, Bulgaria, Macedonia, Turkey, India, Indonesia, and New Zealand. A search of the Philippines-specific job portal onlinejobs.ph with a “Pro” account turned up a dozen more profiles who listed past freelance illustration experience at the site.

尽管如此，可以在线找到操作的痕迹。在LinkedIn和Behance等网站上的数十个专业档案提到了Wikihow的自由插图。菲律宾的绝大多数列表位置，与阿根廷，罗马尼亚，保加利亚，马其顿，土耳其，印度，印度尼西亚和新西兰在内的国家。搜索菲律宾特定的作业门户网站在线jobs.pph与“pro”帐户出现了十几个人资料，他们在网站上列出了自由插图体验。

Two Philippines-based illustrators attached specific examples of art they created for wikiHow guides to their profiles or websites. Their work is currently featured on multiple live wikiHow articles. One of the artists noted that she’d “illustrated 3000+ articles” over a span of more than four years. Eight artists based in the Philippines, and one in the U.S., confirmed to OneZero that they’d created art on a freelance basis at some point for wikiHow.

两位基于菲律宾的Illustrator附上了他们为其档案或网站为WikiHow指南创建的艺术的具体示例。他们的工作目前在多个实时Wikihow文章中出现。其中一位艺术家指出，她在四年多的跨度中“说明了3000多篇文章”。总部位于菲律宾的八名艺术家，以及在美国，一位在美国，他们在Wikihow的某个点在自由职业的基础上创造了艺术。

LinkedIn profiles of wikiHow’s in-house team also indicate a worldwide illustration operation. Daisy Chung, wikiHow’s current creative director, lists one of her responsibilities as “provid[ing] creative direction to a large global team of illustrators.” Brooke Matthews, who, according to her LinkedIn, worked at wikiHow from 2012 to 2017, first as a “Project Manager of Visual Content” and eventually “Director of Visual Content,” notes that she “led the creation of over 2.5+ million custom digital media assets,” “traveled globally to meet with content producers and hold artist workshops,” as well as “mentored two in-house visual team project managers.”

Wikihow的内部团队的LinkedIn概况也表明了全球的例证操作。 Wikihow的目前的创意总监Daisy Chung列出了她的职责之一，作为“提供给一个大型全球插画队的创意方向”。据她的Linkedin的说法，Brooke Matthews从2012年到2017年开始，首先是“视觉内容的项目经理”，最终“视觉内容主任”指出，她“导致了超过2.5万于30万美元的创建数字媒体资产，“”全球旅行，以满足内容制作人员和艺术家研讨会，“以及”媒体上的两个内部视觉团队项目经理。“

“The artists on wikiHow really are people all over the globe,” said Hadley. “Probably the only place you won’t find some sort of wikiHow artist is in Antarctica.”

“Wikihow上的艺术家真的是全球的人，”哈德利说。 “可能是你唯一找不到某种Wikihow艺术家的地方在南极洲。”

Contracts with nondisclosure agreements could potentially be to blame for how few artists mention working for wikiHow publicly relative to the site’s humongous volume of illustration work. For instance, one self-employed illustrator listed their work experience as “2 years as illustrator in WikiHow” on one online professional profile, but, on another, only said they’d been “hired in NDA contract with certain company as illustrator” for two years and 10 months. When contacted by OneZero, this artist confirmed she’d signed an “NDA form (non disclosure agreement),” and asked which website listed the information so she could take it down.

与非歧视协议的合同可能会责备很少有艺术家在公开相对于该网站的有时间的插图工作中公开工作。例如，一个自雇的插画师在一个在线专业简介上列出了他们的工作经验，作为一个在线专业简介，但另一方面，他们只表示他们已被“聘请与Allustrator的某些公司的NDA合同聘用”两年和10个月。当Onezero与Onezero联系时，这位艺术家已确认她签署了“NDA表格（非披露协议）”，并询问哪个网站列出了该信息，以便她可以将其缩小。

The eight Philippines-based artists OneZero interviewed said they found out about the wikiHow opportunity in a variety of ways. One former freelancer told me she’d started drawing for wikiHow after a “guy randomly went to my university and start[ed] asking around who’s good at drawing.” Another said he was referred to a hiring manager by a friend. Several more said that they found work at wikiHow via third-party companies, either by finding their listings on sites like Upwork and Indeed, or by being contacted by representatives from these groups.

菲律宾的八人艺术家onezero采访说，他们以各种方式发现了WikiHow机会。一位前自由职业者告诉我，在一个“盖伊随意去我的大学并开始[ed]问谁在绘画时，她开始为wikihow绘制wikihow。”另一个人说他被一位朋友提交给招聘经理。有几个人越来越多，他们在通过在春天的网站上找到他们的列表，或者确实通过这些群体的代表联系，他们在WikiHow在WikiHow工作。

A company named “ArtLab Branding Solutions,” which appears to be based in Cagayan de Oro in the Philippines, is one of these middlemen. When reached for comment, the company’s CEO Freddie Ray Cornella confirmed that wikiHow was a client.

一家名为“Artlab品牌解决方案”的公司似乎位于菲律宾的Cagayan de Oro，是这些中间人之一。当达到评论时，该公司的首席执行官Freddie Ray Cornella证实了WikiHow是客户。

“We do bulk illustrations for them,” Cornella said in a Facebook message, adding that ArtLab is able to create hundreds of unique images per day, “even up to a thousand if the contract is long term.” He provided a document showing the kind of art the company creates, adding that, “Wiki illustrations are just very basic.”

“我们为他们做批量插图，”Cornella在Facebook留言中表示，添加Artlab可以每天创建数百个独特的图像，“如果合同长期以来，甚至高达一千。”他提供了一份文件，展示了公司创造的艺术品，补充说，“Wiki插图非常基本”。

When asked about the specifics of his business, Cornella declined to provide details, saying the information was “confidential.” OneZero found details of ArtLab’s operation online. An onlinejobs.ph profile of one Philippines-based IT specialist lists that, in 2017, he’d been “working with for WikiHow for 3 years with ArtLab Branding Solutions as the middle man.” An Upwork profile of an artist working near Cagayan de Oro notes that he spent 2015 “simplifying instructions doing article illustrations for WikiHow under Artlab Branding Solutions.” Another graphic designer’s onlinejobs.ph profile simply stated that he’d “done quite a bit of articles for Wikihow from our Company Cornella Design Agency” in 2014.

当被问及他的业务的具体细节时，Cornella拒绝提供细节，说信息是“机密”。 onezero发现了Artlab在线运营的详细信息。一个基于菲律宾的IT专家列表的一家在线杂志，2017年，他已经“与WikiHow与Artlab品牌解决方案为中间人合作。”在Cagayan de Oro ORO附近工作的艺术家的一个营造档案指出，他花了2015年“简化了在Artlab品牌解决方案下为WikiHow提供了文章插图的说明。”另一个图形设计师的在线杂志杂志简单地说明他在2014年在我们公司Cornella Design Agence的Wikihow完成了相当多的文章。

Cornella did offer an estimate of how many illustrations his company had provided to wikiHow in one word: “countless.”

Cornella确实提供了他公司在一个单词中向WikiHow提供的有多少插图的估计：“无数。”

Hadley said wikiHow “recruited initially through a variety of online platforms” but that most artists were now found via freelancers referring friends and classmates.

Hadley表示，Wikihow“最初通过各种在线平台招募”，但大多数艺术家现在都发现通过自由职业者参考朋友和同学。

Once sourced, artists are required to complete a simple application exercise. “There’s a sample article where you have to do sample illustrations as a test,” a Manila-based illustrator who recently started freelancing for the company told me. OneZero found an apparent example of this application from a different artist, a series of six sketches on a Behance account titled “Old Drawing Demo for HowTo / WikiHow Job Application.”

一旦采购，艺术家必须完成简单的应用程序练习。 “有一个示例文章，您必须做样品插图作为测试，”最近开始为公司自由职业的马尼拉的Illustrator告诉我。 Onezero发现了来自不同艺术家的这个应用程序的明显例子，这是一系列六个素描，标题为“Howto / Wikihow工作申请的旧绘图演示”。

The current freelancer described an online platform where artists can choose a list of available articles that need illustrations. When the art is complete, he said, you simply email the art in a .zip file, and revisions come back via email. Hadley confirmed that wikiHow has “some internal CMS tools that allow people to reserve articles and submit them and get feedback,” and said that Chung oversees a team of three full-time project managers who focus on video and still images.

当前的自由职业者描述了一个在线平台，艺术家可以选择需要插图的可用文章列表。他说，当艺术品完成后，您只需在.zip文件中通过电子邮件将艺术发送电子邮件，并通过电子邮件重新恢复修订。哈德利证实，WikiHow拥有“一些内部CMS工具，让人们预订文章并提交并获得反馈，并表示，钟监督专注于视频和静态图像的三个全职项目经​​理团队。

Though freelancers mostly told OneZero that their experience at wikiHow wasn’t remarkable in either a positive or negative way, several mentioned low pay.

虽然自由职业者大多被告知onezero，但他们在维基罗的经验并不是一种积极或消极的方式，几个提到的低薪。

The Manila-based illustrator currently under contract at wikiHow (who, notably, said the gig “becomes an enjoyable task to do” because he chooses to illustrate articles he’s already interested in) wouldn’t say how much he was compensated. He did say that artists were paid per image, there wasn’t a “fixed quota per month,” and illustrators could earn an incentive if they submitted eight articles’ worth of art.

目前在Wikihow的基于Manila的Illustrator（特别是谁，特别是说，演出成为一个令人愉快的任务，因为他选择说明他已经感兴趣的文章）不会说他有多赔偿。他确实说艺术家是每张图片支付的，如果他们提交了八篇文章的艺术品，则没有“固定配额每月”。

One former freelancer who said she did work for wikiHow “a long long time ago” recalled earning a flat rate of 20,000 Philippine pesos per month, about US$395. Another, who listed working for the company from 2014–2015, told me he got $1 per image. A third, who quit freelancing for wikiHow in 2016 after three months, told me he was paid just $.40 cents per image by a third-party broker.

一位前自由职业者谁说她为维基世界工作了“很久以前”召回了每月赚取20,000菲律宾比索的固定率，约为395美元。另一个人从2014 - 2015年开始为本公司工作，告诉我他每张图片获得1美元。在三个月后，将在2016年退出WikiHow的自由职业者，告诉我，第三方经纪人每张图片只需支付.40美分。

Hadley said that the range of pay per image “can be in the low single dollars for somebody brand new on projects.”

哈德利表示，每张图片的支付范围“可以在项目上以较低的单笔资金投入项目。”

“We’ve paid over $1,000 for some images,” he said. “It’s a really wide range, and that diversity is a function of where we’re hiring and what the project is, and the special skills involved in doing it.”

“我们已经支付了超过1,000美元的图像，”他说。 “这是一个非常广泛的范围，多样性是我们在招聘的地方以及项目是什么，以及所涉及的特殊技能。”

A U.S.-based artist who worked as an off-site freelance illustrator for wikiHow before “the current style of illustration” was adopted said she made enough money making graphics for the company that she could count on the gig for rent, “but after a couple of years, they let all of us go and are currently outsourcing to artists in the Philippines and India.”

在“当前的例证现状”之前，作为Wikihow的一个非自由职业者的艺术家，被采用，以“目前的插图”表示，她为本公司制作了足够的资金，以便她可以依靠Gig出租，“但是之后几年来，他们让我们所有人都去，目前正在外包给菲律宾和印度的艺术家。“

“Their art director emailed me and told me they were going with these new illustrators for cost and consistency,” she said via Instagram DM. “There was no way I could match like $5/hr.”

“他们的艺术主任通过Instagram DM表示，他们通过Instagram DM表示，向我发送给我并告诉我他们正在与这些新的插画家一起使用。” “我无法像5美元/小时一样匹配。”

Hadley said that a great deal of wikiHow’s current art has been the product of a core group of artists in the Philippines who’d freelanced for the company for “six to seven years.” He mentioned that wikiHow has purchased software for these illustrators to learn new skills, loaned people money to buy equipment, and highlighted how the work-from-home nature of the gig allows artists in the Philippines to avoid the “brutal commutes that exist in places like Manila to get to a call center.”

哈德利表示，大量的维基热门艺术一直是菲律宾核心艺术家核心群体的产品，为“六到七年来”。他提到Wikihow为这些插画家购买了软件，以学习新技能，借给人们的钱购买设备，并突出了Gig的家庭自主性如何让菲律宾的艺术家避免“存在的残酷通勤”像马尼拉到达呼叫中心。“

“Our overarching goal with what we’re doing here is that we want to make our content as good as possible,” he said. “And we can’t do that without good people and we want those people to feel that they’re treated well, and that they’re satisfied with their experience.” Hadley also mentioned that wikiHow sends “team members” to locales where wikiHow has a high concentration of artists to meet up and share information.

“我们在这里我们在做的总体目标是我们希望尽可能地使我们的内容成为尽可能好的，”他说。 “如果没有善良的人，我们就无法做到这一点，我们希望那些人觉得他们对待很好，他们对自己的经历感到满意。”哈德利还提到了Wikihow将“团队成员”发送到Locales，WikiHow拥有高集中艺术家，以满足和分享信息。

在2017年，WikiHow在网站获得了“粉刷”的热水之后制作了国家新闻，为“Whitewashing”一张巴拉克奥巴马，Beyoncé和Jay-Z出现在“如何成为国会议员”中出现的照片。 WikiHow的官方Twitter帐户发布了一系列推文，为错误道歉，解释了图像是“由插画家的团队制作的，他们在其中一个人的草图，其他人的颜色。”

该指南根据该网站的臭名昭着的“软调色板”作为其插图的第一个“关键要求”，然后要求艺术家确保“每个主题的适当角色”，“性别和族裔多样性”以及“现实身体比例，“其他指导方针。

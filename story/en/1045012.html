Now, the fact that states with higher total confirmed cases, are seeing fewer cases now (per capita, compared to their peak in the last 3 months) is not in itself surprising. That's the way pandemics normally work. The fire burns through most of the dry tinder, and then it goes out. What is surprising is that we are seeing this already with a number as low as ~12%, as in the Dakotas. How can a number that low lead to herd immunity?

现在，总确诊病例数较高的州现在所看到的病例数有所减少（人均数量与最近三个月的峰值相比），这一事实本身并不令人惊讶。这就是流行病正常工作的方式。大火烧过大部分干火，然后熄灭。令人惊讶的是，我们已经看到这一数字低至达科他州的约12％。这么低的数字怎么会导致羊群免疫呢？

One thing that should be mentioned here is that "herd immunity" has nothing to do with "culling the herd", and also does not mean that every single member of the "herd" is immune. What it means is that the percentage of the population that are immune, is too high for an epidemic to be self-sustaining. If another person gets sick, most of the people they expose to the virus are already resistant, and not enough people get sick to keep things going. It's kind of like how the ash left behind after a campfire is not all, completely 100% consumed; there is often still more fuel there. But enough of it was turned to ash, that the fire was not self-sustaining. Once a population has reached "herd immunity", new exposures to the virus sputter and die out quickly. The odd thing is, that this should happen with a number as low as 12%.

这里应该提到的一件事是“畜群免疫”。与“淘汰牛群”无关，也不意味着“牛群”的每个成员都是免疫的。这意味着免疫人群的百分比对于流行病来说太高了，无法自我维持。如果另一个人生病了，那么暴露于该病毒的大多数人已经具有抗药性，并且没有足够的人生病以保持疾病的发展。有点像篝火后灰烬还没有全部被完全消耗掉；那里通常还有更多的燃料。但是有足够的东西变成了灰烬，表明火并不能自我维持。一旦种群达到了群免疫力，新的病毒暴露就会迅速消失。奇怪的是，这应该以低至12％的数字发生。

One possibility, is that the previous estimates of needing 70% of the population to be resistant before you reach herd immunity, were simply incorrect. A lot depends on who exactly gets it, and if the people who are "super-spreaders" (people with service industry jobs who interact with a lot of other people in a day, for example) are also the first to get it (not an unreasonable guess), then the number could be a lot lower. If your 12% includes all the potential super-spreaders, maybe 12% is enough. That would be hard to manage, though, and my guess is that's not what's going on here.

一种可能是，先前的估计需要达到人群免疫力之前需要70％的人口抗药性，这完全是错误的。很大程度上取决于谁真正得到了它，以及是否是超级传播者。 （例如，从事服务行业工作的人在一天内与很多其他人互动）也是第一个获得该职位的人（这不是不合理的猜测），然后这个数字可能要低得多。如果您的12％包括所有潜在的超级传播者，那么12％也许就足够了。不过，这很难管理，我的猜测不是这里发生的事情。

The main issue is that not all people who get infected with covid-19, get this confirmed with a test. Some of them are asymptomatic, and never know they are infected. Some are not very sick, and don't get tested because there's no reason for them to (they're just going to stay home, regardless). Some people are afraid to know the truth. There may also be a few people who get counted more than once, if they get tested multiple times and test positive each time. So, we need a way to guess at the ratio of confirmed cases (including those who are double-counted), to actual cases (with no double-counting).

主要问题是，并非所有感染covid-19的人都能通过测试确认这一点。其中一些无症状，从不知道它们已被感染。有些人病得不是很重，也没有接受检查，因为没有理由让他们去（无论如何，他们只是要待在家里）。有些人害怕知道真相。如果他们经过多次测试并且每次测试都呈阳性，那么可能还会有一些人被计数一次以上。因此，我们需要一种方法来猜测已确认的案例（包括重复计算的案例）与实际案例（没有重复计算的案例）的比率。

Estimates of the actual mortality rate for covid-19 are contentious, but John Ioannidis published a paper which collected together many different seroprevalence studies, to estimate something like 0.23% of people who get covid-19 will die from it. He points out that it could be different for different regions, though, either because of different strains or differences in the local population or healthcare response, so let's take an estimate of twice that for the U.S., or 0.46%. At about the same time as the data was collected (April and May), the U.S. had .05% confirmed cases per week, and 0.0015% deaths per week.

关于covid-19的实际死亡率的估计是有争议的，但是John Ioannidis发表了一篇论文，该论文收集了许多不同的血清阳性率研究，以估计约有0.23％的covid-19患者会因此而丧生。他指出，由于不同的压力或当地人口或医疗保健对策的差异，不同地区的情况可能有所不同，因此，我们估算的费用是美国的两倍，即0.46％。大约在收集数据的同时（4月和5月），美国每周确认病例为0.05％，每周死亡0.0015％。

It would be nice to have some kind of double-check of this ratio. Fortunately, Slovakia provided one. "The country made international headlines as, over the last weekend of October, Slovak authorities tested almost all the country's adult population for coronavirus. A total of 3.6 million people out of an estimated 4 million target population were tested that weekend with a countrywide positivity rate of 1.06%." Given that the test was (necessarily, being cheap and capable of being deployed on such a massive scale) not as accurate as more conventional tests, the rate may have been as high as 2%. Given that conventional measures ("confirmed cases") was around 0.25%, that would give us a ratio of actual cases to confirmed cases somewhere in the range of 4-8.

对这种比率进行某种形式的仔细检查会很好。幸运的是，斯洛伐克提供了一个。该国成为国际头条新闻，因为在10月的最后一个周末，斯洛伐克当局测试了该国几乎所有成年人口的冠状病毒。到本周末估计的400万目标人口中，共有360万人接受了测试，全国阳性率为1.06％。考虑到该测试（有必要便宜且能够大规模部署）不如常规测试那么准确，因此该比率可能高达2％。鉴于常规措施（“确诊病例”）约为0.25％，这将使我们得出的实际病例与确诊病例的比率在4-8之间。

Now, neither of these numbers are super-precise, and in any case, we should not expect the ratio of confirmed cases to actual cases to be the same everywhere. Different governments and different healthcare systems would be expected to result in a different likelihood for a person who is actually infected with covid-19, to go get tested to confirm it. But the fact that both methods, based on different datasets in different countries, provide similar answers, is encouraging that we are somewhere in the ballpark. For every "confirmed case", we have something like 4-8 people who have actually been infected with covid-19.

现在，这些数字都不是非常精确的，无论如何，我们不应该期望已确认病例与实际病例的比率在任何地方都相同。预期不同的政府和不同的医疗保健系统将给实际感染了covid-19的人带来不同的可能性，使其接受测试以确认这一点。但是，这两种方法都基于不同国家/地区的不同数据集，它们提供了相似的答案，这一事实令人鼓舞，因为我们处于领先地位。对于每个“确诊病例”，实际上有4-8个人感染了covid-19。

If the number of confirmed cases per capita is 12.545% (North Dakota) or 11.862% (South Dakota), then the actual percentage of the population that have been infected is more like 70%. No wonder they have seen a precipitous decline in new cases in both states. But what about other states?

如果人均确诊病例数为12.545％（北达科他州）或11.862％（南达科他州），则实际感染的人口百分比更像是70％。难怪他们看到这两个州的新病例急剧下降。但是其他州呢？

Note that, not only are the Dakotas very low (recently), but also all of the states with something like 10% cases per capita, are declining (numbers lower than 1). In fact, it appears that once a state hits 8%, it will start to have declining numbers of new cases each week. If 8% confirmed cases means something more like 48% of population having been already infected with the virus, this is not too surprising.

请注意，不仅达科他州的人口数量非常少（最近），而且人均占有率约为10％的所有州都在下降（数字低于1）。实际上，一旦一个州的命中率达到8％，每周新案件的数量就会下降。如果8％的确诊病例意味着大约48％的人已经感染了这种病毒，这并不奇怪。

Well, it depends on what you think the vaccine is for. If you think it is to end the current pandemic, and we were all hiding out so that we would not get infected until the vaccine showed up, then yes, I'm afraid it means that the vaccine is probably showing up just too late to help with that (especially if you have to get both doses before you are immune). If you live in a state like Hawaii, Maine, or Vermont, with a total confirmed cases far below the levels of the Dakotas, then perhaps the vaccine may save some people even in this pandemic. Given that all of these states are experiencing record or near-record levels of new confirmed cases, though, I have my doubts.

好吧，这取决于您认为疫苗的用途。如果您认为这是结束当前的大流行的原因，并且我们都躲藏起来以便在出现疫苗之前不会被感染，那么是的，恐怕这也意味着疫苗也可能会出现来帮助您解决这一问题（特别是如果您必须在免疫之前同时服用两种剂量）。如果您居住在夏威夷，缅因州或佛蒙特州等州，确诊病例远低于达科他州的水平，那么即使在这种大流行中，这种疫苗也许也可以拯救一些人。但是，鉴于所有这些州都在经历新的确诊病例的记录或接近记录的水平，我对此表示怀疑。

It should be mentioned that currently North and South Dakota are two of the top three states in getting the vaccine rolled out. However, since they have only gotten about 5% of the population (and that's counting people who have only gotten one dose), I doubt that it is the main factor driving the recent decline. But it sure doesn't hurt.

应该提到的是，目前北达科他州和南达科他州是推出疫苗的前三个州中的两个。但是，由于他们仅获得了5％的人口（并且仅计算一剂的人数），我怀疑这是推动近期下降的主要因素。但这肯定不会造成伤害。

But vaccines developed in response to a new virus have never showed up in time to help much with the first pandemic, so that is not really a knock on the people who developed these. We have had measles, mumps, rubella, etc. for many decades, and we still have to get vaccinated, because even though there's no pandemic of those three currently (knock on wood), we know they are still out there, and will eventually return. Vaccines help us to avoid repeating this whole pandemic every 20 years or so (for every virus), once there are enough people born after the last wave to provide "fresh fuel" for the fire. Given how stupid and crazy this pandemic has made people, I think we can all be thankful for that.

但是针对新病毒而开发的疫苗从未及时出现，可以对第一次大流行起到很大的帮助，因此，这并不是对那些开发这些疫苗的人们的真正打击。几十年来，我们已经患了麻疹，腮腺炎，风疹等，我们仍然需要接种疫苗，因为即使目前这三者均没有大流行（敲打木头），我们知道它们仍然在那里，并将最终返回。一旦在上一轮浪潮后出生的人足够提供“新鲜燃料”，疫苗将帮助我们避免每20年左右（对于每种病毒）重复整个大流行。为火。鉴于这种流行病使人们多么愚蠢和疯狂，我想我们都应该为此感到感激。

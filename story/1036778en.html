Advice for Newsletter-ers
给时事通讯的建议

2020-11-27 05:47:32

It used to feel cozy; a little neighborhood. But now, thanks almost entirely to the efforts of the company called Substack, newsletter-ing has become the enticing next step for a whole host of writers.

过去感觉很舒适；有点邻居。但是现在，几乎完全归功于Substack公司的努力，时事通讯已成为整个编写者的诱人下一步。

I was just reading Patrick Tanguay’s thoughts about how he might add some chunkier projects to his long-running Sentiers newsletter— which is excellent—and, in his post, he mentioned an approach that I want to emphasize and sharpen.

我只是在阅读Patrick Tanguay关于他如何在他长期运行的Sentiers通讯中添加一些更笨拙的项目的想法-这是很棒的-在他的帖子中，他提到了我想强调和强调的一种方法。

A personal email newsletter ought to be divided into seasons, just like a TV show.

就像电视节目一样，个人电子邮件通讯应按季节划分。

By “personal” I don’t mean “diaristic” but rather “produced almost entirely by one writer, in order to pursue some interest and/or establish a small business.”

“个人”不是指“对话”，而是“几乎全部由一位作家创作，目的是为了引起兴趣和/或开办小生意”。

Here’s what you get from the nomenclature, the metaphor, of the “season”:

这是您从“季节”的命名法，隐喻中得到的：

When do you break between seasons? Anytime! When life gets weird. When you’re feeling burnt out. When you sense a new obsession taking shape. When you want to bring in a guest writer. When it’s raining outside. Really: anytime.

您何时在两季之间休息？任何时间！当生活变得怪异。当您感到疲倦时。当您感觉到一种新的困扰正在形成时。当您想请客座作家时。外面下雨的时候。真的：任何时候。

Dan Hon was the first newsletter-er I saw to use this nomenclature. I am not 100% sure he is its originator, but if he is, it wouldn’t be surprising, given his crackling recombinant energy. Dan is all the way to season 8 now! Some of his seasons have been long, others short, and the gaps between them have been variable, too: many months, a few days.

丹·汉（Dan Hon）是我看到的第一个使用此术语的新闻通讯。我不确定100％是他的创始人，但如果他是他的创始人，考虑到他的重组能量，这也就不足为奇了。丹现在已经到了第8季！他的某些季节很长，其他一些季节很短，而且它们之间的差距也很不固定：很多月，几天。

A break, no matter how short, and a refresh, no matter how minor, is a shot of energy for writer and reader alike; totally a little magic trick.

无论是多么短暂的休息，无论多么微小的刷新，对于作家和读者而言都是一滴活力。完全是个魔术。

But that’s all secondary to this, the metaphor’s most precious gift:

但这仅次于隐喻最宝贵的礼物：

Now, particularly if you’re beginning a paid newsletter with dreams that it will one day provide most of your income, “stopping” is the last thing on your mind. “I’ll send this email newsletter to a happy, engaged audience who will pay me $200,000 every year, forever!” You won’t—not forever. The fact that it feels so strange, even unpleasant, to contemplate an ending before you’ve even begun—before you’ve enjoyed the tiniest sip of success—is precisely why it’s important to do so.

现在，尤其是如果您开始以梦想着有一天能提供大部分收入的付费新闻简报时，“停下来”是您的最后决定。 “我会将这封电子邮件通讯发送给一个满意的，热情的听众，他们将永远每年向我支付200,000美元！”您不会-不会永远。在您还没开始任何尝试之前就想着结局，这真是令人感到奇怪，甚至令人不快，这正是为什么这样做如此重要的原因。

The first is my year-long newsletter from 2019, which was titled Year of the Meteor. It was launched with an expiration date, and I swear to you, that ticking clock was like a power source: a lump of uranium decaying, spraying off energy. I mailed an edition every Sunday, always with the week number incremented in the subject line. Everyone knew the denominator; it felt like a progress bar.

首先是我从2019年开始的为期一年的时事通讯，标题为``流星年''。它是在到期日启动的，我向你保证，滴答时钟就像一种动力：一团铀在腐烂，散发着能量。我每个星期日都邮寄一个版本，在主题行中总是增加星期数。每个人都知道分母。感觉就像是进度条。

The expiration date wasn’t even binding! I could easily have said, at the conclusion of that year, “What a terrific project! Let’s do it again. It’s time for a new newsletter; welcome to the Year of the—” whatever. I don’t think anyone would have been unhappy about that. And, if they were: what a convenient, graceful time to step off the carousel. “Well, I liked the first season, but after that, the plot got a bit silly…”

到期日期甚至没有约束力！在那年结束时，我很容易说：“这真是一个了不起的项目！让我们再来一次。现在是时候发行新的通讯了；欢迎来到“国际年”。我认为不会有人对此感到不满。而且，如果是这样的话，这是下轮转盘的便捷，优美的时间。 “好吧，我喜欢第一个赛季，但是之后，情节变得有些愚蠢了……”

In fact, I did start a fresh newsletter later in 2020, chronicling the development of a simple video game. You could call this my cautionary tale, but, in truth, I’m still not sure how to parse it. The pandemic broke the back of the game project, but/and I haven’t given up on it; I remain totally devoted to the idea. However, I’ve been too busy with other work to add a line of code in three months, so, that newsletter sits now in suspended animation.

实际上，我确实在2020年晚些时候开始发布新的新闻通讯，记载了一个简单的视频游戏的发展。您可以将其称为我的告诫故事，但实际上，我仍然不确定如何解析它。这场大流行打破了游戏项目的序幕，但/而且我还没有放弃。我仍然完全致力于这个想法。但是，由于我忙于其他工作，无法在三个月内添加一行代码，因此该新闻简报现在处于暂停动画状态。

The crispness of my Year of the Meteor was the exception. In practice, basically every email newsletter runs like my dev diary:

我的“流星年”的清脆感是个例外。实际上，基本上每个电子邮件通讯都像我的开发日记一样运行：

I will tell you—anyone has, who’s been through this cycle—it feels bad to have a channel out there, inert but never closed. If it takes a tiny bit of forethought, some cautionary design, to hedge against that bummer, it’s worth it.

我会告诉您-曾经经历过此循环的任何人-在那儿有一个呆板，惰性但从未关闭过的渠道真是太糟糕了。如果需要一点先见之明，一些警惕性的设计来对付那令人讨厌的事情，那是值得的。

Seasons aren’t right for every newsletter. My regular dispatch to readers—which I’ve been sending consistently for ten years??—doesn’t use that nomenclature, because its editions are so spaced-out and irregular that each is effectively a season of one. Maybe, instead of a TV show, it’s a languorous sequence of arthouse movies…

并非每个季刊都适合季节。我定期发给读者-我已经连续发送十年了-??不使用该术语，因为它的版本间隔很大且不规则，实际上每个版本都是一个季节。也许不是电视节目，而是一系列令人毛骨悚然的艺术电影……

Anyway, that’s my advice. Closing out, I’ll address a few potential objections to sending a newsletter with “seasons”:

无论如何，这是我的建议。结束语，我将解决一些有关发送带有“季节”的新闻通讯的潜在异议：

Do you think it’s “cute” when a dark, gleaming HBO show produced by ultra-professionals arrives in modular, graspable seasons? Come on.

当超专业人士制作的黑暗，闪闪发光的HBO节目在模块化，易于掌握的季节到来时，您认为这是“可爱”吗？来吧。

If the popular and profitable video game Fortnite can swipe the term “season” for its evolving releases, you can, too. I think this one is well on its way toward a totally generalized, cross-media definition.

如果流行且盈利的视频游戏Fortnite可以针对不断发展的版本滑动“季节”一词，那么您也可以。我认为这正朝着完全通用的跨媒体定义迈进。

Maybe it should! But, okay: Frasier didn’t evolve and change that much, and it still had seasons. Turns out, they provide a convenient, comfortable scaffolding for any kind of ongoing creative work. If nothing else, a season implies a season premiere and a season finale; don’t those sound like fun newsletters to write?

也许应该！但是，好的：Frasier并没有发展和改变那么多，它仍然有季节。事实证明，它们为任何正在进行的创作工作提供了方便，舒适的支架。如果没什么别的，一个季节意味着一个季节的首映和一个结局。听起来像是有趣的新闻通讯吗？

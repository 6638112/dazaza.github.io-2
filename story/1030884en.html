研究人员提供了迄今为止地球上最后一次磁逆的最完整记录

2020-10-24 08:08:51

Earth's magnetic fields typically switch every 200 to 300 millennia. Yet, the planet has remained steady for more than twice that now, with the last magnetic reversal occurring about 773,000 years ago. A team of researchers based in Japan now has a better understanding of the geophysical events leading up to the switch and how Earth has responded since then. The researchers published their results on September 1in the Progress in Earth and Planetary Science journal.

地球的磁场通常每200到300年转换一次。然而，这颗行星现在保持稳定的时间是这个数字的两倍多，上一次磁场逆转发生在大约77.3万年前。日本的一组研究人员现在对导致这一转变的地球物理事件有了更好的理解，以及自那以后地球是如何反应的。研究人员于9月1日在“地球与行星科学进展”杂志上发表了他们的研究结果。

Named for the geophysicists who discovered past geomagnetic reversals, the Matuyama-Brunhes geomagnetic reversal is one of the most studied paleomagnetic events, according to paper author Yuki Haneda, project researcher at the National Institute of Polar Research and a postdoctoral research fellow at the National Institute of Advanced Industrial Science and Technology in Japan. Such reversals swap the magnetic poles of the planet, which could have implications for plant and animal life. Such extrapolation to life, including humans and our ancestors, depends on the duration of the magnetic flip.

据论文作者羽田由纪(Yuki Haneda)表示，松山-布鲁内斯地磁逆转是研究最多的古地磁事件之一。羽田由纪是日本国家极地研究所(National Institute Of Polar Research)项目研究员、日本国家先进工业科学技术研究所(National Institute Of Advanced Industrial Science And Technology)博士后研究员，以发现过去地磁反转的地球物理学家的名字命名。这样的倒转交换了地球的磁极，这可能会对动植物的生命产生影响。这种对生命的推断，包括人类和我们的祖先，取决于磁翻转的持续时间。

Previous studies by other researchers examined samples taken from , which offer a good snapshot of moments in geophysical history, according to Haneda.

羽田说，其他研究人员之前的研究检查了取自的样本，这些样本提供了地球物理史上时刻的良好快照。

"However, lava sequences cannot provide continuous paleomagnetic records due to the nature of sporadic eruptions," Haneda said.

羽田说，然而，由于零星喷发的性质，熔岩序列不能提供连续的古地磁记录。

A series of studies based out of Italy suggested that the magnetic reversal took place within a single century—a blink in the scale of hundreds of thousands of millennia.

意大利的一系列研究表明，磁反转发生在一个世纪内--在数十万年的尺度上只是一眨眼的功夫。

"In this study, we collected new samples and conducted paleo- and rock-magnetic analyses of samples from the Chiba composite section, a continuous and expanded marine succession in Central Japan, to reconstruct the full sequence of the Matuyama-Brunhes geomagnetic reversal," Haneda said. The Chiba composite section is widely considered to contain the most detailed marine sedimentary record of the Matuyama-Brunhes geomagnetic reversal, according to Haneda, and serves as the international standard for lower boundary of the Middle Pleistocene Subseries and Chibanian Stage—when Homo sapiens emerged as a species.

羽田说，在这项研究中，我们收集了新的样品，并对千叶复合剖面的样品进行了古和岩石磁性分析，千叶复合剖面是日本中部一个连续和扩大的海相序列，以重建松山-布鲁内斯地磁反转的全序列，羽田说。羽田表示，千叶复合剖面被广泛认为包含松山-布鲁内斯地磁反转最详细的海洋沉积记录，并作为中更新世亚系和千班纪阶段下界的国际标准-当时智人作为一个物种出现。

The researchers found that the geomagnetic field became unstable at least 10,000 years prior to the magnetic direction change 773,000 years ago, and the full reversal process took at least 20,000 years.

研究人员发现，在77.3万年前磁方向改变之前，地球磁场至少在1万年前就变得不稳定了，而完全逆转的过程至少需要2万年。

"Our data is one of the most detailed paleomagnetic record during the Matuyama-Brunhes geomagnetic reversal, offering deep insight into the mechanism of the geomagnetic reversal," Haneda said.

羽田说，我们的数据是松山-布鲁内斯地磁反转期间最详细的古地磁记录之一，为深入了解地磁反转的机制提供了深刻的见解。

Next, the researchers will investigate how the geomagnetic reversal influenced plankton and plants, using marine microfossil and pollen data found in their samples. More information: Yuki Haneda et al, A full sequence of the Matuyama–Brunhes geomagnetic reversal in the Chiba composite section, Central Japan, Progress in Earth and Planetary Science (2020). DOI: 10.1186/s40645-020-00354-y

接下来，研究人员将利用在样本中发现的海洋微化石和花粉数据，研究地磁逆转是如何影响浮游生物和植物的。更多信息：羽田由纪夫等人，日本中部千叶综合地段松山-布鲁内斯地磁逆转的全序列，地球和行星科学进展(2020年)。电话：10.1186/s40645-020-00354-y。

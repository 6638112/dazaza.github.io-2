Ars Technica’s best games of 2020
Ars Technica 2020年最佳游戏

2020-12-23 04:21:40

When the world at large looks back at 2020, how much will video games figure into our memories? Frankly, humanity has a pretty massive bullet list of crazy, important, and scary moments that will likely outweigh the importance of, say, knocking out your dailies in an MMO.

当全世界回顾2020年时，电子游戏将为我们留下多少印象？坦白说，人类拥有大量的疯狂，重要和可怕时刻的清单，这些清单可能会超过在MMO中​​淘汰日常工作的重要性。

But at Ars, we know that you've still been keenly interested in gaming articles this year—whether because you had questions about sold-out consoles and graphics cards, because you happened to be home near your gaming machines more often, or because your social life began revolving less around the local pub and more around a Discord channel. In an increasingly stressed out and homebound year, video games provided equal parts refuge and escape. Thankfully, development studios quickly figured out the work-from-home thing well enough to finish and launch some incredible video games. (Well, some more than others.) Hence, we've again polled the Ars gaming braintrust to rank the games that provided the most comfort in a year where comfort was in seriously short supply.

但是在Ars，我们知道您今年对游戏文章仍然非常感兴趣-是因为您对售罄的游戏机和图形卡有疑问，还是因为您碰巧经常在游戏机附近居住，或者因为您的社交生活开始较少围绕本地酒吧旋转，而更多围绕Discord渠道旋转。在日益紧张和无家可归的一年中，视频游戏提供了平等的避难所和逃生场所。值得庆幸的是，开发工作室很快就找到了在家工作的东西，足以完成并推出一些令人难以置信的视频游戏。 （嗯，比其他游戏更多。）因此，我们再次对Ars游戏人才信任度进行了调查，以对在舒适度严重不足的一年中提供最舒适体验的游戏进行排名。

(As the creators of this unscientific but heavily researched poll, we look forward to your thoughts and responses in the comments section—so long as you're kind about it. Remember, 2020's been rough. Be nice.)

（作为这项不科学但经过大量研究的民意调查的创建者，我们期待您在评论部分中的想法和回应，只要您对此有所了解。请记住，2020年是艰难的一年。 ）

Games that come packaged with a new console on launch day carry a heavy burden. They have to represent the promise and potential of the new hardware to an eager audience without slipping into the dry and dull obscurity of a mere tech demo. Astro’s Playroom walks this tightrope perfectly, highlighting by example the best of what the PlayStation 5 can do while serving as a light yet surprisingly deep platform game in its own right. As we noted in our first impressions back in October, Astro’s Playroom serves as the perfect showcase for the PS5’s new DualSense controller. The game mixes subtle, positional vibrations, perfectly synced audio feedback from the controller’s speaker, and well-tuned resistance in triggers to extend a game's sensory experience past the screen in a new and unique way. The game’s use of ray-traced reflections and high-definition rendering, while more subtle, also highlight the PS5's cutting-edge CPU, GPU, and SSD.

发行日附带新游戏机的游戏负担沉重。他们必须向渴望的观众展现新硬件的前景和潜力，而又不会陷入纯粹的技术演示的枯燥乏味的晦涩感。 Astro的Playroom完美地走过了这条绳索，以身作则，彰显了PlayStation 5可以做的最好的事情，同时它本身就是一款轻巧而又令人惊讶的深度平台游戏。正如我们在十月份的第一印象中所指出的那样，Astro的Playroom是PS5新型DualSense控制器的完美展示。该游戏混合了微妙的位置振动，来自控制器扬声器的完美同步的音频反馈以及触发器中经过良好调整的阻力，从而以新颖独特的方式将游戏的感官体验扩展到了屏幕之外。该游戏使用光线追踪的反射和高清渲染，虽然更加微妙，但也突出了PS5的尖端CPU，GPU和SSD。

More than that, though, Astro’s Playroom serves as an interactive museum of 25 years of PlayStation history littered with collectible trinkets and Easter eggs that evoke memories of the best hardware and software under the PlayStation umbrella. Combine that with the tongue-in-cheek exploration of the PlayStation 5’s innards, and you get a corporate love letter that rivals Super Smash Bros. for its sheer fanboy glee.

不过，不仅如此，Astro的Playroom还是一个具有25年历史的PlayStation互动博物馆，里面散布着可收藏的小饰品和复活节彩蛋，让人联想起PlayStation最好的硬件和软件。将其与对PlayStation 5内幕的深入探究相结合，您会收到一份与Super Smash Bros.相当的公司情书，其纯粹的狂热狂喜。

This quick pack-in game only takes a few hours to beat, though hidden secrets and speedrun optimizations can easily add more hours of fun. Still, this endearing little adventure through the innards of the PlayStation 5 will probably stick with Sony fans for as long as they enjoy their new console purchase. -Kyle Orland

尽管隐藏的秘密和Speedrun优化可以轻松地增加更多的娱乐时间，但这款快速的打包游戏仅需几个小时即可击败。不过，只要他们喜欢购买新的游戏机，在PlayStation 5的内部进行的这种有趣的小冒险活动就可能会吸引他们。 -凯尔·奥兰德

If you read Ars Technica’s review of this game, you might be surprised to see it on this year-end list. And yes, back in June I took TLOU2 to task for a plot that devolves into borderline nonsensical character motivations, muddying a valiant attempt to tell a cohesive story about a cycle of trauma driven by endless revenge. Compared to the tight, driven, emotional plot of the first game, Part II felt like a step back into a more self-indulgent, less affecting storytelling form. But those structural flaws can’t take away from the intense, memorable moments to be found within the sweep of Naughty Dog’s latest grand epic: plucking notes on a guitar in a quiet, abandoned building; exploring the ruins of a long-decaying museum as a joyful birthday present; gawking at the marvel of a sports stadium converted to military housing for thousands; stealing a moment of romance while hiding from a raging storm; riding a horse through the flaming wreckage of a doomed village.

如果您阅读了Ars Technica对这款游戏的评论，您可能会惊讶地发现它在今年年底的榜单上。是的，早在6月，我就带着TLOU2参与了一个情节，该情节演变成边缘性的无意义的人物动机，使试图讲述一个连绵不断的报复故事的勇敢尝试变得混乱起来。与第一款游戏的紧凑，主动，情感情节相比，第二部分感觉像是退回到更具自我放纵，影响较小的叙事形式。但是，这些结构上的缺陷并不能消除顽皮狗最新的宏伟史诗中发现的激烈而令人难忘的时刻：在安静，废弃的建筑中，在吉他上拔下音符；探索一个衰败的博物馆的废墟作为生日礼物；凝视着一个体育馆的奇迹，它变成了成千上万的军人住房；躲在汹涌的暴风雨中，躲藏着浪漫的时刻；在一个注定要发生的村庄的燃烧残骸中骑马。

These moments and more stick out, months later, shining amid the tangled and messy plot as some of gaming’s most well-constructed setpieces. And while those pieces don’t come together into a satisfying narrative whole like its predecessor did, their construction still merits recognition and observation by anyone interested in the modern era of video game storytelling. -Kyle Orland

数月后，这些时刻以及更多时刻凸现出来，在游戏中结构最复杂的一些作品中，在纠结而凌乱的情节中熠熠生辉。尽管这些作品并没有像其前辈那样构成令人满意的叙事整体，但它们的构造仍然值得任何对现代电子游戏叙事感兴趣的人认可和观察。 -凯尔·奥兰德

The Longing gets a lot from a little. This little-hyped game from German developer Studio Seufz is part point-and-click adventure, part idle game, part Animal Crossing-esque home furnishing simulator, and part existential Tamagotchi.

渴望从一点中得到了很多。来自德国开发商Studio Seufz的这款小宣传游戏，既是点击游戏，又是闲置游戏，又是类似动物穿越的家居装饰模拟器，又是存在的Tamagotchi。

You play as a frail, soot-covered character simply known as A Shade. Deep below the surface, in a massive network of underground caves, you’re borne from the hand of a giant earthy king. He tells you he must slumber to regain his strength and to wake him in 400 days, at which point he promises to end all fear and longing in the world.

您扮演的是一个脆弱的，被煤烟覆盖的角色，简称为A Shade。在地表深处，庞大的地下洞穴网络中，您是由一位土生土长的国王所担负的。他告诉你，他必须沉睡以恢复力量并在400天内唤醒他，此时他承诺将结束世界上所有的恐惧和渴望。

Then, a persistent timer at the top of the screen starts ticking down—in real time. 400 actual days. The rest is on you. Will you follow orders, bound by a sense of duty? Will you seek an escape, unsure of what the consequences may be? Will you risk exploring for secrets? How exactly will the king fulfill his promise? Whatever you do, the timer is always ticking toward an ending. That might sound familiar, especially as we all wait for the last few ticks before a vaccine ends Our Pandemic Year™.

然后，屏幕顶部的固定计时器开始实时滴答。实际400天。剩下的就在你身上。您会遵守责任心吗？您是否会逃脱，不确定后果如何？您会冒险探索秘密吗？国王将如何确切地履行诺言？不管您做什么，计时器始终会朝着结尾滴答作响。这听起来可能很熟悉，尤其是当我们都在疫苗结束我们的大流行年™之前等待最后的几个滴答声时。

You can speed up the in-game countdown, mercifully, by reading books, sketching artwork, and furnishing your in-cave home. The timer continues even when the game is closed, and multiple endings don’t require anywhere close to the full 400 days. (My playthrough took about 25 hours.)

您可以通过阅读书籍，素描作品和布置洞穴居所，来有条不紊地加快游戏中的倒数计时。即使游戏关闭，计时器也会继续计时，而且不需要结余就可以满足整整400天的需要。 （我的播放大约花了25个小时。）

Still, this is a game about patience and isolation. You are alone in the caves—there are no enemies to confront, no dense gameplay systems to nestle into. You can’t starve to death or anything like that. You just sit, explore, or kill time. You can bookmark specific spots in the caves for the Shade to remember, but he walks painfully slow, and returning home means walking all the way back.

尽管如此，这还是一个关于耐心和孤立的游戏。您独自一人在山洞中-没有敌人要面对，没有密集的游戏系统可以嵌套。你不能饿死或类似的事情。您只是坐着，探索或消磨时间。您可以为洞穴中的特定地点添加书签，以便Shade记住，但他的步伐缓慢而痛苦，返回家园意味着一路走回去。

This is a titanic subversion of popular video game design, and it’s certainly not for everyone. But in the process of playing, The Longing forces you to live in the experience of waiting, wondering, being alone with your thoughts, the unanswerable, and what it means to be content. All of this captures the state of being in 2020 tremendously well, but more than that, The Longing creates a fundamentally human conflict. One way or another, we all have lords we must serve. Right? -Jeff Dunn

这是对流行视频游戏设计的巨大破坏，当然并不适合所有人。但是在演奏的过程中，《渴望》迫使您生活在等待，好奇，独自思考，无法回答以及满足的含义中。所有这些都很好地反映了2020年的状况，但更重要的是，《渴望》从根本上造成了人类冲突。一种或另一种方式，我们所有人都有我们必须服务的主人。对？ -杰夫·邓恩

Plastic Is Far More Toxic than We Thought
塑料比我们想象的要多得多

2021-06-26 03:26:41

You may already be trying to cut down on your plastic usage because the material doesn’t biodegrade and it can pollute the planet for hundreds of years. But there’s another reason you might want to stay away.

您可能已经试图削减塑料使用量，因为材料不会生物降解，它可以污染数百年的地球。但是还有一个可能留下的原因。

A new study finds that plastics release many more toxic chemicals throughout their life cycle than previously thought, posing significant risks to both people and the planet. Until now, only a small number of these chemicals have been properly studied. But the new paper published in the journal Environmental Science & Technology provides the most comprehensive database of chemicals in plastic, and offers a terrifying look into just how harmful the material may be.

一项新的研究发现，塑料在整个生命周期中释放了更多毒性化学物质，而不是以前认为，对人类和地球构成重大风险。到目前为止，只有少数这些化学品进行了妥善研究。但新文发表在环境科学＆amp杂志上;技术为塑料提供最全面的化学品数据库，并提供了一种可怕的材料，只有如何有害。

The team of researchers, led by Stefanie Hellweg, a professor of ecological systems design at the Swiss university ETH Zurich, identified a whopping 10,500 chemicals in the plastics they studied. The team spent two and a half years studying a wide variety of plastic products, using scientific, regulatory, and industry databases to identify all the chemicals contained within them. They then cross-referenced these chemicals with scientific databases that identified whether the chemicals were hazardous, benign, or not adequately studied.

由瑞士大学Eth苏黎世的生态系统设计教授LED，由Stefanie Hellweg领导的研究人员在他们学习的塑料中确定了10,500个化学物质。该团队花了两年半，研究了各种各样的塑料制品，使用科学，监管和行业数据库来确定其中包含的所有化学品。然后，他们用科学数据库交叉引用这些化学品，该数据库确定了化学品是否有害，良性，或不充分研究。

Of these chemicals, about a quarter—2,480—were “substances of potential concern,” which means there is some evidence to indicate they are harmful. In some cases, the chemicals are known to be toxic to aquatic life, cancer-causing, or damaging to specific organs. In others, the chemicals can accumulate in humans and animals, causing problems such as memory loss over time.

在这些化学品中，大约2,480季度 - 是“潜在关注的物质”，这意味着有一些证据表明它们有害。在一些情况下，已知化学物质对水生寿命，癌症引起或损害特定器官有毒。在其他方面，化学物质可以积聚在人类和动物中，导致随着时间的推移等内存损耗等问题。

Helene Wiesinger, lead author of the study and a doctoral student at ETH Zurich, says that at least 1,000 of the chemicals identified as substances of concern can be harmful even in small doses. “If something is a carcinogen, it is agreed upon in the scientific community that there is no safe level of exposure,” she tells Fast Company. “Even at very low levels, these can lead to cancer, and obviously [it] gets worse with high doses. With endocrine disruptors, small doses can be problematic. With these chemicals, using any at all is a problem.”

该研究的主要作者和Eth苏黎世的博士生威伯林格表示，即使小剂量也可能有害的至少1,000名被认为的化学品。 “如果某些东西是一种致癌物，它就在科学界同意的是，没有安全的曝光程度，”她告诉快速公司。 “即使在非常低的水平下，这些也可以导致癌症，显然[它]高剂量变得更糟。对于内分泌破坏剂，小剂量可能是有问题的。用这些化学品，使用任何一个问题是一个问题。“

The researchers could not properly categorize a further 39%—4,100—of the chemicals, because there is no clear evidence about whether or not they are hazardous. There are many ingredients in plastic that scientists agree are not harmful, but this study reveals that there are many chemicals that have just not been adequately studied. “Our research was designed to nudge the scientific community to devote more resources to studying these chemicals,” Wiesinger says. “So far, scientists have focused on a limited number of dangerous chemicals in plastic, but there’s a lot we don’t know.”

研究人员无法妥善分类39％-4,100-化学品，因为没有明确的证据是是否危险。科学家同意的塑料中有许多成分不害处，但本研究表明，有许多刚刚研究的化学物质。 “我们的研究旨在轻推科学界致力于研究这些化学品的更多资源，”威伯格说。 “到目前为止，科学家们专注于塑料中有限数量的危险化学品，但我们不知道很多。”

Plastics are all around us. More than 350 million metric tonnes are produced worldwide every year, adding to the billions of metric tonnes that are clogging up our landfills and oceans. The harmful chemicals the researchers identified were found in many plastic objects we use everyday, including toys, medical devices, masks, food packaging, and textiles.

塑料是我们周围的。每年全球生产超过3.5亿公吨，增加了数十亿美元的公吨，这是堵塞我们的垃圾填埋场和海洋。有害化学品发现的研究人员在我们每天使用的许多塑料物体中发现，包括玩具，医疗设备，面具，食品包装和纺织品。

They also eventually invade our ecosystems, where they can harm both people and animals. Some plastics end up in the ocean, where sea animals mistake them for food, and eventually become part of the human food chain. Chemicals can also be released in recycling processes, which means they can make recycled plastic unsafe.

他们也最终侵犯了我们的生态系统，在那里他们可以伤害人和动物。一些塑料最终在海洋中，海洋动物将它们误认为食物，最终成为人类食物链的一部分。化学品也可以在回收过程中释放，这意味着它们可以使再生塑料不安全。

So why are plastics so jam-packed with chemicals? Plastic is a cheap substance that can be transformed into almost any material you can imagine, from silk-like synthetic fabrics to sturdy furniture. To create these different materials, factories use additives to give the plastic its desired properties, such as antioxidants to prevent degradation, flame retardants, and plasticizers to reduce brittleness. During production, catalysts, solvents, and other chemicals are used to help process the material.

那么为什么塑料如此卡纸用化学品包装？塑料是一种廉价物质，可以将几乎任何材料转化为您可以想象的材料，从丝绸的合成织物到坚固的家具。为了创造这些不同的材料，工厂使用添加剂来给予塑料，使其所需的性质，例如抗氧化剂，以防止降解，阻燃剂和增塑剂减少脆性。在生产中，催化剂，溶剂和其他化学物质用于帮助加工材料。

Researchers have known that some of these chemicals are dangerous. A study by the U.S. Consumer Product Safety Commission about phthalates (which make materials more flexible) found that they cause asthma, breast cancer, type 2 diabetes, disruption to reproductive health, and a slew of other health problems. The Environmental Protection Agency also determined that brominated flame retardants found on furniture, in house dust, and indoor air have been linked to hormone disruption and development problems in children. But this latest study identified other chemicals in plastics that researchers aren’t paying enough attention to. A propanol commonly used as a solvent in colorants is known to cause damage to the reproductive system. A benzene used in controlling polymerization is known to cause skin sensitivity and harm aquatic life.

研究人员已知一些这些化学品是危险的。美国消费者产品安全委员会关于邻苯二甲酸酯（使材料更加灵活）的研究发现，它们引起哮喘，乳腺癌，2型糖尿病，繁殖健康的破坏，以及其他健康问题的扭转。环境保护署还确定家具，房屋粉尘和室内空气中发现的溴化阻燃剂与儿童的激素中断和发育问题有关。但这种最新研究确定了塑料中的其他化学品，研究人员没有足够的重视。已知一种常用作为着色剂中溶剂的丙醇可以造成对生殖系统的损害。已知一种用于控制聚合的苯会导致皮肤敏感性和水生寿命。

Even regulators aren’t keeping track of these chemicals. A full 53% of chemicals the researchers identified as potentially concerning are not regulated in the United States, the European Union, or Japan. In fact, 901 chemicals known to be hazardous are actually approved for use in contact with food in these regions.

甚至监管机构也没有跟踪这些化学品。完整的53％的化学品的研究人员在美国，欧洲联盟或日本没有监管。事实上，已知有901种具有危险的化学物质实际上被批准用于与这些地区的食物接触。

A big part of the problem, the researchers say, is that there isn’t a central database that offers information about all of these different chemicals and their potential risks. Indeed, the researchers spent two and a half years combing through 190 publicly available data sources to figure out what constituted a substance of potential concern. There is also a lack of transparency in the plastics industry about the chemicals that go into their products. In the study, the researchers call on regulators to compel plastics manufacturers to disclose all the ingredients in their products.

研究人员所说的问题是，没有一个中央数据库，提供有关这些不同化学品的所有信息及其潜在风险的信息。实际上，研究人员通过190个公开的数据来源花了两年半，以弄清楚是什么构成了潜在关注的物质。塑料行业缺乏缺乏透明度，这些化学品进入其产品。在研究中，研究人员呼吁监管机构强迫塑料制造商，以披露其产品中的所有成分。

What’s a consumer to do? We can opt for organic materials whenever possible, but it is virtually impossible to eradicate plastic from our lives completely. The modern world is saturated with plastic, from the coatings on our wood furniture to the garments that fill our closets to the paints that cover our walls. To Wiesinger, the onus is on companies to better track and eliminate these chemicals.

什么是消费者要做的？我们可以尽可能选择有机材料，但几乎不可能完全从我们的生活中消除塑料。现代世界与塑料饱和，从我们的木材家具上的涂料到填充我们壁橱的衣服上的衣服上的衣服。对于威伯格，ONU是在公司上更好地跟踪和消除这些化学品。

“It’s an overwhelming burden to ask consumers to be responsible for keeping the environment they live in safe by choosing the right kinds of plastics,” Wiesinger says. “This is a problem that needs to be tackled by the industry and regulators, rather than by the consumers themselves. But what every citizen can do is demand more transparency from companies that produce plastic.”

“通过选择正确的塑料，要求消费者负责将消费者负责保持环境的压倒性负担，”威伯格说。 “这是一个需要由行业和监管机构解决的问题，而不是由消费者自己解决。但每个公民可以做的是，从生产塑料的公司需要更多的透明度。“

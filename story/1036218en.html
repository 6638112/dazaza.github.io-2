Dosing error turns into lucky punch for AstraZeneca and Oxford
加药错误成为阿斯利康和牛津大学的幸运拳

2020-11-24 08:42:38

FRANKFURT/LONDON (Reuters) - A stroke of good fortune from a dosing error will pave the way for AstraZeneca and Oxford University to submit their COVID-19 vaccine for regulatory clearance.

伦敦/伦敦（路透社）-剂量错误带来的好运将为阿斯利康和牛津大学提交其COVID-19疫苗进行监管铺平道路。

The British drugmaker said on Monday that the vaccine could be around 90% effective, when administered as a half dose followed by a full dose a month later, citing data from late-stage trials in Britain and Brazil.

这家英国制药商周一表示，该疫苗引用了英国和巴西后期试验的数据，以半剂量给药，一个月后再以全剂量给药，效果可能约为90％。

“The reason we had the half-dose is serendipity,” Mene Pangalos, the head of AstraZeneca’s non-oncology research and development, told Reuters.

阿斯利康非肿瘤学研发部负责人梅内·潘加洛斯（Mene Pangalos）告诉路透社：“我们服用半剂量药物的原因是偶然性。”

A larger group who had received two full doses - as planned - resulted in an efficacy read-out of 62%, leading to an overall efficacy of 70% across both dosing patterns.

较大的一组按计划接受了两次全剂量，结果显示药效为62％，两种给药方式的总体药效为70％。

Around the time when Astra was initiating its partnership with Oxford at the end of April, university researchers were administering doses to trial participants in Britain.

大约在4月底，阿斯特拉（Astra）与牛津大学建立伙伴关系的时候，大学研究人员正在向英国的试验参与者给药。

They soon noticed expected side effects such as fatigue, headaches or arm aches were milder than expected, he said.

他们很快注意到预期的副作用，例如疲劳，头痛或手臂酸痛比预期的要轻。

“So we went back and checked ... and we found out that they had underpredicted the dose of the vaccine by half,” said Pangalos.

Pangalos说：“因此，我们回去检查...，我们发现他们低估了疫苗剂量的一半。”

He added the company decided to continue with the half dose and administer the full dose booster shot at the scheduled time.

他补充说，该公司决定继续使用半剂量，并在预定时间进行全剂量加强注射。

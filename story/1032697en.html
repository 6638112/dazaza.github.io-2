科技初创公司称H-1B签证的新薪酬规定负担不起

2020-11-02 08:32:03

Tech New restrictions intended to protect U.S. workers curb access to pool of highly skilled foreign labor

旨在保护美国工人的技术新限制限制了高技能外国劳动力的进入。

New rules from the Trump administration restricting skilled foreign workers are unnerving U.S. startup hubs, as founders and investors say the limitations will hamstring their ability to recruit top-tier talent to grow their businesses.

特朗普政府限制外国熟练工人的新规定让美国的创业中心感到不安，因为创始人和投资者表示，这些限制将阻碍他们招聘顶级人才来发展业务的能力。

The changes to the H-1B visa program announced in October will make qualifying for the work visas much tougher and compel employers to pay foreign workers drastically higher wages.

10月份宣布的H-1B签证计划的变化将使获得工作签证的资格变得更加困难，并迫使雇主向外国工人支付大幅提高的工资。

Those rules hit especially hard for technology startups, whose founders and rank-and-file are often immigrants and which usually pay employees a lower salary but compensate with stock options. Many salaries under the new rules start at $208,000, even for inexperienced workers.

这些规定对科技初创公司的打击尤其严重，这些公司的创始人和普通员工往往是移民，通常向员工支付较低的工资，但用股票期权进行补偿。根据新规定，许多人的起薪为20.8万美元，即使是没有经验的工人也是如此。

“It’s already expensive, it was already a high bar, and we are making it prohibitive,” Kate Mitchell, co-founder of venture-capital firm Scale, said of the H1-B program.

风险投资公司Scale的联合创始人凯特·米切尔(Kate Mitchell)在谈到H1-B项目时说，“它已经很贵了，门槛已经很高了，我们正在让它变得令人望而却步。”

The administration has said the rules are designed to ensure U.S. workers get priority for jobs.

政府曾表示，这些规定是为了确保美国工人获得优先就业机会。

“For too long, foreign worker programs have been abused at the expense of American workers,” a spokesperson for the Labor Department said. The new rules “will help put an end to these harms.”

劳工部的一位发言人说：“长期以来，外国工人计划一直被滥用，损害了美国工人的利益。”新规定“将有助于结束这些伤害”。

The H1-B rules are the latest in a string of immigration restrictions dating back to the travel ban against citizens from predominantly Muslim countries that Mr. Trump issued a week after his inauguration. The cumulative effect has left some tech startups weary of doing business here, founders say.

H1-B规定是特朗普在就职一周后发布的一系列移民限制措施中的最新一项，这些限制可以追溯到针对来自以穆斯林为主的国家的公民的旅行禁令。创始人们表示，累积效应让一些科技初创公司厌倦了在这里做生意。

Some founders say they are shifting hiring and growth plans away from the U.S., establishing engineering hubs in Eastern Europe and sending new recruits from American universities who would require a U.S. visa to work instead at satellite offices in Canada.

一些创始人说，他们正在将招聘和增长计划从美国转移出去，在东欧建立工程中心，并从美国大学派遣新员工，这些人需要美国签证才能在加拿大的卫星办公室工作。

Nearly a third of all venture-backed startups are founded by immigrants, according to a 2016 report from the National Bureau of Economic Research. More than half of startups valued at $1 billion or more have at least one immigrant founder, according to a 2018 paper from the National Foundation for American Policy, a nonpartisan think tank.

根据国家经济研究局2016年的一份报告，近三分之一的风险支持初创企业是由移民创立的。无党派智库美国政策国家基金会(National Foundation For American Policy)2018年的一篇论文显示，超过一半的估值在10亿美元或更高的初创企业至少有一名移民创始人。

Several of the highest-valued venture-backed companies today, including payments company Stripe and stock-trading app Robinhood, have at least one immigrant founder and collectively thousands of employees.

如今估值最高的几家风险投资公司，包括支付公司Strike和股票交易应用Robinhood，至少有一名移民创始人，总共有数千名员工。

Much of the high-tech industry has long wanted overhauls to the H-1B program so companies have an easier path to obtain visas in a competitive hiring environment. The administration says low-cost foreign workers are taking jobs from Americans.

许多高科技行业长期以来一直希望对H-1B计划进行彻底改革，以便公司在竞争激烈的招聘环境中更容易获得签证。政府表示，低成本的外国工人正在抢走美国人的工作。

The salary jump isn’t the change U.S. tech companies had in mind. Under the changes, an entry-level computer programmer in San Francisco would be required to receive a salary of $111,946, compared with the previous wage requirement of $78,125, according to Labor Department data.

加薪并不是美国科技公司心目中的变化。根据劳工部的数据，根据这些变化，旧金山的入门级计算机程序员将被要求获得111,946美元的工资，而之前的工资要求为78,125美元。

For more than 18,000 jobs, including a number of common tech jobs such as a San Francisco-based software developer, the Labor Department has determined a salary floor of $208,000 annually, regardless of skill. Many startups aren’t able to bear these costs. Equity, bonuses and other types of compensation don’t count toward the salary requirements, according to government rules.

对于超过1.8万个工作岗位，包括一些常见的技术工作，如旧金山的软件开发人员，劳工部已确定年薪下限为20.8万美元，无论技能如何。许多初创企业无法承担这些成本。根据政府规定，股权、奖金和其他类型的薪酬不计入工资要求。

“I just honestly don’t see how we can hire other people who need H-1B sponsorship,” said Marcie Black, co-founder and chief executive of Massachusetts-based medical technology startup Advanced Silicon Group, which builds sensors that could be used to improve the accuracy of rapid tests to detect diseases—crucial technology in a pandemic. That poses a problem: Roughly nine out of every 10 applicants for open positions at her startup aren’t U.S. citizens.

总部位于马萨诸塞州的医疗技术初创公司Advanced Silicon Group的联合创始人兼首席执行官玛西·布莱克(Marcie Black)说，“老实说，我就是不明白我们怎么才能雇佣其他需要H-1B赞助的人。”该公司生产的传感器可以用来提高快速检测疾病的准确性，这是大流行中的关键技术。这带来了一个问题：在她的创业公司申请空缺职位的申请者中，大约每10人中就有9人不是美国公民。

An analysis from the National Foundation for American Policy found the wages mandated under the new rule are significantly higher than current market wages.

美国国家政策基金会(National Foundation For American Policy)的一项分析发现，新规则规定的工资明显高于目前的市场工资。

A second set of rules set to take effect in December will narrow the types of degrees that could qualify an applicant and shorten the length of visas for certain contract workers. Ken Cuccinelli, the No. 2 official at the Department of Homeland Security, said he expects about one-third of H-1B visa applications would be rejected under the new restrictions.

将于12月生效的第二套规定将缩小可能符合申请人资格的学位类型，并缩短某些合同工的签证期限。国土安全部(Department Of Homeland Security)二号官员肯·库奇内利(Ken Cuccinelli)表示，他预计在新的限制下，大约三分之一的H-1B签证申请将被拒绝。

Advocates of H-1B visa overhauls point to outsourcing companies that pay foreign workers below-market wages, a practice that has slowed under the Trump administration. The U.S. rejected 21% of new applicants in 2019 compared with 6% in 2015, according to an analysis of government data. The Trump administration also reversed an Obama-era initiative to create a temporary pathway for foreign-born entrepreneurs to build startups in the U.S.

H-1B签证改革的倡导者指出，外包公司向外国工人支付的工资低于市场工资，这种做法在特朗普政府执政期间已经放缓。根据对政府数据的分析，2019年美国拒绝了21%的新申请者，而2015年这一比例为6%。特朗普政府还推翻了奥巴马时代的一项倡议，该倡议为外国出生的企业家在美国创业创造了一条临时途径。

The U.S. chapter of the Institute of Electrical and Electronics Engineers, a trade group for technical professionals, supports the higher salary mandates.

电气和电子工程师协会(Institute Of Electrical And Electronics Engineers)是一个技术专业人士的行业组织，其美国分会支持更高的工资要求。

“If they are that talented, they should be paid more than average because that’s what talented people get paid,” said Russell Harrison, director of government relations for IEEE-USA. But he also wants to see changes that would make green cards more accessible to skilled foreign workers and entrepreneurs. Because recipients of H-1B visas need to be sponsored by an employer, startup founders have a more complicated path qualifying for them—green cards would make them less dependent on those employers.

IEEE-USA的政府关系主管罗素·哈里森(Russell Harrison)表示：“如果他们那么有才华，他们的薪酬应该高于平均水平，因为这就是有才华的人的薪酬。”但他也希望看到一些变化，使熟练的外国工人和企业家更容易获得绿卡。由于H-1B签证的获得者需要雇主提供担保，初创企业创始人有一条更复杂的道路来获得他们的资格-绿卡将使他们对这些雇主的依赖程度降低。

Technology companies, trade groups, universities and health-care organizations have filed lawsuits against the new rules, arguing, among other things, that the administration circumvented the established rule-making process and didn’t adequately weigh the effect of the changes on employers and existing visa holders. A preliminary ruling on at least one of the lawsuits could come as soon as mid-November. The future of the H-1B visa program also will be shaped by the outcome of presidential election Tuesday.

科技公司、贸易团体、大学和医疗保健组织已经对新规定提起诉讼，其中一个理由是，政府绕过了既定的规则制定程序，没有充分权衡这些变化对雇主和现有签证持有者的影响。对至少一起诉讼的初步裁决最快可能在11月中旬做出。H-1B签证计划的未来也将取决于周二总统选举的结果。

Jagadishwar Sirigiri received approval for three H-1B applicants to join his Massachusetts-based company just weeks before the new wage rules took effect. Bridge12 Technologies Inc., which Mr. Sirigiri founded 11 years ago and partially bootstrapped with his own money, builds advanced high-frequency microwave systems used by the Defense and Energy Departments and the National Institutes of Health, among other customers. Mr. Sirigiri said he is constantly struggling to find qualified workers.

贾加迪什瓦尔·西里吉里(Jagadishwar Sirigiri)在新工资规定生效前几周获得批准，允许三名H-1B申请者加入他位于马萨诸塞州的公司。Bridge12 Technologies Inc.是西里吉里在11年前创立的，部分是靠自己的钱创业的，该公司制造先进的高频微波系统，供国防部、能源部和美国国立卫生研究院(National Institutes Of Health)等客户使用。西里吉里说，他一直在努力寻找合格的工人。

He said he likely can’t pay the higher salaries and will focus on hiring less-qualified U.S. residents and training them on the job, and hope they stick around.

他说，他可能付不起更高的工资，将专注于雇佣资历较差的美国居民，并对他们进行工作培训，并希望他们留下来。

“As an entrepreneur I really don’t understand how it’s possible to make money at those insane wages,” he said. “I have to run a business and balance the books.”

“作为一名企业家，我真的不明白怎么可能以如此疯狂的工资赚钱，”他说。“我必须经营一家企业，实现收支平衡。”

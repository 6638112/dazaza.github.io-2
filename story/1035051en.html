报告发现，不断上升的二氧化碳水平增加了澳大利亚的极端天气事件

2020-11-14 09:15:30

Climate agencies say fossil fuel burning is driving the increase of dangerous bushfires and days of extreme heatwaves

气候机构说，化石燃料燃烧正在推动危险的丛林大火和持续数天的极端热浪的增加。

Australia’s climate has entered a new era of sustained extreme weather events, such as dangerous bushfires and heatwaves, courtesy of rising average temperatures, a new report by the nation’s two government climate science agencies has found.

澳大利亚两家政府气候科学机构的一份新报告发现，由于平均气温上升，澳大利亚气候进入了一个持续极端天气事件的新时代，如危险的丛林大火和热浪。

Rising levels of carbon dioxide in the atmosphere, mostly from fossil fuel burning, has driven more dangerous bushfires, rising sea levels and a rapid rise in the days where temperatures reach extreme levels, the Bureau of Meteorology and the CSIRO said in Australia’s latest State of the Climate Report.

澳大利亚气象局和CSIRO在澳大利亚最新的气候状况报告中表示，大气中二氧化碳水平的上升(主要来自化石燃料燃烧)导致了更危险的丛林火灾，海平面上升，在气温达到极端水平的日子里迅速上升。

“What we are seeing now is beyond the realm of what was possible previously,” said Dr Jaci Brown, director of CSIRO’s Climate Science Centre.

CSIRO气候科学中心主任Jaci Brown博士说：“我们现在看到的情况超出了以前可能的范围。”

While 2019 was Australia’s hottest on record that helped deliver unprecedented bushfires, those temperatures would be seen as average once global heating reaches 1.5C, the report said.

报告称，虽然2019年是澳大利亚有记录以来最热的一年，导致了史无前例的丛林大火，但一旦全球气温达到1.5摄氏度，这一温度将被视为平均气温。

Among the key findings, the report said Australia’s climate had warmed by 1.44C since 1910 with bushfire seasons getting longer and more dangerous. Australia’s oceans had warmed by 1C and were acidifying.

报告称，在关键发现中，澳大利亚的气候自1910年以来已经变暖了1.44摄氏度，森林大火季节变得更长、更危险。澳大利亚的海洋升温了1摄氏度，并且正在酸化。

In a briefing to reporters on Tuesday, Dr Karl Braganza, manager of climate environmental prediction service at the bureau, said conditions in Australia were in line with projections over recent decades.

在周二对记者的简报中，该局气候环境预测服务经理卡尔·布拉甘扎(Karl Braganza)博士表示，澳大利亚的情况符合近几十年的预测。

But he said: “What we are seeing now is a more tangible shift in the extremes and we are starting to feel how that shift in the average is impacting on extreme events.

但他表示：“我们现在看到的是极端情况发生了更为切实的变化，我们开始感受到平均水平的变化对极端事件的影响。”

“So we don’t necessarily feel that 1.44C increase in average temperature, but we do feel those heatwaves and we feel that fire weather.”

“所以我们不一定感觉到平均气温上升了1.44摄氏度，但我们确实感觉到了热浪，我们感觉到了火灾天气。”

Between 1960 and 2018, the report said there were 24 days when the nation’s average maximum temperature reached 39C or higher. But 2019 alone had delivered 33 such days.

报告称，从1960年到2018年，全国平均最高气温达到39摄氏度或更高的时间有24天。但仅2019年就提供了33天这样的时间。

Australia’s long-term greenhouse gas monitoring station at Cape Grim, on the northwest tip of Tasmania, showed levels of CO2 were accumulating on the atmosphere at an accelerating rate.

澳大利亚位于塔斯马尼亚西北端格里姆角的长期温室气体监测站显示，大气中的二氧化碳水平正在加速累积。

From 1980 to 1989, the amount of CO2 rose by 14 parts per million, but in the decade ending 2019 the amount rose by 23 parts per million.

从1980年到1989年，二氧化碳排放量上升了百万分之14，但在截至2019年的十年里，二氧化碳排放量上升了百万分之23。

Brown said while the global economic slowdown from the Covid-19 pandemic had seen a slowdown in emissions, this was just a blip.

布朗说，虽然新冠肺炎疫情导致的全球经济放缓导致排放放缓，但这只是昙花一现。

“Another way to think about this is if you have been eating junk food for 10 years and then you go on a diet for one day and jump on the scales the next morning and expect to see some change or drop a dress size. It’s not that simple. This is about a long-term change,” she said.

“另一种思考方式是，你已经吃垃圾食品10年了，然后你节食了一天，第二天早上跳到磅秤上，希望看到一些变化或减掉衣服的尺码。”事情没那么简单。这关系到一个长期的变化。“她说。

“The big challenge for our children and our grandchildren will be how to flatten this curve.”

“对我们的子孙来说，最大的挑战将是如何使这条曲线变平。”

Emissions from fossil fuels – coal, oil and gas – were the main contributor to the growth in atmospheric CO2, the report said, and were responsible for about 85% of emissions from 2009 to 2018.

报告称，化石燃料(煤炭、石油和天然气)的排放是大气二氧化碳增长的主要贡献者，从2009年到2018年，这些燃料的排放量约占总排放量的85%。

Rising ocean temperatures, marine heatwaves and acidifying waters were also projected to continue, posing a significant threat to Australia’s coral reefs, Brown said.

布朗说，海洋温度上升、海洋热浪和海水酸化预计也将继续，对澳大利亚的珊瑚礁构成重大威胁。

Since 1970, rainfall in the southwest of Australia had dropped by about 16% in the cooler months between April and October, but most of northern Australia had seen a rise in rainfall.

自1970年以来，澳大利亚西南部的降雨量在4月至10月间较凉爽的月份下降了约16%，但澳大利亚北部大部分地区的降雨量却有所增加。

Streamflow gauges also showed less water flowing through rivers across the south of the country since 1975.

径流流量计还显示，自1975年以来，流经该国南部河流的水量有所减少。

“Australian agriculture has already faced significant challenges and disruption from climate change, seen through record droughts, heatwaves and rising temperatures,” Dr Michael Battaglia, CSIRO agriculture and food research director, said.

CSIRO农业和食品研究主任迈克尔·巴塔格利亚博士说：“澳大利亚农业已经面临着气候变化带来的重大挑战和破坏，从创纪录的干旱、热浪和气温上升中可见一斑。”

“The effects of these are widespread, impacting food production, supply chains, regional communities and consumer prices. Our farmers are resilient and capable, but climate change exposes them to significant risks.”

“这些影响是广泛的，影响到粮食生产、供应链、区域社区和消费者价格。我们的农民有韧性、有能力，但气候变化使他们面临重大风险。“。

Projections for Australia’s temperatures in the next two decades showed every year was warmer than it would have been “in a world without human influence”.

对未来20年澳大利亚气温的预测显示，每年的气温都比“在一个没有人类影响的世界里”的温度要高。

While the current decade was warmer than any other over the last century, the report said it was likely to be the coolest decade of the century ahead.

报告称，虽然当前的十年比上个世纪的任何一个十年都要暖和，但很可能是未来一个世纪最凉爽的十年。

Australia would experience further warming, more extremely hot days and fewer cool days. Extreme heavy rainfall events would also increase.

澳大利亚将经历进一步的变暖，更多的酷热天气和更少的凉爽天气。极端暴雨事件也会增加。

There would probably be fewer tropical cyclones in the future, the report said, but those that did form would be more likely to be more intense.

报告称，未来可能会有较少的热带气旋，但那些确实形成的热带气旋更有可能更加强烈。

Brown told Guardian Australia while the findings in the report were “confronting” it showed that “now is the time to act.”

布朗告诉澳大利亚卫报，虽然报告中的发现是“面对”的，但它表明“现在是采取行动的时候了。”

The report’s release comes as the prime minister, Scott Morrison, faces pressure to follow major trading partners and commit Australia to a target of net zero emissions by 2050.

这份报告发布之际，澳大利亚总理斯科特·莫里森(Scott Morrison)正面临压力，要求他效仿主要贸易伙伴，承诺到2050年实现净零排放的目标。

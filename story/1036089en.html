Analysis: Facebook and Instagram ads appear to be reaching a saturation point after rising by a ~30% quarterly average since Q3 2015
分析：自2015年第三季度以来，Facebook和Instagram广告在达到季度均值约30％的增长后似乎已达到饱和点

2020-11-23 23:00:42

Facebook FB -0.44% platforms are starting to feel like homes with too much furniture. It is getting difficult to move around.

Facebook FB -0.44％平台开始感觉像是家具过多的房屋。移动变得越来越困难。

In addition to ads on its legacy Blue app, those on Instagram, which the company acquired for $1 billion in 2012, accounted for roughly a quarter of Facebook’s overall revenue in the 12 months ended Sept. 30, according to consensus estimates compiled by Visible Alpha. Ads on both platforms now seem to be reaching a saturation point at just the wrong time.

根据Visible Alpha汇编的共识估计，除了旧版Blue应用中的广告外，该公司在2012年以10亿美元收购的Instagram上的广告约占Facebook截至9月30日的12个月总收入的四分之一。 。现在，两个平台上的广告似乎都在错误的时间到达饱和点。

Amid an increasingly strict regulatory environment, it seems unlikely Facebook can simply buy the next Instagram to populate. That could present growing pains for a mature company that has consistently increased its ad impressions to boost overall revenue over the last five years.

在日益严格的监管环境中，Facebook似乎不可能简单地购买下一个Instagram。对于一家在过去五年中不断增加广告展示次数以增加整体收入的成熟公司而言，这可能会带来越来越大的痛苦。

Open Facebook or Instagram before a friend has posted since your last visit and your newsfeed might well appear to be nothing but a lengthy, vertical banner of stuff for sale. That experience is by design: According to an analysis of transcripts of multiple investor calls available on FactSet, Facebook has increased the number of ads served on its platforms by a quarterly average of nearly 30% year on year since the third quarter of 2015.

自从您上次访问以来，在朋友发布之前打开Facebook或Instagram，您的新闻提要很可能不过是长长的垂直待售商品横幅。这种经历是有意为之的：根据FactSet上多个投资者致电记录的笔录分析，自2015年第三季度以来，Facebook的平台上投放的广告数量同比平均每季度增加近30％。

Interestingly, while ad pricing has risen over that period on the same basis, prices began to decline in the second half of 2018, with the greatest declines this year. In the second quarter, for example, Facebook said ad prices declined 21% versus the prior year, while ads served increased 40%.

有趣的是，尽管广告价格在同一时期已经上涨，但价格在2018年下半年开始下降，今年降幅最大。例如，在第二季度，Facebook表示广告价格与去年同期相比下降了21％，而投放的广告则增长了40％。

There are multiple explanations. Initially, much of the pricing decline came from a mix shift toward Stories, where ads are fleeting, and geographies Facebook said monetize at lower rates. But lately competition from social media peers appears to have become more formidable. Third-quarter sales from Snap Inc.’s Snapchat and Pinterest grew 52% and 58%, respectively, versus last year, for example. Facebook’s sales grew less than 22% over the same period, albeit off a much larger base.

有多种解释。最初，价格下降的大部分原因是广告向转瞬即逝的Stories转变，Facebook地理区域表示以较低的利率获利。但是，最近来自社交媒体同行的竞争似乎变得更加艰巨。例如，Snap Inc.的Snapchat和Pinterest的第三季度销售额分别比去年增长了52％和58％。同期，Facebook的销售额增长不到22％，尽管基数更大。

Advertisers’ boycotts of Facebook this summer couldn’t have helped either, pushing customers’ business toward its competitors in some cases. This year’s more significant pricing declines also seem to be a reflection of the toll the coronavirus pandemic has taken on Facebook’s customers, the majority of which are small businesses.

广告商今年夏天对Facebook的抵制也无济于事，在某些情况下将客户的业务推向了竞争对手。今年更大幅度的价格下降似乎也反映了冠状病毒大流行给Facebook客户造成的损失，其中大多数是小型企业。

The combination of lower prices and more ads served has meant Facebook has at least kept investors happy amid the pandemic. But, especially since many of its users have fled its legacy app for the younger, “cooler” Instagram, oversaturation of that platform could risk flight of its users from Facebook platforms entirely.

较低的价格和投放的更多广告的结合，意味着Facebook在大流行中至少使投资者感到高兴。但是，尤其是由于许多用户已经将其旧版应用程序转移到了更年轻，更“酷”的Instagram上，该平台的过饱和可能会冒其用户完全逃离Facebook平台的风险。

While Facebook is hard at work to change this, most people are still using Facebook and Instagram to read news, engage with their friends or snoop their exes, not to shop. Indeed, on its third-quarter conference call, Facebook Vice President-Finance Susan Li noted that, post pandemic, lower user engagement should equate to more limited ad growth, making revenue growth in 2021 more reliant on ad pricing.

尽管Facebook努力改变这种状况，但大多数人仍在使用Facebook和Instagram阅读新闻，与朋友互动或窥探他们的exe，而不是购物。确实，在第三季度的电话会议上，Facebook副总裁苏珊·李（Susan Li）指出，在大流行之后，较低的用户参与度应等同于更有限的广告增长，从而使2021年的收入增长更加依赖广告定价。

With regulators threatening to break it up, Facebook isn’t likely to be able to acquire anything meaningful for now. And adding ads to WhatsApp, a messaging service for which Facebook paid a whopping $19 billion for 2014, would likely be a non-starter for many of its more than two billion users there for its encrypted privacy. Facebook has said it would instead try to monetize the app through hosting services paid for by merchants.

由于监管机构扬言要分拆，Facebook目前不太可能获得任何有意义的东西。并向WhatsApp添加广告，WhatsApp是一种消息传递服务，Facebook在2014年为其支付了高达190亿美元的费用，对于那里超过20亿用户中的许多用户而言，由于其加密的隐私权，这可能根本就不会启动。 Facebook已经表示，它将尝试通过商家支付的托管服务来通过该应用获利。

Still, Facebook’s attempts to generate revenue outside its ad business are unproven: Nearly 99% of overall revenue in the third quarter came from advertising.

尽管如此，Facebook在广告业务之外创收的尝试仍未得到证实：第三季度总收入的近99％来自广告。

Inside Facebook’s multifaceted network, there are no doubt many internal levers the company can try to pull. Spending big money to make big money in the ad business may no longer be on the table.

在Facebook的多元化网络内部，毫无疑问，该公司可以尝试利用许多内部手段。花大钱在广告业务中赚大钱可能不再是摆在桌面上。

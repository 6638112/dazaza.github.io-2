Artists in the Stone Age Were High at Work, New Study Suggests
新的研究表明，石器时代的艺术家们很高，建议

2021-04-11 23:54:33

Image via Shutterstock It seems that creating art while being in a state of altered consciousness isn’t new. Recent research by Tel Aviv University archaeologists gives reason to believe that ancient cave painters used to be stoned too, and they’d perhaps even use work as an excuse to get high. In a new paper entitled Time and Mind: The Journal of Archaeology, Consciousness and Culture, picked up by Salon, the researchers detailed that caveman artists who lived during the Upper Paleolithic, 50,000 to 12,000 years ago, would have required some form of light to work in the dark enclosures. They would thus rely on light torches, which could lower oxygen levels in caves and possibly leave ancestors on a state of hypoxia (oxygen deprivation). Without sufficient oxygen, the painters’ consciousness would have been altered, potentially pushing them into a state of euphoria. They might have even had out-of-body experiences and hallucinations. However, the Israeli archaeologists posited that painters then would not have grasped the science behind tripping, and probably would have put it down to cave magic or metaphysics. Nevertheless, the researchers surmised that ancestors would have enjoyed the experience of being stoned and return to caves just to reel in their mysterious effects. “[We] contend that entering these deep, dark environments was a conscious choice, motivated by an understanding of the transformative nature of an underground, oxygen-depleted space,” they noted. Stone Age artists were apparently so taken by such experiences, they could have painted just to get high. “It was not the decoration that rendered the caves significant,” believed the authors. “Rather, the significance of the chosen caves was the reason for their decoration.” [via Salon, cover image via Shutterstock]

通过Shutterstock图像似乎在改变意识状态时创造艺术并不是新的。 Tel Aviv大学考古学家最近的研究使其有理由相信古老的洞穴画家曾经被扔石头，而且他们甚至可能使用工作作为获得高处的借口。 在一个题为时间和思想的新文件中：考古学，意识和文化杂志，沙龙捡起，研究人员详细说明了在旧石器时代，50,000到12,000年前生活的穴居人艺术家将需要某种形式的光线在黑暗的外壳工作。因此，它们将依靠轻质火炬，这可能降低洞穴中的氧气水平，并且可能会在缺氧状态下留下祖先（氧剥夺）。 没有足够的氧气，画家的意识将被改变，可能将它们推入欣快感状态。他们可能甚至可能有身体的经历和幻觉。 然而，以色列考古学家假定画家然后不会抓住绊倒背后的科学，并且可能会把它放在洞穴魔法或形而上学。 尽管如此，研究人员猜测，祖先会享受被扔石头的经验，并在他们神秘的效果中卷起洞穴。 “[我们]争夺进入这些深度，黑暗环境是一个有意识的选择，通过了解地下，氧气耗尽空间的变革性质，”他们指出“。 石器时代艺术家显然被这样的经历所采取，他们可以画得很高。 “这不是让洞穴显着的装饰，”作者相信。 “相反，所选择的洞穴的意义是他们装饰的原因。” [通过沙龙，通过Shutterock封面图像]

Why an Apple search engine has a real shot at competing with Google
苹果搜索引擎为何真正与Google竞争

2020-12-10 04:04:25

Small corners of the internet are ablaze with the news that Apple has significantly ramped up its search bot activity. Search bots typically scan websites in order to rank and index them for search engine results. When you look for something on a search engine, the results that appear are ordered by “ranking,” meaning that the result that is most accurate to what you are looking for appears at the top.

有消息称苹果已大大提高了搜索机器人的活动量，互联网的各个角落都闪闪发光。搜索机器人通常会扫描网站，以便对它们进行排名和索引以获取搜索引擎结果。当您在搜索引擎上查找内容时，显示的结果将按“排名”排序，这意味着最符合您要查找的结果的结果会显示在顶部。

This increase in activity also appears alongside pressure from the U.K. competition commission to break up Apple’s multibillion dollar sweetheart deal with Google. The deal ensures that Google is the default search engine for Apple’s iOS devices. Many are now anticipating that Apple is on track to launch its own search engine soon.

活动的增加也与英国竞争委员会施加的压力，要求打破苹果与谷歌的数十亿美元甜心交易。这项交易确保了Google是苹果iOS设备的默认搜索引擎。现在，许多人预计苹果将很快推出自己的搜索引擎。

Apple’s entry into the search engine market comes 11 years after Microsoft’s Bing made its debut, the only other noteworthy competitor to Google to date. Bing is by no means a success story, despite what Microsoft’s PR team claims, and it continues to pale in comparison in terms of economic performance and market power to Google’s ubiquitous search platform. In fact, every academic year I and my colleague, Dr. Kamal Munir, teach a case on Bing to the Cambridge MBA as a cautionary tale of what happens if you choose to go head on against entrenched platforms. Unlike Microsoft’s Bing, however, Apple’s opening gambit is quite different and is likely to produce a better outcome.

苹果公司进入搜索引擎市场是在微软必应（Bing）首次亮相11年之后，这是迄今为止Google唯一值得关注的竞争对手。尽管微软的PR团队宣称，必应绝不是成功的故事，与Google无处不在的搜索平台相比，必应在经济表现和市场支配力方面仍然显得苍白无力。实际上，我和我的同事Kamal Munir博士每学年都会向剑桥MBA教授关于Bing的案例，这是一个告诫性的故事，说明了如果您选择直面根深蒂固的平台会发生什么。但是，与微软的必应不同，苹果的开放方式大不相同，并且可能会产生更好的结果。

One of the bigger mistakes Microsoft made with the launch of Bing was to follow the same ad-based business model that Google was using. In this business model, search users enter what they are looking for, and based on that the search engine also shows relevant ads that might interest them. For such a business to be profitable, you need a very large number of users searching, as well as a large number of advertisers willing to sell to them, alongside millions of websites scanned by the previously mentioned search bots. All three are needed to display useful search results for the user and bring the right customer to the advertiser.

微软推出Bing时犯的最大错误之一就是遵循Google所使用的基于广告的商业模式。在此业务模型中，搜索用户输入他们要寻找的内容，并以此为基础，搜索引擎还会显示他们可能感兴趣的相关广告。为了使这样的企业盈利，您需要大量的用户搜索，以及大量愿意向他们出售产品的广告商，以及前面提到的搜索机器人扫描的数百万个网站。这三者都需要为用户显示有用的搜索结果，并将合适的客户带到广告客户。

In between, Google gets paid for matching the right ads with the right users. The more searches are made, the more useful the results. The more useful the results, the better is the ad targeting. Bing struggled to get this virtuous cycle started, and never really got to the scale that Google enjoys with its search offering.

在这两者之间，Google会因将合适的广告与合适的用户匹配而获得报酬。搜索次数越多，结果越有用。结果越有用，广告定位就越好。必应（Bing）努力使这个良性循环开始，并且从未真正达到Google在其搜索产品中所享有的规模。

Apple’s search engine will have a different future if rumors about its business model are true. Apple has been focusing heavily on user privacy recently, including but not limited to publicly refusing to give secret access to its devices to the FBI. It will be very much in line with this “privacy first” position that Apple chooses not to make money from advertising, which involves exposing customer usage data to third parties. Instead, it could simply sell more of its highly profitable devices and subscriptions to privacy-conscious customers. By not following Google’s footsteps, Apple does not have to engage with the search giant on its terms.

如果有关苹果商业模式的传言属实，那么苹果的搜索引擎将有一个不同的未来。苹果最近一直在高度关注用户隐私，包括但不限于公开拒绝将其设备秘密访问给FBI。苹果选择不从广告中赚钱与这种“隐私至上”的立场非常吻合，这涉及将客户使用情况数据暴露给第三方。取而代之的是，它可以简单地将更多的高利润设备和订阅销售给注重隐私的客户。通过不遵循Google的脚步，Apple不必与搜索巨头就其条款进行互动。

When Bing was launched, it had features that Google did not have at that time. This included a “hover preview” of search results as well as a specialization of sorts in travel, shopping, local businesses, and health searches. In terms of quality of search results, Microsoft’s claimed similar or better output to Google’s. Despite its apparent product superiority, Bing never won the search engine wars.

Bing推出时，它具有Google当时没有的功能。其中包括搜索结果的“悬停预览”，以及旅行，购物，本地企业和健康搜索中的各种特殊化。就搜索结果的质量而言，微软声称其输出与Google类似或更好。尽管必应具有明显的产品优势，但必应从未赢得过搜索引擎大战。

This aspect of search engine history plays in favor of Apple, which does not need to differentiate itself from Google. In fact, Apple’s search results need to be “just good enough” to be adopted by its users en masse. We can see this with the results of Apple maps, which was launched back in 2012. Despite a publicly rocky launch thanks to its poor geographic coverage, Apple Maps gained a dominant market share of 60% in the U.K.’s iPhone users in just under a year of launch. The same holds true for Apple Music, which has become the second-biggest player in streaming music despite a nine-year lead by Spotify.

搜索引擎历史记录的这一方面对苹果有利，它不需要与Google区别开来。实际上，苹果公司的搜索结果必须“足够好”，才能被其用户广泛采用。我们可以从2012年推出的Apple Maps的结果中看到这一点。尽管由于其地理覆盖范围不佳而公开上市，但Apple Maps在英国iPhone用户中获得了60％的主导市场份额。推出一年。苹果音乐公司也是如此，尽管在Spotify的九年领导下，苹果音乐公司已成为流媒体音乐的第二大播放器。

With its latest iOS 14 update, Apple has already started swapping out Google search results in favor of its own. Most iOS users have barely noticed the change for all the reasons given above. But this silent swapping does not come without its own set of challenges. By defaulting to its search engine instead of Google on its devices, Apple will open itself to monopoly criticism from competition commissions in a variety of markets. It is also likely to upset the advertising industry, which could lose their reach to Apple customers. The Apple customer base is a coveted one thanks to its better than average buying power, and by making it easier for users to avoid search ads, Apple might just create a tectonic shift in the advertising industry as a whole.

通过最新的iOS 14更新，Apple已经开始换出Google搜索结果以支持自己的搜索结果。由于上述所有原因，大多数iOS用户几乎都没有注意到这一变化。但是，这种无声交换并非没有挑战。通过默认使用其搜索引擎而不是其设备上的Google，Apple将会公开接受各种市场竞争委员会的垄断批评。这也可能使广告行业不安，这可能会失去他们对苹果客户的影响。苹果的客户群之所以令人垂涎，是因为它具有比平均水平更高的购买力，并且通过使用户更容易避免搜索广告，苹果可能会在整个广告行业中产生构造性的转变。

Google’s dominance on internet search will not come to an end with Apple’s entry into the foray, but it would definitely weaken in the face of increasing consumer preference for privacy. Given that Google’s business model differs dramatically from Apple’s, it is likely that the search giant would have to learn to uncomfortably live with its rival’s search engine instead of pivoting to compete with it head on.

谷歌在互联网搜索领域的统治地位不会随着苹果公司的进军而终结，但是随着消费者对隐私的偏好日益提高，它肯定会减弱。鉴于Google的业务模式与Apple的业务模式大不相同，这家搜索巨头可能将不得不学会与竞争对手的搜索引擎配合，而不是转向与之竞争。

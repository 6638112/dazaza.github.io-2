The Sound So Loud That It Circled the Earth Four Times
声音如此之大，以至于绕地球四圈

2020-12-01 03:34:30

On 27 August 1883, the Earth let out a noise louder than any it has made since.

1883年8月27日，地球发出的声音比其以来的任何声音都要响。

It was 10:02 AM local time when the sound emerged from the island of Krakatoa, which sits between Java and Sumatra in Indonesia. It was heard 1,300 miles away in the Andaman and Nicobar islands (“extraordinary sounds were heard, as of guns firing”); 2,000 miles away in New Guinea and Western Australia (“a series of loud reports, resembling those of artillery in a north-westerly direction”); and even 3,000 miles away in the Indian Ocean island of Rodrigues, near Mauritius* (“coming from the eastward, like the distant roar of heavy guns.” 1) In all, it was heard by people in over 50 different geographical locations, together spanning an area covering a thirteenth of the globe.

这是当地时间上午10:02，声音从位于印度尼西亚爪哇岛与苏门答腊岛之间的Krakatoa岛发出。在安达曼和尼科巴群岛的1,300英里外听到了声音（“枪声不断，听到了异常的声音”）；在新几内亚和西澳大利亚州相距2000英里（“一系列响亮的报道，类似于西北方向的炮兵报道”）；甚至在毛里求斯附近的印度洋小岛罗德里格斯岛（Rodrigues）上也有3,000英里*（“从东方来，就像遥远的重型枪支咆哮声。” 1）总共有50多个不同地理位置的人们听到了覆盖了全球十三分之一的区域。

Think, for a moment, just how crazy this is. If you’re in Boston and someone tells you that they heard a sound coming from New York City, you’re probably going to give them a funny look. But Boston is a mere 200 miles from New York. What we’re talking about here is like being in Boston and clearly hearing a noise coming from Dublin, Ireland. Travelling at the speed of sound (766 miles or 1,233 kilometers per hour), it takes a noise about 4 hours to cover that distance. This is the most distant sound that has ever been heard in recorded history.

暂时想一想，这有多疯狂。如果您在波士顿，并且有人告诉您他们听到了纽约市发出的声音，那么您可能会给他们一个有趣的外观。但是波士顿距离纽约仅200英里。我们在这里所说的就像是在波士顿，清楚地听到爱尔兰都柏林发出的噪音。以音速（每小时766英里或1,233公里）行驶时，需要大约4个小时的噪音才能覆盖该距离。这是有记录的历史中听到过的最遥远的声音。

So what could possibly create such an earth-shatteringly loud bang? A volcano on Krakatoa had just erupted with a force so great that it tore the island apart, emitting a plume of smoke that reached 17 miles into the atmosphere, according to a geologist who witnessed it 1. You could use this observation to calculate that stuff spewed out of the volcano at over 1,600 miles per hour—or nearly half a mile per second. That’s more than twice the speed of sound.

那么，有什么可能造成如此惊人的轰隆声呢？目击者说，据一位地质学家说，克拉卡托阿火山上的火山爆发时爆发出巨大的力量，以至于将岛撕裂了，散发出一股烟气，直达大气层17英里。1以每小时1600英里（或每秒近半英里）的速度喷出火山。这是音速的两倍以上。

This explosion created a deadly tsunami with waves over a hundred feet (30 meters) in height. One hundred sixty-five coastal villages and settlements were swept away and entirely destroyed. In all, the Dutch (the colonial rulers of Indonesia at the time) estimated the death toll at 36,417, while other estimates exceed 120,000 2,3.

这次爆炸造成了致命的海啸，波浪高度超过一百英尺（30米）。 165个沿海村庄和定居点被冲走并被完全摧毁。总的来说，荷兰人（当时是印度尼西亚的殖民统治者）估计死亡人数为36,417，而其他估计数字则超过120,000 2,3。

The British ship Norham Castle was 40 miles from Krakatoa at the time of the explosion. The ship’s captain wrote in his log, “So violent are the explosions that the ear-drums of over half my crew have been shattered. My last thoughts are with my dear wife. I am convinced that the Day of Judgement has come.” 2

爆炸发生时，英国船只诺勒姆城堡（Norham Castle）距克拉卡托（Krakatoa）40英里。该船的船长在他的日志中写道：“爆炸是如此剧烈，以至于我一半以上的船员的耳鼓都被打碎了。我最后的想法是和我亲爱的妻子在一起。我坚信审判日已经到来。” 2

In general, sounds are caused not by the end of the world but by fluctuations in air pressure. A barometer at the Batavia gasworks (100 miles away from Krakatoa) registered the ensuing spike in pressure at over 2.5 inches of mercury 1,2 . That converts to over 172 decibels of sound pressure, an unimaginably loud noise. To put that in context, if you were operating a jackhammer you’d be subject to about 100 decibels. The human threshold for pain is near 130 decibels, and if you had the misfortune of standing next to a jet engine, you’d experience a 150 decibel sound. (A 10 decibel increase is perceived by people as sounding roughly twice as loud.) The Krakatoa explosion registered 172 decibels at 100 miles from the source. This is so astonishingly loud, that it’s inching up against the limits of what we mean by “sound.”

通常，声音不是由世界末日引起的，而是由气压波动引起的。巴达维亚煤气厂（距克拉卡托100英里）的气压计记录了随后出现的压力峰值，该压力峰值超过2.5英寸汞柱1,2。转换成超过172分贝的声压，这是难以想象的大声噪音。为了说明这一点，如果您使用手提电钻，则可能会受到大约100分贝的影响。人类的痛苦阈值接近130分贝，如果您不幸站在喷气发动机旁，则将听到150分贝的声音。 （人们认为声音增加10分贝，大约是声音的两倍。）喀拉喀托爆炸在距震源100英里处记录到172分贝。这是如此惊人的响亮，以至于超出了我们所说的“声音”的极限。

When you hum a note or speak a word, you’re wiggling air molecules back and forth dozens or hundreds of times per second, causing the air pressure to be low in some places and high in other places. The louder the sound, the more intense these wiggles, and the larger the fluctuations in air pressure. But there’s a limit to how loud a sound can get. At some point, the fluctuations in air pressure are so large that the low pressure regions hit zero pressure—a vacuum—and you can’t get any lower than that. This limit happens to be about 194 decibels for a sound in Earth’s atmosphere. Any louder, and the sound is no longer just passing through the air, it’s actually pushing the air along with it, creating a pressurized burst of moving air known as a shock wave.

当您哼出音符或说出一个字时，您每秒就会在空气分子之间来回摆动数十次或数百次，从而导致某些地方的气压低而另一些地方的气压高。声音越大，这些摆动越剧烈，并且气压波动越大。但是声音的响度是有限制的。在某个时刻，气压的波动很大，以至于低压区达到零压力（真空），而您再也不能低于这个压力。对于地球大气中的声音，此限制恰好约为194分贝。声音更大了，声音不再仅仅是通过空气，而是实际上推动了空气的流动，从而产生了被称为冲击波的加压流动空气。

Closer to Krakatoa, the sound was well over this limit, producing a blast of high pressure air so powerful that it ruptured the eardrums of sailors 40 miles away. As this sound travelled thousands of miles, reaching Australia and the Indian Ocean, the wiggles in pressure started to die down, sounding more like a distant gunshot. Over 3,000 miles into its journey, the wave of pressure grew too quiet for human ears to hear, but it continued to sweep onward, reverberating for days across the globe. The atmosphere was ringing like a bell, imperceptible to us but detectable by our instruments.

离克拉卡托亚（Krakatoa）更近，声音远超过此极限，产生了强大的高压空气冲击声，使40英里外的水手的耳膜破裂。随着声音传播到数千英里，到达澳大利亚和印度洋，压力的摆动开始消失，听起来更像是遥远的枪声。历经3,000英里的路程，压力波变得太安静了，以至于人耳无法听到，但它继续向前扫，在全球范围内回荡了好几天。气氛像铃铛一样响，我们无法察觉，但我们的乐器可以察觉。

By 1883, weather stations in scores of cities across the world were using barometers to track changes in atmospheric pressure. Six hours and 47 minutes after the Krakatoa explosion, a spike of air pressure was detected in Calcutta. By 8 hours, the pulse reached Mauritius in the west and Melbourne and Sydney in the east. By 12 hours, St. Petersburg noticed the pulse, followed by Vienna, Rome, Paris, Berlin, and Munich. By 18 hours the pulse had reached New York, Washington DC, and Toronto 1 .  Amazingly, for as many as 5 days after the explosion, weather stations in 50 cities around the globe observed this unprecedented spike in pressure re-occuring like clockwork, approximately every 34 hours. That is roughly how long it takes sound to travel around the entire planet.

到1883年，全球数十个城市的气象站都使用气压计来跟踪大气压力的变化。 Krakatoa爆炸六小时四十七分钟后，在加尔各答发现气压峰值。到了8个小时，脉搏到达了西部的毛里求斯和东部的墨尔本和悉尼。到了12小时，圣彼得堡注意到了脉搏，随后是维也纳，罗马，巴黎，柏林和慕尼黑。到18小时时，脉搏已到达纽约，华盛顿特区和多伦多1。令人惊讶的是，爆炸发生后多达5天，全球50个城市的气象站大约每34小时观察到一次前所未有的压力峰值再次出现，就像发条一样。那大概是声音在整个星球上传播要花费多长时间。

In all, the pressure waves from Krakatoa circled the globe three to four times in each direction. (Each city felt up to seven pressure spikes because they experienced shock waves travelling in opposite directions from the volcano 1.) Meanwhile, tidal stations as far away as India, England, and San Francisco measured a rise in ocean waves simultaneous with this air pulse, an effect that had never been seen before. It was a sound that could no longer be heard but that continued moving around the world, a phenomenon that people nicknamed “the great air-wave.”

总之，来自Krakatoa的压力波在每个方向上绕地球旋转了三到四次。 （每个城市都感觉到七个压力峰值，因为它们经历了从火山1沿相反方向传播的冲击波。）同时，远至印度，英国和旧金山的潮汐站观测到与此空气脉冲同时引起的海浪上升。 ，这是前所未有的效果。这种声音已经不再能听见，但仍在世界范围内传播，这种现象被人们昵称为“伟大的空气波”。

Recently, an incredible home video of a volcanic eruption taken by a couple on vacation in Papua New Guinea started making the rounds on the Internet. If you watch closely, this video gives you a sense for the pressure wave created by a volcano.

最近，一对夫妇在巴布亚新几内亚度假拍摄的火山喷发的令人难以置信的家庭录像开始在互联网上巡回演出。如果您仔细观看，此视频可让您了解火山造成的压力波。

When the volcano erupts, it produces a sudden spike in air pressure; you can actually watch as it moves through the air, condensing water vapor into clouds as it travels. The people taking the video are (fortunately) far enough away that pressure wave takes a while to reach them. When it does finally hit the boat, some 13 seconds after the explosion, you hear what sounds like a huge gunshot accompanied by a sudden blast of air. Multiplying 13 seconds by the speed of sound tells us that the boat was about 4.4 kilometers, or 2.7 miles, away from the volcano. This is somewhat akin to what happened at Krakatoa, except the ‘gunshot’ in that case could be heard not just three but three thousand miles, away, a mind-boggling demonstration of the immense destructive power that nature can unleash.

火山爆发时，气压会突然升高。您实际上可以观察到它在空气中的流动，并在水传播时将水蒸气冷凝成云。录制视频的人（很幸运）距离足够远，压力波需要一段时间才能到达他们。爆炸大约13秒钟后，当它最终击中船时，您会听到听起来像是巨大的枪声并伴有突然的空气爆炸。将声速乘以13秒可知，该船距离火山约4.4公里（2.7英里）。这有点类似于在卡卡托亚发生的事情，除了在这种情况下，不仅可以听到三声，而且可以听到三千英里之外的“枪声”，这令人难以置信地证明了自然可以释放的巨大破坏力。

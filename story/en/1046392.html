Parents' income, not smarts, key to entrepreneurship
父母的收入而非聪明才智是创业的关键

2021-01-28 23:36:09

What leads someone to create a start-up? A new study by the Finance Ministry found that the income of someone’s parents is the factor that correlates most to entrepreneurship, with higher wealth connected to a greater likelihood of being a start-up founder. The study analyzed a unique data set related to Israeli entrepreneurs age 25 to 35, charting a person’s learning ability – as measured by academic achievements and standardized test scores – as well as demographic data about his or her parents. “A strong economic base may benefit those interested in setting up a start-up, providing an economic safety net in the event of failure,” the study noted. In addition, it is likely that individuals from strong economic backgrounds receive greater exposure to messages about entrepreneurship and innovation, and thereby model their career paths. A significant connection between the parents’ level of education and entrepreneurship was also noted. That connection is even stronger if the parents studied science or technology. Interestingly, there was no difference whether the father or mother was the one who came from that background. Those whose parents ranked in the top 20% of income but scored in the lower 50% in math on the Meitzav academic achievement test had a higher chance of becoming an entrepreneur than those whose parents’ income was among the lower 60% but scored in the top 10% in math. “The main takeaway from this study is that Israel has a reservoir of unrealized potential,” said Yona Hackett, a chief economist in the Finance Ministry. “We have a lot of children who are very talented, and it’s a shame to let that go to waste.” Research like this is an early part of a process that can eventually turn into government planning, he explained. Entrepreneurship has very little to do with being smart, the study found. The learning ability component explained only about 11% of the gap in the probability of being a start-up entrepreneur between individuals from different economic backgrounds. However, the report said, the ability to learn quickly is a significant factor for finding work in the hi-tech sector. According to the statistical model, learning ability accounted for 33% of the gap between different income classes to work in hi-tech. The report noted that understanding these factors is more important than ever as the coronavirus crisis is shifting more of Israel’s economy toward hi-tech. “In this context, understanding the mechanisms that lead to the ‘formation’ of hi-tech entrepreneurs is of great importance because they constitute an important growth engine for the sector as a whole,” the report said.

是什么导致某人创建了一家初创公司？财政部的一项新研究发现，某人父母的收入是与创业精神最相关的因素，更高的财富与成为创业者的可能性更大。这项研究分析了一个与25至35岁的以色列企业家有关的独特数据集，绘制了一个人的学习能力（以学业成绩和标准化考试成绩来衡量）以及有关其父母的人口统计数据。研究指出：“强大的经济基础可能会使那些有兴趣建立一家初创企业的人受益，并在失败时提供经济安全网。”此外，经济背景很强的个人可能会更多地接触到有关企业家精神和创新的信息，从而为自己的职业道路建模。还指出了父母的教育水平与企业家精神之间的重要联系。如果父母学习科学或技术，这种联系会更加牢固。有趣的是，父亲或母亲是来自这一背景的人也没有区别。父母的收入在收入的前20％中排名最高，但在Meitzav学业成绩测验中的数学得分低于50％的人，其父母要比父母的收入在收入的60％以下但收入的低者高得多。数学排名前10％。财政部首席经济学家约纳·哈克特（Yona Hackett）表示：“这项研究的主要成果是，以色列拥有未实现的潜力。” “我们有很多非常有才华的孩子，丢掉这是很可惜的。”他解释说，这样的研究是该过程的早期部分，最终可以转变为政府计划。研究发现，企业家精神与聪明无关。学习能力的组成部分仅解释了来自不同经济背景的个人之间成为创业企业家的可能性的大约11％的差距。但是，该报告称，快速学习的能力是在高科技领域找到工作的重要因素。根据统计模型，学习能力占从事高科技工作的不同收入阶层之间差距的33％。报告指出，了解这些因素比以往任何时候都重要，因为冠状病毒危机正在使以色列的经济更多地转向高科技。报告说：“在这种情况下，了解导致高科技企业家'形成'的机制非常重要，因为它们构成了整个行业的重要增长引擎。”

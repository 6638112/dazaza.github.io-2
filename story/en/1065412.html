He was instrumental in developing all aspects ofRedox OS, from the kernel, to relibc, to porting programs. His work wasdetailed in his own words in a number of news posts authored by jD91mZM2 at https://www.redox-os.org/news/.

伊德尔·雷诺·雷诺OS贡献者JD91MZM2的一个亲爱的朋友，在2021年3月，18岁。他参与了2018年，2019年和2020年的德累克斯OS夏天。他在开发所有重新开发的各个方面都是有助于操作系统，从内核到Relibc，移植程序。他的作品在JD91MZM2在HTTPS://www.redox-os.org/news/撰写的一些新闻帖子中以他自己的话语方式用他自己的话语。

Yesterday, I was messaged by another contributor regarding the length of timejD91mZM2 was offline and the fact he was not responding to emails. I reachedout via what I had, to no avail. I revealed his true name to the othercontributor, who then found his obituary. We verified that his name, location,and date of birth matched. While no cause of death was listed, I believe theevidence we found points to suicide after a mental health episode.

昨天，我被另一个贡献者发放了一个关于TimeJD91MZM2的长度的贡献者离线，事实并没有回复电子邮件。我通过我拥有的东西到达，无济于事。我透露了他的真实名称，另一个是谁发现了他的ob告。我们核实他的名字，位置和出生日期匹配。虽然没有列出死亡的原因，但我相信在心理健康发作后，我们发现有点自杀。

After learning all of this, I was shocked. How could such a prolificcontributor, not just to Redox but to many projects, feel that death waspreferable to life? This was a person who was boundlessly competent, and whoseemed until recently to have a good handle on their life. But the longer Ihave lived, the more I realize how much of an illusion this can be, and howrapidly things can deteriorate.

在了解所有这一切之后，我感到震惊。这么多产的人怎么可能只是氧化还原，而是对许多项目来说，觉得死亡是人生的选择？这是一个无限的人，谁直到最近在他们的生活中有一个很好的句柄。但是，我的生活越长，我意识到这可能是多大的错觉，而且为什么如何恶化。

I last communicated with jD91mZM2 in February, one month prior to his death.This communication was purely technical, regarding the aarch64 port of theRedox kernel. I can’t help but think, that perhaps this was a factor in hisdecision to choose death.

我上次在他死亡前一个月与2月份与JD91MZM2通信。这一沟通纯粹是技术的，关于Theredox内核的AARCH64港。我不禁想到，也许这是他选择死亡的一个因素。

In open source, we often emphasize the importance of good code. After all, thedeliverable of every open source project, is the source, right? We often forgetthat good code is written by good people, and that retaining those people andkeeping them happy should be the primary concern of any project maintainer.

在开源中，我们经常强调良好代码的重要性。毕竟，每个开源项目都有它，是源头，对吗？我们经常忘记了良好的代码是由好人写的，并且保留这​​些人并希望他们应该是任何项目维护者的主要关注点。

There are lots of aspects to mental health episodes. On the one hand, there isusually a genetic component to mental illness. On the other, these geneticprecursors usually require both chronic and acute environmental triggers.These chronic triggers can be a long-lasting poor home or work environment, andlead to manifestation of the mental illness itself. The acute triggers couldbe, for example, an argument with someone, leading to a mental illness episode.These episodes can be serious enough to overcome the extreme instinct tosurvive, leading to suicide.

心理健康剧集有很多方面。一方面，有精神疾病的遗传组成部分。另一方面，这些遗传患者通常需要慢性和急性环境触发。这些慢性触发器可以是一个持久的贫穷家庭或工作环境，并为精神疾病本身表现出来。例如，急性触发器可以与某人的争论导致精神疾病剧集。这些剧集可能足够严重，以克服极端本能的痛苦，导致自杀。

In this way, suicide is not a display of weakness. In fact, it is a display ofextreme conviction and strength. Even with the backdrop of mental illness,there are parts of the brain that are usually unaffected. These parts are soancient in development, we have little conscious control of them. To attemptsuicide requires overcoming conscious desires to survive. To succeed, is toovercome extreme subconscious desires. This means that, for suicide, often thesmartest and most capable people are able to succeed.

通过这种方式，自杀不是弱点的显示。事实上，它是一种展示的一种信念和力量。即使有精神疾病的背景，也有部分大脑通常不受影响。这些部位在发展中是超级的，我们对他们有很小的意识控制。尝试需要克服有意识的欲望来生存。为了成功，是难以实现极端的潜意识欲望。这意味着，对于自杀，通常最终和最有能力的人能够成功。

This anti-selection of capable people is a terrifying epidemic. Humanity ingeneral is in desperate need of artificial solutions to long-standing problems.Take climate change, for instance. Out of the 800,000 people each year whocommitted suicide, on average perhaps more capable than the rest of us, what ifa few would have been instrumental in developing fusion power?

这种反能力的人是一种可怕的流行病。例如，人类ingeneral in绝望需要人工解决方案到长期存在的问题。例如，气候变化。每年在80万人中出来的自杀，平均可能比我们其他人更有能力，其中很少有人在开发融合力量方面是什么呢？

And yet, we as a society have taken the position that these events are anunstoppable force. That the factors leading to suicide are internal, notexternal. I refuse to believe this, on principle. We must search out causes andmitigate them, for every problem, even if it ends up being impossible.

然而，我们作为一个社会已经采取了这些事件是不可移植的力量的立场。导致自杀的因素是内部的，偏不到。我拒绝相信这一点，原则上。对于每个问题，我们必须搜索原因并介绍它们，即使它最终不可能。

And so, I am forced to look into my own actions, to see what could have beendone differently. To see if I could have saved a life, and to see what livesI can save in the future.

所以，我被迫研究自己的行为，看看可能有什么不同的脑子。看看我是否可以拯救生命，并看看Livei将来可以拯救什么。

There are aspects of open source that seem to attract the strangest ofhumanity, myself included. The insistence that all things be inspectable, isperhaps driven by obsessive compulsive behavior. And those prone to suchbehavior, often inherit it from other disorders. ADHD, autism, bipolardisorder, depression, and other disorders are incredibly common among opensource contributors.

有些开放来源的方面似乎吸引了陌生人的陌生人，我自己包括在内。坚持认为所有事情都是无法检查的，这是由强迫行为驱动的。和那些易于这样的障碍，通常继承来自其他疾病。 ADHD，自闭症，Bipolardisorder，Despression和其他障碍在OpenSource贡献者之间非常常见。

As a result, there is also an obvious lack of soft skills among open sourcecommunities. This has the clear effect of splitting communities and isolatingopen source itself from the “normal” world. Fortunately for us, open sourceended up being a profitable business to be in. This injection of capital haslead to a significant diversification of talent in open source.

因此，开放源社会的开放性缺乏软技能也是明显的。这具有拆分社区和隔离源本身从“正常”世界的效果明显。幸运的是，对于我们来说，开放归零是一个有利可图的业务。这种资本注入的是开源的大量人才多样化。

This has, however, come at a cost. Those who do not fit into the new businessinterests of open source projects are often cast aside. Projects are furtherdivided into forks on forks, as disagreements with the original maintainerslead to insurmountable changes in the projects themselves. I myself haveparticipated in all sides of this.

但是，这是成本的。那些不适合新的开源项目的新商务兴趣的人通常会抛弃。项目进一步融入叉子上的叉子，因为与原始维护人员对项目本身的不可逾越的变化分歧。我自己在这个方面都有participated。

Lacking in most of this has been an investigation into the human cost. To lookinto the numerous mental health episodes among open source contributors, andto try to identify some common elements. Sometimes these episodes lead to newprojects, sometimes to burn out contributors who then leave open source, andsometimes to suicide.

缺乏这一切都是对人力成本的调查。在开源贡献者中寻找众多心理健康剧集，并尝试识别一些共同的元素。有时这些剧集导致NewProjects，有时会烧掉然后赋予留出开源的贡献者，并且对自杀。

We have to recognize our role in creating both the chronic stressors leading tomental illness, as well as the acute stressors leading to dangerous episodes.

我们必须认识到我们在创造慢性压力源的主要成本疾病，以及导致危险发作的急压力源。

I am not immune to mental illness. I often get messages to the effect of, “Youseem to have your stuff under control, how do you do it?” The brutal truth is,I doubt any of us actually do. And what we define as “under control” may bevery different. Having successful projects is not the same as having generalhappiness.

我对精神疾病并没有免疫。我经常得到消息的效果，“reSeem让你的东西在控制下，你是怎么做到的？”野蛮的事实是，我怀疑我们中的任何一个实际上。我们定义为“在控制下”可能是不同的。具有成功的项目与具有一般性的项目不同。

I do have to recognize I am much happier now than I have ever been. So, perhapscompared to the average, I do have things under control. My life has been anodyssey of therapists, psychiatrists, drugs, and isolation. It could haveeasily ended as others have. I was lucky enough to find my chronic stressors,and dutifully eliminate them.

我确实必须认识到我现在比曾经幸福快。所以，也许在平均值的情况下，我确实有了控制事物。我的生活一直是治疗师，精神科医生，毒品和孤立的讽刺。就像其他人一样，它可以很脆弱。我很幸运能够找到我的长期压力，尽职尽责地消除它们。

My freshman year of college, around the same time in my life as when jD91mZM2decided to end his own, was particularly hard. I gained nearly fifty pounds inweight. I lived with three other roommates, two of which have also died young.I alternated between ADHD medication, anti-depressants, and even smoking - tofind what would “fix” me. The whole time, I programmed, often ignoring myschool work to do so.

我的大学大学，大约在我的生活中同时，就像JD91MZM2一样结束自己，特别努力。我超重近五十英镑。我和其他三个室友一起生活过，其中两个也死了。我在ADHD药物治疗，抗抑郁药之间交替，甚至吸烟 - Tofind是什么“修复”我。整个时间，我编程，经常忽略myschool工作要这样做。

Prior to college, I had worked as an intern writing defibrillator software atZoll Medical. I have two patents from this work. I’ll be honest, I was leapsand bounds above others in my understanding of computers. And I’ll be honestwith myself, I was leaps and bounds below others in my understanding of people,including myself.

在大学之前，我曾担任实习生书面除颤器软件atzoll医疗。我有两项工作的专利。我会诚实，我在我对计算机的理解中跳过了其他人。我会诚实地，我在我对包括自己的人的理解中突飞和偏袒其他人。

In that time, I developed a relationship with the Vice President of Researchand Development, who became my defacto boss. Near the end of my freshman year,he reached out to me to ask if I wanted to continue working. I said yes.

在那个时候，我与研究和发展的副总裁建立了关系，成为我的失败老板。在新生年底附近，他向我求助地问我是否想继续工作。我说是。

My sophomore year was very different. I spent most of my time writing software,and making good money. I had no interest in school. I failed a few courses,while testing out of many of the high-level CS classes. I soon dropped out ofcollege to pursue software engineering full time.

我的二年级学生是非常不同的。我花了大部分时间写软件，并赚了好钱。我对学校没有兴趣。我失败了一些课程，同时在许多高级CS类中测试。我很快就掉了出来追求软件工程的全职。

This had a huge positive influence on my mental health. I lost weight. I keptin touch with other alumni from my college, and eventually through them, metmy wife. We bought a house together. I started Redox OS. My wife and I gotmarried. I started working at System76. My wife and I had a beautiful daughter.And never since my sophomore year of college have I ever even thought about myown mental health, or had to go to therapy or use medication. All of mystressors were gone.

这对我的心理健康有巨大的积极影响力。我的体重减轻了。我从我的大学里与其他校友接触，最终通过他们，Metmy Wife。我们一起买了一所房子。我开始了redox操作系统。我的妻子和我得救了。我开始在System76工作。我的妻子和我有一个漂亮的女儿。从来没有从大学生的大学生开始，我甚至曾经考虑过Myown心理健康，或者不得不去治疗或使用药物。所有的Mystressors都消失了。

One man’s mental health success does not always translate into others. Alongthis journey, I have created and destroyed (through neglect) hundreds ofrelationships. I have to admit, that while I am happy, I have a tendency tocause quite the opposite in others. I keep the relationships that bring me joy,and ignore the ones that need work. And at some point, perhaps I forgot tokeep in touch with jD91mZM2 and make sure he found the same happiness I have.

一个人的心理健康成功并不总是转化为别人。在旅途中，我创造和摧毁了（通过忽视）数百个。我必须承认，虽然我很高兴，但我有一个倾向于在别人中相反。我保留了带给我快乐的关系，并忽略需要工作的关系。在某些时候，也许我忘了与JD91MZM2联系，并确保他发现了我所拥有的幸福。

There is no solution, every case is different. But I feel compelled to check inon the many people I have lost in the years, and I hope you do too. We arestill in the dark ages of understanding the human mind, and the more we checkin with each-other, the better we will do. I know I could have made adifference, had I just done a little more. Maybe not for jD91mZM2, but forsomeone feeling much the same. I will no longer value contributors by the codethey crank out. The code doesn’t write itself, and the person writing the codeneeds even more maintenance than the “open source” itself.

没有解决方案，每种情况都不同。但我觉得被迫检查一下我多年丢失的许多人，我希望你也能做。我们在理解人类思想的黑暗时代奄奄一息，我们越多，我们越少，我们将越好。我知道我可以做出积极的好处，我刚刚做了一点。也许不是jd91mzm2，但面积很多是相同的。我将不再有价值的代码曲柄曲柄。代码不写自己，并且写入代码的人比“开源”自身更具维护。

The First AI-written Play Isn't Shakespeare - but It Has Its Moments
第一部由AI编写的剧本不是莎士比亚-但它有它的瞬间

2021-03-01 03:51:22

One hundred years ago, a play by the Czech author Karel Čapek introduced the word “robot,” telling the story of artificial factory workers designed to serve humans. Now, in a metanarrative twist, a robot itself has written a play. And it premieres online today.

一百年前，捷克作家卡雷尔·卡佩克（KarelČapek）的戏剧中引入了“机器人”一词，讲述了旨在为人类服务的人工工厂工人的故事。现在，在叙事的转折中，机器人本身已经编写了一部剧本。它今天在网上首映。

“It’s a kind of futuristic Little Prince,” says dramatist David Košťák, who oversaw the script. Like the classical French children’s book, the 60-minute production— AI: When a robot writes a play—tells the journey of a character (this time a robot), who goes out into the world to learn about society, human emotions, and even death.

监督剧本的剧作家大卫·科沙克（DavidKošťák）说：“这是一种未来主义的小王子”。就像法国经典的儿童读物一样，这部60分钟的作品-AI：当机器人写剧本时，讲述了一个角色（这次是机器人）的旅程，他走遍世界了解社会，人类情感和甚至死亡。

The script was created by a widely available artificial intelligence (AI) system called GPT-2. Created by Elon Musk’s company OpenAI, this “robot” is a computer model designed to generate text by drawing from the enormous repository of information available on the internet. ( You can test it here.) So far, the technology has been used to write fake news, short stories, and poems. The play is GPT-2’s first theater production, the team behind it claims.

该脚本由称为GPT-2的广泛使用的人工智能（AI）系统创建。这种“机器人”是由埃隆·马斯克（Elon Musk）的公司OpenAI创建的，它是一种计算机模型，旨在通过从互联网上可用的大量信息库中提取文字来生成文本。 （您可以在此处进行测试。）到目前为止，该技术已被用于撰写假新闻，短篇小说和诗歌。该剧是GPT-2的第一部戏剧作品，其背后的团队声称。

Here’s how it works: First, a human feeds the program with a prompt. In this case, the researchers—at Charles University in Prague—began with two sentences of dialogue, where one or two characters chat about human feelings and experiences. The first input they gave to the AI, for example, was: “Hello, I am robot and it is a pleasure to invite you to see a play I wrote.” The software then takes things from there, generating up to 1000 words of additional text.

它是这样工作的：首先，一个人向程序提供提示。在这种情况下，研究人员-布拉格查尔斯大学的研究人员开始使用两个对话句子，其中一个或两个字符谈论人类的感受和经历。例如，他们给AI的第一个输入是：“您好，我是机器人，很高兴邀请您观看我写的剧本。”然后，该软件从那里获取内容，最多生成1000个单词的附加文本。

The result is far from William Shakespeare. After a few sentences, the program starts to write things that sometimes don’t follow a logical storyline, or statements that contradict other passages of the text. For example, the AI sometimes forgot the main character was a robot, not a human. “Sometimes it would change a male to female in the middle of a dialogue,” says Charles University computational linguist Rudolf Rosa, who started to work on the project 2 years ago.

结果远非莎士比亚。经过几句话之后，程序开始编写有时不符合逻辑故事情节或与文本其他段落矛盾的陈述的内容。例如，AI有时会忘记主要角色是机器人，而不是人类。查尔斯大学计算语言学家鲁道夫·罗莎（Rudolf Rosa）说：“有时在对话过程中，男性会变成女性。”他两年前开始从事该项目。

That happens because the program doesn’t really know the meaning of the sentences, says Chad DeChant, an AI expert at Columbia University. “It just puts together words that are likely to be used together, one after the other,” says DeChant, who was not part of the play but is curious to watch it. As it keeps going, there is more room for nonsense.

发生这种情况的原因是该程序并不真正了解句子的含义，哥伦比亚大学的AI专家Chad DeChant说。 “它只是将可能会一起使用的单词放在一起，” DeChant说，他虽然不是这出戏的一部分，但很想观看。随着它的不断发展，还有更多的废话余地。

To prevent that, the team didn’t let GPT-2 write the entire play at once. Instead, the researchers broke the show down into eight scenes, each less than 5 minutes; each scene also only contained a dialogue between two characters at the same time. In addition, the scientists sometimes changed the text, for example altering the passages where the AI changed the character’s gender from line to line or repeating their initial text prompt until the program spat out sensible prose. Rosa estimates that 90% of the final script was left untouched, whereas 10% had human intervention.

为了避免这种情况，团队没有让GPT-2一次编写整个剧本。相反，研究人员将表演分为八个场景，每个场景不到5分钟。每个场景同时只包含两个角色之间的对话。此外，科学家有时会更改文本，例如更改AI逐行更改角色性别的段落，或者重复其最初的文本提示，直到程序散发出明智的散文。罗莎（Rosa）估计，最终剧本的90％保持不变，而10％是人为干预的。

Because the computer didn’t come up with the whole script itself, DeChant says he wouldn’t call the play “AI created.” He believes it will take about 15 years for the technology to be good enough to generate a complex and cohesive text like a theater play from beginning to end. But he thinks the experiment is still a nice way to show audiences what AI is currently able to do and “get people excited about it.”

由于计算机无法提供完整的脚本本身，因此DeChant表示他不会将这出戏称为“ AI创造”。他认为，这项技术要花大约15年的时间才能足以生成复杂而具有凝聚力的文字，就像戏剧从头到尾一样。但他认为，该实验仍然是向观众展示AI目前能够做什么并“使人们对此感到兴奋”的好方法。

Rosa says the fact that the program is generating line after line of mostly intelligible writing is remarkable in of itself. “Ten years ago, you could only generate a sentence that looked like a sentence if you were really lucky.”

罗莎（Rosa）表示，程序在逐行编写大部分可理解的代码这一事实本身就很引人注目。 “十年前，如果您真的很幸运，那么您只能生成一个看起来像一个句子的句子。”

Still, Košťák says the resulting text was tough for the actors to interpret, especially because the AI was not very good at staging the type of action and emotion needed to make a play work. “One of the actresses told me it was the most challenging work of her career,” he says.

尽管如此，科沙克表示，由此产生的文字对于演员来说很难解释，尤其是因为AI并不太擅长演绎戏剧作品所需要的动作和情感类型。他说：“一位女演员告诉我，这是她职业生涯中最具挑战性的工作。”

The play may be a challenge for the audience as well. Tomáš Studeník, a Czech entrepreneur and AI fan who came up with the idea for the project, notes a particular scene where a boy asks the robot to tell him a joke. The robot says that when the boy gets old and dies, and the boy’s children and grandchildren are also dead, he, the robot, will still be around. “It is funny and, at the same time, it sends shivers down your spine when a piece of metal is joking about your mortality,” Studeník says.

该剧对观众来说也是一个挑战。捷克企业家，人工智能迷TomášStudeník提出了该项目的构想，他提到了一个特殊的场景，一个男孩要求机器人给他讲个笑话。机器人说，当男孩长大并死亡时，男孩的孩子和孙子也都死了，机器人仍然会在身边。 Studeník说：“这很有趣，同时，当一块金属在嘲笑您的死亡率时，它会使您的脊椎发抖。”

The AI author was also a bit kinky. Košťák says sex and violence kept showing up in the script. In one of the scenes, an initial prompt about euthanasia ended up in a fight between characters about who had whose finger in whose anus.

AI的作者也有点怪癖。科沙克说，剧本中不断出现性暴力。在其中一个场景中，有关安乐死的最初提示最终出现在有关谁的手指在谁的肛门中的角色之间的斗争中。

Don’t blame the computer, Košťák says. The AI only reflects what people have been writing about on the internet, without any filter or shame. “It is like a child that listens to his parents at home talking about something that they wouldn’t talk about in public,” he says. “Then the kid goes to school and starts to talk about it openly.”

科什卡克说，不要怪电脑。 AI仅反映人们在互联网上正在写的内容，没有任何过滤条件或羞耻感。他说：“就像一个孩子在家里听父母说的话，他们不会在公共场合谈论他们。” “然后孩子上学，开始公开谈论它。”

If you want to judge the play’s suitability for yourself, it debuts today at 1 p.m. EST. There will be English subtitles.

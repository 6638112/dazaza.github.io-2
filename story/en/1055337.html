Jump in cancer diagnoses at 65 implies patients wait for Medicare: study
65次跳跃癌症诊断意味着患者等待Medicare：学习

2021-04-01 19:55:12

Analyzing a national cancer database, researchers find a bump in diagnoses at 65, suggesting that many wait for Medicare to kick in before they seek care.

分析国家癌症数据库，研究人员在65次诊断中发现了一个凹凸，这表明许多人在寻求护理之前等待医疗保险。

A couple of years ago, Joseph Shrager, MD, professor of cardiothoracic surgery at Stanford School of Medicine, noticed a statistical anomaly in his practice. It seemed that patients were diagnosed with lung cancer at a surprisingly higher rate at 65 years old than, say, at 64 or 66.

几年前，斯坦福医学院的Cardothoracic手术教授Joseph Shrager，MD在他的实践中注意到了统计异常。似乎患者在65岁时令人惊讶地诊断患有肺癌，而不是在64或66岁的比赛。

“There was no reason rates should differ much between the ages of 63 and 65,” Shrager said. He talked it over with his thoracic surgeon colleagues at Stanford who said they were seeing something similar. “If this were true, and patients were delaying screenings or treatments for cancer, it could impact their survival,” Shrager said. A quick exploratory analysis of their own practices showed a twofold increase in lung cancer surgeries in 65-year-old patients compared with 64-year-olds.

“如果这是真实的，并且患者延迟癌症的筛查或治疗，它可能会影响其生存，”Shreger说。与64岁的患者相比，对自己做法的快速探索性分析表明，65岁患者的肺癌手术中升高了两倍。

“We decided to explore this, and its broader implications, in a larger population,” Shrager said.

“我们决定探索这一点，并且在较大的人口中，它的更广泛的影响”塞格尔说。

In a follow-up study published March 29 in Cancer, the researchers found a substantial rise nationwide in new cancer diagnoses at 65 — not only for lung cancer but also for breast, colon and prostate cancer. The four are the most common cancers in the United States.

在3月29日发表的后续研究中，研究人员发现在65年的新癌症诊断中的全国性大幅上升 - 不仅适用于肺癌，还用于乳腺癌，结肠癌和前列腺癌。这四个是美国最常见的癌症。

“Essentially we showed there is a big jump in cancer diagnoses as people turn 65 and are thus Medicare-eligible,” said Shrager, the senior author of the study. The study’s lead author is Deven Patel, MD, a surgical resident at Cedars-Sinai Medical Center in Los Angeles who spent a year as a research fellow at Stanford. “This suggests that many people are delaying their care for financial reasons until they get health insurance through Medicare.”

“基本上我们展示了癌症诊断的巨大跳跃，因为人们转了65岁，因此符合医疗保险条件，”学习的高级作者Shreager表示。该研究的领先作者是MD，MD，洛杉矶的洛杉矶Cedars-Sinai医疗中心的外科居民，作为斯坦福等研究员。 “这表明许多人在通过Medicare获得健康保险之前推迟他们的财务原因。”

Researchers analyzed data from hundreds of thousands of patients who were 61-69 years old and were diagnosed with lung, breast, colon or prostate cancer from 2004 to 2016. The patients, identified from a national database, included 134,991 with lung cancer, 175,558 with breast cancer, 62,721 with colon cancer and 238,823 with prostate cancer.

研究人员分析了来自2004年至2016年61-69岁的数十万名患者的数千名患者的数据，并从2004年至2016年被诊断出患有肺癌，乳腺癌，结肠癌或前列腺癌。从国家数据库中确定的患者，包括134,991名，肺癌175,558乳腺癌，62,721，结肠癌和238,823带前列腺癌。

There was a greater jump in lung, breast, colon and prostate cancer diagnoses at the transition from 64 to 65 than at all other age transitions, the research showed. Lung cancer rates showed a consistent increase of 3-4% each year for people aged 61 to 64, then at 65 that percentage doubled. The increase was even more pronounced in people with colon cancer, which showed an annual growth rate of just 1-2% in the years leading up to Medicare eligibility, then jumped to nearly 15% at 65. In the years following age 65, diagnosis rates declined for all cancers, the study found.

研究显示，肺癌，乳腺癌，结肠和前列腺癌的诊断较大，从64升到65，这项研究显示。肺癌率每年均为61至64岁的人持续增长3-4％，然后在65岁时加倍。在结肠癌的人们中，增加的增加甚至更明显，这在导致Medicare资格的年份显示年增长率为1-2％，然后在65年跃升至近15％。在65岁以下的诊断期间研究发现，所有癌症都拒绝了。

The study showed that insured cancer patients (lung, breast, colon, prostate) older than 65 are more likely to undergo surgical intervention, and they had lower five-year cancer-specific mortality rates than did their younger uninsured counterparts.

该研究表明，超过65岁的被保险癌患者（肺，乳房，结肠，前列腺）更有可能进行手术干预，而且他们的癌症特异性较低的死亡率比其年轻的未经保险的同行更低。

“Collectively, these results demonstrate that Medicare eligibility, an event coincident with becoming 65 years old, is associated with a rise in early-stage cancer diagnoses and a resulting survival benefit,” the study concluded.

“集体，这些结果表明，医疗保险资格，一项与65岁重合一致的事件与早期癌症诊断的兴起和产生的生存效益相关，”这项研究结束。

The researchers proposed that a number of factors cause 61- to 64-year-olds to wait for medical treatments and screenings until Medicare eligibility kicks in.

研究人员建议，许多因素导致61至64岁，等待医疗治疗和筛查，直到Medicare资格踢进。

“These individuals often lack insurance as a result of early retirement, pre-existing conditions hindering renewal, the high cost of private insurance and other causes,” the study said, noting that 13-25% of this group of adults are uninsured or have a gap in medical coverage at some point preceding Medicare eligibility.

“这些人经常缺乏保险，由于早期退休，预先存在的条件妨碍续期，私人保险和其他原因的高成本，”这项研究表示，这一组成年人中的13-25％是未受保险的或拥有医疗保险资格前面的医学覆盖范围内的差距。

Previous research has shown that medical insurance is a strong predictor of receiving appropriate care, promoting earlier diagnosis and improved outcomes, the study noted.

研究表明，医疗保险是接受适当护理的强烈预测因素，促进早期的诊断和改善的结果，该研究指出。

“If you don’t get the right screening or prompt diagnosis you are going to have lower cure rates,” Shrager said. “This study underlines the important difference that some sort of Medicare expansion could make.”

“如果你没有得到正确的筛选或提示诊断，你将获得较低的治愈率，”Shreger说。 “本研究强调了某种医疗​​保险的扩张可以制造的重要差异。”

California close to regaining control of tailpipe emissions from EPA
加利福尼亚州即将从EPA手中恢复对尾气排放的控制

2022-02-17 08:18:32

The Environmental Protection Agency is on the verge of restoring California’s ability to set strict tailpipe emissions limits, according to news reports, while at the same time looking at adopting a version of the state’s stringent rules for heavy-duty trucks in an effort to cut smog-forming pollution.

据新闻报道，环境保护署即将恢复加利福尼亚州制定严格尾气排放限制的能力，同时考虑采用该州对重型卡车的严格规定，以减少烟雾形成的污染。

The EPA’s restoration of California’s Clean Air Act waiver reverses the Trump administration’s revocation, and the new truck rule aims to drastically reduce nitrogen dioxide emissions from trucks. Nitrogen dioxide pollution can cause and aggravate respiratory diseases, including asthma and certain kinds of cancer. It can also react with water and oxygen in the atmosphere to form acid rain. The last time the EPA updated emissions limits for heavy-duty trucks, in 2001, it cut nitrogen dioxide by 95 percent over 10 years. That caused nitrogen dioxide pollution to fall 40 percent nationwide.

环保署恢复加州《清洁空气法》豁免令推翻了特朗普政府的撤销，新的卡车规则旨在大幅减少卡车的二氧化氮排放。二氧化氮污染可导致并加重呼吸道疾病，包括哮喘和某些癌症。它还可以与大气中的水和氧气发生反应，形成酸雨。环保署上一次更新重型卡车排放限值是在2001年，它在10年内将二氧化氮减少了95%。这导致全国二氧化氮污染下降了40%。

California has long had the ability to set stricter emissions standards than the rest of the country because of its long history with harmful air pollution caused by cars and trucks. Under the Clean Air Act, other states can adopt the same stricter standards. Seventeen other states and the District of Columbia follow California’s emissions rules.

加州长期以来有能力制定比全国其他地区更严格的排放标准，因为它有着汽车和卡车造成有害空气污染的悠久历史。根据《清洁空气法》，其他州也可以采用同样更严格的标准。其他17个州和哥伦比亚特区遵循加利福尼亚州的排放规定。

When Donald Trump took office, automakers petitioned the EPA under Scott Pruitt to review fuel economy standards, and he obliged. Ultimately, Pruitt and his staff rewrote the rules and rescinded California’s waiver, prompting lawsuits.

唐纳德·特朗普上台后，汽车制造商在斯科特·普鲁伊特的领导下向环境保护局请愿，要求其审查燃油经济性标准，而他也履行了这一义务。最终，普鲁特和他的工作人员改写了规则，撤销了加州的豁免，引发了诉讼。

But scrutiny of the Trump-era rules revealed that in the process of rewriting the rules, EPA officials ignored the conclusions of their agency’s own scientists and relied on a flawed analysis of traffic fatality data to claim that cars and trucks with worse fuel economy were safer.

但对特朗普时代规则的审查显示，在重写规则的过程中，环保署官员忽视了该机构自己科学家的结论，并依靠对交通事故死亡数据的有缺陷的分析，声称燃油经济性较差的汽车和卡车更安全。

The California Air Resources Board is in the process of writing regulations that will phase out the sale of new gasoline and diesel cars and light trucks by 2035. Heavy-duty trucks will have to produce zero emissions by 2045. Twelve other states have announced that they’ll also follow California in requiring zero-emissions passenger cars and trucks by 2035.

加州空气资源委员会（California Air Resources Board）正在起草法规，将在2035年前逐步停止销售新的汽油、柴油汽车和轻型卡车。到2045年，重型卡车必须实现零排放。其他12个州已经宣布，他们也将效仿加利福尼亚州，在2035年之前要求零排放乘用车和卡车。

In December, the EPA announced its own more stringent fuel economy standards that would increase fuel economy by 5–10 percent annually between model years 2023–2026. Less than five years from now, fuel economy stickers on new vehicles will average about 40 mpg, a significant increase from today’s average of about 25 mpg. The move jibes with automakers’ increased interest in electric vehicles. While even the most efficient gas-powered cars and trucks struggle to exceed 40 mpg without hybrid assistance, even the least-efficient EV on the market today—the Porsche Taycan—hits an equivalent of 69 mpg.

12月，美国环保局宣布了自己更严格的燃油经济性标准，将在2023-2026车型年期间每年提高燃油经济性5%-10%。在不到五年的时间里，新车上的燃油经济性标签将平均达到每加仑约40英里，比今天的平均每加仑约25英里大幅增加。此举与汽车制造商对电动汽车的兴趣增加相一致。即使是效率最高的汽油动力汽车和卡车，在没有混合动力辅助的情况下也难以超过40英里/加仑，而即使是当今市场上效率最低的电动汽车，保时捷泰康也能达到69英里/加仑。

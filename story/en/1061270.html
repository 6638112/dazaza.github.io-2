A New Printer Uses Sawdust To Print Wooden Objects
一台新打印机使用锯末打印木制物体

2021-05-07 18:22:56

A new printer called Forust is using scrap wood to 3D print wooden objects that are as structurally sound as regular carved wood. Created by Andrew Jeffery and a team of researchers at Desktop Metal, the printer prints using fine sawdust that is formed into solid objects.

一个名为Doustust的新打印机正在使用废木头到3D打印木质物体，这些物体与结构上的常规雕刻木材一样。由Andrew Jeffery和台式金属的研究人员创建，打印机使用形成固体物体的精细锯末打印。

“Since we began in 2019, our focus has been on the mission of using sawdust to create wood products sustainably,” said Jeffery. “This R&D effort has led to today with Desktop Metal, launching our Forust process.”

“既然我们始于2019年，我们的重点一直在使用锯末的使命，以便可持续地创建木材产品，”杰弗瑞说。 “这个R＆amp; D努力今天带来了桌面金属，推出了我们的迫使流程。”

The printer works similarly to an inkjet printer and squirts a binding agent onto a layer of sawdust. Like most 3D printers, the object rises out of the bed of sawdust and then, when complete, can be sanded and finished like regular wood.

打印机与喷墨打印机类似地工作，并将绑定剂喷在锯末层上。与大多数3D打印机一样，物体升起锯末床，然后，完成后，可以像普通木材一样砂磨和完成。

“Two years ago we started looking into how we might be able to 3D print in new material,” he said. “Wood waste was one of the materials we started with early on and realized it could be repurposed and upcycled with 3D printing technology. From there, we focused on building out the process using wood byproducts in order to create real wood-crafted results. We formed the company really to save forests.”

“两年前，我们开始研究如何在新材料中3D打印，”他说。 “木材废物是我们早期开始的材料之一，实现了它可以重新培养和升高3D印刷技术。从那里，我们专注于使用木材副产品建立过程，以创造真正的木制品结果。我们形成了公司真正拯救了森林。“

Desktop Metal went public last August via an SPAC, and Forust is a spin-off product that will include an online sample design system for manufacturers to try the technology.

Desktop Metal于去年8月通过SPAC公开，Forust是一个衍生产品，包括制造商的在线样本设计系统，以试验该技术。

Tony Hsieh, an Internet entrepreneur and former CEO of Zappos, an Amazon company, died from injuries incurred during a house fire when visiting family in Connecticut on November 27, 2020. He was only 46. Needless to say, it was shocking to all of us in Silicon Valley.

互联网企业家，亚马逊公司Zappos的前首席执行官托尼·谢恩（Tony Hsieh）于2020年11月27日在康涅狄格州探望家人时在一场房屋大火中受伤身亡。他只有46岁。不用说，这震惊了所有人我们在硅谷。

So sad to hear of the loss of Tony Hsieh. I was Tony’s lawyer 23 years ago and have been his friend ever since. The Lobby will not be the same without him. We’ll miss you, Tony.

于是伤心地听到谢家华的损失。我23年前是托尼的律师，从那以后一直是他的朋友。没有他，大厅将不一样。托尼，我们会想念你的。

Tony’s passing has taken me down a memory lane to the earliest days of the Internet when we were all very young and innocent. Tony, then, only 23, had started work on LinkExchange, an early banner ad-exchange network that launched in early 1996. Sanjay Madan was a co-founder. Ali Partovi joined later. Tony met Alfred Lin at LinkExchange, and little did they know how close their lives would be in decades to come. Lin would go on to become the COO of Zappos & is now partner @Sequoia. Incidentally, Sequoia funded LinkExchange with an initial investment of $3 million.

托尼（Tony）的逝世使我走上了记忆之路，回到了互联网的最早时代，那时我们还很小，也很纯真。当时只有23岁的托尼（Tony）开始从事LinkExchange的工作，LinkExchange是一个早期的横幅广告交换网络，于1996年初启动。桑杰伊·马丹（Sanjay Madan）是联合创始人。 Ali Partovi随后加入。托尼（Tony）在LinkExchange上遇到了林（Alfred Lin），他们几乎不知道他们的生活在未来几十年将有多近。 Lin将继续担任Zappos的首席运营官，现在是合伙人@Sequoia。顺便提一句，红杉资本向LinkExchange出资300万美元。

In late 1999, Microsoft bought LinkExchange for $265 million. LinkExchange had some serious Internet talent. Partovi, Tony, Lin, and Scott Banister, for example. They are all stars in their own right. Banister is one of the most successful angel investors. The Partovi Brothers, too are Internet icons.

1999年底，微软以2.65亿美元的价格收购了LinkExchange。 LinkExchange有一些认真的互联网人才。例如，Partovi，Tony，Lin和Scott Banister。他们本身就是明星。 Banister是最成功的天使投资人之一。 Partovi兄弟也是Internet图标。

With winnings from the sale of LinkExchange, Tony and Lin started an incubator called Venture Frogs. If memory serves me right, it was in early 2000. It was also a restaurant, located at 1000 Van Ness Avenue in San Francisco. Tony’s mom Judy ran it. VentureFrogs were investors in many startups. The three most famous ones: Ask Jeeves, OpenTable & Zappos.

凭借出售LinkExchange的收益，Tony和Lin建立了一个名为Venture Frogs的孵化器。如果我没记错的话，那是在2000年初。它也是一家餐厅，位于旧金山Van Ness Avenue 1000号。托尼的妈妈朱迪（Judy）负责。 VentureFrogs是许多创业公司的投资者。最著名的三个：Ask Jeeves，OpenTable和Zappos。

Nick Swinmurn started Zappos in 1999, raised $500,000 in funding from Tony & Alfred. It was originally called Shoesite.com Tony later became CEO in 2000. Swinmurn left the company, in 2006. Amazon bought Zappos for $1.2 billion in 2009.

Nick Swinmurn于1999年创立Zappos，从Tony＆Alfred筹集了500,000美元的资金。 Tony后来于2000年成为CEO。Swinmurn于2006年离开公司。2009年，亚马逊以12亿美元的价格收购了Zappos。

"I believe that getting the culture right is the most important thing a company can do." - Tony Hsieh

“我相信正确的文化是公司可以做的最重要的事情。” -谢天华

I wrote about LinkExchange for Forbes, even though I never met Tony or Alfred till much later in life. My startup was housed in the same office as LinkExchange in SOMA through some strange twist of fate. I later got to know Tony socially through non-tech friends. Quiet, kind, quirky, but always open to the impossible.

我写过有关《福布斯》的LinkExchange的书，尽管直到后来我才见过托尼或阿尔弗雷德。我的创业公司因命运的某种奇怪转折而被安置在与SOMA中LinkExchange相同的办公室中。后来我通过非技术朋友在社交上认识了Tony。安静，友善，古怪，但总是对不可能的事物持开放态度。

With Tony’s passing, I feel something special has ended. I can’t put my finger on it. Maybe a certain innocent aspect of the early possibilities of the Internet. Maybe I feel the contrast of those days to a now that is more mercenary, less friendly, and more polarized. Whatever, without knowing Tony as well as I should, I mourn him deeply.

随着托尼（Tony）的逝世，我觉得特别的事情已经结束了。我无法将手指放在上面。互联网的早期可能性也许是无辜的。也许我觉得那些日子与现在的情况形成了对比，现在的情况是雇佣军，友善程度更低，两极化的。无论如何，在不尽我所能了解托尼的情况下，我深深地哀悼他。

“Zappos is a customer service company that just happens to sell shoes.” -Tony Hsieh

“ Zappos是一家客户服务公司，恰好卖鞋。” -谢国eh

Tony and Zappos’ biggest achievement was that it showed long before everyone else: you can build an Internet company anywhere. Unlike so many pundits and Twitterati, it didn’t take a pandemic for Tony to have that insight. Zappos was also an example of how tech companies could care about their customers and not treat them as data.

Tony和Zappos的最大成就是，它比其他所有人早就展示了：您可以在任何地方建立互联网公司。与众多专家和Twitterati不同，Tony无需大流行就能获得这种见解。 Zappos还是科技公司如何关心客户而不将其视为数据的一个例子。

Tony Hsieh might be the most original thinker I’ve ever been friends with. He questioned every assumption and shared everything he learned along the way. He genuinely delighted in making anyone and everyone happy. The earth has lost a beautifully weird and helpful person. RIP

谢华（Tony Hsieh）可能是我曾经与之成为朋友的最原始的思想家。他质疑每个假设，并分享了自己在此过程中学到的一切。他真诚地使每个人都高兴。地球迷失了一个奇特而有用的人。 RIP

My life’s biggest regret will be not talking to him about him, why holacracy, and, most importantly: why did he call the incubator, Venture Frogs. Every time I drive down 1000 Van Ness, I wonder why: Venture Frogs. Maybe that is why?

我一生中最大的遗憾是不会与他谈论他，为何狂喜，最重要的是：为什么他称他为孵化器Venture Frogs。每当我驶下1000辆Van Ness时，我都想知道为什么：Venture Frogs。也许那是为什么呢？

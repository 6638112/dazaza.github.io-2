NASA gives up on taking Mars’ temperature
美国宇航局放弃接受火星的温度

2021-01-15 20:57:46

Today, NASA announced that it was giving up on its attempts to place a temperature sensor several meters under the Martian surface. Part of the agency's InSight lander, the hardware was supposed to be placed deep enough to avoid the influence of Mars' weather, seasons, and daily temperature changes. But because of the unusual conditions at the landing site, the hardware never made it below the surface.

今天，NASA宣布放弃在火星表面以下几米处放置温度传感器的尝试。作为该机构InSight着陆器的一部分，该硬件应该放置得足够深，以避免火星的影响。天气，季节和每日温度变化。但是由于着陆点的异常情况，硬件从未将其放置在水面以下。

The InSight lander carried several instruments meant to provide a clearer picture of Martian geology. One of those instruments, the SEIS seismometer, has been successfully tracking marsquakes to provide a better perspective on Mars' structure and the local composition under the surface near the landing site. A second measures the wobbling of Mars' axis of rotation, which will be influenced by a combination of the red planet's composition and the gravitational influences of the rest of the Solar System.

InSight着陆器携带了几种仪器，旨在提供更清晰的火星地质图景。 SEIS地震仪就是其中一种仪器，它已经成功地跟踪了地震，以更好地了解火星。结构和着陆点附近地面下的局部组成。第二秒衡量了火星的摇摆旋转轴，这将受红色行星的成分和太阳系其余部分的引力影响的共同影响。

InSight's other major experiment is the Heat Flow and Physical Properties Package, or HP 3. While previous landers hadn't put any hardware deeper than about 20 centimeters, HP 3 was designed to operate several meters below the Martian surface. From there, the fluctuations on the surface would be somewhat averaged out, and HP 3 could measure the heat flow from the Martian interior to the surface. This would allow an estimate of the energy still left in the Martian core from a combination of its formation and radioactivity, a key element in understanding what geological activity might still be possible there.

InSight的另一项主要实验是“热流和物理特性包”或HP3。虽然以前的着陆器没有将任何硬件深于20厘米，但HP 3的设计目的是在火星表面以下几米处工作。从那里开始，表面上的波动将被平均化，HP 3可以测量从火星内部到表面的热流。这样就可以通过其形成和放射性的结合来估计火星岩心中仍然剩余的能量，这是了解那里仍可能进行何种地质活动的关键因素。

A 3-meter drill, however, wouldn't fit neatly on a lander. So HP 3 was to be delivered to its operating depth by a device called "the mole." The mole was designed to drive the hardware beneath the surface by leveraging the friction between it and the Martian soil; it would anchor itself, drive part of itself deeper using that anchor, and then inch forward and repeat the process.

但是，三米长的钻头不能整齐地放在着陆器上。因此，HP 3将通过称为“ the鼠”的设备达到其工作深度。鼠被设计成通过利用其与火星土壤之间的摩擦力将其驱动到地表以下。它会锚定自己，使用该锚定将自己的一部分深一些，然后向前推进并重复该过程。

There turned out to be a problem with this plan: the soil didn't allow enough friction for any of this to work. Now, after nearly a year of trying and a huge variety of troubleshooting techniques, the mission managers are accepting that the plan just isn't going to work. This comes after the latest attempt, which involved using a robotic scoop on InSight to pile soil on top of the mole and attempt to press it down to provide more leverage.

原来，该计划存在一个问题：土壤不允许足够的摩擦力来使任何这种方式起作用。现在，经过将近一年的尝试和各种各样的故障排除技术，任务经理开始接受该计划只是行之有效。这是在最近的尝试之后进行的，该尝试涉及在InSight上使用机器人铲将土壤堆在痣上，并尝试将其向下按压以提供更大的杠杆作用。

Obviously, things didn't work out because the properties of the soil at the InSight landing site were different from the expectations derived from elsewhere on Mars. So, in that sense, we learned something from the experience. And InSight's remaining instruments continue to operate as planned. But we're going to have to wait for another lander in order to take Mars' temperature.

显然，事情没有解决，因为InSight着陆点的土壤特性与火星其他地方的预期不同。因此，从这个意义上讲，我们从经验中学到了一些东西。 InSight的其余仪器将继续按计划运行。但是我们要等另一个着陆器才能带上火星温度。

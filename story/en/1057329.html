Uber Won’t Let California Drivers Set Their Own Prices Anymore
优步不会让加州司机设置自己的价格了

2021-04-10 03:19:26

Uber will no longer allow drivers in California to set their own prices after rider cancellations increased 117% over the past year, the latest change to Uber’s service following the passage of Proposition 22, which made ride-hailing drivers independent contractors.

优步将不再允许加州的司机在骑士取消后设定自己的价格，在过去一年增加117％，最新改变了优步的服务，在主题22之后，这使得乘坐驾驶司机独立承包商。

In a blog post, Uber said it is removing the ability of drivers to set their own fare multiplier because “over the last few months, 80% of riders matched with a driver with a fare multiplier above 1x declined the higher fare and did not re-request a ride on Uber.”

在一个博客文章中，优步表示，它正在删除司机设定自己的票价倍增器的能力，因为“过去几个月，80％的骑手与驾驶员匹配的驾驶员以上1倍下降较高票价并且没有重新 - 最新的骑行。“

Uber implemented the feature last year in an effort to show that drivers were independent enough to be classified as contractors instead of employees.

优步实施了去年的努力，以表明司机独立于归类为承包商而不是员工。

For riders, Uber will no longer show an estimated price range before requesting a ride, meaning the app will only show a single upfront price.

对于骑手，优步将不再在请求乘车之前显示估计的价格范围，这意味着该应用程序只会显示一个单一的前期价格。

Uber was reportedly considering nixing a feature that lets drivers in the state see destinations before they accept a ride, the San Francisco Chronicle reported, but that will remain in place for now.

据报道，据据据报道，优步曾经考虑过一个功能，让州司机在接受骑行之前看到目的地，旧金山纪事报告，但这将留在现在。

“We’ve seen that most riders are simply not taking trips with fare multipliers above 1x or without an upfront price. As California reopens, we need to make changes so all drivers can get more trip requests and riders can count on getting a ride when they request one,” the company said in the blog post.

“我们已经看到大多数骑手根本不在1倍以上的票价乘数旅行，或者没有前期价格。作为加州重新打开，我们需要做出改变，因此所有司机都可以获得更多的旅行请求，骑手可以依靠他们在博客帖子中举行乘车。“

The question of how much control ride-hailing companies have over their drivers was central to determining whether they are employees under a California law called AB-5 that was passed in 2019. The law was an effort to make app-based drivers employees, but Uber argued in court it was exempt because drivers could set their own prices and see destinations. But with the passage of Prop. 22, the recently passed state ballot measure keeping gig workers independent contractors, Uber no longer needs to demonstrate how little control it has over drivers.

乘车公司对其司机有多少控制权的问题是确定他们是否是2019年通过的AB-5所在的加州法律的雇员。法律是制定基于应用程序的司机员工的努力，但是优步在法庭中争论它是豁免，因为司机可以设定自己的价格并查看目的地。但随着道具的通过.22，最近通过的国家投票措施保留了演出工人独立承包商，优步不再需要展示它对司机的控制程度较少。

Green recovery must end the reign of GDP, argue Cambridge and UN economists
剑桥大学和联合国经济学家认为，绿色复苏必须结束国内生产总值的统治

2020-12-14 23:40:53

Our fixation with Gross Domestic Product for over half a century as the primary indicator of economic health has rendered nature "invisible" from national finances, intensifying the biosphere's destruction by omitting its value from the systems that govern us. This is according to leading economists from Cambridge University and the United Nations, who meet on Tuesday 15 December to help launch a "statistical standard" that allows governments and banks to calculate the worth of natural "dividends": from fish stocks and carbon 'sinks' to reduced health burdens from purified air.

半个多世纪以来，我们一直将国内生产总值作为经济健康的主要指标，这使自然变得“无形”。国家财政的资助，通过从管理我们的体系中忽略其价值来加剧对生物圈的破坏。根据剑桥大学和联合国的主要经济学家的说法，他们于12月15日星期二举行会议，以帮助启动“统计标准”。从而使政府和银行能够从鱼类种群和碳汇中计算自然股息的价值。减少净化空气带来的健康负担。

Almost a decade in the making, the new statistical approach, called "Ecosystem Accounting," had its final consultation on the first of this month, and will go before the UN General Assembly next year with hopes of ratification as the global standard for measuring how the natural world underpins national economies.

近十年来，这种新的统计方法称为＆＃34; Ecosystem Accounting，＆＃34;。在本月初进行了最终磋商，并将于明年参加联合国大会，希望获得批准，成为衡量自然世界如何支撑国民经济的全球标准。

"A focus on GDP without proper regard for environmental degradation or inequality has been a disaster for global ecosystems and undermined ," said Prof Diane Coyle, who leads "Beyond GDP' research at Cambridge's Bennett Institute for Public Policy and is a key speaker at Tuesday's public event.

＆＃34;在没有适当考虑环境恶化或不平等的情况下，仅关注GDP已经成为全球生态系统的灾难，并且遭到破坏，＆＃34; Diane Coyle教授说，他领导着“超越GDP”在剑桥大学贝内特公共政策研究所进行研究，并在星期二的公共活动中担任重要发言人。

"Statistics are the lens through which we see the world, but they have made nature invisible to policymakers. Twenty-first century progress cannot be measured using 20th-century statistics," she said.

＆＃34;统计数据是我们观察世界的镜头，但它们使决策者看不到自然。使用20世纪的统计数据无法衡量21世纪的进步，＆＃34;她说。

While many talk of the need to 'build back better' from the ravages of COVID-19, we cannot recover better without better information to guide us, says United Nations Chief Economist Elliot Harris, who will also speak at the Cambridge-hosted event.

尽管许多人谈到有必要“更好地重建”。联合国首席经济学家埃利奥特·哈里斯（Elliot Harris）表示，如果没有更好的信息来指导我们，那么从COVID-19的惨剧中我们将无法更好地康复，他还将在剑桥主办的活动中发言。

"It is we moved beyond GDP and measured our wealth and success with tools that recognize the value of nature and people. The developments to our System of Environmental Economic Accounting are a giant leap in the right direction," Harris said.

＆＃34;这是我们超越了GDP，并通过承认自然和人的价值的工具来衡量我们的财富和成功。我们的环境经济核算体系的发展是朝着正确方向的巨大飞跃，＆＃34;哈里斯说。

As part of a global team, economists from Cambridge's Bennett Institute for Public Policy such as Dr. Matthew Agarwala have been working with the UN to develop aspects of the new accounting methods. With his colleague Dimitri Zenghelis, Agarwala has written a guide for treasuries and central banks that the UN will roll out as a training program.

作为全球团队的一部分，来自剑桥市贝内特公共政策研究所的经济学家马修·阿加瓦拉（Matthew Agarwala）博士一直在与联合国合作开发新会计方法的各个方面。阿加瓦拉（Agarwala）与他的同事迪米特里（Dimitri Zenghelis）一起，为国库和中央银行撰写了指南，联合国将其作为培训计划推广。

"Some of the ways we currently value nature, what we term 'natural capital," are just absurd," said Agarwala. "Most parks in the UK, including huge parks in major cities, have an asset value of £1, because they can't be sold.

＆＃34;我们当前评价自然的一些方式，我们称之为自然资本，＆＃34;只是荒唐的，＆＃34;阿加瓦拉说。 ＆nbsp;＆nbsp;＆nbsp;＆nbsp;＆nbsp;＆nbsp;＆nbsp;＆nbsp;＆nbsp;＆nbsp;＆nbsp;＆nbsp;＆nbsp;＆nbsp;＆nbsp;＆nbsp

"Local Authorities have a balance sheet with a £1 asset that costs many thousands in annual upkeep. But this ignores revenues from higher property values in the vicinity. Even worse, it ignores the value of outdoor recreation, cleaner air, and the greatly reduced impact on local health services this creates.

＆＃34;地方当局的资产负债表上有£1的资产，每年的维护费用为数千。但这忽略了附近较高房地产价值的收入。更糟糕的是，它忽略了户外娱乐，更清洁的空气以及由此产生的对当地医疗服务的影响大大减少的价值。

"We now have the framework for putting that information into everyday economic decisions and scaling it up to the national level," he said.

＆＃34;现在，我们有了将这些信息纳入日常经济决策并将其扩展到全国范围的框架，＆＃34;他说。

The Bennett Institute also works closely with the UK's Office for National Statistics, early adopters of Ecosystems Accounting during its previous "experimental" phase. ONS work published last year used these methods to reveal the startling value of nature.

贝内特研究所（Bennett Institute）还与英国国家统计局（Office for National National Office）密切合作，在此之前的“实验性”研究期间，他们都是生态系统会计的早期采用者。相。去年发表的ONS工作使用这些方法揭示了自然的惊人价值。

"Shading and cooling services" provided by greenery and waterways were valued at almost a quarter of a billion pounds a year in the UK through improved worker productivity and air-conditioning energy savings alone.

＆＃34;阴影和冷却服务＆＃34;在英国，仅通过提高工人的生产力和节省空调节能，每年由绿色植物和水道提供的价值就近十亿英镑的四分之一。

Just the and rivers in urban areas saved almost £163m annually in healthcare costs, and urban woodland was estimated to be worth £89m a year through carbon removal. Recreation spent in nature just in urban areas was valued at some £2.5 billion a year in the UK.

仅城市地区的河流和河流每年就节省了近1.63亿英镑的医疗费用，而城市林地通过除碳每年估计价值8900万英镑。在英国，仅在城市地区的自然娱乐支出每年就价值约25亿英镑。

"We need statistics that can guide us through the new challenges we're facing—biodiversity loss, inequality, climate change, and automation," said Agarwala. "We are only just scratching the surface of what these accounting methods can reveal."

＆＃34;我们需要统计数据来指导我们应对我们所面临的新挑战-生物多样性丧失，不平等，气候变化和自动化，＆＃34;阿加瓦拉说。 ＆＃34;我们只是在摸索这些会计方法可以揭示的内容。

Two Cambridge graduates at the Central Statistical Office—the precursor to the ONS—James Meade (later a University professor) and Richard Stone, laid the foundations for GDP as we know it: essentially, the value of things and services produced by a given country.

中央统计局的两名剑桥毕业生-ONS的前身-詹姆斯·米德（James Meade，后来成为大学教授）和理查德·斯通（Richard Stone），为我们所知道的GDP奠定了基础：从本质上讲，一个特定国家生产的事物和服务的价值。

But Cambridge is also home to Prof Sir Partha Dasgupta, considered the father of the modern movement to knock GDP from its pedestal and infuse economics with the worth of life on Earth: from nature to the value of human connection.

但是，剑桥还是Partha Dasgupta爵士的故乡，他被认为是现代运动之父，旨在将GDP从其基础上打破，并将经济学与地球上的生命价值相融合：从自然到人与人之间的联系。

Prof Dasgupta will also be speaking at the Ecosystem Accounting event, discussing his landmark commission from the UK Treasury to investigate the economic benefits of global biodiversity—and the costs of its rapid loss.

达斯古普塔教授还将在“生态系统会计”活动上发言，讨论他从英国财政部获得的具有里程碑意义的委员会，以调查全球生物多样性的经济利益及其迅速丧失的成本。

"Ecosystem services are simply absent from most national statistics," he said. "Vast intellectual energy is given to estimating GDP, but there is little data on the biosphere's capacity to meet human demand for natural goods and services."

＆＃34;大多数国家统计资料中都根本没有生态系统服务，＆＃34;他说。 ＆nbsp;＆nbsp;＆nbsp;＆nbsp;＆nbsp;＆nbsp;＆nbsp;＆nbsp;＆nbsp;＆nbsp;＆nbsp;＆nbsp;＆nbsp;＆nbsp;＆nbsp;＆nbsp;＆nbsp;＆nbsp;＆nbsp;＆nbsp;＆nbsp;＆nbsp;＆nbsp;＆nbsp;＆nbsp;＆nbsp;＆nbsp;＆nbsp;＆nbsp;＆nbsp;＆nbsp;＆nbsp;＆nbsp;＆nbsp;＆nbsp;＆nbsp;＆nbsp;＆nbsp;＆nbsp;＆nbsp;＆nbsp;＆nbsp;＆nbsp;＆nbsp;＆nbsp;

Dasgupta describes natural capital as a necessary step towards the creation of "inclusive wealth," in which economics accounts for everything from health and skills to the value of communities—all fundamental to productivity, and all currently gaping holes in national balance sheets.

达斯古普塔（Dasgupta）将自然资本描述为创造“包容性财富”的必要步骤。从健康和技能到社区价值，经济学都应运而生，这一切对生产力至关重要，而目前在国家资产负债表中所有空缺都是空缺。

At the event, Prof Coyle will discuss the major Bennett Institute report she produced with Agarwala called "Building Forward: Investing in a resilient recovery." Published last month, it outlines how inclusive wealth could be developed in response to the pandemic and the UK's longstanding "productivity puzzle."

在活动中，Coyle教授将讨论她与Agarwala共同撰写的Bennett研究所的主要报告，该报告名为“ Building Forward：投资于有复原力的复苏”。该指南于上个月发布，概述了如何应对流行病和英国长期以来的“生产力难题”来发展包容性财富。

"Gaps in economic measurement have contributed to chronic underinvestment in natural and social capital," said Coyle. "Assets such as public green space or personal networks do not have a market price and so are not counted in economic statistics."

＆＃34;经济衡量标准的差距导致了对自然和社会资本的长期投资不足，＆＃34;科伊尔说。 ＆＃34;诸如公共绿地或个人网络之类的资产没有市场价格，因此也不计入经济统计数据中。

This omission of life's fundamentals in national economic calculations is not just a missed opportunity for governments, but a massive risk. "The halls of power have yet to grasp how vital it is to include natural capital in the economy," added Agarwala. "Look at the precipitous falls in fossil fuel value, and that's just one small part.

国民经济计算中生命基础的这种遗漏不仅是政府错失的机会，而且是巨大的风险。 ＆＃34;权力大厅尚未掌握将自然资本纳入经济的重要性，＆＃34;添加了Agarwala。看看化石燃料价格的急剧下降，那只是一小部分。

"The extreme human and economic cost of the pandemic arise from a failure to manage natural capital. It has proved far more costly than it would have been to protect wild habitats and biodiversity in the first place to avoid such zoonotic spillover."

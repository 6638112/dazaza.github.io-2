报道称，苹果大楼搜索引擎将与谷歌展开竞争

2020-10-28 20:53:35

I am a consumer tech expert writing about Windows, PCs, laptops, Mac, broadband and more.

我是一名消费者技术专家，撰写关于Windows、PC、笔记本电脑、Mac、宽带等的文章。

Google’s dominance of the search engine landscape could be under threat with reports that Apple is ramping up its own search engine.

谷歌在搜索引擎领域的主导地位可能会受到威胁，因为有报道称，苹果正在加强自己的搜索引擎。

The Financial Times reports that Apple is planning to attack Google, just as the company faces intense scrutiny from antitrust authorities, who are also threatening to curb the search leader’s grip on the industry.

英国“金融时报”报道称，苹果正计划攻击谷歌(Google)，与此同时，该公司正面临反垄断机构的严格审查，后者也威胁要遏制这家搜索领军企业对该行业的控制。

The FT notes that Apple has quietly beefed up its own search offering in iOS 14, but the default search engine continues to be Google - something for which the search giant pays Apple between $8-$12 billion per year, according to a recent report in The New York Times.

英国“金融时报”指出，苹果在iOS 14中悄悄地加强了自己的搜索服务，但默认的搜索引擎仍然是谷歌-根据“纽约时报”最近的一篇报道，这家搜索巨头每年向苹果支付80-120亿美元。

That is just one of the factors being considered by antitrust authorities, who are concerned that such deals create “continuous and self-reinforcing monopolies in multiple markets”.

这只是反垄断机构正在考虑的因素之一，他们担心此类交易会在“多个市场造成持续的、自我强化的垄断”。

There are increasing signs that Apple is ready to launch a pre-emptive strike before the regulators get their teeth into Google, the FT claims.

英国“金融时报”称，越来越多的迹象表明，苹果准备在监管机构对谷歌采取行动之前发动先发制人的打击。

The company hired Google’s head of search, John Giannnandrea, over two years ago and has been openly recruiting search engineers since, to the point where Apple has a “credible team” to “build a more general search engine”, according to a former Google engineering chief, Bill Coughran, who spoke to the FT.

谷歌前工程主管比尔·考夫兰(Bill Coughran)向英国“金融时报”表示，该公司在两年多前聘请了谷歌搜索主管约翰·詹南德雷亚(John Giannnandrea)，此后一直在公开招聘搜索工程师，以至于苹果拥有一支“可信的团队”来“打造一个更通用的搜索引擎”。

There are signs that search engine may already be operational, with webmasters reporting increased activity from Applebot, the company’s web crawler, which explores the web in search of new sites and content to index.

有迹象表明，搜索引擎可能已经开始运行，网站管理员报告称，该公司的网络爬虫程序Applebot的活跃度增加，该程序在网络上搜索要索引的新网站和内容。

Apple is one of the few companies that has the resources to take on Google. With a market cap of over $2 trillion and around $200 billion of cash in hand, the company has the ability to not only turn away the money received from Google but to invest in the server infrastructure required to create a competitive search engine.

苹果是少数几家有资源挑战谷歌的公司之一。拥有超过2万亿美元的市值和约2000亿美元的现金在手，该公司不仅有能力拒绝从谷歌获得的资金，而且有能力投资于创建一个有竞争力的搜索引擎所需的服务器基础设施。

An Apple search engine would almost certainly have one massive selling point: increased user privacy.

苹果搜索引擎几乎肯定会有一个巨大的卖点：增加用户隐私。

Apple has made great efforts to distance itself from rivals in recent years by insisting that “privacy is a fundamental human right” and “one of our core values”. Like rivals such as DuckDuckGo, Apple may attempt to build a search engine that doesn’t store personal information nor track users across the web.

近年来，苹果公司通过坚持“隐私是一项基本人权”和“我们的核心价值观之一”，努力与竞争对手保持距离。与DuckDuckGo等竞争对手一样，苹果可能会尝试建立一个搜索引擎，既不存储个人信息，也不跟踪整个网络的用户。

That would be easier for Apple because, unlike Google, it’s not reliant on advertising income, which benefits from personalized data with which to target users.

这对苹果来说会更容易，因为与谷歌不同的是，它不依赖广告收入，而广告收入受益于个性化的数据，用来瞄准用户。

The big question is whether Apple would look to open up a search engine to the world at large or restrict its usage to owners of Apple devices or browsers, making it another reason to buy iPhones, iPads and computers. While Safari is used for a quarter of all mobile browsing sessions, according to NetMarketShare, its desktop share is tiny, with less than 4% market share, almost all of which is on the Mac platform.

最大的问题是，苹果是会寻求向整个世界开放搜索引擎，还是会限制其仅供苹果设备或浏览器的用户使用，从而成为购买iPhone、iPad和电脑的另一个原因。根据NetMarketShare的数据，虽然Safari在所有移动浏览会话中占四分之一，但其桌面份额很小，市场份额不到4%，几乎所有的市场份额都在Mac平台上。

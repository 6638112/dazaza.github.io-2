Fuck Amazon Vine
他妈的亚马逊藤

2020-12-22 06:39:17

Thingamagig is now live on Amazon  as of a couple of weeks ago. In order to get some traction and reviews (to boost organic SEO, apparently?), we decided to sign up for Amazon Vine and provide 15 free units to “qualified” reviewers.

几周前，Thingamagig现在已在Amazon上发布。为了获得一些关注和评论（显然是为了提高有机搜索引擎优化？），我们决定注册Amazon Vine，并为“合格”审阅者提供15个免费单元。

In one instance, Amazon gave a free Thingamagig to a woman who doesn’t even play guitar . This woman foisted it on her children who didn’t want it, didn’t pay for it, and didn’t understand it. The son rejected it outright without trying it and the daughter, predictably, trashed it for dubious reasons on ~5 mins use.

有一次，亚马逊向一位甚至不弹吉他的女性免费赠送了Thingamagig。这个女人把它强加给了她的孩子，这些孩子不想要它，不付钱，也不了解它。儿子没有尝试就直接拒绝了它，而女儿在使用约5分钟时出于可疑的原因将其丢弃了。

This Viner then violated Amazon Community policy rules by writing a review about someone else’s  experience.

然后，该Viner通过撰写有关他人体验的评论违反了Amazon Community Policy规则。

This is a huge problem in speciality categories: A non-golfer can Vine a club for a husband/wife who didn’t ask for it. A non-painter can Vine an easel for a friend. A non-hunter can Vine a scope for a hunter.  Etc. These purchases are highly personal choices.

这在专业类别中是一个巨大的问题：非高尔夫球手可以为不需要的丈夫/妻子藤蔓俱乐部。非画家可以为朋友放一个画架。非猎人可以为猎人放下望远镜。等等。这些购买是高度个人选择。

But it’s also a problem in general categories. Do you really know whether you like a TV set, phone or pair of headphones right away? If you’re not the full end user, your assessment is somewhat illegitimate based on limited (or even zero) use.

但这也是一般类别的问题。您真的知道您是马上喜欢电视机，电话还是耳机吗？如果您不是最终用户，则基于有限（甚至零使用）的使用，您的评估有些不合理。

Obviously, this practice should be banned and violators subject to removal from the program, but Amazon doesn’t care. Both Seller support nor Community support declined to take any action in this case.*

显然，应禁止这种做法，违反者应从该计划中删除，但Amazon不在乎。在这种情况下，卖方支持和社区支持均拒绝采取任何措施。*

The psychological profile of someone who saw a free thing and said “Sure, I’ll take it” is very, VERY different than someone who saw the product, grokked it, and then wanted it enough to pay actual $ for it. This is always true to some degree for everything, but is especially the case for Thingamagig, which is a totally new experience never seen before.

看到免费产品并说“当然，我会接受”的人的心理特征与看到产品，摸索它，然后想要它足以支付实际价格的人非常不同。在某种程度上，这一切都是正确的，但是Thingamagig尤其如此，这是前所未有的全新体验。

This entire “review” is lazy, rooted in the psychology described above. The second paragraph basically says “I want more controls” and then “There are too many controls”. (The skill’s screen is fully tappable. No voice required.)

整个“评论”是懒惰的，植根于上述心理学。第二段基本上说“我想要更多控件”，然后是“控件太多”。 （该技能的屏幕可完全轻按。无需语音。）

The reviewer describes setup, voices a desire for more physical controls, then says maybe there are other options. That is, there is barely a crumb of description about the actual experience of playing Thingamagig. A thinner, less meaty review is hard to imagine. Reading this, I get the feeling she checked the checkbox required by Vine to leave a review...

审阅者描述了设置，表达了对更多物理控件的需求，然后说也许还有其他选择。也就是说，对于玩Thingamagig的实际经验几乎没有任何描述。难以想象的是，更薄，更少肉的评论。阅读此书后，我感觉到她选中了Vine所需的复选框以进行评论...

…which is not surprising considering the person spent less than 8 minutes  using it.

……考虑到该人用了不到8分钟的时间，这不足为奇。

Obviously we can’t make someone like Thingamagig. And plenty of people won’t. That’s fine. But this person was never truly motivated to like it in the first place . This is a recipe not just for negative reviews, but also for poor  reviews; reviews that don’t offer much insight, reviews that don’t “dig in”.

显然，我们无法像Thingamagig这样的人。很多人不会。没关系。但是，这个人从一开始就从来没有真正地喜欢它。这不仅是负面评论的食谱，也是不良评论的秘诀;评论不多，洞察力不强的评论。

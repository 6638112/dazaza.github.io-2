Facebook lowers priority for moderating hate speech against some groups
Facebook降低了缓和针对某些团体的仇恨言论的优先权

2020-12-21 18:24:54

Facebook ranks deleting anti-Black and 'most harmful' hate speech over comments about white people and men

Facebook对删除反黑人和“最有害”的排名讨厌对白人和男人的评论讲话

Facebook puts a higher priority on detecting and deleting racist slurs and hate speech against Black people, Muslims, Jews, the LGBTQ community and people of more than one race than on statements such as “White people are stupid” and “Men are pigs.”

与发现诸如``白人是愚蠢的''和``人是猪的''这样的说法相比，Facebook在检测和删除针对黑人，穆斯林，犹太人，LGBTQ社区和一个以上种族的人的种族歧视和仇恨言论方面具有更高的优先权。

The company said Thursday its automated moderation systems are being retrained to focus on hate speech targeting historically marginalized and oppressed groups, which “can be the most harmful.”

该公司周四表示，对其自动审核系统进行了重新培训，以专注于针对历史上处于边缘地位和被压迫的群体的仇恨言论，这可能是最有害的。

“We have focused our technology on finding the hate speech that users and experts tell us is the most serious,” Facebook spokesperson Sally Aldous said in a statement.

Facebook发言人萨利·阿尔多斯（Sally Aldous）在一份声明中说：“我们将技术重点放在寻找用户和专家告诉我们的最严重的仇恨言论上。”

The policy shift acknowledges years of complaints that Facebook’s hate speech policies disproportionately stifle Black users while failing to protect them from racially motivated harassment.

政策转变承认了多年的抱怨，即Facebook的仇恨言论政策严重扼杀了黑人用户，却未能保护他们免受出于种族动机的骚扰。

Facebook has been reckoning with its role in systemic racism in the aftermath of George Floyd’s death. An internal civil rights audit faulted Facebook for prioritizing free expression over nondiscrimination. The audit was released in July as civil rights groups led a massive boycott of Facebook, and nearly 1,000 companies pulled millions of dollars in advertising to protest the spread of hate speech, violent threats and misinformation on Facebook's platforms.

在乔治·弗洛伊德（George Floyd）死后，Facebook一直在考虑其在系统种族主义中的作用。内部民权审计指责Facebook认为自由表达优先于非歧视。这项审计于7月份发布，当时民权组织大规模抵制Facebook，近1000家公司撤出数百万美元的广告，以抗议仇恨言论，暴力威胁和错误信息在Facebook平台上的传播。

"Over the past year, we’ve also updated our policies to catch more implicit hate speech, such as content depicting Blackface, stereotypes about Jewish people controlling the world and banned Holocaust denial," Aldous said. "Thanks to significant investments in our technology, we proactively detect 95% of the content we remove, and we continue to improve how we enforce our rules as hate speech evolves over time."

Facebook bans hate speech based on race, gender and other characteristics. It relies on a set of rules called "Community Standards” to guide decisions about what violates that ban. The standards are enforced by computer algorithms and human moderators.

Facebook禁止基于种族，性别和其他特征的仇恨言论。它依赖于称为“社区标准”的一组规则来指导有关违反该禁令的决定。该标准由计算机算法和人工主持人实施。

According to Facebook’s hate speech policy, derogatory statements about men and white people are treated the same as anti-Semitic statements or racial epithets.

根据Facebook的仇恨言论政策，关于男性和白人的贬损言论与反犹太言论或种族ra义一样。

For years, civil rights activists have lobbied Facebook to change its policy of protecting all groups equally. Neil Potts, public policy director at Facebook, told USA TODAY last year that applying more "nuanced" rules to the daily tidal wave of content rushing through Facebook and its other apps would be challenging.

多年来，民权主义者一直游说Facebook改变其平等保护所有群体的政策。 Facebook公共政策总监尼尔·波茨（Neil Potts）去年对《今日美国》（USA TODAY）表示，申请更多＆＃34; nuanced＆＃34;每天涌入Facebook及其其他应用程序的内容的潮汐规则将具有挑战性。

Facebook still considers statements about men and white people to be in violation of its hate speech policy, and users can still report these statements, but the company’s algorithms will no longer automatically flag and delete them, resulting in about 10,000 fewer posts being removed each day, Facebook said.

Facebook仍然认为有关男女的言论违反了其仇恨言论政策，用户仍可以举报这些言论，但该公司的算法将不再自动标记和删除，导致每天删除的帖子减少约10,000 ，Facebook说。

The changes to Facebook’s automated moderation systems were first reported by The Washington Post.

Facebook的自动审核系统的更改最早由《华盛顿邮报》报道。

Black users say hate speech policies and content moderation systems formulated by a company built by and dominated by white men fail the very people Facebook claims it's trying to protect. But, they  told USA TODAY, they have no choice but to stick with Facebook and Instagram, both critical lifelines to their communities.

黑人用户说，由白人建立并由白人主导的公司制定的仇恨言论政策和内容审核系统未能使Facebook声称要保护的人们感到失望。但是，他们告诉《今日美国》，他们别无选择，只能坚持使用Facebook和Instagram，这两个都是他们社区的重要生命线。

Movements on social media have helped put the deaths of Black Americans by police officers on the public agenda, along with racial disparities in employment, health and other  areas, they say.

他们说，社交媒体上的运动已将警官的黑人美国人的死亡以及就业，卫生和其他领域的种族差距列为公共议程。

To elude bans when talking about racism, Black users said they resort to using a white avatar, digital slang such as "wypipo" or "huite" or a cloud emoji as phonetic or visual stand-ins for white people. They operate under aliases and maintain backup accounts to avoid losing content and access to their community. They've developed a buddy system to alert friends and followers when someone has been sent to Facebook jail, sharing the news of the suspension and the posts that put them there.

One of the challenges, Black users said, is the dearth of underrepresented minorities at Facebook, particularly in positions of influence, after years of failed pledges to close the racial gap.

布莱克用户说，挑战之一是Facebook少数群体的代表性不足，尤其是在影响力职位上，这是在多年未能消除种族鸿沟的承诺之后。

From 2013 to 2018, the company failed to meaningfully increase the number of employees from underrepresented groups in its U.S. workforce, according to a  USA TODAY analysis. As its U.S. employee base grew more than sixfold to 27,705, Facebook’s ranks grew by fewer than 1,000 Black people, according to the EEO-1 reports the company files each year with the federal government. In 2013, Facebook had zero Black executives. In 2018, the most recent data available, it had 32, 3% of all executives. In that same time frame, the number of Facebook executives increased more than sixfold, according to the 2018 EEO-1 report, the most recent data available during the COVID-19 pandemic.

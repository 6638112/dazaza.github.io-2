Which species: All three 17-year species, Magicicada septendecim, Magicicada cassini and Magicicada septendecula. How to tell the difference between the species.

哪个物种：三个17年种，Magicicada septendecim，Magicicada cassini和Magicicada septendecula。如何分辨物种之间的差异。

Typically beginning in mid-May and ending in late June. These cicadas will begin to emerge approximately when the soil 8″ beneath the ground reaches 64 degrees Fahrenheit. A nice, warm rain will often trigger an emergence. Back in 2004, people began reporting emergences around May, 13th.

通常从5月中旬开始，到6月下旬结束。当地下8英寸的土壤达到华氏64度时，这些蝉将开始出现。一场温暖的大雨经常会触发暴雨。早在2004年，人们就开始在5月13日左右报告紧急情况。

Why do they stay underground for 17-years? The prevailing research suggests they’ve evolved a long, 17-year lifecycle to avoid predators that can sync up with their lifecycle & emergence. Why are there so many?! Research suggests that their huge numbers allow them to overwhelm predators, so enough of them will live on to breed and perpetuate the brood.

他们为什么在地下呆了17年？主流研究表明，他们进化了漫长的17年生命周期，以避免掠食者可以与其生命周期保持同步。紧急情况。为什么有那么多？！研究表明，它们的数量庞大，使它们不堪重负，因此足够多的人将继续繁殖和繁殖。

Here’s something I wrote in 1996 to chronicle the Brood II cicada emergence in 1996. It’s probably meant to be semi humorous.

这是我在1996年写的，记载了1996年Brood II蝉的出现。这可能是半幽默的。

Sunday, May 19th: Metuchen, New Jersey; I found the first desiccated cicada nymph exoskeleton on my patio. My cat disappears.

5月19日，星期日：新泽西州梅图兴；我在露台上发现了第一个干燥的蝉若虫外骨骼。我的猫不见了。

Tuesday, May 21st: I found about 40 nymph exoskeletons on my patio, a pine tree, and a maple tree. I also spotted an adult climbing the maple and two crippled adults rolling about the base of the tree.

5月21日，星期二：我在露台上发现了大约40个若虫外骨骼，一棵松树和一棵枫树。我还发现一个成年人爬上枫树，两个残废的成年人在树的底部滚动。

Wednesday, May 22nd: Bonanza! I found about 500 adults perched on just about everything in my yard: trees, patio furniture, the foundation of my home, the garden hose, garbage cans, the missing cat’s water dish, my hair…just plain everywhere! Gruesome!  Saturday, May 25th: Avenel, New Jersey; Party at the Ritzow’s. Literally, hundreds of adult cicadas perched high above in oak trees sneer at decadent humans sipping martini’s, playing croquet. Bourgeois homo sapiens…bah humbug!

5月22日，星期三：富矿！我发现大约有500位成年人栖息在院子里几乎所有东西上：树木，露台家具，我的房屋的地基，花园软管，垃圾桶，失踪的猫的水盘，我的头发……随处可见！太可怕了！ 5月25日，星期六：新泽西州阿弗内尔；在Ritzow的聚会。从字面上看，数百只成年蝉栖息在高高的橡树上，嘲笑at腐的人着马提尼酒，打槌球。资产阶级智人………骗子！

Thursday, May 30th: Metuchen, New Jersey; Still no sign of the cat. Sitting outside on my patio around 8:30 pm I hear a “snap”, “crackle” and “popping” sound. Rice Crispies? No. More like cicadas nymphs crawling out of their holes and on to my garden wall to molt into adulthood. Not the loveliest sight.

5月30日，星期四：新泽西州梅图兴；仍然没有猫的迹象。晚上8:30左右坐在户外露台上，我听到“ s”，“ cra”和“ and”的声音。大米脆皮？不，更像蝉仙女从洞里爬出来，爬到我的花园墙上，蜕变成了成年。不是最可爱的景象。

Saturday, June 1st: Westfield, New Jersey; Dave Wilson and Claire Adas’ wedding. A beautiful ceremony and reception, with the exception of the 9000 uninvited cicadas: crawling up people’s legs, crunching underfoot, landing in refreshments…a moment to cherish and remember!

6月1日，星期六：新泽西州韦斯特菲尔德；戴夫·威尔逊（Dave Wilson）和克莱尔·阿达斯（Claire Adas）的婚礼。除了9000只不请自来的蝉之外，还有漂亮的仪式和招待会：爬上人们的双腿，嘎吱嘎吱地踩在脚下，小憩片刻……值得珍惜和记住的时刻！

Tuesday, June 4th: North Edison, New Jersey; The cicadas have begun to sing! All together they sound like a Boeing 767 is circling 40 feet overhead. The sound is that awesome. 10 inch deep piles of dying post-coitus adults litter the base of trees. The invasion has only begun!  Wednesday, June 5th – Monday, June 17th: Metuchen, North Edison, Colonia, Avenel, New Jersey; The invasion is in full effect! Homeowners in North Edison and Colonia report having to haul away the dying bodies of cicadas in wheelbarrows! Residents describe the cicadas’ combined mating screams as “loud as a UFO” [how do they know what a UFO sounds like?] and “like a Mack Truck was floating ten foot above your head”! Someone even told me cicadas taste like shrimp! I guess they made the best of a bad situation.

6月4日，星期二：新泽西州北爱迪生；蝉已经开始唱歌！总的来说，它们听起来像是一架波音767在空中盘旋40英尺。声音真棒。 10英寸深的垂死的性交后成虫堆放着树木的根。入侵才刚刚开始！ 6月5日，星期三– 6月17日，星期一：梅图兴，北爱迪生，科洛尼亚，新泽西州阿弗内尔；入侵已完全生效！北爱迪生和科洛尼亚的房主报告说，他们必须搬运独轮车中垂死的蝉体！居民将蝉鸣叫声描述为“像不明飞行物一样响亮”（他们怎么知道不明飞行物听起来像什么？）和“就像马克卡车漂浮在头顶十英尺处”！甚至有人告诉我蝉的味道像虾！我想他们在糟糕的情况下会尽全力。

Wednesday, June 26th: Metuchen, New Jersey; It appears the invasion is over. All that remains is the dismembered, rotting corpses and the memories, sweet, sweet, memories. But remember, They’ll be back…in the year 2013!

6月26日，星期三：新泽西州梅图兴；看来入侵已经结束。剩下的只是被肢解，腐烂的尸体和美好的回忆。但是请记住，他们会在2013年回来的！

Saturday, August 3rd: Metuchen, New Jersey; Looking out my second story window I can clearly see the damage done by the 17-year cicadas. Brown patches of dead leaves speckle local oak and maple trees, revealing the branches where the female cicada has chosen to lay her eggs; an interesting “natural disaster”, but, not as heart-breaking as an earth quake or a flood. Clearly the most provocative news regarding cicadas is the current hatch of annual cicadas, which are larger than the “17-year” cicadas (thoroughly illustrated within this web page) and greener. Another dissimilarity is the difference in their respective mating calls: while the “17-year” cicada makes a whirring sound somewhere between the motor of a vacuum cleaner and a car alarm, the “annual” cicada sounds more like a lawn sprinkler or maybe a sewing machine. Although I can clearly hear hundreds of “annual” cicadas and I have found their shells, I haven’t visually located a single one ! Another cicada related event has been the recent hatch of “cicada killer” wasps. These two-inch long giant wasps only prey upon, our friend, the defenseless cicada. I haven’t located these creatures either, but, they are definitely out there. Cicadas beware!

8月3日，星期六：新泽西州梅图兴；从第二个故事的窗户向外看，我可以清楚地看到17年蝉所造成的破坏。枯叶的褐色斑块散布着当地的橡树和枫树，露出了雌蝉选择产卵的树枝；有趣的“自然灾害”，但不像地震或洪水那样令人心碎。显然，与蝉有关的最具挑衅性的新闻是当前年度蝉的孵化，这比“ 17年”蝉（此网页中已详细说明）要大，而且更环保。另一个相异之处是它们各自的配对呼声有所不同：“ 17年蝉”在吸尘器的马达和汽车警报器之间发出呼wh的声音，而“年蝉”的声音听起来更像是草坪洒水器或缝纫机。尽管我可以清晰地听到数百个“年度”蝉的声音，并且已经找到了它们的外壳，但我在视觉上并没有发现一个外壳！与蝉有关的另一事件是最近孵化的“蝉杀手”黄蜂。我们的朋友，这只两英寸长的巨型黄蜂只捕食没有防御的蝉。我也没有找到这些生物，但是，它们肯定在那里。蝉提防！

Wednesday, November 6th: Metuchen, New Jersey; they are all dead or sucking on roots underground.

11月6日，星期三：新泽西州梅图兴；他们都死了或吸根在地下。

Every 17 years a fearsome biological monstrosity drags its way to the surface of the earth. It comes only to mate and spawn; however, it imparts terror and disgust in the hearts and minds of every man, woman, child, and beast unfortunate enough to cross its path. This is no Hollywood fantasy, ladies and gentlemen, this creature is real, horribly real! Cast your trembling eyes upon mother nature’s most disturbing insectoid aberration…   T

每隔17年，可怕的生物怪兽就会将其拖到地表。它只在交配和产卵时出现。然而，不幸的是，它给每个男人，女人，孩子和野兽带来了恐怖和令人厌恶的恐惧，使他们无法走过它的路。女士们，先生们，这不是好莱坞的幻想，这个生物是真实的，可怕的真实！颤抖的双眼注视着大自然中最令人不安的类昆虫畸变……T

Seriously, folks, the cicada isn’t a locust, it’s, well, a cicada (a member of the Homoptera order of insects). These charming creatures are best known for their intense mating call, which sounds more or less like a lawn sprinkler, or a miniature AH-64 Apache Black Helicopter. When 10,000 or so of these suckers start screaming in your neighborhood you’ll think you’re in the middle of Apocalypse Now. Now is the time! Depending on where you live, these heinous herbivores should be dive-bombing your friends and family any day now.

严重的是，人们不是蝉，而是蝉（昆虫同翅目成员）。这些迷人的动物以其强烈的交配声而闻名，这听起来或多或少像草坪洒水器或微型AH-64阿帕奇黑色直升机。当附近有10,000个此类吸盘开始尖叫时，您会认为自己正处在《现代启示录》的中间。现在是时候了！根据您的居住地，这些令人讨厌的草食动物现在应该每天都在轰炸您的朋友和家人。

Actually, they are more likely to fall out of a tree than fly, but, rest assured, they will be landing somewhere on your body sometime soon.

实际上，它们比苍蝇更容易从树上掉下来，但是请放心，它们很快就会降落在您身体上的某个地方。

I live in central New Jersey and right now we are up to our mandibles in a plague of these sap slurping oddities. Some breeds, including the green/yellow striped cicadas, rear their ugly heads every year, but, fortunately not in great numbers. The “periodic”, black-bodied, red-eyed, spawn of Beelzebub cicadas only present themselves once every 17 years; unfortunately, there are literally millions of them. Worst of all, they have a face, just like you or me!

我住在新泽西州中部，现在我们要面对下颌骨下垂的奇怪困扰。某些品种，包括绿色/黄色条纹的蝉，每年都会举起丑陋的头，但幸运的是数量并不多。 Beelzebub蝉的“周期性”，黑体，红眼产卵仅每17年出现一次；不幸的是，实际上有数百万个。最糟糕的是，它们像您或我一样有一张脸！

Periodic cicadas live to be 17 years old (13 years in southern states), which means they’re the only insect that qualifies for a driver’s license in New Jersey. Actually, these creatures only spend two to four weeks of their lives as an adult. They spend the first 17 years underground sucking on roots! Exciting! Once the adults have mated, the female drills a hole in a tree branch with her butt (technically her ovipositor) and lays her eggs. The eggs soon hatch and the “nymphs” fall to the ground where they feed on root sap. As soon as the adults mate they croak and drop to the ground where they will decay and stink. If I were you I wouldn’t hang out under any trees this year. In the end, your best offense will be a shovel and a bucket, or, a hungry golden retriever.

周期性蝉活到17岁（南部各州为13岁），这意味着它们是新泽西州唯一具有驾驶执照资格的昆虫。实际上，这些生物成年后只花两到四个星期的时间。他们花了头17年在地下吸根！令人兴奋！成虫交配后，雌性就用屁股（技术上说是产卵器）在树枝上钻一个洞，然后产卵。卵很快孵化，“若虫”掉到地上，以根液为食。成年人交配后，他们就会嘶哑，跌落到地面，在那里它们会腐烂和发臭。如果我是你，我今年不会在树下闲逛。最后，最好的进攻是铲子和水桶，或者是饥饿的金毛寻回犬。

Many periodical cicadas emerged four years early in the Chicago area in 2020. These cicadas belong to the Brood XIII (13) which is set to emerge in 2024, and last emerged in 2007. Periodical cicadas often emerge in years proceeding or following the year their brood is expected to emerge. This phenomenon is called straggling. Most of the time these “stragglers” emerge in small numbers and are quickly eaten by predators, and do not go on to sing, chorus (synchronized singing for the purpose of attracting females), mate, and lay eggs. Sometimes they emerge in numbers large enough to survive, chorus and reproduce — this seems to have happened in the Chicago area in 2020. It is thought this this is how new broods formed over the millennia — cicadas emerge 4 or 1 year early in significant numbers and form a new brood. When enough stragglers emerge to successfully reproduce it is called an acceleration.

许多定期蝉在2020年在芝加哥地区提前4年出现。这些蝉属于Brood XIII（13），定于2024年出现，最后一次出现在2007年。周期性蝉经常出现在其繁殖的第二年或次年。雏鸟有望出现。这种现象称为流浪。大多数时候，这些“流浪者”数量很少，被掠食者迅速吃掉，并且不会继续唱歌，合唱（为了吸引雌性而同步唱歌），交配和产卵。有时它们的数量大到足以生存，合唱和繁殖-这似乎发生在2020年的芝加哥地区。人们认为这是千年来形成新幼体的方式-蝉大量或早4或1年出现。并形成一个新的群体。当出现足够多的散布者成功繁殖时，这称为加速。

So, is a new brood forming around Chicago? Is this due to climate change or localized “heat islands”? Will the progeny of these stragglers emerge in 13, 17 or 21 years? Lots of questions — but we’ll need to wait quite some time to answer them.

那么，是否在芝加哥周围形成了新的雏形？这是由于气候变化还是局部的“热岛”造成的？这些流浪者的后代会在13、17或21年出现吗？许多问题-但我们需要等待一段时间才能回答。

1969年，芝加哥郊区1出现了大量定期蝉（Williams，K.S.＆amp; Simon，C. 1995）。

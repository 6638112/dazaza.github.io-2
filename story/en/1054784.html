The Annie’s mac and cheese origin story has been told before, but if you aren't familiar with it, then you’re in for a twist and a treat. In 1984, when Withey was 21, she and her then-husband Andrew Martin came up with the product known as Smartfood in the kitchen of their Boston home. That’s correct: Annie, the Annie’s lady, is also the cheddar popcorn lady.

之前的Annie的Mac和奶酪起源故事已经被告知，但如果你熟悉它，那么你就是为了扭曲和善待。 1984年，当赫蒂是21时，她和她的那个丈夫安德鲁马丁想出了被称为智能食品的产品在他们的波士顿家的厨房里。这是正确的：Annie，Annie的女士，也是Cheddar Popcorn Lady。

Around the same time, Withey incorporated the dried powder cheese from the popcorn to create her mac and cheese recipe. “The shelves in my rather small refrigerator were dominated by half-gallon sized plastic tubs of powdered cheddar cheese samples from Land O'Lakes,” Withey wrote to me over email, her preferred communication method. “I was in pursuit of an all-natural cheese popcorn recipe that used white cheddar cheese (not glow-in-the-dark orange!) While scooping the white cheddar powder one day, I had a ‘eureka’ moment.”

在同时，Hyey将干燥的粉末乳酪纳入爆米花，以创造她的Mac和奶酪配方。 “我相当小的冰箱里的架子由来自土地O＆＃39的粉末切达干酪样本的半加仑大小的塑料浴缸占主导地位;”湖泊，“我通过电子邮件写信给我，她的首选通信方法。 “我追求了一颗全自然的奶酪爆米花配方，用白色的切达干酪（不是黑暗的橙色！），同时挖出白皮达粉的一天，我有一个'eureka'时刻。”

She boiled elbow pasta from Kraft, measured an identical amount of white cheddar cheese as was in the Kraft packet, added butter and milk, and then... “Whoa!” was the summation of her first bites.

她从牛皮纸中煮肘面，测量了相同数量的白色切达奶酪，如牛皮纸包，加入黄油和牛奶，然后......“哇！”是她的第一次叮咬的总结。

She didn’t immediately pivot to mac and cheese, though. She says that idea “remained on the back burner” until Smartfood was sold to Frito-Lay in 1989, a $15 million windfall that enabled her to turn her attention back to her preferred venture.

Withey’s face was almost plastered on the original early '90s rollout of mac and cheese boxes, but she “promptly put an end to that idea,” she says. She went with her bunny named Bernie instead. At the same time, Withey was growing wary of the day-to-day side of the business. There are plenty of wealthy folks who talk a big talk about their commitment to the environment and their community, then live in excess privately. I do not think Withey is one of those folks; I’d bet a lifetime supply of mac and cheese that she has never munched on gold-flaked pasta, for instance. She’s remained in Connecticut in an early 1900s Victorian farmhouse with her husband because that’s what brings her happiness. She has four cows, seven sheep, a burro, two horses, two flocks of chickens, a dog and of course two house rabbits, who she says offer “entertainment and comfort.” She considers her job to be “keeping Mother Nature at bay.” When I asked about her hobbies, especially during the pandemic, she responded, “I guess my love of birds is a hobby.” Also, monitoring her house plants. And in case you were wondering, Withey still eats Annie’s once or twice a month, which she buys from her local co-op. “I don’t feel like I have reached mac and cheese burnout,” she says.

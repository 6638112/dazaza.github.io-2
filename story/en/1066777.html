Anti-aging protein in red blood cells helps stave off cognitive decline
红细胞中的抗衰老蛋白有助于避免认知下降

2021-06-19 06:01:38

Research conducted by Qiang et al has discovered a link between a protein in red blood cells and age-related decline in cognitive performance. Published in the open access journal PLOS Biology on 17th June 2021, the study shows that depleting mouse blood of the protein ADORA2B leads to faster declines in memory, delays in auditory processing, and increased inflammation in the brain. Because the amount of oxygen in the blood also declines with age, the team hypothesized that aging in the brain might be naturally held at bay by adenosine receptor A2B (ADORA2B), a protein on the membrane of which is known to help release oxygen from the blood cells so it can be used by the body. To test this idea, they created mice that lacked ADORA2B in their blood and compared behavioral and physiological measures with control mice.

Qiang等人进行的研究已经发现了红细胞蛋白质与认知性能的年龄相关的蛋白质之间的联系。在2021年6月17日在开放式通道期刊PLOS生物学上发表，研究表明，蛋白质ADORA2B的耗尽小鼠血液导致记忆中的速度更快，听觉处理延迟，以及大脑中的炎症增加。正如世界各地的增加，那些会遇到的人数也是如此。由于血液中的氧气量随着年龄的增长，该团队假设大脑中的老化可以通过腺苷受体A2b（adora2b），其膜上的蛋白质是已知从中释放氧气的蛋白质血细胞使其可以用身体使用。为了测试这个想法，他们创造了缺乏血液的小鼠，并与对照小鼠进行比较行为和生理措施。

The team found that as the mice got older, the hallmarks of cognitive decline—poor memory, hearing deficits, and in the brain—were all greater in the mice lacking ADORA2B than in the control mice. Additionally, after experiencing a period of oxygen deprivation, the behavioral and physiological effects on young mice without ADORA2B were much greater than those on normal young mice.

Thus, aging in the brain is naturally reduced by ADORA2B, which helps get oxygen to the brain when needed. Further testing will be needed to determine whether ADORA2B levels naturally decline with age and whether treatment with drugs that activate ADORA2B can reduce cognitive decline in normal mice.

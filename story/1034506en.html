DISH关闭Slingbox，设备将在2022年变得无法操作

宣布将永久关闭所有Slingbox服务，并在两年内终止对这些设备的支持，届时这些设备将不再起作用。

On Monday, Dish’s Sling Media unit announced that Slingbox servers will be permanently taken offline 24 months from Nov. 9, 2020.

周一，Dish的Sling Media部门宣布，Slingbox服务器将从2020年11月9日起永久离线24个月。

“Until then, most Slingbox models will continue to work normally, but the number of supported devices for viewing will steadily decrease as versions of the SlingPlayer apps become outdated and/or lose compatibility,” the company said in a message posted Monday.

该公司在周一发布的一条消息中表示：“在此之前，大多数Slingbox型号将继续正常工作，但随着SlingPlayer应用程序的版本过时和/或失去兼容性，支持观看的设备数量将稳步减少。”

In an FAQ about the shutdown, Dish said Slingbox is being discontinued because “We’ve had to make room for new innovative products so that we can continue to serve our customers in the best way possible.” Sling will not be releasing any new products; most authorized resellers have been out of stock of the Slingbox devices “for a couple years,” according to the company.

在一次关于关闭的常见问题中，迪什说Slingbox停产是因为“我们不得不为新的创新产品腾出空间，这样我们才能继续以最好的方式为我们的客户服务。”Sling将不会发布任何新产品；根据该公司的说法，大多数授权经销商已经“几年”缺货了。

Sling Media was acquired by EchoStar in 2007 for $380 million, which at the time was Dish’s parent company. Years before Netflix became a streaming powerhouse, the Slingbox “place-shifting” devices let customers watch pay-TV channels over the internet. But the products never became a mainstream category in the way streaming-media players like Roku and Amazon’s Fire TV have.

Sling Media在2007年被EchoStar以3.8亿美元的价格收购，当时EchoStar是Dish的母公司。早在Netflix成为流媒体巨头的几年前，Slingbox的“位置转换”设备就可以让客户在互联网上观看付费电视频道。但这些产品从未像Roku和亚马逊的Fire TV这样的流媒体播放器那样成为主流类别。

At the height of the devices’ popularity, Sling Media raised the ire of content owners, including Major League Baseball, which contended the device illegally transmitted its TV programming online (although MLB never pursued litigation).

在这些设备最受欢迎的时候，Sling Media引起了包括美国职业棒球大联盟(Major League Baseball)在内的内容所有者的愤怒，他们声称该设备非法在网上传播其电视节目(尽管MLB从未提起诉讼)。

In 2014, Fox Broadcasting filed a lawsuit challenging Dish’s Sling-based feature, in addition to a suit over the satellite broadcaster’s ad-skipping features. After a court ruled Sling technology didn’t violate copyright law, Fox and Dish settled the disputes in 2016.

2014年，福克斯广播公司(Fox Broadcast)提起诉讼，挑战Dish基于Sling的功能，此外还起诉这家卫星广播公司的广告跳过功能。在法院裁定吊索技术没有违反版权法后，福克斯和迪什在2016年解决了这些纠纷。

Sling Media was founded in 2004 by brothers Blake and Jason Krikorian. Blake, a tech entrepreneur and investor, died in 2016.

Sling Media是由布莱克和杰森·克里科里安兄弟于2004年创立的。布莱克是一位科技企业家和投资人，于2016年去世。

The Krikorian brothers came up with the concept for the Slingbox after they were frustrated that they couldn’t watch their favorite baseball team — the San Francisco Giants — playing when they were on the road. They code-named the product “Lebowski” because of the catchphrase “The Dude abides” in the Coen brothers’ “The Big Lebowski,” given that the Slingbox device adapted the format of the video stream in real time “abiding by what is possible at the time,” Blake Krikorian said in a 2008 interview with UCLA’s alumni magazine. Such adaptive bit-rate video streaming tech is now ubiquitously used by internet media services to account for variations in bandwidth.

克里科里安兄弟在旅途中不能观看他们最喜欢的棒球队旧金山巨人队(San Francisco Giants)的比赛，这让他们感到沮丧，于是他们提出了Slingbox的概念。布莱克·克里科里安(Blake Krikorian)在2008年接受加州大学洛杉矶分校(UCLA)校友杂志采访时表示，考虑到Slingbox设备实时调整了视频流的格式，他们将该产品的代号命名为“LeBowski”，因为科恩兄弟的“The Big LeBowski”中有一句流行语“The Dude abides”。这种自适应比特率视频流技术现在被互联网媒体服务普遍使用，以应对带宽的变化。

Sony delists PlayStation version of Cyberpunk 2077, offers refunds to all owners
索尼将赛博朋克2077改成PlayStation版本，向所有车主退款

2020-12-18 15:12:08

Cyberpunk 2077's woes on "last-gen" gaming consoles mounted on Thursday with an unprecedented about-face by Sony. Effective immediately, the CD Projekt Red adventure game has been fully delisted from the PlayStation Store, and existing owners have been told they can request refunds for any purchases of the game's digital-download version.

赛博朋克2077在上一代游戏中的困境游戏机于周四安装，索尼史无前例。立即生效，CD Projekt Red冒险游戏已从PlayStation商店中完全除名，并且现有所有人被告知可以为购买该数字下载版本的游戏索取退款。

Shortly after  Sony's Thursday evening statement went live, a button appeared for users signed in with their PlayStation Network account to request a no-questions-asked refund of their CP77 purchase. (This button doesn't appear for all users as of press time, likely owing to a swell of traffic to Sony's servers.) The refund's processing "may vary based on your payment method and financial institution." Ars Technica has confirmed that the game's PS Store listing can no longer be accessed on PS4 or PS5 consoles, while existing owners can still find the game in their digital library interface and play it. The news follows CD Projekt Red's own admission that the game's performance on base consoles like Xbox One and PlayStation 4 suffered from "bugs and crashes." Therefore, Red  encouraged customers to request refunds from both digital and physical retailers—and the company opened an email hotline for anyone who struggled with attempts to get refunds. Sure enough, game owners in both console camps found the default refund process unworkable—and Sony's sweeping action here may point to a requirement on their part to have a refund process be compatible with its terms of service. In other words, if the game is "broken," then Sony may need to acknowledge such issues with a top-to-bottom wipe of the game from PSN to work with CDPR's public refund plan, as opposed to offering selective refunds.

在索尼周四晚上的声明发布后不久，出现了一个按钮，供用户使用他们的PlayStation网络帐户登录，以请求无疑问的CP77购买退款。 （截至发稿时，此按钮并未向所有用户显示，这可能是由于Sony服务器的流量激增。）退款处理可能会因您的付款而异方法和金融机构。＆＃34; Ars Technica已经确认，该游戏的PS Store列表不再可以在PS4或PS5控制台上访问，而现有的所有者仍可以在其数字图书馆界面中找到该游戏并进行游戏。该消息是CD Projekt Red自己承认的，该游戏在Xbox One和PlayStation 4等基本控制台上的性能受到bug和崩溃的影响。因此，雷德（Red）鼓励客户向数字零售商和实体零售商要求退款-该公司为任何尝试退款的人开设了电子邮件热线。可以肯定的是，两个控制台营地的游戏所有者都发现默认的退款程序不可行-索尼的全面行动可能表明他们要求退款程序与其服务条款相兼容。换句话说，如果游戏被打破了，那么索尼可能需要从PSN彻底清除游戏中的此类问题，以使用CDPR的公共退款计划，而不是提供选择性退款。

With more clarity on the refund process, which appeared to be automatic at first glance (owing to the missing "refund" button), users may very well elect not to follow through on this refund option, in spite of woeful performance on base PS4 (and better performance on PS4 Pro, albeit not perfect by any means). CD Projekt Red previously told console owners of the game to expect "two large patches," scheduled for January and February of 2021.

乍看之下，退款程序一目了然（由于缺少“退款”按钮），因此更加清晰，尽管很糟糕，用户还是可以选择不遵循此退款选项基本PS4上的性能（以及PS4 Pro上的更好性能，尽管无论如何都不完美）。 CD Projekt Red之前曾告诉游戏的游戏机所有者，希望有两个大补丁，定于2021年1月和2月。

As of press time, CD Projekt Red had not offered its own announcement on this development (owing to it breaking very early in the morning as per time zones in the publisher and developer's native country of Poland). Shortly after Sony's announcement went live, CDPR senior game designer Andrzej Zawadzki posted a meme of David Tennant crying, with no other statement or context.

截至记者发稿时，CD Projekt Red尚未就此事态发展发表自己的公告（由于它很早在出版商和开发商的祖国波兰的时区中中断）。索尼发布公告后不久，CDPR高级游戏设计师Andrzej Zawadzki发布了David Tennant哭泣的模因，没有其他声明或上下文。

The game suffers from arguably harsher performance issues on base Xbox One consoles, while its Xbox Series X/S versions include a variety of sliders that boost performance. Since those SKUs are combined under Xbox's new "Smart Delivery" program, it's unclear whether Microsoft will follow suit with a similar delisting plan. Xbox representatives did not immediately reply to Ars Technica's questions on the matter. This article has been updated since its publication to clarify that the refund process is not automatic.

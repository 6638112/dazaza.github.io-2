ANNALS OF FINANCE about Clarence Saunders of Memphis, who in 1919, founded the Piggly Wiggly Stores, a chain of retail self-service markets situated mostly in the South & West, with headquarters in Memphis. Saunders is remembered on Wall St. as the last man who engineered a real corner in a nationally traded stock. By 1922 the stores had flourished so that its shares were listed on the N.Y. Stock Exchange. When a group of bears began selling Piggly Wiggly short to force the price of the stock down, Saunders began a buying campaign to support the price of the stock in order to protect his own investment & that of other Piggly Wiggly stockholders. He supplemented his own funds with a loan of about ten million dollars from a group of bankers. His buying campaign was an attempt at a corner. The stock went up wildly, reaching a high of 124. At this point the Exchange suspended further trading & postponed the short sellers' delivery deadline. This resulted in eventual bankruptcy for Saunders & he was finally forced to step out of the Pigp]y Wiggly Company. Saunders came back, however, In 1928 he started a new grocery chain called the Clarence Saunders, Sole Owner of My Name, Stores, Inc. The depression hit these stores in 1930, & they went bankrupt & he was broke again. After that he started another grocery chair, called the Kedoozle, an electrically operated store. It was found that the machinery was too complex & expensive to operate. In his last years Saunders was working on an even more intricate mechanism - the Foodelectric. It was unfinished when he died in 1953.

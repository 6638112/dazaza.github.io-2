Out of a heap of broken images, Chernobyl seeks World Heritage status
在堆积的图像中，切尔诺贝利寻求世界遗产

2021-04-26 17:47:38

A mothballed nuclear power station surrounded by wasteland, rubble and abandoned buildings is not what most people associate with a UNESCO World Heritage site. But that is what Ukraine has in mind for Chernobyl. Hoping that such an assignation could draw funding and more tourists, the government has begun a process that could eventually allow it to apply to the UN’s cultural, scientific and education body for protection.

被荒地包围的樟脑核电站，瓦砾和被遗弃的建筑物不是大多数人与联合国教科文组织世界遗产联系起来的。但这就是乌克兰对切尔诺贝利的思想。希望这样的分配可以吸取资金和更多游客，政府开始了一个最终可能允许它适用于联合国文化，科学和教育机构的进程。

On April 26, the former Soviet republic marks the 35th anniversary of the 1986 Chernobyl nuclear disaster, when a reactor at the plant, which was some 108 km (67 miles) north of the capital Kyiv, exploded during a botched safety test. The result was the world’s worst nuclear accident. Clouds of radiation wer sent across much of Europe and tens of thousands of people to forced to evacuate.

4月26日，前苏联共和国标志着1986年的切尔诺贝利核灾害的35周年，当时植物的反应堆，在首都Kyiv以北北北北北部，在拙劣的安全测试中爆炸。结果是世界上最糟糕的核事故。辐射WER云横跨欧洲的大部分和数万人被迫撤离。

Thirty-one plant workers and firemen died in the immediate aftermath of the disaster, mostly from acute radiation sickness. Thousands more later succumbed to radiation-related illnesses such as cancer, although the total death toll and long-term health effects remain contested.

三十一位植物工人和消防员在灾难的立即死亡，大多来自急性辐射疾病。虽然总死亡人数和长期健康效应仍然有争议，但虽然患有癌症等辐射相关的疾病，但数千人屈服于癌症。

“We believe that putting Chernobyl on the UNESCO heritage list is a first and important step towards having this great place as a unique destination of interest for the whole of mankind,” said Oleksandr Tkachenko, the Ukrainian culture minister .

“我们认为将切尔诺斯在联合国教科文组织的遗产名录上是一个朝着这个伟大的地方成为整个人类兴趣的独特目的地的第一步，最重要的一步，”乌克兰文化部长的Oleksandr Tkachenko说。

“The importance of the Chernobyl zone lays far beyond Ukraine’s borders ... It is not only about commemoration, but also history and people’s rights,” he said.

“切尔诺贝利区的重要性远远超出乌克兰的边界......这不仅仅是纪念，也是历史和人民的权利，”他说。

Before sending an application to the UN, locations seeking UNESCO protection had to be included on a national cultural and historic heritage list, according to the minister. Tkachenko said his ministry had recently decided to include a huge military radar built near the city of Chernobyl in the 1970s in the list. It was discussing expanding the application to include the whole 30km Chernobyl Exclusion Zone.

据部长称，在向联合国申请申请到联合国申请，寻求教科文组织保护的地点必须包括在国家文化和历史悠久的遗产名录上。 Tkachenko表示，他的部门最近决定在列表中的20世纪70年代建造了一个巨大的军事雷达。它讨论扩大申请以包括整个30km切尔诺贝利禁区。

Most of the area around the abandoned nuclear plant is a wilderness of empty buildings, scrubland and rubble. All of the buildings in Pripyat, a ghost town that was once home to 50,000 people mostly working at the plant, are in need of repair.

废弃的核植物周围的大部分地区都是空建筑，灌木丛和瓦砾的荒野。曾经是曾经在工厂工作过50,000人的幽灵镇，这是一个鬼城，需要维修。

Tkachenko said he hoped that Chernobyl, which had already become a popular site for adventure tourists before the coronavirus pandemic prevented most international travel, would bounce back and begin to lure visitors again. In 2019, HBO’s Chernobyl was behind a jump in the number of visitors to the power plant and nearby Pripyat, with 120,000 people visiting the area.

Tkachenko表示，他希望切尔诺贝利已经成为冠心病大流行之前冒险游客的热门网站，阻碍了大多数国际旅行，会反弹并开始再次引诱访客。 2019年，HBO的切尔诺贝利在电厂和附近Pripyat的游客人数上跳起来，12万人访问该地区。

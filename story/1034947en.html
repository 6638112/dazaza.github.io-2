LinkedIn表示，它发现了两个视频和广告指标测量错误，导致41.8万广告商在两年多的时间里为广告支付了过高的费用

2020-11-14 07:15:18

LinkedIn said Thursday it discovered a pair of measurement errors that led to more than 400,000 advertisers overpaying for campaigns on the professional networking platform.

领英(LinkedIn)周四表示，它发现了两个测量错误，导致40多万广告商在这个专业社交平台上为广告支付了过高的费用。

In a blog post, the Microsoft Corp.-owned company said its engineering team found and fixed two measurement issues in its ad products, which led to overreporting of video views and ad impressions on sponsored-content campaigns. The bugs affected more than 418,000 advertisers over the course of more than two years, it said.

微软(Microsoft Corp.)旗下的这家公司在一篇博客文章中说，其工程团队在其广告产品中发现并修复了两个测量问题，这两个问题导致赞助内容活动中视频浏览量和广告印象的过度报道。该公司表示，在两年多的时间里，这些漏洞影响了41.8万多个广告商。

With video ads, LinkedIn discovered that some organic videos and video ads would play while they were off-screen on Apple Inc.’s iOS devices.

LinkedIn发现，通过视频广告，一些有机视频和视频广告可以在苹果公司(Apple Inc.)iOS设备上的屏幕外播放。

If a LinkedIn user scrolled past a video ad while the video was buffering, for example, the ad would autoplay even when out of view, but still be tracked and logged as a video view or completion.

例如，如果LinkedIn用户在视频缓冲期间滚动经过视频广告，即使在看不到的情况下，该广告也会自动播放，但仍会被跟踪并记录为视频观看或完成。

That may have resulted in overstated measures including video views and view-through rates, as well as overcharging advertisers paying by the view, according to a LinkedIn spokesman.

LinkedIn的一位发言人表示，这可能导致视频点击量和点击率等措施被夸大，并向按点击量付费的广告商收取过高费用。

The company also said it may have been overreporting impressions on sponsored-content campaigns in the LinkedIn feed—for example, in cases when users would rotate their phones or quickly move to other parts of the app, the spokesman said.

这位发言人说，该公司还表示，它可能在LinkedIn订阅源中夸大了赞助内容宣传活动的印象--例如，当用户旋转手机或快速移动到应用程序的其他部分时。

Overall, the company said more than 90% of the advertisers affected overpaid by less than $25, adding that it would provide them with credits for future ad campaigns.

总体而言，该公司表示，超过90%的受影响广告商多付了不到25美元，并补充说，它将为他们未来的广告活动提供积分。

LinkedIn’s error comes after years of advertisers demanding more transparency and third-party auditing of the metrics provided by social-media giants. In 2016, Facebook came under fire after advertisers learned that it had overestimated average viewing time for video ads on the platform for two years. Facebook had only been counting video views that lasted for at least three seconds, ignoring those of shorter duration and therefore artificially pushing the average length of a view higher. In some cases, the earlier counting method likely overestimated average time spent watching videos by between 60% and 90%.

多年来，广告商一直要求提高社交媒体巨头提供的指标的透明度，并对其进行第三方审计，LinkedIn出现了这一错误。2016年，在广告商了解到Facebook两年来高估了该平台上视频广告的平均观看时间后，Facebook遭到了抨击。Facebook只统计了持续时间至少3秒的视频点击量，忽略了持续时间较短的视频点击量，因此人为地推高了点击量的平均时长。在某些情况下，早期的统计方法可能将观看视频的平均时间高估了60%到90%。

Facebook eventually settled a lawsuit from a group of small advertisers over the miscalculated video metrics.

Facebook最终解决了一群小广告商就错误计算的视频指标提起的诉讼。

LinkedIn said it began talks in June with the Media Rating Council, the industry’s measurement watchdog, on an audit of its metrics. “We are working with them to proceed with an audit of our metrics,” a LinkedIn spokesman said.

LinkedIn表示，该公司已于6月份开始与该行业的衡量监督机构--媒体评级委员会(Media Rating Council)就其衡量指标的审计进行谈判。LinkedIn的一位发言人表示：“我们正在与他们合作，对我们的指标进行审计。”

The company is also working with Oracle -owned measurement firm Moat to measure so-called viewability—the portion of the video that appears on screen and for how long—on its platforms.

该公司还与甲骨文(Oracle)旗下的测量公司Moat合作，测量所谓的可看性-视频在其平台上显示的部分和持续时间。

Humanity is in danger of becoming obsolete technology in LX 2048
人类有可能成为LX 2048中过时的技术

2020-12-20 23:22:13

A fatally ill man tries to secure the future of his family in a near-future world where the toxicity of the sun forces people to stay inside during the daytime in LX 2048, starring James D'Arcy ( Agent Carter, Homeland). It's a flawed yet thought-provoking surreal science fiction film, chock-full of big ideas on our relationship to technology and what it means to be human, and anchored by D'Arcy's fantastic performance.

一名身患重病的人试图在一个近乎未来的世界中确保自己家庭的未来，在该世界中，太阳的毒性迫使人们白天在LX 2048（詹姆斯·达奇·阿奇（James D＆Arcy），国土局特工）的陪伴下呆在室内。这是一部有缺陷但发人深省的超现实科幻电影，充满了关于我们与技术的关系以及对人类的意义的大观念，并以D'Arcy的出色表演为基础。

D'Arcy plays Adam Bird, a married father of three on the brink of divorce from his wife, Reena (Anna Brewster). The year is 2048, and people are largely living indoors during the day because the sunlight is powerful enough to scald human skin instantly. Everyone spends most of their time in a virtual world known as The Realm. (The fact that Reena caught Adam virtually cavorting with his AI lover is just one of their many marital issues.) Everyone also takes regular doses of LithiumX to ward off depression. Adam, however, clings to his old habits, driving a convertible to the office in a hazmat suit, and refusing to take the drug.

达西（Arcy）饰演亚当·伯德（Adam Bird），这是已婚的三个孩子的父亲，与妻子里纳（安娜（Anna Brewster））离婚。那是2048年，白天人们主要生活在室内，因为阳光足够强大，可以立即烫伤人体皮肤。每个人的大部分时间都花在称为The Realm的虚拟世界中。 （Reena抓住亚当实际上是在与AI情人打交道，这只是他们许多婚姻问题之一。）每个人还需要定期服用LithiumX来预防抑郁症。然而，亚当坚持他的旧习惯，驾驶着一辆穿上危险品西装的敞篷车去办公室，并拒绝服药。

Then he learns he has a terminal heart condition, and must shore up his struggling VR business long enough to maintain their premium insurance policy. It provides for a clone, complete with all Adam's memories and traits, to raise his family in his place once he dies. In fact, the clone would be an improved version of himself, and Adam isn't quite ready to face this bitter reality. Things become much more complicated and weirder from there. We sat down with Director Guy Moshe ( Bunraku) to learn more about the genesis of the film and its themes.

然后，他得知自己患有晚期心脏病，必须为挣扎中的VR业务提供足够长的支持，以维持其优质保险政策。它提供了一个具有亚当所有记忆和特质的克隆，一旦他去世，他就可以代替他的家人。实际上，该克隆版将是他本人的改良版，而亚当尚未准备好面对这一痛苦的现实。事情变得更加复杂和怪异。我们与盖伊·莫西（Guy Moshe）（文乐）导演坐下来，以了解更多有关电影的起源及其主题的信息。

Ars Technica: Can you tell us a little bit about the genesis of this film?

Ars Technica：您能告诉我们有关这部电影的起源吗？

Guy Moshe: It started with the human aspect. I'm happily married, got three kids. We were spending a lot of time with other families, and in a span of a year or so, there were a lot of these relationships going up in flames all around us—people separating and getting divorced. Even though every story is different and every individual is different, I could see a through line: people reaching adulthood and feeling like, at some point along the way, they didn't get a chance to be all that they wanted to be, or that they could be. That gave birth to the idea of a person trapped inside a situation like this within their relationship. I also couldn't escape the thought that part of the reason the family structure in today's world is a little less sacred than it used to be, is because of all this technological revolution going on around us, which reduces the need for social interaction. It makes us less of the social creatures that we were intended to be.

Guy Moshe：从人的方面开始。我结婚很幸福，有了三个孩子。我们与其他家庭在一起度过了很多时间，并且在大约一年的时间里，我们之间的关系越来越多-人们分居并离婚。即使每个故事都不同，每个人都不同，但我仍然可以看到一条贯穿始终的线：人们到了成年期，并感觉到，在此过程中的某个时刻，他们没有机会成为自己想要的一切，或者说可能是。这催生了一个想法，那就是一个人被困在这种关系中。我也无法逃脱这样的想法：当今世界的家庭结构比以前少了一些神圣的原因，部分原因是由于我们周围发生的所有这种技术革命，这减少了社交互动的需要。它使我们减少了原本打算成为的社交动物。

With these two linking ideas in mind, the film started evolving. I wanted to focus on one character's journey.  We all have the image of ourselves that we want to be, that we project to the outside world. But there's also another image of who we really truly are, which a lot of us either never discover or never have the courage to admit to ourselves. Then there is another image of the way other people see us on the outside, and finally, the potential of the best version of ourselves that we can be. I wanted to examine the journey of a person as he discovers these different facets of himself.

考虑到这两个相互联系的想法，电影开始发展。我想专注于一个角色的旅程。我们每个人都有我们想要成为的自我形象，我们向外界投射。但是，还有另一幅关于我们真正的真实形象，我们当中很多人从来没有发现过，或者没有勇气承认自己。然后是其他人在外面看到我们的方式的另一幅图像，最后是我们可能成为的最佳自我的潜力。我想考察一个人发现自己不同方面的旅程。

It was one of the scripts that I thought about the most of everything I've ever written. But when I finally sat down to write it, the first draft of the script came out of me almost like a stream of consciousness. When I knew I was going to cast James [D'Arcy] in the lead role, I shaped it to his character a little bit more.

这是我考虑过的大部分脚本之一。但是当我最终坐下来编写它时，脚本的初稿几乎像是意识流中一样冒出来。当我知道我要扮演詹姆斯[D＆＃39; Arcy]担任主角时，我对他的角色塑造了一些。

Ars Technica: How did James become involved in the project? He's well-known for his supporting roles, and it was such a pleasure to see him in a meaty leading role for a change.

Ars Technica：James是如何参与该项目的？他以他的配角而闻名，很高兴看到他担任变革的主要领导者。

Guy Moshe: He's such a wonderful actor. I was actually discussing a bigger project with James that was very close to happening, while working on the script [for LX 2048]. When that other film ended up postponed, I couldn't escape the feeling that James would be so great in this role. There's something about James, he has such great humility, such an easy ability to command the stage. Because of his nature, his personality, he's holding a lot back, and he has a great self-deprecating humor. Also, I love seeing these great actors who don't always get to be at the center of things, play these types of roles.

盖·莫西（Guy Moshe）：他是一位了不起的演员。我实际上正在与James讨论一个更大的项目，而这个项目正在接近[LX 2048]的脚本工作。当另一部电影推迟发行时，我无法摆脱詹姆斯会扮演如此出色的角色的感觉。关于詹姆斯的事，他谦虚谦虚，很容易指挥舞台。由于他的天性，个性，他退缩了很多，并且拥有一种自嘲的幽默感。另外，我喜欢看到这些伟大的演员总是不会成为事情的中心，扮演这些角色。

Ars Technica: When Adam confronts his clone, he discovers that his wife knew him better than he thought. The clone is more like what Adam, deep down, wished he could be. It was funny in a sad way.

Ars Technica：当Adam面对他的克隆人时，他发现他的妻子比他想像的更加了解他。克隆人更像亚当，深深地希望他能成为那样。以一种悲伤的方式很有趣。

Guy Moshe: None of us, I think, knows truthfully, who we would be, if by some magic somebody gave you the option to be all that you be. A lot of us don't have the capacity to even imagine ourselves like that, because we live inside ourselves a lot. Oftentimes it's the people that are closest to us who can see and understand us more objectively. Yet the tragedy of a lot of these relationships falling apart is that we're not able to communicate with one another, on the positive side, about the things that brought us together to begin with. So I knew that Reena would know better than him what would make him happy, but I also knew that there was no way in the real world that she could ever help him get to that place.

盖·莫西：我想，我们谁也不会真实地知道我们会是谁，如果有人通过某种魔法让您选择了成为自己的一切，那我们将是谁。我们很多人甚至没有能力想象自己那样，因为我们生活在自己的内心深处。通常，离我们最近的人可以更客观地看到和理解我们。然而，许多这样的关系破裂的悲剧是，我们无法就积极的一面就彼此之间的交流进行沟通。因此，我知道Reena会比他更了解让他高兴的是什么，但我也知道，在现实世界中，她无法帮助他到达那个地方。

Ars Technica:  In many ways, the film is quite timely. The sun has not become fatally toxic, but we're in the middle of a pandemic, and we're having to engage with each other mostly in the virtual realm.

Ars Technica：在许多方面，电影拍摄的时机很及时。太阳尚未产生致命的毒性，但是我们正处于大流行的中期，我们必须与彼此进行互动，主要是在虚拟领域中。

Guy Moshe:  During the lockdown, I had the opportunity to be in some big Zoom meetings where suddenly I'm talking to 30 people [virtually]. On the one hand, you tell yourself, well,  I'm actually interacting with people right now. Some people would tell you they've spoken more with their families [during the pandemic] than they did before.

盖·莫西（Guy Moshe）：在封锁期间，我有机会参加了一些大型Zoom会议，突然间我与30个人[虚拟地]交谈。一方面，您告诉自己，好吧，我现在实际上正在与人互动。有些人会告诉您，[在大流行期间]与家人的交谈比以前多了。

But the first time, when [social distancing measures] were relaxing a little bit, we went out to a small birthday [gathering], everybody in that small event couldn't stop talking about how they didn't realize how much they missed being in the presence of other people. So there is clearly a huge difference. I think that the sense of touch is something that is so important to human beings; it's essential to our existence. To me, is the scary part is this idea that we're losing the ability to interact with the world in the way that we were biologically intended to do.

但是，第一次，当[社交疏远措施]放松一点时，我们去了一个小的生日[聚会]，那个小事件中的每个人都无法停止谈论他们如何没有意识到他们非常想念别人不在场。因此，显然存在巨大差异。我认为触摸感对人类非常重要；这对我们的生存至关重要。对我来说，最可怕的是，这种想法使我们失去了按照生物学原本的意图与世界互动的能力。

Ars Technica: The film explores this notion of the darker side of upgrades—viewing people as obsolete technology. And while the clones are deemed to be superior to the humans, there's a virtual chip waiting in the wings that will one day make the clones obsolete as well.

Ars Technica：电影探讨了升级的阴暗面的概念-将人们视为过时的技术。尽管克隆被认为比人类优越，但有一种虚拟芯片在翅膀上等待着，有一天也将使克隆也过时。

Guy Moshe: What I loved is that the clone himself is aware of the fact that it is just a transitional upgrade. This cannot be the end result, because the moment you start taking it all digital, there's no reason for the biology to exist anymore. To me it's a very big question. Of course, there's much more successful people than myself that are walking around now wondering if we're all currently existing in some computer simulation. The film talks about it from the biological standpoint. Maybe that's an admission on my part of what I would love to believe. There is this moment where [Adam's AI lover] Maria [talks about] the fact that we might all be cells of a bigger body. If you could transfer this to the digital realm, that would equate to an existence that is 100 percent virtual. Everything, all the data that we interpret, even with our sense of touch, is eventually transformed into electrical impulses. So maybe it's possible.

Guy Moshe：我喜欢克隆人本人意识到，这只是过渡升级。这不可能是最终的结果，因为从您开始将其全部数字化的那一刻起，就没有理由再存在生物学了。对我来说，这是一个很大的问题。当然，有很多比我自己成功的人现在正在四处走走，他们在想我们是否全部都存在于某些计算机模拟中。这部电影从生物学的角度谈论它。也许这是我愿意相信的一部分。在这一时刻，[亚当的AI恋人]玛丽亚[谈论]我们可能都是更大身体的细胞这一事实。如果您可以将其转移到数字领域，那将等于100％虚拟存在。我们所解释的所有内容，即使是具有触觉的所有数据，最终都会转化为电脉冲。所以也许是可能的。

Whether or not that's where humanity is headed, I'm sure I'm never going to live for enough time to know, but it certainly begs the question. I can tell you one interesting little thing. I have kids who love basketball. But if I give them the option to play physical basketball, or to play NBA 2K21 on the PlayStation, they'll take the digital game any day of the week. I don't know what to think about it, because I didn't grow up like this. Maybe it doesn't mean anything. Maybe it shows where we're headed.

不管这是人类前进的方向，我确信我永远都不会活得足够长的时间去知道，但这无疑引出了问题。我可以告诉你一件有趣的小事。我有喜欢篮球的孩子。但是，如果我给他们选择打篮球或在PlayStation上打NBA 2K21的选项，他们将在一周的任何一天进行数字游戏。我不知道该怎么想，因为我不是这样长大的。也许没有任何意义。也许它显示了我们的前进方向。

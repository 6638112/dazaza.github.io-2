加州通过24号提案，将隐私权再次推上风口浪尖

2020-11-05 14:21:14

This story is part of Elections 2020, CNET's coverage of the run-up to voting in November. California voters approved on Tuesday a ballot measure designed to beef up consumer privacy protections, according to unofficial returns published by the state's secretary of state. Proponents of the measure, known as Proposition 24, say the initiative would close a loophole in the state's current privacy law that lets major tech companies continue to target ads with user data, even when users opt out.

这个故事是2020年选举的一部分，CNET报道了11月投票前的准备情况。加州州务卿公布的非官方申报单显示，加州选民周二通过了一项旨在加强消费者隐私保护的投票措施。这项名为24号提案(Proposal 24)的措施的支持者表示，这一举措将堵塞该州现行隐私法中的一个漏洞，该法律允许大型科技公司继续使用用户数据投放广告，即使用户选择退出。

The proposition is the brainchild of Alastair Mactaggart and Californians for Consumer Privacy, his advocacy group.

这一提议是阿拉斯泰尔·麦克塔格特(Alastair Mactaggart)和他的倡导团体加州人争取消费者隐私(California Ans For Consumer Privacy)的创意。

"We are at the beginning of a journey that will profoundly shape the fabric of our society by redefining who is in control of our most personal information and putting consumers back in charge of their own data," Mactaggart said in a statement Wednesday.

麦克塔格特周三在一份声明中表示，我们正处于一场将深刻塑造我们社会结构的旅程的开始，重新定义谁控制着我们最私人的信息，并让消费者重新掌控他们自己的数据，麦克塔格特在周三的一份声明中说，这场旅程将深刻地塑造我们的社会结构，重新定义谁控制着我们最私人的信息，并让消费者重新掌控自己的数据。

The proposition's success will let consumers opt out of data collection in a powerful way, Mactaggart said in an interview, supporting companies with business models that don't require data collection to turn a profit. Those businesses will "feast on the companies whose model is pervasive tracking," Mactaggart said.

麦克塔格特在一次采访中表示，这一提议的成功将让消费者以一种强有力的方式选择退出数据收集，从而支持那些不需要数据收集就能盈利的商业模式的公司。麦克塔格特说，这些公司将大饱眼福，因为这些公司的模式是无处不在的跟踪。

The measure met with some criticism from privacy advocates who said it didn't go far enough and that it entrenched regulations that could force lower income consumers to let companies collect their data in exchange for paying less for online services. Small-business groups said new regulations would be too onerous for small companies still struggling to recover from the economic damage caused by the coronavirus pandemic.

这项措施遭到了隐私权倡导者的一些批评，他们表示，这项措施做得还不够深入，而且它根深蒂固的规定可能会迫使低收入消费者让公司收集他们的数据，以换取为在线服务支付更低的费用。小企业团体表示，对于仍在努力从冠状病毒大流行造成的经济损失中恢复过来的小公司来说，新规定将过于繁重。

A sweeping privacy law passed by the state legislature in 2018 gave Californians the right to request records of data that companies have collected about them, or ask the companies to delete the data or refrain from selling it. The law reverberated through the US, and some tech companies opted to offer the rights it outlined to all US users. Several other states considered similar laws, and some passed them.

2018年，州立法机构通过了一项全面的隐私法，赋予加州人要求公司收集的有关他们的数据的记录的权利，或者要求公司删除这些数据或禁止出售这些数据的权利。这项法律在美国引起了反响，一些科技公司选择向所有美国用户提供它概述的权利。其他几个州考虑了类似的法律，有些州通过了这些法律。

Proposition 24 will create a new state agency to enforce the law, which is currently the job of the California Department of Justice.

第24号提案将创建一个新的州机构来执行这项法律，这目前是加州司法部的工作。

Mactaggart said that as California's attorney general began writing regulations for enforcing the 2018 law, it became clear that major social media and internet companies wouldn't be required to stop targeted ads. They didn't consider the ads to be a "sale" of user data, even though they made money from the transactions.

麦克塔格特表示，随着加州总检察长开始起草执行2018年法律的规定，主要社交媒体和互联网公司显然不会被要求停止定向广告。尽管他们从交易中赚钱，但他们并不认为这些广告是对用户数据的销售。

Mactaggart's group got Proposition 24 on the ballot to close that loophole, he said, by letting users ask companies not to "share" their data.

他说，麦克塔格特的团队让第24号提案通过允许用户要求公司不要分享他们的数据，从而堵住了这个漏洞。

Proposition 24 will also likely set in motion an effort to create a system to let internet users automatically tell websites not to collect their data. Many websites show a pop-up to new visitors letting them either accept their tracking policies or go into a settings section and uncheck several boxes to opt out.

24号提案还可能启动一项努力，创建一种系统，让互联网用户自动告诉网站不要收集他们的数据。许多网站会向新访问者显示一个弹出窗口，让他们要么接受自己的跟踪策略，要么进入设置部分，取消选中几个框以选择退出。

Other sites make it even more complicated, offering a list of several third-party web tracking services that users can visit individually to opt out of their data tracking.

其他网站让事情变得更加复杂，它们提供了几个第三方网络跟踪服务的列表，用户可以单独访问这些服务，以选择退出他们的数据跟踪。

Mactaggart said he struggles with these user interfaces himself. Proposition 24 could push website owners to streamline the user experience, forcing them to either display a simple "do not sell my data" button, or to honor a setting on the user's web browser that tells websites not to sell their data.

麦克塔格特说，他自己也在为这些用户界面而苦苦挣扎。24号提案可能会推动网站所有者简化用户体验，迫使他们要么显示一个简单的不要出售我的数据的按钮，要么遵守用户网络浏览器上的一项设置，该设置告诉网站不要出售他们的数据。

That setting, and a standard that would let websites automatically detect and defer to it, doesn't yet exist. But Mactaggart said Proposition 24 will prompt browser makers to create it to help tech companies comply with the law.

这一设置，以及让网站自动检测并遵守它的标准，目前还不存在。但麦克塔格特表示，24号提案将促使浏览器制造商创建它，以帮助科技公司遵守法律。

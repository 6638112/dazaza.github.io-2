亚马逊因使用商家数据而面临欧盟反垄断指控：来源

2020-11-10 22:26:43

BRUSSELS (Reuters) - The European Union has charged Amazon with damaging retail competition, accusing the U.S. company of using its size, power and data to give it an unfair advantage over smaller merchants that sell on its online platform.

布鲁塞尔(路透社)-欧盟指控亚马逊破坏零售竞争，指责这家美国公司利用其规模、实力和数据，使其相对于在其在线平台上销售的较小商家拥有不公平的优势。

The move on Tuesday by competition chief Margrethe Vestager, the latest European salvo against U.S. tech giants, comes at a time when the COVID-19 pandemic has amplified Amazon’s role in the global economy, with online sales soaring in lockdowns.

周二，亚马逊竞争主管玛格丽特·维斯特格(Margrethe Vestager)采取了这一举措，这是欧洲针对美国科技巨头的最新一轮攻势。与此同时，新冠肺炎疫情放大了亚马逊在全球经济中的作用，在线销售额在封锁中飙升。

The European Commission has been investigating Amazon’s dual role - as a marketplace for merchants and also a rival seller.

欧盟委员会一直在调查亚马逊的双重角色--既是商家的市场，也是竞争对手的卖家。

The regulator looked into how the company collects data on competitors that sell on its platform, offering everything from electronics and toys to food and kitchenware. It says that Amazon uses that sensitive information, which shows what is proving popular or not, to better target its own products.

监管机构调查了该公司如何收集在其平台上销售的竞争对手的数据，提供从电子产品和玩具到食品和厨房用具的各种产品。该公司表示，亚马逊使用这些敏感信息来更好地瞄准自己的产品，这些信息显示了什么是受欢迎的，什么是不受欢迎的。

“The use of these data allows Amazon to focus on the sale of the best-selling products and it marginalises third-party sellers and caps their ability to grow,” European Competition Commissioner Vestager told a news conference.

欧盟竞争事务专员维斯特格在新闻发布会上表示：“这些数据的使用使亚马逊能够专注于畅销产品的销售，这使第三方卖家处于边缘地位，限制了他们的增长能力。”

Vestager, who has a reputation of being one of the world’s toughest antitrust enforcers, said that regulators had to ensure that dual-role platforms with market power, such as Amazon, did not distort competition.

以全球最严厉的反垄断执法者之一著称的维斯特格表示，监管机构必须确保亚马逊等具有市场支配力的双重角色平台不会扭曲竞争。

“Amazon represents less than 1% of the global retail market, and there are larger retailers in every country in which we operate,” the company said.

“亚马逊在全球零售市场的份额不到1%，在我们开展业务的每个国家都有规模更大的零售商，”该公司表示。

The charges are the latest example of how watchdogs around the world, led by Europe, are grappling with the challenges of regulating Big Tech, companies that have achieved unprecedented dominance in their fields and command vast troves of user data.

这些指控是以欧洲为首的世界各地监管机构正在努力应对监管大型科技公司(Big Tech)挑战的最新例证。大型科技公司在各自领域取得了前所未有的主导地位，掌握着海量的用户数据。

A final EU decision could come next year. Amazon faces being fined up to 10% of its global turnover if found guilty of breaching antitrust rules. However it can avoid a hefty penalty and a finding of wrongdoing by offering concessions to settle.

欧盟的最终决定可能会在明年做出。如果亚马逊被判违反反垄断法，将面临高达其全球营业额10%的罚款。然而，它可以通过提供和解让步来避免重罚和不当行为的发现。

The EU competition enforcer has been investigating Amazon since July last year after rival traders voiced their grievances. The regulator said the charges related to Amazon’s activities in France and Germany, its two biggest markets in Europe and where it is the dominant player.

欧盟竞争执法者自去年7月以来一直在调查亚马逊，此前竞争对手的交易员表达了他们的不满。该监管机构表示，这些指控与亚马逊在法国和德国的活动有关，这两个市场是亚马逊在欧洲最大的两个市场，亚马逊在这两个市场占据主导地位。

The case focuses on its use of merchant data on its platform. Vestager said her officials had trawled 80 million transactions and reviewed 100 million products on Amazon’s platform to put the case together.

此案的焦点是它在其平台上使用商家数据。维斯特格说，她的官员在亚马逊的平台上收集了8000万笔交易，审查了1亿种产品，以整理案件。

The European Commission also opened a new investigation into Amazon on Tuesday, over the possible preferential treatment of its own retail offers and those of marketplace sellers that use Amazon’s logistics and delivery services.

欧盟委员会(European Commission)周二还对亚马逊展开了新的调查，原因是亚马逊自己的零售产品以及使用亚马逊物流和送货服务的市场卖家可能受到优惠待遇。

That probe will look into the possible preferential treatment of Amazon’s own retail offers and those of marketplace sellers that use Amazon’s logistics and delivery services.

这项调查将调查亚马逊自己的零售优惠以及使用亚马逊物流和送货服务的市场卖家可能获得的优惠待遇。

Regulators will investigate the criteria the company uses to select winners of its “buy box”, which allows customers to add items from a specific retailer directly into their shopping carts.

监管机构将调查该公司用来选择“购买盒子”(Buy Box)获胜者的标准。“购买盒子”允许顾客将特定零售商的商品直接添加到他们的购物车中。

“Its rules should not artificially favour Amazon’s own retail offers or advantage the offers of retailers using Amazon’s logistics and delivery services,” Vestager said, citing the importance of e-commerce, an area which has gained importance with the COVID-19 pandemic.

维斯特格说：“它的规则不应该人为地偏袒亚马逊自己的零售产品，或者利用亚马逊物流和送货服务的零售商的产品。”他提到了电子商务的重要性，这一领域随着新冠肺炎疫情的爆发而变得越来越重要。

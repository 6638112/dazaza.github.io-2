用温水而不是冷水冷却红热钢材可提高钢材质量

2020-11-15 06:17:12

Ph.D. student Camila Gomez mimicked the cooling process of Tata Steel's blast furnaces in the lab and found out that it's better to cool with warmer water. Cooling red-hot after it has been flat-rolled into slabs of the desired thickness is a rather delicate operation. Ph.D. candidate Camila Gomez copied the cooling process of Tata Steel's blast furnaces in her lab, and learned that using warmer water can be better.

博士生卡米拉·戈麦斯(Camila Gomez)在实验室里模仿了塔塔钢铁(Tata Steel)高炉的冷却过程，发现最好用更温暖的水降温。在平轧成所需厚度的板坯后进行红热冷却是一项相当精细的操作。博士生卡米拉·戈麦斯(Camila Gomez)在她的实验室里模仿了塔塔钢铁(Tata Steel)高炉的冷却过程，并了解到使用更温暖的水可以更好。

In the blast furnaces at Tata Steel in IJmuiden, thick slabs of steel with a temperature of around 1200 degrees Celsius are flat-rolled in various stages from slabs with a thickness of approximately 20 centimeters to just a few centimeters. Within a few seconds, these slabs have to be cooled to such an extent that they can be rolled into something resembling a huge toilet paper roll.

在IJmuiden的塔塔钢铁公司的高炉里，温度在1200摄氏度左右的厚钢板坯从大约20厘米厚的板坯到只有几厘米厚的板坯，分不同阶段进行平轧。在几秒钟内，这些厚板必须冷却到可以卷成一个巨大的厕纸卷的程度。

In order to cool the steel, the slabs pass underneath water flowing at high , Camila Gomez explains. "The water that falls on the steel starts to boil and rapidly extracts heat from the material. The exact speed and uniformity with which the slab cools will determine the material's eventual characteristics, so it's a rather delicate process."

卡米拉·戈麦斯(Camila Gomez)解释说，为了冷却钢材，厚板会在水流很高的情况下通过。落在钢材上的水开始沸腾，并迅速从材料中吸收热量。板坯冷却的准确速度和均匀程度将决定材料的最终特性，因此这是一个相当微妙的过程。

During the rolling process, the steel slabs grow in length from 20 to more than 200 meters, which is also why the speed at which they move increases ten-fold. "At the end, the steel passes through the jets of water at a speed of almost 80 kilometers per hour; since the cooling process thus takes place at an extremely high speed, it's difficult to study it at the factory."

在轧制过程中，钢板的长度从20米增长到200多米，这也是为什么它们的移动速度增加了十倍。最后，钢材以每小时近80公里的速度通过水柱；由于冷却过程是以极高的速度进行的，因此很难在工厂进行研究。

Still, in order to better adjust the industrial process to the production of new types of steel, it's important to know exactly what happens to the cooling water near the steel's surface. So Gomez, who was born in Argentina and emigrated in Spain at the age of 10, was asked within a collaboration between the NWO, Tata Steel and TU/e to make a test setup to analyze in detail how the cooling water starts to boil once it comes into contact with hot steel.

尽管如此，为了更好地调整工业流程以适应新型钢的生产，准确地了解钢表面附近的冷却水发生了什么是很重要的。因此，出生于阿根廷并在10岁时移民到西班牙的戈麦斯，在NWO、塔塔钢铁和TU/e的合作下被要求进行一项测试，以详细分析冷却水与热钢接触后如何开始沸腾。

"Until now, there had been only experiments using stationary and slow-moving setups," Gomez says. "We've now built a setup in the lab in Gemini that allows us to move a piece of hot steel underneath a jet of water at a speed of almost 30 kilometers per hour as we make recordings close to the surface using high-speed cameras." She used a so-called borescope for this, comparable to an endoscope for internal medical examination of the body, which she placed in the jets of water.

戈麦斯说，到目前为止，只有一些实验是用静止的、移动缓慢的装置进行的。我们现在已经在双子座的实验室里建立了一套装置，可以让我们在水柱下以每小时近30公里的速度移动一块滚烫的钢材，同时我们可以用高速摄像机在接近地表的地方进行记录。为此，她使用了一种所谓的内窥镜，相当于用来对身体进行内部医疗检查的内窥镜，她把它放在了水柱里。

She found that the cooling water can come into contact with steel with a temperature of no less than 900 degrees. "That was a kind of mystery, because you would expect the water to heat up rapidly to 300 degrees, after which it evaporates in an explosive manner. We've now seen that vapor bubble formation does indeed occur locally, but that these bubbles subsequently implode because the cold water falls down on them. That occurs up to 40,000 times per second—a process you can detect only when you record at a high frame rate and study those images one by one."

她发现，冷却水可以与温度不低于900度的钢材接触。这是一个谜，因为你会认为水会迅速升温到300摄氏度，之后就会以爆炸性的方式蒸发。我们现在已经看到，蒸汽泡的形成确实发生在局部，但这些气泡随后会内爆，因为冷水落在它们上面。这一过程每秒发生多达4万次--只有当你以高帧速率记录并逐个研究这些图像时，你才能检测到这一过程。

That discovery is the greatest scientific result of her doctoral project, as far as she's concerned. However, on top of that, there is another discovery that might have serious practical implications. When the surface does not cool uniformly due to constant local vapor explosions, it results in imperfections and steel of lesser quality. "Therefore, you want to cool the steel as evenly as possible. Our measurements show that warmer allows you to create a stable water vapor layer above the steel. Admittedly, that slows down the process, but it does produce a better result."

就她而言，这一发现是她博士项目最伟大的科学成果。然而，最重要的是，还有另一项可能具有严重实际意义的发现。当表面由于持续的局部蒸汽爆炸而没有均匀冷却时，就会导致缺陷和钢材质量下降。因此，你想要尽可能均匀地冷却钢材。我们的测量表明，变暖可以让你在钢材上方形成一个稳定的水蒸汽层。诚然，这会放慢进程，但它确实会产生更好的结果。

When Gomez raised the water temperature from 25 to 60 degrees, she was able to cool the steel in her test setup a further 50 degrees without entering the unstable regime. This is knowledge that could be of high value to steel manufacturers, she says, since the temperature can be easily adjusted without having to alter the entire production line.

How did Neanderthals and other ancient humans learn to count?

Francesco d’Errico, an archaeologist at the University of Bordeaux, France, has an idea about the marks. He has examined many ancient carved artefacts during his career, and he thinks that the hyena bone — found in the 1970s at the site of Les Pradelles near Angoulême — stands out as unusual. Although ancient carved artefacts are often interpreted as artworks, the Les Pradelles bone seems to have been more functional, says D’Errico.

He argues that it might encode numerical information. And if that’s correct, anatomically modern humans might not have been alone in developing a system of numerical notations: Neanderthals might have begun to do so, too 1.

他认为它可能会编码数字信息。如果这是正确的，解剖学上现代人类可能并不是一个人在开发一个数字符号系统方面是：尼安德特人可能已经开始这样做，这也是如此。

When D’Errico published his ideas in 2018, he was venturing into territory that few scientists had explored: the ancient roots of numbers. “The origin of numbers is still a relatively vacant niche in scientific research,” says Russell Gray, an evolutionary biologist at the Max Planck Institute for Evolutionary Anthropology in Leipzig, Germany. Researchers don’t even agree, at times, on what numbers are, although a 2017 study 2 defined them as discrete entities with exact values that are represented by symbols in the form of words and signs.

Now the origin of numbers is attracting increasing attention as researchers from a variety of fields address the problem from different vantage points.

现在，数字的起源是由于各种领域的研究人员引起了越来越关注的问题，从各种领域解决了不同的有利点的问题。

Cognitive scientists, anthropologists and psychologists are looking at contemporary cultures to understand differences among existing number systems — defined as the symbols that a society uses for counting and manipulating numbers. Their hope is that clues buried in modern systems might illuminate details of their origins. Meanwhile, archaeologists have begun looking for evidence of ancient numerical notations, and evolutionary biologists with an interest in language are exploring the deep origins of number words. These studies have spurred researchers to formulate some of the first detailed hypotheses for the prehistoric development of number systems.

认知科学家，人类学家和心理学家正在寻找当代文化，了解现有数量系统之间的差异 - 定义为社会用于计数和操纵数字的符号。他们的希望是埋藏在现代系统中的线索可能会照亮他们起源的细节。与此同时，考古学家已经开始寻找古代数值符号的证据，并且具有语言感兴趣的进化生物学家正在探索数字词的深度起源。这些研究已经刺激了研究人员，以制定史前系统史前发展的第一个详细假设。

And an infusion of funding will stimulate more studies in this area. This year, an international research team with a €10-million (US$11.9-million) grant from the European Research Council will start to test different hypotheses, as part of a broader effort to study when, why and how number systems appeared and spread around the world. The project, called the Evolution of Cognitive Tools for Quantification (QUANTA), might even provide insights into whether number systems are unique to anatomically modern humans, or were conceivably present in nascent form in Neanderthals.

Although researchers once thought that humans were the only species with a sense of quantity, studies since the mid-twentieth century have revealed that many animals share the ability. For instance, fish, bees and newborn chicks 3 can instantly recognize quantities up to four, a skill known as subitizing. Some animals are also capable of ‘large-quantity discrimination’: they can appreciate the difference between two large quantities if they are distinct enough. Creatures with this skill could, for example, distinguish 10 objects from 20 objects, but not 20 from 21. Six-month-old human infants also show a similar appreciation of quantity, even before they have had significant exposure to human culture or language.

虽然研究人员曾经认为人类是唯一具有数量感，自二十世纪中期的研究的物种，但自二十世纪中旬透露了许多动物的能力。例如，鱼类，蜜蜂和新生儿小鸡3可以立即识别最多四个，一种称为副的技能。一些动物也能够“大量歧视”：如果它们截然不同，它们可以理解两种大量之间的差异。例如，具有这种技能的生物可以区分来自20个物体的10个物体，但是从21中没有20个。六个月的人类婴儿也表现出类似的数量的升值，甚至在他们对人类文化或语言都有显着暴露之前。

What all of this suggests, says Andreas Nieder, a neuroscientist at the University of Tübingen, Germany, is that humans have an innate appreciation of numbers. That arose through evolutionary processes such as natural selection, he says, because it would have carried adaptive benefits.

德国大学的神经科学家Andreas Nieder表示，德国大学的神经科学家都是人类对数量的先天升值。他说，通过自然选择等进化过程来源，因为它会带来自适应益处。

Others interpret the evidence differently. Rafael Núñez, a cognitive scientist at the University of California, San Diego, and one of the leaders of QUANTA, accepts that many animals might have an innate appreciation of quantity. However, he argues that the human perception of numbers is typically much more sophisticated, and can’t have arisen through a process such as natural selection. Instead, many aspects of numbers, such as the spoken words and written signs that are used to represent them, must be produced by cultural evolution — a process in which individuals learn through imitation or formal teaching to adopt a new skill (such as how to use a tool).

其他人以不同的方式解释证据。加州大学的认知科学家RafaelNúñez和Quanta领导者之一，接受了许多动物可能会有天生的数量升值。然而，他认为，人类对数字的感知通常更复杂，并且不能通过自然选择等过程出现。相反，必须通过文化演进来制作数量的许多方面，例如用于表示它们的口语和书面标志 - 一个流程通过模仿或正式教学来采用新技能，以便个人学习（例如如何使用工具）。

Although many animals have culture, one that involves numbers is essentially unique to humans. A handful of chimpanzees have been taught in captivity to use abstract symbols to represent quantities, but neither chimps nor any other non-human species use such symbols in the natural world. Núñez suggests that a distinction should therefore be made between what he has dubbed the innate ‘quantical’ cognition seen in animals and the learnt ‘numerical’ cognition seen in humans 2.

虽然许多动物有文化，但涉及数字的人对人类基本上是独一无二的。少量的黑猩猩以囚禁教授，以使用抽象符号来表示数量，但既不是黑猩猩也不是任何其他非人类物种在自然界中使用这种符号。因此，因此应该在被称为动物中看到的天生的“数值”认知和人类2中所看到的学习“数值”认知之间的区别。

But not everyone agrees. Nieder argues that neurological studies show clear similarities between the way in which quantities are processed in the brains of non-human animals and how the human brain processes numbers. He says that it is misleading to draw too firm a line between the two behaviours 4, although he agrees that human numerical abilities are much more advanced than those of any other animal. “No [non-human] animal is able to truly represent number symbols,” he says.

但不是每个人都同意。 Nieder认为神经学研究表明，在非人动物的大脑中处理量的方式以及人脑过程的数量之间的方式进行了明确的相似性。他说，在两个行为4之间绘制过于坚定的线条误导，尽管他同意人类数值能力比任何其他动物的数量更进一步更先进。 “没有[非人类]动物能够真正代表数字符号，”他说。

D’Errico’s analysis of the Les Pradelles bone could help to provide some insights into how the earliest stages of number systems took shape. He studied the nine notches under a microscope, and says that their shapes, depths and other details are so alike that all seem to have been made using the same stone tool, held in the same way. This suggests that all were made by one individual in a single session lasting perhaps a few minutes or hours. (At some other time, eight much shallower marks were carved on the bone, too.)

D'Errico对LES Pradelles Bone的分析有助于为数字系统最早阶段的形状提供一些见解。他在显微镜下研究了九个凹口，并说它们的形状，深度和其他细节如此相似，似乎已经使用相同的石刀制成，以相同的方式保持。这表明所有人都在单个会议中由一个人持续几分钟或几个小时。 （曾在其他时间，八个较大的标记也被雕刻在骨头上。）

However, D’Errico doesn’t think that this individual intended to produce a decorative pattern because the marks are uneven. For comparison, he has analysed the seven notches on a 40,000-year-old raven bone from a site of Neanderthal occupation in Crimea. Statistical analysis shows that the notches on this bone are spaced with the same sort of regularity seen when modern volunteers are given a similar bone and asked to mark it with equally spaced notches 5. But this type of analysis also shows that the marks on the Les Pradelles bone lack such regularity. That observation — and the fact that the notches were generated in a single session — led D’Errico to consider that they might have been merely functional, providing a record of numerical information.

然而，D'Errico并不认为这个个人旨在产生装饰模式，因为标记是不均匀的。相比之下，他分析了来自克里米亚尼安德特职业的40,000岁的乌鸦骨骼上的七个凹口。统计分析表明，当现代志愿者给出类似的骨骼时，这种骨骼上的凹口与相同类型的规律间隔开，并要求用同等间隔的凹口5标记。但这种类型的分析也表明了LES上的标记普拉德尔骨头缺乏这样的规律性。该观察 - 以及在单个会议中生成凹口的事实 - LED D'Errico认为它们可能仅仅是功能，提供了数字信息的记录。

The Les Pradelles bone is not an isolated find. For instance, during excavations at Border Cave in South Africa, archaeologists discovered an approximately 42,000-year-old baboon fibula that was also marked with notches. D’Errico suspects that anatomically modern humans living there at the time used the bone to record numerical information. In the case of this bone, microscopic analysis of its 29 notches suggests they were carved using four distinct tools and so represent four counting events, which D’Errico thinks took place on four separate occasions 1. Moreover, he says that discoveries over the past 20 years show that ancient humans began producing abstract engravings, which hint at sophisticated cognition, hundreds of thousands of years earlier than was once thought.

Les Pradelles骨不是孤立的发现。例如，在南非边境洞穴的挖掘过程中，考古学家发现了大约42,000岁的狒狒腓骨，也标有缺口。 D'Errico怀疑当时在那里生活的解剖学现代人类使用骨骼记录数值信息。在这种骨骼的情况下，其29个缺口的微观分析表明它们是使用四个不同工具雕刻的，因此代表四个计数事件，D'Errico认为在四个独立的场合进行了1.此外，他说过去的发现20年来表明，古代人类开始生产抽象雕刻，其中暗示了经过复杂的认知，数十万年前的曾经思考。

In the light of these discoveries, D’Errico has developed a scenario to explain how number systems might have arisen through the very act of producing such artefacts. His hypothesis is one of only two published so far for the prehistoric origin of numbers.

鉴于这些发现，D'Errico开发了一种方法来解释通过生产这些人工制品的行为可能产生的数字系统。他的假设是迄今为止史前数量的两个发布之一。

It all started by accident, he suggests, as early hominins unintentionally left marks on bones while they were butchering animal carcasses. Later, the hominins made a cognitive leap when they realized that they could deliberately mark bones to produce abstract designs — such as those seen on an approximately 430,000-year-old shell found in Trinil, Indonesia 6. At some point after that, another leap occurred: individual marks began to take on meaning, with some of them perhaps encoding numerical information. The Les Pradelles hyena bone is potentially the earliest known example of this type of mark-making, says D’Errico. He thinks that with further leaps, or what he dubs cultural exaptations, such notches eventually led to the invention of number signs such as 1, 2 and 3 7.

他暗示了这一切都是偶然的，因为他们在屠宰动物尸体时，早期的Hominins无意中留下了骨头上的标记。后来，当他们意识到他们故意标记骨头以产生抽象设计时，他们的母亲是一种认知的飞跃 - 例如在印度尼西亚特立尼亚特立尔的约430,000岁的壳牌上看到的那些。在某些时候，另一个飞跃了发生：个人标记开始承担意义，其中一些可能编码数字信息。 D'Errico说，LES Pradelles Hyena Bone可能是这种标志的最早已知的例子。他认为，通过进一步的跨利赛，或者他被设计为文化展开，这种凹口最终导致了数字标志，如1,2和3 7。

D’Errico acknowledges that there are gaps in this scenario. It isn’t clear what cultural or social factors might have encouraged ancient hominins to begin marking bones or other artefacts deliberately, or to then harness those marks to record numerical information. QUANTA will use data from anthropology, cognitive science, linguistics and archaeology to better understand those social factors, says D’Errico, who is one of the project’s four principal investigators.

D'Errico承认这种情况存在差距。尚不清楚文化或社会因素可能会鼓励古代母语故意开始标记骨骼或其他人工制品，或者为了记录这些标记来记录数值信息。 Quanta将使用人类学，认知科学，语言学和考古学的数据来更好地了解这些社会因素，他是该项目的四个主要调查人员之一。

However, QUANTA researcher Núñez, along with some researchers who are not involved in the project, cautions that ancient artefacts such as the Les Pradelles bone are challenging to interpret. Karenleigh Overmann, a cognitive archaeologist at the University of Colorado in Colorado Springs, highlights those difficulties by citing the example of message sticks used by Aboriginal Australians. These sticks, which are typically flattened or cylindrical lengths of wood, are adorned with notches that might look as though they encode numerical information — but many do not.

然而，Quanta研究员Núñez以及一些没有参与该项目的研究人员，引人注意，古代人工制品如Les Pradelles Bone的挑战性挑战。科罗拉多州科罗拉多大学的认知考古学家Karenleigh Overmann突出了那些难以引用原住民澳大利亚人使用的消息棒的例子。这些通常扁平或圆柱形木材的棍子装饰有可能看起来好像编码数字信息的缺口 - 但许多人没有。

Piers Kelly, a linguistic anthropologist at the University of New England in Armidale, Australia, who conducted a review of message sticks 8, agrees with Overmann’s point. He says that some message sticks are carved with tally-like marks, but these often act as a visual memory aid to help a messenger recall details of the message they are delivering. “They call to mind the act of recounting a narrative rather than accounting a quantity,” says Kelly.

Wunyungar, an Aboriginal Australian who is a member of the Gooreng Gooreng and Wakka Wakka communities, says that the sticks might transmit one of any number of distinct messages. “Some are used for trading — for foods, tools or weapons,” he says. “Others might carry messages of peace after war.”

Wunyungar是一位土着澳大利亚·澳大利亚，澳大利亚是Gooreng Gooreng和Wakka Wakka社区的成员，他说，棍棒可能会传送任何数量不同的消息之一。 “有些人用于交易 - 用于食品，工具或武器，”他说。 “其他人可能会在战争后携带和平的信息。”

Overmann has developed her own hypothesis to explain how number systems might have emerged in prehistory — a task made easier by the fact that a wide variety of number systems are still in use around the world. For example, linguists Claire Bowern and Jason Zentz at Yale University in New Haven, Connecticut, reported in a 2012 survey that 139 Aboriginal Australian languages have an upper limit of ‘three’ or ‘four’ for specific numerals. Some of those languages use natural quantifiers such as ‘several’ and ‘many’ to indicate higher values 9. There is even one group, the Pirahã people of the Brazilian Amazon, that is sometimes claimed not to use numbers at all 10.

Overmann开发了自己的假设，解释了在史前的系统中可能已经出现的数字系统 - 这是一项在世界各地仍在使用各种各样的数字系统方面更容易。例如，康涅狄格州新避风港耶鲁大学的语言学家Claire Bowern和Jason Zentz于2012年报告的2012年调查中，139个土着澳大利亚语言具有特定数字的“三”或“四”的上限。其中一些语言使用自然量词，如“几个”和“很多”表示更高的值9.甚至有一个组，巴西亚马逊的Pirahã人有时声称不在所有10中使用数字。

Overmann and other researchers stress that there’s nothing intellectually lacking about societies that use relatively simple number systems. But she wondered whether such societies might provide clues about the social pressures that drive the development of more elaborate number systems.

Overmann和其他研究人员强调，没有任何智力上缺乏使用相对简单的数字系统的社会。但她想知道这些社会是否可以提供关于推动更多详细数字系统发展的社会压力的线索。

In a 2013 study 11, Overmann analysed anthropological data relating to 33 contemporary hunter-gatherer societies across the world. She discovered that those with simple number systems (an upper limit not much higher than ‘four’) often had few material possessions, such as weapons, tools or jewellery. Those with elaborate systems (an upper numeral limit much higher than ‘four’) always had a richer array of possessions. The evidence suggested to Overmann that societies might need a variety of material possessions if they are to develop such number systems.

在2013年研究11中，奥曼分析了世界各地33个当代猎人会员社团的人类学数据。她发现，数字系统简单的人（上限不高于'四'）通常有很少的物质财产，例如武器，工具或珠宝。那些具有精心制作的系统（高于“四”的上数字限制）总是有一个更丰富的财产。如果他们要开发这样的数字系统，那么证据表明奥姆曼可能需要各种材料财产。

In societies with complex number systems, there were clues to how those systems developed. Significantly, Overmann noted that it was common for these societies to use quinary (base 5), decimal or vigesimal (base 20) systems. This suggested to her that many number systems began with a finger-counting stage.

在具有复杂数字系统的社会中，有线索是如何发展的。值得注意的是，Overmann指出，这些社会是常见的，使用Quiary（基座5），小数或剧力（基地20）系统。这向她建议，许多数字系统开始具有指计数阶段。

This finger-counting stage is important, according to Overmann. She is an advocate of material engagement theory (MET), a framework devised about a decade ago by cognitive archaeologist Lambros Malafouris at the University of Oxford, UK 12. MET maintains that the mind extends beyond the brain and into objects, such as tools or even a person’s fingers. This extension allows ideas to be realized in physical form; so, in the case of counting, MET suggests that the mental conceptualization of numbers can include the fingers. That makes numbers more tangible and easier to add or subtract.

根据Overmann的说法，这种手指计数阶段很重要。她是倡导材料参与理论（遇见），这是一个框架，该框架在英国牛津大学的认知考古学家Lambros Malafouris展出了大约十年前。见过认为，心灵延伸到大脑之外，并进入物体，如工具或工具甚至是一个人的手指。此扩展允许以物理形式实现的想法;因此，在计数的情况下，满足表明数字的心理概念化可以包括手指。这使得数字更有形，更容易添加或减去。

The societies that moved beyond finger-counting did so, argues Overmann, because they developed a clearer social need for numbers. Perhaps most obviously, a society with more material possessions has a greater need to count (and to count much higher than ‘four’) to keep track of objects.

超越手指计数的社会是如此讨论，因为他们开发了更清晰的数字的社会需求。也许最明显的是，一个具有更多重要性财产的社会更需要计数（并且计算远高于“四”）以跟踪对象。

Overmann thinks MET implies that there is another way in which material possessions are necessary for the elaboration of number systems. An artefact such as a tally stick also becomes an extension of the mind, and the act of marking tally notches on the stick helps to anchor and stabilize numbers as someone counts. These aids could have been crucial to the process through which humans first began counting up to large numbers 13.

奥姆曼认为遇见意味着还有另一种方式，其中材料财产是制定数字系统所必需的。诸如Tally棒的人工制品也成为心灵的延伸，并且标记杆上的标记凹口的行为有助于锚定和稳定数量。这些艾滋病可能对人类首先开始抵消大量13的过程至关重要。

Eventually, says Overmann, some societies moved beyond tally sticks. This first happened in Mesopotamia around the time when cities emerged there, creating an even greater need for numbers to keep track of resources and people. Archaeological evidence suggests that by 5,500 years ago, some Mesopotamians had begun using small clay tokens as counting aids.

最终，奥曼说，一些社会超越了棍棒。这首先发生在距离城市出现的城市的梅索多塔岛，创造了更大的数字，以跟踪资源和人。考古证据表明，在5500年前，一些中索托族人开始使用小粘土代币作为计数艾滋病。

According to Overmann, MET suggests that these tokens were also extensions of the mind, and that they fostered the emergence of new numerical properties. In particular, the shapes of tokens came to represent different values: 10 small cone tokens were equivalent to a sphere token, and 6 spheres were equivalent to a large cone token. The existence of large cones, each equivalent to 60 small cones, allowed the Mesopotamians to count into the thousands using relatively few tokens.

据奥曼称，遇见表明这些令牌也是思想的延伸，并且他们促进了新的数值的出现。特别地，令牌的形状来表示不同的值：10个小锥形令牌等同于球体令牌，并且6个球体相当于大锥形令牌。大锥体的存在，每个锥体相当于60个小锥体，允许中索托纳斯人使用相对较少的令牌计算成千上万。

Andrea Bender, a psychologist at the University of Bergen in Norway and another leader of the QUANTA project, says that the team members plan to gather and analyse large amounts of data relating to the world’s numeral systems. That should allow them to test Overmann’s hypothesis that body parts and artefacts might have helped societies to develop number systems that ultimately count into the thousands and higher. But Bender says she and her colleagues are not presupposing that Overmann’s MET-based ideas are correct.

Andrea Bender是挪威卑尔根大学的心理学家以及Quanta项目的另一个领导者表示，团队成员计划收集和分析与世界数字系统有关的大量数据。这应该允许他们测试奥姆曼的假设，即身体部位和人工制品可能有助于社团开发最终计算成千上万的数字系统。但弯腰表示，她和她的同事们没有预先展示奥斯曼的欧元的思想是正确的。

Others are more enthusiastic. Karim Zahidi, a philosopher at the University of Antwerp in Belgium, says that although Overmann’s scenario is still incomplete, it has real potential to explain the development of the elaborate number systems in use today.

其他人更热情。在比利时安特卫普大学的哲学家Karim Zahidi说，虽然Overmann的情景仍然不完整，但它有可能解释今天使用的精心设计系统的发展。

Overmann acknowledges that her hypothesis is silent on one issue: when in prehistory human societies began developing number systems. Linguistics might offer some help here. One line of evidence suggests that number words could have a history stretching back at least tens of thousands of years.

Overmann承认她的假设在一个问题上是沉默的：当在史前人类社会中开始开发数字系统。语言学可能会在这里提供一些帮助。一行证据表明，数字词可以让历史延伸至少数千年的历史。

Evolutionary biologist Mark Pagel at the University of Reading, UK, and his colleagues have spent many years exploring the history of words in extant language families, with the aid of computational tools that they initially developed to study biological evolution. Essentially, words are treated as entities that either remain stable or are outcompeted and replaced as languages spread and diversify. For instance, English ‘water’ and German ‘wasser’ are clearly related, making them cognates that derive from the same ancient word — an example of stability. But English ‘hand’ is distinct from Spanish ‘mano’ — evidence of word replacement at some time in the past. By assessing how frequently such replacement events occur over long periods, it is possible to estimate rates of change and to infer how old words are.

在英国阅读大学的进化生物学家标记帕尔斯和他的同事们借助他们最初开发学习生物学进化的计算工具探索了现存语言家庭中的文字史。基本上，单词被视为仍然稳定的实体，或者被脱颖而出并被置于传播和多样化的语言。例如，英语“水”和德语“瓦斯尔”显然是相关的，使他们从同一个古老的单词中获得了同源 - 一个稳定的例子。但英语“手”与西班牙语“Mano”不同 - 过去的一段时间内替换的证据。通过评估长期发生这种替换事件的频率，可以估计变化率并推断出多大的单词。

Using this approach, Pagel and Andrew Meade at Reading showed that low-value number words (‘one’ to ‘five’) are among the most stable features of spoken languages 14. Indeed, they change so infrequently across language families — such as the Indo-European family, which includes many modern European and southern Asian languages — that they seem to have been stable for anywhere between 10,000 and 100,000 years.

使用这种方法，Pagel和Andrew Meade在阅读中显示，低价值数字（“一个'到'五'）是口语语言最稳定的特征。实际上，他们在语言家庭中如此不经常改变 - 如印度欧洲家庭，包括许多现代欧洲和南方亚洲语言 - 他们似乎对10,000到10万年的任何地方都稳定。

This doesn’t prove that the numbers from ‘one’ to ‘five’ derive from ancient cognates that were first spoken tens of thousands of years ago, but Pagel says it’s at least “conceivable” that a modern and a Palaeolithic Eurasian could have understood one another when it came to such number words.

这并不能证明“一个”到“五”的数字来自古代同源，这些人是千年前的第一次出现的古代同源，但Pagel表示，它至少是一个现代和古罗石欧亚人所能理解的“可以想象”彼此出现在这样的号码时。

Pagel’s work has its fans, including Gray, another of QUANTA’s leaders, but his claims are challenged by some scholars of ancient languages. Don Ringe, a historical linguist at the University of Pennsylvania in Philadelphia, says it isn’t clear that the stability of lower-number words can just be projected far back into prehistory, regardless of how stable they seem to be in recent millennia.

Pagel的工作有其粉丝，包括灰色，另一个古老的领导者，但他的索赔是由古代语言的一些学者挑战。宾夕法尼亚州大学宾夕法尼亚州的历史语言学家唐·罗德说明尚不清楚，低数量单词的稳定性可以追溯到史前，无论他们似乎如何在近千年才能稳定。

That all adds up to a slew of open questions about when and how humans first started using numbers. But despite the debate swirling around these questions, researchers agree it’s a topic that deserves a lot more attention. “Numbers are just so fundamental to everything we do,” says Gray. “It’s hard to conceive of human life without them.”

所有这些都会增加关于何时以及人类首先开始使用数字的开放问题。但是，尽管围绕这些问题遍布争论，但研究人员同意这是一个值得更多关注的主题。 “数字对我们所做的一切都很基础，”雷霆说。 “如果没有他们，很难怀孕。”

Numbers might even have gained this importance deep in prehistory. The notched baboon bone from Border Cave is worn smooth in a way that indicates that ancient humans used it over many years. “It was clearly an important item for the individual who produced it,” says D’Errico.

数字甚至可能在史前深入了解这一重要性。来自边境洞穴的缺口狒狒骨骼以一种方式磨损，这表明古代人类多年来使用它。 “这显然是制作它的个人的重要项目，”D'Errico说。

Not so for the Les Pradelles specimen, which lacks this smooth surface. If it does record numerical information, that might not have been quite as important at the time. In fact, although D’Errico and his colleagues have spent innumerable hours analysing the bone, he says it’s possible that the Neanderthal who chipped away at that hyena femur some 60,000 years ago spent very little time using it before tossing the bone aside.

不是那种Les Pradelles标本，它缺乏这种光滑的表面。如果它确实记录了数值信息，那么当时可能没有那么重要。事实上，虽然D'Errico和他的同事花了无数的时间分析骨头，但他说，在鬣狗约60,000年前将尼安德特人们在靠近骨头之前花了很少使用它。

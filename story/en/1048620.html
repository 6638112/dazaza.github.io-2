Canadian Town Derails Diesel Train to Provide Emergency Power (1998)
加拿大城镇脱轨柴油列车提供应急电源（1998）

2021-02-21 07:36:31

Even in a suburb of Montreal that’s well versed in dealing with winter storms, inclement weather can still overwhelm basic services like power, an issue with which millions of people in Texas are still currently struggling. After an especially bad ice storm in 1998, the mayor of Boucherville, Quebec, had a clever idea to borrow a diesel-electric locomotive and use it as a super-sized emergency generator.

即使在精通应对冬季暴风雨的蒙特利尔郊区，恶劣的天气仍会使基本服务（如电力）不堪重负，目前德克萨斯州仍有数百万人仍在努力解决这一问题。在1998年特别严重的冰暴之后，魁北克省Boucherville的市长有了一个聪明的主意，借用柴油电力机车并将其用作超大型应急发电机。

In most Canadian cities grabbing a snow brush and cleaning off your car before you can head to work in the morning is all part of enduring the winter months. In cities like Montreal, however, it’s not uncommon to have to first find your car in the morning after an overnight snowstorm leaves it completely buried. But, for the most part, snowstorms are mostly an inconvenience in northern Canadian cities, leaving side streets impassible until plows finish with major routes.

在大多数加拿大城市中，在您早晨不能上班之前，先抓雪刷并清理汽车，这是整个冬季的一部分。但是，在蒙特利尔这样的城市中，隔夜暴风雪将汽车完全掩埋后，早晨必须首先找到您的汽车并不少见。但是，在大多数情况下，暴风雪在加拿大北部城市大多是不便之处，直到小道上的主要路段都无法通行。

Ice storms, when temperatures result in freezing rain falling to earth instead of picturesque snowflakes, are a different story. The ice sticks to whatever it lands on and accumulates, often adding thousands of extra pounds of weight to trees or power lines and towers which eventually pushes them past their structural capacities until they collapse. That’s exactly what happened across parts of northeastern Canada over a week in January of 1998. Three successive ice storms brought down power lines and the towers supporting them that take much longer to fix, leaving over 1.5 million people without power in the middle of Winter.

当温度导致冰冻的雨落到地上而不是风景如画的雪花时，冰暴是另一回事。冰块会附着在其上并积聚的任何东西上，经常使树木或输电线和塔架增加成千上万磅的重量，最终使冰块超过其结构能力，直到它们坍塌。这就是1998年1月一周内加拿大东北部发生的一切。连续三场冰暴使电力线和塔架瘫痪，修复所需的时间更长，整个冬季中期，超过150万人无电。

A lack of power doesn’t just make it difficult for people to stay warm and feed themselves, it also makes it difficult for elected authorities and emergency personnel to plan and coordinate repairs and efforts to assist the people they represent. So while staring down endless questions about when power would potentially be restored, the mayor of Boucherville, Quebec, Francine Gadbois, came up with a clever plan to at least get the town’s city hall and emergency warming shelters up and running in the immediate future.

缺乏力量的不只是让人们很难保持温暖和养活自己，这也使得它难以当选机构和急救人员规划和协调维修和努力，协助他们所代表的人民。因此，在盯着关于何时才能恢复供电的无休止的问题时，魁北克省Boucherville市长Francine Gadbois提出了一个聪明的计划，至少要在不久的将来恢复该镇的市政厅和应急暖风庇护所并使其运转。

Modern locomotives run on diesel fuel instead of coal or wood as their steam-powered ancestors did, but the fuel is actually used to power onboard generators that create electricity to power the electric motors that drive the train’s wheels. In other words, modern locomotives are really just giant mobile generators, and Gadbois had recalled stories about them being used to provide power to small towns in rural northern Quebec several years prior. After some calls to the Canadian National Railway, the diesel locomotive M420W 3502 rolled into Boucherville to provide some much-needed assistance.

现代机车使用的是柴油，而不是像蒸汽一样的祖先，而是用柴油或木材为燃料，但实际上，这种燃料被用于为车载发电机提供动力，发电机产生电能来驱动驱动火车车轮的电动机。换句话说，现代机车实际上只是巨型的移动发电机，盖博伊斯（Gadbois）回忆起几年前有关将其用于向魁北克北部农村小城镇供电的故事。接到加拿大国家铁路的电话后，柴油机车M420W 3502驶入布歇维尔，以提供一些急需的协助。

The rail line was close to the town’s city hall, but not close enough for this unusual application, so a crane was used to lift the diesel engine off its tracks and place it on Boulevard de Montarville. From there the locomotive completed the last 1,000 feet of its journey rolling down the street under its own power while its extreme weight caused the train’s flanged wheels to cut deep tracks into the asphalt road which later had to be repaired.

铁路线靠近该镇的市政厅，但对于这种不常见的应用来说距离不够近，因此使用起重机将柴油发动机从轨道上提起并将其放在蒙塔维尔大道上。从那里开始，机车在自己的动力下完成了最后1000英尺的行程，沿着街道滚动，而其极重的重量导致火车的法兰轮将深铁道切成柏油马路，后来不得不对其进行维修。

Cables were run from the diesel engine to the nearby municipal buildings and its throttle was set to a speed which would produce roughly 375 kilowatts of power at 60 hertz (well below the locomotive’s maximum power of around 2,000 horsepower) which is the alternating frequency the North American power system runs on, and is what all of our electronics are expecting. The diesel engine produced enough electricity to power several municipal buildings at the same time allowing the local authorities to better organize their emergency and recovery planning.

电缆从柴油发动机运行到附近的市政建筑物，它的油门设置为速度，它将在60赫兹（远低于机车最大功率约为2000马力大约2000马力的最大功率）中产生大约375千瓦的速度。美国电力系统运行，并是我们所有的电子产品的期望。柴油发动机同时生产足够的电力电力，同时允许地方当局更好地组织紧急和恢复规划。

A second locomotive was also brought in with the intention of using its output to power a local high school that was to be used as an emergency warming station for local residents. But getting to the shelter would have required the engine to cross an overpass, and it was eventually decided that there was a very good chance the overpass would collapse under the weight of a 260,000-pound locomotive, leaving the city with yet another emergency to deal with, and some concerned phone calls from the Canadian National Railway. As a result, it was left parked down the street on Boulevard de Montarville as an emergency backup.

第二个机车也被带来了利用其产出来为当地高中供电，该高中将被用作当地居民的紧急变暖站。但是，到避难所将需要发动机交叉立交桥，最终决定在260,000英镑的机车的重量下，立交桥会崩溃，将城市与另一个紧急情况进行崩溃与加拿大国家铁路的一些有关电话呼叫。结果，它被留下了街上的街道，蒙塔尔维尔作为紧急备份。

When power was eventually restored to the region the two diesel engines were returned to the rails and put back to work hauling cargo, but not before being sent for repairs to damage caused to their gear cases after their unorthodox cruises through Boucherville.

当电力最终恢复到该地区时，两只柴油发动机被返回到铁轨并重新开始工作牵引货物，但在他们的非正统巡航通过Boucherville之后被送往他们的齿轮箱造成的损坏之前，并非如此。

As much as the city would have liked to keep the trains around for the next emergency (during their stay the locomotives also became quite a tourist draw with locals stopping by to have their pictures taken with the trains parked on the street) a diesel locomotive can cost well north of two million dollars and keeping one sitting idle “just in case” is a tough sell, especially when you’re borrowing someone else’s.

与城市一样，可以希望在下一个紧急情况下保持火车（在他们的逗留期间，机车也成为了一个与当地人停止的旅游画，以便将他们的照片拍摄街上的火车）柴油机机车可以北方成本良好，距离两百万美元，保持一坐在闲暇“以防万一”是一个艰难的卖出，特别是当你借用别人的时候。

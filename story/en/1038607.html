AT&T will report its fourth-quarter financial results, as well as updated numbers, in January. Stankey said the company is “pleased” with the progress toward its stated goal of 50 million U.S. subscribers and 75 million to 90 million globally by 2025. “We’re actually ahead of plan,” he said.

Stankey also addressed the game-changing move by to release its entire 2021 slate on HBO Max at the same time it is available in theaters. He described it as a “win-win-win” during Covid-19, providing theaters with films during the pandemic while also showcasing HBO Max and giving customers options.

Stankey还解决了改变游戏规则的举动，同时在影院上映的同时在HBO Max上发布了2021年的全部内容。他将其描述为Covid-19期间的“双赢”，在大流行期间向电影院提供电影，同时展示HBO Max并为客户提供选择。

The company made the dramatic decision because of the “psyche of the population” during the pandemic and the likelihood of a longer return to theaters and other public spaces. “Snowplowing all theatrical content into late-2021 and early-2022 probably isn’t going to help anybody,” he said.

该公司之所以做出如此重大的决定，是因为在大流行期间出现了“人口心理”，并且有可能更长久地返回剧院和其他公共场所。他说：“将所有戏剧内容放到2021年末和2022年初可能不会帮助任何人。”

Asked by moderator and UBS analyst John Hodulik about the blowback from Hollywood’s talent community and the exhibition business, Stankey said, “We’re all participants in a market that serves customers. The longer-term impacts are going to be dictated by what consumers wish to do.”

当主持人和瑞银分析师约翰·霍杜利克（John Hodulik）问及好莱坞人才界和展览业务的反响时，斯坦基说：“我们都是服务于客户的市场的参与者。长期影响将由消费者的意愿决定。”

He said the move acknowledges “the reality of where markets are going right now” in the movie business. “Customers have a tremendous amount of choice as to how they choose to engage with content. If we just simply sit here and say, ‘This is about whether or not people go to movie theaters,’ I think we’re missing the broader point. Today, even before made this decision, customers could go watch great, two-hour content on a variety of competitive services … some of them very significant releases. Customers are going to drive what happens in a market, ultimately.”

他说，此举承认电影业“市场正在走向的现实”。 “客户在如何选择与内容互动方面拥有大量选择。如果我们只是简单地坐在这里说：“这是关于人们是否去电影院的事情，”我想我们错过了更广泛的意义。如今，即使在做出此决定之前，客户仍可以观看两小时的精彩内容，包括各种竞争性服务……其中一些是非常重要的版本。客户最终将推动市场中发生的事情。”

As to the post-pandemic world and whether studio release patterns will return to some semblance of the previous ones, Stankey said the company will “adjust and work the model differently.” Overall, though “having choice” will remain a constant, he predicted. “We’re not putting one over the other,” Stankey said of theatrical and streaming. “This to me seems like a very friendly and innovative approach.”

至于大流行后的世界，以及工作室的发行模式是否会恢复到以前的相似之处，Stankey说公司将“以不同的方式调整和运作模型”。总体而言，尽管“有选择权”将保持不变，但他预测。斯坦基谈到戏剧和流媒体节目时说：“我们不会互相推翻。” “在我看来，这是一种非常友好和创新的方法。”

HBO Max has gotten off to a much slower start than other direct-to-consumer streaming services. Disney+ has reached almost 74 million global subscribers in its first year, already ahead of its initial targets. It was the last of a handful of billion-dollar outlets to reach the market as traditional media companies increasingly look to close the gap with Netflix and enter the direct-to-consumer space.

与其他直接面向消费者的流媒体服务相比，HBO Max的起步速度要慢得多。 Disney +在成立的第一年就吸引了将近7400万全球订阅者，已经超出了最初的目标。随着传统媒体公司越来越希望缩小与Netflix的差距并进入直接面向消费者的领域，这是数十亿美元的市场中最后一个进入市场的公司。

Confusion over who can get access to HBO Max has been a marketing and communications challenge, executives have conceded behind the scenes even though Stankey has described the rollout as “flawless.” Only about 30% of U.S. subscribers to HBO have exercised their right to activate access to HBO Max at no extra charge.

尽管谁能获得HBO Max的使用权仍是一个难题，但高管们却在幕后承认，尽管斯坦基（Stankey）将此次推广描述为“完美无瑕”。仅约30％的HBO美国订户已行使其权利以免费激活HBO Max的权限。

At $15 a month, HBO Max is at the high end of the price scale. (Apple and Disney came to market at $5 and $7 a month, for example.) WarnerMedia and AT&T executives have repeatedly emphasized that it is a general entertainment service with dozens of original films and TV shows and a deep library.

HBO Max每月15美元，处于价格范围的高端。 （例如，苹果和迪斯尼以每月5美元和7美元的价格进入市场。）华纳媒体和AT＆amp; T高管反复强调，它是一种具有数十种原创电影和电视节目以及一个深书库的通用娱乐服务。

Stankey said the price point helps in terms of a key metric in streaming: average revenue per user. While the initial subscriber base is smaller than rivals like Disney, he maintained (without naming names) that other companies have “a flip problem,” meaning a large footprint but lower ARPU. With Max, he said, “We’re not giving it away” and the decision was made to leverage the “big, embedded base of HBO” rather than start from scratch and try to scale a direct retail service.

Stankey说，价格点有助于流媒体的一项关键指标：每位用户的平均收入。尽管最初的订户规模小于迪士尼这样的竞争对手，但他（没有命名）坚持认为其他公司存在“翻转问题”，这意味着占地面积大但ARPU较低。他说，对于Max，“我们不会放弃”，因此决定利用“庞大的HBO嵌入式基础”，而不是从头开始尝试扩展直接零售服务。

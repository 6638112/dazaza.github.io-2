How to Solve the Homeless Problem, Once and for All
如何解决无家可归的问题，一次和所有人

2021-05-12 12:33:15

California saw its homeless population rise by 31 percent even as the number of homeless declined 18 percent in the rest of the United States between 2010 and 2020.

加州在2010年至2020年间在美国的其余部分下降了18％，加利福尼亚州的无家可归人口升高了31％。

As a result, there are today 161,000 total homeless in California, with about 113,000 of them “unsheltered,” meaning they’re sleeping in tents on sidewalks, in parks, and alongside highways.

因此，加利福尼亚州今天有161,000人无家可归，其中大约113,000个“未受欢迎”，这意味着他们在人行道上的帐篷里，在公园和高速公路旁边。

In response, California Governor Gavin Newsom proposed in a press conference today to spend another $12 billion on housing and services for homelessness.

作为回应，加州州长Gavin Newsom在今天的新闻发布会上提出了一九十亿美元的家庭和服务为无家可归。

Unfortunately, he is doubling down on the exact same solutions that have worsened the problem over the last decade.

不幸的是，他在过去十年中的完全相同的解决方案上涨了一倍。

While there are homeless people in other developed nations, there exists nothing like the open drug scenes and homeless encampments that have expanded across California in recent years.

虽然其他发达国家有无家可归的人，但近年来，近年来在加利福尼亚州扩展的开放式药物和无家可归者营地等没有任何东西。

Nor does there exist a drug overdose crisis approaching ours. In the early 2000s, around 17,000 people died from drug overdoses every year in the U.S.; by 2019, the rate of death had risen four-fold, to 70,000.

毒品过量危机也没有冒险。在2000年代初，每年在美国每年从药物过量死亡约17,000人;到2019年，死亡率上升了四倍，达到70,000。

The way every other developed nation solves homelessness is through assertive case management, mandatory psychiatric care and drug treatment, and sufficient facilities of different kinds for different populations, from psychiatric hospitals to homeless shelters to permanent supportive housing.

所有其他发达国家的方式解决无家可归者是通过自信的案例管理，强制性精神病护理和药物治疗，以及不同种类的不同种类的足够设施，从精神医院到无家可归的避难所到永久支持住房。

California is overdue for a turn toward pragmatism and moderation when it comes to these issues. Californians do not want to return to mass incarceration nor to mental institutions like the kind depicted in “One Flew Over the Cuckoo’s Nest.” At the same time, we want and deserve public order, which is breaking down across the state.

加利福尼亚州逾期，转向这些问题时，转向务实和适度。加州人不想返回大规模监禁，也不要到像“一只飞过杜鹃鸟巢”所描绘的那样的心理机构。与此同时，我们想要并应得的公共秩序，这在州突破。

There is a better way. To halt the degradation of human dignity and restore public order, California must provide, and require the homeless to use, basic shelter. And the limited housing for the homeless that exists should be available as a reward for positive behavioral outcomes, like overcoming addiction, taking one’s psychiatric medications, and working.

有个更好的方法。加州必须提供人类尊严和恢复公共秩序的退化，并要求提供无家可归者的基本庇护所。而且无家可归者的有限住房应作为积极行为结果的奖励，如克服的成瘾，服用一个人的精神病药物和工作。

California’s homelessness crisis stems from the state’s lack of shelters, the lack of mandatory addiction and psychiatric services, and the absence of requirements to use them.

加州的无家可归危机源于国家缺乏庇护所，缺乏强制性成瘾和精神病服务，以及没有要求的要求。

Two-thirds of the homeless in Los Angeles struggle with addiction or mental illness and  half of San Francisco’s homeless struggle with both.

洛杉矶的三分之二无家可归者争取成瘾或精神疾病，以及旧金山的一半与两者都无家可归地斗争。

Newsom said he would put “billions” into mental health care, but California already spends 50 percent more per patient than the national average and has a  91 percent higher rate of mentally ill homeless than the rest of the U.S.

Newsom表示，他将把“数十亿美元”进入心理保健，但加州每位患者比全国平均水平更多地花费50％，并且比美国其他人的无家可归的肢体弊端更高了91％。

Newsom must know this. “There’s a shitload of money,” acknowledged the governor’s top mental health advisor, Dr. Thomas Insel, when we spoke by Zoom in late March

新闻纪必须知道这一点。 “有一股糟糕的钱，”承认总督的最高心理健康顾问托马斯·纳尔博士，当我们在3月下旬谈话时

“We think $11 billion goes into California’s state mental health budget every year,” he said. “That makes California the number one in per capita spending in the United States. No state spends as much as we do. And yet when you look at the rankings by Mental Health America, and NAMI [National Alliance on Mental Illness], we’re not in the top half.”

“我们认为110亿美元每年进入加州的国家心理健康预算，”他说。 “这使加利福尼亚州在美国的人均支出中的第一名。没有国家花费尽可能多。然而，当你看看精神卫生美国的排名和纳米[国家联盟对精神疾病]时，我们不在上半场。“

The underlying problem is that Newsom and other California leaders insist on “Housing First,” and oppose expanding shelters and the requirement that people use them. According to Housing First, homeless people should just be given their own apartments with no requirement that they address their self-destructive behaviors.

潜在的问题是，新闻和其他加州领导人坚持“住房第一”，并反对扩大庇护所和人们使用它们的要求。根据住房的说法，无家可归的人只应给自己的公寓，没有要求他们解决自我破坏性的行为。

As a result, there is a large and growing body of empirical evidence showing that Housing First may be increasing addiction and overdose deaths by making quitting drugs more difficult and using them easier.

因此，存在大量和不断增长的经验证据，表明房屋首先可能会增加成瘾并通过使戒烟更困难并且更容易使用它们来吞噬死亡。

“One potential risk [of Housing First’s harm reduction approach],” warned a multi-author  review back in 2009, “would be worsening the addiction itself, as the federal collaborative initiative preliminary evaluation seemed to suggest.”

“一个潜在的风险[住房第一是减少伤害方法]，”2009年警告了多作者审查，“将恶化成瘾本身，因为联邦协同倡议初步评价似乎建议。”

That’s what may have happened in 2020 with the program Newsom intends to scale up, Project Roomkey. During the pandemic, many homeless people were given their own hotel rooms where they overdosed and died. Had they been on the street, surrounded by people, they might have had their overdoses reversed.

这就是2020年可能发生的事情，程序新闻打算扩大，项目室。在大流行期间，许多无家可归的人被送到自己的酒店房间，他们过量和死亡。他们一直在街道上，被人们包围，他们可能会让他们的过度逆转。

One  study found that, while the Housing First group kept people in housing longer, the comparison group saw greater reductions in alcohol consumption, problematic drug use, and greater improvements to mental health, after two years.

一项研究发现，虽然住房第一组保持壳体的人们更长的时间，但比较组在两年后，对比较群体的减少量更加减少了酒精消耗，有问题的药物使用和更大的改善。

“One reason for the surprising results,” wrote the authors, “may be that aspects of the Housing First intervention, such as the privacy afforded by Housing First and harm reduction approach, might result in slower improvements around substance use and mental health.”

“令人惊讶的结果的一个原因，”作者写道，“可能是住房的第一次干预的方面，例如住房第一和伤害减少方法所带来的隐私可能导致物质使用和心理健康的改善较慢。”

In 2018, the National Academies of Sciences, Engineering, and Medicine published a  review of the scientific literature of Housing First. “On the basis of currently available research,” wrote the report’s authors, “the committee found no substantial evidence that [permanent supportive housing] contributes to improved health outcomes, notwithstanding the intuitive logic that it should.”

2018年，国家科学院，工程和医学院发表了对住房科学文学的审查。 “在目前可获得的研究的基础上，”报告的作者写道，“委员会发现没有大量证据表明[永久支持住房]有助于改善健康成果，尽管它应该是直观的逻辑。”

Housing First doesn’t even do a very good job of keeping people in housing.  In the spring of 2021, a team of Harvard medical experts found that after ten years, just  12 percent of the homeless remained housed in Housing First housing.

住房甚至没有做一个非常好的保持住房里的人。在2021年春天，一支哈佛医学专家团队发现，十年后，只有12％的无家可归者仍然容纳在住房第一住房。

California’s state auditor and the Legislative Analyst’s Office recognize the need for centralization, but say that California can improve its decentralized, county-by-county approach to homelessness through “coordination” by existing agencies.

加州的国家审核员和立法分析师的办公室认识到需要集中化，但是说加州可以通过现有机构的“协调”来改善其分散的县逐个县的无家可归者。

Such coordination efforts have been tried repeatedly and failed for over three decades. Efforts for “coordination” create a collective action problem at the agency level. Employees respond to their superiors, not their peers. Everybody knows that when you want to get something done, you need a hierarchy, with a clear line of command and with leaders at the top who have both authority and accountability.

此类协调努力已经反复审判并失败了三十年。 “协调”的努力在原子能机构层面创造了集体行动问题。员工不回应他们的上司，而不是他们的同龄人。每个人都知道，当您想完成某些事情时，您需要一个层次结构，具有清晰的指挥线以及顶级领导者，谁具有权限和问责制。

California’s homelessness crisis is also a problem of over-delegation. California counties have failed to solve the problem because they cannot solve it. Instead of governments providing such services directly, they give grants to nonprofit service providers who are unaccountable for their performance.

加州的无家可归危机也是过度代表团的问题。加州县未能解决问题，因为他们无法解决。而不是直接提供此类服务的政府，他们向非营利组织服务提供商提供赠款，这些服务提供者无法对其表现不负责任。

“There is no statutory requirement for government to address homelessness,” explained University of Pennsylvania researcher Dennis Culhane to me. “It’s mainly the domain of a bunch of charities who are unlicensed, unfunded, relatively speaking, run by unqualified people who do a shitty job.”

“政府没有法定要求来解决无家可归者，”宾夕法尼亚州大学研究员Dennis Culhane对我来说解释道。 “这主要是一群慈善机构的领域，他们是未经证实的，相对而言，由做出糟糕的工作的不合格的人来说。”

To solve the problem, the state of California needs to take over psychiatric and addiction services from the counties, significantly expand involuntary hospitalization for those who break the law, and break up the open air drug scenes and homeless encampments that endanger public safety.

为了解决问题，加利福尼亚州的状态需要接管来自县的精神病和成瘾服务，为那些违法的人显着扩大非自愿住院，并分解公共安全的露天药物场景和无家可归的营地。

The low-hanging fruit is getting the twenty-something year-old heroin and meth addicts off the street and into medically assisted treatment programs. The higher-hanging fruit is helping service-resistant people suffering from serious mental illness.

低悬挂的水果正在达到街上的二十多个夏年龄的海洛因和甲基上瘾者进入医学辅助治疗方案。较高悬挂的水果正在帮助患有严重精神疾病的服务抗性人。

Researchers have found ways to use housing to reduce addiction. Between 1990 and 2006,  researchers in Birmingham, Alabama, conducted clinical trials of abstinence-contingent housing for 644 homeless people with crack cocaine addictions. Two-thirds of participants remained abstinent after six months, a very high rate of abstinence, compared to other treatment programs.

研究人员已经找到了使用住房减少成瘾的方法。 1990年至2006年间，阿拉巴马州伯明翰的研究人员在镇静的戒指中对644名无家可归者的临床试验进行了呼吸，可卡因裂缝成瘾。与其他治疗方案相比，六个月后，三分之二的参与者仍然令人难以置信，这是一个非常高的禁欲率。

In four  randomized controlled trials, homeless people were given furnished apartments and allowed to keep them unless they failed a drug test, at which point they were sent to stay in a shelter. Sixty-five percent of participants completed the program. And participants in abstinent-contingent housing had better housing and employment outcomes than participants assigned housing for whom abstinence was not required.

在四项随机对照试验中，无家可归的人被带家具的公寓，允许保留它们，除非他们失败了药物测试，那么他们被送到庇护所。六十五名参与者完成了该计划。围困的住房的参与者比没有必要的禁欲的住房的住房更好的住房和就业结果。

In a  major recent review of the literature, out of 176 controlled studies, 151 of them, or 86 percent, found contingency management to be effective for treating addiction, with the average effect size ranging from moderate to large. It significantly increased participation in therapy, a key component of addiction recovery.

在最近的文献综述中，在176项受控研究中，其中151项，或86％，发现应急管理有效治疗成瘾，平均效果大小范围从中度到大。它显着增加了治疗的参与，成瘾恢复的关键组成部分。

Contingency management  can also reduce psychiatric hospitalizations, improve financial management, and raise quality-of-life for the mentally ill suffering substance abuse disorder.

应急管理也可以减少精神病院住院，改善财务管理，并为精神病患者滥用疾病提高生活质量。

Participants who received the contingency management intervention were 2.4 times more likely to be abstinent than the control group,  found a study using a large, randomized controlled trial among the seriously mentally ill, two-thirds of whom were homeless.

收到应急管理干预的参与者比对照组释放的可能性更大2.4倍，发现一项使用大型随机对照试验的一项研究，在严重的精神病患者中，三分之二是无家可归的。

Contingency management is based on the well-supported psychological theory that concrete reinforcements, such as housing or gift cards, are necessary to encourage positive behaviors, such as abstinence, work, or accepting medical care.

应急管理是基于支持的良好的心理理论，即需要鼓励诸如禁欲，工作或接受医疗保健等积极行为的混凝土增强剂。

A new state institution is required. Let’s call it Cal-Psych. It would be built as a skunkworks, wholly separate and superior to existing institutions, including health departments and health providers.

需要一个新的国家机构。让我们称之为Cal-Faly。它将作为臭鼬制造，完全分开，优于现有机构，包括卫生部门和健康提供者。

Cal-Psych’s CEO would be best-in-class and report directly to the governor. Cal-Psych would be built as a mobile and digital agency. Cal-Psych vans would have DMV embeds, tele-psychiatry, the ability to prescribe methadone or Suboxone, and instant access to psychiatric beds and drug treatment facilities. It would arrive immediately after people overdose from drugs to provide treatment.

Cal-Fish的CEO将是一流的，直接向州长报告。 Cal-Psy将被建造为移动和数字代理机构。 Cal-Falce Vans将有DMV嵌入，远程精神病学，开禽或亚毒品的能力，即时进入精神病床和药物治疗设施。它将在人们从药物过量以提供治疗后立即到达。

Cal-Psych would have significant buying power and be able to move clients to where they need to be for treatment. It would be able to purchase psychiatric beds, board and care facilities, and treatment facilities from across the state. And it would be able to offer the mentally ill and those suffering from substance use disorders the treatment they need, and somewhere other than in an open air drug scene.

Cal-Psy将具有重要的购买力，并能够将客户迁移到他们需要治疗的地方。它将能够购买精神病床，董事会和护理设施，以及跨国的治疗设施。它能够提供精神病患者，患有物质使用障碍的人，他们需要的治疗方法，以及除了露天药物现场的其他地方。

Cal-Psych would start in one city, learn rapidly, and then grow, ultimately taking over from the counties. We must learn from the mistakes of the past. We shut down state mental hospitals before we had created the alternative. Cal-Psych would be scaled up over time, gradually replacing various institutions that are currently doomed to failure.

Cal-Fish将从一个城市开始，迅速学习，然后成长，最终从县接管。我们必须从过去的错误中吸取教训。在我们创造替代方案之前，我们关闭了州立心理医院。 Cal-Schy会随着时间的推移而扩大，逐渐取代目前注定失败的各种机构。

Cal-Psych would do as much as legally, ethically, and practically possible to establish voluntary drug treatment and psychiatric care. It would also work with the courts and law enforcement to enforce involuntary care through assisted outpatient treatment and conservatorship.

Cal-Schy将在法律上，道德地和实际上尽可能多地建立自愿药物治疗和精神科护理。通过辅助门诊治疗和保护统一，它还将与法院和执法执行不自主。

Assisted outpatient treatment and conservatorship protect individual rights through the courts and allow non-jail and non-prison alternatives focused on psychiatric care. The ACLU has, in the past, done much good to protect rights, but they have taken an extreme position against conservatorship such that they may be actively contributing to the current crisis.

协助门诊治疗和保护统治通过法院保护个人权利，并允许非监狱和非监狱替代品关注精神科护理。在过去，ACLU有很多善于保护权利，但他们对保护统统带来了极端的位置，使他们可能会积极促进目前的危机。

And while some counties may resist Cal-Psych, many will recognize that they cannot solve the problem. They may be happy to let go of the responsibility of caring for the chronically homeless, so long as they are reassured that they will not lose funding or control over county programs for those with milder mental illness such as depression, which are best handled at the county level.

虽然有些县可能抗拒Cal-Psys，但许多人将认识到他们无法解决问题。他们可能很乐意放弃关心长期无家可归的关怀责任，只要他们放心，他们不会失去对那些具有更温和的精神疾病（如抑郁症）的县级计划的资金或控制，这是最好的处理县级。

When I spoke to Newsom’s advisor, Dr. Insel, he agreed that the state needed to make sweeping reforms. But, he said, “Everybody says the same thing to me. ‘You’re going to have to change the constitution because the way that we got to where we are was through a series of ballot measures.’”

当我与Newsom顾问博士谈话时，他同意国家所需要的彻底改革。但是，他说，“每个人对我说同样的话。 “你将不得不改变宪法，因为我们到达我们所在的方式是通过一系列选票措施。”

But that’s not as daunting a challenge as it may seem. California governors raise money and pass ballot initiatives all the time, I pointed out. In fact, in the last 20 years, several major measures put on the ballot by former Governor Jerry Brown contributed to the state’s homeless addiction crisis.

但这并不像似乎令挑战那样令人生畏。加州州长一直筹集资金并通过投票倡议，我指出。事实上，在过去的20年里，前总督杰里布朗投票的几项主要措施促使了该州的无家可归的瘾危机。

Why hasn’t Newsom been pushing ballot initiatives? After Insel somewhat halfheartedly pointed to COVID-19 and the looming recall campaign, I scowled. He knew as well as I did that Newsom has been governor for more than a year before the pandemic and had been lieutenant governor and mayor for sixteen years before that.

为什么Newsom一直推动投票倡议？在既半心弹一点地指向Covid-19和迫在眉睫的召回运动后，我皱着眉头。他知道，我确实是在大流行前一年多的州长一直在州长，并且在此之前已经被中尉和市长和市长。

Insel didn’t criticize Newsom directly, but when I read the transcript from our interview I was struck by how often he returned to one theme in particular.

Insel没有直接批评新闻，但是当我从我们的采访中阅读成绩单时，我被他特别恢复了一个主题的频率。

“It’s been really hard to get any real leadership,” Insel said. “I don’t think money is the problem. I think it’s the leadership. . . . we need state leadership . . . There’s no state leadership. . . . We have to create state leadership. . . . I don’t think it’s a money problem. I think it’s a leadership problem.”

“真正的领导力真的很难，”Insel说。 “我认为金钱不是问题。我认为这是领导力。 。 。 。我们需要国家领导力。 。 。没有国家领导力。 。 。 。我们必须创造国家领导力。 。 。 。我不认为这是一个钱问题。我认为这是一个领导力问题。“

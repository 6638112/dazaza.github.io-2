Why Red Hat killed CentOS—a CentOS board member speaks
红帽为何杀死CentOS — CentOS董事会成员发表讲话

2021-01-28 23:15:04

This morning, The Register's Tim Anderson published excerpts of an interview with the CentOS project's Brian Exelbierd. Exelbierd is a member of the CentOS board and its official liaison with Red Hat. Exelbierd spoke to Anderson to give an insider's perspective on Red Hat's effective termination of CentOS Linux in December, in which the open source giant announced CentOS Linux was to be deprecated immediately—with security upgrades to CentOS Linux 8 ending later in 2021 rather than the 2029 end of support date CentOS users expected.

今天早上，Registers的Tim Anderson发布了CentOS项目的Brian Exelbierd的访谈摘录。 Exelbierd是CentOS董事会的成员，并且是与Red Hat的官方联络人。 Exelbierd与安德森（Anderson）进行了交谈，以提供内部人士对Red Hat在12月有效终止CentOS Linux的看法，其中开源巨头宣布将立即弃用CentOS Linux，并将安全升级到CentOS Linux 8将于2021年晚些时候终止，而不是CentOS用户预期的2029年支持终止日期。

"CentOS is a [Red Hat] sponsored project," Exelbierd told the Register. "We are the funding agent (the entity which receives and disburses grants), and we also happen to be a heavy contributor. We have learned that open source communities do well with independence. We let those governing bodies govern."

＆＃34; CentOS是[Red Hat]赞助的项目，＆＃34; Exelbierd告诉名册。 ＆＃34;我们是资助机构（接受和支付赠款的实体），并且我们也恰好是一个重要的捐助者。我们了解到开源社区在独立性方面做得很好。我们让那些理事机构来治理。＆＃34;

The devil in these particular details, of course, is that "the CentOS board doesn't get to decide what Red Hat engineering teams do." This is the contribution that Exelbierd mentioned earlier—specifically, the labor of Red Hat engineering teams. According to Exelbierd, Red Hat decided "we're going to make some fundamental changes in how we direct our investment," then "went to the CentOS project and said, here is a thing Red Hat is going to do."

当然，在这些特定细节中，魔鬼是CentOS董事会无法决定Red Hat工程团队的工作。这就是Exelbierd前面提到的贡献，特别是Red Hat工程团队的工作。根据Exelbierd的说法，Red Hat决定我们将对我们指导投资的方式进行一些根本性的改变，然后进入CentOS项目并说，这是Red Hat要做的事情。＆＃34;

That thing was the cessation of Red Hat's support for CentOS Linux while prioritizing its investment in CentOS Stream, which Exelbierd describes as "critical" to Red Hat. "We laid out our case and we said that we're moving our engineering contribution, people time in some cases... we want to call your attention to them because depending on what you decide to do, there are potential liability issues that could result, so we want to make sure you have a plan."

那件事是在优先考虑对CentOS Stream的投资时，停止了对Red Hat对CentOS Linux的支持，Exelbierd将其描述为“关键”。红帽。 ＆＃34;我们整理了案子，并说我们正在转移我们的工程贡献，在某些情况下需要人们的时间...我们希望引起您的注意，因为取决于您决定做什么可能导致的潜在责任问题，因此我们要确保您有一个计划。＆＃34;

Exelbierd says that short-term profits—the migration of some end users from free use of CentOS Linux to paid RHEL subscriptions—was not a key motivator for the company. He pointed out that the email address offered for those considering a switch to RHEL doesn't go to the sales department—"it goes to me and two of my colleagues in the business unit," he said.

Exelbierd说，短期利润（一些最终用户从免费使用CentOS Linux到付费RHEL订阅的迁移）不是公司的主要动力。他指出，为那些打算改用RHEL的人员提供的电子邮件地址不会发送给销售部门，而是发送给我和该业务部门的两位同事的电子邮件地址。他说。

Rather than a bunch of small sales, Exelbierd says looking for feedback—the sort of feedback Red Hat has not traditionally received from CentOS users, most of whom "never called, never write, they don't interact with us." The feedback address, he says, "is the business model. It is absolutely not a mailing list for salespeople [...] nobody wants to go after the person with one server, two servers, 16 servers. There are definitely going to be some folks for whom their CentOS Linux... will become paid RHEL, absolutely. But our goal was not to sit down and make every CentOS Linux user a revenue RHEL customer."

Exelbierd并不是在寻找小额销售，而是在寻找反馈-Red Hat传统上从未从CentOS用户那里收到反馈，大多数CentOS用户从未打电话，从未写信，他们不与我们互动。 。＆＃34;他说，反馈地址是业务模型。绝对不是给销售人员的邮件列表，没人想去追随拥有一台服务器，两台服务器，16台服务器的人。肯定会有某些人为其CentOS Linux ...绝对会成为他们的付费RHEL。但是我们的目标不是坐下来让每个CentOS Linux用户成为RHEL的收入客户。＆＃34;

Regarding the quality and readiness of code in CentOS Stream, the forward-tracking rolling release CentOS has been replaced with, Exelbierd says "this stuff has been tested. It is not work in progress." If our customers could consume change that fast, it's what we would ship. [A] lot of CentOS Linux users [run] yum update every single night... Stream's going to do the same thing for [them]. [They're] already leading the Stream life, in a lot of ways."

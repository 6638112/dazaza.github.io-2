Car crashes killed 36,096 people in the US in 2019
车祸在2019年导致36,096人在美国死亡

2020-12-20 00:54:36

Motor vehicle traffic crashes killed 36,096 people in 2019, according to new data from the National Highway Traffic Safety Administration (NHTSA). That's a 2 percent decline from the 2018 figure and a continuation of a decades-long trend toward safer roads.

根据美国国家公路交通安全管理局（NHTSA）的新数据，2019年机动车交通事故导致3606人死亡。与2018年的数字相比下降了2％，并且延续了数十年的趋势，道路更加安全。

Traffic fatalities peaked in the late 1970s, with more than 50,000 people dying per year. Since then, annual deaths have generally been trending downwards.

交通事故死亡人数在1970年代后期达到顶峰，每年有50,000多人丧生。从那以后，每年的死亡人数一直呈下降趋势。

Progress on road safety saw a brief reversal between 2014 and 2016, when fatalities rose from 32,744 to 37,806. But since then, the figure has declined three years in a row—though it's still not below the record lows of the early 2010s.

道路安全方面的进展在2014年至2016年期间发生了短暂的逆转，死亡人数从32,744人上升至37,806人。但此后，这一数字已连续三年下降-尽管仍未低于2010年代初的纪录低点。

Progress has been even more dramatic when you factor in America's rising population and miles traveled. Fatalities per 100 million miles of driving fell from 3.35 in 1975 to 1.10 in 2019.

当您考虑到美国人口的增长和行驶里程的增长时，进步就更加显着。每1亿英里行驶中的死亡人数从1975年的3.35下降至2019年的1.10。

As I wrote a couple of years ago, a number of factors have contributed to the long-term trend toward safer roads, including cars engineered for better crash-worthiness, increasing use of seat belts and airbags, and stricter enforcement of drunk-driving laws. In the 1970s, 60 percent of traffic fatalities in the United States involved alcohol use. In 2019, it was less than 30 percent.

正如我几年前所写，许多因素促成了长期向更安全道路发展的趋势，包括为提高防撞性能，增加使用安全带和安全气囊而设计的汽车以及对酒后驾驶法律的更严格执行。在1970年代，美国有60％的交通事故死亡涉及酒精的使用。在2019年，这一比例还不到30％。

While the news is good overall, the latest NHTSA report does highlight a few areas of concern. The NHTSA says that 3,142 people died as a result of distracted driving—a 10 percent increase over the 2018 figure of 2,858. However, it's not clear if there's a long-term trend here. For example, 3,263 deaths in 2015 were attributed to distracted driving.

总体而言，这是个好消息，但最新的NHTSA报告确实突出了一些令人关注的领域。 NHTSA说，由于分心驾驶而导致3,142人死亡，比2018年的2,858人增加了10％。但是，目前尚不清楚是否存在长期趋势。例如，2015年有3263人因驾驶分散注意力而死亡。

Over the last decade, rural highways have gotten safer while urban streets have gotten more dangerous. Rural highway deaths fell from 18,089 in 2010 to 16,340 in 2019 as rural miles traveled stayed flat. In contrast, urban roadway deaths increased by 34 percent as urban miles traveled increased only 15 percent.

在过去的十年中，农村公路变得更加安全，而城市街道变得更加危险。农村公路死亡人数从2010年的18,089人下降至2019年的16,340人，原因是农村公路行驶平缓。相比之下，城市道路死亡人数增加了34％，而行驶的英里数仅增加了15％。

That's partly because significantly more pedestrians and cyclists have died on urban roadways. "Pedestrian fatalities in urban areas increased by 62 percent since 2010," NHTSA says, while they fell by 5 percent in rural areas. Similarly, urban cyclist fatalities rose by 49 percent between 2010 and 2019, while declining by almost 5 percent in rural areas.

这部分是因为明显有更多的行人和骑自行车的人在城市道路上死亡。 ＆＃34;自2010年以来，城市地区的行人死亡人数增加了62％， NHTSA说，尽管在农村地区下降了5％。同样，在2010年至2019年之间，城市骑自行车的人死亡人数增加了49％，而在农村地区则下降了近5％。

This may partly reflect the growing popularity of bicycle commuting and walkable urban neighborhoods—NHTSA's report doesn't estimate how the numbers of urban pedestrians and cyclists on the road have changed since 2010.

这可能部分反映出自行车通勤和城市步行街区的日益普及-NHTSA的报告并未估计自2010年以来城市道路上的行人和骑自行车者的人数如何变化。

In any event, there's reason to hope that better automatic emergency braking technology could help reduce deaths among pedestrians and bicyclists in the coming years. Many cars today have AEB features, but many aren't sophisticated enough to detect pedestrians and bicyclists. A new generation of AEB systems equipped with lidar sensors could greatly improve these systems over the next five years.

无论如何，有理由希望更好的自动紧急制动技术可以在未来几年内减少行人和骑自行车者的死亡。当今，许多汽车都具有自动包围曝光功能，但许多汽车还不足以检测行人和骑自行车的人。配备激光雷达传感器的新一代AEB系统可以在未来五年内大大改善这些系统。

Unfortunately the decline over time is being lost now; it looks like 2020 vehicular fatalities are trending higher, despite fewer vehicles on the road. While this seems illogical, there’s a sadly simple explanation: Those who kept going out during a pandemic were doing so recklessly and exploiting the benefits of everyone else staying home (which matches their behavior otherwise). It’s no coincidence that speeding and reckless driving rapidly rose as highways emptied, and the reckless were the ones still out and about daily...

不幸的是，随着时间的流逝，这种损失正在消失。尽管道路上的车辆较少，但看起来2020年的车辆死亡人数呈上升趋势。尽管这似乎不合逻辑，但有一个可悲的简单解释：那些在大流行期间一直出门的人鲁were地这样做，并利用其他人待在家里的好处（否则与他们的行为相匹配）。并非如此，随着高速公路的空转，超速驾驶和鲁ck驾驶迅速上升，而且鲁re的人仍然每天都在外面……

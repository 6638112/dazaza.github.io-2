Big Tech CEOs Told 'Time For Self-Regulation is Over' By US Lawmakers
大科技CEO被告知我们由美国立法者讲述“自我监管的时间”

2021-03-26 21:45:56

WASHINGTON (Reuters) - In their first appearance before Congress since Trump supporters stormed the U.S. Capitol, the chief executives of Facebook, Google and Twitter were asked by U.S. lawmakers whether their platforms bore some responsibility for the riot: “yes or no?”

华盛顿（路透社） - 由于特朗普支持者在大会之前的第一次出席，因为特朗普支持者冲动了Facebook，Facebook，Google和Twitter的首席执行官，由美国立法者询问他们的平台对骚乱的一些责任：“是或否？”

Social media has been widely blamed for amplifying calls to violence and spreading misinformation that contributed to the Jan. 6 attempt to violently overturn the election results.

社交媒体被广泛归咎于放大对暴力和传播捐赠给1月份的错误信息的调查.6试图猛烈地推翻选举结果。

Only Twitter Inc CEO Jack Dorsey replied “yes” to the question, but said the “broader ecosystem” had to be taken into account. Sundar Pichai, chief executive of Google parent Alphabet Inc, said the company always feels a sense of responsibility but it was a complex question.

只有Twitter Inc Ceo Jack Dorsey回答“是”的问题，但表示必须考虑“更广泛的生态系统”。谷歌父母字母公司的首席执行官Sundar Pichai表示，该公司总是感到责任感，但这是一个复杂的问题。

Facebook Inc’s CEO Mark Zuckerberg said his company was responsible for building “effective systems”. He also said that the rioters and former President Donald Trump should be held accountable.

Facebook Inc的首席执行官Mark Zuckerberg表示，他的公司负责建设“有效系统”。他还说，骚乱者和前总统唐纳德特朗普应该持责任。

Lawmakers widely slammed the platforms’ approach to false or dangerous content. The three companies have taken steps to curb misinformation but researchers have shown it is still widely present on the platforms.

立法者广泛抨击平台的虚假或危险内容的方法。这三家公司已经采取了措施来遏制错误信息，但研究人员已经表明它仍然广泛存在于平台上。

“We fled as a mob desecrated the Capitol, the House floor, and our democratic process,” said Democratic Representative Mike Doyle, who asked the CEOs about their responsibility. “That attack, and the movement that motivated it, started and was nourished on your platforms,” he added.

“我们逃离了一个暴徒亵渎了国会大厦，房屋地板和我们的民主进程，”民主党代表迈克多伊尔问过首席执行官的责任。 “那个攻击，以及激励它的运动开始并在你的平台上滋养了，”他补充道。

The hearing was virtual but advocacy group SumOfUs erected cut-outs of the three CEOs dressed as Jan. 6 rioters on the National Mall near the Capitol. One showed Zuckerberg as the “QAnon Shaman,” a shirtless rioter wearing horns.

听证会是虚拟的，但宣传小组舒适的三个首席执行官的剪裁裁员，作为1月6日在国会大会附近的全国商场上的6个暴徒。一个人展示了扎克伯格作为“Qanon Shaman”，一个赤膊骚扰角。

In the joint hearing, held by two subcommittees of the House Energy and Commerce Committee, lawmakers also questioned the executives on the proliferation of COVID-19 and vaccine misinformation and raised concerns about the impact of social media on children - including asking questions about Facebook’s plan to create a version of Instagram for kids.

在众议院能源和商务委员会的两个小组委员会举行的联合听证会中，立法者还质疑关于Covid-19和疫苗错误信息的高管，并提出了对社会媒体对儿童影响的担忧 - 包括提出关于Facebook计划的问题为孩子们创建一个Instagram版本。

“Your business model itself has become the problem and the time for self-regulation is over. It’s time we legislate to hold you accountable,” said Democratic Representative Frank Pallone, chair of the Energy and Commerce committee.

“您的业务模式本身已成为问题，自我监管的时间结束了。是时候我们立法抓住了责任，“能源和商业委员会主席弗兰克钯（民主代表）表示。

Some lawmakers are calling for Section 230 of the Communications Decency Act, which shields online platforms from liability over user content, to be scrapped or rejigged. There are several pieces of legislation from Democrats to reform Section 230 that are doing the rounds in Congress, though progress has been slow. Several Republican lawmakers have also been pushing separately to scrap the law entirely.

一些立法者正在呼吁沟通十足法案的第230条，该行为屏蔽了对用户内容的责任的在线平台，被报废或重新缩短。民主党人来说，改革第230条正在进行大会的几个立法，尽管进展缓慢。几家共和党立法者也在分开推动，完全推动法律。

In written testimony released on Wednesday, Facebook argued that Section 230 should be redone to allow companies immunity from liability for what users put on their platforms only if they follow best practices for removing damaging material.

在星期三发布的书面证词中，Facebook认为，第230节应该是重做，只有在他们遵循损坏材料的最佳实践时，才能获得责任的责任。

Pichai and Dorsey said in the hearing they were open to some of the changes in Facebook’s proposal. Pichai said there were some “good proposals.” Dorsey endorsed some of the suggestions from Zuckerberg but said it would be difficult to distinguish between small and large services.

PICHAI和DORSEY在听证会上说，他们对FACEBOOK的提案的一些变化开放。 pichai说有一些“良好的建议”。 Dorsey赞同Zuckerberg的一些建议，但表示难以区分小型和大型服务。

Republicans on the panel also criticized the tech giants for what they see as efforts to stifle conservative voices.

小组的共和党人也批评了科技巨头，他们认为他们认为是扼杀保守的声音的努力。

Trump was banned by Twitter over inciting violence around Jan. 6, while Facebook has asked its independent oversight board to rule on whether to bar him permanently. He is still suspended from YouTube.

Twitter在1月6日煽动暴力时被Twitter禁止了特朗普，而Facebook已经要求其独立的监督委员会批判是永久禁止他的。他仍然暂停了YouTube。

At one point in the hearing, Dorsey appeared to grow frustrated and tweeted “?” with a poll asking Twitter users to vote “yes” or “no.” There were more than 40,000 votes cast in about 30 minutes.

在听证会中的一点上，Dorsey似乎会变得沮丧和发推文“？” 询问推特用户投票“是”或“否”。 大约30分钟内有超过40,000票。

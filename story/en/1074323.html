Maximize value, not quantity
最大化价值，而不是数量

2022-02-20 23:47:53

I was chatting with a manager who was once a PO on a team I coached many years ago. This is only my best memory of the conversation (I didn't record it at the time). I may have slightly embellished it with snippets from conversations that followed over the course of days or weeks, but I try to be faithful. One day she took me aside and asked what she should be doing to accelerate the team and get more work pushed through.

我和一位经理聊天，他曾是我多年前执教的一支球队的首席执行官。这只是我对这段对话最好的记忆（当时我没有录制）。我可能会用接下来几天或几周的谈话中的片段稍微修饰一下，但我尽量做到忠实。有一天，她把我拉到一边，问我她应该做些什么来加速团队的发展，完成更多的工作。

“Nothing? But I thought that I’m supposed to be getting maximum work and speeding up the team…?”

“什么都没有？但我认为我应该得到最大的努力，加快团队的速度……”

“Nope. Your job as PO is to maximize the value of the work, not the quantity of the work. Given that they’re doing roughly the same amount of work each week (barring emergencies and vacations), your job is to make sure that what they are working on is the most valuable work that they could do that week - that it is impactful and useful.” The Product Owner is accountable for maximizing the value of the product resulting from the work of the Scrum Team. - the scrum guides

“不。作为采购员，你的工作是最大化工作的价值，而不是工作的数量。考虑到他们每周做的工作量大致相同（紧急情况和假期除外），你的工作是确保他们正在做的是他们那一周能做的最有价值的工作——这是有影响力和有用的。”产品所有者负责使Scrum团队的工作产生的产品价值最大化。-scrum指南

“I was told that I’m supposed to push for more, and there was a healthy tension between PO pushing for more and SM pushing back.”

“有人告诉我，我应该争取更多，PO争取更多和SM反击之间存在着健康的紧张关系。”

“Yeah. That’s nonsense. You’re all on the same team and should be all working for the same results. If you push people beyond their capacity to work, they’ll turn in crappy work and that won't be the best value for time spent. Don’t do that.”

“是的。那是胡说八道。你们都在同一个团队中，应该为同样的结果而工作。如果你把人们推到工作能力之外，他们会交上糟糕的工作，而这并不是花费时间的最佳价值。不要这样做。”

“OMG! That’s GREAT! I was so worried because I don’t know how to make people work harder. All I have to do is deal with content and not execution?”

“天哪！太好了！我太担心了，因为我不知道如何让人们更努力地工作。我所要做的就是处理内容，而不是执行？”

“That’s incredibly good news. I just felt a lot of stress roll off of my shoulders. That’s great! I can totally do this job!”

“这是一个难以置信的好消息。我只是感觉到肩膀上有很多压力。太棒了！我完全可以做这项工作！”

And she did. Soon she was known for being the best PO in the building, having the best teams, having solid relationships with her teams, and making wise decisions.

她做到了。很快，她就以成为大楼里最好的PO、拥有最好的团队、与团队建立牢固的关系以及做出明智的决策而闻名。

Her career as a product manager was launched there and has progressed to this day.

她的产品经理生涯就是在那里开始的，并一直发展到今天。

Your job is not to fight your team. You’re all supposed to work together on this thing.

你的工作不是对抗你的团队。你们应该在这件事上合作。

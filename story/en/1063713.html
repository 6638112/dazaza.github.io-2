Humans Can Learn How to 'Echolocate' in 10 Weeks, Experiment Shows
实验表明，人类可以学习如何“偏离”'蚀刻'

2021-06-06 06:43:38

With enough training, most humans can learn how to echolocate, using their tongue to make clicking sounds, and interpreting the sounds of the echoes that come back, reflected from the surrounding environment.

有足够的培训，大多数人可以学习如何使用他们的舌头来点击声音，并解释从周围环境反映的回声的声音的声音。

In as few as 10 weeks, researchers were able to teach participants how to navigate obstacles and recognize the size and orientation of objects using the rebounding calls of their clicks. The experiment involved 12 participants who'd been diagnosed as legally blind during their childhood, and 14 sighted people.

在10周内，研究人员能够教授参与者如何使用点击击败呼叫来教导障碍物，并识别对象的大小和方向。实验涉及12名参与者＆＃39; D被诊断为在童年期间合法失明，14人遭到了一定程度的人。

Echolocation is a skill we usually associate with animals such as bats and whales, but some blind humans also use the echoes of their own sounds to detect obstacles and their outlines. Some use the tapping of a cane or the snapping of their fingers to make the necessary noise, while others use their mouths to make a clicking sound.

Echolocation是我们通常与蝙蝠和鲸鱼等动物联系的技能，但是一些盲人的人也使用自己声音的回声来检测障碍及其轮廓。有些人用舔拐杖或抓住手指的敲击来做出必要的噪音，而其他人则用嘴巴来点击声音。

Despite how useful this skill can be, very few blind people are currently taught how to do it. Expert echolocators have been trying to spread the word for years now, and this new study suggests a simple training schedule is all that's needed.

尽管这项技能有多有用，但很少有盲人目前被教导如何做到这一点。专家Echolocators现在一直在尝试传播这个词，这项新的研究表明简单的训练时间表是所需的。

"I cannot think of any other work with blind participants that has had such enthusiastic feedback," says psychologist Lore Thaler from Durham University in the UK.

＆＃34;我不能想到与盲人参与者有任何其他热情的反馈，＆＃34;心理学家称来自英国达勒姆大学的心理学家拉洛雷尔。

Over the course of 20 training sessions, which were about 2 to 3 hours long, researchers found that blind and sighted participants, both old and young, all improved considerably at click-based echolocation.

在20个培训课程的过程中，大约2到3个小时，研究人员发现，盲人和年轻人的参与者都在基于点击的回声机会上大大提高。

For weeks, participants were trained to navigate virtual mazes – corridors arranged in T-intersections, U bends, and zig-zags – and identify the size and orientation of objects using mouth clicks.

几周后，参与者培训以导航虚拟迷宫 - 排列在T-Condersects，U弯曲和Zig-Zags中的走廊 - 并使用嘴巴单击识别对象的大小和方向。

In the final two sessions, participants had their new navigation skills tested in a virtual maze they'd never tackled before. Even while blinded in this unknown environment, collisions were fewer than they had been at the start of the program.

在最后的两个会议上，参与者在虚拟迷宫中测试了他们的新导航技能，他们以前从未解决过。即使在这个未知的环境中蒙蔽了蒙蔽，碰撞率少于程序的开始。

Clearly, the echoes of their own clicks were helping people navigate the course with greater ease than before.

显然，他们自己点击次数的回声正在帮助人们比以前更轻松地导航课程。

In fact, the authors found these newly trained echolocators performed nearly as well in the maze as seven expert echolocators, who had been using this skill for years.

事实上，作者发现这些新培训的呼应器几乎在迷宫中表现为七位专家呼应器，多年来一直在使用这项技能。

In additional tests to determine the shape and orientation of certain surfaces, participants in the study actually performed equally to the experts.

在额外的测试中，以确定某些表面的形状和取向，研究中的参与者实际上是对专家进行的。

Previous studies have also found sighted individuals can learn click-based echolocation in a series of training sessions, but this is the first study to test whether the results extend to blind people and people of various ages as well.

以前的研究也发现有视觉的个人可以在一系列培训课程中学习基于点击的回声机，但这是测试结果是否扩展到盲人和各种年龄的人的研究。

The visual parts of the brain are what allow echolocators use to 'see' the world around them, and it's been unclear until now if those who grow up without vision can use the same neural networks to the same degree.

大脑的视觉部分是允许呼应器使用的蚀刻＆＃39;查看＆＃39;他们周围的世界，它甚至不清楚，直到现在，如果长大没有愿景的人可以使用相同的神经网络到同样的程度。

What's more, many people lose their vision and hearing as they age, and the older a person is, the less plastic their brain.

什么＆＃39;更多，许多人失去了他们的愿景和听到他们的年龄，而一个人的年龄是，他们的大脑的塑料越少。

This can make learning new skills more difficult as you get older, but the new research suggests that's not a limiting factor in learning echolocation. In the study, blind individuals as old as 79 were able to pick up the skill with the right training.

这可以让学习新技能更加困难，但新的研究表明，这不是学习回声的限制因素。在这项研究中，旧的盲人79人能够接受正确的培训技能。

When the authors analyzed their results (of their admittedly small experiment), they found older age in itself was not linked to more collisions in the maze task.

当作者分析他们的结果时（允许的小实验），他们发现年龄较大的年龄本身并没有与迷宫任务的更多碰撞有关。

"Importantly, when we quantified the degree to which participants improved from session 1 to session 20 in their abilities across each of the tasks, there was no evidence for an association between age and performance in the practical tasks," the authors write.

＆＃34;重要的是，当我们量化参与者在每个任务中的能力中量化参与者从会议上提高到会议的程度时，在实际任务中，没有证据表明年龄和表现之间的关联;作者写道。

Younger age did allow some participants to finish the mazes faster, but practically, the authors say, "training led to remarkable behavioral changes for all participants", regardless of age.

年龄较小确实允许一些参与者更快地完成迷宫，但实际上，作者说，＆＃34;培训导致所有参与者的非凡的行为变化＆＃34;无论年龄如何。

Three months after the training sessions ended, blind participants said they had experienced improved mobility using echolocation. In a follow-up survey, 10 out of the 12 participants said the skill had benefited their independence and wellbeing.

培训课程结束后三个月，盲目参与者表示，他们经历了使用回声定位的改善的流动性。在一项后续调查中，12名参与者中有10人表示，这项技能使他们的独立和福祉受益。

"We are very excited about this," says Thaler," and feel that it would make sense to provide information and training in click-based echolocation to people who may still have good functional vision, but who are expected to lose vision later in life because of progressive degenerative eye conditions."

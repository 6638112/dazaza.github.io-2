U.S. Retrieves Millions in Ransom Paid to Colonial Pipeline Hackers
美国赎金中的数百万支付给殖民地管道黑客

2021-06-08 06:03:26

WASHINGTON—U.S. law-enforcement officials said Monday they had recovered millions of dollars in digital currency paid to the hackers who hit Colonial Pipeline Co. with a ransomware attack last month, a strike that prompted the shutdown of the main conduit for gasoline and diesel fuel to the U.S. East Coast.

华盛顿州。律法执法官员周一表示，他们已经恢复了数百万美元的数字货币支付给殖民地管道公司的黑客，上个月遭遇赎金软件攻击，这是一个促使汽油和柴油燃料主管队的罢工罢工的罢工东海岸。

Investigators seized nearly 64 bitcoin, valued at roughly $2.3 million, that were allegedly the proceeds from the ransom hack on Colonial Pipeline, the Justice Department said.

司法部长据称，调查人员占据了大约24米的比特币，大约有230万美元，据称，据称，殖民地管道上赎金的收益。

“The extortionists will never see this money,” Stephanie Hinds, acting U.S. attorney for the Northern District of California, told reporters. “This case demonstrates our resolve to develop methods to prevent evildoers from converting new methods of payment into tools and extortion for undeserved profits.”

“勒索者永远不会看到这笔钱，”斯蒂芬妮的后斯，即加州北部地区的美国律师告诉记者。 “这种情况表明我们的决心开发方法，以防止恶作者将新的付款方式转换为工具和敲诈勒索的不足的利润。”

Colonial Pipeline, which transports gasoline, diesel, jet fuel and other refined products from the Gulf Coast to Linden, N.J., was shut down for six days in May as the company responded to the ransomware attack. The stoppage spurred a run on gasoline along parts of the East Coast that pushed prices to the highest levels in more than six years and left thousands of gas stations without fuel.

将汽油，柴油，喷气式燃料和其他精炼产品从海湾海岸运送到林登的殖民地管道，在该公司回应赎金软件攻击时，在5月份关闭了六天。停工停止沿着东海岸的一部分刺激汽油，将价格推向超过六年以上的最高水平，留下了数千个没有燃料的燃气站。

Colonial Pipeline CEO Joseph Blount told The Wall Street Journal last month that his company paid a $4.4 million ransom to the hackers because executives were unsure how badly the cyberattack had breached its systems, and consequently, how long it would take to bring the pipeline back online.

殖民地管道首席执行官约瑟夫Blount在上个月告诉华尔街日报，他的公司他的公司向黑客支付了440万美元的赎金，因为高管不确定网络攻击违反其系统的糟糕程度，并因此需要多长时间才能将管道恢复在线。

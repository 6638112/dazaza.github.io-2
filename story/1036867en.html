约翰·霍普金斯大学（Johns Hopkins）在周日发表了这项研究，认为科维奇离我们所知道的灾难还很遥远。我会为您总结一下，也可以提供引号，但老实说，您只需要自己阅读一下，因为它令人耳目一新。出于某种原因，现在已从Johns Hopkins网站上删除了原始文章。幸运的是，互联网是永远存在的，并且可以通过Wayback Machine来使用。这是本文的全文：

According to new data, the U.S. currently ranks first in total COVID-19 cases, new cases per day and deaths. Genevieve Briand, assistant program director of the Applied Economics master's degree program at Hopkins, critically analyzed the effect of COVID-19 on U.S. deaths using data from the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC) in her webinar titled "COVID-19 Deaths: A Look at U.S. Data."

根据新数据，美国目前在总COVID-19病例，每天新病例和死亡人数中排名第一。霍普金斯大学应用经济学硕士学位课程的助理计划主任Genevieve Briand在其名为“ COVID-19死亡：看一下美国数据。”

From mid-March to mid-September, U.S. total deaths have reached 1.7 million, of which 200,000, or 12% of total deaths, are COVID-19-related. Instead of looking directly at COVID-19 deaths, Briand focused on total deaths per age group and per cause of death in the U.S. and used this information to shed light on the effects of COVID-19.

从3月中旬到9月中旬，美国的总死亡人数已达到170万，其中20万（占总死亡人数的12％）与COVID-19相关。 Briand没有直接查看COVID-19的死亡人数，而是关注了美国每个年龄段和每个死亡原因的总死亡人数，并使用此信息阐明了COVID-19的影响。

She explained that the significance of COVID-19 on U.S. deaths can be fully understood only through comparison to the number of total deaths in the United States.

她解释说，只有通过与美国的总死亡人数进行比较，才能完全了解COVID-19对美国死亡的重要性。

After retrieving data on the CDC website, Briand compiled a graph representing percentages of total deaths per age category from early February to early September, which includes the period from before COVID-19 was detected in the U.S. to after infection rates soared.

在CDC网站上检索数据后，Briand编制了一张图表，代表从2月初到9月初每个年龄段的总死亡百分比，其中包括从在美国检测到COVID-19到感染率飙升之后的时期。

Surprisingly, the deaths of older people stayed the same before and after COVID-19. Since COVID-19 mainly affects the elderly, experts expected an increase in the percentage of deaths in older age groups. However, this increase is not seen from the CDC data. In fact, the percentages of deaths among all age groups remain relatively the same.

令人惊讶的是，在COVID-19前后，老年人的死亡人数保持不变。由于COVID-19主要影响老年人，因此专家预计老年人群中的死亡百分比会增加。但是，从CDC数据中看不到这种增加。实际上，所有年龄段的死亡百分比都保持相对不变。

"The reason we have a higher number of reported COVID-19 deaths among older individuals than younger individuals is simply because every day in the U.S. older individuals die in higher numbers than younger individuals," Briand said.

布莱恩德说：“我们报告的老年人中COVID-19死亡人数高于年轻人的原因仅是因为美国每天的死亡人数都比年轻人多。”

Briand also noted that 50,000 to 70,000 deaths are seen both before and after COVID-19, indicating that this number of deaths was normal long before COVID-19 emerged. Therefore, according to Briand, not only has COVID-19 had no effect on the percentage of deaths of older people, but it has also not increased the total number of deaths.

Briand还指出，在COVID-19之前和之后都发生了50,000至70,000例死亡，这表明在COVID-19出现之前很久，这种死亡人数是正常的。因此，根据Briand的说法，COVID-19不仅对老年人的死亡百分比没有影响，而且也没有增加死亡总数。

These data analyses suggest that in contrast to most people's assumptions, the number of deaths by COVID-19 is not alarming. In fact, it has relatively no effect on deaths in the United States.

这些数据分析表明，与大多数人的假设相比，COVID-19的死亡人数并不令人震惊。实际上，它对美国的死亡没有影响。

This comes as a shock to many people. How is it that the data lie so far from our perception?

这让很多人感到震惊。数据与我们的看法相去甚远？

To answer that question, Briand shifted her focus to the deaths per causes ranging from 2014 to 2020. There is a sudden increase in deaths in 2020 due to COVID-19. This is no surprise because COVID-19 emerged in the U.S. in early 2020, and thus COVID-19-related deaths increased drastically afterward.

为了回答这个问题，Briand将注意力从2014年到2020年的每个原因造成的死亡转移了。由于COVID-19，2020年的死亡人数突然增加。这并不奇怪，因为COVID-19于2020年初在美国出现，因此与COVID-19相关的死亡此后急剧增加。

Analysis of deaths per cause in 2018 revealed that the pattern of seasonal increase in the total number of deaths is a result of the rise in deaths by all causes, with the top three being heart disease, respiratory diseases, influenza and pneumonia.

对2018年每个原因造成的死亡进行的分析显示，死亡总数季节性增加的模式是各种原因导致死亡人数增加的结果，其中排名前三位的是心脏病，呼吸系统疾病，流感和肺炎。

"This is true every year. Every year in the U.S. when we observe the seasonal ups and downs, we have an increase of deaths due to all causes," Briand pointed out.

布莱恩德指出：“每年都是如此。在美国，每年我们都观察到季节性起起落落，由于各种原因，死亡人数在增加。”

When Briand looked at the 2020 data during that seasonal period, COVID-19-related deaths exceeded deaths from heart diseases. This was highly unusual since heart disease has always prevailed as the leading cause of deaths. However, when taking a closer look at the death numbers, she noted something strange. As Briand compared the number of deaths per cause during that period in 2020 to 2018, she noticed that instead of the expected drastic increase across all causes, there was a significant decrease in deaths due to heart disease. Even more surprising, as seen in the graph below, this sudden decline in deaths is observed for all other causes.

当Briand查看该季节的2020年数据时，与COVID-19相关的死亡人数超过了心脏病死亡人数。这是非常不寻常的，因为心脏病一直是导致死亡的主要原因。但是，在仔细查看死亡人数时，她注意到了一些奇怪的现象。布赖恩德（Briand）比较了2020年至2018年这段时间内每个病因的死亡人数时，她注意到，并非所有病因的预期急剧增加，而是由心脏病引起的死亡人数显着减少。如下图所示，更令人惊讶的是，在所有其他原因下都观察到死亡的突然下降。

Graph depicts the number of deaths per cause during that period in 2020 to 2018.

该图描述了2020年至2018年这段时期内每个原因的死亡人数。

This trend is completely contrary to the pattern observed in all previous years. Interestingly, as depicted in the table below, the total decrease in deaths by other causes almost exactly equals the increase in deaths by COVID-19. This suggests, according to Briand, that the COVID-19 death toll is misleading. Briand believes that deaths due to heart diseases, respiratory diseases, influenza and pneumonia may instead be recategorized as being due to COVID-19.

这种趋势与以往所有年份的模式完全相反。有趣的是，如下表所示，其他原因造成的死亡总数下降几乎完全等于COVID-19造成的死亡总数上升。根据Briand的说法，这表明COVID-19的死亡人数具有误导性。 Briand认为，由心脏病，呼吸系统疾病，流感和肺炎引起的死亡可以归类为COVID-19。

The CDC classified all deaths that are related to COVID-19 simply as COVID-19 deaths. Even patients dying from other underlying diseases but are infected with COVID-19 count as COVID-19 deaths. This is likely the main explanation as to why COVID-19 deaths drastically increased while deaths by all other diseases experienced a significant decrease.

CDC将与COVID-19相关的所有死亡简单地分类为COVID-19死亡。甚至死于其他潜在疾病但感染了COVID-19的患者也算作COVID-19死亡。这可能是为什么COVID-19死亡率急剧上升而所有其他疾病的死亡率却显着下降的主要原因。

"All of this points to no evidence that COVID-19 created any excess deaths. Total death numbers are not above normal death numbers. We found no evidence to the contrary," Briand concluded.

“所有这些都表明没有证据表明COVID-19造成了任何额外的死亡。总死亡人数没有超过正常死亡人数。我们没有发现相反的证据，” Briand总结道。

In an interview with The News-Letter, Briand addressed the question of whether COVID-19 deaths can be called misleading since the infection might have exacerbated and even led to deaths by other underlying diseases.

在接受《新闻通讯》的采访中，布莱恩德谈到了COVID-19死亡是否可以被称为误导性的问题，因为这种感染可能会加剧甚至导致其他潜在疾病的死亡。

"If [the COVID-19 death toll] was not misleading at all, what we should have observed is an increased number of heart attacks and increased COVID-19 numbers. But a decreased number of heart attacks and all the other death causes doesn't give us a choice but to point to some misclassification," Briand replied.

“如果[COVID-19死亡人数]完全没有误导，那么我们应该观察到的是心脏病发作的数量增加和COVID-19的数量增加。但是心脏病发作的数量和所有其他死亡原因的减少却没有“给我们一个选择，但要指出一些错误的分类，”布莱恩德回答。

In other words, the effect of COVID-19 on deaths in the U.S. is considered problematic only when it increases the total number of deaths or the true death burden by a significant amount in addition to the expected deaths by other causes. Since the crude number of total deaths by all causes before and after COVID-19 has stayed the same, one can hardly say, in Briand's view, that COVID-19 deaths are concerning.

换句话说，仅当COVID-19对死亡的影响增加了，除了其他原因造成的预期死亡之外，COVID-19对美国的死亡是有问题的。由于在COVID-19之前和之后各种原因造成的总死亡人数中的粗略数字保持不变，因此在Briand看来，几乎不能说COVID-19的死亡是令人担忧的。

Briand also mentioned that more research and data are needed to truly decipher the effect of COVID-19 on deaths in the United States.

Briand还提到，需要更多的研究和数据来真正破译COVID-19对美国死亡的影响。

Throughout the talk, Briand constantly emphasized that although COVID-19 is a serious national and global problem, she also stressed that society should never lose focus of the bigger picture — death in general.

在整个谈话中，Briand始终强调，尽管COVID-19是一个严重的国家和全球性问题，但她还强调，社会绝不应失去对更大局面的关注-一般而言是死亡。

The death of a loved one, from COVID-19 or from other causes, is always tragic, Briand explained. Each life is equally important and we should be reminded that even during a global pandemic we should not forget about the tragic loss of lives from other causes.

Briand解释说，亲爱的人死于COVID-19或其他原因，总是很悲惨。每个生命都同样重要，我们应该提醒我们，即使在全球大流行期间，我们也不应忘记其他原因造成的悲惨生命损失。

According to Briand, the over-exaggeration of the COVID-19 death number may be due to the constant emphasis on COVID-19-related deaths and the habitual overlooking of deaths by other natural causes in society.

根据Briand的说法，COVID-19死亡人数的过度夸张可能是由于不断强调与COVID-19相关的死亡，以及社会上其他自然原因对死亡的习惯性忽视。

During an interview with The News-Letter after the event, Poorna Dharmasena, a master's candidate in Applied Economics, expressed his opinion about Briand's concluding remarks.

活动结束后接受新闻通讯记者采访时，应用经济学硕士研究生Poorna Dharmasena表达了对Briand总结的看法。

"At the end of the day, it's still a deadly virus. And over-exaggeration or not, to a certain degree, is irrelevant," Dharmasena said.

Dharmasena说：“归根结底，它仍然是一种致命的病毒。在某种程度上，过度夸张与否与否无关紧要。”

When asked whether the public should be informed about this exaggeration in death numbers, Dharmasena stated that people have a right to know the truth. However, COVID-19 should still continuously be treated as a deadly disease to safeguard the vulnerable population.

当被问及是否应告知公众死亡人数的这种夸大时，达摩塞那指出人们有权了解真相。但是，仍应继续将COVID-19视为致命疾病，以保护弱势群体。

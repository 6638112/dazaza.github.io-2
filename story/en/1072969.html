Photographer's death casts harsh light on the cold streets of Paris
摄影师之死给巴黎寒冷的街道投下了刺眼的光芒

2022-02-14 08:10:04

PARIS — On a cold night last month, René Robert, an 85-year-old Swiss photographer, fell onto the pavement of a busy Parisian street and remained there for hours — seemingly unassisted, apparently ignored by a stream of passers-by. When a medical team eventually arrived, Mr. Robert was found to be unconscious and died later in the hospital of severe hypothermia.

巴黎——上个月的一个寒冷的夜晚，85岁的瑞士摄影师雷内·罗伯特（Rene Robert）摔倒在巴黎一条繁忙街道的人行道上，在那里停留了数小时——似乎无人帮助，似乎被一大群路人忽视。当医疗队最终抵达时，罗伯特被发现昏迷，后来因严重体温过低在医院去世。

Many in France were appalled by what appeared to be a blatant lack of compassion in the nation’s capital. But making the episode even more poignant were the identities of those who first found him and called for help — two homeless people all too familiar with the daily indifferences of bystanders.

许多法国人对法国首都明显缺乏同情心感到震惊。但让这一事件更加令人痛心的是那些最先找到他并寻求帮助的人的身份——这两个无家可归的人对旁观者的日常冷漠非常熟悉。

“They say, ‘I am almost invisible, I feel invisible,’” Christophe Robert, the executive director of the Abbé Pierre Foundation, a housing advocacy organization, said of his conversations with homeless people. “And that really resonated with this incident.”

“他们说，‘我几乎看不见了，我觉得自己看不见了’，”住房倡导组织ABB彼埃尔基金会的执行主任Christophe Robert说，他与无家可归的人交谈。“这真的引起了这次事件的共鸣。”

The two homeless people — a man and a woman — called emergency services after spotting Mr. Robert, who was best known for his black-and-white photography of flamenco’s most famous artists, while walking their dog in the early hours of Jan. 20.

这两名无家可归的人——一男一女——在1月20日凌晨看到罗伯特在遛狗时打电话给急救中心。罗伯特以拍摄弗拉门戈最著名艺术家的黑白照片而闻名。

“Even if you are assaulted, no one moves a finger,” said Fabienne, 45, one of the two homeless people who found the photographer around 5:30 a.m. on a stretch of street that includes a cocktail bar, a smartphone repair shop and an eyewear store.

45岁的法比安说：“即使你遭到袭击，也没人动手指。”法比安是两个无家可归的人之一，他在凌晨5:30左右在一条街道上找到了这位摄影师，那里有一家鸡尾酒酒吧、一家智能手机修理店和一家眼镜店。

The exact circumstances of the episode are still unclear, but Mr. Robert was in a state of severe hypothermia when an ambulance finally picked him up, according to the Paris Fire Department. For those close to Mr. Robert, that strongly suggests he spent most of the night sprawled on the busy sidewalk.

目前尚不清楚这一事件的具体情况，但据巴黎消防局称，罗伯特当时处于严重的体温过低状态，救护车最终将他救起。对于罗伯特身边的人来说，这有力地表明他晚上大部分时间都四肢伸开躺在繁忙的人行道上。

On a recent chilly, windswept afternoon, Fabienne said she had been living on the streets of this central Paris neighborhood for the past two years, after she was fired from a woodworking job at a shipyard on France’s Atlantic coast. She declined to give her last name.

在最近一个寒冷、狂风肆虐的下午，法比安说，在法国大西洋海岸一家造船厂的木工工作被解雇后，她过去两年一直住在巴黎市中心的街道上。她拒绝透露自己的姓氏。

Her home is a small camping tent pitched on a narrow pedestrian street that runs along the side of a church, several hundred feet from where Mr. Robert fell, on Rue de Turbigo.

她的家是一个小露营帐篷，搭在一条狭窄的步行街上，沿着一座教堂的一侧，距离罗伯特先生倒下的地方几百英尺（约合1米）。

Fabienne, who was wearing baggy purple pants and a scarf wrapped around her head for protection against the cold, said that Mr. Robert and his partner were some of the few neighborhood regulars who stopped by to chat or give some change, but that most people walked by without a glance.

法比安穿着宽松的紫色裤子，头上缠着一条围巾以御寒。她说，罗伯特先生和他的伴侣是为数不多的来这里聊天或找零钱的常客，但大多数人走过时都不看一眼。

In January, a nighttime census led by Paris City Hall estimated that about 2,600 people were living on the streets of the French capital.

今年1月，由巴黎市政厅领导的一项夜间人口普查估计，大约有2600人生活在法国首都的街道上。

Mr. Robert was born in 1936 in Fribourg, a town in western Switzerland, and in the 1960s, he settled in Paris, where he became captivated by flamenco and started documenting famous singers, dancers and guitarists, like Paco de Lucía, Enrique Morente and Rocío Molina.

罗伯特于1936年出生在瑞士西部的弗里堡，20世纪60年代在巴黎定居，在那里他被弗拉门戈迷住了，并开始记录著名的歌手、舞蹈家和吉他手，如帕科·德卢西亚、恩里克·莫伦特和罗西奥·莫利纳。

Mr. Robert was found with small bruises on his head and arm, but his cash, credit card and watch were still on him, suggesting he was not mugged, but might have felt ill and crumpled to the ground.

人们发现罗伯特的头部和手臂上有小伤痕，但他的现金、信用卡和手表仍在他身上，这表明他没有被抢劫，但可能感觉不舒服，瘫倒在地。

The Paris hospital authority, citing medical confidentiality, declined to say if doctors who examined him had been able to assess why he had fallen or how long he had spent on the street. The Paris Police Department also declined to comment.

巴黎医院管理局（Paris hospital authority）以医疗保密为由，拒绝透露为他做检查的医生是否能够评估他摔倒的原因，以及他在街上呆了多长时间。巴黎警察局也拒绝置评。

Michel Mompontet, a journalist and friend who first drew attention to Mr. Robert’s death in social media posts that went viral, said it was a cruel irony that Mr. Robert — a “humanist” who relished the emotional openness of flamenco artists — seemed to have suffered from the apathy of bystanders.

记者和朋友米歇尔·莫庞特（Michel Mompontet）是在社交媒体上迅速传播的帖子中首次引起人们对罗伯特之死的关注的，他说，罗伯特——一个喜欢弗拉门戈艺术家情感开放的“人道主义者”——似乎受到了旁观者的冷漠，这是一个残酷的讽刺。

“The only person who had the humanity to call emergency services was a homeless person,” said Mr. Mompontet, who works for France’s national radio and television broadcasters and who had known Mr. Robert for the past 30 years. His video denouncing Mr. Robert’s death was widely shared online.

莫姆庞特先生说：“唯一有人性去呼叫紧急服务的人是一个无家可归的人。”他在法国国家广播电视台工作，在过去30年里认识罗伯特先生。他谴责罗伯特之死的视频在网上被广泛分享。

“We have gotten used to something intolerable,” Mr. Mompontet said, “and this death could help us reconsider this indifference.”

“我们已经习惯了一些无法忍受的事情，”蒙庞特说，“这次死亡可能会帮助我们重新考虑这种冷漠。”
